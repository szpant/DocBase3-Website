    Show cause (Marine Safety)
    Show cause (Marine Safety)
      The Chief Executive or General Manager MSQ may cancel or suspend a Qld RMDL or Qld PWCL through a show cause action. The action is based on an evaluation of the fitness of the person to hold the licence.

    Disqualification
    Disqualification
      A court issued order disqualifying a person absolutely or for a stated period from holding or obtaining a Qld marine licence.

      A court ordered disqualification will result in the cancellation of all Qld marine licences held by the customer. The customer may apply for a new marine licence at the end of the disqualification period.

    Marine Infringement Notice
    Marine Infringement Notice
      On the spot fine issued by enforcement partners and shipping inspectors for a breach of marine safety laws. Common offences include speeding, unlicensed operation, unregistered ship.

Show Cause cancellation or suspension
Show Cause
Requirements of the Chief Executive/GM MSQ
Requirements of the Chief Executive/GM MSQ
    If the Chief Executive or General Manager MSQ consider that reasonable grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel a person's RMDL and/or PWCL, the person must be given written notice to invite them to show cause, within a specified time (at least 30 days), why the proposed action should not be taken.
Grounds
Grounds
    Marine Safety:
    The Chief Executive or General Manager MSQ may cancel/suspend a RMDL or PWCL if the licence holder:
    • Has contravened marine safety legislation;
    • Was issued a licence in error or
    • As a result of false or misleading information;
    • Obtained the licence by improper means, e.g. fraudulent Statement of Competency;
    • Has not complied with a licence condition;
    • Has been convicted of an indictable offence;
    • Has not paid any outstanding fees
    • Has been convicted of a breath analysis offence (drink driving);
    • The show cause grounds are located in legislation.
    Note:MSQ manages show cause actions related to marine safety.

      Not Marine Safety:
      The Chief Executive may cancel/suspend a RMDL or PWCL if the licence holder's:
      • Digital photo given at the time of the application does not verify the person's identity (failed 1:N image verification).
      • The department is no longer satisfied with the holders identification for the licence.
      Note:CSC manages show cause actions not related to marine safety

    Show Cause Marine Licence Suspension
    Show Cause Marine Licence Suspension
      A show cause action may result in one or all of a licence holder's marine licences being suspended for a defined period.
      • MSQ will record the marine licence suspension in TICA.
    Show Cause Marine Licence Cancellation
    Show Cause Marine Licence Cancellation
      A show cause action may result in one or all of a licence holder's marine licences being cancelled.
      • A marine licence cancelled through a show cause process is cancelled indefinitely.
      • MSQ will record the marine licence cancellation in TICA.
    Effective date of a Show Cause
    Effective date of a Show Cause
      A suspension or cancellation by show cause commences on the date specified in the written notice.
    Decision, Review and Appeal
    Decision, Review and Appeal
      Decision
      Decision
        • The licence holder is given at least thirty (30) days to show cause why the proposed action should not be taken.
        • After consideration of all written representation MSQ will make a decision.
        • The licence holder is advised of the decision.

      Review and Appeal
      Review and Appeal
        The licence holder may apply to MSQ for a review of the decision within 28 days of the decision notice.
        • To stop the decision from taking effect the licence holder may apply to the Qld Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) to have the decision stayed.

          Note:If the decision is stayed by the court the customer is entitled to an MLI.
        • If the customer requires more information about QCAT go to the website: www.qcat.qld.gov.au or by telephone on 1300 753 228.
        • On request for a review of the decision MSQ has 28 day to confirm or amend the decision or to substitute another decision.
        • To appeal the reviewed decision the licence holder may apply to the QCAT within 28 days of the reviewed decision notice.

        Requests for review of decisions by Maritime Safety Queensland should be made in writing to:
        General Manager Maritime Safety Queensland

    Disqualification by court order
    Disqualification by Court Order
    Grounds
    Grounds
      The Magistrate Court may disqualify a person from holding or obtaining a Qld marine licence for:
      • Contravention of the general safety obligation of ship owners and masters about condition of ships;
      • Contravention of the general obligations on persons involved with operation of ship to operate it safely;
      • Contravention of a safety equipment obligation;
      • Contravention of registration obligations;
      • Operation of ship as master etc, without a licence;
      • Unlawful interference with a ship;
      • Operating ship other than under a restricted licence;
      • Non compliance with the collision regulations;
      • Operating a ship whilst under the influence of liquor or drugs or with a prescribed concentration of alcohol in blood or breath;
      • Failure to supply specimen of breath in relation to the operation of a ship;
    Disqualification process
    Disqualification process
      Disqualification process
        Cancellation
        Cancellation
          MSQ Compliance branch records the court ordered disqualification in TICA and cancels all recreational marine licences.

          MSQ Compliance branch will also record the cancellation of any commercial licences in the MSQ commercial licensing database.

        Licence not in a Cancelled Status on TRAILS/TICA
        Licence not in a Cancelled Status on TRAILS/TICA
          If a Marine Licence holder attends a CSC and presents a Court Order that sanctions a disqualification, restricted marine licence or a variation of a restricted marine licence that is not on TICA the operator is to:
          • Contact Maritime Safety Queensland Compliance Unit (Maritime Projects & Compliance) to arrange for this to be placed on TICA. A copy of the court order will need to be emailed.
          • The MSQ Manager Compliance will contact the CSC by telephone or email once the details of the Court Order including the text restriction is on TICA.
      Effective date of a disqualification
      Effective date of a disqualification
        Cancellation occurs on the date specified on the court order.

        A court may order sanctions to be served concurrently or cumulatively.
      Appeal
      Appeal
        If a customer wishes to appeal a court ordered disqualification they must:
        • Appeal within twenty-eight (28) days of the court order;
        • Lodge a completed appeal disqualification order at the District or Supreme Court (form available from Courts).

        An Appeal Hearing will be scheduled and the Notice of Appeal will be forwarded to MSQ for action.
        • Upon Notice of the appeal MSQ Compliance Branch will re-instate the marine licence/s in TICA.
        • Once the appeal hearing has concluded, MSQ Compliance Branch will make the necessary changes to the customer's marine licence record in TICA.
        • Contact Maritime Safety Queensland Compliance Unit (Maritime Projects & Compliance).
      Return of Marine Licence Indicator
      Return of Marine Licence Indicator
        A current DL with MLI or Smartcard MLI is required to be returned if a marine licence is cancelled.
        • An enforced return notice is sent requesting the return of the DL with MLI or Smartcard MLI when the licence/s is cancelled in TICA.
        • The DL with MLI or Smartcard MLI is required to be returned within 7 days.

      Application for a marine licence after disqualification
      Application for a marine licence after disqualification
        After a disqualification period has elapsed a person may apply for the granting of a new marine licence/s.

        MSQ policy applies different requirements to the granting of a marine licence after a period of disqualification. When granting a marine licence after disqualification:
        • Equivalent interstate boat licences and PWC licences are not recognised;
        • Interstate and National commercial marine master licences are not recognised.
