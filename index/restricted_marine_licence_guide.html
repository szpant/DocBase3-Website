Restricted Marine Licence Guide (3356)


About Restricted Marine Licences
Definitions
Marine Definitions
Marine Definitions
    Marine Definitions
What it is
Marine licence restricted by court order (restricted marine licence)
Marine licence restricted by court order (restricted marine licence)
    A court may make an order that a person who has been disqualified from holding or obtaining a Queensland marine licence be granted a restricted marine licence.
    A restricted marine licence allows a person to operate a ship in specified circumstances. The circumstances must be directly connected with the person's means of earning a living.

    Note:A restricted marine licence is recorded as a conditional licence.
When to issue
When to issue
When to issue
    A Restricted marine licence is to be issued to a customer who:
    • Has been disqualified by a Queensland court from holding or obtaining a marine licence; and
    • Who has obtained an order from the Court for the grant of a restricted licence.
When required
When required
When required
    A restricted marine licence is required when a disqualified person needs to operate a ship during their disqualification period.

    Operation of the ship must be directly connected with their means of earning a living, e.g. a person who lives on an island who must use a ship to commute to work.
Getting a Restricted Marine Licence
Apply for Court Order
Apply for a Court order
Apply for a Court order
    A licence holder who requires a restricted marine licence must apply to the Court for an order to be granted a restricted marine licence. Application is made at the hearing, before the Magistrate determines the penalty.

    TMR cannot grant a restricted marine licence without an order from the court.

    Note:Once the Magistrate disqualifies the applicant, they may not apply for a restricted marine licence.
Obtaining an RML
Obtaining a Restricted Marine Licence
Obtaining a Restricted Marine Licence
    An applicant who has an order from a Queensland court for the issue of a restricted marine licence must attend a TMR CSC or agent and must have their court order to obtain the licence.
Where to apply
Where to apply
Where to apply
    All TMR Customer Service Centres and on-line QGAP agencies may accept applications and payment for a Marine Licence.

    Customers in remote Qld areas
Eligibility
Eligibility
Eligibility
    The applicant must provide the court order for the grant of a restricted marine licence.
Not eligible
Licensees not eligible for a Restricted RMDL/PWCL
Licensees not eligible for a Restricted RMDL/PWCL
    A person who does not have an order from a Queensland court ordering the issue of a restricted marine licence is not eligible for a restricted marine licence.
T.R.C.Transaction Requirements Checklist
Licence Period
Licence Period
Licence Period
    A restricted Recreational Marine Licence is granted for One (1) year from date of application.

    Disqualification greater than one year
    • If the period of disqualification is greater than one year the customer must obtain another restricted marine licence after the first restricted marine licence has expired. The new restricted marine licence must be subject to the restrictions stated in the court order.

    Disqualification less than one year
    • If the period of disqualification is less than one year the restricted marine licence is still granted for one year.
      • The customer may apply for the grant of a new marine licence after the disqualification period has elapsed.
      • A fee is payable.
Using a Restricted Licence
Conditions of use
Conditions of use
Conditions of use
    When granting a restricted marine licence, the terms of the restriction (as shown on the court order) must be recorded as a TEXT condition in TICA.
    • The Text field must also show the expiry date for the restricted marine licence.
      Restricted Marine Licence holders must;
      • Comply with any licence conditions and/or restrictions imposed by the court order and only operate in accordance with these conditions and/or restrictions, and
      • Carry the court order with them while operating the ship.

    Conditions recorded on the Smartcard and smartcard chip
    Conditions recorded on the Smartcard and smartcard chip
      The smartcard for a restricted marine licence will have:
      • A 'Y' in the conditions field and is displayed on:
        • The face of Smartcard MLI, or
        • The rear of the driver licence with MLI.

      The MLIR will display the conditions at the bottom in full detail.
      The smartcard chip will show 'TEXT' in the condition field.

      Note:The expiry date for the restricted marine licence is NOT displayed or recorded on the smartcard

Disobey Restriction
Operating outside a Restriction
Operating outside a Restriction
    Where the holder of a restricted marine licence has been convicted of operating a ship contrary to the restrictions of a specific court order, the person may be issued with a complaint and summons to appear in court before a Magistrate as prescribed.
Varying Court Order
Changes to Restriction - Varying the court order
Changes to Restriction - Varying the court order
    A restricted marine licence holder may apply to the Court (in the district where they live) for a change to the court ordered restrictions and/or conditions due to a change in their means of earning their livelihood.
    • The Application for an Order Varying Restrictions and/or conditions on a Restricted Marine Licence is lodged at a Magistrates Court and there is no requirements for TMR Staff to lodge any applications on TICA for this process.
Records not updated
Court order or restriction/variation not on TICA/TRAILS
Court order or restriction/variation not on TICA/TRAILS
    If a Marine Licence holder attends a CSC and presents a Court Order that sanctions a restricted marine licence or a variation of a restricted marine licence that is not on TICA the operator is to:
    • Contact Maritime Safety Queensland Compliance Unit (Maritime Projects & Compliance) to arrange this to be placed on TICA.
    • A copy of the court order will need to be emailed.

    The MSQ Maritime Officer will contact the CSC by telephone or email once the details of the Court Order including the text restriction is on TICA.
Transactions details
Application after completion of disqualification
Application after completion of disqualification
    Once a customer has finished their disqualification period they may apply for another marine licence. Different application requirements apply after a period of disqualification.
    • Full requirements for the licence must be met.

    Refer: Marine Licence > 4. Issue Marine Licence > The RMDL/PWCL Guide > Transaction > Grant
Voluntary Surrender
Voluntary Surrender
    A Restricted marine licence may be surrendered at any time for any of the following reasons:
    • Voluntary surrender by the customer;
    • On behalf of the deceased;
    • Merged;
    • Multi-licence holder.
Refunds
Refunds
    There are no refunds given for the unused period of a surrendered or cancelled restricted marine licences.
Progressing from a restricted RMDL/PWCL
Progressing
On expiry of a restricted marine licence
On expiry of a restricted marine licence
    Once a restricted marine licence has expired the customer will require a new marine licence if they wish to continue to operate a ship.

    Disqualification Period has ended
    Disqualification Period has ended
      Once the disqualification period has ended they may apply for a marine licence. Different application requirements apply to the granting of a marine licence after a period of disqualification. Full requirements for the licence must be met.

      Customers whose disqualification period has ended are not required to wait for the expiry of their restricted marine licence before being eligible to apply for an unrestricted marine licence.

      E.g. A customer disqualified for 6 months is granted a restricted marine licence for 1 year. They may apply for an unrestricted marine licence after the 6 disqualification period has elapsed.

      Note:If the customer continues to use the restricted marine licence after their disqualification period has ended they must operate the ship in accordance with the licence restrictions.

      Refer: Marine Licence > 4. Issue Marine Licence > The RMDL/PWCL Guide > Re-application after period of cancellation

    Disqualification period has not elapsed
    Disqualification period has not elapsed
      A new restricted marine licence can be issued if:
      • The disqualification period is still enforce when the restricted marine licence expires, and
      • The court order authorises the issue of a restricted marine licence.
