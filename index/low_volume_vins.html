Low Volume VINs (3255)

What are theyA Low Volume VIN (LVV) is a VIN issued to manufacturers who produce less than 500:
  • 500 non-motorised vehicles (trailers <=4.5t) per annum
  • 100 motorised vehicles (scooters, custom motorcycles)

LVV's are also issued when the original identifiers are missing.
Types of LVV's
  • Standard LVVs are issued by TMR
  • Unique LVVs are issued to vehicle manufacturing individuals/organisations who require specific details in the VIN decode (e.g. Axle assembly or braking system). NEVDIS will be notified by the manufacturer of the code.
When required
TMR Issued LVV
TMR Issued LVV
    A TMR LVV is to be issued:

    By a SPECPRO operator if the trailer ATM is <=4.5t and:
      • Trailer has never been previously registered or allocated and stamped with an identifier, or
      • No identifiers or previous evidence of registration can be found for the trailer and customer can provide evidence of purchase.

    By the VIU if:
      • A surrogate VIN is authorised by QPS
      • The vehicle is an individually constructed vehicle
      • The trailer has an ATM >/=4.5t and the customer has:
        • An Identification Plate Approval or
        • A declaration from a registered Road Vehicle Certification System (RVCS) agent.

        A customer can only receive a maximum combination of three TMR issued low volume VINS or Trailer chassis numbers per calendar year. System enhancements have been put in place to enforce this rule.

        Customers who require more than 3 TMR identifiers must be referred to NEVDIS.

        The limit is based on the type of identifier not the type of trailer:
        • A customer may be issued two LVV's and 1 Trailer chassis numbers in a calendar year
        • A customer cannot be issued 3 LVV's for a Box trailer then a further 3 LVV's for a Boat trailer

        This limit applies to the SPECPRO profile only, the VIU are able to exceed this limit.

    Manufacturers should be provided with the following information documents:

    Low Volume Manufacturer NEVIDIS Hand Out
NEVDIS Issued LVV
NEVDIS Issued LVV
    NEVDIS is responsible for LVVs for:
    • New motorised vehicles (e.g. scooters)
    • All imported vehicles (including trailers)
    • Low volume manufacturers who have a World Manufacturer Identifier (WMI)
    • Trailers with an ATM >4.5t

How to apply
TMR LVV
TMR LVV
    Trailer with an ATM </=4.5t
    Trailer with an ATM </=4.5t
      The applicant must:
      • Meet standard EOI
      • Complete the LVV application form for a trailer with an ATM </=4.5t (F3648)
      • If they are not the manufacturer declare that they have inspected the trailer and cannot sight any existing identifiers

    Trailer with an ATM >4.5t
    Trailer with an ATM >4.5t
      Only VIU issued LVV's can be used to create trailers with an ATM >4.5t.
      The applicant must:
      • Meet standard EOI
      • Complete the LVV application form for a trailer with an ATM >4.5t (F5005)
      • Provide either:
      • Their Identification Plate Approval number
      • A declaration from a registered Road Vehicle Certification System (RVCS) agent

      The operator must contact the VIU who will verify the eligibility of the trailer and issue the LVV. The operator can use the search TMR Identifier functionality in TICA to print the LVV Notification Report for the customer.

      The VIU only authorises the issue of a LVV to a trailer >4.5t in special circumstances. For example, a customer (with supporting documentation) wishes to register a trailer >4.5t and requires a VIN prior to applying for an identification plate approval (IPA) from the Department of Infrastructure and Transport (DoIT) in Canberra.

      If this is not the case and the customer cannot provide proof of previous registration:
      • Advise them to contact an engineer. A list of engineers is available at http://rvcs-prodweb.dot.gov.au/ (To search Select "List of Agents" > Press Ctrl + F and Enter (07) to locate Queensland agents)
      • The engineer will certify that the trailer complies with ADR's, attach a compliance plate and issue a VIN

      LVV's issued in error by a CSC to a trailer with an ATM >4.5t will require the customer to make a new LVV application to the VIU and provide the supporting documents.

      Due to the format of the LVV a new VIU LVV will need to be issued and the customer have this number stamped on the chassis and the trailer identification plate prior to registration.

NEVDIS LVV
NEVDIS LVV
    Manufacturers requiring more than 3 LVVs in a calendar year must apply directly to NEVDIS via email to create their own LVV series with a World manufacturer Indicator (WMI) that uniquely identifies them.

    Note:Once a manufacturer has been issued with their own WMI they can no longer obtain LVVs at a Customer Service Centre.
