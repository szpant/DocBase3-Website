About the RMDL/PWCL

What it is
What it is
What it is
    A Recreational Marine Driver Licence (RMDL) is a licence that allows a person to operate a Queensland regulated ship (recreational and other).

    A Personal Watercraft Licence (PWCL) is a licence that allows a person to operate a Personal Watercraft (e.g. Jet Skis).
Why Required
Why Required
Why Required
    These licences ensure that operators:
    • Possess the required competencies;
    • Have knowledge of marine safety legislation;
    • Understand their general safety obligation.
When Required
Equivalent Marine Licence NOT Held
Equivalent Marine Licence NOT Held
    A Recreational Marine Driver Licence is required to operate a Queensland regulated ship boat which is powered by an engine power greater than 4.5kw (over 6HP).

    A Personal Watercraft Licence is required to operate a PWC (e.g. Jet Skis).
Equivalent Marine Licence Held
Equivalent Marine Licence Held
    There is no requirement for the holder of an equivalent interstate or foreign Recreational Marine Licence or holder of a Queensland, AMSA or interstate or foreign Commercial Marine Masters Licence to convert to a Queensland Recreational Marine Driver Licence (RMDL).

    There is no requirement for the holder of an equivalent interstate or foreign PWC licence to convert to a Queensland Personal Watercraft Licence (PWCL).

    However, if the foreign RMDL or PWCL holder becomes a permanent Australian Resident or Citizen their authority may be withdrawn. Refer: Marine Licence > 2. Commercial and Non Qld Marine licences > Foreign Licences

    Note: To satisfy the requirement to hold an RMD when applying for a PWCL, an equivalent interstate RMDL or commercial marine masters licence can be used.

Where to apply

Eligibility
Eligibility criteria
Eligibility criteria
    All requirements listed in this section must be met at time of application. If the customer has not met all requirements:
    • The application must not be accepted;
    • No details are to be recorded on TRAILS/TICA

    Eligibility Criteria
    Age
    Recreational Marine Driver Licence - Age
    Recreational Marine Driver Licence - Age
      To be eligible to apply for a RMDL the applicant must;
      • Be at least sixteen (16) years of age (at time of application)

      Note:Applicant may complete the required competencies before they are 16, however, the competency certificate is only valid for 6 months.

    Personal Watercraft Licence - Age
    Personal Watercraft Licence - Age
      To be eligible to apply for a PWCL the applicant must;
      • Be at least sixteen (16) years of age (at time of application)

      Note:Applicant may complete the required competencies before they are 16, however, the competency certificate is only valid for 6 months.
    Competency
    Recreational Marine Driver Licence
    Recreational Marine Driver Licence
      An applicant who holds a recognised equivalent interstate Recreational Marine Licence or a Queensland, interstate or national commercial Marine Masters Licence do not require a BoatSafe Statement of Competency.

      Recognised Equivalent Licences must be current or expired less than 5 years.

      Note:Junior licences are not recognised.

    Personal Watercraft Licence
    Personal Watercraft Licence
      A recognised equivalent interstate Recreational Marine Licence or a Queensland, interstate or national commercial Marine Masters
      Licence can be used to satisify the prequisite requirements to hold a RMDL.

      Interstate and foreign PWCL holders do not require a PWC BoatSafe Statement of Competency.

      Recognised Equivalent Licences must be current or expired less than 5 years.
    Overseas Marine Driver Licences
    Overseas Marine Driver Licences
      A person who holds a Recognised overseas marine licence will need to contact an approved BoatSafe Training Provider to assess recognition of prior learning before applying for a Queensland RMDL.

      A list of current BoatSafe Training Providers is available on the TMR website.

      Refer: Transaction Requirements Checklist

      Note:Course fees vary between training providers. Please refer customers to individual BoatSafe Training Providers directly for their fees.
    Recognised Licences
    Personal Watercraft Licence - licence requirements
    Personal Watercraft Licence - licence requirements
      Applicants must hold a current Qld RMDL or equivalent interstate RMDL or Commercial Marine Masters Licence to be granted a PWCL.
    Required to hold
    Boatsafe Statement of Competency
    Boatsafe Statement of Competency
      Applicants for a RMDL or PWCL must provide a current BoatSafe Statement of Competency (if required) issued by a BoatSafe Training Provider or BoatSafe Assessor.

      A Statement of Competency is:
      • Not a licence to operate a recreational vessel/personal watercraft.
      • Valid for six (6) months from the date of issue
      • Can be issued to a person under the age of 16 however, a marine licence cannot be issued until the applicant is sixteen (16) years of age.

      If customer presents a Statement of Competency on the next business day (i.e. expires on a weekend, or one day after expiry), Managers can use their discretion to make a decision. If the SOC is accepted, the expiry date should be amended and an email of manager's approval attached to filing.

      Commercial Marine Master Licence
      Commercial Marine Master Licence
        A commercial marine master licence holder will require a PWC Boatsafe Statement of Competency to obtain a PWCL.
        • Only the original (white copy) of a Statement of Competency is acceptable as proof of competency when granting a licence.
        • If the customer has lost their Statement of Competency they are to be directed back to the issuing BoatSafe Training Provider for a replacement.

      Alterations, Corrections and Amended Statement of Competency
      Alterations, Corrections and Amended Statement of Competency
        Any alterations, corrections and amendments to a Statement of Competency RMDL and PWCL for a BoatSafe training course must be:
        • dated, and
        • initialled by the Boatsafe Training Provider

        If any alteration is not dated and initialled by the BoatSafe Training Provider, the Statement cannot be accepted and the customer is to be referred back to the BoatSafe Training Provider.

        From the 1 January 2018, any alterations, corrections or amendment must now be stamped with the BoatSafe Training Organisation's logo/name.

    No marine sanctions
    No marine sanctions
    No marine sanctions
      A marine licence applicant must not:
      • Be serving any Queensland marine licence sanctions e.g. disqualification, show cause cancellation, or suspension; or
      • Have been disqualified from holding or obtaining a marine licence by an interstate court within the last 5 years; or
      • Have had an interstate marine licence cancelled or suspended within the last 5 years.

      Refer: Marine Licence > 1. Marine Licensing > Marine Enforcement Guide and Marine Licence > 2. Commercial and Non-Qld Marine Licences > Commercial and Non Qld Marine Licences

Application for RMDL/PWCL when Driver Licence is suspended
Application for RMDL/PWCL when Driver Licence is suspended
    If a customer holds a suspended driver licence they will need to:
    • Produce their QLD driver licence or evidence of identity;
    • Satisfy marine licence application requirements;
    • Pay the prescribed marine licence fee.

    The customer will not be eligible to apply for a driver licence with Marine Licence Indicator (MLI) until the current driver licence suspension period is completed. The customer will be issued with a Marine Licence Indicator Receipt (MLIR). This will provide evidence of their marine licence until a driver licence with MLI is issued.

    Note:Should the MLIR be lost, stolen or destroyed during thesuspension period of the driver licence, the licence holder can apply for and be issued with a replacement MLIR report free of charge.

    Issuing driver licence with MLI when suspension complete
    Issuing driver licence with MLI when suspension complete
      Once the suspension is completed, the customer may apply for a replacement driver licence with an MLI by:
      • Completing a Replacement Driver Licence application form F3006

        Note:The driver licence is fee free as the fee was paid when the marine licence was granted.

      Suspension expired but a new suspension applied
      Suspension expired but a new suspension applied
        If the customer completes their current DL suspension but is suspended again before they have applied for their driver licence with MLI, they will be required to pay the replacement fee to obtain a driver licence with MLI.

      No longer hold a Driver Licence
      No longer hold a Driver Licence
        If the customer no longer holds a driver licence (for whatever reason) they may apply for a Smartcard MLI by completing the Replacement Marine Licence Indicator form (F4817). No fee is payable.

      Driver licence - Lost, Stolen or Destroyed
      Driver licence - Lost, Stolen or Destroyed
        If the customer returns after driver licence suspension to add the MLI and their driver licence smartcard has been lost, stolen or destroyed a separate replacement driver licence fee is to be paid. The smartcard fee paid when the marine licence was granted cannot be used for replacing the smartcard product.
Issue period
Licence Term
Licence Term
    Recreational Marine Driver Licences and Personal Watercraft Licences are granted for life, that is, they do not require renewal.

    A restricted RMDL or PWCL is issued for 1 year.

    Note:Five (5) year licences issued/renewed between 1/1/1996 and 18/2/2000 were automatically converted to lifetime licences.

    Replacement MLI while driver licence is suspended
    Replacement MLI while driver licence is suspended
      If the marine licence was granted during the current driver licence suspension period the customer may be given a replacement MLIR. No fee is payable.

      If the marine licence was not granted during the current driver licence suspension period, the customer may apply for an ROI statement. A fee applies

      The customer may apply for a replacement driver licence smartcard with MLI when their driver licence suspension is complete. A fee applies.

    Marine licence holders who do not have a smartcard with MLI may obtain a smartcard with MLI at any time.
    Applying for a smartcard MLI
    Applying for a smartcard MLI
      A customer may apply for the smartcard MLI if:
      • They hold a marine licence but do not hold a driver licence;
      • Have had their driver licence cancelled but continue to hold a marine licence;
      • Their marine licence indicator has expired.

      Use Recreational Marine Driver Licence/Personal Watercraft Licence and Marine Licence Indicator Application form F2334. No fee is payable.

    Applying to have MLI included on driver licence
    Applying to have MLI included on driver licence
      A customer who has a marine licence that is not currently indicated on their driver licence may apply to have the MLI code added at any time.

      Use Replacement driver licence form F3006. A replacement driver licence fee applies.

      Note:The MLI will automatically be added to the driver licence at renewal

Surrender
    A Marine Licence can be surrendered at any time for any of the following reasons:
    • Voluntary surrender by the customer;
    • On behalf of the deceased;
    • Merged;
    • Multi-licence holder.

Refuse to Grant
Refuse to Grant
    A refusal to grant means the application for a marine licence has been accepted and considered and the grant of the licence has been refused on certain grounds. A Refuse to Grant decision can be appealed.

    Refuse application for Marine Licence (Safety)
    Refuse application for Marine Licence (Safety)
      Maritime Safety Queensland may refuse an application for the granting of a marine licence if it is satisfied the applicant has:
      • Contravened marine safety legislation;
      • Had an authority or marine licence under marine safety legislation cancelled or suspended;
      • Been convicted of an indictable offence;
      • Not paid fees as required by marine safety legislation.

      CSO Role and Responsibilities
      CSO Role and Responsibilities
        If a CSO identifies possible grounds to refuse an application for a marine licence they should contact the Director (Vehicle and Vessel Standards):
        Maritime_standards@tmr.qld.gov.au
        Phone: (07) 3066 3881
      MSQ Role & Responsibilities
      MSQ Role & Responsibilities
        If possible grounds exist to refuse a marine licence application Maritime Safety Queensland is responsible for reviewing and investigating the matter. Refusals may concern matters of marine safety or licence candidate medical fitness.

      Notification of Refusal
      Notification of Refusal
        The Director (Vehicle and Vessel Standards) will communicate the grounds for the refusal to the licence applicant in accordance with the regulation.

        This letter will be sent within fourteen days of making the decision. Licence applicants will also be informed of their right to appeal.

      Appeals Process
      Appeals Process
        The licence holder may apply for a review of the decision made by the General Manager MSQ or CEO within 28 days of the decision notice.

        On request for a review of the decision MSQ/TMR has 28 days to confirm or amend the decision or to substitute another decision.

        To appeal the reviewed decision the licence holder may apply to the Qld Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) within 28 days of the reviewed decision notice.

        If the customer requires more information about QCAT go to the website: www.qcat.qld.gov.au or by telephone on 1300 753 228

        Requests for a review of a decision made by Maritime Safety Queensland should be made in writing to:

        General Manager
        Maritime Safety Queensland
        GPO Box 2595
        Brisbane Q 4001

        Requests for review of a decision made by the CEO or a departmental officer as a delegate of the CEO should be made in writing to the Business Area Manager (BAM) or Regional Director if you do not have a BAM in your region.

      Approval of Application
      Approval of Application
        If the application is to be granted, the applicant will be advised in writing to attend the originating office.
        • The application documentation and a letter outlining the decision will be forwarded to the office.
        • The originating office will be kept informed of the status of the application.

    Waterski Licence
    Waterski Licence
      Prior to 1979 a separate licence was issued for the towing of water skiers. This ceased on 01 July 1979. After this date applicants were issued with a marine licence which allowed the holder to operate a speed boat and private towing of water skiers.

      Validity of Water Ski Licence Post 1979
      Validity of Water Ski Licence Post 1979
        All water ski licences current at 01 July 1979 are now lifetime licences.

        Customers with expired licences at that time were given the option to obtain a lifetime licence by filling the application form and paying the application fee. The expired licence was taken as proof of a previous test being passed.

        Anyone still holding a water ski licence that expired before 01 July 1979 must meet full requirements to get a RMDL.
