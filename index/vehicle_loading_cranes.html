Vehicle Loading Cranes (7362)

What is itA Vehicle Loading Crane (VLC) is a crane mounted on a vehicle or trailer to load and unload that vehicle or trailer and may be used for other lifting purposes within its rated capacity.



Stabilisers are used to increase stability but not used to hoist or level.
Outriggers are extendable members attached to a crane or hoist which rest on supports at the outer ends to increase stability.

Relevant licences / competencies to operate a VLCVehicle loading crane operators must be trained and competent in operating the crane. Training received covers the controls, instruments, working load limits, load charts, safe working procedures for slinging and lifting and any operating limitations of each type of crane they operate.

A person must hold a High Risk Work (HRW) licence to operate a VLC that has a capacity of 10 metre tonnes or more. This can be a:
  • VLC HRW License, or
  • one of the four slewing mobile crane HRW licenses

Further information is available at https://www.worksafe.qld.gov.au/licensing-and-registrations/work-health-and-safety-licences/what-licence-do-i-need/cranes-and-hoists/other-cranes.
Joint Inter-agency CampaignIn QLD, between 2009 to 2018, three fatalities and several incidents of extensive property damage on public roads has occurred as a result of VLC stabiliser arms unintentionally extending during travel.

A Joint Interagency VLC compliance campaign, between WHSQ and TMR Compliance, will commence 01 August 2018 to prevent and control the risks of unintentional extending of manually operated VLC stabilisers and outriggers.

VLC Assessment ToolPart A of the VLC Assessment Tool must be completed by all Transport Inspectors during on-road intercepts and vehicle inspections.

The form must be completed electronically via desktop or I Pad for every VLC inspection.

All submitted forms are forwarded to WHSQ. However, if enforcement action is taken as a result of conducting the VLC Assessment, a WHS Site Inspection to be undertaken by WHSQ will be initiated.

VLC Stabilizers Locking and Alarms
Locking Mechanisms
Locking Mechanisms
    Manually operated stabilisers rely solely on the operator securing the locking mechanism to ensure the stabiliser remains in the travel position. The stabiliser can extend:
    • if the stabiliser is not returned to and effectively locked into the travel position
    • if the stabiliser locking mechanism fails
    • due to incidental forces caused by, for example, the vehicle going over a bump or around a corner.

    Hydraulically operated stabilisers are required to be locked in the travel position during road travel.

    The locking mechanism for manually extended VLC stabilisers should include two separate latching devices for each stabiliser and at least one of these should be automatically operated (e.g. a manually operated securing pin and an automatic spring latch).

    Note: Extreme care should be taken with VLCs that incorporate a two-stage telescopic extension that has additional locking pins. This is to ensure both the main and second stage extensions are secured properly for transport.
Alarms
Alarms
    The 2014 version of the Australian Standard AS1418.11-2014 Part 11: Vehicle-loading cranes specifies the provision of an indicator to show that the stabilisers are not locked in the transport position. Cranes manufactured to comply with the current standard should include a warning system visible and audible to the driver (i.e. a warning light with a buzzer in the truck cabin).
Assessing Stabilizer Locking
Inspect the restraint mechanisms
Inspect the restraint mechanisms
    • Is the stabilizer restrained effectively?
    • Are suitable restraining mechanisms provided?
    • Are the locking mechanisms damaged?
    • Are both primary and secondary locking mechanisms provided?
    Note: Stabilizers on VLCs with hydraulic extension systems are unlikely to extend during road travel due to the valving and resistance from the hydraulic fluid.
Driver Demonstration
Driver Demonstration
    Instruct the driver to demonstrate the operation of the stabilizers and restraining mechanisms
    • Do not provide assistance as the driver must demonstrate their competency and that the restraining mechanisms are effective.
    • When in the restrained position, have the driver confirm the stabilizers can’t be pulled past the locking mechanisms.
    • Then have the driver release the primary and demonstrate the stabilizer can’t be pulled through the secondary.
Check Alarms
Check Alarms
    Where alarms are provided confirm these are working effectively
    Alarms should activate when the stabilizer/s are not in the travel position.

Appendix Q NHVIM
Appendix Q NHVIM

    Applicable to all QLD Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) and TMR Inspections, Appendix ‘Q’ of the National Heavy Vehicle Inspection Manual provides information on the requirements for stabilisers/outriggers mounted on VLCs.

    The objective is to ensure all stabilisers/outriggers are retracted and securely locked to prevent travel while the vehicle is in motion.
