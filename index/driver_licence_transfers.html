Driver Licence Transfers (3169)


What it isA Qld driver licence may be issued to a person holding a current or expired < 5 years Interstate, New Zealand or Foreign driver licence.

A Qld driver licence may also be issued to current serving members of the Australian Defence Force (ADF) who hold a current ADF driver licence.

The place of issue will determine if a Written and/or Practical Driving Test is required.
Refer: Written Road Rules Tests Practical Driving Tests

TICAWhen transferring a non Qld licence to a Qld licence, TICA will calculate:
  • The amount of tenure met by the licence holder
  • The type and expiry date of the Qld licence to be issued from the following information entered by the operator:
Issue date
Issue date
    The licence issue date is an optional field and represents the original issue date of the licence being transferred.

    This date may be earlier than the issue date of the current licence being presented, especially in the case of P licences issued for less than the required P period or learner licences that have been renewed.

    It is important to note that to ensure correct tenure periods are calculated, the original issue date must be entered when transferring a provisional licence.

    You are not required to obtain an original licence issue date that is more than 5 years before the transaction date.

    If no date is entered it will default to the same date as the effective date.
Licence effective date
Licence effective date
    The licence effective date is a mandatory field and represents the effective date of the licence being transferred. Examples of the effective date are:
    • Licence renewal date
    • Duplicate licence issue date
    • The same date as the issue date if it is the customer's first licence
Licence expiry date
Licence expiry date
    It is important to note that the licence expiry date field is mandatory.

    If the licence is a lifetime licence, you must enter a date of 31/12/2999.

    The actual expiry date of the licence should be entered unless there is a system restriction. For example - the Queensland licence overlaps the interstate licence and the expiry date has to be recorded as the day before the issue date of the Queensland licence.
Invalid tenure
Invalid tenure
    Total period of time that the licence was under disqualification, suspension, cancellation and / or expired. Only include invalid tenure periods that occurred between the issue date and licence expiry dates that are being recorded.

    Note:Does not include any period where a licence holder is disqualified but the licence is not cancelled.
P expiry date
P expiry date
    The expiry date of the provisional period if licence being transferred is a provisional.
a
Provisional Licences
Provisional Licences
    A P1/P2 provisional, P1/P2 probationary, or open licence will be issued determined by the persons age and period licence has been held.

    Note:If the customer is under 18 years they must not be issued or be permitted to upgrade to a P2 type licence until they turn 18 years.

    Refer: Provisional/Probationary Licence Guide > Tenure & Licence Progression > Time Required to Hold Prov/Prob & Progression
Open Licences
Open Licences
    The new effective date field is also required for open licence transfers. An instance when you may need to obtain and record the issue date as well as the effective date is if the person wants a class upgrade and the effective date recorded will not meet the required tenure for a future class upgrade.

You will need to contact the relevant jurisdiction to request further details if:
  • You confirm with the customer that their licence was issued before the issue/effective date shown on their licence or on NEVDIS and the date is required to help calculate tenure.
  • The issue / effective date is not recorded on the licence or NEVDIS.
  • The customer declares prior driving disqualifications, cancellations or suspensions.
  • For Open licence holders - the time spent on the interstate Open licence (obtained from the issue/effective date) is only used when calculating tenure when the customer wishes to upgrade to a heavy vehicle class or add an RE learner.

    Therefore the open licence transfer;
    • Can still be processed using the current processing date as the effective date. The licence can then be issued immediately.
    • The customer is to be advised that should they wish to upgrade/add a class, then TMR will need to contact the interstate jurisdiction at that time and verify the effective/issue dates for correct tenure calculation.

    Note:When the interstate details are returned, the operator will Update (not invalidate) the NQPR screen with the correct details including invalid tenure periods. Retain evidence of confirmation in daily filing.

    Note:If the customer is under 20 years they must not be issued or be permitted to upgrade to an open type licence until they turn 20 years.

Surrender and validity of Interstate licence on transfer

Validity of New Zealand or Foreign licence on transfer
Validity of New Zealand or Foreign licence on transfer
    Once a NZ or Foreign driver licence holder is issued with a Queensland driver licence, they cannot drive in Qld using their NZ or Foreign licence.

    A Foreign licence issued by Ontario, Canada is required to be surrendered to TMR at time of transfer to a Queensland driver licence.

    There is no requirement to surrender the licence on TICA or issue a surrender receipt. If a customer refuses to surrender their Ontario licence, staff are not to insist on the surrender or refuse to process the transaction.

    TMR must mail the Foreign licence and a covering letter* to:
    Ausroads Program Manager, Registration and Licensing
    c/ - Ausroads
    Suite 2, Level 9
    287 Elizabeth Street
    Sydney NSW 2000

    *Wording to be used in the covering letter:
    "Please find enclosed Ontario licence number <insert licence number> surrendered at <insert CSC> on the <insert date>

    Foreign licences issued by other Canadian provinces may be returned to the customer.

    All other Foreign or NZ licences are never to be defaced, marked or destroyed in any way. The licence may be returned to the customer.

    The customer must be advised:
    • That their NZ or Foreign driver licence is no longer a valid driver licence to drive on in Queensland
    • That they should advise the NZ or Foreign licensing authority that they are now the holder of a Qld driver licence (this will protect them against the fraudulent use of their NZ or Foreign driver licence by somebody else if it is later lost or stolen)

Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program
Applicants currently subject to a Non-Queensland interlock requirement
Applicants currently subject to a Non-Queensland interlock requirement
    A person who transfers an interstate or overseas driver licence that is subject to an interlock requirement on or after 6th December 2010 will be subject to the AIIP if at the time of transfer:
    • their interstate or overseas driver licence has been subject to the non-Queensland interlock requirement for less than two years; and
    • they have held a valid interstate or overseas driver licence, subject to an interlock requirement, and had an interlock fitted to a vehicle (or an interlock exemption) for less than twelve months.

    As part of the application to transfer to a Qld driver licence, the person will be required to complete an Interstate/Overseas Interlock Requirement Declaration (F4865) and provide supporting documentation.

    If the customer has an interlock device already fitted to their vehicle, they must be advised that they need a Qld approved interlock device fitted but the tenure served on their interstate device will be recognised. This may mean that their interstate device will need to be removed and a Qld approved interlock device fitted which may incur removal and installation costs.

    Customers who are transferring a non-Qld licence but are subject to a Qld Interlock requirement due to a relevant Qld drink driving disqualification will be reissued a Qld drivers licence subject to the 'I' licence condition upon transfer.

    The Interlock Processing Unit (IPU) will assess all transfer applications for interstate/overseas driver licences that have a non-Queensland interlock requirement.
Additional requirements - NSW Mandatory Alcohol Interlock Program (MAIP)
Additional requirements - NSW Mandatory Alcohol Interlock Program (MAIP)
    A NSW licence holder who is subject to the NSW MAIP and has relocated to Qld may apply for a Qld licence and be subject to the Qld Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program (AIIP).

    Before proceeding with the transaction, the NSW licence holder must be advised that when the requirements of the QLD AIIP are completed (either the I condition is removed or the I condition is not required on their Qld licence), NSW may reimpose the remainder of their 5 year MAIP disqualification period. They should contact Transport for NSW before they drive in that state as it may be prohibited. For this reason, it is imperative that customers are advised about the requirement to contact Transport for NSW before driving while their 5 year MAIP is still in effect in NSW.

Applicants subject to a Queensland interlock condition
Applicants subject to a Queensland interlock condition
    A person convicted and disqualified for a relevant Qld offence who obtains an interstate/overseas driver licence, then transfers back to Qld will be subject to the AIIP if at the time of transfer:
    • the two year interlock period (which commenced at the end of their disqualification period) has not ended; and
    • they have not accumulated 365 days of prescribed period tenure.

    If at the time of transfer the person declares that they have accumulated prescribed period tenure while driving under the authority of an interstate/overseas licence the person will be required to complete an Interstate/Overseas Interlock Requirement Declaration (F4865) and provide supporting documentation.

    If at the time of transfer the person’s interstate or overseas driver licence is also subject to an interlock requirement imposed outside of Queensland – refer to the process above (Applicants currently subject to a non-Queensland interlock requirement).

    The IPU will assess all transfer applications by interstate/overseas driver licence holders that have an interlock requirement or state they have accumulated prescribed period tenure while driving under the authority of an interstate/overseas licence.
For further information regarding the Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program (AIIP), please Refer: Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program (AIIP)

    NSW Driver Knowledge Test
    NSW Driver Knowledge Test

      NSW Provisional licence holders who twice exceed their demerit points limit within 5 years are required to pass the Driver Knowledge Test before the suspension will be lifted from their licence.

      NSW Unrestrictedlicence holders who exceed their demerit points twice within 5 years must complete the following before they are licensed to drive again:
      • Pass the Driver Knowledge Test (this is the test that all driver licence applicants must pass before they get their learner licence)
      • Complete a driver education course such as the Traffic Offender Intervention Program, at the offender’s cost. The driver education course must be delivered by one of the approved providers in NSW.

      NSW Drivers who commit a second or subsequent drink driving offence in five years must pass an appropriate knowledge test before they can get their licence back.

      NSW Licence holders are advised of these requirements when the suspension notice is generated in NSW. While the tests are not available to be completed online, customers have at least 7 months to complete the requirements before their licence will be indefinitely suspended.

      NoteCustomers are not eligible for a Queensland driver licence if they are currently indefinitely suspended from holding or obtaining a licence in another state. They must be advised to contact Transport for NSW for advice on how they can undertake the necessary test/course to lift the suspension from their traffic record.

      There are no provisions in Queensland legislation that allow TMR to provide a written road rules test to a person whose licence is suspended in another jurisdiction. As such, a passed Qld written road rules test cannot be used to remove a licence suspension applied in NSW. Likewise, an interstate written test cannot be sent to TMR for the customer to take in Qld. Customers who are unable to return to the jurisdiction in which the suspension was applied must be advised to contact the relevant interstate authority to discuss the matter.

        Reinstate licence status

    Hong Kong licences
      Verifying Hong Kong licences
      Verifying Hong Kong licences
        Hong Kong licences must be held for a minimum of twelve months before they can be recognised for transfer to a Queensland licence.

        If a customer has transferred from another non-recognised country to a Hong Kong licence within the last 12 months the licence will not be accepted for transfer and all testing requirements must be met.

        The licence details and the issue date must be verified by the Hong Kong authorities before the transfer can be processed.
        Acceptable verification methods
        Acceptable verification methods
          Customer can verify their Hong Kong licence by supplying an official confirmation letter from the Hong Kong Authorities. Operators must verify that the confirmation letter is genuine by emailing codlr@td.gov.hk to request confirmation before the licence can be transferred. The email must include a completed copy of the below Mandate Form and a copy of either the customer's Passport or Identity Card.

          CSO can verify the Hong Kong Licence by email using the template in Email Templates. The email must include a completed copy of the below Mandate Form, copy of the Driver Licence and a copy of either the customer's Passport or Identity Card.

          The Hong Kong Licence number will be taken from either the passport (this can be in the machine-readable zone) or their identity card number. When the licence is sent for verification, with either the official confirmation letter from the customer or with a copy of the licence by the CSO, a copy of the relating passport/identity card must also be supplied.

    Singapore Driving Licence
    Singapore Driving Licence
      Provisional Driving Licence
      Provisional Driving Licence
        A Singapore Provisional driving licence is equivalent to a Qld learner licence.
        From 1 December 2017, this licence is only issued in digital form.
        Acceptable Validation Method
        Acceptable Validation Method
          Customers can provide confirmation of their digital licence by logging in to the Singapore Police Force website at http://www.police.gov.sg/e-services or the Police@SGmobile app using their SingPass account.

          The customer can show the operator that they are logging in to the website/app and the operator can verify the licence details. The customer must email the licence details to the Centre so the operator can retain the copy of the licence for daily filing. If the customer provides a pre-printed copy of their licence, the operator can retain this after validating the digital licence via the website/app log-in.

      Class 3C Licence
      Class 3C Licence
        In Singapore, work permit and S-Pass holders who also hold a foreign driving licence (other than a Singapore licence), may obtain a Singapore class 3C or 3CA (automatic only) licence.

        A Singapore class 3C or 3CA licence only requires the holder to have passed a theory test, therefore, this licence only equates to a Queensland class C learner licence. However, the customer’s foreign licence (other than the Singapore class 3C or 3CA licence) may equate to a higher type of Queensland licence, for instance, a Queensland open licence.

        Transfer requirements
        Transfer requirements
          Customers wanting to transfer a Singapore class 3C or 3CA licence to Queensland should be encouraged to produce their other foreign licence for licence transfer.

          The requirements for transferring the other foreign licence will depend on whether it is from an Austroads approved country or not.

          If a customer is unable to present the other overseas licence, they will be required to pass a practical driving test prior to being issued with a Queensland P1 or P2 provisional licence (depending on their age). As Singapore is a recognised country, they are not required to complete a written test, and the requirement must be exempted in TICA.

    Thailand Type 1 temporary car licences
    Thailand Type 1 temporary car licences
      It appears that a Thailand Type 1 (Temporary Car) driver licence is valid only within Thailand. This means that a person presenting this licence for transfer to a Qld licence should be issued with a learner licence, unless they can provide a statement from the issuing authority in Thailand confirming its validity outside Thailand and any limitations or conditions which apply.

      To have tenure on a Thailand Type 1 licence recognised towards Queensland learner licence tenure, the holder must provide evidence from the issuing authority, which satisfies TMR that the licence is equivalent to a Queensland learner licence.

      Note:The format in which the evidence is supplied is unrestricted, however the evidence supplied must be from the authority/agency that issued the product and must clearly state that the licence is equivalent to a Qld Learner Licence (for example, that the product is issued for the purpose of learning to drive or the person has passed a written road rules test in order to have the product issued to them etc).
