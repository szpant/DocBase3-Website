Stock Control Guide (15)


Stock Control
What it isTMR needs to ensure there is adequate control over:
  • Saleable stock held and sold by offices
  • Accountable stock used by offices

Misuse of these items exposes TMR to criticism and potentially diminishes the reputation of, and public confidence in TMR.

A high level of control should be exercised over these items to ensure any discrepancies in stock on hand are promptly identified and resolved.
Types of Stock
Accountable Stock
Accountable Stock
    Accountable stock are either required for use for TMR products or used to convey a benefit.

    They include:
    • Written tests
    • Cab Charge Vouchers

    The above stock should be secured at the close of each business day.
Saleable Stock
Saleable Stock
    Saleable stock is divided into 2 categories:
    Secure Stock
    Secure Stock
      • National Driver Work Diary
      • Safety Certificates
      • Pilot/Escort Record Movement Books (numbered books only)
      • Certificates of Inspection (COI)
      • Standard Number Plates
      • Modification Plates and Books
      • Towing Authority Books
      • Driver Assessment Record (DAR) Books

      Note:Unused and undamaged modification certificate books that are receipted incorrectly, for example to a business instead of an approved person, may be resold by the customer service centre. The operator would need to raise a system BIID to the Business Systems area requesting the modification book number be removed from TICA. The modification book should not be resold until confirmation is received from Business Systems that this has been done.

    Low Risk
    Low Risk
      Some saleable items are considered low risk due to their low cost and/or low turnover:
      • VIN Plates (trailers)
      • Road Trip to your licence - a bootful of info
      • Qld Learners Logbook (Physical book or app flyer)
      • Your Keys to Driving Books
      • Pilot/Escort Record Movement Books (unnumbered books only)
Managing Stock
Managing Stock
The CSC Stock Register
The CSC Stock Register
    https://intranet.tmr.qld.gov.au/teams/csb-csa/Pages/StockReg/StockRegMain.aspx must be used to maintain stock, sell stock and complete monthly stocktakes of secure stock.

    All actions are digitally recorded in the register via the operators userid.

    The CSC Stock Register can be accessed via the above link or Inside CSB > Our Tools > CSC Stock Register.

    Note:When a Driver Assessment Record (DAR) book is taken out of stock and put into circulation by a Driving Examiner, the book is to be actioned in the stock register as 'sold' for record keeping purposes. The information on who is responsible for the book is to be recorded under Customer as "Assigned to (name of Driving Examiner)". If a DAR book is assigned to multiple Driving Examiners, this can be recorded as "Assigned to (CSC/QGAP)". The Manager of the CSC/QGAP would then be responsible for the DAR book.
Maintaining Stock Levels
Maintaining Stock Levels
    To ensure adequate stock levels are maintained it is necessary to have a system in place to identify when the re-order point has been reached. For example place a piece of paper saying re-order now at the re-order point. The staff member who takes the item immediately before the re-order now reminder is required to advise the stock officer.

    Setting re-order levels
    Setting re-order levels
      When identifying the reorder level consideration must be given to:
      • The demand for the stock
      • Order delivery times
      • The impact on the customer of not having the item e.g. it is an offence for the operator of a pilot/escort vehicle not to carry a Pilot/Escort Book when operating as a pilot/escort vehicle.
Receipt and Storage of Stock
Receipt and Storage of Stock
    Storage
    All saleable and accountable stock should be:
    • Stored in a secure environment within the office
    • Inaccessible to the public
    • Protected from:
      • Unauthorised access and removal
      • Accidental loss
      • Physical deterioration

    Receipt of Stock
    The stock officer is responsible for:
    • Sighting the physical stock delivered
    • Confirming all stock received against original order placed (Acknowledgement of Consignment Note attached to deliveries from Licensys)
    • Comparing and confirming original order with stock delivered

    Once the order has been confirmed to be correct the officer must:
    • Sign the Consignment Note:
      • Print Full name
      • Signature
      • Date
    • File copy of Consignment note
    • Ensure the secure storage of the office stocks
    • Maintain the Saleable Stock/Accountable Forms register

    Important:Advise the Plates Administrator by email should the courier have over-delivered or under-delivered a quantity of plates to the Storage Location. An issue should also be lodged in the interACT system.

    Offices may elect to use the below template to capture the details of stock when it is received, so the information can then be entered into the Stock Register:

    Received Stock to be entered on Stock Register

    Receipt of Standard Number Plates
    Receipt of Standard Number Plates

    Where differences exist, update interACT to reflect this.
    Refer: Stocktakes > Plate Management below

Stock Shortages
Stock Shortages
    Where a request is received for an item not currently in stock the CSC should attempt to obtain the item from another CSC.

    If an item cannot be obtained the same day:
    • Process transaction
    • Provide customer with duplicate receipt
    • Write on the receipt that the item is currently unavailable and provide CSC contact details should the purchaser require verification
    • Organise with customer how best to have item delivered
    • Ensure an order has been placed for the item

    Stock shortage alert
    If WINC indicate that stock is unavailable, please send an email to policyproceduressupport@tmr.qld.gov.au, advising:
    • Item being ordered
    • Quantity required
    • Next stock availability

    This will assist in early action on stock shortages by TMR.
Lost/Damaged and Expired/Unusable Stock
Lost/Damaged and Expired/Unusable Stock
    Lost/Damaged
    Lost/Damaged
      Where stock which has a value* is damaged or lost (other than stolen), operators must:
      • Complete and submit a Losses of Property/Monies (M3819) form to financialstatements@tmr.qld.gov.au with supporting documentation to a delegated officer.
      • Write off the stock in the office's Stock Register (once approved by delegated officer)
        • Securely destroy the stock (damaged stock only), as per secure destruction procedures (i.e. the use of the secure document destruction bins that are currently in use at CSC's).

      *For lost or damaged stock without value that is considered 'accountable stock' (e.g. some accountable forms), it is still advisable to complete form M3819 as a notification tool for management. It is also recommended that lost or damaged forms with a value should only be reported using form M3819 where there is a significant loss (e.g. an entire box of forms).

      Note:Although the cost to agencies who issue National Driver Work Diaries is $0 from Staples, these are included in CSB's yearly stocktake with a unit cost. Currently TMR pays the cost of printing and they are considered stock with value. Form M3819 is to be completed when NDWD are damaged or lost. When completing the Form M3819 ensure the current unit cost is used.

      Where stock is lost due to theft, the Queensland Police Service (QPS) must be contacted and, in addition to completing M3819, the incident must be reported according to the instructions outlined in the Security Guide Refer: Admin > 1. Admin > Security > Reporting Security Incidents

      For Motor Vehicle Plate Inventory Losses, Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Write-off public money/property

    Expired/Unusable
    Expired/Unusable
      When offices have stock which is expired (or due to expire shortly), making it unusable or not able to be sold, the stock is to be:
      • Written off in the office's Stock Register
      • Securely destroyed as per secure destruction procedures (i.e. the use of the secure document destruction bins that are currently in use at CSC's)

      Forms with a value should be reported using form M3819 where there is a significant loss (e.g. an entire box of forms).
      Note:For further information, refer to the Financial Management Practice Manual Loss of Public Property and Public Monies G3.10.01
Stocktakes
Stocktakes
    A stocktake is required to identify any discrepancy in stock numbers held. Any identified discrepancy must be investigated and resolved promptly.

    A monthly Stocktake for Secure Stock is to be completed via the CSC Stock Register and actioned electronically.

    Refer to the Guide for completing monthly stocktakes for your location.

    Complete Stock Take Guide - October 2020

    Note:There is no requirement to print or physically sign the stocktake sheet.

    Note:For monthly Standard Plate Stocktakes performed at non-TMR led agencies, the supporting CSC is to print the InterAct Reports and email to the QGAP/QPS/Magistrates Court for completion. Once the stocktake is completed, the report is to be emailed back to the supporting CSC for co-signing. These reports are retained by the supporting CSC and made available for audit purposes.

    Solo Site Stocktake Procedure
    Solo Site Stocktake Procedure
      • Secure Stock - Stocktake is completed via the CSC Stock Register and submitted to the supporting CSC/Manager for approval.
      • Plate Stocktake - Stocktake sheet is to be completed and forwarded to support CSC/Manager for approval and return to comply with CSA.
      • Independent monthly float check is to be completed when visiting manager visits - CSA is not to be failed if supporting manager only visits quarterly. Notes are to be made on float check and CSA.
      • First aid - Solo Site first aid officer is not to be part of the Solo Site CSA check.
    Completion of Stocktake
    Completion of Stocktake
      Confirmation of the completion of the stocktake is via the CSC Stock Register.

      Note:There is no requirement to print or physically sign the stocktake sheet.
