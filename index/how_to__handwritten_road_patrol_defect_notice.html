Handwritten Road Patrol Defect Notice (7581)

Applies to
Applies to
    This procedure will outline how to complete a handwritten road patrol defect notice.
1
Driver/Vehicle Owner/Registered Operator details
Driver/Vehicle Owner/Registered Operator details
    • The defect notice can be issued to the driver or the owner.
    • Strikethrough text not required.
    • Strikethrough licence and enter CRN if issuing to the owner.

2
Vehicle Details
Vehicle Details
    • The VIN/Chassis number must be taken from the vehicle.
    • The odometer reading must be recorded (if applicable).
    • Make, model and type can be taken from TICA or what you see.

3
Inspection Details
Inspection Details
    • Date, Time is in 24hour format, Strikethrough am/pm.
    • Location to include road, suburb or town and post code.
    Type of Inspection
    Type of Inspection
      • Select Random for Road Patrol
      • Select Light or Heavy vehicle

4
Defect Notice Category
Defect Notice Category
    • Self-Clearing should not be used as a category as there is no requirement under HVNL(Q) for the owner to return the defect notice for clearance so the defect notice can not be entered in TICA.
    • Select the category based on the safety risk posed by the continued use of a defective vehicle on the road.
    • If you select Major as a category, a Defective Vehicle Label must be attached to the vehicle.

      Categorising Defects

5
Defect Report
Defect Report
    • Select the components that are defective.

6
Details
Details
    • Number each defective item separately.
    • Use plain language to describe the defects.
    • When describing the defects, use the following format ‘Component’, ‘Fault’, ‘Additional’, to ensure an accurate and informative description of the fault is provided to the person who will be carrying out the adjustments or repairs.



    • Do not use "ensure complies with". You must prove the vehicle is defective.



    Conditions
    Conditions
      Conditions may be added, for example:
      • The vehicle may be allowed to be driven during the day only if a head light is not operational.
      • If a Major category defect notice is issued to a heavy vehicle you can enter the details where the vehicle can be driven to.

7
Cease Use
Cease Use
    • All defect notices must stipulate a cease use time (24 hour format) and date.
    • If you issue a minor defect notice to a heavy vehicle you may allow the vehicle to continue its journey to a place of repair or back to the depot, for example:
      1. You intercept a heavy vehicle at Mt Isa with a bald tyre on a drive axle, a cracked passenger side windscreen and an engine oil leak. The driver can change the bald tyre and you may allow the vehicle to be taken back to the depot in Townsville for repairs.
      2. You intercept a taxi in the middle of Cairns at night and one head light is not operating. You would only allow the vehicle enough time to get back to the depot to have the head light repaired.


8
Clearance Instructions
Clearance Instructions
    • Select the most appropriate clearance method relative for the defects identified
    • Strikethrough text not relevant:
      • If a TMR reinspection is required nominate the time and date an appoint must be made by.
      • If clearance is by a qualified person this must be stipulated in the details section.
      • If a full inspection and a Safety Certificate is required for a light vehicle this must be stipulated in the details section.

        Note:An AIS cannot issue a COI to a registered COI vehicle that requires inspection by TMR.

      • If only the defects identified are to be checked after the repairs have been carried, strike out full in the AIS section.
      • Provide a postal or email address for the customer to return the notice.
      • Return date may be 7 or 14 but must not be more than 28 days.


9
Declaration of Service
Declaration of Service
    • Enter the name of the person, if the notice was issued to a person (Strikethrough if not applicable).
    • Tick the box if a label was attached or the notice was left on an unattended vehicle. (Strikethrough if not applicable)
    • Enter the time (24hour format) and date of the inspection.
    • Enter your details and sign.
    • Enter Infringement Notice number, if applicable.
    • If a Defect Notice Continuation is required, record the defect notice number on the continuation and the continuation number on the defect notice.

10
Complete the Reverse Side of the Notice
Complete the Reverse Side of the Notice
    • On the reverse side of the notice, highlight the applicable clearance method and strike out non-relevant sections.

11
Issuing a Defect Notice to a Person
Issuing a Defect Notice to a Person
    • Explain the notice, clearance method and requirement to give the notice to the owner if issued to a driver.
    • If the notice was issued to a person, ask the person to sign the notice.
    • If they refuse to sign the notice, make a note on the defect notice and in your note book (No Offence).
12
Record the notice in TICA
Record the notice in TICA
    As soon as practicable, the defect notice must be recorded in TICA and the copy batch filed.
13
Clearing a Returned Defect Notice
Clearing a Returned Defect Notice
    • When a defect notice has been returned and the clearance section completed correctly, it must be cleared in TICA.
    • If the clearance is completed correctly an Authorised Officer may sign the Defect Notice Clearance section to allow Compliance Administration staff to clear the notice in TICA and issue a COI if applicable.
