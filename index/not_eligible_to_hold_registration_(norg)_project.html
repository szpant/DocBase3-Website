Not Eligible to Hold Registration (NORG) Project (5218)

Project NameNot Eligible to Hold Registration (NORG) Project
When24 April 2015
BackgroundA vehicle is required to be registered in the name of a legal entity (for example, a living person, an organisation with a status of ‘registered’ with ASIC or a trust established by a legal process). This ensures that the department is able to identify the person or entity responsible for the operation of a vehicle, safety of that vehicle on a road and the payment of any associated fees/fines.

The purpose of this project is to ensure that customers who are no longer a legal entity (deceased individuals and closed organisations) are restricted from undertaking registration business with TMR.
Who will be impacted and how?Customers who are updated as deceased or closed will be impacted as follows:
  • A new restriction, Not Eligible to Hold Registration (NORG) will be applied to the record which will restrict:
    • A registration from being renewed (unless the renewal was raised prior to the record being updated as deceased/closed); and
    • Any new business registration transactions from being performed; and
    • A registration from being transferred into the deceased/closed CRN
  • A No Online Access (NOAC) restriction will be applied to the record. This will restrict the customer from performing any online transactions (including paying the renewal if it was raised prior to the record being updated as closed/deceased). Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer > Qualifications, Restrictions & Text > Customer Restrictions
  • If the customer has any current/expired registrations or plate products, a letter will be sent to the last known address of the customer advising of the options available (i.e. cancel or transfer to a legal entity).
  • Letters to deceased individuals will be addressed to the ‘Estate of’ and will include a list of up to 50 registration/plate products.
  • Letters to closed organisations will not include a list of registrations/products.
  • If a paying agent (2nd registered operator) is closed, the customer can remove the paying agent’s name without obtaining written advice.
  • Organisation/individual customer details (i.e. their address, personal details) and vehicle details can be updated, however at all times the customer should be encouraged to transfer/cancel the registration.
Data CleansingAll existing ‘closed’ organisation records will have the NORG and NOAC restrictions applied and a letter sent where they have expired/current registration/plate products. There are approximately 350 organisations who will receive this letter. A list of the organisation postcodes has been sent to the regional managers so that they can assess any potential impacts to their region.

The data cleansing of deceased records will be delayed and Registration Policy will review this decision after 3 months. You will be notified via daily message when the deceased records data cleanse is being completed.
System ChangesThe following system changes have been made as part of this project:
  • If a ‘Parent’ organisation is closed, any linked ‘Child’ records will automatically be closed. A business exemption will be created.
  • If a ‘closed/deceased’ CRN is re-activated, the user must enter a reason and a business exception will be created.
