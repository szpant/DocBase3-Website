Light Vehicle Modifications (5238)

Statement
A Certificate of Modification (Form F1853) and a blue modification plate are to be used in the circumstances outlined in this instruction.

In Queensland, there are currently two (2) approved codes of practice (CoP) for modifications for light vehicles:

National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modifications; and
Queensland Code of Practice: Vehicle Modifications.

All applications for vehicle modifications under a Code of Practice must be referred directly to those Approved Persons holding the appropriate codes.

Note: Not all parts of the NCOP have been accepted for use in Queensland, with variations applied for certain sections. For further information about these variations, Transport Inspectors should refer to Vehicle Standards Instructions.
Arrangements for modification approvals by Transport Inspectors
If the region/district does not have an Approved Person with the relevant Code under the Code of Practice - Light Vehicles, then the owner may approach the local Transport Inspector for approval under the following circumstances:
  • The nearest Approved person involves the vehicle owner travelling more than 150 kilometres each way.
  • Where, for any other reason, in the opinion of the Director-General, that it is un-economical or unreasonable to travel to the nearest Approved Person.
  • The relevant alteration/modification is contained within the National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modifications (NCOP) and the Queensland Code of Practice; Vehicle Modifications.
  • The Transport Inspector holds the qualifications, relevant to the requirements of the National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modifications (NCOP) and the Queensland Code of Practice; Vehicle Modifications.
  • The Transport Inspector follows the details outlined in a Letter of Approval issued by Vehicle and Vessel Standards Unit, Transport Regulation Branch.
Procedure - Inspection
  • The vehicle must meet the requirements of the relevant CoP or the appropriate Information Bulletin.
  • If the modification is satisfactory, a modification plate is to be issued to the owner. The plate is to be engraved 'Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR)' and 'the Inspector's identification number' is in the section allocated for 'A/PERSON No.'.
  • The vehicle owner must fit the modification plate in the position as indicated by the Transport Inspector.
Distribution of Forms
  • ORIGINAL to owner
  • DUPLICATE to be forwarded to:
    Department of Transport and main Roads (TMR)
    Industry and Operator Accreditation
    Floor 8, Transport House
    PO Box 673
    Fortitude Valley QLD 4006
  • TRIPLICATE to be batch filed with a copy of the Motor Vehicle Inspection Report form.
Modification Plate details to be recorded
  • In the office register for saleable stock;
    and
  • In section 10 of the Register of Motor Vehicle Inspection Administrative Procedures.
Fees
The following fees are applicable:
  • Booking and Inspection fees - (as specified in the TO(RUM-VSS) Regulations)
  • Modification plate - current fee
Modifications not covered by the above schemes
Very complex modifications, for which there are no Approved Persons, will continue to be processed by Vehicle Standards
