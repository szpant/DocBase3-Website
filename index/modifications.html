Modifications (3051)


What are they?Modifications are changes made to a vehicle.

Some modifications are quite minor and do not require any approval, while other modifications are major changes to a vehicle and require approval from an Approved Person or TMR.
Types of Modifications
Types of Modifications
Types of Modifications
    Changes within Manufacturer’s Specifications
    Changes within Manufacturer’s Specifications
      With many vehicles, the manufacturer often produces the same vehicle, with a number of different options that a customer may choose from. Commonly these are items such as transmission (manual or auto), different size engines (6 or 8 cylinders) etc.

      If a vehicle owner modifies a vehicle by fitting a piece of equipment that was an option for that vehicle, no approval is needed provided all manufacturers’ specifications for the option have been met.

    Modifications not requiring approval
    Modifications not requiring approval
      The Vehicle Standards Regulation and the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator's Code of Practice for the Approval of Heavy Vehicle Modifications, excludes some simple modifications to vehicles from the need for approval. Modifications like the installation of a new stereo, CB radio, long range driving lights etc do not need to be approved by an Approved Person, TMR or the NHVR provided the safety of the vehicle is not affected and the vehicle complies with all ADRs and the Vehicle Standards Regulation (light vehicles) or the Heavy Vehicle National Law (heavy vehicles).

      Light vehicle specific modifications
      Light vehicle specific modifications
        The National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modification includes two specific types of modifications that do not require approval.
        Minor Modifications
        Minor Modifications
          For example: Minor modifications are small changes to a vehicle that do not require approval.

          Commonly, these will involve the installation of an accessory (e.g. long range aerial, tow bars on light vehicles, roof racks etc) and provided the vehicle continues to meet the vehicle standards, do not require approval.

          For light vehicles, further information about minor modifications can be found in section 3.14 of the introduction of the National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modification, available online.

        Basic Modifications
        Basic Modifications
          For example: Basic modifications are changes to a vehicle that are more significant than a minor modification, but not significant enough to require formal approval from an Approved Person or TMR.

          The technical requirements are provided for basic modifications in the National Code of Practice Light Vehicle Construction and Modification. Any person performing a basic modification must ensure they meet all the requirements listed in the National Code of Practice, and any other requirements of the TO(RUM-VSS) Regulation 2010.


    Modifications requiring approval
    Modifications requiring approval
      Modifications that affect compliance with Vehicle Standards or could adversely affect the safety of the vehicle require approval.

      Modifications under a Code of Practice (CoP)
      Modifications under a Code of Practice (CoP)
        For example: Modifications that are included in a CoP are complex modifications to vehicles, that have the potential to affect the safety of the vehicle. These modifications are certified by an Approved Person (AP), shown by the attachment of a modification plate and issue of a certificate of modification.

        Currently in Queensland, there are 2 approved codes for modifications to light vehicles that provide for these types of modifications:
        • National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modifications
          (NCOP)
        • Queensland Code of Practice – Vehicle Modifications (QCOP)

        For heavy vehicles, the approved code of practice is the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator's Code of Practice for the Approval of Heavy Vehicle Modifications.

      Other Modifications
      Other Modifications
        For example: All other complex modifications not included in the 2 categories above, are considered on a case by case basis and must be approved by TMR (for light vehicles) or the NHVR (for heavy vehicles).

        Before beginning this type of modifications, the owner of a vehicle must make contact with TMR or the NHVR to have their proposed modification assessed. If a customer does not seek assessment of their proposal, the owner of the vehicle may find their modification does not meet TMR's or the NHVR requirements and this could result in approval delays, refusal of the application and costly re-modification of the vehicle.

        Application Process for Heavy Vehicles
        Application Process for Heavy Vehicles
          For further information about applying for approval for a heavy vehicle modification, the vehicle owner should contact the NHVR on 1300 MYNHVR (696 487) or www.nhvr.gov.au
        Application Process for Light Vehicles
        Application Process for Light Vehicles
          To apply for in-principle approval to modify, the vehicle owner must:
          • Complete a Motor Vehicle Modification Application (F1854); and
          • Provide detailed information on the modification/s they wish to perform; and
          • Provide plans, drawings or diagrams (if available); and
          • Provide details of any components they intend to use

          This must be submitted to Vehicle Standards

          If approved, TMR will provide the customer a letter that details the requirements they will be required to meet, and require that they submit a final application once the modifications are completed.

          An in-principle approval to modify is not a final approval, and must not be accepted as proof that a modification has been approved.

          Once the modification is completed and the vehicle owner has provided all necessary documentation to TMR, a final approval letter will be issued, and a final inspection by a Transport Inspector may be required.

          Once this approval letter has been issued (and signed by a Transport Inspector if required) the vehicle can then be processed for registration or the vehicle details updated.

        Type Approved Modifications
        Type Approved Modifications
          Type Approved modifications are special types of other modifications that have been given prior in-principle approval by TMR or the NHVR.

          Type Approvals are issued for a particular modifications and to particular modifiers, and approval is given in writing.
Updating Vehicle Details in TICA
Updating Vehicle Details in TICA
Updating Vehicle Details in TICA
    The Vehicle Registration Regulation requires that when a person modifies a vehicle in a way that changes the description of the vehicle in TICA or changes the registration fee payable, the owner must notify TMR within 14 days.

    Notification for modifications that increase the registration fee payable, must be made and the outstanding fees must be paid before the modified vehicle is driven on a road. The requirement for updating the vehicle registration record varies by the type of modification.


    Note:Modifications to heavy vehicles that have been approved under the Heavy Vehicle National Law are acceptable in Queensland. For more information, please see the heavy vehicle section of 'Modifications approved in another jurisdiction' below.
    Form
    Form
      Change of Vehicle Particulars Notifications (F3524)

    Supporting Documentation
    Supporting Documentation
      Specific information about the supporting documentation requirements for many common modifications are provided in the Specific Modifications section.

      For Modifications under a CoP, the supporting documentation required is the Certificate of Modification or if the certificate is not available, the vehicle must be inspected to sight the Modification Plate fitted to the vehicle.

    Inspection
    Inspection
      Inspection of some vehicles may be needed, to re-measure the vehicle, confirm the new engine number etc. For further information please refer to the ‘Specific Modifications’ section (below).

      For a Modification under a CoP, if a Certificate of Modification is provided, the vehicle does not need to be inspected to sight the modification plate (provided all required details can be obtained from the certificate and nothing else requires inspection)

    Pro-rata fees
    Pro-rata fees
      If the change to the vehicle means additional registration fees are payable, the customer must pay the outstanding amount at the time the update is made.

      If the notification is made by post, a letter must be sent to the registered operator advising payment is required within 28 days.

Modifications prior to first registration
    Under the national system to regulate vehicles, the Commonwealth Government has responsibility for complying all vehicles prior to their first registration in Australia. Once registered it is the responsibility of the registering jurisdiction to set the compliance standards for modifications.

    Vehicles that have been modified prior to their first registration in Australia must be fitted with a second stage manufacture plate in addition to their original compliance plate. This second stage manufacture plate 'replaces' the original identification (compliance) plate and the details on this plate must be used to create the vehicle description.

    When a vehicle is modified before first registration, there are issues around who has responsibility. To clarify this, the Commonwealth allows a person to modify up to 4 vehicles each year, before they are registered, and the state or territory determines what modification requirements apply. If a person modifies more than 4, the Commonwealth Government must provide certification.

    Common times when a SSM is used to certify a vehicle are:
    • Ambulance
    • Motorhome
    • GVM Increases to light vehicles
    • Cabin modifications to light vehicles
    • Cabin Modifications to vehicles allow wheelchair accessibility

    Examples of SSM Plates
    Examples of SSM Plates
      • Second Stage of Manufacture - Low Volume Approval (Limited number of vehicles per year)



      • Second Stage of Manufacture - Full Volume Approval (Unlimited number of vehicles per year)



    If a vehicle is presented for its first registration with a modification plate other than a second stage manufacture plate should be refused registration. The applicant should be advised to return their Approved Person to undertake a second stage manufacture process.

    The following exceptions apply to the fitment of a blue modification plate prior to first registration:
    • Customer has sought and received approval from vehicle standards. Customer must present the approval letter when registering.
    • K5 modifications for the installation of wheelchair restraint system (no approval required)
    • Heavy Vehicles with the following modifications:
      • P1 Tow Coupling Installation
      • P2 Fifth Wheel and King Pin Installation
      • J1 Body Mounting
Missing Modification Plates
Missing Modification Plate
Missing Modification Plate
    If the modification plate is missing from the vehicle, the modification certificate can be used if the VIN/Chassis number is the same on the vehicle. If the registered operator does not have the modification certificate or the modification plate they will need to arrange to have modification recertified and a new plate issued. If assistance is required contact Vehicle Standards.

Light Vehicles
Light Vehicles
    Modifications to light vehicles, performed and certified in another jurisdiction, are not automatically recognised in Queensland. Before any vehicle with interstate modifications can be registered in Queensland, the following process must be followed:
    1. Reciprocal Recognition
    1. Reciprocal Recognition
      The vehicle owner will be required to make an application to Vehicle Standards requesting formal acceptance of the modification.

      Applications for Reciprocal Recognition
      To make an application, the vehicle owner must:
      • Complete a Vehicle Modification Application (F1854) form;
        and
      • Provide details of the certification from the interstate jurisdiction;
        and
      • Provide photos of the modification;
        and
      • Provide proof of registration in the other jurisdiction.

      Email the application to Vehicle Standards

      If approved, the vehicle owner will be sent an approval letter, which must be presented with the registration application.

      Note:When presenting to an AIS for a safety certificate or certificate of inspection, the owner should give the examiner a copy of the approval letter issued by TMR to show that the modifications have been approved.

      If the certification or modification can not be recognised, the vehicle owner will be advised of the process to have the modification certified in Queensland.
    2. Recertification in Queensland
    2. Recertification in Queensland
      If the vehicle owner chooses to, and the modification is a Modification under a CoP, they may simply opt to have the modification recertified by an Approved Person. The reciprocal recognition system is in place to ensure that there is a low cost option to vehicle owners.
Approved Persons
Approved Persons
Approved Persons
    Approved Persons are members of private industry who are accredited by TMR to provide certification of vehicle modifications on our behalf.

    Details of Approved Persons currently accredited to certify vehicle modifications can be found in RUMIIS. TMR staff are not to access the RUMIIS database as a means of providing an Approved Persons information to the public.

    TMR staff are only permitted to provide the name and contact phone number for an Approved Person listed on the Approval of Vehicle Modifications webpage, under the Find an Approved Person online service. Members of the public can also access the "Find an Approved Person" service on the Queensland Government website.

    Applying to be an Approved Person
    Applying to be an Approved Person
      Should a person wish to apply to become an Approved Person, details can be found at www.tmr.qld.gov.au and follow the links to Business and Industry > Accreditation > Approved Person Scheme. Alternatively, the person may email AP Administrator for further information.

      Refer: Accreditation > Approved Person > Approved Persons (AP) Guide
