Complaint Entity (7146)

The Complaint entity captures complaints made to TMR. Where a number of complaints are captured, or a pattern emerges, consideration can be given to the whole not just the individual parts.

What is a complaint - A complaint is the verbal or written expression of dissatisfaction about products or services. It is recognised that it is not always feasible or necessary to record every expression of dissatisfaction by a customer. You will need to assess the difference between a general enquiry or request which contains a minor and one off negative comment. Sometimes, it's possible to ask the customer directly if they would like to lodge a complaint.


    Note:It is important when recording a complaint that details are recorded in the description e.g. for an AIS complaint, what vehicle is involved, what Inspection station etc. If a customer advises that they are unhappy with an AIS Station, but does not want to provide details of registration, AIS. This would not be considered a complaint.

Where a complaint has been received through DocTrak into a region, a region can create a complaint in order to track the progress of the follow up of that matter however it must be responded to through normal DocTrak processes.

Compliance staff must search CID using the Tow Truck Hotline reference number prior to recording Tow Truck complaints. Tow Truck complaints received through the Tow Truck Hotline will periodically be loaded into CID by the HV Analysis Unit.

Nothing in CID replaces existing corporate complaint management rules.

All staff have the ability to create this new entity type via CID. Simply log into CID

Recording DocTrak, DMS or other "Corporate Complaints" in CID
    The CID system and these instructions do not replace any corporate complaint management system, policy and/or procedure. In the event of any conflict or ambiguity, the corporate policy, procedure and/or system must always prevail.

    If a matter is of such a nature as to require a level of confidentiality/security it should not be entered in CID but advice sought as to whether to, and if so, how to secure the information.
    To Record or Not?
    To Record or Not?

    The decision to record a complaint, web enquiry, DocTrak matter or other "corporate" Complaint in CID is discretionary to a region.

    Ordinarily a complaint will be recorded for:
      • An allegation against a transport entity;
      • An allegation against an AIS; or
      • An allegation against an entity that already has information or allegations recorded in CID.

    A Complaint entity will ordinarily be the trigger for a Case. Complaints should not exist unconnected to anything else in the database. Details of what a Complaint can link to can be found above in section 2 - What the Complaint entity links to in CID.

    If, having reviewed the steps below, it is deemed there is no need for a Case but the information still needs to go into CID, record the complaint and mark the record as 'No Further Action'.



    Reasons to capture a complaint, web enquiry, DocTrak matter or other "corporate" complaint may include:
      • Capturing time (by way of Tasks linked to a Case) spent on conducting enquiries/investigations.
      • If the matter becomes or is likely to become an investigation - the Case will be the place holder for the investigation.
      • Capturing details of the tasks undertaken in conducting enquiries/investigations by way of running sheet entries and/or other task types in CID.
      • Capturing working versions and/or draft documents with short-term informational value - classed as 'ephemeral documents' for record keeping purposes, and/or
      • If a region sees information value in capturing the information contained in the complaint.

    In making a decision to record a complaint or enquiry (such as a web enquiry) from a corporate system, in addition to the considerations above, factors to consider in deciding whether to put it in CID may also include:
      • Is the complaint or information connected to an entity such as a transport operator or AIS (or other entity in CID including persons) that may in future be useful to know, even if minor or only specific to one point in time?
      • If yes, consider recording it in CID,
      • If no, consider not recording it in CID.
      • Is the complaint or information going to be resolved by way of a quick phone call or email to the customer, and was specific only to their question?
      • If yes, consider not recording it in CID (noting that the action/outcome will be recorded in DocTrak etc anyway).
      • Is the complaint or information likely to become part of a protracted set/series of matters that will have some connection which it would be useful to be able to see the connections over time?
      • If yes, consider recording it in CID and seek advice from compliance support as to how it might be best to do this.

    If the decision is made to capture a Complaint as an entity, the steps outlined above should be followed exactly the same as for a complaint received directly in the region.

    The region should take the action to create the record in CID. Although Heavy Vehicle Analysis (and Compliance Intelligence) are sometimes cc'd on DocTrak notifications to regions, they are not notified of all. Previous occurrences of Heavy Vehicle Analysis creating events in CID from DocTrak matters has created duplication as regions have then entered their own information, unaware Heavy Vehicle Analysis had done so. To avoid duplication, and allow regions control of the decision; Heavy Vehicle Analysis will not create this information unless requested.

    Finalising CID records
    Finalising CID records

    Where a complaint or other matter has been 'managed' in CID in addition to any corporate system, the final record/evidence of business must always be recorded in the corporate system.

    Otherwise, CID case outcomes such as:
      • Education session,
      • Improvement notice and/or,
      • Infringement
    Can be used to record the finalisation of a case.

    CID is not a correspondence system and has not correspondence type entity. Should you wish to record something as a case outcome that is not in the list above, please contact Compliance.Intelligence@tmr.qld.gov.au
