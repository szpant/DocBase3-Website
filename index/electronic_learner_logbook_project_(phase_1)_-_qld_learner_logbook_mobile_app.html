Electronic Learner Logbook Project (Phase 1) - Qld Learner Logbook Mobile App (6047)

Project NameElectronic Learner Logbook Project (Phase 1) - QLD Learner Logbook Mobile App
WhenOfficial launch by minister on Wednesday, 25 May 2016
BackgroundThe QLD Learner Logbook mobile app has now been released and is available from the App Store and Google Play.

Queensland learner drivers are required to complete and record 100 hours of supervised driving experience, before they can undertake a practical driving test to progress to a Provisional licence. Paper based logbooks are currently manually processed by a team of specialists in CSB’s Central Operations and Support (CO&S) area.

The Qld Learner Logbook Mobile App has been developed as an alternative to the paper logbook and aims to enhance a customer’s experience by making one of their first interactions with government contemporary, intuitive, on-line and digital, rather than manual, time consuming and paper-based.

The mobile app uses convenient and modern technology to allow for the digital capture and storage of logbook entries. Once complete, the logbook can then be electronically submitted directly to TMR for assessment through the app, streamlining and automating a previously manual process.
Further informationThe following methods were used to notify the public of the change;
  • An official media release and launch event at Balmoral State High School on Wednesday, 25 May 2016 attended by Mark Bailey MP (Minister for Main Roads, Road Safety and Ports).
  • The Learner Logbook app flyer
    • where applicable (see attached distribution list) Customer Service Centres have received copies of the Learner Logbook App flyer and staff are strongly encouraged to promote the app to new and renewing learner drivers. To replenish their stock, Customer Service Centres are now able to order copies of the flyer from Staples. Low volume centres are able to print the flyer from DocBase.
  • Web updates in the relevant areas of TMR and QGov sites.
  • Social media (TMR Facebook and Twitter accounts).
  • A new CSC video loop slide.
  • A Department of Education bulletin to schools.
  • The paper logbook and supervisor brochure will be updated to include references to the app, for the next print run.

TMR staff can obtain further information from DocBase procedures, the department website, insideTMR, and the documents included in this update.
