About Provisional/Probationary Licences
Definitions
Driver Licence Definitions
What it is
P/P1/P2 Provisional/Probationary Licences
P/P1/P2 Provisional/Probationary Licences
    P
    P Provisional Licence
    P Provisional Licence
      P Provisional licences were issued prior to 01/07/07 to a person who had limited driving experience.
    P Probationary Licence
    P Probationary Licence
      A P Probationary licence is issued after a person's previous provisional or open licence was cancelled because of a court imposed disqualification period.
    P1
    P1 Provisional Licence
    P1 Provisional Licence
      A P1 Provisional licence is issued to a person under 25 who has limited driving experience. The licence has specific restrictions.
    P1 Probationary Licence
    P1 Probationary Licence
      A P1 Probationary licence is issued after a person's previous P1 Provisional licence was cancelled because of a court imposed disqualification period.
    P2
    P2 Provisional Licence
    P2 Provisional Licence
      A P2 Provisional licence is issued to a person who has limited driving experience. The licence has less restrictions than a P1 Provisional licence.
    P2 Probationary Licence
    P2 Probationary Licence
      A P2 Probationary licence is issued after a person's previous P2 Provisional licence was cancelled because of a court imposed disqualification period.

    P Provisional Licence
    P Provisional Licence
      P Provisional licences are only issued to customers who:
      • Held a P Provisional licence issued before 01/07/07
        and
      • The P Provisional licence was valid within the 5 years before application
        and
      • The holder has not yet met the required period
        and
      • The P Provisional licence is being renewed, reissued, upgraded or transferred to a further P Provisional licence

      • Is a returning driver applying for a provisional licence and has passed the relevant practical driving test
    P Probationary
    P Probationary
      A Probationary licence is issued to a customer:
      • Has completed a court imposed disqualification period, has held a P Type or Open licence within the last five years and the Chief Executive does not require a practical test
      • Transfers an Interstate licence equivalent to a Qld P Probationary but has not completed the required total period
      • Has a cheque payment dishonoured on an existing P Probationary licence and has held a current P Type or Open licence in the last 5 years
    P1
    P1 Provisional Licence
    P1 Provisional Licence
      A P1 Provisional licence is issued to a customer who is at least 17 years old and:
      • Holds and has held a learner licence for the required period and:
        • Has passed a practical driving test
          and
        • Is under the age of 25
      • Transfers an Interstate licence equivalent to a Qld P1 Provisional and has not completed the required total period
      • Transfers a Foreign licence equivalent to a Qld P1 Provisional and has not completed the required total period
      • Transfers a Defence Force licence equivalent to a Qld P1 Provisional and has not completed the required total period
      • Transfers an Interstate, Foreign or Defence Force licence equivalent to a Qld P2 Provisional and is under 18
      • Had a cheque payment dishonoured on an existing P1 Provisional licence and has held a current P1 Provisional licence in the last 5 years
      • Had a P1 Provisional licence cancelled as a result of a show cause action and the licence was valid within the 5 years before application
      • Is a returning driver applying for a provisional licence and has passed the relevant practical driving test
    P1 Probationary Licence
    P1 Probationary Licence
      A P1 Probationary licence is issued to a customer who:
      • Completes a period of court disqualification on a P1 Provisional licence
      • Transfers an Interstate licence equivalent to a Qld P1 Probationary but has not completed the required total period
      • Has had a cheque payment dishonoured on an existing P1 Probationary licence and has held a current P1 Type licence in the last 5 years
      • Is a returning driver applying for a probationary licence and has passed the relevant practical driving test
    P2
    P2 Provisional Licence
    P2 Provisional Licence
      A P2 Provisional licence is issued to a customer who is at least 18 years old and:
      • Holds and has held a learner licence for the required period, and:
        • Has passed a practical driving test
          and
        • Is over the age of 25
      • Has held a P1 licence for the required period and has passed and paid for the Hazard Perception Test
        Note: A HPT is only required in the P1 phase if the customer was issued a P1 licence prior to 29th March 2021.
      • Transfers an Interstate licence equivalent to a Qld P2 Provisional and has not completed the required total period
      • Transfers an Foreign licence equivalent to a Qld P2 Provisional and has not completed the required total period
      • Transfers a Defence Force licence equivalent to a Qld P2 Provisional & has not completed the required total period
      • Had a cheque payment dishonoured on an existing P2 Provisional licence and has held a current P2 Provisional licence in the last 5 years
      • Had a P2 Provisional licence cancelled as a result of a show cause action and the licence was valid within the 5 years before application
      • Transfers an Interstate, Foreign or Defence Force licence equivalent to a Qld Open Licence and is under 20
      • Is a returning driver applying for a provisional licence and has passed the relevant practical driving test
    P2 Probationary Licence
    P2 Probationary Licence
      A P2 Probationary licence is issued to a customer who:
      • Completes a period of court disqualification on a P2 Provisional licence
      • Has held a P1 licence for the required period and has passed and paid for the Hazard Perception Test
        Note: A HPT is only required in the P1 phase if the customer was issued a P1 licence prior to 29th March 2021.
      • Transfers an Interstate licence equivalent to a Qld P2 Probationary but has not completed the required total period
      • Had a cheque payment dishonoured on an existing P2 Probationary licence and has held a current P2 licence in the last 5 years
      • Is a returning driver applying for a probationary licence and has passed the relevant practical driving test

    Motorcycle/Moped Passenger
    Motorcycle/Moped Passenger
      A person can only carry a passenger when riding a motorcycle on a road if they:
      • Hold a class RE provisional/probationary or open licence, which they have held for at least one year, and are riding a class RE motorcycle (does not include any period where the licence was not valid); or
      • Hold a class R licence.

      Note:A passenger is someone that sits on the pillion (behind the rider) or in a sidecar attached to the motorcycle.

      All pillion passengers must be at least eight years old and must be able to place their feet on the footrests designed for use by a pillion passenger.

      A person learning to ride a class RE or R motorcycle is NOT permitted to carry a pillion passenger. Instruction by a suitably licensed person may be given from either a sidecar attached to the motorcycle, another motorcycle, or a car following the learner rider.

      Refer: 6. Learner Licence > Learner Licence Guide > Conditions of Use > Drive Accompanied/Under Direction > Motorbike

      Note:A moped is defined as a motorcycle. A person riding a moped under their class C Provisional, P1, P2 or Open licence must not carry a passenger while riding the bike on a road.
    Learning to Drive the next eligible class or another class
    Learning to Drive the next eligible class or another class
      A Provisional or Probationary licence holder must comply with the same conditions of use as a learner licence holder if learning to drive a vehicle:
      • Using the authority of their Provisional or Probationary licence to learn to drive the next eligible class (eg, learning to drive a class LR vehicle under the authority of a class C licence), or
      • Using a Learner licence (eg class RE Learner licence endorsed on a class C Provisional licence), or
      • Using the authority of a returning driver certificate, if they are a returning driver (Refer: Returning Drivers).

      Progressing from a Provisional/Probationary Licence
      Tenure & Licence Progression
      Time required to hold Prov/Prob & Progression
      Time may be accumulated over a 5 year period
      Time may be accumulated over a 5 year period
        The required period:
        • May be accumulated over the preceding 5 years
        • May be broken by expiry, suspension or cancellation
        • Can include time spent serving a period of good driving behaviour (GDB)
        • May include time spent on other P/P1/P2 licences including Interstate / Overseas / Defence Force / Restricted

        Only those periods that the licence is actually valid will be considered toward the required period.
      P/P1/P2 Tenure after Cancellation (1 year min.)
      P/P1/P2 Tenure after Cancellation (1 year min.)
        A P/P1/P2 licence period after a cancellation sanction completed (court disqualification or show cause) must be a minimum of 1 year. This may be met by holding a combination of P1 and P2 licences for the total 1 year period.
      TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s15(1)(g),(h)(i)
        Example
        Example
          The Applicant:
          • Held P1 licence for 6 months prior to disqualification
          • Has completed disqualification sanction of 6 months

          Licence issued = P1 Probationary for 1 year

          Notes for this example:
          • A P1 Probationary must be issued for at least 1 year as this is the minimum period for any licence issue (other than a fee free transfer)
          • The person may apply to upgrade to a P2 Probationary licence after holding the P1 Probationary licence for 6 months, as they have met the P1 required period (1 year) by:
            • Holding the P1 Provisional for 6 months
              and
            • Holding the P1 Probationary for 6 months
              and
            • Has passed and paid for the HPT
          • The person may only progress to a P2 Probationary (not an Open), as a Probationary licence must be held for a minimum 1 year period after a disqualification/cancellation.

      Progressing from a P1 licence to P2/Open licence
      Progressing from a P1 licence to P2/Open licence
        P1 issued prior to 29 March 2021
        P1 issued prior to 29 March 2021
          A person who was issued a P1 licence prior to 29 March 2021 and passes the HPT and pays the test fee online may be eligible to have their P1 licence automatically upgraded and their P2/Open licence sent to them in the mail. A batch process will run overnight to upgrade the licence.

          The P1 licence will not be automatically upgraded if:
          • the P1 licence is expired, suspended or cancelled
          • it is less than 6 weeks before the expiry date of the P1 licence
          • the customer does not have a valid digital image set
          • the customer has an existing 'M' condition and a current medical certificate is not recorded
        P1 issued on or after 29 March 2021
        P1 issued on or after 29 March 2021
          When tenure is met a batch process will run to upgrade the licence and send the P2 licence in the mail.

          The P1 licence will not be automatically upgraded if:
          • it is less than 6 weeks before the expiry date of the P1 licence
          • the customer does not have a valid digital image set
          • the customer has an existing 'M' condition and a current medical certificate is not recorded.
        Automatic recognition of time credit
        Automatic recognition of time credit
          From 1 January 2014, P1 tenure over 12 months will automatically be recognised and credited towards P2 tenure when the licence is upgraded.
          Examples
          Examples
            The applicant:
            • Purchases 5 year P1 licence
            • Meets P1 tenure at 12 months
            • Passes and pays for HPT at 13 months
            Licence issued = P2 licence issued for 23 months, Open licence for 2 years

            The applicant:
            • Purchases 5 year P1 licence
            • Meets P1 tenure at 12 months
            • Passes and pays for HPT at 36 months
            Licence issued= Open licence

          Note: This process won’t apply to customers who passed the hazard perception test at the learner licence phase.
