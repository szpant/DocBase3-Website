Pre-Registration Inspection (PRI) (7138)

What are they?A Pre-registration inspection (PRI) is an inspection conducted prior to registering a vehicle/trailer. Its purpose is to confirm the:
  • Accuracy of vehicle identifiers
  • Need to issue a surrogate identifier (motor vehicles only)
  • Compliance with regulation dimension limits
  • TMR issued identifier is attached to the correct trailer type has been appropriately stamped and displayed on the motor/vehicle or trailer.

Light Vehicle PRI
    This PRI is to confirm the identity of light vehicles (excluding Trailers) and must be conducted by TMR:
    • If there are inconsistencies with the information provided on a Self Assessed Vehicle and TICA or other inspection documents and the RAV:
      Self Assessed Vehicle
      VIN does not decode
      Duplicate VIN/Chassis
    • For all unmodified motorhomes/campervans (including second stage manufactured) not previously registered in Australia must have a PRI at new business any GVM using Motorhome/Campervan Inspection Sheet (F4550) form.
    • For Modified motorhomes/Campervans with proof of the modification (including a LH11 modification certificate if under 4.5t GVM) not previously registered in Australia must have PRI using Motorhome/Campervan Inspection Sheet (F4550) form.
    • For all Personally Imported Motorcycles
    • At the discretion of an Office Manager if there is any doubt about the validity of information, or suspicion about a vehicles' identity.

    Note:The purpose of a PRI for modified Motorhomes/Campervans not previously registered in Australia is for an identity check, a measure up is not required.

    The following must be sighted:
    • VIN (must be stamped into the body/chassis of the vehicle)
    • Identification Plate e.g: Compliance/Import Plate noting the Make/Manufacturer, Model, Year of compliance (applicable for non-RAV vehicles)
    • For Motorhomes up to or 4.5t GVM not previously registered as Motorhome TMR PRI is required using Motorhome/Campervan Inspection Sheet (F4550) form.

    Note:If a customer presents a branded trailer (excluding caravans) that has been registered interstate and:
    • the VIN has not been been stamped onto the chassis
    • the trailer has a VIN plate attached with a legible VIN
    The trailer can be registered if the customer can present proof of origin.

    The customer must be advised that, although the trailer is registered, it won't comply with the Vehicle Standards Regulation until the number has been stamped onto the chassis.

    Note: Contact VIU for advice should a caravan be presented for inspection that doesn't have a VIN stamped onto its chassis.

    Completing a Special Vehicle Details form or marking an inspection document (e.g. HVRAS or AIS Safety Certificate) provided by the applicant meets the requirement for the TMR identity check.
Trailer Compliance PRI
Trailer Compliance PRI
    This is a full measure up of the trailer to ensure compliance with the relevant Australian Design Rules for trailers up to 3.5t.

    It must be conducted when a customer who while eligible for a self assessment or HVRAS has chosen not to.

    This PRI can only be conducted at a TMR location identified as able to inspect trailers.

    Operator must complete the relevant inspection sheet.

    (This inspection meets the requirement of the TMR Identity Check)
Heavy Vehicle PRI
Heavy Vehicle PRI
    HVRAS inspection ensures compliance with the Heavy Vehicle Standards National Regulation for measurements of motor vehicles >4.5t GVM and trailers >750kg ATM.

    It must be conducted by TMR when a customer has not provided an inspection by an approved HVRAS officer as part of the New Business requirements.

    This PRI can only be conducted at a TMR location identified as able to inspect trailers.

    Operator must complete the relevant inspection sheet.

    (This inspection also meets the requirement of the TMR Identity Check)
TMR Identity Check
TMR Identity Check
    This PRI is required the first registration of:
    • An imported trailer
    • A trailer with a TMR issued Low Volume VIN (LVV)
    • An Individually Constructed Vehicle (ICV) with a TMR issued LVV

    The purpose of this inspection is to:
    • Confirm the trailer/vehicle description
    • Sight the identifier stamped on the trailer/vehicle
    • Ensure the attachment of the trailer plate
    • Confirm that there are no existing identifiers if issued a TMR LVV

    Operator may complete a Special Vehicle Details form or mark an inspection document (e.g. HVRAS or AIS Safety Certificate) provided by the applicant to meet the requirement of the Identity Check.

    Note:Photographs are not acceptable in lieu of a TMR Compliance check at non-inspecting locations.

    When performing the identity check the operator is to:
    • Ensure the LVV is stamped on the trailer/vehicle and no other identifiers are visible
    • Ensure that the trailer/vehicle description is correct. This is particularly important for LVV issued due to missing identifiers. The trailer/vehicle description must not be homemade.

    If a customer who is eligible for self assessment or HVRAS:
    • Has provided a self assessment form or HVRAS inspection the operator can use this form (by initialling the relevant sections) to conduct the TMR Identity Check instead of completing a vehicle inspection.
    • Has not provided a self assessment/HVRAS inspection sheet a trailer compliance PRI is also required.

Conducting a PRI for a reason other than those listed above is at the discretion of the Office Manager.
When PRI not RequiredPRI is not required when registering a vehicle after WOVI has been completed. Restriction for PRI requirement is to be expired.

Rego Easy Scheme Dealers
Rego Easy Scheme Dealers
    Rego Easy Scheme Dealers are authorised to conduct PRI on motor vehicles with a GVM <4.5 tonne GVM (excluding vehicles that TMR must conduct the PRI) and trailers with an ATM <.75t ATM or less).

    A Vehicle Details Inspection Sheet (F3529) or equivalent document must be completed at new business by:
    • Easy Pay or Plates Dealers
    • Easy Business Dealers lodging transactions in person or correcting a new business transaction with a transaction date exceeding 21 days.

    Note:The equivalent document must contain the same information as the F3529 at a minimum.

    PRIs on imported trailers
    PRIs on imported trailers
      Trailer Dealer/Manufacturers on the Easy Plates option of the Rego Easy Scheme must have an accredited HVRAS Inspector (on site) in order to conduct pre-registration inspection on trailers with an ATM over 750kg.

      HVRAS accredited persons generally cannot perform inspections for imported vehicles/trailers that have never been registered in Australia before. However under the Rego Easy Scheme dealerships can be given authorisation by the State to conduct inspections of imported trailers.

      Should a dealership wish to conduct inspections of imported trailers with an ATM under 4.5t the following steps must be undertaken:
      • Dealership is to apply in writing to the CSC Manager
      • The request must be forwarded it to the Rego Easy Unit with an endorsement from the CSC Manager. Should the CSC Manager not endorse the request, the CSC Manager is to respond back to the dealership directly in writing. The letter should clearly outline the reasons why the application has been refused (e.g. poor performance identified at a recent audit or history of identified issues in relation to previous trailers the dealer has presented for PRIs ).
      The Rego Easy Unit should be provided with a copy of this correspondence.
      • After considering the request and the CSC Manager's endorsement, the Rego Easy Unit will issue a notice to the dealership advising them on the outcome. A copy of this correspondence will be provided to the CSC Manager and archived electronically by the Rego Easy Team.
      • If the application is approved, the CSC is responsible for monitoring the dealerships ongoing performance in relation to conducting PRIs on imported trailers.
