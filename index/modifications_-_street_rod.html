Modifications - Street Rod (76)


Modifications to Street Rods must be:
  • Certified by an Approved Person (Codes LH9 or LH10)
  • Accredited by the Australian Street Rod Federation (Qld Division)
  • Authorised by the Department of Transport and Main Roads
Note:Prior to registering any modified pre-1949 vehicle (which is not a street rod certified under either the LH9 or LH10 codes) please contact Vehicle Standards and provide the modification codes. They will confirm if the vehicle is cleared for registration. This number operates 10:00am - 4:30pm Monday to Friday. 

Urgent enquiries from internal staff only before 10:00am or after 4:30pm can be directed to Vehicle Standards

Any non-urgent enquiries from either internal staff or customers can also be emailed to Vehicle Standards

Street rods have modification plates with either LH9 or LH10 codes.

LH9 street rods are eligible for Special Interest Vehicle (SIV) concessional registration only.

LH10 street rods are eligible for either SIV concessional registration or full registration.

Convert from LH9 to LH10If a vehicle previously certified under the LH9 code is to be changed to LH10, provided there has been no change of registered operator, a certificate of modification or photographic evidence must be produced to verify that a modification plate for LH10 is attached.

For further information about converting from LH9 to LH10, customers may be referred to the Australian Street Rod Federation (ASRF) website at https://www.asrf.org.au/
Convert from LH10 to LH9If a vehicle previously certified under the LH10 code is to be changed to LH9, provided there has been no change of registered operator, a certificate of modification or photographic evidence must be produced to verify that a modification plate for LH9 is attached.

For further information about converting from LH10 to LH9, customers may be referred to the Australian Street Rod Federation (ASRF) website at https://www.asrf.org.au/

Identification Numbers
    Many street rods were not issued nor stamped with chassis numbers. The vehicle register requires a vehicle identification number to be allocated to the vehicle so that it can be recorded on the register.

    The majority of these vehicles are authenticated by the Australian Street Rod Federation (ASRF). As these vehicles are subjected to a stringent identification process, an agreement was reached between TMR and ASRF whereby this organisation can provide a special chassis number to its members for each approved vehicle.

    This enables the organisation to record and provide a history of individual vehicles which in turn provides further consumer protection to its members in the event of the vehicle being stolen.

    Alternatively, TMR may allocate a chassis number provided the procedure for “surrogate identifier” is followed. The chassis number must be stamped on the vehicle prior to registration.

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Identifiers and Descriptors > Surrogates

    Note:While many of these vehicles may be re-built in recent years, the actual year of manufacture must still apply for the vehicle to be considered a street rod.

    Therefore a VIN is not to be issued to these vehicles.
