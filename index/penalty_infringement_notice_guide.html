Penalty Infringement Notice Guide (7306)


Penalty Infringement Notice Guide
What it isA Penalty Infringement Notice (PIN) is a notice under the State Penalties Enforcement Act Schedule 1 that has a PIN Code.

The issue of a PIN offers the person to whom it is issued an opportunity to have the offence alleged in the notice dealt by paying an amount specified in the notice.
When to Issue a PINA PIN:
  • Should only be issued when an authorised officer reasonably believes that there is sufficient evidence to prove all of the elements of the alleged offence.
  • Must be issued within 12 months of the date of the alleged offence.
  • Cannot be issued to persons under the age of 18 years.
  • Cannot be issued at the scene of a road accident.
Caution
Caution
    When a Transport Inspector has reasonable grounds to believe that an offence has been committed, the ‘official caution’ must be given to the person before interviewing them. This is a requirement under local jurisdiction evidence laws.

    The official caution is:
    “I warn you that you don’t have to answer any questions or make any statements unless you wish to do so.
    Anything you say or statements you make will be recorded and may later be given in evidence.
    Do you understand this?”
Investigating an offence
Investigating an offence
    The alleged offence must have been investigated and the alleged offender given the opportunity to be interviewed and raise any possible defences or exculpations. This includes CoR offences, DG offences, permit unregistered, AIS complaints and no current CoI.

    Interviews for the purpose of issuing a PIN cannot be conducted over the phone as the outcomes of the phone discussion are inadmissible in court. If the offender is not within the region the offence was detected the investigating TI should discuss the facts and evidence with their STI with the view to sending the information to the region of the alleged offender for investigation.

    Investigation Management
Issuing multiple infringements
Issuing multiple infringements
    A maximum of 3 non-mass related PINS can be issued per intercept per offender. Upon the detection of a fourth non-related offence a Brief to Adjudicator must be completed.

    A maximum of 4 PINS can be issued for axle overloads on multi-combination vehicles. The customer must also be provided with a weighing device notice with the PIN numbers recorded in the top right hand corner.
Issuing PINs and a Brief to Adjudicator
Issuing PINs and a Brief to Adjudicator
    A PIN can be issued in addition to the Brief to Adjudicator where the offences are not related and the offence is a fineable offence.

    Related offences are those of the same type of offence.

    For example during an intercept where work diary and mass overload offences (one axle overloaded 5% and another overloaded 20%) have been identified. The TI should issue a PIN for the work diary offence and while the 5% overload is a finable offence the 20% overload puts it into the severe category and a Brief to Adjudicator should be progressed for both mass offences.
Issuing a PIN
Issuing a PIN
Issuing a PIN
    A PIN must be issued by the Authorised Officer that either:
    • Detected the initial offence.
    • Conducted, reviewed or approved the investigation that proved the offence.

    A PIN can be issued on the spot or at a later date and forwarded to the alleged offender in person or by ordinary mail.

    When issuing a PIN care should be taken to ensure accuracy. Prior to completion, the corroborating officer must check the details on the PIN are accurate.

    PINS for offences detected during on road intercepts can be issued via QCIS.
    Refer: Transport Compliance > 01 Procedures > Queensland Compliance Information System (QCIS)

    PINs to organisations or as the result of an investigation must be issued manually.

    Transport Inspectors must carry a PIN book while on duty.
Finalising a PIN
Finalising a PIN
Finalising a PIN
    After receiving a PIN an alleged offender may:
    • Pay the penalty amount either in full or by entering into a Voluntary Instalment Plan (VIP).
    • Complete a statutory declaration in relation to the PIN.
    • Elect in writing to have the matter dealt with in a Magistrates court.
    • Take no action, which will result in the matter being referred to SPER.

    Under no circumstances is an Authorised Officer to accept payment for the PIN.

    For more information on how a PIN is finalised through a Customer Service Centre,
    Refer: Offence Management > Infringement Notices > Infringement Notice Guide

Cancelling a PIN
Cancelling a PIN
Cancelling a PIN
    STI approval is required to cancel a PIN. TIs wanting to cancel a PIN must provide information to the STI for a decision.

    Only an STI or Offence Processing can cancel a PIN in TRAILS.
    When a PIN can be cancelled
    When a PIN can be cancelled
      A PIN can only be cancelled when all copies are in the possession of the issuing officer and the infringement has not been issued unless:
      • the PIN has been returned by Offence Processing because errors have prevented it being entered into TRAILS;
      • the infringement is unissued and has been lost, stolen or damaged;
      • the pink and green copies have been lost and the infringement has not yet been finalised.

      If an infringement has been cancelled due to TI error, consideration should be given to continuing enforcement action.

      A cancelled infringement number can never be re-used.

    Cancelling at point of issue (manually issued PINs only)
    Cancelling at point of issue (manually issued PINs only)
      When cancelling an infringement that has not been issued:
      • draw two parallel lines across the infringement and write the word cancelled between the lines;
      • give all 3 copies to the STI. The STI will forward the copies to Offence Processing.

    Cancelling once a PIN has been issued
    Cancelling once a PIN has been issued
      When an infringement is cancelled:
      • If the cancelled infringement is recorded in CID as an outcome, email a request to have the infringement removed, including details of why the infringement has been cancelled to Compliance Intelligence (if a replacement PIN is issued enter the new PIN after the old one is removed).
      • Send an explanation letter to the alleged offender advising that no further action is required or, if a replacement PIN has been issued, provide details of the new PIN. The letter must be reviewed by the STI before being sent.
Reviewing Issued PINSSenior Transport Inspectors are responsible for checking PINs for accuracy.

The Daily On Road Compliance Return contains a list of all PINs issued by each Transport Inspector. PINs can be checked by entering the intercept ID in the search function of the QCIS portal.
Managing Issued PINs
PIN complaints
PIN complaints
    Offence Processing will forward any PIN complaints to the STI. The STI will create a case in CID.

    In response to the complaint the Transport Inspector that issued the PIN will draft a Briefing Note outlining the facts relevant to the issuing of the PIN and supply all supporting evidence for the STI to review.
Waivng a PIN
Waivng a PIN
    A PIN can be waived after it has been issued. Waiving a PIN withdraws the infringement notice and discontinues the enforcement action.

    STIs must have approval from Manager (Compliance) to waive a PIN.

    PINS being considered for waiving should be recorded in TRAILS. While a decision is being made, the STI should contact the Offence Processing Unit to have the PIN suspended, this will avoid finalisation of the PIN. Only STIs may waive a PIN in TRAILS.

    PIN Inquiry Letters - CID Model v3

    When to waive a PIN
    When to waive a PIN
      An infringement should be considered for waiving if the prosecution of the PIN is likely to fail due to the infringement notice containing:
      • Incorrect customer details
      • Incorrect offence date or locations
      • Incorrect penalty details
      • Being issued outside the time period for issuing
      • Reflecting multiple offences
      • Being issued to an interstate or overseas visitor and the prosecution copy of the infringement notice shows the alleged offender has left the state
      • A complaint was received and a decision was made to waive the infringement.
    Proceeding with the PIN
    Proceeding with the PIN
      If a decision is made to proceed with the PIN the issuing officer will:
      • Complete the Decision to Continue letter template for review by the STI.
      • Update CID.

      The STI will:
      • remove the PIN suspension in TRAILS,
      • extend the payment date by 28 days,
      • finalise the CID entry.
    Waiving the PIN
    Waiving the PIN
      Refer: Transport Compliance > Penalty Infringement Notices > The How To: Penality Infringement Notice Procedures > Waiving a Penalty Infringement Notice (PIN) (TRAILS)

      Waiving without reissue
      Waiving without reissue
        If the decision is to waive the PIN the issuing officer must:
        • Complete the Decision to Waive letter template.
        • After approval by STI, send the letter to the customer.
        • Update CID.

        The STI will waive the PIN in TRAILS and finalise the CID entry.

      Waiving with reissue
      Waiving with reissue
        If the decision is to waive the PIN and reissue another PIN the issuing officer will:
        • Complete the Decision to Waive and Issue New PIN letter template.
        • Complete a new PIN.
        • Submit the letter and PIN to STI for review.
        • After approval by STI, send the letter and new PIN to the customer.
        • Update CID.

        The STI will waive the PIN in TRAILS and finalise the CID entry.
