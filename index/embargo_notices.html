Embargo Notices (5214)


Statement
Under the HVNL An Authorised Officer may seize a thing where the thing can not, or can not readily, be physically seized and removed. In these cases the Authorised Officer may issue an embargo notice, prohibiting any dealing with the thing or any part of it without the written consent of NHVR or an Authorised Officer.

Under TO(RUM) Act An Authorised Officer may seize a document, device or other thing in relation to a heavy vehicle, a prescribed dangerous goods vehicle or the transport of dangerous goods where the thing can not, or can not readily, be physically seized and removed.

When issuing an embargo notice it must be in the approved form F4560 and list the activities it prohibits.
Serving the Embargo Notice
The Authorised Officer may issue the embargo notice by causing a copy of it to be served on the relevant entity; or

If a relevant entity can not be located after all reasonable steps have been taken to do so, by fixing a copy of the notice on the thing that is the subject of the notice in a conspicuous position and in a reasonably secure way.
Securing an Embargoed Thing
Authorised Officers may take reasonable action to restrict access to an embargoed thing. Authorised Officers may for example:
  1. Seal the embargoed thing, or the entrance to the place where the embargoed thing is located, and mark the thing or place to show access to the thing or place is restricted;
    or
  2. For equipment - make it inoperable; (for example, dismantling equipment or removing a component of equipment without which the equipment can not be used);
    or
  3. Require a person the authorised officer reasonably believes is in control of the embargoed thing, or the place where the embargoed thing is located, to do (a) or (b) above; or anything else an authorised office could do to restrict access to the embargoed thing.
Definitions
For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply:
Term
Definition
Relevant entity
  • the driver of the heavy vehicle to which the thing the subject of the notice relates;
    or
  • the occupier of the place in which the thing the subject of the notice is located.
Dealingwith a thing or part of a thing, includes:
  • moving, selling, leasing or transferring the thing or part;
    and
  • changing information on, or deleting information from, the thing or part.
Non-Compliance with an Embargo Notice
Under HVNL and TO(RUM) Act the relevant person who knows an embargo notice relates to a thing must not:
  • do anything the notice prohibits;
    or
  • instruct someone else to do anything the notice prohibits:
    • anyone from doing an act prohibited by the notices;
      or
    • the relevant person or other person from doing an act prohibited by the notices.
Where a person/s does not comply with an Embargo Notice the matter should be dealt with by way of a Brief to Adjudicator.
Withdrawal of an Embargo Notice
Where an authorised officer has issued an embargo notice for a thing; and the thing has not been forfeited, the authorised officer must withdraw the embargo notice:
  • Generally - at the end of three (3) months after it is issued;
    or
  • If a relevant tribunal or court has made an order extending the time for withdrawing the notice – at the end of the extended time;
    or
  • If a proceeding for an offence involving the thing is started before the notice must be withdrawn – at the end of the proceeding and any appeal from the proceeding.

Despite the above, if the embargo notice is issued on the basis that the thing may provide evidence of an offence against the Law, the authorised officer must as soon as practicable withdraw the notice if the officer is satisfied:
  • the thing is no longer required as evidence of an offence against this Law;
    and
  • it is not necessary for the notice to continue to prevent the thing being used to continue, or repeat, the offence.
