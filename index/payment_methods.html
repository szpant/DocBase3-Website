Forms of Payment
The authorised receiving officer is responsible for ensuring all payments receipted on behalf of TMR are in an acceptable form of payment.

Where payment is received which is not in an acceptable form, it should be returned immediately to the customer with an explanation as to why it cannot be accepted.
Acceptable Forms of Payment
Acceptable Forms of Payment
    The following forms of payment may be accepted by TMR:
    CashNotes and coins must be legal tender in Australia.
    The limits of acceptable legal tender for Australian coinage are as follows:
    Coinage
    Maximum
    5c, 10c, 20c and 50c
    (in any combination)    		$5.00
    $1$10.00 (ie. 10 times face value)
    $2$20.00 (ie. 10 times face value)
    Note:EFTPOS - there is no 'cash-out' facility at CSCs
    ChequesPayments to the Department must be in Australian Dollars.

    The date on all cheques/money orders must be the current date or a date within the past 12 months.
    Details to be Recorded on Cheques
    Details to be Recorded on Cheques
      To enable the identification of a cheque paying customer the receipt number should be recorded on the back of the cheque in the top right hand corner.

      'Not negotiable - Payee Account Only' should be stamped on the front of every cheque.

      For miscellaneous non-customer products that are not linked to a customer (eg Your Keys) the name and address of the customer should be recorded on the back of the cheque and/or added as text against the receipt. These details maybe required for dishonoured payments and debt recovery process.
    Details to be Recorded on TICA/TRAILS
    Details to be Recorded on TICA/TRAILS
      The full cheque number, including any zeros, is to be entered as the reference number on the TICA/TRAILS payment screen.

      The cheque number is usually the first six digits located at the bottom of the cheque. Bank cheques will sometimes have a longer number, as do some personal cheques.


    Personal/Company Cheques
    Personal/Company Cheques
      Personal or Company cheques must be:
      • Completed correctly
      • Signed by the payer
      Note:
      • All cheques are receipted subject to clearance by the bank
      • Change is not to be given on any cheque under any circumstances
    Travellers Cheques
    Travellers Cheques
      Travellers cheques must have been previously signed by the payer.

      The payer must then sign the cheque in the presence of the receiving officer. The officer must be satisfied that the two signatures match.

      Where the payment is for an across the counter transaction, suitable
      identification should be obtained from the payer before accepting the travellers cheque (eg passport).

      Note:Change is not to be given on any cheque under any circumstances
    Bank Cheques
    Bank Cheques
      Bank cheques are purchased from a bank and made payable to the Department of Transport and Main Roads.

      If the payer submits a bank cheque made payable to someone other than TMR, it cannot be 'signed over' and should be returned to the payer.
    Money Orders
    Money Orders
      Australian Money Orders are:
      • purchased:
        • from a physical Australia Post office or
        • online then printed by Australia Post and delivered by mail
      • made payable to the Department of Transport and Main Roads
      • valid for 12 months from date of issue

      If the payer submits a money order made payable to someone other than TMR, it cannot be 'signed over' and should be returned to the payer to claim at Australia Post.

      Note: Online money orders printed from email are not accepted by TMR.
    Bank Drafts
    Bank Drafts
      Bank drafts are payments made by overseas companies to TMR. They must be in Australian dollars.
    CardsA card may only be accepted if:
    • The terminal recognises the card
    • The card is not expired
    • The card or signature has not been visibly tampered with
    • The signature (for a signature based transaction) matches the signature on the back of the card
    • The card is not damaged.
    Note:A payment cannot be split into more than one transaction using the same card.
    Credit/Debit Cards
    Credit/Debit Cards
      Authorisation
      Authorisation
        Most debit/credit cards require PIN only authorisation. However there are some signature based transactions.

        Signature authorisation may be required for the following cards:
        • Prepaid Visa or MasterCard
        • Overseas Credit card
        • Cards that contain no chip
        • Special issue cards that have been provided to visually impaired cardholders

        The EFTPOS device will determine the type of authorisation required.

      Surcharge
      Surcharge
        ALL Visa/MasterCard branded cards will incur a Surcharge regardless of what account type is selected on the eftpos device or whether the customer chooses to tap, insert or swipe their card.

        When a customer presents a Visa/MasterCard branded card they must be advised that their payment will incur a card surcharge, regardless of the type of account their card is linked to (savings, cheque or credit.) The only acceptable card types that won't attract a surcharge are BasicsCards or eftpos cards with no Mastercard or Visa logo.

        Operators should select the following card types from TICA to ensure the correct surcharge is charged:
        • DRCARD - Used for payments made by BasicsCard or eftpos debit card. This includes contactless payments made by eftpos Tap& Pay.
        • VISA - Used for any card displaying a Visa logo, including Visa debit cards
        • MCAR - Used for any card displaying a MasterCard logo, including a MasterCard debit card.
      Contactless payments
      Contactless payments
        Contactless (Visa PayWave, MasterCard PayPass and eftpos Tap & Pay) payments are now accepted on eftpos devices. Customers can make contactless payments using a bank card that displays the contactless icon or by other methods such as an App on their mobile phone or smart watch.

        All contactless transactions >$100 will require the customer to enter their PIN. Refunds can also be issued using the tap method, a PIN is required for all refund amounts.
    Debit Cards and BasicsCards
    Debit Cards and BasicsCards
      Debit Cards including BasicsCards, are acceptable only where EFTPOS facilities are provided and can only be made in TICA, not in TRAILS.

      There is no 'cash-out' facility when using EFTPOS at CSCs.
Unacceptable Forms of Payment
Unacceptable Forms of Payment
    The following are unacceptable forms of payment:
    • Incomplete or Badly Damaged Bank Notes Refer: Incomplete or Badly Damaged Banknotes
    • Postage stamps
    • Foreign Currency whether by cash or cheque or other negotiable instrument
    • Credit/Debit Cards where:
      • Receipting office does not have the facilities to accept the type of card offered
      • Expiry date on the card has passed
      • Credit card is listed on a 'warning bulletin'
      • Credit card not signed by the credit card holder when the credit card is being used as a signature based transaction
    • Diners Club cards are not accepted by TMR.
    • American Express is not accepted at a counter or by direct debit (TMR online and PPQ do accept it but a 1.4% surcharge applies)
    • Cheques that are:
      • Incomplete (eg. unsigned)
      • Incorrectly completed (eg. the amount in words does not agree with the amount in figures)
      • Stale (eg. Pre-dated by more than 12 months)
      • Post-dated (dated in the future)
      • 'Third Party' cheques
        • These are personal or company cheques that are made out to the payer and 'signed over' to the Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR) as payment or part-payment. 'Third party cheques' should not be accepted as there is a certain element of risk as to whom the rightful owner of the cheque is and any recourse may then become the responsibility of TMR. For this reason, third party cheques should not be accepted unless special circumstances exist and the request is approved by a manager.
      • Account Payee Only cheques
        • 'Account Payee Only' cheques (eg. cheques made out to the payer with the words "Not negotiable account payee only" stated) cannot be 'signed over' and should be returned to the payer.
          Note:If the cheque is a TMR issued refund cheque and the cheque and original customer are present:
          • Contact Cash, Receipting, Banking & Reconciliation (TRAILS unclaimed cheques) by phone to stop the cheque; and
          • Re-bank funds back to original customer's trails account (if they wish to pay for a TMR product)
    • Online money orders:
      • Printed from email are not acceptable by TMR (these are considered vouchers. They do not have BSB or Account number encoding required for banking). Online money orders printed by Australia Post and delivered by mail or money orders purchased from a physical Australia Post office can be use (these do have BSB and Account number encoding required for the banking system)
