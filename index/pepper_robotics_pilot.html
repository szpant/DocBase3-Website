Pepper Robotics Pilot (7142)

Project NamePepper Robotics Pilot
WhenCommenced Mid-May 2017 for an initial period of 6 months
WherePepper has joined our teams at the:
  • Brisbane City Customer Service Centre (Elizabeth Street)
  • Chermside Customer Service Centre.

From mid-August, Pepper will move on from Elizabeth St to join the team at Toowong CSC for approximately one month and will then join the team at Carseldine CSC.
BackgroundAs part of the Advance Queensland initiative, a number of programs have been planned and developed to create the knowledge-based jobs of the future. This includes partnering with different industry groups and agencies to reinvigorate science and innovation and turn great ideas into commercial products and businesses.

The Queensland Government is advancing Queensland’s robotics capabilities, with a historic partnership between Advance Queensland and SoftBank Robotics Corporation (SoftBank). The partnership aims to position Queensland as the leader in the development and application of humanoid robotics in Australia, expand the robotics industry in Queensland and create a strong business ecosystem to secure jobs of the future in this emerging field.

TMR was chosen to participate in the Proof of Concept (PoC) project to trial the application of robotics in a customer service environment. As leaders in customer service within Government, CSB will test the functionality of Pepper in a customer service environment, and investigate how Pepper can enhance our customer’s experience.

By engaging in this PoC, Queensland is aiming to be a leader in humanoid robotics by developing a business ecosystem of robotics and autonomous systems. This PoC provides opportunities to explore ways in which human-robot interaction and collaboration can leverage the potential for improved efficiencies and productivity gains offered by robotic technology to extend our capabilities in customer service and education, accelerate business ecosystem growth, and to increase in research and development within the state. Piloting this technology now offers Queensland an exciting and unique opportunity to develop a solid foundation on which to make informed choices and play a key role in shaping the direction for adoption and integration of robotic technology into our future workplaces and communities.
How does the service work
How does the service work
How does the service work
    Pepper is a humanoid robot designed to enrich experiences by engaging with customers in a conversational manner, and with the added option of engaging through a touch user interface it means Pepper is for everyone. Customers can easily and naturally interact with Pepper. Pepper is friendly, expressive and designed to support and assist people.

    Customers will be able to interact with Pepper by speech or touch screen input. Pepper has been programmed to understand key phrases and pronunciations of key words relating to the transaction or feedback.

    When asking for a speech response, Pepper will decipher the user’s speech to extract pre-determined statements that are assigned to the options available on Pepper’s screen. As such, Pepper requires simple words or sentences to ensure that the correct option is selected by the customer.

    Additional rules have been included so that if Pepper does not understand what a customer has said, it will ask them to choose an option from the screen, but continue to accept speech and touch screen inputs.

    TMR will collect data relating to the customer’s selections, the time of each response and the number of instances where Pepper has not understood what the customer has said.
What is included in the service
Applications / Services
Applications / Services
    Concierge application
    Pepper will provide a concierge service greeting our customers upon arrival and help them to prepare for licensing, registration and adult proof of age card transactions. This includes providing advice on the correct forms, evidence of identification and other items they may need to take to a counter.

    Feedback application
    Pepper will seek and record feedback from our customers about their experience in the centre and the level of service provided by staff. This will allow the CSC to receive feedback from our customers on a daily basis.

    Data logging
    In both applications, Pepper records the customer’s journey and Pepper’s estimate of the customer’s gender, age, and emotions during their interaction. The system does not record customer photos, detailed speech responses or personal information. All person and speech recognition information is processed locally without the use of external cloud engines.

    Data is managed in accordance with TMR’s information security and privacy policies.

    Public consultation
    An online survey has been created to enable customers to provide feedback about their experience with Pepper. Flyers containing a link to the survey are being distributed to customers visiting the centres where Pepper is working.
Operating hours
Operating hours
    Pepper will be assisting staff for shifts of up to 2 hours at a time, during business hours. Specific times will be at the discretion of the CSC.
Who will be impacted and how
How will this impact customers?
How will this impact customers?
    Pepper will provide TMR customers with an innovative and engaging means of assistance in completing everyday standard transactions within TMR’s customer service environment.
How will this impact service delivery staff?
How will this impact service delivery staff?
    This initiative will not impact customer service jobs. Pepper is designed to enhance our customers experience, provide support and assist our staff, Pepper is not designed to replace staff.

    Staff should be aware that Pepper will soon be working alongside the staff at Brisbane City and Chermside CSCs.
