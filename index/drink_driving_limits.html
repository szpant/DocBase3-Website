Drink Driving Limits (2569)

There are four alcohol limits:

No Alcohol Limit The driver will be over the no alcohol limit if the concentration of alcohol in the blood or breath is more than 0.00% (zero).
General Alcohol LimitThe driver will be over this limit if the concentration of alcohol in the blood or breath is equal to or more than 0.05%
Middle Alcohol LimitThe driver will be over this limit if the concentration of alcohol in the blood or breath is equal to or more than 0.10%
High Alcohol Limit The driver will be over this limit if the concentration of alcohol in the blood or breath is equal to or more than 0.15%.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alcohol Limits by Age / Licence Type / Vehicle:
0.00% appliesThe 0.00% alcohol limit must be observed when a person is driving or in charge of any motor vehicle and any of the following applies (regardless of age):
  • Holds a learner licence
  • Holds a probationary or provisional licence
  • Holds a restricted licence
  • Holds a licence subject to the ‘I’ condition
  • Not the holder of a driver licence
  • Learning to drive the next higher class of licence under the authority of their provisional, probationary or open licence
  • RE motorcycle licence holders for the first 12 months* of holding their class RE motorcycle provisional, probationary and open licence
  • RE motorcycle licence holders learning to ride a class R motorcycle under the authority of their class RE provisional, probationary and open licence
  • a person driving a tractor or specially constructed vehicle.
*means a class RE provisional, probationary or open licence (including a licence issued under a law of another State, the Commonwealth or another country) held for 12 months during the previous 5 year period. (Period does not need to be continuous.)
  • A person driving or in charge of any of the following at any time:
    • Truck (any motor vehicle with a GVM > 4.5t)
    • Articulated motor vehicle
    • B-Double
    • Taxi / Limousine
    • Bus
    • Road train
    • Specially constructed vehicle, including a tractor
  • A person who holds a replacement licence to which a s79E order applies (X4 condition)
  • A person driving or in charge of any of the following:
    • Licensed Tow truck while operating under the Tow Truck Act 1973
    • Pilot or escort vehicle while escorting an oversize vehicle
    • Vehicle while being used by a driver trainer to give driver training
    • Vehicle while carrying a placard load of dangerous goods

Note:Any person driving or in charge of a vehicle while being used as a public passenger service for which driver authorisation is required must observe a 0.00% alcohol limit under their driver authorisation
Less than 0.05% appliesIf not listed above
