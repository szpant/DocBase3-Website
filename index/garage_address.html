Garage Address (3029)


What is it?A garage address is the address where the vehicle is garaged on a regular basis. For vehicles with:
  • Private use it is usually the same as the residential address
  • Commercial use can be the principal depot or base of operations of a vehicle or when the base of operations is in a different state form F4408 is required to be completed by the employer nominating a Queensland garaging address for the vehicle.
Why is it Required?
Why is it Required?
    It is a requirement of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Registration) Regulation 2010 that for a vehicle to be registered in Queensland it must have a garage address in Queensland. Proof of garage address must prove that the address is real and must link the registered operator to the garage address.
Requirements
  • Must be a Queensland address
  • Once a customer has EOR recorded in TICA they will not need to supply further EOR documentation unless it is determined that they have moved away from Queensland and returned.
When Required
When Required
    • Individuals:
      • when first recording a garage address (where it is different to the residential address)
      • when recording a customer's EOI for registration purposes
      Note: Evidence is not required for subsequent changes.
    • Organisations:
      • when garage address is different to the business address
      • when recording a customer's EOI for registration purposes
      Note: Once the garage address has been established in TICA, further evidence is only needed if a new garage address is recorded.

Acceptable Garage Address Documents
Acceptable Garage Address Documents
    Where the vehicle register (or any other current TMR product in the customer's name) already shows an address that the customer wishes to nominate as their garage address, no further proof is required.

    Otherwise, any of the following Garage Address Documents are acceptable:
    • Category A EOI document
    • Category B EOI document
    • Evidence of residency recorded in TICA
    • Council Rates Notice
    • Lease agreement, rent book, Notice of Body Corporate Fees
    • Insurance papers which show the complete garaging address including street number, street name and suburb.
    • Electricity, Phone (including mobile phones), Gas accounts
    • Taxation Notice of Assessment (advise customer that the ATO recommends that the customer blocks out TFN when using the notice for this purpose)
    • Mortgage/ Certificate
    • Official letter from Centrelink/Department of Human Services
    • Queensland Land Tax valuation notice
    • Australian electoral document
    • Queensland vehicle registration notice or certificate
    • Defence Housing Australia - Tenant Confirmation Letter or Email
    • Queensland driver licence renewal notice
    • Bank Statement issued within the last 6 months.
      Note:The bank statement must be issued from the same financial institution as debit/credit card supplied (account details do not need to match). Printed internet/electronic bank statements are acceptable.
Document Requirements
Document Requirements
    • Documents do not have to be originals
    • Must clearly display:
      • Customers name
      • Address
      • Issuer of document
    • Document must:
      • Establish address exists
      • Link applicant to that address

    In some instances additional documents may be needed to:
    • Establish address exists
    • Establish link from registered operator to an individual/organisation
    • Link individual/organisation to the garage address

Exceptions
Exceptions
    The following do not need to supply evidence of garaging address:
    • Government bodies
    • Vehicles to be registered in a dealers name
    • Vehicles to be transferred into a dealers name
    Persons exempt from having a Qld Garage Address
    Persons exempt from having a Qld Garage Address
      Domestic and family violence (DFV)
      Domestic and family violence (DFV)
        Persons who have relocated interstate to escape a DFV situation, may transfer the Queensland registered vehicle into their name only, in Queensland for the purpose of cancellation and re-registration in another state without a Queensland Garage address.

        Note:The customer must present a transfer form and a cancellation form for this transaction to be accepted
        Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer

      Notice of disposal (NOD)
      Notice of disposal (NOD)
        If a NOD is received at a CSC or Agency, it can still be processed if the acquirer does not have a Queensland garaging address.

        Note:This exception is only allowable to let a seller remove a vehicle from their name.

    How to use form F4408
    How to use form F4408
      It is a requirement of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management – Vehicle Registration) Regulations 2010 that for a vehicle to be registered in Queensland it must have a garage address (principal depot or base of operations) in Queensland.

      Therefore, if a customer is able to record a verifying persons garage address they must do so however, if a customer is unable to record a verifying persons garage address they may, as a last resort, still complete form F4408 as long as they provide a declaration that the vehicle will be garaged in Queensland.

      The declaration in form F4408 is only to be used in 3 scenarios. These 3 options are on the form under heading 3 ('Reason for completing form') and are to be used for:

      1.a person who has a garage address in Queensland but can't prove it (example: a person living at an address with no bills to that address in their name).
      2.a person who has no residence but is a resident of Queensland (example: a displaced person without a home).
      3.a traveller from interstate or overseas (example: a person registering a vehicle for the purpose of travelling around Queensland or Australia).

      For all three options, the customer must have a Queensland address recorded on their CRN (either residential or garage type) so that the transaction can be processed. For options 2 and 3 this could be a relative/friend or other address (e.g. shelter or hotel). There customers must be advised to:
      • Keep their postal address (can be non-Queensland) up to date to ensure that they receive notifications; and
      • Sign up for eNotices/eReminders Refer: Customer > 1.Customer > Customer Details > eContact Details > reminders and notices and be aware of the My Infringement online service so they can check and pay for outstanding fines.

      Verifying Person's Requirements
      Verifying Person's Requirements
        The verifying person must:
        • be at least 18 years of age; and
        • reside at the address where the vehicle mentioned in 2 Vehicle details, is garaged and must either:
          • be a current Qld licence holder;
            or
          • must provide a garage address document in addition to EOI. EOI can be one document from either Category A or Category B.


      Retention of form
      The form must be filed with the application as the customer won't need to provide further EOR documentation once it has been recorded in TICA.
