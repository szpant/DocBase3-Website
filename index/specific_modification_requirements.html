Individually Constructed Vehicles (ICVs)
Individually Constructed Vehicles (ICVs)
    An ICV is a vehicle:
    • that is not a standard production vehicle eg Kit Cars where the kit can be purchased ready to assemble in Australia and no VIN number has been allocated; or
    • assembled from parts, from various sources, that do not result in a vehicle identical to or closely resembles one produced by the original manufacturer.
      Note:A person or company may only produce 3 ICVs in any 12 month period.

    TypeA person building an ICV can certify their vehicle under a code of practice through consultation with an Approved person (AP) accredited with LO1.
    Process for Approval
    Code of Practice
    Code of Practice
      The process for certifying an ICV under a Code of Practice involves consultation between the certified AP (LO1 modification) and the customer with minimal interaction with TMR.
      The following steps must be followed:
      1.Customer consults with an (AP) to discuss the design of their vehicle
      2.Customer, in consultation with an AP, builds the ICV. Both must decide on a build number for the vehicle which must be stamped on a substantial part of the vehicle.
      3.Once the vehicle is completed, the AP and customer completes the Individually Constructed Vehicle Completion Certificate (F5050).
      4.Vehicle is presented to an AIS or the approved person engineer for a safety inspection. If no faults are found, F5050 is endorsed by the Approved Examiner or approved person engineer.
      5.F5050 is submitted to Vehicle standards by the AP.
      Vehicle Standards approves the application and requests a VIN from the VIU.
      VIU issue the VIN to Vehicle Standards.
      Vehicle Standards issue a compliance plate exemption permit and provide it with the VIN to the AP.
      AP issues Certificate of Modification to the customer.
      VIN stamped on vehicle.
      6.Customer registers the vehicle at a CSC.
    Supporting Documentation
    • Compliance Plate Exemption Permit/RAV entry exemption permit issued by Vehicle Standards
    • Modification Certificate or Plate
    Inspection Requirement
    • Pre-Registration Inspection (PRI): TMR Identity Check
    Important TICA updates required
    TICA Processing
    TICA Processing
    • The make and model for an ICV must always be shown as a “Custom Built Specially Constructed <Vehicle type/Body shape>”. This will be entered by the VIU and cannot be updated.
    • The compliance type for an ICV must always be R – Engineer’s report. This will be entered by the VIU and must not be changed.
    • When processing the New Business, the vehicle details screen should be updated to fill in all missing details. VIU will create the vehicle in TICA with minimal details.
    • The vehicle must be registered in the name of the person who constructed the vehicle. After first registration the vehicle may then be transferred or registered in the name of another registered operator.

Converting an Imported Campervan/Motorhome to a Passenger Vehicle
Converting an Imported Campervan/Motorhome to a Passenger Vehicle
    It is possible to change the purpose of use of a vehicle that was originally imported as a campervan/motorhome into another type of vehicle after it is first registered. To do so the owner must ensure any necessary modifications are undertaken, and if necessary, approved.

    Seating Requirements
    Allowable Seating
    Allowable Seating
      To calculate the allowable seating:
      • Subtract the Tare from the GVM.
      • Divide the difference by 81.5kg (this is the required allowance for each passenger seat).
      • Round the number down to obtain the allowable number of seats.
      Example: Vehicle has a GVM of 2600kg and a Tare of 1980kg, the difference (2600-1980) is 620kg, 620/81.5 = 7.607. Rounding down the number of seats allowed is 7.
      Obtaining the Correct GVM and Tare
      Obtaining the Correct GVM and Tare
        GVM
        GVM
          The GVM must be taken from the RAWS Import Plate that is fitted to the vehicle or be verified from the RAWS website -
          http://raws.infrastructure.gov.au/rawswebpublic/RAWPubVehSearch.asp.
        Tare
        Tare
          The Tare must be taken from the RAWS Certificate (Consumer Information Notice) or be verified from the RAWS website -
          http://raws.infrastructure.gov.au/rawswebpublic/RAWPubVehSearch.asp unless the customer provides a weighbridge certificate showing a Tare weight.

          Note: TMR may also request a customer to provide a weighbridge certificate if the tare weight needs to be clarified.

Installation of a Side Car
Installation of a Side Car
    A side car is an additional module that can be added to the side of a motorcycle. While there are specific requirements for the attachment of the side car to the motorcycle, there are more general requirements that apply to all side cars

    General Requirements
    General Requirements
    General Requirements
      PlacementMust be fitted to the left hand side (excludes historic motor cycles >30years old)
      BrakesBikes manufactured on or after 01 March 1976 must have a mechanical parking brake
      LightsBikes manufactured on or after 01 October 1991 must have reverse lights fitted if reverse gear is fitted as per ADR01/19/02 Subsection 8.5.1.

      If fitted with sidecar must have a sidelight located within 150mm of the left have extremity of the side car.
      Side Car ConnectionConnection between bike and side car must be bolted.

      Approval from TMR is required for welding of the frame or fitting of leading link forks.
      Road ClearanceMust be greater than 100mm measured from a horizontal road surface
      Compliance PlateA sidecar does not require a compliance plate
      Seating CapacityA side car is counted as a seat. CTP class will be 13.
      Body ShapeAdding a sidecar will change the body shape from motorcycle to sidecar
      Tyres and RimsAll tyres and rims fitted to the motorcycle must be motorcycle tyres and rims
    Type
    • Manufacturer’s Specification – only when the motorcycle is built to handle a side car without any frame or suspension modifications
    • Other Modification – if any welding or change to the frame is needed, or if leading link suspension is fitted.
    Supporting Documentation
    • Manufacturer’s Specification – Nil
    • Other Modification – Approval letter issued by TMR
    Possible TICA Updates Required
    • Tare
    • Body Shape
    • CTP Class

Rebodied Vehicle
Rebodied Vehicle
    A rebodied vehicle is a vehicle that has been constructed using the chassis and running gear (engine, brakes etc) from a compliant vehicle and another compliant body (production or custom built) is then attached.

    In this type of modification, the vehicle is considered to be the vehicle the chassis was taken from.

    Example: 1980 Nissan Patrol Wagon Chassis, New custom made body = 1980 Nissan Patrol Wagon (Rebodied)

    Type
    • Modification under a Code of Practice (CoP)
    Approval Process
    Approval to Rebody a Vehicle
    Approval to Rebody a Vehicle
      • Apply to QPS for a surrogate VIN (if required)
      • Engage an Approved Person – Engineer to complete the required certification under the National Code of Practice for Light Vehicle Construction and Modification codes LH5 and LH6.
    Supporting Documentation
    • Certificate of Modification or Modification Plate and surrogate number
    Possible TICA Updates Required
    • Body shape
    • Registration Category
    • CTP Class
    • Make and Model
    • Seating Capacity
    • Engine number

    TICA Processing
    TICA Processing
      • The vehicle must always be identified by the year, make and model of the chassis used, not as the body that has been used.
      • The word ‘(Rebodied)’ should be inserted into the ‘Badge’ field in the vehicle model details in TICA (A new make and model may be required)
      • The compliance type of the completed vehicle should be recorded as ‘R - Engineer’s Report’
