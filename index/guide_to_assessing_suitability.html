Guide to Assessing Suitability (4756)

What it isA person who is authorised by TMR to undertake an industry licence role is expected to be competent in performing the role and maintain high standards of conduct. It is important to recognise that the community places a high degree of trust and confidence in these persons. This guide has been developed to ensure that public confidence in industry licensing roles are maintained and enhanced.

TMR has broad discretion under legislation to consider the suitability of an applicant for a role. This guide is intended as a tool to help with confident decision-making. Your decision should take into account the individual circumstances of each application

Assessing suitability of an applicant applies to any of the following industry licensing roles:
  • Traffic Controller Accreditation;
  • Pilot Vehicle Driver Accreditation;
  • Escort Vehicle Driver Accreditation;
  • Driver Trainer Accreditation;
  • Rider Trainer (i.e. Q-Ride Trainer) Accreditation;
  • Tow Truck Driver Certificate;
  • Tow Truck Assistant Certificate;
  • Tow Truck Operator Licence; or
  • Dangerous Goods Driver Licence.
DelegationsDecisions to approve an application for renewal of an industry licence must be made by officers with the appropriate delegation. Refer to the delegations database

https://inside.tmr.qld.gov.au/corp/legislation/Delegations%20database/Forms/AllItems.aspx

Applicants may apply for decisions to be reviewed by TMR and subsequently appeal that decision by applying to the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT).
