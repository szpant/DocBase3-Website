Refunds - General Info (219)

Who can RefundOperators with a suitable TICA profile are authorised to process a refund payment to a customer with a credit balance in their account.
Using funds for another product/ transactionAll transactions that result in a refund amount being used for another product or transaction must be checked/authorised by an independent authorised officer or the business unit that generated the refund prior to the funds being used.

Funds paid to account for a separate purpose (e.g. SPER installment) or rejected via BPay are not to be refunded or used for another product or transaction.

Note:Solo Sites, QPS and non-TMR led locations will be required for audit purposes to have the refund and Business Exceptions raised approved by their supporting office prior to release of funds.
Refund MethodsRefunds can be issued by:
  • EFTPOS/Credit Card (Electronic)
  • Cheque
  • Cash

In line with the department's cheque reduction initiative, where a customer presents in person and a refund is required, the monies must be refunded electronically rather than by cheque.

Residual funds left from a cheque payment must not be electronically refunded until 12 clear days have passed from the date the cheque was receipted. This time frame is to allow for any possible dishonoured payments.

Any type of refund, including refunds of the residual funds left after utilisation of funds (eg pro-rata credit for concession update), must have text added as per below Refund Text requirements.

For duplicate payments and system errors, the refund must be made by the same method and, for electronic payments, to the same account that it was made from.

Refer below for the requirements for each refund type.
Electronic Refunds (EFTPOS/Credit Card)Electronic Refunds are only available to individual customers (excluding deceased#) and can be issued for all products/reasons where policy allows a refund providing the following requirements are met:
  • The refund can only be processed at a centre that is approved to refund electronically.
    Refer below:     List of authorised Customer Service Centres/ Agencies for electronic refunds
  • The customer must present photo id.
  • The EFTPOS/Credit Card must have the customers name displayed. If not displayed, the customer must supply a confirmation letter from the bank
  • Refunds cannot be split between cards
  • Refund amounts cannot be split.
    Refer below:     Refund limits
  • If the refund is due to an operator or system error, the card surcharge must also be refunded (will happen automatically when a receipt is cancelled). Operator and system errors are the only scenarios when the card surcharge can be refunded. A detailed text of including operator and authorising officer details must be recorded.
  • An operator cannot authorise an electronic refund when they were the operator that processed the originating transaction.
    Refer below:     Authorising refunds

#If a registration is transferred out of a deceased person's name into an individual's name and then cancelled, the refund can be made by EFTPOS providing all other EFTPOS refund requirements are met.

Additional requirements for vehicle registration refunds:
Additional requirements for vehicle registration refunds:
    • If the vehicle has joint registered operators with:
      • No concession applied, the refund can be refunded to the primary or secondary registered operator.*
      • A concession, the refund can only be issued to the primary registered operator.
      • A business name and an individual second registered operator, the electronic refund can only be issued to the listed second registered operator.*

    *The money is to be adjusted from the primary registered operator to the second registered operator's account, using adjustment code TRFCUST, if the refund is to be issued to the second registered operator. Refer: Create Adjustment
Additional requirements for cancelled receipts/operator error:
Additional requirements for cancelled receipts/operator error:
    • The refund must be completed on the same card that was used in the initial transaction
    • The four digits on the EFTPOS receipt must match the last four digits on the customer's card

All requests for an electronic refund to an organisation credit card must be refused (even if the individual registered operators name is also on the card).

If the requirements for an electronic refund are not met the refund will be issued by cheque.

Once an electronic refund has been issued, it cannot be reversed.

Refund timeframe
Refund timeframe
    Customers must be advised that the refund could take up to 5 business days to appear in their bank account. This is because the refund is subject to the internal controls and processes of the customer's bank.
Refund limits
Refund limits
    • The maximum amount that can be refunded by EFTPOS is $1000 (inclusive). Refunds cannot be split to get around this rule.
    • The only exception to this is for cancelled receipts/operator errors. In these instances, operators must request their SASM to contact the CBA 24hr Help Desk on 1800 022 944 to request authorisation to increase the limit and authorise the refund on the device.
    • Prior to calling the CBA Help Desk the following details are required:
      • The CSCs physical address
      • BSB and Account number details from the CSC deposit book

      Note: When quoting the BSB and Account number from the deposit slip, you will need to add to the beginning of the BSB ‘06’, as this represents the bank name – CBA.

    • If a portion of the funds are to be used for another product (either on the same day or within 28 days), and the remaining funds are $1000 or less, then the remaining amount can be refunded by EFTPOS. This scenario is not considered splitting the refund.

Authorising refunds
Authorising refunds
    An electronic refund must be approved by an authorised PIN holder at an authorised CSC/Agency.
    Refer above:     List of authorised CSC/Agencies for electronic refunds

    An incorrect PIN can only be entered three times before the EFTPOS machine is locked. You will need to contact CBA to unlock the EFTPOS machine.
    Business Exceptions
    Business Exceptions
      If the registration cancellation and refund:
      • Are processed by the same operator, the registration cancellation Business Exception will be automatically approved in TICA when the manager's PIN is entered. An independent officer must authorise the release of funds and enter the manager's PIN.
      • Are processed by different operators, a registration cancellation Business Exception will be raised. The Business Exception is to be checked and cleared by an independent officer who is authorised to enter the manager's PIN.

    Note: Although some CSOs can issue refunds by cheque they are generally not approved to be authorised PIN holders.
    Refer: Admin > 1. Administration > Security > Security of Information > EFTPOS Authorisation PIN
Refund by Cheque
Conditions for refund by cheque
Conditions for refund by cheque
    The following conditions should be explained to the customer:
    • The amount available for refund is the amount calculated by the initial transaction (eg. registration cancellation or concession pro-rata.) Credits in the account due to rejected/failed BPAY or Direct Debit payments can not be refunded to the customer. These payments are investigated by the E-Business unit.
    • If the payment being refunded was by cheque there is a twelve (12) day delay (before it can be released) to ensure the cheque is honoured
    • An administration fee applies to refunds
    • The refund cheque may take up to 15 working days to be received.
Refund not received
Refund not received
    If a refund cheque has not been received, the following steps must be completed before escalating to cash and banking:
    • Check the customer's account to see if it has been released
    • If not released, investigate and where appropriate, release the funds and apologise to the customer.

    Enquiries should only be escalated to Cash and Banking if it has been more than 15 working days since the cheque was released.
There must be a clear separation of duty, an operator cannot issue a cheque refund when they were the operator that processed the originating transaction.

Refunds that can't be issued by cash or EFTPOS/Credit card should be released by cheque.

Cheques are to be released as soon as possible to avoid money sitting in customer’s accounts. (Excludes monies for duplicate payments and online service payments requiring a full refund these will be dealt with by the EBusiness Team).

All released cheques are batched and sent to SAP for processing on a Friday evening, therefore any corrections can be made prior to COB on the Friday after the release of the cheque. These can be managed at a local level. Requests to stop a cheque after it has been batched must be emailed to TRAILS unclaimed cheques.
Cash RefundsCash refunds can only be made if the original payment was made in cash.

Cash refunds must be authorised by a Supervisor or Manager and are only applied when:
  • Customer received incorrect change and returned within 2 working days
  • Customer returns a refundable miscellaneous non-customer product within 2 working days

Proof of purchase must be provided.

Cash refund for incorrect change
Cash refund for incorrect change
    Customers may demand a cash refund if they receive the incorrect change.

    If they return the same day the operator should balance their cash. If there is a corresponding or greater cash surplus, refund to the customer the requested amount.

    If they return within two (2) working days:
    • Check the banking to see if the operator was over that day and refund is appropriate.

    If there were no operator overs for the relevant day inform the customer of this.

    It is up to the manager's discretion whether a refund is given.
Product RefundsA customer may receive a refund for a product if:
  • Publication is incomplete
  • Product that was damaged prior to purchase
  • Product purchased in good faith but not required (excludes Learner Licence Logbook)
  • Outdated stock sold after new stock available

Once the return is accepted the customer account is credited for amount. The customer may request a cash (if eligible), EFTPOS or cheque refund.
Online Payment Refunds Requests for BPAY and Australia Post payment refunds should be sent to EBusiness Unit.

A customer may receive a refund for a service paid online by credit card or direct debit if the assessing centre/hub has emailed the Refund email template to Cash and Banking (for credit card payments) or Direct Debit (for Direct Debit payments) confirming the customer is eligible to receive a reimbursement.

If the payment was made by credit card the payment item must be Unmatched and a text placed on the record before the email template is sent to Cash and Banking. This will place a credit in the customer’s account for the full payment amount (less merchant fee).

Direct Debit payments must not be unmatched or invalidated by anyone other than the Customer Account Management Unit.

Refer:Admin > 3. Money > The How To: Money > Account Management Procedures > Refunds > Refund by credit card (customer not present and original payment made online or by direct debit)

Refund textText explaining the reason for the refund must be recorded in the "Provide details for the refund" field in TICA.

Standard text examples:
CANC REGO <123ABC up to 5 registration numbers are to be included in the text >
BULK CANCELLATION OF REGISTRATIONS DD/MM/YY (Used when over five registration cancellations are refunded at the same time)
BACKDATE CONCESSION <123ABC>
APPLY CONCESSION <123ABC>
DL SURR
OPERATOR ERROR <DESCRIPTION>
<DESCRIPTION OF ANY OTHER REASON>

In addition to the above, if the refund has been issued by EFTPOS, the user ID of the officer who entered the Manager PIN must also be recorded.
AUTHORISED BY <USER ID>

Note:The number of PIN holders should be kept to a minimum. In the event that the processing operator knows the Manager PIN, an independent person who also knows the Manager PIN must authorise the paperwork and enter the Manager PIN. This ensures that the refund details are correct and not compromised.
