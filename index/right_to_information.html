Right to Information (7430)

What is it?The Right to Information (RTI) Act is intended to provide a right of access to information held by government, this right of access is subject to the grounds for refusal which include public interest considerations.
What can be releasedWhile the department meets the object of the RTI Act and provides access to information/documents wherever possible, it does not guarantee access to all or any parts of the relevant documents.

In situations where the proposed release of the requested documents concern another person or entity, consultation would be required under the RTI Act.

Access to documents that contain information relating to a third party (including a business, company, council, private individual and so on), depending on the information in question, is likely to be refused.

Never advise a customer that they ‘will’ be given access a document through RTI as there is no guarantee the information will be released. Only advise that they have a right to apply for release of the information.
Making application and feesApply and pay the application fee online at:
https://www.smartservice.qld.gov.au/services/information-requests/home.action

Where an application will take more than five hours to finalise, processing charges are also applicable. The application fee and processing fees are non-refundable irrespective of whether access to the documents will be granted.
Further informationFurther general information about Right to Information is available on the department's website:
http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/About-us/Right-to-Information.aspx

or on the Queensland government website:
http://www.qld.gov.au/about/rights-accountability/right-to-information/.
