Proof of Origin (Ship) (3119)

What it isProof of origin:
  • Proves the history of a ship
  • Serves as a deterrent to those involved in stolen vessels
  • Assist the QPS in prevent the re-registration of stolen vessel
Types of Proof
Registration Number
Registration Number
    • Evidence that the ship was previously registered interstate (e.g. registration certificate)

Bill of Sale/Receipt
Bill of Sale/Receipt
    A bill of sale or receipt:
    • Showing the details of purchase
    • Issued by a:
      • Recognised finance company, Government auction sale or Dealer
      • Private seller (must include seller’s full name, address and phone number)
Personal Property Securities Register
Personal Property Securities Register
    Personal Property Securities Register searches includes ships registered in:
    • Qld
    • Interstate
    Certificates must be < 7 days old.

    Personal Property Securities Register searches are available from:
    http://www.ppsr.gov.au/Pages/ppsr.aspx

Legal Documents
Legal Documents
    Legal documents
    Legal documents showing the applicant as being legally entitled to the ships such as:
    • court order, court decision, judgement, property settlement naming the applicant as being entitled to the vessel
    and
    • identifying the ship (eg HIN/SIN or registration number and make)

    or
    • deceased estate documents detailing the applicant as the executor or administrator
    and
    • a statement by the applicant that the ship is being registered for the purpose of finalising the estate.
No Proof
No Proof
    If customer is unable to provide one of the above options a search of TICA using the provided identifiers can be conducted.

    If the search is unsuccessful in the CSC and;

    Identifiers Recorded
    If there are identifiers recorded on the ship, the customer must contact QPS to obtain written confirmation that the ship has not been recorded as stolen (in Queensland or Interstate).

    If the QPS search does not identify the ship as stolen the customer must complete a Statement of Acquisition containing:
    • Details of who the ship was purchased from,
    • Date of acquisition,
    • Amount paid

    A copy of the QPS confirmation must be attached to the statement.

    If QPS are unable to provide written confirmation that the ship hasn't been stolen, the customer must complete a Statutory Declaration containing:
    • Details of who the ship was purchased from
    • Date of acquisition
    • Amount paid

    No Identifiers Recorded
    If there are no identifiers recorded on the ship, the customer must complete a Statutory Declaration containing
    • Details of who the ship was purchased from
    • Date of acquisition
    • Amount paid
