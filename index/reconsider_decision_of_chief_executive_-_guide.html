Reconsider Decision of Chief Executive - Guide (1339)


About Reconsideration of Decisions
What it isUnder certain circumstances, applicants have the right to have a decision of the Chief Executive reconsidered.
Decisions that can be Reconsidered
Granting Driver Licences
Granting Driver Licences
    • Refusal to grant a driver licence under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s23(1)
    • Refusal to grant a class or type of driver licence under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s23(1)
    • Refusal to renew a driver licence under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s23(1)
Logbooks/Driving Experience
Logbooks/Driving Experience
    • Decision not to approve a logbook under TO(RUM-DL)Reg2010 s32
    • Refusal to grant credit towards the 100hrs of supervised driving experience under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s33
    • Refusal to grant an exemption from the logbook requirement under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s34
Granting Exemptions
Granting Exemptions
    • Refusal to issue a Certificate of Exemption allowing a person to drive a high powered vehicle under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s71
    • Refusal to issue a Certificate of Exemption allowing late night driving under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s73
    • Refusal to grant an interlock exemption allowing a person (subject to the Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program) to drive a vehicle that is not fitted with a prescribed interlock and nominated to TMR under TO(RUM) Act 1995 s91Q(1) and (7)
    • Withdrawal of an application for an interlock exemption on the grounds that further information was not provided as requested by TMR under TO(RUM) Act s91Q(5)
Amending/Suspending/Cancelling Driver Licences
Amending/Suspending/Cancelling Driver Licences
    • Immediate suspension of a Qld driver licence on medical grounds under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s126
    • Immediate amendment of a Qld driver licence on medical grounds under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s126
    • Amendment, suspension or cancellation of a licence due to show cause action under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s126
    • Withdrawal of a person's authority to drive in Qld on a non-Qld licence because the person has a mental or physical incapacity under TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s128(3)
Amending/Suspending/Cancelling Exemptions
Amending/Suspending/Cancelling Exemptions
    • Immediate suspension of an interlock exemption under TO(RUM) Act 1995 s19
    • Amendment, suspension or cancellation of an interlock exemption due to show cause action under TO(RUM) Act 1995 s19
    • Amendment, suspension or cancellation of a high powered vehicle/late night driving certificate of exemption due to show cause action under TO(RUM- DL) Reg 2010 s74C
Extending Prescribed Periods (AIIP)
Extending Prescribed Periods (AIIP)
    • Extension of a prescribed period (for a person subject to the Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program) under TO(RUM) Act 1995 s91V(4)
Applying for Reconsideration
Application RequirementsAn applicant must provide:
  • For reconsideration of decisions relating to the Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program, a written request for the Chief Executive to reconsider the decision must be submitted within 28 days of the original decision.
  • For reconsideration of all other decisions, a completed Application for Reconsideration of a Decision by Chief Executive(F2981) lodged within 28 days of written notice of the decision or,
  • sufficient information in writing for the decision to be suitably reconsidered lodged within 28 days of written notice of the decision
Where to ApplyApplications may be lodged by:
  • Mail to the responsible Director of the area where the decision was made or the Interlock Processing Unit (for decisions relating to the Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program) or the Central Processing Unit (Exemptions) (for decisions relating to High Powered Vehicle/Late Night Driving Certificates of Exemption):

Consideration of Application
DecisionA person must request the Chief Executive to reconsider the decision before making an application to the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) for an external review of the decision.

The Chief Executive must reconsider the original decision and either:
  • Confirm the original decision
  • Set aside the original decision & substitute a new decision

The Chief Executive must promptly give the applicant written notice of the decision.

If the applicant is still dissatisfied with the decision following Chief Executive reconsideration, they may then appeal this decision through QCAT.

Note:Appealing to the QCAT is not available to persons whose authority to drive in Qld has been withdrawn under TO (RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s128(3); s132(6)&(7)
