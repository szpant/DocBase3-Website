About Q-Safe Practical Driving Tests
What it is
Assesses applicant's driving skills and knowledge
Assesses applicant's driving skills and knowledge
    A practical driving test is done to assess an applicant's suitability to obtain or retain a driver licence.

    It enables TMR to determine whether an applicant possesses the necessary skills and knowledge to safely use the road system.

All applicants for a Provisional/Probationary/Open Licence
All applicants for a Provisional/Probationary/Open Licence
    A practical driving test is required by an applicant for a Qld Provisional, Probationary or Open Driver Licence when:
    • Applying for a higher class of Qld licence
    • Removing a Condition A or B on an existing class of Qld licence
    • Who reside outside the 100km Q-Ride radius who are applying for a class RE or R motorcycle licence
    • Licence holder from an experienced driver recognition country (formerly called a recognised country) under the age of 25 years
    • Licence holder from a recognised country (formerly called a prescribed country) applying to transfer a class of licence, other than a C, RE, or R licence
    • Licence holder from any country that is not a recognised country or experienced driver recognised country applying to transfer any class of licence
    • Applying for Provisional/Probationary/Open licence as a returning driver
      Note:There are some exceptions when a practical driving test is NOT required.
      Refer: When Practical Test is Not Required

      Note:A foreign licence holder who fails a practical driving test will have their authority to drive withdrawn. They may use their foreign licence to undertake a subsequent practical driving test. However if they wish to gain further practical driving experience they will be required to obtain a Queensland learner licence.

QPS Police Officers / Emergency Service Officers
QPS Police Officers / Emergency Service Officers
    Police Officers and Emergency Service Officers that are required to drive large vehicles as part of their official duties, are tested by the Police Driver Skills Unit. They are not required to undertake a TMR practical driving test.

    Once the test is successfully completed the Officer in Charge of the Driver Skills Unit will issue a Licence Endorsement Request.

    The requirement that an applicant must hold a licence in the lower class for the appropriate period still applies.

    This is presented at a CSC and the licence upgraded to that class of vehicle.

    Note:Emergency Service Officers can include an officer of the Qld Ambulance Service, Qld Fire and Rescue Service, and the State Emergency Service.


    Note:Testing is only conducted by the Police Driver Skills Unit and does not include other Police community units such as PCYC.

Purpose of Fee
    This is to ensure applicants:

Fee Payable Depends on Booking Date
Fee Payable Depends on Booking Date
    As the practical driving test fee usually increases each year on the 1 July, the fee payable is that which is current as at the booking date.

    Customers paying test fees in the period prior to July 1 should be asked when they intend booking the test, to ensure the correct fee is receipted.

    Example
    Example
      Scenario
      • Customer wishes to pay test fee on 28 June
      • Customer advises they will be making the test booking on 5 July

      Resolution
      • Customer must pay the fee applicable for a test as at 5 July (new fee)


    If the fee has increased between the date the customer pays and the date the booking is made, and the customer has only paid the old fee, the customer must pay the difference between the old and new fee.

    Example
    Example
      Scenario
      • Customer paid $65.00 fee to account on 1 June
      • Fee increases 1 July to $67.00
      • Customer makes test booking on 10 July

      Resolution
      • The customer needs to pay the difference between the new and old fee as the test booking is made on a date when the current fee is $57.00.

Forfeit of Fee
Forfeit of Fee
    A test fee is forfeited if the applicant:
    • Fails the test
    • Gives inadequate notice of cancellation/alteration of test time (failure to give 2 working days notice)
      Note:Use discretion if cancellation is <2 working days, particularly if the test booking can be re-allocated to another applicant, or swapped.
    • Provides a defective vehicle where applicant has shown disregard for safety requirements (Eg bald tyres)
    • L Plate/s are not correctly displayed
    • Arrives late or does not attend
    • Fails to meet Logbook requirements
    • Test taken when Logbook requirement not met
    • Commercial Vehicles:
      • Does not present a current vehicle inspection label or certificate of exemption
      • Certificate of Exemption issued under Motor Vehicle Safety Amendment Act 1993 s.30(2)
    In all cases, management discretion is to be used. Customers should not be penalised unless there is evidence of abuse of the appointment system.
Re-instatement of Forfeited Fee
Re-instatement of Forfeited Fee
    The office manager or supervisor can authorise the re-instatement of forfeited test fee payments.

    The test fee must be credited to customers when the below vehicles are presented for a PDT and the test is refused:
    • Alpha Takata Airbag Recall - vehicle has not been cleared
    • All personally imported vehicles that have airbags fitted (refer to 'Presenting for Test - Test Vehicles')

Eligibility to Take Test when Logbook Requirements Apply
Eligibility to Take Test when Logbook Requirements Apply
    Applicants for a C class P1 Provisional licence who must meet the Logbook requirements, can not take a practical driving test until the Logbook requirement is met. If the Logbook requirement has not been met, the customer must be advised to reschedule their test.

    Note:It is the responsibility of the CSC/QGAP Manager or Officer in Charge (QPS), to ensure that the Logbook and tenure requirements have been met before the Driving Examiner conducts the test.

    Should a customer present for a test stating that they have lodged their logbook for assessment, Refer: Driver Licence > 6. Learner Licences > Learner Logbook Guide > Learner logbook and/or Prior Driving Experience Application Enquiries

    If a test does go out and it is later identified that the person had not met their logbook and/or tenure requirements, the Manager should immediately review centre practices and put in place procedures to ensure it doesn't happen again.

    For Logbook requirements, Refer: Driver Licence > 6. Learner Licence > Learner Licence Logbook Guide > Driving Experience Required - C Class Learner Licence Holder Under 25

    Test taken when Logbook requirements not met
    Test taken when Logbook requirements not met
      If a test is taken when the person has not met the Logbook requirements, the test is invalid and the test fee is forfeited. The test must be undertaken again once the Logbook requirements have been met.

      Note:If a person takes a test when they have met the logbook requirements but not met tenure, the test is valid. If they pass the test, the person is to be advised to return for their DL upgrade once their tenure has been met.

Applicant requirements on arrival
Applicant requirements on arrival
    Applicant must present:
    • Standard EOI (photo identification and driver licence only is required if scheduled for an 8am or Saturday test).
    • Completed but unsigned Driver licence Application/Renewal (F3000) if the test is not conducted at a formless centre.
    • Translation of foreign licence if test is occurring on a Saturday or the licence details have not been previously recorded in TICA and the test is occurring on a standard operating day.

    If the applicant's image quality of their EOI (for example, passport, learner licence, non-Qld licence) is insufficient to confirm the identity of that person (to a reasonable standard), the DE/CSO may:
    • take a 1:1 image comparison;
    • use discretion to refuse the test, and have the test fee refunded/rescheduled.

    An image comparison may assist in reducing the risk of someone other than the applicant completing the test.

    If a customer presents at a non-formless centre the customer is to sign in the presence of the CSO. The CSO must complete the Authorising Person's Checklist, with the exception of the Application Approved/Refused item and sign the Authorising Person's declaration BEFORE the applicant commences the test. The CSO to check the booking comments to see if an Interpreter is booked. The Application Approved/Refused item is completed when the applicant has completed their test.

    Fees and Forms > Formless > Formless Customer Applications


    If the CSC is not open Refer: The How To: Licence Testing Procedures > Customer Sits Practical Driving Test

Test Vehicles
Test Vehicles
    Applicant must provide a vehicle for the practical driving test which is:
    • Roadworthy.
    • Currently registered (with the registered operators permission to use the vehicle).
    • Representative of the class being tested.
    • Not part of the Takata Airbag Recall (Alpha Version) that has not been cleared* Refer: Takata Airbag affected vehicles.
    • Not a personally imported vehicle (with airbags fitted) as there is no way for TMR to determine if a Takata Airbag has been fitted or, if fitted, it has been replaced *. However, if the customer advises that the vehicle did have a faulty Takata airbag and produces evidence that it has been replaced, the vehicle may be used.
    • Correctly displaying L Plate/s.
    • Inspectable vehicles must have a current COI recorded (unless exempt) or have been granted an extension of time.
    • must not have registered purpose of use 'Dealer'

    *Note:If a customer presents for a PDT with a vehicle fitted with an Alpha Takata Airbag, or has a personally imported vehicle fitted with an airbag and the test is refused, the test fee is credited to customer for re-schedule of test.

    Primary Production Vehicle
    Primary Production Vehicle
      If the applicant is using a primary production vehicle (i.e. vehicle is registered under a primary producer concession) for the practical driving test, the applicant must provide written advice from the owner of vehicle that:
      • the owner of the vehicle is a primary producer; and
      • the applicant undertaking the practical driving test is currently employed by or will be working for the primary producer after passing the practical driving test.

    Booked Hire Pupose of Use
    Booked Hire Pupose of Use
      A vehicle with a POU of booked hire (BKHR) or booked hire rental (BKRN) can be used for a practical driving test, provided:
      • The booked hire service signage is removed (as booked hire signage should only be displayed when the vehicle is being used to provide a booked hire service. This relates to the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994 which limits the use of signage to vehicles that are able to be booked or currently providing a ride share service).
      • Any security camera facing inward is turned off.

    Taxi Purpose of Use
    Taxi Purpose of Use
      A vehicle with a POU for Taxi companies (TAXI) can be used for a practical driving test, provided:
      • The applicant has the registered operators permission (this may be company).
      • The hail light has been turned off (as per Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994 which limits the use of a taxi service to vehicles that are available for on-the-spot hire by members of the public).
      • Any security camera facing inward is turned off.
      • Any mandatory taxi equipment is turned off (e.g. taxi meter, card reader) to limit driver distraction.

    Heavy Vehicles and public passenger services
    Heavy Vehicles and public passenger services
      Heavy vehicles (for example, buses used for urban services or public passenger services) may be used for a practical driving test, provided:
      • Any security camera facing inward is turned off.
      • Any signage that may indicate the vehicle is available for a service is turned off.

    Takata Airbag affected Vehicles
    Takata Airbag affected Vehicles
      Prior to taking a vehicle out for a Practical Driving Test (PDT) Driving Examiners (DE) should check that the vehicle has not been affected by the Takata Airbag Recall (only required for Alpha/Critical version), or is not a personally imported vehicle that is fitted with airbags.

      The DE can go to the Product Safety Australia website and check the make and model of the vehicle presented for PDT. If the make and model are listed with or notation; only then do you need to conduct further searches.

      If the vehicle is recorded as being fitted with Alpha/Critical type airbags, DEs can check the vehicle registration on the Is My Airbag Safe website. The links will then lead you to the manufacturers website which will advise if the vehicle has been cleared. As there may be delays in updating the website, the DE can also request the customer provide evidence from the manufacturer or an authorised repairer that the airbags have been replaced.

      If the customer is unable to provide this evidence, the DE should inform the customer that the vehicle is unable to be used on a driving test due to safety concerns.

      If the customer presents a vehicle that has been personally imported, and has airbags fitted, then the CSO/DE should inform the customer that the vehicle is unable to be used for a driving test due to safety concerns. This is because there is no way for TMR to determine if a Takata Airbag has been fitted or, if fitted, it has been replaced. If the customer advises that the vehicle did have a faulty Takata airbag and produces evidence that it has been replaced, the vehicle may be used.

      If the customer is required to reschedule the PDT they must not be charged another booking fee to reschedule.

Test Failed
Cooling Off Period
Cooling Off Period
    A cooling off period will apply for customers who fail a class C (or CA) practical driving test.

    First fail - Customers are not able to re-sit the practical driving test on the same calendar day.

    Second fail - Customers must wait at least seven days before they can re-sit the test (may re-sit the test on the 8th day after the second fail test date).

    Third and subsequent fail - Customers must wait at least 28 days before they can re-sit the test (may re-sit the test on the 29th day after the third or each subsequent fail test date).

    Note:The cooling off period will not apply if a customer fails a practical driving test undertaken:
    • for a class LR, MR, HR, HC, R or RE licence, or
    • by the Indigenous Driver Licensing Unit (IDLU) for any class
Exemption from Cooling Off Period
Exemption from Cooling Off Period
    There are no legislated exemptions from the cooling off period, however a Manager may exercise their discretion if the customer has extenuating circumstances.

    An exemption is only valid for one cooling off period.

    If granted, a text restriction must be applied to the customers record. The text restriction must be expired by SBS once the test is booked.
Foreign Licence Holder
Foreign Licence Holder
    A foreign licence holder who fails a practical driving test will have their authority to drive withdrawn.

    The Driving Examiner must give the customer Notice of Withdrawal of Authority to Drive (F5044) before the customer leaves and advise the customer that they may use their foreign licence to undertake a subsequent practical driving test. Notation to be recorded on Driver Assessment Report -"F5044 issued to customer -/-/-. "

    Notice of Withdrawal of Authority to Drive (F5044) can also to be emailed/mailed to customer if required.

    However, if they wish to gain further practical driving experience they will be required to obtain a Queensland learner licence.
