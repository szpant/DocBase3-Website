Mobile Phone Double Demerit Points Project Update (5360)

Project NameMobile phone double demerit points
When1 September 2015
BackgroundDouble demerit points for using a mobile phone while driving are being introduced from 1 September 2015 to discourage this dangerous driving behaviour.

Currently, there is a 3 demerit point penalty for each mobile phone offence committed by a driver in Queensland. Under the double demerit point scheme commencing on 1 September 2015, a second or subsequent mobile phone offence committed within one year of an earlier offence will attract double demerit points. This means that a first mobile phone offence will attract 3 demerit points; another mobile phone offence committed within a year of the first offence will attract another 3 demerit points, plus an additional 3 demerit points for it being within a year of the first offence. The person will therefore have 6 (3 + 3) demerit points allocated to their traffic history for the second offence.
Who will be impacted and how?CSC and Call Centre staff may receive enquiries from the public about this initiative, particularly about which offences incur double demerit points.

The mobile phone offences that could result in double demerit points are:
  • any driver using a hand-held mobile phone while driving (including when stopped in traffic or at traffic lights); and
  • any use of mobile phones by learner and P1 provisional licence holders under 25 years of age or by P1 probationary or P1 restricted licence holders.
Any combination of the above offences will incur double demerit points; the offences don’t need to be exactly the same.
Further informationChanges are being made to relevant forms, publications and websites to advise the public of the change.
