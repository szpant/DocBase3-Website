Process Show Cause Amend, Suspend or Cancel Action (4532)

Adding Show Cause
Adding Show Cause
    Applies to:
    Applies to:
      Issuing a show cause (to amend, suspend or cancel) to an authority for any of the following:
      • Tow Truck Driver/Assistant
      • Dangerous Goods Driver
      • Pilot/Escort Vehicle Driver
      • Driver/Rider Trainer
      • Traffic Controller
    1
    Advice received
    Advice received
      Receive written documentation
    2
    Determine action to be taken
    Determine action to be taken
      Refer to the Compliance Management Policy and Procedures for accreditation schemes and industry authorities when determining the action to be taken when contraventions by an authority holder have been identified.

      Compliance Management Policy and Procedure

      Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an authority holder when grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel an authority.

      Show Cause Policy and Procedure

      If you are not satisfied that reasonable grounds for a show cause action exist, you may:
      • take no further action
        or
      • communicate further with the authority holder
    3
    Issue Show Cause
    Issue Show Cause
      Prepare a written notice of either:
      • Show Cause Notice
      • Notice of Immediate Suspension
      • Notice of Immediate Suspension and Proposed Action to Cancel

      Note:For further assistance for Dangerous Goods Drivers, Tow Truck Driver/Assistant's, Pilot Escort/Vehicle Drivers, Driver/Rider Trainers and Traffic Controllers, please contact the Industry Accreditation and Licensing Unit
    4
    Record the show cause action
    Record the show cause action
      Record show cause action
    5
    File documents
    File documents
      • File copy as per office procedure

Customer Response and Decision
Customer Response and Decision
    Applies to:
    Applies to:
      Analysing and processing the customers response to make an informed decision.
    1
    Customer provides a submission in response to the Show Cause Notice
    Customer provides a submission in response to the Show Cause Notice
      A customer can make written or personal representations. If personal representations are made the representations should be digitally recorded.

      Notes:
      • An authority cannot be surrendered (and status changed, once a Show Cause Notice has been issued).
      • A physical card can be returned and recorded, but this does not alter the effect of the show cause.
      Extension of time
      Extension of time
        • If TMR agrees to alter the response time this can be updated in TICA and a business exception created.
      If there is no response
      If there is no response
        Once the initial response period (i.e. 28 days) has passed, the delegate of the Chief Executive must make a decision to either suspend/cancel/amend the authority as outlined in the Show Cause Notice, make a different decision or contact the authority holder to see if they will be providing a submission.

        Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an authority holder who has not provided a submission on the proposal to amend, suspend or cancel an authority.

        Show Cause Policy and Procedure

        A Decision Notice must be sent to the authority holder.

        Refer to the Show Cause Template Letter Guide for template letters when the delegate of the chief executive is proposing amending, suspending or cancelling an authority.

        Show Cause Template Letter Guide
      If the customer responds
      If the customer responds
        Once the initial response period (i.e. 28 days) has passed a decision notice must be sent to the authority holder.

        The decision can be to:
        • continue with decision to suspend, amend or cancel
        • make a different decision to suspend, amend or cancel
        • take no further action
    2
    Customer does not provide a submission in response to the Show Cause Notice
    Customer does not provide a submission in response to the Show Cause Notice
      Once the initial response period (i.e. 28 days) has passed you must make a decision to either suspend/cancel/amend the authority as outlined in the Show Cause Notice, make a different decision or contact the authority holder to see if they will be providing a submission.
    3
    Show Cause decision
    Show Cause decision
      At the hearing, the delegate of the Chief Executive considers personal or written representations made by the customer.

      On making the decision, the delegate of the Chief Executive considers, if provided, personal or written representations made by the authority holder.

      Refer to the Compliance Management Policy and Procedures for accreditation schemes and industry authorities when determining the action to be taken when contraventions by an authority holder have been identified.

      Compliance Management Policy and Procedure

      Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an authority holder when making a decision to amend, suspend or cancel an authority.

      Show Cause Policy and Procedure
    4
    Chief Executive's decision
    Chief Executive's decision
      After considering representations, the Chief Executive must decide whether or not to take the proposed action stated in the Invitation to Show Cause Notice or to take a different action.

      Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an authority holder when grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel an authority.

      Show Cause Policy and Procedure
    5
    Notice of decision
    Notice of decision
      Written notification of either:
      • Decision Notice – Action Taken
        or
      • Decision Notice – No Action
      must be sent to the customer advising them of the outcome of the Show Cause.

      Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures and the Show Cause Template Letter Guide, for further information and letter templates.

      Show Cause Policy and Procedure
      Show Cause Template Letter Guide
    6
    File documents
    File documents
      • File copy as per office procedure
