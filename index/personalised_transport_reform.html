Personalised Transport Reform (7238)

Project updatePersonalised Transport Reform - March 2021 TICA release
OverviewAs a result of the Personalised Transport review a new framework has been developed to improve safety standards, provide customers with greater choice and flexibility, improve customer service and ensure accountability in the Personalised Transport Industry.

Since the introduction of the Manage Booked Hire (MBH) Staff User Interface (UI) in October 2017, there have been several major phases and releases for PTR. The reforms' focus now is about making enhancements and improving usability for customers and staff. Some new initiatives are also being released.
Who will be impacted and how?

    March 2021 Release
    March 2021 Release
      MBH Enhancements
      MBH Enhancements
        A number of improvements to the display and functionality of the MBH staff UI are being made, including:
        • Reducing the MBH lockout period from 30 minutes to 5 minutes. This means you'll be able to edit MBH records when you need to instead of being locked out for a significant period.
        • A double entry for MBH email addresses to confirm it has been entered correctly. This means a customers email address will be entered twice if it's added to MBH and the email address isn't recorded as an eContact.

          Note:If the email address is recorded as an eContact, the email can't be updated via MBH. It will need to be updated in eContacts (this is existing functionality).

        • 'Application and owner' tab for BEA and BHSL will be condensed to fit more information on the one page, and it will also display the 'Edit' button closer to the top of the page. This means you'll be able to edit records more efficiently. No functionality changes have been made to the this tab.
      BHSL Vehicle Restriction
      BHSL Vehicle Restriction
        • MBH and online services have been updated to prevent left hand drive vehicles being attached to a BHSL. The error messages will read:
          • Vehicle must not be a left-hand drive vehicle (MBH);
            and
          • This vehicle does not meet the vehicle requirements. Please nominate another vehicle (online).
        • TICA has been updated to prevent the POU changing to booked hire, booked hire rental, taxi or limousine if the vehicle isn't a right-hand-drive vehicle. The error message will read:
          • CO482E - STEERING MECHANISM CANNOT BE LEFT HAND DRIVE.

      November 2018 Release
      November 2018 Release
        Booking Entity Authorisation invoicing
        Booking Entity Authorisation invoicing
          As part of the November TICA release, TICA has been updated so that increases to fleet sizes are displayed in the payment/invoice screens and decreases to fleet sizes appear as a credit in the customer's account (to be refunded). As a result, CSO's are now able to decrease fleet sizes and refund (if applicable).
        Bulk Booked Hire Service Licences (BHSL)
        Bulk Booked Hire Service Licences (BHSL)
          Also part of this release is the ability for organisations (who have a credit account) to apply or reapply for bulk BHLS's online. This change is timely and means that all of those organisations who applied for multiple BHLS's at the end of 2017 will now be able to reapply online. The bulk service allows a customer to apply/reapply for between 10 and 60 BHSL's per day.

          TransLink have been liaising directly with organisations who hold multiple BHSL's to encourage them to set up a credit account and then reapply online. The ability to apply/reapply for bulk BHLS's is part of the existing BHSL online service. If an organisation with a credit account logs in to this service, the option to apply for bulk BHSL's will be displayed as the first question. This question will not display for ineligible customers.

          Once the customer applies online, the BHSL is lodged and then their bank account is swept over night. If the payment dishonours and payment is not made by another method, the customer's credit account will be suspended and the BHSL product status changed to VOID.

      September 2018 release
      September 2018 release
        New Qualification Foreign Entity (FOEN)
        New Qualification Foreign Entity (FOEN)
          TICA is being updated to include a new Qualification for foreign organisations who apply for a BHSL or BEA and do not have an ARBN or ACN. A Foreign organisation that:
          • is registered in Australia can have either an ARBN (qualification) or ACN (recorded in the ACN field).
          • does not have an ARBN qualification or ACN must have the new qualification FOEN added to their TICA record.

          The new qualification is added in the same matter as existing customer qualifications.
        Reapplication
        Reapplication
          TICA is being updated to change the way that a reapplication is processed so that “Reapply” is a separate option under the Booked hire service licence drop down menu. The separation of reapplication from application makes it clearer and easier for operators to process.
        Storage of BHSL details
        Storage of BHSL details
          Vehicle and registered operator details will be stored when the BHSL is created, each time the BHSL is updated and when the BHSL expires. The history item for the BHSL will show the point in time information for that action. In addition to the new historical information, if a change occurs in TICA to the:
          • vehicle details (registration number, purpose of use and CTP class), the changed information will be displayed in the current view only (not history) of the BHSL.
          • registered operator/s, the Registered Operator field will still display as it was when the original application was made, however a new “Show Current” field will display the current R/O.

        PTR filing report
        PTR filing report
          Invalidated applications:
          A fix was implemented in this release so that if a BHSL/BEA is invalidated on a different day to the received transaction, no filing item will appear on the PTR filing report (for the invalidated transaction).

          However, if a BHSL/BEA is invalidated on the same day as it was received, it will appear on the PTR filing report as "Received". Where a mistake has been made (e.g. recorded on wrong CRN), there will be no associated filing. In this scenario, the operator checking filing can check TICA and write "in error" next to the "received" filing item. This issue should be fixed in the September release.

        Reapply for BHSL
        Reapply for BHSL
          A new option to Reapply for a BHSL has been introduced which allows a customer to reapply for their BHSL up to 6 weeks prior to the expiry date.

      April 2018 release
      April 2018 release
        On 27 April 2018, phase five of the PTR will be implemented. The major changes affecting CSC's and customers are:
        Renewal online (being implemented in this release but not published until August when first lot of renewals are due):
        • The BEA can be renewed online using the BEA holder's CRN, name and BEA number.
        • Partnerships and trusts cannot use the online service to renew and must apply in person.
        • Renewals and existing BEA's will both display as separate entries in the MBH UI.
        • BEA's can be renewed up to 6 weeks before and 30 days after the effective (expiry) date of the BEA.
        • A criminal history check is performed for each person same as new applications.
        • The renewal fee is calculated as at the effective date (regardless of when application is received).
        Driver Authorisation (DA) public register online:
        Driver Authorisation (DA) public register online:
          • Allows the status and classes of a driver authorisation to be checked (no personal information will be returned)
          • A user can either upload a file containing DA numbers for checking or manually enter up to 5 DA numbers
          • Any enquiries about:
            • The results returned (currency or class of DA) can be referred (via warm phone transfer or email) to the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office
            • Using the register can be referred to Passenger Transport Authorisation Unit
        Filing Report:
        Filing Report:
          • PT filing will now display as "Personalised transport document filing report" with the CRN reference and description of action (e.g. Receive BEA renewal). Operators no longer need to manually record PTR filing details.
          • The filing cover sheet (F3419) and TRAILS Filing envelopes are being updated to include "Industry or Personalised Transport" as one tick box. Until the envelope/cover sheet are updated, operators can manually write "or Personalised Transport" next to Industry Authority. PT filing should no longer be filed with "Other".
        Auto invalidation of unpaid received application:
        Auto invalidation of unpaid received application:
          • If an application is received and not paid within 30 days, it will automatically be invalidated.

      October 2017 Release
      October 2017 Release
        From 1 October 2017 CSC/Agencies will receive applications for Booked Hire Service Licences (BHSL) and Booking Entity Authorisations (BEA) into the new 'Manage Booked Hire' Staff User Interface (UI), which is accessed via the Individual or Organisation Hubs in TICA under the 'Products' drop down.


        Confirmation page prior to receiving the BHSL/BEA application
        Confirmation page prior to receiving the BHSL/BEA application
          An operator will be entering the BHSL or BEA application as usual via the 'Manage Booked Hire' Staff UI. Once the operator has completed the application and then click on ‘Receive application’ a new confirmation page will be displayed.

          Check the application details and if correct click on ‘Confirm application’.

          If details are incorrect click on ‘Change details’ and update with the correct details.

          Confirmation page for BHSL & BEA applications

        Update or Invalidate BHSL/BEA applications in a status of 'Received'
        Update or Invalidate BHSL/BEA applications in a status of 'Received'
          Update
          Update
            To update the details on a BHSL or BEA application in a 'Received' status, open the application in the 'Manage Booked Hire' staff UI, then click on ‘Edit application and owner’ button to update the details.

            Once updated, click ‘Save’. If the operator doesn't want to keep the updated details then click on ‘Reset’ or ‘Back to application’ if no action was taken.
          Invalidate
          Invalidate
            To invalidate a BHSL or BEA application in a 'Received' status, open the application in the 'Manage Booked Hire' staff UI, then click on ‘Invalidate’ button to remove the application.

            A message will then display:-
            • ‘Do you want to invalidate the application?’ (if no application fee was paid)
            • 'Payment must be un-matched first' (if an application was paid)

            To invalidate the application click on ‘Confirm Invalidate’ or click on ‘Back to application’ if you don’t want to proceed with the invalidation.

            If 'Invalidate' was clicked, a message will display ‘Record updated successfully’ and the status of the application will show as ‘Received in error’.

        CSC/Agency responsibility
        CSC/Agency responsibility
          CSC/Agencies staff are responsible for:
          • Ensuring that key areas of the BHSL/BEA application form are completed,
          • Checking Evidence of Identity
          • Updating details including aliases, town and country of birth,
          • Creating new customer (forms and full documentation required) if required,
          • Updating registration for BHSL (refer: Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) Guide > Update Registration),
          • Entering the application details on the 'Manage Booked Hire' staff UI under the application type and
          • Receipting the relevant fees (including the invoicing of Criminal History Check (CHC) on the BEA application only, example below).

          Once receipted the CSC/Agency operators must scan the application form and all of the supporting documentation and email to their local Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office

          The original BHSL and BEA application forms and the supporting documentation are to be filed with the operator's daily filing but will need to be manually added to their TRAILS filing sheets until an update to the filing sheets in April 2018.

          Note:Supporting documentation will include copies of relevant certification of registration of a company, company extracts identifying the current directors or registration of business name from ASIC as well as legal documentation for Trusts and Partnerships.

    More detailsTo provide a personalised transport service a person must attain and maintain a BHSL using an appropriate vehicle which is stated in the licence. They must also be affiliated to a Booking Company that holds a current BEA.

    Application for a BHSL (new and reapply) and BEA (renew and new) can be completed online via the TMR website or in person at a TMR CSC or QGAP centre.
