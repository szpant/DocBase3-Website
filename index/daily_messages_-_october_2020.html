    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records
    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records

      Please be aware that the confidentiality of customer records is paramount at all times. The TMR policy Access to Customer Records provides clear direction to all employees not to take action to access a customer record unless the access is authorised.
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
        • There is a genuine “need to know” and the information is being used as part of the employee’s official duties and they would be hindered in their performance if they didn’t access the information;
        • The employee is entitled to access the information under law;
        • A customer has given permission for their login details to be used to access online services - this is for the customer contact centre/Help Direct/Online Support/Ebus/CAM only following completion of verbal EOI;
        • There is an official business transaction to be completed, including a transaction for a work colleague. The official business transaction must be done over the counter or via the call centre and the employee treated the same as any other customer (e.g. a ticket must be taken, full transaction requirements must be met). A SASM/Manager must also be informed to remove any perceived or potential conflict.

      Unauthorised access is:
      Unauthorised access is:
        • Accessing your own customer record;
        • Accessing customer records of friends or relatives (even if they have given permission) including online services if using a work computer;
        • Accessing a record where an actual, perceived or potential conflict of interest exists;
        • Accessing information because it would be convenient for a staff member to know;
        • Accessing a record on behalf of another TMR employee unless it is part of an official business transaction (e.g. contacting help direct or asking a colleague to access a record to assist with a transaction is acceptable);
        • Assisting a third party to access a customer’s record (e.g. mother calls and asks about an issue her family member is having with an online service and the employee assists the mother using the family member’s details).


      Please note: Customers contact the department in person, over the phone, via the internet or in writing. Transactions should be in line with your current role. If you are in doubt – ask your supervisor or manager.

      What can happen if I access a customer’s record inappropriately?

      Misuse of confidential information is a criminal offence under s408E Criminal Code. Unauthorised access and use of the information contained in this system may lead to criminal prosecution and/or disciplinary sanctions including:
      • Criminal prosecution;
      • Termination of employment;
      • Suspension from duties;
      • Reduction of classification level and a consequential change of duties;
      • Transfer or redeployment to other public service employment;
      • Forfeiture or deferment of a remuneration increment or increase;
      • Reduction of remuneration (increment) level;
      • A monetary penalty (which can be deducted from your pay);
      • A formal reprimand;
      • Record of disciplinary action - a permanent record of the incident on their file which they would need to disclose during pre-employment screening for all public sector roles.

      All staff are required to be familiar with the Access to Customer Records and Releasing Customer Information policies which are published in DocBase, available via the below links.

      Access to Customer Records policy summary v2
      Access to Customer Records Policy v5
      Releasing Customer Information

      Staff should also view the Access to Customer Records Policy page on SharePoint for more resources.

      For more information about access to customer records, please talk to your manager or supervisor.

    26/10/2020 - TICA Tenure Calculator
    26/10/2020 - TICA Tenure Calculator

      Staff are advised that the TICA tenure calculator calculates one month as 30 days, rather than calculating the calendar month. As a result, some customers are being told the incorrect date they can upgrade/reapply for a licence.

      For example; a customer who has obtained an RE learner licence on 26 October 2020 will be eligible to upgrade to an open licence on 26 January 2021. However, the tenure calculator will calculate 90 days and advise the customer is eligible to upgrade on 23 January 2021.

      Staff can use the calculator as a help tool when calculating tenure for customers, but will need to manually calculate if there’s a requirement for an exact date. A fix for the issue is being investigated.

    09/10/2020 - Special Need – Learner or Provisional Licence Applications (Age Rule) - Reminder
    09/10/2020 - Special Need – Learner or Provisional Licence Applications (Age Rule) - Reminder

      Driver Licensing Policy (DLP) are receiving a high volume of special need (age rule) licence applications (SNAR’s).

      DLP recognise that these licences are a popular request, as many young people seek to obtain their licences early, before they meet the minimum age requirements. However, young drivers continue to be over-represented in fatal crashes. The minimum age for a licence is an important part of the graduated licensing system and is based upon a strong body of evidence. As such, exemptions to this safeguard are granted only in the below strict circumstances.

      The customer needs to prove (with necessary evidence) that they:
      • Have a need (as opposed to a want) to drive. This must be for either medical, educational or work purposes and
      • Do not have reasonable access to other forms of transport and
      • Would experience severe hardship if the licence is not granted. Severe hardship can also be proven if the applicant has taken on a role of special responsibility in relation to their family, and that family member would suffer if the licence is not granted.

      Severe hardship is more than ordinary hardship and more than inconvenience or preference.

      Some customers who apply for SNAR licences are not aware of or misunderstand the strict criteria.

      To assist in setting reasonable and realistic expectations, please inform potential applicants about the strict criteria that they must satisfy before providing them with an application.

      Nadine Dumont
      Manager

    08/10/2020 - Prior Driving Experience Application - Updated
    08/10/2020 - Prior Driving Experience Application - Updated

      The Learner Logbook Hours Verification Letters issued by VicRoads may be accepted as supporting evidence of prior driving experience. The letter verifying drive hours is not provided in hard copy but always emailed to the recipient in PDF. The verification letter will be provided on the VicRoads letter head.
      It is the customer's responsibility to obtain a copy of this document.
      The customer may also request VicRoads send a copy of this letter to TMR. In this instance, VicRoads will send the letter via email as a PDF to both the customer and to Learner.Logbook.CPU@tmr.qld.gov.au.
      Alternatively, on the spot confirmation is available by phoning VicRoads on 13 11 71. This is on the provision that the customer either initiates the call or the team member calls on their behalf, and the customer provides authority for the information to be conveyed. The number 13 11 71 to contact VicRoads is on the verification letter.

    09/10/2020 - National Transport Commission Amendment Package No. 6 - Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Dangerous Goods) Regulation 2018 and Edition 7.7 ADG Code
    09/10/2020 - National Transport Commission Amendment Package No. 6 - Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Dangerous Goods) Regulation 2018 and Edition 7.7 ADG Code

      The Transport Operations (Road Use Management – Dangerous Goods) Regulation 2018 (the Regulation) is updated every two years on the basis of nationally-agreed amendments co-ordinated by the National Transport Commission (NTC). These amendments align with international best practice and meet changing user needs in Australia.

      The amendments include the recently-adopted 6th package of amendments and Edition 7.7 of the Australian Code for the Transport of Dangerous Goods by Road and Rail (ADG 7.7).

      Key changes include:
      • An increase in the placarding threshold for a vehicle transporting dangerous goods packed in Limited Quantities, depending on the make-up of the load.
      • New duties and associated offences for consignors and prime contractors, ensuring they have information readily ascertainable during transport.
      • Consigners, loaders, prime contractors and drivers must not transport nominally empty storage vessels that do not comply with Chapter 7.2 of the ADG Code.
      • A Prime Contractor must not direct or induce a driver of a vehicle transporting a placard load to park the vehicle, or leave the vehicle standing, in a public or private place and
      • A Prime Contractor must not direct or induce a driver of a road vehicle that is transporting a placard load, that is equipped with a burner to heat the load, to operate the burner or permit it to be operated, except in accordance with Part 13 of the ADG Code.

      ADG 7.7 can be used from 1 October 2020 and has a 12 month transition period until it is mandatory from 1 October 2021. The latest edition of the Code is available on the NTC website at www.ntc.gov.au/sites/default/files/assets/files/ADG%20Code%207.7.pdf.

      A summary of the differences between ADG 7.6 and ADG 7.7 can be found on the NTC web page at www.ntc.gov.au/codes-and-guidelines/australian-dangerous-goods-code.

      Queensland has amended the Regulation to align with changes in ADG 7.7. The Regulation commences on 1 October 2020 and is expected to be available on the Queensland Parliamentary Council (OQPC) website at www.legislation.qld.gov.au/browse/inforce#/sl/title/t around this date.

      To assist industry transition to the new ways of complying under the ADG Code, from 1 October 2020 until 30 September 2021, they will be able to choose to comply with either the new ADG Code requirements (ADG 7.7) or those that currently apply (ADG 7.6).

      The commencement date in some states may be later than 1 October 2020, so industry is encouraged to check directly on commencement dates with their competent authority.

      Should you wish to discuss the Regulation or the ADG 7.7 further, please do not hesitate to contact the Dangerous Goods Unit on this email address. DGU@tmr.qld.gov.au

      Wayne Claydon
      Dangerous Goods Transport Advice
