Certificate of Competency (COC) LR,MR,HR,HC,MC (4994)

What it is
Certificate of Competency (CoC)
Certificate of Competency (CoC)
    A practical driving test is not required for licence holders who have completed an approved training course delivered by a NSW or VIC licensing authority approved Registered Training Organisation for:

    LR classTLILIC2014 Licence to drive a light rigid vehicle
    MR classTLILIC2015 Licence to drive a medium rigid vehicle
    HR classTLILIC2016 Licence to drive a heavy rigid vehicle
    HC classTLILIC3017 Licence to drive a heavy combination vehicle
    MC classTLILIC3018 Licence to drive a multi combination vehicle

    On completion of the above units of training for the respective heavy vehicle licence class, the applicant will be issued a CoC.

    The CoC will show the relevant driver licence issuing authority (NSW or VIC).
EligibilityAll eligibility including licence tenure and written road rules test must be met prior to issue of a licence.

Refer: Driver Licence > 1. Driver Licence > 1. Qld Driver Licence > Classes - Qld Licence > Class Progression/Regression > Driving Experience Required to Progress to Higher Class

Refer: Driver Licence > 5. Testing > Written Road Rules Tests
ValidityThe customer must present with a current CoC which will remain valid for 12 months from the date of issue for the purpose of obtaining a heavy vehicle licence class.

Note:Under no circumstances can a licence be issued using a 'Statement of Attainment' certificate only, the applicant must present a valid CoC from NSW or Vic.

    Process for Accepting
    NSW or Vic CoC    		As part of the national agreement, Queensland will mutually recognise a CoC issued in NSW from 1/1/13 or VIC from 1/7/13 if presented for the issue of a Queensland driver licence.

    The CoC is not required to be verified by the VIC or NSW licence issuing authority. The original CoC (not a copy) should be accepted on face value, provided it is valid (i.e. date of issue is within the last 12 months) and there is no doubt about it's authenticity.

    An original CoC includes:
    • Hardcopy provided by the RTO
    • Electronic certificate (where it can be verified that the email has been sent from an approved RTO)

    If there is any doubt about the CoC's authenticity, it should not be accepted. The customer is to be advised they must obtain written verification from the VIC/NSW licence issuing authority that the CoC is valid. Where this occurs, record the following text on TRAILS/TICA to prevent the CoC from being accepted without the written verification from VIC:

      'Original CoC not accepted due to concerns about it's authenticity. Written verification is required by VIC/NSW before the CoC is accepted.'

    The customer must then present the written verification and the original CoC to a customer service centre before a Queensland driver licence can be issued.

    Note:The original CoC and the written verification is to be filed with the application form.
    Process for Recording
    NSW or Vic CoC    		Operators will be required to exempt the practical driving test prerequisite for customers presenting a valid CoC.

    Refer: Driver Licence > 1. Qld Driver Licence > The How To: Qld Licence > Qld Driver Licence Procedures > Upgrade Driver Licence

    In this scenario operators will also be required to:
    • Exempt Practical Driving Test with reason of OTHER
    • Enter the CoC certificate number the Reason field
    • Record a text against the customer advising:
      • The State CoC was issued
      • CoC certificate number and
      • Date of issue
    • Expire text the same day
