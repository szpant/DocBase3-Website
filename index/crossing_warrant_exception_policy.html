Purpose/Rationale
Purpose/Rationale
    The purpose of this policy is to provide clarification on requests received from schools or other interested parties (for example, developers, local government) for a School Crossing Supervisor position on a crossing/potential crossing that does not meet current warrants.
Relates to Transport Policy
Relates to Transport Policy
    This policy relates to the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme policies only.
Scope
Scope
    The policy only applies to requests received from schools or other interested parties (for example, developers, local government) for a School Crossing Supervisor on a crossing that does not meet current warrants.
Replaces Current Policy
Replaces Current Policy
    SCSS database policy 4.1.17 – School Crossing that do not meet current Warrants.
Background
Background
    Current policy states that schools assessed as having a low risk threshold generally do not meet the current warrant criteria and should be notified in writing of this decision by the Manager (Road Safety).

    Schools will also be advised that the school Principal has the option to:
    • Reapply at a later date if their circumstances change.
    • Work with a SafeST Committee and road safety staff to investigate other options for a safe road environment.
    • Negotiate with the appropriate road authority to establish an unsupervised children’s crossing.
    • Establish a volunteer children's crossing.

    The scheme is open to all Queensland primary schools, including special schools and is funded based on the highest risk.

    The policy does not define primary schools or special needs schools. The policy does not take into account primary schools or special schools located in or near other primary schools, high schools, colleges or schools that share split campus facilities that cross roads or cater for requests received from other interested parties (for example, developers, local government).
Definitions
Definitions
    For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply.
    • Warrants:
      A school crossing warrant is based on a set of objective criteria linked to the road safety risks of an individual school's situation. Emphasis is placed on the most common problems in school zones, which are excessive vehicle speed, traffic volume and visibility (road design and fixed hazards).
    • Schools:
      All primary schools, special schools, primary schools or special schools located on or near other primary schools, high schools, colleges or schools that share split school facilities that cross roads.
    • Interested parties:
      Includes but is not restricted to developers, local government, shopping centres and day care centres.

Policy
Policy
    All schools assessed as having a low risk threshold and that generally do not meet the current warrant criteria are ineligible for funding for a School Crossing Supervisor (SCS) position. A low risk threshold is deemed to be a Risk Assessment score of between 0 - 24.9 points.

    The Manager (Road Safety) is to notify, in writing, school principals and/or interested party who requested a SCS of this decision and provide alternative options other than a TMR paid SCS position. Refer: Procedures below.

    The scheme is open to all Queensland primary schools, including special schools and is funded based on the highest risk.

    No exceptions apply to TMR funding a SCS position based on the warrant eligibility policy. However a policy exception exists to fund certain operational costs associated with a SCS position at ineligible school crossings. Refer above: Attachment A - Table 1 - Variations that may apply to ineligible school crossings

    An ineligible crossing must have:
    • appropriate infrastructure in place through the local road authority owners
    • authorisation, training and assessment of a SCS position delivered by TMR.

    Other variations may exist where a SCS position is not warranted but some type of supervision is required around schools to resolve ongoing SafeST committee road safety concerns. Regional managers have the discretion to develop a written agreement or Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to resolve the issue. Refer: MOU Traffic Monitors as an example of a type of supervision around schools but not a SCS position.

    Any individual agreements/MOUs developed are to be approved by the Regional Director and Regional Director (SEQ North) as lead Regional Director for Road Safety. The lead Regional Director for Road Safety will decide if further approval is required by the Executive Director (Services Division).
Procedures
Procedures
    SCS policy database procedure: 3.1.05 School Community Consultation.

    Schools assessed as having a low risk threshold generally do not meet the current warrant criteria and should be notified in writing of this decision by the Manager (Road Safety). Refer above: Template – Letter to School notifying decision of assessment by the Manager Road Safety.

    Schools are also to be advised that the school principal has the option to:
    • Reapply at a later date if their circumstances change.
    • Continue to work with the SafeST Committee and/or TMR road safety staff to investigate other options for a safe road environment.
    • Negotiate with the appropriate road authority to establish an unsupervised children’s crossing.
    • Establish a volunteer children's crossing if appropriate infrastructure can be negotiated, volunteers must wear the standard personal protective equipment, undertake training by TMR, be issued with an authority card and have regular assessments as determined by TMR.
    • TMR will pay for personal protective equipment for up to two volunteers, other equipment, training, and assessments. Schools utilising a volunteer are responsible for workers' compensation and public liability cover which may be arranged through the P&C/P&F. See SCSS related policy 7.1.03 – Volunteer School Crossing.
    • Establish a paid SCS position if appropriate infrastructure can be negotiated. Refer above: Attachment A - Table 1 - Variations that may apply to ineligible school crossings

    Managers (Road Safety) are to:
    • Seek advice from other managers state wide to establish if a similar situation has been negotiated.
    • Document any agreement negotiated on who will pay for initial and ongoing costs and seek endorsement for the arrangements by the Regional Director, the Regional Director (SEQ North) as lead Regional Director for Road Safety. The lead Regional Director for Road Safety will decide if further approval is required by the Executive Director (Services Division).

    Road safety staff are to process the recommendation onto the SCS database.
