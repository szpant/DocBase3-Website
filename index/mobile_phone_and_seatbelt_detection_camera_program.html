Mobile Phone and Seatbelt detection camera program (7643)

Project NameMobile Phone and Seatbelt detection camera program
WhenFrom 26 July 2021
BackgroundThe project to roll out mobile phone and seatbelt detection cameras is being implemented in phases.

Once fully rolled out the cameras will be placed throughout Queensland and will detect a driver illegally using a mobile phone or not wearing a seatbelt. Passengers not wearing a seatbelt in the front seat will also be detected. Infringements will eventually be issued in the same manner as speeding and red-light cameras. The phases are detailed below.

Trial: July - December 2020
The Queensland Government ran a trial of new camera technology to detect mobile phone and seatbelt offences in 2020. Following its success, the project will move to the Warning Letter phase.

Warning Letters – From 26 July 2021
  • This phase is designed to:
    • raise awareness of the cameras and the penalties for mobile phone and seatbelt offences;
      and
    • encourage drivers to leave their phones alone and ensure that all occupants wear seatbelts.
  • The Warning Letter will not include a photo of the event.
  • No fines or demerit points will be applicable.
  • The Warning Letter will not appear in the correspondence hub as the letters are being generated manually by prosecutions services.

Infringement Notices – 1 November 2021
  • Fines and demerit points will apply.
  • Additional details will be provided closer to implementation.

Seatbelt exemption certificatesFrom 8 July 2021, customers who have a seatbelt exemption certificate, can register their certificate with TMR so that they don't receive a "Not wear a seat belt while driving" Warning Letter (or Infringement Notice).

System changes were made in the June TICA release to enable the certificates to be recorded. A business rule has also been incorporated into the camera detection process so that customers with a certificate recorded will not receive seatbelt Warning Letters or Infringement Notices for driver seatbelt offences. During the Warning Letter phase, the business rule will be manual, that is, a Prosecutions Officer will manually check for a seatbelt exemption certificate.

If a certificate is not recorded before they receive the Warning Letter or Infringement Notice, they can lodge it after the fact. If it is lodged:
  • During the Warning Letter phase, they won't receive any further seatbelt Warning Letters or Infringement Notices for driver seatbelt offences until the expiry on the certificate.
  • After they receive an infringement, their fine will be waived if the certificate was valid at the date of the offence.

    Note: If someone else was driving who holds a certificate, the registered operator can nominate the driver. The driver will also need to lodge their exemption for their fine to be waived.

Passenger seatbelt offences
Passenger seatbelt offences
    If an exemption certificate is recorded, it will not stop a customer from receiving a "Driving with an unrestrained passenger" notice. Although the passenger may also have an exemption recorded, there is no way of knowing who the passenger is at the time the camera detects the offence.

    If an unrestrained passenger has an exemption certificate and the registered operator receives:
    • a Warning Letter, the certificate holder should lodge it with TMR so that it's recorded for the future (for example, if they are the driver for a future offence, no notice will issue). No other action is required.
    • an Infringement Notice, they must lodge it so that the fine can be waived. More details about the waiving of fines will be provided closer to the start of this phase.
Lodging a seatbelt exemption certificate:
Lodging a seatbelt exemption certificate:
    1.Online - An online submission service is being developed to allow customers to easily lodge their certificate.
    Note:The online service will be hidden until the minister announces details (likely to be 26 July 2021)
    2.Email to seatbeltexemptions@tmr.qld.gov.au
    3.Post to PO Box 645 Toowoomba Qld 4350
    4.CSC/QGAP

    Certificates lodged:
    • Online and by email will be:
      • received at a new mail box (seatbeltexemptions@tmr.qld.gov.au);
      • processed by CSC operators from around the region (access will be granted to those areas with capacity).
    • By post, will be processed at the Toowoomba CSC.
    • At a CSC, will be processed over the counter.

    The Exemption from Wearing a Seatbelt Certificate form has been updated with amendments to encourage customers to lodge it online with TMR but also includes the other lodgement methods.

    No EOI is required to lodge the certificate and a third party can lodge on someone's behalf, for example a health professional. Once lodged, a confirmation letter or email will be sent to the customer advising that the seatbelt exemption certificate has been recorded.

    No fee will be applicable to lodge a seatbelt exemption certificate.
Change to mobile phone road rulesNew illegal phone rules will commence from 26 July 2021.

The new rules make it illegal to hold a phone or have it resting on any part of your body (e.g. lap) regardless of whether it's turned on.

You can only hold a phone when safely stopped to:
  • Pay for goods and services (i.e. at a drive through)
  • Gain access to or from a road-related area (i.e. car park)
  • Present a digital driver licence or other document to police when asked
  • Get a card or money out of a phone wallet for the above reasons.
Warning Letter process
1.Potential offence detected.
2.Warning letter processed (a manual check will be conducted to see if an exemption certificate is recorded. If so, a seatbelt notice won't be issued.
3.A Warning Letter will be issued by post to the legal entity registered operator (individual or organisation) of the vehicle as at the date of event - it will follow the same rules as camera infringement notices.
4.No action is required by the registered operator (no fine or demerit points apply).
5.If the registered operator has a seatbelt exemption which hasn't been recorded, they can lodge it to avoid receiving future notices for seatbelt offences.

Further information will be provided prior to the commencement of Infringement Notices.
Who will be impacted and how?
(Warning Letter stage only)
Customers
Customers
    • May receive a Warning Letter due to a potential mobile phone or seatbelt offence being detected.
    • Can lodge their seat belt exemption certificate to avoid getting Warning Letters and Infringement Notices for driver seatbelt offences.
    • Will be able to lodge their seat belt exemption online, via email, post or in person. Third party lodgements are permitted.
Call Centre Consultants
Call Centre Consultants
    May receive calls from registered operators who:
    • Received a Warning Letter but have a seatbelt exemption certificate (not yet lodged).
    • Received a Warning Letter but have previously lodged a seat belt exemption certificate.
      Note: This could occur due to a processing error during the Warning Letter phase (where the officer checking missed that the registered operator held an exemption certificate).
    • Advise they weren't driving as at the time of event.
    • Advise that they have sold or disposed of the vehicle.
Customer Service Centre Operators
Customer Service Centre Operators
    May receive face-to-face enquiries from registered operators who:
    • Received a Warning Letter but have a seatbelt exemption certificate (not yet lodged).
    • Received a Warning Letter but have previously lodged a seat belt exemption certificate.
      Note: This could occur due to a processing error during the Warning Letter phase (where the officer checking missed that the registered operator held an exemption certificate).
    • Advise they weren't driving as at the time of event.
    • Advise that they have sold or disposed of the vehicle.

    Will be able to lodge, then approve or refuse seatbelt exemption applications.
