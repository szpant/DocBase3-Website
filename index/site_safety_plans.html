Site Safety Plans (5261)

Site Safety Plans
What it isThe Site Safety Plan is used to document the assessment of the risk posed to the public and Transport Inspectors by the activity to be performed at the site.
Completing a Site Safety PlanThe Site Controller is responsible for completing the Site Safety Plan. The plan must be completed prior to any enforcement activity being undertaken at a Static Interception Site.

Completing a Plan
Completing a Plan
    • The site diagram may be hand drawn or a copy of a map.
    • The Site Safety Plan can be pre populated and the relevant information completed for the activity, such as:
      • Time, date
      • Staff present
      • Weather conditions
    • When completing a Site Safety Plan, consideration should be taken regarding the presence of artificial light sources when assessing the rating to be assigned to the 'time of day' factor. E.g. a proposed interception site under bright street lighting or with bright artificial light provided by floodlighting may be considered as a 'day' level of risk rather than night or dawn / dusk.
    • Cross reference the suggested control strategy obtained from the Site Safety Plan with the Control Strategy Implementation table.
    • The suggested control strategies, listed in order of stringency, and associated control options are:
      • Eliminate - cancel the activity if possible, or implement detours, stop all traffic or create a 'safety zone' in which to work if the activity cannot be avoided.
      • Separate - create a 'safety zone' in which to work or use available environmental features such as side streets or enforcement bays to work in.
      • Standard - use personal protective equipment and comply with the safety provisions for officers, particularly 'intercepting officers' and 'interviewing officers'.
    • The site controller must be aware of any changing conditions and act accordingly (E.g. weather, traffic volume). If necessary suspend or cancel the activity and report to the STI as soon as practicable.
    • The site controller must conduct a site briefing with all staff on site (including Qld Police) prior to the commencement and at the completion of the activity.
    • Any issues raised must be documented and reported to the STI.
    • The completed Site Safety Plan must be given to the STI for review then captured in DMS.
