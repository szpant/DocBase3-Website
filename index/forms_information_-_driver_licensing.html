Assisting Customer Completing F3000
Assisting Customer Completing F3000
    What it isAn applicant is expected to complete the Driver Licence Application (F3000) form by themself. However if an applicant requests help, the CSO may agree to do so (a refusal is acceptable and is not discrimination).

    Whether assistance is provided or not, the declaration at section 5 must be signed by the applicant.
    Advise CustomerBefore commencing assistance, advise the customer that the CSO cannot provide the answers to any of the questions, any suggestions or advice given by the CSO is simply being given as a guide to help them to decide their answer to the particular question.

    Once the questions are answered, re-read all answers provided on the form to the customer and ensure customer agrees. Advise the customer that you have made notes on the form indicating their agreement with the details recorded on the form.

    Before the customer signs the declaration, advise the customer that there are penalties for providing false and misleading information and that by signing the declaration they are confirming that the information provided on the form is true and correct.
    Notes required by CSOTo minimise the risk that the applicant in the event of prosecution denies providing the answers on the form, the CSO must make comprehensive notes in the 'Office Use Only" section including the:
    • reason why assistance was given (e.g poor understanding of English, temporary physical incapacity)
    • type of assistance given (e.g. completed question 7)
    • applicant indicated they understood the assistance and advice given, and that by signing the declaration they are stating the answers to the questions on the form are true and correct and there are consequences for providing false information.
    • form was signed by the applicant.
