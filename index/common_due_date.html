Common Due Date (3013)


What is itA common due date (CDD) allows the registered operator/s of multiple vehicle/trade plate to align the expiry dates of these vehicles.
Eligibility for Light VehiclesA CDD applicant must have 5 or more registered vehicle and/or dealer trade plates with the same CRN as the first registered operator.

An applicant may have more than one CDD provided there are five or more vehicle/trade plates attached to the specified date.

A common due date does not impact on the vehicles concessional and/or road usage entitlements.


Eligibility not met
Eligibility not met
    Each case is decided on its merit. If customer cannot meet the eligibility requirements but still wishes to have a CDD they can forward a written request outlining their requirements to the Manager of Common Due Date (Central Operational and Support (Common Due Dates))
Eligibility for Heavy VehiclesA CDD applicant must have 2 or more registered heavy vehicles and/or dealer trade plates with the same CRN as the first registered operator.

An applicant may have more than one CDD provided there are 2 or more heavy vehicles for each specified date.

A CDD does not impact on the vehicles concessional and/or road usage entitlements.

CDD's cannot be applied to vehicles/trade plates with Seasonal or Drought registration.

Due to system functionality, the TICA record of a registered operator with between 2 and 4 heavy vehicles (inclusive) will not always show a CDD after the first application but the registration periods will have aligned. Customers should be advised that to maintain the chosen date they must pay 12 month terms only.
Application Requirements
Where to lodge
Where to lodge
    CDD applications must be made in writing and can be lodged:
    • In person
    • By email, mail or fax to the CDD Processing Unit

    CDD application cannot be lodged via TMR Online Services.

    The application must be processed where it was lodged. The CDD Processing Unit is primarily a support function and can be contacted for enquiries only, applications should not be forwarded to this unit.
Application requirements
Application requirements
    All requests must contain
    All requests must contain
      • Registered operator details
      • A list of currently registered vehicles (minimum of 5 for light vehicles and 2 for heavy vehicles) by CRN and their respective due dates
      • Three different dates in different months (one preferred date and two alternative dates)
      • Signature of all registered operators or an authorised representative of the organisation. If being lodged by email, the signature requirement is met if the registered operator or authorised representative is identifiable from the email (position title, full name, DOB, CRN).
    Additional requirements for organisations
    Additional requirements for organisations
      If lodging:
      • in person/fax/mail, request must be on official organisation letterhead
      • by email, request must come from email address identifiable as the organisation. An authorised representative must include their position title, full name, DOB and CRN).
    Unavailable dates
    Unavailable dates
      The following dates cannot be used:
      • 28th and 29th of February
      • ALL dates in June and October
      • The first day of July
      • 31st of December

    TMR will choose the most convenient date for renewal. If dates submitted are not suitable to either parties further negotiations will be undertaken.

Applying a CDD
Applying a CDD
    A CDD must be added to both the CRN and relevant registration.
    The CDD will not be applied automatically and will be effective for the next renewal period. If that period is less than 12 months the surcharge will be applied.
Renewing a CDD
Renewing a CDD
    CDD renewals can be lodged:
    • In person at a TMR vehicle registration site
    • By mail to the Customer Service Operations - CDD
    • Online in batches of 24 renewals per payment

    The renewal must be processed where it was received and not forwarded to the CDD Processing Unit. The CDD Processing Unit is primarily a support function and can be contacted for enquiries only.
Calculation of Fees - First renewal
Calculation of Fees - First renewal
    Where the common due date is:
    • within two months of the current registration expiry date the renewal amount will be the pro-rata amount calculated from the expiry date to the CDD. The maximum pro-rata period will be 14 months of registration.
    • Greater than two months the renewal amount will be the pro-rata amount calculated from the expiry date to the CDD

    Beyond the first renewal the CDD renewal period is 12 months.
    The Quote Tool can be used to calculate CDD fees.
Removing a CDD
Removing a CDD
    A customer may request the removal of individual vehicles from the CDD:
    • In person at a TMR vehicle registration site
    • By email, mail or fax to the CDD Processing Unit

    TICA/TRAILS will automatically remove a CDD where there are less then five vehicles associated to the date for the relevant CRN.

    Removing a CDD from a vehicle will not change the expiry date. The customer will receive a standard registration renewal notice for each vehicle the next registration period.
Expiring a Common Due Date
Expiring a Common Due Date
    To expire a CDD a request must be made to the CDD Processing unit by email. The request must contain the CRN and the CDD to be removed.

How it works
Once set up on a CDD
Once set up on a CDD
    • Renewal fees will be raised per usual (6 weeks prior to expiry)
    • A Registration Renewal Notice schedule (F4103 or F4104 for inspectable vehicles) will be automatically posted to the customer with all of the vehicles and payment amounts.
    • A trails batch report with a copy of the schedules is emailed to the CDD team

    Note:If the customer doesn't receive their schedule, the CDD team can email them a copy.
Rules
Registration periods
Registration periods
    • Minimum period is 2 months and 1 day
    • Maximum period is 14 months

    Vehicles with a CDD cannot have:
    • Seasonal/Drought registration or
    • 1, 3 or 6 month pay terms
QFleet and Government Vehicles
QFleet and Government Vehicles
    All government registered vehicle (excluding QFleet) have the CDD of 1 October (01/10), but they may choose different dates.

    QFleet Registered Vehicles
    QFleet Registered Vehicles
      All government vehicles managed by QFleet may be:
      • Registered in the name of QFleet
      or
      • In the respective government/organisation name with QFleet recorded as the paying agent
      • Have a CDD of 01 July (01/07) unless alternative date suggested
      • Use the QFleet CRN of 60513159 only

    Queensland Fire and Rescue Service (QFRS)
    Queensland Fire and Rescue Service (QFRS)
      All operational vehicles and appliances registered in QFRS' name:
      • Use CRN 78098772 only
      • Have CDD of 01 February (01/02)
      • Use individual garaging addresses (eg station where vehicle garaged)

      All non operational Vehicles and appliances (i.e. trailers) registered in QFRS' name:
      • Use CRN 61757648 only
      • Have CDD of 01 September (01/09)
      • Use individual garaging addresses (eg station where vehicle garaged)

    Queensland Ambulance Service (QAS)
    Queensland Ambulance Service (QAS)
      Vehicles registered to QAS with the Registration category of AMBU must:
      • Use CRN 78098772
      • Have CDD of 01 July (01/07)
      • Have individual garaging address (eg station where ambulance garaged)
