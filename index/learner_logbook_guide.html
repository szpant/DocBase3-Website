Learner Logbook Guide (3185)


The learner logbook is designed for use by class C learner licence holders under 25, but may be used voluntarily by drivers 25 and older.

What it isA Queensland learner logbook is used by a learner licence holder to record their supervised on road driving experience in Australia.

The learner logbook is not used to measure the learner licence holder's competence for the practical driving test.
When a learner logbook is required
Learner logbook required
Learner logbook required
    A Queensland learner logbook is compulsory for all learner licence holders under 25.

    Notes: If the class C learner licence holder also holds a class RE or R provisional, probationary or open licence they are not required to complete the learner logbook requirement.

    The 60 hour learner logbook requirement no longer applies. Learner licence holders who obtained their licence during the transitional period but who have now renewed their learner licence must meet the 100 hour requirement (the 60 hour transitional arrangements do not apply after licence renewal).

Supervised on road driving experience requiredThe learner licence holder must accumulate at least 100 hours of supervised on road driving experience, including at least 10 hours of night driving.

Note:Night driving is defined as driving between sunset on a day and sunrise on the next day.

The supervised on road driving experience may be met by completing 100 hours of supervised driving and/or applying for recognition of prior driving experience.

Evidence of meeting this requirement is provided through one or a combination of the followig methods:
  • The Queensland Learner Logbook app;
  • A Queensland Learner Logbook;
  • A learner logbook from another recognised third party provider;
  • Demonstration of prior driving experience (through a prior driving experience application; refer below).

Prior Driving Experience Application
Prior Driving Experience Application
When a learner logbook is not requiredA learner licence holder 25 or older is NOT required to accumulate 100 hours of supervised on road driving experience.

Drivers 25 or older may use a learner logbook voluntarily, but it is not a requirement to obtain a Queensland provisional licence.

Foreign licence holders whose authority to drive has been withdrawn because they failed a practical driving test, who have subsequently been issued a Queensland learner licence, are not required to complete a learner logbook.

Learning to drive a class of vehicle other than a class C.

A returning driver is not required to complete a learner logbook Refer: Driver Licence > 1. Qld driver licence > Returning Drivers
Exemptions from learner logbook requirements
Medical Condition allows daylight driving only
Medical Condition allows daylight driving only
    What it isLearner licence holders who have a medical condition restricting them to driving in daylight hours only are not required to complete 10 hours of night driving.
    Application requirementsThe learner licence holder must provide a medical certificate that shows they are restricted to driving during daylight hours.
    Requirements if exemption grantedAll 100 hours must be completed in daylight hours.
Exemptions from Logbook Requirements
Options to record supervised driving hoursA customer may use one or a combination of the following tools to record their supervised driving hours:
  • the Queensland Learner Logbook app;
  • A Queensland Learner Logbook;
  • A learner logbook app from a recognised third party provider.

When a learner licence is issued, customers should be given the Learner Logbook app Brochure.

Customers are encouraged to use the free Learner Logbook app, however they may choose to use a hard copy of the Queensland Learner Logbook which they must be provided

Learner Logbook App
Learner Logbook App
    Benefits of using the app:
    • Logbook can be submitted electronically for approval
    • The instant submission means that TMR will start checking the logbook sooner
    • Supervisors do not need to register for the app, they approve using email
    • logbook data is stored securely by TMR and can not be lost.

    Information for customers about how to use the App is available on the TMR Website, via the links below:

    Step by step guide for Android devices/phones (Samsung etc) -
    Learner logbook app techhelp-android

    Additionally, a Tutorial for set up and a Help section is available within the App itself.
Queensland Learner Logbook
Queensland Learner Logbook

    If the customer requests a hard copy logbook, they should be given the Queensland Learner Logbook, a Learner Logbook app brochure and the Road Trip for supervisor brochure. Customers should be advised that they can change to the app at any time.

    A learner logbook is issued free of charge to customers upon request when:
    • issued with a Queensland learner licence
    • renewing a Queensland learner licence
    • changing how they choose to record their supervised driving hours
    • their logbook has been refused and a new one is required

    Alternatively, RACQ Learn2go provides an online learner logbook which is also free of charge. However, this logbook is required to be printed and submitted to TMR.
Third Party Apps
Third Party Apps
    Customers may choose to use a learner logbook app from another third party provider that is recognised by TMR.

    Recognised third party provider apps are:
    • RACQ Learner Driver app
    • RACQ Learn2go app
    • You Live Only Once (YLOO)

    Any complaints, questions or issues from customers regarding a third party learner logbook apps are to be directed to the third party provider. TMR is not responsible for the maintenance or operation of a third party learner logbook app. It is the duty of the third party provider to ensure personal user information is not misused, lost or stolen.
Recognised third party learner logbook app TMR will only accept learner logbook apps from approved third party providers.

Approved third party learner logbook apps is listed on the TMR website:

Testing will also be required to ensure the app integrates with TMR's electronic interface.
Electronic Learner Logbook Third Party App Checklist for Providers' FormA standard email package is set up for third party app providers who enquire about having their electronic learner logbook app recognised by TMR. This package includes the ‘Electronic Learner Logbook App Checklist For Providers’ form. All third party electronic learner logbook apps must meet the requirements outlined in this form.

Replacing a TMR learner logbookA hard copy logbook should be provided free of charge to customers who have not previously received one.

A fee is applicable for customers who require a replacement hard copy logbook due to their original logbook being lost, stolen or damaged.

TICA will not reflect whether a customer has received a hard copy logbook.

Learner logbooks may be purchased from:
  • TMR Customer Service Centres or TMR led QGAPs
  • TMR website

Note:Even if the operator is unsure of the validity of a request for a free replacement, the operator is to provide the logbook free of charge.
Details required to be recorded in the logbook
Learner licence holder
Learner licence holder
    The learner licence holder must:
    • Record in the appropriate area of the logbook all on road driving experience including:
      • Date
      • Supervisor's details
      • Vehicle registration number
      • Start and finish times
      • Journey details
      • Duration of session
      • Vehicle odometer reading
      • Total columns

      Driving experience from foreign countries cannot be recorded in the logbook. Only experience gained on Australian roads can be recorded in the learner logbook.

      Assess Logbook and/or Prior Driving Experience Application
Supervisor
Supervisor
    Definition of a supervisor:
    A supervisor is defined as a person who:
    • Directs the driving of a vehicle by another person
    and
    • Holds a current O type licence for a class C vehicle and has held that licence for at least 1 year
    and
    • If the vehicle has passenger seating capacity - sits next to the learner driver while directing the driving
Completing and lodging the learner logbook appWhen the customer has reached their target hours in the the logbook app, the customer can submit their logbook directly to TMR. This must be done at least 12 business days before their practical driving test. The option to submit the logbook is not available until the target hours are complete.

A customer may amend the target hours in the app for one of the following reasons:

1.the customer has partially completed a hard copy logbook; and/or
2.the customer has submitted (and been approved) for recognition of prior driving experience; or
3.the customer has been notified by the Logbook processing unit to complete additional driving hours

The submission is emailed directly to the Logbook processing unit within Central Operations and Support. The customer is not required to print and mail the logbook information from the app.

Where the customer is submitting a partial hard copy logbook and partial electronic logbook, processing of the logbook will not begin until both the hard copy and electronic logbook have been received by the Logbook processing unit.

Supervisor
Supervisor
    Once the learner has entered a trip in the app, a trip validation email will be sent to the supervisor. The supervisor will choose to approve or reject the trip. If the trip is not approved the hours will not be counted.

    If the Supervisor is a Driver Trainer the learner will have to indicate this when they add them into the app.
Issues
Issues
    A troubleshooting guide for the learner logbook app has been developed to support staff in assisting customers with technical difficulties:

    Learner Logbook App Troubleshooting Guide

    For any issues relating to the use of the app and its functionality that can't be resolved using the guide, the customer should be directed to Online Services Support.
Completing the hard copy learner logbookEnsure all learner logbook entries are:
  • clearly printed using ink (not pencil)
  • complete and signed by the supervisor
  • entered in only one column of the logbook
Amendment of logbook entries
Amendment of logbook entries
    If individual details of entries need amending (eg time/registration number), it is recommended that the whole entry/line is ruled through and full details re-written on the next available line and signed by the supervisor. Crossing out and/or liquid paper should not be used on any individual trip details fields as this may lead to concerns about the accuracy of the entry.

    However, if totals at the bottom of page are identified as being incorrect, it is acceptable for the learner licence holder to cross out and/or use liquid paper to amend the error.
Supervisor requirements
Supervisor requirements
    A supervisor must provide their licence details and sign each relevant entry in the logbook.
Lodging a hard copy learner logbook and/or prior driving experience application with TMRBefore a learner logbook and/or prior driving experience application can be lodged with TMR for checking, the learner licence holder must have accumulated at least 100 hours of supervised on road driving experience, recorded in their learner logbook and/or prior driving experience application.

The learner logbook and/or prior driving experience application must be approved by TMR before a Q-SAFE practical driving test may be undertaken.

The result is recorded on TICA
    One of the following prerequisite codes is passed on TICA:
    All Supervised
    		100 hrs of 1 to 1 supervised driving experience including 10 hrs of night driving recorded in a Queensland learner logbook
    School + Supervised
    		100 hrs of combination of driving school and supervised driving including 10 hrs of night driving recorded in a Queensland learner logbook
    Prior AUS/NZ
    		100 hrs of prior driving experience declaration for Australia and NZ learner licence holders, including 10 hrs of night driving
    Prior Overseas
    		100 hrs of combination of prior driving experience and supervised driving to an equivalent where 50 hrs was gained on a (Recognised Country) foreign learner licence, including 10 hrs of night driving.

Validity An approved learner logbook/prior driving experience application is valid indefinitely.

Expired logbooks/applications
Expired logbooks/applications
    If a learner logbook/prior driving experience application is showing as expired in TICA, a new driving experience application can be processed without the customer providing a physical application. Once the new application is approved, the expiry date will be updated.
Lost or damaged learner logbook appIf the customer’s device is lost, stolen or damaged, the customer can still access their electronic Learner Logbook. The data from the logbook is stored securely and can be retrieved using another device by installing and logging into the QLD Learner Logbook app on the new device.

Customer inquiries relating to a learner logbook app from a third party provider listed below must be directed to, and handled by, the third party provider.
  • RACQ Learn2go app
  • AAMI SmartPlates app
  • You Live Only Once (YLOO)
Lost or damaged hard copy learner logbooks
If logbook is lost or damaged
If logbook is lost or damaged
    If a learner logbook is lost, stolen or damaged, the learner licence holder should:
    • Re-record the entries in a newly purchased learner logbook.
    • Have the relevant supervisor (including driver trainers*) approve the entries in the replacement learner logbook by signing in the paper logbook.
    • Submit the learner logbook with a covering letter explaining that entries have been copied into a new learner logbook.

    Driver trainer unable to sign
    Driver trainer unable to sign
      If the learner logbook contained driver trainer entries and the driver trainer is unable to sign, a letter from the driving school to verify the learner licence holder's driving experience is acceptable ONLY in the following circumstances:
        1. The driver trainer has left the driving school and cannot be contacted to sign the entries in their new learner logbook; or
        2. The learner licence holder has left the area and it would be unreasonable for them to travel to obtain the driver trainer's verification of the entries in their new learner logbook.

    An electronic logbook app (TMR or RACQ) can only be used as a replacement for a lost, stolen or damaged learner logbook if the learner has all required fields for the app (this includes odometer readings). The app will not allow a record to be submitted without all details completed.
If lost logbook handed in to TMR
If lost logbook handed in to TMR
    If a learner logbook is returned to a TMR CSC:
    • Complete the following letter:
      Learner logbook returned to TMR letter
    • Send the letter and the learner logbook by registered mail to the customer's last known address on TICA
    • Record a Text Restriction on TICA with the following information:
      Learner logbook has been handed in at TMR Office [location] and posted back to the customer on [date - XX/XX/XXXX] by registered mail. Please expire this text when customer next does business with TMR.
Learner logbook and/or Prior Driving Experience Application Enquiries
For logbooks/applications being assessed by the LLBCPU
For logbooks/applications being assessed by the LLBCPU
    If the customer enquires at a CSC or TMR led QGAP about the status of their learner logbook and/or prior driving experience application assessment by the Learner Logbook CPU (LLBCPU), check TICA to see if text has been recorded.

    If text has not been recorded in TICA, view the Learner Logbook Lodgement and Processing Data Portal to ascertain the latest service delivery timeframes for the LLBCPU. All applications are subject to a timeframe of 12 business days, however the information on the portal will assist enquiries made by customers who have booked a Practical Driving Test.
Penalties
Ineligibility to take test (logbook/application false/misleading)
Ineligibility to take test (logbook/application false/misleading)
    If the Chief Executive deems:
    • A completed learner logbook
    and/or
    • Other documentation provided to show evidence of driving experience to be false or misleading in any part, the person is not eligible to take a Q-SAFE practical driving test for a period of 6 weeks from the date the resubmitted learner logbook and/or prior driving experience application is approved.
