Non Qld Licence Holder - Medical Incapacity (1177)


What it isThe Chief Executive may immediately withdraw a non-Queensland licence holder’s authority to drive in Queensland if:
  • the non-Queensland licence holder provides information, or a health professional provides information about the non-Queensland licence holder;
    and
  • the information indicates the licence holder has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely
    and
  • chief executive reasonably considers that public safety has been endangered or is likely to be endangered because the licence holder’s ability to drive safely is likely to be adversely affected;
    or
  • it is otherwise necessary in the public interest.
Notifying TMR and actions to be taken
Notification methodDetails
In personAny TMR Customer Service Centre (CSC) or licence issuing centre
Mail / Email / Fax / PhoneMedical Conditions Reporting Unit (MCR)

The Chief Executive will take action to withdraw a non-Queensland licence holder’s authority to drive in Queensland if information is received from a health professional or the Queensland Police Service (QPS), or interstate authorities, which indicates the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely.

Notification by Health Professional
Notification by Health Professional
    Health professionals may notify TMR about a non-Queensland licence holder’s medical condition via:
    • Online
    • Email to the MCR Unit
    • Advice on official letterhead, or with an office stamp
    • Medical Condition Notification (F4842)
    • Medical Condition Notification form from the Assessing Fitness to Drive publication.
    Actions required
    Actions required
      • The chief executive needs to be satisfied from the information supplied that the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, and public safety has been endangered or is likely to be endangered, or it is necessary in the public interest.
      • If the chief executive is satisfied, a Withdrawal of Authority Medical Grounds notice should be sent to the person. Their authority to drive in Queensland will be withdrawn from the date stated in the notice.
    A health professional means the following:
    A health professional means the following:
      A health professional means a doctor registered under the Health Practitioner Regulation National Law to practise in the medical profession, other than as a student.
Notification by Queensland Police Service (QPS) or interstate authorities
Notification by Queensland Police Service (QPS) or interstate authorities
    The QPS or interstate authorities through their enforcement of transport legislation and/or attendance at police incidents may notify TMR about a non-Queensland licence holder’s medical condition via:
    • police incident report;
      or
    • advice on official letterhead
      or
    • email from a QPS email account, another Australian police jurisdiction or an Australian federal police email account.
    Actions required
    Actions required
      • The chief executive needs to be satisfied from the information supplied that the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, and public safety has been endangered or is likely to be endangered, or it is necessary in the public interest.
      • If the chief executive is satisfied, a Withdrawal of Authority Medical Grounds notice should be sent to the person. Their authority to drive in Queensland will be withdrawn from the date stated in the notice.
Notification by Third Party
Notification by Third Party
    A relative, neighbour, friend of the licence holder or a member of the public
    may notify TMR about a non-Queensland licence holder’s medical fitness to drive. Action must not be taken unless the person has provided information and based on the weight of that information, it is reasonable to withdraw the person’s authority to drive in Queensland on their non-Queensland driver licence. Consideration should also be given to whether the person has a current medical certificate and if so, when the medical certificate was issued and when it expires?
    Examples
    Examples
      An example of when it is reasonable for TMR to withdraw the person’s authority to drive on their non-Queensland driver licence:

      TMR has received written advice from a driver licence holder’s family member advising that the non-Queensland driver licence holder is driving in Queensland and has a permanent, or long-term medical condition and as a result they have been admitted to hospital on a number of occasions over the last few months. The person has also recently been involved in a crash where their medical condition was a contributing factor.

      An example of when it is unreasonable for TMR to withdraw the person’s authority to drive on their non-Queensland driver licence:

      TMR has received written advice from a driver licence holder’s family member stating that in their opinion the person is not medically fit to drive. But the family member has not provided any evidence to support their claims that the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition. It is identified that the person has a current medical certificate, which has only just been issued and has a validity of 12 months.


    Third party notifications and the information provided must be considered on a case by case basis. If there is concern about whether it is reasonable to commence show cause action, please contact the Medical Conditions Reporting Unit (MCR) or Help Direct for advice.

    A Third Party Acknowledgement letter is sent to the person who notified TMR about the non-Queensland licence holder.
