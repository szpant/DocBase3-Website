Accessory Slimline Plates (3091)

What are they? Accessory Plates are an additional plate that is:
  • The size of a smaller trailer plate
  • Only available in maroon and white
  • Used to display the plate number of either:
    • A vehicle if the existing number plate is obscured by an accessory (for example bicycles or wheelchairs)
    • A tow truck that is towing an unregistered vehicle
    • A heavy vehicle that is floating a forklift which has obstructed the existing number plate
Note:For specific information about attachment requirements, refer to the 'Rules" section below.

Slimline plates:
  • Are narrow plates that can replace the normal sized number plate on a vehicle
  • Display the registration number in the same colours (no slogans)
RequirementsSlimline:
  • Plate number must be either customised or personalised
  • One or both standard plates surrendered
RulesAttaching the Plate
Accessory Plates
Accessory Plates
    Accessory plates can be attached:
    • To an accessory(eg bike rack) fitted to the rear of a vehicle. The plate must be attached in a way that allows the accessory plate to be legible from a distance of at least 20 metres from the rear of the accessory.
    • To an accessory (eg bike rack) fitted to the front of a motor vehicle. The plate must be attached in a way that allows the accessory plate to be legible from a distance of at least 20 metres from the front of the accessory.

      Refer to Minor Modifications brochure for more about the requirements for attaching vehicle accessories and equipment.

    • On the rear of an unregistered vehicle being towed by a tow truck. The accessory plate must be attached to the end of the vehicle being towed in a position as close as possible to where the standard (or personalised, customised, etc) plate would be displayed/attached if it was a registered vehicle.
    • On the rear of a conditionally registered forklift that is being floated on a heavy vehicle and is obscuring the existing number plate of the heavy vehicle. This may involve covering the forklift's conditional number plate to ensure only the heavy vehicle accessory plate is visible from the rear of the vehicle.

      Note:If a vehicle is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS), an accessory plate cannot be attached to a vehicle during the confiscation period. For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at
      http://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound.htm
