Conducting a PRI/HVRAS Inspection (62)

Applies to
Applies to
    Conducting a pre-registration inspection (PRI), and can include a Heavy Vehicle Registration Assessment Scheme (HVRAS) inspection or Identity Check, on a vehicle or trailer to either:
    • Process vehicle/trailer registration application where a PRI is required
    • Issue a Low Volume VIN (LVV)
Before you Start
Before you Start
    Check that the customer has met the following pre-transaction requirements:
    • Correct Inspection type (AIS, COI, HVRAS, Self-Assessment)
    • EOI
    • Forms completed and signed
    Note: If the customer has not met the pre-transaction requirements, the PRI can be completed and held at the CSC for no more than one week. The completed assessment report can be used for registration purposes once the customer has met all pre-transaction requirements.
1
Check Documents & EOI
Check Documents & EOI
    To conduct a PRI collect one of the following (if available):
    • Safety Certificate (SC)
    • Certificate Of Inspection (COI)
    • Dealer Completed Vehicle Details form (F3529)
    • Self Assessment of Single Axle Trailer form (F3970)

    and then it is recommended the following forms be taken to the inspection as they may be required:
    • Blank Vehicle Details form (F3529) - required if major errors found on inspection document
    • Blank Vehicle Inspection Sheet (if applicable)
2
Determine type of inspection required
Determine type of inspection required
    The type of inspection is determined by the transaction and vehicle type.

    Refer: Pre-Registration Inspection (PRI)
    Identity Checks
    Identity Checks
      PRI identity checks are to sight VIN numbers and VIN plates and do not require measurements.
    Vehicles
    Vehicles
      PRI vehicles must have a full check for all identifiers and vehicle details. HVRAS vehicles must have all measurements, TARE and GVM details checked.
    Trailers
    Trailers
      PRI trailers must have a full check for all identifiers and trailer details (e.g. dimensions, axles etc).
      Issue of Low Volume VINS
      Issue of Low Volume VINS
        Ascertain whether the trailer is new or used:
        New Trailers
        New Trailers
          • No visual inspection necessary
        Trailers previously registered
        Trailers previously registered
          • Visual inspection required to ensure that there are no existing VIN or chassis numbers on the trailer. If a VIN or Chassis number exists, that number must be used to register the trailer.
3
Prepare for PRI
Prepare for PRI
    Operator is to:
    • Ensure customer has moved vehicle to the appropriate inspection location
    • Use appropriate PPE
    • Ensure they have flashlight, inspection document and writing equipment
4
Refer customer to inspection location (if required)
Refer customer to inspection location (if required)
    Not all TMR locations are able to conduct PRI inspections.
    If the customer is required to travel to a different location, the operator should:
    • Contact the location the customer wishes to attend
      PRI Locations
    • Arrange a time suitable to both parties
    • Ensure that if:
    Registering a vehicle, the customer has:
    Registering a vehicle, the customer has:
      • Completed Vehicle Registration application (F3518)
      • Pre-purchased CTP
    Applying for a Low Volume VIN (LVV)
    Applying for a Low Volume VIN (LVV)
      • Completed Vehicle Registration application (F3518)
      • Completed Low Volume VIN application (F3648 or F5005)
      • Completed Self-Assessment form (F3970)
        Refer: Vehicle Registration > 5. Vehicle Requirements > Inspections > PRI > Where are PRI's conducted
5
Conduct PRI
Conduct PRI
    Light Vehicle PRI
    Light Vehicle PRI
      Conduct PRI by either:
      Using the AIS document
      Using the AIS document
        Inspect the vehicle and ensure the following information matches the AIS document:
        • VIN/Chassis number
        • Engine number
        • Compliance Plate (applicable for non-RAV vehicles)
        • Import Plates (applicable for non-RAV vehicles)
        • Make/Manufacturer
        • Model
        • Year of Manufacture or Compliance (applicable for non-RAV vehicles)
        or
        Completing all fields on the Vehicle Details form F3529
    Trailer PRI
    Trailer PRI
      Conduct Visual Inspection for Trailers Identifiers
      Conduct Visual Inspection for Trailers Identifiers
        Trailer should be inspected to determine if any identifiers exist. Operators should:
        • Inspect the box of the trailer for an attached identification plate
        • Inspect the chassis/drawbar for a stamped VIN/Chassis number
          If identifiers exist, ensure that the VIN or chassis number is stamped on the trailer plate and the chassis/drawbar. If not, advise customer of the requirements and do not proceed with registration until identifiers have been stamped

        If no identifiers exist, record on the LVV Application form that a Visual Inspection was conducted and no identifiers were located.
      Conduct Trailer PRI
      Conduct Trailer PRI
        Note:If the trailer to be inspected is <750kg ATM, a PRI should only be conducted when the customer is unable, or had chosen not to, complete a Self Assessment. All caravans are to be inspected including <750kg ATM.

        • Identify trailer type and select correct inspection form
        • Measure trailer as indicated on the form
        • Review inspection form to ensure it complies with all requirements

          Special attention must be paid to:
        • The dimensions of the trailer to avoid registering non-compliant trailers. Eg trailers with doors on the right hand side are not compliant
        • Accessory requirements such as fire extinguishers in caravans/campervans
      Heavy Vehicle Inspection
      Heavy Vehicle Inspection
        • Identify vehicle type and select correct inspection sheet
        • Vehicle details sheet (F3529) to be completed as well as HVRAS inspection sheet
        • Measure vehicle as indicated on inspection sheet
        • Record tyre details to calculate safe tyre limits after inspection
          Tyre Weights Calculator

        Special attention must be paid to:
        • The dimensions of the trailer to avoid registering non-compliant trailers. Eg trailers with doors on the right hand side are not compliant
        • Whether the trailer is a lead or middle trailer as this will impact the registration fees paid. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 2. Registerable vehicles > Heavy Vehicles > Sample Graphics > Lead/Middle Trailer Diagramsfor guidelines
        • Accessory requirements such as fire extinguishers in caravans/campervans
    6
    Action any identified issues
    Action any identified issues
      Identifiers are illegible/dirty
      Identifiers are illegible/dirty
        If the customer is:
        • Able to clean the affected area, continue with the PRI
        • Unable to clean the affected area and it is the:
          • Engine number that is not visible, note on the relevant inspection form "Visual sighting of engine number not possible
          • VIN, refer customer back to the relevant AIS as the VIN must be sighted on the body, not taken from the compliance plate
      Descrepancies between RAV/COI/SC and vehicle (RAV entry vehicle)
      Descrepancies between RAV/COI/SC and vehicle (RAV entry vehicle)
        Contact VIU for advice.
      Discrepancies between COI/SC and vehicle (non-RAV entry vehicle)
      Discrepancies between COI/SC and vehicle (non-RAV entry vehicle)
        • If the error is minor (eg one wrong character on VIN), update the vehicle details to match the inspection certificate on TICA.
        • For all other errors (incorrect or incomplete COI/SC):
          • Customer must return to AIS for COI/SC to be re-issued
          • Transaction is refused
      Vehicle Identifiers Missing/Damaged
      Vehicle Identifiers Missing/Damaged
        Vehicle
        Vehicle
          • If the customer can provide evidence relating to the identifier (eg Repairs), the CSC can request a surrogate identifier.
          • If there is no evidence the customer must be referred to QPS (Queensland Police Service) for the issue of a surrogate identifier.
        Trailer
        Trailer
          • If the trailer is >4.5 tonne GVM and the customer cannot provide proof of previous registration, advise the customer to contact an engineer (List of engineers is available at http://rvcs-prodweb.dot.gov.au/). The engineer will certify that the trailer complies with ADR's and will attach a compliance plate and issue a VIN.
          • If the trailer is <4.5 tonne GVM and the customer can provide proof of previous registration, the trailer needs to be referred to QPS for a surrogate.
          • Check if the caravan/campervan has an approved fire-extinguisher fitted

        Refer: Vehicle Rego > 5. Vehicle Requirements > The How to: Vehicle Requirements > Issue a Surrogate Vehicle Identifier to request a surrogate vehicle identifier.

    Give Customer
    Give Customer
      • EOI documents
      • The Customer copy of the SC
