Limousine and Special Purpose Limousine (SPL) Registration (6017)


Limousine Registration
Limousine Registration
    What is it?A licensed limousine provides a booked hire service using a vehicle that is stated in the Limousine licence which meets the regulated vehicle requirements. The vehicle must be either at least 30 years old from the date of manufacture or has a wheelbase of at least 2800mm.
    What is a Limousine Licence (LL)Limousine licences are issued by the chief executive under which the holder may provide booked hire services to the public using the motor vehicle stated in the licence.

    No new Limousine licences will be issued however existing original Limousine licences may be leased, sold or otherwise transferred subject to approval.

    CTP
    • Class 26 is required (cars and station wagons)
    • Class 10A or 11 (for buses)

    If the vehicle is currently registered and the customer is wishing to change their Purpose of Use to a Limousine (LIMO), a CTP upgrade letter from the existing CTP Insurer (class 26 for cars and station wagons), class 10A or 11 for buses) is required.


    Note: Limousines (cars and station wagons) will be automatically converted from Class 4 CTP insurance to Class 26 CTP insurance. Starting in stages during the week commencing 1 October 2017. This conversion is being done manually by the Registration Policy Unit during this first week.

    A Class 4 to Class 26 CTP update may also be performed upon request by the customer if their vehicle has not been converted at the time through the above process. No application is necessary from the registered operator for this change.

    The registered operator will be sent a new registration certificate showing the new CTP insurance class which the registered operator can take to their CTP insurer for any entitlement to a refund.
    Limousine PlatesA unique limousine plate is issued to the vehicle. The plate is in the format LNNNNN. No other plate type is allowed to be fitted to the vehicle.

    Limousine plates are eligible to be customised.

    The allocation of specific regional area limousine plates is no longer applicable due to the removal of service areas.

    Personalised plates cannot be attached to a limousine.
    COIThe customer must have obtained a certificate of inspection or exemption before presenting the vehicle for registration

    Note:Updating the purpose of use for a vehicle from Commercial COML or PRIV to LIMO, LSPL or TAXI is exempt a certificate of inspection in some cases. For more information Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Inspections > Cert of Insp > Exemptions > TICA Error.

Special Purpose Limousine (SPL) Registration
Special Purpose Limousine (SPL) Registration
    What is it?
    What is a Special Purpose Limousine (SPL) Registration
    What is a Special Purpose Limousine (SPL) Registration
      A Special Purpose limousine provides a booked hire service using a vehicle that is stated in the Special Purpose Limousine licence which meets the regulated vehicle requirements.

      The vehicle must be either at least 30 years old from the date of manufacture or has a wheelbase of at least 2800mm.

      The journey must be pre-booked and the fare for the journey agreed to, before the journey begins. SPLs are restricted to only providing a service to a wedding event, student event or a tourist service.
    What is a Special Purpose Limousine Licence (SPLL)Special Purpose Limousine licences are issued by the chief executive under which the holder may provide booked hire services to the public using the motor vehicle stated in the licence.

    No new Special Purpose Limousine licences will be issued and existing licences may not be leased, sold or otherwise transferred.

    CTP

    If the vehicle is currently registered and the customer is wishing to change their Purpose of Use to an Special Purpose Limousine (LSPL), a CTP upgrade letter from the exiting CTP Insurer (Class 26 for light vehicles, Class 10a or 11 for buses) is required.

    The registered operator will be sent a new registration certificate showing the new CTP insurance class which the registered operator can take to their CTP insurer for any entitlement to a refund.
    SPL PlatesA unique SPL plate is issued to the vehicle. The plate is in the format SL.NN.AA. No other plate type is allowed to be fitted to the vehicle.

    An SPL plate cannot be customised or moved to another vehicle, nor can a personalised plate be attached to a SPL vehicle

    For storage locations Refer: Vehicle Rego > 11. Plates > Standard Plates > Rules >Special Plate Types -Storage Locations
