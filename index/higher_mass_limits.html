Higher Mass Limits (5225)

Statement
This policy provides clear instruction for Authorised Officers identifying and enforcing non-compliance with eligibility criteria under the Guideline for Higher Mass Limits for Vehicles with Road Friendly Suspensions in Queensland – Form 10
Definitions
For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply:
Term
Definition
Eligible Vehicle The following classes of vehicles or combinations of vehicles:
  • Tandem drive rigid trucks and prime movers;
  • Tandem and triaxle semi-trailers;
  • Tandem and triaxle dolly trailers;
  • Road trains;
  • B-doubles.
Non-Eligible VehiclesThe following vehicles, or combinations of vehicles, are not eligible to operate at higher mass limits:
  • Truck and dog trailer combinations;
  • Truck and pig trailer combinations;
  • Pig trailers (except converter dollies used in multi-combinations);
  • Single drive axle trucks;
  • Single axle trailers;
  • Vehicles which operate under permit for excess dimension or mass;
  • Vehicles operating under mass concession schemes, or guidelines that allow above regulation mass limits.
Form 10 A current version of the Guideline for Higher Mass Limits for Vehicles with Road Friendly Suspensions in Queensland - Form 10
Road Friendly SuspensionAn axle group that is operating at higher mass limits must be fitted with a road friendly suspension that complies with the following:
  • For vehicles built before 1 January 2000, all suspension systems that use air bags in combination with effective hydraulic dampers, with the air bags being the principal suspension medium, are considered to be road friendly;
    or
  • For vehicles built on or after 1 January 2000, only vehicles fitted with road friendly suspension certified by the Commonwealth Department responsible for approving such suspension types can receive an increase in mass. Verification of the certification, in the form of a certification label, must be fitted on or near the vehicle’s suspension, or a copy of a letter from the vehicle or suspension manufacturer stating that the suspension is certified as road friendly must be carried in the vehicle and produced to an authorised officer when requested.
Eligibility
Higher Mass Limits (HML) allows particular heavy vehicles to access additional mass entitlements providing:
  • Operators of vehicles or combinations running HML on tri-axle groups are accredited under the Mass Management Module of the National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS), with an accreditation label fitted to the hauling unit.
  • Participation in the Intelligent Access Program (IAP).
  • Vehicles are fitted with certified road friendly suspension.
  • Vehicles are on an authorised HML route.
Road Friendly Suspension
To be eligible for HML, vehicles must be fitted with certified road friendly suspensions. For a suspension system to be considered as road friendly, it must be certified to the requirements stipulated in the Vehicle Standards Bulletin 11 - Certification of Road Friendly Suspensions.

http://www.infrastructure.gov.au/roads/vehicle_regulation/bulletin/vsb_11.aspx
Intelligent Access Point (IAP)
In Queensland operators running HML must participate in IAP.
Refer: Transport Compliance > 01. Procedures > On Road > Intelligence Access Program
Authorised Routes
When operating at higher mass limits, operators must ensure that the route is authorised for their particular vehicle. Higher mass limit vehicles may operate in Queensland on the following routes.
  • Routes approved for higher mass limits as outline in the higher mass limits maps.
  • Connecting 23 metre and 25 metre approved B-double and road train routes identified in the multi-combination routes in Queensland to a radial distance of 500 metres from Queensland Major Highway System.
Non-Compliant Vehicles
HML ceases to apply to an eligible vehicle found to be non-complaint with any of the following HML guideline criteria:
    Section 3 – Application
    Section 4 – Operating Conditions
    Section 5 – Mass Management Accreditation
    Section 6 – Standards
    Section 7 – Intelligent Access Program (excluding section 7.3 - Self Declaration)

Eligible vehicles that are non-compliant with the required criteria are to be assessed at regulation mass on all axles, axle groups and Gross Vehicle Mass/Gross Combination Mass (GVM/GCM).

Non-compliance with a regulation mass limit will result in enforcement action by way of Infringement Notice or Brief to Adjudicator.
Mass Limits
Eligible Axles and Axle Group Mass Limits
Eligible Axles and Axle Group Mass Limits
    The eligible axle groups and total mass permitted are:
    • Tandem axle group fitted with 8 tyres - 17.0 tonnes
    • Tri-axle group fitted with 12 tyres - 22.5 tonnes
    • Six-tyred tandem axles - 14 tonnes

    All axle groups on a vehicle or within a vehicle combination do not need to be road friendly. In this instance, the increased axle group masses only apply to the individual road friendly axle groups.
Prime Mover Steer Axle Mass Limit
Prime Mover Steer Axle Mass Limit
    Steer axles on prime movers operating in road train and B-triple combinations, and fitted with single tyres with a nominal section width of at leas 375 mm are permitted to operate up to 6.7 tonnes.
Gross Mass Limits
Gross Mass Limits
    The mass of a vehicle or vehicle combination must not exceed any of the following:
    • The sum of eligible higher mass limits axle group loads;
      or
    • The sum of eligible and non-eligible higher mass limits axle group loads;
      or
    • Vehicle manufacturer's ratings and component limits.
Procedure - On-Road Compliance Checks
1.Check that the vehicle or combination of vehicles are within the eligible class.
2.Check that the vehicle or combination of vehicles complies with the Road Friendly Suspension requirements.
3.Check that the operator is participating in IAP and is meeting the requirements of IAP.
4.Check that operators of vehicles or combination of vehicles fitted with any tri-axle group are accredited under the Mass Management module of the NHVAS.
5.Check that the vehicle or combination of vehicles is operating on an approved HML route.
6.Weigh vehicles or combination of vehicles in accordance with exiting compliance manual instructions.
7.Check that the vehicle or combination of vehicles is fitted with eligible axles and or axle groups and complies with the axle group mass limits.
8.Check that the mass of a vehicle or combination of vehicles does not exceed Gross Mass Limits.
