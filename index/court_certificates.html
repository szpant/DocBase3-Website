What it is
TMR Register of Information
TMR Register of Information
    TMR is governed by legislation to maintain a register of motor vehicles, driver licences, marine licences, industry authorities and Photo Identification cards. Information recorded in TICA registers, includes:
    • Customer information (incl. name, date of birth, sex, height)
    • Residential and postal address details and history
    • Digital photo
    • Digitised signature
    • Licence details
    • Traffic history
    • Photo Identification card details and history
    • Industry Authority details and history
    • Registered operator of vehicles
    • Vehicle number plate details
    • Payment receipt details
Information required for Court
Information required for Court
    The information recorded in TMR registers may be requested by third parties in any legal proceedings, not limited to enforcement proceedings.
Court Certificate to Provide Information
Court Certificate to Provide Information
    As it is not viable for TMR to release an employee to attend every court hearing to provide this information, a "facilitation of proof" provision exists in most legislation which allows for certain information to be produced and recognised by a court, through the issue of a certificate by a delegated person.

    Some cases may still require a delegated officer to physically attend court and produce and explain documents and information held by the department.

    Refer: Offence Management > 1. Court Cases > Appearing in Court
Evidentiary Aids to Accompany Court Certificates
Evidentiary Aids to Accompany Court Certificates
    On the 29 November 2019, the Transport Operations (Road Management) Act 1995 was amended to remove the requirement to provide evidentiary aids to accompany Court Certificates except when specifically required.

    The following evidentiary aids are no longer required to be attached to a Court Certificate unless specifically directed by the Court:
      • TO (Road Use Management) Act 1995 Instrument of Delegation & Schedule
      • Affidavit
      • Copy of gazetted appointment/confirmation letter of delegated position

    Note:Section 123A(n) of the Act confirms that for ‘a person who is a delegate of the chief executive to sign a certificate or document’ their proof of appointment is unnecessary for certificates produced in relation to an offence against a Transport Act (for example, licencing, traffic infringements and so on). Transport Act is defined as TORUM or another Act, or a provision of another Act, administered by the Minister that is prescribed under a regulation.

    Note:Section 37(3) of the Transport Planning and Coordination Act 1994 provides that proof of delegation is also not required in a proceeding under a relevant Transport Act unless the defendant to the Court case gives notice of an intention to challenge the delegation at least 10 business days before the hearing date. This means that you are only required to produce an affidavit exhibiting your appointment and delegation instrument when such a notice is issued.

    Summary
    The documents that need to be provided to QPS when issuing a court certificate are:
      • Court certificate
      • TO(RUMS) Act 1995 Instrument of Delegation & Schedule (only if requested)
      • Affidavit (only if requested)
      • Copy of gazetted appointment/confirmation letter of delegated position (only if requested)
    If Evidentiary Aids are requested
    If Evidentiary Aids are requested
      Court Officers require evidence that a Court Certificate is prepared by an appropriate authorised officer.

      Delegated staff who sign court certificates are required to complete an affidavit stating their delegated position and provide either of the following supporting documents:
        • A copy of their gazetted* appointment
        • An appointment letter or other written confirmation on official TMR letterhead of their appointment.

      Personal details such as residential address and salary should not be visible on the statement. The information should be deleted using the Microsoft Redaction tool or physically blacked out and then photocopy provided.

      Note:An appointment letter may include a letter from the Regional HR manager where the officer is performing higher duties or confirming permanent appointment to the role.

      *Note:Gazetted appointments only occur for the following:
      • All senior executive and senior officer appointments from an advertised vacancy
      • Promotions of tenured public service officers from an advertised vacancy
      • Promotions of tenured public service officers arising from the use of recurring vacancy provision

      The affidavit refers to the gazetted appointment/confirmation letter as 'Exhibit A' and the Instrument and Schedule as 'Exhibit B'. It is important to mark the documents accordingly.

      The procedure to obtain a copy of the gazetted appointment/confirmation letter is as follows:
      Gazetted appointment
      Gazetted appointment
        • Step 1
          If you do not know the date you were appointed to the delegated position, you will need to contact your Regional HR Manager by email and provide your name, delegated position title, and email address.
        • Step 2
          When you have the appointment date you were appointed to the delegated position you will need to go to Ask a Librarian and complete the "Your Request" section asking for a copy of your gazetted appointment including your:
        • name
        • appointment date
        • delegated position
        • Step 3
          A scanned copy of the appointment will be returned to you via email within 24 hours of the request.
      Written confirmation of appointment
      Written confirmation of appointment
        Send an email to your regional HR Manager including your name, delegated position title and appointment date (if known) and request written confirmation of your appointment to the position.
      Relieving Staff in a Delegated Position
      Relieving Staff in a Delegated Position
        When you are relieving in a delegated position, you are required to provide a copy of the Approval of the Temporary Assignment of Staff to Perform Other Duties (F548), instead of a copy of the gazetted appointment/confirmation letter.

        This document will need to be noted in the affidavit and marked as "Exhibit A". In addition, the Affidavit should be varied at paragraph 2 to state "I was appointed to act in this position on <insert date>."

Who may request a Court Certificate
Who may request a Court Certificate
    The following organisations may request a Court Certificate:

    • Interstate Police
    • Federal Police
    • A Court via Subpoena
    • Department Public Prosecutions
    • Commission for Children and Young People and Child Guardian

    • Transport Inspector
    • Contact Legal Services for advice for any other Departments or Authorities who request information relating to an Act or Legislation
Format of Request
Format of Request
    Requests must be made in writing on the organisation's letterhead by either:
    • Letter
    • Fax
    • Email (QPS only)*
    *
    • Each centre/central processing hub may provide to QPS a generic email address for their centre. Personal email addresses are not to be provided.
    • Requests must be received from a QPS email address (ie ...@police.qld.gov.au) and contain the appropriate signature block that clearly identifies the person requesting the information and a postal address for the delivery of the completed certificate if it is not to be collected in person.

    The request must include the:
    • Reason the information is required
    • Legislation which allows the request
    • Contact details of requesting party (including name, organisation, telephone number and postal address)
    • Method of delivery eg. to be collected or posted
Where to request a Court Certificate
Where to request a Court Certificate

    Court certificate requests can be made at all TMR customer service centres, via email or fax.

    SEQ North and SEQ South regions have a central processing hub and CSCs that sit within these regions should forward the request to the relevant central processing hub for completion.

    Once the certificate/s has been prepared, the central processing hub will make arrangements with the requesting party for the collection/postage of the completed certificate/s.

    Section 123C, Schedule 1 Evidentiary Aids
    Section 123C, Schedule 1 Evidentiary Aids
      Section 123C, Schedule 1 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 defines a "certificate" as a certificate purporting to be signed by the chief executive.

      The chief executive is the Director-General of the Department of Transport and Main Roads.

      The Director-General has delegated this function to designated positions within the department. Only those persons designated have the authority of the Director-General
      to act on his/her behalf in those matters.

      Delegated Officers:
      • Manager (Customer Service Centre)
      • Senior Advisor (Service Management)
      • Team Leader (Customer Service Direct)

      For a complete list of designated positions delegated this power, please see the following webpage link: https://inside.tmr.qld.gov.au/corp/legislation/Delegations%20database/Forms/AllItems.aspx

    Section 95 Evidence Act 1977
    Section 95 Evidence Act 1977
      Information that is not specifically catered for by the evidentiary aids provisions of either Section 123C, Schedule 1 or Section 124 of TORUM may be requested and can be provided in a certificate under Section 95(4) of the Evidence Act 1977(Admissibility of statements produced by computers).

      This includes information about address details to which correspondence was sent, list of addresses recorded against a customer.

      This certificate may be used to avoid the necessity of an officer having to attend
      court and may be prepared upon request. The Evidence Act certificate must be signed by one of the following delegated officers.

      Delegated Officers:
      • Manager (Customer Service Centre)
      • Senior Advisor (Service Management)
      • Team Leader (Customer Service Direct)


    Section 87X Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994
    Section 87X Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994
      Section 87X (4b) of the Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994 defines a "certificate" as a certificate purporting to be signed by a person authorised by the commission or transport administration to issue the certificate.

      The commission may appoint a person as an authorised person if the commission considers the person has the necessary expertise or experience to be an authorised person.

      Delegated Officers:
      • Manager (Customer Service Centre)

    Obtaining advice from Legal Services
    Obtaining advice from Legal Services
      If the Court Certificate request is not covered by the standard templates, an email can be sent to Legal Services for assistance.

      If you require legal assistance with a court certificate, you are required to:
      • Draft a proposed certificate in the current court certificate format
      • Email the draft certificate to Legal Requests with details of why the existing templates do not cover the request
      • Include "Urgent" in the email subject line if a 24 hour turnaround is required
      • Legal Services will:
        • Respond with advice/court certificate
        • Send a copy of the final version of the Court Certificate to TSD Legal Requests
      Note: If the email to Legal Requests does not include details of why the request is not covered by the existing templates, it may be rejected.
