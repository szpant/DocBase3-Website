Register of Approved Vehicles (RAV) (7655)


What is itThe Register of Approved Vehicles (RAV) is an online, public searchable database of vehicles approved for road use in Australian under the Road Vehicle Standards Act 2018 (RVSA). It is administered by the Federal Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development and Communications (DITRDC).

The RAV replaces the need to fit most road vehicles with a compliance plate. The information/import approval will be stored in the RAV.

All vehicles provided to the Australian market will need to be recorded on the RAV (ie, provided by manufacturers and personal importers).

To be entered on the RAV, a road vehicle must first be granted either a vehicle type approval or a concessional RAV entry approval. An individual or organisation may apply for a vehicle type approval via the DITRDC's ROVER online service. There is no fee to set up a ROVER account. There are fees for the Vehicle Type Approval and Concessional RAV entry approvals. Refer customers to the DITRDC's for current fees.

Transitional period
Transitional period
    Although the changes commence from 1 July 2021, there will be a transitional period before it becomes mandatory for eligible vehicles to be recorded on the RAV. More details on the transitional dates will be published as it become available.

Vehicle Type ApprovalA Vehicle type approval indicates evidence of the vehicle's compliance with the Australian Design Rules (ADR) has been submitted to the DITRDC for that vehicle type. The approval holder is responsible for entering the VIN onto the RAV in accordance with the World Manufacturer Identifier code protocols.

Manufacturers and importers of 'mass produced' vehicles (e.g. Toyota Camry) will have a vehicle type approval for that type (make and model) of vehicle.
Concessional RAV Entry ApprovalA Concessional RAV entry approval applies to individual vehicles (rather than a vehicle type) that need to be granted a concessional entry pathway because they have not met the full type approval certification. The VIN is added to the RAV by DITRDC on behalf of the approval holder.

Applications for concessional RAV entry approval must include a declaration that the vehicle complies (or will comply) with the relevant national road vehicle standards at the time the vehicle is entered on the RAV.

Examples of Concessional RAV entry approval holders are importers of individual vehicles, or light trailer manufacturers producing 4 or less per calendar year.
New BusinessPrior to completing a new business transaction, operators have to check to see if the vehicle is recorded on the RAV if:
  • The Safety Certificate/COI indicates there is a RAV entry
    or
  • There is no compliance plate/personal import plate
    and
  • The date of certification under RVSA (compliance or import approval) is on or after 1 July 2021

If a vehicle is recorded on the RAV, all of the relevant information previously recorded on the compliance plate (make, model, build date, gross mass, seating etc) must be entered into TICA according to the details the RAV.
