Official Transport Inspector Notebook (5248)

Official Notebooks
What it isAn official notebook (notebook) is used by Transport Inspectors, and any Senior Transport Inspectors or Principal Advisors (Investigations) who elect to use a notebook, to record evidence and information relating to compliance activities.

The notebooks entries are required to be a comprehensive record that enables an officer to recall the maximum amount of information when giving evidence in court.
Using Official Notebooks Officers with a notebook must carry their notebook at all times unless their duties prevent them from doing so, such as covert operations.

Between rostered shifts the notebook must be stored it in a safe place on TMR premises. If an officer is rostered away from the office the notebook must be secured when not on duty.

Officer should only have one notebook in use at any time.

Completed notebooks must be returned to the Senior Transport Inspector as soon as practicable.

Making Notebook Entries
Making Notebook Entries
    If enforcement action is taken during an intercept, information and/or evidence, such as mass or dimension calculations must be recorded in the notebook. The intercept must also be recorded in QCIS.

    Record details of the compliance activity with clear rationale for any decisions made. The level of detail should be sufficient to enable the officer to recall the full facts during any court proceedings. Officers can record all matters or facts they consider appropriate.

    Facts entered into a notebook may be used for many purposes, so entries should be clear, precise and readable by supervisors and any person who may be required to examine it at a later date. A comprehensive notebook entry can also provide valuable protection for the officer when asked to recall an event or incident some weeks or months later.

    Rules for notebook entries
    Rules for notebook entries
      The following rules apply to when making notebook entries:
      • Notebook entries should be recorded in black or blue ink in English and be clearly legible. A pencil should never be used.
      • At the start of a shift, the time, day and date should be written on the next clear line on the page and underlined.
      • The time each entry is made must be written on the left-hand side of page.
      • Notes and pages should be in chronological order.
      • Record time using the 24 hour clock e.g. 3.30PM is 1530 HRS.
      • Statements must be written in direct speech in “quotation marks” or capitals for example.
        Q. “ Who instructed you to load the truck”
        A. “Fred the supervisor”
      • Write surnames in capitals e.g. Mr John SMITH
      • If a mistake is made cross the word/s out with a single line and initial the mistake. The crossed out word/s must still be legible.
      • Remember - No ELBOWS
        No Erasures
        No Leaves torn out
        No Blank Spaces
        No Overwriting
        No Writing between Lines
      • The page must be ruled off at the completion of each entry.
      • Never record personal notes such as private phone numbers, shopping lists in your notebook.

    If the notebook is not available
    If the notebook is not available
      If an officer does not have their notebook or, it is not available for any reason and there is the need to make notes, they may make their notes on any available piece of paper. The general rules for note taking should be followed wherever possible. The note should be formally preserved as an ‘exhibit’ and attached to any subsequent report or statement. It may be used as a reference when giving evidence and should therefore be available for inspection at court.
    Multiple officers recording same event
    Multiple officers recording same event
      Where there are a number of officers that have witnessed the same event or have been present at an interview, they may confer when preparing notes. Their notes must only include the officer's genuine recollections. Where joint notes have been made officers should note in their own notebook that this has happened and include the times between which the note-writing took place, the location and who was present.
    Notebook use with statements
    Notebook use with statements
      There is no requirement for officers to make a full notebook entry when completing a statement(s) provided the statement is contemporaneous and there is an abbreviated entry in the notebook referring to it.

      If a statement is not completed after an incident at which an officer is present, then a full notebook entry must be made detailing the events. For example, Transport Inspectors involved on the periphery of an incident who do not complete a statement must make a full notebook entry of the events. The events may need to be recalled for both criminal and civil proceedings at some time in the future.

      Officers who do complete a statement after an incident will need to complete a notebook entry of those details not contained in the statement for continuity of events.

      Supervisors, when checking and signing notebooks must ensure that where an abbreviated notebook entry is made, there is a contemporaneous form of statement in existence.
Providing Notebook Entries to Prosecutions
Providing Notebook Entries to Prosecutions
    Copies of notebook pages should be sent to Prosecution Services with any Brief to Adjudicator or Full Brief of Evidence and saved with any evidence relating to an investigation.

    The relevant notebook pages should be saved as a pdf file with the naming convention to include:
    • Officers' ID Number/Officers Surname/Defendants' Name/notebook number. This will allow Compliance staff and/or the Prosecutions Unit to identify the nature of the content ‘prior’ to opening the file.

    The pdf should be imported\cross referenced and linked in CID.
Managing Notebooks
Notebook Register
Notebook Register
    Official notebooks are numbered and are assigned to an Authorised Officer.

    A notebook register must be maintained in each office that stores new or used notebooks to record the:
    • numbers of all new and issued notebooks held by the office
    • date the notebook was received into the office
    • date of issue, and receiving officers name and Q number
    • date of return and name of officer receipting the notebook.

    New and returned notebooks, and the notebook register, must be securely stored.

    Only an authorised person of at least the position of Senior Transport Inspector can issue a notebook. It is recommended that one person is designated as being responsible for this function, however, Regions or Compliance Managers/Principal Advisor (Investigations) may adopt procedures to suit local conditions.
Annual Reconciliation of the Notebook Register
Annual Reconciliation of the Notebook Register
    Annually during June, all notebooks held on site should be reconciled with the notebook register. The reconciliation must be recorded in notebook register and signed by an STI.
Issuing a Notebook
Issuing a Notebook
    When issuing a new notebook, complete the inside front cover of the notebook and the notebook register, then have the receiving officer sign both the notebook and register as confirmation.
Returning a Notebook
Returning a Notebook
    Completed notebooks must be returned to the Senior Transport Inspector as soon as practicable.

    If the notebook contains entries that relate to activities that have a retention period greater than 7 years, Refer: Retention and Storage of Notebooks below, the entries should be copied and stored to DMS before the book is returned in the register.

    The returning officer must complete and sign the inside back cover.

    The receiving officer must complete and sign both the inside back cover of the notebook and the notebook register.

    Where an officer who has been issued with a notebook leaves TMR or ceases to be an Authorised Officer, any notebook/s in their possession must be returned to their line manager.
Lost Notebooks
Lost Notebooks
    Notebooks are TMR property and contain sensitive information which in unauthorised hands can pose significant security and privacy risks. Staff should take all appropriate steps to ensure their safekeeping and must report any loss at the earliest opportunity. A failure to do so may be a breach of the Code of Conduct for the Queensland Public Service and could result in a misconduct investigation.

    If the notebook cannot be located, the officer will need to formally report the matter to their Senior Transport Inspector/line manager at the earliest opportunity and obtain a replacement notebook.

    Senior Transport Inspectors will confirm with the officer that all reasonable steps have been taken to try to locate the notebook. Once the Senior Transport Inspector is satisfied that all reasonable steps have been taken, they must inform the Compliance Manager of a Breach of Information Security via email.
Supervisor ResponsibilitiesSupervisors should examine their officer’s notebooks at least once every 3 months. To ensure officers adhere to notebook policy.
Checking Notebooks
Checking Notebooks
    When checking a notebook the Supervisor must check the following:
    a.the entries are in chronological order and up-to-date
    b.all required entries have been made
    c. there are no superfluous notes
    d.the records are concise
    e.the entries are legible, clear recorded correct
    f.where an abbreviated notebook entry has been made there is a contemporaneous form of statement.

    Supervisors should record their examination, observations and, if necessary, future required corrections in the notebook. The entry should be recorded in ink (ideally in a different colour to that used in the notebook), in English and clearly legible with the date and time of the examination.
