Customer - Organisations (3227)

An organisation can be one of the following:
Registered business
Queensland
Queensland
    A business name is a name under which someone may conduct a business. Business names are not legal entities and legal ownership remains with the proprietor. The proprietor may be an individual or organisation.

    To register a vehicle in a Business Name, the business name must be recorded as the first registered operator, then the Proprietor is recorded as the second registered operator (the legal entity).
    Registration of Business Names Prior to the 28 May 2012
    Registration of Business Names Prior to the 28 May 2012
      Prior to the 28 May 2012 Each person operating under a business name must have registered with the Qld Office of Fair Trading (OFT) and issued with a:
      • Certificate of Registration of a Business Name
      • Business Number (BN)
    Registration of business names after 28 May 2012
    Registration of business names after 28 May 2012
      The Australian Securities Investment Commission (ASIC) took over responsibility for business names from 28 May 2012 and now issue registered business names with a:
      • Certificate of Registration, and
      • issue an Australian Registered Body Number (ARBN) qualification instead of a business number.
Interstate
Interstate
    Where the business name is registered by an Interstate Authority
    An interstate business name registered by an Interstate Authority cannot purchase CPRO or MPRO products as a customer cannot be created.

    Where the business name is registered by ASIC
    A business registered interstate with ASIC can be recorded as a TMR customer and can purchase CPRO and MPRO products (Refer: QT Shop).

    A vehicle can only be registered to an interstate business if it has a Queensland garage address. Proof must be provided. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 4. Registration > Garage Address > List of Garage Address Scenarios.
Trust
Trust
Trust
    A trust is an obligation imposed on a person – the trustee – to hold property or assets (e.g. business assets) for the benefit of others (known as beneficiaries).

    The trustee is responsible for a Trust’s operation.

    Setting up a trust requires a formal deed. It is a name under which the trust members conduct their affairs.

    Types of trusts include:
    • Family trust
    • Trust including friends and business associates
    • Business trust which may include the words Trust/Trustee/Fund or Foundation.

    A Trustee of a Trust can be a company registered with ASIC and have an Australian Company Number (ACN) - therefore regarded by TMR as a legal entity.

    If the trust does business under a name other than its own, the name must be registered as a business name with ASIC.

    A Trust itself is regarded as a non-legal entity and must have a trustee appointed to manage the affairs of the Trust. These require the nomination of a legal entity which is recorded as the second registered operator.

    The legal entity must be the trustee of the trust and can be either an individual or any other legal entity, such as a company.

    See Trust Documentation Required at EOI for Organisations
Partnership
Partnership
Partnership
    A partnership is two or more people or entities who do business as partners or receive income jointly.

    In a partnership, control or management of the business is shared. A partnership is not a separate legal entity so partners are liable for all debts and obligations of the business.

    Partners may be an individual, company, registered body or other incorporated body.

    For TMR purposes, partnerships are a common type of business structure and therefore, are not legal entities.

    Partnerships require the nomination of a legal entity as the second customer. The second customer is the registered operator and must be one of the partners.

    A partnership can be one of three entity types:
    • a family partnership – where two or more partners are related to one another
    • a limited partnership – where the liability of one or more partners for the debts and obligations of the business is limited; formed when the partnership is registered as such with the relevant state or territory authority
    • an ‘other’ partnership – commonly known as a general law partnership; where all the partners are equally responsible for the management of the business and each has unlimited liability for the debts and obligations it may incur.

    See Partnership Documentation Required at EOI for Organisations
Body Corporate
Body Corporate
Body Corporate
    The owners of Group Title Dwellings (ie Units and Townhouses) are members of a body corporate. A body corporate is responsible for repairs and maintenance of common property areas and for insuring the building.

    A body corporate is a legal entity under the Body Corporate and Community Management's Act 1977

    Under this legislation a body corporate is issued with a Registration Confirmation Statement by the Department of Natural Resources displaying a Community Management Scheme (CMS) number.
Government Body
Government Body
Government Body
    A Government body includes:
    • Local, state and federal government
    • Statutory Authorities
    • Semi- Government
    • Commonwealth authority/agency
    • Governor of Queensland
Associations
Associations
Associations
    Organisations/associations are community organisations
    and include services such as:
    • School and P&C associations
    • Clubs such as sporting clubs, car clubs, community service clubs etc.

    These organisations are either:
    • Incorporated and registered with the Office of Fair Trading (OFT)
    • Legal entities under specific Qld legislation
    • Legal entities under Federal legislation

    An organisation or association that is not registered with the OFT or is not a legal entity under QLD or Federal legislation can purchase CRPO and MPRO products (Refer: QT Shop) but cannot register vehicles.
Interstate Associations
Interstate Associations
    An Organisation/Association incorporated interstate and registered with one of the following, is considered a legal entity:
    • ASIC as an Australian Registerable Body
    • Interstate equivalent of the Queensland Office of Fair Trading (OFT)
Company (ACN)
Australian Company
Australian Company
    A company is:
    • A legal entity
    • Registered with ASIC
    • Issued with a Australian Company Number (ACN)

    Companies may be identified by having Pty Ltd or Ltd after their name.
Overseas Company
Overseas Company
    Overseas companies operating in Australia must be registered with ASIC. They are issued with a Certificate of Registration of a Registerable Australian Body displaying an Australian Registered Body Number (ARBN).

    If they are registered with ASIC they are a legal entity.
Licensed Motor Dealer
Queensland
Queensland
    A motor dealer is an entity involved in the purchase and sale of new and/or used vehicles. A motor dealer must be a legal entity (individual or organisation).

    The requirement to be licensed with the Office of Fair Trading (OFT) depends if the dealer sells new or used vehicles:

    New motor vehicles, caravans, trailers, tractors and farm machinery
    • Not required to hold a Dealer Licence
    Used motor vehicles and caravans (not trailers)
    • Motor dealer must hold a Dealer Licence issued by OFT
    Used trailers, ships, tractors and farm machinery
    • Motor dealer must hold a Second Hand Dealer Licence issued by OFT.
Interstate
Interstate
    An interstate licensed motor dealer is an individual or an organisation involved in the purchase and/or sale of new and used vehicles in another State/Territory.

    These licensed motor dealers are registered with an interstate authority similar to the Queensland Office of Fair Trading.

    All interstate licenses are recognised under reciprocal arrangements.

    Interstate motor dealers that are legal entities.
Cooperative
Cooperative
Cooperative
    A cooperative is a legal entity owned and democratically controlled by its members.

    An autonomous association of persons (persons meaning an individual or corporation) may become a member of a cooperative by voluntarily joining together to meet common economic, social and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly owned and democratically controlled enterprise.

    A cooperative may be formed to provide goods or services to its members or to supply goods and services to the public. A registered cooperative may carry out any activity or activities outlined in its rules. These acts reflect the nature of the cooperatives’ involvement in areas such as primary production, manufacturing, trading or community or social activities.
Subsidiary
Subsidiary
Subsidiary
    A subsidiary is an organisation that is owned by another organisation but is a legal entity in its own right (ie has it's own ACN). The owning organisation usually has a controlling stake in the subsidiary.

    For example: Bobs Fishing Hut Pty Ltd is owned by Larry's Tackle Pty Ltd both have an ACN therefore transfers of vehicles between these organisations does not exempt duty requirements.
Child Organisation
Child Organisation
Child Organisation
    For the purposes of vehicle registration a child organisation is not a separate legal entity but a part of a larger organisation. Both the Parent and the Child organisations must have the same name.

    For example: Sarah's Boat's Pty Ltd has a head office in Brisbane and several locations in Queensland. Head office locations are typically created as the parent organisation with the ACN and each location is a child organisation sharing the Parents ACN and removing the need for a second registered operator. Transfers of vehicles between parent and child and between child organisation is exempt duty as there is no change in legal entity.
