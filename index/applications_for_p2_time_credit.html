Applications for P2 Time Credit (3500)

Applications for P2 Time Credit
When can tenure be recognised
P1 licence holders who were eligible to upgrade prior to 1 January 2014 but did not attend a TMR licensing centre, may be required to complete a P2 Time Credit Application (F5010) for consideration to upgrade their licence.
P1 licence holders who have passed and paid for the HPT but not upgraded, may have any additional time over the minimum tenure period (12 months) recognised towards the P2 licence tenure.
Note: This process won't apply to customers who passed the hazard perception test at the learner licence phase. These customers will have passed the HPT in the learner licence phase, their licence will be automatically upgraded when tenure is met.
Tenure will be automatically recognised when the licence is upgraded or later renewed. If a person wishes to apply for credit at another time they may visit a licence issuing centre and complete a P2 Time Credit Application towards an Open Licence (F5010).
If a person has served the whole of the required P2 licence tenure period on a P1 licence, the P2 licence can be expired and they can progress immediately to an open licence (effective from the date they attend the CSC and their time credit application is approved, or the time credit is recognised automatically), provided they have passed and paid for the HPT.
If a person has served less than their required P2 licence tenure period on a P1 licence, the tenure can be recognised and the P2 expiry date can be amended, provided they have passed and paid for the HPT. However, the person must serve the balance of the P2 tenure requirement before being eligible to progress to an open licence.
If a person has obtained a P2 licence but served more than the necessary one year tenure requirement on their P1 licence, any extra time over the minimum one year can be recognised towards the time they need to hold their P2 licence (ie the expiry date of their P2 licence can be amended or if eligible they can progress to an open licence).

Note:Under no circumstances is the effective date of an open licence to be backdated. If a person is eligible for an open licence, the P2 licence expiry date is updated to the date one day prior to the day they attend a CSC, and an open licence is issued (eg. if the customer attends a CSC today, the P2 expiry date is yesterday).

Note:Ensure that the person has not had the time credit automatically applied before approving an application for time credit.

Note:The HPT test must have been passed and the test fee must have been paid before a person is eligible for a P2 or open licence. However, the date the test was passed and the fee was paid are not relevant for calculating tenure.

Examples
Examples
    Note:The below examples apply to licence holders who were under 23 when they obtained their P1 licence. A licence holder who was 23 when they obtained their P1 licence would need to hold their P2 licence for one year to progress to an open licence. A licence holder who was 24 when they obtained their P1 licence would be eligible to progress to an open licence after holding their P1 licence for one year.

    P1 licence holder eligible to progress to an open licence
    P1 licence holder eligible to progress to an open licence
      If a P1 licence holder has served three or more years on a P1 licence the tenure may be recognised and an open licence may be issued (provided they have passed and paid for the HPT).

      Example 1
      • P1 licence obtained - 1 June 2009
      • P1 tenure requirement met - 31 May 2010
      • Eligible to upgrade to a P2 licence – 1 June 2010 (did not attend a CSC)
      • Attended CSC to upgrade to open licence - 1 July 2013

      As it is more than two years since the date the person was eligible to upgrade to a P2 licence, the P2 licence can be expired as at 30 June 2013 and an open licence issued effective 1 July 2013.

    P1 licence holder eligible to have P2 expiry date amended
    P1 licence holder eligible to have P2 expiry date amended
      If a P1 licence holder has served more than one year but less than three years on a P1 licence, the tenure over the minimum one year period may be recognised and the P2 expiry date can be amended (provided they have passed and paid for the HPT). However, the person must serve the balance of the P2 tenure requirement before being eligible to progress to an open licence.

      Example 1
      • P1 licence obtained - 1 September 2011
      • P1 tenure requirement met - 31 August 2012
      • Attended CSC to upgrade to an open licence - 1 July 2013

      As it is less than two years since the date the person was eligible to upgrade to a P2 licence, the P2 expiry date can be amended to 31 August 2014 , recognising the tenure, however an open licence cannot be granted until the full P2 tenure has been met.

    P2 licence holder eligible to progress to an open licence
    P2 licence holder eligible to progress to an open licence
      If a P2 licence holder served more than the minimum one year tenure requirement on their P1 licence, any extra time over the minimum one year period can be recognised towards the time they need to hold their P2 licence. If the additional P1 time combined with the time they have held a P2 licence is at least two years an open licence may be issued.

      Example 1
      • P2 licence obtained - 20 March 2013
      • P1 tenure requirement met - 21 January 2011
      • Attended CSC to upgrade to an open licence - 1 July 2013

      As it is more than two years since the date the person was eligible to upgrade to a P2 licence, the P2 licence can be expired as at 30 June 2013 and an open licence issued effective 1 July 2013.

    P2 licence holder eligible to have P2 expiry date amended
    P2 licence holder eligible to have P2 expiry date amended
      If a P2 licence holder served more than the minimum one year tenure requirement on their P1 licence, any extra time over the minimum one year can be recognised towards the time they need to hold their P2 licence. If the additional P1 time combined with the time they have held a P2 licence is less than two years then the person must serve the balance of the P2 tenure period before being eligible to progress to an open licence.

      Example 1
      • P2 licence obtained - 20 March 2013
      • P1 tenure requirement met - 21 August 2011
      • Attended CSC to upgrade to an open licence - 1 July 2013

      As it is less than two years since the date the person was eligible to upgrade to a P2 licence, the P2 expiry date can be amended to 21 August 2013, recognising the tenure, however an open licence cannot be granted until the full P2 tenure has been met.

When tenure cannot be recognised No tenure can be recognised until a person has passed and paid for the HPT. Any periods of time when a licence is suspended, expired or cancelled cannot count towards tenure. A P1 licence holder is not eligible to apply for a P2 licence or open licence until they have held a P1 licence for at least 12 months and have passed the HPT. However, once they have passed and paid for the HPT, credit towards P2 licence tenure can be calculated from the date they completed their one year P1 licence tenure (see examples above).

Returning drivers cannot rely on tenure accumulated more than five years ago.
