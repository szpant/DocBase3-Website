Prescribed Service Person
Prescribed Service Person
    Exemption ReasonEXSERVICEPERSON
    What is it?This exemption reason provides a duty exemption to an applicant who is entitled to concessional registration fees for the vehicle as they have been assessed at a specified rate of impairment as a result of their service with the Australian Defence Service (ADS).
    Requirements
    New Business
    New Business
      • No further proof required if the customer has been established in TICA and PENE qualification is on the customer record.
      or
      • letter from the Department of Veterans' Affairs that shows you are at least 70% incapacitated under the Veterans' Entitlements Act 1986 (Cwlth).
      or
      • have been assessed as having at least 50 impairment points under the Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (Cwlth)”.
    Transfer
    Transfer
      • No further proof required if the customer has been established in TICA and PENE qualification is on the customer record.
      or
      • letter from the Department of Veterans' Affairs that shows you are at least 70% incapacitated under the Veterans' Entitlements Act 1986 (Cwlth)
      or
      • have been assessed as having at least 50 impairment points under the Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (Cwlth)”.
    RulesA duty exemption may be granted on a motor vehicle registered to a Prescribed Service Person if:
    • Applicant is entitled to Prescribed Service person's concession on that vehicle. For eligibility, Refer: Concessional Registration < PSP Condition; and
    • Applicant is recorded as first registered operator (if applicable).

    Note:
    • A vehicle does not necessarily need to have a concession applied to be eligible for a duty exemption.
    • However, in line with legislation and rules regarding concessions, only one concession is permitted per spousal couple.
    • If a concession is applied to another vehicle registered to the Prescribed Service Person or their Spouse, this would mean that the new vehicle is not eligible for a registration concession and therefore is not eligible for the duty exemption.
    • But, if there are no existing vehicles with concessions and the customer choses not to apply one, they are still eligible for the duty exemption.

Same Name
    Exemption ReasonSAME REG OPER
    What it isThis exemption reason provides a duty exemption when re-registering / transferring a vehicle to either the same registered operator and/or their relative.

    Note:Vehicles previously issued with a Club Permit (similar to SIVE concession) in Victoria under the same name must provide proof that duty was paid if claiming a duty exemption when registering the vehicle in Queensland. Vehicles issued with a Club permit in Victoria do not pay duty. Under the Club Permit scheme in Victoria the vehicle is issued with a number plate, however, the fees paid are not classified as registration fees and duty is not charged. If evidence is not supplied that duty has previously been paid then the customer must pay duty when registering the vehicle in Queensland.

    Relative includes:
    • their spouse (includes de facto and same sex couples)
    • a parent or grandparent,
    • a parent or grandparents spouse,
    • a child, stepchild or grandchild,
    • a child, stepchild or grandchild of their spouse,
    • the spouse of any of the above

Family Gift
Family Gift
    Exemption ReasonFAMILY GIFT
    What it isThis exemption reason provides a duty exemption for:
    • relatives or
    • from one or both spouse names to one spouse following dissolution of a marriage.
      Note:This includes transfers from a company name to a spouse / de-facto listed on a court issued document (with court seal) or a recognised agreement under the Property Law Act 1974, section 266 (no court seal).

    Family Gift exemption applies to New registration as well as transfer of registration.
    Family Gift can be selected when exempting duty for a transfer from a deceased registered operator if:
    • The other deceased exemptions do not apply;
      and
    • The acquirer is a relative;
      and
    • The purpose of use is private

    Duty Relative includes:
    • Their spouse (includes civil partnerships, same sex couples and de facto relationships; provided the couple have lived together for at least two years at the time of application),
    • Parent, grandparent or their spouse
    • Spouse's parent, grandparent or their spouse,
    • Child, stepchild, grandchild or their spouse,
    • Spouse's child, stepchild, grandchild or their spouse.
    Evidence of Exemption
    Evidence of Exemption
      Non-Formless Locations

    RequirementsWhere the FAMILY GIFT exemption is being claimed for a relative, and the exemption declaration is not on the Transfer of Vehicle Registration Application form (version date prior to July 2014) or the Vehicle Registration Application form (version date prior to November 2014), the customer must complete the Transfer of Vehicle Registration Statutory Declaration. Please refer to the OSR website for this declaration.

    Requirements for spouses/de-facto after separation or dissolution of a marriage
    Requirements for spouses/de-facto after separation or dissolution of a marriage
      The customer must provide the original or a copy of one of the following documents (with court seal):
      (a)an agreement registered or approved under the Family Law Act 1975 (Cwlth);
      (b)an order of a court under the Family Law Act 1975 (Cwlth);
      (c)a decree nisi;
      (d)a recognised agreement under the Property Law Act 1974, section 266 (no court seal);
      A recognised agreement is a document signed by the former partners and witnessed by a JP or solicitor; and contains a statement of all significant property, financial resources and liabilities of each partner and how it will be divided.
      (e)an order of a court under the Property Law Act 1974, part 19.
    Requirements for transfers from company name to a spouse/de-facto
    Requirements for transfers from company name to a spouse/de-facto
      The customer must provide the original or a copy of one of the following documents (with court seal):
      (a)An agreement registered or approved under the Family Law Act 1975 (Cwlth); or
      (b)An order of a court under the Family Law Act 1975 (Cwlth)

      For the exemption to apply, the transfer of registration must be dated after the date of the court order or financial agreement and be clearly stated on the court order or financial agreement.

      Family Gift must be selected in TICA for transfer transactions.
    RulesWhere a document other than the application form is used to claim the exemption there is no requirement to keep a copy. However, it must be sighted by the CSO and the type of document presented noted on the application form. The document should then be returned to the customer.

    What is it?This exemption reason provides a duty exemption for vehicles registered with a primary producer concession and a GVM of more than six tonnes.
