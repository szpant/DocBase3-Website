Wheelchair Accessible Taxi (WAT) Inspections (7510)


Wheelchair Accessible Taxi (WAT) Inspections
What it isAs a result of a complaint from an Approved Person about the fitment of wheelchair lifting devices, TMR inspected 40 WATs and found 39 vehicles failed to meet the required safety standards.

In response, TMR Transport Inspectors will be conducting all Programmed Vehicles Inspections (PVI) for WATs during the 12 months from 1 February 2020. In addition to the standard items checked as part of a PVI, Inspectors will also inspect the modifications associated with the wheelchair accessibility.

TMR has written to registered owners of WATs, Taxi Booking Companies and held information session with Approved Persons to ensure they are aware of the issues.


WAT letter to operators - Dec2019
Scope
Scope
Scope
    The WAT Inspection procedures take effect from 1 February 2020 and only apply to approximately 648 Wheelchair Access Maxi Taxis at this stage.
     
    For wheelchair accessible passenger transport vehicles that are not taxis, TIs can continue to use the standard 30 minute inspection period to inspect such vehicles as normal.

    Until further intelligence is gathered in relation to taxi modifications and a communication strategy is developed in relation to other vehicles, no attention toward the wheelchair accessibility modifications of other passenger transport vehicles is required or necessary at this stage.

Procedure
Inspection
Inspection
    When conducting an inspection of Wheelchair lifters (R2), Wheelchair Restraints (K5), Seating alteration (LK1) and Child restraint (LK6) the following must be checked:
    • Seat and Seatbelt underfloor fixing plates not to be less than 3750mm² in area and minimum 3mm thickness.
    • Seat and Seatbelt underfloor fixing plates must be contoured to floor, blocked/packed and no sharp edges.
    • Child restraints fitted as per the ADR’s. (ADR 5/34)
    • Wheelchair restraints fitted as per manufacturer instructions

    The inspection of the modifications carried out for the fitment of a wheelchair lifting device and seat modifications can be conducted while conducting the PVI.
Seat anchorage
Seat anchorage
    VSB 14 NCOP7 Section LK Seating and Occupant Protection
    Seating requirements 1.1

    Single seats may be adequately anchored to sheet steel floors by using not less than four 8mm ISO Grade 8.8 (5/16 inch UNF SAE Grade 5) bolts, with a mild steel reinforcement backing plate of an area not less than 3750mm² in contact with the mounting surface and a thickness not less than 3mm at each anchorage point.

    For double and triple seats, the number/size of anchorage points should be increased accordingly.

    The shape of the backing plates must match the contour of the floor material.

    The corners of the reinforcement plates must have a minimum radius of 5mm and the edges adjacent to the floor material must be chamfered.

    The reinforcement backing plate and anchor bolt should be positioned so that the backing plate is loaded approximately centrally. Edge loading should be avoided.

    Note:Backing plates that are mounted using off centre holes or are smaller than the prescribed size, must be supported by test reports that indicate use on specific vehicle make/model and specific locations in the vehicle.
Seatbelt anchorage
Seatbelt anchorage
    VSB 14 NCOP7 Section LK Seating and Occupant Protection
    Seatbelt Anchorage 2.1

    Seatbelt anchorages must be able to meet the applicable strength requirements given in VSB 5A or VSB 5B.

    Seatbelt Anchorage Construction 2.2
    Seatbelts must be adequately anchored to a steel floorpan, upper body or pillar by using bolts designed specifically for anchoring seatbelts.

    Seatbelts are usually supplied in kit form with the appropriate anchor bolts and backing plates.
    The mild steel reinforcement backing plate must have an area not less than 3750mm² in contact with the mounting surface and a thickness not less than 3mm.

    The shape of the backing plates must match the contour of the floorpan/pillar material in the region of the anchorage. The corners of the reinforcement plates must have a minimum radius of 5mm and the edges adjacent to the body must be chamfered.

    The reinforcement backing plate and anchor bolt should be positioned so that the backing plate
    is loaded approximately centrally. Edge loading should be avoided.


    Note:Backing plates that are mounted using off centre holes or are smaller than the prescribed size, must be supported by test reports that indicate use on specific vehicle make/model and specific locations in the vehicle.

Head Strike Zone
Head Strike Zone
    To determine the head strike zone:
    • Measure the vertical distance from top of the seat cushion to 965 mm above the seat.
    • Measurement must be repeated from the front edge of the seat cushion and the rear edge of the seat cushion.
    No sharp metallic objects must be present in the area below the head strike zone boundary.

    If wheelchair occupant restraint mounting or any other WATs related sharp metallic fitting lies below the head strike boundary, the risk can be mitigated by:
    • Relocating the offending part outside the head strike boundary or
    • Covering the offending part with a suitable smooth cover made of structural foam such that it is permanently tied to the part it covers. This is to make sure it remains in place for use when needed. (example Fuel Filler Cap in many cars)

Cease use/Rectification date
Cease use/Rectification date
    All Defect Notices issued for WAT related defects must have a cease use and rectification date 28 days from the inspection date.

    Advise the operator they may apply for an Extension of Time to comply with the Defect Notice.

Hierarchy of Approval
Manufacturer’s Instructions
Manufacturer’s Instructions
    Vehicle or system manufacturer’s instructions override everything else. But only if those instructions are provided in a specific and well described context.
    for example, a smaller backing plate supplied in a WORS kit is acceptable, but only if the manufacturer specifies which make/model of vehicle they can be used on and where exactly on that make/model they can be used and under what circumstances they cannot be used, if so. A general backing plate provided in a kit, without specific instructions about its application, may not be safe to use in all situations. Then the next requirement in this series, “Prescriptive Requirements in Code of Practice” applies.
Prescriptive Requirements in Code of Practice
Prescriptive Requirements in Code of Practice
    Prescriptive requirements in Code of Practice override everything else, except applicable Manufacturer’s Instructions.
    for example, backing plate of size 3750 sq mm and 3 mm thickness and made of mild steel.
Performance Requirements in Code of Practice
Performance Requirements in Code of Practice
    If the code of practice has not specified prescriptive requirements (or even if it has specified, but other performance requirements exist), then design that is tested to performance requirements is acceptable.

    For example, child restraint anchorage that is tested to ADR 34/… loads rather than prescriptive requirements in LK6 is acceptable. Testing report should clearly state that it is relevant to the particular make/model of the vehicle. Again a general test report is not useful just like a general item in manufacturer’s kit.
Good Engineering Practice
Good Engineering Practice
    In absence of everything else, common sense and commonly held notions of good engineering practice should apply.
    for example, use of nylock nuts, ensuring sufficient thread engagement, avoiding welding of critical structural members, backing plates that don’t follow the surface contour and so on.
Manufactures Instructions
Manufacturers instructions
Manufacturers instructions
    If the modifications have been carried out in accordance with the supplied manufacturers instructions they are acceptable modifications however:
    • Amind
      - Upper seat belt mount. Toyota does not approve of drilling holes in the roof section. (no testing or engineering approval)
    • Xinder-Tech
      - Fitting instructions refer to under floor support plate 2mm thick, this is only for the wheel chair restraint not acceptable for seats.
      - Screws or rivets for upper seat belt anchorage. No evidence of this mounting method in the vehicles inspected to date. APs are instructed to ensure all modifications copy with the Code of Practice. If detected report to Vehicle Standards.
    • Quick Connect
      - If the floor or under floor washer is deformed or nuts are loose, fail the inspection. APs are instructed to ensure all modifications copy with the Code of Practice.
    • Standard Engineering
      - Minimum requirement of 1 ½ treads protruding through all nuts.

Re-inspection
Procedure
Procedure
    If a vehicle is presented for re-inspection at a centre other than the centre that did the original inspection consult your STI.

    Re-inspection of a defective WAT vehicle is not a full inspection, only the defective items require reinspection.

    Check the details in RUMIIS of the AP who has signed the defect notice to ensure they are current and hold the appropriate codes (LK2) at the time of the certification.

    The following items must be photographed during the re-inspection:
    • Vehicle and identifiers
    • Rectified Defects
    • New Modification (blue) plate,

    Create a new folder "Reinspection" in the relevant vehicle folder and upload photos.
    If the vehicle fails the reinspection, list only the items that failed the reinspection. DO NOT copy the full list of text from the word document "Defect Wording to be used for WAT Defective Vehicles." Refer Above: Defect Notice Wording

Major defects
Major defects
    If major structural changes form part of the modification, an appropriate assessment report signed by a Registered Professional Engineer Queensland (RPEQ) must be produced to support the structural modification. No such report is necessary for minor structural changes.

    Examples of a major structural change are:
    • Cutting, welding, drilling extensively in the chassis frame, particularly in the flange area. Reducing or adding reinforcement members to frame. Extending the frame overhang. Deleting or repositioning of cross members.
    • Cutting, welding, drilling extensively in the body hoop frame. Deleting or repositioning of hoop arches.
    • Changes to the frame to fit cartridge style wheelchair loader.

    Examples of what is not a major structural change are:
    • Fitting of gussets or additional brackets.
    • Fitting of anchorage backing plates that are profiled to match the original structure.
    • Drilling of access holes in the body panels to insert/position anchorage backing plates.

    The vehicle must be reinspected by TMR to have the defect notice cleared. The reinspection will be conducted at no cost to the operator. Copy and paste the re-inspection information below from the "Defect Notice Wording for WATs" word document.

Process to re-register a WAT
Re-register a WAT with Defect Notice
Re-register a WAT with Defect Notice
    During the COVID-19 emergency owners were able to cancel registration, obtain a refund then re-register at a later date.
    The process developed for other vehicles cannot be applied to a WAT that had a defect notice issued prior to cancelling registration.

    The below process is to be followed when a WAT is re-registered that had a defect notice issued prior to cancelling registration.
    • Customer to supply COI
    • CSC contact Compliance for approval to re-register (STI approval)
    • CSC re-register
    • Customer to contact Compliance to arrange re-inspection
    • Compliance create adhoc booking in SBS for new registration number.
      • 1 hour inspection
      • Programmed Inspection (Taxi WATS)
      • Exempt fee (Account Error) This will create a Business Exception.
      • Comments - Reinstating defect notice from registration (previous)
    • Enter inspection detail in TICA and FAIL
    • Enter detail from previous Defect Notice
    • Create an adhoc booking in SBS for the defect reinspection. Exempt fee. (Account Error) This will create a Business Exception.
    • Compliance conduct re-inspection pass/fail
    • Enter results in TICA
    • Remove previous text
    • Enter new text referencing previous registration number.(Not to expire).
