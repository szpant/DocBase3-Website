IA Hub Pilot - 18 June 2015

Project NameIndustry Authority (IA) Hub Pilot
WhenThe hub model will be delivered in a staged roll-out between 2nd February 2015 and 16th March 2015. Please refer below for the roll-out schedule and implementation dates for your Region.

RegionRoll-out date
SEQ South2nd February 2015
SEQ North16th February 2015
Southern and Central2nd March 2015
Northern16th March 2015
BackgroundAs a result of solutions identified in the Lean workshops for Industry Licensing (IL) and Driver Authorisation (DA), the Client Service Delivery (CSD) Product Leadership Team (PLT) endorsed a 5 month pilot of a hub model for new IL and DA applications.
Who will be impacted and how?
How will this impact customers?
How will this impact customers?
    There will be little impact to customers as a result of this process. Customers will still be able to lodge their IA applications in any CSC.

    It is anticipated that the hub model will reduce the turnaround time frame for the assessment and processing of new IA applications.
How will this impact Customer Service Officers and Call Centre Consultants?
How will this impact Customer Service Officers and Call Centre Consultants?
    From the scheduled roll-out date for your Region, all new applications for IA must be lodged in TICA, then all application documentation (including the Lodgement Checklist, form, supporting documents etc) must be scanned* and emailed to the applicable Hub, before being placed in the operator’s daily filing.

    All new IL applications must be lodged in TICA and all relevant documentation scanned and emailed to the IL Hub at Carseldine CSCindustry.authority.hub@tmr.qld.gov.au.

    All new DA applications must be lodged in TICA and all relevant documentation scanned and emailed to the DA Hub at Southport CSC driver.authorisation.hub@tmr.qld.gov.au.

    The subject line of the email must contain the following information:
    • Customer CRN
    • Customer Name
    • Type of licence/authority being applied for
    • Lodging CSC

    For example: “123456789, John Smith – Taxi Driver Authorisation application, Macgregor CSC”.

    Applications received before the scheduled roll-out date for your Region must be finalised in your CSC. Under no circumstances are these applications to be forwarded to the processing hubs.

    *Important Note:
    When scanning application documentation, operators must ensure that both sides of each page (where there is information on both sides) are scanned before being sent to the applicable hub for assessment and processing.

    Additionally, please ensure the documents are scanned in black and white, with the resolution 200x200 fine dpi, to minimise the size of files sent to the Hubs and the impact on the network servers.

    Upon receipt of the application documentation, each Hub will:
    • Log the application in IDAAT
    • Assess the application, including any returned Traffic and Criminal Histories
    • Make a decision regarding the approval of the application
    • Finalise the application in TICA and IDAAT
    • File any additional documentation applicable to the application

    Addressing enquiries about the status of an application

    Interim Industry Authorities
    Interim Industry Authorities

      Interim Industry Authorities (IIA) will be printed and mailed to customers in remote areas (as per current processes) by the relevant Hub.

      Where a customer presents at a CSC and requests an IIA, the CSC operator should check TICA, and if the application is approved, print the IIA for the customer.

      If the customer requests an IIA when they come in to lodge additional documentation, provided this documentation is the final information required to approve the application, the operator must forward the additional document/s to the relevant Hub. The operator can then phone the relevant Hub to see if the application can be approved so the IIA can be printed and handed to the customer.

      Where there are delays experienced in contacting the Hub by phone, operators should obtain the customer's email address so the IIA can be emailed to them once the application is approved. Operators must advise the customer that, upon receipt of the IIA via email, the customer must immediately print and sign it.


    Criminal history results will continue to be sent to the CSC where the application was lodged throughout the Pilot. From the date your region rolls out, the CIB report must be forwarded to the relevant Hub each day.

    Note:There will be no change to the current remote area process. Applications made using the Industry Authority Remote Area kit will continue to be processed as per the current procedures. Refer to the Industry Authority Remote Area Kit information in DocBase for further information.
