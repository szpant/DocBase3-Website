Booking Entity Authorisation Guide (7233)


About Booking Entity Authorisation (BEA)
What it isA Booking Entity Authorisation (BEA) is a qualification that an operator of a booking service must attain and maintain to arrange a booking to provide a booked hire service.

A booked hire service may be provided by licensed taxis, limousine or booked hire vehicles (for example, vehicles operating through a booking platform like Uber or GoCatch).
When is it requiredA booking entity authorisation can be held by an individual, corporation, incorporated association, partnership, trustee of a trust or Council (Shire or Regional) that operates a booking service.

From 1 December 2017, only authorised booking entities will be able to provide booking services, including a booked hire service provided by a taxi, limousine or a booked hire vehicle.

Note:From October 2017, operator accreditation requirements no longer apply to taxi services and booked hire services (including booked hire services provided in limousines). Other services such as General and Scheduled services will still require an Operator Accreditation.

Where to apply
Online
Online
    Applications can be lodged online for individuals and corporations with an ACN that have existing TMR CRNs.

    Unpaid online applications
    Unpaid online applications
      If a BEA applicant does not finalise (pay) for their online application, they can complete one of the below options:
      New applications:
      New applications:

        • Attend a CSC and pay for their un-finalised application within 30 days. The transaction will be processed in an overnight batch and show as lodged in the Manage Booked Hire (MBH) the next day. There is no requirement to send any paperwork to TransLink as the application will flow through as an online application.
      Renewals:
      Renewals:
        • Attend a CSC and pay for their un-finalised application within 30 days. The transaction will be processed in an overnight batch and show as lodged in the Manage Booked Hire (MBH) the next day. There is no requirement to send any paperwork to TransLink as the application will flow through as an online application; or
        • If the customer is unable to attend a CSC, the operator can raise a datafix SCO to remove the pending invoice in the MBH so that they can complete a new renewal application online. The SCO must include details that the customer cannot attend a CSC.

In person

      • Medical Practitioner
      • Police Officer
      • Consular or Ambassadorial Officer
      • Solicitor, Barrister or Judge

Eligibility requirements
Who can hold a Booking Entity Authorisation
Who can hold a Booking Entity Authorisation
    • Individuals (including overseas residents)
    • Companies/Corporations (including overseas registered)
    • Incorporated Associations
    • Trustees who manage a Trust or Superannuation fund
    • Partnerships
    • Councils (Shire or Regional)

    The applicant can't be a 'Trading Name' but the trading name can be added to the Manage Booked Hire staff UI in the trading name field.

    Note:Booking entities must have proof of incorporation or other registered business structure in Australia. If the organisation is located overseas and doesn’t have an ARBN (or ACN), they will need the Qual FOEN added to their CRN for the application to be processed as well as a local nominee.
    Refer to: Local Nominee below.

    Related body corporate
    Related body corporate
      If the applicant has a related body corporate, this must be declared on the application. A related body corporate means:
      • Individual applicant:
        • If the individual is a majority shareholder or director of a company, the company they are the shareholder or director of is the related body corporate.
      • Company applicant:
        • If the company:
          • has another company which is linked; or
          • is controlled by a holding company,
          the other company is the related body corporate.
    Individuals
    Individuals
      All individual/s must supply original EOI.

      Note:If more than one individual is listed on the application, they are added under the 'Additional holders' field on the Manage Book Hire staff UI. Additional Holders can only be added or removed from an application/renewal when the status is received/lodged.

      Suppressed Customers

    Companies/Corporations/Councils
    Companies/Corporations/Councils
      All current directors must be listed on the application and must sign the application.

      A copy of a current Company Extract listing the directors of the corporation/company (available from ASIC) must be submitted.

      Overseas registered companies are to supply Certified copy of Registration of Company issued by equivalent authorised body (including NAATI translation if required) All directors must sign the application.

      For Regional and Shire Councils, refer to the Passenger Transport Documentation for requirements.


      Note:Directors and Council Mayors/delegates are entered on the Managed Booked Hire staff UI as 'Associated Personnel'.

    Incorporated Associations
    Incorporated Associations
      All current management committee members must be listed on the application and must sign the application.

      A copy of an official extract about the incorporated association from the register of incorporated associations listing the current management committee members (issued by the Queensland Office of Fair Trading) must be submitted.

      Note:Committee members are entered on the Managed Booked Hire staff UI as 'Associated Personnel'.

    Trustees who manage a Trust or a Superannuation Fund
    Trustees who manage a Trust or a Superannuation Fund
      All names of the trustees must be listed on the application. All trustees, including the directors/executives (if the trustee is a company), must sign the application.

      A copy of the Deed of Trust documents which outlines the trustee's powers, lists all involved parties and proves the validity of the trusteeship must be submitted.

      For Trusts, the customer qualifications must be 'Trust' in TICA to be able to receive an application into the Manage Booked Hire staff UI. If the customer has the old qualification of 'Trust or Partnership' it will need to be updated with the 'Trust' qualification.


      Note:Either the first or principal trustee must be added under the 'Trustee' field on the Manage Booked Hire staff UI. All other trustees need to be entered in the 'Associated Personnel' field.

    Partnerships
    Partnerships
      All parties who form the partnership must be named on the application and must sign the application.

      A signed, formal Deed of Partnership document, or another form of *partnership agreement, which lists all involved parties and proves the validity of the partnership must be submitted.

      For Partnerships, the partnership name is entered into TICA as an organisation (e.g. John and Marc Enterprises) and the customer qualification must be 'Partnership'. If the customer has the old qualification of 'Trust or Partnership' it will need to be updated with the 'Partnership' qualification.

      *Note:Partnership agreements may include:
      • listing all parties involved in the partnership (compare this list against the parties listed on the BEA application)
      • stated intent to form a partnership
      • date the agreement was entered into
      • signed agreement.

      Note:The 'Local Nominee' can never have the same CRN as the applicant.
      Partners are entered on the Managed Booked Hire staff UI as 'Associated Personnel'.

    Associated Personnel overseas/interstate
    Associated Personnel overseas/interstate
      If an associated person (e.g. director) is overseas or interstate, certified copies of EOI will be accepted by post to Translink. Where non-original EOI is provided, the EOI is not recorded in TICA and the CRN will be created by the Translink BHSL/BEA Hub. Original EOI must be presented for any TMR product to be issued.
Criminal History Checks
Criminal History Checks
    As part of the BEA application (both new and renewal) each individual and associated personnel (e.g. Directors of a company, Trustees, Partners, Local Nominee, etc.) of the applicant will require to have a national Criminal History Check (CHC) undertaken unless they are exempt.

    Note:All aliases must be added in TICA prior to receiving the application into the Manage Booked Hire staff UI.

    Each applicant or associated personnel will also require a New Zealand (NZ) CHC if they have indicated on the application form that they have resided in NZ. The CSC/Agency operator will need to indicate this on the 'Manage Booked Hire' staff UI by checking the relevant box.

    Note:As the CHCs are automated it is very important that all individuals including all associated personnel must be entered into the 'Manage Booked Hire' staff UI by the operator.

    Once the application is received into the 'Manage Booked Hire' (MBH) staff UI the CHCs will be calculated and the amounts and the CRNs will be shown for each individual, at the top of the confirmation screen. The application fee and all of the CHCs will need to be receipted under the applicant's CRN therefore each CHC, except for the applicant's own, will need to be invoiced over prior to payment.

    If any individuals or associated personnel indicated that they have lived in another country for more than 12 months in the last 10 years and have been aged at least 18 years of age for any period during this time, they must provide a clearance form or criminal offence record from the relevant law enforcement authority in each country they nominate on their BEA application form.

    Note:The CHCs are exempt when the applicant or their associated personnel holds a current Driver Authorisation (DA) or has an application lodged for a DA. If an application for a DA is lodged at the same time as a BEA application, process the DA application first.

    If an application for a OA is lodged at the same time as a BEA application, process the BEA application first. If a positive CHC result is returned the TRO will share the result with the office processing the OA.
Local Nominee - Foreign Person
Local Nominee - Foreign Person
    BEA applications are opened to a 'Foreign Person' who is:
    • An individual who usually lives in a foreign country; or
    • A corporation incorporated in a foreign country (if it doesn’t have an ACN, they will need the qual FOEN applied to their CRN)

    Applicants who are a 'Foreign Person' must nominate 1 person to be its representative known as the entity's 'Local Nominee'. The Local Nominee must be an individual who usually lives in Australia or a corporation incorporated in Australia.

    Foreign Persons must provide a clearance form or criminal offence record from the relevant law enforcement authority in each country they nominate on their BEA application form.

    If the 'Foreign Person' is a corporation incorporated in a foreign country they must provide a certified copy of a Certificate of Registration of a Company and a Company Extract identifying the current directors or an equivalent document from the country the corporation is incorporated/registered in. Details of the corporation structure and the responsibility for the provision of the booking services including the organisational structure based in Australia and personal details for each director of the corporation.

    If the applicant is a foreign entity, a local nominee (an individual who usually lives in Australia or a corporation incorporated in Australia) must be nominated at the time of application and must be in place at all times during the booking entity authorisation.
Fleet Size
Fleet Size
    The CSC/Agency operator will need to add the total number of vehicles nominated on the BEA application form (indicated on the bottom or the Fleet Detail table) into the 'Manage Booked Hire' staff UI. The total fleet size is used to calculate the applicable annual fee (together with the term of the authorisation).

    The 'Fleet Details' are provided by the applicants of the booking services that they intend to provide and are made up of:
    • Each fleet category (e.g. taxis, limousines, or booked hire vehicles)
    • Each area of operation for a booking service.
    • For taxis, the applicant should nominate the relevant taxi service area.
    • For limousines and booked hire vehicles, the applicant should nominate Queensland, or if they only operate within specific areas of Queensland, the relevant area/s must be selected from the list.
    • Booking services that they provide under a subcontract arrangement for other booked hire service providers (e.g. a bureau arrangement). The details for the number of affiliated licence holders, drivers and vehicles must be for the subcontracted area.
Training Requirements
Training Requirements
    Drivers of taxis, limousines and booked hire vehicles in Queensland are required to be trained in disability awareness and anti-discrimination, including sexual harassment.Training in providing wheelchair accessible services is also required for drivers of wheelchair accessible vehicles.

    From 1 August 2019, drivers must be trained before they provide a service and authorised booking entities (BEAs) and service operators must ensure their drivers are trained prior to providing a service. Penalties may apply for drivers, BEA holders and operators.

    Compliance with training requirements will be managed via the BEA audit process therefore no details of training are required to be presented during the application process.

    For further details refer: Personalised Transport Industry - Driver Training and PT Transport Manual

Fees
Fees
    The annual fee for booking entity authorisation is based on fleet size.
    Refer: Fees and Forms > 1. Fees > Fees (Current year)

    Note:Included in the above fees is a $80 (Eligibility Assessment Fee) which is non-refundable if the application is withdrawn or unsuccessful.

BEA Transactions
Transaction details
Issue
Issue
    Checks are part of the process of assessing the eligibility for a BEA and are undertaken by the TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub.

    TRO will verify the relevant passenger transport history and criminal history of all the people listed on the application before making a decision. For example, a booking entity authorisation application can be refused if anyone named on the application has:
    • had another booking entity authorisation cancelled or suspended within the previous year
    • within the previous year, committed an offence against a provision of relevant transport legislation relating to:
      • providing a booked hire service or a booking service for a booked hire service or taxi service; or
      • using a taxi, limousine or booked hire vehicle to provide a taxi service or booked hire service.
    • has been convicted of a disqualifying offence or charged with a disqualifying offence and the charge has not been finally disposed of;
    • for a foreign person, the person does not have a proposed local nominee.
    Note:Checks undertaken for a BEA are different to those undertaken for a Driver Authorisation (DA).
Renewal
Renewal
    Renewal application must have the same primary holder (the applicant) and the same additional holders. They will retain their same Authorisation number and can be received:-
    • 6 weeks prior to expiry date of current BEA (effective date from previous expiry date)
    • up to and including 30 days after the expiry date (effective date from approval date)

    Applications for renewal that must be treated as a new application and will be issued a new BEA authorisation number, when they:-
    • Are presented or lodged online 31 days or more after the current BEA expiry date
    • Have a different applicant or additional holders from their current BEA

    When renewing the application via the Manage Booked Hire (MBH) staff UI, all Holders, nominee and associated personnel will be shown on the MBH renewal screen.
    The operator:-
    • Can add or remove the nominee and associated personnel, as required
    • Enters the fleet details and term (stated on the application form)

    Criminal History Checks (refer above) are required on renewals (unless exempt).

    Checks are part of the process of assessing the eligibility for a BEA and are undertaken by the TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub and include any relevant history (refer below).
    Relevant history
    Relevant history
      TRO will verify the relevant passenger transport history and criminal history of all the people listed on the application before making a decision. For example, a booking entity authorisation application can be refused if anyone named on the application has:
      • had another booking entity authorisation cancelled or suspended within the previous year
      • within the previous year, committed an offence against a provision of relevant transport legislation relating to:
        • providing a booked hire service or a booking service for a booked hire service or taxi service; or
        • using a taxi, limousine or booked hire vehicle to provide a taxi service or booked hire service.
      • has been convicted of a disqualifying offence or charged with a disqualifying offence and the charge has not been finally disposed of;
      • for a foreign person, the person does not have a proposed local nominee.

Deceased BEA holder
Deceased BEA holder
    • If a representative of a deceased BHSL/BEA holder presents to a CSC with proof of death, the operator must do the following (in addition to Updating the CRN as deceased):
      • Check for BEA/BHSL products
      • Email a copy of the proof of death document toTransLink BHSL/BEA Hub. TransLink will then surrender the BHSL/BEA.
    • If an operator does not notify TRO that the customer is deceased and they have BHSL/BEA products, the customer will display on a TransLink non-compliance report. In this case, TRO can check TICA and print out a copy of the Individual Customer Hub showing the deceased details and surrender the BHSL/BEA.
Surrender a BEA
Surrender a BEA
    A BEA can be surrendered, if the customer decides to voluntarily surrender their BEA for reasons such as:
    • Medical or personal reasons
    • Changes in employment
    • On behalf of deceased
    • An immediate suspension or show cause notice issued to the BEA

    Refunds
    Refunds
      Refunds will be requested by TransLink in accordance with the policy outlined in the PT Database. Once authorised, TransLink will forward the request to process the refund via email to the CSC which received the original application, or CAM if it was lodged online.

      Refer: Personalised & Passenger Transport > 1. Booked Hire Service Licence > The How To: Booked Hire Service Licence Procedures > Refund BHSL/BEA Product

    Voluntary Surrender
    Voluntary Surrender
      Advice of the surrender must be submitted in writing and include:
      • The BEA reference number
      • BEA holders CRN (primary holder/applicant)
      • Date the surrender is effective (current date or a future date)
      • Name and CRN of the person advising of the surrender

      The advice should be received from:
      • Individuals – the BEA holder/s (primary or additional holders). If the BEA holder is deceased the BEA should be surrendered by the executor of the estate, a proof of death document and a Will or Probate must be sighted.
      • Partnerships – any partner of the partnership
      • Corporations – one of the nominated directors
      • Incorporated Associations – one of the nominated executive officers who is involved in the management of the booked hire service
      • Trustee – any trustees of the trust

      Operators should email the paperwork to the TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub. Subject of email: Update existing BHSL - CRN <XXXXXXX>

      Refer: Personalised and Passenger Transport > 2. Booking Entity Authorisation Procedures > The How To: Booking Entity Authorisation > Booking Entity Authorisation Procedures > Surrender a BEA application

    Surrender due to immediate Suspension or Show Cause Notice
    Surrender due to immediate Suspension or Show Cause Notice
      If the surrender is the result of an Immediate Suspension of a Show Cause Notice the customer should contact the TRO that is stated in the notice. No further action is required from the CSC/Agency.

Signage
Signage
    A Booking Entity Authorisation must provide their signage to TMR for approval prior to distributing it to their BHSL holders. BHSL holders must then display the approved signage on their vehicle when they are providing booked hire services.

    It is not a mandatory requirement for signage to be provided with a BEA application, however if it is provided, the CSC must scan and email to the TRO along with the application/supporting documents.
