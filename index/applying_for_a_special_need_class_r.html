Applying for a Special Need Class R (123)

What it is
Class RE
Class RE
    A person who has a special need for a class RE licence but is not eligible for the licence because they have not held a class C licence for at least 12 months during the previous 5 years may be eligible to apply for a Special Need Class RE licence.
Class R
Class R
    A person who has a special need for a class R licence but is not eligible for the licence because they have not held a class RE licence for at least 2 years* during the previous 5 years may be eligible to apply for a Special Need Class R licence.

    The person must have held, a P, P1, P2 or O type licence of any class within 5 years before the day of the application.


    *Note:1 year if the class RE licence was issued before 1 October 2016

    Note:This also includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a P, P1, P2 type or an open licence.
Eligibility
Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility Criteria
    The person must be able to prove that, because of a permanent physical incapacity, the only type of motorcycle the person is able to ride is one or both of the following:
    • a 2-wheeled motorcycle with a sidecar attached to it
    • a 3-wheeled motorcycle
s38 TO(RUM-DL) Regs 2010

Note:A person granted a Special Need - Class RE or R is not eligible to apply for Special Need - Tenure. Refer to 6. Learner Licences > Learner Licence Guide > Tenure and Licence Progression > Time required to hold Learner Licence and Progression

How to ApplyThe applicant must:
  • complete an Application for Special Need—Class RE or R Licence (form F3980)
Note:The Medical Verification on form F3980 to be completed by the doctor supporting the applicant's claim that, because of the permanent incapacity, the only type of motorcycle the person is able to ride is:
  • a 2-wheeled motorcycle with a sidecar attached
  • a 3-wheeled motorcycle.
  • provide a current medical certificate (form F3712) supporting the application for the driver licence. The medical certificate (form F3712) must also state that despite the medical condition the person meets the medical criteria for a conditional licence.
Note:The medical certificate details must be recorded in TICA and an 'M' condition applied to the licence.
  • lodge the application forms with the manager of their nearest CSC.
Approval of ApplicationIn deciding whether a person has a special need for the licence, the delegated officer must consider:
  • The person's permanent incapacity has been confirmed by the doctor in writing
  • The person meets the medical criteria for a conditional licence
  • The only type of motorcycle the person is able to ride is one or both of the following:
    • a 2-wheeled motorcycle with a sidecar attached
    • a 3-wheeled motorcycle.
  • The person's traffic history
The application will be considered and written advice sent to the customer within 10 business days of all necessary information being received.

    Additional steps for class R
    Additional steps for class R
      Under normal circumstances a rider must have held a motorcycle (class RE) provisional, probationary or open licence for at least 2 years* before they can learn to ride a class R motorcycle. To overcome this requirement, a Rider's Certificate - Class R Motorcycle (form F4342) is to be issued for a class R motorcycle. See below for instructions.

      This certificate authorises the rider to learn to ride a class R motorcycle on their class RE learner, provisional, probationary or open licence.


      *Note:1 year if the class RE licence was issued before 1 October 2016

    Currency of the decision
    Currency of the decision
      The decision is not affected if the applicant subsequently fails a practical driving test. The applicant may undertake as many further driving tests as is necessary for them to pass the test.

If Refused

    Special Need - Class RE or R - Not Approved

    The application should be forwarded to Iron Mountain for filing.
Rider's Certificate

(ONLY REQUIRED FOR CLASS R)		Rider's Certificate (form F4342)
A Rider's Certificate - Class R Motorcycle (form F4342) authorises the holder of a class RE learner, provisional, probationary or open licence to learn to ride a class R motorcycle while carrying the certificate.

A Rider's Certificate is to be issued to approved applicants. The certificate only authorises the holder to learn to ride a class R motorcycle while they hold a valid class RE learner, provisional, probationary or open licence.

How to issue a Rider's Certificate
How to issue a Rider's Certificate
    A Rider's Certificate - Class R Motorcycle (form F4342) can only be issued by the Exemptions Central Processing Unit (CPU).

    To obtain a certificate, an email request is to be sent to:
    cpu.exemption@transport.qld.gov.au

    The email must contain:
    • Customer name, address and date of birth
    • CRN
    • Review/expiry date of Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (form F3712)
    • Authorising person's details.

    Exemption CPU
    Exemption CPU
      • The Exemption CPU will complete the certificate with the necessary information as provided in the email request.
        Note:The Rider's Certificate expiry date is to be the same as the review date shown on the form F3712.
      • The completed Rider's Certificate will be sent in PDF format by return email to the CSC/Agency within 2 business days.
    CSC operator
    CSC operator
      When the completed Rider's Certificate is received back in the originating CSC/Agency the authorising person should ensure:
      • the details on the certificate are correct
      • the expiry date of the Rider's Certificate is the same as the review/expiry date of the current medical certificate (form F3712).

      Once confirmed, the Rider's Certificate - Class R Motorcycle (form F4342) must be signed by the authorising person.

      When the Rider's Certificate is issued to the class RE learner, provisional, probationary or open licence holder, please ensure that the rider is advised that:
      • for the Rider's Certificate to be valid, it must also be signed and dated by the certificate holder; and
      • the Rider's Certificate only authorises the holder to learn to ride a class R motorcycle while they hold a valid class RE learner, provisional, probationary or open licence.

      If a person already holds a class RE provisional, probationary or open licence, a Rider's Certificate is only to be issued once the special need class R licence application has been approved.

      Alternatively, the Rider's Certificate may be given to the person when their special need learner licence is issued.
Conditions of Use
Conditions of Use
    The person must:
    • carry the Rider's Certificate at all times when learning to ride a class R motorcycle
    • show the Rider's Certificate to police when required to do so.

    The Rider's Certificate will not be valid if:
    • the licence holder's learner, P1, P2, P type or open licence is expired, suspended or cancelled
    • the licence holder is disqualified, by order of an Australian court, from holding or obtaining a driver licence.
