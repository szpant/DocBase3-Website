Mobile Compliance Information System (MCIS) (7195)

Project NameMobile Compliance Information System (MCIS)
WhenPilot - 22 February to 1 June 2018
Implementation date - Monday 4 June 2018
BackgroundThe objective of this project is to develop and implement the Queensland Compliance Information System application (QCIS), for use by Transport Inspectors (TI’s) on a tablet device.

The aim of this initiative is to:
  • streamline searching and data entry pertaining to intercepts
  • improve the accuracy of records
  • reduce the time taken to document intercepts

A pilot with 40+ inspectors commenced in February 2018 and provided valuable input to the project team.
Who will be impacted and how?
What does the QCIS do?
What does the QCIS do?
    The app will allow searching of the different databases and improve access to real-time information. It will also allow TI’s to create customers in TICA.

    Similar to the QPS QLite electronic infringement system, QCIS will allow TI’s to issue by email or standard mail:
    • Infringement notices
    • Weighing device notices
    • Formal warning notices
    • Property receipts,
    This will ultimately improve efficiency and the customer experience.

    Defect notices and prohibition notices will be considered for inclusion at a later stage.

    If the device does not have connectivity, the users will be able to create, store locally and queue the following items until connectivity is restored:
    • Create the driver / person off line;
    • Create the vehicle off line;
      and
    • The data recorded.

    For reporting purposes, the app will include a feature for the Transport Inspector to indicate that a hardcopy notice has been issued to the customer.

    The app will be supported by a desktop portal, which can be used by Inspectors and Managers to:
    • Edit and print records
    • Issue accountable forms
    • Add electronic media (for example, photos)
    • Create an operation and edit other manifest items
    • Cancel and reissue notices
How will this impact customers?
How will this impact customers?
    Customers will experience improved efficiency as TI’s will be able to conduct most activities on their tablet.

    When the customer is issued with a notice, they will be able to nominate to receive their notice by email by providing their email address to the Transport Inspector during the interaction, and the customer will provide their signature by signing the Transport Inspector’s iPad.

    The signatures captured through this process do not authorise the electronic issue of any other notices, and will not be stored in the staff UI. The customer’s signature will not be used by the department for any other purpose

    If the customer does not have an email address or does not want to receive the accountable form by email, the:
    • Infringement notices will be sent by standard post.
    • Weighing device notices will be created and stored in QCIS however a physical copy will not be provided to the customer unless requested.
    • Property receipts will be issued from the book, as per current operating process.
    • Formal warning notices will be issued from the book, as per current operating process.
How will this impact CSB staff?
How will this impact CSB staff?
    QCIS will streamline data entry and activity reporting through the following:
    • Allowing access to multiple information sources in real time and reducing data entry
    • Enabling forms to be issued electronically
    • Enabling inspectors to use standard tablet features such as the camera and GPS to assist with data capturing and reporting
    • Inspectors’ signatures will be loaded into the app when the account is set up.
