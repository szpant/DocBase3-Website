Getting the Correct Medical Assessment (2821)

There are two types of medical assessments that can be conducted:
  • Private; and
  • Commercial

The type of medical assessment required depends on:
  • the class of licence required for the motor vehicle to be driven:
    and/or
  • whether the motor vehicle is to be used privately or commercially (e.g. to carry public passengers, dangerous goods or a driver trainer)

The table shown below is taken from the Assessing Fitness to Drive (Commercial and Private Vehicle Drivers) publication produced by Austroads and identifies the type of medical assessment required.

Steps to Getting a Completed Medical Certificate

The following process is required:
1.The person must complete Part 1 of the Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712) and if they wish to, the person may also complete the Private and Commercial Vehicle Driver’s Health Assessment (F3195).
2.The person takes the completed form/s to their treating health professional.
3.The treating health professional must then complete Part 2 Medical Assessment of the Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712).
4.If required, Part 3 Eyesight Assessment is to be completed by an optometrist or ophthalmologist if needed.

Note:Where a person is a commercial vehicle driver (i.e. holds a class MR licence or higher or carries public passengers, dangerous goods or is a driver trainer) and there is a delay before a specialist can be seen and their treating health professional has assessed them as not meeting the criteria to hold an unconditional licence, but they may meet the criteria to hold a conditional licence, the person may be permitted to drive while they are waiting to be seen by the specialist/s if:
  • the treating health professional is able to confirm that the person has an appointment to see the relevant specialist/s at the earliest practicable opportunity;
    and
  • in the opinion of the treating health professional, the condition/s is not likely to adversely affect the person’s ability to drive safely before the assessment occurs.

Where this is the case, the treating health professional must provide the person with a medical certificate that covers the period of time required to see the appropriate specialist/s as well as attend a follow-up appointment back with the treating health professional (i.e. an interim medical certificate).

Note:If a person is applying to upgrade to a class RE driver licence and they have a current medical certificate for their class C driver licence, the licence can still be issued. However, the person should be advised that for enforcement purposes, their current medical certificate only applies to their class C drive licence and that they should return to their health professional to obtain another medical certificate that covers a class RE driver licence, before they ride a motorcycle on the road.
