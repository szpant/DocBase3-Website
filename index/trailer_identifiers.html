Rules
Unknown Date of Manufacture
Unknown Date of Manufacture
    • Manufacturer can estimate the date provided the trailer has not been modified

    If trailer has been substantially modified:
    • It is treated as a new design
    • Date of manufacture is the date of rebuild
Trailer Manufacturer
Trailer Manufacturer
    The plate must show the full name of the legal entity that manufactured the trailer.
    For an:
    • Individual - the manufacturers family name (for trailers up to and including 4.5t) or full name (for trailers over 4.5t)
    • Business or company - the business or company name

    The words "Home made" are not to be placed on the plate. Home made is a term used by TMR to describe the manufacturer in TICA only.
Imported Trailers
Imported Trailers
    All imported vehicles/trailers must have a VIN and the VIN will be displayed on the RAV or the Import Approval document.

    When registering a vehicle/trailer, if the VIN does not decode or is not on the NEVDIS master file:

    Refer customer to the Department Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development and Communications if the trailer is on the RAV.

    Or, for Non-RAV entry trailers, contact the VIU for advice and fax the form below to them.

    VIN Problem Sheet (March 2013)

    Note:No imported trailer should be issued with a LVV by a Customer Service Centre unless advised to do so by the VIU.
Missing Trailer Identifiers
Missing Trailer Identifiers
    Trailers must have an identification plate attached for registration purposes.

    If a customer presents a branded trailer (excluding caravans) that has been registered interstate and:
    • the VIN has not been been stamped onto the chassis
    • the trailer has a VIN plate attached with a legible VIN
    The trailer can be registered if the customer can present proof of origin.

    The customer must be advised that, although the trailer is registered, it won't comply with the Vehicle Standards Regulation until the number has been stamped onto the chassis.

    Note: Contact VIU for advice should a caravan be presented for inspection that doesn't have a VIN stamped onto its chassis.
