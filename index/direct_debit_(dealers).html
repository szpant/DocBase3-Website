Direct Debit (Dealers) (1973)

What it isDirect debit is a banking facility which allows TMR to automatically withdraw funds from a customer’s bank account at a predetermined time.

These withdrawals are to pay for transactions completed with TMR.
Requirements
Who Can Use it
Who Can Use it
    Direct debit is available to:
    • DAIS dealers
    • Dealers and businesses who regularly transact registration and licensing business with TMR
How Does it Work
How Does it Work
    At the close of business the total amount of the transactions performed is transferred to SAP.

    SAP system then organise the withdrawal of funds direct from the customers bank account.
Application Process
Application Process
    Customer must complete & provide:
    Customer must complete & provide:
      Direct Debit System Account Debit Authority (M3975)
      Credit Account Application (M3255)
      Current copy of Motor Dealer Licence
      Current Signatories for dealership & identification of nominated persons
      Current copy of:
      • Certificate of Registration of a Business Names
      or
      • Certificate of Registration of a Company

    Where to Lodge
    Where to Lodge
      These forms are to be lodged at the CSC where the dealer transacts business.

    CSC Check & Forward Application to CAM
    CSC Check & Forward Application to CAM
      The CSC must check that all details have been completed on the forms, and all copies of certificates are current.

      The application and all relevant paperwork must be sent to Customer Account Management (CAM)
    CAM consider application
    CAM consider application
      The application will be considered by Customer Account Management (CAM) and forwarded to (Revenue and Debt Administration Section) Financial Services.

      The originating CSC will be informed by CAM when the application has been approved.

    Financial Services apply Direct Debit Facility
    Financial Services apply Direct Debit Facility
      Revenue and Debt Administration Section, Financial Services finalise and apply the direct debit facility.

    CSC contact applicant to inform of decision
    CSC contact applicant to inform of decision
      The CSC is required to inform the customer that the Direct Debit facility has been applied.
Credit Limits
Credit Limits
    The customer is allocated a credit limit by Customer Account Management. Once this limit is reached TICA will not process any more transactions.

    To process more transaction the customer must either:
    • Clear the debt by a direct debit
    • Have TMR make an account adjustment to reduce the debt
    • Increase the credit limit

    Credit limits are set by the Customer Account Management (CAM)
Dishonoured Payments
Dishonoured Payments
    When a payment is dishonoured by the customer's financial institution, a report is generated and the credit limit and direct debit qualification is removed.

    At this time no further transactions for the dealer should be processed until the debt has been cleared, and the credit limit and direct debit qualification is reapplied by Customer Account Management.

    The organising CSC will be advised by Customer Account Management that a direct debit failure has occurred and the CSC will be required to contact the motor dealer concerned to arrange for further payment of the amount outstanding.

    This amount should be paid by cash, eftpos, credit/debit card or bank cheque/money order into the customers account. A text message must be recorded explaining what the payment relates to.

    The CSC is then required to advise Customer Account Management that the outstanding debt has been applied to the dealers account.

    Customer Account Management will then take the necessary steps to finalise the transaction in the SAP system, and re-apply the credit limit and direct debit qualification.
