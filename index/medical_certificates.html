Medical Certificates (3402)


What it isA Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Drivers (F3712) (medical certificate) is the approved form under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management – Driver Licensing) Regulation 2010 and is completed by the person’s treating health professional so that they may provide a recommendation regarding the person’s medical fitness to drive.
What it is used forA medical certificate completed by the person’s treating health professional assists the Chief Executive with determining whether a person is medically fit to hold a licence and any conditions associated with the licence.

The December 2016 version of the medical certificate may be used as the person’s agreement for TMR to amend their driver licence, provided the person has answered ‘yes’ to Question 6 in Part 1 of the medical certificate.

If a person presents the old version of the medical certificate (March 2015) after 5 December 2016, it should still be accepted as a current medical certificate as TICA will provide for both forms. If the driver licence requires amendment a Driver Licence Amendment (F4358) form is required.
When required
Medical condition
Medical condition
    Any licence holder or an applicant for a licence that:
    • has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely;
    • has a permanent or long-term increase in or other aggravation of an existing medical condition, that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely
Non-progressive medical conditions
Non-progressive medical conditions
    A non-progressive medical condition is a medical condition that is static and will not change e.g. a limb amputation. A non-progressive medical condition could also have existed at or before the person’s birth e.g. the person was born without an arm.
    Is a medical certificate required
    Is a medical certificate required
      Initial grant of a driver licence
      Initial grant of a driver licence
        Where a person is applying for the initial grant of a driver licence and it is visually obvious to the operator that the person has a congenital or non-progressive medical condition (e.g. the person is limb deficient), the operator should politely communicate their observation to the person and advise that they will be required to provide a medical certificate before a driver licence can be granted. This includes applicants for a Queensland learner licence and any person transferring a non-Queensland driver licence to a Queensland driver licence.
      Renewal of a driver licence
      Renewal of a driver licence
        Where a person is applying to renew their driver licence and it is visually obvious to the operator that the person has a congenital, childhood or non-progressive medical condition (e.g. the person is limb deficient) and the person has not previously provided a medical certificate, there is no requirement to now provide a medical certificate. A medical certificate is only required for the initial grant of a driver licence and not each renewal thereafter.

        The person may have developed coping mechanisms and therefore, their ability to drive safely may not necessarily be adversely affected. In many cases the congenital or non-progressive medical condition may have been present when the person passed their practical driving test.

    Unconditional or conditional licence
    Unconditional or conditional licence
      Unconditional driver licence
      Unconditional driver licence
        The person is eligible for an unconditional driver licence if the health professional has not recommended any conditions or restrictions on the medical certificate.
      Conditional driver licence
      Conditional driver licence
        The person is eligible for a conditional driver licence if the health professional has recommended conditions or restrictions that the person must drive in accordance with. For example, the person must only drive a modified vehicle or a vehicle fitted with an automatic transmission.

        Conditions or restrictions stated on the person's medical certificate should be enforceable by the Queensland Police Service.

        Note:Refer conditions or restrictions must be enforceable.

        Where the health professional has indicated on the medical certificate that the medical condition is non-progressive, the person is not required to provide any further medical certificates in relation to that condition.

        If the health professional has recommended a modified vehicle, a V condition should be added to the person’s driver licence. An M is not shown on the person’s driver licence unless the person has another permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely. Refer below: Modified Vehicle

        Modified Vehicle
        Modified Vehicle
          If the health professional has recommended a modified vehicle, a V condition should be added to the person’s driver licence. The customer must be given a Licence Condition Code V Notice F4843. The customer is required to carry the Licence Condition Code V Notice F4843 while driving.
          If the customer has a non-progressive condition Refer: Non-progressive medical conditions\Conditional Licence


      New medical conditions that are not congenital, childhood or non-progressive
      New medical conditions that are not congenital, childhood or non-progressive
        If in addition to the person’s congenital, childhood or non-progressive medical condition, the person develops a new permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, a new medical certificate is required.

        An M condition may be shown on the person’s driver licence. If added the person’s medical condition will be subject to periodic review.
    75 years or older
    75 years or older
      Any person 75 years or older who holds a licence, must carry a current medical certificate at all times when driving.
    When a medical certificate must be presented to TMR
    When a medical certificate must be presented to TMR
      A medical certificate must be presented to TMR if the licence holder, or an applicant for a licence, has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely.

      A person 75 years or older is required to present a current medical certificate to TMR when:
      • They apply for, or renew their licence and they do not have a current medical certificate recorded on TICA or
      • They obtain a new medical certificate that requires a condition to be added to or removed from their licence.
      Refer: Driver Licence > 4. Medical Condition Reporting > Medical Condition Reporting Guide > Notifying TMR and actions to be taken > How to notify to view the options for submitting a medical certificate

      Note:Licence holders who will turn 75 on their next birthday are sent a Medical Certificate Notice (F3024) six weeks prior to their 75th birthday advising of the requirement to carry a current medical certificate at all times when driving.
    When a medical certificate is required to be carried
    When a medical certificate is required to be carried
      A licence holder must carry a current medical certificate and comply with any stated conditions at all times when driving, if the licence holder:
      • has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, and their licence has been amended to show an ‘M’ or 'V' condition;
        or
      • is 75 years or older.
    Medical certificate expiry dateA medical certificate expires at midnight on the date specified on the medical certificate.


    Medical certificate expiry date - 75 years or older
    Medical certificate expiry date - 75 years or older
      A medical certificate issued to a person who is 75 years or older is valid for a maximum period of 13 months from the date of issue.

      If a person 75 years or older presents a medical certificate that has been issued for more than 13 months to TMR, the medical certificate must be recorded with an expiry date of 13 months from the date of issue. The person must be advised that they must not drive using this certificate for longer than 13 months from the issue date and that they must go back to their treating health professional every 13 months for a new certificate.
    Conditions or restrictions must be enforceableAny conditions or restrictions stated by a health professional on a person's medical certificate should be enforceable by the Queensland Police Service (QPS).

    Examples of conditions or restrictions that can be enforced by the QPS:
    • must not drive on roads where the speed limit is more than 60km/h
    • must not drive on highways or freeways
    • must not drive at night
    • must only drive during daylight hours

    Examples of conditions or restrictions that cannot be enforced by the QPS:
    • must take medication
    • may only drive in their local area
    • must not drive when feeling unwell

    If the operator determines that the conditions or restrictions stated by the health professional on the person's medical certificate can't be enforced by QPS they must follow the procedure for incomplete and/or incorrect medical certificates.

    Note: Refer below: Incomplete and/or Incorrect Medical Certificates
    When the health professional must be contactedWhere a person’s most recent driver licence has been cancelled/downgraded/surrendered on medical grounds, or where there is a current show cause process being undertaken on medical grounds, the health professional must be contacted to ensure the health professional was aware of this action.

    Every medical certificate presented to TMR must be checked in TICA to identify if any of the following apply:
    • The medical certificate is presented in response to a current show cause action
    • The customer’s most recent driver licence was cancelled on medical grounds
    • The customer’s driver licence has been downgraded on medical grounds and the licence hasn’t been upgraded since that transaction.
    • The customer's most recent driver licence was surrendered on medical grounds

    Where any of the above apply, additional action is required.

    Actioning medical certificate
    Actioning medical certificate
      Current show cause action
      Current show cause action
        If the medical certificate supports the proposed show cause action, or relates only to adding or removing an S condition, there is no requirement to contact the health professional.

        Where the medical certificate does not support the proposed action the operator must contact the MCR Unit unless there is a TEXT on the customer record:
        • from the MCR Unit advising that the certificate can be accepted
        • showing a refused medical certificate and the medical certificate being presented is from the SAME health professional who issued the refused medical.

        Note:Contact the show cause action location if you are accepting the medical certificate and finalising the show cause action.

        If the health professional must be contacted but is not available e.g. they are on extended leave:
        • Do not finalise the show cause action.
        • Email the medical certificate to the MCR Unit. The MCR Unit will be responsible for making further reasonable attempts to contact the health professional and any subsequent action that may be required.
        • Advise customer they will be contacted within 5 business days.

        Note:The MCR Unit should consider all of the information that is available to them and determine on a case by case basis what further attempts to contact the health professional is reasonable.
      Cancelled on medical grounds
      Cancelled on medical grounds
        Where the customer’s most recent driver licence has been show caused cancelled on medical grounds:
        • Do not re-issue the licence.
        • Email the medical certificate and application to the MCR Unit. The MCR Unit will be responsible for contacting the health professional and any subsequent action that may be required.
        • Advise customer they will be contacted within 5 business days.

        Refer: Driver Licence > 4. Medical Condition Reporting > The How To: Medical Condition Reporting > Medical Condition Reporting Procedures > Show Cause > Re-Issue After a Medical Show Cause Cancellation
      Downgraded on medical grounds
      Downgraded on medical grounds
        Where the customer’s driver licence has been downgraded on medical grounds and the licence has not been upgraded since that transaction, the operator must contact the MCR Unit unless there is a TEXT on the customer record:
        • from the MCR Unit advising that the application can be accepted
        • showing a refused medical and the medical certificate being presented is from the SAME health professional who issued the refused medical.

        If the health professional must be contacted but is not available e.g. they are on extended leave:
        • Issue the upgrade if all other requirements have been met.
        • Email the medical certificate and application to the MCR Unit. The MCR Unit will be responsible for contacting the health professional and any subsequent action that may be required.
        • Advise customer they will be contacted within 5 business days.

        Refer: Driver Licence > 4.       Surrendered on medical grounds
      Surrendered on medical grounds
        Where the customer's most recent driver licence has been surrendered for medical reasons, the operator must contact the MCR Unit unless there is a TEXT on the customer record:
        • from the MCR Unit advising that the certificate can be accepted.
        • showing a refused medical certificate and the medical certificate being presented is from the SAME health professional who issued the refused medical.

        If the health professional must be contacted but is not available e.g. they are on extended leave:
        • Do not issue the licence
        • Email the medical certificate and application to the MCR Unit. The MCR Unit will be responsible for contacting the health professional and any subsequent action that may be required.
        • Advise customer they will be contacted within 5 business days.
      Customer with NOLR restriction
      Customer with NOLR restriction
        If a customer with a NOLR presents with a medical certificate, it must be referred to the MCR unit for clearance and removal prior to renewing the driver licence.
    Contacting the health professional
    Contacting the health professional
      Where the CSC is required to contact the health professional, the CSO must confirm if the health professional was aware of the show cause action when preparing the medical certificate.

      If the health professional was not aware, the CSO must confirm whether this information changes the health professional’s recommendation regarding the person’s medical fitness to drive and whether the person requires further medical assessment. The health professional must be given the option of rescinding the medical certificate that they issued to the person.

      Note:The health Professional must not be given any information about the customer’s medical condition or the circumstances associated with the current show cause process or the previous cancellation/downgrade of the person’s driver licence.
      Actioning the health professional advice
      Actioning the health professional advice
        Health professional does not change recommendation
        Health professional does not change recommendation
          If the health professional advises that there is no change to their recommendation regarding the person’s medical fitness to drive, the operator must record the contact with the health professional using Report for File or the Health professional contact summary and include it with their daily filing.
        Health professional changes recommendation
        Health professional changes recommendation
          If the health professional advises that as a result of this information, there is a change to their recommendation regarding the person’s medical fitness to drive they must consent to the CSO amending the medical certificate.

          The operator must record the contact with the health professional using a Report for File or the Health professional contact summary and include it with their daily filing.
        Health professional agrees to amendment of the medical certificate
        Health professional agrees to amendment of the medical certificate
          Where the health professional agrees to the amendment of the medical certificate, the operator should amend the certificate as instructed and initial the changes.

          The operator must record the contact with the health professional using a Report for File or the Health professional contact summary and include it with their daily filing.
        Health professional does not agree to amendment of the medical certificate
        Health professional does not agree to amendment of the medical certificate
          If the health professional does not agree to the amendment of the medical certificate, the CSO must ask the health professional if it is necessary to take action to immediately suspend or amend the person’s driver licence pending the outcome of further medical assessment.

    When to record a medical certificateIf a medical certificate is presented to TMR it must be recorded on TICA. Where the medical certificate indicates the person meets the criteria for a conditional licence, any conditions or restrictions specified on the medical certificate must be recorded as Additional Information on the MCR Hub, Add New Report screen.

    Where a medical certificate has been recorded on TICA, the person will be sent a     Medical Certificate Notice (F3024) six weeks prior to the recorded expiry date reminding them that they must go back to their treating health professional for a new certificate.
    Incomplete and/or incorrect medical certificatesSome examples of incomplete or incorrect information includes:
    • The health professional has not provided a recommendation regarding the person’s medical fitness to drive (they have not completed Question 5)
    • The health professional has stated a condition or restriction that is not enforceable by the Queensland Police Service.
    • The expiry date of the medical certificate does not align with the AFTD
    • The health professional has indicated a conditional licence and/or has also ticked “other stated ‘recommended’ conditions”, but has not provided any conditions
    • The health professional has indicated a modified vehicle, but has not provided any details about the vehicle modifications.
    • The customer has presented an occupational therapist's (OT) driving assessment. TMR does not accept OT assessments, the customer must return to their doctor and obtain a Medical Certificate. Refer below: Occupational therapists reports.

    Note:If the health professional hasn’t provided a recommendation in Part 2 of the medical certificate about whether the person needs to wear glasses or contact lenses when driving, the operator should refer to Part 3 of the medical certificate for this information. If this information is not available in Part 3, or the optometrist/ophthalmologist has not signed Part 3 of the medical certificate, the medical certificate is incomplete and the person’s health professional must be contacted.

    If the health professional is able to provide the required information via the phone including revised conditions or restrictions, amend the medical certificate if required and initial all of the amendments. The outcome of the discussion with the health professional must be recorded using a Report for file or the Health professional contact summary and include it with the daily filing.

    HP Contact Summary - Incorrect or Incomplete Medical Certificate (New)
    HP Contact Summary - Incorrect or Incomplete Medical Certificate (Old)

    If the health professional is unable to resolve the issue via the phone, for example the health professional assessed the person against the private standard instead of the commercial standard, or the health professional is not available, advise the person we cannot accept the medical certificate. Mark the medical certificate Part 2 with a diagonal line, write "Refused", apply a TMR date stamp and write user ID. Return the medical certificate to the customer. TMR does not retain the original or a copy of the medical certificate.

    Record the following customer text:
    Med cert issued by DR XXXXXXX dated XX/XX/XX refused, (description of error)

    If the health professional disagrees that the conditions or restrictions stated on the medical certificate are not enforceable by the Queensland Police Service, accept the medical certificate and record the discussion with the health professional using a Report for file or the health professional contact summary and include it with the daily filing.
    Occupational Therapists reportsCustomers may be referred to an occupational therapist by their doctor to have a driving assessment to help assess their fitness to drive. The results should be returned to the treating doctor and only the Medical Certificate, completed by the treating doctor, presented to TMR.

    A licence may be issued if a Medical Certificate is produced and the doctor has stated the person is fit to drive with a restriction of driving only for the purpose of undergoing an occupational therapist assessment. The person must only drive in accordance with the restriction.
