Client Aggression (7267)

What it is
The term “Customer Misbehaviour” refers to the spectrum of negative acts perpetrated by customers and directed at employees, ranging from incivility (lacking in good manners, rudeness, threatening and anti-social behaviour), to verbal abuse, and escalation to physical aggression and violence.

Work-related Violence (also known as client aggression) is defined as any incident where a person is abused, intimidated, threatened or assaulted at work. This definition covers a broad range of actions and behaviours that can create a risk to the health and safety of workers.

The actions and behaviours may occur inside or outside the workplace and during or outside normal working hours.

Examples of work-related violence include:

  • Physical intimidation or assault such as biting, spitting, scratching, hitting, kicking, pushing, shoving, tripping, grabbing, throwing objects or using or threatening to use a weapon.
  • Verbal intimidation (including the threat to spread a virus or infection) or assault such as making threats (including threats of physical intimidation as outlined above), yelling, screaming or using inappropriate language
  • Serious crimes such as armed robbery and sexual harassment or assault
  • Any other form of violence or attempt at intimidation
Actions to be taken
For all instances of work-related violence, the following actions are to be taken:
  • Try to remain calm
  • If the customer is displaying aggressive behaviour, politely advise them that this kind of behaviour will not be tolerated and may result in the transaction/phone call being terminated and that they may be required to leave the centre, or the call may be terminated.
  • If the customer does not calm down, remove yourself from the situation and seek the support of your manager/supervisor.
  • If the behaviour escalates and the customer persists with the aggressive behaviour and refuses to leave a centre, you can activate the duress alarm and/or escalate to Qld Police by calling Triple 000, if an emergency response is required.
  • Refer below: Reporting an incident and future interactions.
Physical work-related violence
Physical work-related violence
    If the customer becomes physically aggressive before the Police arrive:
    • Remove yourself from the situation and retreat to a place of safety. Consider the safety of all customers in the centre and whether they should also be asked to leave or shelter in place.
    • Try to remain calm
    • You can activate the duress alarm and/or escalate to Qld Police by calling Triple 000 if an emergency response is required.
    • Request all witnesses to remain in a place of safety until either QPS arrive or the situation has ended. If members of the public are unwilling to stay, request their contact details for referral to QPS. Members of the public may not wish to provide this information, as is their right.

    If the perpetrator has left before Qld Police arrives, assess the situation and what action is required to keep staff and members of the public safe, including whether to close the centre. It may be appropriate to consult with the Manager (Client Service Delivery) and/or Regional Director prior to making this decision.
Reporting an incident and future interactions
Reporting an incident and future interactions
    Once the event is over:
    • Brief the event to the PCM and others as appropriate.
    • Lodge an incident report with the WHS Connect Hotline
    • Consider placing a text message (include an expiry date if appropriate) against the customer's CRN to warn other CSC staff encase the customer immediately attends another CSC, stating:

    "Customer has displayed aggressive behaviour at <insert CSC name> on <insert date>"

    Customers displaying aggressive behaviour may also be issued with a letter that sets our expectations for their future interactions with TMR. In this instance, the Manager/SASM should place a text should be placed against the customer's CRN stating:

    "Customer has been issued with a Tier <insert level> letter as per CSB's Client Aggression Procedure, dated <enter date>"

Roles and Responsibilities
Managers/Supervisors
Managers/Supervisors
    • Ensure staff affected by work-related violence are supported and immediately removed from any risk of injury.
    • Report all work-related violence incidents to the WHS Connect Hotline.
    • Ensure all staff have completed the mandatory Managing Client Aggression Accelerate training and other related training and toolbox talks. Have regular conversations with your team abut how you would respond under different scenarios.
    • Ensure all staff are provided with the contact details for TMR's Employee Assistance Program provider (Benestar) and all other services such as medical attention, temporary relocation from normal duties and so on. Consider whether critical incident debriefing is required for staff.
    • Provide timely briefs to the appropriate level of management on serious incidents
    • Ensure issues of work-related violence are discussed in staff meetings and other appropriate forums as "lessons learned".
    • Conduct regular reviews of issues that may impact on work-related violence level (for example work practices, staffing levels, waiting times and so on).
    • Check that office accommodation is appropriate, for example security doors, duress alarms, signage and design of public waiting areas.
    • Contact TMR Internal Security if a security assessment of the site required.
Staff
Staff
    • Attempt to avoid or de-escalate work-related violence where possible.
    • Immediately report the circumstances of any client aggression to the supervisor to allow appropriate follow up action.
    • Report instances of work-related violence to the WHS Connect Hotline
    • Raise any concerns they may have with their manager/supervisor if they are assigned to a work situation where they feel they may not have enough expertise to deal with client expectations.
    • Actively participate in any client aggression and security awareness training.
