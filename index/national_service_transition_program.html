National Service Transition Program (7650)


Project NameNational Services Transition (NST) Program
WhenNST process begins in June 2021 with possible implementation by the second half of 2023
BackgroundThe NHVR is responsible for administering the Heavy Vehicle National Law (HVNL) across Australia, except for Western Australia and the Northern Territory. Participating jurisdictions have been working closely with the NHVR for many years through service agreements and the NST program to streamline, coordinate and improve regulatory services for the heavy vehicle industry. Queensland will be the last participating jurisdiction to undertake this journey with the NHVR.

The NST program will start in July 2021 with the due diligence information gathering process. This will allow ​​​both TMR and the NHVR to understand our existing operations and processes, and to identify opportunities for future service delivery models that will provide the best outcomes for our staff, the heavy vehicle industry and the Queensland community. It is not until this due diligence process is completed and a proposal for the future – a concept of operations outlining the best approach for Queensland – is developed that any decisions will be made.

The earliest a transition plan for Queensland could be implemented would be in the second half of 2023, but there are many stages to work through that can impact on this indicative timeframe. ​​

There are 4 phases in the proposed transition:

    1. Due Diligence
    2. Development of the Concept of Operations
    3. Approval of the Concept of Operations
    4. Transition Planning and Implementation

The Due Diligence phase allows both TMR and the NHVR to fully understand our existing operations and processes, and to identify opportunities for future service delivery models that will provide the best outcomes for our staff, the heavy vehicle industry and the Queensland community. It is not until this due diligence process is completed and a proposal for the future—a concept of operations outlining the best approach for Queensland—is developed that any decisions will be made.

Collaboratively reviewing, redesigning and implementing a new service delivery model across two organisations is a complex process and the experience of other transitioned states and territories confirms the entire project takes around two to three years.
What does this mean for you?You may be asked to participate in the due diligence process and contribute to information gathering activities where relevant.

Employment security for our permanent employees is a priority for the Queensland Government. The NHVR and TMR are both committed to ensuring that any staff transferred under potential future arrangements would receive pay and conditions that are equivalent to or better than those they currently enjoy.

At this early stage there is little detailed information available about potential future arrangements. As we work through the process and more information becomes available it will be shared with you as a priority to allow you to make informed decisions about your future. As with any major organisational change, the normal consultative processes with the relevant union and employees will be undertaken.
Further informationOnce the NST process begins, the NST project team will be engaging with staff regularly to provide as much information as we can and gain your input along the way.

Staff are encouraged to access information from the following sources:
  • The NST SharePoint site has been set up as your main source of reliable and verified information for the duration of the process. Here you will find regular updates, answers to frequently asked questions, as well as the ability to post a new question or fact-check a rumour.
  • Project Updates in DocBase will be updated as information becomes available throughout the process.
  • Your manager to answer questions and seek clarification from the NST project team. They can talk to you about your specific circumstances, pass on your feedback and support to you.
