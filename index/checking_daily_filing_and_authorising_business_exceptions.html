Checking Daily Filing, Authorising Business Exceptions and Error Follow-Up (6048)


Roles and Responsibilities
Processing Officer
Processing Officer
    • At the end of the day prepare filing.
      Refer: Admin > 5. Filing and Business Exceptions > Document Filing > Preparing Filing
    • Next day, while not required, CSO’s may choose to check their own filing before it goes to the Checking Officer for review.
    • Follow-ups are to be actioned by the CSO within 7 days of the error being recorded in the register.
    • Ensure the Follow-up Register is kept up to date for any follow actions assigned to them.
    • Advise the PCSO/SASM when an error follow-up action has been completed.
    • Solo Sites (other than QPS and QGAP) that have any TRAILS Reports that require Authorisation from a second officer must scan and email these to their supporting Centre.
Checking Officer
Checking Officer
    Filing
    Filing
      • Obtain the Document Filing Report
      • Check the Document Filing Report and transactional documents according to the Guidelines for Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions
      • Any Solo Site authorisations must be checked same day, authorised and returned to the Solo Site.
      Note:A Checking Officer cannot check their own transactions.

    Business Exceptions
    Business Exceptions
      • Check TICA Business Exceptions according to the Guidelines for Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions.
      • Authorise approved BEs.
      • Authorise BEs when the follow-up actions are completed.
      • Any Solo Site authorisations must be checked same day, authorised and returned to the Solo Site.
    Errors and Follow-up
    Errors and Follow-up
      • Add filing errors in the Follow-up Register.
      • Record Follow-up Register reference number on front of filing documents and on the Document Filing Report.
      • Where the error is associated with a BE, record the Follow-up Register reference number as text against the BE.
      • Scan required documents to the Follow-up Register. Original filing is sent to Iron Mountain.
      • Assign follow-up actions.*
      • Where follow-up has been added incorrectly - for example: missing paperwork located in another operators filing on the same day - Checking officer is to refer follow-up record to SA(SM).
      • Finalise actions in the Follow-up Register.
      • Any Solo Site follow ups must be created and noted on the report then returned to the Solo Site.

      Note:*The process for allocating follow-up tasks is determined by individual CSC management.
      Where the processing CSO is not undertaking the follow-up action advise CSO of the error for training purposes.

    Administration
    Administration
      • Complete and sign the daily Document Filing Reports.
      • Bundle filing into products.
      • Check all operator filing is present.
      • Prepare the TICA/TRAILS filing envelope for each product type.
      • Ensure any Solo Site reports are returned to the Solo Site for retention and filing.
      • Send filing to Iron Mountain as per CSC schedule.
SASM/Manager
SASM/Manager
    • Ensure filing is securely stored.
    • Ensure the Follow-up Register is accurately maintained.
    • Review any requests for follow-up records to be removed from Follow-up Register. When a record needs to be removed the SA(SM) is to email HelpDirect@tmr.qld.gov.au with details of reason for deletion.
    • Ensure the Follow-up actions are progressed as per the procedures or as otherwise approved.
    • Regularly review the Follow-up Register to identify training needs.
    • Review Solo Site authorisations have been processed as per procedure and reports are available for review by visiting manager.
CSC Supporting a non-TMR led Agent
CSC Supporting a non-TMR led Agent
    The nominated supporting CSC is responsible for checking the agents filing, recording errors in the error Follow-up Register, all error follow-up actions and preparing and sending filing to Iron Mountain.
    Filing
    Filing
      • Print the agents Document Filing Report
      • Check the Document Filing Report and transactional documents according to the Guidelines for Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions
    Business Exceptions
    Business Exceptions
      • Check the agents TICA Business Exceptions according to the Guidelines for Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions
      • Authorise the agents BEs.
    Transactions with errors
    Transactions with errors
      Error follow-up is managed by the supporting CSC according to the Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions (6048).

      Agency errors must be recorded in the Follow-up Register under the agent location. All agency errors entered into the Follow-up Register will be marked as unprocessed unless the "Has this agency transaction been processed" box is checked.
      Online agencies
      Online agencies
        CSC records agency errors in the Follow-up Register under the agency location. Filing is sent to Iron Mountain.
      Offline agencies
      Offline agencies
        Transaction able to be processed
        Transaction able to be processed
          The supporting CSC should process the transaction if there is sufficient information to allow the transaction to be processed. Any errors are then recorded in the Follow-up Register under the agency location. Filing is sent to Iron Mountain.
        Transaction not able to be processed
        Transaction not able to be processed
          If the transaction cannot be processed because there is missing or isnufficient information, record the error in the Follow-up Register under agency location. Filing is retained in the CSC until the transaction is processed.

          When the error is resolved and submitted for authorisation in the Follow-up Register, the email sent to the authorising officer will indicate it is an unprocessed transaction.

    Checking Filing and Business Exceptions
    Filing and business exception should be checked in accordance with the Guidelines for Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions
    Error Follow-up Managing the error follow-up process:
    Error follow-up procedures
    Error follow-up procedures
      Errors identified when reviewing daily filing and business exceptions should be resolved using the Error Correction procedures.

      Refer: Admin > 5. Filing and Business Exceptions > The How To: Filing and Business Exceptions > Filing and Business Exceptions > Error Correction Procedures
    Follow-up Register
    Follow-up Register
      The Follow-up Register must be used to record errors identified through the filing review or BE authorisation process.
      To remove a follow-up record the SA(SM) is to email HelpDirect@tmr.qld.gov.au with details of reason for deletion
      Follow-up Register reference number
      Follow-up Register reference number
        Each error recorded in the Follow-up Register will be assigned a unique reference number.
        Recording the Follow-up Register reference number
        Recording the Follow-up Register reference number
          The follow-up reference number is recorded:
          • on the front of the original filing documents sent to Iron Mountain
          • as the reference number on letters and/or emails sent to customers
          • on the Document Filing Report
          • as text against the business exception (if applicable)
          • in any error related customer texts recorded in TICA

    Access to the Follow-up Register
    Access to the Follow-up Register
      • All staff have access to the Follow-up Register to view and update register items.
      • Only officers approved by the CSC Manager are permitted to add and finalise register items, and extract reports.
      • Only SA(SM) can request removal of follow-up records added to the register.
      Note:The Accessing Customer Records policy applies to the Follow-up Register.
      Staff should only be accessing the register when necessary to perform their role.
      SharePoint logs all access and activity in the Follow-up Register.
    Updating the register
    Updating the register
      Assigned action officers must update the Follow-up Register to record actions related to their assigned error, including:
      • Attempted customer contact, including method of contact and date
      • A brief summary of customer contact including UserId of operator who contacted the customer
      • Upload letters and emails sent to customers, IMU or other parties as relevant
      • Help Direct will action any requests from SA(SM) for removal of follow-up records from the register
    Contacting customers to correct errors
    Contacting customers to correct errors
      Methods for contacting customers, in the preferred order:
      1. Telephone
      2. Email
      3. Fax
      4. Post

      Note:If the error relates to a high risk transaction any letters posted to the customer must be mailed using the Priority mail service.
      Using email or fax for missing signatures and incomplete documents
      Using email or fax for missing signatures and incomplete documents
        For the purposes of error follow-up, letters, and documents that require signatures or other information, should be sent to the customer by email if an email address is available.

        Customers may provide a signature or produce a document in electronic form (that is via email or fax).

        Where a customer chooses to return or provide a document in electronic form the quality of the document image must be sufficient to allow the checking officer to reasonably determine the authenticity of the document.

        All amendments and/or additions to a form must be initialled by the customer.
    Call Centre Contacting CSCs to correct errors
    Call Centre Contacting CSCs to correct errors
      Where the Call Centre is unable to resolve an identified issue that requires escalation back to the processing CSC, an email will be sent to that CSC for action and follow-up.

      Emails will be sent directly to the CSC office email address for the following identified common issues:
      • incorrect registration transfer;
      • vehicle details incorrectly recorded or not updated from SC/COI;
      • customer EOI/e-contact details incorrectly recorded or missing;
      • customer details not recorded/not updated/updated incorrectly (for example; address change, email address, phone number);
      • non-Qld product information supplied by customer but product not recorded or incomplete (in particular, overseas driver licences);
      • practical test details not recorded (pass/fail/other);
      • disability parking paperwork not emailed/emailed with incorrect subject line/missing pages;
      • other (such as, customer passing on a compliment for CSC)

      Customer's will be informed by Call Centre Consultants that the CSC will be in contact with the customer within 24 hours.

      Customer Issue Follow-Up Required by CSC
    Assessing risk
    Assessing risk
      If a form, or a question on a form is used to determine a customers' eligibility for product, or to establish/amend their identity, it should typically be treated as a high risk.

      Note:We should not be pursuing completeness for completeness sake. Given the inconvenience to the customer we should only pursue missing information or documents when there is a risk to TMR. Your SASM/Manager can assist with assessing the organisational risk.

      If the SASM/Manager uses their discretion to take no action, the error must still be recorded in the Follow-up Register and the reason for no action recorded.
      Missing Signatures
      Missing Signatures
        For missing signatures on history detail reports or driver licence receipts at formless centres Refer below: Missing/incomplete information
        High risk
        High risk
          If a form is used to determine a customers' eligibility for product, or to establish/amend their identity, a missing signature should be treated as a high risk error.

          For example
          Where the signature is missing from a product application form, such as Driver Licence Application/Renewal (F3000), Recreational Marine Driver Licence Application (F2334), Driver Authorisation Application (F2978), Vehicle Registration Application (F3518), the signature is very important as the customer is declaring the information provided on the form, and which we have used to make our decision to issue the product, is true and correct. Without making this declaration the customer is not eligible for the product.

          For forms that serve a number of different purposes, such as Change Customer Details (F4214), it will depend on what the form is being used for. For example if it is being used to change the customer's name then the missing signature would be a high risk error. If the form is being used to add a postal address, it is not a high risk error.

          Other examples of forms where the missing signature would be high risk:
          • Licence and accreditation application forms
          • Vehicle and Vessel Registration forms
          • Smartcard replacement forms
          • Customer Identity Declaration (F2031)
          • New Customer Application (F3503)
        Not high risk
        Not high risk
          Where the item is not high risk, SASMs/Managers have discretion to decide the appropriate response.

          Signatures missing from forms related to concession or fee exemption, change of vehicle make/model/year of manufacture, should be followed-up using the not high risk procedure.

          Where a form is not critical to the product or fees such as completion of a Change of Customer Details (F4214) to add a postal address, or a Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification (F3524) form to change vehicle colour, a decision may be made to not to pursue the missing signature.

          If the SASM/Manager uses their discretion to take no action, the error must still be recorded in the Follow-up Register and the reason for no action recorded.
      Missing/incomplete information
      Missing/incomplete information
        Formless centre - missing signatures/history detail report or driver licence receipt
        Formless centre - missing signatures/history detail report or driver licence receipt
          The below process should be followed when an operator at a formless centre fails to:
          • retain a copy of the driver licence receipt (for driver licence replacements only)
          • retain a copy of the history detail report (for all other eligible transactions)
          • have the customer sign the required history detail report or driver licence receipt

          The error should be recorded in the Follow-up Register and then immediately finalised. The operator should be made aware of their error and advised to be more careful in the future.

          Data from the Follow-up Register can be used in performance discussions with staff.

        High Risk
        High Risk
          If a missing document is used to determine a customers' eligibility for a product, including eligibility for exemptions, or to establish/amend their identity, it should be treated as a high risk error.

          It is high risk because we have made a decision to issue a product or create/amend an identity without complete information. The customer may therefore not be entitled to hold a licence, the vehicle may not be safe to be on the road or we may have created a false identity.

          We have grounds to show cause the product if full requirements have not been met.
        Not high risk
        Not high risk
          Where the item is not high risk, SASMs/Managers have discretion to decide the appropriate response.

          Items such as proof of entitlement to a concession or fee exemption, change of vehicle make/model/year of manufacture should be followed-up using the not high risk procedure.

          Where the information is not critical to the product a decision may be made to not pursue the missing information.

          If the SASM/Manager uses their discretion to take no action, the error must still be recorded in the Follow-up Register and the reason for no action recorded.

    SASM / Manager Discretion
    SASM / Manager Discretion
      For any transaction NOT associated with a high risk business exception, the SASM/Manager has discretion to allow the follow-up procedures or time frames to be varied to suit business and customer needs.

      Where a different procedure or time frame is applied the reason for the change and the amended processes must be recorded in the Follow-up Register.

      The Error follow-up procedures should be adhered to for high risk business exceptions.
    Multiple errors
    Multiple errors
      If there are multiple errors with a transaction, each error must be recorded as a separate error in the Follow-up Register.

      If one of the errors is associated with a BE, the BE should be authorised once the follow-up action associated the BE is completed, even though other errors for the transaction may still be outstanding.

    Finalising follow-up action
    Finalising follow-up action
      When the follow-up action is completed:
      • Upload letters and emails to the Follow-up Register
      • Finalise the action in the Follow-up Register
      • Authorise the BE as applicable
      • Send file notes, additional or amended documents to Iron Mountain as additional filing

      If show cause action is commenced, filing associated with the show cause action should be filed in accordance with show cause requirements.
    Authorising Business Exceptions (BE)
    Authorising Business Exceptions (BE)
      Where follow-up action is required for a BE, a text must be added to the BE to record the Follow-up Register reference number.

      BEs are authorised when the follow-up action associated with the BE is finalised
