Seating Capacity
Seating Capacity
    • Is the number of adult seats in the vehicle, including the driver.
    • Used to determine CTP Class.
    Location
    Location
      Recorded on either the:
      • Identification plate
      • Manufacturers plate
    Mopeds/Motorcycle
    Mopeds/Motorcycle
      For a motorcycle/moped, the seating capacity is not shown on the identification (compliance) plate.

      The seating capacity can be taken from either a:
      • safety certificate issued for the motorcycle/moped; or
      • Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification (F3524) form; ensuring the applicable checkbox for motorcycle/moped seating capacity is selected in Section B of the form.

      The following rules determine what the seating capacity of motorcycle/moped is:

      Configuration
      Seating Capacity
      Seat is less than or equal to 500mm (50cm) in length and with no pillion foot pegs.
      1 seat
      Seat is greater than 500mm (50cm) in length with no pillion foot pegs and manufactured from new as a single seat.
      1 seat
      Seat is greater than 500mm (50cm) in length or pillion foot pegs are fitted.
      2 seats

      Application can be lodged In person or by Mail or Electronic lodgement with supporting documentation and CTP payment confirmation. Refer: Update Trails/TICA Record


      Notes:
      • When measuring the length of the seat, it is the upholstered part of the seat that is measured. Refer to the attachment below for examples of upholstered motorcycle/moped seats.
      Motorcycle_Moped Seating Examples
      • Pillion foot pegs are any part of the motorbike which is designed or intended for a pillion to place their feet.
      • For some two seat mopeds and scooters, the rear part of the vehicle’s running board (floor) is designed to act as the pillion foot pegs. When these vehicles are being converted to single seat vehicles, there is no need for the running boards to be shortened (the running boards are the structural part of the moped / scooter, so cutting this away could make the vehicle unsafe). In this case, provided the seat has been shortened so it is not more than 500mm, it can be classed as a single seat vehicle.

    Buses
    Buses
      Seating capacity:
      • Is displayed as an option in buses manufactured before October 1989.
      • Should not be displayed on the identification plate in buses manufactured after October 1989.

      A bus may have no seating capacity displayed but it must be recorded in TICA. The CSO may need to count or measure the seats. One bus seat equals 410mm.
    Additional Seating
    Additional Seating
      There are three classes of additional seats that can be installed:
      • Class 1, Adults
      • Class 2, Children up to 12 years of age
      • Class 3, Children up to the age of 8

      Adults must not be seated in child seats that are installed in the luggage compartment due to the lack of head and foot room.

      If additional class 2 or 3 seats are added the identification plate will show eg 6 + 3 meaning 6 adult seats and 3 child seats. The seating capacity is recorded as 6 in TICA.
      Wheelchair seating
      Wheelchair seating
        Vehicles that have been modified to accommodate wheelchair seating will have a certificate of modification or modification plate that will show eg 3 + 2, meaning 3 permanent seats and 2 spaces for wheelchairs. The seating capacity is recorded as 3 in TICA.

Year of Manufacture
Year of Manufacture
    The year of manufacture:
    • Is the date the vehicle was manufactured or built (some vehicles have two plates, build plate and compliance plate. The date the vehicle was certified as compliant (RAV entry date) will be recorded on the RAV. The manufacture date is on the build plate).
    • May differ from the date of compliance.
    • Is used when selecting the year for the make and model in TICA.
    • Is displayed on the registration certificate.

    Note: If the month of manufacture is not displayed on the build plate it should be recorded in TICA as December. Applications should not be refused on the basis of the plate not displaying a month of manufacture.
    Location
    Location
      It is located on the manufacturers (build) plate. The year of manufacture should not be confused with the year of compliance, which is when the vehicle met the relevant Australian Design Rules and may differ from the year of manufacture.

      Example: Vehicles manufactured overseas in 10/2005 may not be brought to Australia and complianced until 02/2006. TICA records both the year of manufacture (the make and model description) and compliance.

Corrections
Corrections of Record
Corrections of Record
    If a customer notifies TMR that details on their record are incorrect the CSC operator must:
    • Obtain the original application or documentation (Iron Mountain) and alter the record if an error has been made.
    • If the documentation does not prove an error has been made, the customer is required to complete a Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification (F3524) to update the record.

    Example: Year of Manufacture, Make, Model.

    Pro-Rata Raised
    Pro-Rata Raised
      When a debt is raised in the customer's account due to:
      • a correction
      • a change
      • update to a record
      the customer must pay the debt in person at time of the update.

      If the update is processed from a written change request, a letter is required to be sent to the customer requesting payment of the additional fee within 28 days.
      Note:For any correction to the record that creates a debt to the customer, the correction should be backdated to the start of the current registration period if the registered operator is the same. If the registered operator was different at the start of the current registration period, backdate the correction to the date of transfer into the current registered operator's name.

    Refund
    Refund
      If confirmed that a genuine error has been made (eg engine number is the same but the number of cylinders was recorded incorrectly, incorrect concession type granted) a refund can be made back to when the error occurred provided that the applicant was the registered operator for that period of registration.

      It is at the CSC Manager's discretion whether a refund can be given. If the refund is authorised by the Manager, the CSC operator will then perform an adjustment to credit the customer's account. The operator will have to work out the difference in fees paid and the correct fee payable.

      For previous years' fees prior to 2001 refer to the Quote Tool.

      Process a REVCUST revenue adjustment to the customers account:
      • DR 420005828 Registration Fee
      • CR Customer's Account
      Operator must add text detailing the reason for adjustment.
      For example:
      • Vehicle registered as a 6 cylinder should have been 4 or incorrect weight recorded

        Phone XXXXX
