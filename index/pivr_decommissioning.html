PIVR Decommissioning (7242)

      Project NamePayments Integrated Voice Response (PIVR) Decommissioning
      When27 November 2017
      BackgroundThe Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR) is decommissioning the Payments Integrated Voice Response (PIVR) as a payment method. This change is required to enable TMR to comply with a new level of financial standards. This new level of financial standards will ensure that our customers’ credit card details remain securely managed.

      Customers can currently use the PIVR to pay for registration renewals and fines using their credit card, by phone. After the payment method is switched off, customers will have to pay using one of the a range of other payment methods available, including:

      • BPay (online or by phone through a customer's financial institution, transactional accounts only)
      • Direct debit registration renewal (credit card or transactional account)
      • Online services (credit card only)
      • Face to face at a CSC, QGAP or Australia Post (Cash, credit/debit cards, cheque)
      • Cheque by mail
      Who will be impacted and how?Customers who have traditionally used PIVR to pay for their registration renewals and fines will need to pay by alternative methods.

      There is expected to be an increase in enquiries and complaints regarding the closure of this payment method. Staff will need to assist customers to find a suitable alternative payment method.
      Further informationCustomers who phone 132380 and elect to pay their registration renewal are currently directed to the PIVR. From 27 November, there will be an automated message advising customers this option is no longer available and advice will be provided as to alternative payment methods. Customers can now be advised that this service is being decommissioned.
      FAQs / Q&AsWhy can't I use IVR to pay anymore?
      With fraudulent payment transactions and behaviours on the rise, TMR is taking a stronger stance on customer security to maintain your peace of mind when dealing with us. You may still be able to pay by phone through your financial institution as most offer BPay by phone – contact your financial institution for advice.

      How can I pay for registration renewals now?
      We offer a range of secure online payment options including:
      • BPay – including BPay by phone if offered by your financial institution – contact your financial institution for advice
      • TMR online services
      • direct debit
      • in person at a TMR customer service location
      • in person at Australia Post.
