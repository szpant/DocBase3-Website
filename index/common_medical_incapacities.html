Common Medical Incapacities (1284)

Diabetes
Diabetes
    The medical condition reporting requirements for diabetics depend on the method of controlling the diabetes and the type of vehicle being driven. It is important to note that the below information is general in nature. The customer should be informed to discuss their medical condition in detail with their doctor to ensure all the medical standards have been addressed.

    Private Vehicle Drivers
    Private Vehicle Drivers
      Method of ControlRequirement
      Diet and exercise aloneNo requirement to notify TMR

      Unconditional licence may be issued if no other medical condition reported. They should be reviewed by their treating doctor periodically regarding progression of diabetes.
      Glucose-lowering agents other than insulin (e.g. tablets)Must notify TMR, medical certificate required verifying fitness to drive (subject to periodic review)

      Subject to advice from a treating doctor, an unconditional licence may be issued if the person does not have end-organ complications that may affect driving, or has not had a recent 'severe hypoglycaemic event'.

      Otherwise a conditional licence with M Code may be considered based on advice of treating doctor.
      Insulin Must notify TMR, medical certificate required verifying fitness to drive (subject to at least two yearly review)

      A person is not fit to hold an unconditional licence if the person has insulin-treated diabetes. Only a conditional licence with M code may be considered based on information provided by the treating doctor.

      Note:If it is clear that a medical certificate is inconsistent with the above Assessing Fitness to Drive requirements then the customer should be referred back to their doctor to get the certificate amended.

    Commercial Vehicle Drivers
    Commercial Vehicle Drivers
      Method of ControlRequirement
      Diet and exercise aloneNo requirement to notify TMR

      Unconditional licence may be issued if no other medical condition reported. They should be reviewed by their treating doctor periodically regarding progression of diabetes.
      Glucose-lowering agents other than insulin (e.g. tablets)Must notify TMR, medical certificate required verifying fitness to drive (subject to at least annual review)

      Only a conditional licence with M code may be considered based on advice of treating doctor.
      Insulin Must notify TMR, medical certificate required verifying fitness to drive (subject to at least annual review)

      Only a conditional licence with M code may be considered based on advice of treating doctor.

      Note:If it is clear that a medical certificate is inconsistent with the above Assessing Fitness to Drive requirements then the customer should be referred back to their doctor to get the certificate amended.

Epilepsy
Epilepsy
    All driver licence holders and applicants who have epilepsy or experienced a seizure are required to notify TMR of their medical condition unless the exceptions listed below apply. A medical certificate is required verifying their fitness to drive, and an M condition will be endorsed on their licence.

    Persons who have experienced a seizure are not fit for an unconditional licence. However generally, a conditional licence may be considered, taking into account advice from the treating doctor, in the following circumstances:
    • For a private vehicle driver, if there have been no seizures for at least 12 months.
    • For a commercial vehicle driver, if there have been no seizures for at least 10 years.

    Note:If a customer has been issued with a conditional commercial licence, but they advise TMR that they have not met the 10 year seizure-free period, they should be referred to their doctor.

    If a driver undergoing treatment for epilepsy has experienced an extended seizure free period (more than 20 years) TMR may consider reduced review requirements based on independent specialist advice.

    Exceptions:
    An unconditional licence may be granted if no seizures have occurred after the age of 11 years. If a seizure has occurred after 11 years of age, the default standard applies.
    A private vehicle driver may be eligible for an unconditional licence, taking into account information provided by the treating doctor as to whether the following criteria are met:
    1. the person has had no seizures for at least five years; and
    2. has taken no anti epileptic medication for at least the preceding 12 months.

    A commercial vehicle driver who has experienced a seizure after the age of 11 years is not eligible for a unconditional licence.
    Possible reductions in the non-driving seizure-free periods for a conditional licence
    The standard applies to all drivers who have had a seizure unless their situation matches one of a number of defined situations listed in the AFTD. These situations are associated with a lower risk of a seizure related crash and therefore driving may be resumed after a shorter seizure-free period. However, the need for adherence to medical advice and at least annual review still applies.

    However, if there is any doubt, the customer should be referred to their treating doctor for assessment.
Hearing
Hearing
    Only commercial vehicle drivers are required to meet medical standards for hearing. As such, only commercial vehicle drivers (and applicants for commercial vehicle licences) who suffer from deafness or significant hearing loss are required to notify TMR. For commercial vehicle drivers, a medical certificate is required verifying their fitness to drive, and an M condition will be endorsed on their licence.
Musculo-Skeletal disorders
Musculo-Skeletal disorders
    All driver licence holders and applicants who have a musculo-skeletal disorders are required to notify TMR of their medical condition.

    Examples of a musculo-skeletal disorders include:
    • Arm or leg disability
    • Short stature
    • Loss of limb function
    • Reduced limb strength
    • Loss of limb
    • Short limbs

    Whether an unconditional or conditional licence may be granted will depend on the person’s ability to perform the required driving activity. As such, a medical certificate is required verifying their fitness to drive, and an M condition will be endorsed on their licence if required.

    Medical practitioners may specify that vehicle modifications, such as additional controls, are necessary. If vehicle modifications are required, a V condition will be endorsed on their licence.
Vision and Eye Disorders
Vision and Eye Disorders
    Driver licence holders and applicants who need to wear glasses/contact lenses for driving are required to notify TMR of their medical condition. An S condition will be shown on their licence indicating that they may only drive while wearing corrective lenses.

    All driver licence holders and applicants who have any other vision or eye disorder are required to notify TMR of their medical condition. A medical certificate is required verifying their fitness to drive, an M condition is to be endorsed on their licence if the treating doctor indicates the customer meets the criteria for the conditional licence.

    The person’s treating doctor may choose to refer the patient to an optometrist or ophthalmologist to complete page 3 of the medical certificate. The treating doctor must complete the medical assessment (page 2) of the medical certificate. Optometrist/Ophthalmologist reports and/or assessments are not accepted by TMR.

    Examples of vision or eye disorders that require TMR to be notified:
    • Poor visual acuity (congenital or acquired nystagmus)
    • Visual field defects (monocular vision, scotoma, sudden loss of unilateral vision)
    • Diplopia (also known as “double vision”)

    People with progressive eye conditions such as cataract, glaucoma, optic neuropathy and retinitis pigmentosa should be advised to have their condition monitored regularly regarding the potential future impact on their driving ability.

    A person is not fit for an unconditional licence if the person is monocular. A conditional licence may be considered based on the treating health professional’s opinion.

    Note:If the customer presents with an obvious eye or vision disorder e.g. monocular vision (one-eyed vision), and has not indicated “yes” at question 7b of the driver licence application form, or has not verbally indicated "yes" through the mandatory questions, the customer should be advised of the requirement to notify the department of any other vision or eye disorder.

    It is a requirement that the customer present a medical certificate if they have indicated that they have any other vision or eye disorders. The driver licence application form should be amended accordingly if presented at ineligible formless locations.
