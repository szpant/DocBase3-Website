Document Filing (561)


Document Filing Report
Document Filing Report
Document Filing Report
    The Document Filing Report is:
    • System generated daily for the previous days transactions
    • A list of transactions performed by an operator for which documents must be retained
    • Sent to the processing locations TRAILS report inbox.

    Separate filing reports are produced for:
    • Driver Licensing
    • Industry/Personalised Transport
    • Vehicle Registration
    • Marine Licensing
    • Marine Registration
    • Other

    Not all items that require filing are listed on the report and operators should check the Retention & Filing of Documents to determine if additional items should be retained.

    The filing report contains:
    Sequence numberIdentifies where the transaction can be located in the entire series of transactions
    Reference numberAll transaction have a reference number which is reproduced here
    Description of actionIdentifies the type of transaction which occurred
    User IDIdentifies the operator who performed the transaction
    Run dateThe date the report was printed
    Date enteredThe date the transaction occurred
Preparing Daily and Additional Filing
Collate Filing
Collate Filing
    Filing is either
    • Daily filing which is managed by the Document Filing Reports
    • Additional Filing which relates to a previously filed transaction
    • Original transaction filing from registered motor dealers processed from e-mail/fax

    Documents should be placed in the same order as listed on the Document Filing Report.

    Multiple entries for one transaction
    Multiple entries for one transaction
      If a filing report lists multiple entries which are the result of one transaction, draw a bracket around the transactions for which there is only one document filed.

      If the filing report entries are in different filing categories, for example Driver Licensing and Other, the filing is to be placed against one entry and a reference such as “Refer to D/L Filing” written against the other entry. Similarly if the filing is relevant to more than one operator, place the filing in one operator's filing and write a reference such as “Refer to D/L Filing for USERID" on the other operator's filing report.
    Report for File
    Report for File
      An operator is required to complete a report for file where there is no documentation for an item listed on the Document Filing Report.

      A report for File is not required if an operator in a formless centre forgets to retain a copy of the Driver Licence Receipt/Driver Licence History Detail. Refer: Admin > 5. Filing and Business Exceptions > Checking Daily Filing and Authorising Business Exceptions

      Registration transactions that do not require a report for file include changes to the:
      • Pay mode
      • Pay term
      • Purpose of Use
      • Registration category where the vehicle details remain unchanged
    Transactions not on the Filing Report
    Transactions not on the Filing Report
      Documentation for transactions which have a retention requirement but are not listed on the Document Filing Report can be manually added to the relevant products filing report by writing the CRN and transaction type after the printed items.

      For example:
      • Tow Truck Operator and Dangerous Goods Vehicle transactions that are processed in SILAS to be filed under 'Industry Licensing'.

Solo Sites Procedures

Prepare Filing Cover Sheets
Prepare Filing Cover Sheets
    Operators are required to complete Filing Cover Sheets (F3419) for each product group on the Document Filing Report.

    Secure the cover sheet to the front of the filing for each product group.
Prepare Additional Filing
Prepare Additional Filing
    Additional filing is added to the original transaction once it has reached Iron Mountain (RECALL). For this reason it is essential that each transaction is prepared and sent individually.

    Once all additional documents for the transaction have been collated a Document Filing coversheet must be prepared and the rego number/CRN/Licence number recorded at the bottom. Every transactions requires their own coversheet.
Documents kept in office - Not forwarded with filing
Documents kept in office - Not forwarded with filing
    Refer to the Recordkeeping Intranet and Retention & Filing of Documents for information regarding documents that are required to be retained in the office.

    QPS and Offline Agency Procedures
    QPS and Offline Agency Procedures
      QPS Stations and all offline agencies must batch and send the following products to their supporting TMR CSC (or an identified CSC, as per local filing arrangements) to be filed within the office accordingly:
      • Driving Examiner Test Books
      • National Heavy Vehicle Work Diary Applications
      • Overseas vehicle permit documentation (if processed at the agency)
      • Unregistered vehicle permit books (if used)
      • Driver licence surrender receipt books (if used)
      • Registration number plate surrender books (if used)
      • Interim registration label books (if used)

      Bundles of the above products must be prepared by the agency office by product type, in date order and with a completed Batch Filing Cover Sheet (F5074) detailing the office, relevant product and relevant date range (start date is first date recorded on book/receipts; end date is last entry recorded on book/receipt).

      Batches are then sent by post to the supporting CSC (or according to local filing arrangements). Agency staff should then advise of remittance of a batch with a courtesy email or phone call to the CSC advising of the products batched and the date posted.

      CSC's receiving this filing will store the items in their office; filed by agency, then by product and date/date range.

      Once documentation has met the retention period and is no longer required, the procedure for the destruction of these records should be followed. Refer to the Recordkeeping Intranet for instructions.
Documents not to be kept
Documents not to be kept
    The following TRAILS documents can be destroyed and do not need to be sent for filing:
    • Application forms that aren't required. For example - if a customer presents a Licence Application form at a formless centre
    • Cancelled application forms (forms with errors)
    • Photocopy of marriage or birth certificates
    • Receipts
    • Traffic history reports where the application has been approved and there were no concerns accessing the history
    • Clear criminal history reports (no offences identified)
    • Change of Customer Details (Individuals) if the address is the only change
TICA/TRAILS Filing Envelopes
Prepare Filing Envelopes
Prepare Filing Envelopes
    The TICA/TRAILS Filing envelope must only contain the filing for one product or one set of documents if Additional Filing.

    All filing must be for the same processing day. Iron Mountain (RECALL) rely on this to store the documents in a way that minimises the time and effort required to locate documents when requested.

    The TICA/TRAILS envelopes are available in 2 sizes, C4 and C3. The C4 envelope should be used where appropriate. Ensure envelopes are not overfilled as this leads to breakage in transit and may result in lost documents.

    Details to be recorded on the envelope include:
    • Product type - please note; each category Product must have an individual envelope
    • Processing Office
    • Processing date for the documents eg 01/01/2001 (must only be one date per envelope) (unless exempt)
    • User ID's of all operators whose work is contained in the envelope.
Sites permitted to have filing for multiple days in one envelope
Sites permitted to have filing for multiple days in one envelope

    All QPS, non TMR led QGAPS, Richmond QGAP and Julia Creek QGAP may have up to one week of filing in a TICA/TRAILS filing envelope.

    The dates contained in the filing envelope must be recorded on the front of the envelope.

    Any other sites wanting approval to have multiple days of filing in one envelope must contact Capability Engagement and Learning
Additional Filing
Additional Filing
    Additional filing must:
    • Have the date of the original transactions recorded as the processing day.
    • Only contain the documents for one transaction and a Document Filing cover sheet must be completed and attached to the documentation.
    • Have Additional Filing clearly circled
    • Have the rego number/CRN or licence number written on the front of the envelope.
Rejected Filing
Rejected Filing
    Iron Mountain (RECALL) will reject filing envelopes:
    • that contain filing for multiple dates, must only be one date per envelope.
    • that contain more than one category, must be one envelope used per product group e.g. Licensing, Registration, 1300 Agency Arrangements, Dealers Interface System DAIS.
    • that contain Additional Filing that has no identifier ie. Rego/CRN/Licence No.

    Rejecting filing will be returned to the processing location for correction.
When to forward filing
When to forward filing
    Filing documentation is forwarded to Iron Mountain (RECALL) for storage and retrieval when necessary, using either TOLL Couriers or Messenger Post.

    The option of using Iron Mountain's reply paid service should be used only by agencies and by centres in rural/remote areas of the state who are unable to access Toll or Messenger Post.

    Large Volumes
    Large Volumes
      Large volumes of transactions, it is recommended that filing is picked up in the morning to ensure delivery to Iron Mountain (RECALL) by 10am the next working day.

    Low Volumes
    Low Volumes
      For lower volumes of transactions, filing can be sent to meet operational needs.

      Note: Separate filing envelopes must still be used for each individual day. (excluding 1300 and 1800 Agency Arrangements)

Refer to Security
Refer to Security
    Filing relating to records marked "Refer to QT Security" are sent to Identity Management Unit (IMU)

    Do not forward any security documentation to Iron Mountain (RECALL).

Retrieving Filed Docs
Retrieving Filing
Retrieving Filing
    Operators may need to retrieve filed documents for:
    • Show Cause Procedures (original documents)
    • Verification/Correction of records (copies of documents)
    • A performance evaluation of a Rego Easy Business Client

    Documents can only be retrieved for individual specific transactions.
    For example:
    • Driver Licence Renewal application, processed 5/01/2004
    • Change of GVM, processed 31/12/2002

    To retrieve documents the specific transaction details in TRAILS need to be accessed and the relevant request completed.
    Refer: How to.. Retrieve Filing

    Note:Iron Mountain will redact any payment card details from documents prior to emailing to the requesting operator.
Original Documents
Original Documents
    • Must be approved by via email by a manager (record of email must be saved in shared mail box e.g. CSC mail box)
    • If an original single document is recalled, it is the responsibility of the person receiving it to redact (black out in permanent marker) any payment card details.
    • If an original box of documents are recalled, they will be transported to a secure location in COS who will arrange redaction of payment card details and then forward the box onto the requesting area. There will be a delay in delivery due to this process.

Redirection of Filing
Redirection to Supporting TMR Location
Redirection to Supporting TMR Location
    QPS and non-TMR led QGAPS filing, BEs and TRAILS reports are sent to a supporting TMR location for checking and authorisation prior to the filing being sent to Iron Mountain.

    The QPS/nonTMR led QGAP site will receive the RG61 Daily filing report to allow them to prepare the filing and attach a filing cover sheet.

    Reply paid accounts have been established to allow the sites to mail their filing at least once per week to their supporting location.

    Filing is actioned as per current guidelines including the use of the Follow up register.

    Refer: BE Support review_Feb 2020 for a list of sites and their supporting location

    The redirection is managed through the FILE involved party qualification. All changes to the supporting arrangements must be approved by the Manager (CSD) within the region and a copy of the approval forwarded to Service Delivery Review email.
