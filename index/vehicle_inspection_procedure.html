Vehicle Inspection Procedure (7602)

Applies to
Applies to
    All TMR inspectable vehicles that require a Certificate of Inspection (COI) for registration purposes.
1
Identification
Identification
    1. Ensure accurate identification of the vehicle presented for inspection:
    • Locate the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) or chassis number (on either the vehicle *compliance plate or stamped in the chassis).
    • Check the VIN or chassis against the identification details on the Vehicle Check List before starting the inspection.
    2.If a discrepancy exists between TMR’s vehicle record and the identification number on the vehicle, the inspector must cross-check additional vehicle details such as the manufacturers plate, modification plates, the make and model number and record all discrepancies on the Vehicle Check List.
    3.The inspection may continue if the inspector believes that the discrepancy is an administrative error, however action must be taken at the earliest opportunity to rectify the error. Refer: VIN/Chassis
    4.If the inspector believes that the discrepancy may indicate fraudulent activity and requires further investigation, then the inspection should not proceed and a Senior Transport Inspector is to be notified immediately. The driver is to be advised of the reason why the inspection will not continue and any subsequent action e.g. cease use the vehicle (consider using a voice recorder if issued).
    5.When checking for the compliance plate, look for modification plates and note any modification codes on the Check List, modifications section.
    6.Once the vehicle has been identified the inspection process should follow the site-specific SOP.
    7.Any defects identified during the inspection are to be numbered and noted in the comments section of the Vehicle Check List. If necessary, continue on the reverse side of the Vehicle Check List.

    *Note:A compliance plate will not exist for vehicles recorded on the RAV.
2
Tow couplings
Tow couplings
    Inspectors are not to uncouple or re-couple vehicles under any circumstances.

    1.If a tow coupling is fitted ensure a mod plate or manufactures certification is attached to the vehicle.
    2.Measure and test tow coupling or component using the appropriate gauge or tool.

    Note:If a prime mover is presented for inspection without a trailer, make a note on the check sheet - No trailer, jaws not checked.
    3.If the tow couplings cannot be satisfactorily inspected because of excessive lubricant e.g. kingpins and fifth wheels, the inspector may either:
    • direct the driver to clean the area to allow inspection if time permits; or
    • continue the remainder of the inspection and complete a defect notice directing the owner to clean the components; and
      • make a booking with SBS for a reinspection; or
      • have the reinspection conducted by AIS/Qualified Person.
    4.When the inspection of tow couplings is completed, and where a combination requires re-coupling, direct the driver to do so while observing for excessive or abnormal movement in vehicle structures, components and couplings which may indicate excessive wear or failure.
    5.During re-coupling observe the tow couplings to ensurer they have re-coupled satisfactorily and are secure. Direct the driver to conduct a ‘tug test’ to confirm.
    6.If required the trailer breakaway system may be checked as part of the tow coupling inspection process or during the brake roller test depending on site conditions. If the trailer breakaway system is not able to be tested the reason must be noted on the Check List.
    7.During re-coupling of vehicles, it is the inspector’s responsibility to ensure the vehicle area is clear of people e.g. assisting inspectors, prior to the vehicles being moved.
    8. Where hydraulic fluid, grease or other contaminants have spilled from a vehicle onto the inspection area, request the driver to clean the area. If necessary, a spill kit may be utilised.
3
Roller Brake Test
Roller Brake Test
    Not all vehicles can be tested using Roller Brake Testing equipment. Due to the park brake system fitted to some vehicles, damage may result if a Roller Brake Test is conducted. Examples of these types of vehicles are:
    • Light vehicles fitted with electric actuated park brake:
      • If the vehicle is fitted with electronically activated park brake direct the driver to hold the park brake control on to prevent the automatic release of the park brake when the throttle is depressed.
    • Vehicles fitted with drive line drum brakes, Toyota Coaster, Mitsubishi Rosa buses and some single drive light trucks:
      • Where there is an emergency/park brake that is likely to be unsafe or cause damaged by doing a dynamic brake test, a stationary test must be conducted instead. This can be done applying the emergency/park brake and attempt to drive forward using a light throttle.
      • ADR 35/.. applies to vehicles from 1 July 1988, as such vehicles after this date are unlikely to have a dedicated emergency brake (they will instead have split brake systems). It goes without saying, if there is no emergency brake, it cannot be tested.

    TMR vehicle inspection training covers the operation of brake testing equipment and the brake test requirements for these type of vehicles.

    1.Direct the driver to move the vehicle forward on a straight axis so the the first axle wheels sit uniformly in the brake rollers. If necessary, block the rear wheels to keep the front wheels in the rollers (site specific/trailer mounted brake tester).
    2.Explain to the driver to apply the service/foot brake slowly and evenly and if fitted with air brakes maintain full air pressure or the vehicle may not reach the minimum brake efficiency.
    3.Direct the driver to:
    • Ensure the vehicle is in neutral while the brake rollers are operating.
    • Disengage power divider or differential lock if fitted.
    • Disable hill start function if fitted.
    • Release all brakes.
    • Keep both hands on the steering wheel whilst the rollers are in motion as the vehicle may slew sideways – particularly light vehicles.
    • Control the steering wheel to keep the vehicle straight.
    4.When you are satisfied that the driver and vehicle are ready, commence the brake test procedure on each axle as required.
    5.Test the service/foot brake and park/hand brake on each axle, if an axle fails to reach the minimum efficiency, retest the axle before saving result in the Roller Brake Testing Equipment system. More than one test may be required if:
    • Brake rollers or tyres are wet.
    • Low air pressure.
    • Brakes are not being applied slowly.
    6.Record the overall deceleration percentage for brake system where indicated on the Vehicle Check List or any imbalance in the comments section of the check sheet:
    • Where possible, print the results on the reverse of the Inspection Checklist or attach the printed brake report to it and indicate that a brake report is attached in the space provided.
    • It is not mandatory to give the driver a copy of the brake report, however if the vehicle fails the brake test, a copy of the brake report should be given to the operator to assist in rectifying the fault.
    • Where brake testing is completed and no printed brake record has been produced, record the brake test results on the Inspection Checklist and indicate that no brake report is attached in the space provided.
    Alternate Brake Testing
    Alternate Brake Testing
      1.If not conducting brake roller testing, details of brake testing procedure and results must be recorded in the comments section of the Inspection Checklist, example:
      • Site speed limit 10kph, skid test, all wheels locked/1st axle drivers side failed to lock.
      • Tail shaft park brake, stall test, passed.
      2.If you are required to be in the vehicle to conduct the brake test and the vehicle is a Cab Over Engine (COE), ensure the cab locking mechanism is secure before getting in the vehicle:
      • Wear a seat belt.
      • Check the cabin is free of hazards.
      3. If conducting a brake test with electronic brake testing equipment that provides peak and average deceleration, both values must meet the minimum requirements. The printed results must then be attached to the Inspection Checklist.
      4. Where due to local restrictions dynamic testing cannot be done from the required speed (i.e. 35km/h), or under the required conditions (i.e. hard level surface that is free from gravel or loose material) the testing may be done, including at lower speeds. This must be noted in the comments section of the Inspection Checklist.
      5.However, if a test is not conducted as per the NHVIM, the reasons for rejection for the test in the NHVIM should not be used. It may give grounds for belief that the vehicle does not perform to the inspectors satisfaction. however this should be a trigger for further investigation, (i.e. inspect the wheel ends visually and identify potential reasons for the performance issue) and one of the general brake reasons for rejection used instead.

4
Exterior inspection
Exterior inspection
    1.Follow the site-specific SOP for inspection procedure.
    2.Check the vehicle dimensions by construction:
    • length
    • width
    • height.
    3.Once the brake test is completed direct the driver to move the vehicle forward and position the steer axle tyres centrally on the shaker plates. Direct the driver to:
    • select neutral/park
    • apply the park/maxi brake
    • turn the engine off, if not required for specific testing or inspection of components,
    • remain in the driver’s seat.
      • The Inspector must observe the vehicle carefully as it moves forward over the pit, some drivers become extremely anxious when driving over the open pit and may operate the vehicle controls erratically.
      • The engine must be switched off, if not required to be running during the inspection, to reduce the accumulation of toxic exhaust emissions and noise in the inspection area.
    4. Direct the driver (use radio if required) to operate all side/rear lights as required and check operation and fitment of:
    • tail
    • side/marker
    • stop
    • indicators
    • reverse
    • reflectors
    • rear marker plates
    • number plate
      • if the rear number plate of the vehicle is not visible it is defective and an accessory plate will be required, example rear mounted fork lift.
    5. Inspect the right and left side of the vehicle:
    • wheels/tyres
    • body
    • glassing/tint/mirrors
    • chassis
    • suspension
    • battery
    • exhaust
    • if the vehicle is fitted with a tipper body, the body must be raised to inspect the chassis, body and hoist.
      • Refer site specific SOP where to park the vehicle to raise the body.
      • First check for overhead lines.
      • DO NOT place any part of your body between the chassis and the tipper body.
      • If fitted, engage the body safety support.
    6.Move to the front of the vehicle, inspect the windscreen and direct the driver to:
    • sound the horn
    • operate washers and wipers
    • operate all lights.
5
Interior inspection
Interior inspection
    1.Prior to inspecting the cabin, direct the driver to:
    • select neutral/park
    • apply the park/maxi brake
    • switch the engine off
    • remove any wallets or other valuables from the cabin.
    2.Inspect the structure and components:
    • seats/seat belts
    • steering wheel and grip
    • windscreen
    • de-mister
    • low air/vacuum warning (if fitted)
    • dash warning lights ABS/EBS/Air bag/seat belt/check engine
    • fire extinguisher (bus/tow truck/DG)
    • emergency exit (bus)
    • door interlock (bus).
6
Under the vehicle
Under the vehicle
    1.If the vehicle isn't sufficiently clean to allow the inspector to adequately view a vehicle’s components or structures due to:
    • excessive grime or lubrication.
    • contamination that would affect the health or safety of the inspect.
    • this may only be obvious to determine from under the vehicle.

    With approval from the STI the inspector may not proceed with the inspection. If the inspection is terminated the driver is to be advised that the inspection will not proceed and a new booking must be made and paid for. Complete a defect notice directing the owner to:
    • clean the components;
      and
    • make a booking with SBS for a reinspection.
    2.Request the driver restart the engine.
    3.Request the driver swing the steering wheel rapidly from side to side to allow inspection of the steering components for any excessive or abnormal movement, observe steering components at the front wheel arches of the vehicle, if visible, before progressing underneath the vehicle.
    • Prior to proceeding underneath the vehicle the Inspector must comply with PPE requirement in the site SOP.
    • Consider the use of a hand-held radio to communicate effectively with the vehicle driver (Refer site SOP).
    4.While operating the shaker plates inspect the vehicles steering and suspension components from under the vehicle for excessive or abnormal movement.
    5.When shaker plate testing is completed request the driver to switch the engine off to reduce exposure to exhaust fumes, noise and moving parts.
    • Consider the use of a hand-held radio to communicate effectively with the vehicle driver (Refer site SOP).
    6.Procedure for jacking steer axles to inspect steering components and wheel bearings:
    • Vehicle Inspection Trailer (VIS) or fixed inspection location utilising in ground suspension testing plates and a jack for the check:
      • ensure the park brake is applied;
      • place a wheel chock in front and behind one drive wheel;
      • raise the jack to support the weight of the vehicle, maintaining that the tyre remains in contact with the suspension testing plate;
      • ensure the jack is fully lowered once the check of the axle is completed, so as not to damage the vehicle undercarriage when the vehicle is driven forward.
    • Jack only (no suspension testing plates):
      • ensure the park brake is applied;
      • place a wheel chock in front and behind one drive wheel;
      • do not raise both steer wheels at the same time. Lift one side then other;
      • lower and remove the jack before moving the vehicle.
    7.Continue inspection of the underbody.
    • Ask the driver to move the vehicle forward stopping with each axle on the shaker plates and turn the engine off.
      • Consider the use of a hand-held radio to communicate effectively with the vehicle driver (Refer site SOP).
    • Vehicles fitted with air brake systems, check for air leaks at each axle with maxi/park brake release then apply service/foot brake. Wheels may require chocking when both park brake and service brake are released. Hearing protection must be worn when service brakes are released, or park brake applied.
    8.When the underbody inspection is complete exit the pit. Consider directing the driver to move the vehicle off the pit if the area is required for other vehicles (Refer site SOP).
7
Vehicle Loading Crane (VLC)
Vehicle Loading Crane (VLC)
    If the vehicle is fitted with a Vehicle Loading Crane (VLC) it should be checked as part of the inspection process.

    Refer: Vehicle Loading Cranes (7362)
