Personalised Plate Transaction (4561)

Note:Personalised Plates recorded in joint names require both plate owners signatures for all transactions.

OrderAn order is the first instance when a personalised plate is being made for the applicant.

If an operator is unsure of the plate specifications when presented with a number plate application, it is encouraged to call PPQ to place the order and then take the payment in TICA.

This is because the system allows orders for most formats and specifications without stopping the user if an incorrect specification is chosen. This results in TMR being charged twice when an order needs to be corrected, reprocessing by PPQ and stop-requests for the manufacturing.

Remaking a Plate
    Remaking a plate refers to requests where the customer requires another set
    of personalised plates the same as the original. This may be due to the original plates being:
    • Damaged
    • Lost
    • Stolen
    • Slimline being required

    There is a fee involved.

    If only one plate is requested, two plates must be ordered. Advise the customer that:
    • the cost is the same
    • two plates will be produced
    • one plate must be surrendered if plates are damaged
    • they must surrender the other plate/s (both for remake/upgrade) when the new set is received.


    Note:If the plates are recorded as being attached to a vehicle currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice, plates can be remade, but cannot be attached to the vehicle during the confiscation period.
TransferAll personalised plate ownerships can be transferred.

A person who acquired a personalised number from its current owner (including an administrator/executor/Next of Kin of an estate) must:
  • Apply to TMR for the plate to be transferred Personalised Number Plates Transfer Application (F2963) or Vehicle Registration Transfer Application (F3520) if the plates are attached to a vehicle that is also being transferred.
  • Apply within 14 days of the acquisition
  • Pay the appropriate fee (unless exempt) Refer: Vehicle Rego > 11. Plates > Personalised Plates Guide > Transfer Fee Exemptions
The seller of the personalised plates can also apply to transfer the plate from their name by lodging the Seller's copy of the Personalised Number Plates Transfer Application (F2963.) They aren't required to pay the transfer fee. A debt will be raised in the acquirer's account and a Customer Account Statement will be automatically sent.

SurrenderIf the owner of personalised plates wishes to surrender the plates they may do so. They remain the owner of the plate number and may remake a set at any time. They should only cancel ownership if they no longer want to own the number or the customer is deceased/closed and the plates are not being transferred.
