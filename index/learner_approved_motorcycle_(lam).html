Learner Approved Motorcycle (LAM) (3236)


What is it?A learner approved motorcycle (LAM) is a production motorcycle that is fitted with an electric motor, or has an internal combustion engine with an engine capacity of not more than 660mL; and
  • has a power to weight ratio of not more than 150kW per tonne; and
  • that has not been modified other than for an allowable modification (LK7 seating modifications and/or mobility modifications); and
  • not be an Individually Constructed Motorcycle; and
  • that is stated to be a learner approved motorcycle in a list kept by the chief executive and published on the Department of Transport and Main Road’s website.
Eligibility
Applying for LAM Approval
Applying for LAM Approval
    Some motorcycles do not appear on the LAM list and have never been assessed for LAM eligibility. In these cases a LAM Eligibility Application form F4744 can be completed and forwarded along with copies of the motorcycle's LAM information to Vehicle Standards (email is preferred method).


    Note to CSO's:
    When a LAM Eligibility Application Form and Fact Sheet are requested please check the following (This ensures an application is not made for a motorcycle model which has previously been assessed or is not eligible):
    • the motorcycle is registered
    • the motorcycle's details on the system are complete and correct
    • the system has a LAM indicator of 'not assessed' recorded
    • the motorcycle's engine capacity is 660mL or under

    A LAM Eligibility Application form F4744 can be obtained from the Corporate Forms Database. The attached LAM Eligibility Fact Sheet must be given to a customer when an application form is requested.
    LAM Eligibility Fact Sheet
What is LAM Information
What is LAM Information
    LAM information includes:
    • Make/Model
    • Engine Capacity
    • Engine power output
    • Tare Mass
What is Acceptable Proof of LAM information
What is Acceptable Proof of LAM information
    LAM information maybe provided two ways when applying for LAM eligibility:-
    • by providing specifications obtained from the vehicle manufacturer (eg, copy of a vehicle's handbook).
      or
    • If manufacturer’s information cannot be obtained, a learner approved motorcycle test report must be performed on the vehicle.

    A learner approved motorcycle test report is documentation from a suitable measuring system to determine the LAM information (e.g. weighbridge, dynamometer).

    The test results supplied must include:
    • the name and address of the entity who conducted the test
    • the date of the test
    • the LAM information
    • details about the measuring system used to conduct the test.
RulesA motorcycle must be registered before submitting a LAM Eligibility Application. This ensures an application is not made for a motorcycle model which has previously been assessed.
How to identify a learner approved motorcycle
How to identify a learner approved motorcycle
    The department’s website will list those motorcycles that are a learner approved motorcycle:
    http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/licensing/motorcycles/learner-approved/

    Alternatively, you can use the Check Registration Tool online at: https://www.service.transport.qld.gov.au/checkrego and enter the registration number to see if the motorcycle has a LAM status.
What is a production motorcycle
What is a production motorcycle
    A "production motorcycle" is a motorcycle that is manufactured and marketed in volume for normal road use and conforms with the requirements applying to the motorcycle under vehicle law.
What is a motorcycle's engine capacity?
What is a motorcycle's engine capacity?
    The term “engine capacity” refers to the capacity of the engine, expressed as a stated number of mL or cc (1mL = 1cc), under the manufacturer’s specifications for the motorcycle.
What is a motorcycle's tare mass?
What is a motorcycle's tare mass?
    The term “Tare Weight” when relating to a motorcycle means the mass of the vehicle ready for service, unoccupied and unladen, with all fluid reservoirs filled to normal capacity except for fuel and with all standard equipment and any options fitted.
How a motorcycle's power to weight ratio is calculated
How a motorcycle's power to weight ratio is calculated
    The term “Power-to-Weight” refers to the kilowatts generated by the motorcycle’s engine in relation to the overall motorcycle weight.

    A LAM approved motorcycle must have a power to weight ration of 150kw per tonne or less. To calculate the power to weight ratio of a motorcycle:

    The motorcycle’s maximum engine power in kilowatts is divided by the prescribed weight of the motorcycle then multiplied by 1000 for conversion to kilowatts per tonne. The prescribed weight of the motorcycle means the tare mass, in kilograms, of the motorcycle plus 90 kg (which is generally accepted as the average weight of a rider) that is:

    Maximum Engine Power (kW)
    ----------------------------------
    Prescribed Weight (kg)
    X 1000
    		= Kilowatts per tonne (kW/t)

    Example
    Example
      The power to weight ratio of a motorcycle that has a maximum engine power of 54kW and a tare mass of 360kg is calculated by dividing 54kW by the sum of 360kg and 90kg (that is, 450kg) and multiplying the product of the division by 1000. In this example, the power to weight ratio is 120kW/t.
What is a prescribed motorcycle?
What is a prescribed motorcycle?
    A prescribed motorcycle is a motorcycle that has an engine capacity of not more than 250mL but has a power to weight ratio of more than 150kW per tonne.

    The following six models of motorcycles have been identified as a prescribed motorcycle:
    • Aprilia RS250
    • Honda NSR250
    • Kawasaki KR250 (KR-1 model)
    • Kawasaki KR250 (KR1s model)
    • Suzuki RGV250
    • Yamaha TZR250

    Note:These prescribed motorcycles have been excluded from the learner approved motorcycle list as they are capable of high speed and rapid acceleration and are regarded as being unsuitable for inexperienced riders due to their sports style power delivery, handling and riding position.
What is a Individually Constructed Motorcycle?
What is a Individually Constructed Motorcycle?
    Individually Constructed Motorcycle means a motorcycle that:
    • is built as a one-off motorcycle; and
    • is comprising of new and second-hand vehicle parts from one or more production motorcycles; and
    • before construction gains approval to construct by the Chief Executive; and
    • before registration receives final approval from the Chief Executive.

    Note:An Individually Constructed Motorcycle will not be considered for LAM eligibility.
What about my conditionally registered motorcycle?
What about my conditionally registered motorcycle?
    What if my conditionally registered motorcycle is a production motorcycle?

    If the motorcycle meets the criteria for a LAM, the motorcycle will be on the LAM list. Any conditionally registered motorcycle that is not a production motorcycle has been excluded.
Modifications
What are allowable modifications?
What are allowable modifications?
    Modifications can significantly increase a motorcycle's power to weight ratio. As such, any motorcycle which has had modifications performed (apart from the below mentioned) will not be acceptable as a LAM.

    Allowable modifications for LAM eligibility are limited to the following:
    • Motorcycles which have undergone an LK7 seating modification and were originally LAM approved; and
    • Motorcycles modified for mobility impaired purposes will continue to be LAM approved provided the motorcycle was originally LAM approved.
What is a manufacturers option?
What is a manufacturers option?
    Many manufacturers produce a series of models with an identical frame fitted with engines of differing capacity. The fitting of a larger or smaller capacity engine (provided the applicable brakes and suspension components are also fitted and the frame is not modified) which was a manufacturer’s option in the motorcycle’s model series is considered a manufacturer's option and not a modification.

    Once these changes have been made the registration details can be updated. A Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification application form, F3524 must be completed.
Replacement Engine within manufacturers specifications
Replacement Engine within manufacturers specifications
    If a replacement engine is installed within manufactures specifications and the engine is an option provided by the manufacturer for the frame, which does not require an approval process, the customer must advise the department of the replacement engine number and engine capacity by completing a Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification application (F3524).

    When updating the record in TICA the CSO will need to check:
    • whether the motorcycle is a LAM; and if so
    • the motorcycle model which the replacement engine was sourced is also a LAM.

    Where an engine capacity does exceed 660mL or the model which the replacement engine was sourced is not a LAM:
    • create a new Make / Model without LAM data (eg. engine capacity, engine power output, tare Mass), no LAM data will result in a "LAM Not Assessed" indicator on the system.
    • when creating the new make / model enter "modified" in one of the descriptor fields (as done with imports, where "import" is added into the badge descriptor).
    • the record can then be updated with the created Make / Model which will result in a "LAM Not Assessed" indicator on the system. The motorcycle will no longer be eligible as a LAM.
Modification outside of manufacturers specification
Modification outside of manufacturers specification
    If a modification outside of manufactures specs goes through an approval process with Vehicle Engineering and Access (VE&A) making the motorcycle ineligible for LAMS, Vehicle Engineering and Access will contact Systems and Performance Reporting (SAPR) to update the vehicle's record with the created Make / Model which will result in a "LAM unassessed" indicator on the system. The motorcycle will no longer be eligible as a LAM.
