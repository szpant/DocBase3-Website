Livestock Loading Scheme in Queensland (7487)

What it is
What it isThe revised Livestock Loading Scheme was introduced in February 2019, recognises:
  • the difficulty of estimating the mass of livestock when it is being loaded and transported,
  • that livestock travels better when loaded to a comfortable density which provides better load stability and improved safety.
While the scheme allows for exemptions from some strict mass and dimension regulations, loaded mass is effectively controlled by three key principles:
  • Internal trailer dimensions (deck length) are specified and checked as part of the certification process.
  • Trailer unladen mass is regulated and checked as part of the certification process.
  • Manufacturers ratings for all components must not be exceeded.
    The transition period for operators to comply with the revised conditions of the Livestock Loading Scheme ended in September 2019.
    Eligibility
    Eligible Vehicles
    Eligible Vehicles
    Eligible Vehicles
      The Livestock Loading Scheme in Queensland provides additional mass concessions for heavy vehicles transporting livestock utilising:
        • rigid trucks;
        • rigid trucks towing a five or six axle dog trailer;
        • prime movers towing a semi-trailer;
        • B-double combinations;
        • B-triple combinations;
        • Road Train combinations.
    Certification
    Certification
    Certification
      All vehicles and trailers (excluding tri-axle converter dollies) must be certified under the Concessional Livestock Loading – Vehicle Rating Code (S10). Read more about the S10 code:
      https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/s10modification

      The S10 vehicle rating sets maximum tare mass limits for livestock transport vehicles (prime movers, trucks and specifically constructed trailers) to allow for the variable weight of livestock to be transported.

      The Queensland Class 3 Livestock Loading Exemption Notice under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (HVNL) provides mass and dimension exemptions that support the Queensland Livestock Loading Scheme.

      The Notice is intended to work with the Queensland Livestock Loading Scheme, and compliance with that Scheme is a condition of exemptions contained in the Notice.
    Documentation for S10
    Documentation for S10
      • An approved person (livestock operator) needs unladen mass data from a reliable source to operate a livestock vehicle under the Notice (e.g. weighbridge certificate or a vehicle manufacturer’s documented specification of tare mass, provided it can be verified that vehicle is not modified in any way that affects its original tare mass). The tare mass of each vehicle/component of a combination must be identified on the modification plate.
      • Combined weight of fuel, water etc. is to be deducted to obtain the correct tare mass for prime mover/truck and trailers.
      • The methods of calculating tare mass and the requirements for S10 modification plates are available in the TMR 'Incomplete or inaccurate S10 modification plates' document.

        https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/-/media/busind/accreditations/Approved-person-scheme/incomplete-inaccurrate-S10-modification-plates-0616.pdf?la=en

      • The information shown on modification plates can and will be used for roadside enforcement, particularly the rating information for steer axle and rear axle groups.
    Unladen mass
    Unladen mass
    Unladen mass
      The S10 Vehicle Rating Certification requires the maximum unladen mass (tare) of trailers only:
      • total unladen mass of livestock a semi tailer must not exceed 15 tonnes.
      • total unladen mass of both trailers in a B-double combination must not exceed 22 tonnes.
      • total unladen mass of all trailers in a B-triple combination must not exceed 32 tonnes.

      Note: Unladen mass (tare) is the mass of the vehicle in running order unoccupied and unladen with all fluid reservoirs filled to nominal capacity including fuel, and with all standard equipment.

      Caution if weighing trailers with portable scales to check unladen mass ensure trailer landing legs are not placed directly on the scales. Wooden blocks/boards must be placed on top of the scales so as not to damage the scale.
    Non-Compliance
    Non-Compliance
    Non-Compliance
    Non-Compliance
      To be eligible to operate under the Notice, all conditions of the notice must be complied with.
      The maximum loaded mass and dimension limits and other mechanical specifications are found in schedule 1 Conditions of the Queensland Class 3 Livestock Loading Exemption Notice 2019 (No.2 ).
    S10 Certification
    S10 Certification
      To operate under the Queensland Class 3 Livestock Loading Exemption Notice, the combination must be an eligible vehicle. An eligible vehicle is a heavy vehicle under section 8 of the Notice.

      An express exception exists where tri-axle converter dollies do not require an S10 plate.

      Where all parts of a livestock combination are not S10 certified, the maximum mass must not exceed Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation [HV(MDL)NR] mass limit.
      For example:
      • Where the hauling unit is not S10 plated, the combination must not exceed any HV(MDL)NR mass limit.
      • Where the hauling unit is S10 plated but a trailer is not S10 plated, the maximum mass on the non-s10 plated trailer/s must not exceed HV(MDL)NR mass limit (i.e. tri axle group 20t).
    B-Triple exceed 26 tonne
    B-Triple exceed 26 tonne
      Where a S10 plated livestock vehicle or combination exceeds a mass limit of the Notice, the vehicle or combination is deemed not operating under the Notice and will therefore be assessed against Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation mass limits.

      The risk category will be assessed at 20 tonne and Prohibition/Direction Notice to comply with allowable mass of 26 tonne.

      Caution when issuing a Prohibition/Direction notice. Animal welfare must be considered.
    Manufacturers rating exceeded
    Manufacturers rating exceeded
      If any part of a combination exceeds the manufacturer’s ratings shown on the Compliance Plate (e.g. GCM, GVM or ATM) or a Livestock Loading (S10) Plate, it is deemed not operating under the Notice and will therefore be assessed and enforced against Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation mass limits .

      The GCM of a hauling unit must equal or exceed the sum of:
      • The GVM of the hauling unit; and
      • The tare mass of all trailers in the combination; and
      • 26t for each trailer in the combination.

      The risk category will be assessed against general mass limits and Prohibition/Direction notice to comply with allowable mass.

      Caution when issuing a Prohibition/Direction notice. Animal welfare must be considered.
    Unladen mass exceeded
    Unladen mass exceeded
      If a trailer/s exceeds the unladed mass requirement, it is to be treated as a condition offence as there is no unladed mass limit in Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation.
      Offence code N674 section 129 (2) HVNL (Q).
    Tri axle converter dolly
    Tri axle converter dolly
      The maximum mass on a tri-axle convert dolly must not exceed 20 tonne.
      If the mass on a tri-axle convert dolly exceeds 20 tonne it is to be dealt with as a mass offence.

      The risk category will be assessed at 20 tonne and Prohibition/Direction Notice to comply with allowable mass of 20 tonne.

      Caution when issuing a Prohibition/Direction notice. Animal welfare must be considered.
    Livestock loading S10 plate
    Livestock loading S10 plate
      For a vehicle to be eligible to operate under the exemption notice the vehicle must be fitted with a compliant Queensland Livestock Loading S10 Plate, including the maroon colour plate.
      Vehicles previously certified under the S10 Code with a blue modification plate will be recognised as eligible for the Scheme.


      An approved S10 modification plate must include the following details:
      • Date (at least month/year)
      • Accreditation (AVE/approved person) number
      • Certificate number
      • Vehicle identification number (VIN)
      • Vehicle S10 Laden Mass
      • Front Axle Group/Kingpin S10 Laden Mass
      • Rear Axle Group S10 Laden Mass
      • Unladen Mass

      If the plate does not include the required information a defect notice may be issued and or PIN for failing to comply with conditions of the notice.
      Offence code N674 section 129 (2) HVNL (Q).
      Consider who is the responsible person, the driver or the operator?
    Operators Guide
    Operators Guide
      Failing to comply with the requirements of the Livestock Loading Scheme in Queensland, Operators Guide is an offence for failing to comply with conditions of HVNL Queensland Class 3 Livestock Loading Exemption Notice 2019(No.2).
      Example:
      • Unladen mass of a semi-trailer 15 tonne
      • Insufficient D value on a tow coupling
      • No vehicle can carry livestock in the upper deck unless the lower deck is fully loaded with live stock.
    Interstate Vehicles
    Interstate Vehicles
      Vehicles registered in another State or Territory may operate under the Livestock Loading Scheme in Queensland provided the vehicle complies to all conditions of the Scheme.

      Mutual recognition will be applied to vehicles approved to operate as a Livestock Loading Vehicle in the State or Territory of scheme registration

      Interstate operators will be required to provide evidence of their respective jurisdiction’s Scheme approval if requested to do so by a member of the Queensland Police Service or an Authorised Transport Inspector.
