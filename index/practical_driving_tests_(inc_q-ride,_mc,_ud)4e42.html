Applicant requirements on arrival
Driving Examiner
Test Vehicles
    Applicant must provide a vehicle for the practical driving test which is:
    • Roadworthy.
    • Currently registered (with the registered operators permission to use the vehicle).
    • Representative of the class being tested.
    • Not part of the Takata Airbag Recall (Alpha Version) that has not been cleared* Refer: Takata Airbag affected vehicles.
    • Not a personally imported vehicle (with airbags fitted) as there is no way for TMR to determine if a Takata Airbag has been fitted or, if fitted, it has been replaced *. However, if the customer advises that the vehicle did have a faulty Takata airbag and produces evidence that it has been replaced, the vehicle may be used.
    • Correctly displaying L Plate/s.
    • Inspectable vehicles must have a current COI recorded (unless exempt) or have been granted an extension of time.
    • must not have registered purpose of use 'Dealer'

    *Note:If a customer presents for a PDT with a vehicle fitted with an Alpha Takata Airbag, or has a personally imported vehicle fitted with an airbag and the test is refused, the test fee is credited to customer for re-schedule of test.


    Primary Production Vehicle
      If the applicant is using a primary production vehicle (i.e. vehicle is registered under a primary producer concession) for the practical driving test, the applicant must provide written advice from the owner of vehicle that:
      • the owner of the vehicle is a primary producer; and
      • the applicant undertaking the practical driving test is currently employed by or will be working for the primary producer after passing the practical driving test.

Validity of TestWhen an applicant passes a Q-SAFE practical driving test, they must obtain that class of licence within 5 years. If not, they must pass the practical test again.
