Family Vehicle Registration Freeze (4939)


What is it?The Family Vehicle Registration Fee Freeze was part of the government’s election commitment to assist families struggling with the cost of living and is included in the CanDo LNP First 100 Day Action Plan.
Who will be
impacted and how?		The freeze applies to eligible vehicles due for renewal from 1 July 2012 (i.e. vehicles with a registration period commencing on or after 1 July 2012). It also  applies to eligible vehicles being registered in Queensland and to interstate vehicles being transferred to Queensland from 1 July 2012.
FeesThis initiative freezes the vehicle registration vehicle fee and the traffic improvement fee components of the registration charges for vehicles not over 4.5t GVM registered for private use from 1 July 2012 for 3 years.

Refer: Fees and Forms > 1. Fees > Vehicle Registration > Standard Vehicle Registration
Rules
Eligibility Requirements
Eligibility Requirements
    To be entitled to the Family Vehicle Registration Fee Freeze, all of the following eligibility requirements must be met.

    Eligibility Criteria
    Vehicles
    Eligible Vehicles
    Eligible Vehicles
      The freeze applies to vehicles not over 4.5t GVM registered for private use such as:
      • Passenger vehicles (e.g. sedans, wagons, convertibles)
      • Light commercial vehicles (e.g. panel vans, utilities, dual cabs)
      • Minibuses
      • Motorised campervans/motorhomes
    Ineligible Vehicles
    Ineligible Vehicles
      The freeze does not apply to:
      • Light motor vehicles not over 4.5t GVM that are not registered for private use
      • Heavy motor vehicles over 4.5t GVM
      • Trailers and caravans
      • Motorcycles
      • Mobile machinery
    Purpose of use
    Purpose of use
    Purpose of use
      Vehicles must be registered for private use.

      Note:Private use is defined as a vehicle, registered to an individual/s, which is used exclusively for personal and domestic activities 

      Refer: Purpose of Use > Vehicle Registration > New Business > Registration > Purpose of Use
    Concessional Registrations
    Concessional Registrations
    Concessional Registrations
      The freeze applies to eligible vehicles with private POU with the following concessions:

      PSS1, PSS2, SENR, PVA1, PVA2, EXSV, SIVE and RESA  
Refunds / Pro-ratas
Refunds / Pro-ratas
    If a registered operator notifies TMR of a change of POU to 'private' from another purpose during the currency of a registration period, the registered operator may receive a credit in their account.

    Where a registered operator receives a credit of less than $5, the refund should not be granted unless specifically requested by the registered operator.    

    However, if the customer requests the refund and the credit amount has been adjusted to a $0 balance due to the weekly 'sweep' in TICA, the operator may reverse the adjustment to generate a refund to the registered operator.

    Processing adjustments for Rego Freeze refunds
    Processing adjustments for Rego Freeze refunds
      This adjustment is only to be used for refunds in the following scenario for Rego Freeze eligible vehicles only:
      • as a result of the registered operator notifying TMR of a change of POU during the currency of a registration period; AND
      • where the registered operator receives a credit of less than $5; AND
      • the credit amount was adjusted to $0 due to the weekly 'sweep' in TICA.

      Adjustment Type: REVCUST

    • $20 or more, the amount must be paid
    • less than $20, the amount does not need to be paid (as a corresponding credit will be applied)
