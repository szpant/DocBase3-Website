Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licence Guide (1880)

What it is
What is a Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licence
What is a Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licence
    A dangerous goods vehicle licence authorises a business to use a specific vehicle/s to transport dangerous goods by road. A licence may list more than one vehicle.
When is a vehicle licences required?
When is a vehicle licences required?
    Any vehicle transporting dangerous goods in a receptacle in excess of 500L or 500kg by road needs to be appropriately licensed.

    Note:Vehicle does not include prime movers or a converter dolly.
When is a Vehicle Licence not Needed
When is a Vehicle Licence not Needed
    It is not required when the dangerous goods are being transported:
    • in an IBC (intermediate bulk container); and
    • the IBC is not packed or unpacked on the vehicle; and
    • the total capacity of IBC's containing dangerous goods on the vehicle is not more than 3000L.
Period of Licence
Period of Licence
    The dangerous goods vehicle licence is issued for a maximum of one year. The licence is suspended if the vehicle is not registered.

    Vehicles added to the licence during its currency are charged a pro-rata fee calculated on the number of months remaining on the licence.
T.R.CTransaction Requirements Checklist
Conditions of Use
Conditions of Use
Conditions of Use
    When transporting dangerous goods, licence holders must:
    • ensure the vehicle is registered in Queensland;
    • ensure all persons performing any task relating to the transport of dangerous goods have had appropriate training;
    • ensure any persons driving a vehicle transporting dangerous goods hold a current DGDL;
    • have the correct placarding and signage displayed on the vehicle;
    • ensure required transport documents relating to load are carried in the emergency information holder.
Conditions
Conditions
    Contact the Industry Licensing Team if a Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licence requires conditions.

    Note:Do not draft a Conditions letter until you have contacted Industry Licensing, as the document is for specific conditions only
Disposal of Licensed Vehicle
Disposal of Licensed Vehicle
    The licensee must notify TMR of any licensed vehicles that have been replaced / deleted / disposed of. These vehicles must be removed from the licence.
Insurance Requirements
Insurance Requirements
    The vehicle owner must have adequate insurance or other form of indemnity (of at least $5,000,000) to transport dangerous goods, as outlined in the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Dangerous Goods) Regulation 2018.

    Note:Applicants are not required to provide proof of insurance to CSC staff.
Road Tanker Approval
Road Tanker Approval
    Road tank vehicles being used for the purposes of transporting dangerous goods must be issued with a road tank approval by a state or territory dangerous goods authority.

    Road tanker approval is issued to the vehicle manufacturer and a compliance plate displaying the following information must be attached to the vehicle:
    • the name of the manufacturer of the tank
    • the date on which the tank was manufactured
    • the tank serial number
    • the maximum allowable working pressure for the tank
    • the test pressure
    • the metallurgical design temperature of the tank if the temperature is above 50°C or below -20°C
    • the capacity of the tank
    • the maximum mass of dangerous goods that may be transported in the tank under the design approval
    • the maximum gross mass of the tank
    • the name of the Competent Authority who granted the approval and the approval number
    • the initial hydraulic test date and subsequent test dates for the tank
    • the name of the authority or organisation that witnessed the last hydraulic test
    • if the design approval is based on compliance with an Australian Standard or other standard or code, the standard or code to which the tank or vehicle has been designed.

    There is no requirement to capture the road tanker approval number in TICA/SILAS. There is also no requirement for a manufacturer to capture the VIN or make and model of a vehicle/s produced against an approval.

    Where an application for a Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licence includes road tank vehicles, the approval number must be captured on the Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licence Application (F2259) form against each applicable vehicle listed on the application.

    The format of the approval number is different in each jurisdiction. There are currently no road tankers manufactured in Northern Territory, Tasmania or Canberra.

    A reference table outlining the format of each jurisdiction's road tank approval number has been provided below:

    JurisdictionApproval number format
    QueenslandQRT123
    New South WalesDG2/123
    VictoriaVDGT/123
    Western Australia8 number sequence, where the:
    • first number is the class of dangerous goods
    • second number is “6” (for WA)
    • third and fourth numbers represent the unit number of the vehicle built against the design approval number (for example, the first vehicle built against the design approval number 36001317 would have the road tanker approval number 36011317)
    • fifth and sixth numbers represent the sequential WA register approval number for the year
    • last two numbers represent the year the approval was issued

    For example: 36021317
    South AustraliaDGSA123/RT

    If an operator believes an approval number captured on the application form is not valid, an email can be sent to the Dangerous Goods Unit to verify the number.
