Flexi Rego - One Month Registration Term (7489)

Project NameOne-month registration term and direct debit enhancements
WhenOne-month registration - 15 February 2020

Direct debit enhancements - 30 November 2019
BackgroundIn response to customer feedback, a one-month registration term became available as a renewal term for customers enrolled in direct debit from 15 February 2020. It is hoped the introduction of this shorter registration term will reduce cost of living pressures by allowing customers to renew their registration in smaller, more manageable payments.

In addition to the development of a one-month term for direct debit, legislation changes were undertaken to formalise eligibility requirements, and structure surcharges (for renewal terms less than 12 months in duration) and introduce a specific one-month RRRF (1MRRF.) These changes will help ensure customers experiencing cost of living pressure are not subject to excessive charges which may be incurred with the increased frequency of one-month renewals.

It has also become mandatory for all new customers to sign up to e-correspondence.

On 30 November 2019, a number of enhancements to the direct debit service were released including:
  • reducing the eligibility requirements to allow more vehicles to use the service,
  • reducing restrictions on when customers can enrol in the service direct debit so they can now enrol up to their registrations expiring
  • improving the timing when direct debit payments are processed so they are now closer to the registrations’ expiry
  • Direct debit electronic communications have been refreshed, with changes identified through customer testing implemented as part of the one-month project.
  • Communications have a stronger focus on providing customers with clear and simple information that is delivered in a timely manner. To ensure direct debit communications work effectively, customers enrolling in direct debit are required to sign up to TMR's e-notice service, as well as providing a mobile phone number which is used in the event a direct debit payment fails. From 15 February 2020, customers enrolling in direct debit will also be required to sign up to TMR's e-reminder service.
February 2020 ChangesThe following changes were implemented on 15 February 2020.

One-month term
One-month term
    From 15 February, customers enrolled in direct debit will be able to choose a one-month renewal term in addition to the 3, 6 or 12 month renewal terms currently available through this service. Changes to a registration term will come into effect at the next renewal, or if the registration expires in the next 17 days, a manual payment for the initial registration term is required to be paid by the customers - subsequent renewals will then be processed through direct debit.

    The cost of the one-month registration term is 1/12th of the annual renewal amount term plus the surcharge. As at 15 February 2020, the cost of a one-month renewal for a 4-cylinder private passenger car will be $63.45. The one-month fees will be made available in DocBase and on the Quote Tool from Monday, 17 February.

    Operators will not have the option to select the one-month term in TICA if the registration is not added to direct debit. Customers who aren’t enrolled in direct debit and wish to access the one-month term should be sent the "Enrol in Direct Debit and Add Vehicle" email template which is available in the Email Templates section of DocBase.

    If a vehicle is added to direct debit and the customer contacts the department to change the term operators can update the term to one-month in TICA but must first check that the customer is signed up for e-correspondence and have their mobile number recorded as these are mandatory eligibility requirements. A daily report will be generated for any operator who selects the one-month term in TICA to check that the customer has met the eligibility requirements.

    Providing customers access to a one/3-month term outside of legislated eligibility requirements (DDRR/DDRR, Heavy Vehicles, Booked Hire, drought/disaster relief) is not permitted and reports will be run to identify when this has occurred.

Surcharge
Surcharge
    New one-month registration fee surcharges have been introduced. The 2019/2020 surcharges are:

    Light Vehicle$1.45
    Concessional Light Vehicle$1.00
    Heavy Vehicle$6.40
    Conditional $1.45
RRRF
RRRF
    A specific one month RRRF (1MRRRF) and Concessional (Special Interest Vehicles, light trailers and Conditional) 1MRRRF will be introduced for the one-month registration term. The RRRF for 3, 6 and 12 month terms will stay the same.

    The current 1MRRRF (01/12/2019-30/06/2020) is $21.80 and the Concessional 1MRRRF is $5.40.

    The RRRF amount is dependant on the future registration term.
    For example - Payment fails for a one month registration term and the registration expires. Before making payment the term is updated to 3 months. The customer will have to pay the full RRRF as the future term is 3 months.
Catch-Up Payments
Catch-Up Payments
    If the direct debit payment for a one-month registration payment fails, the customer has up to the registration's expiry date to make the payment using the alternative payment options on the VRRN. This is the standard process for all renewals, regardless of term or payment option.

    If the customer lets their registration expire, then their renewal payment needs to be made online through the registration renewal service. Payment needs to be made through this service because the amount due to renew their registration will vary depending on if a late payment, or a catch-up payment is required. On a one-month VRRN, a late payment amount is not shown because of this, instead a link is provided for the customer to access the registration renewal service

    The two payment scenarios are detailed:
    • Late payment: If payment is made soon after expiry you will need to pay the registration renewal amount (1-month period) PLUS a late payment fee.
    • Catch-up payment: If payment is made too close (or after) your next scheduled direct debit payment you will need to pay the next 2 registration renewal amounts (2 x 1-month periods) PLUS a late payment fee.

    These payments are specific to one-month renewals and were developed to help customers remain on one-month renewals.

    If the registration is not renewed within 25 days of expiry (regardless of term) it will be removed from the direct debit service.

1M VRRN email date
1M VRRN email date
    The one-month Vehicle Registration Renewal Notice (VRRN) will be emailed to the customer 27 days before the registration expiry date.

    Three, six and 12 month VRRNs will continue to be sent 42 days before the registration expiry date.
Mandatory e-reminders
Mandatory e-reminders
    It will become mandatory for all new direct debit customers to also be signed up for e-reminders before they can enrol in direct debit.
Absolute Floor and Financial HardshipAs customers can now enrol in direct debit until their registration expires they should be encouraged to enrol and access a one-month term rather than be granted the Absolute Floor when claiming financial hardship. It is expected that more customers who are experiencing financial hardship will choose the option to enrol now that they can access a one-month term.

Only a SA(SM), Senior Call Centre Consultant (SCCC) or Manager will be able to approve for the customer to pay the Absolute Floor.

Customers with an expired vehicle registration can't enrol in direct debit. If a customer with an expired registration is experiencing Financial Hardship the current process for the Absolute Floor applies.

The Absolute Floor Amount has not been changed with the implementation of the one-month term.
Who will be impacted and how?
Operators
Operators
    Operators may notice an increase in enquiries relating to direct debit and how to enrol. Operators should refer to the FAQs (below.) An email template with instructions to assist in the "Enrol in Direct Debit and Add Vehicle" processes has also been added to the "Email Templates" section of DocBase.

    To assist customers who enquire about direct debit operators should ensure that the customer's email address and mobile phone number is captured and they have signed up for e-correspondence. Doing this will make the process for enrolling in Direct Debit smoother for the customer. Operators should also check to see if an Immediate Payment is required and advise the customer of this.
Customers
Customers
    It is expected that a large number of customers will be interested in the one-month registration product and will choose to enrol their vehicles in direct debit. It is hoped this will result in a decrease in financial hardship applications to TMR.
November changes
Eligibility requirements
Eligibility requirements
    Customers
    Customers
      Since 1 December 2019, all new direct debit customers (regardless of selected registration term) must have signed up for e-notices and recorded their mobile phone number.

      Customers who are enrolled in Direct Debit will be unable to:
      • opt out of e-notices
      • remove their email address
      • remove their mobile number.
    Vehicles
    Vehicles
      Heavy vehicles were added to the direct debit service.

      The only vehicles that will be ineligible for DDRR are those with a:
      • Charitable concession on a Heavy Trailer or Caravan
      • Seasonal registration
      • Registration Category of:
      • Special Purpose
      • Dealer or Trade Plate
      • Wheelchair
Sweep date
Sweep date
    The direct debit payment date has been reduced from 21 days to 14 days. Customers will now receive their "Direct Debit payment reminder email" 17 days prior to the registration expiry date.
Immediate Payments
Immediate Payments
    Customers can now add their vehicles to the direct debit scheme until their registration expires, regardless of the registration term selected. However, if their registration is expiring within the next 18 days it is too late for their account to be swept so they must make an immediate payment using a Visa/MasterCard/American Express branded card.

    After the customer has enrolled in direct debit and added the vehicle they will be sent an 'Add registration successful with immediate payment' email advising that a one-off payment is required, they will be instructed to use the "Pay using your registration number" online service. Refer below: Other resources > Updated Direct Debit Email Templates > Direct Debit Add Registration Successful - Immediate Payment required
