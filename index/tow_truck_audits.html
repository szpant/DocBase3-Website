Tow Truck Audits (7333)


Tow Truck Audits
What it isAn organisation or person who is approved by TMR to hold a Tow Truck Licence (TTL) is expected to maintain high standards of conduct and be competent in performing their role. They must also comply with the relevant Acts, Regulations and administration requirements that govern the Tow Truck Licensing and Accreditation Scheme. Where a person fails to maintain a high standard of conduct or they contravene their legislative obligations there are a range of options that TMR may use to respond.

The following framework ensures TMR conducts compliance audits on all Tow Truck Licence holder’s business premises, holding yards, vehicles and records, to ensure continued compliance with the requirements, statutory and imposed conditions on the tow truck licence.
Roles and Responsibilities
Senior Transport Inspector (STI)
Senior Transport Inspector (STI)
    The STI is responsible for:
    • Managing the scheduling of audits (scheduled, triggered and record keeping)
    • Reviewing audit outcome recommendations
    • Receiving and/or assessing requests for a triggered audit directly from a customer or from a CSC or the TTU.
    • Quality assurance and endorsement of evidence to support recommendation for the issue of PINS or show cause/immediate suspend action where applicable as a result of a compliance matter such as investigation or failed audit
    • Conducting show cause or immediate suspend action as a result of compliance activity
    • Ensuring audits are accurately recorded in CID
    • STIs may undertake audits if required
Transport Inspector (TI)
Transport Inspector (TI)
    The TI is responsible for:
    • Arranging and conducting audits (pre-licence, recordkeeping, compliance and triggered) and associated administrative preparations
    • Completing and finalising the Tow Truck Compliance Audit checklist or Tow Truck Record Keeping Audit Checklist and issuing Audit Reports and Corrective Actions (CAs)
    • Ensuring that the TTL holder is aware of the process and requirements to finalise any CAs issued
    • Completing the Audit Report and discussing with the STI within 5 business days of audit completion
    • Where appropriate recommend the issue of PIN’s or show cause/immediate suspend action to the STI
    • Monitoring return of CAs by the TTL holder within the specified time frame
    • Entering audit outcomes in the CID
    • Updating the Sharepoint audit schedule
Audit typesRecord Keeping and Compliance audits of TTL holder business premises, holding yards, vehicles and records are undertaken to confirm compliance with the legislated conditions of their approval.
These audits are:
Pre-licence compliance audit (conducted prior to TTL being granted)
Pre-licence compliance audit (conducted prior to TTL being granted)

    Prior to granting a TT licence all new applicants must be able to demonstrate that their business premises, holding yard/s and towing vehicles comply with the legislative requirements of the TTLAS. To ensure that new applicants meet these requirements, CSB Compliance TIs will attend an applicant’s business premises and holding yard/s and conduct a pre-licence compliance audit prior to the approval being granted.

    TTL Prelicence Inspection CA v102018

    Refer: Transport Compliance > 11. Tow Trucks > Tow Truck Premises/Holding Yard/Vehicle Inspections
Record keeping audit (conducted within 6 months of approval)
Record keeping audit (conducted within 6 months of approval)
    New applicants will be subject to a record keeping audit within 6 months of the licence being granted to ensure the licence holders are complying with their record keeping requirements.

    TIs will conduct a record keeping audit, using the Tow Truck Record Keeping Audit Checklist, to ensure:
    • QPS are being notified of vehicles towed from private property
    • Records are being maintained of crashed vehicles and seized vehicles and vehicles towed from private property
    • Records are being maintained of property found in towed vehicles
    • Found property is able to be kept in safe custody
    • The tow truck licence is kept in safe custody
    • Confirm that Towing Authorities are being correctly completed for crash vehicles
    • Confirm that Towing Consents and contracts are in place and are current for each private property from where vehicles are towed
    • Records are stored in safe custody where they are free from fire, flood and vermin.
    • Fees charged do not exceed the maximum regulated fee (unless it is for a non-standard tow and the the additional charge can be justified by the TTL).

    Note:The Tow Truck Regulation 2009 contains the record keeping and retention requirements. Under the Acts Interpretation Act 1954, if a licence holder chooses to keep electronic records (whether on a computer or other device) they must still be able to produce a document containing the information to a court, tribunal or person (such as TIs). For example, if a licence holder is keeping records on an "app", they must be able to either download the data or reproduce it for court, a tribunal or a TI for inspection, copying or taking an extract.
Compliance audit (conducted at least once every two years from completion of the Record keeping audit)
Compliance audit (conducted at least once every two years from completion of the Record keeping audit)
    Compliance audits will be conducted of the business premises and holding yards at least once every 2 years from when the record keeping audit was conducted.

    A compliance audit will include the checks that are undertaken at a pre-licence compliance audit and the six-month record keeping audit as per the Tow truck licence compliance and triggered audit checklist and the Tow truck licence record keeping audit checklist.

    It will not be necessary for tow truck vehicles to be inspected at a compliance audit (after the initial pre-licence compliance audit) as the programmed vehicle inspection for the issue of a Certificate of Inspection will be expanded to cover:
    • Tow trucks display correct signage, business name and contact details
    • No other advertising is displayed on the tow trucks
    • The equipment on tow trucks is in working order.
Triggered audit (as required)
Triggered audit (as required)
    Triggered audits will be conducted to investigate complaints and other intelligence suggesting non-compliance (using relevant sections of the Tow Truck Compliance Audit checklist).

    Triggered audits are not scheduled and may be random. A triggered audit may consist of checks specific to the nature of a complaint, breach pattern or issue identified. If deemed necessary, a compliance audit may be undertaken to confirm the TTL holder is complying with all of the operating requirements and conditions of the TTLAS scheme. If relevant, this compliance audit may include an inspection of the tow trucks

Note:The Tow Truck Unit (TTU) will notify the relevant compliance region when a new licence holder is granted so they may be added to the Sharepoint audit schedule. TTU will also notify Compliance Intelligence Unit of new licence holders.

Conducting the audit
Pre-audit checks
Pre-audit checks
    Pre-audit checks can be fulfilled utilising TMR databases and without visiting the TTL holder’s business premises and/or holding yards. A Pre-audit check should be undertaken before the visit to the TTL holder’s premises but no sooner than 5 business days before the audit.

    Pre-Audit checks should include but are not limited to the following:
    • Contact the TTL holder (director or owner) to arrange a date and time for the audit providing 5 working days’ notice and issuing them a Entry Meeting Confirmation

    Audit Confirmation Template

    • Confirm the addresses of the business premises and holding yards that are to be inspected (SILAS will need to be checked to confirm these are the premises and holding yards recorded against the TTL)

    SILAS Instructions for Tow Trucks

    • Advise the TTL holder of the relevant records and the number of vehicles that must be available on the arranged audit date
    • Advise that either one of the TTL holders (director or owner) must be present at the audit
    • Confirm with the TTL holder the names of the drivers and assistants that are employed by the TTL holder
    • Check the relevant databases (TICA, TRAILS, SILAS) to confirm the status of the TTL, TTD’s and TTA’s certificates. For each licensed tow truck attached to the TTL, check the status of the vehicle registration and certificate of inspection
    • Check CID for intelligence holdings and the outcome of any previous audit conducted.
Preparing for the audit
Preparing for the audit
    The Primary Auditor conducting the audit must prepare by:
    • Checking the CID to find the date and outcome of the last compliance audit
    • Preparing the relevant TTL holder compliance audit checklist or record keeping audit checklist
    • Ensuring any PPE and other equipment required to conduct the audit, such as, recording equipment, camera, tape measure and a torch or other light source are available
Entry Meeting
Entry Meeting
    On arrival at the TTL holders premises, TIs should introduce themselves to the TTL holder/s, show their TMR identification (if requested to do so) and explain the objectives, scope and requirements of the audit. The TI must ensure that at least one licence holder (director or owner) is present for the audit. This can be confirmed by asking for proof of their identification.
Conducting the audit
Conducting the audit
    The audit must be conducted in accordance with the Tow Truck Compliance and Triggered Audit Checklist or Tow Truck Record Keeping Audit checklist. The audit must be evidence based and the Primary Auditor is responsible for collecting objective evidence to verify the TT licence holder’s compliance with the legislation and conditions of their approval. Objective evidence may include, for example, statements made during interview, records made and observations made during an audit. Objective evidence is not based on gut feel.

    Evidence of non-conformance will normally be collected during the course of an audit however, evidence of a non-conformance may also be gathered through intelligence or through information provided by members of the public. Corrective Action Requests (CAR) must not be used where non-conformance is suspected but has not been substantiated by collection of objective evidence.

    The Tow Truck Compliance and Triggered Audit Checklist or Tow Truck Record Keeping Audit checklist must be fully completed when conducting an audit. The auditor should document and investigate potential non-conformances, even where these are not covered by the checklist.

    When completing the Tow Truck Compliance and Triggered Audit Checklist or Tow Truck Record Keeping Audit checklist please use the Compliance code legend at the bottom of each page for example, NA = Not Applicable, NIL = Not assessed, NC = Non-compliance requiring rectification and V = Compliance verified.
    Sampling size
    Sampling size
      The number of records to be checked needs to be sufficient to determine if the TTL is complying with the requirements. The number of records to be checked therefore should be proportioned according to the number of entries as follows:
      • Less than 20 entries – inspect not less than 5
      • Between 20-60 entries – inspect not less than 10 entries
      • Between 60 -100 entries – inspect not less than 20 entries
      • More than 100 entries – inspect more than 20 entries.
Exit Meeting
Exit Meeting
    At the Exit meeting the TTL holder is given an overview of the audit and advised of the result of the audit. The TI should summarise the audit findings and non-conformances should be explained to ensure they are understood by the TTL holder.

    Should the TTL holder dispute the non-conformance this should be recorded on the checklist by the TI.

    A date of rectification and/or follow-up audit of any non-conformances should be established with the TTL holder prior to completion of the Exit Meeting.

    When determining deadlines and proposed follow-up dates for closure of non-conformances, the Primary Auditor must be open to negotiation of these deadlines depending on the seriousness of the non-conformances identified.

    If the TTL holder cannot immediately rectify the non-conformance they must rectify within 14 days of receiving the Audit Report, unless a later date is negotiated either at the Exit Meeting or later by email to TMR.

Note:All audits are to be recorded in the Audits Database on the Compliance Intelligence Portal.

Audit Report and Corrective Actions A TTL Audit Report and 'Corrective Action'(CA) must be completed by the Primary Auditor following each on-site audit including for follow-up audits. The audit outcome must be completed giving brief details of any CAs attached to the report and Primary Auditor’s recommendations and any actions which require a follow-up audit.

The Audit Report and attached CAs (if any) are to be forwarded to the STI within 5 working days of completion of the audit.

Once approved for release, the Audit Report and CAs are to be emailed to the TTL holder giving them 14 days to rectify non-conformances and respond to TMR.

The Audit Report and CAs are to be embedded in CID against the Audit record.

Issue of Corrective Action
Issue of Corrective Action

    A CA must be issued to the TTL holder when there is an occurrence of non-compliance. The TTL holder is required to notify the Department when the issue/s identified in the (CA) have been rectified.

    Non-compliances identified during the audit must be recorded on the relevant checklist.

    When completing the audit checklist, the TI must decide whether:
    • The TTL holder has satisfied the TTL conditions or whether breaches are identified
    • Depending on the type of breach, the CA may be resolved and closed out during the audit, if the TTL holder provides sufficient evidence to clear the non-conformance. A CA must still be issued and recorded with the close out date recorded as the date of the audit.

    A TTL holder is typically given 14 days to rectify CAs.

    Note:It may be decided that due to the seriousness or the number of breaches detected that the recommendation to the STI is for show cause/immediate suspend action to be initiated. If the STI supports the recommendation, the matter will be dealt with by the Compliance Region. A copy of the show cause/immediate suspend notice will be emailed to tow.trucks@tmr.qld.gov.au
Management of Corrective Action Requests
Management of Corrective Action Requests
    The Primary Auditor who conducted the audit is to manage the completion of the Audit Report and CAs by:
    • Monitoring return of CAs by the TTL holder within the specified time frame
    • Reviewing the returned CAs and ensuring the TTL holder has provided sufficient evidence that the CAs have been rectified (It may be appropriate to undertake a site visit or follow-up audit)
    • Managing non-completed CAs by:
    • Contacting the TTL holder to identify the reason for non-compliance
    • The TI has discretion to allow an extension of time to comply with the CAs. Such requests must be documented and included with the compliance audit documentation
    • Where a TTL holder can demonstrate that there was no non-conformance and the non-conformance is invalid, the TI can withdraw the CA.

    Note:Any extension of time granted must be recorded in the running sheet in the CID Audit record and the CA bring-up date amended.

    If notification that the CA has been rectified is not received within 14 days, a TI will contact the license holder to ascertain when the CA will be finalised. In the event the CA has not been finalised, written notification will be sent the TTL holder advising that failure to comply with the CA may result in the issuing of PIN/s or show cause action to amend, suspend or cancel the licence.

Finalisation of Corrective Action Request
Finalisation of Corrective Action Request
    Once a CA has been cleared to the satisfaction of the Primary Auditor the outcome should be updated in CID and the documents filed according to current business practices.

    A follow-up audit may be necessary to ensure CAs have been rectified. Follow-up audits focus on the non-conformance identified during the scheduled or triggered audit however, the scope of the audit may be broadened during the follow-up audit to examine any or all aspects of the conditions of approval if deemed appropriate by the TI.

    If the follow-up audit indicates that the TTL holder has satisfactorily corrected the non-conformance, the TI completes the close out section of the CR.

    If the non-conformance has not been satisfactorily rectified, the details are recorded on the CA and it is closed out. A new CA is raised and cross referenced. CID is updated.
