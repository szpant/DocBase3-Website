Plate Warranty (3086)

Standard Plate and National Heavey Vehicle Number Plate
Standard Plate and National Heavey Vehicle Number Plate
    What it isThe plate warranty is a guarantee against faulty material and workmanship.
    Requirements
    • Standard Number Plate/s Replacement Application (F3540);
      and
    • Standard EOI;
      and
    • Plates subject to the warranty claim.
    Rules
    • Warranty only applies when a vehicle is operated under normal conditions.
    • Warranty begins from the recorded date of the plates being despatched from the manufacturer.
    How long are plates covered by warranty?Standard issue number plates are covered under warranty for a period of 10 years from the recorded date of the plates being despatched from the manufacturer.

    National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates are covered under warranty for 10 years from the recorded date of the plates being despatched from the manufacturer.
    Customer Options
    ActionsRequirements
    Purchase standard plate/sRemove plates/s currently attached to vehicle:
    • Surrender the plates in TICA
    • Purchase and attach replacement standard plates
    • Fee will be refunded if warranty claim is approved
    Remove plate/s and await decisionThe vehicle cannot be used on the road until the outcome of the claim is decided.

    Once the warranty claim has been assessed, the customer will be required to attend a CSC to:
    • get their replacement standard plate/s, issued free of charge (if warranty claim is approved); or
    • arrange for replacement standard plate/s and pay the replacement fee (if the warranty claim is refused).
    Customise number plates to retain plate combinationIf the customer has requested a warranty for two standard plates and wants to retain the combination it must be customised before the warranty is lodged. The customisation fee is applicable and is not refundable.

    Once the plates are customised the customer must follow the Customised Plates warranty process.
    Forward plate/s for assessmentForward a copy of the Standard Number Plate/s Replacement Application (F3540) and the plate/s to the Contracts Unit (Plates). The plates will be sent to the manufacturer for assessment.
    How long before a response is received? The manufacturer will provide the outcome of the warranty claim in writing to the Contracts Unit within 10 business days of receipt of the plate/s.
    Warranty ApprovedContracts Unit will advise the CSC operator via email of the outcome and request the customer is advised.

    If replacement standard plates were purchased, the CSC operator is to arrange a refund of the fee to the customer.
    Warranty DeniedContracts Unit will advise the operator via email of the outcome and request the customer is advised.
Personalised/Customised Plates
Personalised/Customised Plates
    What it isThe plate warranty is a guarantee against faulty material and workmanship.
    Requirements
    • Personalised/Customised Plate/s Replacement/Change/Upgrade Application (F3543);
      and
    • Standard EOI;
      and
    • Plates subject to the warranty claim.
    Rules
    • Warranty only applies when a vehicle is operated under normal conditions.
    • Warranty begins from the recorded issue date of the plates on the system
    Note:
    • Do not process a remake of a plate for a warranty process.
    • Do not surrender a personalised plate in TICA for a warranty process.
    How long are plates covered by warranty?Warranty periods for personalised plates vary.

    Plates issued prior to 1 December 2013:
    • Issued prior to 14 February 2011:
      • Personalised number plates - coloured, theme, corporate and EURO have a 10-year warranty period regardless of manufacture process.
    • Issued from 14 February 2011 - 30 November 2013:
      • Digitally printed number plates have a 5-year warranty period (see list below).
      • Screen printed number plates had a 10-year warranty period.
      • Frameless prestige number plates have a 4-year warranty period.

    Plates issued from 1 December 2013:
    • Prestige and Flexi prestige number plates have a 5-year warranty period. This also covers any supplied frame.
    • All coloured theme, corporate and EURO personalised number plates have a 7-year warranty period

    Digital Printed Personalised Plates - 5 Year Warranty
    Customer Options
    ActionsRequirements
    If the vehicle has 2 plates:
    Retain 1 plate
    • If both plates are faulty, the most legible plate is to be attached to the rear of the vehicle.
    • This option cannot be taken if the most legible plate of a customised set is a slimline, as customised slimline plates cannot be attached to the rear of a vehicle.
    Purchase standard plates
    • Remove (DO NOT surrender) both plates currently attached.
    • Purchase and attach standard plates.
    • Fee will be refunded if warranty claim approved.
    Remove both plates and await decisionVehicle cannot be used on the road.
    If vehicle has only one plate (eg motorcycle):
    Purchase standard plate
    • Remove (DO NOT surrender) currently attached plate.
    • Purchase and attach standard plate.
    • Fee is refunded if warranty claim approved.
    Remove plate and await decisionVehicle cannot be used on the road.
    Forward plate/s for assessmentForward a copy of the Personalised/Customised Plate/s Replacement/Change/Upgrade Application (F3543) and the plate/s to Personalised Plates Queensland (PPQ) who will forward to the manufacturer for assessment.
    How long before a response is received? The manufacturer will provide the outcome of the warranty claim in writing to PPQ within 10 business days of receipt of the plate/s.
    Warranty ApprovedPPQ will contact the CSC to advise the warranty claim is approved.

    The CSC must contact the customer and advise that they will have new plates made at no expense to the customer.

    The new plates will be sent directly from the manufacturer to the customer and will take approximately 10 business days to be received depending on delivery location once despatched.

    If standard plates were purchased, the fee will need be credited to customers account and refunded. The customer should be advised to surrender the standard plates before a refund is provided.

    Any plate that was not returned as part of the assessment process must be returned by the customer as soon as possible.
    Warranty Denied
    TMR ActionCustomer Action
    Customer kept one plate
    • Advise customer that they can either:
      • Purchase/remake plates
        or
      • Have original plates returned to them
    Customer purchased standard plates
    • Advise customer that fee paid for standard plates will not be refunded.
    • If plates were personalised or customised customer may:
      • Remake plates;
        or
      • Have original plates returned to them.
    Customer surrendered both plates and awaited Decision
    • Advised customer that they can either
    • Purchase/remake plates, or
    • Have original plates returned to them
    Appealing Warranty DecisionAdvise Customer to forward dispute in writing to Contracts Unit (Director)
    Plates outside of warranty periodCustomer is to be advised the warranty claim will not be actioned and they are to arrange for the plates to be remade or purchase replacement plates.
