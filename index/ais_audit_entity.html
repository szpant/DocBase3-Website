AIS Audit Entity (7151)

The AIS Audit entity captures AIS Audits conducted by compliance.

Recording audits in CID will allow capture of time spent on conducting audits.

Capturing AIS audits on CID will also ensure they are captured with other compliance holdings about companies and/or individuals who may also be connected to AIS stations.

This does not replace the need to record audits in RUMIIS as evidence of business and as a corporate record for the scheme owner in TRB who utilise RUMIIS as their source of truth.

All staff have the ability to create this new entity type via CID. Simply log into CID

What the AIS Audit entity links to in CID
What the AIS Audit entity links to in CID
    AIS Audit entities link to a number of other entities in CID, and are one of the more complicated entities.
    The AIS Audit entity can be linked to:
        • Tasks

          AIS Audits link to tasks in the same way cases do. The individual tasks record the actions taken and also hours of effort spent. Hours of effort should be for all compliance officers involved in the task. For example if two TI’s attend one AIS audit and the activity takes two hours – that is four hours of effort.

        • AIS Audit Triggers

          Because an AIS Audit can be triggered from a number of sources, it can be linked to a Disseminated Intelligence Product, Complaint, Crash, Case or an Event as triggers of the AIS Audit.

        • AIS Audit Involved Parties

          Organisations and Persons can be linked to an AIS Audit as an involved party.
          Additionally the Accreditation/Scheme entity can also be linked where it is needed.

        • AIS Audit Outcomes

          Finally, as an AIS Audit can have a number of outcomes once it is completed, it can be linked to those entities in CID to show the outcome.

          The outcomes can include an Education Session, an Infringement and/or a Case (i.e. an investigation) because the effort required to determine any wrong doing is more than simply issuing an infringement.

          Note, corrective action requests (CAR’s) are listed on the AIS Audit entity itself, not linked.
