Smartcard replacement (3343)

Replacement Smartcard reasons
When to replace - Summary
  • If a customer's licence is lost, stolen, damaged or destroyed, they may be able to apply for a replacement online.
    Refer: Services > 1. Online Services > TMR Online Services Guide > TMR Online Services List > Licensing - General > Replace driver licence
  • An operator may renew, rather than replace, a damaged, destroyed, lost or stolen driver licence if it is within 12 months of the expiry date.
    Refer:     Driver Licence > 8. Open Licences > Open Licence Guide > Transaction Details > Renew > Renewing Licences > When to Renew > Early renewal prior to 6 weeks (Prov/Prob/Open only)

When to Replace
- Explanative
Lost
Lost
    • The card holder has physically lost or misplaced card;
      or
    • The card holder has notified that their smartcard product has not been received after 90 days and IMU/CSC Manager has rejected the customer's statement about the non-delivery and therefore have not approved a free replacement.

    Note:Driver Authorisation and Dangerous Goods driver licence holders are exempt from paying replacement smartcard fees provided they hold no other IA products. Code DA/DGD exempt is to be used regardless of the reason indicated by the customer on the application form.
Stolen
Stolen
    • The card has been unlawfully taken from the card holder.
Destroyed
Destroyed
    • Smartcard has been completely destroyed (deliberate or unintentional) as a result of an event eg. destroyed by fire, water etc.
Damaged
Damaged
    • Visible damage to the smartcard or the smartcard chip which is not the result of the manufacturing process eg. 'card chewed by dog'; 'exposure to heat'; 'damaged by washing machine'.

    Note: For customers who are ineligible for a replacement on the grounds of Faulty and disagree with the CSC's decision to treat as Damaged Refer: Smartcard > 1. Smartcard > The Smartcard Guide > Transactions > Replacing > Faulty / Damaged - Refund Requested
Change Customer Details
Change Customer Details
    • To change the card holder's personal information on the face of the card e.g. change of name (TO(RUM-DL) Reg. s.39).
    • To change the digital photo/digitised signature on the face of the card e.g. injury/medical procedure.
    • For an APA card, to remove gender from the face of the card.
    • For a DL or MLI card, to remove gender and height from the face of the card.
Condition Change
Condition Change
    • To add or remove the condition on the face of the card. e.g. adding a medical condition, spectacles etc.
Court Order
Court Order
    • To add a X3 condition code for a Special Hardship Order (provisional/open driver licence only) (TO(RUM-DL) Reg. s.30ZC).
    • To add a X4 condition code for a s79E court order (TO(RUM) Act s.79F) .
Marine Licence Transitional
Marine Licence Transitional
    • A marine licence holder who does not hold a current Qld driver licence requests to convert an existing Marine Licence to a smartcard.
Product Exists - Includes MLI removal on DL
Product Exists - Includes MLI removal on DL
    Product exists (PROD):
    • To remove an X3 or X4 condition code after the period has expired.
    • To remove a Marine Licence indicator (MLI) on the driver licence if the marine licence is cancelled or surrendered under TO(RS) Act and the drivers licence is current. (TO(RUM-DL) Reg. s.43C)
    • To convert a marine licence from the old system to TICA and issue a driver licence with MLI.
    • When the driver licence was suspended or cancelled because of a court disqualification and the cardholder has successfully lodged an appeal with the court (other than medical grounds).
    • When the driver licence was suspended or cancelled because of show cause action and the cardholder has successfully lodged a review with QCAT (other than medical grounds).
    • When a cardholder is granted a marine licence during a driver licence suspension period and the suspension period has expired.
    • When a condition is changed on a MLI and a replacement driver licence with MLI is required.
    • When a recreational marine licence type has been surrendered or cancelled and the customer continues to hold a recreation marine licence and/or driver licence.
    • When a driver licence has been surrendered, cancelled, voided or is expired and the customer holds a current recreational marine licence.
    • Where multiple authorities exist on the smartcard and one (or more) authority is surrendered, cancelled or suspended and the customer is to retain one or more authority.
    • Where an authority is cancelled due to the driver licence being cancelled because of a court disqualification and the cardholder has successfully lodged an appeal with the court (other than medical grounds).
    • Where a review of a TMR decision to suspend or cancel the industry authority has been upheld or the cardholder has successfully lodged a review with QCAT (other than medical grounds).
    • To correct the expiry date of a P1, P2 or P on the driver licence.
Customer records merged
Customer records merged
    • To issue a replacement card after a customer record has been merged.
Manager Approval
Manager Approval
    • To change incorrect information on the face of the product eg clerical error.
    • Where exceptional circumstances apply and the Manager has used their discretion to issue a free replacement driver licence.
Smartcard Lost in Mail
Smartcard Lost in Mail
    • To issue a replacement product (free of charge) if the person has notified that their smartcard has not been received within 90 days from date of application.
    • To issue a replacement product (free of charge) if the person has notified that their smartcard has not been received in the mail (regardless of the amount of time that has elapsed since and application was lodged), and:
      • it has been at least 14* days since their application was lodged;
        and
      • the product has a status of "Returned" in TICA;
        and
      • the smartcard history indicates the card has been returned by Customer Service Support (CSS.)

    *Note:If the smartcard delivery time has been temporarily changed, it will be published in DocBase messages.

    Online replacement service - unavailable for free smartcard replacements
    Online replacement service - unavailable for free smartcard replacements
      The online replacement service cannot be used for free replacements.

      When a replacement Photo ID or MLI is generated, the card is issued for a 10 year period from the date the replacement was processed. The shelf life of an image is 11 years, but may be extended by a maximum period of 6 months, making the total maximum shelf life of an image 11 years and 6 months.

      As a result, where a person applies for a replacement Photo ID or MLI more than 6 months from the date of application, they will be required to attend a CSC to have a new image captured, because the maximum shelf life of the existing image has been exceeded.

      If the card was lost in the mail and is "Returned" by IMU in TICA, the replacement product should be issued free of charge.

      Refer: Smartcard > Smartcard not received in mail
Faulty
Faulty
    • No visible damage to the smartcard (unmarked indentations, scratchings),
      and:
      • CSO cannot access or verify the smartcard in the card reader,
        or
      • Any information printed on the card, (including facial and signature image) is not clearly legible, text is not aligned, blank information fields, ink lines running through card or any fault that can clearly be attributed to the manufacturing process,
        or
      • Card has delaminated or begun to delaminate and the effective date on cards is pre 2012 - these cards may be affected by know manufacturing issues,
        or
      • Card has delaminated or begun to delaminate and has a DCD notation on rear of card – these cards may be affected by known manufacturing issues,
        or
      • The facial or signature image is not a likeness to the cardholder (incorrect image or signature reproduced on card).

    Note:For customers who are ineligible for a replacement on the grounds Faulty. Refer above: Damaged

Expired Smartcard - Photo ID and MLI only
Expired Smartcard - Photo ID and MLI only
    • Photo ID or MLI smartcards operational life has expired. (After 10 year period)
    Note:Photo ID & MLI applications are treated as a new issue on expiry but processed as a replacement in TICA as a system workaround.
    Refer: Photo Identification Card > Transaction Requirement Checklist > Replace, OR
    Marine Licence > Transaction Requirement Checklist > Replace MLI
Image not alike
Image not alike
    • Customer requests a new digital photo and/or signature to be captured (non medical reasons eg. dislike image, change of hair colour/style).
Driver Authorisation and Dangerous Goods Replacements ONLY
Driver Authorisation and Dangerous Goods Replacements ONLY
    • Driver Authorisation and Dangerous Goods driver licence holders are exempt from paying replacement smartcard fees provided they hold no other IA products.
    • Code DA/DGD exempt is to be used regardless of the reason indicated by the customer on the application form.
Replace smartcard online

A customer whose licence has been lost, stolen or destroyed is eligible to replace their licence online if they:
  • have a driver licence which is not due for renewal* or has expired
  • have a valid digital photo and digitised signature captured
  • have not changed their name and have no changes to their licence conditions
  • have not used the replace driver licence online service within the last 12 months
  • are an individual who does not have:
    • a current address (ADDR) restriction
    • a suppressed record
    • a consular qualification
    • a disqualified, cancelled or suspended licence in Queensland, or any other Australian state or territory

*Note:The online service cannot be used of the driver licence is due for renewal within 42 days of the expiry date. This is because it should be renewed rather than replaced.
Replaced smartcard locatedIf a licence/authority/card holder applies for a replacement Smartcard because it is lost, stolen or destroyed and the replaced Smartcard is located after the issue of the replacement, the card holder is not required to return the licence/authority/card.

The card holder must cut up the superseded licence/authority/card.

If the licence/authority/card is returned to TMR, it must be destroyed.
