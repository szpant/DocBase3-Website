Schemes, Guidelines, Permits and Concessions (5258)

Statement
Transport Inspectors are to identify and enforce non-compliance eligibility criteria under the relevant scheme, guideline, permit or concession.
Procedure
Heavy vehicle guidelines and class permits information is available through the TMR website: Business-industry > Heavy-vehicles > Compliance and enforcement > Heavy vehicle guidelines and class permits.

When calculating mass break points and prohibitions/s the following applies:
  • Breaches of Mass condition whilst the vehicle, trailer or combination is operating under a Permit, Concession and / or Guideline must NOT be assessed against Regulation Mass. The Permit, Concession and / or Guideline weights are to be applied for the calculation.
  • For the purposes of prohibition, the same applies: vehicles, trailers and combinations must NOT be assessed against Regulation Mass. The Permit, concession and / or Guideline weights are to be applied.

Note: It is not a requirement in Queensland to carry a guideline in the vehicle.

Authorised Officers are to:
  • Intercept heavy vehicles in accordance with interception procedures.
  • Undertake checks to ensure that the vehicle or combination of vehicles complies with the relevant scheme, guideline, permit or concession being operated under.
  • Weigh vehicle/s or combination of vehicles in accordance with existing weighing procedures.
  • Take appropriate enforcement action where required.
Permits (Vehicle Standards, Mass and Dimension) Permits
From 10 February 2014, the Heavy Vehicle National Law has introduced new offences about vehicle standards exemption and Mass Dimension permits.

An Authorised Officer has the powers to issue an Infringement Notice for such offences.
Section 79 - Return of Permit Vehicle Standards
Section 181 - Return of Permit Mass and Dimension
If a person's vehicle standards exemption or Mass and Dimension (permit) is amended or cancelled, the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) may, by Notice, require the person to return the person's permit of the exemption to the Regulator.

The person must comply with the Notice within 7 days after the notice is given to the person or, if a longer period is stated in the notice, within a longer period.

If the exemption has been amended the Regulator must give the person a replacement permit for the exemption as amended.
Section 80 - Replacement of Defaced Vehicle Standards Permit
Section 182 - Replacement of Defaced Mass and Dimension Permit
If a person's permit for a vehicle standards exemption or Mass and Dimension (permit) is defaced, destroyed, lost or stolen, the person must as soon as reasonably practicable after becoming aware of the matter, apply to the Regulator for a replacement permit and if the Regulator is satisfied, the Regulator must give the person a replacement permit as soon as practicable.

The Regulator can also deny the person a replacement permit and must advise the person an information notice for the decision.
