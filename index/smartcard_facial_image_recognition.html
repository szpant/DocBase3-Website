Smartcard facial image comparison (3341)


Facial Image Comparison
What is it?Facial image matching software is used to assist with verifying the customer:
  • Against all other images on the system the first time an image is taken for a customer in a one to many comparison (1:N)
  • Against the most recent facial image of the person in a one to one comparison (1:1)
How it works
How facial imaging works
How facial imaging works
    When a photograph is taken a biometric template will be derived from it. This template and the photograph are stored separately in a secure system

    The facial recognition software is based on the ability to recognise a face and measure the key features of the face.

    It will then electronically compare the key measurements of these features (an algorithm) to the algorithms of other images and establish any one to many (1:N) matches or failed one to one (1:1) comparisons.
Image Restrictions
Facial Image restrictions
Facial Image restrictions
    Facial Image restrictions that will be displayed to an operator in a dialogue box are:
    IMGR - Image under review; Refer to IMU:
    Image has failed 1:1, 1:N or ISO and is awaiting completion of investigation by IMU.

    IMGD - Image declined; Refer to IMU:
    IMU have investigated a 1:1, 1:N or ISO failure and have determined that the image is not acceptable to be used.

    Note:Removal of image restrictions will be conducted by the IMU only.
One to many (1:N) image comparison
One to many (1:N) image comparison
One to many (1:N) image comparison
    A 1:N image comparison is a comparison of the initial photograph taken for a customer against the most recent valid photograph stored for all other customers. A 1:N facial image comparison is used to identify customers who may have multiple records or identities.

    This match is conducted at the end of each business day and prior to the system undertake the smartcard production requests for that day.
Failed 1:N image comparison
Failed 1:N image comparison
    Failed 1:N image comparison
    If the image matchesIf the 1:N facial image comparison does match another person’s digital photo, the production process for the smartcard driver licence, Photo Identification card, marine licence or industry authority will remain in a pending status.
    IMU considerationThe exception (failed 1:N) will be reported to the Identity Management Unit for their consideration.

    1.If IMU determines that they are not the same person, it will accept the image and allow the smartcard production process to proceed.
    2.Should IMU consider that they are the same person, IMU will provide notification, and where necessary a detailed image analysis report, to the senior officer at the licence issuing centre which may result in:
    • Instructing the merge of the records
    • Recommending a show cause action for a driver licence or recreational marine licence
    • Recommending the refusal to grant the product - for Photo Identification Card applications and lodged Industry Authority applications
    • A request for the CSC to contact QPS.
    Note:Where identity fraud is suspected, the CSC is required to report the incident to WHS Connect, who manage security incident notifications for TMR, on 1800 867 947.
    Merged recordsIf the records are to be merged, IMU will approve or reject the image.
    Refer to: Customer > Merge Customer Records
    Show cause statusThe IMU will advise if a show cause action is required for failed 1:N image comparison.
One to one (1:1) image comparison
What it isA 1:1 image comparison is a comparison between a new photograph taken and the latest stored valid photograph for that customer.

This match is conducted prior to consideration of an application.
1:1 FIC transactions
Transactions that undertake a 1:1 Facial image comparison
Transactions that undertake a 1:1 Facial image comparison
    1:1 must be undertaken for:
    • Replacement smartcard transaction where the customer has not verified their identity using their latest smartcard
    • Transact smartcard business for a customer with a suppressed Record and already issued with a smartcard; and
    • Driver Licence written test applications.
    1:1 may be undertaken (if CSO elects):
    • If CSO elects to conduct a 1:1 where there is doubt regarding the customer's identity.
    • Applying a 'Acceptable Variation to EOI';
    • When a subsequent digital photo is captured for that customer for use on a smartcard.
    • Replacement smartcard transaction where the customer has verified their identity using their latest smartcard.

Check Customer records
Check Customer records
    The customer record should be checked by the CSO to make sure the image has been captured for the right customer record.
Image failure to be referred to IMU
Image failure to be referred to IMU
    The transaction is not to proceed and the CSO is to refer the matter to the IMU.

    1.The customer record will automatically have an IMGR (Image Restricted) restriction added in TICA preventing any further smartcard transaction until the image failure has been resolved by IMU.
    2.The 1:1 comparison failures will be given immediate priority once reported to the IMU. The customer is to be requested to remain at the CSC while the IMU conducts its assessment as further information may be required from them to complete the assessment.
    3. However, if a customer wishes to leave the CSC/Agency while the assessment is being undertaken, do not stop them.

    Contact details for IMU - INTERNAL USE ONLY
Application retained at the CSC/Agency pending outcome
Application retained at the CSC/Agency pending outcome
    Retain the application form/s at the CSC/Agency until the IMU advises the outcome and any further action required.
Assessment & Result
The IMU will:
The IMU will:
    • Assess the failed 1:1 comparison
    • Analyse the digital photos and any other available images
    • Analyse the customer record and the associated product records

      The IMU may ask to speak to the customer or may ask that the CSC/Agency operator to obtain further information from the customer as part of their assessment.

      Note:Such action may require the completion of a Customer Identity Checklist.
Assessment completed by the IMU
Assessment completed by the IMU
    Once the assessment has been completed the IMU operator will decide whether to:
    Digital photo accepted
    Digital photo accepted
      IMU accept the digital photo that failed the 1:1 comparison as an acceptable likeness to the customer's current digital photo.
    Digital photo not accepted (Further identifying information required from customer)
    Digital photo not accepted (Further identifying information required from customer)
      IMU do not accept the digital photo that failed the 1:1 comparison as an acceptable likeness to the customer's current digital photo, and require further identifying information and documents from the customer

    Digital photo not accepted (Suspected Identity Theft)
    Digital photo not accepted (Suspected Identity Theft)
      If IMU do not accept the digital photo that failed the 1:1 comparison, advise the customer that the relevant application can not be considered as there is insufficient connection between the digital photo taken and the most recent digital photo kept on the record.

      Refuse to Consider

      The following is required:
      TMR CSC:
      • Reports the incident to WHS Connect on 1800 867 947
      • Reports the matter to the local police for investigation
      • Advises the IMU of the details of the local police that are investigating the matter.

      QPS:
      • The police will be asked to undertake a local investigation. The IMU will provide all available information surrounding the determination of the document.
