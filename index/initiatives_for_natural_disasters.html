Initiatives for Natural Disasters (103)

Initiatives for natural disasters
What it isIn the event of a natural disaster, TMR may provide disaster assistance for Queenslanders impacted by the disaster by enacting the TMR Natural Disaster Assistance Package.

Assistance may be provided to people in areas where the Personal Hardship Assistance Scheme (PHAS) has been activated by the National Disaster Relief and Recovery Arrangements (NDRRA) or the State Disaster Relief Arrangements (SDRA).

The benefits include:
  • Reduced pay terms with no surcharge
  • Exemption from RRRF
  • Extended Dormant Period
  • Fodder Transport by Vehicles with PRIM Concession
  • Extension of Time for COI for heavy vehicles (incl. public transport vehicles)
  • Replacement of lost/damaged Drivers Licences, IA, APA Cards or MLI Cards
  • Replacement Learner log books
  • Replacement AIS Approval, Books of Inspection Certificates
  • Options for lost or damaged infringement notices, for an AIS, Approved Examiner Accreditation Document, Approved Person Accreditation Document, Books of Modification Certificates.

More information about the package and associated procedures is contained under the sections below.
Eligibility RequirementsWhen the TMR Natural Disaster Assistance Package is enacted:
  • An eligibility time period will be set
  • Customers (including Approved Persons) may be eligible if the PHAS has been activated in an area where:
    • They live
    • Their vehicle is garaged
    • Their Approved Inspection Station (AIS) premises is located

Vehicle Registration
Vehicle Registration
    The vehicle registration Natural Disaster Assistance Package provides assistance to persons who reside and/or have a vehicle garaged at an address that has been declared a disaster area.

    Where a customer's vehicle/s is garaged in an area where the PHAS has been activated, and their vehicle registration is due within the eligibility period, the following options apply:
    • option for three or six month registration period with no administration surcharge and
    • an exemption from the fee for late payment of a vehicle registration renewal

    Customers who fall into this category will automatically have the disaster relief qualification applied to the TICA registration record.

    This qualification will automatically allow the customer to select a three, six or twelve month pay term without incurring surcharges or RRRF fees.

    For those eligible customers who meet the above criteria and have already made their registration payment prior to the qualification being applied are entitled to a reimbursement of the RRRF and the surcharge.

    Note:For procedures relating to vehicle registration Refer Below: Procedural Flowcharts

    Transfer
    Transfer
      If a vehicle with a disaster relief qualification is transferred to another person, the disaster relief will:
      • Continue to apply if new registered operator garaging address is also located in an area where PHAS has been activated
      • Be removed if the new registered operator’s garaging address is not located in an area where the PHAS has been activated

    Exemption from RRRF where payment has not been made
    Exemption from RRRF where payment has not been made
      Registered operators eligible for the Natural Disaster Assistance Package are exempt from payment of the RRRF for renewals due within the eligibility period.
      Apply Disaster Relief (TICA)

    Reduced payterm
    Reduced payterm
      Registered operators eligible for the natural disaster relief package are:
      • eligible for three or six month registration periods; and
      • exempt from the surcharge fee for renewals due or new registrations within the eligibility period.
      Note: CTP is charged for the reduced payterm.

      The TMR surcharge exemption will be automatically exempted when the reason DISASTER is used to change the term to 3 or 6 months.

      Customers who are unable to present at a CSC or QGAP can pay a 3 month payterm by contacting the call centre on 13 23 80
      Apply Disaster Relief (TICA)

    Extend dormant seasonal vehicle registration period up to two years
    Extend dormant seasonal vehicle registration period up to two years
      TMR will extend the dormant period for up to a maximum of 2 years if the vehicle with seasonal registration is registered with a garaging address in an area where the PHAS has been activated.

      Note: Applicants must renew the registration for a season within the two year period for TMR to reinstate the registration.

    Options for Fodder Transport by Vehicles with PRIM Concession
    Options for Fodder Transport by Vehicles with PRIM Concession
      Vehicles registered with a PRIM concession can cart fodder for another primary producer who resides in an area where the PHAS has been activated for a natural disaster provided:
      • Only actual transport costs are charged
      • Vehicles comply with legal axle loading
      • Vehicles do not exceed the registered/manufacturers capacity
      • Fodder transported from another state complies with that states road rules.

      Extension of time for COIs
      Extension of time for COIs
        Customers eligible for the Natural Disaster Assistance Package can apply through the SBS for a 2 month Extension of Time (EOT) to comply with the legislative requirement for a COI, or to clear an outstanding defect notice.

        The interim EOT process is managed through SBS and customers must contact the Call Centre number 13 23 90 to access this option.

        This process applies to:
        • all COI inspectable vehicles (TMR & AIS)
        • requests within the eligible period
        • vehicles garage at an address within the PHAS area
        Note:
        1. If the customer directly contacts regional compliance or a CSC they are to be advised to ring the call centre on 13 23 90. However, if the customer is able to send a faxed EOT application to an STI the request can be processed local. This is an option so as to give flexibility to the regions.
        2. If a customer does not recontact TMR there will be no direct follow up action by the Call Centre relating to an outstanding EOT. It is the responsibility of the customer to have a COI or EOT at all time.

Replacement of lost/damaged Drivers Licences, IA, APA Cards or MLI Cards
Replacement of lost/damaged Drivers Licences, IA, APA Cards or MLI Cards
    Until the end of the eligible time period, a customer from a PHAS area can be issued with a free replacement smartcard if their card has been:
    • Lost
    • Damaged
    • Destroyed
    • Any other reason relating to the disaster

    The customer must complete the correct replacement Application form and present suitable EOI. The replacement reason of "Disaster Relief" is to be used. This will exempt any replacement fee.

    The operator is to record in the office use only section of the application form that "the licence was replaced as per Disaster Relief Package."

    If a customer has lost their smartcard, they may have also lost their EOI. All care is to be taken when issuing duplicate licences as per current procedure and staff are to use discretion when dealing with disaster affected customers.

    Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity > Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI

    Note:Before refusing disaster affected customers, please consult with your SASM or Manager.

    Business Exceptions raised due to disaster relief transactions must be checked prior to authorisation.

    Note: No fee to be charged for replacement of lost or damaged Driver Licences/Authorities or APA cards for customers who reside in PHAS areas as they are deemed to have been granted personal hardship assistance.

    Refer: Driver Licence > Open Licences > Specific - Open Licence > Open Licence Guide > Replace > Replacing Licences > When to Replace
Replacement of Lost/Damaged number plates
Replacement of Lost/Damaged number plates
    Until the end of the eligible time period, a customer from a PHAS area can be issued with a free set of replacement standard number plates if their existing plates have been:
    • Lost
    • Damaged
    • Destroyed

    The customer must complete a Standard/Customised Number Plate replacement Application (F3540) form and present suitable EOI. The replacement reason of "Disaster Relief" is to be used to exempt the replacement plate fee.

    The operator is to record in the office use only section of the application form that "number plates were replaced as per Disaster Relief Package."

    Personalised and customised plates:
    • are not exempt from the replacement plate fee
    • can be replaced at full cost to the customer
Learner log books
Learner log books
    A replacement learner log book can be issued free of charge to customers residing in a PHAS area, who have lost or received damage to their learner log book as a result of a natural disaster.
Practical test bookings/expiring learner licence
Practical test bookings/expiring learner licence
    Where a customer has booked a practical test, and due to a natural disaster (eg floods or dangerous weather conditions) has been unable to undertake the test and subsequently their learner licence has expired; consideration may be given to renewing the learner licence, rebooking the practical test free of charge, and if the customer passes, providing a credit to the customer account for the amount of the learner licence for the issue of the provisional licence.

    CSC Managers, Senior Advisors (Service Management) and equivalent agency staff, are approved to make this decision.

    Criteria: Customer must have booked a test, which was unable to be delivered by TMR or the customer was unable to attend due to natural disaster (eg flooding).
Options for lost or damaged Infringement notices
Options for lost or damaged Infringement notices
    Customers may present without an infringement notice. Whilst we are unable to take a VIP payment without the infringement being recorded in TICA, we can take payment for the full amount of the infringement if the customer can provide the following details (to be recorded on receipt):
    • CRN
    • Time of Offence
    • Date of Offence
    • Road or Street where offence was committed
    • Registration of Vehicle being driven at time of offence
    • Make, Model and Colour of vehicle driven at time of offence
    • Fine amount (if amount is unknown, please refer to your SASM or Manager)
    • Offence committed

    The customer may know most of the above and the more details they can provide the easier it is to match once the infringement is recorded in TICA.

    The total of the fine is to be paid into the customer account and all the above details recorded in the text box with the "As per Disaster Relief Package" also added to the text. CSCs to keep a duplicate copy of the receipt and perform regular checks to enable the payment to be applied to the correct infringement.

    For customers who are unable to present at a CSC or QGAP Office:

    For lost or damaged infringement notices, please contact 13 23 80 for assistance.

    Call centre staff will check that the infringement exists, then provide PRN or infringement notice number and refer the customer to online services for payment using a credit card (as per standard procedure for lost or damaged infringement notice).

    Refer: Offence Management > Infringement Notices > The How To: Offence Management > Pay an Infringement Notice

Industry and Operator Authorisation
Industry and Operator Authorisation

    There are a variety of accreditation schemes managed by Industry and Operator Authorisation, within Customer Services, Safety and Regulation Division for which relief is available to accredited operators affected by natural disasters.

    This includes:
    • Dangerous Goods Driver Licence
    • AIS Approval
    • Books of Inspection Certificates for an AIS
    • Approved Examiner Accreditation Document
    • Approved Person Accreditation Document
    • Books of Modification Certificates
    • Tow Truck Driver Industry Authority
    • Tow Truck Assistant Industry Authority
    • Tow Truck Towing Authority
    • Pilot Vehicle Driver Industry Authority
    • Escort Vehicle Driver Industry Authority
    • Pilot/Escort Movement Record Book
    • Traffic Controller Industry Authority
    • Accredited Driver Trainer or Rider Trainer Industry Authority.
    If an eligible customer from a PHAS area applies to replace any of the above items which were damaged or destroyed during the incident then these products are to be issued free.

    Customers will be required to satisfy all of the other existing application requirements and must (if possible) return the damaged or destroyed products at the time of application or complete a statutory declaration if the products have been lost. Operators will be required to select the credit action in TICA (Miscellaneous Customer Product Hub) against the relevant product. This will place a credit in the customer’s account. The new product can be paid for using the credit in the customer’s account.

    This action should only be taken for items that are to be replaced and not to provide refunds for damaged/destroyed books or accreditation documents.

    Staff are to record the details of the products being replaced as well as the new book numbers on the application form and note that the products were replaced free of charge as part of the disaster package.

    Completed applications for replacement of the following items are to be sent to the     AIS Administrator or AP Administrator:
    • AIS Approval
    • Approved Examiner Accreditation Document
    • Approved Person Accreditation Document

    All other replacement applications are filed as per normal CSC procedure.

    Replacement Q-Ride RSP Approval Certificates and Q-Ride Competency Declaration Certificate books are available at no cost for the customer by emailing the Q-Ride Administrator

    If you have any questions please contact Industry Licensing Policy Unit.

Disability Parking
Disability Parking
    Disability Parking Permit holders residing in declared disaster areas will receive an additional 3 month extension in which to apply for a renewal of their permit. This means that permit holders in these areas will have 6 months from their permit expiry date to apply for a renewal of their permit and will not need to make a new application or pay the application fee.

    Applicants required to complete Part B of F4814, are not exempt from the requirement for Part B of F4814 to be completed by either a medical practitioner/or occupational therapist.

    The application can be lodged at a Customer Service Centre or mailed to Disability Parking Permit Scheme
