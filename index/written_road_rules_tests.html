      Taking the Test
      PrepL
      PrepL
        Eligibility to take the test
        Eligibility to take the test
          • Applicants may only do PrepL for a C Class licence
          • Applicants may enrol online or in-person at a CSC.
      Written Test (At a CSC/QGAP)
      Written Test (At a CSC/QGAP)
        Eligibility to take the test
        Eligibility to take the test
          • Minimum age to sit the written test for C Class Learner is 16 years.
          • Applicants may only take the road rules test for those classes of vehicle which they are eligible to apply for, but may only take one test per day.
          • Applicants for motorcycle written test must :
            • Have completed a Q-Ride pre-learner course or been granted an exemption from it;
            • Hold a current Queensland provisional, probationary or open licence;
            • Have held a valid class C (or heavy vehicle) provisional, probationary or open licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years;
            OR
            • Hold a valid class RE or R equivalent provisional, probationary or open licence from:
              • a non-recognised overseas jurisdiction or
              • an Experienced Driver Recognition country if the applicant is under 25 years of age.
          • Applicants must pass a 1:1 facial image comparison before sitting any written road rules test.
        Test conditions
        Test conditions
          Written Road Rules tests are taken under closed book exam conditions. This means that the customer must:
          • Have their mobile phone turned off
          • Not use a dictionary or have any other books or notes with them
          • Not to enter into any discussion with another person once they have been issued with the test

          Prior to undertaking the test, the customer must be advised that if they are seen cheating or doing any of the above they will have their tests removed and a fail recorded. They will not be eligible to a refund of the road rules test fee.
      Online Motorcycle Knowledge Test
      Online Motorcycle Knowledge Test
        Eligibility to take the test online
        Eligibility to take the test online
          The motorcycle knowledge test may be undertaken online. To be eligible to undertake the online test a person must:
          • Have completed a Q-Ride pre-learner course or been granted an exemption from it.
          • Hold a current Queensland provisional, probationary or open licence;
          • Have held a valid class C (or heavy vehicle) provisional, probationary or open licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years.
          • Have a valid digital image set

          Overseas RE or R holders
          Overseas RE or R holders
            Overseas RE and R equivalent licence holders who are from:
            • A Non-recognised overseas jurisdiction; OR
            • An Experienced Driver Recognition country (if the applicant is under 25 years of age)
            must complete a motorcycle written test as they cannot do the online test.

            Note:If the customer transfers an overseas RE or R equivalent licence after having previously obtained a Queensland class C licence, they must also complete a motorcycle written test. They cannot do the online test.


        Online Test Conditions
        Online Test Conditions
          • Only 1 test may be undertaken each day
          • Test has a 2 hour time limit. A fail will be recorded if the test times out
          • If test is passed and the fee is paid online, a new licence may be sent to the address recorded by TMR. It is the customer's responsibility to ensure the address is current.

      Recording Test Results
      Recording Test Results
        Results of all written tests (both passes and fails) must be recorded on TICA on the day the test was taken.

        PrepL will automatically record a Pass prerequisite in TICA when the PrepL test is passed.

        The online motorcycle test will be automatically updated at the time of the pass or fail result as well.

        If a motorcycle test is timed out, the result will be recorded the next time the customer attempts to undertake a test. The fail result will be applied effective from the date the timed out test was initiated.

      Online Motorcycle Knowledge Test - Adding the class RE result
      Online Motorcycle Knowledge Test - Adding the class RE result
        If a customer passes and pays for the test online they may be eligible to have the class RE learner automatically added to their licence and a new licence sent to them in the mail.

        The class RE learner will not be automatically added if:
        • the prescribed fee is not paid online*
        • the licence is expired, suspended or cancelled (class will be added once the licence is reissued)
        • it is less than 6 weeks before the expiry date of their licence (class will be added upon renewal^)
        • the customer's existing licence displays a 'M' condition and a current medical certificate is not recorded*
        • the customer does not have a valid digital image set*

        *Notes:Where a customer has passed the online knowledge test but the prescribed fee is not paid online, the pass result will be recorded in the system and the customer will be required to attend a driver licence issuing centre to have the class RE learner added to their licence.

        Customers without a valid digital image set may take the test online, however, must attend a driver licence issuing centre to have their digital image captured and the class RE learner added to their licence.

        Where the customer's existing licence displays an M condition, and a current medical certificate is not recorded, the customer will be required to attend a driver licence issuing centre with a current medical certificate to have the class RE learner added to their licence.

        ^Notes:Eligible licences can be renewed online.

        Refer: Driver Licence > 6. Learner Licences > Learner Licence Guide > Renew > Renewing Licences > Renewing on the internet

      Online Practice Road Rules Test
        Customers are able to practice the written road rules test on the Department of Transport and Main Roads website.

        The Online Practice Road Rules Test provides an interactive environment for customers to practise the test and increase their road rules knowledge.

        The practice tests contain randomly generated questions to ensure all tests taken are unique and feedback is provided on the correct answer as the test progresses.
