SC Exemptions (3037)

Vehicles Exempt a Safety CertificateThe following are exempt from requiring a SC
  • Light trailers up to <=.75t ATM
  • Conditionally registered vehicles
  • Wheelchairs
  • Tractor Based Cranes with Reg Categories of MMAC or SVMM
Exemptions at New Business and Transfer
New Business
New Business
    • New vehicle (including trailers) that has not travelled more than 500km and has never been previously registered in Queensland, another state or foreign country.
    • Dealer
    • Street Rod (TAC applicable)
    • The intended garage address is on a Queensland island and the island does not have an AIS to inspect that type of SC vehicle
    • Garage address of the vehicle is in an Exempt or Partially Exempt Area: Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Garage Address > Requirements > Acceptable Garage Address Documents

    Note:
    • Personally imported vehicles can not be considered 'new' as the vehicle must have been owned and operated by the owner in a foreign country previously to be classed as a personal import.

    List of Exempt or Partially Exempt Areas for vehicles other than Motorcycles and Trailers
    Remote Locations for Motorbikes and Trailers
    Remote Locations for Motorbikes and Trailers
      Motorbikes, trailers and caravan trailers (>.75tATM and </=3.5tATM) with a garage address in an area 50km or more from the nearest AIS authorised to inspect that type of vehicle are exempt.
Transfer
Transfer

    When a registered vehicle is being sold, the requirement for a Safety Certificate is set by garage address of the disposer of the vehicle eg disposer does not need to provide a SC if in an exempt area therefore the acquirer does not need to produce one when submitting the transfer.

    Individuals
    Individuals
      • Deceased Estates:
        • Beneficiary
        • Next of kin
        • Person with Letters of Administration
        • Executor
        • The Estate of
        • Surviving registered operator
      • Between spouses (including separated married couples and registered partners, until such time that the divorce or application to end the registered relationship is finalised).
      • Between current de facto partners. (De facto partner means either 1 of 2 persons, whether of the same or opposite sex, they are not married to each other, or related by family, they are living together, on a genuine domestic basis. NOT 2 persons who are co-tenants).
      • To/from joint names provided one registered operator remains the same and operates the vehicle both before and after the transfer (refer warning below)
      • Transfer to a non-beneficiary immediately prior to cancellation (where vehicle is being cancelled but executor, administrator or next of kin has advised that cheque is to be issued to a non-beneficiary)

      Note: Refer to Transfer Transaction Requirement Checklist for detailed list of exemptions. Navigation Path: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Transfer Transaction Requirements Checklist
    Organisations
    Organisations
      Company
      Company
        • Company merger; and
        • Between company and subsidiary company

          Company is to provide a declaration (on their letterhead) stating that a company merger has taken place and provide the names of the companies and the ACN's involved.
        • Transfers from a company name to a spouse / de-facto listed on a court issued document (with court seal) or a recognised agreement under the Property Law Act 1974, section 266 (no court seal). For requirements, Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Duty > Payment of Duty > Duty Exemptions > Family Gift > Requirements

      Business
      Business
        • If the second customer is to be changed to the spouse of the current registered operator
        • If the owner remains as the registered operator and the business name is being added or removed
        • If business is owned by more that one person and registered operator is changing to a different owner

      Trust
      Trust
        • Between registered operators name to Family Trust (vice versa) with registered operator remaining the same

        Transfers between individual trustees must be treated as transfers between individual persons eg transfer from father to son with the trust remaining as a registered operator is not exempt, transfer from husband to wife with trust remaining would be exempt.

    Caravan/Trailer Dealer
    Caravan/Trailer Dealer
      Transfer of new caravan/trailer from dealer to acquirer.
      SC exempt only if the new caravan/trailer has:
      • Been registered for up to one month
      and
      • Has not left the dealers premises
    Repossession/Return of Vehicle
    Repossession/Return of Vehicle
      • Transferring from registered operator to:
      • Repossessor (Finance Company or private person)
      • Redeemer (must be registered in the same name as prior to the repossession)
      • Leasing Company voluntarily or upon termination of a leasing agreement, not hire-purchase agreement
    Islands
    Islands
      A Safety Certificate is not required for a transfer if the garage address of the vehicle before it was disposed of was on a Queensland Island that does not have an AIS to inspect that type of vehicle.

      Note:This applies regardless of whether the vehicle will be garaged on the island or the mainland after the registration is transferred.
    Remote Locations
    Remote Locations
      Vehicles excluding motorbikes and trailers
      Vehicles excluding motorbikes and trailers
        A Safety Certificate is not required for a transfer if the garage address of the vehicle before it was disposed of was either:
        • in an exempt shire; or
        • was in a partially exempt area.
        Refer Below: List of Exempt or Partially Exempt Areas for vehicles other than Motorbikes and Trailers

        Note:This applies regardless of whether the vehicle will be garaged in the exempt area after the registration is transferred.

      Motorbikes and Trailers
      Motorbikes and Trailers
        A Safety Certificate is not required for a transfer if the garage address of the vehicle before it was disposed of was 50km or more from the nearest AIS authorised to inspect that type of vehicle.

        Note:This applies regardless of whether the vehicle will be garaged in the exempt area after the registration is transferred.

    WARNING
    WARNING
      Adding a registered operator is Safety Certificate exempt.

      This should only be done if the original registered operator continues to use the vehicle after transfer into joint names.

      The exemption must not be used as a means of disposing of a vehicle, when the customer has no intention of continuing to use the vehicle. In this scenario, a valid SC is to be supplied at the time of transfer.

      There have been instances where disposers of vehicles have been advised to circumvent SC requirements by adding the acquirers name as a joint registered operator then removing their own name later. This action is not to be encouraged as it is contrary to transfer inspection requirements.
