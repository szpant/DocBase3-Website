Plate Management (2815)


InterACTInterACT is the plate inventory management system used to monitor the status and location of standard number plates.

If you have access to interACT, you can log in here: https://interact5.licensys.com
Obtaining accessTo obtain access to interACT operators must complete a Add a New User Account (INT01) form (available in interACT). The new user's SA(SM) or Manager must forward the application to Plates Administration and indicate which location/s the user needs access to. Once processed by Plates Administration, the original copy of the form must be stored by the manager in the operator's personnel file.

Once access is granted you will only be able to view the plate stock details of the location/s assigned to you.

Annual Stocktake
Annual Stocktake
    An annual number plate stocktake must be completed for end of year financial reporting purposes by 31 March. This stocktake covers stock held at all:
    • CSCs
    • TMR Agents
    • Dealerships on an Easy Plates or Easy Business scheme

    Plates Administration will advise of the final date when the results of the annual stocktake need to be completed and submitted.

    Any Customer Service Centres that are not responsible for Motor Dealers or Agents will only have to complete a stocktake for their own centre.

    For further information Refer: Admin > 4. Stock Control > The How To Stock Control > Stock Control Procedures > Conduct Number Plate Stocktake

    Other Stocktakes
    Other Stocktakes
      InterACT Manual Inventory Transactions (21D-1030) Report
      The InterACT Manual Inventory Transaction (21D-1030) report is required to be run monthly by each CSC/QGAP by SASM/QGAP Manager to ensure that for each written off plate for the month, there is an accompanying Form 3819 with RD signature and the relevant SAP journal has been processed.

      As per audit requirements, number plate stocktakes must be conducted monthly by all CSCs and TMR Agents.

      Dealerships on an Easy Plates or Easy Business scheme must have a number plate stocktake conducted every 6 months when performance evaluations are being completed. CSC Managers are responsible for ensuring their allocated dealerships have had a performance evaluation conducted every 6 months by a nominated CSC Client Liaison Officer (CLO).

      The person completing the motor dealer performance evaluation does not need to sign the plate stocktake printout. This is because a copy of the plate stock report is attached to the final evaluation and the Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation (F4811) form must be signed by the person conducting the evaluation as well as the Manager/SASM.

      For more information Refer: Vehicle Rego > 01. DTMR Agencies > Dealer Registration Schemes/Arrangements

    Stock Ageing Analysis
    Stock Ageing Analysis
      A Stock Ageing Analysis must be conducted every 6 months at all locations that carry TMR number plates. CSC and QGAP Managers are responsible for ensuring that a Stock Ageing Analysis is conducted on behalf of all associated Motor Dealers and QGAP Agencies.
      Allocated Timeframes
      Allocated Timeframes

        Region
        Biannual report submission

        May and November
        Central
        January and July
        Southern
        April and October
        SEQ North
        February and August
        SEQ South
        March and September

        Number plate stock rotations requested by Plates Administration, are to be actioned within 28 days.
      Conducting a Stock Ageing Analysis
      Conducting a Stock Ageing Analysis
        To conduct a Stock Ageing Analysis, print a 21D-1040 Inventory Ageing by Plate Type and Location report from the Management suite of reports in interACT. The 21D-1040 report should be printed for that SLOC using 1, 2 and 3 ageing periods.

        If a dealership is identified as holding aged number plates older than 12 months, the following actions must occur:
        • make arrangement to physically remove aged plates from the SLOC
        • email Plates Administration to amend reorder levels for the SLOC
        • transfer plates to your CSC SLOC on interACT

        Note:It may take a few days for interACT to replicate when reorder levels are amended by Plates Administration. As a result, please wait three business days before transferring plates in interACT from the dealership's SLOC to your CSC location.

        The 21D-1040 report, regardless of whether the SLOC is holding aged plates or not, is to be emailed to Plates Administration. Plates Administration will make a determination on whether or not this stock needs to be rotated.

        Note:For CSC’s and QGAPS this plate aging email is then to be printed and filed along with the CSC/QGAP plate stocktake for that month.

        Note:For Dealers this email is to be attached to the Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation (F4811) form. For more information Refer: Vehicle Rego > 01. DTMR Agencies > Dealer Registration Schemes/Arrangements

      Discrepancies
      Discrepancies
        Where differences arise between physical quantities of number plates and quantities recorded in interACT, they must be adjusted so they reflect the physical count.

        When conducting the stocktake it is important to indicate on the physical printout whether a plate is physically present.

        For example:
        Each plate is individually ticked or a bracket that covers each plate number in an unopened box. Where a discrepancy occurs a summary of the action taken must be written beside the relevant plate number.

Transferring plates between locationsPlates can be transferred in or out of locations for reasons such as:
  • Plates held at nominated offices only
  • SLOC requires extra plates (transfer in to build up stock)
  • SLOC does not hold plates (may need plates from time to time, for example, fleet)
  • Plates receipted into incorrect SLOC (needs transferring in because it was sent to incorrect SLOC)
  • SLOC closed down (plates may need to be returned to the supporting CSC)
  • Rotation of plates (move plates in to another SLOC so they can be used up)
Plates can only be transferred if their status is "in stock" or "in transit."

Instructions for transferring stock can be found in the Inventory Management section of the interACT User Guide.
Manual Stock RequestA manual stock request is only completed for a one-off order (such as when a Dealer has a fleet of cars coming in and need a certain number of plates to allocate to that shipment). A manual order should not be used regularly.

Instructions for manually ordering plates can be found in the Placing an Order section of the interACT User Guide.
Writing off platesPlates may need to written off for reasons such as:
  • Plate stolen (police report required to be filed at the CSC or QGAP office)
  • Plate lost in centre
  • Plate not found at Stocktake or Audit
  • Plate damaged
  • Plate out of warranty (Refer to Plates Administration)

Refer: Admin > 4. Stock Control > Stock Control Guide > Lost/Damaged and Expired/Unusable Stock
and
Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Write-Offs > Who can write off public money/property > Motor Vehicle Plate Inventory Losses

Instructions for writing off stock can be found in the Inventory Management section of the interACT User Guide and DocBase Refer: Admin > 4. Stock Control > The How To Stock Control > Stock Control Procedures > Write Off Plate Inventory
Manually issuing platesA plate needs to be manually issued when there has been a failure of the interface file to update the plate status in interACT. For example, a plate has been issued in TICA but the interface file has failed to include the plate.

Always check in TICA to ensure the plate has been issued before processing a manual issue. The manual issue will change the status in interACT to 'issued'.

Instructions for manually issuing plates can be found in the Inventory Management section of the interACT User Guide.
Accepting a delivery
Accepting a delivery
Accepting a delivery
    When accepting a delivery please ensure the following is checked:
    • Is the location number, name and address correct?
    • Are the plates in a good condition?
    • Does the number of plates being delivered match the quantity on the item count quantity on the delivery label (eg 1 of 6)*?

    *Note:Consignment notes are not provided by Star Track.

    If any of these requirements are not met the delivery cannot be accepted.

    If plates are incorrectly accepted please contact the Plates Administrator.
User ReviewA review of all users of interACT must be completed by a Manager, SA(SM) or designated person of every Storage Location (SLOC) quarterly. The 41D-1130 User List report must be run listing all users for the location.

Access should be removed for any user who is no longer required to have access. Once this happens the report should be re-run, exported to a PDF or Excel and printed. The report will then need to be signed by the CSC Manager, SA(SM) or designated person.

Copies of the signed report must be retained by the SLOC (electronic or hard copy) and made available for audit purposes.

The 41D-1130 report does not need to be sent to the Plates Administrator unless accompanying a request to remove user/s.
Setting up new locations Only the Plates Administration and Rego Easy Unit can set up and maintain locations in interACT.

Instructions for adding/editing a location can be found in the Locations Management section of the interACT User Guide.
