Entitlement to Work in Australia (5377)

Entitlement to Work in Australia
What it isA person is entitled to work in Australia if they are:
  • an Australian Citizen, or
  • a permanent resident of Australia, or
  • a New Zealand citizen who is the holder of a special category visa as defined by the Migration Act 1958(Commonwealth), section 32, or
  • entitled, under a visa issued under the Migration Act 1958(Commonwealth) to work in Australia

If the customer does not have evidence of their entitlement to work in Australia then their application for a Driver Authorisation cannot be accepted.
Evidence of Work Entitlement A person applying for DA (new and renewal) is required to provide documentary evidence of their entitlement to work in Australia before their application can be accepted.
Standard evidence of entitlement to work in Australia
Standard evidence of entitlement to work in Australia
    The following documents evidence a customer’s entitlement to work in Australia:
    • A green Medicare card
    • Repatriation Health Card – Issued by Dept of Vet Affairs – For all conditions (Gold Card (must be current)
    • Australian Birth Certificate
    • Australian Citizenship Certificate
    • Australian Declaratory Certificate
    • Australian Passport (current or expired <2 years)
    • Australian Extract of Citizenship
    • Australian Naturalisation Certificate
    • Customer Identification Declaration – Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander (F4168)
    • Department of Home Affairs Certificate of Evidence of Residence Status
    • DIBP Visa Evidence card (must have PL056 visa label)
    • DIBP Immicard Permanent Resident Evidence (PRE)
    • Qld Adoption Extract
    • Qld Birth Extract
Non-Standard evidence of entitlement to work in Australia
Non-Standard evidence of entitlement to work in Australia
    Verification of a persons entitlement to work in Australia is required when the following documents are presented:
    • Foreign passport
    • Immicard - Evidence of Immigration Status (EIS)
    • Immicard - Australian Migration Status (AMS)
    • Visa Evidence card
    • Titre de Voyage or Document for Travel to Australia
Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO)
What is itThe Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO) system is administered by the Department of Home Affairs.

VEVO is used to verify a persons entitlement to work in Australia.

Customer Requirements
To undertake a VEVO check the customer must authorise the search by completing an Authority to Check a Visa Holder's Work Entitlement (F4595) and provide documentation.
For a list of the satisfactory documents, Refer above: Evidence of Work Entitlement > Non-Standard evidence of entitlement to work in Australia

      Work Limitations
      Where an applicant shows a work limitation (eg. 8102, 8105, 8107 etc) the department must recognise the applicants work entitlement, irrelevant of the type of work limitation. The department is not required to assess the work limitations. It is the customer’s responsibility to work within their limitations and the employer’s ongoing responsibility to monitor their employees work entitlement.

      Work entitlement expires within one month of application
      Work entitlement expires within one month of application
        Where a customer’s work entitlement expires within one month of the application date the customer must provide evidence of a pending visa application such as a letter or email from DIBP (see examples below, also referred to as a bridging visa). The DIBP letter or email must be sighted by the operator and the details and information recorded on the driver authorisation application must be verified.

        Some migration or permanent residency visa applications can take a long time to process, this could mean that the letter or email provided by the applicant is dated some months ago. The document is still acceptable provided the VEVO result confirms the person has an entitlement to work in Australia on the day of lodgement.

    Unable to identify customer
      If VEVO result is unable to identify the customer, check that all details are correct. If correct complete the VEVO online referral form. VEVO will automatically direct you to the online referral form see example below.

      The result from the online referral is sent directly to the Passenger Transport Authorisation Unit (PTA). An email must be sent to       Passenger Transport Authorisation Unit advising that an online referral has been submitted along with the customers details (name and CRN) and details of the requesting officer.

      Referrals are generally completed within two business days. PTA will forward the results to the requesting location once received. If you have not received the result within five business days contact Passenger Transport Authorisation Unit

    Refusing to accept an applicationThe DA application cannot be accepted if the customer:
    • does not have an entitlement to work on the day of lodgement, or
    • cannot provide the letter/email (bridging visa) from Department of Home Affairs, or
    • Information recorded on the letter/email do not correspond with the customer details.
