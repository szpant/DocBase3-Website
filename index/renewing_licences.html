Renewing Licences (318)

About licence renewal
About RenewalsA licence may be renewed for any Licence Type (Open / Provisional / Probationary/ Restricted / Learner).

A learner licence may be renewed:
  • On its own
  • Automatically if attached to a Provisional, Probationary or Open licence.
When to RenewUnder normal circumstances a driver licence is able to be renewed up to 6 weeks before the expiry date of the licence. An applicant may apply for renewal of their licence at any time prior to their current licence expiry, or up to 5 years after expiry.

Notification that a driver licence is due for renewal is shown in the customer's My Account portal.

Early renewal prior to 6 weeks (Prov/ Prob/ Open only)
Early renewal prior to 6 weeks (Prov/ Prob/ Open only)
    Customer may choose to renew their licence outside of the normal 6 week renewal period for many reasons such as but not limited to:
    • The customer is travelling interstate or overseas at the time their licence is due to expire.
    • The customer is living in an area where there is no TMR Customer Service Centre.
    • Another reason that may suit the individual circumstances of the customer.

    Renewing outside the 6 week renewal period

    Up to 6 Months
    Operators can renew a licence up to 6 months prior to the expiry date using the normal renewal process however they must select an override reason in TICA. The only override reason an operator may select for an early renewal is 'Interstate or Overseas Travel'. This reason should be selected regardless of the circumstances surrounding the early renewal.

    More than 6 months
    More than 6 months prior to expiry, surrender licence with action reason Interstate or Overseas Travel and issue new licence. The unexpired portion is 'fee free'.
When to Renew instead of ReplaceAn operator may renew, rather than replace, a damaged, destroyed, lost or stolen licence upon the customer’s request, if it is within 12 months of the expiry date.

A SASM/CSC Manager should approve the renewal rather than replacement of a damaged, destroyed, lost or stolen licence if the request is more than 12 months from the expiry date of the driver licence. The SASM/CSC manager should consider the individual circumstances of the applicant when deciding whether to approve the request.

Refer to process to renewing outside the 6 week renewal period.

    Note:Staff should not encourage Learner licence holders to renew their licence rather than replace as Learners only have a three year renewal option and may upgrade to a Provisional licence prior to the current licence expiring.

Where to Renew
  • TMR Online Services (Refer: Renewing online section below for eligibility requirements)
  • TMR Customer Service Centre
  • Police Station that issues Driver Licences
  • Queensland Government Agency Program office (QGAP) that issues Driver Licences
  • TMR Internet Site (Refer: Renewing on the internet below for eligibility requirements)

The Notice
Renewal NoticeOne of the following notices is sent to the customer's postal address (if available - otherwise residential), or email address if they are signed up to receive e-notices, approximately 6 weeks prior to expiry of their current licence:
  • Driver licence renewal notice (F3004); or
  • Queensland Driver licence renewal notice (F4750) (indicating they can renew online)

A renewal notice:
  • will not be issued if the customer's licence is cancelled due to a disqualification.
  • will be issued if the customer's licence is suspended, however they won't be able to renew until the suspension period has ended/been removed.

If the customer has signed up to receive e-reminders an email will be sent to them three days prior to the expiry date if they haven't yet renewed their licence.

The email reminder notice will include:
  • Driver Licence expiry date
  • Link to renew Driver Licence online service
Sanction/Suspension and failure to renew
Suspended Licence/
Customer Sanction		A licence cannot be renewed
  • During a period of suspension
  • If the holder has been disqualified in another state or country from holding a driver licence
  • If the licence has been cancelled
  • If there is an outstanding restriction (e.g. mail sanction, image restriction)
Failure to RenewA person who drives after their licence has expired may be charged with unlicensed driving. In addition, their insurance may be void if involved in an accident.
Renewing online
Renewing online
Who can renew on the internet
Who can renew on the internet
    Customers who:
    • hold a licence that is current or is expired by not more than 5 years
    • pay by credit card (American Express, Mastercard and Visa only a surcharge will apply)
    • have a current medical certificate recorded in TICA
    • need to add or remove their S Condition
    • have any other licence condition that is not changing
    • meet all other eligibility criteria

    The renewal term that the licence holder is able to renew for will also be determined by the validity period of their current image set.

    Note:A customer may have been eligible at the time the renewal notice was generated but may subsequently become ineligible due, for example, to a licence suspension.
Who cannot renew on the internet
Who cannot renew on the internet
    Customers who:
    • have had their licence cancelled
    • have been disqualified in another state or country from holding a driver licence
    • have a licence suspension at the time of the renewal
    • require a name change
    • have a consular qualification in TICA
    • have a suppressed customer record
    • hold a licence that need a condition added or removed (excluding S conditions)
    • have a restriction on their CRN e.g. mail sanction or image restriction
    • do not have a current digital image set
    • wish to renew their licence for a period that extends past the expiry date of their digital image set
    • have a NOAC restriction Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer > Customer Restrictions > NOAC
    • have a NOLR restriction Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer > Customer Restrictions > NOLR
