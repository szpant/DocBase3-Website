Use of Managerial Discretion (6036)


About Managerial Discretion
What it isManagerial discretion is the ability for a manager to make a choice about whether to act or not act, to approve or not approve or approve with conditions taking into account all relevant information.
When can it be usedDiscretion can be applied only where it is lawful (ie does not contravene legislation) and as part of:
  • Fulfilling responsibilities to ensure the efficient and effective management of the Queensland Government
  • Taking action or making decisions under TMR specific Legislation
Rules for Applying Discretion
Must be LawfulThe decision made must not contravene legislation, discretion can only be applied to policy, if it is a clear legislative requirement no discretion can be applied.
Who can exercise discretionFor the purposes of this document discretion is primarily exercised by the manager. Where the manager is not on-site or available and the customer is present, discretion can be applied by the next senior officer at the location.
Supporting Resources Queensland Government Good Decision Making Guide
Common situations in which discretion may be exercised
Registration
Registration Renewal - Financial Hardship
Registration Renewal - Financial Hardship
    Refer: Vehicle Rego > Renew > Variable Term Facility > When VTF can be applied
Registration Renewal - RRRF Exemption
Registration Renewal - RRRF Exemption
    A manager may approve a RRRF exemption that is not on the approved list where the reason for the late payment could not have been foreseen by the customer or an error was made by TMR, QPS, QGAPS or Rego Easy dealer.

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 07. Renewal > Registration Renewal Reinstatement Fee (RRRF) > Rules > Exemption Guidelines
Collector Plates
Collector Plates
    Where a applicant for a collector plate is causing disruptions to the provision of services to other customers, the manager may refuse the applicant access to collector's plates and if required request they leave the premises. The access can be refused verbally and it is recommended that a detailed file note be made in the Managers Diary (or alternative device) be made or an incident report completed if warranted.

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 11. Plates > Standard Plates > Rules > Collector Plates
Proof of Origin
Proof of Origin
    If a customer is unable to complete a Statement of Acquisition the office manager must be satisfied that they are entitled to be recorded as the registered owner of the vehicle. They must be able to provide sufficient details to support their claim as well as provide a satisfactory reasons for why they are unable to provide the documentation.

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Proof of Origin (Vehicle)
Refund of Personalised Plates Fees
Refund of Personalised Plates Fees
      If a customer changes their mind about the details (excluding plate number) of a plate or they no longer want the plate the manager has the discretion to either refund the entire amount paid or retain the manufacturing and plate fees and refund the difference.

      A full refund can be provided when the plate is in a:
      • Sell status
      • Ordered status and the Contracts Team are able to stop manufacture.

      If manufacture is unable to be stopped or the plates are in an issued status a partial refund can be granted upon surrender of the unwanted plates.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 11. Plates > Personalised Plates Guide > Rules > Corrections > Customer Error
Refund of Registration Fees (excluding cancellations)
Refund of Registration Fees (excluding cancellations)
    A refund of overpaid registration fees should be made where:
    • Operator error has been identified
    • A genuine error has been made, eg vehicle incorrectly registered was not corrected when a transfer has occurred and the new registered operator has been paying the incorrect fees

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Identifiers and Descriptors > Corrections > Corrections of Record

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 03. Types of Registration > Concessional Registration > Rules > Corrections of Record
Refund of Registration Renewal Payment Guidelines
Dispute registration transfer online
Dispute registration transfer online
    If a customer presents at a CSC and advises that a vehicle has been incorrectly transferred into their name and the disposer disputes this it is the managers role to investigate and make a decision regarding the transfer.
    Supporting documentation from the disposer
    Supporting documentation from the disposer
      If the disposer confirms that the transfer has been processed correctly they should provide supporting documentation:
      • a statutory declaration confirming:
      • the registration is not in their possession, and that they sold it to the acquirer
      • the acquirer's name
      • the acquirer's receipt of purchase
      • date, place and time of settlement of the transfer
      • any other documents to show the transfer occurred such as a copy of a cheque or electronic funds transfer
      • any emails, text messages or letters between the parties
    Supporting documentation from the incorrect customer
    Supporting documentation from the incorrect customer
      The incorrect customer must provide supporting documentation, including:
      • a statutory declaration confirming:
      • they were not involved in a transfer of the registration
      • the registration number, date of transaction and date of sale (obtained from the notification email)
      • they are not and have never been in possession of the vehicle
      • if applicable - information to show the documents/information provided by the disposer are incorrect
      • any other supporting documentation that can be obtained
    Disposer cannot be contacted
    Disposer cannot be contacted
      In circumstances where the disposer cannot be contacted, a statutory declaration should be obtained from the incorrect customer advising:
      • they were not involved in the transfer of the registration
      • the registration number, date of transaction and date of sale (obtained from the notification email)
      • they are not and have never been in possession of the vehicle

    Note:A Statutory Declaration can also be signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a conveyancer.

    The CSC Manager is to determine the appropriate action to be taken. The customers should be informed of the action taken.

    If a decision is not able to be made by the Manager the matter should be referred to Procedures and Training for discussion with Registration Policy or Vessel Standards.
Reinstatement of Registration
Reinstatement of Registration
    A Manager has the discretion to reinstate an auto-cancelled registration if payment was not made due to extenuating personal reasons.

    Applications must be made in writing and detail the circumstances and may be accompanied by any supporting documentation.

    Each case must be assessed on it's merits and similar to the RRRF exemptions examples of extenuating circumstances may include:
    • Military personal deployed overseas
    • Unplanned hospitalisation/medical incapacitation
    • Natural disaster
    • Victim of domestic and family violence
    • Departmental error

    Extenuating circumstances do not include:
    • Change of Address
    • Insufficient money
    • Did not receive vehicle registration notice
    • Away on holidays
    • Paid incorrect vehicle renewal
    • Forgot to pay
    • Thought some one else paid it

    If approved the processing officer must use the reinstate action with the reason Manager Approval. A copy of the letter with the Managers approval marked on the letter must be retained for filing.

    The customer should be advised that the registration period RRRF is payable.
Driver Licensing
Forfeiting and reinstatement of practical driving test fee
Forfeiting and reinstatement of practical driving test fee
    A customer is entitled to a refund where the test was either failed/forfeited/cancelled for a reason that was not foreseeable by the applicant. This includes:
    • Cancellation of testing by TMR
    • Late or no show due to weather conditions eg flooding
    • Test vehicle registered to another person (other than a driving school vehicle) and applicant was not aware of a minor fault (as defined in the QSafe Manual)
    • Customer was not required to undertake a practical driving test
Exemptions from the Cooling Off Period
Exemptions from the Cooling Off Period
    An exemption from the cooling off period may be granted if the customer forfeited the test fee for a reason other than:
    • Failure of test
    • Fail to give adequate notice
    • Major defective vehicle
    • Fail to appear for a reason that was foreseeable by the applicant
    • Driving school vehicle did not have a current COI or EOT
Customer
Acceptance of Cheques
Acceptance of Cheques
    Where a customer has a history of dishonoured cheques it is at the managers discretion whether the customer be allowed to use this payment method for future transactions.
    Consideration must be given to:
    • Number of dishonours - a customer who has had two or more dishonoured cheques in a two year period is required to use an alternative payment method
    • Customer type - if customer is a TMR Rego Easy dealer and has a history of dishonoured cheques consideration must be given to removing that customer from the scheme
    • Transaction type - transactions such as driver licensing where the failure to honour a dishonoured cheque is the voiding of the licence carry more risk as the person may have a physical product but not be licensed. A customer who has had two or more dishonoured cheques for a licensing transaction should be required to use an alternative payment method.

    Note:TMR is currently progressing the removal of cheques as a payment method, no implementation date has been provided.

    Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer > System Information > Qualifications, Restrictions & Text > Customer Restrictions > PMET
Unsigned Bank Cards
Unsigned Bank Cards
    A customer may sign an unsigned bank card in the presence of an operator only when they have another identity document that contains a signature. This document does not need to be a Category A or B document but must contain the customers name and signature. Once signed the bank card can be used as an acceptable Category B document.
Release of Information
Providing copies of documents to QPS or Interstate Authorities
Providing copies of documents to QPS or Interstate Authorities
    A manager has the discretion to provide copies of registration documents to an authorised person. This discretion should only be exercised when the manager has either sighted relevant evidence of identity or confirmed the legitimacy of an email contact.

    Refer: Release of Information > 6. Vehicle Registration > Vehicle Rego Release of Information >Types > Requests for Originals and Copies of Documents
Marine Licensing
Boatsafe Statement of Competency (SOC)
Boatsafe Statement of Competency (SOC)
    Managers can exercise discretion and consider the customer's circumstances if they present a SOC the next business day after it expires (i.e. expires on a weekend, or one day after expiry).
Smartcards
Replacement Smartcards
Replacement Smartcards
    In exceptional circumstances Managers can use their discretion to offer a free replacement smartcard to customers. The replacement reason of "Manager Approval" should be selected in TICA.
Evidencing DecisionsWhere the Manager has exercised their discretion, evidence of that decision should be available should the decision be revisited. When recording discretionary decisions the following information should be recorded:
  • Date of decision
  • Customer details
  • Brief outline of situation
  • Reasons for decision
  • Name of any other parties to the decision

This does not have to be lengthy documentation, but must be sufficient to make the decision defensible.

This information can be recorded in a variety of tools such as a Work Diary, electronic document (eg spreadsheet, one note notebook).

Where a transaction is to be performed on the system by a person other than the manager a notation must be made in the Office Use Only authorising the transaction and identifying the decision maker.

Note:A discretionary decision does not need to be "right" as such but it must be defensible and as a result of a reasoned process and made on the information available.
