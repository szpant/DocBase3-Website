Driver/Rider Trainer Guide (1881)


About Driver/Rider Trainers
Definitions
Definitions
Definitions
    Accredited driver trainer
    Means a person who holds an appointment under part 2 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management- Accreditations and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015 as an accredited person with the function of a driver trainer.

    Accredited rider trainer
    Means a person who holds an appointment under part 2 of the Transport Operations (Road use Management - Accreditations and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015 as an accredited person with the function of a rider trainer.

    Q-Ride training
    Q-Ride training includes the delivery of:
    • Pre-learner course - to get an RE Learner Licence,
    • Restricted (RE) course - to get an RE provisional or open licence,
    • Unrestricted (R) course - to get an R provisional or open licence.

    Further Definitions
What it is
Driver trainer accreditation
Driver trainer accreditation
    Driver trainer accreditation authorises an individual to provide pre-licence driver/rider training in Queensland for reward.
Rider trainer accreditation
Rider trainer accreditation
    Rider trainer accreditation authorises an individual to provide motor bike training under the Q-Ride training and assessment scheme.
When required
Driver trainer accreditation
Driver trainer accreditation
    A person must hold accreditation in all cases if the person is providing pre-licence instruction/training in Queensland for reward, this includes benefit, commission, fee, salary and wage.

    Pre-licence instruction refers to the training that is given to a driver/rider on a class of vehicle that the learner is not yet licensed to drive/ride unsupervised.
Rider trainer accreditation
Rider trainer accreditation
    Any individual who provides advice, demonstration, instruction, training or assessment for riding a motorbike through the Q-Ride Scheme must have Rider Trainer Accreditation.

    To deliver Q-Ride the person must be an Accredited Rider Trainer, whereas a person delivering pre-licence motorbike training outside of Q-Ride only needs to be an Accredited Driver Trainer (see table below).

    Q-Ride is a TMR competency based training and assessment scheme for motorbike riders. Q-Ride is an alternative method of assessment to the Q-SAFE practical riding tests offered by TMR.

    Accreditation required
    Role Accreditation level needed
    To deliver pre-licence training for RE and R Classes (outside of Q-Ride)Accredited Driver Trainer for R Class
    To deliver training/assessment through the Q-Ride schemeAccredited Rider Trainer
When not requiredA Driver trainer accreditation/Rider trainer accreditation is not required when providing tuition for current licence holders improving their driving skills (i.e. Defensive Driving Course).

Cancel/Suspend/Void/Expired
    Cancelled
    Cancelled
      If a driver licence is cancelled, the Driver/Rider Trainer Accreditation is:
      • Automatically cancelled;
      • Not to be granted or renewed on application during the cancellation period;
      • To be surrendered and returned if requested by the Chief Executive.

      The applicant will need to re-apply for the Driver/Trainer Accreditation meeting full eligibility requirements once the cancellation period has expired.

      Note:Applicants with a Multiple Industry Authority smartcard which has another authority that is not affected by the cancellation will require a replacement Industry Authority smartcard which is to be issued for the non affected industry authority/s. (eg Tow Truck Assistant or Traffic Controller)

      Refer: Smartcard > Replacement

      The Policy unit will receives a report (IA55) of any driver licence that has been cancelled or suspended while holding a current Industry Authority. If information contained in an IA55 report indicates that action may need to be taken against an Industry Authority Holder, an email will be forwarded to the Manager of the Customer Service Centre closest to the applicant for appropriate action.

      Refer: IA55 report

    Suspended
    Suspended
      If a driver licence is suspended the Industry Authority is:
      • Suspended (applicant is unable to perform the duties of a Driver/Rider Trainer during suspension period);
      • Automatically reinstated on the completion of the driver licence's suspension period;
      • Not surrendered and no action is required on TICA.

    Void
    Void
      If a payment for a Driver/Rider Trainer Accreditation has been dishonoured, the Policy unit will receive advice from finance. The Policy unit will advise the applicant that they must pay the relevant fees immediately. Any follow up action required due to non payment of fees will be handled by the Policy unit.

    Expired
    Expired
      If the customer submits an application for renewal and the Driver/Rider Trainer accreditation has expired more than 3 months, the application is to be a new issue application with full requirements including a new CrimTrac search and fee.
    Note:Drivers returning from drink driving suspensions/cancellations who have been issued with a probationary licence, are not eligible for a driver trainer accreditation regardless of Magistrates Court Order authorising otherwise. Applicants must hold an open licence for 12 months before they can apply.
    Refer: Industry Authority > 03. Driver and Rider Trainer > Driver and Rider Trainer Guide > Eligibility requirements > Eligibility Criteria > Licence
Surrender
Surrender
    The Driver/Rider Trainer accreditation must be surrendered and returned for the following reasons:
    • Refused on renewal of application;
    • Merged;
    • Multiple Industry Authority holder (if removing one or more of the current authority types);
    • Cancelled.

    The Driver/Rider Trainer accreditation can be surrendered for the following reasons:
    • No longer required (voluntary);
    • On behalf of the deceased.

    Note:Multiple card holders wanting to voluntarily surrender one authority but retain others are to complete a Replacement Industry Authority (F7429) form.

    • Renewals are not finalised within 3 months of the expiry date

Complaints
Complaints Against Driver Trainers
Complaints Against Driver Trainers
    Who may complain
    Who may complain
      A student (past or present), parent/guardian of a student or other directly affected person may lodge a complaint regarding a driver trainer who does not comply with the Statutory Conditions or the Code of Conduct.

      The Driver Trainer Code of Conduct includes the following information about Complaints procedures:
      • For complaints regarding the quality of the training or the conduct of the driver trainer, the complainant should direct their complaints to the employer of the driver trainer in the first instance, and if no resolution is reached then the complainant is to be directed to TMR. The complaint to TMR should be made in writing providing all pertinent details.
      • For issues of a criminal nature, such as assault, the complaint should be reported to police in the first instance, with advice given to the Manager of the TMR Customer Service Centre. The advice to TMR should be made in writing providing all pertinent details.
      • For issues regarding discrimination, the complainant may contact the Anti-Discrimination Commission Queensland.
      • For disputes regarding fees or service contract matters, the complainant should contact the employer of the driver trainer in the first instance and if no satisfaction is achieved the complaint may be lodged with the Queensland Office of Fair Trading.

    Lodging a complaint with TMR
    Lodging a complaint with TMR
      All complaints should be lodged with the Customer Service Centre Manager (or representative) by either:
      Written
      Written
        The written complaint must contain:
        • Detailed issues of the complaint
        • Complainant's name and contact details

      Verbal
      Verbal
        The individual should be advised that it is extremely difficult for TMR to take any action unless details of the complaint are in writing.

        Any verbal complaints should be noted and kept on file at the relevant CSC. A copy of these notes should also be sent to - Industry Licensing Team

        Full details of the complaint
        • For a complaint to be properly considered, the complainant must provide their name and contact details.

        Anonymity Concerns
        Anonymity Concerns
          If the person making the complaint is concerned about anonymity they should be assured that as far as possible (Refer: TMR complaints policy), TMR will protect their identity.

          Note:No action will be taken in relation to an 'anonymous' verbal complaint unless the issues indicate the potential for an unacceptable risk to the public or other learner drivers.

Mutual Recognition
Mutual Recognition
Mutual Recognition
    Under the Mutual Recognition Act 1992, a current interstate or New Zealand driver trainer or rider trainer accreditation can be recognised and transferred to the equivalent Queensland accreditation for the same classes. However applicants must make application for recognition of their existing interstate or New Zealand accreditation and pay the applicable fee.

    Applicants eligible for mutual recognition are therefore exempt from the qualification requirement (e.g. course certificate/statement of attainment) for the same class/es but must meet all other application requirements (such as driver licence tenure, medical certificates, criminal and traffic history checks).

      • Driver Trainer & Rider Trainer Accreditation Application (F3948) and
      • Equivalent Occupation Registration Notice (F2762)

Vehicle requirements
Vehicle Requirements
Vehicle Requirements
    All vehicles provided by a driver trainer for driver training must comply with section 29 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Accreditation and Other Provisions) and registered with the Purpose of Use DRIVER TRAINING OR TUITION (TICA.)

    A car provided by a driver trainer for driver training must display/be fitted with the following:
    • Contact name and contact information (such as a business telephone or website address)
    • L plates at the front and rear of the vehicle
    • Training controls that are easily accessible for immediate use by a driver trainer sitting in the front passenger seat of the vehicle
    • An additional internal mirror for the trainer
    • An external driving mirror on each side of the car

    Note:The above requirements do not apply to a vehicle the learner provides for a driving lesson with a driver trainer other than L plates must be displayed at the front and rear of the vehicle.
