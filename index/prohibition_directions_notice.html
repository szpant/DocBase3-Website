Prohibition/Directions Notice (5257)


Prohibition/Directions Notice
What it isA Prohibition/Directions Notice may be issued to a person to prohibit the use of a vehicle when unsafe, or to prohibit or defer from driving a vehicle if using the vehicle would contravene a transport law or HVNL (Q).

A court may prohibit a person operating in the transport industry for a period up to 12 months

Prohibition Notices are referred to as "Direction Notices" under HVNL(Q).

Directions Notice can be issued under HVNL(Q) for Fatigue, Mass, Dimension & Loading and any other direction under HVNL (Q).

Prohibition Notices can be used under TO(RUM) Act, for light vehicles, prescribed dangerous goods vehicles and driver licensing.
Procedure
Direction Notice
Direction Notice
    A Direction Notice must be issued:
    • to the driver or owner of a heavy vehicle to rectify the cause of minor, substantial or sever risk breach of Mass, Dimension, or Loading requirements or
    • to the driver of a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle for a sever or critical risk breach of fatigue work and rest requirements.

    A direction Notice may be issued to the driver of a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle:
    • for a minor or substantial risk breach of fatigue work and rest requirements or
    • the drivers work diary can not be produced or is unreliable.
Prohibition Notice
Prohibition Notice
    A Prohibition Notice may be issued under TO(RUM) Act directing the driver or owner of a vehicle to do something or not do something to comply with:
    • Dangerous Goods regulation,
    • Licence requirements
    • A light vehicle for
      • Loading or
      • Dimension
Procedure
Procedure
    • Complete the Prohibition/Directions Notice (F3162) and issue the blue copy of the notice to the person.
    • Advise the driver why a Prohibition/Direction Notice has been issued and what actions need to be taken to comply with legislative requirements.
    • Record the Notice Number in QCIS.
    • The details why the Prohibition/Direction Notice was issued must be entered in the notes section in QCIS for the intercept. This will show the driver has been issued with a Prohibition/Direction
    • Notice and the reason, if the driver is intercepted at a later date.
Queensland Compliance Information System (QCIS)
Record Prohibition Notice
    • Caution the person that failure to comply with the requirement will result in a further offence being committed.
    • Scan and file an electronic copy of the Prohibition/Directions Notice, in the Document Management System (DMS).
    • Where necessary when a Prohibition/Direction Notice has been issued roadside, assistance to Drivers and Passengers is to be given.
    Assistance to Drivers and Passengers
Animal Welfare
Animal Welfare
    Consideration must be given to animal welfare. A vehicel carrying live stock must only be prohibit from moving if the vehicle is unsafe; or if the driver has not had sufficient rest from driving as described in regulation as a critical breach.

Unsafe is defined in the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act as: 'Unsafe' - for a thing, means the thing is likely to cause loss of life, bodily injury or damage to property if used in a normal way.
