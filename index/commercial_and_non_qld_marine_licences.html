Commercial and Non Qld Marine licences (1396)


Qld Commercial and Non Qld Recreational & Commercial Marine licences
Operating in Qld using an Interstate, Commercial or Foreign Licence (Mutual Recognition)The Department of Transport and Main Roads mutually recognises current equivalent recreational and commercial marine licences issued by other Australian States or Territories.

A person may use a valid recreational marine licence issued by another Australian state or territory in Queensland if all of the following apply:
  • the person is at least 16 years old
  • the marine licence is current
  • the person operates within the conditions on their interstate marine licence.

If the interstate marine licence expires, the person must either:
  • renew their licence with the issuing state; or
  • meet the full requirements for a Queensland marine licence and apply for a Queensland boat or personal watercraft licence. Equivalent recreational and commercial marine licences issued interstate may be converted (refer 'RML, CMML, Licence conversions' below).

The department recognises selected foreign licences for international visitors and Australian citizens returning from overseas. The licence must be current. The list of current recognised overseas marine licences can be found at:
http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/boating/licences/recreational/#overseas

For information about withdrawal of authority to operate a foreign licence, refer to 'Foreign Licences' below.
RML, CMML, Licence conversions
Class Conversions and requirementsA customer is able to use an equivalent interstate marine licence, an interstate or national (AMSA) commercial marine masters licence, or a Queensland commercial marine masters licence to satisfy the competency requirements for obtaining a Queensland recreational marine licence. The equivalent licence being produced for a conversion to a Queensland recreational marine licence must be current or expired less than 5 years.

Note:Recognised foreign licences cannot be used to satisfy the competency requirements for obtaining a Qld RMDL or Qld PWCL. Foreign marine licence holders, whether their licence is recognised or not, are required to obtain a Certificate of Competency from a BoatSafe Training Provider to satisfy competency requirements.

Note:Junior/Young Adult licences are not recognised as an equivalent marine licence.

Licences that cannot be converted
Licences that cannot be converted
    Licences that cannot be converted are:
    • Junior/Young Adult licences (not recognised as an equivalent marine licence),
    • Overseas licences and Defence force licences,
    • Licences expired for 5 years or more, and
    • A 2 month confirmation slip issued by Transport for NSW.

    Note:Applicants who hold a licence that is not recognised as an equivalent licence may apply to a BoatSafe Training Provider (BTP) for recognition of prior learning. The applicant should contact a BTP for further information. A list of BTPs is available from the MSQ website.

Marine sanction on interstate marine licence
Marine sanction on interstate marine licence
    Approval is required before a Qld Marine Licence can be granted, if the applicant has:
    • Been disqualified from holding or obtaining a marine licence by an interstate court within the last 5 years and/or
    • Had an interstate marine licence cancelled or suspended within the last 5 yrs.

    The CSO is to:
    • Create customer in TICA;
    • Record text restriction stating Vehicle and Vessel Standards approval required.
    • Forward application form and copy of Interstate Marine licence to MSQ.

    Transport for NSW (Maritime)
    Transport for NSW (Maritime)
      If customers are unable to provide evidence of their NSW licence, Transport for NSW will accept the customers' written consent to verify their details with TMR for the purpose of a licence transfer.

      In addition to sending an email request for Marine Licence details to Transport for NSW, you must also include a scanned copy of:
      • A completed Recreational Marine Driver/Personal Watercraft Licence or Marine Licence Indicator Application (F2334)
      • Section 5; Current marine licence details, must be completed
      • Section 11; Applicant's declaration (signature and date), must be completed
      Note:Transport for NSW and MSQ both currently have restrictive ROI provisions that do not allow information sharing without written consent. Once the Applicant's declaration on F2334 is signed, this may be taken as written consent for the information on the form to be verified with the issuing authority.
      MSQ is seeking to amend the Qld ROI provisions so that information sharing can be simplified.

I/S ML after conversion
Validity of Interstate Licence after Conversion
Validity of Interstate Licence after Conversion
    Once a Queensland Recreational Marine Driver Licence or Queensland Personal Watercraft Licence has been issued, the applicant may either:
    • Retain the interstate RMDL, PWCL or Commercial Marine Masters licence, or
    • Formally surrender the licence to the interstate issuing authority and seek a pro-rata refund from the interstate authority if available.

    What is a Resident Visa
    What is a Resident Visa
      Is a permanent or special category visa given under the Migration Act 1958(Commonwealth), allowing the holder to stay indefinitely in Australia.

    A person's passport is used to verify when residency was granted and the date of entry to Australia.
