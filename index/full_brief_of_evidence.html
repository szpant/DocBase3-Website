Full Brief of Evidence (7056)


What it isA Full Brief of Evidence (FBOE) will be requested by Prosecutions Services Unit (PSU) when a defendant has entered a plea of ‘not guilty’ in Court and has elected that the matter will go to a hearing.
ProcedurePSU will notify the investigating TI, as well as their supervising STI, that a FBOE is required. PSU will advise a date that the FBOE must be provided to them. THIS DATE MUST BE STRICTLY COMPLIED WITH. PSU have a legislative requirement to disclose the FBOE to the defendant or their legal representatives within certain time frames. The due date is determined to allow PSU to comply with the legislation. The supervising STI should monitor the progress of TI’s who have been allocated a FBOE to ensure that the FBOE is completed within the required time frame.

When notified that an FBOE is required the TI is to:
  • Complete their statement in relation to this matter.
  • Request any corroborating TI to complete their statement in relation to the matter.
  • Compile statements from any civilian or other witnesses in relation to the matter.
  • All statements must contain an Oaths Act Declaration.
  • Prepare a photograph grid of all photographic images collected throughout the investigation.
  • Complete the Index to FBOE ensuring it includes all evidence gathered during the investigation.
  • Ensure PSU are in possession of any recordings, photographs or other evidence in relation to the matter.
  • Compile the FBOE in the following order:
  • Index to FBOE
  • Witness statement/s
  • Photographs
  • All other evidence (e.g. Weigh Device Notice, work rosters, copies of work diaries, Digital Voice Recordings, etc)
  • Sign and date the declaration on the FBOE.
  • Make a pdf copy of the FBOE and retain all original items of evidence.
  • Forward the compiled FBOE to their STI for checking.

The STI who receives the FBOE is to:
  • Evaluate the FBOE to ensure:
  • All evidence obtained in relation to the matter has been disclosed.
  • All statements contain contain sufficient evidence to prove the relevant offences beyond a reasonable doubt.
  • Sufficiency of evidence to prove charges alleged
  • All exhibits and statements are attached
  • If any deficiency is identified:
  • discuss the issue with the TI who completed the FBOE and provide guidance or advice for further investigation or evidence.
  • ensure that all know witnesses to the event are listed and their statements are attached.
  • Once satisfied, the STI is to
  • sign and date the declaration on the FBOE.
  • Submit FBOE by email or mail to Legal and Prosecutions Branch within three (3) days, but by the date specified by the Prosecutions Unit (whichever is the sooner).
  • File in relevant Document Management System file.
