AIS Compliance Checks (5172)


AIS Compliance Checks
What it isCompliance checks (previously called AIS Audits) are conducted as part of the ongoing performance management of Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) including Approval Holders, Nominees and Approved Examiners (AEs).

The intent of the Compliance Check is to ensure the AIS is compliant with:
  • Conditions of approval
  • The Code of Practice
  • The Business Rules AIS (BRAIS)
  • Legislation

All Approved Inspection Stations are subject to Compliance Checks.

Types of Compliance ChecksThe three types of Compliance Checks are:
Confimation
Confimation
    Confirmation checks verify that the AIS has provided correct information in their application for:
    • new AIS
    • relocation;
      or
    • additional categories.

    Confirmation Compliance Checks are to be conducted within the first six (6) months of approval.
Triggered
Triggered
    An AIS can have a Compliance Check triggered by a compliant/investigation or they may be randomly selected.
Scheduled
Scheduled
    An AIS must be checked at least once every four (4) years. An AIS that has not had a confirmation or triggered Compliance Check in the last four (4) years will have a scheduled check.
Responsibilities
Senior Transport Inspector
Senior Transport Inspector
    The STI will:
    • assign Compliance Checks to a primary auditor and specify time frame
    • roster two Transport Inspectors to undertake Compliance Checks
    • when an approved AIS application is received from the AIS administrator forward to the selected primary auditor
    • review the outcome of all Compliance Checks including the completed checklist, any corrective actions (CAs) issued and sanctions as required
    • ensure requirements for any show cause action are met
    • ensure all documentation is captured in DMS
Primary Auditor
Primary Auditor
    The Primary Auditor will:
    • ensure the Compliance Check is carried out within the required time frame, or if unable to meet the required time frame, advise the STI.
    • complete the Compliance Check adhering to the requirements and actions detailed in the sections below:
      • Preparing for the Compliance Check
      • Conducting an AIS Compliance Check
      • Issuing Corrective Actions
      • Determining the Compliance Action
      • Conducting the Exit Meeting
      • Recording Compliance Check Outcome
      • Show cause if applicable
    • monitor CAs to ensure they are returned within the required time frame and that requirements have been met.
    • after the completion of the Compliance Check including return of any CAs, give all documents to the STI within 5 business days.
Assisting Inspector
Assisting Inspector
    The assisting inspector will:
    • assist the primary auditor as required
Compliance Administration
Compliance Administration
    Compliance administration will:
    • monitor the Compliance email inbox for approved AIS applications from the AIS Administrator
    • for a new AIS create a new AIS entity in the CID audit hub
    • forward the AIS Administrator email to the STI
    • when advised by the STI, capture all documentation electronically in DMS.
    • once all documents have been captured in DMS destroy hard copies.

    Refer: Transport Compliance > Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) > The How To: Approved Inspection Station (AIS) > Approved Inspection Station Procedures
Preparing for the Compliance Check
Preparing for an AIS Compliance Check
Preparing for an AIS Compliance Check
    • Compile and review TICA, RUMIIS and CID records for the Approval Holder, Nominees and all AEs attached to the AIS. This information must include:
    • accreditation information
    • equipment listed in RUMIIS and required for accreditation
    • inspection books issued to the station
    • compliance history
    • compliant history
    • check if the AIS is enabled in ICO by using Find an AIS
    • if an AIS enabled and approved to issue 'CL' type vehicle inspections, brake reports must up loaded to the ICO inspection record.
    • Make an appointment with the Approval Holder to conduct the Compliance Check, 3 - 5 working days’ notice should be given. Confirm with the Approval Holder that:
    • all relevant records, documentation, personnel and equipment will be available on the date arranged.
    • if the AIS has a Mobile AIS, that the mobile Unit/s will be available during the compliance check.
    • Print out the AIS Compliance Checklist

    Ensure the following equipment is available for the Compliance Check:
    • high visibility vest
    • body worn camera (BWC)
    • camera
    • tape measure and
    • torch or other light source
Conducting an AIS Compliance Check
Entering an AIS premises
Entering an AIS premises
    Power to enter
    Power to enter
      The power to enter the premises is provided in section 26 (1)(c) of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act.

      The BRAIS also provides for the entry by Authorised Officers to the AIS premises and/or access Mobile AIS to conduct Compliance Checks.
    Commencing the onsite compliance check
    Commencing the onsite compliance check
      Both Authorised Officers must:
      • activate their BWC prior to entering the premises
      • identify themselves as Transport Inspectors
      • produce their ID cards,

      The primary auditor will explain the reason for the Compliance Check and advise the AIS representatives the interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes, and verbally provide the privacy collection notice.

      For example, 'Good evening/morning/afternoon. My name is John Smith, I am a Transport Inspector with Transport and Main Roads, here is my ID (SHOW ID), we are conducting confirmation audit as part of the AIS approval process. This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes'.
AIS Compliance Checklist
AIS Compliance Checklist
    The AIS Compliance Checklist must be used when conducting a Compliance Check.

    Regardless of the type of Compliance Check, (confirmation, triggered or scheduled) the Compliance Checklist must be fully completed, that is all AIS Compliance Checks are full compliance checks.

    AIS Compliance Checklist 01 September 2017
    Sampling Books and Certificates
    Sampling Books and Certificates
      The number of inspection certificate books or issued inspection certificates to be checked is not set, the primary auditor should review documents until they have collected enough objective evidence to make an informed decision about the Approval Holder's compliance with their conditions of approval.

      Inspection certificates either physical or completed in ICO should be checked for all AE/s and classes of vehicles the AE/s are accredited to inspect.

      For the purpose of auditing certificates, the following sample size may be used as a guide.
      • less than 20 completed certificates/used books – inspect not less than 5 certificates/used books.
      • between 20-60 completed certificates/used books – inspect not less than 10 certificates/used books.
      • between 60 -100 completed certificates/used books – inspect not less than 20 certificates/used books.
      • more than 100 completed certificates/used books – inspect more than 20 certificates/used books.

      Do not inspect records previous to the date of the last Compliance Check.
      Certificate books should be marked in red pen on the front cover with the date of the Compliance Check, the primary auditor's ID number and the number of the last certificate completed.

      Record on the Compliance Checklist the numbers of the inspection certificates sampled including paper based and inspection certificates issued via ICO.

      Refer: Transport Compliance > Systems > Inspection Certificates Online (ICO)

      Refer: Transport Compliance > Systems > The How To: Compliance Systems > Using ICO for AIS Audit
Collecting evidence of non-compliance
Collecting evidence of non-compliance
    If non-compliance is detected, collect sufficient evidence to prove non-compliance.
    Certificates may photographed or photocopied and books may be seized.

    Do not seize single certificates from a book, seize the book.

    If certificate books are seized, the primary auditor must give a receipt to the Approval Holder.

    Any certificate books seized must be stored in accordance with TMR policy.

    The primary auditor must record in the official notebook:
    • the name of the AIS
    • name of the person
    • the address of the AIS
    • the number of the first and last certificate in each book.
Recording compliance check outcome
Recording compliance check outcome
    All compliance checks must be recorded in CID and RUMIIS.

    The Scheduling/Tracking Current & Future Audits in CID guide details the procedure for adding and searching for future scheduled and triggered audit in CID.

Issuing Corrective Actions (CA)
Corrective Action (CA)
Corrective Action (CA)
    A ‘Corrective Action’ must be issued to the Approval Holder when any non-compliance is detected. The Approval Holder is required to notify the Department when the issue/s identified in the CA have been rectified.

    An AIS is typically given 14 days to complete the CA.

    Multiple non-compliances can be listed on a single CA however if, due to the severity of the non-compliance, different dates of completion are required they should be done on a separate CA.

    If the CA is resolved during the Compliance Check, the CA must still be issued, the close out date recorded is the date of the compliance check.
Management of Corrective Action
Management of Corrective Action
    The Compliance Check cannot be completed until all CAs are returned and cleared.

    The primary auditor monitors the return of the CA and reviews the returned CA to ensure sufficient evidence has been provided to satisfy them the issues have been rectified. Another site visit may be required.

    If a CA has not been complied with contact the Approval Holder to determine if there are any valid reasons for non-compliance. The primary auditor has discretion to allow an extension of time to comply with the CA. If an extension of time to comply with the CA is granted, this must be recorded on the CA, Checklist and in the CID AIS Audit running sheet. The CA bring update must also be amended in CID.

    If compliance cannot be achieved, consult the STI regarding options for further compliance action.
Completion of Corrective Action
Completion of Corrective Action
    Once all CAs have been completed, the Compliance Check outcomes must be recorded in RUMIIS and CID and all documentation must be forwarded to the STI within 5 business days.

    When all documents have been approved by the STI, they are to be electronically captured in DMS.

    Refer: Transport Compliance > Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) > The How To: Approved Inspection Station (AIS) > Approved Inspection Station Procedures > AIS DMS Capture Procedure
Determining Compliance Action
Compliance Action
Compliance Action
    The TMR Compliance Management Policy and Procedures details a four tiered approach to compliance action:
    1. inform the Approval Holder and AEs about the conditions of their approval
    2. help the Approval Holder and the AEs comply
    3. put checks in place to monitor compliance
    4. take enforcement action

    When considering compliance action the seriousness of any potential or actual non-compliance and the success or otherwise of previous compliance action should be taken into account.

    As a general rule; enforcement action should be reserved for serious non-compliance or for situations where informing, helping and checking has not been successful in achieving compliance.

    When completing the Compliance Check, the primary auditor must decide whether the Approval Holder has met the conditions of approval for the AIS.

    If non-compliance has been detected, the primary auditor must ensure they have collected sufficient evidence and decide if further compliance action is required. Serious or repeated non-compliance may lead to the following sanctions:
    • issue of PINs,
    • court action via a Brief To Adjudicator (BTA)
    • show cause action to amended, suspended or cancel their approval

    Breach Categories - Risk Levels for AIS and AE
    Breach Categories - Risk Levels for AIS and AE
      Minor Breach
      Minor Breach
        A minor breach is administrative in nature. The following table details Minor breaches:

        Minor
        Risk/Breach        		AIS Approval Holder fails to mark inspection certificate cancelled
        AIS Approval Holder fails to ensure AE is available unless reasonable excuse
        AIS Approval Holder charges more than prescribed fee for Safety Certificate
        AIS Approval Holder fails to notify details of AE or AP
        AIS Approval Holder fails to notify change of business name/location
        AIS Approval Holder fails to notify change of business ownership
        AIS Approval Holder fails to notify cease of business
        Failure to notify Chief Executive of inspection certificate destroyed/lost/stolen W/I 7 days
        AIS Approval Holder fails to return unused certificates/labels
        AIS Approval Holder fails to keep copies of Inspection report for 2 years
        AIS Approval Holder fails to keep copy of Inspection certificate for 2 years
        AIS Approval Holder fails to keep copy of certificate of modification for 7 years
        AIS Approval Holder fails to keep cancelled inspection certificate for 2 years
        AIS Approval Holder fails to return documents after ceasing business
        Examiner fails to be satisfied under 16A(2) about the identity of the vehicle
        AIS Approval Holder to ensure vehicle properly inspected for the issue of a replacement certificate, completed within 7 days and at a time convenient to the person

      Moderate Breach
      Moderate Breach
        A moderate breach can be administrative in nature. A significant number of minor breaches clearly showing an ongoing pattern of behaviour of non-compliance can be a moderate breach. The following table details Moderate breaches:

        Moderate
        Risk/Breach        		Examiner signs a certificate not correct, legible or entry altered
        AIS Approval Holder fails to ensure examiner has not signed a certificate that is not correct, legible or has been altered
        AIS Approval Holder fails to sign the certificate
        The same person who signed the inspection report for the vehicle must carry out the reinspection of the vehicle
        AIS Approval Holder fails to ensure AE is accredited for AIS inspections
        AE inspects vehicle type which AIS is not approved
        AE inspects vehicle not accredited to inspect
        Examiner inspects vehicle at a place that is not AIS

      Major Breach
      Major Breach
        A major breach is anything that affects road safety outcomes and can include:
        • the scheme participant not having the necessary skills, equipment required to undertake a thorough inspection. (Note: Equipment requirements means equipment as outlined in the application and necessary for conducting inspections on the category of vehicle approved to inspect).
        • having provided false or misleading information at the time of application or renewal or in the performance of their duties as an AIS.
        • the accreditation holder having been charged with a disqualifying offence.
        • a medical fitness or condition that impacts the accreditation holder's ability to perform the role.
        • not knowing how to operate the necessary equipment to perform the duties of an accreditation holder.
        • falsely completing or issuing documentation (such as Safety Certificates or Certificates of Inspection).
        • other non-compliances which present an unacceptable risk to public safety and confidence.

      Extreme Breach
      Extreme Breach
        An extreme breach may result from a serious crash and/or fatality involving a defective vehicle where there is evidence that defects would have been present at the time of issue of the inspection certificate.

        Conviction of the AIS Approval Holder with a disqualifying offence is also an extreme breach.

Conducting the exit meeting
Exit Meeting
Exit Meeting
    The primary auditor must discuss with the AH, or their representative that attended the opening meeting the findings of the compliance check. This must include any identified non-compliance.

    Best practice is to complete all paperwork and provide the Approval Holder with any CA notice during the exit meeting.

    A copy of the completed Compliance Checklist may be provided to the AH within five (5) working days if requested.

Recording the outcome of the AIS Compliance Check

Show CauseA Show Cause Notice for immediate suspension may be issued if there is a major breach or there are large numbers of non-compliance.

If show cause action is considered appropriate the primary auditor must collate the evidence and prepare a Briefing Note for the consideration by the STI

When issuing a Show Cause Notice details of the CAs must be included in the Grounds for the decision.

Refer: Transport Compliance > Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) > Approved Inspection Station (AIS) Show Cause
