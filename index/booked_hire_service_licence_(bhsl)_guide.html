Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) Guide (7230)


About Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL)
What it isA Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) authorises an individual, company, incorporated association, trustees of a trust or partnership to provide a booked hire service using the vehicle stated in the licence.

Note:This is to ensure that drivers providing booked hire services in a vehicle other than a taxi or limousine are suitable and the vehicle is safe.
When is it requiredTo provide a booked hire service in a vehicle other than a taxi or limousine, a person must attain and maintain a BHSL using an appropriate vehicle which is stated in the licence. They must also be affiliated to a booking service that holds a current Booking Entity Authorisation (BEA)
When not requiredLimousines and Special Purpose Limousines Licence holders are not required to have a BHSL or change their purpose of use to Booked Hire. They may provide a booked hire service to the public using the motor vehicle stated in their current Limousine licence.
Receive a BHSL application
Where to apply
Online
Online
    Applications, reapplications and amendments of current BHSLs can be lodged online for individuals and corporations (with an ACN) that have existing TMR CRNs. The nominated vehicle must also have a current Certificate of Inspection recorded on TMR's system.

    Unpaid online applications
    Unpaid online applications
      If a BHSL applicant does not finalise (pay) for their online application, they can complete one of the below options:
      New applications:
      New applications:
        • Submit a new online application and pay (preferred option). The previous unpaid invoice will automatically be removed after 30 days; or
        • Attend a CSC and pay for their un-finalised application within 30 days. The transaction will be processed in an overnight batch and show as lodged in the Manage Booked Hire (MBH) the next day. There is no requirement to send any paperwork to TransLink as the application will flow through as an online application.

      Re-applications:
      Re-applications:
        • Submit a new application (not a re-application) and include the previous BHSL number in the "please enter any additional information to support your application" field. The previous unpaid invoice will automatically be removed after 30 days; or
        • Attend a CSC and pay for their un-finalised application within 30 days. The transaction will be processed in an overnight batch and show as lodged in the Manage Booked Hire (MBH) the next day. There is no requirement to send any paperwork to TransLink as the application will flow through as an online application.

In person
In person
    A new application and a reapplication can be received in person at a:
    • TMR Customer Service Centre
    • Queensland Government Agency

    Note:Applications are received into the Managed Booked Hire (MBH) staff user interface (UI), accessed via TICA's Individual or Organisation Hub.
By Mail
By Mail
    Applications and reapplications lodged by mail must have the following:
    • Completed Booked Hire Service Licence application (F5189)
    • All supporting documentation attached
    • Additional forms (if required) as stated on the application (F5189)
    • Witnessed copies of EOI documents Refer: Transaction Requirements Checklist for those applicants that must provide their EOI.
    • Payment of the required fees

    Witnessing of EOI document
    Witnessing of EOI document
      The EOI documents must be endorsed with the words “I have sighted the original documents and certify this to be a true copy of the original”

      The witness must sign and print their full name, position title/designation and contact details (including office stamp). All documents are to be witnessed in English or have an English translation.

      An approved witnesses is either a:
      • Medical Practitioner
      • Police Officer
      • Consular or Ambassadorial Officer
      • Solicitor, Barrister or Judge
      • Justice of the Peace
      • A Commissioner for Declarations
      • Notary Public or a person authorised by law to witness and sign declarations

      Applications can be posted to:
      TransLink Regional Operations Toowoomba

    Eligibility requirements
    Who can be issued a Booked Hire Service Licence
    Who can be issued a Booked Hire Service Licence
      • Individuals
      • Companies/Corporations (including overseas registered)
      • Incorporated Associations
      • Trustees of a Trust
      • Partnerships

      The applicant can't be a 'Trading Name' but the trading name can be added to the Manage Booked Hire staff UI in the trading name field.

      Related body corporate
      Related body corporate
        If the applicant has a related body corporate, this must be declared on the application. A related body corporate means:
        • Individual applicant:
          • If the individual is a majority shareholder or director of a company, the company they are the share holder or director of is the related body corporate.
        • Company applicant:
          • If the company:
            • has another company which is linked;
              or
            • is controlled by a holding company,
            the other company is the related body corporate.

      Individuals
      Individuals
        All individual/s:
        • must supply EOI.
        • must be encouraged to sign up for e-notices and e-reminders. Being signed up to e-reminders will ensure that they receive an email reminder 7 days prior to their COI being due.

        Note:If more than one individual is listed on the application, they are added under the 'Additional holders' field on the Manage Book Hire staff UI. Additional Holders can only be added or removed from the BHSL on application or reapplication status when it is in a 'Received or Lodged' status.

        Suppressed Customers

      Companies/Corporations
      Companies/Corporations
        All current directors must be listed on the application and must sign the application.

        A copy of a Current Company Extract listing the directors of the corporation/company (available from ASIC) must be submitted.

        If the organisation is located overseas and doesn’t have an ARBN (or ACN), they will need the Qual FOEN added to their CRN for the application to be processed.

        Note:Directors are entered on the Managed Booked Hire staff UI as 'Associated Personnel'.

      Incorporated Associations
      Incorporated Associations
        All current management committee members must be listed on the application and must sign the application.

        A copy of an official extract about the incorporated association from the register of incorporated associations listing the current management committee members (issued by the Queensland office of Fair Trading) must be submitted.

        Note:Committee members are entered on the Managed Booked Hire staff UI as 'Associated Personnel'.

      Trustees of a Trust
      Trustees of a Trust
        Applicants who wish to operate through a Trust must nominate the name/s of the trustee of the trust. All trustees, including directors/executives (if the trustee is a company), must be listed on the application and must sign the application.

        A copy of the Deed of Trust document which outlines the trustee's powers, lists all involved parties and proves the validity of the trusteeship must be submitted.

        For Trusts, the customer qualifications must be 'Trust' in TICA to be able to receive an application into the Manage Booked Hire staff UI. If the customer has the old qualification of 'Trust or Partnership' it will need to be updated with the 'Trust' qualification.

        Note:Either the first or principal trustee must to be added under the 'Trustee' field on the Manage Booked Hire staff UI. All other trustees need to be entered in the 'Associated Personnel' field.

      Partnerships
      Partnerships
        All parties who form the partnership must be named on the application and must sign the application.

        A signed, formal *Deed of Partnership document, or another form of **partnership agreement, which lists all involved parties and proves the validity of the partnership must be submitted.

        For Partnerships, the partnership name is entered into TICA as an organisation (e.g. John and Marc Enterprises) and the customer qualification must be 'Partnership'. If the customer has the old qualification of 'Trust or Partnership' it will need to be updated with the 'Partnership' qualification.


        Note:*


        **        		Deed of Partnership is prepared by a Lawyer/Solicitor. It can be registered with the Office of Fair trading if liabilities of partners are limited.

        Partnership agreements may include:
        • listing all parties involved in the partnership (compare this list against the parties listed on the BHSL application)
        • stated intent to form a partnership
        • date the agreement was entered into
        • signed agreement.

        Note:Partners are entered on the Managed Booked Hire staff UI as 'Associated Personnel'.

    Vehicle Requirements
    Vehicle Requirements
      To apply for a BHSL, the nominated vehicle stated on the licence must meet the following requirements:
      • COI Requirements - TMR or AIS Issue (unless Exempt)
      • Registered in Queensland with the appropriate Purpose of Use (POU)
      • Correct Compulsory Third Party (CTP) insurance class
      • The following vehicle types that have no more than 12 seats (including the driver):
        • Passenger Car
        • Light Commercial (only for Utility, Dual Cab and Ute Cab 'n' Chassis with a maximum GVM of 4.5t)
        • Minibus
        • Omnibus
      • Must be right-hand drive


        Note:The nominated vehicle must not have passenger accessible controls (accelerator, clutch or footbrake controls accessible for immediate use by a person other than the driver of the vehicle).

        Certificate of Inspection (COI)
        Certificate of Inspection (COI)
          The nominated vehicle must have a current Certificate of Inspection (COI) recorded on the inspection screen unless exempt a COI.

          If the customer doesn't have a COI, they will be able to choose from either an AIS or TMR to get a COI. An AIS COI will need to be issued electronically using the ICO system and have a CL prefix code. There are no manual COIs for light vehicles. A COI must be obtained prior to receiving the BHSL application into the 'Manage Booked Hire' Staff UI.

        Purpose Of Use
        Purpose Of Use
          The nominated vehicle must be registered with the following POU. There may be an increase in registration costs if the update is during a current registration period.
          • Booked Hire (for booked hire vehicles other than rental vehicles), or
          • Booked Hire Rental (for rental vehicles used to provide a booked hire service).

          Note:If the registration is expired, it must be paid prior to updating the POU and/or CTP.

          Dual purpose vehicles
          Dual purpose vehicles
            If a customer wants to have one vehicle on both a Booked Hire Service Licence and Operator Accreditation, there is no dual purpose of use option to select in TICA. Until systems changes are implemented, the following applies to these vehicles:
            • CTP must be Class 4, 10a or 11
            • To lodge a BHSL, the POU must be updated as BKRN
            • Once a decision is made by the TRO, the TRO will advise the CSC by email that the POU can be changed to PPSE*
            • 6 monthly (PPSE) COI requirements apply
            • Suspension action will NOT be taken against vehicles listed on a BHSL which have changed their POU from BKRN to PPSE to get around validation rules.

            *The TRO email can be printed and attached to the filing associated with the POU change.

          Compulsory Third Party (CTP)
          Compulsory Third Party (CTP)
            The nominated vehicle must be registered with one of the following CTP classes.

            Passenger Cars and relevant Light Commercial vehicles:
            • Class 26 for Booked Hire, or
            • Class 4 for Booked Hire Rental

            OR

            Minibus or Omnibus with 9-12 seats (including the driver):
            • Class 10A or 11

            Note:For changes to the CTP Class during a current registration period a letter of confirmation is required from the CTP Insurance Company confirming the change of class and the date.

          Using another person's vehicle (applicant not the registered operator)
          Using another person's vehicle (applicant not the registered operator)
            If the applicant is not the registered operator of the vehicle, they must obtain written consent from the registered operator to apply for a BHSL.

            If lodging online or received over the counter the applicant must submit the Registered Operator Consent for Vehicle to be stated on Booked Hire Service Licence application (F5190) signed and completed by the registered operator.

            Notes:Form F5190 can be used to update the POU and CTP on the nominated vehicle and will need to be filed under the registration TRAILS daily filing report for the operator.
            If the applicant is not the registered operator of the vehicle, then the ‘Show the registered operator’ box in the ‘Manage Booked Hire’ staff UI will need to be checked and the Registered operators CRN populated in the relevant field. This includes the legal entity (2nd registered operator) for Trusts and Partnerships.

      Training Requirements
      Training Requirements
        Drivers of taxis, limousines and booked hire vehicles in Queensland are required to be trained in disability awareness and anti-discrimination, including sexual harassment.Training in providing wheelchair accessible services is also required for drivers of wheelchair accessible vehicles.

        From 1 August 2019, drivers must be trained before they provide a service and authorised booking entities (BEAs) and BEA holders must ensure their drivers are trained prior to providing a service. Penalties may apply for drivers, BEA holders and operators.

        Compliance with training requirements will be managed via the BEA audit process therefore no details of training are required to be presented during the application process.

        For further details refer: Personalised Transport Industry - Driver Training and PT Transport Manual

      Apply for BHSL
      Apply for BHSL
        A BHSL application can be applied for/amended electronically via the online service or applied for in person at a Customer Service Centre.

        Customers may print/email/view their BHSL via online services. For online amendment services, Refer: Edit BHSL.

        When received at a Customer Service Centre, the operator is responsible for ensuring the application is complete, monies are receipted and the documents are emailed to the TransLink BHSL/BEA Hubfor processing.

        Individual customers must be encouraged to sign up for e-notices and e-reminders. Being signed up to e-reminders will ensure that they receive a COI reminder 7 days prior to its expiry.

        Bulk applications - organisations only
        Bulk applications - organisations only
          Organisations can apply for bulk BHSL's (between 10 and 60 per day)online if they have a credit account with TMR. To apply for a credit account, organisations must complete a Credit Account Application (F5168) and a Direct Debit Request (M3975), provide EOI and submit to Customer Account Management (CAM) by email. Once their credit account is approved, the organisation can login to the Booked Hire Service Licence online service, select "yes" to the question "Are you applying for multiple booked hire service licences?", answer the questions and then follow the bulk upload process.

          Once the customer applies online, the BHSL is lodged and then their bank account is swept over night. If the payment dishonours and payment is not made by another method, the customer's credit account will be suspended and the BHSL product status changed.

          Bulk BHSL's can only be submitted online. If an organisational customer presents in a CSC to apply for 10 or more BHSL's, they must be advised of the online service and provided with the above forms which the operator can email to Customer Account Management (CAM) on their behalf. If they do not wish to apply for a credit account and lodge in bulk, they can submit multiple individual applications using the online service.
      Reapply for BHSL
      Reapply for BHSL
        A BHSL can be reapplied for up to 6 weeks prior to the expiry date of the existing BHSL (of any status) or once a BHSL has expired, electronically via the online service or in person at a Customer Service Centre.

        When the reapplication is received at a Customer Service Centre, the operator is responsible for ensuring the application is complete, monies are receipted and the documents are emailed to the TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub for processing.

        Individual customers must be encouraged to sign up for e-notices and e-reminders. Being signed up to e-reminders will ensure that they receive a COI reminder 7 days prior to its expiry.
        Bulk reapplications - organisations only
        Bulk reapplications - organisations only
          Organisations can re-apply for bulk BHSL's (between 10 and 60 per day) online if they have a credit account with TMR. To apply for a credit account, organisations must complete a Credit Account Application (F5168) and a Direct Debit Request (M3975), provide EOI and submit to Customer Account Management (CAM) by email. Once their credit account is approved, the organisation can login to the Booked Hire Service Licence online service, select "yes" to the question "Are you applying for multiple booked hire service licences?", answer the questions and then follow the bulk upload process.

          Once the customer applies online, the BHSL is lodged and then their bank account is swept over night. If the payment dishonours and payment is not made by another method, the customer's credit account will be suspended and the BHSL product status changed.

          Bulk BHSL's can only be submitted online. If an organisational customer presents in a CSC to reapply for 10 or more BHSL's, they must be advised of the online service and provided with the above forms which the operator can email to Customer Account Management (CAM) on their behalf. If they do not wish to apply for a credit account and lodge in bulk, they can submit multiple individual reapplications using the online service.
      Approve/Refuse BHSL
      Approve/Refuse BHSL
        Checks are part of the process of assessing a holder's eligibility for a BHSL and are undertaken by TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub.

        TRO will verify the relevant passenger transport history of all the people listed on the application, or a related body corporate, before making a decision. For example, the BHSL application can be refused if anyone has:
        • Had a taxi, limousine or BHSL cancelled or suspended within the previous year,
        • Within the previous year, committed an offence against a provision of relevant transport legislation relating to:
          • providing a taxi service or a booked hire service; or
          • using a taxi, limousine or booked hire vehicle to provide a taxi service or booked hire service.

        Notes:Checks undertaken for a BHSL are different to those undertaken for a Driver Authorisation (DA).
        No Criminal History Checks (CHC's) are completed for BHSL's.

        Once all checks are completed the application is approved or refused and the customer notified.

        Customers may print/email/view their BHSL via online services.

      Email/print BHSL
      Email/print BHSL
        A current/approved BHSL can be printed upon request, except if changes have been referred to the TRO for processing.

        If there is a text that a manual PDF is required then the operator should contact their closest TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub

        A current/approved BHSL can be viewed, emailed, or PDF document downloaded/printed via Online Services.
      Deceased BHSL holder
      Deceased BHSL holder
        • If a representative of a deceased BHSL/BEA holder presents to a CSC with proof of death the operator must do the following (in addition to Updating the CRN as deceased):
          • Check and locate all BHSL's/BEA's
          • Email a copy of the proof of death document to TransLink BHSL/BEA Hub. TransLink will then surrender the BHSL/BEA
        • If an operator does not notify TRO that the customer is deceased and they have BHSL/BEA products, the customer will display on a TransLink non-compliance report. In this case, TRO can check TICA and print out a copy of the Individual Customer Hub showing the deceased details and surrender the BHSL/BEA.
      Surrender of a BHSL
      Surrender of a BHSL
        A BHSL can be surrendered, if the customer decides to voluntarily surrender their BHSL for reasons such as:
        • Medical or personal reasons
        • Changes in employment
        • On behalf of deceased
        • An immediate suspension or show cause notice issued to the BHSL holder where the vehicle no longer complies with the requirements to be listed on the BHSL

        BHSLs may be surrendered via Online Services.

        Refunds
        Refunds
          A Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) holder will generally not be entitled to a refund of any fees paid unless specific circumstances exist as outlined in the PT Database. If a refund is requested by TransLink they will forward the request to process the refund via email to the CSC which received the original application, or CAM if it was lodged online.

          Refer: Personalised & Passenger Transport > 1. Booked Hire Service Licence > The How To: Booked Hire Service Licence Procedures > Refund BHSL/BEA Product

        Voluntary Surrender
        Voluntary Surrender
          Advice of the surrender must be submitted           by completing a Booked Hire Service Licence Request (F5289) form and returning via:
          • email to bookedhire@translink.com.au; or
          • post to PO Box 629, TOOWOOMBA QLD 4350

          Refer: Surrender Your Booked Hire Service Licence for further information.


          The advice should be received from:
          • Individuals – the BHSL holder/s (primary or additional holders). If the BHSL holder is deceased the BHSL should be surrendered by the executor of the estate, a proof of death document and a Will or Probate must be sighted.
          • Partnerships – any partner of the partnership
          • Corporations – one of the nominated directors
          • Incorporated Associations – one of the nominated executive officers who is involved in the management of the booked hire service
          • Trustee – any trustees of the trust

          If the POU/CTP is being changed, the operator can process the vehicle change prior to emailing the BHSL surrender to the TransLink bHSL/BEA Hub for processing. Subject of email: Surrender BHSL - CRN <XXXXXXX>

          Refer: Personalised and Passenger Transport > 1. Booked Hire Service Licence > The How To: Booked Hire Service Licence > Booked Hire Service Licence Procedures > Surrender a BHSL

          Note:During COVID-19 only, changes have been made to the above process for customers to surrender their BHSL. Please refer to the Personalised Transport Relief Package for further information.

        Surrender due to Immediate Suspension or Show Cause Notice
        Surrender due to Immediate Suspension or Show Cause Notice
          If the surrender is the result of an Immediate Suspension or a Show Cause Notice the customer should contact the TRO that is stated in the notice. No further action is required from the CSC/Agency.
