Direct Debit (5346)


What it isDirect Debit is an automatic way for individual customers to pay their registration renewal every one, 3, 6 or 12 months from a nominated credit card or bank account. The payments can only be made every one, 3, 6 or 12 months and align with the registration pay term.
How does it work?If a customer and vehicle/ship meet the eligibility requirements listed below for direct debit:
  • They can enrol in and change their direct debit on the TMR website according to the rules outlined below.
  • Their Registration Notice will include the option of paying by direct debit, or if the vehicle/ship is already enrolled, it will advise that the renewal is being paid by direct debit.

Once a registration is added, the direct debit payments will be requested from the customer's nominated account as outlined below in Rules > Payments.

Further information can be found on the Direct Debit Support Materials SharePoint page.
Customer contact details
Customer contact details
Customer contact details
    All new customers who enrol in direct debit must have their eContact details (email address and mobile number) recorded and must be signed up to receive eNotices and eReminders. A customer enrolling in direct debit will directed to the "my eNotices and eReminders" page to capture these details.

    Customers who are enrolled in Direct Debit will be unable to:
    • opt out of eNotices or eReminders
    • remove their email address address
    • remove their mobile number.

    The mandatory requirement for customers to have their eContact details recorded and to sign up for eNotices was implemented in December 2019, eReminders became a requirement in February 2020. If a customer who enrolled in direct debit prior to December 2019 updates their term to one-month they must also have meet these requirements. If they haven't, they will be directed to the "my eNotices and eReminders" page to capture these details.

    Refer below: Rules > Customer Communications for more information.
Requirements
Customer eligibility requirements
Customer eligibility requirements
    Customers can enrol in the direct debit service if they meet the below eligibility requirements.

    Customers must:
    • Be an individual
    • Have at least one current eligible Registration;
    • Be the first or second registered operator for the eligible registration;
    • Be 18 years or over;
    • Have an Australian Visa or MasterCard credit card/debit card, or a savings or cheque account;
    • Have an active email address; and mobile phone number recorded
    • Be signed up for eNotices and eReminders
    • Not be recorded in our system as being deceased, suppressed or restricted from accessing online services.
Vehicle/Ship eligibility requirements
Vehicle/Ship eligibility requirements
    All vehicles (light and heavy), trailers and ships are eligible to be paid by direct debit.

    The only registration types that can't enrol in direct debit are those with:
    • A Charitable concession
    • Seasonal Registration
    • A Registration Category of:
    • Special Purpose
    • Wheelchair
    • Dealer/Trade plate

    Fire and Rescue Vehicles and Ships can enrol in direct debit but they can only select the 12 month term.

    Ambulances can only select a 6 or 12 month term.

    If a vehicle/ship meets the above criteria, it may still be ineligible due to other factors. Refer below: Ineligible Vehicles/Ships section.
Ineligible vehicles/ships
Ineligible vehicles/ships
Ineligible vehicles/ships
    If a vehicle/ship meets any of the criteria below, they are not eligible for Direct Debit (even if they meet the POU/registration categories above):
    • Registrations recorded as wrecked or written off (statutory or repairable);
    • Registrations with a current notice of disposal recorded
    • Registrations associated with a paying agent unless the enrolled customer is the responsible paying agent
    • Vehicle with a NORA restriction
    • Vehicles/Ships registered solely in the name of persons who have a “Restriction on Transacting Business”
    • Vehicles currently assigned with a common due date (where two or more vehicles are registered in the same registered operator’s name and expire on the same day)
    • New Business registrations (direct debit will only apply to renewals of registration and cannot be applied to initial registrations)
    • Registrations exempt from registration fees such as wheelchairs
    • Registrations recorded as:
      • Dormant
      • Pending
      • Suspended
      • Expired
      • Cancelled.
    • Registrations recorded with a current restriction from the following list are ineligible:
      CDCHDishonoured cheque at Conversion

Rules
Enrolment
Enrolment
    The customer must enrol and add the eligible registration on the TMR website.

    Customer's can enrol in direct debit and add their registration up until the registration expires.

    There is no limit on the number of eligible vehicles that can be added to direct debit, however when using the online service a customer can only add 10 vehicles at a time. If a customer has more than 10 vehicles, they would need to start the add vehicle process again to add the additional vehicles.

    If there are 18 days or less before the registration expires the customer must make an immediate payment. Refer below: Immediate Payments

    Operators are unable to assist in the enrolment process, it must be completed using TMR's online services as it involves the customer entering their credit card or bank details into a secure website. To provide further support, customers enquiring about enrolling in direct should be sent the "Enrol in Direct Debit and Add Vehicle" email template. Refer below: Correspondence templates
Registration Terms
Registration Terms
    Registration Terms available on Direct Debit are:
    • For vehicles – One, 3, 6 or 12 months
    • For ships – 12 months

    There is no limit on the number of eligible registrations a customer may add to direct debit, however, only ten registrations can be added in one transaction.

    Note: If a customer changes the pay term after a Vehicle Registration Notice (VRN) has been issued for a vehicle enrolled in direct debit, a new VRN will be generated which will indicate:
    • that the vehicle is enrolled for direct debit; and
    • the pay terms displayed will be the previous pay term and the new pay term
Eligible bank accounts and credit cards
Eligible bank accounts and credit cards
    The customer must nominate an Australian Visa or MasterCard credit card/debit card (American Express is not available), savings account or cheque account for the direct debit payment to be deducted from.

    Gift cards and prepaid cards cannot be used for direct debit payments as card issuers do not allow gift cards to be used for ongoing periodic payments.

    Note:A card surcharge applies if using a credit or debit card to pay for registration by direct debit.
Costs associated with Direct Debit
Costs associated with Direct Debit
    A standard shorter period surcharge is applied for the one, 3 and 6 month pay terms. Refer: Fees and Forms > 1. Fees for surcharge amounts.

    If a customer’s direct debit payment is unsuccessful (for example, due to insufficient funds), and the customer does not make the payment by alternative means prior the registration expiry date, the customer will be charged the RRRF fee.

    The customer’s financial institution may also apply fees for supplying a direct debit service. Customers should contact their financial institution to discuss any additional fees.
    Note:A card surcharge applies if using a credit or debit card to pay for registration by direct debit.
Changes/cancellations
Changes/cancellations
    Changes/cancellations by customer:
    Changes/cancellations by customer:
      If a customer wants to change their direct debit (e.g. add registrations or change account details), advise them to:
      • Login to the My Account portal
      • Click on My Registrations
      • Click on the relevant link (e.g. change account details, add registrations)
      • The registration term can be updated by expanding the registration details on the "My registration" page and selecting "Change details." Customers will be taken the “Change CTP details” web service where they can update the renewal term.

      Changes can be made up until the business day before the scheduled payment date.

      If a customer cancels their direct debit agreement, it will automatically remove any registrations enrolled in Direct Debit and cancel the authority for TMR to deduct the registration renewal fee/s from the customer's account.

      While the cancellation of the direct debit request is immediate, the cancellation of the direct debit request will not cancel any payment(s) already sent to the bank.

      Note:Customers must make changes to direct debit account details themselves because TMR do not store the details or have access to amend them.
      Changes/cancellations by TMR:
      Changes/cancellations by TMR:
        Cancelling direct debit agreement/removing vehicle from direct debit
        Cancelling direct debit agreement/removing vehicle from direct debit
          TMR can immediately cancel a direct debit in the event that:
          • The customer has not had an active registration associated with the service for more than 90 days;
          • The customer becomes ineligible (TMR is notified that the customer is deceased, the customer record is suppressed by IMU or has NOAC applied)

          TMR will also remove a vehicle from direct debit if a registration is not paid within 25 days after the registration expiry date. This will not affect other vehicles that the customer has enrolled in direct debit.

          An email will be sent advising the customer that TMR has cancelled their Direct Debit agreement or their vehicle has been removed from direct debit.

          TMR can also manually remove vehicles from a direct debit enrolment and place a NODD restriction on the customer's account if a request is received from a bank to cancel a direct debit.
        Updating the registration term
        Updating the registration term
          TMR operators can update the registration term for customers enrolled in direct debit. However, TICA will allow the term to be changed to one-month without the customer meeting the mandatory requirements for eNotices, eReminders and a recorded mobile phone number.

          When changing the registration term to one-month operators must first check whether the customer is signed up for eNotices, eReminders and has their mobile phone number recorded. If not, these details must be captured and preferences updated before the term can be changed.

          Note: One-month registration terms are based on a calendar month. Customers using thise renewal term must be signed up to eNotices/eReminders to ensure they receive their Vehicle Registration Renewal Notice before their account is swept and reminders about their vehicles expiring in the days leading up to the expiry date.

      Payment
      Payment
        Payment date
        Payment date
          • The expected payment date is 14 days (or the next business day) prior to the registration expiry date.
          • 3 days prior to the expected payment date, the customer will be sent an email outlining the expected payment date and amount (including surcharges).
          • It is the customer's responsibility to ensure funds are available on the expected payment date.
          • A bank may take up to 14 days to process a direct debit and clear the funds from the customer's account. Therefore, the customer should check their account before assuming the payment has been deducted.
        Payment made by another method
        Payment made by another method
          • Direct debit payments can be made by any other method, except at an Australia Post outlet. Customers should not attempt to pay by another method on or after their sweep date until they receive the email advising that their payment was not debited from their account.
          • If a payment is made by another method:
            • at least four days before the scheduled date, the direct debit email reminder will advise that the payment won’t be attempted as it’s already been paid.
            • 4 days or less before the scheduled date, the unsuccessful email sent on the scheduled payment date won’t recognise whether the payment was made by another method or not.
        Direct Debit unsuccessful payments
        Direct Debit unsuccessful payments
          TMR will only attempt to deduct a Direct Debit payment once.
          If a Direct Debit payment is unsuccessful, the customer will be sent an email and an SMS notification (where a mobile number is recorded) advising that the payment has been unsuccessful. The advice will direct the customer to make the registration payment via another method.

          If the customer does not pay the renewal by another method within 25 days of expiry the vehicle will be removed from direct debit and reverted to a 12-month term.

          For one-month registrations, if payment is not made after the registration expires customers are to be directed to the renew registration online service which will determine if a catch-up payment is required.

          Note:A NODD restriction may be applied when a 3rd consecutive direct debit payment fails.

        Monies paid in error
        Monies paid in error
          If the customer believes an error has occurred in the debit of their bank account or credit card, advise them to send their enquiry to Direct Debit

          The enquiry must include their Name, CRN and details of error etc. Advise the customer not to include their bank details as these are not needed.

          Note:A response should be provided within 5 working days.

      Immediate payments
      Immediate payments
        Customers can add their vehicles to the direct debit scheme until their registration expires, regardless of the registration term selected. However, if their registration is expiring within the next 18 days it is too late for their account to be swept so they must make an immediate payment using a Visa/MasterCard/American Express branded card.




        Note:Online payment for immediate payments is preferred, however if a customer presents at a CSC to make the payment it can be accepted.

        After the customer has enrolled in direct debit and added the vehicle they will be sent an 'Add registration successful with immediate payment' email advising that a one-off payment is required, they will be instructed to use the "Pay using your registration number" online service.
      Catch-Up payments
      Catch-Up payments
        If the direct debit payment for a one-month registration payment fails, the customer has up to the registrations expiry date to make the payment using the alternative payment options on the VRRN. This is the standard process for all renewals, regardless of term or payment option.

        If the customer lets their one-month registration expire, then their renewal payment needs to be made online through the registration renewal service because the amount due to renew their registration will vary depending on if a late payment, or a catch-up payment is required. These two payment scenarios are detailed below:
        • Late payment: If payment is made soon after expiry you will need to pay the registration renewal amount (1-month period) PLUS a late payment fee.
        • Catch-up payment: If payment is made too close (or after) your next scheduled direct debit payment you will need to pay the current expired term and the next renewal (2 x 1-month periods) PLUS a late payment fee.
        Note:TICA is unable to display 2 one-months terms on the payment screen, during this period only the first one-month period will display. If the first one-month term and RRRF is paid the second term won't display on the payment screen until the following day.

        Customers should be advised that they must pay their renewal using the "Pay by registration number" Online Service so that the full amount can be paid. If the customer is unable to pay using the Online Service please contact the Customer Account Management (CAM) unit for advice.
        • Has expired (Over 25 days after expiry date) - The registration is removed from direct debit. The customer must pay 6 or 12 months registration + full RRRF by another method. Customer Account Management

      Customer Communication
      Customer Communication
        The customer will be sent an electronic confirmation of their enrolment including their contact details and nominated bank account details.

        The enrolment confirmation received by the customer will be determined by the account the direct debit is to be deducted from:
        • Savings or Cheque account will receive the Direct Debit Request Service Notice; or
        • Credit Card account will receive the Direct Debit Standing Order Service Notice
        The below registration statuses may be mentioned in the customer communications:
        • Payment due - an Immediate Payment is required to renew registration. The customer has added their vehicle to direct debit too late for their account to be swept.
        • No action required - the registration is current and the next renewal payment will be paid by direct debit.
        • COI required - customer must pass a COI before the registration can be renewed. If there is less than 17 days until the registration expiry date the registration must be paid by another method once the COI has been recorded.
