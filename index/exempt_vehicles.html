Exempt Vehicles (3007)


What are they?These vehicles are exempt from registration automatically exempted under the TO (RUMS - Vehicle Registration) Regulations 2010.
Exempt VehiclesThese vehicles are automatically exempt under Section 4 of the Regulation and may access the road network without registration under the specified circumstances.

Proof of exemption is not required.
List of Exempt Vehicles
List of Exempt Vehicles
    Agricultural Plant
    Agricultural Plant

      Towed Agricultural Implements

      Can access the road if it is being towed by:
      • Agricultural machine suitably matched to the implement
      • Another vehicle of a suitable size for towing the vehicle

      Drawn Plant

      Can access the road if it is being towed by a conditionally registered vehicle of a size suitable for towing the plant.
    Motorcycles
    Motorcycles
      Motorcycles

      Used in an observed trial in an observed trial area.

      Definitions
      Observed Trial
      Observed Trial
        An observed trial is:
        • A competition to compare competitor's skills
        or
        • Event to demonstrate a riders skills in riding a motorcycle around a marked course one at time

      Observed Trial Area
      Observed Trial Area
        An area not dedicated to public use as a road, from which other traffic is excluded while it is being used as a course for an observed trial.


      Freestyle Motorcycle

      Bikes are to be used in a freestyle motocross in a freestyle motocross area.

      Definitions
      Freestyle Motocross
      Freestyle Motocross
        A freestyle motocross is either a:
        • Competition to compare competitors skills
        or
        • Event to demonstrate a riders skills in performing motorcycle acrobatics on a motorcycle that has been ridden off a ramp.

      Freestyle Motocross Area
      Freestyle Motocross Area
        An area:
        • Not dedicated to public use as a road,
        • From which other traffic is excluded while it is being used as a course for an observed trial.
    Scooters
    Scooters
      Low Powered Vehicles and Motorised Scooters

      Includes motor vehicle with either:
      • One motor with maximum power </= 200 watts
      or
      • Two or more motors with a combined maximum power of </= 200 watts
    Golf Buggy
    Golf Buggy
      Motorised Golf Buggy and Motorised Golf Course Machinery

      Exempt if either:
      • Used on a golf course
      • Used in a golf course car park
      • Crossing a road dividing a golf course (buggy only)
    Racing Vehicle
    Racing Vehicle
      If being used:
      • On a racing track
      • In a related area for a racing track

      Definitions
      Racing Vehicle
      Racing Vehicle
        A vehicle that is used in a motor race or for motor race driver trainer
      Racing Track
      Racing Track
        A circuit or track:
        • Not dedicated to public use as a road
        • Used as a course for a motor race or motor race driver training
        • Other traffic is excluded from the track or circuit during its use for the race or training
      Racing Track Related Areas
      Racing Track Related Areas
        An area:
        • Adjacent to the racing track where a racing vehicle is parked or driven for a purpose associated with the motor race or driver training
        • Not dedicated to public use as a road
    Ride-on Mower
    Ride-on Mower
      Is used for mowing and is either:
      • Crossing a road from one road related area to another
      • Directly crossing a road
      • On a road related area

      A ride-on mower can be conditionally registered if greater access to the road network is required

      Excludes:
      • A tractor hauling a slasher
      • A ride-on mower that is an agricultural vehicle
    Trailers
    Trailers
      If being towed by either a:
      • Agricultural machine that is being used for its intended agricultural purpose
      • Conditionally registered vehicle with a limited access registration category

      Operator must remove the rear plate of the towing vehicle and attach it to the rear of the vehicle being towed.
    Towed Vehicles
    Towed Vehicles
      A vehicle being towed by a Tow Truck

      This exemption is intended for a vehicle being towed by a genuine tow truck that has lifting/loading capability not vehicles being towed in general:

      Tow Truck Operator Options
      Tow Truck Operator Options
        • Remove the plate from the rear of the towing unit and attach it to the vehicle being towed
        or
        • Order an accessory plate which may be attached to the vehicle being towed.


      The towing unit must be registered but there is no need for a dealer plate to be displayed on an unregistered vehicle being towed.
    Not an MV
    Not an MV
      Vehicle that is not a Motor Vehicle or Trailer

      Vehicles that do not meet the definitions contained in TO(RUMS) Act 1995 Schedule 4:
      Vehicle
      Vehicle
        Any type of transport that moves on wheels.

      Motor Vehicle
      Motor Vehicle
        A vehicle propelled by a motor that forms part of the vehicle, and includes a trailer attached to the vehicle.
      Trailer
      Trailer
        A vehicle that is built to be towed, by a motor vehicle but does not include a motor vehicle being towed.


      Examples of some that do not fit the definition:
        • Pram
        • Strollers
        • Trolleys
        • Skateboards


      Walking Speed Equipment

      If on road only for either:
      • Loading/Unloading to/from another vehicle
      • Repositioning it at a work site
    Rural Fire Brigade
    Rural Fire Brigade
      Exempt from registration under the Fire and Emergency Services Act 1990.
      Definition
      Definition
        A vehicle used on a road and :
        • operated by a rural fire brigade or an emergency services unit to carry persons or equipment for the purpose of:
        • preventing, controlling or extinguishing a fire
        • (fire fighting) training
        • licence testing for brigade members (Refer to attached advice from Emergency Services)
        Authority Rural Fire Brigade License Testing

        • Other activities authorised in writing by the Fire Commissioner
        and
        • it bears clear identification as a rural fire brigade vehicle
        and
        • there is in force a contract of insurance providing indemnity such as is prescribed in s23(1) of the Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994in respect of injury caused by, through or in connection with the vehicle. This means that the Rural Fire Brigade requires insurance that mirrors Compulsory Third Party insurance. The Rural Fire Brigade should contact Department of Community Safety who should hold an overarching insurance policy to cover Rural Fire Brigades. This insurance is issued by Queensland Government Insurance Fund (QGIF).
    Unregistered Vehicle that is Dealer stock
    Unregistered Vehicle that is Dealer stock
      • An unregistered motor vehicle that is dealer's stock being used on the dealer's premises.

        Definition
        dealer's premises means the premises where a dealer carries on business -
        (a)including any part of the premises used for car parking or for inspecting, repairing, servicing or cleaning motor vehicles; and
        (b)excluding any road-related area adjacent to the premises.
    Motorised Wheelchair
    Motorised Wheelchair
      If the motorised wheelchair being used by a person who
      • does not usually reside in Queensland; or
      • has resided in Queensland for not more than 14 days
    Personal Mobility Devices (PMDs)
    Personal Mobility Devices (PMDs)
      PMDs, or rideables, are permitted to be used on road-related areas and local streets provided users comply with a number of conditions.

      A PMD/rideable in Queensland must:
      • be designed for use by one person only
      • have 1 or more wheels
      • be propelled by an electric motor
      • have a maximum speed of 25km/h
      • Fit the following dimensions
      • 1250mm length x 700mm width x 1350 height or
      • 700mm length x 1250mm width x 1350mm height
      • have a maximum height of 1350mm
      • have a maximum weight of 60kg (when not carrying a person or load)
      • have an effective stopping system controlled by using brakes, gears and/or motor control
      • have no sharp protrusions
      Rules for Operation
      Rules for Operation
        Conditions and Restrictions
        PMD rideable users must:
        • Be aged 16 and over to operate a PMD unsupervised
        • Be supervised by an adult if aged between 12 and 15
        • Wear an approved bicycle helmet that is securely fitted
        • Keep left when travelling on a path
        • Keep left to oncoming bicycles and PMDs on a path
        • Have a working flashing or steady white light (front), red light (rear) and a red reflector (rear) for use at night or in hazardous conditions
        • Travel at a speed that allows them to stop safely to avoid colliding with a pedestrian
        • Travel at a safe distance from a pedestrian so that they can avoid a collision
        • Only use the bicycle side of a shared path
        • Not travel faster than 25km/h
        • not be under the influence of alcohol
        • not use a handheld mobile phone
        • not carry passenger/s
        • not travel past a 'Personal Mobility Device Prohibited' sign
        PMD/rideable users are not required to have a warning device, however, if they do they are encouraged to use it.
        Limited access to roads is permitted. Riders may use their device:
        • to cross a road or avoid an obstruction on a path for up to 50m
        • to cross a road at designated crossing
        • on local streets, where it is safe to do so. A local street is a road with a speed limit of 50km/h or less. It must have no driving line or median strip and if it is one-way road, it can't have more than one lane
        Riders must:
        • not ride on main roads or streets in the Brisbane CBD
        • keep as far to the left side of the road as practicable
        • not ride alongside more than 1 other person or vehicle travelling on a road in the same direction
        • not cause a hazard by moving into the path of a driver or unreasonably obstruct the path of any other road user
        • not be towed by another vehicle
        • not hold on to the back of another vehicle
        • ride within 2m of the rear of a moving vehicle continuously for more than 200m
