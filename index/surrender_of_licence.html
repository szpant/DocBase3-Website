Surrender of Licence (1748)

Surrender of Licence
A licence may be surrendered at any time.
Where a licence is surrendered the Smartcard must be returned.

Note:If a customer is unable to surrender the smartcard (lost, stolen or destroyed) when surrendering their licence, they are still eligible for a refund if all other requirements have been met. The smartcard should still be returned in TICA using the appropriate reason.
Interstate / Overseas Travel
Licensee will be outside Qld within the six week renewal window - licence can be surrendered and reissued to ensure it remains current during this time.

Surrender and reissue of a Provisional/Probationary licence on the same day does not constitute a break in the required total period.

Note:Provisional/ Probationary/Open licences may be renewed up to 6 months prior to expiry

A credit is given for the unexpired portion of the licence (laminate and smartcard). The person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card.
Medical
If a person has a medical condition that prevents them from driving and they wish to voluntarily surrender their licence, they must return their driver licence together with a completed Medical Condition Notification (F4355) Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712) or written notice, to a licence issuing agency or by mailing it to the Medical Condition Reporting (MCR) Unit

The person should be issued with/mailed a TICA generated Licence Surrender Receipt and advised that they may be eligible for a free Photo ID card.
Refer: QT Shop > Customer Products > Photo Identification Card Guide

Once the surrender is processed, a pro-rata refund of unexpired portion of licence fee will be credited to the customer account. The amount can be refunded by EFTPOS/Credit card (if the customer presents in person) or cheque.
Refund of Licence Fee
Voluntary
Licensee no longer wishes to drive. The operator must return the smartcard and surrender the licence.

No refund given but they may be eligible for a free Photo ID card.
Refer: QT Shop > Customer Products > Photo Identification Card Guide
Multiple Licence HolderA person may not hold a licence in more than one Australian State or Territory simultaneously. Any surplus licences must be surrendered.

No refund given. The person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card.
On behalf of deceased
If a person wishes to surrender a licence on behalf of a deceased person, they must provide written evidence for the surrender (e.g. death certificate, funeral notice, coroner's report or other written evidence of proof of death) and the deceased person's licence, to a licence issuing agency or by mailing it to Department of Transport and Main Roads

The operator must return the smartcard and 'surrender' the licence on TICA. The person should be issued with TICA generated Licence Surrender Receipt.

Once the surrender is processed, a pro-rata refund of unexpired portion of licence fee will be credited to the customer account. The amount will be refunded by cheque to the "Estate of <deceased>".

Refund of Licence Fee

Note:The deceased customer’s CRN should be checked for registered vehicles or TMR products that have been issued, e.g. industry authority or Photo ID card. If necessary, advise the representative of the deceased that these products should be surrendered to TMR.
MergedSmartcard must be returned and licence is surrendered before a merge with another record.

No refund given. The person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card.
Interstate licence heldLicence is surrendered because the person now holds an interstate driver licence.

No refund given. The person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card.
Overseas licence heldLicence is surrendered because the person now holds an overseas driver licence.

No refund given. The person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card.
Cancelled driver licenceIt is a requirement that Queensland driver licences smartcards are returned if disqualified.

Note: Driver licence is not to be "surrendered" in TICA as status already shows as cancelled.
