Dealer/Trade Plates (3083)


What is it?A dealer/trailer trade plate is used by persons employed in a Motor Related industry to operate/transport unregistered vehicles/trailers in their normal course of business.
EligibilityThe following applicants are automatically eligible:
  • Licensed Motor/Trailer Dealer
  • New Car Retailer
  • Charity organisations conducting art unions
  • Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) who road test vehicles after repair

Other persons may be eligible if the nature of their business supports its use.
Note:A Dealer/Trailer Trade plate must not be used on a vehicle that is eligible to be conditionally registered with limited access registration or zone access registration. If an applicant's business only involves these types of vehicles, then a Dealer/Trailer Trade plate cannot be issued.

A Dealer plate can be used on a vehicle that is eligible to be conditionally registered with unrestricted access. However, the vehicle must still abide by the conditions that would apply if the vehicle was to be registered, e.g. permit for overmass and/or dimensional.

Rules
Use of Dealer/Trailer Trade Plate
Use of Dealer/Trailer Trade Plate
    The vehicle to which the plate is to be attached must:
    • Be roadworthy
    • Comply with vehicle laws
    • Only be used for one of the following purposes
      Allowed uses
      Allowed uses
        Demonstration for:
        • Sale
        • Conducting art union (Charitable and Non-profit Gaming Act 1999)
        • Delivery to purchaser after sale
        • Travel between workshop/other facility, or testing after:
          • Painting, repairs or motor body construction work
          • Fitting equipment before delivery to a purchaser

        Driven or towed:
        • To an AIS to obtain an Inspection Certificate
        • To written-off vehicle inspection site for a written-off vehicle inspection report
        • To a place as required by TMR
        • To TMR office to be registered
        • From vehicle manufacturer/distributor to a dealer
        • Between dealer business premises

        Being driven for:
        • testing to confirm the safe operation as part of an inspection for obtaining an inspection certificate

      Note: Other uses can only be approved by the Chief Executive.

      Any unapproved uses stated on the application form must be crossed out and initialled by the applicant.

      Issuing Dealer/Trade Plates
      Issuing Dealer/Trade Plates
        For a list of CSC's that stock SIV plates refer to Special Plate Types - Storage locations.

      Note: A Vehicle trade plate can be attached to either a vehicle or trailer where the Trailer trade plate can only be attached to a trailer.

    • Is eligible for conditional registration with limited access registration or zone access registration; and
    • Is non complying

    Conditions of Use - Dealer/Trade Plates
Authority to Use
Authority to Use
    A person must have authorisation from the registered plate owner to use
    a vehicle on the road with a Dealer/Trailer trade plate attached.

    The vehicle must only be used in the conduct of the business of the person in whose name the dealer plate is registered.
When Plates Must be Surrendered and Registration Cancelled
When Plates Must be Surrendered and Registration Cancelled
    The registered operator of a dealer plate must notify the Department of Transport and Main Roads within 14 days, if they stop carrying on the business for which the plate was registered or if there is a material change in the nature of their business as stated on the application for the plate. The plate must be surrendered to the Department, and the registration cancelled.

    For example, if a dealer plate is issued on the basis that the registered operator
    is a motor dealer and they cease to be a motor dealer, the plate is no longer valid and must be surrendered and the registration cancelled.
