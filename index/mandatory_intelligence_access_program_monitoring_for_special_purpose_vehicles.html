Mandatory Intelligence Access Program Monitoring for Special Purpose Vehicles (7084)

Project NameMandatory Intelligent Access Program (IAP) monitoring requirements for Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)
When and What?From 1 December 2016, TMR will implement mandatory IAP enrolment for Class 1, 2 and 3 SPVs.

These class categories may include vehicles such as heavy mobile cranes, concrete pumps and drilling rigs.

IAP registration will become mandatory from 1 June 2017.
BackgroundThe IAP is a national program developed in partnership with all Australian road agencies and jurisdictions. It remotely monitors, to an evidentiary standard, where, when and how heavy vehicles are being operated on the road network. A module that provides on board mass information can also be included as part of the IAP.

In 2010, TMR introduced the voluntary application of the IAP for selected SPVs in Queensland, including heavy mobile cranes, concrete pump trucks and drill rigs, to improve the management of road network access. By participating in IAP, operators were able to qualify for excess mass period permits of extended duration and increase road network access. Operators that chose not to participate in the IAP remained eligible for more restricted permit durations and road network access.
Further informationFor more information visit the Intelligent Access Program page on TMR’s website.
