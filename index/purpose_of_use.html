Purpose of Use (3032)


What is it?The Purpose of Use (POU) identifies what the vehicle is to be used for.

POU is recorded by TMR to:
  • Identify vehicles subject to annual or biannual inspections
  • Assist in the identification of vehicles that may be eligible for the Family Vehicle Registration Fee Freeze
  • Enhance the accuracy of fee determination, in particular CTP
  • Assist on-road enforcement
  • Assist research in the development of new policy or business initiatives

Selecting the Correct POU
Selecting the Correct POU
    It is the customers responsibility:
    • To advise a vehicle's POU
    • To ensure compliance with any conditions

    Note: Businesses, trusts and organisations are not eligible for Private POU.

    Generally, if an operator claims there is more than one POU, they are to decide what the main purpose of use is. However, vehicles registered with private POU must be used exclusively for personal and domestic activities. If the operator does not use the vehicle exclusively for personal and domestic activities, the registered operator must nominate a POU other than private. In some cases, a higher class of CTP or inspection requirement for one purpose of use will dictate the POU.

    Rental Minibuses used for Public Passenger Services
    Rental Minibuses used for Public Passenger Services
      Rental companies buses with 10-12 seats and a GVM </or= 4.5t that are used as Public Passenger Vehicles Services (PPSE) for all or part of the time may choose to register either with:
      • Purpose of Use of Public Passenger Service (PPSE) and CTP class 10a or 11
        or
      • Purpose of Use Rental (Self Driver Rental) (RENT) with Class 4 CTP.

      The use of PPSE ensures these light vehicles are identified as being inspectable which is a requirement to operate the vehicle as a public passenger service.

      The Motor Accident Insurance Commission supports the process of having vehicles used for hire registered with a POU of Public Passenger Service (PPSE), regardless of whether the vehicle is used for any other purpose. This is a fleet management decision and the responsibility of the registered operator to nominate the relevant POU.

      Example:
      A vehicle that is used for commercial purposes for 80% of the time and as a public passenger service for 20% of the time, must be registered with the POU of PPSE and charged the higher rate of CTP insurance.
Booked Hire (BKHR) and Booked Hire Rental (BKRN)
Booked Hire (BKHR) and Booked Hire Rental (BKRN)
    Dual purpose of use
    Dual purpose of use
      A booked hire vehicle used for dual purposes (e.g. Private and Booked hire) is required to be registered with a POU of Booked Hire (BKHR).

      A rental vehicle used to provide a booked hire service is required to have its POU updated from Rental (RENT) to Booked Hire Rental (BKRN).

      If the vehicle is to be used for Operator Accreditation and a Booked Hire Service Licence, the POU must be BKRN until a decision is made on the BHSL application. Once a decision is made, the POU can be changed to PPSE.

      Refer: Personalised & Passenger Transport > 1. Booked Hire Service Licence > Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) Guide
    Change from BKHR/BKRN/PPSE
    Change from BKHR/BKRN/PPSE
      If a Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) Holder wants to change the POU of the vehicle on their BHSL to a non-compliant use, their BHSL must be surrendered unless another BHSL compliant vehicle is being added to the BHSL.

      The operator can process the vehicle change first and then email the customer's voluntary surrender to TransLink for processing. Refer Surrender a BHSL
Non-Profit
Non-Profit
    A non-profit organisation could be a:
    • welfare arm of a church
    • public benevolent institution (who relieve the needs of persons in distress)
    • charitable institution
    • community fund-raising organisation
    Church Based Bus
    Church Based Bus
      Includes church based school buses used for either or both of the following:
      • During the school week for the transport of the students from church operated schools
      • On the weekend or at other times for non-profit use to transport the parishioners of the associated church to their services or to other church sponsored activities

      It also includes non-school buses that are owned by the church and used solely for the work of the church and associated church organisations (i.e, Sunday Schools)
Not to be classified as Non-Profit
Not to be classified as Non-Profit
    Community/Courtesy Buses
    Community/Courtesy Buses
      Non-profit buses:
      • With </= 9 seats
      • Used for community/courtesy purposes

      Should be registered with a POU of Commercial (COML).

    Sporting Clubs
    Sporting Clubs
      If an organisation, including clubs (such as Returned Services Leagues - RSL) is conducted as a company or business, any vehicle registered by the organisation should not be classified as NONP.
      An organisation is deemed to be operating as a company/business if either of the following occur:
      • Poker machines are used to raise revenue to provide facilities or services for its members
      • The club is the holder of a permanent liquor licence that permits it to operate at times other than when the sporting activities are conducted at their home venue.

    School Services and Charters
    School Services and Charters
      Any bus or mini bus (including those owned by religious or church based organisations) that are used for both of the following are not be classified as non-profit:
      • School transport
        and
      • Hire or charter outside of these hours

      They should be classified as either School Activities Only (SCHA) or Public Passenger Service (PPSE).
Translink Urban Service (TLKU)
Translink Urban Service (TLKU)
    Buses contracted to TransLink to provide urban passenger transport services.

    Any contractor using a bus for dual purposes that included any urban transport service is required to have this class regardless of what percentage of time the bus is used for urban transport.
Commercial (COML) - Parking In Loading Zones
Commercial (COML) - Parking In Loading Zones
    The Commercial (COML) POU does not allow the motorist of that vehicle to entitlements for parking in loading zones.

    Customers will have to make inquiries to the particular local council where they wish to use loading zones.
Changing POU
Changing POU
    A person must provide written notification of a change of POU within 14 days of the change.

    When changing POU the CTP class may also need to be updated. Refer: Update a TRAILS/TICA Record

    In some cases, changing the POU will alter the registration charge (including eligibility for the Family Vehicle Registration Fee Freeze, concessions and CTP). Payment of a pro-rata fee may apply.

    Change of POU that can be processed by Mail or Electronic lodgement:
    • Must not involve any pro-rata payment

    Note: As Duty is not paid for dealer use, a copy of the change ie. Registration Certificate should be sent by the dealer to - Office of State Revenue
Changing from Dealer POU
Changing from Dealer POU
    Duty is exempt for vehicles registered to Licensed Motor Dealers with a POU of Dealer.

    Reassessment by the Office of State Revenue

    A dealer must notify the Office of State Revenue (OSR) by completing Form OSR-D9.2 within 28 days of the following events:

    • If the vehicle has ceased to be trading stock or a demonstrator and is used by the dealer for another purpose (change of POU is a good indicator); or
    • If the vehicle has not been sold by the dealer within one year of it's registration.

    Note: Form OSR-D9.2 should be posted to - Office of State Revenue
