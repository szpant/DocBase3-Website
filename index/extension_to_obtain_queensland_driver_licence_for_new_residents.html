Extension to obtain Queensland driver licence for new residents - COVID-19 (7549)

Approved ActionExtension to obtain Queensland driver licence for new residents
WhenEffective immediately and until further notice
BackgroundThose who are driving on an international or interstate licence are currently required to obtain a Queensland driver licence within three-months of becoming a Queensland resident. After three months their authority to drive on a Queensland road is withdrawn. In some instances, this will require a practical driving test (PDT) to be completed.

On 28 March 2020, all PDTs were suspended for three months (subject to review) in the interest of community health and safety and in line with Queensland health advice and guidelines.

In recognition that some will find it difficult to obtain a Queensland licence, particularly if a practical driving test is required, the three-month period for international or interstate licence holders to obtain a Queensland driver has been extended until three months after the COVID-19 emergency period ends.
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers
Customers
    Customers who become a resident of Queensland will now have until 3 months after the COVID-19 emergency period ends before their authority to drive in Queensland is withdrawn. They can continue to drive on their interstate or overseas licence as long as it is current and valid.
CSC and Customer Contact Centre Operators
CSC and Customer Contact Centre Operators
    Operators may notice an increase in enquiries regarding the change to requirements for international or interstate licence holders.

    Operators should advise the customer that the three-month period has been extended and they can continue to drive on their overseas or interstate licence as long as that licence is valid.

Further informationCustomers who are seeking further information or wish to make a complaint should be referred to TMR through the normal communication channels. Standard responses on this issue have been distributed.
