Customer Feedback (7074)


What it isCustomer feedback is crucial to informing improvements to our business.

Customer feedback can be lodged online by the customer in the form of an official compliment or complaint, or can be captured in person, via a customer service survey.
Customer Compliments and Complaints
How to lodge a compliment, complaint or feedbackCustomers can make a compliment or complaint either verbally or in writing.

Verbally
Verbally
    By Phone
    By Phone
      Customers can make a compliment or complaint, or provide feedback by calling 13 23 80 (between 8am and 5pm AEST, Monday to Friday)

      Where a customer calls to lodge a complaint, consultants should follow the procedure outlined in KAOS:
      https://inside.tmr.qld.gov.au/sites/csdkaos/Documents/Complaint%20process%202015.doc

    In person
    In person
      Customers can make a compliment or complaint, or provide feedback in person at a TMR customer service centre.

      Where a customer wishes to make a complaint in person, staff should:
      • actively listen and respond in a positive and objective way
      • try to understand the complaint and ask questions, clarify and confirm the issue/s
      • offer to read out the privacy statement and request for consent
      • gather all relevant information about what’s involved and the requested outcome
      • provide assurance that the issue/s will be appropriately investigated
      • explain how and when we will provide an update and/or a response if applicable.

      In most regions, the Complaints Management Form (F4561) is required to be completed by the staff member, to capture information on behalf of the customer. Please speak with your manager if you are unsure of the correct process.
In writing
In writing
    Customers can make a compliment or complaint, or provide feedback in writing by:
    • completing the online form
    • completing a customer complaint form (QF4603) and submitting it in person or via post to the address below
    • completing the printable customer compliments form (QF4959) and submitting it in person or via post to the address below
    • writing to:
      Compliments and complaints
Customers must be treated with courtesy and respect at all times, however, to meet our duty of care to our staff, employees should not accept any complaints that are abusive, threatening or contain offensive language.

To provide assistance to customers with a hearing impairment or learning/language difficulties, please refer to the Providing Assistance to Customers with Hearing Impairment or Learning/Language Difficulties guide.

More information is available for customers on the TMR Website: http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/About-us/Contact-us/Compliments-and-complaints
TMR Complaints Policy and ProceduresAll compliments and complaints must be dealt with in accordance with the TMR Complaints Policy and Procedures.

These are available for staff and customers online, via the below link:
http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/About-us/Contact-us/Compliments-and-complaints
Customer Service Surveys
Survey formA customer service survey has been developed to assist in the capture of customer feedback relating to service provided in a TMR customer service centre.

Copies of this survey should be printed and made available to customers, for example, on the forms counter.

Customer Service Survey

Completed surveys must be scanned and emailed to CSB Data Analysis so that feedback can be captured and monitored.
Other mechanismsCSB is currently working on identifying how we can capture customer feedback through other means, including electronic surveys. Until these mechanisms are developed and released, please direct customers to provide feedback via the above processes.
