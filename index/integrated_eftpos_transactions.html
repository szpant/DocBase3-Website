Integrated EFTPOS Transactions (4952)

An integrated EFTPOS transaction occurs on an EFTPOS device that functions through TICA for payments, refunds and cancellations.

There is no operator interaction with an integrated EFTPOS device, other than the manual entering of the payment account (DRCARD, VISA or MCAR) type in TICA..

An integrated EFTPOS device is unable to integrate with TRAILS and all payments must be processed in TICA.

Where a cardholder uses an Australian Domestic card the Integrated EFTPOS terminal will drive a PIN (Personal Identification Number) to complete the card transaction unless the customer taps their card to pay an amount less than $100.

In the following circumstances only the Integrated EFTPOS terminal will require a Written (Signature) from the cardholder:
  • Where a Prepaid VISA or MasterCard is used.
  • Where an Overseas credit card is used.
  • Where the cardholder is visually impaired and has obtained a special issue card provided by their financial institution.
  • Manual/Magstripe transactions (i.e. where a merchant swipes the magnetic strip on non-chip cards).

Card details must:
  • Not be written down when provided verbally
  • Be blacked out with permanent marker when provided in writing by the customer.
Refusal of Cards
Refusal of Cards
    A card may only be accepted if:
    • The terminal recognises the card
    • The card is not expired
    • The card or signature has not been visibly tampered with
    • The card is not damaged.
    Note:A payment cannot be split into more than one transaction using the same card.

    If a customer uses a card with a PIN, it is not necessary for staff to check that the card is being used by the card holder eg: use by family members.

    If you suspect that the card may be stolen the receipting officer should perform a "Card Authorisation" with the CBA prior to accepting the card.

Customer Interaction with EFTPOS Device
Customer Interaction with EFTPOS Device
    • Customer to follow all instructions on EFTPOS device.
    • Customer can only use one credit/debit card per card payment transaction.
    • Customer can either:
      • Insert card - chip end into the bottom of the device
      • Tap card - hold a card with a contactless icon displayed within 4 cm of the device
      • Swipe card - magnetic strip faces towards the keypad
    • If a customer inserts or swipes their card, they will select their account (CHQ, SAV, CR*), enter PIN and remove card.

      *If the customer is using a MasterCard or Visa branded card to make payment they will pay a surcharge.
Operator Actions
Operator Actions
    Operator Actions
    Operator Actions
      Operators will need to verify the payment type up-front, if the customer makes payment using a Mastercard or Visa branded card they will pay the surcharge.

      Operators should select the following card types from TICA to ensure the correct surcharge is charged:
      • DRCARD - Used for payments made by BasicsCard or eftpos debit card. This includes contactless payments made by eftpos Tap&Pay.
      • VISA - Used for any card displaying a Visa logo, including Visa debit cards
      • MCAR - Used for any card displaying a MasterCard logo, including a MasterCard debit card.

      Operators do not need to provide additional verbal advice about the surcharge if the centre has clearly displayed signage, unless the customer specifically enquires. For copies of the approved signs Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Payment > Payment Methods > Card Surcharge

    Acknowledge Surcharge
    Acknowledge Surcharge
      If VISA or MCAR is selected a surcharge will be applied. The operator will be required to accept the surcharge on the TICA dialogue box before the transaction can proceed.

    Signature Authorisation
    Signature Authorisation
      • Customer signs the 'Signature Verification Receipt' above 'Please Sign Here'
      • Operator:
        • Checks signature with signature on card.
        • If signature is unacceptable, ask the customer to re-sign, if signature is still not acceptable press 'No' in Signature Verified TICA screen and a receipt will automatically generate.
        • If signature is acceptable press 'Yes' in the Signature verified TICA screen and the EFTPOS receipt will automatically generate.
        • Retain signed merchant copy of EFTPOS receipt.
        • Attach to banking sheet and end of day banking.

    Transaction Approved
    Transaction Approved
      • Give customer a copy of the automatically generated receipt.

    Transaction Declined
    Transaction Declined
      • EFTPOS device will give customer decline reason e.g. insufficient funds.
      • TICA will provide the operator with what action to take e.g. action failed, retry transaction, advise customer if appropriate.
      • Receipt will show card status, approved or decline with an error code e.g. '00' means approved.

    Cancel Transaction
    Cancel Transaction
      • Only an operator can cancel a transaction through the EFTPOS Dialogue Box in TICA. Refer to TICA Help for instructions.
      • A customer can not cancel a transaction on the EFTPOS device.
      Multiple Payment Methods
      Multiple Payment Methods
        • Multiple payment methods (e.g. DRCARD and cash)cannot be processed in the same transaction. The only exception is when an amount is being paid from a customer account along with another payment method.
        • If using multiple payment methods including a card transaction (other than card and pay from customer account), the EFTPOS transaction should be processed first, as a payment to the customer account, to ensure the amount will be approved.
    Receipts
    Receipts
      • TICA will automatically print the EFTPOS device receipt on one A4 sheet from a Kyocera or laser printer.
      • Merchant copies of receipts are not required and will not be produced for a PIN transaction.
      • The EFTPOS receipt will print in a number of ways depending on the transaction performed.

      EFTPOS Receipt

    Correct EFTPOS Transaction
    Correct EFTPOS Transaction
      Transactions to be Reprocessed
      Transactions to be Reprocessed
        • If the transaction needs to be reprocessed (e.g. rego renewal processed on incorrect registration) then the receipt should not be cancelled.
        • Unmatch/Invalidate the payment to ensure the credit is placed in the customer's account.
          Note:If the customer paid a card surcharge on the original transaction only the product amount will be returned to the customers account.

          If the transaction was for a miscellaneous non-customer product (i.e. no CRN attached to payment) the customer account should be credited by following the Miscellaneous Credit process.

          Refer: Admin > Money > The How To: Administration > Credit Miscellaneous Non Customer Product (Miscellaneous Credit)
        • The transaction will be paid using the customer account credit.
        • If the reprocessed transaction is for an amount less than the original amount, there will be a credit remaining in the customer account.
        • If the customer is present the credit may be refunded directly back onto the customer's card (refer Refund for when and how). Advise customer a refund of fees back to a card can take up to 30 business days to appear in their bank account depending on their bank.
        • If the customer is not present the credit can be refunded by cheque, although this is not the preferred option.

      Transactions not to be Reprocessed
      Transactions not to be Reprocessed
        Customer present
        Customer present
          Correcting errors on the transaction date
          Correcting errors on the transaction date
            • If the transaction does not need to be reprocessed (e.g. customer ineligible for product) and it is the original transaction date then the receipt should be cancelled.
            • Cancelling the receipt will refund the EFTPOS amount (including credit card surcharge) back onto the customer's card (refer Cancelling a Receipt for how).

          Correcting errors after the transaction date
          Correcting errors after the transaction date
            • If the transaction does not need to be reprocessed (e.g. customer ineligible for product) and it is not the original transaction date then the receipt cannot be cancelled.
            • Unmatch/Invalidate the payment to ensure the credit is placed in the customer's account.
              Note:If the customer paid a card surcharge on the original transaction only the product amount will be returned to the customers account.

              If the transaction was for a miscellaneous non-customer product (i.e. no CRN attached to payment) the customer account should be credited by following the Miscellaneous Credit process.

              Refer: Admin > Money > The How To: Administration > Credit Miscellaneous Non Customer Product (Miscellaneous Credit)
            • The credit in the customer account may be refunded directly back onto the customer's card (refer Refunds for when and how).
            • Customers should be advised that the refund of fees can take up to 30 business days to appear in their bank account depending on their bank.
        Customer not present
        Customer not present
          • If the transaction is not to be reprocessed (e.g. rego renewal processed on incorrect registration) and the customer is not present then the receipt can not be cancelled.
          • Unmatch/Invalidate the payment to ensure the credit is placed in the customer's account.
            Note:If the customer paid a card surcharge on the original transaction only the product amount will be returned to the customers account.

            If the transaction was for a miscellaneous non-customer product (i.e. no CRN attached to payment) the customer account should be credited by following the Miscellaneous Credit process.

            Refer: Admin > Money > The How To: Administration > Credit Miscellaneous Non Customer Product (Miscellaneous Credit)
          • The credit can be refunded by cheque, although this is not the preferred option.
    Cancelling a Receipt
    Cancelling a Receipt
      • When a product transaction (e.g. registration) with an EFTPOS payment needs to be cancelled or corrected on the same day the transaction was processed, the TMR receipt can be cancelled.
      • The EFTPOS device will automatically populate with the amount receivable (including the card surcharge if the payment was made using CR) that corresponds to the total amount recorded on the TMR receipt being cancelled.
      • Advise customer a refund of fees back to a card can take up to 30 business days to appear in their bank account depending on their bank.

    Downtime Process (Terminal not functioning or Error Message)
    Downtime Process (Terminal not functioning or Error Message)
      TMR prohibits accepting or processing a transaction as either an:
      • Off-line Voucher
      • Manual EFTPOS Downtime Voucher
    Variable Term Facility Registration
    Variable Term Facility Registration
      A Variable Term Facility Registration Payment (VTFRP) using an EFTPOS transaction debits the card for the recalculated amount. If an amount is nominated from the customers account, then the full amount is taken from this, and the residual of the recalculation is debited from the card. In the event there is money left over in the customer's account after paying by card, then the operator is to follow the refund card process.
    Banking
    Banking
      • As EFTPOS is paperless there will be no need to print the Cumulative Total. There is also no need to keep copies of the Approved or Declined EFTPOS receipts.
      • The only EFTPOS receipts that need to be kept are the signature authorisation slips that customers have signed (including electronic refund slips). These are to be handed to the banking officer at the end of each day. Refer: Admin > Administration Money Guide > Actions > Banking > Banking Procedures > Banking Sheet
