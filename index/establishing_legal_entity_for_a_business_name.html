Establishing Legal Entity for a Business Name (7166)


    Establishing the Legal Entity for a Business Name
    You can establish the the Legal Entity for a Business Name using the following steps.
    1
    Legal Entity for a Business Name
    Legal Entity for a Business Name
      1
      Conduct ASIC Search
      Conduct ASIC Search
        • Conduct an ASIC Search as outlined in <ASIC Details Link>
      2
      Select the relevant Business Name in the search results.
      Select the relevant Business Name in the search results.


        Highlight the text of the name and copy it (Crtl C or edit/copy).
      3
      Search the Business Name
      Search the Business Name
        • Select the Search Business Names Register tab (highlighted and circled below)



        Paste the business name exactly as it appeared in the search results you copy/pasted in the field for business name, and Click Go.

        This will either return a list of results, or, directly open the correct entity.
        • If it returns a result screen, select the name and click View Details.
      4
      The Results
      The Results
        • The detailed results screen will show the business name (highlighted at top) and also the details of the business name holder.
        • The business name holder is the legal entity connected to the business name and can be an individual, a partnership (two or more individuals) or a company.
        • In the example shown it is an individual, and the individual has an ABN recorded. Clicking on the link will open the ABN details.

        You will also see (circled in red) the business number identifier to be entered in CID.

      5
      Creating Business in CID
      Creating Business in CID

        Where a business name and a business name holder (legal entity) exist and are not already in CID, they need to be created.
        The instructions for adding an involved party are in DocBase here.

        Note:Note – where an individual is the legal entity of a business name, the business name and the person must be linked in CID (see examples further down).

        A business name cannot hold an ABN, it is not a legal entity. Where an ABN is recorded and the legal entity is an individual, the ABN is recorded on the person entity in the ABN field:
