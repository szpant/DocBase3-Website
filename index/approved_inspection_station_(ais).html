Approved Inspection Station (AIS) (4663)


What is an Approved Inspection Station (AIS)An Approved Inspection Station (AIS) is a fixed establishment such as a service station, garage or workshop where motor vehicles are inspected for the purpose of issuing a Safety Certificate and/or Certificate of Inspection.

An AIS approval holder may be an individual (sole trader), partnership, trustee of a trust or a corporation.

The AIS approval holder is responsible for the operation of the AIS.

Vehicle inspection types are carried out by approved examiners.

An AIS may be approved to conduct vehicle inspections for:
  • CL – COI for light vehicles up to and including 4.5 tonne GVM that are used, or planning to be used, for the following purpose of use:
    • booked hire (BKHR)
    • booked hire rental (BKRN)
    • taxi (TAXI)
    • limousine (LIMO)
    • special purpose limousine (LSPL)
  • LV - Cars or light commercial vehicles (up to and including 4.5 tonne RGVM)
  • MC - Motorcycle
  • LT - Caravans and trailers (over 0.75 tonne ATM up to and including 3.5 tonne ATM)
  • HT - Caravans and trailers (over 3.5 tonne ATM up to and including 10.0 tonne ATM)
  • HV - Vehicles (over 4.5 tonne RGVM up to and including 16.0 tonne RGVM)


Refer customers to the TMR website for further information regarding the scheme:

Application for AIS Mobile Unit
Application for AIS Mobile Unit
    A mobile AIS is an extension of the fixed AIS premises. The nominated vehicle must be registered. No application fee is applicable.

    Applicants who wish to add/remove or replace a mobile AIS must complete the relevant sections of the Approved Inspection Station Change/Replacement Application (F4638).

Alteration to Vehicle Inspection Types

Renew AIS Approval
Renewal of AIS Approval
Renewal of AIS Approval

    The AIS Administrator will send an AIS renewal reminder notice to the customer 30 days prior to their AIS approval expiry date.

    Early renewal
    Early renewal
      An AIS approval may be renewed as soon as they have received their renewal notice, which is normally 30 days before their approval expires.
      However, where there are exceptional circumstances (i.e. the approval holder will not be able to action their renewal notice as they are travelling interstate or overseas), the AIS may request to renew their approval up to three months prior to their expiry date and without having received their renewal notice.
      The applicant must email their request for an early renewal to AIS Administrator and provide details of their exceptional circumstances for consideration.

    Note:The renewal application must be lodged under the organisation's CRN for the AIS and not an individual's CRN.

    Refer: Accreditation > Approved Inspection Station > The How To: Approved Inspection Station (AIS) > Approved Inspection Station (AIS) Procedures > Renew an Approved Inspection Station (AIS) Approval
Expired/Lapsed AIS Approval
Expired/Lapsed AIS Approval Policy
Expired/Lapsed AIS Approval Policy
    From 1 December 2020, if an AIS Approval has expired for a period of 90 days or more, the Approval will lapse.

    If an AIS Approval lapses, a new application must be made together with all supporting information and all relevant application fees must be paid.

Change of Address (Relocation) of an AIS
Relocation of AIS
Relocation of AIS
    An Approved Inspection Station relocation is where an existing station moves to a new address.

    The applicant is required to:
    • Complete the Approved Inspection Station Application (F2272)
    • No fee is required
Change of AIS business/company name:
Change of AIS business name
Change of AIS business name
    A change of business name is defined as an existing Approved Inspection Station (AIS) changing their business name only.

    An AIS approval holder must :
    • Meet AIS EOI requirements
    • Complete the relevant sections of the Approved Inspection Station Scheme Change/Replacement Application (F4638)
    • Supply supporting documents such as Business Names Extract (as outlined on the application)
Change of AIS Company name
Change of AIS Company name
    A change of company name is defined as an existing Approved Inspection Station (AIS) changing their company name AND the ACN remains the same.

    An AIS approval holder must :
    • Meet AIS EOI requirements.
    • Complete the relevant sections of the Approved Inspection Station Scheme Change Replacement Application (F4638)
    • Supply supporting documents such as ASIC Company Extract listing director/s (as outlined on the application)
Surrender of AIS Approval
Surrender of AIS Approval
Surrender of AIS Approval
    The AIS approval holder must within 14 days of ceasing trading:
    • Complete the 'Surrender of AIS Approval' sections of the Approved Inspection Station Scheme Change/Replacement Application (F4638)

    The CSC operator is required to:
    • date stamp and verify the unused books and/or certificates have been returned on the application form under 'Surrender of AIS Approval'
    • Scan all sides of the application form and email the scanned application form the CSC mailbox to the AIS Administrator

    If a CSC receives any unused inspection certificate books they should contact their local regional Compliance Officer who will advise where the documents are to be sent for filing/archiving.
