Removing a Registered Operator
Removing a Registered Operator
    This process applies when one of the joint registered operators becomes a sole registered operator of a vehicle.

    If vehicle/vessel is being transferred from joint to a single registered operator and only one of the registered operators has signed.

    The CSO is to obtain a written declaration from the acquirer stating that they are the registered operator and have the legal right to transfer the registration into their name.

    The declaration can be in any form, provided it is signed and dated by the acquirer and is legible. The acquirer should have their signature verified under EOI requirements.

    This is to prevent situations where, during a dispute, one registered operator:
    • Is trying to prevent the other operator from receiving registration notices
    • Has taken possession of the vehicle without the other's consent

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Rules > Jointly Registered Vehicles - Dispute of Ownership

Disposer signature missingIf the acquirer did not obtain either of the the disposer’s signatures, encourage them to obtain it by:
  • E-signatures on a transfer application form
  • Getting a scanned/emailed copy of disposers signature (no EOI is required for the disposer so a copy is acceptable)

If the above is unsuccessful, the acquirer must provide both:
Proof of Legal Ownership
Proof of Legal Ownership
    Proof of ownership can be:
    • Purchase receipt containing the disposers signature
    • Property Settlement (terms of Settlement)
    • Court issued must have official court seal
    • Out of court agreements must be in writing and signed by both parties
    • Any order of the court (must have official court seal).

Buyers Statement

  • The buyer has legal ownership
  • Details of the acquisition
  • The circumstances why the disposers signature cannot be obtained

Statement must be made in their own words and signed by the buyer.

If a NOD has not been processed online
If a NOD has not been processed online
    If a NOD has not been processed online or at a CSC (and is therefore not recorded on TICA), the acquirer cannot transfer the vehicle online. The transfer must be lodged at a CSC and must meet full transfer requirements, including the disposer’s and acquirer’s signature on the transfer form.


If a NOD has been processed online
If a NOD has been processed online
    If a NOD has been processed online and the customer has not received the acquirer email notification, the acquirer must complete the Registration Transfer Application (F3520 or F3521)and must meet full transfer requirements, including standard EOI and required fees.

    A disposer signature is not required provided that a NOD has been lodged.

    CSC Operators are required to:
    • write NOD in missing disposer signature field
    • when completing transfer from seller, select 'yes' and finalise transaction.

    This removes the need to print the NOD and keep with the filing.
Domestic and Family ViolenceVictims of Domestic and Family Violence (DFV) can remove their name from a jointly registered vehicle registration record, where they are no longer in possession of the vehicle and cannot obtain a signature due to a DFV situation.

The Vehicle Transfer Application (F3520) must be completed and lodged and The following supporting documentation is required:
Statutory Declaration from the victim declaring a DFV situation and that they are not in possession of the vehicle; OR:
  • A statement from the victim stating that they are not in possession of the vehicle and one of the following supporting documents:
  • Domestic Violence Order (DVO) or Apprehended Violence Order (AVO); or
  • Court Order; or
  • Letter from a Shelter

The customer should be advised that the removal of their name will result in a new registration certificate being generated and mailed to the remaining registered operator.
