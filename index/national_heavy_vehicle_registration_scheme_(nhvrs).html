National Heavy Vehicle Registration Scheme (NHVRS) (7318)

Project NameNational Heavy Vehicle Registration Scheme (NHVRS)
When1 July 2018
BackgroundFor many years the Australian Government and the state and territory governments, with input from industry and other stakeholders, have been working towards increased harmonisation of heavy vehicle regulation.

The National Heavy Vehicle Registration Scheme (NHVRS) aims to benefit industry by introducing common practices, processes and fees.

The development of the NHVRS has included 3 main initiatives:
  • Development of national data set
  • Industry benefit measures
  • Development of a National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate
Affected VehiclesAll heavy vehicles (over 4.5t GVM or ATM) will be included in the NHVRS except for vehicles registered under the:
  • Conditional Registration Scheme
  • Special Interest Vehicles
  • Qld Fire and Rescue Authority
  • Farm Plates
Who will be impacted and how?
Customer Service Staff
Customer Service Staff
    From 1 July 2018, CSC and Agency staff will issue a National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate when registering a heavy vehicle, or replacing a number plate for a heavy vehicle.
Call Centre staff
Call Centre staff
    Call centre staff may receive an increase in enquiries from heavy vehicle operators about the new scheme.
Compliance
Compliance
    Compliance staff may receive an increase in enquiries from heavy vehicle operators about the new scheme. The PIN codes will also be updated as heavy vehicles will no longer require labels.
Customers
Customers
    Changes that will affect customers include:
    • Customers who conduct a new registration, or replace a standard plate on a heavy vehicle will be issued the new number plates from 1 July 2018. Heavy vehicles that are already registered will not need to exchange their plates.
    • Registration labels will no longer be required for heavy vehicles.
    • Customers can apply for Common Due Date with 2 or more registered vehicles.
    • When customers transfer their heavy vehicle registration in or out of Queensland, they can retain the same number National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate.
Closure of FIRSThe Commonwealth Government has recently announced the closure of the Federal Interstate Registration Scheme (FIRS). Between 1 July 2018 and 30 June 2019, vehicles registered under FIRS are required to be transitioned to the NHVRS.

FIRS records will have a text restriction placed against them to prevent any action being taken against the vehicle registration. At the time of renewal (or prior) the registered operator will not receive a Vehicle Registration Notice (VRN). Instead a letter will be posted advising the customer to attend a CSC to exchange their number plates for NHV Number plates. This transaction is free of charge.

To complete the transition from FIRS to NHVRS, the Reg Category must be updated. A registered operator is not required to pay vehicle duty. Please read the text carefully and follow the instructions to have the restriction lifted and the vehicle transitioned correctly.
