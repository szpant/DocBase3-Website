Driver Licence Definitions
Driving in Qld by Licence TypeA person may drive or ride in Queensland on their valid non-Queensland licence providing that they only drive or ride the class of vehicle that the licence authorised by their licence. They must abide by any conditions on the non-Queensland licence.

However, an overseas visitor driving in Queensland on a foreign car licence may drive any Queensland class C vehicle regardless of the GVM or seating capacity limitations on their overseas licence.

Speed Limit Restrictions on certain Licence Holders
Speed Limit Restrictions on certain Licence Holders
    Speed limit restrictions are a condition of a NSW, SA, TAS and NT learner/provisional driver licence and a WA learner licence. As such, these speed limit restrictions will apply whenever the licence holder drives in Queensland.

    Before driving in Queensland, non-Queensland driver licence holders should contact their relevant jurisdiction to confirm any conditions of their driver licence, including the speed limit restrictions.
Learner Motorcycle Licence Holders
Learner Motorcycle Licence Holders
    A NSW learner rider licence holder is not required to ride a motorcycle under supervision. Therefore, the holder of a valid NSW motorcycle learner rider licence is not required to ride under supervision whilst visiting Queensland.

    This rule applies to the holder of any non-Queensland learner licence visiting Queensland, where the person is not required to ride or drive under supervision in the state or country where their licence was issued.

    Non-Queensland driver licence holders should contact their relevant jurisdiction to confirm any conditions on their driver licence before driving in Queensland.

    New Zealand paper licences
    New Zealand paper licences
      If a New Zealand paper licence holder returns to New Zealand (for any reason and any length of time) from 3 May 1999 onwards, then their licence is valid to drive on Queensland roads for only 90 days. The 90 days is calculated from the date they returned to New Zealand.

      The New Zealand licence holder can get this information from their passport.

      If the holder of this licence does not return to New Zealand from 3 May 1999 onwards, the paper licence remains valid indefinitely.
Foreign Licence (other than New Zealand)
Foreign Licence (other than New Zealand)

    Foreign Licence (other than New Zealand)
    Foreign driver licence holders may drive in Qld on their current Foreign driver licence. A foreign driver licence can only be updated in TICA if all of the licence details are in English or a recognised English translation is provided.

    English Translations for Foreign Licences
    The customer must provide a recognised English translation of all details on the licence. If any part of the licence is not translated, the licence details must not be recorded in TICA. This is because when the entire document is translated it enables TMR to assess what is relevant, and we can be confident we have all the information required to process the foreign licence. The only exception to this rule is when TMR is satisfied that the wording is not related to the licence (for example, classes, dates, conditions) AND we have a documented translation of this wording. Refer: Exceptions

    This allows for:
    • Recognition of their foreign driver licence status, class/es and conditions (if required)
    • Smoother business transactions (eg enable car hire)

Defence Force Licence
Defence Force Licence

    The holder of a current defence force driver licence may drive in Queensland on their defence force driver licence only if:
    • the holder drives a motor vehicle owned or used by the Australian Defence Force
    • the holder drives a class of vehicle authorised under that licence
    • the vehicle is being driven in the performance of the holder's defence force functions
    Note: For Defence Force Driver Licence Scheme refer to Interstate Licence

        When the Suspension Applies
          The authority to drive in Queensland on the non-Queensland licence is immediately suspended if the person is charged with one of the following :
          • Driving a motor vehicle when the person is over the middle or high alcohol limit (equal to or over 0.10%)

          • Operating a motor vehicle dangerously (under s328A Criminal Code) when the person was adversely affected by alcohol or over the prescribed alcohol limit
