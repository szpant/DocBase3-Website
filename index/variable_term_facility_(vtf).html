Variable Term Facility (VTF) (3066)


What is it?The Variable Term Facility (VTF):

Allows the future registration expiry date to move to reflect the amount paid at renewal. The registration period can be varied by either:
  • Shortening the registration period
  • Lengthening the registration period (up to 14mths)

To adjust an expiry date, the VTF calculates a daily registration cost based on a 12-month registration period.
When VTF can be applied
Correct operator error
Correct operator error
    If the daily filing review or Business Exception authorisation process identify an operator error for a renewal payment that has resulted in an under or over payment, VTF may be used to correct the error if the VTF rules are met.

    Refer: Admin > 5. Filing and Business Exceptions > The How To: Filing and Business Exceptions > Filing and Business Exceptions Procedures > Error Correction Procedures
Correct for RRRF
Correct for RRRF
    VTF can be used when a customer:
    • makes a renewal payment before the expiry date and has incorrectly included the RRRF amount in the renewal payment
    • makes a renewal payment after the expiry date and has not included the RRRF amount.

    VTF rules must be met.

    Note: One-month registration terms cannot be varied by the system. If a customer incorrectly includes/does not include the RRRF in the payment amount the registration term will not be varied. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 07. Renewal > Registration Renewal Reinstatement Fee (RRRF)
Financial Hardship
Financial Hardship
    If a customer is unable to pay a 6 month registration term due to financial hardship and their registration is:
    • current, they must be encouraged to enrol in/add the vehicle to one-month direct debit. This is to ensure that customers who are unable to afford their registration renewal are provided with a long term manageable renewal option.
    • expired or they are not eligible to enrol/add their vehicle to direct debit, the absolute floor can be applied providing the rules (below) are met.

    Absolute floor rules
    Absolute floor rules
      • Check if the customer is eligible for a 3-month non-direct debit pay term Registration Renewal. If eligible, change the pay term rather than applying the absolute floor.
      • No written application is required.
      • A reasonable explanation must be provided (e.g. loss of income/unexpected bill. Not having a bank account is not an acceptable reason).
      • A text restriction "Financial hardship approved <amount>" must be recorded - the text restriction expiry date should align with the registration auto-cancellation date.
      • The RRRF does not need to be manually added.
      • If there are <8 days until expiry/auto cancellation, it must be paid in a CSC to prevent BPAY rejection issues.
      If the registration is:
        • expired, all operators can approve the absolute floor. Advise the customer not to drive until the registration is paid.
        • current and the customer is not eligible to enrol/add vehicle to direct debit, approval can only be granted by an SCCC/SASM/Manager.
        • current and the customer is eligible to enrol/add vehicle to three-month direct debit, refer to the table below.
      • The customer must be advised that the approval is a one off and if eligible, they must enrol in/add the vehicle to Direct Debit for the next registration term.
      • For CSCs – the Enrol in Direct Debit email mail must be sent for all circumstances.
      • Where the customer is eligible to enrol/add their vehicle;
        or
      • When the CSC approves the absolute floor to be paid (to help customers enrol before their next renewal is due).
      • For CCCs – the Enrol in Direct Debit email must be sent unless the CCC approves for the Floor to be paid. If the floor is to be paid the Registration Renewal Template must be sent.

    Providing customers access to a one/3-month term outside of legislated eligibility requirements (DDRR/DDRR, Heavy Vehicles, Booked Hire, drought/disaster relief) is not permitted and reports will be run to identify when this has occurred.

    A daily report will be generated for any operator who selects the one-month term in TICA to check that the customer has met the eligibility requirements.

When VTF cannot be used
When VTF cannot be used
    VTF cannot be used for:
    • Common Due date vehicles
    • Vehicles with pre-purchased CTP Insurance Certificates
    • One-month registration renewals
    • Buses with CTP classes 10 and 11 – this is because there is complexity with the CPT insurance fee component. For these buses, the registration period is automatically adjusted to either a 6 or 3 month period based on the amount paid at renewal. Registered operators will need to contact TMR to obtain a refund or to discuss the registration period adjustment.
Rules
  • Must be a registration renewal payment
  • Renewal payment must be received before auto cancel action taken
  • Amount received cannot extend the registration period past 14 months
  • Underpayment amount is >$1
  • Payment meets Absolute Floor Requirements
Applying VTF		VTF is automatically applied if a customer makes a BPAY payment (excluding one-month) and the amount paid is more or less than is required for the standard registration term, and the VTF Rules are met.

For other payment methods VTF is applied using the VTF function in TICA. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 7. Renewal > The How To: Renewal > Registration Renewal Procedures > Variable Term Facility

Registered operator responsibility
Registered operator responsibility
    It is the responsibility of the registered operator or any person driving a vehicle to ensure that any vehicle they are driving is registered before using it on a road.

    To assist with this, TMR provides a number of mechanisms to reduce the risk of late payments occurring, which includes:
    • Vehicle Registration Renewal Notice (VRRN) - sent at a minimum of four weeks prior to the registration expiry date.
    • eNotices - enables the VRRN to be sent to a nominated email account instead of by standard mail giving customers instant access to these notices (up to six weeks before registration expiry date), reducing the risk of missing their payment expiry date as a result of mail being lost in the post.
    • eReminders - alerts customers to upcoming registration renewal date.
    • Queensland Rego Check mobile app or online tool - provides up-to-date information about a vehicle’s registration status and when it expires.
    • Direct debit payment option - provides enrolled customers with a convenient “set and forget” direct debit payment option.
Shorter period surcharge
Shorter period surcharge
    When VTF is applied and the term is less than 12 months a Shorter Period Surcharge is applies. This charge is an administrative charge to cover the cost of providing registration and CTP for a period less than 12 months.

    The amount payable depends on the vehicle type and concession (if applicable). TICA automatically applies the appropriate surcharge.

    The surcharge fee is triggered when the amount paid is $1.01 below the standard 12 month registration fee (using the lowest insurance premium for that class).

Absolute floor
Absolute floor
    The absolute floor is the minimum amount TMR will accept for a registration renewal (excluding a one-month term) and must be approved by a Manager/SASM/SCCC if the registration is current. The absolute floor amount will vary depending on the particulars of the registration.

    The absolute floor generally equates to between 2 - 3 months of registration. If customers are experiencing financial hardship they should be advised to enrol in direct debit so that they can pay a one-month term. A one-month term can't be applied to vehicles that aren't enrolled in direct debit.

    Use TICA to find the absolute floor. On the TICA page "Pay Vary Registration Period" enter $1 as the cash amount and select "Pay". The absolute floor amount will be displayed in red at the top of the page.
