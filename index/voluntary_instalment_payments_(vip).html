Voluntary Instalment Plan (VIP) (2757)

What it is
What is a VIP
What is a VIP
    A Voluntary Instalment Plan (VIP) is a payment instalment option available when an alleged offender (individual or organisation) receives an Infringement Notice fining them for $200 or more. An initial payment of at least $60 is required. The initial instalment is paid to TMR. The infringement is then referred to State Penalties Enforcement Registry (SPER) who will contact the debtor to arrange monthly payments to finalise the debt.

    Additional court costs normally associated with registration of an infringement notice with SPER do not apply in this instance.

    VIP applications can only be made before the IN has been registered with SPER.
s30 State Penalties Enforcement Regulation 2000
    Note:S23of the State Penalties Enforcement Act 1999 states that an application to pay a fine by instalments is to be made within 28 days after the date of issue of the infringement notice. TMR has decided that an application to pay by instalment will be accepted by TMR at any time prior to the infringement being registered with SPER.

    Please note:
    The State Penalties Enforcement Regulation 2000 was amended to provide that from 1 January 2009 the minimum amount for a fine to be eligible for a VIP is $200.

    Infringement notices and forms have been updated with the amended VIP information.

    Fines less than $200 are therefore not eligible for participation in VIP unless the customer has an (older style) infringement notice which still states the lesser amount of $150 to be eligible for a VIP.

    More information about VIPs is available in the attached FAQ document that may be printed and given to customers if required:
    Voluntary Instalment Plan - FAQ v3
What Happens
What Happens to the VIP Application
What Happens to the VIP Application
    The application for a VIP and the initial payment of the infringement notice is:
    • Made to TMR
    • TICA sends application details electronically to SPER (automatically in overnight update)
    • SPER evaluates the application and will either:
      ActionResult
      Accept SPER sends an Instalment Payment Notice (IPN) to the customer within 10 days outlining a payment plan and a list of collection agencies.
      Reject SPER notifies Processing Operations via the Exception Report For Rejections.

      Processing Operations then follows up the rejected applications.
    • Remaining payments are then made directly to SPER

    For accepted VIPs, failure to pay remaining payments can result in an Enforcement Order being issued (An additional cost may be incurred)

    Withdrawn and recalled infringements
    Withdrawn and recalled infringements
      If an infringement is withdrawn from SPER, the same Infringement notice number cannot be re-lodged with SPER. This means once withdrawn, it will be unable to be paid via VIP.

      If an infringement is recalled from SPER, the same Infringement notice number can be re-lodged with SPER including as a VIP providing it meets the above requirements.

    Note:Fines less than $200 are not eligible for a VIP, except if the customer elects a VIP on an infringement notice which still states the lesser amount of $150.

How the Customer Applies
How the Customer Applies

    Online or BPay

      Electronic Notices
        • Electronic notices (camera detected/QNotices) can be paid by VIP online or by BPAY
        • If the infringement is at least $200, two payment reference numbers will be provided at the top of the IN; one to pay in full and one to enter into a VIP by paying the first instalment.
        • Customer must pay the first instalment using the first instalment payment reference number.
      Handwritten Notices
      Handwritten Notices
        Once a handwritten notice has been processed, it will be available to be paid via VIP online using the 'My Account' portal > Infringements. The option for a VIP will only display if available for the infringement.

        Note:The system still generates a Payment Reference Number (PRN) for on-the-spot hand written traffic infringement notices.
        At this time staff are not to release a PRN to customers wishing to pay hand written traffic infringements by BPAY.

    Cancelling a VIPTMR can cancel a VIP before or after payment of first instalment providing cancellation is on the same day.

    Reasons an operator would cancel an application would include:
    • Customer changes their mind and decides to pay in full
    • Customer did not have enough money for the first instalment
    • Operator made an error in processing the application
