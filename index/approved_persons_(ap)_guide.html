Approved Persons (AP) Guide (4664)


What is an Approved Person (AP)? The Approved Person (AP) Scheme accredits individuals to inspect and certify vehicle modifications.

To become an AP, applicants must have the appropriate formal qualifications, experience and equipment as required by the Department of Transport and Main Roads.

An AP may be accredited to inspect and certify vehicle modifications on the following types of vehicles:
  • Light Vehicles up to 4.5 tonne GVM/ATM
  • Heavy Vehicles over 4.5 tonne GVM/ATM
  • Or a combination of both

Refer customers to the TMR website for further information regarding the scheme:
http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/business-industry/Accreditations/Approved-Person-Scheme.aspx

Renewal
Renewing AP Accreditation
Renewing AP Accreditation
    • The AP accreditation is issued for up to 3 years
    • AP's are responsible for ensuring their accreditation is current at all times while undertaking the role of an AP
    • The AP must complete the relevant sections of the Approved Person Application (F1855)

    The AP Administrator will send an AP renewal reminder notice to the customer 30 days prior to their accreditation expiry date.
    Early renewal
    Early renewal
      An approved person accreditation may be renewed as soon as they have received their renewal notice, which is normally sent 30 days before their accreditation expires.

      However, where there are exceptional circumstances (i.e. the person will not be able to action their renewal notice as they are travelling interstate or overseas) the approved person may request to renew their accreditation up to three months prior to their expiry date and without having received their renewal notice.

      The applicant must email their request for an early renewal to the AP Administrator and provide details of their exceptional circumstances for consideration.

    Note:Application and CHC fees are not applicable
Expired/Lapsed Approved Person Accreditation
Expired/Lapsed AP Accreditation Policy
Expired/Lapsed AP Accreditation Policy
    From 1 December 2020, if an Approved Person Accreditation has expired for a period of 90 days or more, the Accreditation will lapse.

    If an Approved Person Accreditation lapses, a new application must be made together with all supporting information and all relevant application fees must be paid.

Voluntary Surrender
Voluntary surrender of an AP Accreditation
Voluntary surrender of an AP Accreditation
    The AP must:
    • notify the AP Administrator in writing
    • return any unused Certificates of Modification and unused Modification Plates
    • return all used certificates of modification up to seven years old
    • Post the above items to the AP Administrator

    If a CSC receives:
    • used certificates of modification (up to 7 years old) and
    • unused modification plates;
    • Post the above items to the AP Administrator

Public List of Current Approved Persons
List of Current Approved Persons
List of Current Approved Persons
    APs can consent to TMR releasing their contact information and for inclusion on the department’s website as part of their initial application or at time of renewal.

    The Find an Approved Person online service provides the contact information for current APs who have consented for TMR to release their information to the public for the purpose of vehicle modification certifications. This list will be updated on a regular basis.

    You can search the online service by entering the modification code and/or suburb/town/postcode into the searchbar on the webpage.

    Note:TMR staff are not to access the RUMIIS database as a means of providing an Approved Persons information to the public. TMR staff are only permitted to provide an Approved Persons contact name and contact phone number from the Approval of vehicle modifications.
