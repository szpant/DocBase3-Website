Pilot / Escort Licence Guide (1884)


About the Pilot Driver/ Escort Driver Accreditation
Definitions
Definitions
Definitions
    Escort vehicle driver
    Means a person who holds an appointment as an accredited person with the functions of an escort vehicle driver.
    Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Accreditations and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015

    Pilot vehicle driver
    Means a person who holds an appointment as an accredited person with the functions of a pilot vehicle driver.
    Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Accreditations and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015

    Further Definitions
What it is
Pilot vehicle driver accreditation
Pilot vehicle driver accreditation
    The function of an accredited person who is a pilot vehicle driver is to drive a pilot vehicle to warn other road users of the presence of an oversize vehicle.
Escort vehicle driver accreditation
Escort vehicle driver accreditation
    The function of an accredited person who is an escort vehicle driver is to drive an escort vehicle to warn other road users of the presence of an oversize vehicle and perform traffic control duties.

    Note:An Escort vehicle driver can perform the duties of a Pilot vehicle driver as they will also hold a Pilot Vehicle driver accreditation.
When required
Pilot vehicle driver accreditation
Pilot vehicle driver accreditation
    Any person who wants to operate a Pilot Vehicle in Queensland must hold a Queensland issued Pilot Vehicle Driver Accreditation unless they are a recognised interstate operator

    A person must drive a pilot vehicle as required:
    Light Oversize Vehicle
    Light Oversize Vehicle
      • Under the Guideline for Pilot and Escort Operations in Queensland issued under Part 3, Section 14 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010 in accordance with Part 4, Section 22 of the Statutory Instruments Act 1992.
    Heavy Oversize Vehicle
    Heavy Oversize Vehicle
      • A condition of a mass or dimension exemption is issued under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland)
      • In accordance with the Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Road Rules) Regulation 2009
      Note:The offence for driving an escort vehicle for an oversize heavy vehicle is under the Heavy vehicle National Law (Queensland).

Escort Driver Accreditation
Escort Driver Accreditation
    Any person who wants to operate an Escort Vehicle in Queensland must hold a Queensland issued Escort Vehicle Driver Accreditation unless they are a recognised interstate operator.

    A person must drive an escort vehicle as required:
    Light Oversize Vehicle
    Light Oversize Vehicle
      • Under the Guideline for Pilot and Escort Operations in Queensland issued under Part 3, Section 14 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010in accordance with Part 4, Section 22 of the Statutory Instruments Act 1992.
    Heavy Oversize Vehicle
    Heavy Oversize Vehicle
      • A condition of a mass dimension exemption issued under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland)
      • In accordance with the Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Road Rules) Regulation 2009
      Note: The offence for driving an escort vehicle for an oversize heavy vehicle is under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland).

When not required
Pilot vehicle driver accreditation
Pilot vehicle driver accreditation
    A Pilot vehicle driver accreditation is not required to pilot a vehicle where the:
    • WIDTH is 3.5 meters or less and
    • LENGTH is 25 meters or less
Escort vehicle driver accreditation
Escort vehicle driver accreditation
    An Escort vehicle driver accreditation is not required to escort a vehicle where the:
    • WIDTH is 3.5 meters or less and
    • LENGTH is 25 meters or less

Issue period
Issue Period
Issue Period
    A Pilot Vehicle driver and an Escort Vehicle driver accreditation are issued for a three year period.

    Note:Transport Operations (Road Use Management- Accreditations and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015 allows for the issue of a pilot/escort vehicle driver accreditation for a period not longer than 5 years. It is a policy decision that a pilot/escort vehicle driver accreditation is issued for only 3 years at this time.

        • Not to be granted or renewed on application during the cancellation period;

        Note:Applicants with a Multiple Industry Authority smartcard which has another authority that is not affected by the cancellation will require a replacement Industry Authority smartcard which is to be issued for the non affected industry authority/s.       Suspended
      Suspended
        If a driver licence is suspended the Industry Authority is:
        • Suspended (applicant is unable to perform the duties of a Pilot or Escort Vehicle driver during suspension period);
        • Automatically reinstated on the completion of the driver licence's suspension period;
        • Not surrendered and no action is required on TICA.

      Mutual Recognition Interstate Applicants
      Mutual Recognition
      Mutual Recognition
        Under the Mutual Recognition Act 1992, a current Victorian or Northern Territory pilot/escort vehicle driver accreditation can be recognised and transferred to the equivalent Queensland accreditation. However applicants must make application for recognition of their existing interstate accreditation and pay the applicable fee.

        Applicants eligible for mutual recognition are therefore exempt from the qualification requirement (e.g. course certificate/statement of attainment) for the same class/s but must meet all other application requirements (such as driver licence tenure, medical certificate, criminal and traffic history checks).

        Applicants applying under mutual recognition must complete:
        • Pilot/Escort Vehicle Driver Accreditation Application (F4483) and
        • Equivalent Occupation Registration Notice (F2762)

        The IL Hub will email the relevant interstate jurisdiction to confirm that the Pilot/Escort Vehicle Driver accreditation is valid.

        Recognised Jurisdictions with equivalent qualification for mutual recognition:

        QLD
        Pilot Vehicle Driver Licence
        Escort Vehicle Driver Licence
        VIC
        Pilot Level 1
        Pilot Level 2: (Certified Vehicle Pilot Driver)
        NT
        No equivalent
        Accredited Oversize Load Pilot

        Note: Western Australia is not recognised as part of mutual recognition. Queensland does however recognise WA’s formal accreditation and allows them to operate temporarily in Qld. Refer below Interstate Operators temporarily operating in Qld.

        Non Recognised Jurisdictions:
        Applicants from jurisdictions not recognised under mutual recognition must apply for accreditation in Queensland and meet full requirements.

        Pilot vehicle driver/Escort vehicle driver interstate applicants
        Pilot vehicle driver/Escort vehicle driver interstate applicants
          With Formal Accreditation
          If an applicant is from an interstate jurisdiction that has formal accreditation, they must provide their current interstate accreditation, or evidence of achieved competency.

          With No Formal Accreditation
          If an applicant is from an interstate jurisdiction that does not have formal accreditation, they must provide evidence that they carry out their pilot or escort operations as a means of earning a livelihood in their state of origin (ie. a letter from their employer on business letterhead) and a Statement of Attainment from a Registered Training Provider.
