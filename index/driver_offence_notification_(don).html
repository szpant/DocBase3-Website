Driver Offence Notification (DON) (7475)

Project NameDriver Offence Notification (DON)
When1 August 2020 (date not to be provided to customers)
BackgroundThe following information is for internal use only and is not to be shared with customers. Separate public communications, including updated FAQ's and screen shots will be provided in time for training.

On 19 June 2019, legislation commenced that allows TMR to electronically notify a registered operator about a traffic offence committed by someone else using their vehicle. To support this legislation, changes have been made to TMR systems to enable the issue of the courtesy notice, which will be called a Driver Offence Notification (DON).

Registered operators currently receive notifications of camera-detected offences committed in their vehicle. The DON service, in the first instance will expand these notifications to include infringement notices (fines) such as speeding, illegal mobile phone use, and heavy vehicle related fatigue, excess mass, dimension and loading offences, that are intercepted roadside by a police officer or TMR transport inspector. These courtesy notifications will be sent by email, when the offence is finalised. Some offences which are dealt with through the courts (for example, drink or drug driving, unlicensed driving) will not be included in the DON service.

The purpose of the DON is to provide registered operators with greater visibility of the driving behaviour of people using their vehicle/s and to encourage road safety conversations.

Organisations, such as fleet and heavy vehicle operators will be able to better monitor and control the operation of their vehicles. Parents or carers will be able to more effectively monitor the driving behaviour of newly licensed young drivers using the family car.

These conversations and the ability to make more informed decisions about who and how their vehicles are used, will lead to better road safety outcomes.

Traffic offences which are finalised on or after 1 August 2020 will result in a DON being emailed to the registered operator if certain rules are met. The DON will contain the offender's full name and details of the offence including the date/time and the suburb where the offence occurred. This information is based on the details recorded on the Infringement/breach. No other details about the offender will be on the DON or can be provided to the registered operator.
More details
DON generation rules
DON generation rules
    • Offence must be intercepted roadside in Queensland and appear on a traffic history
    • Offender must be someone other than a registered operator
    • Registration number must have been included on infringement/breach
    • Must be a Queensland registered vehicle
    • DON is emailed to primary registered operator (regardless of whether they are a legal entity or not)
    • Registered Operator must have an email address recorded in eContacts
    • Registered operator must not have ‘requested to Opt out’ of receiving DONs (refer below DON preferences)
    • Offender must not be suppressed or deceased
    • Registered operator must not be deceased or a closed organisation
    • Offence must be finalised (paid in full/VIP, sent to SPER, Court result)
    • Any subsequent changes to the infringement/breach after it is finalised do not impact the DON (for example, we don’t send another notice if the Infringement is withdrawn after it has been sent to SPER).
    • The offence information on the DON is derived from the Infringement/breach screen.
EOI rules
EOI rules
    To add/remove/update email addresses or request to opt/in out of the DON service, the following applies:

    Individuals:
      · Standard EOI

    Organisations:
      · Standard EOI for the organisation; and
      · If the request is being completed over the phone, in person or in writing, the person must provide their name and confirm if they are an authorised representative. These details do not need to be recorded in TICA.

    Note: Specific EOI requirements apply for organisations who are adding/updating their email address online. Refer: TICA and Online enhancements > Online Services > Organisations below.
Organisation email addresses
Organisation email addresses
    To ensure all registered operators can receive DONs, the eContact screen has been enabled within COAM for organisations.

    The eContact email will only be used for DON and Booked Hire Service Licence/Booking Entity Authorisation communications.
DON preferences
DON preferences
    DON preferences are managed separately to eNotices and eReminders. Preferences can be updated either upon request by the customer or automatically.
    Automatic opt in/out
    Automatic opt in/out
      Registered operators who have provided TMR with an email address will automatically be opted into the DON service. The “Registered for driver offence notifications” checkbox will be ticked in the DON preference screen in COAM.
      If the same customer removes their email address, they will automatically be opted out of the DON service. The DON preference screen in COAM will display a message that the customer has no email address recorded and the “Registered for driver offence notifications” box will not appear.
    Requested opt in/out
    Requested opt in/out
      A registered operator’s request to either opt in or out of the service will be maintained despite any change to their email address.

      When a customer preference is requested, an email notification is sent.

      Registered operators can request to opt in or out by the following methods:
      • Online service*
      • Over the phone
      • In a CSC
      • In writing (email/post)

      *Note:Individuals can view/manage their DON preferences online. Organisations can add their email address using a new online service which will automatically opt them in. Refer Online Services below for more information.

      DON preference history will be recorded as follows:
      • COAM (View Preference history) will display all requested DON preference changes made online and via COAM
      • Online services (View My History) will display all requested DON preferences changes made online.
TICA and Online enhancements
TICA and Online enhancements
    A number of TICA and Online enhancements will be released to reflect these changes. Below are descriptions of the changes and the relevant screen shots are located in the ‘screen shots’ section:
    TICA and COAM
    TICA and COAM
      New/renamed menu items in COAM
      New/renamed menu items in COAM
        To simplify managing electronic preferences in COAM and the display of eNotices/eReminders and DONs:
        • The menu items 'eNotices and eReminders' and 'View eNotice and eReminder history' will be renamed to 'eCorrespondence' and 'View eCorrespondence History'.
        • A new menu item ‘eCorrespondence Preferences’ has been added which houses eNotice and eReminder preferences and DON preferences.
      Managing preference via COAM
      Managing preference via COAM
        Operators can manage an Individual's or Organisation's DON preference via COAM by selecting eCorrespondence Preferences > Manage DON Preference.

        When an Operator selects the current eNotices/eReminders menu item in TICA, they will be directed to eCorrespondence Preferences in COAM with eNotice/eReminder screen populated by default.
      Adding/updating an Organisation email address
      Adding/updating an Organisation email address
        Operators will be able to access COAM via the Online Account Management top menu bar and enter an organisation’s eContact email address. A mobile number will not be able to be recorded.

        If an organisation had provided their email address for a BHSL or BEA, their email address recorded in eContact will override their previous email recorded in the Manage Booked Hire UI. This is because only one email may be recorded within TMR systems at a time.
      Generated DONs
      Generated DONs
        When a DON is automatically generated a record will be created in the:
        • TICA correspondence hub
        • COAM (which can be viewed/printed/resent)
      Recording and correcting Skeleton infringements
      Recording and correcting Skeleton infringements
        New fields have been added in TICA when recording or correcting skeleton and VIP skeleton infringements. These fields include the registration state, registration number, vehicle make, vehicle body type and vehicle colour. It is imperative that operators capture these details to ensure that DONs can be issued. If unsure of a handwritten registration number, operators should check the registration number against the make and model written on the Infringement. If in doubt, do not record it.

    Online services
    Online services
      Individuals
      Individuals
        Individuals can view and update their DON preference via My Account and
        Online services. The individual will receive an 'opt in' or 'opt out' email from
        TMR confirming their preference when they update it via the service.
          An email is not sent when the DON preference changes automatically.
        Organisations
        Organisations
          An authorised representative (i.e. individual) must authenticate to first
          level online prior to adding or updating an email address on behalf of an
          organisation via Online services. The organisation must have a current ACN
          to be eligible for the online service.

          The individual will:
            1. Login with level 1 security (Full name, DOB, CRN)
            2. Login to level 1 security for the organisation (name, ACN, CRN)
            3. Respond to organisation questions
            4. Add/Update organisation email

          When the individual adds or updates an organisation email address, a confirmation email is automatically sent to the new email address. Additionally, when the email address is updated, a confirmation email is also sent to the old email address. The CRN of the individual who updated the email using the online service will be recorded in COAM.

          Note:This will not occur for emails captured manually.

          Note:An authorised representative is a Manager, Director, Secretary or another type of officer authorised to by the organisation to act on its behalf.


    Who will be impacted and how?
    Offenders
    Offenders
      Offenders:
      • Will now have their name and offence details provided to the primary Registered Operator when an “in person” traffic offence is finalised.
      • May question TMR’s authority to issue a DON and raise privacy concerns which are addressed in the below FAQ’s.
    Operational staff
    Operational staff
      • May receive enquiries about the service and how individuals and organisations can receive notifications.
      • May receive complaints/enquiries from offenders about the issue of a DON.
      • Will now be able to enter email addresses for organisations.
      • Customer Service Centres/Customer Service Operations:
      • Will now have to complete additional fields when entering offence information into SKEL/VIP screens
      • Will need to take care when entering offence details as these will display on the DON and need to be accurate
      • Will need to enter the time 00:01 if the offence time is not provided as a system rule has been created so that the DON will display “not provided” when this time is entered.
      • Will be able to resend a DON if requested (in the same way that an eNotice is resent)
    Registered Operators
    Registered Operators
      Individual registered operators will be automatically opted in to receive DONs where an email address is already recorded. They can update their preference any time using Online services or via phone/in person/in writing, however using the online service will be promoted in communications.

      Organisation registered operators with a current ACN will be able to use a new online service to provide an email address to TMR for the purpose of receiving DONs. Organisations without an ACN or located interstate will be unable to use the online service. Those unable to use the online service can register by email, phone or in person. Using the online service will be promoted in communications to organisations with ACNs.

      To maximise the number of organisations able to receive DONs, TMR will be sending out invitations to register for DON to 22,000 organisations once the service goes live. All organisations with an ACN, business address in Queensland and an authorised officer with a CRN will be able to use the online service to register. The remaining organisations will be able to return their invite by email to a dedicated mail box (don@tmr.qld.gov.au) to register. Two different invites have been created for ACN and non-ACN organistaions to provide tailored instructions for registering.

      When any registered operator requests to change their opt in or opt out preference, they will receive an email notification from TMR confirming their preference.
