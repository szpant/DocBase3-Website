Access to Customer Records
Access to Customer Records
    Personal information stored by TMR must only be used for the purpose for which it was provided or that TMR is lawfully able to use it.

    The confidentiality of customer records is paramount at all times.

    The TMR policy Access to Customer Records provides clear direction to all employees not to take action to access a customer record unless the access is authorised.

    Access to a customer record is only authorised when:
    • the access is necessary to enable you to undertake the specific duties assigned to you, and
    • you are using a profile granted for the position you currently hold, and
    • the access is not prohibited by legislation or policy.

    Every time a record is accessed and/or searched a transaction log is kept of:
    • The date and time the access/search was made,
    • The transactions and procedures that were performed against a customer record, and the Involved Party and their location (ie. UserId)

    This log may be searched at any time to determine the id of the user who accessed a customer record.

    A breach of the Access to Customer Records policy may result in criminal prosecution and/or sanctions, including termination of employment.

    All staff are required to be familiar with the Access to Customer Records policy.

Remote TICA Access
Remote TICA Access
    • Accessing sensitive information, from a non-TMR environment heightens the departments information security risk. As such, all TMR staff accessing TICA remotely (for example personal residences) must comply with the Access Control Policy - Remote Access to TICA. For CSB, this includes:
      • Vulnerable CSC operators
      • All other CSB employees working remotely, who have previously been approved to use TICA from a TMR location.

    Note:At this time Transport Inspectors (TIs) are exempt from the Remote Access to TICA policy and work is in progress to provide provisions relevant to their roles. However, TIs must ensure that all equipment is secure, locked when not in use and customer records are not visible to non-authorised individuals.

    • Staff are only allowed to access TICA remotely if all of the requirements detailed in the policy have been complied with.

    • Before accessing TICA remotely, staff must:
      • read the Access Control Policy - Remote Access to TICA
      • login to their laptop at their usual TMR location and ensure the TICA application is downloaded

    • Filing and Business Exceptions will be sent to the user's usual location and will need to be actioned by the supervisor in that location.

    • All staff working remotely are reminded of their obligations to comply with the Access Control Policy - Remote Access to TICA.

    If an exemption is required for any reasons outlined in section 6 of the policy, staff must speak with their Manager before contacting Manager (Systems Security) Land Transport Safety and Regulation.

CCTV Download Requests
    The Information Privacy Act 2009 contains 11 Information Privacy Principles (IPPs) and is based on the objective that personal information held must be responsibly and transparently collected, stored, used and disclosed in accordance with the requirements of the IPPs.

    IPP10 and IPP11 relate to the use and disclosure of personal information. In general, TMR must only use personal information for the purpose for which it was collected and disclose personal information only if the individual concerned is aware of, or has consented to that disclosure.

    However, in certain circumstances, TMR may use or disclose personal information without consent if the use or disclosure is authorised under legislation, to assist in certain types of law enforcement, or to lessen a serious threat.

    Types of CCTV download requests can be:
    • Security-related CCTV footage
    • Law enforcement/third party requests
    • Public requests
    • TMR internal requests

    Finance and Procurement Branch (F&P) must be advised of all requests for CCTV downloads. All documents are to be forwarded to F&P by emailing CCTVdownloadrequest@tmr.qld.gov.au

    EFTPOS Terminal Checks
    EFTPOS Terminal Checks
      The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) is a requirement intended to optimise the security of credit, debit and cash card transactions and protect cardholders against misuse of their personal information. As a business that processes, stores or transmits cardholder data, TMR has implemented specific security controls in order to protect cardholder data against unauthorised disclosure and consequently fraud.

      Reduce Risk
      To reduce the risk of tampering (skimming) at TMR an operator should be constantly aware of the terminal and how it is being handled. Do not leave the terminal unattended, protect it like cash and if an operator must leave the immediate area make sure the terminal is put out of easy reach of customers.

      Operators must advise their SA(SM) or Manager immediately if:
      • They notice anything different or suspicious during daily checks
      • A visitor arrives to service or replace a terminal or security camera
      • Their terminal is missing
      • They see anyone acting strangely near the terminal or security camera

      Operator Daily Check - EFTPOS Terminal
      Operator Daily Check - EFTPOS Terminal
        Every terminal in each CSC/Agency must be kept out of reach of customers when it's unattended and checked every working day.

        It is the operators responsibility to:

        • Update the EFTPOS Terminal Check SharePoint site, once the check has been completed (User Manual on SharePoint site)
        • Report anything different or suspicious to your SA(SM)/Manager immediately
        • One person agencies will need to contact their supporting CSC's SA(SM)/Manager


        EFTPOS Terminal Daily Check July 2020

        Note:One person agencies will need to contact their supporting CSC for any issues with the 'EFTPOS Terminal Check' SharePoint site data.

        Note:When screws are missing from the back of the EFTPOS Terminal the SA(SM) must be advised.

      SA(SM)/Manager Responsibilities
      SA(SM)/Manager Responsibilities
        It is the SA(SM)/Manager responsibility to update the EFTPOS Terminal Check SharePoint site:
        • Daily by verifying that staff have completed their daily checks (if not all checked, notify staff to updated and complete the missing dates)
        • Monthly to confirm you have personally checked every terminal against the master list for your centre (this can be done when convenient, for example, in one day or over several days). It is imperative that the master list (not Maintain CSC/Agency list) is used for the monthly check to ensure all devices (including those not on a counter) are physically checked.
        • With the maintenance of the terminal full serial numbers to their respective counters in their centres

        • When existing terminals are moved to another counter
        • With details of any service person for the terminal (ID of service personnel, etc.)
        • With reports on any breaches

    Information from Interstate Authorities
      Information obtained from any equivalent interstate authority, requested and used in the execution of your official duty, is subject to the same standards of confidentiality and privacy that apply to information obtained from TMR's computer database systems.

      Robbery/Duress Alarms
      Robbery/Duress Alarms
        Duress alarms are to be used in addition to, and not as a substitute for, general safety principles applicable to workplaces. There are two types of duress alarms in use across CSB:
        Fixed
        Fixed
          A fixed duress alarm is a device that can be activated by someone who requires urgent assistance in an emergency or duress situation.

          Each Customer Service Centre and QGAP office has fixed duress alarms in place. They are usually located at customer service counters. Fixed duress alarms can be audible when pressed, ensure that they are on silent.

          Some Customer Service Centres and QGAP office may have additional duress alarms situation around the office. Please familiarise yourself with these additional duress alarms in these Customer Service Centres.

          A fixed duress alarm is monitored off-site by an alarm monitoring service.


          Note:Some sites only have "local" duress alarms that alert the office staff of an issue. These sites must provide response procedures specific to their site which are documented and practiced by staff.

        Personal
        Personal
          A personal duress alarm is a portable device that consists of a transponder unit that is held with your person and communicates wirelessly to a base monitor. Like the fixed duress alarms, this can be activated by that person who requires urgent assistance in an emergency.

          Personal duress alarms have been provided to officers at some remote QGAPs who are often working alone, to be held on their person when they are away from the counter, when arriving or leaving the office, when outside performing vehicle inspection or when travelling to the bank or the Post Office and so on.
          A personal duress alarm is monitored off-site by an alarm monitoring service.


          Note:Some sites only have "local" duress alarms that alert the office staff of an issue. These sites must provide response procedures specific to their site which are documented and practiced by staff.

        Testing Duress Alarms
        Testing Duress Alarms
          Duress alarms and CCTV systems should be tested on a monthly basis to ensure that they are operating as per the manufacturers specifications and as they are expected and intended.

          The results of the duress alarms and CCTV test is also to be approved by the Regional Director and General Manager each quarter.
        Use of Duress Alarms
        Use of Duress Alarms
          Duress alarms may be used in a wide variety of circumstances such as work-related violence (customer aggression), medical emergency, assault or robbery.
          If it is safe to do so and as soon as it is reasonably practicable, the duress alarm should be activated in all instances of work-related violence, including robbery or if weapon of some sort is involved. Discussion on different scenarios should be held as part of regular staff training. Multiple duress alarms being activated is an indication that the duress alarms have not been activated inadvertently.
          Depending on the circumstances, calling Qld Police on 000 is also an option which may be used on its own or in conjunction with activating the duress alarm and this should be discussed with teams as part of their regular training.
          All staff should be trained (including approximate annual refresher training, exercises/drills) in the use of duress alarms and ensuring all staff understand the procedures and what is required of them, including:
          • Bomb Threats
          • Armed Robbery
          • Client Aggression
          • Forced Entry and Theft
          • Incident Scene Preservation
          • Public Photography and Videography of TMR Facilities
          • Public Threats of Self Harm
          • Shelter-in-Place
          • Suspect Package Management/Response

          Refer to the Internal Security sharepoint page for information about the above.

          Staff should try to maintain awareness of their colleagues' transactions and their mannerisms (tone of voice, facial expressions, hand gestures and so on) to determine signs of distress in order to seek assistance. This may assist in managing or even pre-empting customer aggression or duress situations.

          As a general guiding principle, when the duress alarm is activated, if it is safe to do so, all staff should continue operating as normal and wait for emergency services to arrive. However, if the situation is not safe, staff should implement their shelter-in-place protocol if it is possible to do so. Each site should consider developing their own local duress alarm protocol, taking into account their local circumstances.
