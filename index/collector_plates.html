Collector Plates (3084)

What is it?Collectable plates are standard plates that can be purchased by a member of a plate collecting club.
Requirements
TMR Staff
TMR Staff
    Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR) Customer Services Branch (CSB) staff are not permitted to collect plates from Customer Service Centres.

    It has been determined that access to plates in CSCs by CSB staff could be perceived as a "conflict of interest" - refer to the Code of Conduct.
Australian Collectors
Australian Collectors
    Statement on letterhead from the Collector Club showing:
    • Applicant is a financial member of the club
    • Date of membership
Overseas Collectors (Visitors)
Overseas Collectors (Visitors)
    Evidence of overseas visitor status, either:
    • Passport, or
    • Airline ticket, or
    • Shipping ticket
    Note: Plates must be posted to an overseas address, unless proof of
    departure is established (sight an airline/shipping ticket)
Rules
  • Plates can only be issued from a CSC (not Magistrates Court or Police Stations).
  • Applications can be made in person or in writing
  • Collectors are limited to one visit per month at a given CSC
  • Plates cannot be reserved for a collector
Eligible Plates
Eligible Plates
    Collector plates can be:
    • One plate of a set (remaining plate must be destroyed)
    • Used Qld plates returned for cancellation
    • Dealer/trailer trade plate displaying the year of currency (eg D95)
    • Limited Use plates
    • Cancelled rare number plates
Ineligible Plates
Ineligible Plates
    Collector plates cannot be:
    • Taxi\Private Vehicle Hire Plates
    • New style consular or limousine plates
    • Interstate plates
    • Renewable dealer/trailer trade plates
    • Government plates
    • Limousine or Consular (new style)
    • Any Personalised/Customised plate (cancelled or not)
Stock Register
Stock Register
    Offices issuing plates to registered collectors must maintain a Collectors Plate Stock Register that is:
    • Kept up to date
    • Reconciled with plates in stock
    • Signed, dated and reconciled at least once a month
Application by Mail - Plate not in Stock
Application by Mail - Plate not in Stock
    For applications received by mail and requested plate is not in stock:
    • Money must be returned to the applicant
    • Advise customer to enquire at a later date

    If part of an order can be filled the overpayment must be refunded.
Refusing Access to Collectors
Refusing Access to Collectors
    If a collector causes disruptions to service the Department has the right to refuse access to a collector. The decision to refuse is at the CSC Managers discretion.
