Subject to 5 year NSW Mandatory Alcohol Interlock Program (MAIP)
Subject to 5 year NSW Mandatory Alcohol Interlock Program (MAIP)
    Queensland (QLD) licence holders who have been disqualified for a DUI offence in NSW and have a 5 year NSW MAIP disqualification period imposed may participate in the AIIP after completing their mandatory court imposed disqualification period. In lieu of complying with the NSW MAIP, the customer can obtain a QLD probationary 'I' condition licence, have an interlock device fitted, nominate a vehicle and complete their 12 month AIIP prescribed period. If the customer does not wish to do so, their licence will remain cancelled for the 2 year AIIP interlock period.

    To update the customer's record to reflect that they are subject to a NSW MAIP, the offence code 5892 must be applied to the customer's record. As a NSW court cannot be entered, 'Brisbane Magistrates Court' must be entered in the 'court' field.

    Entering the offence code 5892 will:
    • Cancel the driver licence
    • Add a 2 year interlock period (IPER) from the date that the mandatory court imposed disqualification period is completed
    • not show on the traffic history
    • ensure that the interlock requirement behaves the same as a Queensland AIIP offence
    • Generate all AIIP forms

    The customer must be advised that on completion of the AIIP (either the 2 year interlock period or the 12 month prescribed period), NSW may reimpose the remainder of their 5 year disqualification period. The customer must be advised to seek advice from >Transport for NSW before they drive in NSW as it may be prohibited.

    This offence code is only to be used for Queensland licence holders who are disqualified by a NSW court and subject to the NSW MAIP 5 year disqualification period.
'I' ConditionThe person will be issued a Qld licence endorsed with an ‘I’ condition. The first Qld driver licence issued to the person during an interlock period will incur an ‘I’ licence code fee, in addition to the standard licence fee. Any subsequent driver licence renewal or replacement during the interlock period will not incur the ‘I’ licence code fee.

This condition means that the person may only drive a motor vehicle fitted with an approved interlock that has been nominated to TMR, via an approved interlock provider.

Exemption from Requirements
Exemption from Requirements
    The holder of a Qld licence endorsed with an ’I‘ condition may drive a vehicle that is not fitted with a device or nominated to TMR if the person is:
    • receiving driver training from an accredited driver trainer, in a vehicle provided by the driver trainer;
      or
    • taking a practical driving test;
      or
    • carrying, and driving in accordance with, a current Interlock Exemption Certificate under TO(RUM) Act 1995 s91R - the exemption certificate must be produced if requested by a police officer TO(RUM) Act 1995 s91W(4)
      Refer: Interlock Exemption below.

    The person will also be subject to the no alcohol limit.

    Note:It is not a mandatory requirement that a person:
    • obtain a driver licence with the 'I' condition; or
    • if they do obtain a driver licence with the 'I' condition, for them to fit an interlock to a vehicle
    they may choose to not drive and ‘sit out’ the interlock period.
Duration of the AIIPThe person will be subject to the AIIP for a maximum period of two years (the interlock period). However, a person who meets the prescribed period requirements may complete the AIIP after a minimum period of 365 days.

Interlock Period
Interlock Period
    The interlock period will start on the day:
    • after the relevant disqualification period for the latest offence ends, for persons subject to the AIIP as a result of a conviction for a relevant Qld offence/s.
    • they were first issued a Non-Qld licence subject to the ‘I’ condition imposed in the other jurisdiction, for persons subject to the AIIP as a result of transferring a non-Qld licence with an interlock requirement.

    The interlock period will end, and be expired on the customer’s record, when:
    • two years have lapsed since the commencement of the interlock period (regardless of whether they obtained a driver licence with the 'I' condition).
    • the person meets the requirements of the prescribed period for a minimum period of 365 days.
    • the person’s Queensland driver licence is cancelled for a subsequent relevant drink driving offence.
Prescribed Period
Prescribed Period
    The prescribed period is a minimum cumulative period of 365 days during which the person:
    (a)holds a valid Qld licence with an ‘I’ condition; AND
    • has a nominated vehicle* fitted with a prescribed interlock; and/or
    • has a valid interlock exemption;
    and/or
    (b)holds valid non-Qld driver licence with an ‘I’ condition; AND
    • has a vehicle fitted with an interlock;
      or
    • has a valid non-Qld interlock exemption.

    Each day will only count towards the 365 days of prescribed period if all of the
    requirements above are met; any day where one of the requirements is not met will not count.

    *The unregistered status of a nominated vehicle does not affect the 365 day prescribed period.

    Example:
    If a person obtains a driver licence with an ‘I’ condition on 10
    January and fits an interlock to a nominated vehicle on 25 January, the time from
    10 January to 24 January does not count towards the prescribed period. Time
    from 25 January onwards will count, providing the person has a valid licence and
    keeps the interlock fitted.

    Any time the requirements are not met, such as periods of suspension, licence expiry or if the interlock is removed from the vehicle will not count as tenure for the prescribed period.

    Example:
    If a person obtains a driver licence with an ‘I’ condition and fits an interlock to a nominated vehicle on 3 April, then has their licence suspended for three months from 10 May to 9 August, the three month suspension will not count towards the prescribed period.

    Time from 3 April to 9 May will count as tenure and time from 10 August onwards will also count as tenure, provided the person has a valid licence and keeps the interlock fitted.

    365 days where all of the requirements have been met need to be accumulated to complete the prescribed period.

    The prescribed period will end, and be expired on the customer’s record when the customer has completed 365 days of prescribed period tenure, or if the two year interlock period ends which ever is sooner.

    The prescribed period may be extended by TMR by up to 90 days. Refer: Show Cause

    Note:It is the participant’s responsibility to confirm they have completed the AIIP before removing the interlock. If the participant removes the interlock before they have completed of the AIIP, they will be responsible for the costs to reinstall and remove the interlock.


Note:A person cannot have more than one interlock period or prescribed period at the same time.

Checking the estimated AIIP completion date
Checking the estimated AIIP completion date
    Operators can check the length of time remaining to complete the AIIP by viewing the following TICA screens and utilising the tenure calculator:
    • IPER sanction on the Driver Licence Hub shows the full 2 year interlock period
    • The tenure calculator shows the number of prescribed period days served, the number of days remaining to complete the AIIP and the estimated AIIP completion date
    • the List Nomination screen shows the date the interlock was installed or removed
    • the Licence History screen is used to investigate any discrepancies that the customer may think exists (e.g. SPER suspension etc)

      Note:Any period where the following is applicable does not count towards prescribed period tenure:
      • the licence is cancelled, suspended or expired,
      • the person does not fit an interlock , or removes the interlock from their vehicle and does not hold a valid exemption,
      • the person’s interlock exemption is cancelled, suspended or expired

Removal of 'I' condition
Removal of 'I' condition
Removal of 'I' condition
    An ’I’ condition will be automatically expired and removed from a person’s record when either the prescribed period or the interlock period ends, whichever is sooner.

    The customer will be notified in writing that the condition has been expired and that they need to have their licence reprinted, free of charge. The customer will need to replace their licence.

    Note:There is no requirement to have the 'I' condition removed from the driver licence prior to having the interlock removed from the vehicle. Once the 'I' condition has been expired and removed from the person's record, they may drive without having an interlock fitted. However, they may be fined if they do not attend a centre to have the condition removed from their physical driver licence.

    No written notification will be provided to a person who did not obtain a driver licence with the 'I' condition.
Vehicle NominationWhile subject to the AIIP a person may only drive a vehicle that has been fitted with an approved interlock and nominated to TMR on the approved form (AIIP - Apply/remove a vehicle nomination (F4841)), via an approved interlock provider.

Nominating a vehicle
Nominating a vehicle
    • To nominate a vehicle for the AIIP the person must complete AIIP Apply/remove a vehicle nomination (F4841) and lodge the form with their approved interlock provider.
      • The approved interlock provider will electronically notify TMR of the vehicle nomination and this will be recorded against the person’s customer record.
      Note:Nomination is only via an interlock provider - if a customer presents this form at a DLIC they should be advised that they need to lodge the form with their interlock provider.
    • A person may nominate more than one vehicle - a separate form is required for each vehicle.
    • Multiple interlock drivers may nominate the same vehicle.
    • A nominated vehicle may only be of a class that the person is authorised to drive.
    Note:A nominated vehicle may be fitted with an interlock prior to the reissue of the driver licence with the 'I' condition, however this period will not be counted towards the prescribed period. Refer: Prescribed Period above.
Removing a vehicle nomination
Removing a vehicle nomination
    • To remove the nomination of a vehicle for the AIIP the person must complete AIIP - Apply/remove a vehicle nomination (F4841) and lodge the form with their approved interlock provider.
      • The approved interlock provider will notify TMR of the removal of the vehicle nomination and this will be recorded against the person’s customer record.
      Note:Removal of nomination is only via an interlock provider if a customer presents this form at a DLIC they should be advised that they need to lodge the form with their interlock provider.
    • If the interlock is removed from a vehicle that multiple interlock drivers have nominated, the nomination of the vehicle will be removed for all interlock drivers who have nominated the vehicle.

Approved Interlocks
Approved Interlocks
Approved Interlocks
    Only interlocks that have been approved under the TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 may be used for the AIIP. TMR has approved three interlocks for the AIIP:
    • Guardian WR3
    • Guardian 2030
    • Drager Interlock XT

    A person cannot fit their own interlock to a vehicle to meet the requirements of the AIIP.
Interlock – Fitment, Servicing and Removal Interlock drivers, who elect to fit an interlock, will enter into a legally enforceable arrangement with an approved interlock provider for the provision of interlock related services – the installation, servicing and removal of the interlock. All enquiries about these services should be referred to the relevant approved interlock provider.

Multiple Interlock Drivers
Multiple Interlock Drivers
    • Multiple interlock drivers may nominate and use the same vehicle, however the driver of the vehicle must be able to be identified.
      • If a vehicle is fitted with an interlock that has the technical capability to identify different drivers (for example, via a swipe card or PIN) then this functionality must be used by the interlock driver each and every time they drive the vehicle.
      • If a vehicle IS NOT fitted with an interlock that has the technical capability to identify different drivers then all interlock drivers who nominate that vehicle must use Interlock Driver Record (F4847) each and every time they drive the vehicle.
      Note:Interlock Driver Record (F4847) will be available from the TMR website or from the approved interlock provider
      • If a vehicle is nominated by multiple interlock drivers the approved interlock provider will notify all interlock drivers of the requirement to use the interlock’s identifying capabilities or keep an Interlock Driver Record (F4847).
      • Each interlock driver must have an arrangement with the approved interlock provider who fitted the interlock.

    The Interlock Driver Record (F4847) must be maintained for the period of time that multiple interlock drivers have nominated the vehicle and retained for an additional period of 12 months. The Interlock Driver Record (F4847) must be presented to TMR if requested in writing.

    Drivers of a vehicle fitted with an approved interlock who are not subject to the AIIP do not need to identify themselves and the driver of the vehicle through the above means.

For further information on interlock service arrangements or to arrange for the installation of an interlock the person should be advised to contact an approved interlock provider.

Information Pack
Information Pack
    An information pack, including the documents outlined below, will be sent to the person to advise them that they will be subject to the AIIP on completion of their relevant disqualification period and to provide them with information on the program:
    • Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program Advice Notice (F4835)
    • AIIP – Brochure
    • AIIP – Apply/Remove Vehicle Nomination Form (F4841)

    The information pack is sent:
    • Via mail contractor only
    • As ordinary mail
    • To the customer's postal address
    Note:The information pack cannot be reprinted or resent by mail, however form F4841 can be printed and handed to the customer.

    Customers can also refer to tmr.qld.gov.au/interlocks for more information.
Notices
Notices
    Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program Advice Notice (F4835 )
    Notice is sent, as part the Interlock Information Pack, 14 days after the court result is entered on to TICA. If the court result is entered onto TICA within six weeks of the relevant disqualification period end date the notice will be sent immediately.
    Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program Courtesy Notice (F4989)
    Notice is sent a maximum of six weeks prior to the end of the person's relevant disqualification period. The notice is sent as a courtesy to remind the person of the requirements of the AIIP.

    If the court result is entered on to TICA within four months of the relevant disqualification period end date, this notice will not be sent as the customer will have recently been sent F4835.
    Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program Completion Notice (F4866)
    Notice is sent within 48 hours of the completion of the interlock period or the prescribed period, if the person has been granted a licence with an ‘I’ condition. The notice advises them that they must visit a CSC to have the ‘I’ condition removed from their licence.
    If the person was not re-licensed following their disqualification they are not sent this notice.
