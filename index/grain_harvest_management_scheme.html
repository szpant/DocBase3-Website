Grain Harvest Management Scheme (7636)


Grain Harvest Management Scheme
What it isThe Grain Harvest Management Scheme (GHMS) was established to accommodate the difficulties in accurately loading on-farm bulk commodities such as grains, oil seeds, pulses and bulk fresh cut silage (not bailed).

The GHMS makes allowances for the uncertainties (varying moisture contents and density) for these commodities by providing participating heavy vehicles a tolerance about the regulation general mass limits.

The GHMS scheme operates under the Queensland Class 3 Heavy Vehicle (Grain Harvest Management Scheme) Mass Exemption Notice 2019 (No. 1) and s117 of the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland).
Loading Tolerance'sLoad tolerance's are not a productivity concession and heavy vehicles operating under the GHMS should be loaded according to regulation mass limits where possible.

The following mass limits are allowable under the GHMS:
  • maximum 7.5 per cent above the GML mass limits for the vehicle or combination.
  • maximum 10 per cent above the GML mass limits for the axle/axle group masses;
  • the maximum steer axle mass of a heavy vehicle must not be more than:
    • 6.6 tonne;
      or
    • 7.1 tonne for a prime mover forming part of a road train fitted with tyres with section widths of at least 375mm;
      or
    • The manufacturer’s steer axle mass rating.

Vehicles manufactured with Front Under-run Protection (FUP’s) and a prime mover forming part of a road train fitted with tyres of a section width of at least 295mm, are entitled to 6.5 tonne on the steer axle, however, the 10% loading tolerance does not apply to these vehicles as a maximum of 6.6 tonne is the permitted limit.

Irrespective of these GHMS mass limits, vehicles cannot exceed their manufacturer’s gross or axle mass rating.
Scheme participation
Vehicle Operators
Vehicle Operators
    Participation and ongoing registration in the GHMS for these participants is managed by AgForce.

    For information on eligible vehicle configurations, Refer to: Vehicle Configurations

    Vehicles registered with AgForce in the GHMS are issued with identification stickers.
Receivers
Receivers
    Receivers are approved to participate in the GHMS through the Heavy Vehicle Policy unit.

    When TMR and a receiver enter a signed Memorandum of Understanding their details are added to the approved receiver register.

    For information about the register contact Heavy Vehicle Policy

    Approved GHMS Receivers are listed on the AgForce website.
GHMS Participant Responsibilities
Responsibilities of the Approved Receiver
Responsibilities of the Approved Receiver
    The GHMS receiver participation guide and GHMS receiver MOU provides details about the approved receivers responsibilities under the GHMS.

    Approved GHMS Receivers must weigh all GHMS vehicles at the point of delivery.

    If the vehicle, or combination, does not match the Vehicle Configuration Guide general mass limits apply, Refer: Identification Codes and Mass Limits

    The GHMS mass limits do not apply unless the receiver is approved under a signed GHMS Receiver Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with TMR.

    If a vehicle is overloaded by 20% (severe overload) a slip is completed and provided to Grain Harvest Management Scheme immediately.
Responsibilities of the operator
Responsibilities of the operator
    Vehicles registered in the scheme must display a current GHMS identification label. The GHMS labels are coloured coded. These labels change each year.

    The mass limits allowable under the GHMS only apply if:
    • the operator is registered with AgForce;
    • is displaying the current GHMS label;
    • is delivering to an approved receival site;
      and
    • the heavy vehicle is an eligible vehicle.
Delivering to an Approved Receiver
Delivering to an Approved Receiver
    GHMS vehicles must deliver to the nearest Approved GHMS Receiver accepting the commodity being delivered. If circumstances require the vehicle to go past one or more sites operated by that receiver they must have TMR approval. The Approval Form is Appendix A in the Accreditation booklet.

    To obtain approval the farmer or transport operator can complete and email Appendix A to Grain Harvest Management Scheme. If approved, TMR will sign the form and return via email. The TMR approval must be carried by the driver for the entire journey and be produced if asked by a Transport Inspector. To produce an electronic copy on a smart phone or tablet is acceptable.

    Operators of vehicles that do not comply with the above conditions will be treated as non-participants in the GHMS and general mass limit will apply and will be subject to normal compliance and enforcement action.
AgForce Responsibilities
AgForce Responsibilities
    Some GHMS functions are administered by AgForce on behalf of TMR under a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU).

    The roles and responsibilities document provides an overview of GHMS management roles, Refer: Supporting Documents below.

    AgForce assess all reporting data received from each approved receiver and are responsible for issuing advice letters to vehicle operators who exceed the 7.5% GHMS limit.

    All reports, and copies of advice letters are forwarded to Grain Harvest Management Scheme each month.
Audits for Approved Receivers and AgForceReceiver participation should be in accordance with the participation guide and their MoU.
Site Audit
Site Audit
    Site audit of new receivers operating in the GHMS should be undertaken within 3 months to ensure systems are in place to continue to meet their obligations.

    An approved receiver must notify TMR of the sites operating times. If this has not occurred prior to harvest season, the approved receiver should be contacted to provide these details.

    Ongoing audits should occur yearly or coincide with the expiry of a weighbridge certificate.

    Audit checklist
    GHMS Audit Form - Feb 2021
Desktop Audits
Desktop Audits
    TMR Compliance will regularly conduct desktop audits of reporting data to:
    • determine for example, excessive overloading data or all data coming in at GML or under the 7.5% – may warrant a site visit to check weighbridge function.
    • overload reporting data and advice letters match (advice letters are sent to overloads up to 19% over the GHMS limit).
    • monitor registered vehicles to ensure vehicle given a show cause notice for severe overloading:
      • (whilst suspended from GHMS) was loaded to GML at next site visit;
      • does not continue to severely overload.

    Advice letters should be issued consistently to inform further compliance action on repeated overloading. If advice letters issued do not coincide with overloading stats, AgForce should be audited to determine why.
Identifying GHMS Vehicles
GHMS Vehicle Identification
GHMS Vehicle Identification
    Each GHMS registered vehicle has an individual membership number and displays an identification sticker which must be affixed to the participating vehicle (2 door stickers and 1 windscreen sticker).

    Each operational year (1 July to 30 June) GHMS sticker colour and membership number changes.

    Prior to issue of the sticker and membership number, an email document indicating registration approval and identification details (temporary sticker), will be accepted for 28 days after the date of issue. This emailed document must be produced to a Transport Inspector or Approved GHMS Receiver on request.
Non-Compliance Actions
Actions for Detected Non-Compliance
Actions for Detected Non-Compliance
    A GHMS dashboard, currently under development, has been designed with specific parameters to assist Transport Inspectors manage GHMS compliance activities. At the end of each month the reporting data received by AgForce will be uploaded to the dashboard.
    Severe overload show cause
    Severe overload show cause
      The operator of a severely overloaded vehicle (20% or more above GML) should be show caused by TMR Compliance.

      After a show cause response is considered, TMR may direct AgForce to de-register the eligible vehicle.

      Show Cause Letter - over 20%

      Note:Advice letters are not sent to operators for these breaches.

    Repeat overloading show cause
    Repeat overloading show cause
      If reporting data and advice letters identify repeated overloading (repeated overloading is 5 or more overloads within a reporting period by an individual operator) above 7.5%, a show cause notice must be issued to the operator.

      Show Cause Letter - Repeated - over 7.5%

      After a show-cause response is considered TMR, may direct AgForce to de-register the eligible vehicle.
    Intercepted Vehicles
    Intercepted Vehicles
      If a GHMS vehicle or combination is intercepted by Transport Inspectors and assessed to be exceeding GHMS mass limits, general mass limits are to be applied to determine the overload percentage and the categories of breaches (risk category).

      On-road inspections of GHMS vehicles should ensure information (Refer: Operator responsibilities above) is present.
    Approved receivers
    Approved receivers
      If the Approved Receiver is not complying with the MOU or Participation Guide they should be issued with a show cause notice

      If the Approved Receiver is not complying with the MOU or Participation Guide for possible Chain of responsibility/Safety Duty offences, TMR Compliance should conduct an investigation.

      Actions against Approved Receivers must be recorded in the approved receiver register, contact Heavy Vehicle Policy

      Examples of compliance action against a receiver:
      • The receiver continually fails to provide severe overload slips to compliance. This should be matched with reporting data (monthly).
      • The receiver has non-approved configurations entering and/or they are recording incorrect truck configurations on their reports.
    AgForce
    AgForce
      If AgForce is detected as non-compliant with the MOU or scheme requirements, notify the regional Compliance Manager and Heavy Vehicle Policy
