National Hazard Perception Test (7527)

Project NameNational Hazard Perception Test
When29 March 2021
BackgroundThe Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR) has made changes to the hazard perception test.

These changes include:
  • moving the location of the hazard perception test as part of the car licensing process;
  • introducing a hazard perception test for motorcycle learner licence holders;
  • improving the quality of the test clips with new computer-generated images.

All learner drivers and motorcycle riders will have to pass the updated hazard perception test before they can be issued/upgrade to a Provisional/Open licence.
Who will be impacted and how?Learner drivers and learner motorcycle riders will be required to pass the relevant hazard perception test, before they can be issued/upgraded to a Provisional/Open licence and begin driving or riding unsupervised.

Existing QLD P1 licence holders will still need to complete the HPT before they upgrade to their P2 or open licence.

Customer who take the HPT before the practical driving test or Q-Ride RE course
Customer who take the HPT before the practical driving test or Q-Ride RE course
    Car
    Car
      Before 1 July 2021, learner drivers can take the HPT either before or after their practical driving test. This is to limit the impact of the new HPT requirement on practical test bookings. Customers who choose to take the practical driving test before completing the HPT will have to re-attend a TMR customer service centre or driver licence issuing centre to upgrade their licence after they have passed the HPT.

      From 1 July 2021, learner licence holders will only be able to take the practical driving test, if they have passed the HPT first.

    Motorcycle
    Motorcycle
      From 29 March 2021, all motorcycle learner licence holders must complete the new motorcycle hazard perception test before they can upgrade their licence.

      Motorcycle learners who are eligible to take their Q-Ride RE Course, or Q-Safe RE practical riding test before 1 July 2021 will be able to take the HPT either before or after their Q-Ride Course or Q-Safe test. This is to limit the impacts the new HPT requirement has on Q-Ride and Q-Safe bookings.

      From 1 July 2021, motorcycle learner licence holders will only be able to take the Q-Ride RE Course, or Q-Safe RE practical riding test after they have passed the HPT.

      From 1 July 2021 the eligibility checker will be updated to include the HPT as a pre-requisite to the Q-Ride RE Course. From 1 July 2021, all applicants must have completed the HPT first. Any customers who return an ineligible result should be informed of the HPT requirement and asked to complete this test before they re-book.
Customer who take the practical driving test on 26 and 27 March 20201
Customer who take the practical driving test on 26 and 27 March 20201
    Customers who pass their practical driving test on Friday 26 March 2021 and upgrade their licence on this day will not have to take the HPT to progress to unsupervised driving. If they obtain a P1 licence, they will have to take the HPT to progress to either a P2 or open licence.

    Customers who pass their practical driving test on Saturday 27 March 2021 will have to pass the HPT on or after the 29 March 2021 and prior to attending a centre to upgrade their learner licence. To upgrade their licence, these customers will have to pass the HPT and then attend a TMR customer service centre or driver licence issuing centre.

    The HPT will be offline Saturday and Sunday (27 and 28 March 2021) whilst the old service is decommissioned, and the new service is released.
Customers who upgrade from a P1 to a P2 licence
Customers who upgrade from a P1 to a P2 licence
    Customers who were issued a P1 licence prior to 29 March 2021 and pass the HPT will have their licence upgraded in a batch process that runs overnight. They will be posted their new P2/O licence in the mail once they have met the P1 tenure requirements.

    Customers who were issued a P1 licence on or after 29 March 2021 will only have their P1 type licence displayed on their licence, regardless of the period of issue. Once the customer meets the tenure requirements for their P2 licence to be upgraded the batch process will automatically upgrade their licence and post them a new P2/Open licence in the mail.
