Intercepts (5231)

Intercepts
LegislationTransport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 – Section 31 – relates specifically to the stopping of private vehicles.

Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 – Section 32 – provides for the power to stop prescribed heavy vehicles.

Section 513 of the Heavy Vehicle National Law Act 2012 enables an Authorised Officer to stop a heavy vehicle for the purpose of exercising a power under the HVNL.

The NHVR have released a number work procedure relating to heavy vehicles.

These work procedures have been adopted by the Department of Transport and Main Roads to promote the consistent interpretation and application of the HVNL across participating jurisdictions.

TMR policy over rules NHVR Work Procedures if there is any inconsistency.
Safety
Safety
Safety
    Transport Inspectors must use the utmost care and attention and be aware of the dangers when intercepting vehicles. This includes the safety of themselves, their partners, the intercepted driver/passengers and all other road users. Safety should always be the primary consideration of a Transport Inspector when performing their duties. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) must be worn at all times whilst conducting vehicle intercepts.

    A minimum of two (2) Transport Inspectors are required to conduct mobile or static intercepts, except when the Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) states otherwise.

    It is the responsibility of all Authorised Officers to ensure the safety of all persons on site including drivers and visitors.
    Any hazards, near misses or injuries must be reported to WH&S Connect 1800 867 947 and the STI as soon as practicable.
    If anyone on site requires medical assistance or an emergency situation occurs call 000 immediately, follow the SOP and call the STI when it is safe to do so.

Consideration for Type of Load and Vehicle
Vehicle Types and Loads
Vehicle Types and Loads
    Consideration is to be given to vehicles carrying loads such as:

    1. Livestock
      1. Consider animal welfare (conditions that could cause stress to animals, extreme heat, cold and so on).
      2. Not to be intercepted on steep road shoulders (can be dangerous for multiple deck crates).
    2. Dangerous Goods
      1. If possible, attempt to intercept these vehicles away from residential areas.
    3. Escorted over dimensional/permitted vehicles
      1. These vehicles should be intercepted at a static site.
      2. The interception site must be large enough to accommodate the vehicle and its load and all escort vehicles.
      3. The direction to stop is to be given to the driver/s of the escort vehicle/s initially and subsequently the vehicle itself. (This includes police vehicles when escorting loads of this type).
      4. Approach the police officer/lead pilot vehicle driver first and outline the procedure/s that will be undertaken during the intercept.
      5. Conduct required checks promptly to reduce delay.
Conducting the Compliance Check
Compliance Checks
Compliance Checks
    Compliance checks may be able to be completed when the vehicle is first stopped on the intercept site. However if the vehicle is to be weighed, a more detailed inspection is required or an offence has been detected the vehicle may be directed to another location in the intercept site.
    If the vehicle is to be moved
    If the vehicle is to be moved
    Before any vehicle moves on an interception site, ensure all personal are accounted for and advise everyone of the intention to move a vehicle.
    If a vehicle has been moved and is going to be parked for further checks, consider placing traffic cones around the vehicle to reduce the safety risk.

    Note: Refer to the Site Safety Plan and SOP for vehicle positioning.

    Once the vehicle has stopped in a safe place and you have completed the introduction, begin a systematic assessment of the vehicle and the driver.
    For your safety direct the driver to stay in the vehicle, put the vehicle in Neutral/Park and apply the Park/Brake.

Vehicles of Interest (VOI)
VOI
VOI
    In instances where you have checked the registration status of a vehicle and the results of the check indicate the vehicle as a VOI the following procedure is to be adopted:
    • Do not under any circumstances perform a mobile intercept or follow the vehicle.
    • Contact the police and advise them that you have detected a VOI and advise them of the last known location of the VOI and its direction of travel.

    If a VOI status is identified on a vehicle after it has been intercepted the following procedure is to be adopted:
    • At the point when you establish that the vehicle is a VOI it is to be allowed to leave the interception site.
    • Do not under any circumstances alert the driver of the VOI that you are aware of its status.
    • Immediately contact police to advise them of last known location and direction of travel of the VOI.
    • If you have been able to sight the driver and passengers travelling in the VOI; a description of all occupants and any other relevant facts is to be recorded in your notebook and passed onto the police at the earliest opportunity.
Person of Interest (POI)
POI
POI
    If a registered owner of a vehicle checked or a person checked for driver licence details comes up as a POI; intercepts or actions resulting from intercepts should be dealt with in the normal manner with the following additional actions:
    • Under no circumstances alert the person that you are aware of the POI status.
    • Every effort should be made to establish the person’s current address.
    • A record is to be made in your notebook of the person’s current address, the location the person was detected, the registration number and description of the vehicle that the person was travelling in or driving; and the direction of travel and description of the person.
    • If it is possible relay the information to police during the period that the vehicle is intercepted.
    • If it is not possible to relay the information at the time of the intercept; it should be relayed to the police as soon as possible after the intercept.

Finalising the Intercept
Finalising the Intercept (Static and Mobile)
Finalising the Intercept (Static and Mobile)
    To conclude the intercept:
    • explain any notices, documentation or directions
    • ensure the driver's licence or any documentation is returned to the driver
    • ensure all TMR equipment and accountable forms are accounted for
    • thank the driver for their assistance
    • For mobile intercepts:
      • TIs must return to the patrol car using the corridor of safety
      • move the patrol car to a safe location before commencing the recording actions.
    • Complete recording of intercept including:
      • complete any further paperwork/reports as required.
      • classify and name the BWC recording
      • finalise the intercept in QCIS
Assisting Drivers to Depart a Mobile Intercept
Assisting Drivers to Depart a Mobile Intercept
    If the driver is not ready or is unable to depart immediately, it may be appropriate to assist the departure. Reasons for this include:
    • Public safety
    • Courtesy
    • Traffic flow (slowness of entering vehicle etc.)
