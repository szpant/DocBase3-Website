Legislative changes to Driver Authorisation (DA) (7367)

Project NameLegislative changes to Driver Authorisation (DA)
When1 September 2018 (internal TMR information until this date)
BackgroundOn 1 September 2018, changes to the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Regulation 2018will commence. The regulation update includes the following significant changes to Driver Authorisation:

1.Only two DA categories will exist:
  • Booked hire/taxi (TAXI class in TICA) - will be able to provide any public passenger service that requires a DA
  • General - any service other than booked hire or taxi

Motorcycle tourist and Scheduled DA will no longer exist (see below for more information about these categories)
2.Driver Licence requirements:
  • 3 years of continuous driver licence for DA is changing to be 3 years cumulative (collective) at any time.
  • 2 years of Australian driver licence requirement will be removed for General DA
  • A competency test will no longer be an option for General DA applicants that do not meet DL tenure requirements.
3.Alcohol Interlock - It has been clarified that a person whose licence is subject to an alcohol interlock sanction is not eligible for the issue or renewal of a DA. The requirement now only applies whilst an IPER sanction exists rather than the previous 2 year wait period.
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers
Customers
    • Motorcycle tourist and scheduled DA holders will be automatically authorised to provide General Services.
    • New applicants for Motorcycle Tourist or Scheduled DA's will need to apply for General DA
Customer Service Officers
Customer Service Officers
    • Existing Motorcycle Tourist or Scheduled DA must not be renewed or added in TICA.
    • If a new application or renewal is attempted for a Motorcycle Tourist/Scheduled DA, an error message will display in TICA.
    • Instead of renewing, the Motorcycle Tourist/Scheduled DA will need to be manually removed and a General DA manually added. This will be the same process used for limousine DA holders who transitioned to Booked hire/taxi.
    • A manual check will be required to ensure the customer meets the new tenure requirements (business exceptions will need to be over-ridden)
    • Checklists will be modified to assist CSO's in ensuring that the customer meets the new requirements.
Further informationFurther details will be provided closer to the release date.
