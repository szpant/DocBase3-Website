About Learner Licences
Definitions
Driver Licence Definitions
What it isA learner licence authorises the holder to learn to drive, subject to certain conditions and restrictions.

Required in order to legally learn to drive
Required in order to legally learn to drive
    A learner licence is required for a person:
    • Who does not have a licence that authorises them to drive in Qld
    and
    • They wish to drive on a Qld road
    Example purposes:
    • Learning to drive
    • Taking a practical driving test

When a Learner Licence is NOT required
When a Learner Licence is NOT required
    A learner licence is not required to be issued for:
    Qld Licence - Learning to drive next eligible class
    The holder of a valid Provisional, Probationary or Open licence to learn to drive a vehicle of the next eligible class.
    (TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s4(3), 5(2...8)&(11)
    List of Next Eligible Classes
    Foreign Licence Holder - Fails Practical Driving Test
    If the holder of a foreign driver licence fails a practical driving test and the person's authority to drive is withdrawn, they may use their foreign licence to undertake a subsequent practical driving test.
    (TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s128(6)
    Foreign Licence Holder - Practical Driving Test - Authority to drive withdrawn due to residency
    The holder of a current foreign licence who needs to undertake a practical driving test in the same class of vehicle authorised to be driven under their foreign licence.
    (TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s128(9)

    The person does not wish to drive other than for the purpose and duration of the practical driving test.

    Note:A learner licence is required if the person wishes to drive for any other purpose.

Drive Accompanied/Under Direction
    Motorcycle
    Motorcycle
      Learner licence holder must either:
      • Drive under the direction of a person in/on another vehicle who:
        • Holds and has held a valid open licence or equivalent for at least 1 year, for that class of motorcycle
        TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s4(5)
        • Ensures that an L Plate is conspicuous to anyone else looking at the rear of the learner rider's motorcycle
      or
      • Have a passenger in the side car of the motorcycle who:
        • Holds and has held a valid open licence or equivalent for at least 1 year, for that class of motorcycle
        • Ensures that an L Plate is conspicuous to anyone else looking at the rear of the motorcycle
        TO(RUM-DL)Reg. 2010 s4(5)

      Supervisor
      Supervisor
        A person must not direct the riding of a motorcycle by a holder who is authorised to learn to ride a motorcycle unless the person:
        • Holds a valid O Type licence for the class of motorcycle that the holder is driving
        and
        • Has held the licence for at least 1 year
      TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s4(8)
        There is no prescribed limit in the Queensland Road Rules as to the number of motorcycle learner riders that a supervisor (that is not Q-Ride provider) may direct at one time. However, the supervisor must be able to give sufficient direction to all learner riders they are supervising. The safety of learner riders and other road users is the priority and the number of motorcycle learner riders a supervisor can safely direct is likely dependent on the level of experience of the learners.

    Tenure and Licence Progression
    Qld Learner Licence Tenure Periods & Progression
    Time not required to be continuous
    Time not required to be continuous
      The time the licence is required to be held:
      • May be broken by expiry, suspension or cancellation
      • Can include time spent serving a period of good driving behaviour (GDB)
      • May include time spent on other licences including Interstate (inc. external territories) / NZ/ Foreign

        Non-Qld licence for tenure period
        Non-Qld licence for tenure period
          Proof of the non Qld licence tenure is required.
          Proof may be obtained by:
          • The customer presenting a non-Qld licence
          • The customer presenting confirmation of licence details from the relevant non-Qld licence issuing authority
          • Faxing the relevant interstate/NZ authority to obtain the licence details

          The details will need to be recorded on the NQPR screen in TICA.
      Only those periods that the licence is actually valid will be considered toward the time required to be held.
