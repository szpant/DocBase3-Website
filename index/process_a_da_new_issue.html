Process a DA New Issue (6006)

Applies to
Applies to
    Lodging an application for a Driver Authorisation (DA)for BHTX or GENR.

    Transitional Note:
    From 1 September 2018, there are only two types of DA - BHTX and Genr. If a SCHE/TRMC application was lodged prior to 1 September but not yet approved it cannot be approved. The operator must add the Genr and withdraw the SCHE/TRMC classes. If a new application for SCHE, TRMC or LIMO is attempted after 1 September 2018, an error message will display.

Before lodging in TICA
Before lodging in TICA
    Item
    Details
    PrerequisitesBefore commencing the lodgement process, the customer’s prerequisites must be considered to ensure that the correct driver authorisation term and type is selected and the correct fee is paid. You must take into account:
    • The term requested by the customer;
    • The expiry of the customer’s work entitlement date;
    • The expiry of the customer’s existing or new medical certificate;
    • Any text messages in TICA about the maximum term and type of DA that should be issued to the customer;
    • Any text message in TICA provided by regional PT office/s should be considered if relevant
    • Whether the customer has multiple industry authorities and wishes to align the expiry dates.

    If the fee paid by the customer is for a longer term than it is possible to approve (taking into account the prerequisites), then a refund of the excess fee paid will have to be arranged.

    Refer: PT Documentation Database > Operator Accreditation (OA) & Driver Authorisation (DA) > Part C: DA Policy & Procedures > 1. DA Policy > 1.04 - Application Process - Through a Qld Transport Office/QGAP On-Line Office

    Note: If the Medical Certificate indicates that the customer has a Medical Condition and the results are not on TICA, the details must be recorded against the Driver Licence and on the MCR Hub.
    Verify Work EntitlementsCheck the customer has satisfactory evidence of their entitlement to work in Australia.
    • If the customer holds a foreign passport Visa Evidence Card (or ImmiCard) or Document for Travel to Australia with an entitlement to work in Australia, a VEVO check should be conducted.
    • Check the customer has completed the Authority to Check a Visa Holder's Work Entitlement VEVO (F4595)
    • Undertake a VEVO check on the customer's entitlement to work in Australia
      Login to Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO) Check
    • Complete the Office Use Only section of the form (F4595).


    Refer: Passenger Transport > 5.Driver Authorisation > Driver Authorisation Guide > Eligibility requirements > Work Entitlement > Entitlement to work in Australia

    Refer: PT Documentation Database > Operator Accreditation (OA) & Driver Authorisation (DA) > Part C: DA Policy & Procedures > 2. DA Procedure > 2.31 - VEVO - Checking an applicant's entitlement to work in Australia

    Refer: PT Documentation Database > Operator Accreditation (OA) & Driver Authorisation (DA) > Part C: DA Policy & Procedures > 1. DA Policy > 1.39 - VEVO – Documents that provide satisfactory evidence that a person has an entitlement to work in Australia.

    Capturing facial image
      If TICA prompts to capture the customer's facial image, direct the customer to
      the seat for the default camera and request them to:
      • Sit on the seat provided and to stay within the markings
        Note: If customer uses a mobility aid, retract or remove the chair and have the customer position themselves within the designated markings of the photo area
      • Look straight ahead at the camera (a frontal image of their face is required)
      • Remove any head attire or sunglasses (unless there are valid religious, cultural or medical reasons for not doing so, then refer to Manager)
        The customer must remain in your line of vision during the entire transaction.
      • Once the facial image is successfully captured, request the customer to return to your counter.

Refuse to Consider Application (if required)
Refuse to Consider Application (if required)
    The department must refuse to accept an application if the applicant:
    • Does not provide evidence that they have an entitlement to work in Australia;
    • Does not apply in the approved form;
    • Does not pay the applicable fees, if required;
    • Does not meet EOI requirements;
    • Does not allow the chief executive to take and keep a digital image or collect their digitised signature; or
    • Fails the 1:1 image match
    • Has a current alcohol interlock condition on their driver licence.

    The applicant should be advised of the requirements to successfully lodge an application. Verbal advice may be given, and written notices are available if required.

    Give customer
    • Their original EOI /other documents presented
    • Application form (Unless 1:1 image match is performed)
    • Notice requesting further documents or information relevant to the application (if applicable) refer above
    Advise customer
    • That the application cannot be considered at this stage
    • The reason the application cannot be considered
    • The application may be considered when the required eligibility is met, or requested documents provided
