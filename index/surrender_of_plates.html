Surrender of Plates (3087)

When They Should be Surrendered
  • Replacement of standard plates when damaged or replacing with special edition/National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates
  • Ordering of a new set of personalised plates
  • Remaking/upgrading existing set of personalised/customised plates
  • Re-registering an unregistered vehicle
  • Withdrawing SIVE concession when SIVE plates are attached
  • Automatic/Voluntary Cancellation of a registration
  • National Heavy Vehicle Plates must be surrendered upon cancellation of the vehicle unless the vehicle has been registered in another participating jurisdiction or customised
  • National Heavy Vehicle Plates must be surrendered if being replaced with personalised plates
  • Returned by Compliance or Police Officer (can be done as bulk surrender using the reason "SURR BY OFFICER")
  • Interstate Plates returned by registered operator or registered motor dealer
  • Replacement of Limo/SPL/Taxi/Substitute Taxi plates - operators must email their local Local Translink Regional Office for authorisation.

    Note:There is no requirement to contact the local TransLink office when replacing with standard number plates.
Number of plates to be surrenderedIf standard plates are being replaced with:
  • Standard/special edition. National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates both plates are surrendered at the time of the transaction.
  • Customised/personalised plates - one plate is surrendered at the time of the transaction and the other plate within 14 days of the transaction (only if plates are damaged).

If personalised/customised plates are being:
  • Replaced or removed there is no requirement to surrender either plate
  • remade only one plate is surrendered (only if plates are damaged).

When cancelling registration with:
  • Standard plates both plates must be surrendered
  • Personalised/customised plates there is no requirement to surrender either plate
ExceptionsNational Heavy Vehicle Number Plates (NHVNP)
Registered operators of heavy vehicles issued with NHVNPs are not required to surrender NHVNPs when relocating to or from another participating jurisdiction.

When a cancellation application has been made and the NHVNP are in the status of "Interstate" it is essential that the plates are not surrendered to allow their re-use. The customer is eligible for a refund.
Deceased/closed CRNIf a customer is deceased or closed, personalised or customised plate products cannot remain in their name.
  • Personalised plates must be:
    • Transferred or
    • Surrendered then cancelled
  • Customised plates can be transferred (only with the vehicle) or surrendered then cancelled.

Refer: Vehicle Rego > 11. Plates > The how to: Plates
Interstate Plates
Light Vehicles
Light Vehicles
    TMR does not have the authority to insist that interstate plates are surrendered, however it is encouraged. Receipt for plate can be provided when requested.

    Applicants should be informed that if they do not wish to surrender their interstate plates to TMR or their agents at the time of registration they are required to surrender to the authority who issued the plate.

    Surrender to be processed using the Bulk/QPS/Interstate Surrender Process.

    Reprint of receipts can be requested In person or by Electronic Lodgement
National Heavy Vehicles
National Heavy Vehicles
    National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates issued by another participating jurisdiction can be used in Queensland at new business only.
