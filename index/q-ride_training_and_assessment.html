Q-Ride Training and Assessment (701)

About Q-Ride Q-Ride includes a set training curriculum developed by the Department of Transport and Main Roads (the Q-Ride training curriculum). The Q-Ride training curriculum is a graduated training and assessment program that contains standardised courses with set structures, sequencing and timing of activities.

The Q-Ride training curriculum is delivered by TMR accredited rider trainers who are either:
  • a TMR approved Q-Ride registered service provider,
  • are employed by a TMR Q-Ride registered service provider,
    or
  • an agent for a TMR approved Q-Ride registered service provider.

A learner undertaking the Q-Ride training curriculum:
  • must demonstrate their competence of each module of the curriculum
  • will be issued with a Competency Declaration, once they have been assessed by an accredited rider trainer as competent in the relevant course.

Q-Ride operates in parallel with the Q-SAFE practical riding test as an alternative for those seeking to obtain a motorcycle licence.
Aims of Q-Ride
Aims of Q-Ride
    Q-Ride aims to:
    • Improve road safety for motorcycle riders in Queensland
    • Support identified rider education and safety strategies by improving the quality of pre-licence rider instruction
    • Focus on the attainment of competency in motorcycle riding by
      ensuring that riders demonstrate the skills necessary to safely ride a motorcycle and attain a positive attitude toward using the skills while riding

How to become a Q-Ride Registered Service Provider
    Requirements for Service Providers
    Criteria to be metTo become a Q-Ride registered service provider, applicants must demonstrate they can comply with the requirements of the business rules for providing Q-Ride training.

    Applicants must undergo a desktop entry audit and on-site initial audit to demonstrate the delivery of Q-Ride training curriculum to industry standard.
