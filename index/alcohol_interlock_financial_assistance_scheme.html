Alcohol Interlock Financial Assistance Scheme (919)

What it isThe financial assistance scheme (FAS) is intended to provide financial support to eligible low income earners who are required to comply with the Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program (AIIP), and who without assistance may not have the financial resources to pay for the costs of an approved interlock. A copy of the fact sheet can be obtained from:
https://www.qld.gov.au/transport/safety/road-safety/drink-driving/penalties/interlocks/index.html
What does it CoverFor approved applicants, the cost of fitting, maintaining and removing an approved interlock will be paid directly to the approved interlock provider by TMR. Financial assistance will only apply to the standard costs of the interlock; any additional costs such as those incurred due to non-compliance will not be covered.

Licensing fees, including the ‘I’ condition code fee are not covered by the financial assistance scheme.

Note:Any fitment or service fees paid by the customer prior to the approval of a FAS application will not be covered under FAS and as such, will not be reimbursed to the customer.
Who may ApplyA person who is subject the AIIP under section 91J of the TORUM Act 1995 who meets the eligibility criteria.
When can an Application be madeA person subject to the AIIP as a result of a Queensland drink driving offence can submit an application for financial assistance up to six weeks before the end of their relevant disqualification period or at any time during their interlock period.

The holder of a non-Qld licence with an ‘I’ condition who will be subject to the AIIP on licence transfer can submit an application for a financial assistance at any time. (see Note in Eligibility below)
Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility
Eligibility
    To be eligible to receive financial assistance the applicant must:
    • have assessable income and liquid assets below the maximum allowable limits relevant to the applicant's circumstances; and
    • hold a valid Queensland driver licence endorsed with an I condition; and
    • be an Australian Citizen or permanent resident; and
    • not have received financial assistance in the last five years.

    For information about the maximum income and liquid assets criteria refer customer’s to the TMR website:
    http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Licensing/Licence-suspensions-and-disqualifications/Alcohol-ignition-interlocks.aspx#financeassistance

    Note: A person may apply for financial assistance prior to obtaining a Qld driver licence with an ‘I’ condition. In these circumstances, if the person meets all of the other criteria, they will be conditionally approved, however funding will not be provided until the person obtains a Qld driver licence with an ‘I’ condition and is granted final approval.

    Refer: Conditional Approval below.

Application RequirementsThe applicant must:
  • Complete and submit an Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program – Financial Assistance Scheme Application Form (F4899) (available from DLIC’s or the TMR website)
  • Provide witnessed copies of supporting documentation where necessary (Refer: Application (document) requirements checklist below)
Application Requirements ChecklistWhen an application is lodged in a CSC, the operator must complete the below checklist template and email the application and supporting documents to the AIIP.IPU

Note: This checklist is only required for the initial application. If a customer presents to a CSC with additional information or amendments, they can be emailed to the Interlock Processing Unit.
Application ProcessThe person may submit the application:
  • In person at a Driver Licensing Issuing Centre (DLIC), or
  • By mail to Interlock Processing Unit.
Further InformationThe Chief Executive may request further information that is reasonably believed to be required to make a decision about the application. The request will be made in writing and will allow the applicant at least 28 days to provide the information.

If the applicant fails to provide the requested information within the stated time frame the Chief Executive may declare that the application is withdrawn. If the application is withdrawn the person will receive a written notice stating the reasons for the application being withdrawn. The applicant may reapply for financial assistance.

If the applicant presents further information to a CSC, it can be forwarded via email to the IPU (no checklist is required).
Assessment of ApplicationIn deciding whether to grant financial assistance to the person, the Chief Executive must ensure the eligibility criteria are met. TMR will decide whether to grant financial assistance within 28 days, following which the customer will be notified in writing of the outcome.
Application Status EnquiriesIf the customer enquires at a Driver Licence Issuing Centre about the status of their application for financial assistance staff should firstly telephone the Interlock Processing Unit to confirm the application status. If unable to be resolved, take the customers details and send an email to the IPU.
Application GrantedIf an application is approved the applicant will receive one of the following depending on their driver licence status at time of approval.

Applicant holds a Qld driver licence endorsed with an ‘I’ condition
Applicant holds a Qld driver licence endorsed with an ‘I’ condition
    The person will receive a written approval notice and a financial assistance ‘Authorisation Certificate’. The ‘Authorisation Certificate’ will state the approval date and the level of assistance being provided.

    The person will need to contact an approved interlock provider to arrange for the required interlock services. The approved interlock provider will verify the person’s approval with the IPU prior to any services being performed. The ‘Authorisation Certificate’ must be presented to the approved interlock provider before any interlock services will be performed under the scheme.

    The ‘Authorisation Certificate’ is valid for 42 days, if the person does not enter into an arrangement with an approved interlock provider for the required interlock services within that time, the approval will be void. To be eligible for financial assistance the person will need to reapply.
Applicant does not hold a Qld driver licence endorsed with an ‘I’ condition
Applicant does not hold a Qld driver licence endorsed with an ‘I’ condition
    Conditional approval may be granted to an applicant who fulfils all other eligibility criteria but who has not obtained a Qld driver licence endorsed with an ‘I’ condition. This conditional approval is granted to provide some assurance that if the person undertakes the expense of re-obtaining their driver licence with the ‘I’ condition they will be provided with assistance under the scheme.

    Once the person has obtained their driver licence they will be issued with an approval notice and a financial assistance ‘Authorisation Certificate’.

    Conditional approval is valid for 42 days, if the person does not obtain their Qld driver licence with an ‘I’ condition within that time, the approval will be void. To be eligible for financial assistance the person will need to reapply.
Application RefusedIf the application is refused the person will receive a written notice stating the decision and the reasons for the decision
When does it CeaseFinancial assistance under the scheme will cease if the:
  • person has utilised their allocation of assistance (ie. interlock fitment, maintenance for the equivalent of 12 months, and removal);
  • person is disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence by order of an Australian court;
  • person’s driver licence is suspended;
  • person’s interlock period ends;
  • person advises the department that they no longer require financial assistance; or
  • person is no longer eligible for financial assistance due to a change in their circumstances.

Once a person has received financial assistance, they will be ineligible for further assistance for a period of five years from the date the approval was granted.
Amendments
Amendments
Amendments
    If the person’s circumstances change and they subsequently require a change to their financial assistance, they may apply for an amendment.

    These changes are limited to those that are beyond the person’s control.

    For example, the person is moving residence and the interlock provider they currently have an arrangement with does not offer services in the new location. In this instance the person may apply for additional financial assistance to have the current provider’s interlock removed and the new provider’s interlock installed.

    Amendments will not be granted if the person simply chooses to have the interlock removed and reinstalled at a later date, or if they are required to maintain their interlock arrangement for longer than initially covered under the financial assistance due to an extension of prescribed period.

    If the person requires an amendment they must request one in writing. Their financial situation will be reassessed and as such they will need to complete the application form and provide all supporting documentation.

    These requests do not require:
    • A fee
    • TICA processing by the driver licence issuing centre

    The person may submit the request:
    • In person at a Driver Licence Issuing Centre (DLIC) and forwarded to the IPU via email (no checklist required), or
    • By mail to:

      Interlock Processing Unit
      Department of Transport and Main Roads
      GPO Box 1412
      Brisbane Qld 4001
