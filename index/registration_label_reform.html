Registration Label Reform (5050)

Project NameRegistration Label Reform
WhenCustomers with light, special purpose, conditionally registered vehicles and dealer/trade plates will no longer have to attach a registration label to their vehicle from 1 October 2014.
BackgroundFrom 1 October 2014, it will no longer be a requirement to:
  • attach a label to a vehicle being registered
  • remove old labels and attach new ones for each renewal
  • request replacements (for example, due to a broken windscreen).
  • issue a vehicle registration certificate following a registration renewal payment (if no changes were made with the payment).

This initiative does not apply to heavy vehicles (over 4.5 tonne GVM - excluding Special Purpose Vehicles and conditionally registered vehicles) or recreational vessels.

An initial flyer about this change commenced being sent to customers on 23 September 2013 with their registration label (this insert ceased on 22 September 2014). From 22 August 2014, an updated flyer outlining the final changes commenced being sent to customers with their vehicle registration notice. This will continue being sent for 12 months.

TMR has implemented an online service (www.qld.gov.au/checkrego) and an app (available for free download from iTunes or Google Play) that allows customers to check a QLD registered vehicle’s :
  • Expiry date
  • VIN
  • Description
  • Purpose of use
  • Registration Status
  • LAM indicator
Who will be impacted and how?Operators of:
  • cars
  • motorcycles
  • motorised wheel chairs
  • light trailers
  • light caravans
  • light buses
  • light commercial vehicles
  • light campervans
  • conditionally registered vehicles (both light and heavy)
  • special purpose vehicles (both light and heavy)
  • dealer/trade plates

From 1 October 2014, the above vehicles will be defined as "non-labelled vehicles". All other vehicles will be defined as "labelled vehicles".
Vehicle Registration Renewal Notices
Vehicle Registration Renewal Notices
    The Vehicle Registration Renewal and Inspectable Vehicle Registration Renewal Notices (F4087 and F4089) have been:
    • amended to include registration certificate vehicle details.
    • renamed as Vehicle Registration Notice (F4087) and Inspectable Vehicle Registration Notice (F4089).

    The amended notices will     be issued from 22 August 2014 for vehicles with registration commencement dates from 1 October 2014. The notices will continue to be sent approximately 4 weeks before due dates, as per the existing process.

    For common due date vehicles, the Vehicle Registration Renewal     (Common Due Dates) and Inspectable Vehicle Registration Renewal Notices (Common Due Dates) (F4103 and F4104) will remain unchanged at this time. For these vehicles, registration certificates will continue to be issued upon renewal payment.

Registration Certificates
Registration Certificates
    Vehicle registration certificates will no longer be automatically issued to vehicles not receiving a label following renewal payment, unless changes were made with the payment (e.g. CTP change). Registration certificates will still be issued for other transactions such as:
    • New Business
    • Transfer
    • Renewal when a registration period is varied
    • Renewal of common due date vehicles
    • Vehicle changes

    Replacement registration certificates will continue to be available upon request (including online).

Producing the Registration Certificate for New Vehicle or Transfer of Registration
Producing the Registration Certificate for New Vehicle or Transfer of Registration
    It will no longer be a requirement to produce a registration certificate when registering or transferring a vehicle. Forms have been amended to reflect this change.

Interim Labels
Interim Labels
    There is no change; Interim labels will continue to be used at this stage.

Concession Labels
Concession Labels
    Only labelled vehicles with a concession will require concession labels.

    Concession labels will be issued as follows:
    • Standard labels (concession labels will no longer be printed with a striped border)
    • If a labelled vehicle has a concession with restricted use, the words "Concession for Restricted Use" will be printed on the label body.
Forms
Forms
    The wording surrounding registration renewal notices, certificates and labels is being updated on a number of forms to align with this initiative.
