Managing the Inspection (7601)

Applies to
Applies to
    • Vehicles entering the inspection site must not proceed past the Stop signage or the entrance to the inspection area.
    • Check the vehicle’s registration against the Daily Worksheet for accuracy and, if correct, indicate the vehicles attendance next to the relevant entry on the worksheet.
1
Late Arrivals
Late Arrivals
    • If the vehicle arrives substantially late for inspection, the inspector may decide not to conduct the inspection. Consideration should be given to:
      • the vehicle type and its condition,
      • the current inspection workload,
      • inconvenience to other customers,
      • prior history of late arrival,
      • extenuating circumstances.
    • The decision not to conduct an inspection the customer has paid for, should not be made lightly and the Inspector must be able to justify their decision.
    • If the inspection cannot be carried out, the driver is to be advised that the inspection will not proceed and a new booking must be made and paid for.
    • Advise the driver the owner may apply in writing to the Senior Transport Inspector for the new booking fees to be waived.
    • Note the actual arrival time and the inspectors ID against the relevant entry on the Daily Worksheet.
2
Accessing suitability of drivers clothing
Accessing suitability of drivers clothing
    • Assess whether the driver is wearing suitable clothing and footwear for the safe conduct of the inspection. The inspector should pay particular attention to the vehicle driver’s footwear and consider its suitability for the safe operation of the vehicle through the inspection process e.g. thongs, or similar, which may become entangled in foot pedals must not be worn. A driver not wearing suitable clothing or footwear is to be advised that they must do so for future inspections.
    • For further guidance of appropriate footwear, Refer: WHS PO18 – Procedure – Selection, Issue and Use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), provides the minimum mandatory PPE requirement to visit a TMR inspection facility.
3
Safety advice to drivers
Safety advice to drivers
    • Ask the driver if they have taken a vehicle through the inspection process, including use of the brake testing equipment, at the facility and when this last occurred.
    • If the driver hasn't taken a vehicle through an inspection process, explain the process and requirements to the driver.
    • Advise the driver or any other person present who is associated with the vehicle:
      • The driver must be in the drivers seat when the engine is running.
      • They are not permitted to enter the inspection pit.
      • They are not to step across the open inspection pit.
      • They are not to stand on or walk across the brake rollers.
      • Persons associated with the vehicle, other than the vehicle driver, are to either remain in the vehicle cabin or wait in the designated waiting area.
4
Driver preparation for inspection
Driver preparation for inspection
    To make the most efficient use of the allocated inspection time, the driver should:
    • Bus
      • Locate and fasten all seat belts and restraints ready for inspection.
    • WAT
      • Passenger transport vehicles modified to carry a wheelchair should have any lift equipment, restraints, straps and anchor points assembled and ready for inspection.
    • Trailers
      • Relevant to site SOP, trailers should be uncoupled and any covering removed, if necessary, to allow for inspection of tow couplings. Refer: NHVR VSG4 Inspection of draw bar eye
    • If present during uncoupling of vehicles the inspector must ensure the vehicle area is clear of people e.g. other inspectors, prior to the vehicles being moved. Inspectors are not to uncouple or re-couple vehicles.
