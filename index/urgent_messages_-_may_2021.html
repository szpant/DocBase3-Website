31/05/2021 - Suspension of RTO Zero Harm Providing Multicombination Licence Training and Assessment

    The Registered Training Organisation (RTO) Zero Harm Number 32146 has voluntarily suspended all multicombination licence training and assessment under the unit TLIC4006 Drive Multicombination Vehicle.

    If a customer presents a Statement of Attainment (SOA) from RTO Zero Harm Number 32146 for course number TLIC4006 with an issue date on or after Sunday 30 May 2021 the SOA not to be accepted or recognised. The customer will not be able to upgrade their licence to an MC.

28/05/2021 - Procedure to Follow Before Signing into TICA on Monday 31 May 2021
    Registration and Licensing Systems are conducting an application release this evening, 28 May 2021. From time to time, changes impacting TICA have triggered intermittent issues with TICA post deployment.

    To avoid these issues, we advise you carry out the following procedure before logging into TICA on 31 May 2021.

    It is recommended to clear your cache (in Internet Explorer).

    To clear your cache please follow the below process:

    1. Open Internet Explorer (If you are using Windows 10, please note that it must be Internet Explorer and not Edge. To find Internet Explorer, click on the windows button in the start bar and type in Internet Explorer)

    2. Select 'Tools' from the menu line (or alt X on keyboard when you are in IE) and then 'Internet options'



    3. Choose to 'Delete' Browsing History



    4. Please uncheck the ‘Preserve Favourites website data’ box and then check the boxes as per below in the example. Once completed click ‘delete’ and this will return you to the Internet Option page and click ‘OK’.



    5. Repeat step 2 and select 'Tools' from the menu line (or alt X on keyboard when you are in IE) and then 'Internet options'



    6. Click 'Settings' button



    7. Click 'View files' button as below



    8. Upon clicking View files the windows explorer will launch with directory location on your local machine containing the temporarily cached files. Please verify the directory location looks like the below screenshot " <<logged in username>>\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows\INetCache"



    9. Select All temporary files within the above directory location. You can use your mouse to select all files or you can select one file on the right and click "Ctrl +A" combination to select all files. Once all files within the directory are selected, please click 'Delete' key on your keyboard to delete the files. You will be prompted with a Yes/No dialogue box to confirm, please verify the message looks similar to below screenshot and click 'Yes' to delete the temporary files.



    10. Close ALL IE windows, then only launch TICA

    If, after following these instructions, you are still unable to access TICA, please contact the Service Centre on 3066 2000.

26/05/2021 - Safety Alert VIS Trailers
26/05/2021 - Safety Alert VIS Trailers

    Issue 1
    Hydraulic motor mounting bolts
    Mounting bolts have come loose and sheared off that attach the hydraulic motor to the gearbox to raise and lower the axle on two VIS trailers.
    When the bolts sheared, the hydraulic motor twisted causing the steel hydraulic lines to snap resulting a hydraulic failure.
    This allowed the axle to drop uncontrolled from approximately 3 meters.
    Approximately 20 litres of hydraulic was spilt on the ground creating environmental concerns.

    Action required
    When raising and lowering the axle use low engine speed only.
    Check to ensure the bolts have spring washers and are tight, that attach the hydraulic motor to the gearbox.
    When the unit is next serviced:
    • have the bolts replaced
    • ensure the bolts are of sufficient length to properly secure the hydraulic motor to the gear box
    • ensure the bolts are Loctited in.

    On-going regularly checks to ensure the bolts are tight.

    Refer photos 1,2,3,4,5 and 6 below:

    Hydraulic motor mounting bolt photo 1
    Hydraulic motor mounting bolt photo 2
    Hydraulic motor mounting bolt photo 3
    Hydraulic motor mounting bolt photo 4
    Hydraulic motor mounting bolt photo 5
    Hydraulic motor mounting bolt photo 6

    Issue 2
    Vehicle lift jack mounting bolts
    It has been identified that the mounting bolts that secure the vehicle lift jack into the frame of the trailer have come loose and may be excessively worn.
    If the bolts come loose and the jack falls while the trailer is in transit the consequence could be catastrophic, causing serve damage to the trailer and a possible accident.

    Action required
    When setting up the trailer, check to ensure the nuts and bolts securing the vehicle lift jack to the trailer frame are tight and have sufficient tread protruding through the nut.
    When the unit is serviced have the technician check the bolts and mounting points for excessive wear.

    Refer photos 7 and 8 below:

07/05/2021 - Reminder - QCIS Update Instructions
07/05/2021 - Reminder - QCIS Update Instructions

    Please follow the below process before Saturday 8 May 2021. From Sunday 9 May 2021 the existing QCIS will cease to operate.

    1. Finalise any work in QCIS and log out. All drafts will be lost.
    a. Log out of QCIS

    b. Make sure you get this message.

    2. Close all Safari windows. Please ensure you have finished your work in Safari first.
    a. Open Safari

    b. Press and hold the two squares in the top right until a menu appears. Then select Close All Tabs.

    c. Press Close All Tabs again when it asks if you are sure.

    d. Double tap the iPad button and swipe up on Safari so it closes.

    3. Install the new version of QCIS
    a. Open the TMR app catalogue
    or

    b. Find the GREEN QCIS (version 2.1.5) and press install

    c. Confirm installation if prompted, otherwise, the install button will turn grey.

    d. Press the iPad button (or close TMR apps) to get back to your apps screen. The new app will look like this when it is installing.


    4. Delete the old BLUE QCIS
    a. Press and hold the blue QCIS and select delete app

    b. Confirm the deletion by pressing delete on the pop up.
    5. Open the new green QCIS and log in using your TMR username and password. If you receive the below message, press ok then try to log in again. Motorola are now aware of the error message and will work to resolve it following this release.

    6. If you can log in and work as usual then install process is completed.

    7. If you receive a message saying you need to trust Motorola then please complete the following steps. You do not need to complete these steps if you have successfully logged into the new green QCIS.
    a. Open settings

    b. Select General > Device Management

    c. Select Motorola

    d. Click on the “Trust Motorola”
    e. Close settings
