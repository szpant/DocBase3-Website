Licensing Show Cause
Licensing Show Cause
    What it isIf the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel a person's Qld driver licence, the person must be given written notice to invite the person to show cause, within a specified time, why the proposed action should not be taken. (TO(RUM-DL) 2010 s124)
    Grounds
    Grounds for Show Cause
    Grounds for Show Cause
      The following are the only grounds for show cause:
      Ground 1 -The licensee has a mental or physical incapacity that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely
      Ground 2 -The licence was obtained on the basis of information that the licensee knew was false or misleading in a material particular
      Ground 3 -The licensee has contravened a condition of the licence
      Ground 4 -The licensee has been disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence in another Country
      Ground 5 -The licensee has obtained a non-Qld driver licence
      Ground 6 -The licensee no longer resides in Queensland
      Ground 7 -The licensee no longer has a special need for a class HC Learner licence
      Ground 8 - The licensee was ineligible for the renewal of the licence by electronic communication
      Ground 9 - The person was not eligible for the class or type of licence
    Notifying TMRTMR may receive advice/documentation from the following persons about a Qld licence holder, who in their opinion grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel the person's driver licence. Documents may be lodged at any TMR CSC or QPS.

    Decision to Show Cause
    No Reasonable Grounds for Show Cause
    No Reasonable Grounds for Show Cause
      If the written advice/documentation received does not satisfy the chief executive that reasonable grounds exist to show cause the licensee, then the chief executive may take no further action, or may communicate further with the informant.
    Reasonable Grounds for Show Cause
    Reasonable Grounds for Show Cause
      If the written documentation satisfies the chief executive that reasonable grounds exist to show cause the person’s driver licence, then show cause action should commence. Refer: The How To.. > Show Cause

    Re-apply after cancellation
    Show Cause Cancellation (not due to disqualification)
    Show Cause Cancellation (not due to disqualification)
      A customer is eligible to apply for the same class and type of licence previously held, provided it was held within the last five years.

      Where the customer’s most recent licence was cancelled on medical grounds as a result of a show cause procedure, a licence may not be issued without approval from the MCR Unit. The customer must present a current medical certificate recommending they are medically fit to drive.

      Refer: Driver Licence > 4. Medical Condition Reporting > Medical Certificates > When the health professional must be contacted

    Show Cause Cancellation (overseas disqualification)

Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program
Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program
    What it is
    Show Cause – Interlock Exemption
    Show Cause – Interlock Exemption
      If the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel a person's interlock exemption, the person must be given written notice to invite the person to demonstrate, within a specified time, why the proposed action should not be taken. TO(RUM) ACT 1995 s19
    Show Cause – Prescribed Period Extension
    Show Cause – Prescribed Period Extension
      If the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to extend an interlock driver’s prescribed period, the person must be given written notice to invite the person to demonstrate, within a specified time, why the proposed action should not be taken. TO(RUM) ACT 1995 s91V
    Grounds
    Grounds for Show Cause – Interlock Exemption
    Grounds for Show Cause – Interlock Exemption
      Ground 1The interlock exemption was granted on the basis of information that was false or misleading, or improper in another way.
      Ground 2The interlock exemption holder has contravened a condition of the exemption.
      Ground 3Public safety has been, or is likely to be, endangered because of the interlock exemption.
      Ground 4The interlock exemption holder’s circumstances have changed in a manner that had the circumstance existed at the time exemption was applied for, the exemption would not have been granted.
      Ground 5The interlock exemption holder has failed to comply with a restriction applying to the exemption.
      Ground 6TMR considers it necessary in the public interest.
    TO(RUM) ACT 1995 s18
    Grounds for Show Cause – Prescribed Period Extension
    Grounds for Show Cause – Prescribed Period Extension
      Ground 1The person drove their nominated vehicle without first providing the prescribed interlock with a specimen of breath.
      Ground 2The person drove their nominated vehicle when they knew, or ought reasonably to have known, that the prescribed interlock was not operating properly.
      Ground 3The person drove their nominated vehicle when they knew, or ought reasonably to have known, that the prescribed interlock had been tampered with, damaged, destroyed or removed.
    TO(RUM) ACT 1995 s91U

Young Driver Exemptions
    What it is
    Show Cause – Young Driver Exemptions
    Show Cause – Young Driver Exemptions
      If the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel a person's high powered vehicle or late night driving certificate of exemption, the person must be given written notice to invite the person to demonstrate, within a specified time, why the proposed action should not be taken. TO(RUM) DL Reg 2010 s74C
    Grounds
    Grounds for Show Cause – Young Driver Exemptions
    Grounds for Show Cause – Young Driver Exemptions
      Ground 1The certificate of exemption was granted on the basis of information that was false or misleading.
      Ground 2The certificate of exemption holder has contravened a restriction of the exemption.
      Ground 3The certificate of exemption holder's circumstances have changed in a manner that had the circumstance existed at the time exemption was applied for, the exemption would not have been granted.
      Ground 4TMR considers it necessary in the public interest.
    TO(RUM) DL Reg 2010 s74B
