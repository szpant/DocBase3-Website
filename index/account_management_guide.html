Dishonoured Payments
    Cheque Payments
    Cheque Payments
      If a customer pays a registration by cheque which becomes dishonoured, a 'Returned Cheque Notice' is mailed to the registered operator, advising that:
      • Payment was dishonoured
      • The outstanding amount and the dishonoured cheque admin fee
      • Registration will be cancelled by TMR unless the registered operator either pays again by cash/bank cheque/EFTPOS or applies for cancellation within 28 days of date on notice. (Further personal cheques cannot be used for payment unless approval is provided by CAM).

      If the customer has paid a registration renewal or New Business and has not honoured the payment or cancelled the vehicle within the allowed time frame Customer Account Management Unit cancels the vehicle under Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995. All other registration transactions will be referred to a Debt Collection Agency.

      Note:If the dishonoured payment was made prior to the expiry date then the second payment must be received by TMR before the expiry date or the RRRF will apply.

    Credit Card Payments
    Credit Card Payments
      If a customer pays a product with a credit card via the internet or over the counter at a customer service centre and the credit card holder disputes the transaction with their financial institution, the funds may be returned to the credit card holder. This is treated as a dishonoured payment resulting in the customer having a debt for the outstanding amount.

      Process for dealing with dishonoured credit card payments

      Direct Debit Payments - Online Direct Debit Registration Renewals (DDRR)
      Direct Debit Payments - Online Direct Debit Registration Renewals (DDRR)
        If a customer pays a registration renewal by DDRR which is then dishonoured:
        • an email; and
        • an SMS notification (where a mobile number is recorded) is sent advising to make payment by another method; and
        • the registration renewal is reversed

        If the registration is unable to be reversed, an Unsuccessful Direct Debit Notice (F5095) is mailed to the registered operator advising that:
        • Direct debit payment could not be processed.
        • The outstanding amount.
        • Registration will be cancelled by TMR unless the registered operator either pays again by cash/bank cheque/money order/EFTPOS or applies for cancellation within 28 days of the date on the notice.

        If the customer has not honoured the payment or cancelled the vehicle within the allowed time frame, Customer Account Management Unit cancels the vehicle under Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995.

        Note:
        • The second payment must be received by TMR before the expiry date or the RRRF will apply.
        • Any registration cancellations for the reason FAHP (failure to honour payment) should not be reversed by operators and all enquiries directed to CAM.

    Refunding Overpayments
      Overpayments of up to ten dollars ($10.00) are not refunded by TMR unless requested by the customer. This amount should remain in the customers account and will be adjusted weekly. Overpayments greater than $10.00 should be refunded to the customer, unless they are to be used for a future transaction. A Text message should show the use for these funds.
