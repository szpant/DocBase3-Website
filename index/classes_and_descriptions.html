Trailer
Trailer
    Class 24

    Vehicles covered:
    • Trailers with a GVM >4.5t for which a supplementary policy is sought

    CTP certificates presented for these trailers must be recorded in TICA.    		Class 25

    Vehicles recorded (class does not exist within MAIC Legislation - only used by TICA):
    • Trailers not covered by CTP Class 24

    This class was created for TMR use only.

    The Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994 establishes a scheme under which the insurer of a motor vehicle is the insurer of a trailer in certain situations.

    If a trailer is attached to a motor vehicle registered in Queensland or runs out of control after becoming accidentally detached from a motor vehicle registered in Queensland, the trailer must be regarded as part of the motor vehicle and the insurer of the motor vehicle is the insurer of the trailer but, in any other case, the insurer of a trailer registered in Queensland is the Nominal Defendant whose liability is to be decided as if the Nominal Defendant had issued a separate CTP insurance policy for the trailer.

    However, If a motor vehicle accident involving a trailer with a ATM of more than 4.5t happens outside the State of Queensland, the Nominal Defendant is NOT the insurer of the trailer, and

    If a CTP insurance policy (other than a supplementary policy e.g. Class 24) is issued for, or expressly extends to, a trailer, the insurer under the policy is the insurer of the trailer for all purposes. This may apply to interstate registered motor vehicles towing a trailer registered in Queensland.

    Trailers with class 24 CTP insurance are covered under that class.

    Customers are advised to contact the relevant insurer to verify cover.

Emergency & Other
Emergency & Other
    Class 15

    Vehicles covered:
    • Self-propelled machinery other than Class 14, 19, 20 and 21
    • Fire engines, bush fire brigade vehicles and other emergency vehicles (excluding ambulances)
    • State Emergency Service (SES) vehicles registered in the name of the Department of Community Safety or a local government council, with the purpose of use Emergency Vehicles (EMER), that are used solely for emergency work.


      Note: To be eligible for Class 15 CTP insurance, an emergency vehicle must be fitted with :
      • a repeater horn or siren; or
      • a flashing warning light

      And be driven by:
      • an officer of the Queensland Fire and Rescue Service; or
      • an officer or employee of an entity with written permission from the Commissioner of Police
      OR
      • SES vehicle as outlined above. These vehicles have been approved in consultation with MAIC.
