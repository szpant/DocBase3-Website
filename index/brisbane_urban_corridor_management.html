Brisbane Urban Corridor Management (7383)

Brisbane Urban Corridor (BUC) Management
What it isThe Brisbane Urban Corridor is the route between Archerfield and Wishart consisting of Mt Gravatt – Capalaba Road west of the Gateway Motorway, Kessels Road, Riawena Road and Granard Road.

Due to concerns around the volume of heavy vehicles ( Gross Vehicle Mass (GVM) greater than 4.5 tonne) using the Brisbane Urban Corridor (BUC), Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) enforcement will be reinstated along the corridor to identify through movements of heavy vehicles that do not have a local destination as part of their travel.

Existing ANPR cameras at :

  • Mount Gravatt–Capalaba Road (west of the Gateway Motorway)
  • Ipswich Motorway, Goodna
will be used to determine the travel time of heavy vehicles through the BUC.

Two new ANPR cameras and regulatory signage to be installed on Riawena Road, Salisbury at the existing gantry. This location provides an intermediate location for travel time and is the most feasible due to existing infrastructure availability and accessibility to the site, these additional ANPR cameras will be operational prior to the commencement of the trial.

The signage at Riawena Road includes additional wording to notify truck drivers of the restriction and advises that local destinations are excepted. The signage will be installed by the end of August 2018 and will remain covered until the commencement of the trial on 01 October 2018.

The existing signage on Ipswich Motorway at Goodna, and the Gateway Motorway at Wishart provides advisory information to truck drivers in advance of the decision points to access the BUC. These signs are to remain, however, regulatory components of these signs will be removed to align with road regulations

Trial periodA three-month trial period will commence from 01 October 2018, during the trial period warning letters will be issued to the registered operators of heavy vehicles identified as using the BUC that do not have a local destination as part of their journey.
Post TrialAt the conclusion of the trial period, Infringement Notices will be issued to registered operators of heavy vehicles identified contravening the provisions referred to in section 104 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Road Rules)Regulation. Contravening s104 imposes a penalty of:
  • $130.55 (one penalty unit) and the accumulation of three demerit points for individuals
  • $652.75 (five penalty units) for corporations.
Procedure (trial)During the trial the following procedure will be used to identify and action heavy vehicles identified as using the BUC without have a local destination along the BUC as part of their journey.

Daily report
Daily report

    The Engineering and Technology Unit ( E & T) will send a daily report to DarraMVIC with details of heavy vehicles utilising the BUC that do not have a local destination along the BUC as part of their journey.


    Sample E&T Report BUC

    • Darra Compliance Administration Support Officers will action the reports on a daily basis.
Updating BUC Warning letter tracker
Updating BUC Warning letter tracker

    All vehicles recorded in the E & T report must be actioned by COB each day and added to the BUC Warning Letter Tracker spreadsheet.

    For each vehicle listed in the E&T report the following authentication checks must be carried out and recorded in the BUC Warning Letter Tracker spreadsheet prior to issuing a warning letter:

    • Confirm the registration number in TICA/TRAILS
    • Confirm the vehicle is a heavy vehicle, if it is not a heavy vehicle email advice to E & T cross reference authentication check with the TSDM database may need to be carried for any discrepancies noted.
    • Record registered owner details
    • Record the status of the registration and insurance NOTE – any vehicle identified as unregistered /uninsured must also be actioned follow existing ANPR detected procedures.
    • Record the date a warning letter is issued
    • Record the date a warning letter is sent
    • Record the DMS reference number

    BUC Warning Letter Tracker

    The BUC Warning Letter Tracker spreadsheet must be updated daily and sent to E & T at the commencement of each week for reporting purposes.

Issuing a warning letter
Issuing a warning letter

    Upon completion of all authentication checks, Compliance Administration Support Officers issue a warning letter to the registered operator for each vehicle subject of the E & T report.

    Warning letters must be filed in the Document Management System (DMS) in accordance with record keeping practices.
