Interception Sites (7490)

Interception Sites
What it is A site is any site selected for the interception of a vehicle or any other area designated as a "site" by the Regional Manager (Compliance) or Senior Transport Inspector.
Site Selection
Site Selection
Site Selection
    • Be large enough to accommodate the desired vehicle safely, which includes the safety of the intercepting officer. A site or safe place means a site/place where:
      • for a vehicle - the vehicle is not likely to be a hazard to road users; and
      • for a person - the person is not likely to be injured by road users.
    • Have clear visibility for at least 100 metres in both directions and the intercepted vehicle should not cause traffic congestion or hazard.
    • The area should be hard standing, reasonably level and of sufficient width to allow a vehicle to be clear of the carriageway.
    • The vehicle should not block access to driveways and/or business premises

    Heavy Vehicle Sites
    Heavy Vehicle Sites
      Selection of an interception site suitable for weighing heavy vehicles on portable equipment requires a more thorough process than normal requirements for safe interception areas.

      The following are the minimum requirements that must always be observed:
      • The area selected must be of hard standing and meet the safety standards of the "site safety plan".
      • The area selected must be free of stones, tree roots, pot holes or corrugations.
      • The procedures set out in the instruction Interception Procedures also apply.
      • Changes in grade can be tolerated provided areas of uniform grade are sufficient to accommodate the longest vehicle, including B Doubles (Road Trains in some areas), can be clearly identified.
      • Visibility to the start of the exit taper and the end of the entrance taper should be not less than the heavy vehicle stopping distance and should be carefully considered in the context of the ruling traffic volume and speed.
      • Where weigh sites are required for both sides of the road to cover each travel direction, they need not be located directly opposite each other, although this is desirable due to the logistics of providing personnel for both directions.
      • Concrete pads with nominal dimensions of 40m long x 5.0m wide are required for effective weighing of multi-combination vehicles.

    Load Transfer Considerations
    Load Transfer Considerations
      If the surface of weighing area is not even, weighing of vehicle axle groups will result in incorrect readings because of axle mass transfer know as axle attitude.

      Axle attitude or mass transfer is produced when one axle of a group of axles is not weighed at the same height as the remaining axles of that group. It occurs when the wheel or axle being weighed is in a depression or up on capstone or tree roots, etc. Not blocking all axles to the same height has the same effect.

      There are several conditions and factors that cause variations and load transfers between axle groups. These are:
      • Axle groups within 3 meters not raised to uniform height throughout the length of the vehicle
      • Unlevel and rough weighing sites
      • Friction in suspension systems (Hysteresis)
      • Manipulation of brakes by driver
      • Malfunction of scales. Weighing heavy or light, etc.
Weigh Site Categories
Weigh Site Categories
Weigh Site Categories
    The category of the weigh site effects the Measurement Adjustment (MA) applied when assessing vehicles for compliance with mass and dimension requirements.

    Interception sites used for weighing heavy vehicles are classified into three categories, 1, 2 or 3 based on the specifications set out in the Austroads Guideline 2005.
    • Weigh sites that include a weighbridge facility will generally meet the physical characteristics required to be classified as a Category 1 site.
    • Purpose built roadside weigh sites will generally meet the physical characteristics required to be classified as a Category 1 or 2 site.
    • Mobile intercept sites generally meet the physical characteristics required to be classified as a Category 3 site.

    All weigh sites, including weigh bridges and axle platforms must be assessed to ensure they comply with the requirements for a Cat 1,2 or 3 weigh site. Transport Inspectors can carry out an assessment of a weigh site to ensure it meets the specifications for a Cat 3 weigh site.

    Refer: Weigh Site Requirements below
Site Assessment
Assessment for Category 3 Weigh Sites
Assessment for Category 3 Weigh Sites
    A weigh site assessment can be conducted using a tape measure manufactured to EC Class III, (Refer: Measurement equipment certification - Standard, Certified Tape Measures) string line and a line level. All sections including, approach, weighing area and departure must be measured.

    Refer: Weigh Site Requirements

    Evidence of Site Assessment
    Evidence of Site Assessment
      When the interception site has been assessed the Transport Inspectors that measured the site are required to provide statements verifying the site was measured and meet the criteria for a Cat 3 weigh site. This will negate the requirement to measure the site every time the site is used.

      Evidence of the site assessment should be captured in DMS and the Interception Site SharePoint site.
      Evidence includes:
      • photographs or video of the site assessment
      • drawings, plans and note book entries
      • statements

      ROAMS, the site safety plan and the SOP must also be updated.
Weigh Site Requirements
Elements of a Weigh Site
Elements of a Weigh Site
    There are three sections of a weigh site be assessed to determine the category:
    • the weighing area (A)
    • the approach wheel paths (B)
    • the departure wheel paths (C)



    The 1m gap does not have to conform with the requirements as the vehicles’ wheels will not go here.

    The minimum width for all sections of a weigh site is 3.5, which can include a 1m gape in the centre.

    Weighing Area
    Weighing Area
      The weighing area is the area in which portable scales are used, or the area of the weighing platform(s)

      Length:

      The Minimum length for a weighing area is 5m, however the preferred lengths for the following types of vehicles are:
      • 6.0m for most prime movers
      • 7.0m for twin steer prime movers
      • 11.0m for most rigid vehicles
      • 13.0m for restricted access buses and special purpose vehicles.

      These lengths will generally allow the vehicle to be weighed in a single step given sufficient portable scales.

      There is no maximum length limit

      The following limits apply for a Cat 3 site:

      Longitudinal Gradient
      Transverse Gradient
      Unevenness over 5m
      5% up to 250mm
      Up to 175mm
      50mm

    Approach Wheel Paths
    Approach Wheel Paths
      The approach wheel path is the portion of the roadway that the
      vehicle's wheels move along before they reach the weighing area.

      The minimum length of the combined approach wheel path and weighing area should be 14m.

      The following limits apply for a Cat 3 site:

      Longitudinal Gradient
      Transverse Gradient
      Unevenness over 5m
      5% up to 700mm
      5% more than 175mm
      100mm

    Departure Wheel Paths
    Departure Wheel Paths
      The departure wheel path is the portion of the roadway that the vehicle's
      wheels move along after the weighing area.

      The minimum length of the combined weighing area and departure wheel path should be 14m.

      The following limits apply for a Cat 3 site:

      Longitudinal Gradient
      Transverse Gradient
      Unevenness over 5m
      5% up to 700mm
      no % more than 175mm
      150mm

    Site Maintenance
    Maintenance
    Maintenance
      Any issues identified during the site inspection must be reported to the STI.
      Photograph any damage and send to the STI via email.
      ROAMS and Interception Sites, SharePoint site contain information and documentation about each interception site, including:
      • Site Safety Plan
      • SOP
      • Weigh Bridge/Axle platform calibration certificate and dates
      • Site survey result and dates
      • Interception site category
      • Photos of the site and signage

      ROAMS
      Interception Sites SharePoint

      Example: If the speed limit signs have been changed at the interception site to 80km/h and ROAMS has the speed limit at 100km/h.
      STIs can report any damage or request maintenance to an interception site via ROAMS - Work Orders.
      STIs are responsible to ensure information for each interception site is current.
