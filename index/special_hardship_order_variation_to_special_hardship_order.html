Special Hardship Order/Variation to Special Hardship Order (4552)

Special Hardship Order
What it isA Special Hardship Order (SHO) is a court order which allows a driver whose licence has been suspended to continue driving under restricted circumstances. A person whose Provisional or Open licence is suspended for exceeding demerit points on a GDB or for a high speed offence and who meets eligibility criteria may apply.

If granted, the person is required to be issued with a replacement licence with an X3 condition code. This licence authorises the holder to drive only in the restricted circumstances stated on the court order.
These restrictions include (but are not limited to):
  • purpose for which a motor vehicle may be driven under the licence;
  • class of motor vehicle that may be driven under the licence;
  • times at which or period of time during which a motor vehicle may be driven under the licence; and
  • a requirement to drive a motor vehicle only while carrying a copy of the SHO order.

There is no SHO option for learner or probationary licence holders.

As of 1 July 2019, any driver subject to a SHO has a zero demerit point allowance. The accumulation of any demerit points will result in a licence suspension for double the original suspension period, regardless of time served prior to applying for the SHO.

For drivers granted a SHO prior to 1 July 2019 (regardless of whether their suspension period extends beyond 1 July 2019), the prior demerit point allowance of 3 points will still apply.

    Note:While a person may be authorised to continue driving a class of motor vehicle under their Queensland driver licence the person is not authorised, by law, to learn to drive any class of vehicle while they hold that order.
    s5(9) TO(RUM-DL) Regs 2010

Who Can Apply
Who Can Apply
Who Can Apply
    An eligible holder of a provisional or open Qld licence (as outlined below) who has been suspended because they:
    • have received 2 or more demerit points when driving under an agreement to be of good driving behaviour (GDB) for 1 year; or
    • have been convicted of an offence of driving more than 40 km/h over the speed limit.

    Note:For a customer who is applying for a SHO in relation to a high speed offence, it is important to note that if the customer has a pending Accumulation of Demerit Points Notice to Choose that a GDB must be nominated for the demerit points notice or they will be ineligible for a SHO. The GDB should be applied prior to the commencement date of the high speed suspension period.

    Refer: Driver licence > 3. Enforcement > The How to: Enforcement > Enforcement Procedures (Licensing) > Maintain Application for Special Hardship Order/Restricted Licence

    Note:If the customer's driver licence has been suspended for driving more than 40km/h over the speed limit, as well as being suspended for gaining two or more demerit points when subject to a GDB, both suspensions may be considered by the court together.


    The customer must lodge their application along with their supporting evidence (for example, affidavit from their employer or doctor) at the correct Magistrates Court at any time during the suspension period.

    Eligibility to apply for a SHO
    Eligibility to apply for a SHO
      A person may be eligible to apply for a SHO if:
      • their suspended licence is a Qld provisional or open licence; and
      • the application relates to a licence that is the same class as their suspended licence.

      A person's eligibility to apply for a SHO is not affected by:
      • a suspension because they did not appear in court to answer a charge of drink or drug driving
      • an immediate licence suspension under s79B(2) of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 because of an alleged drink or drug driving offence
      • a 24 hour suspension because of a drink or drug driving related matter
      • a suspension or cancellation of their driver licence that was set aside under s132(4) of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Driver Licensing) Regulation 2010
      • a suspension, cancellation or disqualification that was set aside on appeal other than a demerit points suspension or a high speed suspension
      • a suspension or cancellation because of a mental or physical incapacity
      • a suspension under s105 of the State Penalties Enforcement Act 1999
      • a suspension or cancellation that was set aside on a review by QCAT.
    Ineligible to apply for a SHO
    Ineligible to apply for a SHO
      A person is not eligible if, within the last five years before making the application:
      • their Qld driver licence had been previously cancelled by a court orsuspended due to demerit points or a high speed suspension*

      * Note:This includes any previous SHO's that have been granted in the last 5 years.
      • they have been disqualified from holding or obtaining a Qld driver licence
      • their authority to drive on Qld roads under a non-Qld driver licence previously held has been suspended due to demerit points or a high speed suspension
      • they were made ineligible to apply for a Queensland driver licence because they exceeded their demerit point threshold, or were convicted of driving more than 40km/h over the speed limit, at a time when they were unlicensed
      • they have been convicted of operating a motor vehicle dangerously.

Grounds
Grounds for Application
Grounds for Application
    The person needs to satisfy the court of all of the following:
    • They are a fit and proper person to continue to drive, having regard to the safety of other road users and the public generally
    • A refusal to grant a Special Hardship Order would
      • Cause extreme hardship to the person, or the person's family by depriving them of their means of earning a living and/or
      • Cause severe & unusual hardship for the person or their family other than depriving the person of their means of earning a living
Time Limits
Time Limits for Application
Time Limits for Application
    The licensee must lodge the application and supporting evidence at the correct Magistrates Court at any time during the suspension period.

    They must also give TMR or an approved issuing office a copy of the court approved application including all supporting evidence at least seven days prior to the date of the court hearing (or, if the hearing is within 8 days after the day the application is lodged with the court, as soon as practicable but before the day of the hearing). This allows for time for the Prosecutions Unit to prepare for the hearing.
How to Apply
How To Apply
How To Apply
    Customer:
    • completes a Special Hardship Order Application (F4401) obtained from the Magistrates Court or TMR
    • lodges the form along with their supporting evidence with the correct court after their licence has been suspended. The correct court is:
      • If they reside in the Brisbane Magistrates Court District - a court in the division of the Brisbane Magistrates Court District in which they reside;
      • If they reside in any other Magistrates Court District - a court in that district.

    Note:A fee is payable to the Magistrates Court when lodging this application. The customer should be directed to their nearest Magistrates Court for more information about the fee or court divisions/districts.


    Note:Applicants should take a copy of their Notice of Driver Licence Suspension with them when lodging their application.

    Lodges a copy of the court approved application together with the supporting documents to a Department of Transport and Main Roads Customer Service Centre or approved licence issuing agent as soon as practicable.
    The suspension recorded against the applicants driver licence will be stayed by TMR to allow them to continue to drive until the day before the date of their court hearing.

    Supporting Evidence
    Supporting Evidence
      The supporting evidence is a copy of what the applicant provided to the Magistrates Court to support their SHO application and may include:
      • Signed affidavits
      • Signed affidavits from Employers
      • Bank statements

      An affidavit is a legal document used to detail facts about a matter. For a SHO application, it must be completed by the applicant and include supporting evidence and information that they intend to rely on for the application, for example:
      • the hours that a vehicle is needed to be driven for work purposes
      • there is no other means of transport reasonably available to get to and from work
      • they are the only person available to drive a motor vehicle to take a family member with a medical condition or physical incapacity to a place to receive regular and necessary medical treatment.
      An affidavit can be obtained from a Magistrates court, Aust Post or from a legal representative. It must be signed and witnessed in the presence of a JP or Commissioner of Declarations.

      Clearly legible supporting documents are to be scanned and forwarded daily to prosecutions by email to Prosecutions Unit (SHO Processing Section).
      If the documentation is unable to be legibly scanned, supporting documents are to be placed and sealed in an envelope and forwarded by the CSO to the Prosecutions Unit (SHO Processing Section)
      RSSM Offence Management will then forward the applicant's traffic history to the relevant Magistrates Court
Decision
Decision
Decision
    If the court grants a Special Hardship Order, an Order is issued stating the restricted conditions in which the person can drive. The person must return to TMR to get a replacement licence.

    The operator must record the result of the Special Hardship Order application after determination.
    Upheld
    The person will return to TMR with a copy of their court approved Special Hardship Order.

    A replacement licence must be applied for and a fee is charged. The replacement licence will be issued with an X3 condition code.
    Dismissed/Withdrawn
    If the appeal is dismissed or withdrawn the suspension will start on the hearing date, or the date the court accepts the withdrawal notification. Any time served on the suspension before lodging the appeal will be deducted from the suspension.
Replacement
Replacement
Replacement
    If a replacement Court Order is required the person must apply to the court that granted the SHO.
Variation to Special Hardship Order
What It IsIf a persons’ circumstances change and they are required to drive outside the Special Hardship Order restrictions, they may make application to a Magistrates Court for a variation of the terms and conditions on a previously granted SHO. The Magistrates Court will only grant a variation if there are sufficient grounds to justify a variation to the restrictions.
Who Can Apply
Who Can Apply
Who Can Apply
    Provisional and Open licence holders who are issued with a Special Hardship Order.
Grounds
Grounds for Application
Grounds for Application
    The court may vary the restrictions only if the court:
    • has considered the impact of the changed restrictions; and
    • considers the justice of the case requires it to vary the restrictions
How To Apply
How to Apply
How to Apply
    Form
    Person completes A Variation of Special Hardship Order (F4375) obtained from the Magistrates Court or TMR

    Lodge (Court)
    The form must be lodged at the Magistrates Court in the division
    or district were the person resides:
    • Together with any supporting documents (copies of documents provided to the Magistrates Court eg. Signed affidavit)

    Lodge (TMR)
    There is no recording of the court date in TICA.

    A copy of the court approved application together with the supporting documents must be served to a Department of Transport and Main Roads Customer Service Centre or approved licence issuing agent.
    • with in 7 days (or as close as practicable) before the date of the court hearing.

    This documentation is forwarded by the CSO to the Prosecutions Unit (SHO Processing Section)

    Decision
    If the court grants a Special Hardship Order an Order is issued stating the restricted conditions in which the person can drive.

    This must be carried at all times when driving. The varied conditions are not recorded in TICA.
