    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records
    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records

      Please be aware that the confidentiality of customer records is paramount at all times. The TMR policy Access to Customer Records provides clear direction to all employees not to take action to access a customer record unless the access is authorised.
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
        • There is a genuine “need to know” and the information is being used as part of the employee’s official duties and they would be hindered in their performance if they didn’t access the information;
        • The employee is entitled to access the information under law;
        • A customer has given permission for their login details to be used to access online services - this is for the customer contact centre/Help Direct/Online Support/Ebus/CAM only following completion of verbal EOI;
        • There is an official business transaction to be completed, including a transaction for a work colleague. The official business transaction must be done over the counter or via the call centre and the employee treated the same as any other customer (e.g. a ticket must be taken, full transaction requirements must be met). A SASM/Manager must also be informed to remove any perceived or potential conflict.

      Unauthorised access is:
      Unauthorised access is:
        • Accessing your own customer record;
        • Accessing customer records of friends or relatives (even if they have given permission) including online services if using a work computer;
        • Accessing a record where an actual, perceived or potential conflict of interest exists;
        • Accessing information because it would be convenient for a staff member to know;
        • Accessing a record on behalf of another TMR employee unless it is part of an official business transaction (e.g. contacting help direct or asking a colleague to access a record to assist with a transaction is acceptable);
        • Assisting a third party to access a customer’s record (e.g. mother calls and asks about an issue her family member is having with an online service and the employee assists the mother using the family member’s details).


      Please note: Customers contact the department in person, over the phone, via the internet or in writing. Transactions should be in line with your current role. If you are in doubt – ask your supervisor or manager.

      What can happen if I access a customer’s record inappropriately?

      Misuse of confidential information is a criminal offence under s408E Criminal Code. Unauthorised access and use of the information contained in this system may lead to criminal prosecution and/or disciplinary sanctions including:
      • Criminal prosecution;
      • Termination of employment;
      • Suspension from duties;
      • Reduction of classification level and a consequential change of duties;
      • Transfer or redeployment to other public service employment;
      • Forfeiture or deferment of a remuneration increment or increase;
      • Reduction of remuneration (increment) level;
      • A monetary penalty (which can be deducted from your pay);
      • A formal reprimand;
      • Record of disciplinary action - a permanent record of the incident on their file which they would need to disclose during pre-employment screening for all public sector roles.

      All staff are required to be familiar with the Access to Customer Records and Releasing Customer Information policies which are published in DocBase, available via the below links.

      Access to Customer Records policy summary v2
      Access to Customer Records Policy v5
      Releasing Customer Information

      Staff should also view the Access to Customer Records Policy page on SharePoint for more resources.

      For more information about access to customer records, please talk to your manager or supervisor.

    14/12/2020 - EOI - Representing Individual/Organisation - Updated
    14/12/2020 - EOI - Representing Individual/Organisation - Updated

      DocBase has been updated to provide an option where a customer presents to complete a transaction without the original EOI for a registered operator or plate owner.

      Strict rules and process steps have been included which cannot be deviated from and no managerial discretion is allowed. This is because TMR needs to maintain rigour in our process, ensure that the register contains accurate information to minimise risk of fraud. Maintaining rigour in the process puts all customers first and protects their information from misuse.

      Additionally, the Representing an Organisation or Trust legal entity page has been updated to link to the EOI Organisation Documents page. This was changed because there was confusion in the network about EOI requirements for persons representing organisations.

      When representing an organisation, EOI is required for the Representative AND the Organisation Registered Operator.
      If the Organisation Registered Operator is:
      • a Company, their EOI is "validation of ASIC record"
      • a business, their EOI is "validation of the ASIC/ABR record" AND EOI for the legal entity (either an individual or a company)

    11/12/2020 - Q-SAFE Queensland Driver Licence Assessment Driving Examiner Assessment Procedures Manual

      The Q-SAFE Queensland Driver Licence Assessment Driving Examiner Assessment Procedures Manual has been amended to clarify Vehicle Checks and Stop Light requirements.

      Minor changes have been made to the following sections:

        3.7.2 - Brake lights
        Testing vehicle brake lights must be appropriately fitted and in proper working order.
        Specifically, the brake lights fitted to any testing vehicle must:
        • Be affixed at the rear of the vehicle.
        • When lighted display a clear red light to the rear of the vehicle so that it will be visible at a distance of at least 60m by day or night.
        • When on, must show a red light visible 30m from the rear of the vehicle
          Be affixed as a pair for: so that the centre of the lamp is not higher than 1.5m from the level of the ground on which the vehicle stands and so that it, or where two or more such lamps one of them, is in the centre, or to the right-hand or offside of such vehicle.
        • A four wheel motor vehicle built after 1959
        • A motorbike built after 1959 with two wheels at the rear
        • A trailer built after 1973
        • If only 1 brake light is fitted to a vehicle, it must be fitted in the centre or to the right of centre of the vehicles rear
        • Have the centre of the light at least 350 mm and not more than 1500 mm above the ground
        • Be such that it will light when any service brake on the vehicle or combination of vehicles is applied.
      Note:
      • Any motorcycle which was first registered on or after 1 January 1962 must have at least one brake light that complies with the above requirements.
      • The rear window brake light of a motor vehicle does not have to be operational.
      • If any brake light is damaged and the light from the bulb is not showing a red light, the applicant may, as a temporary measure, cover the brake light fitting with a suitable material that will change the light from the bulb to a red colour.
      • In cases where multiple brake lights are fitted, a minimum of one brake light on each side of the vehicle must be operational.

    01/12/2020 - NHVR Driver Fatigue Work Procedure

      With the approval of two models of Electronic Work Diaries (EWDs) effective 1 December 2020 the NHVR has replaced the 'Work diary and driver fatigue assessment Work Procedure' with the 'Driver Fatigue Work Procedure'.

      The 'Driver Fatigue Work Procedure' contains instructions for both Written Work Diaries (WWDs) and Electronic Work Diaries (EWDs).

      TMR equivalent of terms used in the NHVR 'Driver Fatigue Work Procedure':
      • TMR Transport Inspectors are Safety Compliance Officers.
      • QCIS is the TMR equivalent of the 'Regulatory Compliance Mobile Solution' (RCMS)

      Additional information relating to 'Driver Fatigue Work Procedure':
      • the Offence Codes in DocBase list the PINs that can be issued. This may vary from the information in the work procedure
      • 5.12 of the work procedure contains instructions relating to sending EWD records to an email address. If you send EWD records to your work email address the driver will not be able see the email address.
      • 6.8.2 Commencement of counting time. When counting time, if a driver has not taken the required rest break relevant to their hours option, periods of 24 hours or more should be counted from the end of any rest break.
      • 6.11 Directions. A prohibition/direction notice may be given to the driver for a minor or substantial risk breach of work or rest requirements but must be issued for sever or critical risk breaches

      TMR DocBase policy over rules any conflict with NHVR work procedures.

      All Transport Inspectors and Seniors should review the amended policy.

    11/12/2020 - Blue Card - NOAC restrictions and suppressed records - Updated

      Where a School Crossing Supervisor (SCS) has a No Online Access (NOAC) restriction on their TMR record, they must attend a CSC to have the NOAC expired to allow them to access the Blue Card online service.
