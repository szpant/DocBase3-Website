Traffic Controller Guide (2460)



About a Traffic Controller Accreditation
Definitions
Definitions
Definitions
    Traffic controller
    Means a person who holds an appointment under part 2 as an accredited person with the function of a traffic controller.
    Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015

    TCASAP
    Means the document called Traffic Controller Accreditation Scheme Approved Procedure published by the department.
    Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015

    TCAS
    Means the Traffic Controller Accreditation Scheme.
    Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015

    Further Definitions
What it is
What it is
What it is
    A Traffic Controller is authorised under the Traffic Controller Accreditation Scheme.

    The scheme requires Traffic Controllers' to operate consistently, and ensures standards are met for:
    • Training
    • Traffic control operation
When required
When Required
When Required
    Any individual who controls traffic in Queensland, as part of their job at:
    • Worksites or other events where a road or part closure is necessary, or
    • Where traffic control is otherwise required on a roadway, must be accredited under the Traffic Controller Accreditation Scheme.
When not required
When not Required
When not Required
    The following individuals are exempt from being accredited:
    • Authorised Officers under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 (eg TMR Inspector, QPS)
    • Escort Vehicle Drivers accredited under Part 2 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015
    • Anyone instructed by a police officer to assist them directing or diverting traffic
    • A person operating or directed to operate under the Disaster Management Act 2003
    • Authorised Officers under the Ambulance Service Act 1991 and Authorised Officers under the Emergency Services Act 1990
    • Anyone employed or volunteering as car park attendants who occasionally direct or divert traffic within the car park
    • Queensland School Crossing Supervisors

    Event Traffic Marshal Scheme
    Event Traffic Marshal Scheme
      The Event Traffic Marshal Scheme allows appropriately trained volunteers to conduct limited traffic control activities in low speed, low traffic environments at various special events and to support events in a safe way. It is an alternative option to accredited Traffic Controllers in circumstances where accredited Traffic Controllers may not be required for basic and low risk duties.

      Refer: https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Community-and-environment/
      Planning-and-development/Other-matters-requiring-approval/Traffic-management-at-special-events/Event-Traffic-Marshals

Where to Apply

Eligibility Requirements
Eligibility Requirements
Eligibility Requirements
    All requirements listed in this section must be met (with the exception of the training qualifications) at time of application.

    If the customer has not met all requirements:
    • The application must not be accepted; and
    • No details are to be recorded on TICA

    Note:Applications may be accepted without the relevant training qualifications to allow the criminal and traffic history checks to be commenced.
    The course certificate must be lodged within 3 months from the lodgement date of the application or the application will lapse.

    Eligibility Criteria
    Who may apply
    Must be a person
    Must be a person
      • The applicant must be an individual, (i.e. a natural person not a business or company).
    Driver Licence
    Licence requirements
    Licence requirements
      A Traffic Controller applicant must have held an open or provisional driver licence (Australian or foreign) at any time within the last 5 years (this ensures applicants have an understanding of vehicle manoeuvrability and braking dynamics)
    Traffic and Criminal
    Assessing Traffic and Criminal Histories
    Assessing Traffic and Criminal Histories
      Traffic and Criminal history checks are conducted by the CSC or QGAP Officer after lodgement of the application.

      Assessing Applicant's suitability for Issue/Renewal
      An applicant must have a suitable criminal and traffic history record to be approved as a Traffic Controller. Refer: Guide to Assessing Suitability
      and
      Refer: Industry Authority > 1. Industry Authority > Criminal History Request
    Medical Fitness
    Medical Fitness
    Medical Fitness
      The applicant must satisfy certain medical fitness criteria to enter the scheme and remain in the scheme. The medical fitness criteria is listed on the application form.

      If a medical condition is declared on the application form, a health professional must verify the person's fitness and suitability to perform traffic control duties in Section 9 of the application form.

      The criteria is also detailed in the traffic controller approved procedure along with the ongoing requirements to remain fit for the role.
    Training or experience
    Training providers, course certificate and/or prior accreditation
    Training providers, course certificate and/or prior accreditation
      Traffic controller trainer lists, links and other resources
      Traffic controller trainer lists, links and other resources
        New and renewing applicants must have undertaken a TMR approved traffic controller training course within the previous 12 months. If the training course was undertaken more than 12 months prior to the application being lodged with TMR, the application should be refused.

        The TMR approved traffic controller training course (RIIWHS205 - Control traffic with a stop-slow bat) is only available through approved TMR Registered Training Organisations (RTO's).

        Note:All Statements of Attainment (SoA) presented which are dated after 1 February 2021 must list, RIIWHS205E but may also show the competency units listed below:
          RIICOM201E - Communicate in the workplace, and
          RIIWHS201E - Work safely and follow WHS policies and work procedures.
        All Statements of Attainment (SoA) presented which are dated prior to 1 February 2021 are valid for 12 months and must list RIIWHS205D, but may also show the competency units listed below:
          RIICOM201E - Communicate in the workplace, and
          RIIWHS201E - Work safely and follow WHS policies and work procedures.

        Refer customer to:
        Traffic Controller Accreditation Scheme for the current list of approved Traffic Controller training providers.

        Note:AB Quality Homes are no longer an approved RTO. However any AB Quality Homes course certificates issued up to an including 30 September 2021 can be accepted until 30 September 2022.

        For the Approved Traffic Controller Course Information and Frequently Asked Questions, refer to TMR website: https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/business-industry/Accreditations/Traffic-Controller-Accreditation-Scheme

        Note:If the RTO is NOT on the list, please contact Technical Training Solutions on 07 3066 8672 for further advice.

      New applications
      New applications
        Applicants wanting to apply for the grant of a Traffic Controller accreditation must successfully complete the approved training course with a TMR approved RTO.

        Applicants must produce the Statement of Attainment, for having successfully completed the theoretical and practical components of the TMR approved Traffic Controller training course, Control Traffic with a stop-slow bat RIIWHS205 within the previous 12 months:

        Statement of Attendance is not accepted as evidence of course completion.

      Renewal applications
      Renewal applications
        Renewal applicants are not required to perform the minimum 20 hours on-site supervised experience when undertaking the approved training course to acquire the Statement of Attainment. However, an RTO may require a practical demonstration of competency in a simulated environment in addition to third party confirmation of practical traffic control experience.

        Renewing applicants must produce a Statement of Attainment, for having successfully completed the TMR approved Traffic Controller refresher training course, within the previous 12 months.


      Interstate applicants
      Interstate applicants
        Interstate applicants with a current interstate Traffic Controller Accreditation must contact a TMR approved RTO for assessment of their qualifications.

        In most cases, the applicant will only be required to undertake refresher training in order to apply as a new applicant in Queensland.

        However, an RTO may request further training, if the applicant cannot show suitable supporting work experience evidence.

        Note:When processing an interstate applicant as a new applicant on TICA, the refresher training course is considered equivalent to the full training course.



      Notes:The training course requirements consist of both a theoretical component and a minimum of 20 hours on-site supervised experience for new entry scheme entrants.

      For questions related to course content or on-site supervised experience, refer the customer to the RTO.

      The required Statement of Attainment must be issued by a TMR approved registered training organisation.

      Note:Approved training providers who have a traffic controller accreditation may issue and sign their own training course certificates.

Issue period
Term and possible alignment with other IA products
Term and possible alignment with other IA products
    A Traffic Controller Accreditation (TCA) is issued for a three year period.

    Note:Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Accreditations and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015 allows for the issue of a traffic controller accreditation for a period not longer than 5 years. It is a policy decision that a traffic controller accreditation is issued for 3 years.

Produce a Current Accreditation while performing the role

Transaction details
Grant
Grant
    The following requirements must be met before an application may be accepted and lodged in TICA:
    • The applicant must supply the completed application form F4115. This form must be signed by the applicant in the presence of a TMR officer or QGAP officer.
    • Evidence of Identity documents.
    • Evidence that a course has been completed.

      Refer: Eligibility req's > Eligibility requirements > Training or experience > Training providers, Course certificate and/or prior accreditation > New applications

      Note:the relevant training qualifications must be lodged within 3 months from lodgement of the application.
    • A traffic and criminal search must be conducted and assessed by the CSC or QGAP Officer.
      Refer: Industry Authority > 1. Industry Authority > Criminal history request
      Refer: Eligibility requirements > Industry Traffic & Criminal Histories > Assessing applications suitability
    • Consent to taking and storing of digital facial image and digitised signature.
    • Payment of all prescribed fees.

    If any of the prerequisites are not met, the application is not to be accepted.
    Refer: Refuse to consider below.

    Note:For new applications only - If the applicant has signed up to receive e-reminders, upon approval of their application they will receive their interim industry authority by email.

    Refer: Interim Industry Authority below.
Renew
Renew
    All TMR Customer Service Centres and on-line QGAP agencies may accept applications and payment for renewing Traffic Controller accreditation.

    The following requirements must be met before an application may be accepted and lodged in TICA:
    • The applicant must supply the completed application form F4115. This form must be signed by the applicant in the presence of a TMR Officer or QGAP Officer.
    • Evidence of Identity documents.
    • Evidence that a full or a refresher course has been completed.

      Refer: Eligibility req's > Eligibility requirements > Training or experience > Training providers, Course certificate and/or prior accreditation > Renewal applications

    • Consent to taking and storing of digital facial image and digitised signature.
    • Payment of all prescribed fees.

    Note:Where an applicant declares criminal history charges/offences since last successful application, an adhoc criminal history request must be made.

    Refer: Eligibility Requirements above.

    Note:There is no driver licence requirement for renewals.

    If any of the prerequisites are not met, the application is not to be accepted.

    Note:Customers are mailed a Traffic Controller Accreditation Renewal Notice (F4459) and Traffic Controller Accreditation Application (F4115) approximately 7 weeks prior to the expiry date. Notification that the Traffic Controller Accreditation is due for renewal is shown in the My account portal. If the customer is signed up for e-reminders they will be emailed a reminder 3 days before their Traffic Controller Accreditation expiry date.

    Refer: Refuse to consider below.

Interim Industry Authority
Cancel/Suspend/Void/Expired
Cancel/Suspend/Void/Expired
    Cancelled
    Cancelled
      If a driver licence is cancelled, the traffic controller accreditation is not cancelled as a driver licence is not required to perform the role.

      A traffic controller accreditation can be cancelled due to a CIB report or not complying with conditions of the authority.

      If the authority is cancelled during the term, the smartcard must be surrendered.

    Suspended
    Suspended
      There is no requirement for suspension of a traffic controller accreditation.

    Void
    Void
      If a payment for a traffic controller accreditation has been dishonoured, the Policy unit is advised by the finance section. The Policy unit will advise the applicant by phone that they must pay the relevant fees immediately, and take show cause action if required.

    Expired
    Expired
      If the customer submits an application for renewal which has expired more than 3 months, a new application with full requirements is required. A new CrimTrac search will need to be conducted.
Surrender
Surrender
    The Traffic Controller Accreditation may be surrendered for the following reasons:
    • Refused on renewal of application;
    • Merged;
    • Multiple Industry Authority holder (if removing one or more of the current authority types).

    The Traffic Controller Accreditation can be surrendered for the following reasons:
    • No longer required (voluntary);
    • On behalf of the deceased

    Note:Multiple card holders wanting to voluntarily surrender one authority but retain others are to complete a Replacement Industry Authority (F7429).
Reinstate
Reinstate
    A Traffic Controller Accreditation may be reinstated due to:
    A review of a decision made by Regional Director or delegate.
    The applicant needs to provide:
    • The letter issued by the Regional Director or delegate stating the licence is to be reinstated; and
    • Standard EOI.

    A stay or review of a decision made by QCAT.
    The applicant needs to provide:
    • The letter issued by QCAT stating the licence is to be reinstated; and
    • Standard EOI.

    Note:The applicant does not have to complete another application form. A copy of the letter by the authorised agent is used for filing.
Refuse to consider
Refuse to grant application
Refuse to grant application
    The application may be refused if any of the following are not met:
    • Traffic History
    • Criminal History
    • Failed 1:N (if application is lodged and not approved on same day)
    • For a new licence, already holds a driver's or assistant's certificate under the name in the application or another name

    The decision to refuse an application for a Traffic Controller Accreditation can be made by:
    • Manager (Customer Service Centre)
    • Senior Advisor (Service Management)
    • Principal Customer Service Officer
    • Customer Service Officer

    A written refusal notice must be sent to the applicant within 14 days of the decision advising:
    • The decision
    • The reason for the decision
    • Information regarding reconsideration or appeal of the decision
    • That they can apply for a stay of the decision through QCAT

    Note:If an applicant is refused their accreditation, a copy of the refusal letter and the file should be forwarded to Iron Mountain (RECALL) the next business day and not retained in the office in case of appeal:
    • Filing must be in a filing envelope indicating Industry Licensing filing only (tick the 'Industry Licensing and Dangerous Goods' box on the filing envelope)
    • Name of Office sending work
    • Refusal dates for the documents e.g. 03/08/2009 (there must only be one envelope per refusal date)

    If the applicant wishes to apply for the review of a decision they must:

Review of original decision
Review of original decision
    A Review of a Decision is a customer's request to review a decision made on behalf of the Chief Executive. The review of the decision is made by the:
    • Manager (CSC)
    • Senior Advisor (Service Management)
    • Product Centre Manager (PCM), or
    • Regional Director.

    Following a review of a decision an information notice (sample below) must be given to the applicant to advise them of the decision:
    • to re-affirm the original decision to refuse and advise that they can have the decision reviewed again by QCAT
      OR
    • set aside the original decision and grant the Traffic Controller Accreditation.

    Traffic Controller Info Notice - September 2015

    Notes:
    • A copy of the information notice must be forwarded to the Industry Licensing Hub.
    • If the customer decides to have the decision reviewed again by QCAT the QCAT result must also be forwarded to the Industry Licensing Hub.

Lapsed applications
Lapsed applications
    Lapsed Status on IL applications in TICA occur when:
    • Lodged applications are not finalised (outstanding requirements) within 3 months of the application date; or
    • Renewals are not finalised within 3 months of the expiry date

    In these instances the following will apply:
    • The application is to be refused and the authority term fee and the smartcard production fee are to be refunded to the customer.
      Refer: Refunds

    A lapsed application that has been refused is sent to Recall for filing on the day the application is refused.

    If the customer requests to proceed with an application after a lapsed status, the applicant will be required to re-apply and meet full requirements including the payment of all prescribed fees (including CrimTrac fees).


    Note:If the application has not been finalised due to a possible TMR error please consult with your SASM/Manager for advice on how to proceed.
Fees
Application fee
Application fee
    An application fee must be paid when the application is lodged.
CrimTrac fee
CrimTrac fee
    The CrimTrac fee is a one-off fee paid when the initial application is lodged.

    The CrimTrac fee is not charged on renewal (although a criminal history check may be requested by TMR if deemed appropriate).
Refunds
Refunds
Refunds
    Where an application has been refused (not approved) or withdrawn by the applicant, the applicant is entitled to a refund of the authority term fee and smartcard production fee components only (provided the fees were not exempted). These fees will automatically credit to the customers account to be refunded if required. The refund should be authorised the same day as the refusal letter is sent.

    Note:The smartcard production fee is not refundable if a smartcard was issued. The eligibility assessment fee component is not to be refunded.

    Where an application has been lodged and the criminal history search has NOT been requested by the CSC operator, the criminal history fee may be refunded.

    Process the adjustment from the CrimTrac Search Account to the customer account.

Interstate Traffic Controller requirements
