Card Surcharge (120)

MasterCard/Visa/American Express Branded Card Surcharge
A surcharge is collected by TMR when payment is made by ANY MasterCard, Visa or American Express branded cards to assist in recovering the cost of merchant fees charged to the department by the financial institutions.

A surcharge of:
  • 0.52% applies for VISA and Mastercard branded cards
  • 1.4% applies for American Express branded cards (PPQ and online services only excluding direct debit)

The surcharge amount is a percentage determined and approved by Treasury and is calculated on the total transaction purchase being paid for by a VISA, Mastercard or American Express branded card.
Payment Channels applying the surchargeTMR’s payment channels applying and collecting the surcharge include:
  • Online Services (TMR website and PPQ website) - (American Express/VISA/Mastercard)
  • Australia Post (VISA/Mastercard)
  • TMR’s TICA and SAP receipting sites with either integrated and non-integrated EFTPOS facilities (VISA/Mastercard)
  • QGAP and QPS sites that use TICA (VISA/Mastercard)
Calculating the surchargeThe surcharge amount is calculated as a percentage of the total transaction amount that is being paid for using card facilities at an:
  • Integrated or Non-integrated site when the operator prior to commencing the EFTPOS transaction has confirmed with the customer that they will be paying using a MasterCard or Visa branded card and this has been selected on the TICA “Make Payment” page
  • Online when the customer chooses their credit card type (American Express/VISA/MasterCard)
  • SAP receipting site when the operator prior to commencing the EFTPOS transaction has confirmed with the customer that they will be paying using a MasterCard or Visa branded card and this has been selected on the EFTPOS or Albert device

To calculate a surcharge amount, multiply the total transaction/s value by the current surcharge percentage rate. For example:

Scenario:
Customer purchases a driver licence ($64.20) & vehicle registration renewal ($345.25). The total transaction amount is $409.45. The customer is paying $ 100.00 by cash and the remaining $309.45 by credit card. To calculate surcharge: $309.45 x 0.52% = $1.61 therefore, total amount payable is $411.06.

When is the surcharge calculatedThe surcharge is automatically calculated when a customer uses their MasterCard or Visa branded card or American Express card (Online or PPQ only) to:
  • pay at a site with an integrated or non-integrated EFTPOS device
  • enter their card details into the online service
  • use their card to pay for TMR products at Australia Post
  • use their card to purchase a personalised plate on the PPQ website
· using their card to pay for TMR products at an SAP receipting site using an Albert device
Refund of SurchargeThe card surcharge is non-refundable other than when:
  • Payment of the surcharge was due to operator error in TICA/SAP receipting site.
  • When a receipt is cancelled in TICA/SAP receipting site where the payment originally included the surcharge.

Refunds of the surcharge are not granted for transactions paid via online web services.
Point of SaleIt is a requirement by the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) that businesses passing on the "Merchant Fee" to consumers, must advise the customer at the point of sale.
