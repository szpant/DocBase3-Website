    Public Trustee
    Public Trustee
      ScenarioPublic Trustee
      The Public Trustee may be required to represent a customer for a number of reasons, including:
      • an administration order made by QCAT;
      • an appointment under an Enduring Power of Attorney document;
      • deceased estate;
      • trustee for a minor.

      The Public Trustee may be appointed to manage affairs:
      • under s44 of the Guardianship and Administration Act 2000;
      • as attorney a similar provision exists in s81 of the Powers of Attorney Act 1998;
      • in a deceased estate stems from sections 29, 30, 35 and 36 of the Public Trustee Act 1978;
      • of a minor, the authority can be devolved in various ways including a court order, pursuant to a deceased estate or any of several sections in the Public Trustee Act 1978;
      • of a prisoner's property (Part 7 of the Public Trustee Act 1978);
      • by order of the Court (for example when damages compensation is awarded);
      • of incapacitated persons (part 6 Public Trustee Act 1978).

          Examples:
          • If it’s evident that the representative holds a position of authority within a company, e.g. Director, then the letter of authority/F5225 is not required if that person can establish this position through other means. Such as, a business card or ASIC document listing that person at the Director, etc.
          • Where a representative doesn’t hold a position of authority within a company, and they present in a uniform, they will also need the letter of authority or the F5225 to demonstrate they have the authority to represent the organisation.
          • If a representative presents a F5225 or letter of authority, however the Operator isn’t satisfied with the document, they may request further evidence such a business card, etc, or telephone the organisation for confirmation.

      Scenario
      • Person presenting forms on behalf of an Individual
        or
      • Person signing and/or presenting forms on behalf of a Registered Business or Trust where the legal entity is an individual/s for the purposes of a criminal history request and receipting product applications fees as part of an application for:
        • HVRAS Accredited Person
        • Approved Person
        • Approved Examiner
        • AIS Nominee
        • Approval as an AIS
      Documents RequiredCurrent ASIC business/trust documents displaying the legal entities must be provided.

      EOI is required for the representative AND each individual entity.

      EOI for each legal entity listed on the business/trust/company registration document.
      One of the following must be provided for each of the individual/s listed:
      • Qld Driver Licence valid at any time within the last 2 yrs or
      • 1 Category A document + 2 Category B documents or
      • 2 Category A document + 1 Category B document

      EOI of the Individual/Representative must be either:
      • 1 Category A document or
      • 1 Category B document, the document must show the Representative's name and signature

      Letter of Authority from individual/legal entity/entities including:
      • Transaction details
      • Name of person authorised
      • Name and signature of customer

      Note:EOI requirements for the authorised person/representative apply even if person authorised is an immediate family member
