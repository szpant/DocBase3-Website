Improvement Notice Entity (7150)

The Improvement Notice entity captures Improvement Notices issued to individuals and/or companies as a result of an investigation.

These are recorded in CID for completeness of holdings and visibility of a Case outcome.

This does not replace the need to record an Improvement Notice in DMS as evidence of business and as a corporate record.

Limited staff (PA(I)’s and Heavy Vehicle Analysis) have the ability to create this new entity type via CID.

To have an Improvement Notice recorded in CID, Officers should send an email to compliance.intelligence@tmr.qld.gov.au with the notice attached requesting recording in CID (Case number must be provided).

    What are the fields on the entity? Below is a description of each field and how it should be utilised
    1
    Record ID
    Record ID
      • System generated, cannot be changed
    2
    Name/Title
    Name/Title
      • The title of the Improvement Notice event.
      • This can be thought of as the ‘title’ of the CID entry. This field should contain (at a minimum);
        • The name of the person and/or company the improvement notice was issued to
        • What the improvement notice pertained to generally
        • The issue and follow up due dates
      • For example;
        • Improvement Notice to Swift Shift Couriers on 15/5/2017 to implement fatigue management system by 15/11/2017, or
        • Improvement Notice to Harold Loader on 15/5/2017 to implement loading and restraint training for staff at Rockhampton depot by 15/9/2017.
    3
    Embedded File
    Embedded File
      • One attachment can be loaded to the Improvement Notice entity.
      • This should be the Improvement Notice as it was issued.
      • This does not replace the need to record an Improvement Notice in DMS as evidence of business and as a corporate record.
      • The capture in CID is to ensure the issuing of an improvement notice can be seen with all the other corporate holdings on an individual and/or company.
    4

    Issued Date
      • The date the notice was issued to the person or company.

    Issuing Region
    Issuing Region
      • Select compliance region from list.
    6
    Issued By
    Issued By
      • This is a list of compliance officers only. Select TI from list who has issued/delivered the improvement notice.
    7
    Status
    Status
      • Issued / Current / In Force, or
      • Expired / Completed.
    8
    Follow Up – Due Date
    Follow Up – Due Date
      • Initial compliance date on the initial improvement notice.

    Follow Up – Extended Date
    Follow Up – Extended Date
      • If an improvement notice is granted an extension for completion, do not change the original date; indicate the new date in this field.
      • If there is no extension this should stay blank.

    Follow Up – Completed Date

      • If the follow up took more than one day, use the last day as the relevant date.

What the Improvement Notice entity links to in CID
What the Improvement Notice entity links to in CID
    The Improvement Notice entity can be linked to a limited number of items in CID.
    • An Improvement Notice entity will link to:
      • A case as an outcome,
      • An organisation as an involved party, and/or,
      • A person as an involved party.
    • It will not link to an event or complaint or vehicle or registration (or any other entities that may be involved in an investigation) as it has effect only on the person and/or company related to the case.
    • An Improvement Notice entity must be related to a case and then either a person and/or a company.
    • Where an improvement notice is followed up and a breach alleged, a new triggering event or complaint outlining the allegation should be created to trigger a new case.

Who can enter a Improvement Notice entity
Who can enter a Improvement Notice entity
    Only the following staff can create an Improvement Notice entity:
    • PA(I)’s and
    • Heavy Vehicle Analysis

    This is to ensure the notice has been reviewed by a PA(I) and reviewed prior to being issued.
