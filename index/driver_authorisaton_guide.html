Driver Authorisation Guide (6005)

    About Driver Authorisations
    What it isDriver Authorisation (DA) is a qualification that authorises a person to drive a public passenger vehicle.
    Categories of Driver Authorisation
    Categories of Driver Authorisation
    Categories of Driver Authorisation
      There are two kinds of driver authorisation issued by TMR:
      • Booked Hire/Taxi (BHTX) – authorises the holder to drive any service requiring driver authorisation (including all services provided under General driver authorisation).
      • General (Genr) – authorises the holder to drive any service other than a booked hire or taxi service.

      Note:Customers who hold a BHTX driver authorisation do not need a General driver authorisation. BHTX driver authorisations cover every service that can be provided under a General driver authorisation.

      A Restricted Driver Authorisation is another kind of driver authorisation, but can only be issued by an accredited operator of a Community and/or Courtesy Transport Service. Refer below for further details.

      Holders of Scheduled (Sche) and Tourist Motorcycle (TrMc) driver authorisation issued prior to 1 September 2018 are automatically deemed to hold General (Genr) driver authorisation. Holders of Limo and/or Taxi driver authorisation are automatically deemed to hold Booked Hire/Taxi (BHTX) driver authorisation. As a result, at the next renewal:
      • Sche and TrMc holders need to be transitioned to Genr
      • Limo holders need to be transitioned to BHTX.

      Restricted driver authorisation
      Restricted driver authorisation

        Restricted driver authorisation is a kind of driver authorisation, issued by an accredited operator of Community and/or Courtesy Transport Services, which allows the driver to drive for that operator to provide a community or courtesy transport service. However, operators may also choose to use drivers that hold any category of driver authorisation.

        Restricted driver authorisation is processed in TICA by the local Translink Regional Passenger Transport Offices and Linking CSC/QGAPs

        Restricted driver authorisation holder applies for driver authorisation BHTX or GENR
        Restricted driver authorisation holder applies for driver authorisation BHTX or GENR

          If a restricted driver authorisation holder applies for driver authorisation, the restricted driver authorisation must be surrendered in TICA before the driver authorisation application can be lodged.

          The applicant can continue to provide community or courtesy transport services under their existing restricted driver authorisation while waiting for their driver authorisation application to be approved. Once the driver authorisation is approved, the restricted driver authorisation is no longer required nor is it valid.

          The following wording must be recorded as a text restriction, “RDA surrendered on ( xx/xx/20 xx) to allow DA application to be lodged. Driver can continue to drive for community/courtesy operator who issued restricted driver authorisation until authority is withdrawn by issuing operator or DA is approved”.

          Any applicable fees (including the criminal history check fee) must be paid by the applicant.

    Eligibility Criteria
    Work Entitlement
    Work entitlement
    Work entitlement
      To be eligible for Driver Authorisation (DA), the customer must provide satisfactory evidence of their entitlement to work in Australia.

      Acceptable standard evidence includes an Australian Passport (current or expired < 2 years), a green Medicare card etc.

      Note:The green Medicare card must be sighted unless the Medicare card details can be confirmed through the Identity Documents page in TICA. If a green Medicare card has been captured and recorded correctly this can be used for work entitlement eligibility.

      Where a customer provides non standard evidence such as a yellow reciprocal Medicare card, foreign passport or Titre de Voyage, a VEVO check must be conducted.

      For more information about the documents that provide satisfactory evidence that a person has an entitlement to work in Australia, Refer:
      Entitlement to Work in Australia

      Where a VEVO check is required the customer must complete the Authority to Check a Visa Holder's Work Entitlement VEVO (F4595).

      Please refer below for your region's VEVO login details:
      Vevo Login Details

      Note:Where a customer's work entitlement check returns a result with a work limitation (eg. 8102, 8105, 8107 etc) the department must recognise the applicants work entitlement, irrelevant of the type of work limitation. The department is not required to assess work limitations. It is the customer’s responsibility to work within their limitations and the employer’s ongoing responsibility to monitor their employees work entitlement.

    Driver licence and tenure requirements
    Driver licence and tenure requirements
      All applicants for DA must:
      • Hold a current Australian open or restricted driver licence.
      • Have held an open, restricted, provisional or probationary driver licence (Australian or overseas) for at least 3 years in total.
      • All previous or alias names to be declared and if declared added in TICA.

      Additional requirements for Booked Hire/Taxi
      In addition to the above requirements, Booked Hire/Taxi applicants must have held for a continuous period of at least 1 year any of the following licences or a series of the following licences:
      • an Australian open, restricted, provisional or probationary driver licence;
      • a corresponding foreign licence issued by an experienced driver recognition country or a recognised country listed on the Equivalent Driver Licence information sheet below. (These are countries recognised as having similar driver licensing standards to Australia).

      Driver Licence Requirements for Booked Hire

      For a renewal or add class if these requirements are not met the application must be referred to your local Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.

        A 5 year Qld Traffic History must be assessed off the screen (customer is not charged a fee). If the customer has traffic history, the report can be printed.

        Interstate traffic checks
        Where an applicant has an interstate driver licence or lived interstate or in New Zealand within the last 5 years, a traffic history check must be requested from the issuing authorities. For new applications, these requests are made by the DA Hub.

        Any unsuitable traffic history must be referred to TransLink for assessment.

        Northern Territory Applicants

          Application Form

      Assessing traffic history
      Assessing traffic history
        Any applicant with a driving history that includes any of the following must be referred to the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Offices and Linking CSC/QGAPs for assessment:
        • has had a demerit point suspension in the last 12 months
        • has accumulated 10 or more demerit points on their current licence
        • has been convicted of, or had their driver licence disqualified or cancelled for, driving under the influence of liquor or a drug
        • has had an alcohol interlock condition applied to their driver licence in the last 24 months
        • has a current driver licence suspension or SPER suspension
        • has been charged with a dangerous driving offence in the last 24 months
        • has been convicted of an offence under the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994 or the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Regulation 2005 and 2018
        • has been charged with a drink or drug driving offence in the last 24 months
        • has been charged with unlicensed driving in the last 24 months
        • is on a good driving behaviour period
        • a person who shows a pattern of driver licence suspensions and cancellations within the last 10 years
        • does not hold the appropriate class of licence.
    Criminal history
    Criminal history
      New application
      New application
        When lodging a new application you must select a Criminal History Request Type.

        A National CHC must be requested for all new driver authorisation applications.

        If the applicant:
        • was born in New Zealand, or
        • has resided in New Zealand for greater than 12 months in the last 10 years, and was aged 18 years or above for any period during this time.
        • declared that they have criminal history in New Zealand the Criminal History Request Type that must be selected is 'NATIONAL & NEW ZEALAND CHECK'.
        Note: If the customer is also lodging an application for a OA and/or BEA the DA application must lodged and paid for first (followed by the BEA then OA) to ensure the Criminal History Check fee exemption is applied correctly.

      Renewal
      Renewal
        A criminal history check is only required for renewals if the applicant declares new criminal history that has occurred since their last renewal. If new criminal history has been declared contact the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office for advice on whether a criminal history check is required. The Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office should be aware of any charges/conviction through the daily monitoring process. If Translink is aware of the charges/conviction no CHC is required.

        A criminal history request type must be selected when lodging an application to renew driver authorisation, select 'NO CHECK' unless declared. The criminal history prerequisite may need to be exempted.
    Medical requirements
    Medical requirements
      Driver authorisation (new or renewal) can only be approved if TMR is satisfied that the applicant has a current medical certificate.

      Customers who have a current Commercial Vehicle Medical certificate recorded on the system (certificate could have been presented for any product) and who have no change to their medical condition, may have their driver authorisation issued (new or renewal) until the expiry date of the recorded medical.

      For example, an applicant for DA (who had previously lodged a commercial medical certificate with the department 18 months ago which was valid for five years), may use this medical certificate for the purposes of DA, however could only be granted DA until the expiry date of the medical certificate - a further three years and six months.

      A medical certificate can be supplied at time of lodgement or any time before the driver authorisation is issued. However, it must be issued by a medical practitioner within the last six months.

      A copy of the certificate should be kept by TMR and the original certificate given back to the applicant for their records.

      For driver authorisation, a suitable medical certificate is a:
      • Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712), that certifies the person meets the Assessing Fitness to Drive for Commercial Vehicle Medical Standards, or
      • Trucksafe medical certificate on Trucksafe letterhead, that verifies the doctor's name, address, phone and medicare provider number.
      • An interstate medical certificate issued by a medical practitioner that clearly meets the Assessing Fitness to Drive for Commercial Vehicle Medical Standards may also be accepted. However, an interstate medical certificate with medical conditions cannot be accepted where the applicant holds a Queensland driver licence. Where there are medical conditions the only acceptable evidence for a Queensland driver licence holder is a completed F3712. An interstate medical does not need to be recorded in the MCR hub.

      If there is no clear expiry date on the medical certificate, the following validity can be applied:
      • An unconditional medical certificate is valid for 5 years from the issue date
      • A conditional medical certificate is valid for 1 year from the issue date.

      If there is uncertainty about whether the medical meets the appropriate standard, contact Passenger Transport Authorisation.

      Note: If the Medical Certificate indicates that the customer has a Medical Condition and the results are not on TICA, the details must be recorded against the Driver Licence and on the MCR Hub.

      Refer: Driver Licence > 4. Medical Condition Reporting > The How To: Medical Condition Reporting > Medical Condition Reporting Procedures > Immediate Amendment and Proposed Ongoing Amendment - Medical Grounds
    Previous DA history for a new application only
    Previous DA history for a new application only
      The customer’s driver authorisation history must be checked to determine if they have had a previous driver authorisation application refused, or had a driver authorisation cancelled or suspended.

      If the applicant’s driver authorisation history is satisfactory, the application may continue to be assessed.

      If the applicant’s driver authorisation history is unsatisfactory, the application should be forwarded by the Driver Authorisation Hub to the relevant TransLink Office for assessment.
    Issue Period
    Issue Period
    Issue Period
      Driver Authorisation is generally issued for a period of 1 to 5 years. DA must not be issued past an expiry date of a commercial medical certificate or Australian work entitlement expiry date.

      Refer:Entitlement to Work in Australia > Assessing and recording VEVO results

      Note:The minimum period a driver authorisation can be issued or renewed for is one month.
    Application accepted in error
    Application accepted in error
    Application accepted in error
      In the event an application is accepted and lodged in error the application must be invalidated. This will return all money paid including the criminal history check fee and will not record the application on the customers history. The CSC is responsible for contacting the applicant to advise them of the error.

      If the application has been approved in error, the application must be referred to your local Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office for appropriate refusal action. An approved application must not be invalidated.

    Lapsed applications
    Lapsed applications
      Lapsed Status on DA applications in TICA occur when:
      • Lodged applications are not finalised (outstanding requirements) within 3 months of the application date; or
      • Renewals are not finalised within 3 months of the expiry date

      Applications that lapse should be referred to the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office. Refunds/refusal action or approval can only be processed once the 'TICA Action and Fee Refund request' is received from the Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.
      Refer: Refunds

      A lapsed application that has been refused is sent to Recall for filing on the day the application is refused.

      If the customer requests to proceed with an application after a lapsed status, the applicants will be required to re-apply and meet full requirements including the payment of all prescribed fees (including criminal history check fees).

      Note:If the application has not been finalised due to a possible TMR error please consult with your SASM/Manager for advice on how to proceed.

    Add Class (if required)
    Add Class (if required)
      • When adding a new class to an existing DA an applicant must meet the necessary driver licence and traffic history requirements for that category of DA.
      • A new criminal history check, medical certificate or VEVO check are not required. If new criminal history has been declared contact the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office for advice.
      • When adding the Booked Hire/Taxi (BHTX) class to an existing DA, the expiry date of the Booked Hire/Taxi (BHTX) class automatically aligns with existing class/es.
      • Where the applicant chooses to reduce the term of the existing class/es, the expiry date must be updated prior to adding a new class.

      Important Note about Sche, TrMc and Limo categories of DA:
      From 1 September 2018 there are two types of driver authorisation Booked Hire/Taxi (BHTX) and General (Genr).

      Sche, TrMc and Limo DA can no longer be renewed or added.

      An attempt to lodge an application for categories Sche, TrMc and Limo will cause "action is not valid for application type" error message to display in TICA.

    Upgrade Class (if required)
    Upgrade Class (if required)
      If the applicant is applying to upgrade their driver authorisation from provisional to full:
      • Check that the applicant meets the requirements to be issued with full driver authorisation. Consult with relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office if necessary.
      • Lodge the application to upgrade in TICA
      • If the applicant does not meet the eligibility requirements to be upgraded to full driver authorisation, the application should be referred to the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.
      • If the applicant meets the requirements to be upgraded to full driver authorisation, the application can be approved in accordance with current procedure.

      Refer: Passenger Transport Documentation Database > Operator Accreditation (OA) & Driver Authorisation (DA) > PART C: DA Policy & Procedures > 1. DA Policy > 1.15 Provisional Driver Authorisation

    Reinstate a Driver Authorisation (if required)
    Reinstate a Driver Authorisation (if required)
      Where a driver authorisation has been automatically suspended as a result of a SPER/DPSU suspension it will be automatically reinstated once the driver licence suspension has been lifted and becomes current.

      Where a driver authorisation has been suspended for eg. criminal history (not SPER/DPSU automatic suspension) the CSC officer is required to check with the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.

      ‘Currency of DA’ confirmation notice
      When a driver authorisation is reinstated, a customer may request a 'currency of DA' confirmation letter as evidence that they are authorised to drive (this is usually for the taxi company/employer to know that they have a current DA). When this occurs, the attached letter is to be completed by the CSC officer and issued to the customer.


      Status of Driver Authorisation - May 2016

      Note: If an application is lodged at a Customer Service Centre, the CSC is required to check with the relevant Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office. to determine if there is any reason why the driver authorisation should not be reinstated. Generally the suspension finalisation in TICA would be actioned by the Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.

    Refuse to Accept Application (if required)
    Refuse to Accept Application (if required)
      The department must refuse to accept an application if the applicant:
      • Does not provide evidence that they have an entitlement to work in Australia;
      • Does not apply in the approved form;
      • Has a current Alcohol Interlock condition on their driver licence;
      • Does not pay the applicable fees, if required;
      • Does not meet EOI requirements;
      • Does not allow the chief executive to take and keep a digital image or collect their digitised signature; or
      • Fails the 1:1 image match.

      Note: This does not mean refuse the application in TICA - it provides for the CSO to return the documentation to a customer under particular circumstances.

      The applicant should be advised of the requirements to successfully lodge an application. Verbal advice may be given. (Written refusal to consider an application notices are available if required).

      Refer: Industry Authority > 1. Industry Authority > Refuse to Consider

      When refusing to accept an application all documentation including the application form should be returned to them unless the department has taken their digital photo and is now refusing to accept the application. (e.g. failed 1:1 image match). In these cases the application form should be retained and filed.
