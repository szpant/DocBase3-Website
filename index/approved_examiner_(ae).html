Approved Examiner (AE) (4655)


What is an Approved ExaminerApproved Examiners are solely responsible for vehicle inspections at an Approved Inspection Station (AIS) and are required to conduct a thorough inspection of each vehicle to ensure it is free of defects and safe to use on a road. The vehicle inspection also includes:
  • Verification of the vehicle identifiers from the vehicle itself
  • Road testing the vehicle
  • Certifying the vehicle's condition in the inspection report.

An Approved Examiner must be nominated by either the AIS approval holder or a Nominee of the AIS to conduct vehicle inspections at the AIS.
Approved Examiner New/Renewal Application
Application for approval as an Approved Examiner (AE)
Application for approval as an Approved Examiner (AE)
    A new Approved Examiner applicant is defined as an individual who is not recorded in the RUMIIS database as an Approved Examiner.

    New applicants and existing applicants applying for renewal are required to:
    • Complete the Approved Examiner Application (F2339)
    • Pay the criminal history check fee
    • Pay the Approved Examiner application fee

    New applicants must also provide supporting documents (i.e qualifications/experience) as outlined on the Approved Examiner Application (F2339) form.

    Note:A criminal history check from Queensland Police Service (QPS) or a third party vendor is not acceptable.

    Note:If the applicant also submits a Nominee application, only request one criminal history checks in TICA.

    AE Renewal
    AE Renewal
      The AIS Administrator will send an AE renewal reminder notice to the customer 60 days prior to their accreditation expiry date.
    Early renewal
    Early renewal
      An approved examiner accreditation may be renewed as soon as they have received their renewal notice, which is normally sent 60 days before their accreditation expires.

      However, where there are exceptional circumstances (i.e. the person will not be able to action their renewal notice as they are travelling interstate or overseas) the approved examiner may request to renew their accreditation up to three months prior to their expiry date and without having received their renewal notice.

      If a customer service centre operator receives an application earlier than three months prior to the accreditation expiry, the application must be refused as a criminal history check (CHC) is required, which is only valid for a period of 3 months.

      The applicant must email their request for an early renewal to the AIS Administrator and provide details of their exceptional circumstances for consideration.

    Refer: Accreditation > Approved Examiner > The How To: Approved Examiner Procedures > Process New/Renewal Application for an Approved Examiner

    For further information refer to the Information Sheet 15 - Renewing your approved examiner accreditation on the AIS webpage: www.tmr.qld.gov.au/ais
Expired/Lapsed Approved Examiner Accreditation
Expired/Lapsed AE Accreditation Policy
Expired/Lapsed AE Accreditation Policy
    From 1 December 2020, if an Approved Examiner Accreditation has expired for a period of 90 days or more, the Accreditation will lapse.

    If an Approved Examiner Accreditation lapses, a new application must be made together with all supporting information and all relevant application fees must be paid.

Starting work as an Approved Examiner at an AIS
    An existing Approved Examiner is a person approved by TMR and is recorded in the RUMIIS database in the role of an Approved Examiner and their accreditation has not expired.

    To start working for an AIS, the Approved Examiner     must be nominated by the AIS approval holder (or a Nominee of the AIS) and written notice must be given to TMR within fourteen days of the Approved Examiner commencing work at the AIS.

    If the Approved Examiner     changes employers, the AIS approval holder (or a Nominee of the AIS) must notify TMR within fourteen days of the approved examiner starting work at the AIS.

    An AIS approval holder (or a Nominee of the AIS) must complete the relevant sections of     Approved Inspection Station Scheme Change/Replacement Application (F4638) and scan all sides of the application form and email the scanned documents from the CSC mailbox to the AIS Administrator.

    All enquires should be directed to the AIS Administrator.

    All enquiries should be directed to the AIS Administrator
