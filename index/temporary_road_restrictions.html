Temporary Road Restrictions (5264)

Temporary Road Restriction
What it isAuthorised Officers may be requested to support and enforce temporary road restrictions such as enforcing road closures, load restrictions and\or condition access to roads and bridges.
Offences for Temporary Road RestrictionsBefore issuing a PIN for contravening a restricted road or disobeying a no entry sign consider if a more suitable penalty may be imposed by the court, if so prepare a Brief to Adjudicator.
If the vehicle or vehicle combination has caused road asset damage, review the situation with the coordinator prior to completing a Brief to Adjudicator as compensation submissions may need to be considered.

Contravening a Restricted Road Use Sign
Contravening a Restricted Road Use Sign
    For an offence to apply under section 46 of the TIA 1994, a traffic sign must state both how the use of the road is restricted and the maximum penalty for failing to comply with the notice.

    If satisfied that all elements of the offence can be proven, issue a Penalty Infringement Notice (PIN) Code 2875 Contravening a restricted road use notice TIA s46(4)to the non-compliant driver.


    Signage requirements for PIN to apply
    Signage requirements for PIN to apply
      Road closures: the relevant combination of traffic signs is TC1475 (Road Closed) plus TC1805 Maximum Penalty 200 Penalty Units).
      Weight restrictions: the TC1808 (Gross Load Limit) together with TC1807 (5t Max) and TC1805 (Maximum Penalty 200 Penalty Units) are required on the sign. If a maximum penalty is not shown on the sign then a PIN cannot be issued under the TIA.

Disobey a No Entry Sign
Disobey a No Entry Sign
    Requirements for the offence to apply

    For an offence to apply under section 100 of the TO(RUM - Road Rules) Regulation, the traffic sign must include TC1884 No Entry. (See Appendix 6 - Road Restriction Signs).

    If satisfied that all elements of the offence can be proven issue PIN Code 1921 Disobey no entry sign TO(RUM-RR) s100
Heavy Vehicles
Heavy Vehicles
    A heavy vehicle driver may be breached for an offence against the contravention of the road restriction or disobeying a 'No Entry' sign as well as any other offence/s detected against a transport law. E.g. a heavy vehicle may have entered the restricted road and is able to be dealt with for this particular offence; yet when weighed it is determined over regulation weight. A breach for the mass offence can also be instigated.
Responsibilities of the TRR Co-ordinator The Co-ordinator for a Temporary Road Restriction (TRR) event is appointed by the Regional Director (RD) or Lead RD for Compliance PLT.
Responsibilities of the Co-ordinator
Responsibilities of the Co-ordinator
    The Coordinator will be briefed by the RD on the request for support that has been received from PD&O and the agreed funding arrangements.

    The Co-ordinator has operational command of the TRR event and is responsible for the following:

    Preparation of Documentation
    Preparation of Documentation
      The Co-ordinator must prepare and obtain approval for the 'Temporary Road Restriction Enforcement Action Plan' . Approval should be sought from the CSB Regional Director, for a local event, or Lead RD Compliance, for a significant event.

      Temporary Road Restriction Enforcement Action Plan

      On approval of the Action plan the Coordinator will prepare the Operational Plan.
    Staff Management
    Staff Management
      • Prepare and manage the Authorised Officer roster. Roster must reflect the Shift Rostering Principles Policy and other Human Resource policies regarding hours worked and travel;
      • Advise the CSB Regional Director or Lead Director Compliance if approvals from PD&O are required for Authorised Officers travel on any restricted roads.
    Management of the TRR event
    Management of the TRR event
    The Co-ordinator will:
      • Ensure all staff working on the TRR event are familiar with the approved Temporary Road Restriction Enforcement Action Plan to Authorised Officers before undertaking the compliance and enforcement activities on the TRR.
      • Ensure operational practices meet with Workplace Health & Safety (WH&S) requirements and staff are adequately provisioned.
      • Continually monitor the current traffic and road conditions through Traffic Reports and Road Conditions
      • Check the Restricted Road Use Notice for currency, and consistency with any other TMR public advice.
      • Raise any identified discrepancies about the currency and consistency of information displayed or released to the public with the CSB Regional Director in the case of a local event; and the Lead Director Compliance in a significant event.
      • Obtain approval from PD&O if Authorised Officers are required to travel on any restricted roads.
      • Resolve any issues reported by on-road AOs, particularly any concerns related to:
        • discrepancies reported between the on-road situation and the TMR web page on road closure information. If there are discrepancies between the TMR website on on-road conditions contact QLD Traffic or
        • correct road restriction signage.
        • what enforcement legislation should be used and in what circumstances, that is; enforcement under the TIA or TO(RUM - Road Rules) Regulation.
        • the location of the signs or interception area making sure there is appropriate space for vehicles to turn around and alternative route diversions.
      • Maintain regular contact with the Authorised Officers operating the TRR to ensure their safety
    Reporting
    Reporting
      The Co-ordinator must:
      • Keep CSB Regional Director or Lead Director Compliance inform of any significant issues.
      • Maintain regular contact with CSB senior management and the Compliance Product Leadership Team (CPLT) .
      • Keep Emergency Management informed.
      • Conduct a compliance debrief within 14 days of the operation ceasing, with relevant stakeholders involved, including determining what information to archive.
      • Prepare a debrief report for the TRR event and submit it to the Compliance PLT.
    Facilitating Cost Recovery
    Facilitating Cost Recovery
      The Co-ordinator will facilitate cost recovery by:
      • Ensuring time spent by Authorised officers on TRR duties are recorded in MyTime under Disaster Management.
      • Ensuring Authorised Officers:
        - Use the correct cost centre number and GL code, and
        - Keep required records\receipts, including travel, accommodation.
        - Collecting records and/or receipts from Authorised Officers.
      • Engaging the Regional Advisor (Finance & Business Management) at the end of the TRR activity to prepare the Accounts Receivable Invoice\Credit Memo Details M3260 for the CSB Regional Director in the case of a local event, or Lead Director Compliance in the case of a significant event.
Operating a Temporary Road Restriction (TRR)All officers must be familiar with the Temporary Road Restriction Action Plan (TRRAP) before commencing activities related to the temporary road restriction.

When to contact the Coordinator
When to contact the Coordinator
    Contact the coordinator if there are:
    • discrepancies between the on-road operational situation and signage or the TRRAP.
    • any concerns about what enforcement legislation should be used and in what circumstances, that is enforcement under TI section 46 or TO(RUM - Road Rules) Regulation section 100.
    • is a workplace incident
    • At the end of each shift to report:
    • any incidents reported to QPS.
    • number of intercepts by type.
    • number of vehicles or vehicle combinations weighed.
    • number of non-compliant vehicles.
    • details of non-compliance incidents including details of any potential damage to infrastructure.
    • any other relevant intelligence (e.g. vehicle movement, alternate routes, vehicles parked up the road to avoid compliance checks).
When to contact QPS
When to contact QPS
    Immediately contact the Queensland Police Service (QPS) on the 24 hour contact number, refer TRRAP, if:
    • emergency incident management assistance is required.
    • a driver fails to stop when directed.
    • a driver disregards an Authorised Officers enforcement instruction and continues through a temporary road closure.
Operating a TRR
Operating a TRR
    General guidelines
    General guidelines
      Authorised Officers should:
      • not put themselves or any other road user at risk.
      • position themselves off the road in a way which allows:
      • a clear view of, and for, approaching vehicles
      • easy entry to the road in order to direct vehicles to stop at the checkpoint area
      • a clear avenue of retreat from the road position if required.
      • ensure vehicles or vehicle combinations are intercepted prior to the vehicle passing the temporary restricted road sign or no entry sign.
      • ensure the location allows vehicles to turn around or take an alternative route before passing the temporary restricted road sign or no entry sign
      • ensure the signage complies with the legislative requirements when commencing a TRR.
      Possible Sign Layout

      • never pursue a vehicle that fails to stop through a closed road or no entry restriction.
    Promote Safety
    Promote Safety
      When intercepting vehicles promote safety by:
      • Informing drivers of the dangers of flooded waters and supply TMR's Wet Weather Information sheets if available .
      • directing drivers to TMR's traffic and Travel information website and phone number 131940.
      • advising drivers of alternative routes to their destination.
      • advising heavy vehicle drivers how to get permits and/or approvals to move freight (e.g. grain and water).
      • advising heavy vehicle drivers of relevant load restrictions.
      For information on Intercepting vehicles Refer to (link to Interceptions 5231 and Static Intercepts 7382).
    Heavy Vehicles
    Heavy Vehicles
      For information on Weighing a Heavy Vehicle Refer: Weighing a Heavy Vehicle

      After weighting the vehicle
      • Advise the driver if they can proceed onto the restricted road.
      • If the vehicle cannot proceed, where possible, provide details of alternate heavy vehicle routes.

      Note:A heavy vehicle driver may be breached for an offence against the contravention of the road restriction or disobeying a 'No Entry' sign as well as any other offence/s detected against a transport law. E.g. a heavy vehicle may have entered the restricted road and is able to be dealt with for this particular offence; yet when weighed it is determined over regulation weight. A breach for the mass offence can also be instigated.
    Who may drive past a road restriction notice
    Who may drive past a road restriction notice
      To drive past a road restriction notice a person must:
      • have TMR or Queensland Police Service (QPS) written approval. The person's identity must match the approval letter\phone message\text message or fax.
      • be carrying out road works or inspecting a road for the chief executive and require access to complete their work. The person must have a work identification card (TMR or Local Government Authority) and be in an appropriately marked vehicle.

      Authorised Officers must have written approval from the Co-ordinator to drive past a road restriction notice. To seek approval complete the Drive past a Restricted Road Use Notice Approval (during wet weather and flooding) form (F5065)) and the Checklist for Transport Inspector from the coordinator.

      Checklist for Transport Inspector


      Authorised Officers are not to pursue vehicles that disobey an enforcement instruction not to continue on the restricted road, through a closed road or no entry restriction.
    Receipts and recording activity
    Receipts and recording activity
      CSB may be entitled to reimbursement of compliance and enforcement support costs related to disaster management events.

      Receipts for all expenditure associated with the TRR, including accommodation and travel, must be given to the Co-ordinator.

      In MyTime all TRR related activity must be recorded as Disaster management.
