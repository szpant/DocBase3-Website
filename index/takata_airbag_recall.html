Takata Airbag Recall (7560)

What it isIn 2018 an issue was identified with several types of Takata airbags fitted into a number of makes and models of vehicles. These airbags were considered faulty and a nationwide recall was imposed by the ACCC.

There are several types of Takata airbag which are part of the recall and these have been categorised according to the level of risk.

    1. Alpha – the most critical in need of replacing
    2. Critical Non Alpha
    3. Beta

All of these airbags will need to be replaced as part of the nationwide compulsory recall.

A customer can check if their vehicle is affected by the recall at https://www.ismyairbagsafe.com.au/ or through their vehicle manufacturers website for example, Toyota.

There are also several other types of Takata airbags which are considered faulty, however are not subject to the nationwide compulsory recall. These vehicles are subject to a voluntary recall as stipulated by the vehicle manufacturer.

Transport Ministers in every state of Australia provided support to the Federal Government by agreeing to impose sanctions against those vehicle registered operators who had not complied with the compulsory recall.

In the interests of public safety, TMR is assisting industry and the ACCC with reducing the number of vehicles fitted with these faulty airbags, on the Queensland roads.
Takata Process overviewBelow is an overview of the Takata recall process:
    1.Manufacturer contacts customers about faulty airbags seeking to replace them (free of charge)
    2.ACCC sends TMR a list of customers who have failed to replace their faulty air bags
    3.TMR applies a:
      TEXT (advice only) restriction against all current/expired registrations
      RCAL restriction against all cancelled/auto-cancelled registrations

    Note:The compliance team may also apply a RCAL restriction against a vehicle that has been through the show cause process and the customer has cancelled the registration themselves.
    4.ACCC sends TMR an updated list of customers who have still failed to replace their faulty air bags
    5.TMR applies a RCAL restriction against all cancelled/auto-cancelled registrations
    6.TMR issues Courtesy Notice (by email or post) to the registered operators of all current registrations. An SMS is also issued where a mobile phone number is recorded (14 days to resolve)
    7.TMR issues Show Cause Notice - Propose to Cancel (by post) to the registered operators of all current registrations who have failed to provide evidence of replacing the airbag (28 days to resolve)
    8.TMR issues Decision Notices - Cancellation (by post) advising cancellation date
    9.TMR cancels registration and issues refund (by EFTPOS or cheque)
    10.TMR applies RCAL restriction against cancelled/auto-cancelled registrations


    At any point in the process, the customer can provide evidence that they have replaced the faulty airbag to QLDtakatarecall@tmr.qld.gov.au. Once evidence is verified, the action will cease and the restriction will be removed.

    Escalation: Refer any enquiries that are unable to be answered to Compliance on 38138623 or QLDtakatarecall@tmr.qld.gov.au. Please note that enquires should be emailed in the first instance, the phone number provided is for emergent situations only (eg customer presents to CSC for registration transaction and is unable to completed transaction until issue is resolved).


    Note:For all higher risk Takata vehicles managed in 2020 under the TMR strategy to assist manufacturers manage the recall process, defect notices were issued after the Courtesy Notice and before Show Cause. Legislation now enables TMR to remove the defect notice step from the process.

Critical Updates
The ACCC have advised that any personal imported vehicles that have airbags fitted are unable to be checked to verify if they fall under the Takata Airbag Recall. Therefore, a decision has been made to make all personally imported vehicles with airbags fitted, to be ineligible for a PDT.
