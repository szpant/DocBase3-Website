Appointment Booking System (7629)

Project NameAppointment Booking System (ABS)
WhenLater in 2021
BackgroundThe Appointment Booking System (ABS) is replacing the current Services Booking System (SBS), and will be used by TMR staff and customers (online) to book practical driving tests and vehicle inspections.

SBS has been unable to evolve or improve in performance, leaving little room for enhancements and placing the system at risk of faults. Although ABS is replacing the current bookings, the system will have greater functionalities and the capacity to grow with business needs.

Phase one of the project will introduce a new, intuitive booking system that will reach a wide range of customers, and allow them to self-serve and book appointments online. Contact Centre Consultants will continue to help customers book via telephone when the online service is not used.

Changes will include:
  • booking practical driving tests online (light and heavy vehicles),
  • booking vehicle inspections online,
  • preventing learner drivers from booking driving tests when their logbook requirements haven't been met.

Refer: Further Information for more details about changes.
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers
Customers
    Customers will be able to:
    • Make a booking
    • Swap bookings
    • Cancel bookings
    • Reschedule bookings
    • Update contact details
    • Access a calendar view to manage their bookings
    • Search appointments using an interactive map or their current location
    • Filter appointments by selecting either first available or a preferred date/time.

    Customers that use the Pay Order service will receive an email at 6:00pm the day the booking is made to remind them to pay for their booking.
Learner Drivers
Learner Drivers
    Where a Learner is required to complete a Logbook, the ABS will prevent a practical driving test from being booked when the Logbook requirements haven't been met (i.e. logbook hasn't been approved or lodged, or where there's no Logbook exemption).

    Once the Logbook has been lodged the customer may only book available appointments in 14 (calendar) days’ time or later. The time it takes for the lodged status to update in TICA is dependent on how the customer submits their supervised driving hours:

    Queensland Learner Logbook
    Queensland Learner Logbook
      If the hardcopy logbook is submitted at Australia Post, the lodged status will not appear until the Logbook has been physically received by the Learner Logbook processing unit and updated in TICA.

      Note: The ABS has removed the need for Centres to check the approval of hardcopy Logbooks prior to going out for a test because practical driving tests must be booked on a date that is at least 14 days after the Logbook has been lodged on TICA.

    Learner Logbook App
    Learner Logbook App
      If the logbook is submitted electronically via the App, the lodged status should be updated in TICA by the next business day (this is subject to the volumes being submitted and potential backlog).

      Due to the nature of the App and the Robotics Process Automation working together, a lodged Logbook is almost instantaneously approved (where appropriate). Therefore, to avoid customer confusion, external communications will state that all logbooks should be approved prior to a test booking being made.

      Refer: Further Information below, for details about the Hazard Perception Test.
Driving Schools/Driver Trainers
Driving Schools/Driver Trainers
    Driver trainers (as individuals not as an organisation) must either sign-in or create an account prior to making a booking. They will need to enter in details from their driver licence card.

    A representative (as an individual) for an organisation (i.e. Driving School) must also either sign-in or create an account prior to making a booking. They will need to enter in details from their driver licence card. The representative will need additional details about the organisation, such as the Organisation Name (as shown on TICA) and CRN.

    Once they have logged in to the ABS, they will be able to associate themselves to their students bookings and make and manage bookings on their student’s behalf. They can then:
    • Make a booking
    • Swap bookings
    • Cancel bookings
    • Reschedule bookings
    • Update contact details
    • Access a calendar view to manage their bookings
    • Search appointments using an interactive map or their current location
    • Filter appointments by selecting either first available or a preferred date/time.

    Additional Communications will be sent to the Industry advising Driving Schools/Driver Trainers of the online changes.

    Refer: Further Information below, for details about Auth0 account.
Fleets/COI vehicles
Fleets/COI vehicles
    Customers who are individuals (not an organisation), must either sign-in or create an account prior to making a booking. The individual will need to enter in details from their driver licence card.

    A representative (must be an individual) for an organisation (i.e. Bus Company) must also either sign-in or create an account prior to making a booking. The individual will need to enter in details from their driver licence card. The representative will need additional details about the organisation, such as the Organisation Name (as shown on TICA) and CRN.

    If they have a VIBA qualification applied to the involved party number on TRAILS, then they can be allocated reserved bulk bookings. This will also allow them to:
    • Make bookings for themselves and others into any available slot
    • Make bookings into their reserved bulk allocated slots
    • Access a list or calendar view of their bookings or bookings that have been made on their behalf
    • Reschedule, swap, cancel, or update the contact details for any booking that they have made or made on their behalf
    • View, manage and pay for their allocations of reserved bulk slots
    • Search for reserved bulk slots which have been allocated to them.

    Refer: Further Information below, for details about Auth0 account.
Contact Centre Consultants
Contact Centre Consultants
    Payments for bookings made by customers who are not TMR staff must be processed via the TMR Online Services Shopping Cart.

    Payments for bookings made by TMR staff can be processed via a booking order or deducted from the funds in the customer's TMR account.

    Customers that use the Pay Order service will receive an email at 6:00pm the day the booking is made to remind them to pay for their booking.
Customer Service Centre Operators
Customer Service Centre Operators
    ABS will prevent a practical driving test from being booked where Logbook requirements haven't been met. However, until such time that all bookings have been made via the ABS, Operators should continue to check TICA for Logbook requirements (before a customer goes out on a driving test).
Compliance Officers
Compliance Officers
    Two new types of vehicle inspections have been added: Defect (Major) and Defect (Minor). Customers will be able to book inspections to clear these defect notices.

    Customers that are issued a defect notice should be encouraged to book a vehicle inspection online.

    Once the ABS goes live, the Vehicle Check List must be printed from TICA (instead of SBS), and the Daily Worksheet will be printed from the ABS.
Further information
Important
Important
    The requirement to print two weeks of booking schedules (26 July - 9 August) for all locations will be delayed. New dates for this task will be provided shortly.
Email/SMS notifications
Email/SMS notifications
    Customers will receive an email when they make, swap, cancel or reschedule a booking to the email address they provide. They will also receive a reminder email and SMS one day before their upcoming driving test or vehicle inspection to the contact details provided.
Auth0
Auth0
    Customers must access the ABS by an Auth0 account. To create an Auth0 account, they will need to enter their email address and choose a password. Customers who have already created an Auth0 account for PrepL or HPT, can use these same log in details to access the ABS. The customer will be required to confirm their identity by entering information found on their Queensland driver/learner licence card. They will also need to verify an email that will be sent to their email address.

    The two-factor authentication is a second step to prove who the customer is when they log on to their account. Using two-factor authentication provides a higher level of authentication and helps improve online security.

    There is an Auth0 option for an individual to associate themselves with an organisation to manage the bookings for the organisation. Once the individual has logged in, they can associate themselves to an organisation by entering the organisation’s name and CRN (as shown on TICA).
Hazard Perception Test
Hazard Perception Test
    The ABS will check if a customer has had their Logbook approved. However, the ABS has not had the Hazard Perception Test (HPT) restrictions included in this first release, so manual checking will be required.

    In order for the HPT restriction to be included in the ABS, the requirements for this functionality will need to be documented and agreed as an enhancement, after which time it will be scheduled into a future program of work accordingly.
Training
Training
    Formal training sessions for SMEs, such as, schedulers, customer contact centre consultants and compliance administration staff, was completed on 22 June 2021.

    Further training information for training and revision tasks will be made available shortly.
Communications
Communications
    Separate communication pieces, advising of the new ABS, are being prepared for TMR staff, learners and industry representatives. TMR staff will receive their communications before external distribution to learners and industry representatives.
