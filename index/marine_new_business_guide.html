Marine New Business Guide (770)


What it isNew business is the registering of unregistered ships.
Registration
Ships to be Registered
Ships to be Registered
    Queensland regulated ships (QRS) including 'recreational ships' and 'other Queensland regulated ships' need to be registered.
Recreational Ships
Recreational Ships
    Recreational ships are owned by individuals or organisations for private use only and are:
      • All ships including hovercrafts with a motor of 3kW (4.02hp) or more
      • Yachts fitted with auxiliary motors of 3kW (4.02hp) or more
      • Personal Water Craft (PWC) such as Jet Skis
      • Recreational Boat-share schemes

    Note:For further information and FAQs about the Boat-share scheme, please refer to the MSQ website - www.msq.qld.gov.au
Other Queensland Regulated Ships
Other Queensland Regulated Ships
    Ships that are used for purposes other than recreation and are owned by schools, surf lifesaving or other community groups are called ‘other Queensland regulated ships’ and are:
      • less than 6 metres in length; and
      • designed to carry 12 persons or less; and
      • operating within 15 nautical miles (nm) or less from land

    Other Queensland regulated ships which are 6 metres or more in length, or designed to carry more than 12 persons, or are operating more than 15 nautical miles (nm) from land are known as ‘prescribed other Queensland regulated ships’.

    Note:For requirements relating to registering a Tender Ship Refer: Ship Rego > Types of Rego > Marine Registration

Shipping Registration Act 1981
Shipping Registration Act 1981
    Ships registered under the Shipping Registration Act 1981 are not required to be registered under the Transport Operations (Marine Safety) Regulation 2016.
Currency of Registration
Currency of Registration
    Ships are registered for a period of 12 months. The new business transaction date is the date the paperwork is processed by TMR. (e.g. If the ship is sold on the weekend the new business date is the date that the paperwork has been processed by TMR not the date the dealer sold the ship).
Ships 15m or More
Purpose of Use
Purpose of Use
    The purpose of use for a recreational ship will always be private.
    The purpose of use for other Queensland regulated ships (including prescribed) will be either of the following:
      • School activities only
      • Surf lifesaving
      • Other vessel use
Registration Category
Registration Category
    Reg Category - Rec Motor Boat
    Reg Category - Rec Motor Boat
      Rec Motor Boat means a boat that falls into any one of the following categories:
      • Sailing Boat
      • Displacement hulled boat
      • A boat with an engine capacity between 3.0 and 4.5kwbp.

    Reg Category - Rec Speed Boat
    Reg Category - Rec Speed Boat
      Rec Speed Boat means a boat/personal water craft that is solely powered by a motor in excess of 4.5kwbp with all of the following:
      • Planning or non-displacement hull
      • Capable of travelling in excess of 10 knots
      • Personal Water Craft

    Reg Category - Other Vessel
    Reg Category - Other Vessel
      Other Vessel means a boat/personal water craft that is:
      • less than 6 metres in length; and
      • designed to carry 12 persons or less; and
      • operating within 15 nautical miles (nm) or less from land

    Reg Category - Other Prescribed Vessel
    Reg Category - Other Prescribed Vessel
      Other Prescribed Vessel means a boat/personal water craft that is:
      • 6 metres or more in length; or
      • Designed to carry more than 12 persons; or
      • Operating more than 15 nautical miles (nm) from land

    For Conversion charts (hp - kW)
    Refer: Ship Rego > 2. Types of registration > Marine Registration > Measurements of Ship
Eligibility
Eligible Operators
Eligible Operators
    Only Australian companies can register ships. Ship registration for an overseas company is not allowed.

    Note:Ships must physically be located in Queensland and the organisation must provide a Queensland address.

    Rules
    Rules
      • Registration is only required for ships that are to operate in Queensland waters (regardless of owner's address).
      • Registration is not available or required for recreational ships currently registered under the law of another jurisdiction to non-Queensland residents.
      • Registration is not available or required for recreational ships from a foreign country in Queensland waters for less than 1 year

Duty and CTP
Duty and CTP
    There is no duty or CTP applied to ship registration.

Ship Rego Expired but not Cancelled
Ship Rego Expired but not Cancelled
    If the new business is for a ship that has expired registration but not cancelled, the CSO can cancel the registration and no further documentation from the current registered operator is required.

    Note on the Ship Registration Application that the previous registration was expired and has been cancelled for re-registration.
