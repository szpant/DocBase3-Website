Written Off Vehicle Inspection (4962)

What is the Written-off vehicle inspection?A written-off vehicle inspection (WOVI) is an inspection to verify the identity of a repairable write-off.
Who performs WOVIsWOVIs are provided by external service providers under a contract with TMR.

Currently, the only provider approved to provide WOVIs is Queensland Inspection Service (QIS).

Booking for WOVI can be made through QIS on phone 1300 722 411 or on-line at www.wovi.com.au.

QIS has approved inspection sites in:
Brisbane (Eagle Farm)
Gold Coast
Sunshine Coast (Kunda Park)
Bundaberg
Rockhampton
Mackay

Rules for WOVI
What a customer must bring
What a customer must bring
    To obtain a WOVI, the owner of the vehicle will need to provide a number of items:
    • TMR Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • If the person presenting the vehicle is not the owner or the vehicle, a letter of authorisation.
    • Standard EOI
    • Proof of Ownership
    • Proof of Repairs (receipts for any parts or other work performed to the vehicle)
    • Current Queensland Safety Certificate for the vehicle
Getting the vehicle to the WOVI site
Getting the vehicle to the WOVI site
    An unregistered vehicle permit must not be issued.

    To move a written off vehicle to obtain a Queensland safety certificate and WOVI, a completed Vehicle Registration Application form (F3518) and the CTP insurance certificate, for the required 6 or 12 month registration period, must be carried to enable the vehicle to be moved on the road.

    Note:A completed Vehicle Registration Application Form (F3518) is not required if attending a formless Centre for registration.

    Note:The written off vehicle must be driven from where it is stored, by the shortest and most direct route to obtain a Qld safety certificate and WOVI report, and then to a TMR customer service centre for registration (no diversions allowed). If the written off vehicle does not pass a safety certificate inspection or a WOVI, then the vehicle cannot be driven on the road and must be transported.

    If a vehicle has been presented at a CSC with a Qld safety certificate and WOVI and is unable to be registered (for whatever reason), an unregistered vehicle permit is not required for the vehicle to be driven back to its garaging address, provided the vehicle is no longer recorded as written-off in TICA and the customer has pre-purchased the correct CTP insurance.
WOVIs from other jurisdictionsWritten-off vehicle inspections from all Australian jurisdictions except New South Wales (refer below) are considered equivalent to the inspection in Queensland and can be accepted for the purpose of re-registering a repairable write-off

NSW WOVI

NSW currently has two types of WOVI:
  • Blue Slip
  • VIIU Inspections

The VIU must be contacted to find out if the WOVI can be accepted, or if a full Queensland Inspection is required.
Exemptions from WOVIIn a small number of cases, a WOVI may not be required and can be re-registered post an exemption being issued.

These include:
  • When a vehicle is damaged by hail only; and
  • When a vehicle was written-off before 30 September 2002.

Hail Damage
Hail Damage
    To be eligible for an exemption from obtaining a written-off vehicle inspection (WOVI) for a hail damaged vehicle, the customer must:-
    • Have been the registered operator of the vehicle immediately before the incident that resulted in the vehicle being recorded on the written-off vehicle register (WOVR); and
    • Retain the vehicle directly from the notifier who recorded the vehicle on the WOVR; and
    • Not cancel the registration (the registration of the vehicle is current).

    Eligible customers do not need to apply for an exemption. An exemption from the requirement to obtain a written-off vehicle inspection will be granted to the registered operator automatically. Registered operators are not required to complete an exemption application form or apply for the exemption.

    Eligible customers are able to continue driving their vehicle without restriction, and will receive a confirmation letter from TMR about the write-off and exemption (F5149) after TMR is notified of the write-off.

    Should registered operators not receive an exemption automatically they may apply for a WOVI exemption, in writing by completing 'Written-off Vehicle Inspection for Hail Damaged Vehicle Exemption Application (Form F5018) either:
    • Email: viunit@tmr.qld.gov.au; or
    • Fax: (07) 3066 5222.

    Customer presents without Form F5018
    Customer presents without Form F5018

      If the registered operator/representative presents written correspondence which includes the below information, form F5018 is not required:
      • VIN number
      • Registration number of the vehicle,
      • Contact phone number and
      • An explanation as to why the customer is applying for a WOVI exemption. (For example; hail damage and the owner is retaining the vehicle).


Any enquiries about exemptions for a WOVI, please contact the Vehicle Identification Unit on (07) 3097 3178.
