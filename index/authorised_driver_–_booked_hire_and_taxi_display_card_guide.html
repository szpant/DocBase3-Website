Authorised Driver – Booked Hire and Taxi Display Card Guide (7511)


About the Authorised Driver – Booked Hire and Taxi Display Card
What it isThe Authorised Queensland Taxi Driver display card was renamed to Authorised Driver – Booked Hire and Taxi Display Card (ADDC) to more accurately reflect the type of authorisation the driver holds. Display cards previously titled Authorised Queensland Taxi Driver remain valid until their expiry date. Production of the renamed ADDC card commenced from 13 January 2020.

The ADDC issued by TMR enables a passenger to verify that the driver is an authorised driver. The card displays a photo of the driver, the driver authorisation (DA) number and the DA expiry date. The card must be displayed in taxis in prescribed areas but may also be displayed outside of the prescribed areas or in other vehicles, like a booked hire vehicle. The card is automatically issued upon approval or renewal of Booked Hire/Taxi driver authorisation until the expiry date of the BHTX DA. There is no cost for the ADDC.

Prescribed areas are defined in the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Regulation 2018 Schedule 3.
Displaying the ADDC
Who must display the card?
Who must display the card?
    Taxi drivers must display the ADDC or an interim taxi driver display card when driving a taxi that is available for hire in any of the following prescribed areas—

    Brisbane, Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast, Ipswich, Redcliffe, Toowoomba, Bribie Island, Gympie, Bundaberg, Cairns, , Gladstone, Hervey Bay, Innisfail, Mackay, Maryborough, Mt Isa, Rockhampton, Townsville, Warwick and Yeppoon.

    It is offence for a person to display the card if they do not hold a current BHTX DA.
Who can voluntarily display the card?
Who can voluntarily display the card?
    The card can voluntarily be displayed in other taxi service areas outside of the prescribed areas or in other vehicles, such as booked hire vehicles.

    It is offence for a person to display the card if they do not hold a current BHTX DA.
How to obtain an ADDC
How the card is issued
How the card is issued
    ADDC’s are automatically issued to all holders of Booked Hire/Taxi driver authorisation (BHTX DA) when the following transactions occur:
    • BHTX DA is approved (new, add or renewal)
    • a BHTX DA term is extended.

    An ADDC is not automatically issued with a replacement Industry Authority Card. A replacement ADDC must be requested. Refer: Replacement card below.
Replacement cards
Replacement cards
    A customer must complete a Replacement Authorised Queensland Taxi Driver Display Card form (F4875) for a replacement card where:
    • the ADDC is damaged, destroyed, lost or stolen; or
    • the customers photo no longer resembles their appearance.

    A replacement card:
    • Application form (F4875) is not required if the request for replacement is within 90 days of the card being issued. The replacement can be issued by the call centre.
    • Can not be issued if the driver authorisation is suspended or cancelled

Interim taxi driver display card (taxi only)
Interim taxi driver display card (taxi only)
Interim taxi driver display card (taxi only)
    TMR provides interim taxi driver display cards to taxi operators in prescribed areas. Taxi operators can assign an interim display card to a taxi driver in a prescribed area but only in the following circumstances where the ADDC;-
    • issued to the driver has been damaged, lost or stolen and the driver has applied to TMR for a replacement card; or
    • has not yet been received by a driver and the driver has evidence (interim industry authority or industry authority card) that they hold BHTX DA.

    For more information refer to information bulletin PT330 via link in more information section below.
Enforcement
Intercepting a taxi in a prescribed area
Intercepting a taxi in a prescribed area
    When intercepting a taxi in a prescribed area authorised officers are to:
    • confirm the driver is the holder of a valid BHTX DA;
    • check that an ADDC or interim taxi driver display card is displayed in a prominent position, visible to all passengers;
    • confirm that the DA number is correct and that the photo matches the driver; and
    • for an interim taxi driver display card, check that the period of use has not exceeded the term of the assignment issued by the taxi operator.
Display of the card in any vehicle
Display of the card in any vehicle
    If a vehicle is intercepted and the ADDC is being displayed, confirm the driver is the holder of a valid BHTX DA.

    It is an offence to display the ADDC in any vehicle without holding a current BHTX DA.
