Credit Balances in TICA customer Accounts (4632)

What it isA deposit of monetary value in a customer's account.
Why RequiredThe below procedures have been developed to ensure that credit balances in TICA customer accounts are managed effectively and efficiently.

All officers who receipt money in TICA and officers who monitor TICA customer accounts and refunds should ensure they follow this procedure.
Requirements
Customer Account Report (AA62BE)
Customer Account Report (AA62BE)
    A Customer Account Report (AA62BE) is generated daily for each Customer Service Centre, Customer Service Direct and Agency. These reports are produced by TRAILS and list any action on a customer account that has left a credit/debit balance.

    Staff Requirements
    Staff Requirements
      CSC Manager/supervisor/banking officer is required to:
      • Ensure the report is printed daily
      • Review each item listed against any documentation
      • Verify the reason for the DR/CR against the Customer Account Screen
      • Make a note on action taken in the remarks column. Include the registration number of the vehicle where there is a cancellation of registration
      • Sign and date the report and record USERID
      • File and retain the report. (when all items have been finalised)

      Reports must be actioned by an officer who was not the original author of the transaction.

      0-28 days
      0-28 days
        CSC's to ensure that a follow-up system be implemented to manage accounts. Customer has this time period to confirm inspection times etc.

      28-56 days
      28-56 days
        All customer accounts with a credit balance where the amount has not changed after 28 days will require a business action to be taken. This may be written or telephone advice to the customer whose account is in credit. Customers will have a maximum of 28 days notice to respond to TMR. Within the period to 56 days, every endeavour is to be made to clear all customer accounts which are in credit.

      56-84 days
      56-84 days
        Continue follow-up procedures to clear customer accounts which are still in credit after a total of 56 days. Business areas should refund these funds to customers in this time period.

      84 days and more
      84 days and more
        After a total of 84 days or approximately 3 months, no monies are to remain in customer accounts. CAM will review the account and all credit amounts will be actioned or refunded in consultation with the relevant CSC. Outstanding items requiring further action after 84 days should be noted with an updated text message detailing why items have not been finalised.

Rules
Business Processes
Business Processes
    Each business area should implement business processes to ensure that credit balances in TICA customer accounts are promptly applied to products.

    Payments made under the Planned Payment Scheme are exempt from this policy.

    These business processes should include:
    • Customers should only put money into their TICA customer accounts for an approved purpose. Generally money will only be put into a customer account in anticipation of purchasing a TMR product.
    • Credit balances in TICA customer accounts should be applied to a product or the customer contacted regarding their intentions for the money within 28 days.
    • It is the responsibility of the business unit that accepted the payment into the customer account to apply the money to a customer product or refund the money.
    • Credit balances $10.01 and over should not remain in TICA customer accounts in excess of 3 months.
    • A refund should not be granted if the amount is less than $10.01 unless specifically requested by the customer.
Using customer credit to pay other customer's invoices
Using customer credit to pay other customer's invoices
    When a customer has a credit in their account that should be used to pay another customer's invoice (eg. a waived infringement that has been allocated to the vehicle operator correctly and was previously paid) the invoice should be paid by using the "other customer unpaid" function on the payment screen.

    Any customer enquiries to the Call Centre are to be actioned and paid after return of Payment of Infringement from account form
When to Receipt Money in Customer Accounts
When to Receipt Money in Customer Accounts
    Money should not be receipted in customer accounts unless necessary. The following circumstances are considered appropriate:
    • Registration renewal payments in advance. The customer may pay a renewal in advance if the customer is unavailable for the 6 weeks prior to the expiry date.
    • Periodic Registration
    • Driving examination bookings
    • Vehicle inspection bookings
    • Transactions that are unable to be completed due to TICA technical issues and processing difficulties
    • Missing documentation for work submitted by off-line agencies
