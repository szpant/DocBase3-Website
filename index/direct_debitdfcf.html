Direct Debit (5346)


What it isDirect Debit is an automatic way for individual customers to pay their registration renewal every one, 3, 6 or 12 months from a nominated credit card or bank account. The payments can only be made every one, 3, 6 or 12 months and align with the registration pay term.
How does it work?If a customer and vehicle/ship meet the eligibility requirements listed below for direct debit:
  • They can enrol in and change their direct debit on the TMR website according to the rules outlined below.
  • Their Registration Notice will include the option of paying by direct debit, or if the vehicle/ship is already enrolled, it will advise that the renewal is being paid by direct debit.

Once a registration is added, the direct debit payments will be requested from the customer's nominated account as outlined below in Rules > Payments.

Further information can be found on the Direct Debit Support Materials SharePoint page.

Immediate payments
    Customers can add their vehicles to the direct debit scheme until their registration expires, regardless of the registration term selected. However, if their registration is expiring within the next 18 days it is too late for their account to be swept so they must make an immediate payment using a Visa/MasterCard/American Express branded card.




    Note:Online payment for immediate payments is preferred, however if a customer presents at a CSC to make the payment it can be accepted.

    After the customer has enrolled in direct debit and added the vehicle they will be sent an 'Add registration successful with immediate payment' email advising that a one-off payment is required, they will be instructed to use the "Pay using your registration number" online service.
