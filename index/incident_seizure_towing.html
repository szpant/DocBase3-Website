Crash/Seizure Towing (7304)


Crash/Seizure Towing
What it isThe Tow Truck Actand Regulations are designed to govern the conduct of tow trucks and/or the operators of tow trucks within regulated areas at:
  • the scene of road crashes,
  • police authorised seizures and

It creates the requirement for tow truck operations performing the above functions within regulated areas to:
  • Have a valid Tow Truck licence
  • Use appropriately accredited drivers and assistants
  • Have compliant business premises
  • Have a suitable and compliant holding yards
  • Use compliant vehicles as tow trucks

Definitions
Industry Authority Definitions
Licence ConditionsIn addition to the standard licence conditions a vehicle cannot be towed from the scene of a crash:
  • Unless the consent of the registered operator, their agent or an authorised officer is obtained and evidenced by a signed towing authority
  • If there are any passengers inside the vehicle being towed
Towing AuthorityPrior to towing a vehicle from the scene of an incident/seizure the driver must complete and provide a copy of the towing authority to the driver, their agent or an authorised officer.

The towing authority must:
  • Specify the address where the vehicle is to be towed
  • Where the location is a business, the name of the business must be noted on the authority
  • Be signed by the driver, their agent or an authorised officer

The driver cannot obtain authorities for multiple vehicles and then commence removing vehicles. The first vehicle must be removed before and additional authorities can be sought.
Rules
Authority to Tow
Authority to Tow
    The consent of the registered operator, their agent or authorised person must be obtained before the vehicle can be towed from the scene.
Conduct at the Scene of an Incident/Seizure
Conduct at the Scene of an Incident/Seizure
    The driver/assistant at the scene of an incident, must not either directly or indirectly:
    • cause or threaten wilful injury to a person
    • cause or threaten wilful damage to a person's property;
    • intimidate or harass a person
    • abuse or insult a person
    • prejudice the delivery of first aid or medical treatment to a person who is receiving, or needs to receive, first aid or medical treatment
    • obtain, or attempt to obtain, the signature of an owner of a motor vehicle involved in the incident, or the owner's agent, on a towing authority, if the owner or agent appears unable to make an informed decision about whether or not to sign the towing authority
    • Disclose information about the incident/seizure to any other than the registered operator or their agent, an authorised officer or the tow truck licence holder

    A driver/assistant must not remain at the scene of an incident/seizure if they are not in a tow truck capable of towing the vehicle/s involved unless they are administering first aid.
Record Keeping
Record Keeping
    For each vehicle towed from an incident/seizure the tow truck licence/permit holder must record the prescribed particulars of the vehicle within 48 hours of the particulars being provided to or becoming known to the licence holder.

    Prescribed details include:
    • Name and address of the person providing information about the crash or seizure
    • Place date and approximate time of the incident/seizure
    • Make and registration/identification number of the towed vehicle
    • Registration/identification number of the tow truck attending the incident/seizure
    • Particulars about any property found in the vehicle (including and radio or sound production device)

    This information must be retained securely at the business premises specified on the the tow truck licence/permit for at least one year after the record was created.
