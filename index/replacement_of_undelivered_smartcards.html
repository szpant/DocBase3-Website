Replacement of undelivered smartcards (7637)

NameReplacement of undelivered smartcards
WhenFebruary 2021
BackgroundTMR has been advised that some smartcards (Driver licence, Photo ID Card and Marine licence), High Risk Work licences, Blue Cards and address labels requested in December 2020 may not have been delivered to customers. The affected cards/labels were part of a batch that had an active status on 18 December 2020.

As part of TMR's investigation, the Customer Contact Centre (CCC) conducted sample call outs to customers to confirm receipt/non-receipt of cards in order to gauge impacts.

Following the call outs, a decision was made for all smartcards which had an active status on 18 December to be replaced (excluding those that had already been replaced or confirmed receipt via the call outs).

The replacements will occur as follows:
  • TMR Smartcards will be automatically replaced via batch
  • High Risk Work Licence and Blue Cards will only be replaced if a customer contacts Blue Card Services or Work Safe to request a replacement (this is because TMR cannot replace these products)

A decision was made not to automatically replace the potentially impacted address labels because:
  • the addresses have been updated (therefore customers will receive correspondence from us)
  • there were significant technical complexities in being able to automatically replace labels
  • the amount of time that has passed since the file was created
  • the majority of customers who did not receive their label would have contacted TMR to request a free replacement
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers
Customers
    • Affected TMR Smartcard customers have been sent a tailored email or letter (depending on their product type) advising of the replacement and to destroy their old card (including if they received one in December)
    • No communications have been sent to High Risk Work licence customers - this was decided by Work Safe Queensland as they were not aware of any impact to their customers.
    • Communications have been sent to Blue Card customers (who have not already replaced their card) advising them to contact Blue Card Services for a free replacement if their card was not received.
Operators
Operators
    • May receive queries from customers about non-receipt of their card
    • Before replacing, operators should check TICA to see if a replacement has already been requested
    • If not replaced, follow the standard 'not received in mail' process
    • If replaced by TMR, advise that an email or letter was sent and use the FAQs below to answer any queries
