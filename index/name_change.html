Name Change (4736)


Name Change
When Notification to TMR RequiredA customer must notify the department within fourteen (14) days of a change of name.

Note:Notification within 14 days is not required for Photo Identification Card, however it is recommended.
Updating Customer Products
Updating customer products
Updating customer products
    All customer products held by the person must be updated to show the name change. e.g. Licence/ Industry Authority/ Card 18+/Photo Identification Card, etc.

    All products (laminated and smartcard) held by the customer must be returned for name changes.

    For information on Name Formats Refer: Customer > 1.Customer > Customer Details > Customer Name & Address Standards > Name Formats

    Smartcard Presented
    Smartcard Presented
      The customer is required to apply for a replacement Smartcard but is not required to pay a replacement fee.
      • Replacement action in TICA - Change customer details

      Replacement Smartcard - Reason No.5

    Smartcard NOT presented
    Smartcard NOT presented
      If the customer is unable to return the Licence/ Accreditation/Authority/ Card 18+/Photo Identification Card they must, complete a declaration explaining the reason why they are unable to do so.
      • The customer is required to apply for a replacement smartcard and MUST pay the prescribed replacement fee.
      • Replacement action in TICA - Lost/Stolen/Destroyed
      Replacement Smartcard - Reasons No. 1, 2, 3

Recording of Name Change Document

Individual - Change of Name Documents
What it isThese documents establish official evidence of the customer's change of name.
When Required
When Required
When Required
    A name change document is required when:
    • The same name is not shown on all EOI documents presented (e.g. name on Category A document different to name on Category B documents)
    • The applicant is applying for a TMR product in a different name to the name shown on EOI documents presented (e.g. Customer applying for Photo ID card in married name but all EOI documents are in customer's birth name)
    • A customer requests a TMR customer product in a name different to that shown on TICA

    Note:A marriage certificate issued by the Registrar of the relevant Birth, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) is acceptable EOI for a customer wishing to change their surname either the assumed surname of their spouse, or double barrelled surname that is their maiden name and spouse's family name (combined using a single space or hyphen).

    Refer: Customer > 3. Change Customer Detail > Name Change > Documents Required > Documents > Marriage Certificate

    Note:Change of name documents are listed in the Proof of Identity Framework as Evidence of Identity operating in the community (non-standard Proof of Identity for marriage or legal name change showing link with previous names). Therefore, while Change of Name documents are not classed as Category A or B EOI documents, they provide evidence of the link between names for customers who present EOI documents with different names on them/apply for a product in a name different to the name on EOI documents.

    Customers must still provide full evidence of identity in addition to a Change of Name document.

    Where a customer is unable to provide a Change of Name document there are a number of scenarios in which variations can be considered.

    Refer: When Required and When Required > Variation to Standard EOI Requirements for Individual Change of Name below
About Name Change Documents
About Name Change Documents
About Name Change Documents
    The documents must be originals and:
    • Clearly identify the customer and the issuing organisation
    • Provide a link between both names
    • Be issued in Australia (including external territories)
      Except for overseas Marriage Certificate      * or Divorce Papers** - Refer: details below
Documents Required
Documents
Documents
    • Application form (if application is received at a non-formless centre) (Driver Licence Application/Renewal F3000 or Change of Customer Details F4214)
    • Change of Name Certificate issued by the Registrar of the relevant Births, Deaths & Marriages
    • An amended Birth Certificate (with notations) issued by the Registrar of the relevant Births, Deaths & Marriages
    • Marriage Certificate issued by the Registrar of the relevant Births, Deaths & Marriages
      • Can only be used for change of surname to their spouses surname or a combination of their own and their spouses full surnames. This can either be hyphenated or with a space separating the names. For example Johnson and Smith could be Johnson Smith, Johnson-Smith, Smith Johnson or Smith-Johnson.
      • A change of name certificate issued by BDM is required if the customer wishes to combine their names into a new family name. For example, if Johnson and Smith want to combine their names together creating a new surname of Smithson.
    • *Overseas Marriage Certificate with registration number & official crest and presented with either of the following:
      • 1 Category A document in the married name
      • 2 Category B documents in the married name

      If not in English, a Translation is required
    • Civil Partnership Certificate or Relationship Certificate issued by the Registrar of the relevant Births, Deaths & Marriages.
    • Divorce papers Decree Nisi or Absolute is acceptable providing it shows the name being reverted to
      **      Overseas Divorce Papers Decree Nisi or Absolute is acceptable providing it shows the name being reverted to and is presented with either of the following:
      • Category A document in the name being reverted to
      • Category B document in the name being reverted to

      If not in English, a Translation is required
    • Deed Poll (issued prior to 01/02/2004)
      Until 2004, a person could change their name by filing a deed poll in the Supreme Court of Qld.

      The Births, Deaths and Marriages Act, which has been in force since 1 February 2004, removed the option to change a person's name by registration. Therefore a deed poll document older than this date can not be used to record a change of name on a TMR product, and an alternative document needs to be presented.
    • Children's birth Certificate issued by the Registrar of the relevant Births, Deaths & Marriages. This can be used for a married/maiden name change of a mother where both names are displayed on the certificate.

Organisation - Change of Name
When an Organisation can change their name
When an Organisation can Change Their Name
When an Organisation can Change Their Name
    An organisation may change their name on TICA/TICA only if:
    • The organisation has retained the same registered number e.g. ACN, BN, IA
      and
    • The legal entity attached to the CRN remains the same. Otherwise the CRN must be closed and a new CRN created and any products transferred to the new CRN.
    Or
    • An incorporated association has migrated its incorporation from the Associations Incorporation Act 1981 to the Corporations Act 2001
