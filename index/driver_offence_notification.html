Driver Offence Notification (7576)

About Driver Offence Notification (DON)
What are they?The driver offence notification (DON) is a courtesy notice emailed to registered operators to notify them of traffic offences committed by somebody else using their vehicle when they are intercepted roadside. The DON will be sent when a traffic offence is finalised (i.e. paid in full/VIP or court result). This service in addition to camera detected offences which already get sent to the registered operator.

The DON will contain the offender's full name and details of the offence including the date/time and the suburb where the offence occurred. This information is based on the details recorded on the Infringement/breach. No other details about the offender will be on the DON or can be provided to the registered operator.

TMR is authorised to provide these details to the registered operator under Section 77AAA of the Transport Operations Road Use Management Act 1995.
Why are they sent?DONs are a road safety initiative that are intended to:
  • provide registered operators with greater visibility of the driving behaviour of people using their vehicle/s
  • encourage road safety conversations
  • help people make more informed decisions about who they let drive their vehicle
  • help organisations, such as fleet and heavy vehicle operators to better monitor and control the operation of their vehicles
  • help parents or carers to more effectively monitor the driving behaviour of newly licensed young drivers using the family car.

It is intended that this information will help encourage road safety conversations and lead to better road safety outcomes on Queensland roads.
DON preferencesThe default preference for receiving DONs is 'opt in'.

This means that registered operators with eContact details recorded will be automatically opted in to the service unless they have previously requested to opt out. The “Registered for driver offence notifications” check box will be ticked in the DON preference screen in COAM.

If the same customer removes their email address, they will automatically be opted out of the DON service. The DON preference screen in COAM will display a message that the customer has no email address recorded and the “Registered for driver offence notifications” box will not appear.

DON preferences are managed separately to eNotices and eReminders. Preferences can be updated either upon request by the customer or automatically.

Note:When a customer requests to opt in or out, an email will be sent advising them of the preference recorded.
Where is the DON preference recorded?The DON preference is recorded in the Customer Online Account Management (COAM) user interface which is accessed via the following TICA screens:
  • Individual Customer hub
  • Organisation Customer hub

Note:A preference is recorded by selecting or deselecting the 'Registered to receive driver offence notifications' check box.
Where a customer has no email address recorded the tick in the preference box will not display.

DON preference history will be recorded as follows:
  • COAM (View Preference history) will display all requested DON preference changes made online and via COAM
  • Online services (View My History) will display all requested DON preferences changes made online.
Rules
EOITo add/remove/update email addresses or request to opt/in out of the DON service, the following applies:

Individuals:
  • Standard EOI

Organisations:
  • Standard EOI for the organisation; and
  • If the request is being completed over the phone, in person or in writing, the person must provide their name and confirm if they are an authorised representative. These details do not need to be recorded in TICA.

Note:Specific EOI requirements apply for organisations who are adding/updating their email address online. Refer: Organisations (online) below
How can a preference be updatedRegistered operators can request to update their DON preference at any time. The request to opt in or out can be made via the following methods:
  • Online service*
  • Over the phone
  • In person
  • In writing (email/post)

*Note:Individuals can view/manage their DON preferences online. Organisations can add their email address using a new online service which will automatically opt them in. Refer: Organisations (online) below for more information. Organisations can only opt out by phone, in writing or in person.
DON generation
DON Generation Rules
DON Generation Rules
    • Offence must be intercepted roadside in Queensland and appear on an offender's traffic history
    • Offender must be someone other than a registered operator
    • Registration number must have been included on infringement/breach*
    • Must be a Queensland registered vehicle
    • DON is emailed to primary registered operator (regardless of whether they are a legal entity or not)
    • Registered Operator must have an email address recorded in eContacts
    • Registered operator must not have ‘requested to Opt out’ of receiving DONs (refer below DON preferences)
    • Offender must not be suppressed or deceased
    • Registered operator must not be deceased or a closed organisation
    • Offence must be finalised (paid in full/VIP, sent to SPER, Court result)
    • Any subsequent changes to the infringement/breach after it is finalised do not impact the DON (for example, we don’t send another notice if the infringement is withdrawn after it has been sent to SPER).
    • The offence information on the DON is derived from the infringement/breach screen
    • A DON is sent to the registered operator as at the date of offence (if there was a delay in a transfer being actioned, the previous registered operator may receive the DON).
    • If a registered operator thinks they have received the DON in error (for example, had possession of the vehicle at the time, vehicle was previously written off), then a data entry error may have occurred. This is because the registration number on the DON is derived from the Infringement/Breach screen. If the police officer wrote the wrong registration number on the ticket, then the wrong registered operator will receive the DON. To confirm if this may have occurred, check the following:
    1. Individual Customer Hub > Online Account Management
    • Registration number on the DON
    • Make, Body type and Colour details on the DON (if available)
    2. Go to the Vehicle Registration Hub > enter the registration number and compare the vehicle details with those displayed on the DON.

    If the vehicle description on the DON is different to the vehicle description in TICA, or the registered operator thinks they've received the DON in error, complete the DON Data Entry Error Template


    *Note:Not all breaches will result in a DON. Offences like unlicensed driving or drink or drug driving will not be notified at this stage.
Organisations
Online
Online
    An authorised representative for an organisation (must be an individual) must authenticate to first level online prior to adding or updating an email address on behalf of an organisation via Online services. The organisation must have a current ACN to be eligible for the online service.

    The individual will:
    1. Login with level 1 security (Full name, DOB, CRN)
    2. Login to level 1 security for the organisation (name, ACN, CRN)
    3. Respond to organisation questions
    4. Add/Update organisation email

    When the individual adds or updates an organisation email address, a confirmation email is
    automatically sent to the new email address. Additionally, when the email address is
    updated, a confirmation email is also sent to the old email address.

    The CRN of the individual who updated the email using the online service will be recorded in COAM.

    Note:This will not occur for emails captured manually.

    Note:An authorised representative is a Manager, Director, Secretary or another
    type of officer authorised to by the organisation to act on its behalf.
Manual
Manual
    Organisations that do not have an ACN, or have an interstate business address recorded in TICA are unable to use the online service. These organisations can register by email, phone or in person.

    Request received by email
    Request received by email
      An authorised representative can submit an organisations email address by emailing DON@tmr.qld.gov.au with:
      • Organisation Name
      • Street address
      • Current ABN or ACN
      • Organisation CRN
      • Organisation email address
      • Declaration of authority to represent (must include representatives full name, title and phone number)

      Note:Details must match TMR records exactly.
      An authorised representative is a Manager, Director, Secretary or another
      type of officer authorised to by the organisation to act on its behalf.

Mandatory RequirementsIt is mandatory that all registered operators who are requesting to opt in to the service must have their email address recorded in the first instance.

If the customer tries to register for DON via Online services or COAM and doesn't have an email recorded, a message will populate telling the user to add an email address.
