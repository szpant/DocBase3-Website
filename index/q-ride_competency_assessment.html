Q-Ride Competency Assessment (7098)


About Q-Ride
What it isQ-Ride is a competency based training and assessment program for applicants seeking to obtain a motorcycle licence.
About Q-Ride
Q-Ride Training and Assessment
Test TypesThe Q-Ride training curriculum includes three Q-Ride courses:
  • Pre-Learner - completed to get a class RE learner licence
  • Restricted (RE) course - completed to get a class RE provisional or open licence
  • Unrestricted (R) course - completed to get a class R provisional or open licence
When Q-Ride is required
When RequiredA Q-Ride course must be completed for applicants who reside within a 100km radius of a Q-Ride provider and are applying for a:
  • Motorcycle learner licence
  • Class RE motorcycle licence
  • Class R motorcycle licence
  • Licence holder from any country that is not a recognised country or experienced driver recognised country applying to transfer any class of licence
  • Applying for Provisional/Probationary/Open licence as a returning rider
  • Applicant is requested to undertake a Q-Ride course by the Chief Executive after a period of disqualification

Applicants who reside outside the Q-Ride radius may elect to undertake a Q-Ride course refer to when not required for further information.
When Q-Ride is Not Required
When not RequiredA Q-Ride course is not required if the applicant:

  • Resides outside a 100km radius of a Q-Ride provider and are applying for a:
    • Motorcycle learners (exemption applies)
    • Class RE or R motorcycle licence the applicant opts to undertake a Q-Safe practical assessment
Refer below: Pre-Leaner Exemptions
  • Is a Qld Police Officer applying for a class RE or R motorcycle licence as QPS conduct their own testing.
  • Is an Australian applicant who has in the last five years held or passed a practical driving assessment for an Australian motorcycle learners or class RE/R provisional/open licence and can provide confirmation of passing from the testing jurisdiction
  • Is a currently serving ADF officer who holds a current ADF RE/R provisional/open licence equivalent to the same class being applied for for at least 12 months. Where the applicant is applying for a Class R but has not held the ADF class for 12 months they are able to be issued a RE without a Q-Ride
      Example:
      Example:
        ADF licence = Class R

        Applicant holds no other licence

        ADF licence has been held for 6 months

        Licence class issued without test = RE

        Reason - an R class requires 1 year of RE
  • Is a NZ applicant who has held a a NZ provisional/open class RE/R licence in the last five years
  • Is a foreign applicant who has held a current foreign P/P1/P2 Type or Open licence equivalent in the last 5 years for the class applied for
    and
  • The licence is from a country approved by Austroads as a recognised or experience driver recognised country
    Refer: link below for a list of countries:

    Austroads Approved Countries

    Or
    Have at any time passed an Australian practical riding test for the class being applied for and can provide confirmation of passing for the testing jurisdiction
  • Is a foreign applicant who holds a valid Class RE or R foreign licence and holds the position of:
    • Consular General
    • Deputy Consul General
    • Consol
    • Vice Consul
    From any country or is a staff member of the Taipei Economic and Cultural Office
Undertaking a Q-Ride Course

EligibilityThe licence held by a customer determines which Q-Ride course they may undertake, refer to the following table:

Q-Ride courseEligibility requirements
Pre-Learner courseHolds a current provisional, probationary or open licence of another class (Qld or non-Qld) and has held that licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years.
Restricted (RE) courseQld licence holders
Holds a current class RE learner licence issued on or after 1 October 2016, and:
  • has passed the motorcycle hazard perception test.
  • if they completed the Pre-Learner course, has held that licence for at least 3 months in the last 5 years.
  • if they were exempt from the Pre-Learner course, has held that licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years.

OR
  • Holds a class RE learner licence issued before 1 October 2016 (no minimum tenure requirements).
  • Has passed the motorcycle hazard perception test.

Non-Qld licence holders
Holds a current:
  • Non-Qld licence that corresponds to a class RE learner licence issued on or after 1 October 2016, and has held that licence for at least 3 months in the last 5 years.
  • Non-Qld licence that corresponds to a class RE learner licence issued before 1 October 2016 (no minimum tenure requirements).
  • Non-Qld licence that corresponds to a class RE or R provisional, probationary or open licence.
  • From 1 July 2021 customers will only be eligible to complete a Q-Ride RE Course if they have passed the Queensland hazard perception test. Customers can only take the hazard perception test if they have a Queensland licence.
Unrestricted (R) courseQld licence holders
Holds a current:
  • Class RE provisional, probationary or open licence issued on or after 1 October 2016, and has held that licence for at least 2 years in the last 5 years.
  • Class RE provisional, probationary or open licence issued before 1 October 2016, and has held that licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years.
  • A valid licence and a returning driver certificate authorising them to learn to ride a class R motorcycle.
  • Class RE learner, provisional, probationary or open licence for less than two years and an approved Special Need Rider's Certificate Class R Motorcycle authorising them to learn to ride a class R motorcycle and has passed the motorcycle hazard perception test.

Non-Qld licence holders
Holds a current:
  • Non-Qld licence that corresponds to a class RE provisional, probationary or open licence issued on or after 1 October 2016, and has held that licence for at least 2 years in the last 5 years.
  • Non-Qld licence that corresponds to a class RE provisional, probationary or open licence issued before 1 October 2016, and has held that licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years.
  • Non-Qld licence that corresponds to a class R provisional, probationary or open licence.

Checking eligibility
Checking eligibility
    Before enrolling in Q-Ride, the applicant may check their eligibility to undertake the course by using the Check My Q-Ride Course Eligibility online service and obtain a Q-Ride Course Eligibility Certificate. The certificate may be provided to the Q-Ride registered service provider to confirm eligibility.

    Customers must hold a Queensland driver licence to use this service.

    Refer: https://www.service.transport.qld.gov.au/checkqridecourseeligibility
Pre-learner Exemptions
Resides Outside the 100km Q-Ride Radius
Resides Outside the 100km Q-Ride Radius
    Applicant for a motorcycle learner who live outside the 100km Q-Ride radius are exempt the pre-learner course. Applicants can request the exemption to be added at a CSC or over the phone, which will allow them to complete the written test either in person at a CSC or online.

    The exemption is based on the residential address recorded in TICA. No form is required to apply the exemption.

    As TMR does not offer an equivalent via the Q-Safe program the applicant is required to hold their motorcycle licence for at least 12 months.

    This can be reduced to the standard three months by completing a pre-learner course.
Returning Drivers
Returning Drivers
    A returning driver is not required to undertake the pre-learner course to retain their motorcycle licence.
Overseas Class RE/R Licence Holders
Overseas Class RE/R Licence Holders
    Overseas licence holders with a class RE or R provisional, probationary or open licence issued from their country are not required to undertake the pre-learner course.

    Those customers with a non-recognised overseas licence are required to complete the written and relevant Q-Ride assessment only.
FeesFees are not regulated by TMR and are set by each Q-Ride registered service provider.

Enrolling in a Q-Ride Course
Enrolling for Q-Ride Training
Enrolling for Q-Ride Training
    A Q-Ride registered service provider may:
    • check the applicant's eligibility via a TMR online eligibility check system,
      or
    • require the applicant to complete a Declaration of Eligibility for Q-Ride Training (F3961). Form F3961 is available from the Q-Ride registered service provider or TMR website.

    From 29 March 2021, all motorcycle learner licence holders will be required to complete the new motorcycle hazard perception test before they can upgrade their licence.

    Motorcycle learners who are eligible to take their Q-Ride RE Course, or Q-Safe RE practical riding test before 1 July 2021 will be able to take the HPT either before or after their Q-Ride Course or Q-Safe test. This is to limit the impacts the new HPT requirement has on Q-Ride and Q-Safe bookings.

    From 1 July 2021, motorcycle learner licence holders will only be able to take the Q-Ride RE Course, or Q-Safe RE practical riding test after they have passed the HPT.

    From 1 July 2021 the eligibility checker will be updated to include the HPT as a pre-requisite to the Q-Ride RE Course.

    Customers who take the hazard perception test after the RE Course between 29 March and 30 June 2021 will need to apply in person at a TMR Customer Service Centre and bring evidence of their identity to upgrade their licence. Registered Service Providers will still be able to lodge the Certificate of Competency through the Q-Ride online system for these students.

    From 1 July 2021, all applicants must have completed the HPT first. Any customers who return an ineligible result should be informed of the HPT requirement and asked to complete this test before they re-book.

    Note:F3961 requires the applicant to declare that their licence is current at time of undertaking training. If a false declaration is made the Q-Ride Certificate is void. Refer below: Action to be taken if Q-Ride Competency Declaration is invalid

    If the applicant is unsure of their licence history details, they may purchase their licence history from the relevant licensing authority.

    An applicant for Q-Ride Training must enrol with their preferred Q-Ride registered service provider.

    Q-Ride Registered Service Providers are listed on the QLD government website at:
    http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/licensing/motorcycles/getting/trainer/index.html#
Acceptance of Enrolment
Acceptance of Enrolment
    The Q-Ride Registered Service Provider must be satisfied the applicant is eligible to undertake the Q-Ride training course.

    The Q-Ride registered service provider can confirm eligibility by:
    • requesting the applicant provide a Q-Ride Course Eligibility Certificate from the Check my Q-Ride Course Eligibility online service,
      or
    • entering the applicant's details into the online eligibility check system,
      or
    • requesting the applicant complete and sign a Declaration of Eligibility for Q-Ride Training (F3961).
Competency Declarations
About the Competency Declaration
About the Competency Declaration
    What it isA competency declaration is evidence that the applicant has successfully completed a Q-Ride course.
    How is it issued
    		Electronically
    A competency declaration may be recorded directly into TICA using an on-line service by the Q-ride provider. A copy should be printed and given to the applicant. A copy is also recorded in the customers TMR on-line account under My Documents.

    Paper
    Paper Competency Declarations are issued in duplicate and distributed as follows:
    White CopyApplicant copy (a copy is retained by CSC if presented in person)
    Yellow CopyRetained by Q-Ride registered service provider and attached to the applicant's training record.
    Validity
    Details required to be on Declaration
    Details required to be on Declaration
      A Q-Ride Competency Declaration (either paper or electronic) is only valid if:
      • All sections have been completed with NO alterations
      • It is in the approved format (Refer to Sample)
      • It was issued no longer than 12 months ago
      • The applicant was eligible to undertake Q-Ride training

      If the customer does not obtain their class RE learner licence or upgrade their licence, depending on the course completed, within 1 year they will need to retake the course.
    Action to be taken if Competency Declaration is invalid
    Action to be taken if Competency Declaration is invalid
      Invalid Q-Ride competency declarations must not be processed.

      Record on the Q-Ride Competency Declaration report:
      • Details of the problem
      • Applicant's details
      • Q-Ride Competency Declaration details
      Q-Ride competency declaration report_v2


      Email the report to Q-Ride Administrator
      • Return the white copy of the Q-Ride Competency Declaration to the applicant

    Lost or damagedIf an applicant states that they have lost or damaged their Q-Ride Competency Declaration, they must apply for a replacement to the Q-Ride registered service provider who trained them.

    If the lost or damaged declaration was issued by a Q-Ride registered service provider who has exited the scheme, the person is required to:
    • Provide a CSC officer with a completed statutory declaration about the loss or damage of the competency declaration;
      and
    • Provide Standard EOI.

    CSC Officer satisfied with information provided
    CSC Officer satisfied with information provided
      If the CSC officer is satisfied with the information and documentation provided, they must contact the Q-Ride Administrator

      The Q-Ride Administrator will arrange for a copy of the competency declaration to be provided to the CSC officer to enable the issue of the relevant licence class.
    CSC Officer NOT satisfied with information provided
    CSC Officer NOT satisfied with information provided
      If the CSC officer is not satisfied with the information or evidence of identity provided, a report must be emailed to the Q-Ride Administrator immediately.

Applying for a Motorcycle learner/licenceThe issue of a competency declaration does not entitle the applicant to automatically learn/ride the relevant class of motorcycle. All pre-requisites must be met and the class added to their licence before they can do so.
