Operator Accreditation Guide (6041)


About Operator Accreditation (OA)
What it isOperator accreditation is a qualification that an operator of a public passenger service of a particular kind must attain and maintain to provide a service of that kind.

Operator accreditation categories
Operator accreditation categories
    Operator accreditation categories include:
    • General Services
    • School Scheduled Services - Prescribed Services
    • School Scheduled Services - Unfunded/Declared Area
    • School Scheduled Services - Unfunded/Non-declared Area
    • Urban Scheduled Services
    • Minor Scheduled Services
    • Long Distance Scheduled Services
    • Courtesy Transport Services
    • Community Transport Services
    • Motorcycle Tourist Services
When is it requiredAn individual, partnership or organisation that operates a public passenger service must hold operator accreditation unless exempt.

From 1 October 2017 operator accreditation is not required for taxi, limousine or booked hire services. Operators who provide any other type of service will need to maintain their operator accreditation for those services. Operators are not required to surrender their taxi or limousine operator accreditation it will automatically expire.

Exempt Services
Exempt Services
    Certain services are exempt from operator accreditation.

    Further information is available in -
    PT 23 OA and DA Exemptions

    Please contact your Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office before providing specific advice to a customer.

In person
In person

Remote areas, interstate and overseas applicant requirements
Remote areas, interstate and overseas applicant requirements
    Applicants who apply from a remote area, interstate and overseas must satisfy the following requirements by providing:
    • a completed Operator Accreditation Application (F2982)
    • witnessed copies of EOI documents (refer below)
    • documentation specified in the application
    • Payment of the required fees

    Witnessing of EOI document
    Witnessing of EOI document
      The EOI documents must be endorsed with the words “I have sighted the original documents and certify this to be a true copy of the original”

      The witness must sign and print their full name, position title/designation and contact details (including office stamp). All documents are to be witnessed in English or have an English translation.

      An approved witness is either a:
      • Medical Practitioner
      • Police Officer
      • Consular or Ambassadorial Officer
      • Solicitor, Barrister or Judge
      • Justice of the Peace
      • A Commissioner for Declarations
      • Notary Public or a person authorised by law to witness and sign declarations


    Applications can be posted to the Atherton Customer Service Centre
Eligibility requirements
Who can be issued Operator Accreditation
Who can be issued Operator Accreditation
    Operator accreditation must be held by a legal entity:
    • It can be held by individuals, partnerships, corporations, incorporated associations, trustees.
    • It cannot be held in a business or trading name.

    Organisations must have a nominated executive. A nominated executive officer must be provided by applicants who are a:
    • Corporation
    • Incorporated Association
    • Trust/Superannuation fund

    A local council must provide a responsible person.

    Refer: EOI, Signature and Criminal History Checks below

    Training Requirements
    Training Requirements
      To obtain full operator accreditation, applicants must:
      • successfully complete the TMR Operator Accreditation Workbook;
        or
      • hold an equivalent qualification recognised by TMR.

      For more information on alternative training options refer to -
      Information Bulletin - Operator Accreditation Training PT211

      Applicants who do not meet this requirement can be issued provisional OA for 3 months so that they can fulfil the necessary training requirements.

Issue Period
Issue Period
Issue Period
    Full operator accreditation can be granted or renewed for a period of up to five years (in multiples of one year periods).

    Applicants who have not met the training requirements can be issued a provisional OA for 3 months.

Fees
Fees
Fees
    Application and Renewal Annual Fee
    Application and Renewal Annual Fee
      An annual OA fee is payable at time of lodgement and determined by:
      • Where applying for more than one category of OA, only the highest fee applies.
      • If an applicant is only eligible for provisional OA the 3 month pro-rata fee is payable (F4539).

      This fee may be exempted for charitable and community organisations recorded on the TMR approved Charitable or Community Service Organisation list.

      Note:The calculation of the number of vehicles for general services and motorcycle tourist services is determined by the maximum number of vehicles used at any one time for that type of service.

    Additional Application Fees
    Additional Application Fees
      • All new applicants listed on the Operator Accreditation form must also pay a criminal history check fee unless exempt. Fee exemptions apply where applicants have applied/hold driver authorisation. The criminal history check fee does not apply to renewals.
      • The operator accreditation training workbook package fee may also be payable.

New Operator Accreditation

Renew
Renew
    To renew operator accreditation, a customer must:
    • lodge application (F3871) (or complete Operator Accreditation Renewal Application (F2768)); and
    • lodge Operator Accreditation Fee Schedule and Vehicle Declaration (F3858)
    • provide payment.

    After receipt of application the CSO must:
    • Print a copy of the receipt from TICA
    • Scan and email the application to the Regional Translink Office - Email Template below
    Operator Accreditation Application Renewal email template
    • Retain original documentation in operator filing

    Customers can renew in person at a QGAP or CSC or by post by returning forms along with a cheque or money order.

    For provisional OA holders who are applying to renew, if no evidence of training is presented refer to a Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.

    If an operator allows their OA to lapse, the OA cannot be renewed. Instead, a new application will need to be submitted and a new application fee (including the additional application fee for a criminal history check) will be charged.

    If unsure of the specific requirements please contact your local Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.
Replacement
Replacement
    To obtain a replacement Operator Accreditation Certificate an operator should contact their closest Translink Regional Passenger Transport Office.

Upgrade from Provision level to Full level
