Adjustments Guide (5048)


Adjustments
What it isAdjustments involve the transfer of money to and from accounts.

The type of adjustment to be performed must be determined by:
  • Determining where the money is coming from; and
  • Where the money is going to.
When requiredAn adjustment should only be performed where other methods of balancing an account (such as unmatch payment and misc-credit) can not be used.

Recording of TEXT
Recording of TEXT
    All adjustments must have text recorded, as a minimum this text should:
    • Provide a brief but clear explanation why the adjustment is required
    • The USERID of the approving officer if required for the type of adjustment
Banking Adjustments
What is itAn operator should not be advised by the banking officer if they are over/under prior to handing their banking to the banking officer (known as blind banking), as all cash (excluding the starting float) is required to be surrendered to the banking officer. Where an operators physical cash is different to the TICA Collections summary the operator is required to investigate the discrepancy before the banking officers can close and bank for the day.

When required
Operator Over and Under Adjustments
Operator Over and Under Adjustments
    The Operator Over and Under adjustments amends both the Operator Cashbook, and the TICA totals on the Banking hub to reflect the actual amount of Cash/EFTPOS physically taken. These adjustments are generally performed by the Banking Collection Officer at the time of banking. Explanatory texts are to be recorded against the adjustment.
    Examples
    Examples
      Example: Operator Over
      The TICA Banking hub displays a cash amount of $155.00 for an operator. The operator produces a cash amount of $200.00. An Operator Over adjustment will be performed for the amount of $45.00 cash, which will amend the TICA Banking hub to display the actual amount taken of $200.00.

      Example: Operator Under
      The TICA Banking hub displays a cash amount of $155.00 for an operator. Operator produces a cash amount of $105.00 An Operator Under adjustment will be performed for the amount of $50.00, which will amend the TICA Banking hub to display the actual amount taken of $105.00.


    Note:An effort to locate the missing funds or identify the customer should be undertaken before the adjustment is processed.
Rules
Operator Over/Under >$5
Operator Over/Under >$5
    When an operator is over/under by an amount greater than $5.00 it must be investigated by the operator responsible within 7 days.

    All losses >$500 must be immediately reported to the Director (Financial Operations), Financial Division. Where the loss may have arisen from a cause which could constitute a criminal offence, the matter should also be referred to QPS.
    Customer Identified prior to closing operator
    Customer Identified prior to closing operator
      If the customer is identified the same day the over/under is identified the money is adjusted directly from the operator to the customer account and:
      • Customer accounts that are credited <$10.00 or debited <$20.00 will be automatically adjusted in TICA
      • Refunded if account is credited with >$9.99
      • The debt recovery process is followed if account is in debit >$19.99.
        Refer: Admin > 3. Money > The How To: Money > Money and Banking Procedures > Adjustments > Operator Losses over $19.99, Customer Identified
        • The CSC must send written correspondence to the customer advising of the debt owed to TMR. An Account Statement will be auto generated from the system once the debt has been placed in the customer's account.
    Customer not identified prior to closing operator
    Customer not identified prior to closing operator
      If the customer is not identified the same day the over/under is identified the operator is either over or under banked for that day.

      A Statement of Unders / Overs may be completed in order to capture the adjustment.
      Statement of Unders_Overs

      Although this document is not mandatory, it may assist with maintaining a record of Operator under/overs on site. An Excel Spreadsheet or register may be used as another alternative.

      Once the customer is identified an adjustment is made from the operator over/under account to the customer and:
      • Customer accounts that are credited <$10.00 or debited <$20.00 will be automatically adjusted in TICA
      • Refunded if account is credited with >$9.99
      • The debt recovery process is followed if account is in debit >$19.99.
        Refer: Admin > 3. Money > The How To: Money > Money and Banking Procedures > Adjustments > Operator Losses over $19.99, Customer Identified
        • The CSC must send written correspondence to the customer advising of the debt owed to TMR. An Account Statement will be auto generated from the system once the debt has been placed in the customer's account.
    Customer never Identified
    Customer never Identified
      The operator over must be transferred to the Sundries Miscellaneous Account immediately following the investigation by the operator (within 7 days), if the customer remains unidentified.

      If the amount is an operator under, Refer below: Relief from Payment

      Relief from Payment
      Relief from Payment
        If the customer is not identified, under the Financial Accountability Act 2009 (FA Act) any officer incurring a money deficiency which was not the result of an offence by a third party is responsible for the deficiency. The operator can apply for relief from payment.

        Operators can seek relief from payment from their Regional Directors.

        Where the officer is:
        • Deemed to have taken reasonable care in relation to handling public money; and
        • Has performed their duties in line with relevant legislation;
          and
        • The loss is not considered a failure to fulfil their duties and relief would generally be granted.

        Refer: Admin > 3. Money > The How To: Money > Money and Banking Procedures > Adjustments > Relief from Payment
Operator Over/Under $5 or less
Operator Over/Under $5 or less
    An operator under/over of $5.00 or less:
    • must be recorded in an Unders/Overs Register on the day the under/over occurred
    • reconciled monthly using an approved register

    If the amount held in the register is over $50 this is considered excessive and the excess should be transferred to the sundries miscellaneous account.
