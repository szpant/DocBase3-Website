Change of Sex
What it is
What it is
    Customers can advise the department of a change of sex when they are genuinely undertaking (or have undertaken) gender reassignment therapy.

    Sex is not displayed on Adult Proof of Age cards produced after 18 March 2016 or driver licences and MLI cards produced after 1 October 2016. Sex is still recorded in TICA for reporting purposes.

    If a customer changes their sex they are required to return all licence and APA/Photo ID cards that display a gender indicator.

    If a customer is also changing their name they also will have to meet the name change requirements.

    Refer above: Name Change

Applies to
Applies to
    Only applies to people who:
    • Suffer genuine gender dysphoria;
    • Have undertaken to live permanently in their new gender role;
    • Believe their new gender is their real gender.
      Note:Does not include transvestites or cross-dressers.
Documents required
Documents required
    Sex and Name Change documents
    The relevant Name Change document (if required) and
    One of the following:
    • a valid Australian Government travel document (for example, a valid passport) confirming the person's recorded sex/gender has been updated
    • an Australian birth certificate or recognised details certificate issued by the Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages confirming the person's sex/gender has been updated (i.e. for a Qld birth certificate the following text should be recorded on the back "re-registered under s14 of the BDMR Act")
    • A statement on a registered doctor's (including a psychiatrist) or psychologist's official letterhead stating the following or substantially similar:
      "This is to certify that (person's new name), (new sex), formerly known as (previous name), (previously recorded sex), has been undergoing treatment on a gender reassignment program and should now be regarded as permanently (new sex)."

    Note:A copy of the documentation presented is to be retained for filing with a TEXT restriction added and then expired to reflect the documentation provided.

When to update an address
When to update an address
    It is best practice to confirm the address with the customer every time they transact business with us to ensure they receive all TMR correspondence. This may be in person, over the phone or provided on documentation presented at a TMR Service delivery location or at a location that processes work on behalf of TMR such as a Motor Vehicle Dealership or Australia Post.

    An address should only be updated when the date it was provided ie date of verbal advice or date transactional documentation was signed, is after the date the current address was added.
How a change of address is notified
How a change of address is notified
    There is no requirement to prove the new address, only that the customer providing the address is entitled to do so.

    A change of address details can be accepted by:

    Requirements
    Fax/Mail /Email

    • Requests from Organisations must be on official company letterhead
    • Email to Customer Services Support.
    TMR CSC/ Agency
    • Standard EOI
    • Address change can be advised:
      • verbally, or
      • on TMR documentation as part of a transaction.
    • Standard representation EOI for an organisation
    • Address change for organisations to be advised by:
      • completing Change of Customer Details (Organisation) (F3526);
        or
      • In writing on company letterhead - letter must include previous address, full details of representative providing information, new address, confirmation that ASIC has been updated (if company) and include any other changes that are required for company.

    There is no requirement for the customer to complete a Change Customer Details form unless they have additional customer related (eg name) details to change.
    Telephone
    • Standard phone EOI
    TMR On-line ServicesAn individual customer may self notify of a change of residential and postal address via the TMR on-line service Change your customer address The change of address online service requires extra details

    Note:customers do not need to be signed up to e-notices/reminders to receive this email.

    Many of the other on-line services such as the Driver Licence Renewal service will prompt the customer to confirm or change their address before they progress through the service.
    TMR Agents (eg Dealerships)Notification of a change of address can be accepted by TMR agents where it is included as part of an application that is lodged to TMR for processing.

Address Labels and Confirmation Slips
Address Labels and Confirmation Slips
    Address labels:
    Smartcard Change of Address Label (F4810) will be issued automatically once the address is updated in TICA. It is issued centrally from the production house and mailed to the customer within 10 working days, except for Photo ID cardholders without an address displayed on their card.

    Note: Once an address is updated in TICA, there is no need to manually request a label.

    Multiple labels will be issued if a customer holds multiple active smartcards and their residential address that appears on the smartcard is changed.

    Confirmation slip:
    An address confirmation slip confirming the change of address will be automatically printed once the address is recorded in TICA, except for Photo ID cardholders without an address displayed on their card.

    Request for new smartcard address label (lost, damaged, etc.)
    A customer can request a new address label if it is lost, damaged or stolen. The customer is to be advised that the smartcard address label will be issued centrally from the production house and mailed to the customer within 10 working days.
Organisation - Change of Postal Address
Organisation - Change of Postal Address
    When an organisation changes its business or postal address it is important to determine whether it is a change for either the:
    • current business address
    • another business unit
    • adding new garaging address

    When the change is for a new business unit, enquire if all financial responsibilities are to be maintained by the current CRN holder. If yes then garaging address is to be added to the CRN. If not then a new Child CRN will need to be created with its own address and linked to the parent organisation.
Checking TICA when an address is updated
Checking TICA when an address is updated
    When a customer's address is updated operators must complete a check of TICA to ensure other products/areas don't require updating.

    The following are examples of products that are impacted by a change of address.
    Product
    Action Required
    Disability Parking PermitDisabled Parking permits are managed externally to TICA and do not automatically update when an address is changed in TICA. To ensure customers receive renewal notices:
    • Confirm with customer if they hold a current disability parking permit
    • If they do, use the Disable Customer option on the top menu on the Update customer address screen, to generate an email to the Disability Parking Permit Unit.
    Returned Demerit Point Sanction NoticesTICA Hub: Correspondence Hub
    • Reprint or remove address restriction
    • Reissue letter/Notice
    • Notice to be printed and handed to the customer
    • Advise if drivers licence is due
    InfringementsTICA Hub: Infringement Hub
    • Ensure no infringements from Road Safety Camera Office (RSCO) have been issued.
    • If infringement has been issued recently ask customer if ticket has been received.
    • If customer has not received ticket, refer them to RSCO or advise payment options.
    Outstanding PaymentsTICA Hub: Payment Hub
    • Confirm any outstanding payment items with the customer such as registration renewals

Deceased
Updating a CRN as deceased
Updating a CRN as deceased
    Manual Notification
    Manual Notification
      When a next of kin/executor notifies TMR that a customer is deceased, an original or certified copy of one of the following proof of death documents must be supplied:
      • Death Certificate
      • Cause of Death Certificate
      • Funeral Notice
      • Coroner's Report
      • Certificate of Authority issued by Public Trustee under the Public Trustee Act 1978
      • Grant of Probate or Letters of Administration stamped with court seal
      • Advice from Births Deaths and Marriages

    Fact of Death Notification
    Fact of Death Notification
      A record may be updated automatically or manually following Fact of Death data provided by Births, Deaths and Marriages. Fact of Death data is matched against records in TICA using strict matching rules.

      A CRN will be identified as a full match and automatically updated as deceased where the following rules are met:
      • Full name
      • DOB
      • Sex
      • Place of birth (including birth town/city if born in Australia. If born overseas only the country needs to match)

      If all details except birth town/city match, then it is deemed a partial match.
      If there is more than one CRN that either fully or partially matches, it is deemed a duplicate match.

      Duplicate and partial matches are investigated and, if applicable, manually updated as deceased.

    Note:Where the next of kin is present, the deceased customer's CRN should be checked for other TMR products that have been issued, eg driver licence, industry authority or APA/Photo ID card. If necessary, advise the representative of the deceased that these products should be surrendered to TMR.

    Once a customer is updated as deceased (either manually or automatically):
    • A NORG and NOAC restriction will be automatically applied to their record to restrict registration and plate transactions. Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System Information > Customer Restrictions
    • A Registration Notice Individual Restriction letter (F5094) will be issued if the deceased individual has any current/expired registrations/plate products.
    • Any refund cheques will be automatically issued to the 'Estate of <deceased>'.
Reinstating a deceased CRN
Reinstating a deceased CRN
    If a deceased CRN is reinstated (that is, the deceased details are removed) the NORG and NOAC restrictions will automatically be removed and a medium level business exception will be created. If a manually added NOAC existed prior to the record being deceased, it will remain in place when the CRN is reinstated. Supporting documentation is required to reinstate a deceased CRN and must be retained in the operator's daily filing. Examples of supporting documents are:
    • Standard EOI (photocopy retained) or 1:1 image match for smartcard transaction if customer is alive
    • Note on application if reinstated to allow transaction
    • Report for file if correcting an error
Closed organisations
Closing an Organisation CRN
Closing an Organisation CRN
    An organisation must be updated as ‘closed’ in TICA when:
    • It is no longer a legal entity (for example, a company that has a status of de-registered with ASIC)
    • It is no longer operating (for example, a business or trust has a status of cancelled on the Australian Business Register (ABR), or a child record that has closed their branch).
    • A request is received in writing from the organisation to close their CRN by any of the following methods in person/mail/fax/email

    No supporting documentation is required to close an organisation.

    An organisation can be closed by a CSO or Data Integrity. Requests to close CRN’s from other areas or agencies (for example, TMR prosecutions or SPER) should be emailed to CSB.Data Integrity.

    Refer: Customer > 3. Change Customer Details > The How To: Change Customer Detail > Change Customer Details Procedures > Organisational Customer > Close Organisation CRN

    Once a CRN is closed:
    • A NORG and NOAC restrictions are automatically applied to the CRN to restrict registration and plate transactions. Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > System Information > Customer Restrictions
    • A Registration Notice Organisation Restriction letter (F5093) will be issued if the organisation has current/expired registrations/plate products.
    Parent/child records
    Parent/child records
      • If a Parent record is closed, any linked child records will automatically close.
      • If a child record needs to be closed and the parent is still open, any registrations must be cancelled/transferred from the child record before it can be closed.
Reinstating a closed organisation CRN
Reinstating a closed organisation CRN
    If a closed CRN is reinstated (that is, the closed details are removed) the NORG and NOAC will automatically be removed and a medium level business exception will be created. Supporting documentation (E.g. ASIC print out for re-registered company, evidence supporting the removal for correction or to allow a transaction) is required to reinstate a closed CRN and is to be retained with the operator's daily filing.
    Verbal request to reinstate closed CRN
    Verbal request to reinstate closed CRN
      If an organisation customer contacts the call centre to request their record be reactivated, the following checks must be conducted on ASIC (http://www.asic.gov.au/) /ABN lookup (http://www.abr.business.gov.au/):
      • For a company, the status must be 'registered' on ASIC
      • For a business, the status must be 'active' on ABN lookup and it must have a current legal entity (a living individual or an organisation with a 'current' status on ASIC) as the second registered operator.
      • For any other organisation types, the customer must present to a CSC or send a written request (including EOI) for the record to be reactivated. Refer: Customer > 1.Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity
    Parent/child records
    Parent/child records
      • If a parent record is reinstated, any child record that was automatically closed will automatically be reinstated.
      • A closed child record cannot be reinstated if the parent is closed.

Request for new CRN
What it is
What it is
    A customer may apply for a new TICA Customer Reference Number (CRN) if their existing CRN has been used fraudulently by another individual.

    Due to the importance of a single unique CRN for each customer, changing a CRN is only considered in circumstances where it supports the integrity of the licensing register. Therefore a replacement CRN may only be issued to a customer who can provide written documentation from an enforcement agency that proves their CRN has been used to commit fraud.
    Note: Suppression of Customer Identity
    Note: Suppression of Customer Identity
      The issue of a new CRN to a customer does not prevent the release of their personal information to those agencies that have access to TICA.

      If the customer believes that they also have an acceptable reason for suppressing their record in TICA the CSC should follow the procedure in Suppression of Records

      Refer: Admin > 1. Administration > Security > Suppression of Customer Records



    A fixed PRN will not be changed as a result of a new CRN being issued.
Documents Required
Documents Required
    Police Agency Incident Report
    The incidence of fraud must be confirmed by presenting an Incident Report from a police agency:
    • Queensland Police Service (QPS)
    • Interstate police authority
    • Australian Federal Police (AFP)

    The report/email/letter from the police agency must state that they have established that a person's Qld licence has been compromised and used for fraudulent purposes.

    Confirmation from a police agency that an incident of alleged fraud has been reported to them is insufficient.
    • A written request in the form of a statutory declaration is to be completed by the customer and signed by a Justice of the Peace.
    • Upon approval, full EOI is required when the customer returns to have the new licence/product authority issued.
Approval Process
Approval Process
    Granting approval for a new CRN involves:
    Role
    Responsibility
    CSC ManagerScan and email documents to IMU
    IMUMake recommendation to the Senior Manager (Information Access and Identity Management) (IA&IM)
    Senior Manager (IA&IM)Approve/Refuse the request
    IMUAdvise the customer of the outcome of their request
    IMUEmail all decisions to the CSC Manager - File on DMS
IMU Involvement
IMU Involvement
    • Contact the CSC to advise of the approval of the request for the replacement CRN
    • Create a new CRN
    • Sends request to TRAILS/ESD Maintenance & Support Unit to have the driver licence history transferred from the original CRN to the new CRN
    • Contact CSC Customer Service Operator (CSO) and advise of the need for a replacement CRN to be issued and coordinate the attendance of the customer at the CSC for the issue of new licence/authority/card.
    • Merge CRNs
CSC Involvement
CSC Involvement
    • CSC ensures the customer meets standard EOI requirements, including the performance of a 1:1 image match if a valid image is already recorded for the customer
    • After identity checks have been successfully completed, the CSC processes a fee-free replacement.
    • The customer is required to surrender any TMR products issued with the old CRN.
    Note:Unless lost, stolen or destroyed all existing licence/authority/card products are to be returned before the issue of the replacement products.
