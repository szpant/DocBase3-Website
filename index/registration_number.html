Registration Number (809)


What it isTICA will issue a registration number when the ship is registered.

The plate format consists of:
  • aaannQ
  • aannnQ

Example:
  • SWE78Q
  • SW078Q

Legacy Plate Format
Legacy Plate Format
    The following plate formats will be used for the migration of other and prescribed other Queensland regulated ships:

    Plate Formats
    nnnnnQC
    nnnnnQD
    nnnnnQE
    nnnnQC
    nnnnQD
    nnnnQE

    Any new Queensland regulated ships will use the standard plate format of aaannQ.

Reserve Registration Number
Reserve Registration Number
    Customers can reserve a ship registration number provided it is available in TICA. Previously issued registration numbers cannot be reserved.

    CSC operators can:
    • check ship plate number availability
    • place a hold on (1) or up to (10) ship plate numbers
    • release a hold on (1) ship plate number at a time

    Note:Requests for a hold on more than (10) ship plate numbers (maximum of 50) will be considered on a case-by-case basis. Requests must be approved by the Manager.

    Call Centre consultants can check the availability of a ship number over the phone, however a customer must attend a CSC for it to be placed on hold or released.

    Ship numbers may be reserved for a period of six months. If the number has not been issued within this six month period, it can then be released to a new customer. Staff do not need to seek approval/permission from the initial holder to release the number if the six month period has lapsed.

    Note:The Ship Number Receipt is a PDF generated immediately after the ship plate number is held in TICA and the operator is not able to reprint the Held Plate Number receipt at a later time.
Requirements
Who is Responsible for Display
Who is Responsible for Display
    It is the owners/masters responsibility to ensure that the registration number is displayed on the ship.
Where do I put the Registration Number
Where do I put the Registration Number
    Registration numbers must be:
    • Legible from 30 metres
    • Permanently displayed on the hull, above the waterline when the ship is afloat either in:
      • Dark colours on a light background
      • Light colours on a dark background
      • Displayed on:
        • Planing Ships - both sides of ship
        • Non-planing Ships - both sides or on stern of ship
        • Personal Water Craft (PWC) - both sides
