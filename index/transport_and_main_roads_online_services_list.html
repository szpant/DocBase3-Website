Transport and Main Roads Online Services List (4985)


The Department of Transport and Main Roads offers a wide range of online services for customers. Below is a list of the available services, a brief explanation of each service and what it may be used for, and the requirements for using the service.

Customers must accept the TMR Terms and Conditions before entry to the services is permitted.

For access to the online services, please click on the following: TMR Online Services Page

For access to the Quick Reference Guide - Access Levels by Services job aid, please click the following: Quick Reference Guide Online Services

For all services where a payment is required to be made, an acceptable payment method must be used.

Uploading documents
If customers experience difficulties in uploading documents as part of the online service they are using, they should follow the below tips:
  • Save the file to their desktop
  • Ensure the filename length is <25 characters and does not contain symbols

    Check Q-Ride Course Eligibility
    Check Q-Ride Course Eligibility
      This is a free service eligible customers can use to see if they are eligible to undertake the Q-Ride pre-learner, restricted (RE) and unrestricted (R) courses.

      The eligibility status is either:
      • Eligible - the requirements (tenure and any prerequisites) have been met for the nominated course.
      • Not eligible - the requirements (tenure and any prerequisites) have not been met for the nominated course.
      • Not required - the person is not required to undertake the course to progress through the motorcycle licensing system (eg. they have an exemption from the pre-learner course but can choose to undertake the course to reduce the tenure requirements).
      • Returning driver - the person is a returning rider and their returning driver certificate must be sighted to confirm the classes they are eligible to ride.

    Submit an enquiry
    Submit an enquiry
      Online submit enquiry services are available for all camera detected infringements issued by the Road Safety Camera Office (QPS) and TMR and allows a customer to submit an online enquiry and attach supporting documents the following offence types:
      • speeding
      • disobey red light
      • unregistered/uninsured vehicle
      • dangerous goods in tunnels
      • disobey ‘No Truck’ sign (BUC)


      To be eligible for this service, the infringement must meet the following rules:
      • Must be <6 months old (from issue date)
      • Must not have a status of VIPSPER, PAID or WAIVED
      • Must be within the most recent 99 infringements for the customer (by issue date)

      Individuals can access eligible infringements in their own name. Authorised representatives for an organisation can access an infringement by:
      • Logging in with extra details as an individual
      • Under ‘My Infringements’ selecting Submit an Enquiry
      • Entering the infringement and customer reference numbers for the corporation
      • Selecting the acknowledgement check box
      Submit enquiry transactions for will be directed to the relevant business area for processing as follows:
    • TMR ussued notices:
      • Unregistered/uninsured – prosecutionservices@tmr.qld.gov.au
      • Dangerous goods in tunnels – dgu@tmr.qld.gov.au
      • BUC – offenceprocessing@tmr.qld.gov.au
    • RSCO issued notices will be emailed to the RSCO.

      This will happen automatically once the customer has completed their transaction.

      This service allows the customer to elect for a court hearing for all camera detected infringements issued by the Road Safety Camera Office (QPS) and TMR.

      To be eligible for this service, the infringement must meet the following rules:
      • Must be <12 months old (from issue date)
      • Must not have a status of VIPSPER, PAID or WAIVED
      • Must be within the most recent 99 infringements for the customer (by issue date)
      Court elections for Road Safety Camera Office (RSCO) issued notices go directly to RSCO via a webservice link between TMR and QPS. All other court elections will be electronically forwarded to Offence Processing along with any uploaded documents.

      Individuals can access eligible infringements in their own name. Authorised representatives for an organisation can access an infringement by:
      • Logging in with extra details as an individual
      • Under ‘My Infringements’ selecting Submit an Enquiry
      • Entering the infringement and customer reference numbers for the corporation
      • Selecting the acknowledgement check box

Web chat
Web chat
  • Web chat is a free service available between 7am-5pm and 6pm-9pm Monday to Friday, which allows a customer to communicate via text chat in real time with a TMR operator.
    Note: For direct messaging through an Apple Device Refer below: Apple Business Chat
    • Customers can access web chat by clicking on the blue icon at the bottom of the screen of a tagged online service, or by accepting an invitation to chat, which populates on a tagged page if:
      • The customer has remained on the page for a defined length of time with no further activity; or
      • The customer navigates from the page to the ‘Need help’ or ‘Contact us’ page; or
      • The customer abandons the page.
    • Web chat allows the TMR operator to "co-browse" with the customer so that the operator can see what the customer is seeing on the screen.
    • Online services tagged for chat are:
      • Renew driver licence
      • Replace driver licence
      • Check demerit points
      • Pay infringement/fine
      • Renew registration
      • Enrol in direct debit
      • Change of address
