Proof of Origin (3030)


What is it?A vehicle is defined as a light vehicle/trailer with a GVM/ATM </=4.5t

Proof of a vehicle’s origin needs to be established to ensure that the vehicle exists, is eligible to be registered and helps to identify that it is the same vehicle that is being registered.

Note:CSC operators are not required to ensure that the registered operator is the legal owner of the vehicle.
Proof of origin requirements
Brand new vehicles
Brand new vehicles
    Application for registration can proceed if:
    • RAV entry exists
      or
    Customer provides one of the following:
    • evidence that a compliance plate is fitted to the vehicle/motorcycle (e.g. present a photo of the compliance plate on the vehicle)
    • a receipt from the place of purchase (e.g. motor dealer), which clearly states the vehicle details (including the VIN)
    • if the vehicle is imported (and is not on the RAV), a vehicle import approval that allows registration and use on roads
Second hand/previously registered vehicles
Second hand/previously registered vehicles
    Application for registration can proceed if:
    • previous registration can be established on NEVDIS/TICA/ROLS
    OR
    • the customer can present a previous Registration Certificate from any Australian jurisdiction
    OR
    • RAV entry or the customer can present a vehicle import approval that allows registration and use on roads.

    If none of these are available:

    A statement of acquisition must be completed. The customer must also provide evidence that a compliance plate is fitted to the vehicle/motorcycle (e.g. present a photo of the compliance plate on the vehicle).

    Evidence of a compliance plate is not required if the vehicle/motorcycle is exempt from a compliance plate due to being manufactured:
    • >1/07/2021 (all vehicles)
    • <1972 (motor vehicle)
    • <1975 (motorcycle)

    Sample Statement of Acquisition

    Note:The customer must provide as much detail as possible in the Statement of Acquisition. If they do not know the full name or address of the disposer, they must outline, in detail, how they came into possession of the vehicle. If there is any doubt about the information contained in the Statement of Acquisition, it should be accepted or refused at the Managers discretion.
Conditional Registration
Conditional Registration
    A statement of acquisition from a customer is sufficient as proof of the vehicle’s origin.
