Tow Truck Guide (5265)


Tow Truck Guide
What it isThe Tow Truck Act and Tow Truck Regulation 2009 (tow truck legislation) regulates the towing of vehicles in regulated areas, for the following types of towing:
  • From the scene of a crash;
  • police authorised seizures; and
  • From the 16 April 2018 when towing a vehicle from private property.

The tow truck legislation creates the requirement for tow truck operators performing the above functions within regulated areas to:
  • Have a valid Tow Truck licence
  • Use appropriately accredited drivers and assistants
  • Use a licensed tow truck
  • Have a compliant business premises
  • Have suitable and compliant holding yards
  • Hold towing authorities/towing consents
  • Keep records
  • Not charge more than the maximum regulated fees.

Tow Truck License Conditions
General Conditions of a Tow Truck Licence
General Conditions of a Tow Truck Licence
    A tow truck licence holder must (unless an exemption is granted):
      • offer towing services 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
      • only conduct the business from premises owned or leased by the licence holder
      • conduct the business from premises that is approved by the local council (council approval documentation must be presented upon lodgement of an application for a tow truck licence)
      • supply communication equipment suitable for the deployment of mobile units at the business premises, and in licensed tow trucks
      • have a holding yard for storing towed vehicles in safe custody
      • Keep the licence in safe custody at the place of business stated in the licence and show it to an authorised officer on request
      • Show the licence to an authorised officer on request
      • Show the Towing Consent to a vehicle owner or driver or to an authorised officer on request
      • Take reasonable steps to prevent loss from or damage to a vehicle held in the holding yard
      • Not move a towed vehicle from the holding yard without consent of the vehicle owner or agent
      • Not disclose to another person any personal information about the owner of a vehicle that you gained through towing of the vehicle unless it is to be disclosed to the vehicle owner, their agent or to an authorised officer
      • Give the Department of Transport and Main Roads written notice within 14 days of a change of the holder's name, residential or business address and give approval to the department to note the change
      • Give the Department of Transport and Main Roads written notice if they are charged or convicted of an offence under the Criminal Code. The notice must be provided within 14 days of when the charge or conviction occurs
      • Use equipment and vehicles that are compliant in terms of design, construction and serviceability
      • Comply with any weight and speed restrictions
      • Comply with driver fatigue provisions
      • Comply with the need for the tow truck to have a current COI at all times when attached to a licence
      • Comply with any consent requirements

        The driver/assistant can only work for one licence holder at a time. While representing a licence holder the driver/assistant cannot:
        • Advertise or promote another licence holders business
        • Seek business on behalf of another licence holder

      Uniforms
        The clothes worn while performing the duties of a tow truck driver/assistant for a specific licence must only display the:
        • Name, address or phone number of the licence holder
        • Logo of the licence holder
        • The name of the wearer

        A tow truck licence holder must not:
        • advertise or promote the business of anyone other than the holder of the licence
        • seek business for anyone other than the holder of the licence
        • display any advertising or marking's other than the business name, place of business or telephone number of the approval holder on the tow truck *
        *An exception is the display of an approved organisation's name if the licence holder is the agent of an approved organisation, such as the Royal Automobile Club of Queensland Ltd (RACQ).

Tow Truck Drivers and assistants conditionsThe tow truck legislation specifies that:
  • A driver's certificate authorises the holder to operate a tow truck
  • An assistants certificate authorises the holder to be employed on or in connection with a tow truck.
A person who does not have a valid tow truck driver/assistants certificate must not:
  • Operate a tow truck to perform regulated towing in regulated areas
  • Be employed on or in connection with the use of a tow truck performing regulated towing in regulated area:
  • At or near the scene of an incident/seizure
  • From the 16 April 2018 in private property towing

Conditions of Driver Assistants Certificates
Conditions of Driver Assistants Certificates
    • Tow truck drivers and assistants must produce their industry authority card or interim industry authority upon request to an authorised officer and to the authorising person of a damaged vehicle at the scene of an incident).
    • Tow truck drivers must carry a copy of the Towing Consent (evidencing the written contract between the tow truck licence holder and private property owner/occupier) when towing a vehicle from private property and produce the Towing Consent to a vehicle owner or authorised officer on request.
    • Tow truck drivers must take reasonable steps to locate the vehicle owner before loading a vehicle onto the tow truck. Reasonable steps includes, for example, asking other people (at the carpark or nearby shops) if they know where the driver went; looking around the immediate area to see if the vehicle owner is nearby or approaching the carpark; looking for contact or business name details on the vehicle.
    • If the owner returns when the vehicle is in the process of being loaded and secured on the tow truck, the vehicle must be released immediately without charge. If the owner returns after the vehicle has been loaded onto the tow truck (including secured in every way necessary) but has not yet left the property, the vehicle must be released immediately if the owner pays the on-site release fee
    • If, after taking reasonable steps drivers are not able to locate a vehicle owner or driver, they may tow the vehicle by the most direct route to the tow truck licence holder's nearest TMR approved holding yard.
    • If the vehicle is unlocked any property found in the vehicle must be given to the licence holder for safe keeping until the property can be returned to the vehicle owner or agent. A locked vehicle must not be unlocked without the consent of the owner or their agent.
    • They must not disclose to another person any personal information about the owner of a vehicle that you gained through towing of the vehicle unless it is to be disclosed to the vehicle owner, their agent or to an authorised officer.
    • They must not allow a person to travel in the tow truck from the private property parking area unless the person has a driver’s or assistant’s certificate or they are the owner of the vehicle being towed, the owner’s agent or they were a passenger in the vehicle.
    • They must not tow a vehicle while there is a person inside it.
    • They must not directly or indirectly cause or threaten wilful injury to a person or their property; intimidate, harass, abuse or insult a person or prevent or hinder the delivery of first aid or medical treatment.
    • They must take all reasonable precautions to prevent loss or damage to a motor vehicle and any personal items left in the vehicle while it is being towed and while it is in the holding yard.
    • Tow truck drivers and tow truck assistants must notify the Department of Transport and Main Roads within 14 days of a change of name or address.
    • Tow truck drivers and tow truck assistants must give the Department of Transport and Main Roads written notice if they are charged or convicted of an offence under the Criminal Code. The notice must be provided within 14 days, after the charge or conviction occurs.

Refer: Transport Compliance > 11. Tow Trucks > Tow Truck Driver/Assistants Certificate Guide for information on the application process and conditions of use.

Modifications
Modifications
    All tow trucks must have a T1 modification and T2 design certificate of approval.

    The vehicle must be fitted with a modification plate displaying the T1 code.

    The documentation must be retained and able to be produced upon request. There is no requirement for the documentation to be carried in the vehicle.
Markings and Signage
Markings and Signage
    The tow truck must prominently display on both sides of the vehicle:
      • Name, business address and phone number of the licence/permit holder
      • The classification of the tow truck
      • The licence/permit number under which the tow truck operates
      • Tow Truck number

    The marking's must be legible from a distance of 30m.

    Any additional marking's including abbreviations, words, figures and punctuation marks must not be more that 1000 square centimetres in area.

    The tow truck may be equipped with on illuminated sign that is:
      • Fitted with an incandescent bulb of constant intensity
      • Has a total illuminated surface area of not more than 3000 square centimeter's
Winches
Winches
    A winch fitted to a tow truck must be adequately and securely mounted. The load applied to the winch must not exceed the safe working loaded specified by the manufacturer or certified by an engineer.
Tow Truck Premises RequirementsThe operator of a tow truck business must have a compliant:
    • Business Premises
    • Holding Yards

These do not need to be co-located as a tow truck company may have a single business premises with multiple holding yards.

A compliant holding yard:
    • Is located in Queensland
    • Is owned or leased solely by the licence holder
    • Conforms with the requirement of the relevant local government
    • Allows for the safe and secure storage of towed vehicles and any moveable property found in the motor vehicle.
    • Must be enclosed by a fence or wall that:
      • Is structurally sound
      • Is at least 2.1m in height from the ground
      • Has lockable gates or doors that are closed and securely locked other than when a motor vehicle being moved into or out of the area.

A compliant business premises:
    • Conforms with the requirements of the relevant local government
    • Contains appropriate communication equipment that is able to manage a mobile work force
    • Allows for the safe and secure storage of business records.

A business premises and holding yard must be inspected by a Transport Inspector:
    • Before an new application is approved
    • When a change of premises/holding yard has been notified.
