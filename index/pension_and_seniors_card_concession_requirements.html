Pension and Seniors Card Concession Requirements (3049)

Generic Concession Policy
Generic Concession Policy
    What is itTransport and Main Roads offers reduced motor vehicle registration for persons receiving pensions and benefits through Department of Human Services, Department of Veteran Affairs and Department of Communities.

    Applies to
    Applies to
      Concessional registration only applies to the following vehicles with a POU of Private:
      • Motorcycles
      • Motor Vehicle with GVM <= 4.5t (passenger and light commercial/mini bus type vehicles only)
      • Motorhome/Campervan no GVM limit
      • Recreational Ship
    Concession Card Types eligible for the SchemeConcessional Registration Schemes available that apply to pensioners and seniors:

    Department of Human Services (Centrelink)
    • Pensioner - Pension Concession Card (PCC)

    Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
    • Veteran - Pension Concession Card (PCC)
    • Prescribed Service Persons (PSP)

    Department of Communities
    • Seniors - Seniors Cards
    Customer Eligibility RequirementsAny person issued with a Pension Concession Card (PCC) under Social Security or Family Assistance Law may be entitled to concessional registration.

    Customer Requirements
    Customer Requirements
      To be eligible for concessional registration, a customer must:
      • meet the conditions of the concession type (Refer: Specific Conditions for each Concession type) being applied for
      • complete and sign Registration Concession Application (F3937)
      • be a resident of Queensland or reside in a town situated on the Queensland border and have an interstate postcode, (eg Mungindi, Boggabilla) provided they live in Queensland
      • hold a current:
        • Queensland PCC issued by the Department of Human Services. The customer may produce a physical PCC or a digital PCC on a smart device using the Australian Government Centrelink Express Mobile app.
          Refer: Vehicle Rego > Concessional Registration > Pension and Seniors Card Concession Requirements > Specific Conditions for each Concession Type > Department of Human Services (Centrelink) > Digital Copy of Concession Card
          OR
        • Prescribed Service Persons (PSP) Veteran Gold Card (the card must be current and signed) embossed with TPI (Totally or Permanently Incapacitated) or EDA (Extreme Disability Adjustment) code
          OR
        • A DVA issued Veteran Gold Card (not embossed) or Veteran White Card (both cards must be current and signed) accompanied by a letter confirming they meet the scheme eligibility requirements.

        Note:If concession is to be backdated, in some cases, grant date must be confirmed with DVA. For requirements, Refer: Vehicle Rego > Concessional Registration > Pension and Seniors Card Concession Requirements < Generic Concession Policy > Date of Grant

          OR
        • Queensland Seniors Card

        Refer: Specific Conditions for each Concession type

    Effective DateWhen adding a person's concession to their vehicle, the concessional registration will automatically take effect from:
    • for a vehicle - the date the concession is applied to the vehicle*;
      or
    • for a recreational ship - the start of the next registration period

    *If the start/issue date of the customer's concession card was prior to the current date, the concession application must be manually back dated. Refer below.

    Backdating the application of a concession
    Backdating the application of a concession
      Applying concession from start of registration period (vehicle only)
      Applying concession from start of registration period (vehicle only)
        The concession may be backdated to the commencement date of the current period of registration, if:
        • the registered operator was eligible for the concession at the start of the current registration period
        • the customer was the registered operator at the start of the registration period
        • the full rate of registration fees were paid or raised (applies if the fees have not been paid and payment is after the expiry date of registration)

        Concessional registration cannot be applied to previous registration periods.
      Applying concession from start/issue date of concession card (vehicle only)
      Applying concession from start/issue date of concession card (vehicle only)
        The concession can be back dated to the start/issue date of the concession card if the customer was the registered operator as at the start/issue date of the concession card (but never further back than the current registration period).
      Applying concession during current registration period (vehicle only)
      Applying concession during current registration period (vehicle only)
        A concession may be:
        • applied at the date of application (during a current registration period) or
        • backdated to another date during the current registration period, if the registered operator was eligible for the concession at the date the concession is to be applied.

        Example of backdating a concession to a date during the current registration period:
        • Customer transferred vehicle into their name in October 2014.
        • Customer previously had another vehicle registered in their name with a concession that was cancelled on 23 April 2015.
        • Customer presents at CSC in July 2015 when registration is due on vehicle transferred in October 2014 (pension concession card still current).
        • Concession may be applied to this vehicle from 24 April 2015.

        Note: The customer is not required to be the registered operator at the start of the current registration period or have paid the registration fees for the current registration period in order to receive any pro-rata raised.


      Note:If the above criteria for backdating a concession is met, any pro-rata raised which is greater than $10, is to be refunded Refer:Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Payment > Refunds. Amounts under $10 are not be refunded unless requested by the customer.
    Joint Registered Operators Eligibility
    Spouses
    Spouses
      Definition of Spouse:
      A spouse is one of two people who live together as a couple on a genuine domestic basis who are either a
      • married couple, or
      • de facto couple of the same or opposite gender who are not related by family, or
      • a registered partner, being a person who is party to a registered relationship, registered under the Relationships Act 2011 (regardless of their sex).

      If a vehicle/recreational ship is jointly registered by spouses, only one needs to be eligible to apply for concessional registration. However, if both of the registered operators are eligible for concessional registration, then the:
      • highest rate of concessional registration fees for the vehicle/recreational ship can be applied to the vehicle/recreational ship.
      • customer whose concession is being applied to the registration must be recorded as the first registered operator.

      Only one concession is allowed between spouses for one vehicle and one recreational ship.
    Two registered operators – not spouses
    Two registered operators – not spouses
      If the registered operators of a vehicle/recreational ship apply for concessional registration and they are not spouses, both of the registered operators must meet the above Customer Eligibility Requirements to be eligible for concessional registration.

      The concession that attracts the highest rate of concessional fees must be applied to the vehicle/recreational ship.

      Note:Only one concession is allowed for eligible joint registered operators for one vehicle and one recreational ship.
    Fraudulent Cards
    Fraudulent Cards
    Fraudulent Cards
      If you have any doubts about the authenticity of a concession entitlement, escalate your concerns with your SASM or Manager. The SASM or Manager will assess the application and determine if a concession should be issued. If the SASM or Manager has any reservations, the concession must not be granted. Advise the customer that you cannot accept the application and refer them to the issuing authority for confirmation of their entitlement (eg. letter of entitlement or issue of a new card).
    Moving a concession from one vehicle/recreational ship to another (same registered operator)There may be instances where a concession holder disposes of their existing vehicle for another and the registration/transfer date for the replacement vehicle is before the transfer date of the vehicle being disposed.

    Provided no more than 30 days has lapsed, between the 2 transaction dates, a concession may be applied/swapped to the replacement vehicle, provided the concession is withdrawn from the existing vehicle (at a date prior to applying it to the new vehicle) and the pro rata is paid.

    The concession can be applied to the new recreational ship from the start of the registration period provided:
    • They were the registered operator
    • They were eligible for the concession
    • The concession on the vehicle/recreational ship being sold is withdrawn (by request or cancellation or transfer at that date) and pro rata is paid

    Online process for changing a concession between vehicles/recreational ships
    Online process for changing a concession between vehicles/recreational ships
      • New Business is processed eg. 1/4
      • Concession on old vehicle/recreational ship can be removed up until 1/5 (30 day grace period)
      • Backdate removal of concession until the day prior to the new business date (eg. 31/3)
      • Backdate applying the concession to the new business from the date of the transaction eg. 1/4. This should generate a 12 month refund for the new vehicle/ recreational ship.
      • This may require a manual process to calculate the pro rata.

    Prescribed Service Persons (PSP) through the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)
    PSP Conditions
    PSP Conditions
      Eligibility
        Concession applicants must be a person:
        • who resides in Queensland,
        • who is, or has previously been an Australian Defence Force member,
        • who is issued with a DVA Veteran Gold Card embossed with TPI (Totally or Permanently Incapacitated) or EDA (Extreme Disability Adjustment) (the card must be current and signed) from any state or territory,
          OR
        • who is issued with a DVA Veteran Gold Card (not embossed) or Veteran White Card from any state or territory (the card must be current and signed), and a letter from the DVA verifying that they have:
          • been assessed under the Veterans Entitlements Act 1986 (Cwlth) as having at least a 70% degree of incapacity;
            OR
          • been assessed under the Military Rehabilitation and Compensation Act 2004 (Cwlth) as having at least 50 impairment points.


        Note:Applicants may present a digital version of their Veteran Gold Card (endorsed with TPI or EDA) or Veteran White Card through the DVA MyServiceonline portal. Screenshots of the card are not acceptable.

        Note:If concession is to be backdated, in some cases, confirmation of grant date must be confirmed with DVA.

        Refer: Vehicle Rego > Concessional Registration > Pension and Seniors Card Concession Requirements < Generic Concession Policy > Date of Grant

      • DVA cards (must be signed) and letters must be in the same name as the registered operator of the vehicle/recreational ship.
      • Spouses: the first registered operator recorded for the vehicle/recreational ship must be the concession holder.
