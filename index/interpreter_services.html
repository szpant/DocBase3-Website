Interpreter Services (7144)


What it isTMR has an obligation to assist customers who have difficulty speaking or reading English or have a hearing impairment. This can be provided either by the operator or by engaging a third party such as interpreter services
When to useInterpreter services can be provided upon request for any business that is conducted with TMR and is not restricted to written tests.

Assistance should not be provided for Industry Licensing applicants. Minimum literacy standards are part of the training for a Dangerous Goods Driver and all other industry authority schemes require the accredited person to complete/maintain documentation as part of their duties.
TMR Organised Interpreter ServiceONCALL is the approved provider of interpreter services for TMR.

It is acceptable for a customer to organise their own interpreter as long as the interpreter meets TMR's requirements with regard to NAATI accreditation. Verification can be done via the NAATI website www.naati.com.au by clicking on the Find a Translator or Interpreter link, complete all relevant fields then submit. A list of accredited interpreters will display on the screen for you to check against.

Immediate service via phone
Immediate service via phone
    To access an interpreter immediately, telephone 3018 0333. This service is suitable for use for completing transactions other than written road rules tests.

    Note: This service caters for selected languages only.
On-site bookings
On-site bookings
    On-site bookings can be made:
    • via ONCALL's online booking system oncallinterpreters.com.au (preferred method), or
    • via telephone (3018 0333), and
    • must be made at least 48 hours in advance.

    In person attendance by the Interpreter
    If an interpreter is able to attend your location you must ensure the ONCALL booking:
    • Is made at least 48 hours in advance
    • Is for a period of at least 1 hour
      Practical Driving Tests
      Practical Driving Tests
      Once confirmation of an interpreter for a PDT is received the test booking comments should be edited to indicate that an interpreter is booked.

      If the customer cancels or reschedules their PDT the call centre will contact the CSC so that the interpreter booking can be cancelled/changed.

    Over the phone provision of Interpreter Services
    If an interpreter is not able to attend your location, the service will be provided over the phone (where possible) with the Interpreter being located at the Toowong CSC.

    When the service is to be provided over the phone you must ensure:
    • The ONCALL booking is made at least 48 hours in advance
    • Is for a period of at least 1hr and 30 mins
    • A room booking at Toowong CSC is made (through Toowong’s Outlook Calendar) which commences at the same time as the ONCALL booking

    The below template can be used for creating an appointment to book a room at the Toowong CSC. Please ensure to complete all fields in the subject line and send appointment to the Interpreter Services Meeting Room/Toowong CSC.

    Toowong Interpreter Booking template - outlook

    Extension of Booking time
      The interpreter must seek approval from the location to extend the service beyond the original period.

On arrival and after the booking
On arrival and after the booking
    All ONCALL interpreters have an ONCALL ID card, which they will present upon arrival.

    Example of ONCALL ID Card

    At the end of the appointment, the interpreter will provide a digital assignment record.

    The authorising CSO is to complete the record on the digital device presented by the interpreter.

    A copy of the assignment record is then emailed to the address provided for the booking (this should be the CSC email address). This email should be retained by the CSC for reconciliation with the monthly ONCALL invoices.


    Assignment Booking Sheet - ONCALL
Cancellations
Cancellations
    If the interpreter is unable to attend and cancels the booking, the CSC will need to contact the customer as soon as possible to notify them and arrange a new appointment.

    Similarly, if the customer contacts the CSC prior to the booking to cancel, the CSC must cancel the booking with ONCALL. The CSC should enquire with the customer about making another booking.

    If the booking was for the interpreter to attend the Interpreter Services Booking Room at Toowong, the appointment in the Outlook calendar must also be cancelled.

    To book or cancel an ONCALL appointment, Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > The How to: Customer > Interpreter Services
Reconciliation and payment of invoice
Reconciliation and payment of invoice
    Invoices for interpreter services are actioned as follows:

    1.
    Oncall invoices
    Oncall invoices
      Invoices may be paid by Purchase Order or Expenditure Voucher
      Purchase order
      Purchase order
        Purchase orders are set up by Regional Office for SOA CSB13019.
      Expenditure Voucher
      Expenditure Voucher
        For regions/locations where interpreter services are paid by expenditure voucher, a purchase order is not required.

    TIS Invoices
    TIS Invoices
      Purchase Orders are used to pay TIS invoices. Follow the Lite Procurement and Sole Invitee Processes as appropriate.
    2.The responsible officer collates and reconciles the assignment records against the invoice.

    Where the reconciliation and payment process is centrally managed, assignment records are sent to the central processing location for reconciliation.
    3.The Manager of the service location/processing area approves payment of the invoice.
    Information to include on invoice
    Information to include on invoice
      Prior to payment, the following information must be written/stamped on the invoice:
      • Approving officer's:
        • Name
        • Signature
        • Position
      • Date of approval
      • Purchase order number
      • Vendor number (Oncall - 65349, TIS - 38649)
      • Goods receipt number
      • Cost Centre
      • GL Account Number (use Contractor Other- 51551)
      • "OK to pay"

      If any of these items are missing from the invoice, the payment may not be processed by QSS.

    For centrally managed payment only, approved invoices are provided to the central processing location for payment.
    4.The responsible officer pays the invoice
    To pay the invoice
    To pay the invoice
      The responsible office checks to ensure all required information in on the invoice (Refer: Information to include on invoice section above)

      The goods receipt is entered via SAP transaction MIGO, then submitted to QSS.

      QSS process an invoice receipt (payment via EFTPOS), in line with TMR terms and conditions (30 days after invoice date).

      More information about this process is available on the QSS website: https://www.forgov.qld.gov.au/pay-vendor-or-agency-accounts-payable

Alternative providers to ONCALLIf ONCALL cannot provide an interpreter within a reasonable time-frame or for the required language/service, alternative arrangements can be made.

For customers who have difficulty speaking or reading English
For customers who have difficulty speaking or reading English
    If ONCALL cannot provide an interpreter for a customer who has difficulty speaking or reading English, a booking may be made with the Translating & Interpreting Service (TIS).

    TIS cannot provide 'written to verbal' translations (i.e. they cannot verbally translate a written test to a customer) without the assistance of a CSO.

    If a written to verbal translation is required, a CSO must be available to read the test to the interpreter.

    The minimum on-site booking is 90 minutes.

    To book or cancel a TIS appointment, Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > The How to: Customer > Interpreter Services

    To pay a TIS invoice, Refer: Reconciliation and payment of invoice above.
For hearing impaired customers
For hearing impaired customers
    If ONCALL are not able to provide an Auslan interpreter for a customer with hearing impairment, a booking may be made with Deaf Services Queensland.
