Smartcard not received in mail (3363)

Smartcard not received in mail
Customer notification within 14 days of application
When a customer notifies the department that they have not received their Smartcard in the mail within 14* days from date of application, and:
  • there are no Returned mail or Image restrictions: and
  • the Smartcard Active status is less than 7 working days, advise the customer:
    • they should wait until the full 7 working days from Activation has elapsed. This will allow time for mailing of the Smartcard before they notify the department of the non receipt of their Smartcard.
    • if the address has been changed since the Smartcard was requested, the customer should still wait the full 7 working days.

*Note:If the Smartcard delivery time has been temporarily changed, it will be published in DocBase messages.

Where the Smartcard status is not active refer below to the Smartcard status and action table.

Note:When a replacement Photo ID or MLI is generated, the card is issued for a 10 year period from the date the replacement was processed. The shelf life of an image is 11 years, but may be extended by a maximum period of 6 months, making the total maximum shelf life of an image 11 years and 6 months.

As a result, where a person applies for a replacement Photo ID or MLI more than 6 months from the date of application, they will be required to attend a CSC to have a new image captured, because the maximum shelf life of the existing image has been exceeded.

If the card was lost in the mail and is "Returned" by IMU in TICA, the replacement product should be issued free of charge.

Where the card has been found and returned to TMR, the customer must be advised to apply for a replacement smartcard at a licence issuing office (full replacement requirements must be met).

All enquiries after 90 days has elapsed
    A customer who notifies the department that they have not received their Smartcard after 90 days has elapsed is to be advised to apply for a replacement Smartcard, unless the product has a status of "Returned" in TICA and the smartcard history indicates the card has been returned by IMU.

    A replacement fee will be required to be paid by the customer.
    A customer may request that they not be charged a replacement fee if they can prove a reasonable excuse as to why they were unable to notify the department of their non-receipt of their Smartcard within the 90 day period.

    Note:Driver Authorisation and Dangerous Goods driver licence holders are exempt from paying replacement smartcard fees provided they hold no other IA products. Code DA/DGD exempt is to be used regardless of the reason indicated by the customer on the application form.


    The customer makes application at a CSC and they must submit a written request for exemption of the replacement Smartcard fee to the CSC Office Manager for consideration. CSC does not need to seek IMU approval.

    If customer has contacted the Call Centre, they must be advised to make application through a CSC.

    Written requests for exemption of the replacement Smartcard fee must include the following:
    • Statement from the customer detailing the reason why they were unable to confirm and advise the department of their non-receipt of the Smartcard within the required 90 day period.
    • Copies of acceptable supporting documents that prove the circumstances claimed by the customer as preventing them from making the necessary notification within the required period. (e.g. a copy of a hospital admission document with date details that coincide with the expected delivery date of the Smartcard, or a copy of passport pages that confirm the person's absence from the country at the time of expected delivery of the Smartcard).
    • If the CSC Office Manager approves the exemption then use 'MANAGERS APPROVAL' reason.
