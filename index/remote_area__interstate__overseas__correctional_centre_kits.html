Remote Area/Interstate/Overseas/Correctional Centre Kit
What it is
What it is
What it is
    Remote Area kit
    Remote Area kit
      Customers who reside in remote areas of Queensland may make application for a driver licence, marine licence, industry licence, driver authorisation or Photo Identification card using the remote area kit.

      A person is eligible to use a remote area kit if they live in a remote area of Queensland and cannot get to a licence-issuing office or in circumstances where it is unreasonable or difficult for the person to attend a licence-issuing office.
    Interstate/Overseas Kit
    Interstate/Overseas Kit
      Customers who temporarily reside interstate or overseas may apply to renew or replace their driver licence using the interstate/overseas kit. Additionally, customers who reside interstate or overseas may also apply to complete licensing transactions using a kit.
    Correctional Centre Kits
    Correctional Centre Kits
      Prisoners in a Queensland correctional centre may apply to renew their driver licence if it has expired more than four years but less than five years.

      Prisoners in a Queensland correctional centre may apply for an Photo Identification card prior to their release.
Ineligible criteria
Ineligible criteria
    Customers are ineligible to use a Remote Area/Interstate/Overseas or Correctional Centre Kit if:
    • Their licence is currently cancelled/suspended or expired by more than 5 years;
      or
    • They are disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence in Australia or another country.

    Note:Customers can still use the kit to return or surrender a licence to TMR regardless of the licence status.

        Eligibility

          • the correctional centre kit has previously been used to renew the licence.

            Photograph requirements*
            Photograph requirements*
              The customer must supply 2 identical current colour photographs that meet the following format. These guidelines are in place to provide a suitable photo so that the application is not delayed by having to submit new photos in the required format.
              • Passport quality photographs (automatic machine photographs are not acceptable - e.g. from a photo booth)
              • 35mm wide x 45 mm long in size and be not more than 6 months old
              • Printed on high-quality paper and using high resolution (preferably 600dpi or higher)
              • Must not be manipulated, for example, by removing spots or softening lines
              • Both photographs must be signed on the back by an Approved Witness. One photograph must be endorsed on the back with the words: “I certify this is a true photograph of (the applicant) in my presence” and signed by an Approved Witness.

              The photographs must:
              • Show natural skin tones and have appropriate brightness and contrast and no flash reflections.
                Note:'Red eye' must not be removed in post production software
              • Show a neutral expression and mouth closed
              • Have a plain, light coloured background (e.g. white, cream or pale blue)
              • Show the person looking directly at the camera
              • Must not show hair across the eyes
              • Must show both edges of the face clearly
              • Have a full front view of head and shoulders and not show the person looking over one shoulder (portrait style) or with head tilted
              • Glasses or sunglasses must be removed for the photograph, even if normally worn when operating a vessel
                Note:Glasses or eye patches may be accepted if a doctors/medical certificate states that eye protection is required to be worn by the applicant as protection from the effects of flash photography.
              • Head coverings and hats are not permitted except for religious reasons in which case, facial features from bottom of chin to top of forehead and both edges of the face must be clearly shown.
              • Refer below: Invalid photos if photo is not compliant

            Note: *Not required if surrendering a product or the card is being returned

        Invalid photosIf the photos fail to meet compliance in TICA, the IMU may be contacted in order to assess if the photo can still be used for the issue of a smartcard.

        If the photos fails to meet the compliance standards, and the IMU has not approved them for use, the action required will depend on whether the customer has valid digital images stored and the transaction being processed.

        If the customer has valid images stored with sufficient life to cover the period remaining on the driver licence, then a smartcard can be issued.

        If the customer does not have valid images stored with sufficient life they may submit a new image in an electronic format (for example JPEG) by email.

        The image must have a resolution of 600dpi or higher. An assessment must be made to ensure the person in the electronic image is the same as the person in the physical photograph originally provided. Where any doubt exists it should be referred to the IMU. The electronic image must meet compliance in TICA.

        Troubleshooting Tips
        Troubleshooting Tips
          If staff are experiencing problems when capturing images from remote kits, the below tips on image manipulation may assist with capturing a compliant image:
          • zoom the PDF to 300-400%, then crop the image to save.
          • upload a larger image
          • ensure the image is cropped as close to the border of the passport photo as possible
          • rotate the image, if not straight
          • select a different supplied photo
