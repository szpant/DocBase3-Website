Notice of Requirement to Attend and Supply Information (5252)


Notice Under Section 128(2A) of TO(PT) Act to Attend and Supply Information
Following an approach from the Prosecution Unit it has been decided to implement a uniform “Notice Of Requirement To Attend And Supply Information“ to assist in consistency of an evidentiary aid throughout Compliance.
The notice should incorporate the relevant provisions of section 128(1) TO(PT) Act 1994 that the authorised person is relying upon to hopefully establish an offence through information provided. The inclusion of the provisions firmly places in the suspected person’s mind what the investigation is relating to.
The notice should provide the following:
  • Advise the “person” (suspected offender) of the relevant offence that the authorised person suspects on reasonable grounds to have been committed.
  • The offence specifically relates to either the operation, maintenance or repair of the public passenger vehicle or any combination.
  • It was agreed that the minimum information should include date, description of vehicle and details of the section breached.
  • The notice should be sent registered post or personally delivered. If sent registered post then the authorised person should declare on the reverse side of the office copy details of the postage.
  • A minimum defined period of fourteen days should apply between the issue of the notice and the date the person is required to attend.
  • An example of a notice under section 128(2A) of the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act is attached for your information.
  • To assist enquiries requests to produce records under section 129 of TO(PT) Act 1994 and/or records needed to be maintained under section 142 of TO(PT) Regulation 2005 can be included in this notice.
