^A Grant of Probate is an official document provided by the Supreme Court of Queensland officially recognising a Will as legally valid. This type of grant is provided where a valid Will was written, and an executor named in the Will is the applicant.
Applying for a Grant of Probate is not mandatory, however if Grant of Probate application is in progress –no business can be conducted until the Grant of Probate is finalised. If Grant of Probate has been applied for and finalised, it must be sighted in addition to above to confirm the executors.
*The Will only needs to be sighted to establish executor/s, not the beneficiary.
%Certified copies of the Will and Grant of Probate are acceptable.
@If more than one executor is named in the Will, all executors are required to complete a Statutory Declaration, however only one is required to sign the F2121 form, and present EOI.
