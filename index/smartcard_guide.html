About the Smartcard

Smartcard definitions
What it is
What it is
What it is
    The smartcard is the physical evidence of the person's licence/authority/Photo ID card. It is a credit card-sized plastic card containing a computer chip and a number of security features making them more reliable, durable, credible and difficult to duplicate.

    TMR can issue a smartcard for a non-TMR product such as the High Risk Work Licence.
TMR Smartcard specifications
Card security
Card security
Card security
    The smartcard has advanced security controls that will make the smartcard less susceptible to tampering and fraudulent reproduction.

    Security features
    The smartcard has a number of security features on the face of the smartcard including:
    • Colour facial image of customer (digital photo)
    • Image of the customer’s signature (digitised signature)
    • Unique smartcard number
    • Shadow/secondary facial image of customer
    • Watermark (Qld Coat of Arms)
    • Security overlay
    • Security foil patch
Card dimensions
Dimensions
Dimensions
    The smartcard is credit card size with base dimensions of 85.60mm by 53.98mm.

Front of the card
On the front of the card
On the front of the card
    The face of the card contains:
    • Digital photo
    • Digitised signature
    • Chip or antenna (contactless) icon
    • Licence/Authority/Reference number (the addition of 'CRN' next to the number will display on cards with the antenna (contactless) icon)
    • Conditions (conditions are not on Photo ID card)
    • Name
    • Date of birth (DOB is not on IA card)
    • Gender (only on cards issued before1 October 2016)
    • Height (only on cards issued before 1 October 2016)
    • Issuing authority
    • Licence/authority/card details (if applicable)
    • Card expiry (MLI Photo ID/APA)
    • Smartcard number (only on cards with the antenna (contactless) icon)
Back of the card
On the back of the card
On the back of the card
    The back of the card contains:
    • Cardholders residential address (optional on APA/Photo ID card)
    • Marine Licence Indicator code and effective date (Driver Licence only if MLI holder) including conditions
    • Classes of IA type (IA card only and only if Driver trainer or Tow Truck driver authority granted)
    • TMR internet address on cards with the chip
    • QGov internet address on cards with the antenna (contactless) icon
    • Smartcard number
    • Smartcard security foil

Gender and Height
Gender and Height Information
Gender and Height Information
    The gender indicator was removed from APA cards produced after 18 March 2016, and the gender and height indicators were removed from driver licence and MLI cards produced after 1 October 2016.

    TMR utilises digital facial recognition technology to confirm a person's identity. The Queensland Police also have access to TMR digital images through their Q-Lite tablet devices. Therefore there is no need for height and gender information to appear on the face of the driver licence.

    The gender indicators were removed in response to a 2013 amendment to the Commonwealth Sex Discrimination Act 1984. The changes to the Act were made to provide protection against discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation, gender identity and intersex status.

    TMR still collects gender and height information but doesn’t print it on driver licences, photo identification cards, marine licences or industry licences.

    No other state or territory records gender on their driver licences.

    Cardholders can attend a customer service centre to request removal of the indicators from their cards. There are no fee or form requirements for this transaction.
Address
Address information
Address information
    The Driver Licence, Marine Licence and Industry Authority smartcard will show the residential address on the rear of the smartcard.

    Photo ID card customers can choose whether or not to have their residential address displayed on the reverse of their card. Holders of older PIC cards can request to have their address added (displayed) and an address label will be sent to attach to their existing PIC card.

    Refer: QT Shop > Customer Product > Photo Identification Card Guide > Transaction details > Address displayed on PIC Card

    To change an address, Refer: Customer > 3. Change Customer Detail > Change Customer Detail Guide

Central production
Centralised production
Centralised production
    The smartcard is produced and distributed centrally from a card production house. This is to provide greater security against identity theft by producing cards at a secure location.
Standard mail
Standard mail delivery
Standard mail delivery
    All department issued smartcards will be sent via standard mail in an unmarked envelope, following the production of the smartcard from a central location to the address nominated by the customer on the application form. The customer should receive their card in the mail within 14 days from the date their application was approved.

    Customers who have signed up for e-reminders will be sent an email on the day the smartcard is produced advising their smartcard has been mailed and they will receive it within 10 days.

    Note:If the smartcard delivery time has been temporarily changed, it will be published in DocBase messages.
Register mail
Registered mail delivery
Registered mail delivery
    In some cases the customer may request for their card to be sent via registered mail. Registered Mail provides a secure posting service for smartcards where the cardholder will be required to sign for their smartcard.

    For registered mail requests the CSO needs to:
    • Refer the request to their supervisor/manager;
      and
    • The Supervisor/Manager is to make a decision based on the customers concern (e.g. concerns with normal post service to current address).

    If approved:
    • The CSO is to select the registered mail option;
      and
    • A registered mail fee will not apply.

    Note:Registered mail is NOT to be used for:
    • Post office boxes; or
    • Mail posted to an overseas address

Destroying returned cards /receipts/ confirmation slips
Destroying a returned smartcard
Destroying a returned smartcard
    A smartcard registered as returned must be destroyed (unless replaced as faulty or damaged - refund requested) by cutting the card:
    • Vertically through the smartcard chip and signature, and then
    • Horizontally through the smartcard chip and photo.

    After the Smartcard has been cut, it should be disposed of in a multimedia bin.

    Note:Multimedia bins can be ordered through the Inside TMR Recordkeeping and Information Governance SharePoint site.

    Alternatively, a shredder with a P3 security level (cross cut shredder), that is built to specifically shred smartcards, can be used in place of cutting the card. Once a card has been shredded, it can either be disposed of in a multimedia bin or in general waste.

    Note:Further information about a shredder's security level can be obtained from the shredder manufacturer. The shredder must have a P3 security level.
Destroying a returned receipt or confirmation slip
Destroying a returned receipt or confirmation slip
    When an IIA, driver licence Receipt, MLI confirmation slip and Photo ID confirmation slip are superseded the customer is to be advised to destroy the receipt/confirmation slip by cutting it diagonally in half.

Faulty/ Damaged - Refund Requested
    Smartcards that are Faulty or have been replaced as Damaged and the customer requests a review of the CSC decision must be returned to IT Branch

      Send faulty cards to IT Branch
    Damaged Cards – Customer requests refund
    Damaged Cards – Customer requests refund
      Where a smartcard does not meet the criteria for assessment as Faulty, it must be replaced as Damaged and a fee paid. If the customer requests a review of the decision, the card must be sent to Customer and Business for assessment.

      Customers are required to pay the replacement fee, if after assessment by Customer and Business Solutions it is found to be a faulty card, a refund will be provided.

      Operator must add text against the customer record advising:
      • Damaged – Refund Requested
      • User name & CSC

      The following information must be sent to Registration & Licensing Systems on the day they are received:
      • Damaged smartcard
      • Copy of the customers application form
      • Copy of the replacement licence payment receipt, record CSC contact email on receipt
      Note: Customer and Business Solutions do not have access to TICA if the above information is not provided the card cannot be assessed.

      Send Damaged Cards - Refund Requested to IT Branch

    Outcome of Assessment
    Outcome of Assessment
      Customer and Business Solutions will assess the smartcard and determine outcome, usually within 10 business days of receipt. In some instances additional investigation is required to determine possible manufacturing faults. If a customer enquires inside this timeframe please advise the application is still under assessment. Outside these timeframes CSC can email: PolicyProceduresSupport
      Subject: Smartcard assessment and customer CRN.

      Smartcard faulty - refund due
      Smartcard faulty - refund due
        If Customer and Business Solutions determine the smartcard is Faulty an email will be sent to the CSC. The CSC is to refund the Replacement Licence Fee in full to the customer, advise the customer accordingly and expire text.

        A REVCUST account adjustment must be performed from the Revenue Accounts below Refer: Smartcard > 1. Smartcard > The How To Smartcard Procedures > Identify Faulty / Damaged - Refund Requested Smartcards

      Smartcard damaged - no refund due
      Smartcard damaged - no refund due
        If Customer and Business Solutions determine the smartcard is Damaged an email will be sent to the CSC. The CSC is to then advise the customer of the outcome and expire text.

      Note:If Customer and Business Solutions is unable to determine which CSC the card was returned from, they forward the outcome to PolicyProceduresSupport who will then inform the CSC of the outcome via email.

Request for new digital photo or digitised signature by customer
Request for new digital photo or digitised signature by customer
    • If a holder of a licence, authority or Photo ID card requests a new photograph or signature for their smartcard, the decision to provide a new card is at the Chief Executive's discretion (e.g. CSC Manager).
    • Requests may be approved only if the original photograph is not a true likeness of the customer, or the signature does not match the customer's true signature.
    • If approved by the CSC Manager a new smartcard may be replaced using the MGAP reason code (fee free).

    Unacceptable images identified by Identity Management Unit (IMU)
    Unacceptable images identified by Identity Management Unit (IMU)
      • If IMU consider an image to be of unacceptable quality, IMU will recall the smartcard from the manufacturer for assessment.
      • If appearance of image is not acceptable, IMU will contact the issuing centre and advise them to contact customer.
      • Issuing centre to contact customer to arrange for customer to return to centre for a new image and processing of a fee-free replacement smartcard.

    Note:When replacing a digital photograph and/or digitised signature a new digital photograph and digitised signature (image set) must be captured.
