New Employee Training (NET) (7363)

Project NameCSB New Employee Training (NET)
WhenPhase 2 Essentials – Now Live June 2020
BackgroundThe New Employee Training (NET) provides a consistent and holistic approach to delivering training to new Customer Service Officers (CSOs) while maximising learning and development opportunities and outcomes.

Phase 1 Onboarding has been in use since early 2018 and provides new CSOs with vital week-one onboarding training. New CSOs initially complete their onboarding training in a customer service environment (with a buddy) giving them valuable exposure to the role, processes and workplace to help them get started.

Phase 2 Essentials covers high volume TMR transactions making new CSOs more productive, earlier. To ensure new CSOs are fully equipped, they will receive a combination of online and in-centre training and mentoring before progressing to classroom learning. A soft launch of Phase 2 Essentials was conducted in SEQ South in March 2020. Feedback and analysis have been actioned and the materials are now available state-wide.

Whilst Phase 3 Intermediate and Phase 4 Advanced are in development there is a temporary package called Transition TPT that should be utilised to cover the remaining content.

All previous TPT training materials have been archived.

There is now an online course on Accelerate for Technical Trainers that deliver NET. It will give Technical Trainers the tools to deliver the program effectively. Email learning.solutions@tmr.qld.gov.au to be enrolled.

Learning and Development Advisors (LDAs) are available to support the new training at a local level.
Who will be impacted and how?If you are responsible for recruiting/training new CSOs, contact your regional LDA in advance of your new employee starting so that they can support you through the current ‘NET Onboarding’ NET Essentials and Transition TPT program.
Implementation PlanThe project will be rolled out in four phases:
  • Phase 1 Onboarding – Live
  • Phase 2 Essentials – Live
  • Phase 3 Intermediate – 2022
  • Phase 4 Advanced – 2023
