Driver Assessment Centre (DAC) Project (6103)

Project NameDriver Assessment Centre (DAC) Project
WhenCommencement of Pilot at Carseldine - 5 December 2016, for 6 months
BackgroundA new way of delivering light vehicle practical driving assessments will be trialled in Carseldine from December 2016, with the instruction of the Driver Assessment Centre (DAC) for six months.

From Monday 5 December, all light vehicle practical driving assessments booked at Carseldine will be conducted from the DAC, which is adjacent to the Carseldine CSC at 532 Beams Road, Carseldine.

This trial site will offer centralised light vehicle practical driving assessment services to our customers. It will enable TMR staff to better support our young drivers and new drivers to Australia in their transition to holding a Queensland Drivers Licence.

The DAC provides TMR with opportunities to:
  • enhance the customer experience for our target customer base of learner drivers and their associates (mums, dads, driving instructors)
  • better manage resources to deliver within light vehicle practical driving assessment lead times
  • transition physical light vehicle practical driving assessment requirements for locations in other parts of our network to a centralised site
  • create an environment where we can increase road safety awareness with this vulnerable customer segment

The outcome of the trial at Carseldine will inform a business decision to either expand light vehicle practical driving assessment services from the DAC and then roll out this model to other sites across SEQ; or return to the current testing model at individual locations.

This trial is a part of a key within Customer Services Branch’s (CSB’s) 10 Year Roadmap.
Who will be impacted and how
How will this impact customers?
How will this impact customers?
    This project will offer learner drivers a calm, open environment for what can be a very stressful transaction.

    Associates of learner drivers (such as mums, dads, guardians and driving instructors) will also be offered a comfortable, productive environment while they wait for their applicant to return. This will foster a positive experience for them, during what can sometimes be an uncomfortable and stressful wait.

    During the trial period, all light vehicle assessments scheduled at Carseldine CSC will be completed from the DAC, which is situated in the old "Carseldine CSC 2" space, next to the CSC. This means that customers will need to present at the DAC for their assessment, rather than the CSC. This includes 8am assessments.

    Signage will be developed and installed to assist in directing customers to the new location.

    Articles have been published in the CBU and Driver Trainer newsletters, and the following fact sheet has been provided to Driver Trainers:

    DAC Fact Sheet_October 2016
How will this impact service delivery staff?
How will this impact service delivery staff?
    All light vehicle tests will be moved out of the Carseldine CSC and into the DAC throughout the trial period. Driving Examiners delivering these services will be based in the DAC.

    SBS is being updated to reflect the new Carseldine DAC location, in place of the Carseldine CSC.
