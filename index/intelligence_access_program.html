Intelligence Access Program (7338)

Intelligence Access Program (IAP)
What it isThe Intelligence Access Program (IAP) is a national program developed in partnership with all Australian road agencies. It uses satellite tracking and wireless communication technology to remotely monitor where, when, and how a range of heavy vehicles are being operated on the road network. The IAP delivers increased safety, productivity and access benefits to a range of network stakeholders in exchange for monitoring heavy vehicle compliance with specific network access permit conditions.

In Queensland, the IAP is:
  • mandatory for higher mass limits (HML).
  • mandatory from 1 June 2017 for some special purpose vehicles (SPVs), including heavy mobile cranes, drilling rigs and concrete pumping trucks that operate under permit.
  • available for non-standard freight vehicles and Performance Based Standards (PBS) vehicles that include IAP monitoring as a permit condition.

Further information can be found on the TMR IAP Web Page
IAP dataIAP data will be refreshed by the Heavy Vehicles Assurance and Analysis Unit once a month (end of month).

The spreadsheet contains lists and groupings of current IAP and On Board Mass (OBM) enrolled vehicles, the associated transport operator names, the conditions by which they’re monitored, for example, HML and the approval and expiry date of the enrolment.

The spreadsheet can be used to verify IAP enrolment as a condition of operation and assist with ascertaining the access arrangements that apply to the vehicle.

All enrolments will have a direct relationship with an issued permit and its associated access conditions except for vehicles operating under the Higher Mass Limits application and conditions. Higher Mass Limits vehicles operate under the Higher Mass Limits National Notice.
