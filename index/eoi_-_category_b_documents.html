EOI - Category B Documents (46)


Evidence of Identity - Category B Documents
What it isCategory B documents establish the use of the customer's name in the community.
About Category B DocumentsCurrency of Documents (status)
The documents must be current unless otherwise stated.
If doubt exists as to the authenticity of a document presented refer to Verifying Authenticity of Documents
Documents by Type
Financial
Financial
    • Financial institution debit/credit card (with signature, embossed/printed name and financial institution displayed)
    • Financial institution debit/credit card without embossed/printed name (with supporting letter)
    • Financial institution passbook (with supporting letter)

    Credit Card details must:
    • Not be written down when provided verbally
    • Be Blacked out when provided in writing
    Note:For Department of Human Services BasicsCard information refer to Government Issued documents below.

    Further Information
    Further Information
      Cards from the same financial institution may be counted as separate cat B documents (eg. 1 debit & 1 credit card may be counted as 2 cat B documents.)

      May be issued by any country.

      If the person only has a passbook the document may be accepted if supported by a letter on the financial institution letterhead confirming the holders:
      • Full name
      • Address
      • Signature
      • Account number &/or card number
      Note:If the financial institution will not provide the letter, the Customer Identity Declaration (F2031) may be used instead.

      If the holder's name is not embossed/printed on the financial institution debit/credit card the document may be accepted if supported by a letter/printed transaction update on an original financial institution letterhead confirming the holders:
      • Full name
      • Address
      • Signature
      • Account number &/or card number
      Note:If the financial institution will not provide the letter, the Customer Identity Declaration (F2031) may be used instead.

ID Cards

    • Australian Educational Institution Student Identity document (with photo and/or signature)

Government Issued
Government Issued
    • Australian Medicare card
      Further Information
      Further Information
        May be used by any person listed on the card and must be current.

        Customers may present a physical card or a digital copy of the card on a smart device using the Australian Government Medicare Express Plus mobile app.

        If presenting a digital copy, customers must login to the app and show the card within the digital wallet (in person transactions only). Screen shots are not acceptable.

        Information about the Australian Government Medicare Express Plus mobile app is available online - http://www.humanservices.gov.au/business/services/centrelink/digital-wallet-information-for-businesses-and-organisations/

        Note:A Medicare card is one Category B document; a card presented in two different forms, physical and digital, does not constitute two Category B documents.

        Acceptable Medicare Cards include:
        Green Medicare Card - Issued to Australian Citizens and Permanent Residents.

        Blue Interim Medicare Card - Issued to members of the public while their application for Australian permanent residency is considered.

        Yellow Medicare Reciprocal Care Cards - Issued to visitors to Australia who are residents of countries which Australia has reciprocal health care agreements with.

        Green Medicare Smartcards - Trial cards issued in 2005 to Australian residents who lived in Tasmania (Same as Green Medicare Card).

        Note:The following are NOT acceptable forms of EOI:
        • A Medicare certificate issued to a person stating they are ineligible for Medicare during a certain period of time.
        • A Medicare interim slip issued to a person pending production of a Medicare card.
    • The Department of Human Services BasicsCard
      Further Information
      Further Information
        May only be used by the person whom the card is issued to. The card must be current.
        Note:A BasicsCard is also acceptable EOI for a person issued a card as a nominee for another person.
    • Dept of Veterans' Affairs/ Centrelink/Department of Human Services Pensioner Concession card (inc. Healthcare cards
      Further Information
      Further Information
        May be used by any person listed on the card. Additional signature identification is required if not the primary cardholder.

        Customers may present a physical card or a digital copy of the card on a smart device using the Australian Government Centrelink Express Plus mobile app.

        If presenting a digital copy, customers must login to the app and show the card within the digital wallet (in person transactions only). Screen shots are not acceptable.

        Information about the Australian Government Centrelink Express Plus mobile app is available online - http://www.humanservices.gov.au/business/services/centrelink/digital-wallet-information-for-businesses-and-organisations/

        Note:A Medicare card is one Category B document; a card presented in two different forms, physical and digital, does not constitute two Category B documents.
    • Interstate Government issued or Government approved Proof of Age Card or Photo Card*
      Note:*NSW and WA have replaced their existing Proof of Age Cards with Photo Cards. These cards are considered acceptable Category B EOI documents.
    • Department of Corrective Services - Form 16 Leave of Absence Order
      Further Information
      Further Information
        Only acceptable if:
        • Presented on the date it is issued
        • The person presenting the form is accompanied by a Corrective Services Officer who can verify the authenticity of the form

        ID must be sighted to confirm that the accompanying person is a Corrective Services Officer.
