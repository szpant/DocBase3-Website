Advertising Guidelines - Filling a vacant position
Advertising Guidelines - Filling a vacant position

    If a vacancy is identified CSB road safety staff arrange a relief supervisor, if available, for the period that the position will be vacant.

    CSB road safety staff will forward the recruitment documentation to the school to commence the recruitment process, which includes:
    • Advertisement for inclusion in school newsletter
    • Role description
    • School Crossing Supervisor Employment application (F1843)

    The Principal or delegated officer is required to:
    • advertise, interview and recommend applicants for School Crossing Supervisor (SCS) positions, in consultation with CSB road safety staff and the school's parent or community group
    • advise the department of the preferred applicant immediately to ensure road safety staff can commence health assessment and blue card processes.

Advertising options and opportunities
Advertising options and opportunities
    Attracting people to the role of School Crossing Supervisor can be a significant challenge in both urban and rural areas within the existing tight labour market.

    Strategies for attracting applicants to Crossing Supervisor roles include:

    Advertising vacancies in different locations, for example:

    • School Facebook page or website
    • School Newsletter
    • Other local schools
    • Local newsaper
    • Community Groups
    • Church Newsletters
    • Advertising text should suit target audience
    • Word of mouth and referrals can broaden the applicant pool, but where this occurs applicants must be advised that selection is merit based.
Recruitment decisions - Post Selection Information
Recruitment decisions - Post Selection Information

    Selection of a successful applicant for the role of School Crossing Supervisor is to be:
    • based on merit;
    • subject to the applicant passing the independent health assessment and,
    • subject to the applicant successfully gaining approval for a Blue Card.

    CSB road safety staff must remember the selection process continues well beyond the interview and includes the selection decision (which must be appropriately documented), notification of the decision and follow up of employment screening requirements.

    Successful applicants
    Successful applicants

      When an applicant has been nominated as meeting all pre-determined criteria, regions are to ensure that successful applicants receive standard letters which:
      • advise details of their pre-employment health assessment
      • welcomes them to Transport and Main Roads;
      • lists the relevant employment conditions; and
      • notifies them that a suitable time is to be arranged for induction and training prior to commencing employment.
      Refer: DocBase > Road Safety Policy > 1. School Crossing Supervisor Scheme > Letters and Templates
    Notification to applicants
    Notification to applicants

      Once a recruitment decision is made, all applicants are to be advised of the outcome (whether they have been successful or unsuccessful).
    Providing post-selection feedback
    Providing post-selection feedback

      Employees providing selection feedback are to take care to avoid discriminatory comments or language in this feedback discussion. Assistance in providing feedback can be sought from your local HR Manager or Advisor.

Shows, fetes and displays
Shows, fetes and displays

    The Manager (Road Safety) may employ a school crossing supervisor at shows, school fetes and displays for the purpose of enhancing Transport and Main Roads' (TMR) role and function. Any such employment will be paid at the standard hourly rates in accordance with General employees (Queensland Government Departments) and other employees Award – State 2015

    School event/s
    School event/s

      Where extra hours occur at a school event, the school should apply to the CSB Road Safety Staff in writing one month prior to the event.

      CSB Road Safety Staff will submit the application for approval to the Manager (Road Safety). If approved, they will forward a letter to:
      • The School Principal identifying dates and information and advising them that they will be responsible for the payment of the additional wages; and
      • Each school crossing supervisor required to perform extra duties, outlining the extra duty time and other requirements.
    Non-school events
    Non-school events

      A School Crossing Supervisor may be paid by other agencies e.g. business groups, local government or community groups to operate an approved, marked or children’s crossing, only if:
      • The Regional Director approves the engagement.
      • The crossings on which the supervisors are to operate meet the criteria established in the Queensland Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices.
      • The hiring agency pays the standard hourly rate, in accordance with the General employees (Queensland Government Departments) and other employees Award – State 2015
      • The Region ensures that the appropriate PPE is provided.
      • The hiring agency ensures that they carry public liability and workers’ compensation cover for any school crossing supervisors they employ.
      • Training and assessment of suitably equipped school crossing supervisors is conducted by TMR.

Employment Classification
Employment Classification

    The conditions of employment and terms of engagement differ for various employees working in the field of road safety, according to the requirements of the role.

    School crossing supervisors are classified as casual employees of Transport and Main Roads (TMR). They are engaged under the General employees (Queensland Government Departments) and other employees Award – State 2015.

    Definitions of casual employment relevant to these employees:

    • Casual Employee – an employee who is engaged as such on an hourly basis to work for less than the ordinary working hours of a full-time employee (Public Service Award)
    • Long Term Casual (LTC) – casual employees who work on a regular and systematic basis for several periods of employment, during a period of at least 1 year (or 12 months), as stated by the Industrial Relations Act 2016.
    • School Crossing Supervisors - may be defined as long term casuals, dependent upon their individual employment arrangement and length of service.

    A casual roster system is currently in place for school crossing supervisors to ensure road safety outside Queensland schools is not compromised.

    Seek HR advice

    Given the legal and industrial complexities involved in the employment and management of casual employees, your local HR Manager or Advisor must be advised of any enquiries or challenges about:

    • casual employment status;
    • conditions of employment; or
    • any other management matters (for example, performance or conduct issues) which could have legal or industrial implications for the employee or the department.
InductionInduction is a critical component of the employment process and includes information about:
  • Workplace environment
  • Performance and conduct expectations
  • Conditions of employment;
  • Scope of responsibilities and training relating to specific role
  • Health and safety considerations and processes

Induction for school crossing supervisors has direct impact on their personal safety and the safety of the community. The following information is specific to the induction of school crossing supervisors and lists critical information for inclusion in the school crossing supervisor induction process.

Conditions of Employment
Employment Screening
Employment Screening

    Any person employed in regulated employment as defined in the Working with Children (Risk Management & Screening) Act 2000 is required to hold a Blue Card

    School crossing supervisors will comply with all employment screening requirements and the provisions of S122F of the TO(RUM) Act 1995 which requires that employees declare and report any criminal charge or conviction to their supervisor and states that failure to do so may result in disciplinary action.

    Refer: DocBase > Road Safety Policy > 1. School Crossing Supervisor Scheme > Employment Screening
Hours of Duty
Hours of Duty
    School crossing supervisors will normally work for 1.5 hours in any one day, Monday to Friday (excluding school and public holidays) which includes 30-60 minutes in the morning and 30 minutes in the afternoon – before and after school hours. Each daily engagement shall stand alone, with a minimum payment for 30 minutes work made in respect to each engagement.
Leave
Leave
    School crossing supervisors will not be entitled to paid personal leave (including carer’s leave) or paid recreational leave, appropriate allowances are included in the rate of remuneration. Refer to award for additional unpaid leave entitlements and long service leave entitlements for long term casual employees.
Workers' Compensation
Workers' Compensation
    School crossing supervisors are covered for workers’ compensation under Work Cover.
Payment of Remuneration
Payment of Remuneration
    Remuneration is paid each fortnight directly to the account nominated on the Payroll Commencement Form. A PAYG payment summary (group certificate) will be posted at the start of each financial year. Payment Advice Slips are forwarded directly to the school crossing supervisor's address.
    Relief Entitlements
    Relief Entitlements

This is a standard endorsement available on request from all insurance companies. The policy does not require the actual words ‘Queensland Government’ the word ‘employer will suffice.

      Note:Employees should check their PDS on their insurance policy under the liability section for the standard wording. Where an employee claims GST or uses their private vehicle more than 10% for business use they should contact their insurance company for their requirements relating to their particular insurance policy.

      For more information and requirements see Allowances and Expenses - Vehicle Allowance

      • The kilometres usually travelled for a return trip from home to own school via the most direct route
      • The kilometres travelled for a return trip from home to the relieving school via the most direct route
      • If the distance is greater to relief school, claiming the difference to and from the school per session of relief.

Procedure of Resignation
Procedure of Resignation
    School crossing supervisors will advise CSB Road Safety staff as soon as possible of their intention to resign. Fourteen working days notice should be given using Resignation Advice Form (F3225)
Personal Protective Equipment
Personal Protective Equipment
    All issued equipment must be left at the school upon resignation.
Assessment of Performance
Assessment of Performance
    School crossing supervisors will have their performance assessed at the discretion of CSB Road Safety staff. The assessment will be based on the standard crossing drill or authorised variations.
Reporting of Incidents
Reporting of Incidents
    All incidents deemed by the school crossing supervisor to warrant attention are to be reported on Incident Report Form (M1044) and forwarded to nearest road safety office.

ProbationProbation (as defined under the Probation Management Policy (refer to inside HR) does not apply to employees engaged on a casual basis. School crossing supervisors are therefore not subject to a probationary period.

Road safety staff will closely monitor the performance of newly appointed school crossing supervisors for the first 12 weeks of their engagement and at least annually thereafter, unless determined otherwise by Manager (Road Safety) in accordance with the principles of performance management and TMR’s duty of care to employees and the community.
