Conditions of Use (1330)


Dual Purpose
Dual Purpose Ships
Dual Purpose Ships
    When an other Queensland regulated ship is being genuinely used for private purposes:

    Section 32 of the Transport Operations (Marine Safety) Regulation 2016 provides for the use of an other Queensland regulated ship for private purposes. The owner/operator of the ship must comply with these requirements prior to the ship being used for private recreation.
Other Queensland regulated ship conditionsThe Transport Operations (Marine Safety) Regulation 2016, section 29 outlines the conditions of registration of other Queensland regulated ships.

Note:The registering authority may impose other conditions on the registration.
Overseas Interstate
Ships Travelling Overseas
Ships Travelling Overseas
    It is recommended that Queensland regulated ships wishing to leave Australian waters, register with the Australian Registry of Ships (Australian Maritime Safety Authority www.amsa.gov.au).

    If the owners wish to use the ship for private use in Australian waters as well as international travel they are required to meet the registration requirements of their state of residence.
Overseas Visitors
Overseas Visitors
    Visitors with a recreational ship from overseas may use their ships in Queensland waters for less than one year. If they become a resident they need Queensland registration immediately.
Interstate Registered Ships
Interstate Registered Ships
    Ships registered in another state and operated in Queensland waters by an interstate resident are not required to be registered in Queensland.

    Visitors from interstate may use their ships in Queensland waters provided that it is currently registered.

    Registration of ships is to be in the registered operator's state of residence. This is a legislative requirement.

    Exceptions
    Exceptions
      Ships registered in another state must be registered in Queensland when the:
      • Individual is a Queensland resident
      • Persons principal place of business is in Queensland
      • Principal place of operating the ship is in Queensland


Interstate Unregistered Ships
Interstate Unregistered Ships
    If a customer resides in a jurisdiction which does not require ship registration (e.g. Norther Territory), and wants to temporarily operate it in Queensland waters, it must be registered (for the period that it is in Queensland waters).

    After the registration is no longer required (e.g. end of holiday), the customer can cancel the registration and obtain a pro-rata refund.
