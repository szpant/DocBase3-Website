Photo Identification Card Guide (3332)


About Photo Identification Card
What it isThe Photo Identification (Photo ID) Card is a smartcard product that confirms that the person named on the card is at least 15 years of age.

Prior to 1 April 2019, the Photo ID Card was known as the Adult Proof of Age (APA) Card.
Benefits and ReasonsThe Photo ID Card is a Government issued card that may be used as an evidence of age document or for evidence of identity (EOI) purposes. A Photo ID Card can be issued with or without the cardholder's residential address displayed on the reverse of the card. Gender is not displayed on the Photo ID card.
Adult Proof of Age Cards issued prior to 1 April 2019APA cards issued prior to 1 April 2019 have been officially renamed (in TICA) as Photo ID cards. There is no requirement for an APA card holder to obtain a new card. APA cards are still recognised and remain valid until the expiry date shown on the front of the card.

APA card holders who apply for a replacement card for any reason will be issued a Photo ID card.

Using a Photo Identification Card
Transaction details
Granting
Granting
    What it is
    A Photo ID Card may only be granted if:
    • The identity of the applicant has been confirmed,
    • The applicant is an eligible person Refer: Eligibility for Photo ID Card
    • The prescribed fee has been paid.

    Delivery
    The Photo ID card will be sent to the mailing address stated on the application form. This may be a residential or postal address.
Address displayed on Photo ID Card
Address displayed on Photo ID Card
    A Photo ID cardholder can choose whether or not they want their residential address displayed on the reverse of their card.
    • New applicants will advise their preference on their Photo ID application form.
    • Photo ID card holders who chose to have their address displayed but later changes their mind, will be eligible for a free replacement card without their address displayed (no form required). A new card will be automatically generated, the applicant should be advised to destroy their old Photo ID card when their new Photo ID card arrives in the mail.
    • There is a limit of one free replacement card within a 12 month period for removal of an address from a Photo ID card.
    • Existing Photo ID cardholders may request to have their address displayed, a free address label will be generated and sent to attach to their existing card (no form required). A free replacement card does not apply when adding an address.

    Note:An online service is available for applicants to add or remove their address on their Photo ID card.

Free Photo ID Card
Eligibility for free Photo ID Card
Eligibility for free Photo ID Card
    A person is eligible for a free Photo ID card if at any time:
    • They are adding or removing the display of their address on the rear of their card (only once in a 12 month period is free for removing an address);
      OR
    • They are removing the display of their gender on their existing APA card;
      OR
    • They qualify under the following driver licence surrendered/cancelled section scenarios.
      Driver licence surrendered/cancelled
      Driver licence surrendered/cancelled
        • They have surrendered their driver licence voluntarily because they no longer wish to drive;
          OR
        • They have surrendered their driver licence because of a medical condition;
          OR
        • They have had their licence show cause cancelled on medical grounds;
          AND
        • It is the first issue of a Photo ID card to them since their licence was surrendered or cancelled.

      Note:Customers who have transferred an interstate licence to QLD are eligible for a free Photo ID Card (if they meet another eligibility requirements for a free card).

      Important:The status of the licence must be surrendered or cancelled. A person is not eligible if they have been granted another driver licence or issued an APA or Photo ID card since the surrender or show cause medical cancellation.

Not eligible for free Photo ID Card
Not eligible for free Photo ID Card
    A person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card if the licence they are surrendering is expired* or cancelled due to a court disqualification, or if the licence is being surrendered for any of the following reasons:
    • Interstate/overseas travel
    • Multiple licence holder
    • On behalf of deceased
    • Merged
    • Interstate licence held
    • Overseas licence held

    * A manager has discretion in extenuating circumstances to authorise a free Photo ID card if the licence is recently expired.

    A person is not eligible for a free Photo ID card for removing their address, if they have had a replacement card in the last 12 months for removing their address from an APA or Photo ID card.
Reduced learner licence fee for Photo ID card holder
Photo ID card holders who apply for a class C learner licence for the very first time are eligible to receive a reduced learner licence fee.

TICA will automatically apply the reduced fee when the customer is eligible.

Not eligible for reduced learner licence fee
Not eligible for reduced learner licence fee
    A person is not eligible to receive the reduced fee if they:
    • have at any time held an Australian driver licence
    • hold an Adult Proof of Age card issued before 1 April 2019
    • hold an 18+ card
    • hold a Photo ID card that was issued to them free of charge (for example because of a name change or removal of address displayed on the back of the card)

    TICA will automatically apply the reduced fee when the customer is eligible.
Determining eligibility for discount
Determining eligibility for discount
    To determine if a customer is eligible for a reduced learner licence fee operators will have to confirm that the customer holds a Photo ID card, not an Adult Proof of Age card.

    Operators can access the Photo Id Card History screen in TICA to confirm this. This screen can be accessed through the Photo ID Card hub. The Smartcard History screen will not differentiate between an APA or Photo ID card.
TICA processing for free Photo ID Card, overseas or interstate licences and Returning Drivers
TICA processing for free Photo ID Card, overseas or interstate licences and Returning Drivers
    Customer has been issued with a free Photo ID card
    Customer has been issued with a free Photo ID card
      Customers who replace an Adult Proof of Age Card or Card 18+ and receive a free Photo ID card (for example name change) are not entitled to a discounted learner licence. However, TICA may incorrectly apply a discount when issuing their learner licence.

      To rectify this, the $40 debt must be adjusted into the customer's account from the EAF Learner Licence Revenue Account and then paid with the Learner Licence fee.

      Refer: Driver Licence > 2. Non-Qld Driver Licence > The How To: Non-Qld Licence > Non-Qld Driver Licence Procedures > Transfer > Transfer Driver Licence/Class

    Transferring an overseas learner licence
    Transferring an overseas learner licence
      Overseas learner licence holders who have previously obtained a Photo ID card and apply to transfer to a QLD learner licence are entitled to a reduced licence fee but TICA will incorrectly charge the full learner licence fee. In order to ensure the correct fee is charged operators should "Issue" a learner licence rather than transferring an overseas learner and then update the Non-Queensland product screen to reflect the overseas licence details.

    Transferring an expired interstate learner licence
    Transferring an expired interstate learner licence
      Interstate learner licence holders are not eligible for a reduced learner licence fee. However, as expired interstate learner licences can't be transferred they must be "Issued" the discount is incorrectly applied.

      To rectify this, the $40 debt must be adjusted into the customer's account from the EAF Learner Licence Revenue Account and then paid with the Learner Licence fee.

      Refer: Driver Licence > 2. Non-Qld Driver Licence > The How To: Non-Qld Licence > Non-Qld Driver Licence Procedures > Transfer > Transfer Driver Licence/Class

    Issuing a learner licence to a Returning Driver
    Issuing a learner licence to a Returning Driver
      Returning Drivers that have previously held an Australian licence are not entitled to a reduced learner licence fee. If the previous licence (QLD or Interstate) does not display in the licence history screen TICA will incorrectly apply a discount when issuing their learner licence.

      To rectify this, the $40 debt must be adjusted into the customer's account from the EAF Learner Licence Revenue Account and then paid with the Learner Licence fee.

      Refer: Driver Licence > 2. Non-Qld Driver Licence > The How To: Non-Qld Licence > Non-Qld Driver Licence Procedures > Transfer > Transfer Driver Licence/Class
Void Payments
Void Payments
Void Payments
    A Photo ID Card will be void if:
    • The cheque is not honoured on presentation or is later dishonoured;
    • The credit card payment is not honoured.

    The customer will be advised in writing that:
    • The payment has been voided (cheque or credit card payment);
    • A new application may be lodged for a Photo ID Card at a CSC/agent with full requirements;
    • The voided Photo ID Card must be returned unless the holder has given a written declaration stating the card is lost, stolen or destroyed.
    Note: An APA or Photo ID Card that is valid at any time within the last 2 years will be acceptable as standard EOI.

    The notice will be sent by Finance.
