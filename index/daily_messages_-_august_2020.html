    28/08/2020 - Reminder – Forwarding Applications to the Industry Authority Hub
    28/08/2020 - Reminder – Forwarding Applications to the Industry Authority Hub

      The Industry Authority Hub has noticed a recent increase in errors on IA applications. When forwarding an application to the hub please ensure:
      • The required check list is completed and adhered to in conjunction with the application form.
      • The Driver Trainer Certificate is acceptable. The checklists display all the correct and current training requirements including the correct course codes . All National Registered Training providers can be verified in the eligibility section of each industry authority guide in DocBase.
      • That the Statement of Attainment or medical prerequisite is not recorded in TICA for a new Industry licence. Only the Medical Condition Reporting (MCR) section of TICA should be updated.
      • All application forms are fully completed prior to forwarding to the hub.
      • Dangerous Goods applications must not be submitted without full requirements being met. There has also been instances of incorrect Truck Safe medicals being accepted and submitted. Refer: Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Guide > Eligibility Requirements > Medical Certificate or Truck Safe Medical
      • The Industry Hub is notified when the customer passes their Q-Safe Tow Truck test or Pilot Vehicle written test to finalise the application.
      • If a customer calls enquiring about their application they must be referred to the CSC where the application was lodged. They should not be provided with the IA hub contact number.
      • Renewals and upgrades are processed at the CSC and not forwarded to the HUB

      Peta Berry

      20/08/2020 - Changes to the Disability Parking Permit Scheme - Updated
      20/08/2020 - Changes to the Disability Parking Permit Scheme - Updated

        The eligibility criteria for the disability parking permit scheme will be expanded to include people who are diagnosed as legally blind from Monday, 31 August 2020. People who have been assessed as legally blind should not apply for the scheme until this date.

        However, if an application for a legally-blind person is received at a CSC from Monday, 24 August it can be accepted and forwarded to the Disability Parking Unit. The customer should be advised that the application will be held and not processed until 31 August 2020 or after.

        Additionally, a new application form Australia Disability Parking Permit - Legally Blind Application F5280 will be made available from Friday, 28 August.

        Further information can be found in the Project Update.

      07/08/2020 - New procedure for cancelling a receipt
      07/08/2020 - New procedure for cancelling a receipt

        The SEQN Continuous Improvement Community of Practice trialed a new process where the current cancelled receipt procedure was reviewed by SASM's to reduce the amount of cancelled receipts being completed in CSC's.

        This trial was a success and resulted in a reduction of cancelled receipts being completed by CSO's, which in-turn saw a reduction in re-work by the operators and banking officers. The feedback provided by the SASM and PCSO's was positive.

        The approved process now includes a pre-approval step whereby a CSO must apply for approval by a SASM, SPECPRO or Manager prior to completing the cancelled receipt process.

        The approving officer is responsible for ensuring the appropriate process is taken, the text is sufficient prior to printing and the approval is noted on the operators banking sheet.

      18/08/2020 - Clarification of when a dealer needs a COI
      18/08/2020 - Clarification of when a dealer needs a COI

        A registered operator does not need to possess or give a COI to the acquirer if the vehicle is being disposed of to a dealer. A COI is not needed for the transfer of registration to a Purpose of Use (POU) of DEAL.

        After the transfer, the dealer is not exempt from possessing a current COI for a COI vehicle. If the vehicle is defective, the dealer must arrange for the vehicle to be repaired and obtain a COI within a reasonable period, or de-register the vehicle. The period that is reasonable may vary depending on the type of repairs required.

        The dealer must have a current COI before offering the vehicle for sale, and must not use the vehicle on the road without a COI, unless necessary for the purpose of repairing the vehicle.
