Registered Operators (3022)


What is it?A registered operator is the entity that is responsible for:
  • The operation of the vehicle
  • The vehicle being in a safe condition for use on the roads

All costs including:
  • Registration renewals
  • Infringements

Being the registered operator does not infer legal ownership of the vehicle. The legal owner is the entity that purchased the vehicle. Department of Transport and Main Roads records up to two registered operators, one of which must be a legal entity.
Requirements
  • At least one registered operator must be a legal entity
  • Must be over 18 years old to be the registered operator of a heavy vehicle
  • Must pay the appropriate costs

There are no age restrictions on the registered operators of a light vehicle.

Paying Agents
    A paying agent is:
    • A legal entity that is not a private individual
    • Responsible for the payment of renewal fees
    • Recorded as the second registered operator

    Note: A vehicle registered with a non-legal entity as the first registered operator can't have a paying agent.


    The leasing company/paying agent will receive the vehicle registration notice only, all other documents including certificates and infringements will be sent to the first registered operator.
