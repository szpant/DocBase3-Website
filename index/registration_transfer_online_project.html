Registration Transfer Online Project (6049)

Project NameRegistration Transfer Online Project
WhenAugust 2016
BackgroundThe Customer Services Branch (CSB) “Electronic Service Delivery Strategy” outlined a number of initiatives aimed at encouraging customers to make more effective use of electronic service delivery channels such as phone and internet.

The Registration Transfer Online (RTO) project will deliver an online service that will enable the transfer of vehicles and vessels via the electronic service delivery channel. In order to provide customers with the ability to transfer vehicles online, the application of immediate “Notice of Disposals” (NOD) will be introduced. This approach will not only enable the registration transfer online process but also provide the disposer with a level of assurance that registration related correspondence including infringements and toll evasion notices will be sent to the nominated acquirer.

The RTO project is completely dependent upon the successful delivery of the Inspection Certificates Online (ICO) project managed by the Transport Regulation Branch (TRB). The ICO project will enable an Approved Inspector at an ICO enabled AIS to automatically submit safety certificates, certificate of inspections and defect inspection results online to TMR.
Objective The objective of this project is to provide our customers with increased accessibility for meeting their legal obligations when transferring a registration.

The desired end state of this initiative is that the RTO online service becomes an established service delivery channel for CSB with a goal to:
  • Increase availability of online services to our customers;
  • Reduce call volumes in the Call Centre; and
  • Reduce the pressure on the customer service centre network.

Implementation of quality electronic services aligns with the Department’s strategic priorities from the Transport and Main Roads Strategic Plan 2015-2019 by investing in front-line services and innovative technologies to continually improve the customer experience.
How does the online service work? The current existing process relies on the acquirer completing the transaction by visiting a CSC, until this occurs the disposer continues to receive all registration related correspondence including infringement and toll evasion notices.

The RTO service will enable the disposer to complete the NOD and provide their consent to transfer the vehicle or vessel, at this point a NOD will be applied to the registration which will prevent any infringement or registration related correspondence being sent to the disposer. Disposers will benefit from the assurance provided through the automatic application of the NOD.

The disposer will enter the acquirer’s information including the acquirer’s email address, an email notification will be sent to the acquirer advising the disposer has completed their component of the transfer online. The acquirer completes their part of the transaction via the online service by accepting the pending transfer, paying the stamp duty and transfer fees.

The acquirer can email themselves confirmation of the transfer transaction.
A certificate of registration will be sent to the acquirer confirming that the registration has been transferred into their name and they are now recorded as the registered operator of the vehicle or vessel.
The project includes: The online service will enable the:
  • Recording of the disposer’s authority for the registration to be acquired
  • Recording of the acquirer’s authority for the registration to be transferred
  • Transfer of registration to individuals (it is important to note that joint registration in two individual names is excluded from the online service)
  • Automated immediate NOD processing
  • Ability to renew, and select a new term and CTP insurer if the registration is due for renewal at the time of transfer.
