e-Correspondence and e-Reminder Online Service Project (Phase 2) (7080)

Project Namee-Correspondence and e-Reminder Online Service Project
WhenService Deployment Date – December 2016 and March 2017
BackgroundThe Customer Services Branch (CSB) “Electronic Service Delivery Strategy” outlined a number of initiatives aimed at encouraging customers to make more effective use of electronic service delivery channels such as online services.

With a Whole of Government focus on “digital first” and improving accessibility to our online services, coupled with the high usage of TMR services in the community, Phase 3 of the Online Customer Experience (OCE) Program will continue to deliver online initiatives.

The implementation of e-Correspondence and e-Reminder (Phase 1) in June 2016 will deliver significant benefits and savings by migrating customers away from the existing standard mail delivery channel. These initiatives are expected to reduce the number of standard mail items being delivered for identified high volume correspondence items by up to 30% over four years.

The e-Correspondence and e-Reminder service will be expanded over the next six months to include more notices and reminders to our customers.
Who will be impacted and how?
How will this impact customers?
How will this impact customers?
    The project will increase accessibility and convenience for customers to TMR transactional services. Customers will now be able to enrol at a CSC or via the Call Centre, as well as opt in using the online service.

    When a customer opts into the e-Notices service TMR will:
    • Send the customer an email confirming they have successfully registered to receive notices electronically.
    • Send the customer an email containing a PDF of the applicable notice
    • House the electronic document in the customer’s online history for a period of 13 months.
    • Implement security options to provide customers with a level of assurance that the email is authentic.

    When a customer opts into the e-Reminder service, they will be sent courtesy reminder emails for services/bookings they have with TMR.

    Customers will receive an email confirming they have successfully registered to receive email reminders.
How will this impact Customer Service Officers and Call Centre Consultants?
How will this impact Customer Service Officers and Call Centre Consultants?
    The implementation of this initiative will deliver significant benefits and savings by migrating customers away from the existing standard mail delivery channel.

    Staff are encouraged to actively promote the range of services available to customer and offer to enrol them in the e-notice and e-reminder services.
Further informationInformation about these new services will be included in DocBase for reference by staff upon implementation of the project.

Customers can currently opt in or opt out of the e-Correspondence service through our online services. The customer must pass second-level authentication and provide their email address.

The December TICA release will allow frontline staff to enrol the customer through the Customer Online Account Management screen using the same process that is currently available to cancel e-notices and/or e-reminders.

e-Notices
e-Notices
    The e-notices service will be expanded to include the following notices:

    December Release
    • Queensland Regulated Ship Renewal Notices - F4088
    • Registration Certificates - F3506 - Regulated Ships
    • Overdue Registration Fees Final Notice - F3508

    March Release
    • Driver Licence Renewal Notice - F3004 or F4750
e-Reminders
e-Reminders
    The email reminder service will enable our customers to receive reminders for the following additional services/bookings:

    December Release
    • Industry Authority Renewal
      A courtesy reminder will be sent to the customer to renew their industry authority including driver authorisations prior to the expiry date
    • Smartcard in the mail
      If the customer has completed a transaction that requires a new smartcard to be produced an email will be sent to the customer when the smartcard is sent in the mail

    March Release
    • Industry Authority Approved. A courtesy notification will be sent to the customer when their industry authority including driver authorisations has been approved. This will include their Interim Authority as an attachment

    Note: Customers who are signed up for e-reminders and apply for an Adult Proof of Age Card while they are under 18, will receive an email advising the card will be delivered to them by post. Please ensure that when an application is processed for this scenario customers are advised the Adult Proof of Age Card will not be posted to their address and the customer will be contacted by the customer service centre advising it is ready for collection.
Courtesy Notifications
Courtesy Notifications
    The release will also include emails which will be sent to a customer when changes have been made using the online service to their residential, postal or email address. This will alert customers in the event that changes have been made without their knowledge. These emails will be sent automatically to customers who have an e-contact email address recorded with TMR. Customers do not need to be signed up to e-notices/e-reminders to receive these emails.
Automated Notifications
Automated Notifications
    These services will include the following automated notifications:

    December Release
    • Address Changed Online
      An email will be sent to the customer when their residential or postal address is changed online
    • Email Address Changed Online
      An email will be sent to a customer’s previous email address when their email address is changed online – note, an email is already sent to their new email address

    March Release
    • Driver Licence SPER Suspended
      A courtesy email will be sent to the customer if SPER advises us that the customers driver licence has been suspended
