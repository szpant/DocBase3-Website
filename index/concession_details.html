Charitable Concession
Charitable Concession
    ***Important Message to CSO/TMR Agents***

    CSO/TMR Agents should not provide advice to customers about eligibility for application of this concession type based on the forms lodged or information disclosed at the centre. Registration Policy make thorough assessments by examining the information and documents provided, and in some cases may need to request or source additional information before making a decision.

    What is it?A charitable concession exempts the registration and traffic improvement fees for approved organisations established for the sole purpose of supplying either:
    • Direct help, relief or support
    • Education or instruction (whether spiritual, mental, physical, technical social or otherwise)
    • Care, housing or other assistance to any persons in distress.
    Definition of Person in Distress
    Definition of Person in Distress
      Any person in distress by reason of:
      • The death or disability of another or others on whom they were wholly or partly dependant
      • Disease, sickness, age, any mental or physical infirmity, indigence or unemployment
      • Fire, flood, cyclone, drought, war or any other emergency or catastrophe
    Customer Eligibility and Application Requirements
    Organisation concession eligibility and application process
    Organisation concession eligibility and application process
      Applying for a CHAR Qualification
      Applying for a CHAR Qualification
        Registration Policy is the only area that can approve an organisation for a Charitable Concession and apply the qualification to the Organisation's CRN.

        Note:Organisations must at a minimum be:
        • A registered charity; and
        • Endorsed as a Public Benevolent Institution; and
        • Have Tax Deductible Gift Recipient status.

      Eligibility and application requirements
      Eligibility and application requirements
        As provided on the Assessment for a Charitable or Community Service Concession Application (F4508), organisations must provide a:
        • Completed F4508 application;
        • Copy of the organisation's Constitution or Model Rules outlining the organisations objectives to provide a charitable service purpose to assist persons in distress;
        • Copy of organisation's Australian Business Number (ABN) Look-up summary (which can be obtained by visiting www.abr.business.gov.au), which shows the organisation is:
          • a legal entity
          • registered charity
          • endorsed as a Public Benevolent Institution with Tax Deductible Gift Recipient status
          • established solely for a charitable purpose within the meaning of the Collections Act 1966 to assist persons in distress.

        Note:CSO / TMR Agent does not need to check the above requirements are met.
        Refer: Lodging application > CSO / TMR Agent role below.

      Lodging application
      Lodging application
        An application may be lodged by the organisation or by a CSO/TMR Agent on behalf of the organisation.

        All applications and supporting documentation required are to be forwarded to the Registration Policy Unit for consideration.

        Each subsidiary/sub-branch of an organisation must provide a separate application and meet the requirements listed above under Eligibility and application requirements. For more information, Refer: Vehicle Rego > 03. Types of Registration > Concessional Registration > T.R.C > Concession Requirement Transaction Checklist > Charitable / Community.

        CSO/TMR Agents should not provide advice to customers about eligibility of this concession type based on the forms lodged or information disclosed at the centre. Registration Policy make thorough assessments by examining the information and documents provided, and in some cases may need to request or source additional information before making a decision.

        CSO/TMR agent role
        CSO/TMR agent role
          If CSO/TMR agent emails application to Registration Policy on behalf of an organisation, ALL of the following MUST be provided by the organisation before email is sent to Registration Policy. A:
          • Completed Assessment for a Charitable or Community Service Concession Application (F4508); and
          • Statement in section 6 of F4508 application advising the specific services the organisation will provide through the use of eligible vehicles; and
          • Copy of the organisation's Constitution or Model Rule (if one is available); and
          • Copy of organisation's ABN Look-up summary (which can be obtained by visiting www.abr.business.gov.au). If for some reason the customer is unable to provide a copy of the look up summary, please advise in the email.

          Note:CSO's must not give an organisation a screen printed list of vehicles from TICA. If the organisation requests a list of vehicles, this must be printed from the List Registrations screen in TICA. Refer: Vehicle registration hub > Registration (top menu) > List registrations > Print

The CSO cannot add the qualification to any subsidiary/sub-branch of the organisation. Refer:Vehicle Rego > 03.     Application Date
    Application date
    Application date
      If vehicle registration’s renewal date is prior to the date TMR received the application or when vehicles are being added to an approved organisation, the concession will apply from the next registration period.

      Full registration fees must be paid if an organisation has applied for the concession, but formal approval has not been granted. If approved, the concession is backdated to the date TMR received the application and the difference in fees to be refunded to the organisation.

Consular Concession
Consular Concession
    What is it?A Consular Concession is issued by the Queensland Government as a courtesy to Foreign Consuls. CC is an abbreviation for Consular Corps - the organisation which represents both career and honorary consuls. Consular plates are described as Business/Corporate plates and are issued to eligible Consular Posts, Career Consuls, Honorary Consuls.

    Consular Position Definitions
    Consular Position Definitions
      Consular Post refers to a Consulate-General, Consulate, Vice-Consulate or Consular Agency in Queensland.

      Career Consul refers to any Consul-General, Consul or Vice-Consul who is both a foreign national of the country he/she represents and a career officer of that country’s Foreign Service. It does not refer to any other foreign nationals employed in a Consular Post.

      Honorary Consul refers to any Honorary Consul-General, Honorary Consul, Honorary Vice-Consul or Honorary Consular Agent appointed to head a Consular Post in Queensland.
    Requirements
    • Must have or be entitled to CONS Qualification
    • Must have stamped approval from the Department of the Premier and Cabinet on the application for CC Plates
    • Must have completed Consular Corp Number Plates - Issue/Replace/Remake (F5007) application
    • Must have approval to purchase a motor vehicle from the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade, if applicant is purchasing a new or imported vehicle
    Transaction requirements
    Transaction requirements checklist
    Number Plate Entitlements
    Honorary Consuls
    Honorary Consuls
      • Are entitled to only one set of CC plates at any one time for a vehicle which must be registered in the name of the Honorary Consul (or jointly with their spouse).
      • Honorary Consuls are not entitled to a second set of CC plates for their spouse.
    Consular Posts
    Consular Posts
      • Are entitled to one set of CC plates issued in the name of the Consulate for every two Career Consuls engaged at the Consulate (for example, the Japanese Consulate-General in Brisbane has 8 career consular officials, which would entitle them to have four vehicles registered with CC plates in the name of the consulate).
    Career Consuls
    Career Consuls
      • Are entitled to one set of CC plates for a motor vehicle registered in the name of the Career Consul.
      • A second set of CC plates may be issued for another vehicle registered in the name of a Career Consul, if that person is accompanied by a spouse. Ensure that no more than 2 vehicles with CC plates are registered to the customer before proceeding with the application.
    Rules
    • Consulate employees, except those listed above are not entitled to a Consular Qual or concessional registration.
    • The display of CC plates does not entitle the driver of the vehicle to any immunity in respect of the traffic laws of the State and they may be issued an infringement notice for any breaches of the Road Rules.
    • Vehicle must comply with the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards & Safety) Regulation 2010.
    • Left hand drive vehicles are not eligible for CONS concession.
    • Vehicle must have a compliance plate.
    • Registered company names are not entitled to Consular Registration or Plates.
    • When disposing of a vehicle that has been issued with CC plates, the registration must be cancelled or the vehicle converted to standard registration (including the issue of standard plates) before transferring.
    • If a Career or Honorary Consul ceases to hold such a position in Queensland, entitlement to the concession also ceases and the CC plates must be surrendered to TMR and registration cancelled within 14 days.
    • CC plates must be cancelled if no longer required or eligible.
    • Brisbane City CSC must be advised when CC plates are removed from a vehicle or plates are cancelled.

    Consular plates cannot be transferred, however the vehicle (only) can be transferred between:
    • Consular officials from the same consulate.
    • Career Consuls – must include a letter of explanation from the Head of the Consular Post.

    To process an application for consular concession/plates, Refer: Vehicle Rego > 3. Types of Registration > The How To: Types of Registration

    Consular Posts and Career Consuls

Overseas Mission Concession
    What is it?An Overseas Mission Concession is issued by the Queensland Government as a courtesy to Foreign Overseas Missions.

    Overseas Mission Definitions
    Overseas Mission Definitions
      Designated Overseas Mission means a designated overseas mission under the Overseas Missions (Privileges and Immunities) Act 1995 (Cwlth).

      Note:This will be determined and approved by the Premier's Department as part of their application approval.
      Note:Head of the Mission must be a national of the represented country.

      Officer of a designated overseas mission, means a person entrusted as an officer with the exercise of the functions of the designated overseas mission.

      Note:This does not include a person employed in the technical or administrative service of the designated overseas mission.

      The Premier's Department Application for registration concession form (with their authorised stamp) may contain further definitions and conditions/guidelines for which apply to the overseas mission.
    Requirements
    • Must have OMIS Qual
    • Must have Premier's Department authorised/stamped application for registration concession form

              Ceremonies
                • involving immediate and de facto family members (e.g. weddings, school formals, funerals), or as part of a sanctioned club event.
                • provided there is no fee or reward
                Note:Immediate and de facto family includes parents, grandparents, sons, daughters, grandchildren, adopted children and adopted grand children. Does not refer to extended family relationships such as nephews, nieces, aunts and uncles.
              Road Testing
              Road Testing
                Travel is allowed within a radius of 15kms from the place where the vehicle is garaged or is being repaired. The purpose of the travel must be to road test the vehicle following repair or restoration, or for general vehicle maintenance and not for any other purpose. A passenger can only travel in the vehicle to assist with road testing if it can legally carry passengers.
              Repairs
              Repairs
                Travel in order to have the vehicle repaired is allowed. While there is no distance requirements, all travel must be reasonable and justifiable by the operator.
              Inspections
              Inspections
                Direct travel to and from an Approved Inspection Station for the purposes of obtaining a SC or COI before offering the vehicle for sale.
              Heavy Vehicles
              Heavy Vehicles
                Permitted to carry a load provided the loaded GVM or GCM does not exceed 50% of the maximum permitted mass for the vehicle configuration.
                The mass is based on the lessor of:
                • Individual axle ratings
                • Tyre manufacturer's limits
                • Manufacturer's GVM or GCM
                • Regulation mass limits
                Note:The load carried must be in relation to or part of the reason for the vehicle's use under the SIVE scheme and must not be done for fee or reward.
              Buses
              Buses
                Permitted to carry non-paying passengers for journeys (ie joy rides) commencing at the location of the event (for example, a fete) and returning to the event. The length of the journey cannot exceed 5 kilometres.

                A vehicle with a SIV concession is permitted to carry passengers when travelling to and from an event permitted for SIV use (for example a rally, fete or procession). The vehicle must be able to legally carry passengers and all safety requirements must be met.

                Note:Payment of any kind cannot be accepted.

              Moving Premises
              Moving Premises
                Registered operators who move premises may apply to a CSC Manager (or equivalent) for a letter authorising the use of the vehicle on a road from the garage address to the new premises (one way trip). Proof of moving residence will be required (purchase document, rental agreement or completed Queensland Garage Address Statement (F4408). Document must link registered operator to the new address.
                Authority to move premises Feb 2020

                The attached letter should be completed and printed once evidence of moving premises is established. The operator must keep the original copy in the vehicle for the period of the journey and a copy should be retained in the office for a period of three months to allow for any infringement processes to be finalised.

                To use the vehicle for any other purpose, the concession must be removed and full registration fees paid. The concession may be reapplied but will not take effect until the next registration renewal hence no pro-rata refund.

            Lighting
            Lighting
              If the vehicle does not have permanent lighting the registered operator is required to:
              • Carry a removable lighting system at all times and
              • Use that system when lighting would normally be required

              Carbide lights are acceptable if vehicle originally manufactured with them.
            Modified Pre 1949 Manufactured Vehicles
            Modified Pre 1949 Manufactured Vehicles
              CSC Operator must contact the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU) to confirm the vehicles eligibility for registration.

            Removal of Concession
            Removal of Concession
              The SIV concession must be removed if:
              • the vehicle is to be used for any purpose other than those listed under the 'Conditions and Restriction' section of the SIV Concession Scheme guide
              • the Registered Operator has notified that they are no longer a current member of an incorporated car club or association
              • the vehicles registration is transferred to a person who is ineligible for a FMMC Qual

              Note:If the registered operator chooses to remove the concession so as to use the vehicle for a purpose not allowed under the scheme, the concession may be re-applied but should not take effect until the next registration renewal. (Example: Remove for weekend wedding and attempt to reapply on Monday)
