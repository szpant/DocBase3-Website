Maintain Application for Special Hardship Order/Restricted Licence (580)

Lodge Appeal
Lodge Appeal
    Applies to
    Applies to
      Lodging:
      • An application for a Special Hardship Order as a result of an Accumulation of demerit points whilst on a GDB suspension or a high speed suspension
      • An application for a Restricted licence as a result of a drink driving offence notice

      Important Before you Start
      Important Before you Start
        Special Hardship Order
        Accumulation of demerit points whilst on a GDB Suspension
        Ensure the suspension has commenced either by:
        • The notify action has been taken
        • TICA has batch suspended the licence

        Speed Suspension
        You must check the customer record to ensure that the high speed offence has not resulted in an 'Accumulation of demerit points' sanction notice. If this is the case, the customer must nominate a GDB. Refer below for procedure to be followed:
        • Notify a GDB on the 'Accumulation of demerit points' sanction. (see Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > The How To: Enforcement > Enforcement Procedures > Process GDB/ Suspension/ Reissue/Reprint Sanction Notice)
        • Ensure the high speed suspension has commenced (if not you will need to take the notify action)
        • Customer must then lodge their application to a Magistrates Court
        • The customer cannot drive until the court documents are returned to TMR and the appeal details have been recorded on TRAILS/TICA
        • Check licence status to ensure that it is current

        Restricted Licence
        • Ensure the drink driving offence notice has been paid

    Record receipt of supporting documentation
    Record receipt of supporting documentation
      • The green carbon copy of the SHO and supporting documentation must be forwarded to Prosecutions Unit

      If no supporting documentation provided at lodgement:
      • Forward green carbon copy of application form only to prosecutions
      • Advise applicant that they are required to lodge their supporting documents with TMR at least 7 days before their court date

      Note:TMR should not be receiving or forwarding original documentation, if presented with original document it means they have not provided it to the courts. Advise the customer to give the originals to the relevant court house and provide copies to TMR

    Forward documentation

      Clearly legible documentation is to be scanned and forwarded daily to prosecutions by email to Prosecutions Unit (SHO Processing Section)



      Note:If the documentation is unable to be legibly scanned, operators are to forward documentation by post.
