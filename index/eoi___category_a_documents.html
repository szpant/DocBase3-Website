EOI - Category A Documents
What it isCategory A documents establish the legal existence of the customer's name and date of birth.
About Category A DocumentsCurrency of Documents (status)
The documents must be current unless otherwise stated

Australian issued Documents - Australia includes External Territories.

All TMR smartcard products are considered Standard EOI, therefore they are not listed as Category A documents. This does not include licence accreditation receipts which cannot be used as evidence of identity

Includes:
  • Driver & Rider Trainer Accreditation
  • Pilot & Escort Vehicle Driver Licence
  • Tow truck drivers & assistant's Certificates
  • Traffic Controller Accreditation
  • Dangerous Goods Driver Licence
  • Digital documents displayed and verified in the Qld Digital Licence App
If doubt exists as to the authenticity of a document presented Refer: 4. Evidence of Identity\About EOI Documents\Recording and Verifying Document

        DHA (DIBP) Visas/Documents NOT acceptable
        DHA (DIBP) Visas/Documents NOT acceptable
      DHA (DIBP) (electronically issued)The electronically issued DHA (DIBP) visa is linked to the person's passport and TMR is unable to access this information to verify the person's identity. As such, an electronically issued DHA (DIBP) visa is NOT an acceptable Category A document.
      Special Category VisaA Special Category Visa is granted to eligible New Zealand citizens upon arrival in Australia, however it is not a document required to be attached to the passport. As such, a Special Category Visa is NOT an acceptable Category A document.
      Electronic Travel Authority (ETA)An ETA provides authorisation for citizens of certain countries to to travel to, enter and remain in Australia for a temporary period. An ETA is electronically linked to the person's passport. An ETA has no label (sticker) and TMR is unable to access this information to verify it. As such, an ETA is NOT an acceptable Category A document.
      Entry StampEntry stamps only provide evidence of the date and place of entry into Australia. The Australian government no longer routinely stamps the passport when a person enters or exits Australia. As such, an Entry Stamp is NOT an acceptable Category A document.
      Visa Grant NoticeAny person who is granted an Australian visa receives a Visa Grant Notice. Visa Grant Notices on their own lack security features and are open to fraud. As such, a Visa Grant Notice is NOT an acceptable Category A document.


      DHA (DIBP) ImmiCards
      DHA (DIBP) ImmiCards

        ImmiCards are an official secure immigration credential in the form of a polymer card. They contain a photograph and personal details such as surname, given names, date of birth and sex. ImmiCards contain a unique document number that is linked to biometric and visa details in Australian Government visa and travel systems.
        • Permanent Resident Evidence (PRE) ImmiCard issued to people who have been granted a protection visa (subclass 866). No longer issued from 1 July 2017 but cards issued before that date are still recognised.
        • Evidence of Immigration Status (EIS) ImmiCard issued to people who have been granted a Class WE (subclass 050) Bridging Visa E. From 1 July 2017 it will be issued to all eligible visa holders instead of PRE and RDI cards.
        • Residence Determination ImmiCard (RDI) issued to immigration detainees accommodated in the community who are not Australian citizens and do not hold a visa. No longer issued from 1 July 2017 but cards issued before that date are still recognised.
        • Australian Migration Status (AMS) ImmiCard issued predominately to Refugee and Special Humanitarian Programme (RHSP) visa holders and a small number of non-RSHP visa holders, who do not have, or cannot obtain a passport recognised by the Australian Government. The AMS is issued to clients offshore by either the International Organisation for Migration (IOM) or the Australian Embassy/High Commission, prior to their departure to Australia.

        Note:ImmiCards are valid for EOI purposes for 4 years from the expiry date shown on the card (5 years from the issue date for PRE, EIS and RDI ImmiCards which expires one year after issue, and seven years from the issue date for AMS ImmiCards which expire three years after issue).

        The DIVE (DIBP Visa Evidence Card) TICA input code must be used to record ImmiCards as an interim measure until a new input code is created for ImmiCards.

        Replacing an ImmiCard
        Replacing an ImmiCard

        Clients who lose their ImmiCard can apply for a free replacement online at the DHA (DIBP) website. A replacement card will be sent to the client's residential address within 7-14 days.

        The client will be issued with an ImmiCard Report. It contains the card holder's photograph and the ImmiCard number, but should not be used as a Category A document.

        The ImmiCard Report may be considered under Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI policy.

    Licence/Accreditation
    Licences/ Accreditation
    Licences/ Accreditation
      • Non-Queensland Australian Photo driver licence. Provided the Interstate Driver Licence status is not Transferred, it can be accepted as EOI if it has been Valid within the last 2 years.
      • Photographic Qld High Risk Work Licence (current or expired <2 years)

      The physical licence or accreditation must be presented, digital forms of interstate licences are not acceptable. This is because there is no ability for TMR to verify that the digital licence is authentic.

      A TMR smartcard product, current or expired < 2 years, is considered standard EOI, this includes TMR Driver Authorisations and Accreditations and Digital Driver Licence . As they are Standard EOI they are not listed as Category A documents.
