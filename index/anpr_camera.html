ANPR Camera (6096)

ANPR Camera
What it isThe Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) camera captures images and data to check against TMR records for overdue registration and Compulsory Third Party (CTP) insurance. The system uses fixed ANPR cameras and mobile units fixed in Transport Inspector Vehicles.
Using an ANPR CameraEnsure the ANPR Compliance Vehicle Log Sheet is completed after the ANPR has been activated.

At regular intervals during a shift the ANPR camera should be checked to ensure it is operating.

For court contested matters the Authorised Officer who performed the start-up process and/or data upload may be required to provide a statement.

When the ANPR vehicle is not being used, for example, parked at the end of a shift, the unit should be turned off. Compliance vehicles are NOT to be parked and left static on the side of the road with the camera running when no Transport Inspectors are in attendance.

The ANPR system will be calibrated by the System Provider at 12 month intervals.

Direct all ANPR system issues, equipment damage, concerns and/or questions to a Senior Transport Inspector as soon as possible. Under no circumstances are TIs to tamper or attempt to fix any issues.
ANPR dataANPR data should be uploaded at least once a week and no longer than once a fortnight. When uploading the ANPR data, the vehicle should be parked as near as possible to the designated parking spot at a nominated TMR compliance office. The upload functionality is installed at each TMR compliance office where Transport Inspectors are permanently based.
Infringements for ANPR detected offencesIf an Authorised Officer detects an unregistered or uninsured vehicle during normal on-road compliance and enforcement activities, the driver should be issued a manual PIN for the offence and an unregistered vehicle permit may be issued.

ANPR detected unregistered and uninsured offences are automatically issued infringements via TRAILS for offences where the vehicle has been unregistered for more than 30 days.

If the vehicle has been unregistered for 15 to 30 days a warning letter will be issued.
