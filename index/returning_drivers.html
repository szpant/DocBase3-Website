Returning Drivers (5145)


What is itA returning driver is a person who previously held a class of provisional, probationary or open licence, but who has not held that licence in the five years before making an application to re-obtain it.

Note:A person who previously only held a learner licence is not a returning driver
PurposeThe Returning Driver condition allows the returning driver to reinstate the class not held for the last 5 years without the need to meet tenure.

For example: A customer who held an Open Class C licence was disqualified in 2010 and has not had the licence reissued. As a returning driver once he has passed the written general road rules test and had a learner licence issued with a RD condition he does not have to hold that learner licence for 12 months but can immediately book for a Class C practical driving test. Once passed the licence is upgraded from the learner to the probationary licence.
Eligibility
Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility Criteria
    Returning drivers must:
    • Provide acceptable evidence of their previous licence or class of licence. Acceptable evidence means:
      • If the previous licence was a Queensland licence, a record on TICA; or
      • If the previous licence was a non-Queensland licence:
        • written confirmation (for example, a ROI statement, letter or email) from the authority or agency that issued the previous licence. Written confirmation must show the returning driver’s personal and licence information, and contact details for the authority or agency so that the department can verify the information.
          Note:It is the returning driver’s responsibility to provide acceptable evidence of their previous licence. Non-Queensland evidence may be verified with the issuing authority and NQPR to be updated with licence type and invalid tenure details.
    • Pass the relevant written road rules test (unless exempt under current rules) before the learner licence with a RD Certificate and Condition can be issued.
    • Pass the relevant practical driving test/Q-ride assessment before the licence can be upgraded to what was previously held.

    Returning drivers do not need to meet:
    • Class tenure
    • Type tenure (applicable only for returning drivers who do not hold a provisional, probationary or open licence of another class at the time of applying)
    • Logbook requirements

    Returning Drivers will be issued the same type of licence as previously held (P1, P2, P or Open). If licence disqualified and never re-issued P type licence is to be issued once all testing completed.
Requirements
Issuing a RD Certificate
Issuing a RD Certificate
    The Returning Driver Certificate F5092 will authorise them to learn to drive the highest class(es) of vehicle they are eligible for as stated in the certificate. The ‘RD’ condition will require the licence holder to carry their returning driver certificate while learning to drive the relevant class of vehicle.
    Currently unlicensed RD Applicant
    Currently unlicensed RD Applicant
      Returning drivers who do not hold a licence at the time of application are eligible to apply for their previously held class and type of licence. To commence the process they will be required to pass the relevant written road rules test/s prior to being issued with a class C learner licence (endorsed with an ‘RD’ condition code) and a returning driver certificate.

      To determine class and eligibility type refer to Returning Driver - Determining licence class and type and written test requirements


      Example
      Renee held a class R open licence. She moves to London for seven years, and her licence expires eleven months after her arrival. As she does not need to drive she does not obtain a United Kingdom licence. When she returns to Australia she has not held a valid licence for more than five years.

      Renee will need to pass the general written road rules test and the motorcycle written road rules test. She will then be issued a class C learner licence with an ‘RD’ condition and be given a returning driver certificate authorising her to learn to ride a class R motorcycle (she will also be authorised to learn to ride a class RE motorcycle).

      Once Renee passes a Q-Ride assessment, her licence will be upgraded to a class R open licence.

    Currently licensed RD Applicant
    Currently licensed RD Applicant
      Returning drivers who hold a valid licence at the time of applying to reobtain another class, will be required to pass the relevant written road rules test before having their licence endorsed with an ‘RD’ condition and being given a returning driver certificate.

      Example
      James held a class HC P2 provisional licence. Due to a medical condition his licence is downgraded to a class C P2 provisional licence. After six years his medical condition improves and he is medically fit to drive a heavy vehicle again. He now holds a class C open licence.

      James will need to pass the heavy vehicle written road rules test. He will then have an ‘RD’ condition endorsed on his class C open licence and be given a returning driver certificate authorising him to learn to drive a class HC vehicle.

      Once James passes a practical driving test in a class HC vehicle, his licence will be upgraded to a class HC open licence.
      Upgrading a Returning Driver Licence
      Upgrading a Returning Driver Licence
        After passing the relevant practical driving test if the returning driver:
        • Was previously unlicenced will be issued the type of licence they previously held (provisional, probationary, open) and the class of licence in which they were tested. If the last licence they held was cancelled because of a disqualification, they will be issued a probationary licence.
        • Was licensed when the RD condition was added, they will have the relevant class of vehicle added to their current licence.

        A returning driver is not obligated to obtain the highest eligible class in one upgrade. The RD may opt to apply for a lower class of vehicle.

        The RD condition will be automatically expired once the customer has obtained their highest eligible class and/or type of licence.
