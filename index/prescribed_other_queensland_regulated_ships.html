Prescribed Other Queensland Regulated Ships (6101)

What it isPrescribed – Other Queensland Regulated Ships are:
  • 6 metres or more in length; or
  • Designed to carry more than 12 persons on the ship; or
  • Operate more than 15 nautical miles (nm) from land.

Endorsement by Maritime Standards is required when:
  • Transferring,
  • Registering or,
  • Changing particular details such as ship category, conditions, length to 15m or more.
Application
Applications for New Business, Transfer and Changes
Applications for New Business, Transfer and Changes
    All Applications for the following transactions for Prescribed other Queensland regulated ships are required to be referred to Marine Standards for consideration of the application prior to processing at a Transport and Main Roads Customer Service Centre:
    • New registration,
    • Transfer of ownership,
    • Change of Queensland Regulated Ship Particulars Notification

    For New Registration, CSO’s are to create the ship prior to referring to Maritime Standards, this will allow conditions to be applied to the record.
Prescribed other Queensland Regulated Ships
Prescribed other Queensland Regulated Ships
    Prescribed other Queensland regulated ships may not necessarily be inspected by the department

    New ships and ships that are clearly built for recreational use will generally not be inspected. Maritime Standards will normally only inspect those ships that are of a commercial build, such as tugs and barges.

    All applications require endorsement before processing the application.
RulesPrescribed other Queensland regulated ships require an endorsement letter from MSQ for any new registration, transfer of ownership, and specific change of ship particulars applications.

Look for MSQ Endorsement Prior to Processing
Look for MSQ Endorsement Prior to Processing
    Applications should be first sent to Maritime Standards for endorsement before being processed at a Transport and Main Roads Customer Service Centre or QGAP office.

    Endorsement by Maritime Standards does not constitute a processed application – this can only be done by a Transport and Main Roads Customer Service Centre or QGAP office.
Time of Consideration may take 30 days
Time of Consideration may take 30 days
    Maritime Standards will advise an applicant of their decision within 30 days of receipt of the application.
Fees Involved
Fees Involved
    There is no fee involved in this process.
Transaction
Grounds to Refuse
Grounds to Refuse
    If an applicant presents an application without the accompanying endorsement letter from Maritime Standards, you must not process the transaction.
Enquiries or Complaints
Enquiries or Complaints
    Applicants should contact the Maritime Standards Office.

    Where Maritime Standards declines an application for prescribed Queensland Regulated Ship registration, transfer or change, the applicant should be referred to the Director (Vehicle and Vessel Standards).
