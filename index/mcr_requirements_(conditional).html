MCR Requirements (Conditional) (7535)

Approved ActionMedical condition reporting requirements for drivers who have a medical condition (under 75)
WhenEffective immediately and until further notice
BackgroundThe current health emergency is impacting the ability for some drivers to comply with medical condition reporting (MCR) requirements. In particular, some customers are reporting difficulties in obtaining a medical certificate from their doctor.

In response, the MCR requirements have been reviewed and the following policies have been developed. These policies are effective immediately and are to continue until further notice.

Notes:
  • For medical certificate requirements for customers turning or over 75 Refer: COVID 19 - Medical Certificates for driver aged 75+
  • These policies do not relate to Driver Authorisation (DA) holders and Dangerous Goods Driver Licence (DGDL) holders. Any enquiries related to medicals for DA or DGDL can be referred to the Passenger Transport Authorisation Unit.

Medical Certificates
Medical Certificates
    In the first instance, customers should be encouraged to obtain a medical certificate from their doctor where ever possible. This can be done via telemedicine or a telephone call to the doctor for example.
    Where a customer is unable to obtain a medical certificate and their current certificate is due to expire, but is still current, the following will apply:
    • If a customer holds a conditional licence with an 'M' condition (5B on their last medical certificate), a letter will be issued to extend the current medical certificate for a period of 6 months from the expiry date listed on the certificate. The conditions or restrictions that are listed on the most recent medical certificate will continue to apply and the customer must drive in accordance with these conditions and/or restrictions. The customer must carry this letter and their most recent medical certificate when driving.
    • If a customer holds an unconditional licence with no 'M' condition (5A on their last medical certificate) and they still require a medical certificate to renew their licence, a letter will be issued by TMR to extend the current medical certificate for a period of 6 months from the expiry date listed on the certificate. The customer does not need to carry this letter when driving.
    • If a customer is currently prohibited from driving due to a medical condition, because they don't meet the criteria for an unconditional or conditional licence (5C on their last medical certificate), they must obtain a medical certificate from a doctor in order to apply for a driver licence. No extension can be issued as they don't have a valid licence.

    Note: The customer doesn't need to prove they can't see a doctor. During this time, customer advice is acceptable.
Medical Condition Notifications
Medical Condition Notifications
    Where TMR is notified that a licence holder may have a permanent or long-term mental or physical incapacity that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, the following will apply:
    If the information is provided by the licence holder or a health professional, the person's driver licence is to be suspended immediately and a show cause notice issued.

    Refer: Driver Licence > 4. Medical Condition Reporting > Show Cause – Medical > Immediate Suspension

    If the information is provided by another third party (including a Queensland Police Service Officer), action must not be taken unless it is reasonable to commence show cause action based on the information provided. In this case, the licence is not immediately suspended.
Show Cause Notices
Show Cause Notices
    Where a show cause notice is issued, the date of the proposed action is to be not more than 90 days from the issue date of the notice. However, if it is reasonable that more time is needed, further correspondence can be sent to the customer extending this time again.

Who will be impacted and how?
CSC Staff
CSC Staff
    CSC staff will be required to record the medical certificate extension in TICA and issue the relevant letter to the customer.
    Operators will have to:
    • Record a new Medical Certificate in the customer's prerequisite screen with the correct expiry date (6 months). The Medical Condition indicator will be the same as what is currently recorded against the customer's licence
    • Add a new report to the Medical Condition Reporting Hub with the following information recorded:
      • Method – In person
      • Received from – customer
      • Additional Notes – Automatic extension granted as customer is unable to provide medical certificate due to COVID-19. Extension granted until <Expiry Date.>
      • Finalise the report in the MCR Hub
      • Issue the appropriate letter. Refer Below: Extension Letters
      • Complete a "Report for File," including the customer's CRN and name. The report should detail the reason for the extension and be filed with the daily filing.
Customer Contact Centre Operators
Customer Contact Centre Operators
    CCC operators may notice an increase in enquiries regarding the new medical certificate reporting requirements. If a customer advises that they are unable to obtain a new medical certificate CCC operators should verify if they are eligible for an extension.

    If the customer is eligible the operator should send an email to medcert@tmr.qld.gov.au with the Subject line of "Medical Certificate Extension – CRN." Please include the customer's name, CRN, contact phone number and email address and advise that an extension is required.

    The MCR hub will grant the extension (if eligible) and email the extension letter to the customer.

    Operators should advise the customer that they have submitted a request to have the medical certificate extension granted, and if eligible, they should receive an email within 5 business days.
Further informationChanges are being progressed to allow more customers with medical conditions to use the online service. Further information will be provided when the changes have been made.
Customers who are seeking further information or wish to make a complaint should be referred to TMR through the normal communication channels. Standard responses on this issue have been distributed.
