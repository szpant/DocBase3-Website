Deceased Estate Transfers (5198)

What it is
Transferring the registration out of the name of a deceased registered operator.
ChecklistsDocument Requirements Checklist
Transaction Requirements Checklist
Statutory Declarations
Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person (F5296)
No documentation statutory declaration

Note:A Statutory Declaration can also be signed and witnessed by a lawyer.
Rules
Evidence of Identity
Evidence of Identity
    Standard Vehicle Registration EOI requirements apply for:
    • The acquirer (beneficiary/non-beneficiary); and
    • The executor/administrator/Next of Kin (NOK) who:
      • Presents/Signs the transfer form; or
      • Is to be recorded as the second registered operator; or
      • Is already recorded as the second registered operator
    Refer: Standard Evidence of Identity > Step 1 - Locate the level of EOI required > EOI required by Product/Transaction > Vehicle Registration > Transfer Vehicle
Will
Will
    The Will only needs to be sighted to confirm the executor/s where a grant of probate or letters of administration have not been obtained. Certified copies of will/grant of probate/letters of administration are acceptable.

    As the beneficiaries are declared on the statutory declaration, they do not need to be verified in the Will. Under no circumstances should a Will be tampered with (e.g. removing staples).
Transfers within 30 days of the person passing
Transfers within 30 days of the person passing
    Legally a transfer of a vehicle registered to a deceased person cannot occur until 30 days after their death. This is because a person must survive the deceased by 30 days in order to benefit from the estate. During this period the registration should not be transferred unless:
    • A Will specifically states that the Beneficiary is not required to survive the deceased by 30 days; or
    • The vehicle is being transferred into the “Estate of” the deceased person
    • The vehicle is being transferred from joint registered operators to surviving registered operator (regardless of whether the surviving R/O is also a beneficiary of the Will)

    Information regarding the 30 day survival period has been obtained from the Succession Act 1981. This Act is administered by Department of Justice and Attorney General.
Pro-rata fees and refunds raised
Pro-rata fees and refunds raised
    If a pro-rata is raised when transferring to a beneficiary/administrator of a deceased estate the fees are to be written off.

    Full fees will apply at renewal if the new registered operator is not entitled to a concession.
Proof of death
Proof of death
    To update a customer as deceased, an original or certified copy of an acceptable proof of death document must be provided.
Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person Form (F5296)
Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person Form (F5296)
    Form F5296 is required so that the executor, administrator or next of kin can provide instructions for the transfer or cancellation of vehicle/s and or number plate products.

    If a grant of probate or letters of administration have:
    • been obtained, the original or certified copies must be sighted and the statutory declaration does not need to be completed,
    • not been obtained but a Will exists, the Will must be sighted to confirm the executor/s and the statutory declaration must be completed,
    • been applied for but not yet granted, no business can be conducted. However, if the customer insists on transacting because they have a buyer for the vehicle, the statutory declaration can be completed with additional information stating that they have a buyer for the vehicle.

    No additional forms are required if the vehicle/s or plate/s are being:
    • cancelled (including where a vehicle is transferred then cancelled for the cheque to be issued to another person; or
    • transferred to the estate of deceased or to a beneficiary or someone entitled under the Succession Act 1981.
Signature Requirements
Signature Requirements
    When dealing with a deceased estate it is important that TMR ensures that the correct person/s or organisation that is representing the deceased signs all registration documents. This is because vehicles/plates are being dealt with under a legal process.

    Multiple executors:
    • Only one executor/administrator is required to sign the Application to Transact with Registration Products on Behalf of a Deceased Person (F5296) form.
    • If more than one executor /administrator wants to sign:
      • And they don’t have grant of probate / letters of administration, they will need to complete an F5296 each.
      • And they do have grant of probate/ letters of administration the additional executors/administrators can sign and provide their details in the space on F5296.

    Renunciation of Executor Rights
    Renunciation of Executor Rights
      If any executors wish to renounce their executor rights, they need to provide Supreme Court Documents detailing their renunciation, which is to be submitted at time of dealing with the registration.

      However, if these documents cannot be obtained, send an email outlining the below to Help Direct for review before completing the transaction:
      • Transaction details including registration details
      • Why Court documents cannot be supplied
      • Who are the executors outlined in the Will, and who is applying for renunciation.

      Operators must wait for the outcome before processing any transactions.
    Intestate estate administered by the Public Trustee
    Intestate estate administered by the Public Trustee
      Where the Public Trustee is administering the estate, a representative of the Public Trustee must sign all registration documents.

      The representative of the Public Trustee must also provide a letter of authority to sign on behalf of the Public Trustee.
    Intestate estate administered by Letters of Administration
    Intestate estate administered by Letters of Administration
      Where a person has not left a Will, but letters of administration have been granted by the Supreme Court nominating an administrator/s:
      • the F5296 form must be completed (statutory declaration section is not required)

    Unable to obtain signatures
    Unable to obtain signatures
      Renewal of deceased customer registration
      Renewal of deceased customer registration
        If an executor or administrator's signature is unable to be obtained or another issue unable to be resolved before the expiry date of a registration, a renewal application may be accepted at the discretion of a CSC Manager. Before making a decision, the manager must sight the documentation presented to verify the extenuating circumstances (e.g. executor is overseas and un-contactable). This exemption is a one off exemption and must be conducted in person.

        Refer: Vehicle Rego > 7. Renewal > The How to: Renewal > Registration Renewal Procedures > Renew deceased customer registration
      Interstate or overseas
      Interstate or overseas
        If the executor/administrator/NOK/s are located in a remote area, interstate or overseas, the request (including full requirements) can be lodged by email/fax/mail. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Transferring the vehicle > Transfers Lodged by email/fax/mail
    Public Trustee
    Public Trustee
      When the Public Trustee is dealing with the estate and/or is the executor, a Certificate of Authority must be provided. The Certificate of Authority must be on official letterhead and contain the name and address of the registered operator.

      Generally, the Public Trustee will send a fax/email to Central Service Support who will record the customer as deceased and transfer the registration to the surviving registered operator or the estate of the deceased with Public Trustee as the second registered operator. However, if a customer or a representative of the public trustee presents at the CSC, the transaction can be processed at the CSC.

      The public trustee does not need to complete an Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person (F5296) form.

      Public Trustee has no Interest in Estate
      Public Trustee has no Interest in Estate
        If the public trustee has no interest in the estate (including if they are named as the executor), they will not provide a certificate of authority or sign any forms. Instead, the Next of Kin or Administrator can complete the Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person (F5296) (including the statutory declaration section), make amendments if required (e.g. cross out point 3 if applicable) and sign the form.
    Estate of Deceased
    Estate of Deceased
      Transfers into the estate of deceased
      Transfers into the estate of deceased
        Transfers into the estate of the deceased is an interim transaction until the executor, administrator, next of kin determines what is to happen to the vehicle (transfer or cancellation).

        Note:POU is COMM for vehicles registered in the 'Estate of'

      Transfer out of estate of deceased
      Transfer out of estate of deceased
        Once the vehicle has been transferred into the estate of the deceased, the deceased estate requirements are deemed to have been met. This includes the Certificate of Authority for Public Trustee (it is not required to be re-presented).

        For any further transactions (transfer or cancellation), normal transfer/cancellation EOI and transaction requirements/exemptions apply. Exemptions are declared on the transfer form.
      Cancel Registration
      Cancel Registration
        If the vehicle registration is cancelled whilst still in the name of the deceased customer, any refund cheque will automatically be issued to the Estate of <deceased>.

        If the executor, administrator, next of kin would like the cheque issued to a third party rather than the estate of deceased, it must firstly be transferred. They are exempt transfer requirements, and question 4 (transfer of registration) of the Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person (F5296) is not required to be completed. This is because the transfer is only being processed to facilitate the issue of the refund to another person and it is an administrative requirement.

        If the third party does not have full EOI recorded in TICA, the transfer cannot be processed. In this scenario, operators can create a SKEL customer record and complete an adjustment to enable a refund to the third party.

        If a registration is transferred out of a deceased person's name into an individual's name and then cancelled, the refund can be made by EFTPOS providing all other EFTPOS refund requirements are met.

        Refer: Vehicle Rego > 9. Cancellation > Cancellation
      Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or Legal Guardian
      Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or Legal Guardian
        Signing on behalf of an executor or administrator
        Signing on behalf of an executor or administrator
          A person who has a Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or is the legal guardian for an executor or administrator of an estate cannot sign on behalf of the executor or administrator.

          For example: Person A is named as the executor in a Will and Person B has a Power of Attorney / Enduring Power of Attorney for Person A.

          Person B cannot sign documents that Person A would have to sign in their capacity as an executor or administrator.

        Signing on behalf of a beneficiary
        Signing on behalf of a beneficiary
          A person who has a Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or is a legal guardian (for example, parent of a minor) can sign on behalf of a person who is a beneficiary.

          Normal requirements apply Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity > Representing (Individuals/Organisations > Document Requirements > Power of Attorney or Parent/legal guardian
      Reinstate Cancelled Registration
      Reinstate Cancelled Registration
        If the registration has auto cancelled a request may be made to Reinstate the registration. The appropriate renewal fees must be paid and transfer documents lodged at the same time.
      Will Disputes
      Will Disputes
        If TMR is advised that a Will is in dispute (e.g. Due to the deceased marrying after the Will was made), business should not be conducted unless a grant of probate or letters of administration from the Supreme Court are sighted to confirm the executor/s or administrator/s.

        A text message may be added to the vehicle registration record advising that a grant of probate or letters of administration are required before business can be conducted.
      Automatic Transfers
      Automatic Transfers
        An automatic transfer of registration and/or personalised plate products will occur via an overnight batch process following a customer being recorded as deceased if the following criteria are met:
        • Customer has *14 personalised plate and/or vehicle registration products or less; and
        • All products are in joint names (all products don't have to be the same joint names, however if personalised plates are attached to a vehicle, the plate and vehicle must be the same joint names)
        • Neither the deceased or surviving operator are supressed
        • The surviving operator must be a living individual

        If a joint registered operator/plate owner presents to transfer, the transaction should be processed manually rather than waiting to see "if" the product/s automatically transfer. This is because the transfer may not meet the auto transfer rules.

        *Note:A vehicle registration with a personalised plate attached is considered two products.

        The auto transfer process will:
        • Exempt all requirements;
        • Remove the concession and write off fees for the current and future (if applicable) registration period where the first registered operator has passed away. If the surviving registered operator was secondary prior to auto transfer and has a concession qualification, Refer: Operator Intervention below. If the surviving registered operator does not have a concession qualification but is eligible, they will need to apply for the concession.
        • Keep the concession on the vehicle where the second registered operator has passed away.
        • Issue a Notice of Transfer of Vehicle Registration/Personalised Plates letter to each unique surviving registered operator/owner displaying the products that have been auto transferred.

        Operator Intervention
        Operator Intervention
          The auto transfer process will require operator intervention where:
          • the surviving registered operator has a concession qualification, the concession can be reapplied without the requirement to complete an application form or present the concession card (providing they have no other vehicles with a concession). A report for file can be completed by the operator confirming the concession was re-applied after auto-transfer. Call centre consultants can refer these requests to their SCCC to process.

      No documentation
      No documentation
        If the estate has already been finalised and the documentation is not available (e.g. due to a delay between the deceased date and the date TMR sent the NORG letter), the executor/administrator can complete the No Documentation Statutory Declaration.
      Definitions
      Interpretation of Wills
      Interpretation of Wills
        The Succession Act 1981, provides the law with respect to the assets of a deceased person. This is known as the 'administration' of the deceased estate.

        The following is a list of common terminology which is used in Wills which may assist when interpreting Wills and making decisions under s41 of the Transport Operations Road Use Management Vehicle Registration Regulation 2010:

        TerminologyExplanation
        BeneficiaryA person or entity who receives a benefit under a Will, either as being named in the Will or under the intestacy provisions, is called a beneficiary.

        Note:Operators do not need to view the Will to determine the beneficiary.
        BequeathTo leave personal estate to a person by Will.
        BequestIs a gift of personality (personal property).
        DeviseIs a gift of realty (real property).
        ExecutorUsually under a Will the testator or testatrix appoints a person to administer his/her estate. That person is called an executor (male) or executrix (female).
        GrantMeans grant of probate of the Will or letters of administration of the estate of a deceased person and includes the grant of an order to administer and the filing of an election to administer such an estate.
        Grant of probateIs the legal document issued by the Supreme Court giving authority to administer the estate and disposal of assets and debts (A Grant of probate is not necessary for a vehicle to be transferred, but must be obtained when the deceased estate involves property/land.
        IntestateA person who dies without having executed a valid Will is said to have died intestate. In this situation the law prescribes under the intestacy rules who is to inherit the estate.
        Letters of AdministrationIs a certificate certified by a Registrar of the Supreme Court of Queensland and certified by the Great Seal of the Supreme Court.
        Next of kinA Next of Kin is:
        • The person declared as the Next of Kin on the Next of Kin/Administrator statutory declaration; or
        • A person who is the deceased’s Next of Kin in accordance with the provisions of the Succession Act 1981.
        Note: Next of Kin status does not need to be verified but it must be declared on a statutory declaration.
        ProbateIs the process of proving and registering in the Supreme Court the last Will of the deceased person.
        ResiduaryThe rest and residue of an estate.
        TestateA person who dies and has made a Will is said to have died testate.
        Testator/TestatrixThe person who makes a Will is called a testator (male) or testatrix (female).
      Other products
      Check for other products
      Check for other products
        The deceased customer's CRN should also be checked for other TMR products that have been issued, eg driver licence, industry authority, APA card, BHSL or disabled parking permit. If necessary advise the representative of the deceased that these products should be surrendered to TMR.
