Temporary School Closure
Temporary School Closure

    In the event of a school closure, the school principal will notify school staff, school crossing supervisors and road safety officer of a pending closure. Any change in time of operation away from the normal approved times is to be approved by Transport and Main Roads (TMR). The school principal will consult with the CSB Road Safety staff regarding changes.

    In the event that the school is closed and all students are to leave as arranged by the school at a nominated time eg. cyclone, a special event or emergency such as a fire, the school principal will:
    • Determine the most effective time to utilise the allocated period of time for supervision for each crossing.
    • Negotiate with TMR to vary time of operation.

    In the event that a school is closed and some students will remain at school until the normal finishing time eg. teachers strike or end of term, the children’s crossing will remain unchanged and will operate at the normal approved time. The school principal will be requested to:
    • Arrange for parents to be responsible for students who leave at an earlier time.
    • Ask a member of staff to assist students who leave at a time other than the approved time of operation of the crossing, to cross the road.

    The CSB Road Safety staff will be required to consider the needs of different primary/private schools in the area before making a decision to vary a time of operation of a crossing or to approve additional time for supervision to cater for all students involved.

    NOTE:Department of Education will advise Passenger Transport Regional Operations when school changes occur. Refer below: Changing School Transport Arrangements.
Changing School Transport Arrangements
Changing School Transport Arrangements

    The following circumstances would prompt notification for changes to school.
    • New Schools
    • Expansion of existing schools
    • Proposed Closures
    • Change of bell times (school start and finish times)
    • Infrastructure activities that would affect crossings either through location of construction traffic volumes.
    • School based decisions on student access points that may affect crossing operations- e.g. change of student exit points.

Volunteer Crossing Supervisor
Volunteer Crossing Supervisor

    Volunteer school crossing supervisors may be appointed to school crossings where investigation of a school crossing site by Transport and Main Roads (TMR) identifies that:
    • a TMR funded crossing is not warranted, but other road safety factors exist to suggest benefits in voluntary supervision of the crossing; or
    • supervision of the crossing is recommended but funding is not available to establish an approved crossing.

Shows and Fete Displays
Shows, fetes and displays
Shows, fetes and displays
    The Manager (Road Safety) may employ a school crossing supervisor at shows, school fetes and displays for the purpose of enhancing Transport and Main Roads' (TMR) role and function. Any such employment will be paid at the standard hourly rates in accordance with General employees (Queensland Government Departments) and other employees Award – State 2015

    School event/s
    School event/s

      Where extra hours occur at a school event, the school should apply to the CSB Road Safety Staff in writing one month prior to the event.

      CSB Road Safety Staff will submit the application for approval to the Manager (Road Safety). If approved, they will forward a letter to:
      • The School Principal identifying dates and information and advising them that they will be responsible for the payment of the additional wages; and
      • Each school crossing supervisor required to perform extra duties, outlining the extra duty time and other requirements.
    Non-school events
    Non-school events

      A School Crossing Supervisor may be paid by other agencies e.g. business groups, local government or community groups to operate an approved, marked or children’s crossing, only if:
      • The Regional Director approves the engagement.
      • The crossings on which the supervisors are to operate meet the criteria established in the Queensland Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices.
      • The hiring agency pays the standard hourly rate, in accordance with the General employees (Queensland Government Departments) and other employees Award – State 2015
      • The Region ensures that the appropriate PPE is provided.
      • The hiring agency ensures that they carry public liability and workers’ compensation cover for any school crossing supervisors they employ.
      • Training and assessment of suitably equipped school crossing supervisors is conducted by TMR.
School LiabilitySchools (state or non-state) should seek advice through their department/organisation as to what they should do to meet any legal responsibilities they may have prior to, or if an incident occurs, when a school representative is marshalling students.

Under section 6.5.2 - Relief Supervisor Process in the Memorandums of Understanding between Transport and Main Roads (TMR) and state and non-state schools, schools have agreed to 'Arrange for an appropriate school representative to oversee the safety of children at the crossing where all reasonable attempts to find a relief SCS are unsuccessful. This person will put the flags out, marshal the children on the footpath and advise them when it is safe to cross. They must not use the stop sign, give hand signals or verbal instructions to motorists, unless authorised by TMR to do so.

CSB Road Safety staff may, when requested by the School Principal, assist schools in training how to marshal children. They should advise the school how to follow the instructions found in Memorandum of Understanding under Relief Supervisor Process - Attachment B, above.
Flags for unsupervised crossingsTransport and Main Roads does not supply flags for unsupervised crossings. If a school wishes, it may purchase flags from a supplier such as Artcraft (07) 3248 4848 in Brisbane.

When ordering flags, the dowels must also be ordered separately, as they are not included in the price of the flags.
