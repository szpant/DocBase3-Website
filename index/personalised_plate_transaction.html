Personalised Plate Transaction (4561)

Note:Personalised Plates recorded in joint names require both plate owners signatures for all transactions.

OrderAn order is the first instance when a personalised plate is being made for the applicant.

If an operator is unsure of the plate specifications when presented with a number plate application, it is encouraged to call PPQ to place the order and then take the payment in TICA.

This is because the system allows orders for most formats and specifications without stopping the user if an incorrect specification is chosen. This results in TMR being charged twice when an order needs to be corrected, reprocessing by PPQ and stop-requests for the manufacturing.

Q Plates
Q Plates
    Refer customers to PPQ for all Q Plate sales and to traditionalise any existing Q Plates.

    As Q Plates were originally issued as a set of one, if an existing Q Plate needs to be replaced, it must be traditionalised and a set of two attached to the vehicle.

    When they are traditionalised the customer must order a new set of two through PPQ.

    TMR can only process the remake and transfer of already traditionalised Q Plates.
Re-Issue
Re-Issue of Cancelled Personalised Plates
Re-Issue of Cancelled Personalised Plates
    Personalised plates that have be cancelled can be re-issued if application is made:
    • 3 mths from the date of surrender if all plates have been surrendered
    • 14 mths form date of cancellation of ownership if all plates have not been surrendered

    Note: Plates must be in a "LAPSED" status in TICA

Remake
Remaking a Plate
Remaking a Plate
    Remaking a plate refers to requests where the customer requires another set
    of personalised plates the same as the original. This may be due to the original plates being:
    • Damaged
    • Lost
    • Stolen
    • Slimline being required

    There is a fee involved.

    If only one plate is requested, two plates must be ordered. Advise the customer that:
    • the cost is the same
    • two plates will be produced
    • one plate must be surrendered if plates are damaged
    • they must surrender the other plate/s (both for remake/upgrade) when the new set is received.


    Note:If the plates are recorded as being attached to a vehicle currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice, plates can be remade, but cannot be attached to the vehicle during the confiscation period.
TransferAll personalised plate ownerships can be transferred.

A person who acquired a personalised number from its current owner (including an administrator/executor/Next of Kin of an estate) must:
  • Apply to TMR for the plate to be transferred Personalised Number Plates Transfer Application (F2963) or Vehicle Registration Transfer Application (F3520) if the plates are attached to a vehicle that is also being transferred.
  • Apply within 14 days of the acquisition
  • Pay the appropriate fee (unless exempt) Refer: Vehicle Rego > 11. Plates > Personalised Plates Guide > Transfer Fee Exemptions
The seller of the personalised plates can also apply to transfer the plate from their name by lodging the Seller's copy of the Personalised Number Plates Transfer Application (F2963.) They aren't required to pay the transfer fee. A debt will be raised in the acquirer's account and a Customer Account Statement will be automatically sent.


Personalised Plates attached to a vehicle
Personalised Plates attached to a vehicle
    If the personalised plates are attached to a vehicle that is also being transferred a Personalised Number Plates Transfer Application (F2963) is not required if all disposers of the vehicle are also the plate owners (and have signed the transfer form.)

    However, in situations where the vehicle disposer/s are not also the plate owner/s then F2963 will need to be submitted with F3520. For example, if there is only one registered operator disposing the vehicle, but the personalised plate is owned by two plate owners, then F2963 will need to be completed.

    Additionally, if the vehicle is to be transferred to two acquirers but it is intended for only one acquirer to be the plate owner then F2963 will also need to be completed.

Deceased Estate
Deceased Estate
    The deceased estates transfer policy applies to personalised plate transfers.
    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Deceased Estates Transfers

    Jointly owned plates
    Jointly owned plates
      If one owner is deceased, the plates can be transferred to the surviving owner (exempt of transfer fee). Acceptable Proof of Death document must be provided (original or certified copy) unless already recorded in TICA as deceased.


      Note:No additional documents are required to remove the deceased owner from the jointly owned plates.

      If certain rules are met, the plates will be automatically transferred to the surviving owner. Refer: Automatic Transfer

      If one owner wants to be removed, the plates can be transferred to the other owner (transfer fee applies unless exempt).

CONF Restriction
    The following applies where a CONF Restriction is recorded by the Queensland Police Service (QPS):
    Vehicle is not being transferred along with the plates
    Vehicle is not being transferred along with the plates
      If the vehicle is not being transferred along with the plates (ie only the plates are being transferred), this transaction cannot be processed during the confiscation period as TICA will not allow the plates to be removed from the vehicle while the CONF Restriction is applied.
    Transfer of plates that are attached to a vehicle in TICA
    Transfer of plates that are attached to a vehicle in TICA
      If the plates are not attached to a vehicle in TICA, this transaction may be processed during the confiscation period.

    For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at:
    https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/

Plates Currently Attached
Plates Currently Attached
    If the customer wants to remove customised or personalised plates currently attached to a vehicle they must:
    • Apply for the attached plates to be removed before taking them off the vehicle
    and
    • Apply for replacement standard issue plates to be attached to the vehicle
    or
    • Apply for a different set of customised or personalised plates to be attached
Vehicle Traded to Dealer with Personalised Plate Attached
Vehicle Traded to Dealer with Personalised Plate Attached
    If a vehicle with personalised plates attached is traded to a dealer and only the vehicle is to be transferred to the new registered operator:
    • The Remove and/or Attach Personalised Plates Application (F2964) must be completed and signed by the plate owner/s
    • The plates must be removed from the vehicle and retained by the plate owner/s
    • Pay the fee for standard plates

    If the plates have not been retained by the owner, the following requirements apply instead:
    Plates still physically attached to vehicle
    Plates still physically attached to vehicle
      Dealer must:
      • Surrender the plates to TMR.
      • Provide a statement declaring that the plates were attached to the vehicle when it was traded and were not retained by the owner.
      • Pay the fee for standard plates.

      TMR must:
      • Contact plate owner by phone/email and:
        • If contact is made, post plates to last known address of plate owner/s.
        • If contact is not made, plates are to be surrendered in TICA and a text added to the customer record (outlining reason for surrender).
    Plates not physically attached to vehicle
    Plates not physically attached to vehicle
      Dealer must:
      • Provide a statement declaring that the plates were not attached to the vehicle when it was traded and they were retained by the owner.
      • Pay the fee for standard plates.
Vehicle transfer to Individual Personalised Plates Attached
Vehicle transfer to Individual Personalised Plates Attached
    If a vehicle with personalised plates attached is sold to an individual where
    only the vehicle is to be transferred to the new registered operator and they are unable to obtain the plate owner's signature on form F2964:
    • Acquirer to complete Remove and/or Attach Pers Plates Application (F2964)
    • Provide statement outlining reasons for inability to obtain Pers Plates owner's signature
    • Surrender the personalised plates to TMR
    • Pay the fee for standard plates

    Note: The personalised plates must be surrendered on TICA in case the plate owner decides to re-use them in the future.
Removal of Personalised Plates by Registered Operator and Plate Owner cannot be located
Removal of Personalised Plates by Registered Operator and Plate Owner cannot be located
    If registered operator is removing personalised plates from a vehicle and they are unable to obtain the plate owner's signature on form F2964 as they cannot be located, the registered operator can:
    • Complete Remove and/or Attach Pers Plates Application (F2964)
    • Complete statement outlining reasons for inability to obtain Pers Plates owner's signature
    • Surrender the personalised plates to TMR
    • TMR contact plate owner by phone/email and:
    • If contact is made, post plates to last known address of plate owner/s.
    • If contact is not made, plates are to be surrendered in TICA and a text added to the customer record (outlining reason for surrender)
    • Pay the fee for standard plates.


Note:If the plates are recorded as being attached to a vehicle currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice, you cannot process this transaction during the confiscation period. For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website:
https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/

If removing plates from a BHSL vehicle the managed booked hire staff UI must be updated with the new registration number.
SurrenderIf the owner of personalised plates wishes to surrender the plates they may do so. They remain the owner of the plate number and may remake a set at any time. They should only cancel ownership if they no longer want to own the number or the customer is deceased/closed and the plates are not being transferred.

Surrender of Plates
CancelCancelling ownership of a personalised plate involves:

If plates are attached to a vehicle
If plates are attached to a vehicle

    If plates (only) are being cancelled:
    • Customer surrenders the number plate/s and completes Remove and/or Attach Personalised Number Plates Application (F2964)

      Note:If the plates are recorded as being attached to a vehicle currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice, you cannot process this transaction during the confiscation period.
    • Attach new plates to the vehicle.
    • Once the plates have been surrendered and cancelled the combination can be released for sale 3 months from date of surrender.

    If plates and vehicle are being cancelled:
    • Customer surrenders the number plate/s and completes Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation of Registration Application (F3517)

      Note:If plates have been taken by the QPS, as a result of a Number Plate Confiscation Notice, the applicant must complete the statement on the back of the cancellation form. CSOs must also indicate on the back of the cancellation form, in the 'office use only' section, that the vehicle is currently under a 'Number Plate Confiscation QPS (CONF)' restriction. QPS will surrendered the plates to TMR at the end of the confiscation period.
If plates are not attached to a vehicle
If plates are not attached to a vehicle
    • Customer surrenders the number plate/s and completes the relevant sections on Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation of Registration Application (F3517)
    • Once the plate/s have been surrendered and cancelled the combination can be released for sale 3 months from date of surrender.
Deceased/closed customer
Deceased/closed customer
    If a deceased or closed customer has customised or personalised plates they must be cancelled after being surrendered.
