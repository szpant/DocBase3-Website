Criminal History Check (Adhoc) (4677)


Criminal History Check - ADHOC Requirements
What it isA criminal history check is either a:
  • National search conducted by the Queensland Police Service to obtain an applicant's criminal history.
  • New Zealand search conducted by NZ police to obtain an applicant's criminal history
Why requiredTo ensure that the applicant is a suitable person to either:
  • purchase a product
  • enter into a regulated industry (eg passenger transport)
  • provide a service on behalf of TMR (eg AIS Proprietors).

Note: This document must be read in conjunction with the appropriate product guide.
When Required
When an ADHOC Criminal History Check is Required
When an ADHOC Criminal History Check is Required
    Because some products are not managed in TICA, a criminal history check must be generated from the Criminal History Request screen at the time the application is submitted for:
    • Issue of Approved Person Scheme
    • Issue and renewal of AIS Approved Examiners
    • Issue of AIS Proprietors
    • Issue of Operator Accreditation
    • Issue of Tow Truck Licence
    • Products in TICA (if they declare during the currency of their accreditation that they have been charged or convicted of an offence)
    • Changing/adding any Associated Personnel to a lodged Booking Entity Authorisation

    Note: Many of these applications also require the payment of an application fee. Refer to the appropriate product guides for further information specific to these products.

    Note: For industry authorities that are recorded in TICA (e.g. Driver Authorisation, tow truck drivers/assistants etc) the criminal history check is built into the lodgement process for new issues and a pre-renewal checks is automatically conducted 8 weeks prior to the renewal date (for some products). Refer to the appropriate guide for further information
FeesUnless exempt a criminal history fee must always be paid.

Fee Exemption
Fee Exemption
    The applicant is exempt from the payment of a criminal history check (CHC) fee if:
    • Requesting a New Zealand criminal history check (as this is currently a free service)
    • Declaring criminal history at time of renewing their industry authority
    • Applying for both driver authorisation and operator accreditation on the same day. The operator accreditation criminal history check is not required and no fee is charged.
    • Applying for operator accreditation/ booking entity authorisation and they are an existing driver authorisation holder a criminal history check is not required and no fee is charged.
    • Applying for both driver authorisation (DA) and Booking Entity Authorisation (BEA) on the same day. The BEA's CHC will be exempt and not be charged when receiving the application. Note that the DA needs to be lodged first as the CHC will be charged in this process.
    • Requesting a criminal history for a show cause process
    • Operator error
Criminal History Check Results
Criminal History Check Results
Criminal History Check Results
    After close of business all National requests are batched and forwarded to QPS and the status on the Criminal History Request Page is changed to "Submitted".

    After close of business all NZ requests are batched and forwarded to the relevant policy unit who send the request to the NZ authorities. The status on the Criminal History Request Page is changed to "Submitted".

    If a request is not automatically finalised the next day it may mean either:
    • the search time has been extended due to complexities such as aliases.
    • the customer has a criminal history.

    Outstanding applications should be monitored daily against the TRAILS report inbox and current procedures apply if no response has been received within 4 weeks. Customer should still be advised that it may take up to 6 weeks for the requests to be finalised.

    Criminal histories are extremely sensitive documents and all efforts to protect a customers privacy should be made.

    If a customer's criminal history has not been returned within 10 working days and the customer has presented at the Customer Service Centre to determine the reason for the delay please contact:
    • Passenger Transport Division (for operator accreditation and booking entity authorisation only)
    • Industry Accreditation and Licensing
