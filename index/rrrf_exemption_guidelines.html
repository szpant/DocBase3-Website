RRRF Exemption Guidelines (3107)

Exemption of RRRF
In general there are no exemptions to the RRRF.

An exemption may be granted if:
  • It can be confirmed that TMR contributed to the late payment of the registration
  • Customer paid the additional amount in error before the expiry date and does not want the registration date changed
  • Customer was prevented due to an accident or another unexpected reason from paying by the expiry date by any of the payment methods and can provide supporting evidence (for example: medical records) - request must be in writing
  • Part of Disaster Relief package

If the customer does not meet any of the above criteria, advise them that their request cannot be considered.

Note:If a registration has expired on a weekend, public or local (example: Exhibition) holiday* and was paid on the next business day, TICA will not charge a RRRF.

*For a list of current local show holidays, please follow the below link:
http://www.justice.qld.gov.au/fair-and-safe-work/industrial-relations/public-holidays

    To assess request:
    • Receive request and identify issue
    • Forward to other areas for input if required
    • Investigate taking into consideration:
      • Previous payment history
      • Grounds for exemption
        Exemption can be granted
        Exemption can be granted
          An exemption can be granted if:
          • TMR failed to update customer's address after receiving change of address notification (includes postal address)
          • TMR incorrectly updated the address as a result of information on Australia Post's Postal Address File
          • Payment processed at incorrect date by TMR or Dealer
          • No vehicle registration notice generated due to:
            • Vehicle incorrectly transferred out of customers name
            • Vehicle recorded as Stolen at time renewal payment request generated
          • Credit card payment service system malfunction (both scheduled and unplanned outages)
          • CSC was closed due to unforeseen circumstances when person attempted to make payment (for example - power outage, emergency event)
          • Customer provides written confirmation from their financial institution that a BPay payment received by TMR was initiated on or prior to expiry date. This does not extend to BPay payments made by credit card
          • Where the registered operator or family member was hospitalised due to an unforeseen medical emergency
          • A death in the immediate family has occurred and can be proved
          • Another unexpected reason that prevented them paying by the due date by any method and is supported by evidence.
          • Natural disasters (Refer: Initiatives for Natural Disasters)
          • Droughts (Refer: Vehicle Rego > 03. Types of Registration > Drought Assistance Package > Rules > Exemption of RRRF)

        Exemption cannot be granted
        Exemption cannot be granted
          An exemption cannot be granted if registered operator:
          • Posted on time but TMR received it more than one day after expiry date
          • Was interstate or overseas
          • Did not drive between registration expiring and late payment
          • Did not receive their vehicle registration notice (other than TMR error)
          • Recently purchased the vehicle and did not know that it expired
          • Has no current COI or exemption
          • Registered operator or family member is hospitalised due to scheduled surgery/treatment. That is, the surgery/treatment was not an unforeseen medical emergency
      • Supporting documentation
      • If TMR systems were operable at the time
