Smartcard digital photos and digitised signatures (3340)


Digital photo's and digitised signatures
What it is
  • A digital photo means a facial image encoded in a digital form; and
  • A digitised signature means a person's signature encoded in a digital form.
When they will be captured
When Digital Photo/Signature will be captured
When Digital Photo/Signature will be captured
    A digital photo and digitised signature must be captured:
    • On application for a customers first issued smartcard product;
    • If the term of the smartcard exceeds the image life of the stored digital photo;
    • For applications for a change of name (customer signature changes therefore requiring a new set of images);
    • If attending TMR in relation to a non-TMR smartcard product eg removing a customer restriction to allow for the issue of a High Risk Work Licence, Disability Worker Screening Cards or Working with Children Card (Blue Card).

    Any digital photos and digitised signatures captured for a smartcard product are stored within the system as a set. The TICA system will display the expiry date for the latest image set (face and signature captured on the same day). The IMU will be the only area able to view/access images.

    Note:The Image Management System (IMS) will have the captured date and TICA will calculate and inform an operator if they attempt to issue a licence, authority or card that exceeds the image set life where the images have not been rejected or not under investigation by IMU.
When they can be captured
When Digital Photo/Signature may be captured
When Digital Photo/Signature may be captured
    A digital photo and/or digitised signature may be captured to:
    • Help identify a person to decide whether or not to grant an application for a smartcard product;
    • Help verify the person for security reasons (e.g. conduct a 1:1)
When they are required
When they are required
When they are required
    A person who applies for the grant, renewal or replacement of a smartcard product (both TMR and non-TMR) must allow a digital photo and digitised signature to be taken and kept.

    The issuing officer may take a digital photo on application for a smartcard product:
    • To verify a person's identity; and
    • If they feel the person's most recent digital photo is no longer a true likeness of the person (e.g. medical reasons).
ExemptionA digital image/signature may be exempted when a correction to a customers name was due to operator error.
When is a new image unable to be taken?
Image unable to be taken
Image unable to be taken
    • A facial image must not be captured when the IMU has not authorised or rejected a previous facial image that has:
      • Failed an ISO-compliance check; or
      • Failed a 1:1 or 1:N comparison.
When are digital images used?
When are digital images used?
When are digital images used?
    A person's most recent digital photo and digitised signature may be used, with the person's consent, to;
    • Reproduce the person's digital photo and digitised signature on a smartcard product
    • Verify the person's identity for security purposes relating to a smartcard product (eg. Suppressed customer applications)
    • Help identify the person for deciding an application for a smartcard product
    • Assist in an investigation of, or proceeding for, an offence relating to the application for the grant, renewal, or replacement of a smartcard product or relevant licence, authorisation or accreditation.

Digital image standards (ISO)
Digital image standards (ISO)
Digital image standards (ISO)
    All captured digital photos must comply with the ISO/IEC 19794-5 Standard. The capturing of digital photos to this standard helps maximise the effectiveness of facial recognition technology that the department applies to photos when conducting image comparisons.

    Accepting and using digital photos that are of a standard less then the ISO standard increases the risk of:
    • Unnecessary and time consuming 1:1 image comparison failures;
      and
    • Not identifying multiple and different identities that customers may try to fraudulently create with TMR

    Exemption from ISO compliance
    Exemption from ISO compliance
      For the image to be accepted it must be ISO compliant but in exceptional cases a non-compliant digital photo may be accepted when:
      • A supervisor or manager has confirmed that the camera is unable to capture an ISO compliant image of the customer
      • A valid reason exists for the non-compliance; and
      • The IMU have accepted the image use in facial recognition processes and card production.

      Valid Reasons for referring a non-compliant digital photo to the IMU
      Reason 1 - Medical condition or disability
      Reason 1 - Medical condition or disability
        Issue
        The customer has a medical incapacity that impacts on their ability to provide a digital photo.
        Solution
        If there is a visual indicator that a person suffers from a medical condition (e.g. eye disorder) then the supervisor/manager is to use their discretion. Supporting document may be requested.
        Decision maker
        The supervisor or manager in consultation with IMU

      Reason 2 - Environment
      Reason 2 - Environment
        Issue
        The environment where the image has been captured compromises the capture of a compliant image e.g. environment has too much light and operator has taken measures to reduce the level of light.
        Solution
        The Manager/supervisor in consultation with IMU and equipment supplier to ensure environment situation is rectified immediately. Under no circumstances should this situation be on-going.
        Decision maker
        The supervisor or manager in consultation with IMU

      Reason 3 - Equipment
      Reason 3 - Equipment
        Issue
        The equipment used to capture the image has resulted in a non-compliant image e.g. automatic focus is malfunctioning.
        Solution
        The Manager/supervisor in consultation with IMU and equipment supplier to ensure equipment situation is rectified immediately. Under no circumstances should this situation be on-going.
        Decision maker
        The supervisor or manager in consultation with IMU

      Reason 4 - Scanned image/image file
      Reason 4 - Scanned image/image file
        Dalby Office Only:
        Issue
        Smartcard applications processed by Dalby Office (interstate/overseas/change of name 'transition') only:
        • A scanned image or image file has resulted in a non-compliant captured facial image e.g. passport photo provided for interstate/overseas licence/change of name 'transition' applications does not meet the standard.
        Solution
        The customer should be advised (by Dalby CSC) to provide a new passport photo.

        Note:In very rare cases where the customer has supplied numerous
        passport photos and due to the inability to provide a conforming passport photo (e.g. overseas location and only one passport photo supplier etc), then an exemption may apply.
        Decision maker
        The supervisor or manager (Dalby CSC) in consultation with IMU


      Note:A transaction that requires the issue of a new smartcard product will not proceed until the IMU has authorised the use of the non-compliant image.
Image Capture Guidelines
Image Capture guidelines
Image Capture guidelines
    It is important to capture a digital photo that has a full facial view. This will ensure that the customer's photograph will meet the ISO standard required for facial recognition processes and production of smartcards.

    The operator is to follow the guidelines below for the capture of digital photos.

    Image Capture Guideline - Portrait Final Jan 2019
    TMR Image Capture Guidelines
    TMR Image Capture Guidelines
      Image Capture scenarioTMR image capture guidelines
      Photograph background
      • White background (as supplied to CSC's).
      Style and lighting
      • As supplied to CSC's
      Expression and frame
      • All facial features from bottom of the chin to top of the forehead (including both edges of the applicant's face) are to be clearly shown
      • Customer must look directly at the camera with a neutral expression and closed mouth.
      • The applicant must be alone, with no other person or object visible.
      • An operator may capture a non-compliant image in those cases where an applicant is unable to hold their head straight without assistance due to a disability.
      Glasses
      • All glasses and sunglasses must be removed (regardless of whether glasses are required for driving).
      • Where a person cites a need to continue to wear glasses due to an existing eye/vision disorder, then the manager may allow the capture of an image of the person wearing glasses provided they are satisfied that the customer's claim is genuine. Acceptable eye wear may include thin rimmed glasses where the frames do not obstruct the customer's eyes, tinted or darkened glasses in the event where the camera lights are likely to cause pain, discomfort or damage to the customer's eye. Eye-patches are permitted for the same reasons. All images accepted under these circumstances by the manager will be reviewed for appropriateness by the IMU before final acceptance or refusal for smartcard production.
      Head coverings
      • Hats, caps and any other headwear or clothing that covers the head, such hoodies, scarfs, beanies or colanders, must be removed.
      • Headwear worn for religious reasons is adjusted so that all facial features from bottom of the chin to top of the forehead (including both edges of the applicant's face) are clearly shown.
      Note:It is taken that headwear worn for religious reasons is generally worn in public places, including while the person is driving.
      • Headwear worn solely for the purpose of the licence photograph is not permitted.
      Note:A manager or supervisor at their discretion may allow an image to be captured of the applicant in headwear for medical reasons (for example the customer has lost their hair due to medical treatment).
      Hair and ears
      • Customers are encouraged to make their ears visible in the photo. However, it is not compulsory for ears to be visible.
      • Earrings are acceptable provided they do not obstruct the view of the edges of the face.
      • Most hair types are acceptable provided the hair does not cover the face and the face and eyes are visible.
      • Hair accessories are acceptable provided they do not obstruct the view of any facial feature.
      Facial ornamentsFacial ornaments such as nose rings and studs are acceptable provided they are not easy to remove by the applicant and the image will comply with photo image standards.

How long is a photo 'usable' for?
Shelf life of digital photos and signatures
Shelf life of digital photos and signatures
    The shelf life of a digital photo and digitised signature is 11 years after the photo and signature are taken.

    The 11 year shelf life maybe extended by a maximum of 6 months.

    Note:The shelf life of a digital photo and digitised signature is calculated from the application date when the images were captured and stored within the TMR system.

    The TICA system will indicate to an operator if they attempt to issue a licence, authority or card that exceeds the digital photo and digitised signature shelf life, including any extension of the shelf life, by prompting the operator to capture another image set.
Retention period
Retention period of photos and signatures
Retention period of photos and signatures
    The retention period for a image set is:
    • 30 years after the day taken if a smartcard product is issued
    • Up to 24 hours after the day taken for the purpose of verifying a customer's identity and not used for the issue of a smartcard product
    • 6 months if not attached to a product (e.g. image captured for a High Risk Work licence or Blue Card application but the card is not issued within 6 month)
    • 12 months after the day taken for a smartcard that was not issued or renewed, (e.g. lodged IA applications/driver licence written tests).
    Note:After the retention period has expired the image set is deleted from TICA.

    Exemption to retention period
    Exemption to retention period
      A digital photo and digitised signature may be kept for a longer period if required for an investigation, or proceedings resulting from an investigation, that started before the retention period ended.
Storage
Storage of digital photos and signatures
Storage of digital photos and signatures
    A digital photo and digitised signature will be stored:
    • As an algorithmic representation;
    • In a secure Image Management system (IMS);
    • In a separate database from, but linked (by a unique identifier that is not the customer's reference number), to the customer's other information stored in TICA.
    Note:A digital photo or digitised signature will not be stored on the smartcard chip.

    Scanned images of Passport Photos
    Scanned images of Passport Photos
      Passport photos and signatures that have been scanned and saved to a local or network drive (eg. from interstate/overseas kits), must be deleted immediately after the associated application has been processed.
Camera
Camera connected via FIC USB Network
  • At the end of each day, camera's connected to PC's via USB must be shut down to prevent them going into hibernation mode.
  • The camera USB cord should always be plugged into one of the spare USB ports at the front of the PC.
  • A PC with a camera connected via USB must be turned on in order for the camera to be available for selection. (A user does not need to be logged in).
Types of camera
Type of camera used for taking digital photo
Type of camera used for taking digital photo
    Purpose-built image capture devices which incorporate a camera and lights built into each unit will be used to capture a digital biometric facial image of the customer. The cameras are designed to assist with capturing digital photos and have software to assist the operator in capturing an images that comply with ISO/IEC 19794-5 Standard.

    There will be three types of camera's used for taking digital photos:
    • Fixed (located in most CSC's)
    • Portable (QPS stations); and
    • Mobile (Roving Teams)

Image capture trouble shooting table:
Image capture trouble shooting table:
    Category of Non-Compliance
    Frontal Pose
    Frontal Pose
      Description
      The customer is not looking directly at the camera or has rotated their head.
      • The face is considered frontal if the rotation of the head is less than +/-5 degrees from frontal for yaw and pitch and if roll angle of head is less then +/-8 degrees.

      Actions required
      • Request customer to look directly at camera.
      • Request customer to tilt their head to the left/right or tilt their head up/down
    One Visible face
    One Visible face
      Description
      The customer is not alone (that is there is another person or object visible within the frame). A large amount of these errors occur due to shadows appearing on the backing screen.

      Actions required
      Request the other person to move from the camera area or the customer to remove object from camera area.
    Eyes Open
    Eyes Open
      Description
      The customer’s eyes are:
      • not open and/or
      • not clearly visible because hair is obscuring any part of the eyes
      • obscured due to glasses frames being too heavy.

      Actions required
      Request the customer to:
      • Open their eyes; or
      • Remove hair that may be obscuring their face; or
      • Remove glasses; or
      • If customer is wearing dark glasses for light sensitive eyes and this action is approved by the office manager, accept non compliant ISO image and contact IMU.
    Face Vertically Centred
    Face Vertically Centred
      Description
      The face is not centred vertically within the captured image.
      This is determined when the eyes line is within 50%-70% of the image height.

      Actions required
      Request the customer to move up or down' as their head is too high or too low.
    Face Horizontally Centred
    Face Horizontally Centred
      Description
      The face is not centred horizontally within the captured image. This is determined as the centre of the face should be with 45% and 55% of the image width.

      Actions required
      Request the customer to move to the right or left (centre the customer).
    Head Height
    Head Height
      Description
      For a successful image the head should be within 60% and 90% of the captured image.

      Options Available
      Request the customer to:
      • Move forward or back:
        • If they are too close to the camera, they should sit back; and
        • If they are too far away, they should sit forward

      If the customer does not reach the minimum height required to achieve a successful image contact IMU.

      If an acceptable image cannot be captured IMU may advise the CSO to use the following procedure:
      • Obtain photo using:
        • CSC portable digital camera, or
        • Take a photo using a mobile phone, or
        • Customer may provide a passport photo
      • Have customer complete the Licence Specimen Signature Form F2127
      • Phone Dalby CSC to advise them you have a photo and signature that needs importing into TICA
      • Email photo and Licence Specimen Signature form F2127 to Dalby CSC, include customer name and CRN
      • Dalby CSC will crop the photo, convert images to a JPEG format and import into TICA
      • When photo and signature have been imported the CSO can continue transaction
    Width To Height Ratio
    Width To Height Ratio
      Description
      The width to height ratio should be within 1.25 and 1.34. That is the height should be roughly 125% - 134% longer than the width.

      Actions required
      • The customer is too close to the camera, resulting in lens barrel distortion. Contact support as the set-up is incorrect
      • Ensure seating for photo capture is placed on the marked position on the floor. If in the correct position, contact ITB Service Centre
      • If using a portable screen, check placement of the screen to ensure compliance with distance from camera and moving it forward/back.
    Image Exposure
    Image Exposure
      Description
      There is over or under exposure in the captured image.

      Actions required
      • Ask the customer to move out of the camera field of view and restart the camera.
      • Auto exposure has been incorrect and needs to be re-initialised.
      • In other words, the White Balance needs to be done again.
    Image Lighting Uniformity
    Image Lighting Uniformity
      Description
      The light is not equally distributed in the face area. That means that there is a significant direction of the light from the point of view of the photographer / camera.

      Actions required
      • Request customer to look directly at camera.
      • It could also mean that only one camera light is on.
    Width Between Eyes
    Width Between Eyes
      Description
      This is an indication of whether the face presented is a “real” human face

      Actions required
      • It could be a person with one eye.
      • If the customer is wearing dark glasses for light sensitive eyes and this action is approved by the office manager, accept the non compliant ISO image and contact the IMU.
      • Otherwise contact ITB Service Centre as the set-up may be incorrect.
      • You may be re-directed to the IMU to obtain approval to capture a non-compliant ISO image.
    Greyscale
    Greyscale
      Description
      The face does not have enough unique grey scale values. There needs to be 128 unique intensity values.

      Actions required
      • White balancing will determine the amount of light to provide during the image capture.
      • If the white balance fails, or if conditions change very quickly, then greyscale may occur.
      • Restarting the camera will start white balancing again and this should solve the problem.
    Sharpness
    Sharpness
      Description
      The face area (from chin to crown and from left to right ear) does not fits the focus and depth in field characteristics

      Actions required
      • Contact ITB Service Centre as the set-up may be incorrect.
      • You may be re-directed to IMU to obtain approval to capture a non-compliant ISO image.
    Mouth Closed
    Mouth Closed
      Description
      The customer’s mouth is open.

      Actions required
      Request the customer to maintain a neutral expression and that you require their mouth to be closed.

    If a customer has a physical characteristic that hinders the image capture process, for instance someone missing an eye;
    or
    If staff have attempted to correct a non-compliant indicator using the actions in the above table and are still unable to capture a compliant facial image
