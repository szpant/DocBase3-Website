Personalised Transport Relief Package (7552)


Action NamePersonalised Transport Relief Package
When25 April 2020
BackgroundPersonalised transport services continue to provide essential community access for essential workers and vulnerable people. However, given the significant decline in patronage levels, the Queensland Government have approved a package of fee relief for the personalised transport industry.

The package consists of:
  • Waiving of fees for all existing booked hire/taxi (BHTX) driver authorisations for six months
  • Updating the refund policy for Booked Hire Service Licences (BHSLs) so holders can receive a pro-rata refund for the unused portion of the licence on surrender
  • Extending all taxi and limousine licences for six months
  • Extending the terms of all existing Booking Entity Authorisations (BEAs) for six months
  • Waiving the annual Taxi Industry Security Levy in 2019-2020.

BHTX DAs 6 month relief
    In most cases, BHTX driver authorisation holders who were current on 25 April 2020 (fee package announcement) will automatically have their driver authorisation term extended by 6 months over the coming weeks. These holders will be advised of this and informed that they will receive their updated industry authority and authorised driver – booked hire and taxi cards in the mail in due course.

    However, there are some exceptions to the automatic extension, as follows:-

    1. BHTX driver authorisation holders that were issued a driver authorisation for the maximum five-year term cannot be extended beyond this period under legislation. These holders have received a 6-month credit in their TMR account which can be used for any in person TMR transaction. The credit is not available in the customers online TMR account and therefore cannot be used for TMR online transactions. If requested the credit can be provided to the customer as a cheque.

    2. BHTX driver authorisation holders with current commercial medical certificates issued by their medical practitioner for 12 months or less will need to lodge a renewal application (in person or via the remote area processing kit). Upon renewal the holder will be required to provide a new commercial medical certificate and meet all the necessary renewal requirements, at which time they will receive a 6-month discount on their authority.

    The automatic extension of BHTX driver authorisations by six months will also extend the time taken until holders must obtain a new commercial medical certificate.

    It is considered that this risk is manageable for customers who have been issued a long-term medical certificate (medical certificate was issued for more than 12 months) but not for customers who are required to have a regular medical review (medical certificate issued for 12 months or less.) To ensure that the changes have no undue safety implications the following rules will apply:

BHSL Surrender and RefundsFrom 19 May 2021, customers can't surrender their BHSL for the reason of 'COVID-19' and will not be eligible for an automatic pro-rata refund due to the COVID pandemic. Customers may continue to submit a surrender request in person and by email/post for medical, deceased or other reasons. Refer: Passenger Transport > 1. Booked Hire Service Licence > Booked Hire Service Licence (BHSL) Guide > Transaction details > Surrender of a BHSL.

The automated surrender credit in TICA (switched on to manage the high volumes of COVID-19 surrenders) has been switched off, and eligible refunds will go back to being processed as manual adjustments as per the standard BHSL procedures. For example, TRO will assess eligibility for BHSL surrender refund requests, and will forward the refund email request to the relevant CSC for processing.

BHSL surrender applications received by email will automatically forward to Greenslopes CSC for processing of the vehicle amendments only (i.e. POU downgrade, refund cheque for the change if eligible).

Taxi and Limousine Licences SILAS is being updated to automatically extend the validity of taxi and limousine licences. In the interim, no enforcement action will be taken against out-of-date licences.

Additionally, taxi and limousine licence holders who cancel their registration will not have any action taken against them for not having an appropriate vehicle attached to the licence.
BEAsSystem changes are being progressed to extend BEAs which have a 1-4 year term, this fix may take up to 8 weeks to implement. In the interim BEAs with an expiring authorisation should be advised that the renewal requirement will not be enforced.

BEAs that were issued for the maximum period of 5 years will have a credit equivalent to six months of authorisation applied to their TMR account.
