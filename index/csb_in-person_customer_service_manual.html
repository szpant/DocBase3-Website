CSB In-Person Customer Service Manual (7292)


What is itThe aim of this document is to clearly outline CSB’s in-person customer service expectations. The best practice guidelines will enable staff to get it right the first time and deliver the experience that customers expect.
CSB Customer Service PrinciplesAs defined in the CSB 2016-2020 Strategic Plan we aim to create a customer centric culture that meets that customers’ needs first time every time. To achieve this we:

Understand our customers by:
  • Utilising effective questioning techniques to identify their needs
  • Actively listening to their responses
  • Analysing and interpreting their response to identify the relevant business process or service interaction
  • Confirming our interpretation of their needs with the customer for clarity

Do What We Say and deliver an integrated and professional customer experience by:
  • Providing the customer with relevant services or finding a way by referring to internal and external stakeholders.
  • Ensuring interactions are seamless by providing them with complete, accurate and targeted information.
  • Ensuring all current/pending transactions on a customer’s record are identified, communicated and processed if requested by the customer.

Ensure our customers receive a consistent experience in their channel of choice by:
  • Ensuring state-wide consistency in all of our processes and services
  • Maintaining staff knowledge of operational policy and procedures and service delivery options
  • Providing timely access to accurate and consistent knowledge bases
  • Conducting regular audits of compliance with processes

Concierge Guidelines
Concierge Guidelines
    As the first point of contact for our customer the role of the concierge is to ensure customers progress to the counter as quickly and orderly as possible. To do this the concierge will:
    • Greet the customer in a friendly and professional manner upon entry to the service location
    • Engage the customer prior to them queuing or selecting a Q-Matic ticket to identify their needs
    • Conduct a review of their readiness to transact for example do they have sufficient evidence of their identity or supporting documents. The depth of this this review is time dependant, the priority of the concierge is to ensure customers progress to the counter as quickly and orderly as possible
    • Direct the customer as necessary by providing them with a ticket for service or referral to the correct service location

Guidelines for utilising employees who are a Justice of the Peace
Guidelines for utilising employees who are a Justice of the Peace

    To avoid customer disappointment, unless an agreed appointment has been made, customers requesting a Justice of the Peace (JP) should not be automatically advised to attend a Customer Service Centre (CSC) where a JP is employed (not all CSC locations have a team member who is a qualified JP).

    For customers wanting to access a JP, staff should first use or direct customers to use the Find a JP service.

    Directing customers to a CSC who has a JP amongst their team, without an agreed appointment, is problematic for a range of reasons; including that the staff member's duties/priorities may change throughout the course of the day, illness may occur and break times vary.

    As per the the JP Handbook issued by the Department of Justice and Attorney-General, JPs are appointed to serve all members of the community, not just a select few in the organisation in which they work or for the organisation’s customers. JPs should make themselves available to offer services whenever possible, however if busy, it is reasonable that JPs can advise customers to make an appointment that suits both them and the customer.
