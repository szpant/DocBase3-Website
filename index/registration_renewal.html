Registration Renewal (3064)


What is it?A registration renewal is a payment made to continue registration for a further term of registration.

A vehicle is deemed to be registered when:
  • Application for renewal has been made
    and
  • Appropriate fees receipted and applied

Registration Notices
    • Are only sent if the product attracts a renewal fee.
    • Will not be sent if a No Renewal Allowed (NORA) restriction is applied to the vehicle Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Vehicle Restrictions or Text > Vehicle Restrictions
    • Will usually be sent to the first registered operator unless:
      • the registration is enrolled in direct debit and the second registered operator is the direct debit authority holder. The VRRN will be emailed/posted to the second registered operator and the first registered operator will not receive a notice.
      • there is a paying agent attached to the registration.
    • Will be sent by standard mail if the registered operator has not signed up for e-notices
    • Will be sent by email if the customer has signed up for e-notices. Only individuals can sign up to receive e-notices. If the TMR mail house receives notification that the email bounced the VRRN will then be sent by post.

    Some registration notices excluded
    Refer: Services > 1. Online Services > TMR Online Services Guide or
    Refer below: Types of Notices

    If the other registered operator (first or second) requests a copy of the Registration Renewal notice they can be sent the "Vehicle Registration Renewal Notice information for other registered operator" email template.

    Once the registration fees are raised in TICA, any changes to the record that result in the renewal fees being re-assessed/re-raised will generate a new vehicle Registration Notice, unless payment is made on the same day at a CSC/Agency.


    Note: If a customer changes the pay term after a Vehicle Registration Notice (VRRN) has been issued for a vehicle enrolled in direct debit, a new VRN will be generated which will indicate:
    • that the vehicle is enrolled for direct debit; and
    • the pay terms displayed will be the previous pay term and the new pay term

    Changes include:
    • Pay Term
    • CTP Class
    • CTP Insurer
    • ITCE

    CSC/Call Centre staff can advise the customer of the payment reference number.

        One-month term
        One-month term
          Customers (DDRR Authority holder) with a one-month registration term will be emailed their VRRN 27 days before the registration expires.

As such, they will always receive their VRRN by email. The only exception would be if the email was recorded as bounced by the TMR mail house, the VRRN would then be sent by post.
        3,6 or 12 month term
        3,6 or 12 month term
          For registration notices received by mail, the notice should be received at the registered operator's mailing address approximately 4 weeks before the renewal date of the registration.

          If the customer has signed up to receive e-Notices the registration notice will be emailed approximately 6 weeks before the renewal date of the registration.

        When the customer has signed up to receive e-Reminders and the registration remains unpaid a reminder email will be sent:
        • 3 days before the registration expiry date. Details in this e-Reminder include:
          • Registration number
          • Registration expiry date
          • Link to pay registration online service
        • On the registration expiry date. Details in this e-Reminder include:
          • Registration number
          • Registration expiry date
          • Link to pay registration online service
        • 7 days after the registration expires. Details in this e-Reminder include:
          • Registration number
          • Registration expiry date
          • Auto cancellation date
          • Link to pay registration online service

        21 days after the expiry date the customer is sent an Overdue Fee Final Notice specifying final payment date.

      Note:      		If the first registered operator is signed up for e-Notices the Overdue Fees Final Notice will be sent by email, otherwise it will be sent to the mailing address.

      What makes up Registration Fees
      What makes up Registration Fees

        Registration Fees can include:
        • Registration Fee
        • Plate Fee
        • Duty (if applicable)
        • Nominal Defendant Levy
        • Traffic Improvement Fee
        • Administration Fee (Transport)
        • Statutory Insurance Scheme Levy
        • Hospital and emergency services
          levy
        • CTP
        • Surcharge (for registration renewals less than 12 months)

        Annual renewal registration charges are made up of several fee components:
        • Registration fee
        • Traffic Improvement Fee (TIF)
        • Compulsory Third Party (CTP) insurance premium.

        The Registration Fee is used for:
        • Funding the construction and maintenance of Queensland's road network
        • Managing vehicle identification and data management costs
        • General administration of the vehicle register.

        Traffic Improvement Fee (TIF)
        Traffic Improvement Fee (TIF)
          The TIF is:
          • Non-refundable
          • Statutory fee
          • Paid when the registration comes into effect.
          The Traffic Improvement Fee (TIF) is an integral part of vehicle registration and is collected by the Department of Transport and Main Roads on behalf of Queensland Treasury who determines the allocation of this funding.

          TMR collects CTP as a component of vehicle registration, and this premium is collected on behalf of and paid to the licensed insurer to indemnify against personal injury claims involving registered motor vehicles.
      Requests for refund of renewal fees
      Requests for refund of renewal fees
        The registered operator can cancel the registration either:
        • Prior to the start of the new registration period for a full refund
        • After the start of the new registration period for a pro-rata refund

        In general, once a registration renewal has been paid it cannot be refunded unless the registration is cancelled. However, in extenuating circumstances, a manager/SASM can assess the customer's situation based on the Customer Refund Request Guidelines.

        Refer: Admin > 6. Managerial Procedures > The How To: Managerial Procedures > Managerial Procedures > Customer paid registration without permission

      One-month pay term
      One-month pay term
        A one-month registration term is only available for customers with a direct debit payment method - renewal only.
      3 month pay term
      3 month pay term
        A 3 month pay term is only available for customers with:
        • Heavy vehicles
        • Vehicles in drought declared areas with a FARM or PRIV POU
        • Vehicles with seasonal registration
        • Vehicles in receipt of a disaster relief benefit
        • A direct debit payment method – renewal only
        • Vehicles registered with a personalised transport POU (TAXI, LIMO, LSPL, BKHR, BKRN)

        Note: The relief type must be updated in TICA if a customer is paying a 3 month registration term due to drought.
      Fixed Payment Reference Number (PRN)
      Fixed Payment Reference Number (PRN)
        A fixed PRN is a number that will apply to all types of vehicle/vessel registration and will remain the same while the customer is the registered operator of the vehicle/vessel. If the registered operator changes, a new fixed PRN will be issued when the next renewal is issued to the new registered operator.
        Additional rules
        Additional rules
          • Fixed PRN will not change if the plates on the vehicle change (e.g. attach/remove personalised plates)
          • If the vehicle has two registered operators, the PRN will remain the same as long as the vehicle is registered to the primary registered operator.
          • Fixed PRN applies to CDD based registration
          • Fixed PRN is the same regardless of the pay term
          • TMR should not promote changing a PRN, however in certain circumstances (such as domestic violence situation) a customer can request one. The operator must raise a SCO datafix to request the PRN be marked as inactive. When the next renewal is raised, a new fixed PRN will be allocated.
          • If a customer is issued with a new CRN, they will not be issued with a new fixed PRN.
          • Payments of less/more than the pay term amount are dealt with according to the VTF rules or returned to the payer's bank account if insufficient to vary the pay term.
