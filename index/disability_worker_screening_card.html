Disability Worker Screening Card (7635)


OverviewThe Department of Seniors, Disability Services and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships (DSDSATSIP) is responsible for administering the disability worker screening system in Queensland which includes both NDIS worker screening checks and Queensland disability worker screening checks.

Worker screening checks are one of the strategies used to identify and minimise risk of harm to people with disability, receiving services and support.

DSDSATSIP has partnered with TMR to improve the disability worker screening system in Queensland, leveraging TMRs capabilities, specifically around streamlining the application process and strengthening the identity management system.
How Disability Worker Cards are issued
On-Line Application Service
On-Line Application Service
    The DSDSATSIP website will allow disability worker screening card customers to apply for and renew their disability worker screening card, using their TMR customer details (CRN, name and DOB).

    The customer's information will be validated against the information stored by TMR. The information that will be validated will include:
    • Name;
    • Date of Birth;
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN);
    • Image Set (must have 5 years and 3 months life left on the image);
    • Phone Number
    • Email Address; and
    • Confirmation their record is not restricted (for example, a NOAC restriction).

    Customers who successfully validate their identity will progress through and lodge their application online with DSDSATSIP.

    Customers who are unable to complete validation of their identity will have to attend a TMR CSC or agency to have a CRN created and an image set captured.

    For new TMR customers, staff must provide the 'New Customer Notification – Disability Worker Screening Card' by email. This notification will provide the customer the necessary information to continue their application via the DSDSATSIP website.

    Once an application is approved, DSDSATSIP will send a card production request to TICA and this request will be batched and sent with our standard card production requests. DSDSATSIP are responsible for assessing all applications and ordering cards for approved applicants.

    TMR is responsible for providing the identity piece, there is no requirement for TMR to accept disability worker screening card applications and there is no process (other than image and signature capture) in TICA to undertake.

    Customers     will not be able to use their Disability Worker Screening Card as an identity document with TMR.
TMR's role
Customer has failed validation
Customer has failed validation
    Customers who fail the online identity validation will be referred to TMR to have their record updated/created. There are several reasons why a Disability Worker Screening Card applicant is not able to pass the validation; the main reasons include:
    • they are not a TMR customer
    • customer details are incorrect (including eContact details)
    • customer has a NOAC restriction
    • customer has an image restriction
    • customer has insufficient EOI recorded with TMR
    • customer's TMR image has insufficient shelf life

    Error messaging used on the DSDSATSIP online service will notify customers when their record fails validation and if they need to attend a TMR location. Customers will be referred to DSDSATSIP in the first instance, who may then contact TMR second level support teams for advice, however, privacy requirements will limit the information TMR can provide to DSDSATSIP representatives.

    If a Disability Worker Screening Card customer presents at a CSC or agency due to an issue with their TMR record, operators should check that all the customer details are correct and capture a new image set/record EOI if required.

    Refer below: Customers with a NOAC restriction,for customers who have a NOAC restriction.

    Individuals from 10 years of age can apply for a Disability Worker Screening Card. Current policy and procedures will apply to how a minor’s record is created and managed. Minor’s should be advised to supply a parent/guardian’s email address and mobile phone number if they don’t have their own.

    Every DCDSS customer must have their image and signature captured, E-contact details updated, and evidence of address document recorded in TICA.

    Staff are reminded to ensure that data within TICA is complete, accurate and the highest care is taken when updating customer details.

Capturing an image set for a DSDSATSIP customer
Capturing an image set for a DSDSATSIP customer
    An image and signature must be captured for each customer applying for a Disability Worker Screening Card. You must:
    • Select the 'Disability Worker Card Applicant' option when capturing the image set
    • Provide the customer with the 'New Customer Notification - Disability Worker Screening card' (if they’re a new customer).Refer Below: Documentation to be provided to Customer
    • Record the customer's eContact details (phone AND email)

    Refer: Services > 2. Qld Government Partnerships > The How To: Qld Government Partnerships > Qld Government Partnerships Procedures for how to capture an Image Set in TICA.

    Evidence of Identity
    A customer must have standard EOI recorded, AND evidence of their residential address.

    Note:An address document must be recorded in TICA. If it’s not recorded, the customer won’t be able to validate online to complete their application

    Image Requirements
    The customer's image must be in a 'VALID' state. If you receive an image failure, contact IMU to have this resolved.

    Note:Customer's with a 'verified' image will be restricted from using the Disability Worker Screening Card online service and won’t be able to complete their application.

    Customer Requirements
    New customers need to complete a New Customer Application (F3503) if it is the first time the customer is conducting business with TMR and there is no other product/transaction required.

    All customers must have standard EOI and evidence of their residential address recorded in TICA before progressing with the image set.

    Note:An address document must be recorded in TICA. If it’s not recorded, the customer won’t be able to validate online to complete their application.

    Interstate residents/customers may apply for a Disability Worker Card. If required, the New Customer Application (F3503) should be completed with the interstate proof of address document written in Question 2 on the form. In the ‘Comments by authorising person’ make a note that an interstate residency document was sighted. When an interstate address document is presented for a Disability Worker Screening Card, the following text must be applied to the customer's record:
    'An interstate residency document has been recorded for the purpose of a Disability Worker Screening Card. Customer must provide a Queensland proof of residency document prior to obtaining any TMR product. Please expire this text once a Qld residency document has been presented. USERID, CSC name, CSC phone'

    It is common for customers to require both a Blue Card and a Disability Worker Screening Card. Only one image reason needs to be selected when capturing their image. Any time a customer has an image captured by TMR (TMR product or agency card) their image is allocated the same image shelf life. For the validation to be successful, the customer requires sufficient shelf life for the life of the product they are applying for, this image can then be used for any product with TMR.
    Listing customer images
    Listing customer images
      Disability Worker Screening Cards have a 5 year expiry. An image must be valid and not expire within 5 years and 3 months to be used on a Disability Worker Screening Card.
Documentation to be provided to Customer
Documentation to be provided to Customer
    Staff are to ensure that customer details have been updated, including their eContact details.

    Customers who obtain a CRN for the purpose of applying for a Disability Worker Screening Card must also be provided the following:

    Disability Worker Screening Card - New Customer Notification

    Complete the relevant customer details and email the notification to the customer. If required, a physical copy of this notification can be provided to the customer, refer below.

    New Customer Notification - Disability Worker Screening Card

    This document will provide the customer:
    • Their TMR customer details,
    • DSDSATSIP Online Application Instructions, and
    • DSDSATSIP contact information, for any enquiries.

    All enquiries about the Disability Worker Screening Card application process should be directed to the Department of Seniors, Disability Services and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships
Remote Area Kits
Remote Area Kits
    The DSDSATSIP team has modified our Remote Area Kits for their purposes and customers who are unable to attend a TMR location will be able to use this kit to have a CRN created, update customer details and have their image set imported.

    Remote Area Kits are sent to the Dalby CSC for processing. Staff processing these remote area kits are to complete and send the 'New TMR Customer Notification – Disability Worker Screening Card' to the customer's email, as recorded on their customer application forms.

    Customers are encouraged to contact     the Department of Seniors, Disability Services and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships for any enquiries and to obtain the Remote Area Kit.
Customers with a NOAC restriction
Customers with a NOAC restriction
    There are two types of customers who may have a NOAC restriction against their TMR records:
    • Suppressed customers with a NOAC restriction applied by IMU; and
    • Customers who have voluntarily requested TMR apply the NOAC restriction.

    Customers who have a NOAC, applied for any reason, will be unable to use the DSDSATSIP online verification service. These customers will be required to attend a TMR customer service centre/QGAP in person and provide consent to have the NOAC lifted for a period, to allow them to apply for their disability worker screening card online.

    Customers are required to complete the No Online Access Permitted (NOAC) Customer Restriction Request, Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System information > Qualifications, Restrictions and Text > Customer Restrictions > NOAC > Manually added NOAC > Requested by customer > No Online Access Permitted (NOAC) Customer Restriction Request

    Customers with a NOAC restriction applied by the Identity Management Unit (IMU) due to having a suppressed record are not to be altered without consent from IMU. Contact IMU in the first instance.

    In this scenario, the suppression will remain in place and only the NOAC restriction will be lifted for a 24-hour period. Customers should be advised to access the DSDSATSIP website and submit their application as soon as possible within this timeframe.

    Customers who do not apply via the DSDSATSIP website before the NOAC is reapplied will need to return to TMR and repeat the process. Only in extenuating circumstances, can this timeframe be extended to a maximum of 72 hours. This can be considered at the request of the customer only and will require approval from the manager of the IMU.

    For customers with a voluntary NOAC, it will be their responsibility to reapply for the NOAC once they have submitted their Disability Worker Screening Card application, if they still wish to have this restriction.

    Before these customers leave the office, ensure that you have checked that their details are accurate, including up to date eContact details, and ensure they have a valid image set, EOI and evidence of address recorded in TICA.

    Customers are to contact DSDSATSIP if they have concerns with their application. If customers are not willing to give consent to the lifting of the NOAC, please refer them to the Department of Seniors, Disability Services and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships to discuss their options.

Refer: The How To: Qld Government Partnerships > Manage Disability Worker Screening Card Applicant
SmartcardTo request the production of a Disability Worker Screening Card, DSDSATSIP will send a request to TICA with the information to be printed on the card and this request will be batched and sent to Placard with TMRs standard card production requests.

As such you will be able to see if the customer has had a Disability Worker Screening Card issued via the Smartcard list on the Individual Customer Hub. Only DCDSS will be able to reprint/reorder a disability worker screening card.

Refer: Smartcard > Smartcard Guide > Non-TMR Smartcards for Card Specifics
Contact DetailsTMR operators are responsible for answering enquiries related to a person’s TMR record only.

DSDSATSIP are responsible for providing advice on the application for or issue, renewal, replacement or cancellation of a Disability Worker Screening Card. Customers may contact DSDSATSIP.
