Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI (4735)


What it isWhen an individual is unable to provide Standard EOI documents there are three categories with several variations that can be considered.
Eligibility ProcessWhen considering a customer’s eligibility to use a variation to Standard EOI requirements you must decide:
  • What product is held
  • Which EOI category applies to the customer
  • Does the customer have the EOI to meet one of the acceptable variations
The 3 EOI categories are:
The 3 EOI categories are:
    1.Customer with no EOI record
    TICA shows:
    • No customer record or
    • A customer record with no Standard EOI documents recorded
    2.Customer with EOI record (with no smartcard product)
    TICA record shows:
    • A customer record that has required Standard EOI documents recorded
    • Converted driver licence (CVDL)
    • Converted 18+ Card (CV18)
    3. Customer with EOI record (with a smartcard product)
    TICA record shows:
    • A valid image; and
    • A customer record where Standard EOI documents have been recorded; or
    • Converted driver licence (CVDL); or
    • Converted 18+ Card (CV18).
Guidelines
Guidelines
    • For all initial customer enrolment, required Standard EOI documents must be presented to register the person's identity.
    • A person must not be made to obtain some types of Category A or B documents purely for the purposes of meeting TMR EOI requirements (eg getting a passport when the person has no intention of travelling).
    • In lieu of standard identity credentials, the Acceptable Variation of a 1:1 facial image comparison (FIC) and Customer Identity Checklist (CIC) or a FIC and either a category A or category B document can be used where a person has already established EOI and they have a facial image recorded, and they do not have the required EOI documents on them.
    • Customers must have either Standard EOI or the acceptable variation of EOI for the category, to be issued a product.

    Steps to follow if doubt about identity exists
    Steps to follow if doubt about identity exists

      The person best-placed to make a decision about the truthfulness of a customers' identity claim is the officer serving the customer.

      If after examining the EOI documents provided you are still not satisfied that you can verify the customer's identity, you have two options:

      1. Request the customer to come back with further EOI documents.
      or
      2. If you suspect fraud follow the Suspected Identity Fraud process

      Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity > Suspected Identity fraud

    Customer Identity checklist form F3985
    Customer Identity checklist form F3985
      What it isThe Customer Identity Checklist (CIC) has 2 uses:
      • Section 1 of the form is used for existing customers who do not meet Standard EOI requirements.
      • Section 2 of the form is used for an existing customer when EOI documents are in question and/or fraud is suspected.
      Completing the FormThe checklist is to be used and completed by customer service centre staff only and must not be given to the customer.

      Prior to asking the customer the questions on the CIC (F3985) the operator must read the 'Privacy Disclaimer' to the customer.
      Section 1
      Section 1
        The questions in Section 1 relate to verifying the customer's details from information known to TMR and recorded in TICA.
        • Not every question will apply to each situation
        • The customer must be able to correctly answer at least 6 out of 8 questions
        • Record the customer's response to each question in the space provided

        Authorisation officers may only be one of the following:
        • TMR CSC Senior Advisor (Service Management)
        • TMR CSC Manager
        • QGAP Manager
        • QPS Police Officer authorised to provide TMR services

        This includes officers relieving in the position.

      Section 2
      Section 2
        Section 2 of the form is to be completed by the operator prior to an authorised officer organising further investigation.

        Note: This section is not to be completed in the presence of the customer.

        Details to be recorded are:
        • Customer's description
        • Security camera film number
        • Customer Service officer's details, signature and date
        • Reason why fraud is suspected
        • Authorisation officer's details, signature and date (for a list of authorised officers refer to Section 1 above)
        • Investigation referral details
        • Additional information (details of conversation etc.)

        Where possible, attach a copy of the identity documents. If the documents are abandoned, attach them to this form and retain in the office for 6 months.
      Validity & Retention of formThe form is valid for one use only and must be:
      • if approved, filed with the application.
      • if NOT approved, retained in the office for 6 months

      Refer to the Customer Identity Checklist (F3985) form for further instructions.

        Verifying Persons

          • Have known the customer personally for at least 1 year
          • Not be related to the customer by birth, marriage or defacto relationship

          • Medical Practitioner, Specialist or Surgeon
          • Dentist or Orthodontist
          • Optometrist or Ophthalmologist
          • Veterinary Surgeon

          • Justice of the Peace or Commissioner for Declarations
          • Solicitor, Barrister, Judge, Queens Counsel or Senior Counsel
          • Police Officer or Police Liaison Officer with the Australian Federal Police or Queensland Police Service
          • Commissioned Officer of the Armed Services
          • Member of the State or Federal Parliament
          • Councillor of Local Government or Aboriginal & Islander Council
