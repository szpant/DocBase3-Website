Authorised Officers ID Card and Instrument of Appointment (7563)


Authorised Officers
What it isAuthorised Officers/Persons are granted powers under various legislation administered by Department of Transport and Main Roads, including:
  • Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 (Authorised Officer)
  • Tow Truck Act 1973 (Authorised Officer)
  • Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994 (Authorised Person)
  • Transport Infrastructure Act 1994 (Authorised Person)
  • Heavy Vehicle National Law Queensland (HVNL(Q)) (Authorised Person)

Transport Inspectors, Senior Transport Inspectors, Senior Investigators, Principal Investigators, Safety Examiners and Compliance Managers are required to be Authorised Officers.

The chief executive may appoint a person as an Authorised Officer only if satisfied the person has the necessary expertise to be an Authorised Officer.

Authorised Officers are issued a combined Government Employee/Authorised Officer Identification (ID) Card. The back of the card lists the relevant legislation they can exercise powers as an Authorised Officer. Authorised Officers can not exercise any power until they are issued with an Instrument of Appointment stipulating the powers they have been granted.
Authorised Officer procedure
Requirements
Eligibility
Eligibility
    Transport Inspectors, Senior Transport Inspectors, Senior Investigators, Principal Advisor (Investigations), Safety Examiner and Compliance Managers are required to be authorised officers. Compliance PLT approval must be sought before authorising any other officer.

    Compliance Administration officers and Customer Service officers should not be made authorised officers.
Training and experience
Training and experience
    To be eligible to become an Authorised Officer the applicant must have completed the Department of Transport and Main Roads induction course and relevant training courses including:
    • CSB Staff responsibilities
    • Code of Conduct and Ethics
    • Workplace Health and Safety
    • Privacy, confidentially and the appropriate use of information
    • Conduct Detailed Vehicle Inspection Training (CDVET)
    • Transport Inspector Recruit Course (TIRC)

    A person may be appointed as an Authorised Officer based on their previous employment history and appointed position. For example, Compliance Managers, Senior Investigators and Principal Advisor (Investigations) are not required to complete TIRC or CDVET but they are required to complete mandatory TMR induction training.

    If a Transport Inspector or Senior Transport Inspector has been absent from their substantive position for an extended period of time, they may be required at the discretion of a Manager (Compliance), to complete compliance related refresher training. This may include completing the entire TIRC course or selected modules.

    If there is no record of a criminal history check on SAP then a criminal history check must be completed and reviewed before submitting the Authorised person application to NHVR.
Powers
Powers
    • Customer Services Branch (CSB) staff other than Compliance may given limited power to issue an Extension of Time (EOT).
    • Transport Inspector recruits are given limited powers during the TIRC.
    • Safety Examiners are given limited powers.
    • Authorised officer's powers are limited by the instrument of appoint and this policy.
    • Authorised officers can only exercises powers relevant to the position they hold (appointed or acting).
    • If an authorised officer is acting in a higher position with additional powers they can exercise the additional powers if the powers are listed on their instrument of appointment. When they return to their substantive position they must only exercise the powers relevant to that position.

    Note:Safety Examiners are restricted to the limited powers in their instrument of appointment.

Allocating an Identification Number
Allocating an Identification Number
    Each Authorised Officer is issued a unique Identification (ID) number in the form QXXXX.
    An Authorised Officer ID number must only be used once. If an Authorised Officer ceases to work for TMR compliance their ID number cannot be reused.

    The Authorised Officer ID number register is maintained by Capability, Engagement and Learning (CEL).

    Managers Compliance and Senior Transport Inspectors may request the allocation of a new Authorised Officer ID number by emailing Compliance Support with the following:

    • Applicants full name (including middle name if applicable)
    • Date of birth
    • Position
    • Office location
    • Region

Reissuing Instrument of Appointment
Reissuing Instrument of Appointment
    The Authorised Officer Instrument of Appointment must be re-issued:
    • if legislative changes require,
    • before the instrument appointment expires.

    If a new Instrument of Appointment is issued it revokes all previous Instruments of Appointment made by the chief executive or his duly appointed delegate in relation to the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995, Tow Truck Act 1973, the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994, Transport Infrastructure Act 1994 and the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland).
Revocation of Authorised Officer Status
Revocation
Revocation
    Appointment as an Authorised Officer ceases when one of the following occurs:
    • The Authorised Officer no longer works in Compliance.
    • The Authorised Officer is given written advice their appointment has been revoked.
    • The Instrument of Appointment expires.

    Revocation Authorised Officer

    If an Authorised Officer leaves the Department or permanently moves to a role that does not require them to be an Authorised Officer the following action must be completed within 5 business days.
    Complete the Authorised Officer Report and email to NHVR-Authorisation Applications and Compliance Support so the authorised officers registers can be updated.

    Authorised Officer Report
Identification (ID) Cards
Identification (ID) Cards
Identification (ID) Cards
Identification (ID) Cards
    Protective Services will issue a combined Government Employee/Authorised Officers Identification Card for officers authorised under legislation administered by TMR and the National Heavy vehicle Regulator. This card will comply with both legislative requirements and corporate identity standards as required and set by Premier and Cabinet. The combined ID is to be issued on commencement of employment with Transport and Main Roads and will include the various legislation on the reverse of the card.

    It should be noted that an Authorised Officer can not exercise any powers until the Authorised Officer receives the Instrument of Appointment stipulating their powers as an Authorised Officer.

Issuing ID cards
Requirements
Requirements
    An ID card can be issued when:
    • an employee is appointed to a position as an Authorised Officer.
    • legislative changes occur that require a new card.
    • prior to the expiry of an existing card.
    • to replace a lost or stolen card.

    All Authorised Officer ID Cards have a common expiry which does not exceed five years from the date of issue. The ID cards and appointment letters must have the same expiry date (current expiry date 1 January 2022).
How to apply
How to apply
    To obtain an ID card complete an Identity Card Application (F3860).
    • All ID card applications can be signed by the Regional Director.
    • The application form is to be captured in the applicants personnel DMS file for audit purposes.
    • The applicant must sign the application form and provide the following:
      • A copy of their drivers licence.
      • An email from the Regional Compliance Manager with their appointed position and the ID number of the applicant.
      • A current digital photograph of their face from the shoulders up as per sample ID cards.

    Transport Inspectors, Senior Investigators, Principle Advisors (Investigations) and Compliance Managers are supplied with two ID cards. One must be displayed while on duty, the other may be kept secure in the badge wallet.

    To apply for an ID card contact an officer in your region with approval to request an ID card from Protective Services using the Qld Government ID Card Web System.

Inspector of Explosives
Inspector of Explosives
    Transport Inspectors may be appointed as an Inspector of Explosives (IE) under the Explosives Act 1999 if they have the Inspector of Explosives training from the Department of Natural Resources and Mines (DNRM).

    The Chief Inspector of Explosives, DNRM will issue an Instrument of Appointment as an IE, this must be provided before the Authorised Officers ID card can be updated to include IE.
Lost ID card
Lost ID card
    If an Authorised Officer requires a replacement ID card because it has been lost, they must provide a briefing note to their Senior Transport Inspector outlining the circumstances how they lost their ID card.

Reissue ID cards due to legislative change

Reissue ID cards due to expiry
Reissue ID cards due to expiry
    • One month prior to the expiry date of ID cards the STI must apply for replacement ID cards for their Authorised Officers.
    • The replacement ID cards must distributed prior to the expiry date of the old ID cards.
    • After the expiry date of the old ID cards the STI must:
      • collect all expired ID cards,
      • reconcile expired ID cards, if cards are unaccountable refer Lost ID card,
      • destroy expired ID cards by shredding.
