Stock Control Guide (7556)


Stock Control Guide
What it isTMR needs to ensure there is adequate control over all High Risk Stock.

Misuse of these items exposes TMR to criticism and potentially diminishes the reputation of, and public confidence in TMR.

A high level of control should be exercised over these items to ensure any discrepancies in stock on hand are promptly identified and resolved.
Types of Stock
High Risk Stock
High Risk Stock
    High Risk Stock includes:
    • Penalty Infringement Notices
    • Defect Notices
    • Prohibition Notices
    • Formal Warning Notices
    • Unregistered Vehicle Permits
    • PT Non Compliance Notices
Managing Stock
Managing Stock
High Risk Stock Register
High Risk Stock Register
    A High Risk Stock Register must be maintained by a Senior Transport Inspector within each Compliance office. The Stock Register must include the following details:
    • Date stock is received at the Office 
    • Date an item is assigned to a Transport Inspector 
    • Name of the Transport Inspector receiving the item 
    • Instances where stock is sent to other offices. 

    The following is an example of a suitable manual stock register that may be used:

    Accountable Stock Register Template

Storage of Stock
Storage of Stock
    All high risk stock should be stored in a secure environment within the office such as a locked cabinet.

    Stock must be protected from:
    • Unauthorised access and removal
    • Accidental loss
    • Physical deterioration

    Stocktakes
    A stocktake is required to identify any discrepancy in stock numbers held. Any identified discrepancy must be investigated and resolved promptly.

    Records of the stocktake and the results should be retained.

    Note:The counting and verifying/checking officer must sign and
    write their userid on all Stocktake Sheets.

    A stocktake must be conducted quarterly
Expired, Superseded or Obsolete Accountable Books
Expired, Superseded or Obsolete Accountable Books
    When offices have stock that is deemed unusable, either obsolete or superseded, the stock is to be:  
    • Written off in the office's Accountable Stock Register – noted as destroyed along with the date of destruction 
    • Securely destroyed as per secure destruction procedures (i.e. via the use of the secure document destruction bins that are currently in used within TMR)
