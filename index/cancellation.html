Cancellation (3068)


Automatic Cancellation
Automatic Cancellation
    What it isAutomatic cancellation occurs when a registration payment is not received within a specified time frame.

    A voluntary cancellation application may be made in writing or via email. All requirements must be met.
    Reasons for Cancellation
    Unpaid Registration
    Unpaid Registration
      TMR will not accept a registration renewal payment received more than 3 months after the expiry date. The registration will be cancelled through a batch process. This delay caters for some late payments and enables payment from our off-line agencies to be remitted and processed.

      The exception to this rule is Sunday night and Public Holidays as the auto cancel batch process does not run on these days.

      If the date the vehicle is due to be auto cancelled falls on a Sunday or Public holiday the auto cancellation is delayed by a day.
    Seasonal Registration
    Seasonal Registration
      Seasonal registration is automatically cancelled if the vehicle has been dormant for 12 months.

      Note:If registered in a drought declared area the dormant period may be extended.
    Cancelled by the Chief Executive
    Cancelled by the Chief Executive
      Defect Notice
      Defect Notice
        When a defect notice has been issued and:
        • Has not been complied with within the allowed 14 days
        or
        • No cancellation application has been made

        a warning notice is issued advising of the automatic cancellation of the vehicle if no action is taken within 14 days of the warning notice issue date. At the end of the 14 days the vehicle will be automatically cancelled if no action has been taken.

      Dishonoured Payments
      Dishonoured Payments
        Contact Customer Account Management (CAM) for dishonoured cheque, dishonoured direct debit or dishonoured credit card payments Refer: Admin 3. Money > Account Management Guide > Dishonoured Payments
          Review of Decision
          Review of Decision
          The registered operator can apply for a review of the decision to cancel within 28 days of receiving the letter. The vehicle must not be driven on the road during this period. If the review of decision is unsuccessful, the customer may appeal to the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT).
      Written-Off Vehicles
      Written-Off Vehicles
        When a vehicle is:
        • Written off by an insurance agency/external body
          and
        • Being kept by the registered operator

        The Written off Vehicle Register (WOVR) will generate a Written-off Vehicle Notice (F4056) to the registered operator advising that if they cannot provide sufficient grounds for not cancelling, the registration will be automatically cancelled in 28 days. Cancellation will show as NCIR.

        If after a set period (calculated by TICA) and considering any written response from the registered operator (if any response was received), it is decided there is still a reason to cancel the registration, the WOVR will generate a Decision Notice (F4984/F4983). This letter will notify the registered operator that the decision to cancel the registration has been made and provide the date when TMR will cancel the registration. Cancellation will show as SHOC.
    Offensive Advertising
    Offensive Advertising
      Where an Offensive Advertising restriction has been placed on a vehicle record, the Chief Executive may cancel the vehicle for the reason of Offensive Advertising (OFAD) unless TMR receives advice from the ASB that it has withdrawn its advertising breach notice.

      The cancellation date must be at least 14 days after the registration cancellation notice is given to the registered operator. As refund requirements cannot be met, the customer is NOT entitled to a refund of the remaining portion of the registration fees.


      Note:Customer may contact their CTP insurer to obtain a refund of the remaining portion of the CTP fees.
    Abandoned and Forfeited Vehicles
    Abandoned and Forfeited Vehicles
      Abandoned and Forfeited vehicles can be cancelled at the request of local Government or the Queensland Police Service.

      Local Government may cancel the registration of a vehicle where it considers on reasonable grounds that the vehicle has been abandoned. The Queensland Police Service (QPS) may cancel the registration of a vehicle that has been forfeited to the State.

      Abandoned and forfeited vehicles must be cancelled using the ‘abandoned/forfeited' cancellation reason, with the date of cancellation being the date that the application is received by TMR.

      A Vehicle/Queensland Regulated Ship Cancellation of Registration Application (F3517) form is not required, however the Local Government or QPS representative must complete and lodge the applicable Letter of Authority (template below), and present official council or QPS identification

      Council Abandoned Vehicle under Act - November 2018
      Council Abandoned Vehicle under Local Law - November 2018
      QPS Cancellation Form

      The Letter of Authority details the relevant legislation under which vehicle registration can be cancelled.
    Cancellation Requirements
    Notification of Cancellation
    Notification of Cancellation
      An Overdue Registration Fees Final Notice is sent 15 days after the expiry date advising of TMR's intention to cancel the vehicle registration if payment is not received by the date specified in the letter.
    Return of Plate
    Return of Plate
      Standard plates must be surrendered within 14 days of the Automatic Cancellation.

      There is no requirement to surrender:
      • Customised plates
      • Personalised plates; or
      • Plates confiscated by QPS as a result of a Number Plate Confiscation Notice (QPS will surrender the plate directly to TMR)

      Exceptions (applicant eligible for refund):
      • If plates are lost or stolen registered operator must provide a signed statement outlining the circumstances.

      If a heavy vehicle has a current TIST restriction and the NHVNP are in a status of "Interstate" it is essential the plates are not surrendered to allow their reuse.
    Reinstatement of Registration
    Reinstating (not invalidating) a Registration after it has been auto-cancelled
    Reinstating (not invalidating) a Registration after it has been auto-cancelled
      Reinstating (not invalidating) a registration after it has been auto-cancelled may be done under in the following circumstances:
      • Request from an executor of a Deceased Estate
      • Personal Extenuating Circumstances
      • Registration renewal notice was not generated as the vehicle was recorded as stolen The stolen restriction must be removed and the RRRF Exempted
      • Approved by TMR prosecutions following a court order.

      Reinstatement of registration will return the vehicle into an expired status, and the renewal and RRRF fees will be raised.

      The reinstatement date must be the date the request was approved.

      The effective date of the new registration period will be backdated to the day after the expiry of the previous registration period.
    Invalidation of Auto Cancellation
    Invalidation of Auto Cancellation
      An auto cancellation should only be invalidated and reprocessed due to a departmental error where the:
      • Customer had paid (all required fees) prior to the auto cancel date but the renewal was not processed in the system
      • Customers address was updated incorrectly through the Postal Address File (PAF)

      The reinstatement should be invalidated and the renewal backdated to the day the payment was received.
Voluntary Cancellation
Voluntary Cancellation
    What it isVoluntary cancellation is the cancellation of a vehicles registration at the registered operator request.
    Reasons for Cancellation
    Owners Request
    Owners Request
      Current Registration
      Current Registration
        This is the cancellation of a vehicle's registration at the request of a registered operator (Primary or Secondary) with no specific reason given. Cancellation date is the date the cancellation application form is received, irrespective of when the vehicle was last used on the road.

        If the vehicle is enrolled in Direct Debit, it is the registered operators responsibility to ensure that the registration is removed from the scheme prior to the cancellation.

        Registered operators can remove the vehicle from Direct Debit via the My Registrations portal page on the TMR website. If the vehicle is not removed from the Direct Debit, it will be automatically removed within 24 hours of the cancellation being processed.

        Note:Where the registered operator has sold the vehicle but does not have Part B Notice of Disposal, safety certificate and plates, no action can be taken until the vehicle registration has expired. See expired registration below.

        Cancellation of a Motorised Wheelchair
        Cancellation of a Motorised Wheelchair
          If a motorbike number plate is attached to a motorised wheelchair and the customer wishes to cancel the registration it can be done without replacing the motorbike number plate with a motorised wheelchair plate.
        Cancellation of Dealer/Trade Plate
        Cancellation of Dealer/Trade Plate
          For full requirements to issue refer Dealer/Trade Plates Transaction Requirement Checklist
        Cancellation of Consular Registration/Plates
        Cancellation of Consular Registration/Plates
          When a consular concessional registration is cancelled, the consular plates must be surrendered and a copy of the cancellation application scanned and emailed to Brisbane City CSC at consular_plates@tmr.qld.gov.au with subject line 'TMR Consular Cancellation'

      Expired Registration
      Expired Registration
        Where new business to be processed
        Where new business to be processed
          If all of following apply:
          • The registration is expired but not automatically cancelled
          • New business requirements have been met
          • Number plates have been surrendered

          The CSO can cancel the registration with no further documentation required. The TICA cancellation reason "Re-registered" can be applied to finalise the existing record and vehicle registration application can be processed. Note on the Vehicle Registration application (if application is not processed at a formless centre) that the previous registration was expired and has been cancelled for re-registration.

          Refer: Vehicle Rego > 06. New Business > New Business > Registration Expired/Current at Time of Application > Expired

          If the vehicle has an electronic certificate issued from ICO prior to the cancellation date, it will automatically cancel when the registration is cancelled. Contact Help Direct by phone for advice.

          Note:If the plates cannot be surrendered a declaration explaining the reason why must be completed. The declaration can be in any format provided it outlines why the plates cannot be surrendered.

        Where vehicle sold but no notice of disposal
        Where vehicle sold but no notice of disposal
          Where the registered operator has sold the vehicle but does not have Part B Notice of Disposal, safety certificate and plates, the registered operator can cancel the vehicle registration if all of the following are completed:
          • a cancellation form F3517
          • a statutory declaration form providing as much information as possible about the transaction

      Note:Vehicles that have been written off must be recorded as WRECKED/ DESTROYED.

      Refer below: Written-Off after Accident or Written-off due to natural disaster/major weather event
    Dealer Sale
    Dealer Sale
      If a vehicle is to be sold to a motor vehicle dealer (current or expired), and the dealer is cancelling immediately, the cancellation date is
      • the date the application is received by the CSC, when submitted by a dealer not on the Rego Easy Scheme.
      OR
      • the date on the application form when submitted by a dealer on the Rego Easy Scheme


      TICA Code: DSAL

      Note:When the registration is current the Dealer must provide an acquisition date prior/equal to the date of cancellation.
    Re-registered Interstate
    Re-registered Interstate
      When a vehicle is re-registered interstate:
      • The cancellation date is the day after the vehicle has been re-registered interstate
      • The re-registration date is to be confirmed through the Interstate Authority or NEVDIS.
      • The registered operator may produce a copy of the plate surrender receipt from an interstate jurisdiction or where the CSO can confirm that plates have been surrendered on TICA a plate receipt is not required*.

      Note:*Personalised/customised plates do not have to be surrendered.

      NHVNPs do not have to be surrendered when the vehicle has already been re-registered using that plate in another jurisdiction.

      A plate surrender receipt does not prove the vehicle has been re-registered interstate and cannot be used to determine the cancellation date for the purpose of re-registration interstate.

      For a current registration, the cancellation application can be made by the registered operator only.
    Stolen Vehicles
    Stolen Vehicles
      Stolen and not recovered (Qld and Interstate)
      Stolen and not recovered (Qld and Interstate)

        The registration can only be cancelled 31 days after the date of theft. This is to allow time for the vehicle/ship to be recovered. The registered operator must provide confirmation that the theft has been reported to police by having a Police Officer (Qld or Interstate) complete the required section in question five of the Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation Application (F3517).

        Cancellation date will be backdated to the day after the theft was reported to the Police.

        TICA Code: STOLEN

        For Qld Registered vehicles stolen interstate, a faxed copy of F3517 is acceptable provided all other requirements are met.

        As no stolen restriction is recorded on NEVDIS; the vehicle is cancelled with the reason of OREQ and text recorded.

      Stolen and recovered (Qld and Interstate)
      Stolen and recovered (Qld and Interstate)
        The registered operator must provide confirmation that the theft has been reported to police by having a Police Officer (Qld or Interstate) complete the required section in question five of the Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation Application (F3517).

        Cancellation date will be backdated to the day after the theft was reported to the Police.

        Note:For vehicles stolen interstate the above requirements are to be met. A faxed copy of Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation Application (F3517) from Interstate may be accepted. Cancellation reason of Owners Request is used. As no stolen restriction is added in TICA, text must be placed against the vehicle stating the reason for the cancellation.

        TICA Code: OWNERS REQUEST

      Written Off while Stolen (Qld and Interstate)
      Written Off while Stolen (Qld and Interstate)
        Proof of the theft being reported to the Police must be provided. Cancellation date can then be backdated to the day after the theft is reported.

        If the vehicle has not been assessed by an insurer, the write-off details must be recorded in TICA and text recorded stating the reason for the cancellation.

        TICA Code:WRECKED/DESTROYED (if vehicle recovered in TICA)
        STOLEN (if vehicle not recovered in TICA)

    Written-Off After Accident
    Written-Off After Accident
      The registered operator must provide the following:
      • Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation of Registration Application (F3517) (which includes a statement (Question 7) by the registered operator on the reverse side of the form stating that the vehicle has not been used on a road since the date of the accident or the write-off date) or from when it was declared a write-off by the insurance company.
      • Written-off Vehicle Notification (Individual) (F4069) (only complete if the vehicle is a notifiable vehicle for the written-off vehicle register and the write-off details are not recorded in TICA).
      • Standard EOI
      • The number plates or a statement detailing the status/location of the number plates. Refer below: Unable to Surrender Number Plates

      Evidence of the accident date (Cancellation/Written-off Date)
      Evidence of the accident date (Cancellation/Written-off Date)
        Can be obtained from:
          • the written-off date displayed in TICA, OR
          • a letter from the insurer outlining the date of the accident and the details of the vehicle involved in the accident (rego number or VIN/Chassis number), OR
          • a Police report (date of accident and details of the vehicle involved in the accident)
        Note:A towing authority receipt is not suitable evidence on its own but may support the insurers letter, police report or form F4069 to establish the accident date.


      If the written-off details are not recorded in TICA a copy of all forms and supporting documentation must be emailed to the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU) for investigation.

      Cancelling the vehicle
      Cancelling the vehicle
        Vehicles not yet 16 years old are required to be recorded on the Written Off Vehicle Register (WOVR).

        Written-off Vehicle Register

        Once cancelled as a written-off vehicle, the customer must be issued with the relevant Write-off label (Repairable or Statutory) and instructed that it is a legal requirement that the label be attached to a prominent position on the vehicle (eg clearly visible from the outside of the vehicle according to the instructions on the reverse side of the sticker)

        Vehicles that are at least 16 years old and all conditionally registered vehicles are not captured on the WOVR therefore no damage information is required to be captured.

      Cancellation Date
      Cancellation Date
        If the registered operator complies with the above requirements (being evidence of the accident date, evidence of the vehicle's status as written off and completion of the statement), the cancellation can be backdated to the day after the accident.

        Note:Form F4069 cannot be used on its own to backdate the cancellation date. Proof of write-off date (as listed above) must also be provided. If a towing authority receipt is used to support form F4069, it must contain date of accident and details of the vehicle involved in the accident.

        If any of the above requirements cannot be met, cancellation may only be from the date the plates are surrendered. If unable to surrender plates, for reasons lost, stolen or destroyed, declaration on back of cancellation form must be completed. Cancellation date is the date the cancellation application is processed.

        However, if the insurance company letter states that they have taken possession of the number plates the cancellation may still be backdated to the day after the accident provided all other requirements are met.

        Note: When a vehicle has been acquired by an Auction House after an accident and the Auction House applies to cancel the vehicle, the registration is to be cancelled from the date of acquisition, not from the day after the date of the accident.

        TICA Code: WRECKED/DESTROYED
    Written-off due to natural disaster/major weather event
    Written-off due to natural disaster/major weather event
      The registered operator must provide the following:
      • Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation of Registration Application (F3517) which includes a statement (Question 7) by the registered operator on the reverse side of the form stating that the vehicle has not been used on a road since the date of the disaster or from when it was declared a write-off by the insurance company (*for a major weather event).
      • Written-off Vehicle Notification (Individual) (F4069) (only complete if the vehicle is a notifiable vehicle for the written-off vehicle register and the write-off details are not recorded on TICA).
      • The number plates or a statement detailing the status/location of the number plates Refer below: Unable to Surrender Number Plates
      Evidence of the accident date (Cancellation/Written-off Date)
      Evidence of the accident date (Cancellation/Written-off Date)
        Can be obtained from:
        • the written-off date displayed in TICA, OR
        • a letter from the insurer outlining the date the vehicle was written-off by the insurance company and the details of the vehicle involved in the disaster or event (rego number or VIN/Chassis number)
        Note:A towing authority receipt is not suitable evidence on its own but may support the insurers letter or form F4069 to establish the natural disaster/major weather event date.


      If the written-off details are not recorded in TICA a copy of all forms and supporting documentation must be emailed to the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU) for investigation.

      Note:*When a major weather event has occurred (e.g. hail storm), the assessment of vehicles can take months to complete. Therefore, the vehicle may be driven (providing it is safe and roadworthy) until the time it is declared a write off by the insurer.

      Cancelling the vehicle
      Cancelling the vehicle
        Vehicles not yet 16 years old are required to be recorded on the Written Off Vehicle Register (WOVR).

        Written-off Vehicle Register

      Cancellation date
      Cancellation date
        If the registered operator complies with the above requirements (being evidence of the vehicle's status as written off and completion of the statement), the cancellation can be backdated to the day after the vehicle was written off in the disaster, or for major weather event, the date after it was deemed a write off by the insurance company.


        Note:Form F4069 cannot be used on its own to backdate the cancellation date. Proof of write-off date (as listed above) must also be provided. If a towing authority receipt is used to support form F4069, it must contain date of accident and details of the vehicle involved in the accident.


        If any of the above requirements cannot be met, cancellation may only be from the date the plates are surrendered. If unable to surrender plates, for reasons lost, stolen or destroyed, declaration on back of cancellation form must be completed. Cancellation date is the date the cancellation application is processed.

        However, if the insurance company letter states that they have taken possession of the number plates the cancellation can still be backdated to the day after the vehicle was written off in the disaster, or for major weather event, the day after it was written of by the insurance company provided all other requirements are met.

        Note:When a vehicle has been acquired by an Auction House after a disaster/major weather event and the Auction House applies to cancel the vehicle, the registration is to be cancelled from the date of acquisition, not from the day after the date of the accident

        TICA Code: WRECKED/DESTROYED
    Written-Off due to Takata Airbag Recall
    Written-Off due to Takata Airbag Recall
      If a vehicle has been recalled by the manufacturer due to a faulty Takata airbag that is unable to be replaced, the manufacturer/dealership may choose to buy-back the un-repairable vehicle. If this occurs, the registration should be cancelled using the below process:
      • Customer is to:
        • obtain a letter/email from manufacturer/dealership confirming the vehicle is deemed un-repairable and include a date of when the vehicle was towed/assessed (similar to insurance letters for Written off vehicle)
        • complete Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation Application (F3517) and tick the 'written off by accident or event' box
        • surrender the number plates or provide a statement detailing the status/location of the number platesRefer below:Unable to Surrender Number Plates
      • CSC/Longreach are to backdate the cancellation to the day after the date provided on the letter by the dealership and add the following text restriction to the vehicle:
        "Vehicle has been bought back by manufacturer/dealership due to vehicle being deemed un-repairable"
      • CSC/Longreach to send email notification to VIU
      • VIU will add a Written Off restriction for vehicles eligible for WOVR (<16 years old)
    Deceased Registered Operator
    Deceased Registered Operator
      Where an executor/administrator or next of kin wishes to cancel a registration, the refund will be issued to the Estate of Deceased unless the executor, administrator or next of kin nominated for the cheque to be issued to another person on the F5296 form.

      Where another person is nominated to receive the cheque, the transfer requirements are exempt, and question 4 (transfer of registration) of the Application to Transact with Registration Products on behalf of a Deceased Person (F5296) is not required to be completed. This is because the transfer is only being processed to facilitate the issue of the pro-rata refund to another person, and is purely an administrative requirement.

      Transfer details:
        Duty exemption reason = Transfer to Estate
        Inspection exemption reason = Deceased

      Once the transfer has been completed, the cancellation is considered to be at 'owners request'.

      If the third party does not have full EOI recorded in TICA, the transfer cannot be processed. In this scenario, operators can create a SKEL customer record and complete an adjustment to enable a refund to the third party.


      Cancellation date is the date the cancellation application form is received, irrespective of when the vehicle was last used on the road. All other requirements must be met eg surrender of number plates.

      Note:30 Day Survival Period applies unless a Will specifically states that the Beneficiary is not required to survive the deceased by 30 days.

      If the executor, administrator, or next of kin are in a remote area, interstate or overseas, the request can be lodged by mail to Registration Cancellation. All requirements (including plate return) apply. Where the requirement is an original document, a certified copy is acceptable.

      Direct Debit
      Direct Debit
        If the deceased registered operator had a vehicle enrolled in Direct Debit TMR will cancel the Direct Debit request within 24 hours of the customer being recorded as deceased.

        An email will be sent to the email address recorded on TICA advising of the Direct Debit cancellation.



      TICA Code: OWNERS REQUEST

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 9. Cancellation > The How to Cancellation
    Offensive Advertising
    Offensive Advertising
      Where an Offensive Advertising restriction has been placed on a vehicle record, the vehicle MUST be cancelled for the reason of Offensive Advertising (OFAD) only.

      As refund requirements have not been met, the customer is NOT entitled to a refund of the remaining portion of the registration fees.

      Note:Customer may contact their CTP insurer to obtain a refund of the remaining portion of the CTP fees.

    Personalised/Customised Plates
    Personalised/Customised Plates

    Backdating the date of cancellation
    Backdating the date of cancellation
      The cancellation date can only be backdated where the cancellation is requested for:
      • a stolen vehicle which is or is not recovered or is written-off while stolen;
      • a vehicle which is re-registered either in Queensland or Interstate; or
      • a vehicle which has been declared a written-off vehicle as a result of an accident /Takata airbag recall.

      Note:The above applies to all written off vehicles not only notifiable vehicles required to be recorded on the Written Off Vehicle Register

      Note:If the dealer is a TMR agent the cancellation date is the date the application was received by the dealer.


      Strict requirements must be met before the cancellation date can be backdated. Further detail (i.e necessary documentation) is contained in the relevant 'Reason for Cancellation' section above.

      A plate surrender receipt (from any transport authority, including TMR) cannot be used to determine the cancellation date. A plate surrender receipt is only used to confirm that plates have been surrendered.
    Number Plate Confiscation (CONF) Restriction
    Number Plate Confiscation (CONF) Restriction
      • The vehicle cannot be transferred or cancelled.
      • Once the CONF period is over, the vehicle cannot be driven until the plates are retrieved from QPS and re-displayed on the vehicle.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Vehicle Restrictions or Text
    Reinstatement
    TMR Error
    TMR Error
      If the incorrect vehicle has been cancelled as a result of operator error the cancellation should be invalidated the reinstatement command is not to be used.
    Non-Delivery (if vehicle to be cancelled)
    Non-Delivery (if vehicle to be cancelled)
      Motor Vehicle Dealers
      Motor Vehicle Dealers
        If a non-delivery is not forwarded for invalidation by the close of business the next working day after the new business/transfer the dealer must:
        • Acquire the vehicle into their own name
        • Cancel the registration
        • Apply for a refund of duty
        • Provide statement:
        • Outlining non-delivery circumstance
        • The vehicle has not left the dealership

        The vehicle must not have left the dealership to be eligible for a refund of duty. The cancellation date must be after the original transaction date.

      Non-motor Dealers
      Non-motor Dealers
        If the vehicle has been registered into the purchasers name but there is a non-delivery the disposer must provide:
        • Transfer application
        • Statement regarding the circumstances of non-delivery
        • Cancellation application

      The transfer into the dealer/disposers name is:
      • Safety Certificate Exempt
      • Duty Exempt

      The vehicle is transferred into the disposers name and then cancelled. The transfer date can be the day of the original transaction.

      TICA Code: NON DELIVERY

      Cancellation Date
      Cancellation Date
        Cancellation date can only be after the original transaction for a new business transaction. For transfer transactions the cancellation date will be the date the plates are surrendered.

Refunds
Refunds
    What it isFor a voluntarily cancelled vehicle the refund of registration fees and CTP insurance is for the unexpired portion of the current registration. Full requirements must be met. Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Payment > Refunds
    What is refunded
    Cancelled after Registration Period Starts
    Cancelled after Registration Period Starts
      For a currently registered vehicle the refund is calculated on a daily basis and includes the pro-rata of the:
      • unexpired portion of the registration fee
      • unused CTP insurance Fees

      All other fees and levies (including NIISQ levy) included in the registration are not refundable.
    Cancellation before registration period starts
    Cancellation before registration period starts
      If a renewal payment has been made and the new registration period has not yet commenced. The full amount of the payment will be refunded upon the cancellation of the registration (administration fee is exempted).
    Refund Components
    Refund Components
      Registration Fees
      Registration Fees
        Calculation is from the day after the cancellation of the registration.

      CTP Insurance
      CTP Insurance
        The minimum amount of CTP insurance refunded is $10.00. Refunds are calculated on a daily basis and are refunded from the day after the cancellation of the registration. Refunds are made by TMR on behalf the insurer.

        CTP Levies (including NIISQ Levy) are not refundable. A list of non-refunded CTP levies can be found at CTP Fee Components. Customers are encouraged to contact their CTP insurer for more information.

      Cancellation Fee
      Cancellation Fee
        A cancellation fee is charged to cover the cost of processing the registration refund, it is deducted from the registration refund amount only.

        Cancellation Fee Exemptions:
        • When a renewal payment has been made and the new registration period has not commenced
        • Where only CTP insurance is being refunded
    Existing Debt in Registered Operators account
    Existing Debt in Registered Operators account
      The amount of the refund will be reduced by any existing debit in the registered operators account.

    Abandoned and Forfeited Vehicles
    Abandoned and Forfeited Vehicles
      The registered operator recorded on an abandoned vehicle or a vehicle that has been forfeited to the state immediately prior to the registration being cancelled will be entitled to a refund of the unused portion of the registration fee, refunds should be issued in line with the current Account Management process.
    Payment of RefundThe customers account will be credited with the refund amount.

    The amount can be refunded by EFTPOS/Credit card or cheque.

    EFTPOS/Credit Card refunds
    EFTPOS/Credit Card refunds
      EFTPOS/Credit Card refund requirements
      EFTPOS/Credit Card refund requirements
        To receive a refund for a cancelled registration by EFTPOS/Credit card the following requirements must be met:
        • The transaction must have been processed at a centre that is approved to issue registration cancellation or concession pro-rata refunds by EFTPOS/Credit Card*
        • An electronic refund can only be granted to individual registered operators **
        • for joint registered operators with no concession applied the refund can be refunded to the primary or secondary registered operator***
        • for joint registered vehicles with a concession the refund can only be issued to the primary registered operator.
        • The registered operator must present photo ID
        • The EFTPOS/Credit Card must have the customer name and financial institution displayed. If these details are not displayed the customer must supply a confirmation letter from the bank.
        • Refunds cannot be split over more than one card*
        • EFTPOS/Credit card refunds cannot be issued to deceased estates
        *Note:





        **Note:        		If you are unsure whether you centre is approved to process these refunds please consult with your SA(SM) or view the List of authorised CSC/Agencies.
        Refer: Admin > 3.Money > Administration Money Guide > Refunds > Electronic Refunds (EFTPOS/Credit Card) > List of authorised Customer Service Centres/ Agencies for electronic refunds
        THE CBA enforces that EFTPOS/Credit card refunds will only be issued to the registered operator. The value of the refund may only be returned to one card and not be split over multiples cards.
        ***Note:Create Adjustment from primary to secondary registered operator

    Refunds by Cheque
    Refunds by Cheque
      A cheque should be generated the same day the Business Exception is authorised. The cheque will take at least 15 working days to reach the recipient.

      For an individual the refund should be sent to the registered operator, or paying agent, at the time of cancellation. For an organisation it can be mailed to the registered operator, paying agent or left as a credit in the account.
    Plates surrendered interstate
    Plates surrendered interstate

      The customer is able to obtain a refund if they produce a surrender receipt or the plates have been surrendered on TICA.

      Note:Personalised/Customised Plates do not have to be surrendered
    Deceased EstatesCancellations will be refunded to the "Estate of <Deceased name>", beneficiary or non-beneficiary (depending on who the vehicle was transferred to prior to being cancelled).

    The CSO must advise the representative who the cheque will be issued to prior to processing the refund.

    Refunds for deceased estates must be issued by cheque.

    Direct Debit
    Direct Debit
      If the deceased registered operator had a vehicle enrolled in Direct Debit TMR will cancel the Direct Debit request within 24 hours of the customer being recorded as deceased.

      An email will be sent to the email address recorded on TICA advising of the Direct Debit cancellation.

      If the vehicle registration is subsequently cancelled, any refund due will be issued by cheque.
