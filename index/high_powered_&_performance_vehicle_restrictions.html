Definition of High Powered Vehicle
Definition of High Powered Vehicle
    For vehicles manufactured on or after 1 January 2010 a high-powered vehicle is a car that has at least one of the following:
    • a modification to the engine of any kind that must be approved under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010, section 13
    • a power-to-weight ratio of more than 130kW/tonne

    The power-to-weight ratio (expressed in kilowatts per tonne), is calculated by dividing the car’s maximum engine power in kilowatts by the tare weight (in Kilograms) and multiplying the product of the division by 1000.

    For example, the power-to-weight ratio of a vehicle that has a maximum engine power of 195kW and a tare weight of 1667kg would be calculated as follows: (195 ÷ 1667) x 1000 = 117kW/tonne.

    For vehicles manufactured before 1 January 2010, a high-powered vehicle is a car that has at least one of the following:
    • 8 or more cylinders
    • a turbocharged engine that is not diesel powered
    • a supercharged engine that is not diesel powered
    • an engine that has a power output of more than 210kW under the manufacturer's specifications for the car
    • a rotary engine that has an engine capacity of more than 1146cc under the manufacturer's specifications for the car
    • a modification to the engine of a kind that must be approved under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010, section 13

Certificate of Exemption - High Powered & Performance Vehicles
Certificate of Exemption - High Powered & Performance Vehicles

What it isA Certificate of Exemption for Driving a High-Powered Vehicle allows a person ordinarily restricted from driving high powered vehicles, to drive a high-powered vehicle.
Who may ApplyA person subject to the high powered vehicle restrictions under section 70 of the TO(RUM-DL) Regulation 2010.
Eligibility
Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility Criteria
    A person may be eligible for a certificate of exemption if one of the following circumstances applies.
    Personal Circumstances Exemption
    Personal Circumstances Exemption
      TMR will approve the grant of a certificate of exemption if satisfied that:

      The person needs to drive the high-powered vehicle:
      • To or from, the person’s place of employment
        or
      • In the course of the person’s employment
        or
      • To or from, an educational institution that the person attends
        or
      • To get medical treatment for the person or a member of the person’s family
        and
        • There is no other transport reasonably available to the person
          and
        • Refusal to grant the certificate of exemption would cause severe hardship
          and
        • The person has an acceptable traffic history

      Definition of "severe hardship'
      Definition of "severe hardship'
        "Severe hardship" means severe hardship suffered by the person, or the person's family because they have taken on a role of special responsibility in relation to their family.

    Vehicle Ownership Exemption
    Vehicle Ownership Exemption
      TMR will approve the grant of a certificate of exemption if satisfied that:

      The only car reasonably available to be driven by the person is a high-powered vehicle:
      • the car was manufactured before 1 January 2010, you owned the car on 30 June 2007 and have continuously owned it since that date
      • the car was manufactured on or after 1 January 2010, you owned the car on 1 January 2014 and have continuously owned it since that date
      • the car is owned or leased by an immediate family member
      • the car is in the possession of an immediate family member provided under an employment or salary package
        and
      • There is no other transport reasonably available to the person
        and
      • Refusal to grant the certificate of exemption would cause severe hardship
        and
      • The person has an acceptable traffic history

      Definition of "severe hardship'
      Definition of "severe hardship'
        "Severe hardship" means severe hardship suffered by the person, or the person's family because they have taken on a role of special responsibility in relation to their family.

    Moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle Exemption
    Moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle Exemption
      TMR will approve the grant of a certificate of exemption if satisfied that the vehicle was manufactured before 1 January 2010 and meets all the following:
      • has a turbocharged or supercharged engine that is not diesel powered
        and
      • has a power-to-weight ratio of not more than 125kW/t
        and
      • does not have any other features of a high-powered vehicle as defined above.
    TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s71(6)

      Note: To check if a vehicle is eligible for this type of exemption visit TMR's website at www.tmr.qld.gov.au/hpv

      Note: There is no requirement to prove that refusal to grant an exemption would cause severe hardship, nor is there a requirement to consider the person's traffic history when applying for an exemption to drive a moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle.

    Business Exemption
    Business Exemption
      TMR will approve the grant of a business certificate of exemption if satisfied that:
      • the person/company operates a business; and
      • driving HPVs is necessary to the operation of the business, for example the business provides a service that directly relates to HPVs; and
      • the business' employees are required to drive HPVs as part of their normal duties; and
      • driving HPVs is an essential component of employees’ tasks and the tasks cannot be undertaken in a vehicle that is not a HPV.

      Note: To check if a vehicle is eligible for this type of exemption visit TMR's website at www.tmr.qld.gov.au/hpv

      Note: There is no requirement to prove that refusal to grant an exemption would cause severe hardship, nor is there a requirement to consider the person's traffic history when applying for an exemption to drive a moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle.

Applicants for Vehicle Ownership Exemptions
    A person who is applying because the only vehicle reasonably available to them is a high-powered vehicle must provide the following to support their application:
    • Proof that they own the vehicle and have owned the vehicle since 30 June 2007.

Applicants for Moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle Exemption
Applicants for Moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle Exemption
    A person who wishes to drive a moderately-powered turbocharged or supercharged vehicle must provide the following to support their application:
      • A copy of a document published by the vehicle's manufacturer showing the vehicle's kerb weight and maximum engine power.
        For example: an owner's manual, a letter or email on the manufacturer's letterhead, or information provided on the manufacturer's website: or
      • If they cannot provide a document published by the vehicle's manufacturer, a specification information test report.

      A specification information test report is documentation showing the results of test(s) conducted on the vehicle to work out the specification information. The document must contain:
      • the name and address of the organisation(s) that conducted the test(s);
        and
      • the date of the test(s);
        and
      • vehicle particulars (make, model, VIN/chassis no.)
      • the vehicle's kerb weight and maximum engine power;
        and
      • details about the measuring system used to conduct the test.
        For example: an engine dynamometer

      The specification information test report may be issued by any automotive service provider that has the equipment to measure a vehicle's maximum engine power and/or kerb weight.

      Note: Customers should be encouraged to obtain a document published by the manufacturer showing the vehicle's specification in the first instance. This is due to the cost in obtaining a specification information test report.

      Where a test report is the only option, customers should be advised that their vehicle's maximum engine power is required. Generally, an engine dynamometer (as opposed to a chassis dynamometer) is used to measure maximum engine power.

      A chassis dynamometer only measures power at the vehicle's wheels, which will not meet legislative requirements for an application.

      Note: Information provided on Redbook is only a guide and cannot be accepted as part of the application.
