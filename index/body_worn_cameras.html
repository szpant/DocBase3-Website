Body Worn Cameras (7528)

Body Worn Cameras
What it isTMR provides Body Worn Cameras (BWC) to CSB Officers Authorised by the following legislation:
  • Transport Infrastructure Act
  • Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994
  • Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995
  • Heavy Vehicle National Law

Authorised Officers are authorised to capture, access and update video and audio while performing a function or activity related to their role.
When to operate a BWC
When to operate a BWC
When to operate a BWC
    An Authorised Officer must activate their BWC when:
    • they intend to exercise a power, such as:
      • stopping a vehicle
      • requesting name and address
      • conducting an audit
      • recording an interview
      • the recording may assist the investigation of an offence by providing a record of evidence
      • directed to do so by QPS.

    The BWC may be used when an incident occurs and it is likely that QPS will become involved, such as a major traffic incident.

    When two Transport Inspectors are attending an event that requires the BWC to be operated then both Inspectors must operate their BWCs.

    BWC are not to be used for non-compliance activities including:
    • routine contact with members of the public;
      or
    • to record interactions with or between peers;
      or
    • within the office environment;
      or
    • for private use.
Using with an Official Notebook and QCIS
Using with an Official Notebook and QCIS
    Using a BWC does not change the requirement to record pertinent information or evidence in your Official Notebook. The BWC is another evidence gathering tool that supplements the Official Notebook and QCIS.

    QCIS must be used to record all intercepts.
Actions that must be reported
Actions that must be reported
    The following actions should be reported to your STI or Manager as soon as possible:
    • An interaction that should be recorded is not recorded.
    • An interaction is recorded and uploaded to Evidence.com in error.
    • Any breach or potential breach of the Body Worn Camera and Recordings policy.
    • An incident is recorded because QPS involvement is likely.

    The Authorised Officer must record the circumstances in their Official Notebook and advise their STI\Manager.

    STI actions
    STI actions
      Failure to recordFor the first occurrence have an informal discussion with the TI and send follow-up email.

      For subsequent instances:
      • Complete a file note and record on TI's personnel file.
      • Multiple instances should be reviewed and reasonable management action taken.
      Recording made in error
      • Have an informal discussion with TI and send follow-up email.
      • Record comment against file in Evidence.com.
      • Mark recording as non-evidential in Evidence.com.
      Unlawful recordingRefer matter to regional Compliance Manager and local HR.
      Likely QPS involvementIn Evidence.com:
      • Mark file a evidence.
      • Record comment against file.
      • Tag file as Incident Possible QPS Involvement.

Managing and using a BWC
Obtaining and Managing a BWC
Obtaining a Camera
Obtaining a Camera
    To be issued with a BWC an Authorised Officer must complete the following training:
    • BWC Training - Authorised Officer and Audio-Visual Recording training modules in Accelerate.
    • Read the Body Worn Camera and Recordings policy.
    • Provide the STI an acknowledgement that the above policies have been read.

    At completion of the accelerate training package, the Authorised Officer can submit a Service Centre Online (SCO) request. The SCO is approved by the STI or Manager.
Managing access to Evidence.com
Managing access to Evidence.com
    Allocation of BWCs and access to Evidence.com is managed by the Regional Compliance Manager.

    Email CSB.BWC@tmr.qld.gov.au to:
    • Add a user
    • Assign a camera to a different user
    • Change a persons level of authority
    • Remove a user, including staff on secondment or long term leave.
    • Log a faulty, damaged, lost or stolen camera.
    • Request a hot swap for a faulty, damaged, lost or stolen camera.
Reporting a Lost, Stolen or Damaged BWC
Reporting a Lost, Stolen or Damaged BWC
    If a BWC is lost, stolen or damaged an email must be sent to the STI and Manager Compliance as soon as practicable.
Returning a BWC to AXON for repair
Returning a BWC to AXON for repair
    • If possible upload all recordings to evidence.com
    • Have an STI complete the Axon Product Return form
    • Email the Regional Compliance Manager details of the camera being returned.
    • Send an email to CSB.BWC@tmr.qld.gov.au if a hot swap camera is required.
    • Using an Australia Post Small Parcel box, send the camera and completed Axon Product Return form via Parcel Post to:
      PO. Box 379 Tullamarine, VIC 3043 Australia.
    • Purchase 'Extra Cover' (insurance) from a Post Office to the value of $500 to cover loss or damage, when the camera is sent back to Axon.
    AXON Product Return Form
Ceasing employment or long term leave
Ceasing employment or long term leave
    If an employee that has been issued a BWC ceases employment with Compliance or is on long term leave (more than 3 months), the BWC must be returned and the Inventory list updated.
Using a BWCAlways operate the BWC in accordance with the manufacturers instructions.

Authorised Officers must only use BWC devices that have been approved and supplied by TMR, this is currently AXON.
Activate the BWC
Activate the BWC
    Activate your Body Worn Camera (BWC) and state:
    • the time, day and date, location
    • your partner's details
    • the reason you are exercising a power
    • for example, 09:30 hrs Monday 6th February 2020. About to enter AIS 6529 Central QLD RWC, 3 Smith Street Emerald. In company with Transport Inspector Peter Johns ID Q910 to conduct a Confirmation Audit
Advise people they are being recorded
Advise people they are being recorded
    Individuals must be made aware they are being recorded and why they are being recorded. This information is called the Privacy Collection Notice (PCN). The PCN may be provided verbally in the introduction.

    For example, 'Good evening/morning/afternoon. My name is John Smith, I am a Transport Inspector with Transport and Main Roads, here is my ID (SHOW ID), (give reason for exercising powers). This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes.'

    A BWC recording is not stopped because the person being recorded objects to the recording.

    A red halo will show when the BWC is recoding, this is an overt notification to the customer that they are being recorded.
RecordingsRecordings must be uploaded to Evidence.com as soon as practicable after an event.
Classifying recordings
Classifying recordings
    When recording of an intercept or other event is finished the recording must be classified as:
    • Evidence (retained for 7 years)
    • Non evidentary (retained for 28 days)
    • Fatal (retained indefinitely)

    This should, where possible, be done in the field by the Transport Inspector when the recording is completed by using the AXON View app on a TMR iPad or iPhone. If unsure of the classification, classify as evidence.

    Link to AXON HELP CENTRE 'Pairing with a mobile device'

    All recordings that are not classified in the field will be labelled as unclassified when the camera is docked and downloaded, all recordings will then require classification by logging into Evidence.com.

    There is no ability to delete recordings.
Naming files
Naming files
    When recording of an intercept or other event is finished the recording must be named using the AXON View app on a TMR iPad or iPhone, as soon as practicable. The recording must be named using the following convention:
    ActivityIDTitle
    InterceptRego No, QCIS intercept No and Notice No (if applicable)Customer surname name, CRN
    AuditCID ref noCustomer surname name, CRN

    InvestigationCID ref noCustomer surname name, CRN


    Link to AXON HELP CENTRE 'Tagging videos in the field'
Downloading
Downloading
    BWC recordings are stored in Evidence.com. Each officer, issued a BWC, is given a user access code.

    All BWC recordings must be uploaded to Evidence.com at the end of each shift when the camera is docked for charging. When the content is uploaded to Evidence.com the content on the camera is automatically deleted.
Accessing and sharing recordingsBWC recordings are the property of TMR.

Viewing recordings
Viewing recordings
    • Transport Inspectors may view their own recordings.
    • Lead Inspectors may view the recordings of a corroborating officer.
    • Senior Transport Inspectors may view the recordings of all Transport Inspectors who report to them.
    • Principle Advisors (Investigations) and Senior Investigators may only view recordings related to matters they are investigating.
    • Managers (Compliance) can view recording of Transport Inspectors in their region.
Sharing recordings
Sharing recordings
    Recordings can be shared by sending a link from Evidence.com.

    Recordings may be shared in the following circumstances. All other requests must be sent to your STI.

    Prosecutions
    Prosecutions
      Transport Inspectors, with the approval of their STI, may share a recording with Prosecutions
    Queensland Police Service (QPS)
    Queensland Police Service (QPS)
      QPS may request TMR provide a copy of a recording.

      A Transport Inspector may show a QPS Officer a recording on their BWC while in the field if requested. The Transport Inspector should make a record in their Official Notebook.
    Explosive Inspectors (Dept Mines and Energy)
    Explosive Inspectors (Dept Mines and Energy)
      Explosive Inspectors from the Department of Mines and Energy may request TMR provide a copy of a recording.

      A Transport Inspector may show an Explosive Inspector a recording on their BWC while in the field if requested. The Transport Inspector should make a record in their Official Notebook.
    Right to Information (RTI)
    Right to Information (RTI)
      RTI may be provided with a link to relevant evidence held in Evidence.com.
    Alleged defendant
    Alleged defendant
      TMR is required to give a copy of relevant evidence, including a record of interview, held in Evidence.com to alleged defendants. Transport Inspectors, on approval from their STI, can send alleged defendants a link to the evidence.

Monitoring and Reporting
Monitoring and Reporting
Monitoring
Monitoring
    STIs must review all unclassified recordings on a fortnightly basis and take action to ensure the recordings are classified. STIs may reclassify the recordings or refer it to the relevant Transport Inspector for action.
Reporting
Reporting
    Managers and STIs are able to obtain reports from Evidence.com to show:
    • uncategorised data
    • GPS location of footage
    • recording start and finish times
    • how many times footage has been viewed and by whom.
Privacy principles
Privacy Principles
Privacy Principles
    Queensland government agency systems which involve the collection and storage, use or disclosure of personal information are subject to the privacy obligations in the Information Privacy Act 2009 (Qld) (IP Act).

    The obligations under the IP Act operates subject to the provisions of other Acts that relate to the specific collection, use and disclosure of personal information. Where any primary legislation is silent, TMR must comply with the Information Privacy Principles (IPPs) contained in the IP Act when managing personal information. These IPPs contains requirements relating to collection, security, access, use and disclosure of the information. These obligations do not prevent an agency using camera surveillance however, affect the policies and practices associated with how the camera surveillance system operates.

    If a person captured in the footage is identifiable or the person provides personal information about themselves, the footage will contain personal information and the obligations in the information privacy principles apply.

    IPP2 of the IPA requires TMR to take all reasonable steps to ensure that individuals are aware of the purpose for which they are being recorded and the relevant legislation that authorises its collection and to whom the information would be generally be disclosed to (known as Privacy Collection Notice).

    A full listing of the Information Privacy Principles (IPP) outlined in the IP Act can be found here:
    https://www.oic.qld.gov.au/guidelines/for-government/guidelines-privacy-principles/key-privacy-concepts/overview-of-the-information-privacy-principles.

    To meet the requirements of this IPP, Authorised Officers must be acting in the lawful execution of their duties, only in places where they can lawfully exercise their delegated powers, and the personal information obtained must only relate to the power or powers being exercised.
