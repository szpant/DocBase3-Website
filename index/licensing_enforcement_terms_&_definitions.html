Licensing Enforcement Terms & Definitions (2611)

A-C
A-C
    A
    Appeal
    Appeal
      A written request for reconsideration made by a person who is unhappy about a decision or determination applied to them.

      The decision or determination may have been made by the chief executive, the commissioner of the police service or a Magistrates Court.

      As at 29 October 2007, the appeals against the suspension of a driver licence due to Accumulation of Points whilst on a GDB or High Speed suspension process has been replaced by Special Hardship Order.

      Learner licence holders who receive a 3 month suspension period due to exceeding their demerit point limit cannot appeal the licence suspension and are ineligible for a Special Hardship Order.
    BNo Definitions
    C
    Concurrent sanctions
    Concurrent sanctions
      The practice of imposing 2 or more sanctions on a person that are required to be served at the same time.

      Example -
      A person may have had their driver licence suspended in April by SPER. In June, the person has their driver licence further suspended for 6 months because of the accumulation of an excess number of demerit points under the TO(RUM—DL) Reg.

      The person is not permitted to drive under their licence until such time as both suspensions have ended.
    Court order
    Court order
      A determination on a matter which is made by an Australian court.

      This may include details about the offence, the fine and court costs imposed and the period that the person has been disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence.
    Cumulative disqualification Period
    Cumulative disqualification Period
      A cumulative disqualification period is the sum of each period of disqualification that has been imposed.

      A cumulative disqualification period applies when a court disqualification is imposed:
      • for more than 1 drink or drug driving related offence
        or
      • where the person is disqualified for a relevant unlicensed driving offence committed at the same time as the drink or drug driving related offence

      The person will be required to serve each period in its entirety, one after the other in the order that they were imposed.
D-F
D-F
    D
    Demerit points
    Demerit points
      The individual number of demerit points allocated for a particular demerit points offence that may be recorded on a person’s traffic history under the TO(RUM—DL) Reg.

      When the person has reached their threshold of demerit points, the chief executive must take a particular action against the person.
    Demerit points suspension
    Demerit points suspension
      A stated period of time when a person’s driver licence has been suspended under the demerit points scheme.

      The person is not permitted to drive a motor vehicle during this period.
    Demerit points suspension while driving under a s79E order
    Demerit points suspension while driving under a s79E order
      The suspension of a driver licence for a minimum period of 3 months. If the original drink or drug driving charge that resulted in the licence being suspended has not been decided by a court during the 3 month period, the suspension will continue until the original drink or drug driving charge is dealt with by a court, withdrawn or otherwise discontinued.

      Refer: Original Drink or Drug Driving Charge
    Disqualification
    Disqualification
      A stated period of time when a person is not permitted to hold or obtain a driver licence.

      A court may cancel a person’s licence and, under a court order, disqualify the person from holding or obtaining a driver licence for a stated period.

      A person who has their driver licence suspended under the TO(RUM—DL) Reg (demerit points suspension, convicted of driving more than 40 km/h over the speed limit) or by SPER is also not allowed to hold or obtain a driver licence during the term of the suspension.
    E
    Enforcement order
    Enforcement order
      A written notice given to a person by SPER requiring the person to pay the amount stated in the notice.

      The notice contains details about when payment must be received; the amount that is registered as unpaid, and the period of time the person may be jailed if the amount is not paid.

    F
    Fine
    Fine
      A monetary penalty that may be imposed on a person who has been convicted of breaching a provision of an Act or Reg.
G-I
G-I
    G
    Good Driving Behaviour Period (GDB)
    Good Driving Behaviour Period (GDB)
      A period of 1 year during which a person has agreed to be of good behaviour while driving.

      If the person has 2 or more demerit points allocated to their traffic history during that year, their licence will be suspended under the TO(RUM—DL) Reg for double the suspension period that would have applied had they chosen the licence suspension in the first place.

      Note:As of 1 March 2012, learner licence holders are not given the option to nominate for a GDB
    HNo Definitions
    I
    Immediate medical suspension
    Immediate medical suspension
      A suspension of a driver licence due to the holder notifying that they have a medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely or have had a change to a medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely.
    Immediate medical amendment
    Immediate medical amendment
      An amendment of a driver licence due to the holder notifying that they have a medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely or have had a change to a medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely.
    Immediate drink or drug driving suspension
    Immediate drink or drug driving suspension
      A suspension of a driver licence due to one of the following:
      • Driving a motor vehicle when the person is equal to or over 0.10% (middle or high alcohol limit)
      • Failure to provide a specimen of breath or saliva for testing or a specimen of blood for analysis
      • Charge with a second drink or drug driving offence while an earlier drink or drug driving charge is still to be heard
      • Driving a motor vehicle under a s79E order when the person is over 0.000% (no alcohol limit)
      • Operating a motor vehicle dangerously (s328A Criminal Code) when affected by an intoxicating substance.

      Refer: Open Licence Guide > Conditions of Use > Observe the relevant Drink Driving Limit
    Ineligible person
    Ineligible person
      A person who does not meet the prescribed eligibility criteria under the TO(RUM—DL) Reg for a Queensland driver licence.
    Infringement notice
    Infringement notice
      A notice issued to a person for breaching a provision of an Act or Reg. The notice must state certain information including identifying details about the offender, and the alleged offence.

    L
    Licence suspension
    Licence suspension
      A stated period of time when a person is not permitted to hold or obtain a driver licence.

      A person who has their driver licence suspended under the TO(RUM—DL) Reg (demerit points suspension, convicted of driving more than 40 km/h over the speed limit) or by SPER is also disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence during the term of the suspension.
M-O
M-O
    M
    Magistrate
    Magistrate
      A person who exercises control over a Magistrates Court, and makes all findings about law and fact for matters that are heard in the court.
    Maximum penalty
    Maximum penalty
      The maximum monetary amount, or jail term, that may be imposed by a court on a person who has been convicted of not complying with a provision of an Act or Reg.
    NNo Definitions
    O
    Original drink or drug driving charge
    Original drink or drug driving charge
      An original drink or drug driving charge for a drink or drug driving related offence means one of the following:
      • Driving a motor vehicle when the person is equal to or over 0.10% (middle or high alcohol limit)
      • Failure to provide a specimen of breath or saliva for testing or a specimen of blood for analysis
      • Charge with a second drink or drug driving offence while an earlier drink or drug driving charge is still to be heard
      • Driving a motor vehicle under a s79E order when the person is over 0.000% (no alcohol limit)
      • Operating a motor vehicle dangerously (s328A Criminal Code) when affected by an intoxicating substance

      Refer: Open Licence Guide > Conditions of Use > Observe the relevant Drink Driving Limit
P-R
P-R
    P
    Penalty
    Penalty
      A lawful or official punishment imposed on a person for not complying with a provision of an Act or Reg.

      It may consist of a monetary fine or a jail term. If the punishment imposed is a suspension or cancellation of the person’s driver licence, this punishment is usually called a sanction.
    Penalty unit
    Penalty unit
      Fines for traffic offences in Queensland are based on a system of penalty units. A penalty unit is a set amount of money used to work out each fine. The final amount is then rounded down to the nearest whole dollar.

      On 1 July 2021, the monetary value of 1 penalty unit will increase from $133.45 to $137.85. This is set by the Penalties and Sentences Act 1992.
    QNo Definitions
    R
    Road-side saliva testing
    Road-side saliva testing
      Road-side saliva testing is for the detection of the presence of a relevant drug. It will operate in much the same manner as the random breath testing program for the detection of alcohol.
    Relevant drug
    Relevant drug
      A relevant drug for road-side saliva testing is THC (the active ingredient in cannabis), methamphetamine (speed) and MDMA (ecstasy).
S-U
S-U
    S
    Sanction
    Sanction
      See term ‘Penalty'
    Special Hardship Order (SHO)
    Special Hardship Order (SHO)
      A SHO is a court order which allows a driver whose licence has been suspended to continue driving under restricted circumstances.
      A person may apply if:
      • their provisional or open licence is suspended for either:
        • exceeding demerit points on a GDB
        • high speed suspension
      • they meet the eligibility requirements.

      If granted, the person is required to be issued with a replacement licence with an X3 condition code. This licence authorises the holder to drive only in the restricted circumstances stated on the court order.

      Note:There is no appeal or SHO option for learner licence holders

      For more information refer to:
      Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > Demerit Point Sanctions > Special Hardship Order
    Special Hardship Order (SHO) Variation
    Special Hardship Order (SHO) Variation
      If a persons situation changes which required them to drive outside the SHO restrictions they may apply to the Magistrates court for a variation of the terms and conditions on a previously granted SHO. The Magistrates court will only grant a variation if there are sufficient grounds to justify a variation to the restrictions.
    Speeding suspension
    Speeding suspension
      The suspension of a driver licence for a 6 months period because the holder of the licence was convicted of the offence of driving more than 40 km/h over the speed limit.
    Speeding suspension while driving under a s79E order
    Speeding suspension while driving under a s79E order
      The suspension of a driver licence for a minimum period of 6 months. If the original drink or drug driving charge that resulted in the licence being suspended has not been decided by a court during the 6 month period, the suspension will continue until the original drink or drug driving charge is dealt with by a court, withdrawn or otherwise discontinued.

      Refer: Original drink or drug driving charge
    SPER
    SPER
      State Penalties Enforcement Registry. A registry established under the Department of Justice and Attorney-General.

      Functions of SPER includes the collection of amounts payable under the SPE Act or another Act; administering Enforcement Order's, and taking enforcement action for non-payment of outstanding fines.
    SPER suspension
    SPER suspension
      The suspension of a driver licence by SPER until such time as the holder of the driver licence pays the outstanding fines or the amount is otherwise discharged under the SPE Act.
    Suspension of authority to drive
    Suspension of authority to drive
      A lawful or official process that suspends the authority of a person to drive on a Queensland road under their interstate or foreign driver licence.

      The person will be given a written notice about the suspension of authority to drive.

      Example -
      The authority to drive will be suspended for a stated period because of the accumulation of an excess number of demerit points.

      Also see term ‘Withdrawal of Authority to drive’

V-X
    V
    Voluntary Instalment Plan (VIP)
    Voluntary Instalment Plan (VIP)
      A payment option available through SPER for fine amounts of $200 and greater, where a person can make smaller, more manageable payments of an agreed amount, over an agreed period of time until the amount is paid in full. Fines less than $200 are therefore not eligible for participation in VIP unless the customer has an infringement notice which still states the lesser amount of $150.
    W
    Withdrawal of authority to drive
    Withdrawal of authority to drive
      A lawful or official process that automatically withdraws the authority of a person to drive on a Queensland road under their interstate or foreign driver licence on the grounds of residency.

      The authority to drive will be withdrawn indefinitely. The person will not be given a written notice about the withdrawal of their authority to drive.

      Examples
      • For an interstate licence holder, the authority to drive will be withdrawn 3 months after the holder takes up residence in Queensland.
      • For a foreign licence holder, the authority to drive will be withdrawn after the holder has resided in Queensland for at least 3 months after the person became an Australian citizen, or after the person got a visa that allowed the person to stay in Australia indefinitely,
        or
      • after the person fails a practical driving test.

      Refer: Driver Licence > 2. Non-Qld Driver Licence > Driving on Interstate, NZ, Foreign, ADF Licence > Withdrawal of Authority to Drive in Qld

      Also see term ‘Suspension of authority to drive’
