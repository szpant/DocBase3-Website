Medical Condition Reporting (IA holders) (5049)


Medical Condition Reporting (IA holders)
What it isDependant on the type of industry authority held, legislation requires an IA holder to be either;
  • medically fit to drive a vehicle, or
  • fit to perform the industry authority role.

The purpose of this process is not to determine if a medical condition needs to be added to the IA but to determine if the person is either medically fit to drive a vehicle or fit to perform the IA role.
Note:Medical conditions are added to the driver licence as an outcome of the MCR process. It is an IA requirement to comply with any conditions imposed on the driver licence. Conditions imposed on an IA must relate to a restriction to the duties that can be performed by the authority holder and are only applied by the Industry & Operator Authorisation Unit. Conditions relating to criminal and traffic history are applied by Customer Service Centres assessing IA applications or Regional Operations staff within Translink Division. None of these conditions are related to the holder’s medical fitness.
Evidence of Medical Fitness
Dangerous Goods and Driver Authorisations
Dangerous Goods and Driver Authorisations
    Applicants must provide a Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712) form assessed under commercial standards.

    Note:If a Show Cause Notice has been issued to the Dangerous Goods Driver, the Medical Certificate must be issued after the Show Cause Notice issue date

    Medical certificates issued within the last six months (prior to the Show Cause Notice) for DA may be accepted but only after agreement from the relevant Regional Translink Office.
Tow Truck/Pilot and Escort/Driver Rider Trainer/Traffic Controllers
Tow Truck/Pilot and Escort/Driver Rider Trainer/Traffic Controllers
    Applicant must provide an application form relevant to the IA held with the following sections completed:
    • Applicant’s Details
    • Fitness to perform industry authority role
    • Medical Practitioner Certification – (only where “Yes” has been selected for the question “Fitness to perform industry authority role”)
    • Applicant’s declaration
Actions to be takenWhen a medical notification is received, check TICA to see if they hold an IA.

If the customer holds an IA, further action is required when a driver licence medical condition is alleged/reported and either:
  • no medical certificate has been supplied; or
  • medical certificate has not been assessed under commercial standards; and/or
  • medical certificate has Part 2 Question 6 C or D completed.
All existing MCR requirements for the driver licence must be applied, the following steps are in addition to the Driver Licence MCR guidelines.

Refer: Driver Licence > 4. Medical Condition Reporting > Medical Condition Reporting Guide

Note:All third party medical certificate driver licence notifications received where the person is also the holder of an industry authority should be referred to the MCR Unit for action.

Medical requirements met
Medical requirements met
    No further action is required if the applicant supplies:
    • The application form indicating they are fit to perform the IA role; or
    • Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver indicating commercial standards have been applied and that the person either meets the medical criteria for an unconditional licence or requires an existing M condition to be removed
Does not meet medical Criteria or not assessed to commercial standards
Does not meet medical Criteria or not assessed to commercial standards
    A DA holder who does not provide a passing commercial medical should be referred to the associated Regional Translink Officer (if action is taken against the driver licence, this step is not required)

    Show Cause action (cancel, suspend or amend) should be taken for all other IA’s if the applicant:
    • Does not supply the required documentation;
      or
    • Supplies an IA application form with the section “fitness to perform industry authority role” completed with the answer “Yes”;
      or
    • Supplies a “Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver” indicating on Part 2, 6D of the form that the person does not meet the medical criteria as set out in the Assessing Fitness to Drive or the certificate has not been assessed under commercial standards.
