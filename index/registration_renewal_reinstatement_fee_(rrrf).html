Registration Renewal Reinstatement Fee (RRRF) (3065)


What it isThe Registration Reinstatement Fee (RRRF) is a fee levied when a renewal payment is received by TMR/TMR agents after the expiry date but before the auto-cancel date.

There are two variations of the RRRF for one-month, and 3, 6 or 12 month terms:

One-month3, 6 or 12 month
RRRFOne-month RRRF (1MRRRF) - Applicable for one-month registration termsStandard RRRF - Applicable for 3, 6 and 12 month registration terms
Concessional/ Light Trailer RRRFApplicable for light trailers, SIVE, Conditional and Drought registrations on a one-month term.Applicable for light trailers, SIVE, Charitable, Conditional, Consular, Seasonal and Drought registrations on a 3, 6 or 12 month term
For the current RRRF amounts, Refer: Fees and Forms > Fees > Vehicle Registration > RRRF

Exemption Guidelines
Changing terms when RRRF is due
Changing terms when RRRF is due
    If a customer changes terms when renewing their registration and a RRRF payment is required, the RRRF amount will be calculated on the future registration period.

    For example - Payment fails for a one-month registration term and the registration expires. The customer decides to renew for 12 month so they will have to pay the full RRRF as the future term is 12 months.
Common Due Date Vehicles
Common Due Date Vehicles
    If payment received:
    • Before the expiry date
      and
    • There were insufficient funds to pay all registrations
    • Pay registration in the order they appear on the registration renewal notice
    • Contact the customer to request more funds
      • If requested funds are received after the expiry date RRRF must be paid.
      • If payment is received after the expiry date RRRF must be applied.
Inspectable Vehicles
Inspectable Vehicles
    Renewal payment cannot be accepted for an inspectable vehicle if:
    • No current Certificate Of Inspection (COI) recorded
    • No Certificate of Exemption (COE) is recorded.

    If either of these is not presented before the expiry date RRRF must charged.

Dishonoured Cheques and RRRF
Dishonoured Cheques and RRRF
    If a renewal is paid before the expiry date with a dishonoured cheque the second payment must be made before the expiry date to avoid paying the RRRF.

    If the customer does not pay the RRRF and the bank administration fee, there is a risk that after deducting the fees form the payment the net amount may be below the absolute floor and the payment returned and the registration cancelled.
