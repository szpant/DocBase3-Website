Special Need - Tenure or Age Exemption (2968)

What it is
Tenure
Tenure
    A person who is not eligible for a provisional licence because they have not held their learner licence for the required tenure period, may be eligible to apply for a Provisional licence if they are 17 years of age or over and prove that they have a special need.
(TO(RUM-DL) Reg s 20
    Note:Applicants under 25 years of age must still meet the logbook requirement to be eligible to progress from a learner licence unless an application for exemption from the log book requirements has been approved.
    Refer: Learner Licence Guide > Progressing from a Learner Licence > Driving Experience Required
Age
Age
    A person who is not eligible for either:
    • A learner licence because they are not at least 16 years of age
    or
    • A provisional licence because they are not at least 17 years of age

    May be eligible to apply for the Learner/Provisional licence if they are able to prove that they have a special need.
(TO(RUM-DL) Reg s 8-9
    Note:Applicants under 25 years of age must still meet the logbook requirements to be eligible to progress from a learner licence unless an application for exemption from the log book requirements has been approved.
    Refer: Learner Licence Guide > Progressing from a Learner Licence > Driving Experience Required

Eligibility
Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility Criteria
    The person must be able to prove that all of the following 3 circumstances apply to them:
    1They have a need to drive for one of the following purposes:
    • To/from their place of employment
    • In the course of their employment
    • To/from an educational institution that they attend
    • To get medical treatment for themself or a member of their family
    2There is no other transport reasonably available to them
    Availability of other transport
    Availability of other transport
      A person must clearly demonstrate that they do not have access to other transport.

      The following examples may constitute a person not reasonably having transport available to them:
      • I live in a remote area that is not serviced by public transport and I have no friends or family who can assist me with my transportation needs.
      • The nearest bus/train station is a considerable walking distance from my residence (in excess of 5 kilometers), I am unable to afford the expense of taxi fares and there is no one else who can assist me with my transportation needs.
      • I have a class RE/R licence and I am applying for a class C licence. I do not own or have access to a motorbike and I live in an area that is not serviced by public transport.
      • I have a physical disability which extremely limits my ability to catch public transport, I am unable to afford the expense of taxi fares and there is no one else to assist me with my transportation needs.
      • I own a vehicle and other household family members use it 100% of the time for their own transportation needs. I also live in an area that is not serviced by public transport.
      • I own a vehicle that is unregistered and in an unroadworthy condition. I am unable to afford the substantial expense of repairing it. I also live in an area that is not serviced by public transport.
    3The refusal to grant the licence would cause severe hardship
    Definition of "severe hardship'
    Definition of "severe hardship'
      "severe hardship" means severe hardship suffered by the person, or the person's family because they have taken on a role of special responsibility in relation to their family.
    (TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 schedule 9

      The Macquarie Dictionary defines "family" as:
      (1)parents and their children, whether dwelling together or not;
      (2)one's children collectively;
      (3)any group of person's closely related by blood, as parents, children, uncles, aunts, cousins and so on.

      Note:When assessing applications TMR Officers may also consider Spouses and de facto Spouses (including same sex couples) as part of the definition for family.
      Circumstances that may demonstrate severe hardship
      Circumstances that may demonstrate severe hardship
        It is important to note that when assessing whether an applicant has grounds for severe hardship, applications must be assessed on a case by case basis.

        The following examples below may* demonstrate whether grounds for severe hardship genuinely exist:
        • I am the sole financial provider for my family and would lose my job if I am not granted a driver licence.
        • There is no other form of transport and I urgently require a driver licence to transport myself/or a family member on a regular basis to obtain regular medical treatments for a life threatening illness.
        • My employment has instructed that I attend further skills training at an educational institution. I will lose my job if I do not attend this training and will suffer financial hardship as I am financially independent and do not live at home with my parents

        *Note:The above examples provide some guidelines. However, other circumstances may be considered when determining if a person has grounds for severe hardship.

      Circumstances that do not demonstrate severe hardship
      Circumstances that do not demonstrate severe hardship
        The following examples do not demonstrate grounds for consideration:
        • I live at home with my parents.
        • It is an inconvenience for my family/friends to assist me with my transportation needs.
        • I do not currently have an employer and a licence would assist in obtaining a job or apprenticeship.
        • Public transport does not operate at the time I need to travel.
Application Process
Tenure
Tenure
    Application Form
    Application Form

    Special Need - Provisional Licence Application (Tenure Rule) (F2639)

    The application can be emailed (from the noreply@tmr.qld.gov.au email account) or handed to the customer if they meet the application eligibility criteria. Refer to the Special Need Tenure Application help tool.

    Where to Apply
    Where to Apply
      Applicants who are 17 years of age or older at any CSC or licence issuing agency.

      Note:Applicants who are under 17 years of age may be eligible to apply for a special need age rule.

    Application Requirements
    Application Requirements
      • Standard EOI
      • A completed Special Need - Provisional Licence Application (Tenure Rule) F2639

    Consideration of special need application
    Consideration of special need application
      In deciding whether a person has a special need for the licence, the delegated officer must consider:
      • The times of day when the person must travel
      • How often the person must travel
      • The distance the person must travel
      • The person's traffic history
      • The availability of other transport reasonably available to the person
      • What severe hardship will be caused to the applicant, or the applicant's family, if the application is refused.

      The decision should be made within ten business days after receiving all the necessary information for consideration.

      Assessment Guidelines
      Assessment guidelines have been developed to provide support for decision makers when assessing applications for special need tenure exemptions, in order to assist them in the exercise of their discretion.
      Assessment Guidelines - Special Need Tenure
Age
Age
    Getting an Application
    Getting an Application
      A Special Need (Age Rule) application is supplied and decided by the Director (Registration and Licensing) only.

      An applicant must be provided with the Information Sheet, Special Need - Learner or Provisional Licence (Age Rule) S4484, and invited to write to the stated address to request an application form.

    Application Requirements
    Application Requirements
      • Standard EOI
      • A completed Special Need - Learner or Provisional Licence Application (Age Rule) (F4028) form.

    Where to Submit the Application
    Where to Submit the Application
      Special Need Age Rule application should be submitted to a CSC for lodging on TICA and then forwarded to and decided by Registration and Licensing (Director)
    Consideration of special need application
    Consideration of special need application
      In deciding whether a person has a special need for the licence, the Director must consider:
      • The times of day when the person must travel
      • How often the person must travel
      • The distance the person must travel
      • The person's traffic history
      • The availability of other transport reasonably available to the person
      • What severe hardship will be caused to the applicant, or the applicant's family, if the application is refused.

      The Director's decision should be made within ten business days after receiving all the necessary information for consideration.
