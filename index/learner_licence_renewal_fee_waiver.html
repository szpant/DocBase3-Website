Learner Licence Renewal Fee Waiver - COVID-19 (7548)

Approved ActionLearner licence renewal fee waiver
WhenThis fee waiver is no longer current. It applied between 25 April 2020 until 11.59pm 14 September 2020.
BackgroundOn 28 March 2020, all Practical Driver Licence Tests (PDTs) were suspended in the interest of community health and safety and in line with Queensland health advice and guidelines. The suspension of PDTs meant that some learners had to renew their licence who otherwise would not have had to.

The Queensland learner licence renewal term is a fixed three-year period and currently has an associated fee of $178.95. This renewal fee was waived to recognise that these costs would not have been incurred if learners were able to undertake and pass their practical driving test.

The fee waiver applied to all learner licence renewals from 25 April 2020 until midnight 14 September 2020.

There was no change to the term, learner licences were still issued for the fixed three-year term.

Note:The fee for the issue of a first learner licence was not affected by this change.

From Saturday 18 July 2020 learner licence holders are also able to renew online. The fee waiver also applied to online renewals.

All other eligibility criteria must have been met. The same fixed three-year term applies to online renewals.
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers
Customers
    Customers with a learner licence expiring within the fee waiver period were sent the below email if they had their email address recorded in TICA.

    Learner Licence Renewal Email

    If the customer had no email address recorded but did have a mobile number they were sent an SMS referring them to the TMR website for further information.
CSC Staff
CSC Staff
    From Saturday 18 July 2020, the fee for learner licence renewals was automatically waived in the system.

    Operators will no longer be required to adjust the learner licence fee as a credit to the customer's account so that the raised renewal fee can be paid.
Customer Contact Centre Operators
Customer Contact Centre Operators
    The CCC may notice an increase in enquiries regarding the learner licence renewal fee. From Saturday 18 July 2020, customers who do not wish to attend a customer service centre to renew their learner licence may renew online. All other eligibility criteria must be met. The same fixed three-year term applies to online renewals.
Further informationLearner licence holders were not able to renew their learner licence outside of the 6-week period leading up to their expiry date. If a learner licence expired after 14 September 2020 but the customer presented to renew their licence before this date their renewal fee was to be waived.

Customers who are seeking further information or wish to make a complaint should be referred to TMR through the normal communication channels. Standard responses on this issue have been distributed.

The fee waiver can not be applied in any circumstances on or after 15 September 2020
