New Business (3060)


What it isNew Business is registering a vehicle in Queensland (QLD) that is either:
  • Currently unregistered
  • Registered interstate
  • A new vehicle
  • Issue of Dealer/Trade plate. For full requirements to issue refer Dealer/Trade Plates Transaction Requirement Checklist

Processing a new business adds:
  • A vehicle to the register (TICA) as a controlled object
  • An entity responsible for the operation of the vehicle
When is a Vehicle Registered
When is a Vehicle Registered
    A vehicle is deemed to be registered when either:
    • The application for registration and payment of appropriate fees has been received at a CSC or agent of TMR
    • An application for registration has been transmitted electronically to the department

Transporting a Vehicle for a New Business
Transporting a Vehicle for a New Business
    When operating/towing an unregistered vehicle/trailer on the road for the purpose of registration, a customer must carry a CTP certificate (if applicable) purchased through the CTP Insurer before driving the vehicle to obtain a:
    • Gas Certificate
    • Weighbridge Certificate
    • Safety Certificate/COI/HVRAS (including road testing that is part of the inspection)
    • Written-Off Vehicle Inspection (Repairable write-offs only)
    • Queensland Police Service inspections for surrogates
    • Surrogate numbers and TMR issued LVV's stamped on vehicle/trailer

    Customers who are not travelling to a formless centre or who are registering a vehicle/trailer with a GVM/ATM > 4.5 t GVM/ATM will still need to complete a Vehicle Registration Application (F3518) to register the vehicle. In these instances it is recommended that the customer complete the application form prior to attending the centre.

    The vehicle/trailer can be driven/towed back to its garaging address if it is not possible to register the vehicle/trailer on that day due to insufficient time or other registration requirements that were not able to be obtained.

    Please note that QIS advises WOVI customers that a pass entered into the system by a QIS Inspector does not update in NEVDIS until overnight, therefore the vehicle will not be able to be registered until the following working day.

    Section 15 of the Transport Operations (road Use Management - Vehicle Registration) Regulation 2010

    Note: If a vehicle is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS), the vehicle cannot be registered during the confiscation period.

    For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at:
    https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/default.htm

Repaired Written Off Vehicles Registration
Repaired Written Off Vehicles Registration
    Written-Off Vehicle Inspection can only be accepted if the vehicle is;
    • identified as a repairable write-off (restriction history on TICA)
    and
    • there is a WOVI pass result recorded in TICA (check the List Inspection History Screen)
    and
    • the written off vehicle restriction has been cleared in NEVDIS
    PRI restriction for WOVI is to be expired, PRI is not required for light vehicles.
Over dimensional heavy vehicles
Over dimensional heavy vehicles
    When registering an over dimensional heavy vehicle, the customer is required to contact NHVR prior to registration to make sure they are eligible to operate under a notice, guideline or permit.

    Once the customer confirms their eligibility, the vehicle needs to be registered before NHVR can issue a permit. The customer is required to:
    • attend a CSC to register vehicle
    • produce a completed HVRAS inspection sheet
    • complete the Vehicle Registration Application (F3518)

    If any of the regulation limits or axle group questions on the HVRAS inspection sheet are ticked as "no", then the customer must tick the box in section 3 of the Vehicle Registration Application (F3518) to declare that they are going to operate under a notice, guideline or permit issued by NHVR.

    If the customer does not tick the declaration on the F3518, the vehicle cannot be registered.

    If the customer ticks the declaration on the F5318 and then operates the vehicle without a permit, they may be intercepted by compliance.
Refuse Registration
Refuse Registration
    The application is to be refused if:
    • Documentation incomplete
    • Information in the application is false or misleading
    • An application form is required and signatures are missing
    • Vehicle not present but requires a PRI due to vehicle having identifiers that are:
      • Illegible
      • Unable to be located
      • Damaged
      • Missing
    • EOI not supplied
    • Unable to make payment
    • No Qld garaging address
    • Written-off vehicle restriction
    • Stolen Vehicle restriction
    • SPER Text restriction
    • The vehicle does not meet the required standards
    • The vehicle is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS). For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at -
      https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/default.htm
    • Business name status is anything other than 'Active' on the ABR website - http://www.abr.business.gov.au/
    • Company name status is anything other than 'Registered' on the ASIC website - https://connectonline.asic.gov.au/RegistrySearch/faces/landing/SearchRegisters.jspx?_afrLoop=227346324832401&_afrWindowMode=0&_adf.ctrl-state=s976lfiq0_4

    If the Business/Company name is not ‘Active/Registered’, the CRN should be recorded on TICA as 'closed'. Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > The How to: Customer > Customer Procedures > Close organisation customer
Registration Expired/Current at Time of Application
Registration Expired/Current at Time of Application
    Current
    Current
      If the registration is current at the time of application the registered operator should either:
      • Cancel the vehicle (Refer: Vehicle Rego > 09. Cancellation > Cancellation)
      • If the vehicle is an:
        • eligible heavy vehicle with previously issued National Heavy Vehicle Plates from Qld or another participating jurisdiction, and
        • has a current TIST Restriction applied, and
        • the same registered operator as evidenced by a NEVDIS search intends to attach the same National Heavy Vehicle Plates, the registration should be cancelled but the plates must not be surrendered, but re-attached at New Business.
      • Transfer the vehicle (Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer)

    Expired
    Expired
      If the previous registration is expired (not auto cancelled) and:
      • New Business requirements have been met
      • Number plates have been surrendered (excluding National Heavy Vehicle plates)

      The CSO can cancel the registration and no further documentation from the current registered operator is required. Note on the Vehicle Registration application that the previous registration was expired and has been cancelled for re-registration. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 09. Cancellation > Cancellation> Voluntary Cancellation > Owners Request
      Where National Heavy Vehicle plates are attached and the registered operator remains the same the same plates can be re-used.
      If the vehicle has an electronic certificate issued from ICO prior to the cancellation date, it will automatically cancel when the registration is cancelled. Contact Help Direct by phone for advice.

      Note:If the plates cannot be surrendered a declaration explaining the reason why must be completed by the new registered operator. The declaration can be in any format provided it outlines why the plates cannot be surrendered.
Time Allowed to Re-register from Interstate
Time Allowed to Re-register from Interstate
    All vehicles must be registered in Qld 14 days from Qld becoming the permanent place of garaging.

    Vehicles over 4.5 tonnes GVM operating in Qld on a temporary basis must be registered in Qld if the vehicle has been operating in Qld for a continuous period of 3 months.

    An interstate registered vehicle being used in Qld after either of these time periods is considered unregistered under Qld law.

    Defence Force Exception
    Defence Force Exception
      In view of the special circumstances affecting personnel in the Defence Forces, they are able to retain their interstate registration until it expires. Once expired they must re-register their vehicle in Qld if that is where the permanent garaging address is to be. This exemption includes immediate family members of defence force personnel, provided that have a current civilian Australian Defence Force (ADF) Identity Card.

Motorised Wheelchairs
Motorised Wheelchairs

    A new form Motorised Wheelchair Statement (F4414) must be completed for each motorised wheelchair.

    Individual Applicants must complete page 1 of F4414, Organisations must complete page 2.

    For further information regarding registration requirements/conditions of use of a Motorised Wheelchair Refer:Vehicle Rego>02. Registerable Vehicles>Light Vehicles>Types of Light Vehicles> Wheelchair

Confiscation Restriction
Confiscation Restriction
    A vehicle with a CONF restriction cannot be re-registered until the CONF period has ended or has been removed by QPS.

    If the CONF period has ended/been removed by QPS and the registration status is cancelled, contact Help Direct to have the restriction removed.
Pay Terms
Pay Terms
    • 12 months
    • 6 months
    • 3 months for:
      • Heavy vehicles
      • Vehicles in drought declared areas with a FARM or PRIV POU
      • Vehicles with seasonal registration
      • Vehicles with TAXI, LIMO, LSPL, BKHR & BKRN POU
      • All light vehicles (excluding trailers) during an approved period of time (e.g. COVID-19) where the person meets the criteria for that event. The 3 month term will have an 'on/off' switch and will only be selectable when 'on'. If a 3 month pay term is applied, operators:
          • Must advise the customer that it's a one-off 3 month pay term and that their renewal notice will default to 12 months. They can then opt to pay 6 months or enrol in direct debit for a 3 or one-month term.
          • Should encourage direct debit sign up for future renewals and provide interested customers with the Enrol in Direct Debit and Add Vehicle email template.
Offensive Advertising
Offensive Advertising
    Where a vehicle record has a current Offensive Advertising (OFAD) restriction, an application for registration must not be accepted until the new registered operator has completed a Statutory Declaration stating that the offensive material has been removed.

    The restriction can then be expired and the new business transaction processed.

    Refer: NEVDIS Restrictions
