Transfer (3076)


What is it?A transfer is a change of registered operator during a current registration. This includes adding/removing registered operators.

Different Acquirer Transfers
Different Acquirer Transfers
    Where possible all transactions to be processed in sequence.
    The department has an obligation to record the current registered operator of the vehicle and therefore, where possible, the transfer should be effected when all other transfer requirements have been met.

    Vehicle transferred into the wrong name - acquirers are known to each other
    Vehicle transferred into the wrong name - acquirers are known to each other
      The NOD was completed and the vehicle transferred into the wrong name (example: Husband’s name instead of wife’s name.)

      As the NOD has already been issued, the transaction must be completed and the vehicle transferred into the husband’s name. A new application to transfer into the wife’s name must be lodged and all requirements must be met.

    Vehicle transferred into one name, should be registered in joint names
    Vehicle transferred into one name, should be registered in joint names
      The NOD has been processed and the vehicle transferred into one name but the customer indicates that the vehicle should be registered in joint names.

    The acquirer has not finalised the transfer online
    The acquirer has not finalised the transfer online
      The application must be lodged at a CSC or agency and the acquirer indicated on the NOD must be listed as a registered operator. The other operator can be added if Part A of the transfer form is presented.

      Note:The disposers signature is not required on the application form.

    The acquirer has finalised the transfer online
    The acquirer has finalised the transfer online
      If the acquirer has finalised the transfer online into one name, normal transfer requirements must be met (including completed transfer form with signatures, EOI, and transfer fee) to add an additional operator.

    Vehicle has been transferred into different names
    Vehicle has been transferred into different names
      The NOD is processed online in one name, but person lodging the transfer at CSC is not the nominated acquirer and the disposer is not the previous registered operator.

      Refer below: Different Seller Transfers > Where the acquirer is an Individual/Organisation

      Note:If a NOD restriction applies, another transfer can be processed.

Different Seller Transfers
Different Seller Transfers
    Where possible all transactions to be processed in sequence.
    The department has an obligation to record the current registered operator of the vehicle and therefore, where possible, the transfer should be effected when all other transfer requirements have been met.

    Where the acquirer is an Individual / Organisation
    Where the acquirer is an Individual / Organisation
      The acquirer must provide a written declaration stating that:
      • they have purchased the vehicle;
      • the vehicle is in their possession; and
      • they have the legal right to transfer the registration into their name.

      The declaration can be in any form provided it is signed and dated by the acquirer and is legible.

      Before using the different seller action, ensure that the seller/disposer of the vehicle can be identified on TICA. If the seller/disposer exists as a legal entity, the transfer of registration can be processed.

      Where the disposer/seller does not exist in TICA, the applicant (acquirer) should be advised to seek further information from the disposer/seller and the transfer should be refused at this point in time.


      Note:Where transfer application is a different seller and disposer signature is also missing, see Signature Requirements below under Rules. Additional requirements to the above declaration are listed under Disposer signature missing.

      Note:Staff are to refer to Representing an Individual or Organisation for document and signatory requirements when application is being submitted on behalf of an organisation.
      Refer : Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Registered Operators > Representing an Individual or Organisation > Registration > Other person

    Where the acquirer is a Dealer
    Where the acquirer is a Dealer
      If the dealer has not yet acquired the vehicle, a courtesy call should be made to the Dealer to determine if the acquisition has been lodged at their local CSC and if not request that it be lodged as a priority. Where possible the acquisition should be processed to remove the need to use the different seller functionality. If a dealership has lodged an acquisition at their local CSC and it has not been processed, the CSC should be contacted so that the acquisition is processed.

      Before using the different seller functionality, operators should ensure that:
      • The CRN of the seller/disposer of the vehicle listed on the Transfer of Registration - Vehicle/Vessel Acquisition by a Licensed Dealer F1310 form can be identified in TICA. If the seller/disposer exists as a legal entity, the transfer of registration can be processed
      • The VIN number listed on the application form matches the information in TICA
      • The registration history is reviewed to ascertain whether the acquisition has already been processed or if a Dealer Transfer Note is required. For more information regarding Dealer Transfer Notes, Refer: Vehicle Rego > 01. DTMR Agencies > Dealer Registration Schemes/Arrangements > Transactions > Dealer Acquisitions
Domestic Violence Policy
Domestic Violence Policy
    Removing a registered operator of a jointly registered vehicle
    Removing a registered operator of a jointly registered vehicle
      A victim can remove their name from a joint registration without the signature of the perpetrator if they do not have possession of the vehicle.

      Requirements:
      • Completed Vehicle Registration Transfer Application (F3520) (except for perpetrators signature)
      • Statutory Declaration signed by the victim declaring a DFV situation and that they do not have possession of the vehicle OR:
      • A statement from the victim stating they do not have possession of the vehicle and one of the following supporting documents:
        • Domestic Violence Order (DVO) or Apprehended Violence Order (AVO); or
        • Court Order; or
        • Letter from a Shelter

      The customer should be advised that the removal of their name from the registration record for the vehicle may result in a new registration certificate being sent to the remaining registered operator.

    Customer has moved interstate and wishes to cancel registration
    Customer has moved interstate and wishes to cancel registration
      Where a DFV victim has moved interstate and wishes to transfer a Queensland registered vehicle registration into their name, they may do so without providing a Queensland garage address, provided the transfer form is accompanied by:
      • an application to cancel the registration
      • number plates or plate surrender receipt

      This ensures that the registered operator is able to receive a refund of the unused portion of the Queensland registration fees that they are entitled to.


      Note: the customer still also has the option to "SWAP" registered operators to be listed as the first registered operator. This will also allow the cancellation to be processed and the refund to be issued to the victim of DFV. Customers should be made aware that all jurisdictions have differing duty requirements and they should confirm whether duty will be exempted in the state in which they are intending to register the vehicle. It may be more beneficial for them to transfer into one name for this reason.

Failure to lodge transfer
Failure to lodge transfer
    Disposer Action if Acquirer Fails to Lodge Transfer:
    • Disposer does not pay any fees when Notice of Disposal is processed. The transfer is regarded as pending and the acquirer is responsible for the fees.
    • A restriction is placed on the registration record until the acquirer finalises the transfer application.
    • The restriction will prevent a customer from making a payment to renew the registration. Payment will be required to be made at a CSC.
    • When due, a renewal notice is sent to the acquirer with the following information: TRFR: Advice has been received that you have acquired this vehicle. Before the registration can be renewed you must lodge an Application for Transfer to your name with all requirements.

    Note:When processing a NOD, the date of disposal should only be backdated to the last renewal payment. If the date of disposal is earlier than the last renewal payment date, a text restriction should be recorded with the correct disposal date. This is because if a NOD is recorded prior to the last renewal, the NOD restriction will prevent TICA from raising the renewal again to allow payment.

    Disposer has documents
    • Disposer may lodge completed Notice of Disposal (Part B) signed by acquirer with copy of Safety Certificate
    OR
    • Complete the Transfer Registration as Seller online (if eligible).
    • The acquirer does not need a Queensland address for the NOD to be processed at a CSC or agency.
    • If acquirer does not have a CRN, the NOD can still be processed with a SLEN at a CSC or agency. The acquirer will be required to present full EOI at the next transaction.

    Refer:Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > The How To: Transfer > Registration Transfer Procedures > Transfer Registration > Process Notice of Disposal (In Person)

    • Are eligible

    The disposer must make every effort to contact the acquirer.

When to Refuse Transfer
When to Refuse Transfer
    The application for transfer of registration is refused if the applicant does not have:
    • Correct documents completed and signed
    • Identification
    • Acceptable payment method
    • Qld garage address (exceptions apply)
    • A valid inspection certificate (if applicable)
    or
    • Where the inspection certificate does not match the details on the vehicle, the application is to be refused until such time as a correct and valid inspection certificate is presented.
    • There is uncertainty about the ownership of the vehicle/vessel
    • An Australian court order prohibits the transfer of the vehicle's/vessel's registration (record text against vehicle record describing reasons for court order, type of court, date of court order and a contact person from the court if provided)
    • Where an Offensive Advertising restriction is applied to the vehicle record.
      Refer: NEVDIS Restrictions
    • Where a SPER text restriction is applied to the vehicle record.

    If the application is refused, all documents are to be returned to the applicant.
Adding a Registered Operator
Adding a Registered Operator
    Adding a registered Operator is SC exempt. This should only be done if the original operator continues to use the vehicle after the transfer into joint names. It must not be used solely as a way to dispose of a vehicle without providing a valid safety certificate.
Concession Pro-Rata
Concession Pro-Rata
    • A pro-rata of concessional registration fees is raised and required to be paid if the acquirer is not eligible for a concession. The pro-rata fee is also raised for registration transfers that are lodged online.
    • A pro-rata raised which is greater than $10, is to be refunded when applying a Pension or Seniors concession (PSS1, PSS2, PVA1, PVA2, SENR, EXSC). Amounts under $10 are not be refunded unless requested by the customer.

Transfer of vehicle on alternative compliance
Transfer of vehicle on alternative compliance

    A vehicle currently recorded in a NHVAS scheme member’s maintenance module may be transferred to another person without a COI when the vehicle is remaining in a NHVAS scheme member's maintenance module.

    Check the vehicle is currently in the NHVAS maintenance module in TICA.

    If the NHVAS maintenance module is not current in TICA, real time verification of heavy vehicles in NHVAS can be found at: https://www.service.nhvr.gov.au/#page=informationHub/nHVASAVMSearch

    If the NHVAS maintenance module is current in the NHVR portal AVM search, access the contact details for NHVAS to email or call for recording NHVAS on vehicles.

    Advise the customer that the vehicle will need a COI if it does not remain current in NHVAS maintenance module.

        Road Rules
        Road Rules
          • Use footpaths or nature strips at all times, or if unsuitable, drive as close as possible to the left or right side of the road
          • Use the most direct route available when crossing a road and use a crossing where available
          • Travel at a speed not in excess of 10km p/hr
          • Exercise due care and attention in regard to the safety of others
          • Give way to other road users on a path or nature strip
          • Travel on the road alongside no more than one other pedestrian or vehicle travelling on the road in the same direction, unless it is necessary to overtake them
          • Comply with other road rules as they apply to pedestrians
          • Applicants will be mailed a copy of the road rules relating to motorised wheelchair use with the registration certificate following a new registration and each year with the annual registration certificate.

          More information about motorised wheelchair use can be found at: http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/registration/register/wheelchair/index.html

          • The auction house should transfer the vehicle from the registered operator into their name using the date the registered operator signed the release form to the insurer.
