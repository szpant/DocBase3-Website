Renewal of Ship Registration (1952)


What it isA renewal notice is sent to the registered operator informing them their registration is due for renewal.

The renewal notice should be received by the first registered operator at their mailing address approximately 4 weeks before the renewal date of the registration.

Note:If the first registered operator has signed up to receive e-notices then the registration renewal notice should be received by email approximately 6 weeks before the renewal date of the registration.

A ship is deemed to be registered when both of the following have been accepted by TMR or an authorised agent:
  • Application for renewal
  • Payment of appropriate fees

Renewal Notice Details
Renewal Notice Details
    Details on the Notice include:
    • Ship registration number
    • Registered operator(s)
    • Expiry date of the current registration(s)
    • Amounts payable
    • Payment method

    If the registration is enrolled in Direct Debit the Renewal Notice will also advise that:
    • the payment will be deducted as arranged; and
    • an email reminder will be sent before each automatic payment confirming the payment date and amount.
    Refer: Ship Rego > 5. Renewal and Replacements > Direct Debit
T.R.CTransaction Requirements Checklist
Requirements
Documents Required
Documents Required
    Renewals can be paid with:
    • Original renewal notice
    • Overdue Fees Final Notice
    • Evidence of Identity and verbal notice
    • Renewal Payment Without Notice form F3515 (when paying at offline agencies that are not using TICA)
Rules
Changes at Renewal
Changes at Renewal
    Changes cannot be made if the payer is not the registered operator. Additional procedures apply to the renewal without renewal notice if the payer is not the registered operator.
Certificates
Certificates
    A certificate of registration will be issued for the following transactions:
    • A registration period is varied
    • QRS registered by organisations without a recorded ACN

    No registration certificate will be sent on renewal or for New Business.

    When issued, the registration certificate will be posted to the customer. This should be received within 5 working days of when the payment has been processed by TMR.

    In rural areas it may take longer for mail delivery.

    Certificate Details
    Certificate Details

      A Registration Certificate contains:
      • Ship details
      • Registered operator details
      • Expiry date of the registration
Where to Renew
Timeframes for Payment
Timeframes for Payment
    On or before due date
    On or before due date
      • No consequences
      • Registration is current till midnight of the expiry date
      • New registration period starts on the day following the previous expiry date.

    Less than 90 days after due date
    Less than 90 days after due date
      • The ship is unregistered
      • It is an offence to drive the ship on the water
      • Registration is considered to be expired
      • 15 days after the expiry date the department will forward a reminder notice advising the departments intention to cancel the registration if not renewed within dates set
      • Registration may still be paid but it will be back dated to the expiry date (registration is valid only from the payment date)
        Note: In this case the registered operator may wish to cancel their registration then apply for New Business. This will register the ship with a new due date (Refer: New Business).

        However, this is not a valid cancellation reason and TMR does not have to accept it (Refer: Cancellations).

    Greater than 90 days after due date
    Greater than 90 days after due date
      • The ship registration is autocancelled
      • To continue registration of that particular ship the customer must follow new business procedure

    90 Day Delay Period
    90 Day Delay Period
      The department does not condone nor support late payment of registration fees. The current six week notice is sufficient time for registered operators to renew their registration.

      The delay period of 90 days is meant to cater for:
      • Payments received from off-line agencies
      • Some late payments
Methods of Payment
Proof of BPay/IVR Payment
Proof of BPay/IVR Payment
    Once the customer has completed the transaction for BPAY / IVRU renewals they are issued with a receipt number. This receipt number is proof of payment.
Renewal Payments in Advance
Renewal Payments in Advance
    The customer may pay a renewal up to 6 weeks in advance if they will be unavailable prior to the expiry date.

    Payment request has not yet been raised
    Payment request has not yet been raised
      Renewal payments in advance where the registration renewal payment request has not been raised must be conducted in person at a Customer Service Centre.

      As the payment request would not have been raised:
      • Determine fees (quote tool or contact Help Direct)
      • Pay money to customer account
      • Add text explaining payment (including registration number)
      • Give customer receipt
      • Pay registration once renewal payment request raised

    Payment request has been raised, notice not yet received
    Payment request has been raised, notice not yet received
      Where the registration renewal payment reference number has been generated, but the notice has not yet been received, the registered operator may contact the Call Centre to obtain the payment reference number to make payment using the standard payment methods.
Renewal Payments Cannot be Refunded
Renewal Payments Cannot be Refunded
    A paid renewal cannot be refunded without cancelling the registration.

    The registered operator can cancel the registration:
    • Prior to start of current period (full refund given)
    • After the current period has started (pro-rata refund given)

    They can re-register the ship if required.
    If a ship has been disposed of and the old registered operator inadvertently pays the renewal, it cannot be refunded.

    The matter is not TMR's responsibility. It should be dealt with between the new and old registered operators.
    Renewal Payment Accepted after Cancellation
    Renewal Payment Accepted after Cancellation
      Where payment of a renewal has been accepted after the registration has been cancelled, the payment will be refunded to the customer.

      A direction to apply for registration of the ship will accompany the refund, if it was an automatic cancellation.
Transferring Before Renewing
Transferring Before Renewing
    If the payer is not the registered operator, the CSO must question if the payer has recently acquired the ship.

    If the customer has purchased the ship full transfer requirements must apply before the renewal payment can be effected.

    If transfer not required, process the renewal.
Fixed Payment Reference Number (PRN)
Fixed Payment Reference Number (PRN)
    A fixed PRN is a number that will apply to all types of vehicle/vessel registration and will remain the same while the customer is the registered operator of the vehicle/vessel. If the registered operator changes, a new fixed PRN will be issued when the next renewal is issued to the new registered operator.
    Additional rules
    Additional rules
      • If the vessel has two registered operators, the PRN will remain the same as long as the vessel is registered to the primary registered operator.
      • Fixed PRN is the same regardless of the pay term
      • TMR should not promote changing a PRN, however in certain circumstances (such as domestic violence situation) a customer can request one. The operator must raise a SCO datafix to request the PRN be marked as inactive. When the next renewal is raised, a new fixed PRN will be allocated.
      • If a customer is issued with a new CRN, they will not be issued with a new fixed PRN.
      • Payments of less/more than the pay term amount are dealt with according to the VTF rules <direct link to page 3066> or returned to the payer's bank account if insufficient to vary the pay term.
