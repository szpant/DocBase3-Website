Amending/Imposing Conditions
Amending/Imposing Conditions
    Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program (AIIP)
    The Chief Executive may impose an ‘I’ condition on a Qld driver licence under the AIIP for a maximum period of two years (the interlock period). The first Qld driver licence issued to the person during the interlock period will incur an ‘I’ licence code fee, in addition to the standard licence fee, for the grant of the driver licence. Any subsequent driver licence renewal or replacement during the interlock period will not incur the ‘I’ condition fee.

    Refer: Fees and Forms > 1. Fees > Fees > Driver Licensing

    Medical Reasons
    The Chief Executive may decide to amend a condition on a Qld driver licence as a result of advice received that the holder has a medical condition that may affect their ability to drive safely.

    Refer:
    4. Medical Condition Reporting > Immediate Amendment (Medical)
    and
    4. Medical Condition Reporting > Medical Condition Reporting Guide > Action to be taken > Actions Required - Notification by the licence holder / Actions Required - Notification by a health professional / Actions Required - Notification by a third party

    S Condition
    An S condition may be added to or removed from a driver licence record by customer self declaration. The customer should declare on a driver licence application form (F3000) or a driver licence amendment form (F4358) that they do or do not require glasses / contact lenses for driving if the application is received at a non-formless centre. If customer presents at a formless centre a verbal declaration is suitable evidence to add/remove an S condition.

    A customer may also add or remove an S condition by contacting the TMR Customer Contact Centre or when renewing their licence online.

    Correspondence Template: Driver Licence - QLD Driver Licence - Add/Remove S Condition

    Other Reasons
    The Chief Executive may impose or amend a condition on a Qld driver licence. If the amendment is during the currency of the licence, a new licence may be issued free of charge showing the amended condition/s. A Driver Licence Amendment Agreement form F4358 must be completed by the licence holder.

    Specially Constructed Vehicle
    Specially Constructed Vehicle
    A specially constructed vehicle must not be used as a test vehicle for classes C, LR, MR, HR and HC. This type of vehicle is not representative of the type of vehicle that may be driven under these classes.

    If a class C, LR, MR, HR, HC or MC licence states that the person must only drive a vehicle fitted with automatic or synchromesh transmission, the person may only drive a specially constructed vehicle fitted with that type of transmission under that class of licence.


    Note:For foreign licence holders who are required to undertake a PDT in Qld to transfer their licence, any gearbox condition is determined by the type of vehicle used for the PDT in Qld (it is not determined by their foreign licence).

    Example: A foreign licence holder of an equivalent class C undertakes a PDT in an automatic car. A Qld Class CA is issued. The applicant then passes a PDT in a HR class vehicle with an automatic gearbox. The applicant will be issued a HR class with an A condition. The C class foreign licence cannot be used to endorse a B condition.
