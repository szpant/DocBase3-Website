Load Seals (7466)

Load Seals
What it isSecurity seals or load seals are mechanisms used to seal trailers, body trucks and shipping containers in a way that provides tamper evidence and some level of security. Such seals can help to detect theft or contamination, either accidental or deliberate.
Bonded Freight
Dealing with bonded freight
Dealing with bonded freight
    If the driver produces documentation proving the load is bonded and sealed by Australian Customs or Department of Primary Industries the seal cannot be broken under any circumstances.
    Other freight that may be bonded and must not to be opened include:
    • Alcohol
    • Tobacco
    Only an Inspector of Explosives can remove a seal on a vehicle loaded with explosives.
Removing Load SealsWARNING
Extreme caution is to be exercised where a substance appears to be leaking from a container or load. Before a container is opened or a load searched for leaking container/s, all reasonable efforts are to be made to identify the substance (shipping documents and vehicle markings) to establish that it is safe to conduct a search. If you have reasonable grounds to suspect that a leaking substance falls into the category of dangerous goods (DG) and are unable to identify the substance, the driver/operator is to be advised to contact the appropriate emergency service.

If the situation is unsafe move yourself and the driver to a safe place.

DO NOT put yourself or other road users at risk of harm

When can a seal be removed
When can a seal be removed
    Authorised Officers may only break the seals under the following conditions:
    • The inability of a driver or operator to supply information or documentation relating to proof of load; or
    • a substance appears to be leaking from a load or container, refer above warning; or
    • the marking displayed on the container or load appears to be incorrect; or
    • the documentation carried by the driver or operator is suspect; or
    • a driver or operator is unable to corroborate the shipping documentation; or
    • any other circumstance where an authorised officer believes on reasonable grounds that the goods being carried are not being carried in accordance with Transport Laws.
Loads sealed with padlocks
Loads sealed with padlocks
    Loads sealed with a padlock/s must not be forced in order to gain entry.
    If required a Direction/Prohibition notice may be issued.
Procedure to remove seal
Procedure to remove seal
Procedure to remove seal
    • The driver or owner representative must be present when a load seal is to be broken.
    • Advise the driver or operator that the seal is to be broken in order for the load to be examined.
    • Inform the driver or operator that the load will be resealed with an official TMR seal.
    • Details of the seal/s that is broken and replaced must be:
    • photographed before it is broken and after it is resealed.
    • noted in the TI official note book and
    • any Infringement Notice or Brief to Adjudicator if an offence can be proven.
    • The driver/operator is to be provided with the date, time, place and serial number of the TMR seal used.
    • Advise STI of the relevant details so the STI can update the register.
Tampering
Tampering
Tampering
    When a vehicle is intercepted with a TMR seal that appears to have been tampered with, the seal is to be removed in the presence of the driver/operator and another seal put in its place. Every effort should be made to establish who tampered with the seal. Full particulars of the driver/operator and their activities are to be obtained.

    The authorised officer is to provide the Senior Inspector with details of the seal serial number and any information gained from the driver relating to the fitting and alleged tampering of the original TMR seal.
TMR Official seals
TMR Official seals
Managing seals
Managing seals
    TMR has official seals that must be used to reseal a load when a seal is broken by an authorised officer inspect of a load.

    All Transport Inspectors (TI) should be issued official load seals.

    If a seal/s are lost/stolen the Senior Inspector must be advised within 24 hours of the loss being notice. The Senior Inspector will require the details of the serial number/s; location, time and efforts made to locate the missing seal/s
Seal Register
Seal Register
    All Compliance offices must have a seal register.

    The Senior Transport Inspector is responsible for maintaining the TMR seal register. The register must record:
    • TMR Seal serial number/s;
    • TI name and ID number it was assigned;
    • Date of issue.
    When a seal is used, the register must be updated to show:
    • Date and time used;
    • Location of the vehicle when the seal was applied;
    • Details of vehicles owner/operator; and
    • Vehicle registration.
    If a seal is lost or stolen it must be recorded in the seal register.

    The register must be reconciled to actual seals every 6 months.
