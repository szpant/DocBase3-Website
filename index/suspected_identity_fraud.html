Suspected Identity Fraud (2878)


What to do if Identity Fraud is Suspected:

1
Delay the customerWhere possible the customer should be retained to enable the checking of EOI documents and arrival of police, if necessary.

Exercise caution when dealing with a customer that you suspect is attempting to commit fraud. If the customer wishes to leave, do not stop them.
Suggested Strategies to Delay Customer
Suggested Strategies to Delay Customer
    • Ask the customer to take a seat
    • Explain that you are unable to issue the licence and additional checking is required
      Example statements:
      "Sorry for the delay, but I am required to check the details of your documents."
      or
      "Sorry for the delay, but I am required to get my supervisor's approval to accept these documents."
2
Verify Authenticity
of Customer Identity		If the documents appear to be fraudulent you may:
  • Verify their authenticity using the document checking guides in DocBase
  • Contact the document's issuing authority for verification
If the customer appears to not be who they claim to be and their claimed identity is already recorded on TICA:
  • Perform a 1:1 image comparison should an image be recorded and they are undertaking a smartcard transaction; and
  • Verify their identity using Section 1 of the Customer Identity Checklist F3985.

Note:After a successful 1:1 image comparison you may still suspect fraudulent activity if the EOI documents appear forged or tampered with, or the customer's responses to the Customer Identity Checklist are inaccurate.

It should be the responsibility of the customer to seek, obtain and provide to TMR, further documentation that verifies their identity and meets TMR requirements. It is not the responsibility of TMR to do this on behalf of the customer.
3
Discontinue TransactionThe transaction should be discontinued as soon as fraudulent activity is suspected.
4
Contact PoliceThe Senior Advisor or Manager should contact police as soon as fraudulent activity is confirmed.
5
Photocopy or Retain DocumentsWhere fraudulent activity is identified, and where possible, obtain photocopies of EOI, licences and retain application forms.

Note:Do not copy credit cards

Return original documents to the customer should they request such. Retain any products abandoned by the customer.
6
Enter Text on TICATo alert other operators should the customer go to another CSC, enter a text restriction on TICA outlining the details of the incident (it may be necessary to create the customer).
7
Document the IncidentIf fraudulent activity is suspected it is important to capture the circumstances and the details of the customer using the Customer Identity Checklist.
Customer Identity Checklist F3985 - Section 2
Customer Identity Checklist F3985 - Section 2
    Section 2 of the Customer Identity Checklist has been designed to capture the:
    • Customer's appearance details
    • Reasons fraud is suspected
    • Security camera film number
    • CSC operator details
    • Police reported to
    • Any additional information
    • Copies (or abandoned originals) of documents presented
    Note:Do not copy credit cards or tax file number advice, but note that they were presented
    This information will assist:
    • The police with their investigation
    • The CSC operator should they be required to provide a statement for court
8
Provide Information
to Police		Collate documents and products relevant to the incident and provide to attending police officer/s. Ensure that a Property Receipt is provided by the receiving police officer.

Any request by the police for security camera video footage of the incident is to be processed in accordance with relevant procedures as defined by Security Management.
9
Advise the Identity Management Unit (IMU)
Role of the Identity Management Unit (IMU)
Role of the Identity Management Unit (IMU)
    The IMU was established as a contact point for CSCs and the QPS Identity Fraud Squad to assist with actual and suspected instances of identity fraud.

    After a case of suspected fraud has been reported to the local police, it is
    important that the IMU is also advised. This is to ensure that:
    • Additional information about the outcome of the matter can be associated with any 1:1 image comparison failure incident that has occurred while trying to authenticate the identity of the customer
    • Details of all instances of identity fraud are collected in a central area for analysis and development of useful intelligence that can be shared with the CSC network and other agencies
    • The IMU can follow-up with QPS the case that the CSC has reported
Documents/Information Required by the IMU
Documents/Information Required by the IMU
    To maintain a case file, the IMU will require a copy of all documents relating to the incident including:
    • Driver Licence/Photo Identification Card Application Form
    • Interstate/Foreign driver licence
    • EOI documents produced (Note: Do not copy credit cards)
    • Details of what occurred and any action taken
    • The completed Customer Identity Checklist F3985, including the name, rank & station of the police officer to whom the matter was reported
Identity Management Unit (IMU)
10
Report the incident via the Safety and Wellbeing hotlineAll security incidents, including suspected identity fraud, must be reported through the Safety and Wellbeing hotline on 1800 867 947.

Further Information about Safety and Wellbeing
Further Information about Safety and Wellbeing
    For more information about Safety and Wellbeing, including incident reporting requirements, please refer to the TMR Internal Security website.
