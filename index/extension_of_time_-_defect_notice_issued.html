Extension of Time - Defect Notice Issued (7323)


Extension of Time - Defect Notice Issued
What is it?An Extension of Time (EOT) to comply with a defect notice allows a vehicle to legally operate on a road under specific conditions for the purpose of rectifying defects.

An EOT can also be issued to allow the registration to be paid for a heavy vehicle where the defects have not yet been rectified.

An Authorised Officer may issue an EOT to allow registration to be renewed for a vehicle that has been issued a defect notice as a result of failing a TMR Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI)
LegislationLight Vehicles
s29 Transport Operations Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010

Heavy Vehicles
s529AA, s529A Heavy Vehicle National Law
When Required
When Required
When Required
    An EOT to comply with a defect notice is required if by the end of the stated timeframes the defects have not been rectified.

    An EOT may be granted to vehicle subject to a defect notice where to Authorised Officer is satisfied that:
    • the vehicle will not pose a safety risk
    • the operator has taken reasonable steps to have the defect rectified with the original time frame.
When Not required
When Not required
When Not required
    An EOT to comply with a defect notice is not required if a vehicle subject to a:
    • minor defect notice has had the defects rectified and is otherwise compliant
    • major defect notice has had the defects rectified and/or is being taken to a place of inspection to enable the clearing of the defect and the vehicle is:
      • Not carrying goods and/or passengers and/or
      • Not a safety risk
Eligibility
Eligibility for an EOT to Comply with a Defect Notice
Defect Notice Eligibility
Defect Notice Eligibility
    An EOT to comply with a defect notice can be issued by an Authorised Officer if satisfied that:
    • the vehicle will only be used for the purpose of driving to and from a place where repairs are to be carried out (minor defect/self-clearing only): or
      • the operator had taken reasonable steps to rectify the defect within the original timeframe
      • the request is necessary and reasonable
      • the use of the vehicle does not pose a safety risk
Applying for an EOT to Comply with a Defect NoticeThe operator must submit an Extension of Time Application form F2483 to the relevant Compliance office for assessment.
Assessing the Application
Assessing the Application
Assessing the Application
    When considering an EOT application where the repairs have not been completed the Authorised Officer should:
    • evaluate each application on a case-by-case basis
    • only authorise the EOT if the reason for the request is appropriate; and
    • advise the owner of the outcome
EOT to Pay Registration after Failing PVI
Failed PVI
Failed PVI
    • An Authorised Officer may issue an EOT to allow registration to be renewed for a vehicle that has failed a TMR programmed vehicle inspection for a COI. An EOT application form F2483 is not required under these conditions.
    • The conditions, as per the defect notice, still apply. E.g. to be driven in daylight hours only.
    • An EOT can be issued to a grounded vehicle. This allows the registration to be renewed, but the vehicle cannot be driven on the road because the defect notice prohibits its use.
    • The EOT should only be issued up to the day after the date for the defect notice clearance.
    • Where the defect notice has been issued by QPS or an interstate authority the customer must be referred to the issuing authority.
