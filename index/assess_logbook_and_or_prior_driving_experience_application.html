Checklist for Assessing Supervised on Road Driving Experience

Signatures The Driving Experience Declaration (F4464) and/or Prior Driving Experience Application (F4450) has been completed and signed by:
  • The learner licence holder
    and
  • Supervising Driver (F4450 only)
Note:The date signed must be on or later than the date of completing their 100 hours of supervised on-road driving experience.
OR
  • The applicant has logged their application via the learner logbook app; and
  • All entries have been verified by their supervisor using email verification
Check learner logbook fields
(if submitted)
  • Date
  • Location (From and To)
  • Vehicle Registration Number
  • Odometer readings (Start and Finish)
  • Total number of minutes for each type of experience (accredited driver trainer, daytime and night)
  • Time (Start, Finish and total minutes)
  • Driving Supervisor details:
    • First name and surname
    • Licence/Accreditation number
    • Licence State/territory
    • Signature
    • Driving school (where applicable)
Confirm completed Driving Time
Note:Operators are not required to verify the accuracy of every logbook entry

Total driving experience is a minimum of either:
  • 6000 minutes for drivers required to complete 100 hours
Total night driving is a minimum of (unless exempt on medical grounds):
  • 600 minutes for drivers required to complete 10 hours
Note:The mandatory night driving experience (10 hrs) is part of the 100 hrs minimum total requirement
Recorded as 1:3 ratio up to 600 minutes (10 hours) actual driving time
Accredited Driver Trainer experience may be counted:
  • As triple the amount of time actually spent driving (1:3 ratio)
  • Up to a maximum of 600/1800 actual minutes (10 hours)

For example, 600 actual minutes can be recorded as 1800 minutes in the Accredited Driver Trainer column of the learner logbook.

Note:Any time that exceeds the 600 actual minutes is to be recorded using the 1:1 ratio in the relevant Day or Night columns.

Night Driving
Night driving experience with an Accredited Driver Trainer can be recorded:
  • At a ratio of 1:3 in the Accredited Driver Trainer column of the logbook
  • Up to a maximum of 600 actual minutes recorded as 1800 minutes.
Important - Each driving experience can only be recorded in one column of the learner logbook

Any night driving experience with an accredited driver trainer that is recorded at 1:3 cannot be recorded in the night driving column and cannot be counted towards the 600 minute night driving requirement.

Example
Example
    For 100 hours logbook requirement:
    600 actual minutes experience with an Accredited Driver Trainer at night can be recorded using the 1:3 ratio as 1800 minutes in the Accredited Driver Trainer column.

    A further 600 actual minutes of night driving experience must be completed and recorded in the night driving column at a 1:1 ratio.

    The remaining minutes of driving experience can be gained during day or night and recorded at a ratio of 1:1.

    Alternatively the 600 minutes of driving experience gained at night with an Accredited Driver Trainer can also be recorded at a 1:1 ratio in the night driving column of the learner logbook. Up to 600 minutes of driving experience (day or night) with an Accredited Driver Trainer can be recorded in the Accredited Driver Trainer Column using the 1:3 ratio.

    The remaining minutes of driving experience can be gained during day or night and recorded at a ratio of 1:1.
Additional ChecksIf information is received, or there are concerns about the accuracy of the supervised on road driving experience documentation, the following additional checks can be completed to help establish if the documentation should be referred for further investigation:
Vehicle Registration
Vehicle Registration
    • Do the vehicle registrations exist in TICA/ NEVDIS?
    • Were the vehicle registrations current at time of driving experience?
    • Were vehicle registrations stolen or written off at the time of driving experience?
Driving Records
Driving Records
    • Do odometer readings correspond with locations recorded?
      Eg. Distance travelled from Brisbane to Gold Coast is recorded at 300 km. By road the distance from Brisbane to Gold Coast is approximately 100 km.
    • Do driving times correspond with odometer reading?
      Eg. Start 12:00pm - finish 1:30pm distance travelled 250km. In this instance if experience was gained on a highway, the distance would be approximately 150 km.
    • Does location correspond with driving time?
      Eg. Brisbane to Maroochydore was recorded as 240 mins/ 4 hours. Realistic time travelled from Brisbane to Maroochydore should be recorded as 90 minutes.
    • Inconsistencies with odometer readings on different experience dates.
      Eg. An odometer reading for a vehicle is lower than on a preceding trip for the same vehicle.

    Note:Refer to the brochure Guide to Queensland Roads produced by TMR for more information.
Driving Supervisor
Driving Supervisor
    • Is licence a class C open licence or equivalent?
    • Does the licence or accreditation number exist in TICA?
    • Are signatures of same supervisor signed in different handwriting?
    • Are signatures of different supervisors signed in similar handwriting?
    • Is the supervisor deceased?
    • Has all driving been supervised by an interstate or foreign licence holder?
    • Does the driving school exist?
Other considerations
Other considerations
    • Are learner logbook entries written in the same ink?
      This may indicate that all learner logbook entries were completed on the one day and may not be authentic.

      Note:If a learner receives a replacement learner logbook (whether lost or confiscated), entries may be written in the same ink, however signatures should be different.
    • Learner logbook appears too new for its age.

The preceding additional checks are to be used as a guideline only, and if found, it does not necessarily mean that the logbook is inaccurate. When determining if the documentation is to be referred to Prosecutions Unit for further investigation, the assessing officer should consider all of the elements that they believe to be inaccurate or deficient and whether there is a legitimate reason for them to have occurred. Indications that may raise doubt include multiple entries where doubt exists or a combination of a number of suspicious elements.

Refer to your manager/ supervisor before escalating to Prosecutions Unit
Decision within 28 daysThe Chief Executive must decide to approve or not to approve the learner logbook within 28 days after receiving the logbook or further information or documents relevant to the application.

If the Chief Executive does not decide the application before the expiry of the 28 day period, the Chief Executive is taken to have approved the learner logbook.
