Tow Truck Premises/Holding Yard/Vehicle Inspections (7303)



Tow Truck Premises/Holding Yard/Vehicle Inspections
What it isA premises/holding yard/tow truck vehicle inspection is an on-site compliance inspection of a premises/holding yard/vehicles intended to be used to operate a tow truck business.
When requiredThe inspection is required to be completed:
  • Prior to a new Tow Truck Licence being approved
  • When a change of premises has been notified
  • When a change of holding yard has been notified
  • Within 10 business days of the application being received
PurposeThe purpose of this inspection is to ensure:
  • The business has the required processes in place to operate a compliant regulated tow truck business
  • The premises is suitable for use as a tow truck holding yard
  • The vehicles are suitable for use as a tow truck

Inspection Process
Notification of Inspection Requirement
Notification of Inspection Requirement
    Once an application has been lodged at a Customer Service Centre it will be scanned and emailed to the relevant Compliance Office. You will be required to have completed the inspection within 10 business days of receiving the application.

    Once received the Transport Inspector is required to:
    • Contact the nominated operator/director to arrange a suitable time for the inspection to occur.
    • Advise the operator/director of what will occur and what documentation is required to be made available. Refer: Checklist for Premises and Holding Yard > Checklist for Application for Approved Tow Truck Premises and Holding Yard (below)
Unsuccessful Inspections
Unsuccessful Inspections
    Where the premises, holding yards or vehicles are found to be non-compliant the operator/director should be given an opportunity to rectify the non-compliance.

    The Transport Inspector should:
    • inform the operator/director of what needs to be rectified and provide a Corrective Action with timeframes of no more than 3 months for the required actions to be taken
    • Notify by email the lodging CSC advising of the delay
    • Retain the paperwork on site for later completion

    Corrective Action Request Template

    Upon re-inspection if the premises/holding yards:
    • Comply the application should be approved and finalised
    • Remain non-compliant the inspection should be failed and finalised
Finalising the Inspection
Finalising the Inspection
    The following compliance documents must be completed, signed and emailed to the lodging/receipting CSC and tow.trucks@tmr.qld.gov.au:
    • Tow Truck Licence – Premises/Holding Yard Inspection (F5213)
    • Tow Truck Licence – Vehicle Inspection (F5212)
    There is no requirement to send photographs.

    The original documents and supporting evidence must be entered into the Document Management System (DMS).

Licence Holder Requirements
Licence Holder Requirements
    The licence holder must make available for inspection:
    • Evidence of local government approval for both the premises and holding yards
    • Proof of ownership/lease of premises for both the premises and holding yards
    • Evidence of 24 hour / 7 days operation
    • Communication system
    • Crash/Seizure towing register if undertaking this activity
    • Private Property Towing Register if undertaking this activity
    • Property Inventory Book
Transport Inspector Requirements
Transport Inspector Requirements
    The Transport Inspector is required to complete Tow Truck Licence - Premises/Holding Yard Inspection (F5213) and confirm that the premises/holding yards meets the requirements detailed on the form.

    The Transport Inspector should take sufficient photographs to support the decision to deem the premises/holding yards compliant
Evidence of ownership/lease of premises/holding yard/s
Evidence of ownership/lease of premises/holding yard/s
    The applicant must provide evidence that they own or lease the business premises and each holding yard.

    Evidence may include but is not limited to:
    • Lease agreement
    • Title deeds
    • Rates notices
Local Government Approval
Local Government Approval
    An applicant must provide evidence of approval from the Local Government (council) that the business premises and holding yard conforms with the requirements of the local government for the area.

    Online self-assessments conducted by an applicant are acceptable however if its authenticity is doubtful, they must provide written evidence from the relevant local government.
Vehicle InspectionAn inspection of the licence holders tow trucks is required:
  • For all vehicles listed on a new applications
  • When a vehicle is added to the licence

The purpose of this inspection is to ensure that the tow trucks comply with the requirements for:
  • Modifications
  • Inspections
  • Registration (purpose of use of LTOW)
  • Markings excluding tow truck numbers which are issued by TMR after the application is approved
Licence Holder Requirements
Licence Holder Requirements
    The licence holder is required to:
    • Make all tow trucks available for inspection
    • Produce correct modification certificates for each vehicle (unless it is a purpose built tow truck)
Transport Inspector Requirements
Transport Inspector Requirements
    The Transport inspector is required to complete Tow Truck Licence - Vehicle Inspection (F5212) following inspection of each tow trucks to confirm:
    • Inspect each tow truck as per Schedule 1 Tow Truck Specification in the Tow Truck Regulations 2009
    • Appropriate modification plates are attached to the vehicles
    • That the markings comply
    • That the vehicle complies with its category requirements eg load capacity, winch and any other equipment

    The Transport Inspector should take sufficient photographs to support the decision to deem the vehicles compliant/non-compliant.

    Refer: Transport Compliance > 11. Tow Trucks > Tow Truck Guide for the specific vehicle requirements
