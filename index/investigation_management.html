Investigation Management (7488)



Investigation Management and Timeframes
What it isInvestigation management time frames are designed to bring consistency to investigations undertaken by the TMR Compliance.

The time frames provided are the expected investigation time frames, PAIs or PCMs may alter these time frames to suit local priorities, resources and workloads.

The time frames are not intended to compromise the quality of an investigation.


Note:Investigations undertaken by Senior Investigators (SIs) under the supervision of the PCM will follow these guidelines.
Guidelines
Guidelines
Guidelines
    • The time frames are a guide for the maximum duration investigations of a non-complex nature are expected to take to complete.
    • The time frames are not designed to be a ‘work to’ time frame. Investigations that can be completed sooner should be completed as expeditiously as possible.
    • STIs are expected to be knowledgeable of each investigation being conducted under their supervision.
    • STIs are responsible and have discretion of the investigation management.
    • TIs are responsible for conducting investigations and working to the expected time frames.
    • TIs are responsible for arranging investigation reviews with their STI.
Forwarding Investigations
Forwarding InvestigationsWhere it is not practical to investigate an offence committed in another region, any evidence is to be forwarded to the region where the offence was committed for their assessment to determine if an investigation should proceed.

Procedure
Procedure
    TI responsibility:
    TI responsibility:
      • Discuss the case issue with their STI.
      • If approval is given, create a case in CID clearly outlining the facts as known.
      • Use the template to draft a Briefing Note to your STI, outlining the facts and evidence with a recommendation to forward the case to an STI in the region of the alleged offender for their information and action as they deem necessary.
      • The Briefing Note template can be can be modified to suite any investigation.
      • Replace the red text in the Briefing Note with the appropriate details.
      • Update CID.
        Briefing Note - Transfer to Another Region 2020
    STI responsibility:
    STI responsibility:
      • Assess evidence using the Brief To Adjudicator (BTA) checklist as a guide to assess the evidence collected is sufficient for the investigation to proceed. Discuss any issues identified with the TI.
      • If proceeding discuss the case with the receiving STI.
        • If agreed to transfer the investigation, forward the case to the relevant STI for investigation.
        • Forward evidence via post, email or T drive.
      • Update CID and assign case to receiving STI.
    Receiving STI responsibility:
    Receiving STI responsibility:
      • Review the evidence supplied.
      • Assign the case to a TI.
      • Maintain CID.
    Receiving TI responsibility:
    Receiving TI responsibility:
      • Conduct investigation within the required time frames (see below).
      • Maintain CID.
Responsibilities and
Time frames
Proceedings for offences must be started within 12 months from the date of the alleged offence.

Infringement Notices must be issued within 12 months of the date of the alleged offence.
Investigation Plan
Investigation Plan
    • A TI allocated an investigation should prepare an Investigation Plan for approval by the STI, within 5 days.
    • The STI must review the Investigation Plan with the TI, approve the plan as soon as possible and return it to allow the TI to commence the investigation.

    Compliance Investigation Plan - September 2019
Investigation
Investigation
    Within 14 days
    Within 14 days
      • TI will update the STI on the investigation status, including:
        • progress,
        • outstanding enquiries,
        • time frames and
        • potential issues or extenuating circumstances.
      • TI will update CID as required
      • STI will review gathered evidence
      • STI will review the investigation.
      • STI and TI will agree on actions, lines of enquiry and time frames.
    Within 21 days
    Within 21 days
      • TI will case conference the investigation status with the STI and either the SI or PAI.
      • STI will review the investigation.
      • STI will check the TI has appropriately updated CID
      • STI and TI will agree on actions, lines of enquiry and time frames.
    Within 28 days
    Within 28 days
      • A simple investigation should be completed unless extenuating circumstances exist.
      • If extenuating circumstances exist the STI must be briefed and approve the extension of time.
    Investigation outcome
    Investigation outcome
      • If a PIN or no further action (NFA) is the agreed outcome of the investigation the TI is to submit a Briefing Note to the STI for approval. Once approved the TI issues the PIN (if required). All investigation documentation is to be DMS'd and CID updated.
      • If a Brief To Adjudicator (BTA) is the agreed outcome, the TI submits the BTA to the STI for approval. Once approved the STI forwards the BTA to Prosecution Unit and updates CID.

    Protracted investigations may require weekly reviews as deemed necessary by STI/SI/PAI.
