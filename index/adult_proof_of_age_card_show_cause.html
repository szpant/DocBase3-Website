Photo Identification Card Show cause (3373)

Photo Identification Card Show cause
What it isIf the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to cancel a person's Photo Identification (Photo ID) Card (or Adult Proof of Age Card issued prior to 1 April 2019), the person must be given written notice to invite the person to show cause, within a specified time, why the proposed action should not be taken.
Photo ID Card Act 2008 s15
GroundsThe Photo ID Card was issued because of a false or misleading document or representation that was given at the time of application, for example, the person presented another person's birth certificate when applying for the Photo ID card.
Photo ID Card Act 2008 s14
Notify TMRTMR may receive advice/documentation from the following persons about a Photo ID Card holder.
Who can notify

Third Party

Decision to show cause
Decision to Show Cause
Decision to Show Cause
    No Grounds for Show Cause
    If the written advice/documentation received does not satisfy the chief executive that reasonable grounds exist to show cause the holder, the chief executive may take no further action, or communicate further with the person who gave the advice/documentation.

    Grounds to Show Cause
    A Show Cause action to cancel the Photo ID Card must commence if the written advice/documentation does satisfy the chief executive that reasonable grounds exist.

    Notices
    The following notices have been structured to meet the requirements of Section 13 of the Photo ID Card Act 2008.

    Show Cause to Cancel
    This notice is to be given when it is proposed to cancel the Photo ID Card from a stated date because of false or misleading documents or representations.
Photo ID Card Act 2008 s13

    Show Cause-Cancel - Photo ID False or misleading documents or representations - April 2019
Decision of Show Cause - Confirm decision to cancel
Decision of Show Cause - Confirm decision to cancel
    This notice is to be given when the decision to cancel the Photo ID Card is confirmed.
Photo ID Card Act 2008 s15

    The notice will direct the person to return the Photo ID Card 14 days after the date of the notice unless the person has a reasonable excuse (lost, stolen or destroyed).

    Decision of Show Cause - Photo ID Confirm decision to cancel - April 2019

    When the Show Cause - Confirm decision to cancel notice is given, a second copy of the notice must be printed and filed in the office.

    If the Photo ID Card holder has not returned the card by the stated date in the notice, the Manager (Prosecutions) will request the second copy to be mailed to Prosecutions Unit (Manager).
Decision of Show Cause - Set Aside decision to cancel
Decision of Show Cause - Set Aside decision to cancel
    This notice is to be given when the decision is to set aside the decision to cancel the Photo ID Card and take no further action
Photo ID Card Act 2008,s15

    Decision of Show Cause - Photo ID Set Aside decision to cancel-April 2019
Internal review
Internal Review original decision
Internal Review original decision
    Internal Review
    The holder may ask the Chief Executive for a review of the decision to cancel the Photo ID Card if the holder's interests are affected by the decision.
Photo ID Card Act 2008 s39

    Application Requirements
    The applicant may apply in writing within 28 days of the original decision notice and include any supporting documentation

    Where to apply
    Applications may be lodged by:
    Mail to the responsible Director of the area where the decision was made:
    Decision Type & Responsible Director's address

      Cancelling Photo ID Card
    or
    Delivering to a CSC/Agency, who may then forward to the responsible Director

    Decision
    The Regional Director must review the original decision within 28 days after the date the review application is made and either:
    • Confirm the original decision to cancel the Photo ID Card
    • Set aside the original decision to cancel the Photo ID Card.

    The Regional Director must promptly give the applicant written notice of the decision.

    Notices
    The following notices have been structured to meet the requirements of Section 39 of the Photo ID Card Act 2008
