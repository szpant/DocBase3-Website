Takata Airbag Recall Project Update (7378)

Project NameTakata Airbag recall – Defect Notice and Registration Action
WhenFrom late November 2018
WhereStatewide
Important NoteRefer to the Takata Airbag Recall page in DocBase for latest information. The Project Update has been retained to house background information, Posters, FAQ's, Courtesy Notice and FactSheet information.
BackgroundIn the interests of public safety, TMR is assisting industry with reducing the number of vehicles on the Queensland roads with high risk alpha airbags.

In June 2018 Transport Ministers in every state of Australia provided support to the Federal Government by agreeing to impose sanctions against those vehicle registered operators who had not complied with the compulsory recall. These sanctions include the issue of a vehicle defect notice with a proposal to cancel the vehicle registration if the faulty airbags are not replaced within 21 days.

Initially TMR will target those customers who the ACCC have advised still have Aplha Takata airbag/s fitted and where multiple attempts have been made by the respective vehicle manufacturers to contact the customer.

On 13 November 2019, courtesy letters (attached below) were sent to all identified customers with Takata airbags fitted to a registered vehicle, advising them of the proposed action and giving them 14 days to action the replacement with their manufacturer. If no action is taken during this time or the customer does not contact TMR to request an extension, a defect notice will be issued to the vehicle.

Takata Courtesy Letter to High-Risk Vehicle Owners

Registered operators will be given 21 days to action the Defect Notice. This timing has been based on advice from the ACCC that vehicle manufacturers must replace high risk alpha airbags within 3 weeks of receiving a request for replacement.

If a person contacts their local dealer / manufacturer and is unable to have the vehicle fixed in this time they will be able to seek an extension of time.

Failure to comply with the defect notice may result in cancellation of registration for the vehicle via the show cause process. This show cause process will follow the standard 28 day period.

Customers should be urged to contact their vehicle manufacturer as soon as possible to arrange replacement of the airbags. Requests for extension of time should be directed by email to QLDtakatarecall@tmr.qld.gov.au.

Additionally text will be added to unregistered vehicles in TICA that still have Alpha Takata airbags fitted, advising that the vehicle cannot be registered until advice is received from the manufacturer that the airbags have been replaced.

Note:Extension of time request must be emailed unless it is an emergent issue, eg customer presents to the CSC and requires extension of time to allow transaction. In emergent situations only, Compliance can be contacted on 38138623.

Imported Vehicles and PDT
The ACCC have advised that any personal imported vehicles that have airbags fitted are unable to be checked to verify if they fall under the Takata Airbag Recall. Therefore, a decision has been made to make all personally imported vehicles with airbags fitted, to be ineligible for a PDT.
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers with vehicles listed with a high risk Takata Airbag
Customers with vehicles listed with a high risk Takata Airbag
    Only customers with vehicles listed as still containing a high risk Takata alpha airbag are affected by the defect notice and possible show cause registration action if the issue is not fixed.

    Two of the five affected manufacturers will provide specific information to the ACCC on vehicles within Queensland with high risk alpha airbags fitted. This initial action is estimated to be less than 300 vehicle holders in Queensland.

    The information from the other three manufacturers is not expected until the end of the year and is likely to affect an additional 500 – 1,000 vehicle owners in Queensland, depending on the success of the manufacturer communications.
Driving examiners
Driving examiners
    The department will commence action in late November to issue defect notices for vehicles that have been affected by the Takata Airbag Recall (Alpha version only).

    Prior to taking a vehicle out for a Practical Driving Test (PDT) Driving Examiners (DE) should check that the vehicle has not been affected by the Takata Airbag Recall (only required for Alpha/Critical version), or is not a personally imported vehicle that is fitted with airbags.

    The DE can go to the Product Safety Australia website and check the make and model of the vehicle presented for PDT. If the make and model are listed with or notation; only then do you need to conduct further searches.

    If the vehicle is recorded as being fitted with Alpha/Critical type airbags, DEs can check the vehicle registration on the Is My Airbag Safe website. The links will then lead you to the manufacturers website which will advise if the vehicle has been cleared. As there may be delays in updating the website, the DE can also request the customer provide evidence from the manufacturer or an authorised repairer that the airbags have been replaced.

    If the customer is unable to provide this evidence, the DE should inform the customer that the vehicle is unable to be used on a driving test due to safety concerns.

    If the customer presents a vehicle that has been personally imported, and has airbags fitted, then the CSO/DE should inform the customer that the vehicle is unable to be used for a driving test due to safety concerns. This is because there is no way for TMR to determine if a Takata Airbag has been fitted or, if fitted, it has been replaced. If the customer advises that the vehicle did have a faulty Takata airbag and produces evidence that it has been replaced, the vehicle may be used.

    If the customer is required to reschedule the PDT they must not be charged another booking fee to reschedule.

Further informationAirbags are key occupant safety features in modern light vehicles. Takata Corporation of Japan was a major global supplier of airbags to various automakers.

Due to identification of certain serious defects in Takata airbag deployment, there is a global safety recall underway at present to replace the faulty airbags in light vehicles. As part of this Australia initiated a compulsory recall of affected vehicles on 28 February 2018.

Two types of Takata airbags, 'alpha' and 'beta' airbags have been identified as defective and are being recalled.

The alpha airbags present a significantly higher risk to vehicle occupants due to their age, especially when operated in areas with high humidity. Experts indicate that the risk of these alpha airbags being deployed dangerously is as high as 50 per cent. Consumers are urged to take immediate steps to have their airbags replaced if their car has a Takata alpha airbag installed.

Despite media campaigns and multiple attempts to contact vehicle holders, as of July 2018, there were approximately 1.6 million vehicles in Australia with defective airbags that have not yet been replaced. Of these, as of September 2020, there are still over 90,000 vehicles to be repaired with 15,912 of these vehicles in Queensland. Further information can be found on the ACCC website https://www.accc.gov.au/update/thousands-of-deadly-airbags-remain-on-queensland-streets

To encourage customers to take action and minimise the number of vehicles that would otherwise become subject to registration sanctions, printed materials have been distributed to centres in areas with a high volume of outstanding vehicles.
