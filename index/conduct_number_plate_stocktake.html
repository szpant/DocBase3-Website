Conduct Number Plate Stocktake (2810)

Applies to
Applies to
    Undertaking a stocktake at a specific SLOC.
1
Run Required Reports
Run Required Reports
    Print 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check sheet to confirm stocks held against those listed in the interACT. interACT will allow authorised users to print an 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check sheet by individual plate type or for all plate types.

    If also due, the stock taking officer will also print a 21D-1040 Inventory Ageing by Plate Type and Location and 21D-1080 Inventory Movements by Month report from Reports tab in interACT.

    The movement report will detail the numbers of plates issued in the ‘stock out column’ and should be run for the period from the last stock take (if less than six months) or from 1 July. This will give the stocktaking officer an idea of how many of each type of plates are being used. This will be important to determine usage.

    The 21D-1040 Inventory Ageing report should be printed for that SLOC using 1, 2 and 3 ageing periods. The stocktaking officer should have discussions with the SLOC about stock that is being held for longer than 2 years to determine whether this stock should be removed from the SLOC. Action must be taken if a dealership is holding aged number plates 2 years and over. Refer: Admin > 4. Stock Control > Managing Stock > Stocktakes > Plate Management

    A copy of the report should be sent to the Plates Administrator upon completion of the stocktake.
2
Undertake the Physical Stocktake
Undertake the Physical Stocktake
    Undertake the physical stocktake and make a manual note of any discrepancies on the 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check sheet.
3
Record Adjustments in interACT
Record Adjustments in interACT
    Take any actions required to record adjustments necessary in interACT (for example, Transfer plates at wrong location, plate issued on TICA but failed to interface with interACT).

    This may require one of the following steps to be performed:
    • Write stock on that has been incorrectly written off;
    • Write stock off that has been lost or stolen (refer to section 5.3 for when to write off stock);
    • Manually issue or reverse an issue of plates;
    • Transfers of stock in or out.
4
Actions to take if adjustments are required
Actions to take if adjustments are required
    Should adjustments be required, print an 21D-1030 Manual Inventory Transactions report from the SLOC report menu.

    When the adjustment(s) have been completed, this report is printed and a comparison made between the notation for the stock discrepancies on the 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check sheet and the adjustments made that are noted on this report. If both are the same, continue on to the Manager approval stage. If they are not the same, carry out the next adjustment(s) until each adjustment is done and you are satisfied that the In Stock Plate Check sheet reflects the stock on hand.
5
Reprint the 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check Sheet
Reprint the 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check Sheet
    Reprint the 21D-1010 In Stock Plate Check.

    The system will generate the report at the current date and should list all plates that are held. The adjustments made will no longer be on this report and it will contain the following information:
    • Plate Type or range of plate types
    • List of Plate characters
    • Quantity
    • Status
    • Provision for Comments / Action field to note discrepancies and actions to resolve discrepancies
    • The stocktake officer
6
Approval
Approval
    Verifying/Checking Officer to co-sign and approve.

    Take these sheets to a verifying/checking officer who will review them to ensure they are appropriate. The counting officer and verifying/checking officer must both sign and write their userid on the report. The report is then filed.

    Solo Sites to email the Stocktake to their supporting CSC Manager who will return approved report for retention at the Solo Site.
7
Email Report (if required)
Email Report (if required)
    If this stocktake included an 21D-1040 Inventory Ageing by Plate Type and Location, regardless of the findings, a copy of the report must be forwarded to Plates Administration. Plates Administration will make a determination on whether or not this stock needs to be rotated.
