Authorised Officers Moving Heavy Vehicles (5173)

Authorised Officers Moving Heavy Vehicles
What it isThe powers conferred on Authorised Officers (AOs) to move vehicles is provided in TORUM Act s33 and s516-519 of the Heavy Vehicle National Law (QLD). The provisions in this legislation give increasing powers to AOs to address situations of increased risk. Only in very specific circumstances may an AO move a heavy vehicle without holding the appropriate licence.
When an Authorised Officer may move a heavy vehicle
Prescribed Dangerous Goods Vehicles
Prescribed Dangerous Goods Vehicles
    Vehicle operator is at the scene
    Vehicle operator is at the scene
      The AO is not permitted to move the vehicle. The AO may only require the person in control, or operator, to move the vehicle. TORUM Act s33A.

    Vehicle is unattended and AO intends to exercise a power under TORUM Act
    Vehicle is unattended and AO intends to exercise a power under TORUM Act
      Vehicle is unattended and AO intends to exercise a power under TORUM Act and reasonably believes it is necessary to move the vehicle to enable the power to be exercised. The AO may take steps that are reasonably necessary to move the vehicle, TORUM Act s33B. The AO may only drive or authorise someone else to drive the vehicle if they hold a valid licence for the class of vehicle.

    Vehicle is unattended or broken down, and is causing or creating an imminent risk
    Vehicle is unattended or broken down, and is causing or creating an imminent risk
      In this case the AO may drive the vehicle, or authorise someone else to drive it, if the AO reasonably believes the vehicle is drivable and there is no one else in or near the vehicle who is more capable, fit and willing to drive it. TORUM Act s33(6)(4) provides that in this situation the AO or assistant is not required to be licensed. Advice should be sought from an STI before driving the vehicle.
Other Heavy vehicles
Other Heavy vehicles

    Authorised Officers (AO) do not have the power to move a heavy vehicle by driving it, if they do not hold a valid licence for the class of vehicle unless the vehicle on a road is unattended, and is causing or creating an imminent risk of serious harm to public safety, the environment or road infrastructure or obstructing traffic or likely to obstruct traffic. In this situation the AO is permitted to drive the vehicle if the AO believes there is no-one else in or near the vehicle who is more capable, and fit and willing to drive it. Advice should be sought from an STI before driving the vehicle..

    National Law confers powers on AO to direct the driver or operator of a heavy vehicle to move the vehicle or cause it to be moved to a stated reasonable place. Under TORUM Act s33 the AO may move the vehicle if they a valid licence for the class of vehicle.
