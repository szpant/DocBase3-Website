Webchat (7079)

Project NameWebchat
WhenPilot will commence on 7 December 2016
BackgroundWith a Whole of Government focus on digital first and improving our online services, coupled with the high usage of TMR online services in the community, a need has been created to support customers in their information seeking and/or transactional activities when using TMR online services. Currently, when customers have difficulties either understanding information or navigating the website, they will abandon their attempts and return to the traditional contact methods of phone or face to face.

To support the migration to channels that meet the customer expectations such as online, CSB is introducing webchat to support customers in their online experience.

CSB has elected to progress with a six month webchat pilot, which will enable the chat function on a selected range of TMR website pages (more information in the section 'What is included in the service'). If the pilot proves successful, CSB may investigate continuation of the service and possible expansion to additional TMR online services and supporting content pages.
How does the service workAs a result of the webchat implementation, customers will be able to chat to TMR operators online in real time during operating hours. The operators will be working in the call centre at Carseldine and are known as chat agents.

If the customer is visiting any of the webpages included in the pilot (and an agent is available to chat), they will see a blue icon at the bottom of their screen, indicating that live help is available.

Additional rules have been included which will initiate a pop up window, proactively inviting the customer to chat. Examples of these rules are;
  • The customer has remained on the page for a defined length of time with no further activity.
  • The customer navigates from the page to the ‘Need help’ or ‘Contact us’ page.
  • The customer abandons the page.

Clicking on the button or accepting the invitation will open the chat window, which will enable customers to type their questions and receive a response from a TMR chat agent. Whilst chatting, the customer will be able to continue navigating the TMR website. It is also possible for the TMR operator to ‘co-browse’, which will enable them to see the site that the customer is seeing, and watch their interactions with the site.

What is included in the serviceWebsite pages / services
The webchat pilot will include a range of pages associated with TMR online services, which have been selected based on research relating to complaints and abandonment rates:
  • Renew driver licence
  • Replace driver licence
  • Check demerit points
  • Pay infringement/fine
  • Renew registration
  • Enrol in direct debit
  • Change of address

Operating hours
The webchat service will be offered on weekdays between 8am and 5pm, then 6pm to 9pm.
What will not be included in the serviceDuring the pilot the webchat functionality will not be offered on any TMR website pages that are not associated with the TMR online services listed above.

Note:Support via webchat will be available to support customers through the online authentication process, with the exception of the QGov login method.
Who will be impacted and how
How will this impact customers?
How will this impact customers?
    Customers will be able to chat to TMR operators online in real time, during the chat agent operating hours. As chat is only initiated if a chat agent is available, the immediacy and real time nature of the chat combined with the co-browsing feature is expected to lead to greater issue resolution and transaction fulfilment online.
How will this impact CSB staff?
How will this impact CSB staff?
    Staff should be aware that webchat will soon be offered as an alternative contact method for certain online services.

    Webchat option has popped up?
    Webchat is a free online service that TMR is trialling to allow you to communicate in real time with a customer service representative.
