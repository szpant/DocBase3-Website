Registration Show Cause (3259)


What it isIf the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to cancel vehicle registration, the registered operator must be given written notice inviting them to show cause, within a specified time frame, why the proposed action should not be taken. (Section 19 Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995)
Grounds
Grounds for Show Cause
Grounds for Show Cause
    The following are the only grounds for show cause:
    Ground 1The vehicle registration was issued because of a document that is false or misleading or obtained or made in another improper way.
    Ground 2The vehicle registration was issued without the vehicle having a garage address in Queensland.
    Ground 3The registration was issued in the name of a person that is not the person’s name.
    Ground 4The vehicle registration for a vehicle with a GVM of more than 4.5t was issued to a person that is not a corporation or an individual aged 18 or over.
    Ground 5The vehicle registration was issued to a vehicle that does not conform to the requirements about the construction, equipment and performance under vehicle law.
    Ground 6The vehicle registration or dealer plate was issued to a vehicle when the application was not made in the approved form and was not accompanied by a required document.
    Ground 7The operator has not complied with the requirements of a defect notice or Present Vehicle Order (PVO).

    Note:A warning notice must be sent to the vehicle operator at least 14 days before the show cause letter is sent. A warning letter can be automatically generated by TICA/TRAILS, however this version has some minor inaccuracies caused primarily by changes to the NHVL. It is recommended that the below template is used instead.

Decision to Show CauseIf the chief executive considers a ground exists to cancel a vehicle registration, the Chief Executive must give the registered operator written notice, stating the proposed action and inviting the person to show cause within 28 days why the proposed action should not be taken.
Response received acceptableIf, after considering all written representations made within the stated time frame, the Chief Executive considers the show cause representation acceptable, the registration may continue unaffected.
Response received not acceptable and
QCAT		If, after considering all written representations made within the stated time frame, the Chief Executive considers grounds still exist to cancel a vehicle registration, the Chief Executive may take the proposed action and must inform the registered operator of the decision by written notice – Show Cause Decision Notice.

The written notice contains information advising that the registered operator may apply through the QCAT process to have the decision reviewed.

If the customer lodges an application for review of decision with QCAT, a TMR representative will attend and both parties will be informed of the outcome.
