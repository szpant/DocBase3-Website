Vehicle Rego Release of Information (3070)


What is it?A release of information (ROI) is where information contained in TMR records is made available.
DetailsThe statement:
  • Is released on TMR letterhead
  • Contains the requested details
  • Includes a warning message that should be read by the person requesting the information
ROI Statement Information
ROI Statement Information

    • Registered operator/s at a nominated date excludes renewal paying agents
    • Address of first registered operator (residential, postal and garage)

    Released only on Request
      • Registration Fees

      • Axle Configuration
      • Vehicle Registration Duty paid on a new registration or transfer transaction*

      * Vehicle Registration Duty is a fee collected on behalf of the Office of State Revenue and is different to ‘dutiable value’.
      • Vehicle's dutiable value - may be released to the registered operator provided they were a party to the transaction**

      • Dutiable Value can be released to liquidators if requested. Only the release of the dutiable value as declared by the company on the acquisition and disposal of their transactions can be released to the liquidators.

      **A vehicle's dutiable value is the list price or market value, depending on whether your vehicle is new, used or modified for a person with a disability.

Warning Message Content
Warning Message Content
    Any information which has been provided relating to any matter arising under the Motor Accident Insurance Act should be accepted as evidence of the fact.

    Information about vehicle details is the recorded information and may not be the actual details on the vehicle.

    A person must not make a false or misleading statement in a document given under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 or the Transport Operations (Marine Safety) Act 1994.

    A penalty applies for false or misleading statements or for misuse of this information.

Request for administrative arrangement (agreement) to provide rego information
Request for administrative arrangement (agreement) to provide rego information
    A government agency, local council or other statutory entity may request the department enter into an administrative arrangement (agreement) to provide access to registration information.

    If the entity does not have any other lawful right to access the information (see written requests) an administrative arrangement can only be considered if the approved criteria have been met. These criteria are attached below:

    Approval Criteria

    The Director (Registration & Licensing) must approve any proposed administrative arrangement. A request for an administrative arrangement is to be forwarded to the Registration Policy Unit, Registration and Licensing (Director), at registration.policy@tmr.qld.gov.au.

    Customers who do not meet the requirements for the other "Types of ROI Requests" should not be advised of this criteria or process. Access by way of an administrative arrangement is only to be mentioned if the customer is a representative of a government entity and specifically asks who they can write to discuss access to registration information.

    A statutory entity may also be considered for being charged an amount less than the legislated fee payable for the release of vehicle registration information, provided the information required:
    • is for a law enforcement agency and the statutory entity is the Queensland Government; or
    • the information required is not a full extract of information typically released, but is a partial extract of information about a vehicle only (for example, postcode only); or
    • the information is required to verify data the statutory entity already holds to confirm it as current or correct according to the information recorded in the vehicle register.

    The Director (Registration & Licensing) must also approve any entity under this arrangement. These requests should also be forwarded to Registration Policy

Issuing the Document
Issuing the Document
    The ROI statement:
    • Can be issued immediately to Organisations
    • Must be mailed to individuals unless it is the registered operator or their representative requesting the information
    • Can be emailed to applicant when requested or application received by email

    Once printed CSO should:
    • Sign the document in the Chief Executive space
    • Insert their full name in the fields below
      eg:
      Signature
      Chief Executive
      By Delegate
      Name:       John Smith Position: Customer Service Officer

    All sections of the signature block must be completed before issuing the document to the customer, as legal implications may apply if this document is used in a court of law and has not been completed correctly.

Rules
Information that Cannot be Released
Information that Cannot be Released
    Certain information must not be released as it is only recorded on behalf of other government departments.
    Date
    • Transfer date may only be released to the acquirer or disposer involved in the transaction
    Codes
    • Concession Industry code
    Written Off Vehicle Details
    • Any information about a written-off vehicle
    • A search certificate can only be obtained on the Personal Properties Securities Register (PPSR) website at www.ppsr.gov.au. This search certificate outlines if the vehicle is currently encumbered or stolen and provides written-off vehicle information.
    Registered Operator
    • Name and address of previous (private) owner as distinct from a dealer or finance company
    • Full vehicle histories
    • The address of a joint registered operator when the primary and secondary operator have different addresses
    Insurance
    • Third party insurance policy number or certificate number
    Pension
    • Source of pension (Department of Human Services (Centrelink) or Dept of Veteran Affairs)
    • Pension concession or Seniors card number
    Screen Prints
    • Screen prints from Docbase and TICA/TRAILS must not be given to customers, external agencies or any other third party.
Searching by Name Only
Searching by Name Only
    Name searches can only be conducted through TICA.

    Individuals
    Individuals
      A search by name can only be conducted when the name and one of the following is provided:
      • Date of Birth or
      • Previous known address

      A list of people with the same name cannot be provided for privacy reasons. A search by a registered operator may be conducted provided they are the registered operator at the specified date.
    Organisations
    Organisations
      A search by name can only be conducted if the Directors name and one of the following is supplied:
      • Directors date of birth or
      • Last known address

      A search by a Director name may be conducted provided they are the Director of the organisation at the specified date.

Customers With Restrictions
    • Vehicle/Text restrictions will not appear on ROI statements
    • If a customer's name is suppressed ("Refer to QT Security Officer) cannot have an ROI completed. Refer any enquiries for suppressed customers to the Identity Management Unit (IMU)
