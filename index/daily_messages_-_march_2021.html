    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records
    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records

      Please be aware that the confidentiality of customer records is paramount at all times. The TMR policy Access to Customer Records provides clear direction to all employees not to take action to access a customer record unless the access is authorised.
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
        • There is a genuine “need to know” and the information is being used as part of the employee’s official duties and they would be hindered in their performance if they didn’t access the information;
        • The employee is entitled to access the information under law;
        • A customer has given permission for their login details to be used to access online services - this is for the customer contact centre/Help Direct/Online Support/Ebus/CAM only following completion of verbal EOI;
        • There is an official business transaction to be completed, including a transaction for a work colleague. The official business transaction must be done over the counter or via the call centre and the employee treated the same as any other customer (e.g. a ticket must be taken, full transaction requirements must be met). A SASM/Manager must also be informed to remove any perceived or potential conflict.

      Unauthorised access is:
      Unauthorised access is:
        • Accessing your own customer record;
        • Accessing customer records of friends or relatives (even if they have given permission) including online services if using a work computer;
        • Accessing a record where an actual, perceived or potential conflict of interest exists;
        • Accessing information because it would be convenient for a staff member to know;
        • Accessing a record on behalf of another TMR employee unless it is part of an official business transaction (e.g. contacting help direct or asking a colleague to access a record to assist with a transaction is acceptable);
        • Assisting a third party to access a customer’s record (e.g. mother calls and asks about an issue her family member is having with an online service and the employee assists the mother using the family member’s details).


      Please note: Customers contact the department in person, over the phone, via the internet or in writing. Transactions should be in line with your current role. If you are in doubt – ask your supervisor or manager.

      What can happen if I access a customer’s record inappropriately?

      Misuse of confidential information is a criminal offence under s408E Criminal Code. Unauthorised access and use of the information contained in this system may lead to criminal prosecution and/or disciplinary sanctions including:
      • Criminal prosecution;
      • Termination of employment;
      • Suspension from duties;
      • Reduction of classification level and a consequential change of duties;
      • Transfer or redeployment to other public service employment;
      • Forfeiture or deferment of a remuneration increment or increase;
      • Reduction of remuneration (increment) level;
      • A monetary penalty (which can be deducted from your pay);
      • A formal reprimand;
      • Record of disciplinary action - a permanent record of the incident on their file which they would need to disclose during pre-employment screening for all public sector roles.

      All staff are required to be familiar with the Access to Customer Records and Releasing Customer Information policies which are published in DocBase, available via the below links.

      Access to Customer Records policy summary v2
      Access to Customer Records Policy v5
      Releasing Customer Information

      Staff should also view the Access to Customer Records Policy page on SharePoint for more resources.

      For more information about access to customer records, please talk to your manager or supervisor.

    02/03/2021 - Changes regarding expiry of AP, AE, AIS approvals and HVRAS accredited persons
    02/03/2021 - Changes regarding expiry of AP, AE, AIS approvals and HVRAS accredited persons

      Operator Accreditation and Authorisation has made changes regarding the expiry of approved persons, approved examiners, Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) approvals and Heavy Vehicle Registration Assessment Scheme (HVRAS) accredited persons.

      From 1 December 2020, if any of the above accreditations or approvals has expired for a period of 90 days or more, the accreditation or approval will lapse.

      If the accreditation or approval lapses, a new application must be made together with all supporting information and all relevant application fees must be paid.

      Accreditation and approval holders have been notified of these changes by email and an article published in the second edition of the Approved Person, AIS and Approved Examiner Newsletter.

      The approved person and HVRAS web pages, Information Sheet 10 Renewing your AIS approval and Information Sheet 15 Renewing your approved examiner accreditation have also been updated to reflect the above changes.

      If you require further information, please contact Maria Stevenson, Principal Policy Advisor by email Maria.L.Stevenson@tmr.qld.gov.au or telephone 3066 2196.

    29/03/2021 - Q-SAFE Manual - Updated
    29/03/2021 - Q-SAFE Manual - Updated

      The Q-SAFE Manual needs to be amended from time to time where a clarification or inconsistency is identified. The following seven key amendments to the Q-SAFE Manual have been introduced:
      1. A critical driving error (CDE) is to be recorded by a Driving Examiner (DE) if a person undertaking a practical driving test drives a heavy vehicle while the 'air brake' warning light is on.
      2. A CDE is to be recorded by a DE when a person who is taking a practical driving test fails to shoulder check and signal prior to making a lane change on a multi-lane road.
      3. Clarification has been provided that a tow truck specialist assessment is to be conducted in the equivalent class of vehicle as the driver licence that the tow truck driver is applying for.
      4. Clarification has been provided that young driver mobile phone restrictions apply during a practical driving test.
      5. Clarification has been provided that a modified prime mover must also meet the same requirements as a rigid truck for a Heavy Rigid (HR) test vehicle.
      6. A non-critical driving error (NCDE) is to be recorded if a person undertaking a practical driving test in a heavy vehicle operates the vehicle's engine (compression) brakes in a built-up area. The DE message has been updated accordingly.
      7. The requirements for how brake lights are fitted on a test vehicle have been amended to align with the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010.

      A few additional minor changes were also made to:
      • clarify the term ‘all-round observation’ when reversing to capture physically scanning in all directions using mirrors and the use of vision improvement devices, such as reverse cameras, which improve a driver’s ability to see small objects directly behind the rear of the vehicle;
      • correct a diagram reference error in the 'Judgement' section of the Q-SAFE Manual; and
      • remove references to Transport Inspectors carrying out roadworthiness assessment as these are carried out by Approved Inspection Stations.

    10/03/2021 - Medical Show Cause - Updated
    10/03/2021 - Medical Show Cause - Updated

      The issuing of Medical Show Cause actions has now been centralised to ensure accuracy and consistency. As a result of this change, the procedure for actioning medical notifications by licence holders has been updated. A new email template is now in place and has been added to DocBase.

      If a Medical Certificate (F3712) and/or Medical Notification (F4355) is presented that may require show cause action, operators are to email the certificate/notification to the MCR unit with the completed email template who will then begin show cause proceedings if required.

      If the customer is declaring a Medical Condition and wishes to surrender their driver licence, the current surrender process should be followed.

      Please note that if a customer who has given verbal notification or completed the Medical Notification (F4355), and is unable to present a Medical Certificate, all licensing transactions cannot be completed until a Medical Certificate is presented. The No Licence Renewal Allowed (NOLR) restriction is to be applied by the operator as well as a text restriction.

      The mandatory formless questions for licensing have also been updated to align with the new process.

      Centres are encouraged to escalate any questions/issues to their TICA specialists which can be addressed during the March TICA Release skype meeting.

    01/03/2021 - Weights and Measurements - Measuring Vehicle Width - Updated

    05/03/2021 - Class 4 Tow Truck holding yard exemption - Updated
      The holder of a Class 4 Tow Truck may apply for an exemption from having a holding yard in recognition that the heavy vehicles being towed are most likely being towed to the registered operators nominated location and do not require storage.

    02/03/2021 - Change of frequency of Compliance Checks
    02/03/2021 - Change of frequency of Compliance Checks

      The frequency of scheduled AIS Compliance Checks has been extended from three (3) years to fours (4) years. This will assist in reducing the number of outstanding scheduled AIS Compliance Checks caused by COVID-19 border activities.

      CID has been updated to add 1 year to the Audit Due Date where the Audit Type is "Scheduled" and the Status is "Deferred" or "Not Started".

      All Audits with a status of "In Progress" must be completed.

      There is no change to Confirmation checks or checks triggered by a complaint or investigation.

      All Transport Inspectors and Seniors should review the amended policy.

    01/03/2021 - NOA-0042 – Side Marker Lights Used in Visual Docking Units
    01/03/2021 - NOA-0042 – Side Marker Lights Used in Visual Docking Units

      This NOA is designed to provide information to Authorised Officers and other relevant staff regarding a new style of side marker lights that form part of a visual docking system.

      The NHVR has determined these lights are classified as side marker lights, which are mostly installed at the time of manufacture, are integrated into the side marker light circuit and flash when the vehicle is reversing to provide the driver with information about objects (such as loading docks) directly behind the vehicle. These lights are sometimes referred to as ‘Cobra Lamps’.

      As these lights have been determined to be side marker lights, the requirements for side marker lights are to be applied.
      Additionally, as an ADR approved light, these lights are not to be included when measuring width (see section 8(1)(c) of the Heavy Vehicle (Vehicle Standards) National Regulation).

      For further information about the requirements for side marker lights, please refer to Section 8 of the National Heavy Vehicle Inspection Manual.

      Link to the manufacturer Lucidity.
      https://lucidityaustralia.com.au/product/visual-dock-assistant/

      All Transport Inspectors and Seniors should review the NOA.
