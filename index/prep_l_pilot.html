PrepL (7191)


About PrepL
What it isPrepL is an online learning and assessment program that has been designed to:
  • develop a stronger understanding of road rules and licensing requirements;
  • embed safe driving attitudes and behaviours;
    and
  • better equip young, novice drivers to deal with deliberate risk taking behaviours.

PrepL A4 Flyer

About the test
About the test
    PrepL is expected to take approximately 4 - 6 hours to complete. The final assessment undertaken by applicants will consist of 30 questions and require a pass rate of 90%.

    Participants have 12 months from the date of payment of the PrepL fee to complete the course.
When PrepL is Required
When PrepL is RequiredPrepL is intended to replace the written road rules test. Anyone who has to do the general written test now will be required to complete PrepL once implemented. This includes new learners, returning drivers and drivers from non-recognised countries.

Refer:Road Rules Tests

Enrolling in PrepL
Who can enrolCustomers can only enrol in PrepL if:
  • are at least 15 years and 11 months;
    and
  • retain a valid email address until course completed or for the full 12 month period – whichever comes first.

Refer:Road Rules Tests for details on when PrepL is required
Enrolling in PrepL
Enrolling in PrepL
Enrolling in PrepL
    In person
    In person
      To commence the enrolment process, customers may enrol in person at any TMR Customer Service Centre, QGAP or licence-issuing police station.

      To enrol, customers must provide EOI, a valid email address and pay the PrepL fee.

      For formless centres, the New Customer mandatory question must be asked. Sites still using forms will require the customer to complete the Driver Licence Application (F3000) form.

      Once enrolment fee is paid, customers must be given their CRN on a CRN Card. They will need their CRN to sign up for the PrepL course, this will complete their enrolment.

      Advice to customer:Payment for the enrolment in PrepL is just the first part of the enrolment process. Next you will receive an email that includes your customer reference number and the link to create your PrepL account.

      A welcome email will be sent to the customer's eContact email address once they are enrolled. This will include important information, like their CRN and advice on how to create their account.

      Customers should be advised to use their eContact email address when they create their PrepL login. When the PrepL login email and the TICA eContact are the same the customer will have a level of authentication sufficient to allow them to get their learner licence online once they have passed PrepL.

      Customers should be advised to read the welcome email they receive as it contains the link they will need to log in and begin the course.
    Online
    Online
      Customers may enrol using the online system.

      When enrolling online, customers without an existing CRN are created in TICA and assigned a CRN. Their record is created without EOI. These customers are referred to as low assurance customers (LACs).

      LACs can provide their EOI at any time during their enrolment by presenting in person at a CSC.

      LACs are sent a new customer confirmation email that contains their CRN and name (as recorded at enrolment). They are advised to bring this information with them when attending the CSC.
    Enrol Online Pay PrepL Fee in CSC
    Enrol Online Pay PrepL Fee in CSC
      Customers may create their LAC record online in PrepL but if they can't pay online using a credit card, they must attend the CSC to pay the PrepL fee and complete their enrolment.
      Depending on where the customer exited the PrepL enrolment service, TICA may, or may not, show a pending PrepL enrolment. If there is no pending enrolment in TICA, the operator needs to enrol them which requires EOI to be presented/images captured.

      For formless centres, if the customer is providing EOI and images the New customer questions must be asked.

      The customer is not enrolled and cannot commence PrepL until the PrepL fee is paid.

      Note for CSC Operators:If a customer presents to pay their PrepL fee and does not have their EOI, the payment can still be accepted, however they will have to return at another time to have their EOI recorded/images captured.


      For information about errors or issues when enrolling Refer: TMR Online Services Guide > Common Error Messages > PrepL
Low assurance customers (LACs)
Low assurance customers (LACs)
    When LACs present at a CSC to provide EOI, extra diligence is required to ensure that a duplicate record does not exist in TICA, and the name the customer used to create their LAC record matches their EOI.

    When updating a LAC record to match EOI provided (example, LAC record created as Bec Smith must be updated to match EOI Rebecca Smith), a Report for File detailing the update must be kept in the CSO's daily filing.

    When updating a LAC record for a Learner Licence issue, a note on the TICA Driver Licence History Detail report must be made detailing the name update.

    CSCs cannot create LACs, a customer who wants to enrol at a CSC must provide full EOI at enrolment.

    Refer above: Enrolling in PrepL > Online for more information on LACs.
Image and signature capture
Image and signature capture
    In Person
    In Person
      Customers who enrol in person must have their image and signature captured when they present their EOI at the time of enrolment.

    Online
    Online
      Customers who enrol online can present their EOI at any time during enrolment. They must have their image and signature captured at the time they present their EOI. Some examples include:
      1.a 16 year old customer presents their EOI and has their image set taken when applying for their learner licence in person.
      2.an applicant from a non-recognised country presents their EOI and has their image set taken after completing PrepL, and a learner licence doesn't need to be issued.
    Images and signatures captured prior to the issue of the licence are valid for 12 months. If the learner licence is not issued within this time the captured image and signature will expire and new captures will be required before a licence can be issued.
Recording medical conditions
Recording medical conditions
    If the customer presents a Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712) when enrolling, or anytime while enrolled, the Medical certificate is added to MCR Hub and the licence prerequisite recorded in TICA.

    Where a customer indicates they require glasses to drive, an S condition must be recorded in TICA.
Payment of the PrepL fee
Payment of the PrepL fee
    If the customer enrols at a CSC the fee must be paid at the time of enrolment.

    If the customer enrols online they may pay:
    • Online; or
    • At a CSC

    When enrolling and paying at a CSC in person the customer must provide full EOI and have their images taken. If they have enrolled online and are paying their enrolment fee in person it is encouraged they provide full EOI but this is not a requirement, yet. The customer will have to provide full EOI before they can be issued a licence. If they provide this at the time of enrolment at the CSC, they will be able to apply for their learner licence online as soon as they complete PrepL.
Time to Complete Course
Time to Complete Course
    Applicants who enrol in PrepL have 12 months from the date of payment of the PrepL fee to complete the course. If they do not successfully complete the course in that time, they will be required to re-pay the PrepL fee and enrol again.
Enrolment welcome notification
Enrolment welcome notification
    Once the PrepL fee has been paid, a welcome notification will be automatically sent to the email address that was supplied during enrolment. The notification provides the applicant with all the information they require to complete their enrolment and to log in and begin the course.

Issuing learner licence
Applying for a licence
Applying for a licence
    When a customer passes the PrepL course they are sent a PrepL congratulations email and a Pass result is recorded in TICA. Once they have passed PrepL and are at least 16 years of age, they are able to apply for their learner licence either in person or online. The passed prerequisite is valid for 5 years as per the written test.

    Applying in Person
    Applying in Person
      When applying in person for their licence applicants must complete the Driver Licence Application/Renewal (F3000) form or Mandatory Questions (at formless Centres), provide EOI if they have not done so already and have a Pass result in TICA. Images are captured if they have not already been recorded.

      If the applicant indicates on the F3000 or verbally (at formless Centres) that they have a medical condition but have not provided the Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712) the application is refused.

    Applying Online
    Applying Online
      Learner licence applicants may use the ‘Get My Learner Licence’ online service to obtain their learner licence if they have:
      • Suitable EOI and Evidence of Residency recorded in TICA;
      • A valid image set (facial and signature images) captured in TICA;
        and
      • A valid medical certificate recorded (if required)
      • The required level of authentication to access the service;
      And
      • Passed PrepL;
      • At least 16 years of age;
      • Not a returning driver (including expired >5yr learner licence);
      • Not transferring their licence from another state/country;
        and
      • Provided a valid email address


      Access to "Get My Learner Licence" (GMLL) is only through the congratulations email or from within the PrepL course.

      If the customer cannot access their congratulations email they can log into the PrepL course to order their learner licence.

Exemptions
Transition Period
Transition Period
    During the current transition period customers may choose to do either the Written Road Rules Test or PrepL. An exemption is not required. Due to the expected road safety benefits and positive feedback, PrepL is strongly encouraged. It is intended to become mandatory in the near future.
Refunds
Refunds
    During the transition period if a customer enrols in PrepL and then elects to do the written test a refund of the PrepL fee is not applicable. A refund may be considered by the SASM/Manager if the customer is unable to complete PrepL (i.e language difficulties, insufficient bandwidth in the area that they reside and there is no other reasonable access available).
Suspension/cancel enrolmentIf TMR is reasonably satisfied that a person other than the applicant has taken all or part of the PrepL test, TMR can cancel the person’s PrepL enrolment and disqualify them from re-enrolling in PrepL for a period of six months.

Cancelling a person’s enrolment and disqualifying them from re-enrolling for six months in all circumstances where TMR is reasonably satisfied a person has cheated ensures that cheating is dealt with in a uniform manner. Doing so also sends a strong message of deterrence.

A person may request a reconsideration of this decision within 28 days of the notice. The decision may be confirmed or set aside and substituted with another decision. The latter would be used to reinstate a person’s enrolment.

If the decision is confirmed and the person is still not satisfied, they may apply to QCAT for a review of the decision on the reconsideration.
Step 1
Step 1
    Investigate the allegation. This may include contacting the person who has made the allegation to obtain further information. It may also include contacting the applicant via their provided contact details to obtain information from them. All of this information should be file noted including the reasons for the decision. Ensure the decision maker has the appropriate delegation to make the decision. If the decision is that cheating has occurred, proceed to step two.


    Note:Action can only be taken if it has been determined that the applicant has had someone other than the applicant take all or part of the test and the applicant has not been granted a Driver Licence (includes learner licence). Refer: 10BB TO(RUM - DL) Reg 2010
Step 2
Step 2
    Write to applicant informing them that TMR intends to cancel the persons PrepL enrolment and disqualify them from re-enrolling for a period of six (6) months. The letter must contain (see below template):
    • the decision;
      and
    • the reasons for the decision;
      and
    • the day from which the cancellation has effect;
      and
    • the details of the disqualification;
      and
    • that the applicant may apply for a reconsideration of the decision under section 132 of the Driver Licensing Regulation;
      and
    • that the applicant is also able, under section 65A(1) of the Act, to apply to QCAT for a review of the decision on the reconsideration

      PrepL Cancellation and Disqualification Advice - Nov 2018
Step 3
Step 3
    On the date specified in the letter, contact HelpDirect to advise that an applicant's PrepL enrolment needs to be cancelled.

    Although enrolment is referred to as ‘cancelled’, it is acknowledged that the system will not allow for a cancelled status to be reinstated. Therefore, HelpDirect will record the status as 'Suspended' until appeal processes have been exhausted.
Step 4
Step 4
    Applicant may apply for a reconsideration of the decision within 28 days of the date of the written notice. If successful, contact HelpDirect to re-instate the enrolment.

    The customer must be advised in writing of the outcome.


    PrepL Reconsideration of Decision - Nov 2018

    PrepL Reconsideration of Decision - Reinstate Nov 2018
Step 5
Step 5
    If the applicant is not satisfied after reconsideration of the original decision, they may then apply to QCAT for a review of the decision on the reconsideration. If successful, contact HelpDirect to reinstate the enrolment.
PrepL Supervisor Course
PrepL Supervisor CourseThe PrepL Supervisor Course has been developed to support the supervisors of learner drivers. It draws on PrepL’s road rule information, providing an interactive road rules refresher for anyone who wants to improve their knowledge and brush up on the road rules.

The course is available on the TMR website. Access to the course is unrestricted and costs $15.50. Applicants have access to the course for 12 months from the date of payment.
