Vehicle Security Camera Systems (7227)


Vehicle Security Camera Systems
What it isA vehicle security camera system must be approved by the chief executive for installation in personalised transport vehicles being:
  • Booked hire vehicle
  • Limousine
  • Taxi
where the service meets the risk criteria outlined below.
The risk criteria relate to the risk of assault, theft or fare evasion occurring during the service.
LegislationTransport Operations (Passenger Transport) Regulation 2018 (TOPTR)
Part 9 Vehicle Security Camera Systems
When Required
Mandatory requirement to fit vehicle security camera system
Mandatory requirement to fit vehicle security camera system
    Legislation: TOPTR 204

    A vehicle security camera system is mandatory in personalised transport vehicles (taxi, limousine or booked hire vehicles) where the service is being provided in a relevant security camera area and the service meets one or more of the following criteria:
    • Driver or passenger is anonymous because of rank or hail work
    • Payment for the journey is made in cash
    • Payment for the journey is made in person via EFTPOS, credit card or other means immediately before, during or after the journey

    Relevant security camera areas:

    BrisbaneBundabergCairns
    GladstoneGold CoastGympie

Voluntary Installation
Voluntary Installation
    Personalised transport vehicles not required to install a vehicle camera system may still choose to do so. If installed it must be an approved security camera system and comply with the requirements in the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Regulation 2018.
RequirementsMust be an Approved Security Camera System.

Legislation: TOPTR 213 and 214

To be an approved camera system it must comply with the Queensland Personalised Transport Security Camera Specifications 2017 and be approved by the Chief Executive. Appropriate signage must also be displayed to advise that security cameras are fitted.

Qld Personalised Transport Security Camera Specifications_2017

Approved camera systems are published on the TMR website and currently include:
  • Mark 5 TaxiCam (VerifEye/Lytx)
  • SnapShot Mark 4 with internal camera model PV3006-001 (MTData/MTI)
  • SnapShot Mark 4 with internal camera model PV3006-002 (MTData/MTI)
  • TaxicamHD Mobile CCTV System (Taxicomms)
  • Streamax (G60DT) X5-Lite
  • Streamax (G60DT) X5-Lite V2
  • Streamax (G60DT) X5-Lite Mini
  • 13CABSi (TaxiTech)
  • Crown Cabs Camera Mark 1

Security Camera System must be operational
Security Camera System must be operational
    Legislation: TOPTR Part 9, Division 3

    An operator of a booked hire service or taxi service has an obligation to ensure that an approved vehicle security camera system is properly fitted and that it is fully operational (TOPTR 208).

    If the system is not or will not be operational due to maintenance or repair the operator must advise TMR in writing. A copy of this notification must be kept in the vehicle and state the time it will not be operational. This time must not exceed four days (TOPTR 210).

    A driver also has an obligation not to drive a personalised transport vehicle that is required to have an approved security camera fitted when the approved security camera system is not fully operational
    (TOPTR S209).


    F4760 Personalised Transport Security Camera System Fault Notice

Use of other recording devices
Use of other recording devices
    Personalised Transport vehicles not required to install an approved vehicle security camera system may install cameras that record only the images in front of or behind the vehicle.

    Personalised transport vehicles are not permitted to have an inward facing camera recording images and audio of the driver or passengers unless it is an approved vehicle security camera.

    TMR does not regulate the type of camera required or the downloading of footage from such cameras.
Downloading Security Camera Recordings
What can be downloaded
What can be downloaded
    Legislation: TOPTR 215(3)

    Only recordings made in the vehicle being used to provide a personalised transport service can be downloaded.

    The vehicle is used to provide a personalised transport service when it is:
    • about to be used; or
    • Being used; or
    • Has just been used.
Who can download recordings?
Who can download recordings?
    Only Queensland Police Officers and persons authorised under the Act can download vehicle security camera recordings.
When can recordings be downloaded?
When can recordings be downloaded?

    Legislation: TOPTR 202 authorised purpose

    Recordings can only be downloaded for an authorised purpose being:
    • Examining a complaint against the operator or their employees about a vehicle or an event that happened in or about the vehicle. For example fare evasion, lost property, theft or offensive behaviour
    • Prosecuting or issuing an infringement notice for an offence committed in or about the vehicle
    • Another purpose related to the duties of a Police Officer,
    • Another purpose related to the duties of a Transport Inspector (e.g. conducting an audit)
    • Conducting of maintenance or repairs by the manufacturer or supplier of the camera system,
    • Another purpose permitted by law.
What records must be kept?
What records must be kept?

    Legislation: TOPTR 216

    The person downloading must record the following details:
    • VIN of the vehicle
    • Date, time and location of the download
    • Name of person undertaking the download
    • Reason for the download
    • Details of the person requesting and receiving the download
    • Date and time the recording was made
    • File name of the security recording
Disposal of downloaded recordings
Disposal of downloaded recordings
    Legislation: TOPTR 218

    Where the downloaded recording is not to be used for enforcement purposes it must be destroyed more than 60 but less than 90 days after the making or receiving of the download.
Protection against unauthorised downloading
Protection against unauthorised downloading
    Legislation: TOPTR 217

    The Booking Entity Authority (BEA) must take security measures to protect against unauthorised downloading or loss of security recordings.

Operational Status of a Security Camera System
The visual indicator status light on a security camera system, is used to help identify the operational status of the security camera system.

Refer: Security Camera Status for information on security camera status lights.
Compliance CheckTransport Inspector's conducting an inspection of a personalised transport vehicle in an on road situation or during Programmed Vehicle Inspections (PVI) are to check the security camera system as a part of the inspection process. The check is to include the following:
  • check the service type to determine if it requires a security camera to be fitted;
  • ensure that the security camera/s are fitted if applicable;
  • ensure that the camera/s are operational;
  • ensure that approved signs are fitted on each door and inside the vehicle.
  • check if the driver is holding a fault notification.



Note:An operator does not commit an offence for making a personalised transport vehicle available to a driver of the vehicle without a fully operational or properly fitted security camera where a fault notification is in place. In addition the driver of the personalised transport vehicle does not commit an offence by driving the vehicle without a fully operational approved security camera system during the notice period.

A Penalty Infringement Notice (PIN) can be issued for non-compliance. The applicable PIN is listed against the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Regulation 2018 Offence description in the PIN Codes.
