Services Booking System (SBS) (2647)


What it isThe Services Booking System (SBS) enables statewide scheduling of services and bookings for:
  • Driving Examinations
  • Vehicle Inspections
conducted by the Department of Transport and Main Roads.

SBS directly interfaces with TICA allowing verification of customer details and registrations during the booking process.
Access to Customer RecordsPlease be aware that the confidentiality of customer records is paramount at all times. The TMR policy Access to Customer Records provides clear direction to all employees not to take action to access a customer record unless the access is authorised. Refer: Admin > Security > Access to Customer Records

    SBS Details on TICA
    SBS Details on TICA
      Driving Examinations
      Driving examination bookings recorded in SBS on-line appear in TICA.

      Vehicle Inspections
      Vehicle inspection bookings made in SBS on-line appear on TICA.

      An inspection cannot be recorded in TICA if a booking does not exist in SBS and show as being paid in TICA.
On-line Vs Off-line
On-line Vs Off-line
    There are 2 processing modes in SBS:
    On-line
    Interfaces with TICA to ensure:

    For driving examinations
    • Validation of the CRN
    • Eligibility of customer for class of test (tenure met)
    • No existing booking for requested class

    For vehicle inspections
    • Validation of registration number
    • Vehicle is inspectable for selected product
    • No existing booking that is more than 2 working days away. Where a current booking exists 2 or more working days away this booking must be rescheduled and not processed as a new booking.
    Off-line
    • Interfaces with TICA to validate numbers and ensure eligibility but does not send booking information to TICA. Off-line Allows information to be altered in SBS where it is already correct in TICA.
    • Used at times for workarounds when problems occur.
    • Used for driving tests conducted by TMR examiners at Police Stations and some QGAPs. The bookings and results for these tests are recorded directly in TICA. Details are recorded in SBS for reporting purposes only

    Guide to using On-line and Off-line
    Guide to using On-line and Off-line
      All vehicle inspections are processed On-line. Processing of driving examinations depends on the testing officer and location:
      LocationTesting OfficerProcessing Mode
      Customer Service CentreTMR Driving ExaminerOn-line
      QGAP - TMR Lead AgentTMR Driving ExaminerOn-line
      QGAP - non TMR Lead Agent TMR Driving Examiner from a different locationOff-line
      QGAP YeppoonTMR Driving ExaminerOn-line
      Police StationTMR Driving ExaminerOff-line
      Police StationPolice OfficerNot processed in SBS
Profiles and Access
Profiles and Access
    Actions that can be performed in SBS depend on the user's profile:
    ProfileAccess
    Booking EnquirySearch for slots, view bookings, view slot & driving test/vehicle inspection histories, print daily work sheets, print vehicle checklists, print bulk booking reports, Hold individual slots, search for orders by order number.
    BookingIn addition to the booking enquiry function this profile also allows booking for driving test and vehicle inspection, reschedule and swap of existing bookings, cancel bookings, delete of bulk booking slot.
    SchedulingAccess to the scheduling functions. Create slot templates, create services, add unavailable periods, make slot unavailable, add a slot definition, add/remove products to/from a service, print bulk booking reports, cancel a test and remove a booking slot simultaneously
    AdminAdding or removing location to/from the regional groupings, Add or remove regions from the regional grouping, cancelling of multiple bookings and making slot unavailable, make multiple slot unavailable, create bulk bookings
    How to obtain/amend a Profile
    Form F4191 is to be completed and and emailed to CSB Systems
Appointing an Authorised Officer
Appointing an Authorised Officer
    Any officer granting an extension of time (EOT) must be an Authorised Officer under s29 TO(RUM) Reg and have a unique Authorised Officer Identification Number.

    To appoint Authorised Officer and obtain an Authorised Officer identification number a Team leader in SBS must email Compliance_support with the following:
    • Applicants full name (including middle name if applicable)
    • Date of birth
    • Position
    • Office location
    • Region

    Once the Authorised Officer identification number has been provided the Team Leader must complete the CSO Instrument of Appointment as an Authorised Officer template. All instrument of appointments have a common expiry date which is 1 January 2022.

    The Appointment letter must be signed by a Director.

    CSO Instrument of Appointment as an Authorised Officer

    Issue the signed Letter of Appointment to the Authorised Officer and retain a copy on the officers personnel file.

    If the officer ceases in the role that requires them is grant EOTs their Authorised Officer appointment must be revoked and details of the revocation sent to Compliance_Support so the register of Authorised Officers can be updated

Bulk Bookings
Bulk Bookings
    What it is
    What it is
      Bulk bookings allows a number of booking slots to be reserved for an organisation over a period of time, without the need to nominate the customer or vehicle that the booking is for, until a later date. Example: 5 booking slots a week for 6 weeks.
    Who can create a Bulk Booking
    Who can create a Bulk Booking
      Only schedulers can set up bulk bookings.
    Fees
    Fees
      A booking fee is charged for each vehicle inspection slot at the time of creating the bulk booking. The remaining vehicle inspection fee is paid when TMR is advised of the vehicle to be inspected, prior to the time of the slot.

      No fee is charged at the time of creating a bulk booking for driving examinations, however as this feature is predominantly used in the South East Region, the CBU have a contract with each bulk booking agent.
Fleet Bookings/Saturday Testing
Fleet Bookings/Saturday Testing
    What it is
    What it is
      Fleet bookings and Saturday testing is when a service is set up for a specific customer Examples:
      • An organisation with a number of vehicles due for inspection
      • A driving school with a number of students wanting to be tested on a Saturday
    Who can create a Fleet Booking/Saturday Test
    Who can create a Fleet Booking/Saturday Test
      Fleet bookings or Saturday tests are normally facilitated and booked* by schedulers at the request of resource managers

      * Except Southern Region who fax bookings to the call centre.

      Who can create a special test booking

        Driver Authorisation Competency Tests cannot be booked online. Customers must phone the SBS Call Centre on 13 23 90 to book a slot. There is no fee charged for authorised Driver Authorisation competency tests.

        Where an applicant is eligible to undertake a Driver Authority Competency Test, the local TransLink Regional Operations Officer will place a text message on the system authorising the test and confirming the type of competency test the applicant will be undertaking (e.g. Driver Authority Passenger Car, Driver Authority Motorcycle).
    Swapping Bookings
    Swapping Bookings
      What it is
      What it is
        Two customers/registrations can be swapped between each other's booking slot.
      Rules
      Rules
        • Both customers/registrations must be booked in SBS prior to swap
        • Minimum 3 hours notice required
        • Bookings can only be swapped if the service that the bookings are against supports both products
        • For vehicle inspections only Programmed Inspections can be swapped
        • For driving examinations:
          • Only standard tests can be swapped
          • Number of required slots is the same
      Who can swap bookings
      Who can swap bookings
        A swap booking can be done by an operator with booking access (normally through the SBS call centre) or via the internet.

        The customer must have both BRN's (Booking Reference Numbers) to facilitate a swap booking.
    Holding Slots
    Holding Slots
      When a customer has not pre-paid and contacts the call centre to make their booking their booking will be held:
      • for 30 minutes if the booking is for the same day
      • until midnight of the same day if booking is for another day.
      If the customer doesn't pay for the booking within the required period the booking will be cancelled.
      Special Circumstances
      Special Circumstances
        What it is
        What it is
          A booking slot can be held for a customer without payment being made. This should be done in special circumstances only and with approval from a supervisor.

          A held booking should be monitored to ensure that the booking is finalised prior to the booking date/time.
        Who can hold slots
        Who can hold slots
          • Schedulers
          • SBS Call Centre operators

    Forced Sequential Bookings
    Forced Sequential Bookings
      Forced Sequential Bookings are used in locations where a Driving Examiner (DE) travels long distances to carry out testing. Bookings are made straight after each other, instead of throughout the day so that the DE's time is not wasted waiting for tests. Bookings outside of the forced sequential order but be approved by the DE

      Where there are forced sequential bookings a message will display in the schedule: Force sequential services exist in displayed services, identified in red text.
    Adhoc Bookings
    Adhoc Bookings
      What is an Adhoc Booking?
      What is an Adhoc Booking?
        An adhoc booking is a booking created outside of the existing SBS schedule. Adhoc booking requests for the SBS Unit need to be authorised or approved by the responsible office (eg: by the CSC Manager, SASM, DE or Inspector). The request needs to be in writing and emailed to HelpSBS

        Note:Adhoc bookings for used vehicles intending to become a driver trainer vehicle do not require prior approval.

      Adhoc Bookings for Driver Trainer Vehicles
      Adhoc Bookings for Driver Trainer Vehicles
        An adhoc booking is created when a booking is required for a used vehicle to become a driver trainer vehicle. The purpose of use (POU) must be training (TRAI) before making the booking.

        The first booking to be made in SBS is an Outside (COP) Modification (OMOD) to inspect the vehicles dual controls.

        The second booking is an adhoc Programmed inspection to be made for the same date, time and location as the OMOD booking.

        Note:There will be two fees payable as two bookings are required.

      Adhoc bookings for Heavy Vehicle tests
      Adhoc bookings for Heavy Vehicle tests
        An adhoc booking is created when a customer applies to remove B or A conditions from their Heavy Vehicle Licence.

        A booking should be held in SBS then an adhoc booking is created to record the results.
    OMOD Inspections
    OMOD Inspections
      What is an OMOD Inspection?
      What is an OMOD Inspection?
        This is an Outside (COP) Modification inspection. This is used for modified vehicles requiring inspection. OMOD bookings are required for light vehicles which are to become a driver trainer vehicle and for Individually Constructed Vehicles (ICV) for pre-registration inspection.

        Heavy vehicles and motorcycles becoming training vehicles do not require an OMOD booking as they don’t have dual controls.
    Taxi/Limo Inspection Bookings
    Taxi/Limo Inspection Bookings
      Registered Vehicles
      Registered Vehicles
        • For a registered vehicle that will become a taxi, limo or booked hire service:
          • book using the current vehicle registration plate
          • Select the product code of Programmed Inspection (Booked Hire)/Programmed Inspection (Taxi)
        • For a registered vehicle that already has the correct purpose of use:
          • Select the product code of Programmed Inspection (Other) Refer above: SBS Products > Vehicle Inspections
          • If the vehicle is to be transferred to another registered operator to conduct the Personalised Transport Service you should advise customer to transfer registration to the correct name before booking the inspection as the transfer may cancel the inspection.

        Note:The vehicle cannot operate as a taxi/limo/booked hire service until it either passes an inspection or a system exemption has been granted.

      Unregistered Vehicles
      Unregistered Vehicles
        • For an unregistered vehicle becoming a taxi, limo or booked hire service, book using the VIN.
        • Select the product code of Programmed Inspection (Booked Hire)/Programmed Inspection (Taxi)
        • In the booking select the CRN of the person who will be the registered operator of the vehicle. This will allow the vehicle to be registered in the correct name after the inspection has been passed.
        • In the SBS booking screen, include the VIN in the comments section. This assists with future search enquiries for unregistered vehicles which cannot be searched using a registration number.

        Note:The vehicle cannot operate as a taxi/limo/booked hire service until it either passes an inspection or a system exemption has been granted.

      System Exemption
      System Exemption
        The SBS Unit can only grant a System Exemption for the issue of taxi/limo plates prior to an inspection, if the following requirements are met:
        • Customer has booked the first available inspection date within reason.
        • A System Exemption application has been completed accurately and in full by the registered operator.
        • The System Exemption application is emailed to SBS accompanied by:
          • a current Qld Safety Certificate, and
          • gas certificate (if applicable)
