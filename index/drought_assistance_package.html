Drought Assistance Package (3018)


What is it?The drought assistance package provides assistance to primary producers engaged mainly in the production of primary produce who have vehicles garaged in a drought declared Shire or property.
Requirements
Requirements
Requirements
    • Registered operator is a primary producer
    • Vehicle POU is FARM or PRIV
    • To demonstrate the requirements have been met, the registered operator must provide:
      • For a vehicle garaged in a drought declared local government area. Refer: List of Drought Declared Shires and/or the following website which lists both Drought Declarations and Revocations:
        http://www.daff.qld.gov.au/environment/drought/drought-declarations-and-revocations:
        • The vehicle or another vehicle under the CRN has a primary producer concession;
          and
        • The garage address* recorded in TICA is the droughted property;
          or
        • A written declaration that the registered operator is a primary producer;
          and
        • The garage address* recorded in TICA is the droughted property
      • For a Vehicle garaged on a Drought Declared property:
        • Property Application form certified by Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry must be provided;
          and
        • The garage address* recorded in TICA is the droughted property

    Note: *Refer to garage address requirements 04. Registration > Garage address > Requirement if droughted property is not recorded in TICA.
Rules
Reduced Pay Terms
Reduced Pay Terms
    • 3mth pay terms are available
    • Surcharge for shorter pay terms are exempted
    • CTP is charged for the pay term only
    • The online registration renewal and change CTP services may be used to make payment, and if required, update the term to 3 months without a payment reference number
    • Registration will revert back to standard 12mths after one year, Customers must reapply for a shorter term if still eligible.
    Note: The relief type must be updated in TICA if a customer is paying a 3 month registration term due to drought.
Availability of Package
Availability of Package
    The drought assistance package is available:
    • From when the shire or area a property is located is drought declared
    • To 12 months after the drought declaration is revoked
    Note: This is only available if the registered operator received the drought assistance from TMR during the drought declared period and they still meet the requirements).
Excluded Vehicles
Excluded Vehicles
    A vehicle is not eligible if:
    • POU is anything other than PRIV or FARM
    • Conditionally registered
    • There are distance and road use limits imposed by the chief executive.
Seasonal Registration Vehicles
Seasonal Registration Vehicles
    TMR will approve an extension of up to one year for seasonal registration vehicles to remain dormant before paying a period of registration in drought declared areas.

    These registrations can be dormant up to two years.

    Apply at your local Customer Service Centre to pay a season within the two years period and TMR will reinstate the registration.
Transfer with relief type of Drought
Transfer with relief type of Drought
    If a vehicle with a drought relief type applied is transferred:
    • Relief type of drought will automatically be removed from vehicle registration record on transfer of vehicle
    • New owner can apply (if eligible) at the next renewal period.
Cancellation and Re-registration of Vehicles
Cancellation and Re-registration of Vehicles
    A vehicle may be voluntarily cancelled during drought. The previous COI remains in force when the vehicle is re-registered provided the:
    • Previous COI expiry date is still current at the time of re-registration
    • Re-registration is within 12 months of the cancellation
    • Vehicle is re-registered in the same name
    • Vehicle was in a drought declared shire when cancelled.

    When re-registering:
    • COI is exempted by putting 'N' in the vehicle previously registered field
    • Record text outlining reason for exemption.
Exemption of COI fees
Exemption of COI fees
    The registered operator is eligible for the waiving of TMR issued COI fees if the vehicle:
    • has a garage address recorded in TICA in an area that has been drought declared by the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry (refer to http://www.daff.qld.gov.au/environment/drought/drought-declarations-and-revocations#); and
    • is registered with a POU of FARM; and
    • has a PRIM concession.

    To apply for the exemption customer must:
    • Contact the SBS (13 23 90) at least 2 days before the inspection
    • Confirm garaging address.
    Note:
    • This exemption does not apply to any fee payable to an Approved Inspection Station.
    • No exemptions are given to drought affected primary producers for any other TMR inspection fees, such as a defect re-inspection fee.
    Refund if Fees Already Paid
    Refund if Fees Already Paid
      If the inspection fee has already been paid, the applicant must apply to their local Customer Service Centre for a refund or credit of the fee.

Exemption of RRRF
Exemption of RRRF
    The Registration Renewal Reinstatement Fee (RRRF) that would otherwise apply to late renewal payments, received after the expiry date but before the auto-cancel date, is exempt.
Extended Dormant Period
Extended Dormant Period
    If a vehicle with Seasonal Registration is located in a Drought Declared Shire or property, TMR will extend the dormant period for up to 12 mths. Registrations can be dormant for up to 2 years.
FARM Concessions
FARM Concessions
    Vehicles:
    • With a FARM concession attached
    • Garaged in a drought declared shire and can cart water up to the distances specified for Conditional Registration Zone Access (CRZA).

Fodder Transport by Vehicles with PRIM Concession
Fodder Transport by Vehicles with PRIM Concession
    Vehicles registered with a PRIM concession can cart fodder:
    • To or within a drought declared shire or property
    • On behalf of neighbouring drought declared properties provided:
      • Only actual transport costs are charged
      • Vehicles comply with legal axle loadings
      • Vehicles do not exceed the registered/manufacturers capacity
      • Fodder transported from another state complies with that states road rules.
Stock Grazing on Declared Road Reserves
Stock Grazing on Declared Road Reserves
    For permission to graze stock on a declared road reserve, farmer must contact:
    • Department of Main Roads District Office if public road
    • Relevant local government authority if owned by authority or is a stock route
    • QPS for advice on road safety implications and precautions.
