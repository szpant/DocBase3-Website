    Motorcycles
    Motorcycles
      Registration Category
      • Motorcycle (MCYC)

      Two Wheeled Motorcycles
      Two Wheeled Motorcycles
        Two wheeled motorcycles that comply with the Australian Design Rules (ADR) can be road registered.
      Three Wheeled (Trikes)
      Three Wheeled (Trikes)
        Three wheeled (trikes) includes Tuk Tuks imported after 01 July 1992 can be registered provided:
        • They meet the ADR's and must be approved through full federal compliance (DoI)
        • It has a compliance plate attached or is recorded on the RAV

        A trike that has handlebars and a saddle seat is registered as a Motorcycle (MCYC). The vehicle category on the compliance plate or in the RAV will be LEM.
        Motorcycle licence and road rules apply to these vehicles.

        A three wheeled vehicle that has a steering wheel and convention car seat is registered as a car. The vehicle category on the compliance plate or on the RAV will be LEP.
        Car licence and road rules apply to these vehicles.

        Tuk Tuk
        For information on Tuk Tuks imported prior to 01 July 1992 Refer: Vehicle Rego > 02. Registerable Vehicles > Unregisterable Vehicles > Tuk Tuks

      Learner Approved Motorcycle(LAM)
      Learner Approved Motorcycle(LAM)
        A learner approved motorcycle is a "production motorcycle" that is fitted with an electric motor, or has an internal combustion engine with an engine capacity of not more than 660mL; and
        • has a power to weight ratio of not more than 150kW per tonne; and
        • that has not been modified other than for an allowable modification (LK7 seating modifications and/or mobility modifications); and
        • not be an Individually Constructed Vehicle; and
        • that is stated to be a learner approved motorcycle in a list kept by the chief executive and published on the Department of Transport and Main Road’s website.

        For more on a LAM Refer: Vehicle Rego > 03. Types of Registration > Learner Approved Motorcycle (LAM)
    Wheelchair
    Wheelchair
      Registration Category:
      • Wheelchair (WCHA)

      A motorised wheelchair is a motor vehicle that:
      • Is designed and built for use by a seated person with mobility difficulties,
        and
      • Is not capable of speeds of more than 10km/hr
        and
      • Has a tare weight of </= 150kg
        and
      • Is not propelled by an internal combustion engine.

      Providing the device meets the above requirements, it may be registered as a motorised wheelchair, irrespective of its maximum motor output.

      Note:A motor vehicle which is not designed and built for use by a seated person with mobility difficulties (e.g. for use on a golf course), is not classified as a motorised wheelchair. Refer: Devices that are not motorised wheelchairs below.

      Two seated wheelchairs can be registered only as single seat and must be used by a single person with mobility difficulties.

      Registered in Joint Names
      Registered in Joint Names
        A motorised wheelchair can be registered in joint names provided both operators have met all transaction and documentation requirements specific to motorised wheelchair registration.

        Refer: Vehicle Registration > 06. New Business > New Business > Transaction Requirement and Document Requirement Checklist

        For individuals, the registered operator may allow the motorised wheelchair to be used by another person that due to a physical or medical condition, their mobility is severely impaired and they require the use of a motorised wheelchair for assisted travel.
      Conditions of Use
      Conditions of Use
        Users of the motorised wheelchair must ensure:
        • They are aware of the Queensland Road Rules relating to the use of a motorised wheelchair, and
        • Any person in control of the motorised wheelchair is capable of safely operating the motorised wheelchair.

        This includes a child (as there is no minimum age requirement for registration).
      Devices that are not motorised wheelchairs
      Devices that are not motorised wheelchairs
        If the device was clearly built for another purpose, such as use on a golf course, it cannot be registered as a motorised wheelchair even if it complies with the speed and weight requirements.

        Examples of non-registerable devices are:
        Non-Registerable Devices_Example 1
        Non-Registerable Devices_Example 2

        If the device has a motor that is 200w output or less and was designed and built for another purpose, it is a wheeled recreational device and is not a registerable vehicle.

        If a customer applies for registration of a device designed and built for another purpose (such as use on a golf course) with a maximum motor output of 200w or less, they are to be advised that the device is not registerable as it is a wheeled recreational device. See rules for wheeled recreational devices:
        http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/safety/rules/wheeled-devices/skateboards/index.html

      Note:The TMR website contains motorised wheelchair and mobility scooter brochures. These brochures for individuals and retailers contain information about registration, accessing public transport and a guide for safe travel in Queensland.

    Motorhome
    Motorhome
      A motorhome or campervan:
      • with a GVM </= 4t - Light Commercial (LCOM)
      or
      • with a GVM >4t but </= 4.5t - Motorised Caravan (MCVN)

      A motorhome or campervan is a motor vehicle manufactured/modified to include accommodation space which contains at least the following minimum requirements:
      • table (may be easily removable)
      • sleeping facilities
      • cooking facilities (including a microwave or removable hotplate)
      • storage
      • fire extinguisher
      • outward opening or sliding door opening to the left or rear of the vehicle that allows clear and unobstructed access to the living area.
      • the number of transport seats in the vehicle must be equal to or exceed the number of sleeping positions (e.g. sleeping positions for 4 people must have a minimum seating capacity of 4) (see seating requirements for more information).

      All of the above equipment must be fixed; however the table may be designed to be easily removable.

      Rules
      Rules
        • To be registered as a motorhome, the vehicle must be designed (as described above) for use as a motorhome. If the vehicle has a possible dual purpose, the vehicle must be registered as a truck with the higher class of insurance (eg class 7).
        • A vehicle cannot be registered as a motorhome if it has a designated load carrying area for vehicles/animals etc (not including a bike rack).
        • A motorhome/campervan with a GVM >4.5t requires a Motorhome/Caravan Inspection Sheet(F4550) or proof of previous registration from Queensland showing that the vehicle has been registered as a motorhome / campervan.

      CTP Rules
      CTP Rules
        MAIC describes class 2 insurance as being for a motorised home, but not if the part of the motor vehicle designed for residence is detachable from the part providing the motive power.

        Registration Category:
        • Light Commercial (LCOM)
        • Motorised Caravan (MCVN)
        Body Shape:
        • SVMC
        • MCVN
      PRI requirement for motorhome/campervans
      PRI requirement for motorhome/campervans
        • New business for heavy motorhomes over 4.5t GVM:
          1.All heavy motorhomes need TMR or HVRAS PRI using F4550 Motorhome/Campervan Inspection Sheet
          2.All imported motorhomes also need TMR identity check
        • New business for light motorhomes up to or equal to 4.5 GVMt:
          1. If previously registered anywhere in Australia as a motorhome it does not need PRI. It needs a safety certificate only.
          2. If previously registered in Queensland but not as a motorhome it needs an LH11 modification certificate and/or blue plate
          3. If previously registered in another jurisdiction in Australia as other than motorhome it needs a Queensland LH11 modification certificate and/or blue plate
          4. If imported and not previously registered in Australia it needs TMR PRI using form F4550 Motorhome/Campervan Inspection Sheetand identity check.
      Seating Requirements
      Seating Requirements
        The number of transport seats in the vehicle must be equal to or exceed the number of
        sleeping positions (eg sleeping positions for 4 people must have a minimum seating capacity of 4.)

        Some vehicles may be fitted with additional seats that are used for fold down bedding but not for use as transport seating.

        In this situation a label must be clearly attached to the vehicle indicating which seats can be used as transport seats.

        Note:Not all seats in the vehicle may be able to be used as normal seats when the vehicle is in motion.

        See below for information that should be included on the label.

        "THIS MOTORHOME IS DESIGNED FOR THE CARRIAGE OF (x) PERSONS"
        {A seating plan showing all the provided seats, with an X shown on all seating positions that are NOT designated seating positions}

        WARNING: THE SEATING POSITION(S) SHOWN BY X ON THIS LABEL MUST NOT BE OCCUPIED WHILE THE VEHICLE IS IN MOTION.

    Left Hand Drive Vehicles
    Left Hand Drive Vehicles
      Left hand drive vehicles that are:
      • GVM is </= 4.5t
      • > or equal to 25 years old
      can have standard registration.

      Left hand drive vehicle <25 years old:
      • Cannot be issued a permit for registration purposes
      • Must be converted to right hand drive through the Approved Persons Scheme according to the Code of Practice - Light Motor Vehicles. The Find an Approved Person online service provides the contact information for current Approved Persons who have consented for TMR to release their information to the public for the purpose of vehicle modification certifications. Vehicle Standards & Modifications Advice can be contacted for further information on left hand drive vehicles. Internal and external callers can contact Vehicle Standards, between 8:30am and 4:30pm Monday to Friday (from 9:30am on Wednesdays).
      • Must be transported or towed at the owners expense (excluding vehicles operating on an Overseas Visitors Permit)

      Note:Urgent enquiries from internal staff onlybefore 10.00am or after 4.30pm can be directed to Vehicle Standards

      Any non-urgent enquiries from either internal staff or customers can also be emailed to Vehicle Standards
    Mobile Machinery
    Mobile Machinery
      Registration Category:
      • Mobile Machinery (MMAC, or MMA1)
      GVM:</=4.5t

      Mobile Machinery are light vehicles with a GVM <=4.5t. that are built to transport and operate machinery or equipment forming an integral part of the vehicle.

      Note: Vehicles registered as mobile machinery are to be measured using the Special Purpose Vehicle (exceeding 4.5t GVM) Inspection Sheet (F2874).

      Mobile machinery in excess of 4.5t GVM are referred to as a special purpose vehicle, Refer: Vehicle Rego > 02. Registerable Vehicles > Heavy Vehicles > Types of heavy Vehicles > Special Purpose > Examples of a Special Purpose Vehicle

      Mobile Machinery is not:
      • A truck
      • Semi-trailer
      • Prime mover
      • Tow truck
      • A vehicle that may be conditionally registered.

    Trailers
      A trailer is a vehicle that is meant to be towed, or is towed by a motor vehicle, but does not include a motor vehicle being towed.

      This registration category includes equipment trailers towed by mobile machinery.

      Registration Category:
      • Trailer (TRLR)
      ATM:<=4.5t

      Caravan/Camper Trailer (including pop-top camper trailer)
      Caravan/Camper Trailer (including pop-top camper trailer)
        A caravan is an enclosed trailer designed for people to live in (includes camper trailers).

        A caravan or camper trailer must:
        • be equipped with cooking facilities; and
        • living or sleeping accommodation.
        • must have only outward-opening or sliding doors (with at least one such door located on the left hand side or at the rear). Exempt requirement- trailer based fold out camper trailers (refer diagram in attachment below).
        • must be fitted with a fire extinguisher as designed primarily for human occupation whilst stationary.

      Fire Extinguishers, Smoke Detectors and Fire Blankets
      Fire Extinguishers, Smoke Detectors and Fire Blankets
        It is not a requirement to check the safety or validity of the fire extinguisher, just confirm it is provided.

        Fire authorities strongly recommend that caravans or camper trailers equipped with cooking facilities, or living or sleeping accommodation be fitted with approved fire blankets and smoke detectors for the protection of occupants.

        For more information about building small trailers refer to the VSB1-Building Small Trailers Bulletin which is located on the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development website:

        www.infrastructure.gov.au/roads/vehicle_regulation/bulletin/index.aspx

    Amphibious Vehicles/Vessels
    Amphibious Vehicles/Vessels

      There are some tour vehicles such as Aquaducks/Superducks that are ADR compliant.

      Where these amphibious vehicles are being operated in Queensland waters, they need to be registered both as a vehicle and as a vessel.

      Contact your Regional MSQ Office to obtain appropriate vessel registration advice (for example, either as a recreational or a commercial ship).
