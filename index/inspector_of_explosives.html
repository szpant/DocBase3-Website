Inspector of Explosives (5230)

Statement
This instruction applies to:
  • TMR Officers authorised under the Explosives Act 1999 as an Inspector of Explosives.
  • Vehicles transporting Class 1 or Class 5.1 Explosives.

This instruction provides the procedure to be followed when conducting and finalising vehicle interceptions, as well as procedures to manage non-compliance with specific requirements.
Definitions
For the purpose of this instruction, the following definitions apply:

Term

Background
The TO(RUM) Act allows authorised officers who are appointed an an Inspector of Explosives to stop vehicles and to exercise powers under both the TO(RUM) Act and the Explosives Act 1999. TMR and DEEDI (now known as DRNM) entered into an MOU in 2009 that allows selected Authorised Officers to be trained and appointed as Inspectors of Explosives to ensure compliance during the transport of explosives by road.
Procedures
If an Inspector of Explosives (IE) is not present during an interception, Authorised Officers (AO) must only conduct a standard intercept.

Authorised Officers/Inspectors of Explosives must not handle or transport explosives found; surrendered or seized in the course of their duties.

If there are any concerns about the safety of the vehicle, its load or validity of documentation, IE's should contact the Explosives Inspectorate within the Department of Natural Resources and Mines (DRNM) for instructions (See contacts list).

If advised by the Explosives Inspectorate (DNRM), the Queensland Police Service must be contacted.

Where the vehicle intercepted is displaying Class 1 or 5.1 placards, or the vehicle is found to be carrying Class 1 or Class 5.1 explosives and an IE is present, in addition to the standard intercept actions, the IE is to:
  • For Class 1 Explosives - Inspect the vehicle to ensure all requirements as specified in the Australian Code for the Transport of Explosives by Road and Rail 3rd edition have been met with particular attention given to the security of the explosives being transported.
  • For Class 5.1 explosives - Inspect the vehicle to ensure all requirements as specified in the Australian Code for the Transport of Dangerous Goods by Road and Rail.
  • Complete a Vehicle Inspection Report - AEC and ADG FDU1470 (VIR) and give original (white copy) to the licence holder representative present at the time of intercept.
  • Complete a Remedial Action Dangerous Situation Notice FUD1470 where a vehicle has been identified as in contravention with the transportation requirements or either code and give original (white copy) to licence holder representative present at the time of intercept.

Record the details of the Vehicle Inspection Report AEC & ADG and/or Remedial Action Dangerous Situation Notice in QCIS.

Within seven (7) days, Senior Transport Inspectors are to collate and forwarded the completed documents; Yellow copy of the Vehicle Inspection Report - AEC and ADG and Remedial Action/Dangerous Situation Notice (if required) to:
