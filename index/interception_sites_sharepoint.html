Interception Sites SharePoint (7519)


Interception Sites SharePoint
What it isA SharePoint site has been developed to store additional information for all interception sites as ROAMS can only store 6 photos and cannot store documents.

ROAMS SharePoint

Senior Transport Inspectors, Compliance Administration Officers and Compliance Managers have update access to the ​​​​​​​Interception Site document library. All visitors have read access.

Information to be captured includes:
  • Site Safety Plan
  • Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)
  • Weighbridge/axle platform calibration certificate
  • Site assessment for Cat 3
  • Site survey results
  • Photos of the site and all signage.
Photographs
Photographs
Photographs
    Photographs of the interception site including all signage associated with the site is required;
      • A photo of the site entry in direction of travel
      • A photo of the site exit against direction of travel
      • Naming convention for photos:
        • Site name- description, for example;
        Maryborough north bound entry (attachment 4)
        Maryborough north bound entry (attachment 4)
        Maryborough north bound exit (attachment 5)
        Maryborough north bound exit (attachment 5)

      • Front and back of signs associated with the site. The photo of the back of the sign must show the sign number.
      • Naming of photos must match the site plan:
        • Site name, sign description, sign number, for example;

        Federal 2 80 CL1W Front (Attachment 6)
        Federal 2 80 CL1W Front (Attachment 6)
        Federal 2 110 CL1W Front (Attachment 7)
        Federal 2 110 CL1W Front (Attachment 7)
        Federal 2 80 CL1W Rear (Attachment 8)
        Federal 2 80 CL1W Rear (Attachment 8)
Documents
Naming convention
Naming convention
    The naming convention for documents should be, site name and document title, for example. Jondaryan SOP.
Site Plan
Site Plan
    A site plan with sign description and locations is required for all sites to ensure the signage meets the requirements of the Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices (MUTCD).

    The Site Safety Plan may be used to record the signs and location of the in relation to the interception site. The distance from site entry or exit taper can be measured by using the odometer of the departmental vehicle.

    Federal north bound site plan
