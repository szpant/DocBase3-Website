    2. Message content and 'Who can request?'
    2. Message content and 'Who can request?'
      Who can send a request?
      Requests to send messages can come from any area of the Department, although they should have some connection to customer service business or related topics. The most common requests are received from:
      • Registration Policy Unit (TRB)
      • Driver Licensing Policy Unit (TRB)
      • Systems, Procedures and Training (CSB)

      About the message content.
      • It should be relevant to the Service Delivery Network.
      • Is required to be brought to the attention of all CSB operational staff as soon as possible
      • Simple English
        • Information should be:
          • As concise (brief) as possible
          • Easy to understand
          • Please write in a way the all staff will understand with little difficulty.
      • Please ensure that the information is correct and up to date.
      • Responsibility for the content relies on the authorising officer.
      • Please provide a 'respond to' email address to answer queries generated by the urgent message.

    4. Await response
    4. Await response
      We will:
      • review your request within 2 hours and determine if it is urgent or not.
      • check spelling, punctuation and grammar (although you should ensure this is correct before you send it as errors will delay the message).
      • check validity, if your message is valid, we will send it to the 'Urgent Messages' group.
      • will retain a copy of the message indefinitely.
      • will publish a copy of the message in the 'Urgent Messages' document.
      • If you have not provided enough information or your message is not deemed valid you will receive a response about the outcome.

    2. Message information and who can request
      Who can ask for a Daily Message?
      Anyone can ask but a message should be authorised by the key stakeholders, e.g. information that impacts CSCs should have the consultation of the Systems, Procedures and Training group.

      We receive the most requests from:
      • Registration Policy Unit (TRB)
      • Driver Licensing Policy Unit (TRB)
      • Systems, Procedures and Training (CSB)

      About message content...
      • The message should be relevant to the Service Delivery Network.
      • Is required to be brought to the attention of all staff who use DocBase (CSCs, Call Centre, HelpDirect, QPS, QGAPs, Clerk of the Court etc.)
      • Please use Simple English

      Information should be brief and easily understood. Staff need to understand the information as quickly as possible. Please:
      • avoid repeating information
      • avoid jargon
      • avoid the excessive use of polysyllable words.
      • ensure that the information is correct and up to date.
      • provide a name of the person authorising the message as any correspondence received by DB2 Assist will be forwarded to that person.
    3. Who gets the Daily Message
    3. Who gets the Daily Message
      All staff with access to DocBase3 may read the daily messages.
    4. Send to DB2 Assist
    4. Send to DB2 Assist
      • Input 'DB2 Assist' in the 'To' field
      • cc: authorising officer
      • Push send
    5. Await response
    5. Await response
      We will:
      • review your request within 4 business hours.
      • check spelling, punctuation and grammar (although you should ensure this is correct before you send it as errors will delay the message).
      • will retain a copy of the message indefinitely.
      • refer the message to the information owner and/or Procedures and Training for approval if they have not been consulted.
      • check validity. If your message is valid, we will publish a copy of the message in the 'Daily & Urgent Messages' document.

      If you have not provided enough information or your message is not deemed valid, you will receive a response about the outcome.
Project Update Request Template
Project Update Request Template

    All project updates are to be raised via the Business Issues and Improvement Database (BIID).

    How to raise a "Project Update Request"
    1. Raising a BIID Issue for your project update
    1. Raising a BIID Issue for your project update
      Follow the instructions in the attachment document to raise a project update -

      How to Raise a Project Update via BIID

      Link to BIID database - BIID

      If you don't have access to raise a DocBase BIID, please send an email to DB2 Assist, including your managers approval, requesting access.
    2. Message information and who can request
    2. Message information and who can request
      Message Information
      Information should be brief and easily understood. Staff need to understand the information as quickly as possible. Please:
      • avoid repeating information
      • avoid jargon
      • avoid the excessive use of polysyllable words
      • ensure that the information is correct and up to date
      • provide a name of the person authorising the project update, as any correspondence received by DB2 Assist will be forwarded to that person.

      You can add or remove rows in the project update template as required, depending on how much or how little information is available/required in your update. Keeping in mind you don't want to overload your audience with too much information.

      Note:You can refer to other project updates to assist with the selection of row names and to assist with relevance of information in your update.

      Who can ask for a project update?
      Anyone can ask but the update should be authorised by the key stakeholders, e.g. information that impacts CSCs should have the consultation of the Systems, Procedures and Training group.

      We receive the most requests from:
      • Registration Policy Unit (TRB)
      • Driver Licensing Policy Unit (TRB)
      • Systems, Procedures and Training (CSB)
    3. Who gets the Project Update
    3. Who gets the Project Update
      All staff with access to DocBase3 may read the project update.
    4. Approval process
    4. Approval process
      Before sending your BIID for publishing, please ensure you have approval from the project manager and that it has been reviewed by SDP(CSD). Please refer to 'How to Raise a Project Update via BIID' document for instructions.
