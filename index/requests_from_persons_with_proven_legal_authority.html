Requests from persons with proven legal authority (3361)

The licence/authority/card holder’s written authorisation is not required.

Note:Screen prints from TICA/TRAILS must not be given to customers, external agencies or any other third party.

Queensland Police Service (QPS)
A member of the QPS may apply for a release of licence/authority/card holder information* if they are performing a function/s associated with the role of a police officer.

Requests for a release of licence/authority/card holder information may be accepted at licence issuing centres.

Note:Requests for information from police outside the state of Queensland are not to be treated with equal authority as requests made by the QPS. Refer to "Interstate police agencies and Australian Federal Police"section below.

Requests can be made:
On QPS letterhead or
from QPS email
from QPS email
    • Each centre Central Processing Hub may provide to QPS a generic email address for their centre. Personal email addresses are not to be provided
    • Requests must be received from a QPS email address (ie ...@police.qld.gov.au) and contain the appropriate signature block that clearly identifies the person requesting the information
    • The response will be forwarded by mail

* Including copies of documents (e.g. driver licence application form).
Interstate police agencies and Australian Federal Police (AFP)
Requests from an interstate police agency or the Australian Federal Police are not to be processed unless the request clearly demonstrates that the information is to be used for a court proceeding. If the information is to be used for a pending court proceeding, the interstate police agency or the Australian Federal Police must provide a written request for the information on letterhead which includes a statement advising that the information is required for court purposes including the name of the defendant, the date and place of the trial, and the court case number. These requests should be sent to Customer Service Support (Unpaid Searches).

Where a general request is made that is not related to court proceedings (i.e. it relates to an investigation only) the person requesting the information is to be advised to use their agency’s central information area, which will request the information from QPS if appropriate. The interstate police agency or the AFP must not direct their request directly to TMR.

Power of Attorney holder
A person who holds a power of attorney may apply for a release of licence/authority/card holder information.
The power of attorney that may be accepted as written authorisation for the release of information of another person's licence/authority/card holder's record if they hold one of the following:
  • A general power of attorney authorising the attorney nominated in the form to sign documents on behalf of the person whose digital photo is sought
  • An enduring power of attorney nominated in the form to sign documents on behalf of the person whose digital photo is sought.
    Power of Attorney

Note:A power of attorney about the licence/authority/card holder’s financial matters is not acceptable
Courts
The chief executive may be required, by court subpoena, to release licence/authority/card holder's information to a court.

The information is to be produced by a court by the issue of a court certificate by a delegated officer.

A delegated officer may be required to physically attend court and produce and explain the information or documents held by TMR.

Refer: Offence Management > 1. Court Cases > The How To: Court Cases > Court Cases Procedures > Issue Court Certificate

All internal enquiries about subpoenas can be directed to: Legal Services Unit

All subpoenas are to be processed by Legal Services Unit.

The subpoena must be sent by POST to: Legal Services Unit

Once it has been processed, the Legal Services Unit will then send the subpoena to the relevant are of TMR for them to provide the information to court.
Interstate and Foreign Driver Licensing Issuing Authorities
An interstate or foreign licensing authority, authorised by a corresponding law, to issue driver licences may apply for the release of driver licence related information (including traffic history) if the information is required for the issuing of a driver licence in that jurisdiction.

An interstate authority, authorised by a corresponding law, to issue APA cards may apply for the release of APA card information if the information is required for the issuing of an APA card.

Note:APA card related information cannot be issued to foreign authorities.

Requests from interstate and foreign authorities are processed by Central Operations and Support and must not be accepted at Customer Service Centres. Requests must be directed to:
Interstate ROI - Customer Service Operations (Interstate ROI)

Note: This email address is for interstate/foreign authorities and is the preferred contact method.

Requests will have a 10 working day turnaround.

Authorised government agency
A person representing an authorised Government agency may apply for a release of licence/authority/card holder's information. The person must show the Commonwealth or Queensland legislation entitling the release of information.

Any request to release driver licence/authority/APA card information by an authorised government agency must be directed to: Customer Service Support - Unpaid Searches Unit - Customer Service Support (Unpaid Searches)
Insurance Agency
A representative of an Insurance Agency may apply for the release of a person's licence or registration history and/or copies of documents under the Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994. The representative must present written authorisation from the person authorising them to access the required information.

Any other requests for information, such as CCTV footage, must be referred to Legal Services.
