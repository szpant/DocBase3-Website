Blue Card Screening (7277)

What it isTMR is obligated to ensure that children and young people, who use our services or attend workplaces are protected from harm and are kept safe. Blue Card screening confirms an applicant as a suitable person to undertake the role of a School Crossing Supervisor (SCS).
Issuing AuthorityBlue Card screening is undertaken by the Department of Justice and Attorney General (DJAG). TMR must meet their requirements including identity checks.
Who Requires a Blue CardThe role of the SCS is defined in s 156 of the Public Service Act 2008 as having child related duties and requires Blue Card screening.

It applies to all TMR and school paid employees and volunteers performing the role of a SCS.
When RequiredDJAG has implemented a 'No Card, No Start' policy

https://www.qld.gov.au/law/laws-regulated-industries-and-accountability/queensland-laws-and-regulations/regulated-industries-and-licensing/blue-card/system/review/no-card-no-start

Part of the SCS recruitment process is to confirm an applicants or volunteers eligibility to be issued with a Blue Card (refer to the Blue Card process).

Other relevant reference TMR Employment Screening Policy and Procedure and Risk Management Strategy for roles with Child Related Duties

ValidityA Blue Card is valid for a period of three years.

The DJAG “Check if a Blue Card is valid” Online service allows a point in time check of the validity of a Blue Card.

Access the Blue Card Services (BCS) Organisational Portal to search current Blue Card holder status https://my.bluecard.qld.gov.au/login

Blue Card Process
Obtaining or renewing a Blue Card
Obtaining or renewing a Blue Card
    Applications for a new Blue Card, or to renew a Blue Card can be made through the BCS organisational portal

    Road Safety Officers may assist SCSs with the application process but must not complete the registration or renewal process for them.

    Blue Card application results, for both new issue and renewal, must be recorded on the SCS's Document Management System (DMS) personnel file and in the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme (SCSS) database.

    Refer: 1. School Crossing supervisor Scheme > The How To: SCSS > School Crossing Supervisor Procedures > Blue Card - new employee

    Online application
    Online application
    To apply using the Blue Card online service applicants will need to have a
    • TMR CRN,
    • sufficent EOI
    • econtact details recorded in TICA, and
    • a TMR image set with at least 3 years life remaining.
    Refer: Working With Children Card (Blue Card)
    Issues that will prevent online application
    Issues that will prevent online application
      Insufficent Image life
      Insufficent Image life
        To apply online the applicant must have at least 3 years life remaining on their image set in TICA
      No Online Access Restriction (NOAC)
      No Online Access Restriction (NOAC)
        A NOAC may be applied to a customer’s TICA record at the request of the customer or by the Identity Management Unit if the customer has a suppressed record.

        If an applicant has a NOAC , the applicant must attend a CSC to have the NOAC expired. Once it is expired the applicant has 24 hours to complete their Blue Card application online. TMR automatically reapplies the NOAC after 24 hours.

        Applicants with a Suppressed record only, that is they do not have a NOAC, can access the Blue Card online service.

        Refer: Services > 2. Qld Government Partnerships > Working with Children (Blue Card ) > TMR’s role > Customer has failed validation > Customers with a NOAC restriction for further information on failed Blue Card online service validations.

        Further information on NOAC restrictions is available Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System Information > Qualifications, Restrictions & Text > Customer Restrictions > NOAC

        Renewing
        Renewing
          Blue Card Services will issue reminders to TMR and the Blue Card holder prior to expiry of the Blue Card.

          Renewal applications may be submitted online up to 16 weeks before the expiry date.
          The following handout can be given to SCSs to assist them with the online renewal process
      Application Fees
      Application Fees
        TMR will pay for:
        • a successful applicant to obtain a Blue Card if they do not already have one.
        • Renewal applications for SCS engaged by TMR.

        TMR does not pay:
        • Blue Card costs for SCSs engaged by a third party
        • costs associated with a SCS obtaining a replacement Blue Card.

        Payment is made via the Blue Card Services website refer How To.
        Refunds
        Refunds
          Where both the SCS and TMR have paid a Blue Card application fee, a refund can be requested by emailing info@bluecard.qld.gov.au.
      Existing Blue Card Holders
      Existing Blue Card Holders
        Successful SCS applicants who already hold a Blue Card, and school paid employees with a Blue Card, cannot commence working on a crossing until their card is:
        • validated through the BCS Organisational Portal, and
        • linked to TMR in the BCS Organisational Portal, refer to How To.

        If the card is valid a copy of the validation result must be retained on the employeespersonnel file in DMS.

        If the Blue Card Holder has a volunteer Blue Card status it will need to be transferred to a paid employee and linked to TMR. Refer: How To: Volunteer to Paid Employee Transfer.
      Ceasing employment
      Ceasing employment
        When an SCS ceases to work for TMR, Blue Card services must be notified via the BCS Organisational portal:
        https://www.qld.gov.au/law/laws-regulated-industries-and-accountability/queensland-laws-and-regulations/regulated-industries-and-licensing/blue-card/organisations/update-details
      Blue Card Status
      Blue Card Status
        The status of the Blue Card indicates if the applicant can work with children.

        StatusMeaningOutcome
        Application pendingApplicant has verified identity but has not submitted an applicationPerson cannot work with children
        Application in progressBlue or exemption card application is in progressPerson cannot work with children (unless applying for an exemption card)
        Valid card + application in progressCard holder has a valid card and has submitted a renewal applicationPerson can work with children
        Valid cardBlue or exemption card has been issued and is activePerson can work with children
        Valid clearanceBlue or exemption card issued but the card is not activePerson can work with children
        No valid card- contact BCSPerson dies not have an application in progress, or a valid cardPerson cannot work with children
        Negative noticePerson has a current negative noticePerson cannot work with children
        Disqualified personPerson is disqualifiedPerson cannot work with children
        Card suspendedBlue or exemption card has been suspendedPerson cannot work with children
      Negative Notice Received
      Negative Notice Received
        Blue Card Services will advise the TMR nominated person (this is usually the Manager, Road Safety) by email and will add an advice on the portal record.

        The TMR nominated person is required to advise Blue Card Service of the action taken to to remove the SCS from the children’s crossing.

        The SCS will also receive the negative notice and detailed advice of what action they are required to take.

        When a negative notice received the SCS must be removed from the crossing as soon as practicable. While maintaining the privacy of the SCS, the school must also be informed the person is no longer able to work.

        If an RSO becomes aware that a Blue Card holder has a status of Negative Notice, Disqualified Person or Card Suspended they must advise the SARS\Road Safety Manager immediately.

        Consult the Regional HR about terminating the SCS's employment.

      Authorised Blue Card Contacts
      Authorised Blue Card Contacts

All TMR RS employees that have authorised access to the BCS Organisational Portal are able to liaise with BCS through this up to date information. As authorised portal users RS can update all contact information.(add link).

        • Which contact person has left, and
        • Who has taken over responsibility for Blue Card matters for them
        Or
        2. Lodging an Update organisation details form, available at www.bluecard.qld.gov.au
