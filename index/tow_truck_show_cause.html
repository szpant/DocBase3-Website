Tow Truck Show Cause (7400)


What it isIf the Chief Executive considers that reasonable grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel tow truck licence, tow truck driver certificate and tow truck assistant certificate, the licence or certificate holder must be given written notice inviting them to show cause, within a specified time frame, why the proposed action should not be taken - Tow Truck Act 1973, sections 4C and 21A.

Show cause action to cancel a licence or certificate may be combined with immediate suspension of a licence or certificate.
Grounds for Show CauseIf the chief executive considers a ground exists to amend, suspend or cancel a tow truck licence, tow truck driver certificate or tow truck assistant certificate, the Chief Executive must give the licence or certificate holder written notice, stating the proposed action and inviting the person to show cause within 28 days why the proposed action should not be taken.

Grounds - Show Cause
Grounds - Show Cause
    Ground 1The licence or certificate—
    (i) was issued in error; or
    (ii) was granted or renewed because of a false or fraudulent document, statement or representation.    		21A(1)(a)
    Ground 2The licence or certificate holder—
    (i) is convicted of an offence against the Tow Truck Act 1973; or
    (ii) has, since the issue of the authority, been charged with or convicted of a disqualifying offence; or
    (iii) contravenes a condition of the licence or certificate; or
    (iv) stops being a tow truck operator, driver or assistant.    		21A(1)(b)
    Ground 3For a licence holder that is a corporation—an executive officer of the holder is or has been—
    (i) convicted of an offence against this Act; or
    (ii) charged with or convicted of a disqualifying offence.    		21A(1)(c)
    Ground 4The driver certificate holder can not properly drive the class of motor vehicle stated in the authority because of a medical condition, or physical or mental incapacity.

    Note:The MCR Unit should be contacted as action should be taken against the driver licence in the first instance.
    		21A(1)(d)
    Ground 5The motor vehicle stated in the authority is no longer suitable for use under the authority21A(1)(e)
    Ground 6The authority holder has given false or misleading information to:
    (i) an authorised officer; or
    (ii) an authorised officer under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland)    		21A(1)(f)
    Ground 7Public safety has been endangered, or is likely to be endangered, because of the licence or certificate holder’s conduct.21A(1)(g)
    Ground 8Having regard to the conduct of the licence or certificate holder, when performing activities under the licence or certificate or at any other time, the chief executive believes, on reasonable grounds—
    (i) the person is no longer an appropriate person to hold a licence or certificate; or
    (ii) it is in the public interest to cancel or suspend the licence or certificate.    		21A(1)(h)

Show Cause Outcome and Internal Review
Response received acceptable
Response received acceptable
    If, after considering all written representations made within the stated time frame, the Chief Executive considers the show cause representation acceptable, the licence or certificate holder may continue unaffected.

    Refer below: Notices > Show Cause Decision Notice - Decision Not to Proceed - October 2018
Response received not acceptable
Response received not acceptable
    If, after considering all written representations made within the stated time frame, the Chief Executive considers grounds still exist to suspend or cancel a tow truck licence, tow truck driver certificate or tow truck assistant certificate, the Chief Executive may take the proposed action and must inform the licence or certificate holder of the decision by written notice.
    Refer below: Notices > Show Cause Decision Notice - Decision to Proceed - October 2018
    The written notice contains information advising that the licence or certificate holder may ask for the decision to be reviewed by the Chief Executive.

    If the show cause action relates to a matter that is the subject of a proceeding before a court that has not been finally decided (for example, charges for disqualifying offences), the chief executive:

    • need not make a decision under until the proceeding is decided by the court
    • but must make the decision as soon as reasonably practicable after the proceeding is finally decided by the court.
Internal review
Internal review
    If requested by the licence or accreditation holder, an internal review must be conducted of the decision to suspend or cancel the tow truck licence, tow truck driver certificate or tow truck assistant certificate.
Internal review overturns the decision
Internal review overturns the decision
    If, after considering all written representations made within the stated time frame, the Chief Executive considers the internal review representation acceptable, the licence or certificate holder may continue unaffected.

    Refer below: Notices > Show Cause Decision Notice - Decision Overturned - October 2018
Internal review upholds the decision
Internal review upholds the decision
    If, after considering all written representations made within the stated time frame, the Chief Executive considers grounds still exist to suspend or cancel a tow truck licence, tow truck driver certificate or tow truck assistant certificate, the Chief Executive may take the proposed action and must inform the licence or certificate holder of the decision by written notice.
    Refer below: Notices > Show Cause Decision Notice - Decision upheld - October 2018
    The written notice contains information advising that the licence or certificate holder may apply through the QCAT process to have the decision reviewed.

    If the customer lodges an application for review of decision with QCAT, a TMR representative will attend and both parties will be informed of the outcome.

    If the decision was to suspend or cancel the licence or certificate the written notice must also advise them to surrender the licence or certificate by a specified date (usually within 14 days) – section 21G.
Giving of NoticesNotices may be sent by mail and/or email and may be given in person.

Returned Notices
Returned Notices
    Sent by Email
    Sent by Email
      If a notice sent by email returns a non-delivery error it must be mailed through Australia Post (if it wasn’t already mailed).
    Sent by Mail
    Sent by Mail
      If a notice sent via Australia Post is returned unclaimed, this does not affect the process. The matter must proceed and a decision made based on the evidence mentioned in the notice.

      A text message should be placed on TICA to alert any CSCs transacting business with the licence or certificate holder to obtain a new address then contact the relevant office progressing the show cause or immediate suspend action.
Licence/certificate not returned When a licence or certificate is suspended or cancelled the holder must return the licence or certificate to a CSC or by mail to the address listed on the show cause notice.

The licence or certificate must be returned within the time stated in the show cause or immediate suspend notice. If the licence or certificate is not returned within the time specified in the notice the non-surrender of the licence or certificate should be referred to the local Compliance Area.
