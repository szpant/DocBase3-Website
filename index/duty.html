Duty (3024)


What is it?Duty is a tax imposed under the Duties Act 2001 which TMR collects on behalf of the Office of State Revenue (OSR) on a:
  • New registration
  • Transfer of registration

Exemptions do apply
Important Terms
Dutiable Value
Dutiable Value
    • The value of a vehicle when applying for a new registration or transferring an existing registration
    • The value must be declared on the application, or obtained via Formless Mandatory Questions, and input into TICA even if an exemption applies (excluding vehicles which attract a flat rate of duty or attract no duty (such as, trailers, non-motorised caravans and motorised wheelchairs)

    Determination of Dutiable Value
    Determination of Dutiable Value
      • GST is considered to be part of the supply of a product/service Duties Act 2001 S378 (2)and must be included.
      Vehicles never registered
      Vehicles never registered
        Dutiable value is the total of both:
        • Brisbane list price (inclusive of GST)
          and
        • Value of optional equipment (eg, power steering, disc brakes) not included in the list price

        If vehicle sold at a discount the standard list price is used to calculate dutiable value.

        If no list price available, calculate as per 'vehicle previously registered.

        Modified Vehicle for a Disabled Person - Never Registered
        Modified Vehicle for a Disabled Person - Never Registered
          The dutiable value for a new vehicle modified for a disabled person is the total of:
          • the list price (including GST)
          • any optional equipment not included in the list price
          This amount is then reduced by the value of any modifications fitted for the disabled person.

          The registered operator must present an OSR Statutory Declaration (Modified Vehicle-Disabled Person) with their registration application. The declaration will list the dutiable value to be used for the calculation of vehicle duty.

          This form is available on the OSR website:

          http://www.osr.qld.gov.au/forms/duties-forms/stat-dec-forms/stat-dec-modified-vehicle.pdf

      Vehicle Previously Registered
      Vehicle Previously Registered
        Vehicle previously registered in either Qld or interstate calculate the dutiable value as the higher of the:
        • Total amount payable by the purchaser including any:
          • Deposit
          • Trade-in allowance
          • Optional equipment prices
          • Market Value

        Modified Vehicle for a Disabled Person - Previously Registered
        Modified Vehicle for a Disabled Person - Previously Registered
          The dutiable value of a vehicle modified for a disabled person is the market value (including any optional extras) of the vehicle reduced by the value of the modifications fitted for the disabled person.

          The registered operator must present an OSR Statutory Declaration (Modified Vehicle-Disabled Person) with their registration or transfer application. The declaration will list the dutiable value to be used for the calculation of vehicle duty.

          This form is available on the OSR website:

          http://www.osr.qld.gov.au/forms/duties-forms/stat-dec-forms/stat-dec-modified-vehicle.pdf

      Trucks
      Trucks
        The value of a truck includes:
        • Cab and chassis
        • Optional equipment including:
          • Tray
          • Body fitted and agreed to be fitted by the dealer
          • Any other equipment fitted
Dutiable Value for Luxury Light Vehicles
Dutiable Value for Luxury Light Vehicles
    From 01 July 2018 Luxury Light Vehicles with a Dutiable value of $100,000.01 or more will be charged at higher rate of duty.

    Note:Office of State Revenue have decided to exempt the Luxury Light Vehicle Duty Increase for any vehicles that had a Contract of Sale dated prior to 30 June 2018.
    Luxury Light Vehicles duty increase exemption includes a calculator


    Heavy Vehicles are excluded from these new higher rates of Duty.
Relative
Relative
    A relative is the registered operators:
    • Spouse (includes civil partnerships, same sex couples and de - facto relationships; provided the couple have lived together for at least two years at the time of application)
    • Parent or grandparent
    • Spouses parent or grandparent
    • Child, stepchild or grandchild
    • Spouses child, stepchild or grandchild
    • Relative's spouse (spouse of one of the above)
Market Value
Market Value
    The amount for which the vehicle might reasonably be sold, free of encumbrances, on the open market as estimated by the owner.

    Customers are advised not to contact the Office of State Revenue for assessments of market value of the vehicle.
List Price
List Price
    Vehicle
    Vehicle
      The list price is the highest recommended retail price set by either the:
      • Manufacturer
      • Importer
      • Principal Brisbane dealer of the vehicle

      Luxury Car Tax is included in the list price for duty purposes.

    Truck
    Truck
      The list price is the highest recommended retail price for the cab-chassis make and model set by either the:
      • Manufacturer
      • Importer
      • Principal Brisbane dealer of the vehicle

      Luxury Car Tax is included in the list price for duty purposes.
Optional Equipment
Optional Equipment
    All equipment not included in the list price that is an integral part of the vehicle and without limiting the meaning includes all features actually or usually fitted by a manufacturer including air-conditioning.
Potential false declarations
Potential False Declarations of Dutiable Value
Potential False Declarations of Dutiable Value
    If an operator has serious concerns with the dutiable value declared for a vehicle or there is conflicting information where the customer's eligibility for a duty exemption is in doubt, follow the below:
    Dutiable value:
    Dutiable value:
      • Explain the meaning of dutiable value and how this can be determined. Refer above: Determination of Dutiable Value

        Note: The vehicle invoice (purchase receipt) or Redbook valuation may assist them in this determination.

    Duty exemptions:
    Duty exemptions:
      Where a customer is claiming a Parenting Payment Single concession AND a duty exemption due to marriage/partnership/de-facto, follow the steps below:
      • Clarify with the customer that they have selected the correct duty exemption and/or concession reason.
      • If the customer has selected:
        • De-facto duty exemption, confirm that they were living with the person at the time of transaction and had been living together for at least 2 years
        • Marriage/Partnership duty exemption, clarify that they are no longer living with the person but are still married/legally partnered.

      If the customer confirms they are eligible for the exemption and has evidence of their concession, the transaction must be processed (regardless of the suspicion of entitlement).

      Notes should be recorded on the "Office Use Only" section of the form stating that the customer has confirmed their entitlement to the duty exemption/ concession.

    Suspected false dutiable value/exemptions
    Suspected false dutiable value/exemptions
      If serious concerns remain, the transaction must be processed. This is because TMR is not authorised to share individual cases of suspected false duty value/exemptions with the Office of State Revenue (OSR).

      TMR does however provide regular data extracts of vehicle registration/duty to OSR, which is authorised under the Taxation Administration Act 2001. OSR use this bulk data to monitor/identify duty offences.

      If it is determined by OSR that a customer has falsely declared the dutiable value/exemption, the customer may incur a penalty in addition to the required duty payable.
Payment of DutyDuty is:
  • included in the total cost of the transaction
  • Payable unless exempt

Calculation of duty for motor vehicles other than light vehicles with a dutiable value over $100,000
Calculation of duty for motor vehicles other than light vehicles with a dutiable value over $100,000
    The Dutiable Value of a vehicle is first rounded up to the nearest $100 (e.g. A vehicle with a dutiable value of $15010 would be rounded up to $15100).

    Duty payable for a vehicle is then calculated at a variable percentage rate of the Dutiable Value using the number of cylinders/rotors or fuel type.

    Example of variable duty rate
    A 4 cylinder petrol car has a Dutiable Value of $15010
    • Dutiable Value is rounded up to nearest $100 being $15100
    • Duty calculation rule is $3.00 per $100 on $15,100 (3%)

    Duty payable is $453
Calculation of duty for light motor vehicles (not more than 4.5t) with a dutiable value over $100,000

Calculation of duty for light motor vehicles (not more than 4.5t) with a dutiable value over $100,000
    For example, the rate of duty for a 4-cylinder vehicle with a dutiable value of $120,000 will be $5 per $100 which equates to $6,000 in total.

Refunds
Refunds
    All requests for duty refunds and/or objections must be directed to the Office of State Revenue (OSR) and should be made within 60 days of the new business or transfer transaction. Unless:
    • Duty incorrectly processed by TMR operator/agent to customer declaration at new business or transfer; and
    • Refund requested within 5 years of transaction.

    Note: Duty refund requests for the non-delivery of a motor vehicle processed by a motor dealer or cancelled sales, resulting for example, from the sale of an unroadworthy vehicle, must be made to OSR within 1 year.

    OSR can be contacted on 1300 300 734 or by emailing mvi.mail@treasury.qld.gov.au for more information.
