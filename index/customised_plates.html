Customised Plates (3090)


What are They?Customised are standard plates that have been purchased allowing the purchaser to retain the plate combination.
Why Customise?
Why Customise?
    Plates may be customised to:
    • Retain the same number if the plates need to be replaced (customising a currently issued plate)
    • Get a new plate with a number that was issued in the past but is no longer in use (Retro/Previously Issued plates)
    • Order a slimline plate
Transactions
Customise Currently Issued Plates
Customise Currently Issued Plates
    • Plates must remain attached when customising
    • Plates can then be removed from the vehicle and kept as vacant plates (replacement plates would need to be attached to the vehicle if registration to remain current)
    • If registration is in joint name both operators become the owners of the plates
    Note:If a vehicle the plates are recorded as being attached to is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service, the plates may be customised, however, they cannot be remade while the vehicle is under the notice (see Replace Customised Plates below).
Customise Previously Issued Plates
Customise Previously Issued Plates
    • Standard Number Plates (excluding National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates(NHVNPs) that were issued in the past and are no longer in use can be purchased as customised plates and either:
      • Attached to a vehicle
        or
      • Kept as vacant plates
    • Vacant plates are plates that are not currently attached to a vehicle
    • Previously issued or customised plates may be re-issued as customised plate only if the number plates have been surrendered and cancelled.
    • Plates must be in a lapsed status in TICA
    • Previously-issued NHVNPs can’t be customised as they may have been reused interstate.
    • Surrendered NHVNPS will never display a lapsed status in TICA.
    Note:Customised plates cannot be attached to a vehicle currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS). For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at
    https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/default.htm

    Retro Plates
    Retro Plates
      PPQ refers to previously issued older series customised plates as "Retro" plates.

      Series available as retro are:
      • White on Black, and
      • Start with N, O or P

      Customers should be encouraged to purchase a personalised number plate instead (there is more flexibility with the use of personalised plates).
      • If the customer wants to proceed with a Retro plate:
        • Pay the fee to the customer's account.
          Note:For current fees Refer: Fees and Forms > 1. Fees > Customised Plate Fees > Customise Previously Issued Plates (Lapsed)
        • Raise a SCO to have the processing window opened for the specific combination to be ordered.
        • Advise the customer there will be a delay in delivery of the plates.
        • Process the request once advised by the service centre that the window has been enabled.
      • Remakes are still available.
Upgrade of Standard Plates to Customised/Personalised Plates
Upgrade of Standard Plates to Customised/Personalised Plates
    Standard plates (excluding National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates) can be re-issued as Customised/Personalised Plates if application is made:
    • 3 mths from the date of surrender if all plates have been surrendered and cancelled
    • 14 mths from date of cancellation of registration if all plates have not been surrendered and cancelled.

    As National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates can be reused interstate their plate status will never be updated to cancelled or lapsed.



    Notes:The plate history must show an action of LAPS.
    Unlike personalised plates, customised plate/s can only be issued in the same colour as originally issued.
Replace Customised Plates
Replace Customised Plates
    Only one surrendered
    • Remaining plate is attached to rear of vehicle
    • If both plates are damaged the least damaged plate is to be attached to the rear of the vehicle
    • Attached plate is to be surrendered when new set received
    Remake one plate only
    • One plate must be surrendered
    • Other is attached to rear of vehicle
    • Two new plates must be ordered
    • Advise the customer that:
      • the cost is the same
      • two plates will be produced
      • they must surrender the other plate when the new set is received
    Both plates Damaged/ Lost/Stolen
    • Both plates surrendered on TICA
    • Declaration must be completed if plates not presented
    • Customer to carry replacement receipt in vehicle until new plates received

    Notes:If a vehicle the plates are recorded as being attached to is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS), full requirements must be met when replacing customised plates in a customer service centre (ie plate surrender requirements). For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at
    https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/default.htm

    If the vehicle has a BHSL the customer must also complete Taxi/Limousine/Special Purpose Limousine/Booked Hire Licence Amendment Application F2976 and the plate details must be updated in the Managed booked hire staff UI.
Swap Customised Plates
Swap Customised Plates
    Applies when the plates on two vehicles are to be swapped (only an option if both vehicles/plates are in the same name).

    Note:If a vehicle the plates are recorded as being attached to is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS), you cannot process this transaction on TICA during the confiscation period. For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at
    https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/default.htm

    Note:This transaction can only be completed for cars or motorcycles

    Using Dummy plates
    Using Dummy plates
      • Removes the need for the customer to purchase standard plates during the transaction
      • Dummy plate numbers are available from Help Direct
      Swapping process
      Swapping process
        • Remove Personalised/Customised plates A from vehicle A
        • Attach Dummy plates to vehicle A
        • Remove plates B from vehicle B
        • Attach plates A to vehicle B
        • Remove Dummy plates from vehicle A
        • Attach plates B to vehicle A

        DO Not surrender the Dummy plates at any time.

Remove Customised Plates
Remove Customised Plates
    This is removing the customised plates from the vehicle and attaching another set of plates.

    Notes:If a vehicle the plates are recorded as being attached to is currently under a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service (QPS), you cannot process this transaction on TICA during the confiscation period. For more information on the notice, advise customer to contact QPS directly by visiting the Motor Vehicle Impoundment page on the QPS website at https://www.police.qld.gov.au/programs/roadSafety/vehimpound/default.htm

    If the vehicle has a BHSL the customer must also complete Taxi/Limousine/Special Purpose Limousine/Booked Hire Licence Amendment Application F2976 and the plate details must be updated in the Managed booked hire staff UI.
Jointly owned plates
Jointly owned plates
    Ownership of customised plates can only be transferred when attached to a vehicle.
    To remove one name, the plates must be transferred, along with the vehicle and then re-customised.
    If the customer being removed is deceased, the transfer fee and re-customised transaction fee are exempt. Acceptable Proof of Death document must be provided (original or certified copy) unless already recorded in TICA as deceased.


    Note:No additional documents are required to remove the deceased owner from the jointly owned plates.

Rules
Rights of the Plate Owner
Rights of the Plate Owner
    Customised plate owners:
    • Owns the plate number (TMR always owns the plate)
    • Can retain the plate as a vacant plate if the registration is cancelled
    • Cannot transfer ownership of the plates
      If the vehicle is being transferred the plates must either be:
      • Removed and replaced
      or
      • Transferred with the vehicle which removes its customisation
    • Can be attached to a vehicle registered in exactly the same name as the owner of the plate number
    • Cannot attach plates to a vehicle of which the plate owner is not the registered owner
    • Cannot sell the plates
    • Can upgrade the plates to personalised (plates can be sold then)

    Note:If a customised National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate is transferred interstate the customisation is removed. If the plate is later reused by the same registered operator in Queensland they can re-customise the plate at no cost.
