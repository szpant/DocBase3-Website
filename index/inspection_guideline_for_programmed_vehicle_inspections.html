Programmed Vehicle Inspections (PVI) (7246)


Programmed Vehicle Inspections (PVI)
What it isA Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI) is conducted by TMR inspectors on TMR inspectable vehicles that require a Certificate of Inspection (COI) for registration purposes.
Inspection procedure
Inspection procedure
Inspection procedure
    The Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI) Procedures were developed to provide guidance for inspectors conducting inspections so a consistent standard of inspection is carried out by all inspectors.

    Refer: Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI) Procedures

    TMR DocBase policy over rule where conflict exists with NHVR Work Procedures.

    The Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI) Procedure is the recommended procedure for conducting inspections, however a degree of flexibility in the process is necessary due to a range of variables, e.g. staff availability, vehicle booking numbers, weather conditions, local facilities or equipment availability.

    All inspections should be undertaken with reference to the National Heavy Vehicle Inspection Manual (NHVIM) or Code of Practice - Vehicle Inspection Guidelines (COP-VIG) in respect to causes of rejection when deciding if the vehicle passes or fails the inspection.

    The NHVIM and the COP-VIG provides inspectors and operators a consistent criteria for inspections. The use of the NHVIM and the COP-VIG will ensure a consistent approach to improve vehicle standards compliance.

Roller Brake Testing
    The NHVR released the National Brake Roller Testing (RBT) Procedure to provide a national standard for the equipment and test procedure used to conduct a RBT.

    A RBT is to be conducted in accordance with the brake testing equipment manufactures instructions and the local SOP.
