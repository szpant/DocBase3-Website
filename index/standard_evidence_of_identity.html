Standard Evidence of Identity (5000)


About Evidence of Identity (EOI)
What it isEvidence of identity documents prove:
Individuals
  • The legal existence of the person (Category A documents)
  • Their name and date of birth (Category A documents)
  • The use of their name in the community (Cat B documents)
Organisations - The existence of the organisation
Why EOI is Required
TMR Responsibilities
TMR Responsibilities
    TMR responsibility is to protect and maintain the integrity of the register (TRAILS) and the products issued and provided therein.

    TMR Officers must be satisfied beyond reasonable doubt that the evidence of identity documents provided by the customer:
    • Are authentic
    • Clearly show that the customer is who they say they are

    In person (Individuals Only)
    In person (Individuals Only)
      Applies to:
      • Individuals only (refer to Representing section for organisations)

      Requirements:
      • One of the following:
        • current Qld driver licence (or one that has been current at any time within the last 2 years)
        • adult proof of age or photo identification card
        • industry authority smartcard
        • marine licence indicator smartcard
        • 1 Category A + 2 Category B documents
        • 2 Category A + 1 Category B document

          Note:The Category A and Category B documents should be dissimilar in nature (e.g. if a Full Birth Certificate is presented then a Birth Extract would not be acceptable)

      Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI
      • Customers must always be advised of the standard EOI requirements, however they must not be turned away from a Centre if they do not have all of the required standard EOI and Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI procedures can be applied.
        Similarly, existing Smartcard customers must not be advised to obtain Category A documents that the customer no longer has in their possession simply for the purpose of obtaining a TMR product, if Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI procedures can be applied.

      • In lieu of standard identity credentials, the Acceptable Variation of a 1:1 facial image comparison (FIC) and Customer Identity Checklist (CIC) or a FIC and either a category A or category B document can be used where a person has already established EOI and they have a facial image recorded, and they do not have the required EOI documents on them.

      Refer: Customer > Evidence of Identity > Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI > Acceptable Variation to Standard EOI > Customers with EOI Record (with a current or previously issued smartcard product)
    By phone (Individuals/Organisations)
    By phone (Individuals/Organisations)
      The following provides the EOI requirements for both incoming and outgoing phone calls.

      Standard call introduction (not transferred from SSQ)
      “For identification purposes, I am required to ask you a series of questions. Can I begin with your customer reference number or vehicle registration number please?"

      Individuals
      Individuals
        If the customer can provide their CRN or vehicle registration number, you can display the customer record in TICA, verify the customer's full name and date of birth then continue with the remaining first level identity questions below.

        If the customer cannot provide their CRN or vehicle registration number, they can provide their full name and date of birth, once confirmed continue with the remaining first level identity questions below.
      Organisation
      Organisation
        If the customer can provide their customer reference number or vehicle registration number you can display the customer record in TICA, verify the organisation name then continue with the remaining first level identity questions below.

        If the customer cannot provide their CRN or vehicle registration number, search using the organisation's name and ACN/ANBN/IA* number, once confirmed continue with the remaining first level identity questions below.

        *ACN - Australian Company Number
        ANBN - Australian National Business Number
        IA - Incorporated Associations

      Call transferred from SSQ
      If an SSQ consultant has verified EOI requirements and then transfers the call to a TMR consultant, they will record a text restriction against the customer that includes the date and time that the EOI was verified.

      When a TMR consultant receives the call, they must do the following before answering the enquiry:
      • Check that the EOI was verified within the last 30 minutes
      • Confirm knowledge based questions
      • Expire the text restriction

      If more than 30 minutes have lapsed, the customer will have to satisfy EOI requirements again.

      Identification Requirements:

      Individual
      Individual
        The customer must authenticate to First level before the operator can commence Second level (Knowledge Based Questions).

        First level authentication identity questions
        • QLD Driver Licence Number (CRN)/Registration Number
        • Full name
        • Date of birth
        • Place of birth
        • Current address

        All first level identity questions must be asked and responses must match all fields recorded in TICA.

        The customer's phone number and email address must be checked and updated after the customer has been identified.

        Second Level authentication identity questions
        A number of knowledge based questions from a combination of the below categories:
        • Customer details history
        • Registration details
        • Licence history
        • Other QLD product history (including BEA/BHSL)
        • Traffic history
        • Enforcement history

        Second level questions only need to satisfy the operator that the customer is who they say they are.

        Knowledge based questions provide the customer an opportunity to demonstrate personal knowledge of the record.

        Each verification section has been designed with a lead question; which must be asked before any subsequent questions in the section. If a customer answers the lead question with a No response or cannot demonstrate sufficient knowledge of the record, the operator must move on to the next section. Refer: Using the Identification Tool > Marking the Identification Tool below.

        Qld Driver Licence Holders
        Qld Driver Licence Holders
          If a Qld driver licence holder does not have access to their driver licence and needs their CRN, it can be released via email (only) to the email address recorded by TMR once verbal EOI has been completed using the Identification tool. This can be done by:
          • Resending a document/transaction (which contains the CRN) from their online account management (mandatory if document/transaction history exists); or
          • Emailing their CRN (without any other identifying details) directly to the recorded email address if no document/transaction history exists. This can only be done by using the attached email template.

          Release of CRN Email Template


        Non-Qld Driver Licence Holders and Qld MLI holders
        Non-Qld Driver Licence Holders and Qld MLI holders
          Non-Qld driver licence holders and Qld MLI (only) holders may be provided with their CRN to pay a hand issued infringement, use an online service or lodge a paper application (e.g. BHSL/BEA).

          The CRN can be provided:
          • verbally after completing the EOI Identification tool; or
          • by email after providing sufficient EOI information to identify the record (name, dob, address, offence details etc).


          Release of CRN Email Template

        • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
        • Organisation name
        • Current business address
        • Australian Company Number/ANBN**/Incorporated Association Number
        • Vehicle registration number*
        • Contact phone number

        **ANBN - Australian National Business Number

        The same application of identification standards apply to both Individuals and Organisations.
      Using the Identification Tool
      Using the Identification Tool
        Before you start
        Once the Identification Tool is open operators must enable the tool by:
        • clicking "Enable Content" at the top of the worksheet before you begin,
          and
        • clear the results, using the clear button at the right of each worksheet before you use the tool for the next customer.
        Marking the Identification Tool
        Marking the Identification Tool
          For each correct answer provided, the operator is to tick the box located beside the question.

          Customer confirmation that no product exists is also marked as a correct answer.

          If a customer is unable to provide a response or provides an incorrect response, the operator is to leave the box blank.

          The Identification Indicator box will change to Yes and highlight Green when the customer has sufficiently identified. Once this box turns green operators are not required to ask any further questions.

          Refer to each section below for the rules on meeting identification standards.
        Meeting First Level Authentication
        Meeting First Level Authentication
          To meet First Level Authentication requirements customers must be able to answer 4/5 questions correctly. Once a customer has suitably identified in First level a TICK will appear at the top of the column.

          If a customer is unable to meet requirements they must be advised to attend their local CSC with suitable EOI for the transaction required.
        Meeting Second Level Authentication
        Meeting Second Level Authentication
          Operators must ask a minimum of 8 questions (this includes the First Level Authentication Questions).

          Operators may tailor the order in which they complete the verification section to assist identification of the customer. To ensure integrity of the process the operator should guide the applicant to answer more than one question in at least one verification section.

          The customer must also be able to answer at least 4 of 8 knowledge based questions correctly. The response must match what is recorded in TICA to be marked as correct.

          Four correct responses in at least two verification sections will return a Customer Sufficiently Identified message in the Identification Indicator box.

          If a customer is unable to meet requirements they must be advised to attend their local CSC with suitable EOI for the transaction required.

        To determine when a customer has been suitably identified using a combination of the above questions, please use the below identification tool.

    By SMS (Individuals)
    By SMS (Individuals)
      The following provides the EOI requirements for both incoming and outgoing phone calls,
      where an individual customer has a CRN and a mobile number recorded in TICA.

      This function is for Client Relations Consultants only and cannot be used by CSC operators (unless they are performing Customer Contact Centre work).

      Standard call introduction (not transferred from SSQ)
      “For identification purposes, I am required to ask you a series of questions.
      Can I begin with your customer reference number or QLD driver licence number?

      Authentication identity questions
      Authentication identity questions
        • QLD Driver Licence Number or Customer Reference Number
        • Full Name
        • Date of Birth
        • Mobile Number

        All questions must be asked and responses must match all fields recorded in TICA.

        If questions don't match TICA, revert back to ‘By phone (individuals/organisations)’ process above.

        Once the authentication identity questions have been answered correctly, a verification code may be generated and sent via SMS.
      Scripting to generate the verification code
      Scripting to generate the verification code
        "If I send a text message to the mobile number you have provided, would you be able to read back the verification code to me?"

        If yes, generate code using the code generator.

        If no, revert back to ‘By phone (individuals/organisations)’ process above.

        The verification code is valid for 10 minutes and the customer must read the code back to the operator. Once this code has been verified, the customer has been successfully identified.

        Note: The customer's residential/postal address and email address must be checked and updated after the customer has been identified.
