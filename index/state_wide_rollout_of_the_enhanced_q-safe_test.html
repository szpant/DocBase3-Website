State Wide Rollout of the Enhanced Q-SAFE Test (5328)

The information provided below is for INTERNAL USE only.
Project NameState wide rollout of the enhanced Q-SAFE test
WhenMonday, 29 June 2015
BackgroundThe full rollout of the enhanced Q-SAFE test follows a three month pilot conducted across a number of locations in 2014.

The enhancements will apply to all practical driving tests (car, heavy vehicle and motorcycle) given that these enhancements relate to safe driving skills and behaviours that are relevant to driving all classes of vehicles.
Who will be impacted and how?All TMR licensing issuing centres, Queensland Police Service Stations and Queensland Government Agency Program offices that conduct practical driving tests (car, heavy vehicle and motorcycle) will implement the enhanced test.

Assessment reports
From the rollout date all driving examiners must use Driver Assessment Report (car and heavy vehicles) (F5080) and Rider Assessment Report (F5081) to assess practical driving tests.

These assessment reports replace the current Driver Assessment Report (F2090). F2090 cannot be used from 29 June 2015 and will be unable to be ordered from Staples from 24 June 2015. All remaining stock should be destroyed.

PADAs have been advised to order the new reports to ensure that all testing centres have them available commencing 29 June 2015.

TICA changes
Changes will be made to TICA.

There will be new TICA screens where additional data must be recorded regarding the test elements.

Training will be provided.
Further informationThe key enhancements to Q-SAFE include:
Manoeuvres
Manoeuvres
    Reducing the minimum number of manoeuvres assessed from three to two (a minimum of one must contain a reversing component).

    The current selection of manoeuvres will be retained: turn around, U-turn, hill start, automatic gear change, reversing exercise and reverse park; the driving examiner must assess at least two.
Performance checks
Performance checks
    Increasing the severity of consequences for certain performance checks -
    • Speed – there will be zero tolerance to speeding. The Non-Critical Driving Error (NCDE) and Specific Repeated Driving Error (SRDE) have been removed. This performance check will have a Critical Driving Error (CDE) only.
    • Judgement – including an SRDE if four NCDEs are accumulated.
    • Safety margins – including an SRDE if four NCDEs are accumulated .
    • Sequence; system of vehicle control – removing this performance measure.
Compulsory drive situations
Compulsory drive situations
    • Introducing a compulsory number of driving situations.
    • Introducing a set number of compulsory turns at controlled and uncontrolled intersections. For example a minimum of two right turns and one left turn at T sections and three right turns and two left turns at crossroads.
    • Introducing highly desirable drive situations to be assessed if the infrastructure and/or traffic conditions are available such as high speed merging and/or entering a high speed area.
Applicant feedback
Applicant feedback
    Increasing the ability for driving examiners to provide written feedback to applicants.
