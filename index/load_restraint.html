Load Restraint (5243)


Load Restraint
What it isLoads must be restrained on a vehicle to prevent unacceptable movement during all expected conditions of operation. The load restraint system must, therefore, satisfy the following requirements:
    1. The load should not become dislodged from the vehicle.
    2. Any load movement should be limited, such that in all cases where movement occurs, the vehicle’s stability and weight distribution cannot be adversely affected and the load cannot become dislodged from the vehicle.

Performance Standard
Performance Standard
    The same performance standard applies to both light and heavy vehicles. The performance standard is contained in the Load Restraint Guides and the NHVR work procedure.


    Note:Alternative load restraint methods to those referred to in the guide may be used provided they have been shown to meet the performance standards.

Inspection
Inspection of load
Inspection of load
    Inspection of the load restraint methods, systems and equipment used on heavy vehicles form part of routine on-road inspection processes. Authorised Officers should look for indicators that load restraint may be deficient even if the load is enclosed (for example, van or soft-sided trailer), this may include but is not limited to; the curtains bulging on soft-sided trailers, unused load binders, gates not in place.

    Some vehicles use security seals (commonly known as Load Seals) to ensure the security of their contents throughout a journey. These load seals may be removed, if necessary to examine the effectiveness of the load restraint system.

Enforcement
Sanctions
Sanctions
    If a contravention of loading requirements is detected, sanctions available at the roadside include;
    • Formal Warnings for minor breaches,
    • Infringement Notices for minor and substantial breaches
    • BTA for severe breaches or breaches for which Infringement Notices are not provided.
    A prohibition notice must be issued for any contravention of the load restraint performance standards.
Observations and Evidence
Observations and Evidence
    Authorised Officers should document observations in relation to any movement of the load, and/or evidence of previous movement of the load, and where restraints have been used but appear insufficient, including:
    • Where possible taking photographs of all loading equipment and restraint systems in use. Photos which depict any load movement, incorrect loading positioning and any other items which show the deficiency are also encouraged and can assist in substantiating any sanction or subsequent prosecution.
    • Recording any items that are shrink wrapped or unitised by any other means, accurately describing the load. that is, record number and contents of boxes and so on if known, record any identifiers if available.
    • Recording the types (and number) of restraint systems in use. FOR EXAMPLE, chain, rope, webbing and so on. Including the diameter/rating where possible and the visual condition of the restraints.
    • Recording any specifications visible on the restraint equipment.
    • Recording the type of friction materials between the load and the vehicle (for example, metal on metal or timber on metal); and
    • Copying documents relating to any certification of the load restraint system (if applicable) and record evidence of a departure from the loading procedure required by this certificate, and estimate the weight of the load.
