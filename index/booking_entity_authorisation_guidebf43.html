Booking Entity Authorisation Guide (7233)


About Booking Entity Authorisation (BEA)
What it isA Booking Entity Authorisation (BEA) is a qualification that an operator of a booking service must attain and maintain to arrange a booking to provide a booked hire service.

A booked hire service may be provided by licensed taxis, limousine or booked hire vehicles (for example, vehicles operating through a booking platform like Uber or GoCatch).
When is it requiredA booking entity authorisation can be held by an individual, corporation, incorporated association, partnership, trustee of a trust or Council (Shire or Regional) that operates a booking service.

From 1 December 2017, only authorised booking entities will be able to provide booking services, including a booked hire service provided by a taxi, limousine or a booked hire vehicle.

Note:From October 2017, operator accreditation requirements no longer apply to taxi services and booked hire services (including booked hire services provided in limousines). Other services such as General and Scheduled services will still require an Operator Accreditation.

Deceased BEA holder
    • If a representative of a deceased BHSL/BEA holder presents to a CSC with proof of death, the operator must do the following (in addition to Updating the CRN as deceased):
      • Check for BEA/BHSL products
      • Email a copy of the proof of death document toTransLink BHSL/BEA Hub. TransLink will then surrender the BHSL/BEA.
    • If an operator does not notify TRO that the customer is deceased and they have BHSL/BEA products, the customer will display on a TransLink non-compliance report. In this case, TRO can check TICA and print out a copy of the Individual Customer Hub showing the deceased details and surrender the BHSL/BEA.
