    Proof of Tare Weight
    Proof of Tare Weight
      Motor Vehicles
      Motor Vehicles
        A tare weight is needed for:
        • Light commercial motor vehicles
        • Motor vehicles with a GVM >4.5t

        The tare weight can be recorded from one of the following:
        • Manufacturers Plate
        • Owners manual
        • Previous registration Certificate
        • Registered operator nomination
        • Weighbridge certificate

        Note:A manufacturers plate, owners manual or previous registration certificate or registered operator nomination can only be used to record the tare if the vehicle has not been modified since publication of the proof document.

        No weighbridge within 20km
        No weighbridge within 20km
          If there is no weighbridge within 20km of where the application is lodged the tare may be taken from one of the following:
          • Statement from registered operator
          • Manufacturers specification.

      Trailers
      Trailers
        A tare weight is needed for:
        • Special non-load carrying trailers with the registration category of STRL.

          Registration
          Category:          		STRL - Mobile Equipment trailers with ATM >4.5t
          Non-overmass STRL Trailers
          Non-overmass STRL Trailers
            The tare weight can be recorded from one of the following:
            • Statement from registered operator detailing individual (split) axle/axle group weights and overall weight
            • Proof of previous registration details (previous registration certificate or system details)
            • Manufacturer's plate or a letter from the manufacturer.
            Note:A weigh bridge certificate is not required as proof of tare, however; if presented it can be accepted.
          Overmass STRL Trailers
          Overmass STRL Trailers
            Require a supervised weigh. For requirements Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Weights & Measurements > Tare > Proof of Tare Weight > Mobile Machinery/Special Purpose Vehicle > Cranes
      Mobile Machinery/Special Purpose Vehicles
      Mobile Machinery/Special Purpose Vehicles
        Weighbridge Certificate must include:
        • Tools, Tool kits and any equipment deemed to be an integral part of the vehicle and crucial to its operation
        • Individual (split) axle/axle group weights
        • Overall Tare Weights

        Note:For Trailers, only STRL trailers require proof of tare. For Requirements Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Weights & Measurements > Tare > Proof of Tare Weight > Trailers

      Cranes
      Cranes
        Require a Mobile Machinery Supervised Weigh Sheet (F4015) to be completed by a TMR Transport Inspector/Compliance Officer and verified by an Advisor (Permit Management System).

        A supervised weigh is required as:
        • An ordinary certificate cannot supply the required information
        • Mass may exceed normal dimensions the weight of each axle is needed to ensure no damage to assets (such as roads, bridges) occur
        • Any mass outside the limits allowed in the Vehicle Limits Manual requires an assessment by the Bridge Assessment Management Section

    Changing TARE
    Changing TARE
      Any physical change to a heavy vehicle that alters the tare weight must be undertaken by an Approved Person

      Modifications that alter the tare can include:
      • Replacing a steel body with aluminium
      • Change of boom on a mobile crane

      Requirements
      Depending on the vehicle type, one of the following must be provided:
      • A Mobile Machinery Supervised Weigh Sheet (F4015) completed by a TMR Transport Inspector/Compliance Officer and verified by an Advisor (Permit Management System); or
      • Weighbridge certificate; or
      • Statement if nearest weighbridge is >20km away and CSC Inspection to ensure measurements still comply

      For proof of tare requirements by vehicle type Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Weights & Measurements > Tare > Proof of Tare Weight

GVM
    Determining GVM
    Determining GVM
      GVM can be determined using one or more of the following:
      Manufacturers Rating

      Either:
      • GVM is stamped onto the compliance or manufacturers plate
      • Letter from the manufacturer certifying the GVM (information on individual axle groups should be included)
      • An independent professional Engineers report advising of individual axle and gross mass limits

      The GVM may be dependant on the type of tyre fitted to the vehicle (eg, 15t GVM with 10 ply tyres but only 12t GVM if tyres are 8 ply).

      Applicant may choose the lowest regardless of the tyre ply but may only have the highest rating if tyres of the correct ply.
      Sum of Individual Axle Limits
      • To be used if there is no manufacturers GVM
      • Add together the maximum regulation axle/axle group limits for a rigid vehicle
      Extreme axle distance
      Previous Registration
      S1 Rating obtained from an Approved Person
      Business Systems Management Unit
      • Make and models can be researched in a Ratings book for pre 1985 vehicles (not all makes and models are available)
        Contact         CSB.Data Integrity

    Light vehicle with 10 000LB GVM
    Light vehicle with 10 000LB GVM
      This is an approximation only and applies to vehicle manufactured around 1974 eg Ford F350 Trucks.

      To convert from pounds to kg multiply by .000454
      eg 10000 x .000454 = 4.54t

      This should move the vehicle up into the heavy vehicle category, however a decision has been made to disregard the .04t and register the vehicle as light vehicle with a GVM of 4.5t.

    Determining GCM
      GCM can be determined by one of the following:
      • Manufacturers Rating either:
        • GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
        • Letter from the manufacturer certifying the GCM (can only be provided as proof of GCM at first registration in Australia)
        • Previous Registration
        • Appropriate S rating through the Approved Person Scheme
          • Modification Certificate
          • Modification Plate attached to vehicle
      Note:The GCM is only recorded in TICA when a lead vehicle is a heavy vehicle (GVM>4.5t).

Tyre Placards
Tyre Placards
    Trailers manufactured after July 1990 require a tyre placard showing:
    • Tyre and rim data (eg rim size, load rating and recommended tyre pressure)
    • Axle or axle group loading carrying capacity
    • Tyre speed category
    • Statement saying:

    "The sum of the load carrying capacities of the tyres fitted to any axle or axle group of the trailer shall not be less than the relevant load shown"

    The above details must be either:
    • Embossed
    • Indented
    • Etched
    • Engraved
    in block letters not less that 2.5mm in height

    The plate must be:
    • Permanently affixed
    • In a prominent position
Safe Tyre Limits
Safe Tyre Limits
    Load index chart for safe tyre limits is to be accessed to calculate tyre assessment. https://www.tyresizecalculator.com/charts/tire-load-index-chart

    Tyre assessment requires the conversion of the load index to kg, then add all tyre load ratings to get the total permissible GTM of the trailer/vehicle.

    If GTM is unavailable, the ATM can be used. The total of the load index ratings must be 120% of the GTM or ATM if a light trailer does not have load sharing suspension.

Regulation Limits
Regulation Limits
    • A vehicle must comply with regulation limits to be registered. Some mobile machinery may meet the requirements to operate under a Performance Guideline for Excess Dimension or a letter of approval from TMR Vehicle Standards for vehicles with an ATM less than 4.5 tonne or;
    • An exemption from compliance with a heavy vehicle standard issued by the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR).

    Regulation Limits are as follows:
    • The WIDTH of the vehicle cannot exceed 2.50 metres
    • The HEIGHT of the vehicle cannot exceed 4.30 metres
    • The LENGTH of the vehicle cannot exceed 12.50 metres
    • The REAR OVERHANG cannot exceed 3.70 metres and 60% of the wheelbase

    For vehicles with Axle Group Spacing:
    • The TWIN STEER front axles must be spaced at least 1m but no more than 2m apart
    • The TANDEM rear axles must be spaced at least 1m but no more than 2m apart
    • The outer axles of the rear TRI AXLE group must be spaced at least 2m but no more than 3.2m apart

    Refer to individual inspection sheets for other Regulation Limits.

    REMINDER: Do not include lights when measuring the width of a vehicle.
