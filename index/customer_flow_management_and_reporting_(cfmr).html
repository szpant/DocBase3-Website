Customer Flow Management and Reporting (CFMR) (7302)

Project NameCustomer Flow Management and Reporting (CFMR)
WhenMid to late 2018
BackgroundSince the early 1990s, Customer Services Branch (CSB) has been using a queue management system supplied by Q-Matic Australia in many of its Customer Service Centres (CSCs) across the state. The Q-Matic system (including kiosks) has been unsupported since 31 December 2017, exposing CSB to the risk of unrepairable system faults throughout the network.

The CFMR project will see the current Q-Matic system replaced with Q-Flow, a centrally managed and supported solution that meets TMR's current and future needs.

All CSCs will receive Q-Flow software and 40 CSCs will also receive the new hardware. Q-Flow allows data collection and reporting on the number of customers served, and includes the capability to capture Back of House processing time.
Who will be impacted and how?All CSCs across the state will be impacted by the implementation of Q-Flow. To support the CSC network during the change , the project team will continue to co-ordinate with CSC Managers and SASMs to ensure individual CSCs needs are considered, and provide up-to-date information.

CSC staff will receive training for the new Q-Flow system, and printable training materials are available in DocBase. There will be an opportunity for all staff to experience the new system in a pre-production environment, prior to roll-out.

Once implementation is finalised, all CSCs will be using the new Q-Flow system. The new system will be fully supported and maintained resulting in fewer outages and a smoother experience for our customers and staff.

The roll-out schedule for CSCs requiring hardware (see link below) is subject to change. Any changes will be communicated to the affected CSCs directly. The CFMR project team are continually working and developing future activities and will ensure updates, changes and finalisation dates are communicated when available.

Further information will be provided in the coming weeks.
TrainingTraining for staff will be provided through a short online information package in Accelerate. This package will include information on changes, new equipment, using the software and troubleshooting. Staff will have access to the training environment for practice/training purposes prior to Q-Flow going live.

A training schedule will be distributed to all Customer Service Centres shortly to advise the recommended LED session for staff to complete the training. This will coincide with your centre going live. Staff can self enrol in this course in Accelerate.

It is highly recommended that staff read the latest Project Updates, FAQs and DocBase updates prior to the centre’s LED session.

Training for this initiative is mandatory and completion is recorded in Accelerate.

‘Software only’ sites will be provided with an alternative online information package in Accelerate. As most of these sites have never had any type of customer flow management system in place, this package will be more comprehensive and may require two LED sessions to complete.

Representatives from each region attended train-the-trainer sessions run by the supplier. These representatives will then train the SA(SM)s and Managers from sites that are getting hardware, which will be coordinated locally.

Any questions raised during training should be directed to your SA(SM)s who can contact Rochelle Dent (Rochelle.M.Dent@tmr.qld.gov.au) or Veronica Mommers (Veronica.J.Mommers@tmr.qld.gov.au) if they need further information.

PilotTo test the new software and hardware in a real-life environment and to ensure the new system would meet TMR's needs, a pilot phase was completed. Q-Flow hardware and software was rolled out to five CSCs in Southern and SEQ South regions. These were Ipswich, Toowoomba City, Toowoomba Harristown, Warwick and Logan.

Feedback from the pilot has been overwhelmingly positive, and has helped refine the implementation process.
Technical Support / TroubleshootingDuring the life of the project, any issues with Q-Flow performance should be reported through the project team.

After the roll-out is complete, the on-going support process will be communicated and recorded in DocBase.
