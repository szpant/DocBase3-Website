National Driver Work Diary (2487)


What is a National Driver Work Diary?A National Driver Work Diary (work diary) is used by drivers of fatigue regulated heavy vehicles to record:
  • Rest breaks
  • Work times

It is designed to ensure that all drivers of fatigue regulated heavy vehicles take adequate breaks from work related activities.
When is a National Driver Work Diary required & when is it not required?
Important terms and definitions you need to know
Important terms and definitions you need to know
    Most definitions are sourced from the Heavy Vehicle National Law Act 2012.

    What is a 'fatigue regulated heavy vehicle'?
    What is a 'fatigue regulated heavy vehicle'?
      A heavy vehicle is a 'fatigue regulated heavy vehicle' if it is any of the following:
      • a motor vehicle with a GVM of more than 12t
      • a combination with a GVM of more than 12t
      • a bus >4.5t built or fitted to carry more than 12 adults (including the driver).

    What is excluded as a' fatigue regulated heavy vehicle'?
    What is excluded as a' fatigue regulated heavy vehicle'?
      A heavy vehicle is not a 'fatigue regulated heavy vehicle' if it is any of the following:
      • a tram
      • a motor vehicle that:
        (1) is built, or has been modified, primarily to operate as a machine implement off-road, on a road-related area, or on an area of road that is under construction; and
        (2) is not capable of carrying goods or passengers by road.
      • a motor home
      Note:A truck to which a crane or drilling rig is attached is not excluded.

    What is a 'combination'?
    What is a 'combination'?
      A combination is a motor vehicle connected to 1 or more trailers.
    What is Gross Vehicle Mass (GVM)?
    What is Gross Vehicle Mass (GVM)?
      Gross Vehicle Mass (GVM) is the maximum loaded mass of a vehicle:
      • stated on the vehicle’s identification plate; or
      • if the NHVR has specified the vehicle's maximum loaded mass under section 57 Heavy Vehicle National Law Act 2012- specified by the NHVR under that section.
When is a work diary required?
When is a work diary required?
    A driver is required to carry and complete a work diary if they:
    • Drive a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle
      and
    • Engage in work 100+km from the driver's base
      Note:There is an exemption for primary production if travelling within 160km radius from driver's base.
      Refer: When is a work diary NOT required? > Fatigue Management Exemptions
      or
    • Drive under BFM hours or AFM hours
      Note:There is an exemption for primary production if travelling within 160km radius from driver's base.
      Refer: When is a work diary NOT required? > Fatigue Management Exemptions
      or
    • Drive under a work/rest hours exemption.
      Note:If the driver is required to have a work diary they must keep it with them in the fatigue regulated heavy vehicle for at least 28 days after the last date they were required to complete it.
When is a work diary NOT required?
When is a work diary NOT required?
    A driver does not need to carry and complete a work diary if any of the following apply:
    • The vehicle is a motor home
    • They are working under Standard Hours and working within 100km of the driver's base area (local area work).
      Note:Regardless of whether or not a driver must keep a work diary, they must always work to Standard Hours and the record keeper must maintain and keep records as set out in legislation.

    Fatigue Management Exemptions
    Driver's Base
    Driver's Base
      The driver's base is the place from which the driver normally works and receives instructions. If the driver's base is not the garage address as indicated on the vehicle registration certificate, then the driver may nominate an alternative address in the work diary. If the driver is a local area driver working under Standard Hours, the driver does not need to complete the daily sheets. However, if the driver is working under any other work and rest arrangement they must also complete the daily sheet when working under that arrangement.
      Note:If an address is not recorded in the work diary, the driver's base will be taken from the garage address of the vehicle.

    100km Work
    100km Work
      (also known as Local Area Work)
      The local area is within a 100km radius of the driver’s base in Queensland.
      Requirements for record keepers 100km work
      Requirements for record keepers 100km work
        For each day a driver works under Standard Hours and within the local area, the record keeper is required to keep a record of all of the following:
        • Driver's name, driver licence number and contact details
        • Date of each driver's driving day
        • Registration number of each heavy vehicle the driver drives
        • Total of the drivers work and rest times on each day and each week the driver drives a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle
        • The driver's rosters and trip schedules, including the details of driver changeovers
        • Copies of payment records relating to the driver, including time sheet records if the driver is paid according to time at work

    Outside 100km
    Outside 100km
      (also known as 100+km)
      The driver is required to complete a daily sheet in their work diary for each day they worked under standard hours, an accreditation or a work rest hour exemption.
      Note:For accreditations and work rest hour exemptions, this includes local and non local area drivers.

      Requirements for record keepers outside 100km
      Requirements for record keepers outside 100km
        If a driver is doing 100+km to work, the driver's record keeper must keep a record of all of the following:
        • Driver's name, driver licence number and contact details
        • The driver's rosters and trip schedules, including the details of driver changeovers
        • Copies of all duplicate pages and other copies of work diary entries given to the record keeper (under section 319 of the Heavy Vehicle National Law Act 2012)
        • Copies of payment records relating to the driver, including time sheet records if the driver is paid according to time at work

      Obligations of the driver and the record keeper
      Obligations of the driver and the record keeper
        • A driver must give the record keeper copies of their daily sheets from their work diary within 21 days
        • The record keeper is required to obtain a copy of the driver's daily sheet within 21 days.
        • The records must be kept by the record keeper for at least 3 years
    Where to Apply
    Where to Apply
    Where to Apply
      Work diaries are available from:
      • Most TMR CSC’s
      • Queensland Government Agents
      • Some Police stations in more remote areas

      All applicants for a work diary must apply in person.
    Requirements
    Documents Required
    Documents Required
      Applicants must:
      • Present a valid driver licence
      • Complete and sign a work diary application form (located in the front of each work diary)
    Application
    Application
      The application is the front page of the work diary.
      The application must be:
      • Completed by the driver in the presence of the issuing officer
      • Signed by the applicant and witnessed by the officer
      Interstate licence holders
      Interstate licence holders
        If a National Driver Work Diary is sold to a customer with an interstate driver licence (except VIC and WA), a copy of the application must be forwarded to the respective interstate authority.

        If a National Driver Work Diary is sold to a customer with a:
        • Victorian Driver licence - a copy of the application must be forwarded to the Fatigue Management Unit - National Heavy Vehicle Regulator.
        • WA Driver licence - file as normal at CSC. WA do not require a copy of National Work Diary applications to be sent.

        A list of addresses for the interstate authorities and Fatigue Management Unit is provided in Step 8 of -
        Sell a Work Diary

      Qld licence holder purchases work diary interstate
      Qld licence holder purchases work diary interstate
        Interstate jurisdictions who sell a work diary to a Qld driver licence holder must send a copy of the application form to the following address at TMR:

        Email: North_Stradbroke_QGAP@tmr.qld.gov.au

    Current Driver Licence Required
    Current Driver Licence Required
      The work diary may only be issued (first issue or replacement) to applicants who hold a valid driver licence.
      These include the following types of Australian and Foreign driver licences:
      • Provisional
      • Probationary
      • Restricted
      • Open
      Note:A driver who is learning to drive a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle must comply with all the requirements. If the work requires a work diary to be completed both the learner and supervising driver must complete and carry a work diary. The supervising driver must record driving time and time supervising the learner as "work" in his/her work diary.

      Definition of Australian/Foreign driver licences
      Definition of Australian/Foreign driver licences
        Australian driver licence
        Either of the following:
        • Queensland driver licence (includes a driver licence receipt)
          Driver Licence receipt
        • Corresponding document to a Queensland driver licence issued under a corresponding law to the provision of the TO(RUM) Act 1995 for a Queensland driver licence (eg a licence issued in another Australian State, Territory or External Territory (Inc. Cocos/Keeling, Norfolk & Christmas Islands)

        Foreign driver licence
        • A licence to drive a motor vehicle under the law of another country (Incl New Zealand)
    Conditions of Use
    Conditions of Use
    Conditions of Use
      A driver must always carry their driver licence and ensure that the work diary is:
      • Accurately completed in every detail, in accordance with the instructions
      • Kept up to date
      • Produced for inspection when required by an Authorised Officer
      • Carried for at least 28 days after the last date a daily sheet was completed

    Replacement
    Replacement work diaries
    Replacement work diaries
      Before issuing a replacement work diary, a current driver licence and the old work diary of the applicant must be produced, unless the work diary has been lost/stolen/destroyed:
      If the work diary is lost/stolen/destroyed/completed
      If the work diary is lost/stolen/destroyed/completed
        Driver's Responsibilities
        Driver's Responsibilities
          • The driver must lodge an application within 2 business days after becoming aware that the work diary has been lost, stolen or destroyed.
          • Drivers with completed work diaries:
            • do not have to obtain another work diary unless they wish to continue driving a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle.
            • who want to continue to drive a fatigue related heavy vehicle, must apply for a new work diary or maintain a record that is compliant to requirements noted in the Heavy Vehicle National Law Act 2012.
            • driving a fatigue regulated heavy vehicle, must keep the completed work diary with them for 28 days from the last entry they made in the completed work diary.
          • If a driver cannot obtain a new work diary, they may use supplementary records for up to 7 business days.
            • If a driver wishes to use supplementary records they:
              • must contact the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) to obtain an acknowledgement to use supplementary records.
              • must carry the acknowledgement while using supplementary records.
          • After 7 business days they must cease using supplementary records and:
            • obtain a new work dairy or
            • drive only in a local area (<100km from base) or
            • cease driving fatigue regulated heavy vehicles until they obtain a new work diary

            Note:Drivers must keep supplementary records with their current work diary in the vehicle for 28 days after the last date they were used and give a copy to their record keeper within 21 days of the date of the record.

        TMR Officer responsibilities
        TMR Officer responsibilities
          • Cancel the remaining pages in the completed work diary (if available) and return the work diary to the driver, as the driver must keep the completed work diary with them for 28 days from the last entry they made in the work diary.
          • Do no accept any returned work diaries. It is the customers responsibility to manage their driving records and legal requirements.
          • If the work diary was lost, stolen, destroyed, or the driver is unable to purchase a new work diary, give the below information sheet to the driver.
            National Driver Work Diary Information Sheet - June 2021

          • If there are no work diaries in stock, the driver can use supplementary sheets for up to 7 business days.
          • If the driver wishes to use supplementary records the driver must contact the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) to obtain an acknowledgement to use supplementary records and carry the acknowledgement while using the supplementary records.

          If old work diary is located
          If old work diary is located
            If the lost or stolen work diary is located, the driver must:
            • immediately cancel any unused sheets in the old work diary by drawing two parallel lines across each page and writing CANCELLED in large letters
            • continue using the new work diary
              If old work diary is found within 28 days of being lost/stolen
              If old work diary is found within 28 days of being lost/stolen
              The driver must:
              • immediately notify TMR (as the delegate of the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator), in writing that the old work diary has been found
              • hand the work diary in to TMR within 2 business days after the 28 day period finishes
              If old work diary is found more than 28 days after it was lost/stolen
              If old work diary is found more than 28 days after it was lost/stolen
              The driver must:
              • return the old work diary to TMR (as the delegate of the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator) as soon as possible.
              The office that receives the old work diary should:
              • ensure that all pages have been cancelled
              • return the old work diary to the driver as they must retain it for their records

          If the work diary has been seized
          If the work diary has been seized
            If the work diary cannot be produced due to seizure, ask the applicant to produce either the:
            • Seizure receipt
            • Full details of the seizure (where, time, date, Police or Authorised Officer)
            Note:If the work diary has been seized because it was obtained illegally, the Authorised Officer or Police Officer will make a note on the receipt saying "do not issue a National Driver Work Diary on the basis of this receipt e.g. duplicate work diary or work diary not owned by driver".

          If the work diary has been left behind
          If the work diary has been left behind
            If the:
            • Work diary has been left behind the driver is responsible for retrieving it
            • Driver is unaware of where it is, it can be considered lost
            • Driver states it is lost, refer to the lost/stolen/completed section above

            There is no provision in legislation to use supplementary sheets if an issued work diary has been left behind and the driver is aware of it's location.

            Until the driver retrieves the work diary or determines that it is lost they must:
            • Drive only in a local area (<100km from base) or
            • Cease driving fatigue regulated heavy vehicles
