Gas Certificates (7139)

What are they?As of 1 July 2011 gas system compliance plates are required to be fitted to all gas fuel systems and gas systems. A gas system compliance plate certifies that the gas fuel system/gas system complies with the relevant Standards.

There are two types of gas systems:
  • A gas fuel system is a gas system that supplies gas as a fuel to an engine.
  • A gas system is a system that consists of installed gas devices eg: gas cooker, refrigerator in a caravan, mobile home or food van.

A gas certificate is proof of the safety of gas fuel systems and gas systems on a vehicle/vessel.

A Gas System Compliance Certificate is used for certifying compliance of a gas system, including those installed in a caravan, trailer, recreational vehicle or boat.

A Vehicle/Vessel Gas System Inspection Certificate is used for certifying the installation, alteration or inspection of a gas fuel system in a vehicle.

Note:If a certificate does not list the VIN, Chassis or registration number in the 'Location' box, the certificate should be rejected as incomplete as the certificate cannot be linked.

Note:Vehicles registered with an alternate fuel system are required to affix the appropriate label on the front and rear number plate. Refer customers to gas suppliers/installers or automotive retailers to obtain. For further information Refer: Schedule 1 Part 10, TO(RUM - VSS) Reg 2010.
Requirements
When required
When required
    A vehicle with a gas fuel system or a gas system installed requires a gas certificate:
    • At time of inspection of a 'COI vehicle'

    Note:COI vehicles used solely for private use do not require periodic inspections but the owner must ensure all fittings are maintained in a safe and efficient condition. Refer Information Sheet No 6 of the Code of Practice - AIS).

    • To transfer a vehicle
    • To register a used vehicle
    • When a gas fuel system is installed/removed (for full list of requirements Refer: 05. Vehicle Requirements > Modifications > Specific Modifications > Fuel Changes > Gas)

    A gas certificate can be issued by either:
    • Qld authorised gas examiners/installers
    • Interstate authorised gas examiners/installers (the certificate must refer to compliance with a corresponding law (i.e. In Queensland, the certificate states "I hereby certify that the Gas System listed above, as installed, is fully compliant with the safety requirements and other relevant provisions of the Petroleum and Gas (Production and Safety) Act 2004 and Regulation 2004".The Interstate certificate should include a statement similar to the above, which refers to the interstate Act or Regulation).

    Note:Only inspection certificates Form PGR101/102 (fuel system) or Form PGA734 (cooking facilities) are acceptable. These certificates are individually numbered and the original is to be provided to the Department of Transport and Main Roads. The operator is to photocopy the original and return the original to the customer.

    A gas certificate must be issued within 3 months of the requested transaction
Rules
Currency of Certificate
Currency of Certificate
    Valid for three months from date of issue to:
    • Register a used vehicle/vessel
    • Transfer a vehicle/vessel
    • Can be used for more than one transaction while current
    • Is invalid if alteration to the vehicle's gas fuel system or gas system have been done since the certificate was issued and a re-certification is needed under the Petroleum and Gas (Production and Safety) Regulation 2004.
Exemptions
Exemptions
    New Business
    New Business
      • New vehicle/vessel does not require a gas certificate if it has a gas system compliance plate attached.

      Used vehicle/vessel - registering in the same name/s:
      • Registering an unregistered vehicle/vessel in the same name/s that it was previously registered (includes a dealer re-registering a demonstration vehicle into the same dealer name).
      • Re-registering an interstate registered vehicle/vessel in Qld in the same name/s.

      Islands and Remote Locations
      Islands and Remote Locations
        Islands
        Islands
          • The intended garage address is on a Queensland island
            and
          • There no authorised gas examiner/installer on the island.
          • The exemption applies if a vehicle/vessel is disposed of on the mainland.
        Individual Exemptions
        Individual Exemptions
          The Department of Natural Resources, Mines and Energy (DNRME) may grant exemptions to individuals who are unable to obtain gas certificates if there is no one authorised to issue gas inspection certificates within a reasonable distance of the person's residence. Refer to the Queensland Government website for the exemption process at https://www.qld.gov.au/emergency/safety/home/gas/gas-fuelled-engines

          An individual exemption must be applied for prior to applying for registration of a vehicle, prior to lodging a transfer of registration and prior to lodging change of vehicle fuel type.

          A copy of the exemption issued by DNRME must be provided to TMR with an application for registration, application for transfer or change of vehicle details form.
