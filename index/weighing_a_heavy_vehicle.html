Weighing a Heavy Vehicle
Weighing a Heavy Vehicle
    An Authorised Officer must ensure that the vehicle is weighed in accordance with instructions.

    Procedure - Weighing a Heavy Vehicle
    Measure the mass, using portable scales or a weighbridge, then calculate the measured mass carried by axles, axle groups, vehicle units and the whole vehicle or combination by adding together the appropriate scale or weighbridge readings.

    Important Note:Please note the changes when using portal scales:

    • all axles of the combination must be at the same height/level. (see below diagram 1)
    • if insufficient portable scales are available to weigh all axles of a combination in one vehicle
    movement, blocks may be substituted for scales to ensure all axles are at the same
    height/level
    • where blocks are used, they must be of the same height profile as the portable scale(s) used

    **Please ensure you record the blocking method in your notebooks and/or taking photos of how it was undertaken.**

    Diagram 1:

    Concrete trucks
    Concrete trucks
      Concrete trucks are loaded taking into account the bowl turning at agitating speed. If the agitator was to be turned off during weighing there is a chance that the agitator may not immediately start again which could ruin the load, as well as set the load inside the bowl.
      • Concrete trucks are to be weighed in the same condition that the vehicle is operating in. That is, if the agitator is turning, it must remain turning during the weighing process.
      • When weighing a concrete truck with the agitator turning, Inspectors are to take the lowest reading on the scale.

    Mass checks of heavy haulage combinations
      Standard Quad axle trailers and dollies
      Standard Quad axle trailers and dollies
        A heavy vehicle / combination utilising a quad axle trailer (low loader) with air or mechanical suspension and fitted with an axle/s of 4 tyres per row or a dolly with air or mechanical suspension and fitted with an axle/s of 4 tyres per row are considered complying axle groups under the National Heavy Vehicle Law (NHVR) but without any permit, notice or approval are restricted to 20 tonne on the quad axle trailer and 42.50 tonne on the gross mass value.

        When performing a mass check, measurement adjustment is to be applied for each weighing step on quad axle trailers. All axles within the quad axle group are to be raised to the same height when weighing. For example, if only 4 portable scales are being utilised to weigh 2 of the axles then timber blocking should be placed under the remaining 2 axles. There is no requirement when weighing this type of configuration for the whole combination to be raised to the same level. That is, end to end weighing is acceptable.

        When determining the assessed mass, Mass Measurement Adjustment (MMA) will be deducted from an axle / axle group based on the actual weighing device readings.

        For an axle / axle group deemed to be overloaded and is referred to within Schedule 1 “General Mass Limits”, Part 2 “Axle Table” of the Heavy Vehicle (Mass Dimension and Loading) National Regulation, and the gross mass values of the permit, notice or approval has not been exceeded, will be dealt with as a breach of condition offence, pursuant to section 129(1) of the NHVL. The masses for the other axles groups in the combination as specified in the permit, notice or approval will continue to apply, or

        For a gross mass value over the permit, notice or approval, the combination is to be breached back to regulation gross mass values. That is, 42.50 tonne.

        Regarding prohibition limits, for an axle / axle group that is referred to within Schedule 1 “General Mass Limits”, Part 2 “Axle Table” of the Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation, the standard prohibition mass values apply. That is, 20% of regulation mass values for a standard prime mover, dolly or quad axle low loader. For a breach of a gross mass value, the prohibition limit would be the value over the permit, notice or approval.

        Any breach action can be dealt with as normal under the appropriate Penalty Infringement Notice (PIN) or Brief to Adjudicator.

1-tonne Tri Axle Mass Transfer Allowance (1TMTA)
1-tonne Tri Axle Mass Transfer Allowance (1TMTA)
    The 1TMTA provisions allow increased mass over a tri-axle group on a heavy vehicle so that the tri-axle group may be loaded by up to 1 tonne above the standing 20t General Mass Limit (GML).

    Qualifying vehicles can only run on the HML Routes in Queensland Refer: to HML Maps: Heavy Vehicle Route Maps and Restrictions

    The 1TMTA only applies to tri-axle groups which qualify under GML to be loaded up to 20t.
    It does not apply to heavy vehicles operating under:
      • Concessional Mass Limits (CML);
      • Higher Mass Limits (HML);
      • a notice or permit that provides for increased mass;
        or
      • Performance Based Standards (PBS), unless the PBS vehicle has been approved to operate under the 1TMTA.

    The 1TMTA does not permit any increase to the vehicles maximum (total) mass; but rather it provides more flexibility in how mass may be distributed across axle groups.

    Where a combination operating under the 1TMTA contravenes a mass requirement of the 1TMTA, the mass exception no longer applies and the combination will be assessed against GML.
Mass Risk Categories
Mass Risk Categories
    Authorised Officers must classify a mass offence into minor, substantial or severe risk breach categories, according to the mass limits they are operating under.

      • a mass equalling 105% of the maximum mass (rounded up the nearest 0.1t)

      A contravention of a mass requirement applying to a heavy vehicle is a substantial risk breach if the subject matter of the contravention is:
      • equal to or greater than the substantial risk breach lower limit for the requirement;
        and
      • less than the severe risk breach lower limit for the requirement.

      A contravention of a mass requirement applying to a heavy vehicle is a severe risk breach if the subject matter of the contravention is equal to or greater than the severe risk breach lower limit for the requirement.

    Procedure - Mass Risk Categories
    To calculate a risk category for a vehicle operating under regulation mass, subtract the difference between the assessed mass and the regulation mass limit.

    To calculate a risk category for a vehicle operating under concessional mass, subtract the difference between the assessed mass and the concession mass limit.
Mass Enforcement for Vehicles, Trailers or Combinations Operating Under Permits, Concessions and Guidelines
Mass Enforcement for Vehicles, Trailers or Combinations Operating Under Permits, Concessions and Guidelines
    An Authorised Officer may commence enforcement action if a driver / operator does not meet all of the (technical) conditions of a relevant Permit, Concession and / or Guideline. The offence is classified as a technical breach and therefore falls under section 129 of the Heavy Vehicle National Law Act 2012 (HVNL) .

    However, if the driver/operator exceeds the mass or dimension for the relevant Permit, Concession and/or Guideline, the offence falls under section s96 and s102 Mass & Dimension of the HVNL.

    Mass exceptions
    Mass exceptions
      A mass exception is a concession to the general mass limits set out in the Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation and is available for certain heavy vehicles when specific conditions are met. The following mass exceptions are available under HVNL:
      • Concessional mass limits (CML)
      • Steer Axle mass exception limits
      • Quad axle group mass exception limits
      • Higher Mass Limits (HML)
      • 1-tonne Tri-Axle Mass Transfer Allowance (1TMTA)

      If a vehicle operating under a mass exception is detected breaching a condition of the mass exception, the mass exception does not apply and the vehicle will be assessed against the relevant prescribed mass limits.

      If a vehicle operating under a mass exception is detected breaching a mass requirement of the mass exception, this is not a condition offence. The vehicle will be assessed against the mass exception limits.

      Refer: Schedules 2-5 of the Heavy Vehicle (Mass, Dimension and Loading) National Regulation.

      Note: ‘Mass Exceptions’ as described above, should not be confused with mass exemptions, which may be provided by a notice or permit. Where there is a contravention of a mass or dimension exemption the exemption must be disregarded. The exemption does not operate in the person’s favour while the contravention constituting the offence continues; therefore the percentage is calculated from the regulation limit (Section 129(5) Heavy Vehicle National law).

      Note: Authorised Officers must be aware that if a vehicle is operating under a Permit, Concession and/or Guideline that the vehicle, trailer or combination may already be at the maximum allowable limit for that route that is, pavement, bridge or culvert. When determining whether or not to prohibit the use of a vehicle, trailer or combination on a road, Authorised Officers must take into account all prevailing conditions prior to issuing a prohibition notice.

Weighing of Vehicles for Police
Weighing of Vehicles for Police
    An Authorised Officer may conduct a weigh of a vehicle upon request of the Queensland Police Service.

    The vehicle must be weighed in accordance with Police requirements.

    Procedure - Weighing of Vehicles for Police
    • Record in remarks on WDN 'Weighed for QPS at their request'. Record the Police Officer's name and number.
    • On completion of the WDN, hand both the original and duplicate to the Police Officer. Retain the triplicate and submit to your Senior Inspector.
Refusal to Weigh a Heavy Vehicle
Refusal to Weigh a Heavy Vehicle
    Authorised Officers have the power under section 516 of the HVNL, to require a driver to move a vehicle onto a weighing device.

    Procedure - Refusal to Weigh a Heavy Vehicle
    If driver refuses, the following progressive actions may be taken:
    • A refusal to move the vehicle onto a weighing device - maximum penalty $6000 section 516 of the HVNL Act.
    • The Authorised Officer will then direct the driver to leave the heavy vehicle - maximum penalty $6000 - section 524, of the HVNL Act.
Use of the Public Weighbridges
Use of the Public Weighbridges
    Public weighbridges may be used by inspectors and authorised officers for reasons as set out below:

    Wet road conditions
    • The place of interception is unsuitable for weighing on portable machines and weighbridge facilities are within a reasonable distance (Refer to note).
    • Where traffic congestion is a threat to safety.
    • When an inspector or officer is operating solo and/or portable weighing equipment is not available.

    Note:Under HVNL and TO(RUM) Inspectors are able to direct the driver or operator of a heavy vehicle that is stationary or has been stopped to enable exercise of other powers to move the vehicle, or cause it to be moved, to a stated reasonable place within a 30km radius from:
    • where the vehicle was stationary or stopped;
      or
    • if the direction is given within the course of the vehicle’s journey – any point along the forward
      route of the journey.

    For private vehicles other than a suspected dangerous goods vehicle, Inspectors are only able to direct the vehicle within a 5km radius.

    Procedures - Public Weighbridge
    The following procedure should be followed when a loaded vehicle is directed to a public weighbridge for the purpose of determining mass for prosecution purposes:
    • Ensure the weighbridge is operational.
    • Advise the nominated weigh person of your requirements, i.e. you require gross mass and axle masses.
    • Ensure the driver remains in the vehicle during the entire weighing procedure.
    • Upon completion of weighing, obtain duplicate and triplicate copies of weigh docket. If the vehicle is overloaded, complete Weighing Device Notice (WDN) and pin duplicate copy of the docket to original copy of WDN. Attach triplicate copy of the docket to the payment advice. Submit with normal paperwork for the day’s activities to your respective Senior Inspector.
    • If the vehicle is legally loaded, pin both copies of weigh docket to the payment advice, and submit with normal day’s paperwork.
