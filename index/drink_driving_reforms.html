Drink Driving Reforms (7483)

Project NameDrink Driving Reforms
When10 September 2021
BackgroundOn Friday 10 September 2021, drink driving reforms will commence that will make changes to the Alcohol Ignition Interlock AIIP (AIIP) and introduce drink driving courses for all drink drivers.

Summary of the changes:

Changes to the AIIP
Changes to the AIIP
    From 10 September 2021, the following changes will apply:
    • Mid-range (0.10 blood alcohol content and over) and above drink drivers (including those granted a restricted licence) will also need to participate in the AIIP
    • The AIIP will become Performance-Based where TMR monitors breath samples and attendance at mandatory scheduled interlock services
    • Drink drivers who choose not to participate in the AIIP won't be able to drive for 5 years (the 'sit out' period)

    These changes will apply to drivers who:
    • Are convicted of a drink driving offence committed on or after 10 September 2021
    • Were convicted of an AIIP drink driving offence committed before 10 September 2021 and their IPER starts on or after 10 September 2021
    • Have an interstate or overseas licence with an interlock condition that is applied on or after 10 September 2021 and are transferring to Queensland.

    Note:Refer to the Scenarios section for examples of drink drivers who are/are not required to participate in the Performance-Based AIIP.
New Drink Driving courses
New Drink Driving courses
    All drivers who are convicted of drink driving offences committed in Queensland on or after 10 September 2021 may also have to complete:
    • Plan.Drive.Survive.Foundations - a mandatory short online drink driving course that must be completed before being re-licenced - applies to first time drink drivers
    • Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensiave - a multi-session drink driving course that must be completed before the AIIP can be completed – applies to repeat drink drivers

    Further information about the drink driving courses will be provided closer to 10 September 2021.
Discount for Concession Card Holders
Discount for Concession Card Holders
    Pension Concession Card (PCC) and Health Card Card (HCC) holders who are subject to the Performance Based AIIP will be eligible for the 35% discount for interlock installation, servicing and removal costs.

    Participants who are subject to the existing Time-Based AIIP, will be eligible for the 35% discount if they hold a HCC card only. Participants with PCC's in the Time-Based AIIP are not eligible for this discount.

Extensive system and communication changes will accompany this project. New notices will commence from 1 August and all other changes are being implemented on 10 September 2021.
More details about the Performance-Based AIIP
Performance-Based AIIP
Performance-Based AIIP
    Participants in the Performance Based AIIP will need to:
    • hold a current licence with an interlock condition
    • have a zero blood/breath alcohol concentration when driving
    • only drive a nominated vehicle with an interlock installed (unless they have an exemption)
    • attend scheduled interlock services (these are mandatory services where the interlock will be serviced and data from the interlock will be collected)
    • not tamper with or bypass the interlock device.
Interlocks
Interlocks
    Participants in the Performance-Based AIIP will need to have a different interlock installed to the devices used in the existing AIIP.

    The new interlocks:
    • Will capture a photo of the participant:
    • At the time the interlock is installed or when the vehicle is nominated (if the interlock is already in the vehicle)
    • When a breath sample is taken
    • May be WiFi enabled so that data from the interlock can be retrieved at any point when the interlock is in WiFi range.
Vehicle Nominations
Vehicle Nominations
    Participants in the Performance-Based AIIP will not need to complete a Vehicle Nomination form. Instead, they will need to:
    • Provide written advice (in any form) to the installer/interlock service provider (service provider) of the vehicle they wish to nominate.
    • Present a document from TMR confirming they are on the Performance-Based AIIP

    Note:If the participant attends without a document from TMR they can contact the IPU who can provide an email template (to the participant or installer) confirming they are on the Performance-Based AIIP.

    The Performance-Based AIIP will:
    • Have two parts:
    • Learning period – first 8 months
    • Performance Period – last 4 months (will be a minimum of 4 months)
    • Run for a minimum of 12 months. Time towards the 12 months:
    • Does not count if the participant's licence is cancelled, expired or suspended
    • Does not have to be continuous, but must be a total of 8 months of learning and 4 months of performance.
Learning Period
Learning Period
    The learning period is intended to provide AIIP participants with the opportunity to become familiar with the interlock and to learn about separating drinking from driving so they are ready when their Performance Period starts.

    The participant's driver licence must be current (not expired, suspended or cancelled) for the full 8 months to progress to the Performance Period.
Performance Period
Performance Period
    The Performance Period is intended to make sure participants have learned to separate drinking from driving before having the 'I' condition removed.

    TMR will send a Start of Performance Period notice when the Performance Period has started.

    During the 4-month Performance Period TMR will monitor a participant’s use of the interlock and that scheduled interlock services are completed.

    For the full 4 months of the Performance Period participants must have a zero blood/breath alcohol concentration and ensure that all scheduled services are completed. If they don't, it will be considered a violation.

    Violations
    Violations
      A violation will be recorded during the Performance Period if:
      • Alcohol is detected by the interlock
      • The participant's interlock is 'permanently locked out' due to a missed scheduled service

      If a violation is recorded by the interlock:
      • The service provider will electronically report it to TMR
      • A new customer restriction will be applied (Interlock Violation Processing) – this will be automatically removed once the violation is processed (generally within 24 hours).
      • The violation will display in the Sanction History screen
      • The participant's Performance Period will be restarted (as of the date of violation).
      • A Performance Period Restart notice will be issued to advise a participant:
      • their Performance Period has been restarted; and
      • that they will be in the AIIP for more than 12 months.
      If a participant keeps violating during the Performance Period:
      • Their Performance Period will restart each time
      • Their time in the AIIP will be extended each time
      • The interlock condition will remain on their licence
      • They can only drive a nominated vehicle with an interlock

      This could apply for up to 5 years from the end of their disqualification.

      Violation disputes
      Violation disputes
        A participant can dispute a violation within 28 days of the Performance Period restart notice. The IPU will compare the photo of the person who violated against the photo captured at the time the vehicle was nominated.

        The restart of the Performance Period and the extension of time in the AIIP will only be reversed if it's confirmed that the:
        • Participant did not commit the violation
        • Participant attended the scheduled service and should not have received a permanent lockout
        • Interlock was not working properly at the time the violation was recorded

        The IPU will notify the participant of the outcome. The internal review and QCAT review options apply if they are not satisfied.
Circumventions/tampers
Circumventions/tampers
    Circumventions/tampers may be reported to the IPU at any time during the participant's time in the AIIP if the service provider has evidence that:
    • the interlock had been tampered with; or
    • the participant tried to get around having to use the interlock to start the vehicle

    Based on the evidence, the IPU may send an Intention to extend time in the program notice.

    If the participant does not reply, their minimum 12 month time in the AIIP will be extended by 4 months.

    Circumvention disputes
    Circumvention disputes
      A participant can dispute a Circumvention/Tamper within 28 days of the Intention to extend time in the program notice.

      The IPU will review the evidence and information provided by the participant or installer. If it is confirmed that the participant:
      • Did not tamper/circumvent, TMR will not extend their time in the AIIP
      • Did tamper/circumvent, TMR will extend their time in the AIIP by 4 months. This extension is added to the minimum 12 months and is separate to any extension of the Performance Period due to a violation.

      The IPU will notify the participant of the outcome and the internal review and QCAT review options apply if they are not satisfied.
Interlock Data Upload Request
Interlock Data Upload Request
    Before a person can complete the Performance-Based AIIP, TMR must make sure that no violations have been recorded during the full 4-month Performance Period.

    At the end of the 4 months, the participant will be sent an Interlock data upload request notice advising them to contact their Service Provider to request an upload of their data.

    For participants with:
    • WiFi enabled interlocks, the upload may be done without having to attend an installer.
    • Non-WiFi interlocks, they must attend a Service Provider to have the data retrieved.
Completing the AIIP
Completing the AIIP
    An Alcohol Ignition Interlock AIIP Completion notice will be issued when participant has done all of the following:
    • Participated in the AIIP for a minimum of 12 months
    • Completed their 4-month Performance Period without any violations
    • Completed their Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensive drink driving course (if applicable)

    Note:More information about the Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensive course will be provided closer to 10 September 2021.

    The Interlock Tenure Screen and the Driver Licence Pre-requisite screens are being amended to assist with enquiries about having the 'I' condition removed from a participant's licence.

    Further details on the screen changes will be provided in training.
Removing the Interlock
Removing the Interlock
    An interlock can be removed at completion of the AIIP or any time at the Participant's request.
    For example, if a participant is confident that their data upload (refer Interlock Data upload request above) does not contain any violations, they can request that the interlock be removed at the time the upload is completed.

    If the interlock is removed before a participant receives their completion notice, it is at their own risk. If the AIIP hasn't been completed, they will need to pay to install another interlock.
More details about the drink driving courses
Plan.Drive.Survive.Foundations (PDSF)
Plan.Drive.Survive.Foundations (PDSF)
    The Plan.Drive.Survive.Foundations (PDSF), is a short online course which is mandatory for drink drivers who are convicted of offences committed on or after 10 September 2021. It encourages separating drinking from driving and must be completed before a customer can be re-licenced. This includes customers who are granted a restricted licence.

    If the course is not completed, the customer won't be able to apply for a licence for 5 years from the end of their disqualification or the date they were granted a restricted licence.

    Customers will be sent a PDSF notice upon their conviction being recorded in TICA if they are required to complete the course.

    About the course:
    • It costs $10.35
    • It is not a test and has a series of modules
    • The enrolment process uses Auth0
    • Once enrolled, it must be completed within 12 months
    • Can be completed on any smart device or computer with a connection to the internet
    • It can be completed at any time

    Note:Customers don't need to wait until they are convicted or their disqualification ends. They can do it at any time so they are ready to be re-licenced (this will be important for those who intend to apply for a restricted licence. They can do the course before they go to court so once they are granted the restricted licence, they can come straight to TMR and get their licence.

    • Upon completion, the customer will receive a ‘Plan to Survive’ which is a plan they can use to separate drinking from driving and is tailored to their circumstances. It includes people who can help them and back up options if their first plan falls through.

    • Once completed:
      • A passed Drink Driving Course Certification pre-requisite will be added in TICA
      • It is valid for 5 years. This means if the customer is convicted of another drink driving offence and their prerequisite is still valid, they don't have to do another one.

    Customers can either:
    • Enrol online and pay at a CSC
    • Enrol and pay online
    • Pay at a CSC and complete the enrolment process online

    Note:Customers must provide their CRN online. There is no option to create a CRN.

No internet access
No internet access
    If a customer does not have internet access at home or another location like a library, they can request a printed version from their nearest CSC.

    CSC's must use their local area knowledge before allowing a customer to complete the printed version. Audits will be conducted to ensure that only customers without internet access in their area are completing the printed PDSF.

    The printed PDSF:
    • Will be a new miscellaneous product
    • Will be a new pre-requisite for CSC operators to pass
    • Is not a test – the customer can complete it in their own time (with assistance from family etc) and return it to the CSC once completed. Or they can complete it at the centre.
Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensive
Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensive
    The Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensive (PDSC), is a multi-session in person course for drink drivers who are convicted of 2 or more drink driving offences (committed on or after 10 September 2021) within 5 years. It encourages separating drinking from driving and must be completed before the AIIP can be completed.

    Customers will be sent a PDSC notice once their conviction is recorded in TICA if they are required to complete the course.

    Customers who are required to complete the PDSC will also need to participate in the Performance- Based AIIP. If they do not complete the course, they will not be able to complete the AIIP which will continue to apply for up to 5 years from the end of their disqualification.

    About the course:
    • It's free
    • There are 6 sessions
    • Each session takes about 2 hours
    • Attendance is in person with other participants

    Customers must enrol directly with Approved Training Providers who will be listed on the TMR website from 10 September 2021.
Other delivery methods
Other delivery methods
    Some approved training providers may provide alternative delivery methods (such as video conferencing). Details about these options will be provided at a later date.
Exemptions
Exemptions
    If customers meet strict eligibility criteria, they can apply for an exemption from completing the PDSC.

    Exemption criteria:
    1. No access (due to distance/travel time from Approved Training Provider and no internet
      access)
    or
    2. Severe Hardship (participant or their family will experience severe hardship if they are not
      granted an exemption)

    Application information:
    • Plan.Drive.Survive.Comprehensive Exemption Application form must be completed and supporting documents provided
    • A fee of $44.00 must be paid at lodgement
    • Applications can be lodged:
      • At a CSC/QGAP and then forwarded to the IPU for assessment; or
      • Sent by post directly to the IPU with a cheque / money order.
    • Decisions are reviewable by TMR and QCAT
Communications
Tailored Communications
Tailored Communications
    On 30 July 2021, TMR issued tailored communications via email/post to customers who have been convicted of a drink driving offence and are subject to the:
    • Performance-Based AIIP (due to their IPER starting on or after 10 September 2021)
    • Existing AIIP (due to their IPER starting before 10 September 2021)
New e-notices
New e-notices
    Extensive work including customer testing has been completed to amend the existing AIIP notices and develop new notices required for the Performance-Based AIIP.

    All of the AIIP and drink driving course notices will be e-notices and will have a customer centric style including colour.

    Note:The e-notice functionality will not commence until 10 September 2021.

    On 30 July 2021 the old AIIP pack was discontinued and the amended AIIP notice/AIIP reminder started to be sent. This is because customers from that date will have an IPER that starts on or after 10 September 2021 and will need to receive information about the Performance-Based program.

    To compliment the new AIIP notice, a new 'AIIP Program Overview' brochure has been developed. The new brochure:
    • provides an end to end overview of the AIIP including touch points with the drink driving courses
    • will only be sent when the AIIP is sent by Post
    • will be available on the QGov website

    The brochure won't be included until 10 September because it mentions the drink driving courses (which only apply to offences committed from 10 September).

    From 10 September 2021, the drink driving course notices will commence being issued. They will be bundled where the customer is required to complete multiple requirements (for example, a repeat offender will need to complete the AIIP and the PDSC so the participant will be sent both notices together).
Who will be impacted and how?
Drink driving offenders
Drink driving offenders
    Drink driving offenders:
    • Will be sent a notice from TMR if they are subject to the Performance-Based program
    • May query why they are subject to the Performance-Based program
    • May query why they have to complete a drink driving course
    • Must be encouraged to sign up for e-notices (this is important so that Participants receive timely advice about their program and what they need to do).
    • Should be encouraged to get a WiFi interlock (if available from their provider). This will allow quick data uploads and avoid any delays in receiving advice they can complete the program.
    • May request information about the drink driving courses.
All operators/consultants
All operators/consultants
    Operators/Consultants may receive calls from customers who receive the new notices and want more information about the Performance-Based AIIP, drink driving courses or are unsure which AIIP they are subject to.
    • To assist with these enquiries, operators/consultants can:
      • View the notices (copies will be provided closer to July in the Further information section ).
      • View the IPER sanction start date in TICA to determine which Interlock AIIP customers are subject to.
      • If IPER sanction start date is before 10 September 2021 current AIIP applies
      • If IPER sanction start date is on/after 10 September 2021 the Performance-Based AIIP applies
      • Advise customers about the Performance-Based AIIP
      • Advise customers to check the website https://www.qld.gov.au/transport/safety/road-safety/drink-driving/drink-driving-reforms for updates.
      • Refer customers who are subject to the 2 year AIIP to the existing resources and information about the current AIIP.

    From 10 September, operators/consultants will be able to view and resend the AIIP and drink driving course e-notices.

    CSC Operators
    CSC Operators
      • May receive requests from customers to:
        • Enrol in or pay for the PDSF
        • Complete the printed PDSF
      • Will be required to lodge PDSC exemption applications

    Digital Support Team
    Digital Support Team
      • May need to assist customers with Auth0 issues for the PDSF

    Note:TICA will not show any 5 year Interlock Periods until the release date on 10th September 2021. Operators/consultants must use the IPER start date recorded in TICA to determine if customers are subject to the Performance-Based AIIP before providing further information.

Scenario 4 – IPER starts after 10 September 2021 (Restricted Licence)
