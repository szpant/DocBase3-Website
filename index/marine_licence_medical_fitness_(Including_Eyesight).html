Marine Licence Medical Fitness (Including Eyesight) (1950)


What it isLicence candidates must complete a "Medical Fitness Self Disclosure Statement" as a prerequisite for participation in training and assessment for a recreational marine licence.

Note:The Medical Fitness Self Disclosure Statement is available on the MSQ website. See the below link.
http://www.msq.qld.gov.au/Licensing/Recreational.aspx

The completed medical fitness disclosure statements must be retained by approved BoatSafe training providers, in accordance with marine safety policy.

If a licence candidate is unable to sign the "Medical Fitness Disclosure Statement" due to a concern about the suitability of their medical fitness, the BoatSafe training provider must refer the licence candidate to Maritime Safety Queensland for resolution of the matter.
CSO Responsibilities
CSO Responsibilities
    Customer service officers have no responsibilities regarding marine licence candidate medical fitness.

    However, if a licence candidate presents a BoatSafe Statement of competency in support of their marine licence application and the CSO is genuinely concerned about the licence candidate's medical fitness they should:
    1.Process the licence application;
    and
    2.Refer their concerns to Maritime Safety Queensland for follow up action.
Test RequirementsDue to changes to marine safety legislation, eyesight testing at Transport and Main Roads (TMR) Customer Service Centres is no longer required.
