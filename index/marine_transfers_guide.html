Ship Transfers Guide (1181)


What it isA transfer is a change in registered operator.

Documents Required
Documents Required
    • Queensland Regulated Ship Registration Transfer Application (F3521);
    and
    • Registration Concession Application (F3937) if applicable

    Note:The disposer is to provide to the acquirer a current gas certificate (if applicable), however it is not a requirement for the acquirer to present the certificate(s) for the purpose of transferring the ship registration.
Transfer Registration Online Service
Transfer Registration Online Service
    A ship registration cannot be transferred online if the ship:
    • is 15 metres or more in length;
    • has a purpose of use other than private;
    • is to be registered in joint names;
    • is to be registered as an organisation (legal or non-legal)

    The disposer must be a registered operator of the ship for which the transfer registration consent is being recorded online and must login with extra details

    The disposer's transfer registration consent must be recorded for a registration prior to the acquirer transferring that registration online.

    Transferring to joint registered operators cannot be processed online.

    Note:The disposer's consent lodges a Notice of Disposal (NOD) on TMR systems.

    The acquirer is able to lodge a ship transfer after the NOD by either:
    • Lodging Part A of the transfer application at a CSC or
    • Securely authenticating and lodging the transfer via the Transfer Registration Online Service (if eligible)

Disposer/Acquirers Responsibilities
Disposer/Acquirers Responsibilities
    Disposer
    Disposer
      • Complete and retain Part B (Notice of Disposal)ensuring that they:
        • Record acquirers Drivers Licence number
        • Have the acquirer/s sign
        • Ensure at least one of the disposer has signed the application
      • Give the acquirer the current:
        • Gas Certificate (if applicable)
        Note:Gas Certificates are not required to be presented to the Department.

      • If the registration is enrolled in Direct Debit, it is the disposer’s responsibility to ensure that the ship is removed from the scheme prior to the transfer of registration. Registered operators can remove the ship from Direct Debit via the My Registrations portal page on the TMR website.
        Note:If the ship is not removed from Direct Debit, it will be automatically removed within 24 hours of a transfer being processed.

        If a payment is deducted from the disposer’s account during the transfer process, the disposer must contact the acquirer about reimbursement.
    Acquirer
    Acquirer
      • Complete Part A (Transfer Application)
      • Ensure that all acquirers and at least one disposer has signed
      • Receive from disposer
        • Gas Certificate (if applicable)
        Note:Gas Certificates are not required to be presented to the Department.

      • Lodge transfer application within 14 days with all required documentation
      • Pay transfer fee and duty (if applicable)
      • Pay any concessional pro-rata (if applicable)
T.R.CTransaction Requirements Checklist
Fees
Duty and Pro-Rata Fees
Duty and Pro-Rata Fees
    Duty is not applicable.

    Pro-rata fees do not apply when transferring the ship from concessional to standard registration.

    Note:Pro-rata will be raised when new business and transfer occurs of the same day these fees are to be written off using the following adjustment:

    Adjustment Type: CUSTREV
    Debit Customer Account
    Credit Ship Registration Fee 423035825
Transfer Fee Exemptions
Transfer Fee Exemptions
    There are only 4 valid fee exempt reasons:
    TRAILS CodeDescription
    SDEAShip used as trading stock of a dealer
    SPECFor TMR errors or other special cases
    SPOUTransfer from joint operators to surviving spouse
    SWAPSwap the primary & secondary names

    Note:If Purpose of Use is recorded as DEAL and an exemption reason is not entered, TICA will automatically default the exemption field to SDEA and no fee will raise.
Business Exceptions
Business Exceptions
    If a Transfer fee is exempted a 'TRAFEEEX' business exception will raise. An exception will not raise if the Purpose of Use is recorded as DEAL.

    Note:The fee exempt reason SDEA can only be selected when 'Purpose of Use' is DEAL
Notice of Disposal
Notice of Disposal
    A CSO may manually process a notice of disposal in TICA when a Notice of Disposal application is lodged by the disposer for a ship registration.
    • The registration will be transferred to the acquirer/s.
    • Transfer fees will not be raised (when fees are applicable).
    • A restriction will be added to the acquirer's customer record who is recorded as the primary registered operator (that is, a restriction will not be recorded against the secondary registered operator).
    • A restriction will be added to the ship.
Rules
Organisational Transfers
Organisational Transfers
    Recreational ships can be transferred to organisations including ship dealers with purpose of use recorded as 'private'.
Requirements Not Met
Requirements Not Met
    If requirements are not met all documentation and monies must be returned to the applicant. If the transaction progresses, the operator must have approval from supervisor.
Transfer Not Lodged by Acquirer
Transfer Not Lodged by Acquirer
    Disposer Action if Acquirer Fails to Lodge Transfer

    A disposer does not pay any fees to transfer a ship.
    Refer: Notice of Disposal

    Disposer has documents
    Disposer has documents
      If a transfer is not lodged the disposer may lodge a completed Notice of Disposal (Part B of the transfer application) signed by the acquirer.

      Note:When processing a NOD, the date of disposal should only be backdated to the last renewal payment. If the date of disposal is earlier than the last renewal payment date, a text restriction should be recorded with the correct disposal date. This is because if a NOD is recorded prior to the last renewal, the NOD restriction will prevent TICA from raising the renewal again to allow payment.

    Disposer does not have documents
    Disposer does not have documents
      • The disposer must make every effort to contact the acquirer.
      • If all avenues have been exhausted the disposer must comply with the standard cancellation requirements.
        Refer: Cancellation > Voluntary Cancellation
Disposer not Current Registered Operator
Disposer not Current Registered Operator
    When the seller on the transfer application is not the disposer recorded in TRAILS the operator must:
    • Determine that the disposer on the application form exists in TRAILS
    • If the disposer exists the transaction can proceed using the different seller action in TRAILS
    • If the disposer does not exist the applicant must take reasonable efforts to establish their details

    If the applicant is unable to get more details from the disposer they must complete an Acquirers Statement.
Registration Fees in Arrears
Registration Fees in Arrears
    When there are registration fees in arrears the acquirer may do one of the following:
    • Pay the outstanding fees
    • Lodge a cancellation application with the transfer application and re-register the ship (refer new business)
    • Request that the disposer lodge cancellation papers or pay the fees

    Transfer of registration cannot be done without finalising these fees.

Deferring the Transfer
Deferring the Transfer
    If a request is made to stop the processing of a transfer, the customer should be advised that if a completed & signed transfer application is presented it will be processed.

    Customers requesting to stop the processing of a transfer, (i.e. Ownership dispute) should be advised that being the registered operator is not proof of legal ownership.
