Rosters and Timetables (5074)


Rosters and Timetables
What it isTo ensure the ongoing capacity of Compliance to undertake all aspects of the Transport Inspector role there is a need for staff resources to be deployed in a consistent and effective manner across the state.

Deployment of staff resources should be undertaken in a manner that both increases the broad skill development of team members while reducing the potential and perceived corruption risks.

Rostering of staff resources will be in accordance with employee award conditions.
Roster requirements
Rotation
Rotation
    • Transport Inspectors (TIs) must be rostered so they rotate through the various compliance and enforcement activities to ensure they are suitably skilled to undertake all aspects of the TI role.
    • TIs must also be rostered to ensure no permanent partner arrangements are created or maintained. No TI partnership should be over 28 days unless approved by a Manager (Compliance).
    • The Manager (Compliance) may approve an exception to the TIs rotation policy if a specialist area exist or in specific circumstances. As a guide, the maximum period without rotation within a specialist area should not exceed 6 months. Any deviation outside this policy approved by the Manager (Compliance) is to be tabled and minuted at local ROM meetings outlining the specific circumstances.
Rosters
Rosters
    • Rosters are prepared in a 4 week roster cycle.
    • Rosters are to be released no later than 14 days prior to completion of the previous roster.
    • TIs are required to follow the roster, including:
      • Start and finish times.
      • Working in the required zones, areas, activities or tasks.
      • Any change must be approved by a Senior Transport Inspector or Manager (Compliance).
    • Rosters are subject to regular changes. It is the responsibility of each TI to regularly check their roster to ensure they are aware of any changes. Rosters are confidential and must not be disclosed to any unauthorised person/s.
    • TIs on country duties (that is, working away overnight) must contact their office base daily to check in for any messages. This is not necessary on a Saturday, Sunday or Public Holiday.
    • TIs unable to report for duty for any reason must notify their Senior Transport Inspector as soon as possible and at least prior to commencement of shift.
    Rostered Days Off (RDO)
    Rostered Days Off (RDO)
      • A rostered day off (RDO) is allocated for each 4 week roster cycle.
      • TIs may request a particular day for RDO. However, due to operational factors there are no guarantee that request can be met.
      • There is provision within the employee award conditions for TIs to bank up to 5 RDOs. Advise the Senior Transport Inspector if you wish to bank an RDO and provide details of when you plan to use the RDOs. To assist in future roster preparations, TIs are to advise when it is planned to use the banked RDO.
      • RDO's may be a single day anywhere in the roster cycle and do not need to be attached to consecutive rest days.
Timesheets
Timesheets
    • MyTime must accurately reflect the activities performed.
    • MyTime must to be completed and submitted electronically at the end of each pay fortnight.
    • The submitted MyTime timesheet must be printed and signed if extras were performed, such as overtime and weekends.
    • When completing MyTime the standard hour row must reflect 08.00 hours.
    • When a designated RDO is taken, the timesheet has 0 hours entered in the column for that date, the standard hours is deleted and an RDO is recorded in the last field.
    • If working on a designated RDO, then the RDO is banked by recording work hours and deleting the standard hours and entering the day as an RDO as usual. (This will bank your RDO of 8 hours on the timesheet).
    • When applying for any type of leave, a manual leave form is to be completed and should match the hours on the roster for the applicable dates. For example, if a person takes Recreational Leave for 1 week and it is the week of a scheduled RDO, then the person applies for 32 hours. Any other leave required during the remainder of the 19 day cycle are applied for at 8 hours a day.
    • When applying for Recreational Leave or Long Service Leave and to use a banked RDO this should be taken at the beginning or end of the leave period.
