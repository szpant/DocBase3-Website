Customer Record Suppression (3348)


Suppressed Records
What it isThis function is used to suppress a customer's personal information.

There are 2 levels of suppression available. Level 1 suppression only allows the Identity Management Unit (IMU) to view the customer's personal information. Level 2 suppression allows the information to be viewed by IMU and QPS.

When a suppression is applied the customer record cannot be obtained by a third party unless required by law.

Suppressed records will show the customer name/address as 'Refer to TMR Security Officer' Customer Record Suppressed
Suppression for Victims of Domestic Family Violence, and Organisations who Support Victims
Applications
Applications
    If a customer believes that their personal safety is at risk due to a domestic violence situation they may apply to have their record suppressed. A customer in a domestic violence situation may be given level 1 or level 2 suppression depending on the documentation provided and their situation.

    Typically the level of suppression applied will depend on the documentation provided. If a customer is only able to provide a Statutory Declaration or letter from a sexual assault service confirming they are a victim of Domestic Violence they will be given level 2 suppression. If the customer provides a Court Protection Order they will be eligible for level 1 suppression.

    All applications for suppression are submitted to IMU for assessment.

    Organisations that provide direct support to victims of DFV are also eligible to apply for suppression of records.

    The evidence requirement/s for victims and organisations are different.

    All applications and supporting documents must be forwarded to the Identity Management Unit (IMU) either directly by the customer via mail or by the CSC.

    All records relating to the suppressed record are retained and managed by the IMU to allow for strict security of the customer’s personal details.
Application Requirements
Supporting Documents Required
Supporting Documents Required
    All applicants must complete a Customer Record Suppression Application (F4109) form.

    Applications must include one of the following supporting documents:
    • A current Court Protection Order (for example Domestic Violence Order (DVO), Apprehended Violence Order (AVO)). The Court Order must nominate the period for suppression.
      Domestic and Family Violence (DFV) policy
      Domestic and Family Violence (DFV) policy
        Victims of DFV who cannot provide a report or court order can apply for a suppression by providing one of the following:
        • A Statutory Declaration confirming they are a victim of DFV, that is witnessed by an authorised person
        • A letter from a DFV or sexual assault service confirming they are a victim of DFV.

        Organisations that provide direct support to victims of DFV can also apply for suppression.

        An application from an organisation must be supported by both of the following:
        • A signed statement on the organisation's letter head detailing how the organisation supports victims of DFV;
          and
        • Evidence that the organisation is a legal entity, for example a Certificate of Incorporation or a Certificate of Registration of a Company.
    • A current written report from the Queensland Police Service (QPS); Crime and Corruption Commission (CCC) or a relevant law enforcement agency seeking a suppression of a customer's record.
Duration of suppression
Duration
Duration
    Records will be suppressed for a fixed period, based on documents submitted with the application.

    If the application is supported by a report or court order the suppression will be applied for the period stated on the supporting document.

    To extend a suppression, a new application and current supporting documentation is required.

    If the application is supported by a statutory declaration or a letter from a DFV or sexual assault service the suppression will be applied for a maximum period of 12 months. To extend a suppression, a new application and supporting documentation is required.

    Customers may request a suppression be removed before expiry. To remove a suppression they must attend a CSC and provide standard EOI. CSCs must contact IMU to have the suppression removed.
Engaging with TMR
TMR online services access
TMR online services access
    Customers with a suppressed record will not be able to access TMR's online services.
Call Centre
Call Centre
    Customers with a suppressed record are not able to obtain information from their customer record through TMR's call centre. They must attend a CSC and provide EOI.
Correspondence
Correspondence
    Automatically generated TMR correspondence, such as renewal notices or sanction correspondence are sent to the customer’s address.

    External correspondence, such as camera detected or tolling offences are sent to IMU who on send it to the customer’s nominated address.
Processing TransactionsWhere a customer has suppressed record in TICA the CSO must first contact IMU to:
  • confirm the customer’s personal details and
  • the type of TICA transaction to be performed
  • On completion of the transaction the CSO is required to email the application and associated documents to IMU. The email/s must be deleted and all original documents posted to IMU.
  • If the application needs to be emailed to another location (e.g. TransLink), the email/s must be deleted and all original documents posted to IMU.
  • IMU will provide the CSO with a ‘Report for File’ to satisfy filing requirements.
  • Under no circumstances are applications associated with a suppressed customer to be put in filing.
Customer presents with a Smartcard
Customer presents with a Smartcard
    Where a suppressed customer has presented a smartcard and has a valid image recorded in TICA, a facial image verification (1:1) comparison should be undertaken and only completed whilst in contact with IMU.

    A successful facial image (1:1) verification comparison will automatically lift the suppression for 30 minutes, enabling the transaction to be processed as per standard TICA procedures. The suppression will be automatically re-applied after 30 minutes.

    If this is insufficient time to complete the transaction, another facial image verification (1:1) comparison can be undertaken to lift the suppression for a further 30 minutes.

    If the customer fails the facial image (1:1) verification comparison, IMU must be contacted to review the facial images.
Customer Present without a Smartcard
Customer Present without a Smartcard
    If the customer satisfies IMU and the CSO of their identity, IMU will temporarily lift the suppression to allow a transaction to be processed.

    After the completion of the transaction, the CSO must contact IMU to have the suppression re-applied to the customer’s record.
Suppression and ROI
ROI requests for records relating to Suppressed Customers
ROI requests for records relating to Suppressed Customers
    Any ROI applications or requests for the customer’s actual name and address must be referred to (IMU) for consideration and approval/refusal.
