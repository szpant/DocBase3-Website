Annual PVI Assessment Process (7223)


Annual PVI Assessment
What it isAs part of the process to ensure standards of service, each Authorised Officer is to be monitored, and formally assessed annually to ensure their competency to carry out vehicle inspections is maintained.
Assessment
Assessment Process
Assessment Process
    • Senior Transport Inspectors are expected to continuously monitor the performance and competency of Authorised Officers under their supervision.
    • Annually, each Authorised Officer who performs PVIs is to be assessed for competency.
    • STIs can nominate suitably experienced and\or qualified staff to conduct the assessment.
    • The STI is ultimately responsible for signing off that the Authorised Officers is competent to complete PVIs.
    • The assessment is to be over a minimum of three inspections, of different types of vehicles where practicable.
    • The assessor may require further inspections to be assessed in order to consider competency.
    • The assessor is to use the attached Assessment guide to provide the assessor with evidence of the Authorised Officers performance and knowledge.
    • The assessment is a competency-based assessment and includes observation and oral questioning.
    • After each assessment the assessor will conduct a short/brief appraisal to discuss any issues identified.
    • At the completion of the assessment the STI and the assessor must discuss any issues identified during the assessment with the Authorised Officer and sign the assessment guide.
    • If an Authorised Officer is not deemed competent then remedial guidance and training is to be provided until competency is achieved.
Administration
Administration
Administration
    • If deemed competent, the STI is to scan and send a copy of the cover sheet of the Assessment Guide to the regional Training Record Administrator (TRA).
    • The TRA will record the completion against the participants record in Accelerate with course code PVI:PVIASSESS.
    • You can find your regions TRA email by clicking here: https://inside.tmr.qld.gov.au/sites/tpt/Pages/training-admin.aspx
    • The Assessment Guide and all the evidence including Vehicle Check Lists and copies of Defect Notices issued must be captured in their personal file in DMS.
