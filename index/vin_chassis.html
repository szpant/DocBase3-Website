Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)
What it isA Vehicle Identification Number (VIN):
  • Identifies the frame of vehicles manufactured from 01 Jan 1989
  • Is part of the International System designed to reduce theft
  • Aims to provide each vehicle with a unique identifier
Format
  • Any combination of letters and number (Excluding I, O and Q)
  • 17 digits in length
  • Stamped in a straight line with characters of similar size and spacing

Types of VINs
Low Volume VINs (LVV)
High Volume VINs
High Volume VINs
    • Allocated to manufacturers producing more than 500 cars per annum
    • It is the manufacturers responsibility to maintain records of the VIN's allocated
    • HVV Manufacturers are to apply through SAI Global for the issue of VINs http://www.saiglobal.com/

    HVV manufacturers are issued with a unique World Manufacturing Identifier (WMI) and are responsible for maintaining a record of their allocated VINs with NEVDIS.
Location
  • Stamped or permanently marked in the frame or chassis by the manufacturer, and
  • Displayed on the identification plate
VIN ProblemsIf there is a problem with the VIN failing to decode:
  • Do not proceed with the application
  • Contact VIU for advice, and
    • Email import papers and Consumer Information Notice (if applicable) and VIN problem sheet to VIU viunit@tmr.qld.gov.au (imports only); or
    • Perform a PRI and email a VIN problem sheet to VIU viunit@tmr.qld.gov.au

Trailers
Trailers
    If a customer has presented for registration with a trailer without a VIN/Chassis number and:
    • The trailer was built in Australia but never registered the customer should apply for a LVV
    • It is an import that has never been registered in Australia; Refer: Vehicle Registration > 5. Vehicle Requirements > Imported Vehicles
    • The trailer has been previously registered in Australia but the applicant is unable to locate any existing identifiers the customer should apply for a LVV
    If a customer presents a branded trailer (excluding caravans) that has been registered interstate and:
    • the VIN has not been been stamped onto the chassis
    • the trailer has a VIN plate attached with a legible VIN
    The trailer can be registered if the customer can present proof of origin.

    The customer must be advised that, although the trailer is registered, it won't comply with the Vehicle Standards Regulation until the number has been stamped onto the chassis.


    Note:Contact VIU for advice should a caravan be presented for inspection that doesn't have a VIN stamped onto its chassis.

    Location of Original Identifiers - Trailers
    Location of Original Identifiers - Trailers
      If the original identifiers are located during a registration related inspection (e.g. HVRAS, self assessment or TMR Identity check). The original identifiers must be used for the registration process.

      The VIN should be checked to ensure there is no stolen or other restrictions on the VIN. If unrestricted the customer will need to have the LVV stamped out and the trailer plate amended to reflect the original identifiers.

      The original identifiers must be stamped on the trailer and on the trailer identification plate before registration can proceed.
Updating VIN in TRAILSChanges to VIN's must be done in consultation with the VIU.

Changes to VIN's may be required if either:
  • Error was made when vehicle registered
  • A surrogate identifier has been issued

Requirements:
  • PRI and completed Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification Form F3524
    and one of the following:
  • Customer has proof to identify vehicle (old Registration Certificate)
  • Recall previous registration records
  • Checked ROLS Archive
  • A Safety Certificate or COI showing the VIN number
Chassis Number
What it isA Chassis number identifies the frame of vehicles manufactured prior to 01 Jan 1989.
Format
  • Any combination of letters and number
  • Variable in length
  • Stamped in a straight line of similar size
Location
  • Stamped or permanently marked in the frame or chassis by the manufacturer, and
  • Displayed on the identification plate

Chassis Exemption - Pre 1939 Vehicles (excluding Street Rod)
Chassis Exemption - Pre 1939 Vehicles (excluding Street Rod)
    Most early model motor vehicles and motorbikes constructed before 1939 were not issued or stamped with a chassis number. It is a condition of registration that the vehicle be allocated an identifier (surrogate chassis). It is preferred that the number be stamped on the vehicle but is not mandatory as the stamping of numbers onto the vehicle may affect the authenticity and value of the vehicle.
Updating Chassis in TRAILSRequired if either:
  • Chassis numbers did not convert when ROLS was converted to TRAILS
  • Chassis number was not recorded in ROLS
  • Error was made when vehicle registered
  • A surrogate identifier has been issued

Requirements:
  • PRI and completed Change of Vehicle Particulars Notification (F3524)
    and one of the following:
  • Customer has proof to identify vehicle (old Registration Certificate)
  • Checked ROLS Archive
  • Recall previous registration records
  • A Safety Certificate or COI showing the chassis number
