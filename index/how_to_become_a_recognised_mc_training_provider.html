How to Become A Recognised MC Training Provider (1019)

Preparing a Training CourseCompetency Unit of Training: TLIC4006 - Drive Multi-Combination Vehicle

Note:If a competency unit number is listed with a letter on the end it is still acceptable.

Course Requirements
Course Requirements
    All courses must meet the National Competency Standard for Multi-Combination Vehicles, which is TLIC4006 - Drive Multi-Combination Vehicle.

    Further information can be obtained by visiting http://training.gov.au/ or by contacting the Australian Skills Quality Authority (ASQA) info line on 1300 701 801.
Self Developed Courses
Self Developed Courses
    Registered Training Organisation's (RTO) may add other subjects relevant for driving Multi-Combination Vehicles. While the course must still address the competency standards included in TLIC4006 - Drive Multi-Combination Vehicle, the additional self developed sessions can be presented as part of these units of training.
Registering the OrganisationAll TMR approved training providers must comply with the Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF) and must be approved and registered with ASQA .
Accredited Driver TrainersIf intending to offer the training on a commercial basis the trainers are required to be accredited and possess the appropriate assessment qualification or competency units i.e.
  • TAE40116 or TAE40110 - Certificate IV in Training and Assessment, or units
  • TAEASS401 and TAEASS402

Note:If a competency unit number is listed with a letter on the end it is still acceptable.
Advice to TMROnce ASQA has given approval for the registration of the RTO to deliver the relevant training, the training organisation will need to notify TMR in writing on official company letterhead, requesting that they be included on the TMR list of approved MC training providers.

When TMR receives the request, appropriate checks will be conducted prior to the provider being included on the approved MC training provider list.
