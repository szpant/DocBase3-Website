Road Incidents (5386)


Incident Response
What it isAn incident is a low frequency, high consequence event. It is a sudden, unexpected event which endangers or may potentially endanger people, property or the environment and demands immediate, coordinated response.

Incidents have three common characteristics:

Hazard - either natural or technological. Natural includes storms, flash flooding, etc. TMR is more concerned with technological hazards which include artificial origins such as, transportation, dangerous goods, etc.

A vulnerable community - persons, sections of a community or a community and/or an environment.

A precipitating event - for example, a brake failure in a heavy goods vehicle.
Procedure - Attending incidents
When Compliance should/can attend
When Compliance should/can attend
    The major points to note are:
      • TMR's attendance at major road incidents is in response to a formal request from QPS ONLY, which has been assessed and approved by the appropriate TMR authority. (Senior Transport Inspector (STI) or Compliance Manager). TMR's core business does not embrace a combative role in either incidents or disasters.
      • In a disaster situation, particularly natural disasters, rarely is TMR legislative intervention necessary.
      • In both incident and disaster scenarios, operational support may be provided by TMR.
    Operational support may include assisting the QPS in traffic control at or near an incident scene or inspecting vehicles. Due to the distinctive profile, delegated authority and everyday interface with the transport industry, TMR Inspectors can provide valuable community assistance, when requested and approved in this role.
    Transport Inspectors should not attend an incident while there are fatalities or casualties at the scene. If specifically requested by QPS to attend an incident while victims remain on the scene, a STI must be notified immediately for further consultation with QPS.
On arrival
On arrival
    On arrival at the scene of an incident, TI's are to:
      • make contact with the QPS Investigating Officer or Scene Commander;
      • comply with all reasonable QPS requests for expert assistance;
      • assess the situation;
      • access the QPS requests;
      • consider necessity to inspect/weigh on site;
      • consider security, safety and urgency aspects;
      • consider workplace health and safety implications;
      • collect essential data;
      • all information is to be recorded in Official Notebook.
QPS requestsTMR can provide assistance during normal business hours with requests from QPS for mechanical inspections and or weighing vehicles.
All requests from QPS for assistance are to be made through the Senior Transport Inspector from the applicable region.
