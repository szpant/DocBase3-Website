Show Cause - Medical (1429)


Immediate Amendment
What it isThe Chief Executive may immediately amend a Queensland licence where:
  • the licence holder provides information, or a health professional provides information about a licence holder;
    and
  • the information indicates the licence holder has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely;
    and
  • the chief executive reasonably considers that public safety has been endangered, or is likely to be endangered because the licence holder's ability to drive safely is likely to be adversely affected;
    or
  • it is otherwise necessary in the public interest.
When requiredAn Immediate Amendment and Proposed Ongoing Amendment show cause action may be taken if the information provided indicates that the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, but the person is medically fit to drive:
  • only certain classes of vehicle;
    and/or
  • subject to periodic review;
    and/or
  • under certain conditions.

Note:If action is required, but there is insufficient information to clearly decide which action is most appropriate, a Notice 4 - Notice of Proposed Amendment or Cancellation – Medical Grounds should be issued to the person proposing the cancellation of their licence. This will ensure the person does not continue to drive without TMR receiving a Medical Certificate for Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712), which provides a clear recommendation regarding their medical fitness to drive.
Types of licence amendmentsAn immediate amendment may be used to:
  • impose a condition/s on the licence, which the licence holder must comply with while driving;
    or
  • downgrade the licence class to restrict the types of vehicles that may be driven by the licence holder.

When does the immediate amendment take effect
An immediate amendment of a licence takes effect on the date stated in the Notice of Immediate Amendment and Proposed Ongoing Amendment.

If the notice is sent by mail, this is five business days from the issue date of the notice.

If the notice is handed to the customer, it takes effect immediately.
Immediate Suspension
What it isThe chief executive may immediately suspend a Queensland licence where:
  • the licence holder provides information, or a health professional provides information about a licence holder;
    and
  • the information indicates the licence holder has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely;
    and
  • the chief executive reasonably considers that public safety has been endangered, or is likely to be endangered because the licence holders ability to drive safely is likely to be adversely affected;
    or
  • it is otherwise necessary in the public interest.
When requiredAn Immediate Suspension and Proposed Cancellation show cause action may be taken if the information available indicates that the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely, and:
  • public safety has been endangered, or is likely to be endangered;
    and/or
  • the suspension of the licence is otherwise necessary in the public interest.
Examples of when this may be required:
Note:If action is required but there is insufficient information to clearly decide which action is most appropriate, a Notice 4 - Notice of Proposed Amendment or Cancellation – Medical Grounds should be issued to the person proposing the cancellation of their licence. This will ensure the person does not continue to drive without TMR receiving a Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712), which provides a clear recommendation regarding their medical fitness to drive.

When does the immediate suspension take effectAn immediate suspension takes effect from the date stated on the Notice of Immediate Suspension and Proposed Cancellation.

If the notice is sent by mail this is five business days from the issue date of the notice.

If the notice is handed to the licence holder it is the date of issue.

When requiredA Proposed Amendment or Cancellation show cause may be taken if the information received indicates that the person has a permanent or long-term medical condition that is likely to adversely affect their ability to drive safely.

Licence Holder Does Not Agree with Show Cause Action
Licence holder does not agreeIf the licence holder does not agree with the immediate amendment or suspension, or the proposed amendment or cancellation of their driver licence they may apply to the Chief Executive for a reconsideration of the decision by:
  • completing an Application for Reconsideration of a Decision of the Chief Executive form;
    or
  • providing sufficient information in writing without the reconsideration form.
Note:The Application for Reconsideration of a Decision of the Chief Executive must be made within 28 days after the issue date of the show cause notice

Refer: Driver Licence > 1. Queensland Driver Licence > Reconsider Decision of the Chief Executive - Guide

If the applicant is still dissatisfied with the decision following reconsideration by the Chief Executive, they may then apply to the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) for a review of the decision.

Returned Show Cause Notices
Returned noticesIf a show cause notice is returned unclaimed, any proposed action to amend or cancel the driver licence must be stopped.

A text restriction should be placed on the customer's record stating what action needs to be taken on the licence and include the following:
  • operator ID
  • date of action
  • location

When the person updates their address, the show cause process should be recommenced.
