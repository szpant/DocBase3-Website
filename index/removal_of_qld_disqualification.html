Removal of Qld Disqualification (1446)

What it isAny person disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence by a Queensland Court cannot apply for a Queensland driver licence during the disqualification period. (TO(RUM Act s.127(6))

The licence record shows a 'disqualified' sanction and a 'cancelled' status. If unlicensed, a customer record is created to record the sanction.
Absolute DisqualificationIf a person is convicted of a motor vehicle offence, an absolute disqualification may be imposed by a magistrate or by a judge of the District Court or the Supreme Court.

Removing an absolute disqualification information sheet is available on Corporate Forms, S4849.

Information is also provided on the TMR website and can be accessed from the following link:
https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Licensing/Licence-suspensions-and-disqualifications/Reasons-for-licence-suspension#removal
Surrender of Cancelled LicenceUpon conviction the person must promptly return the cancelled licence to either:
  • The Court where the conviction was recorded
  • A TMR Customer Service Centre
Removal of DisqualificationDepending on the type of disqualification, a person may apply to either a Court or the Department of Transport and Main Roads to have it removed:
Court
Department of Transport and Main Roads
Role of CSC/
Agency		A person may apply for the removal of either:
  • An absolute disqualification
  • Disqualification for period/s of more than 2 years (including cumulative disqualifications)

Note:Minimum of 2 years is required to be served from the start of the disqualification period.

The application for the removal of the disqualification period/s must be made to a Magistrates Court, the District Court or the Supreme Court, depending on which level of court ordered the disqualification.
(TO(RUM) Act s.131(11))


Note:If multiple absolute disqualification periods have been applied, the customer must apply to have each individual period removed. Removal of individual disqualification periods will be considered by the Magistrate hearing the application/s.
If a person is successful ...
If a person is successful ...
    If a person is successful in having a disqualification period of more than 2 years removed, the remaining disqualification period (if any) must still be served.

    The commencement date of any remaining disqualification will be the court result date.

    If there is more than one disqualification period remaining, those periods must be served either cumulatively or concurrently. Refer: Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > Enforcement - Qld Licence/Unlicensed > Service of Sanctions Concurrently/ Cumulatively
A person may apply for the removal of an absolute disqualification:
  • Imposed before 13 March 2002
  • For unlicensed driving offence only

Excludes combined offences
Excludes combined offences
    A person will be required to apply to the courts if they have more than one type of offence, these combined offences can not be dealt with by TMR.

    For example: If a person was convicted for an unlicensed driving offence and a drink driving offence at the same time.

How to ApplyThe customer should enquire with the relevant court regarding the filing fee for lodging the application.

TMR are not involved in the application process. However, if multiple disqualification periods have been applied to the person's traffic history, the customer must apply to have each individual period removed. Removal of multiple disqualification periods will be considered by the Magistrate hearing the application/s.
Assist with Application form only
Assist with Application form only
    If required, officers may assist the applicant to ensure the application form is completed correctly.

    No further action is required by the CSC/Agency until the person attends for the issue of a Learner Licence, if the disqualification is removed.

When to Reapply after RefusalThe person must wait at least 1 year before making a further application for removal. (TO(RUM) Act s.131(15)The person must wait at least 1 year after the date of the written notice before re-applying to TMR or applying to a Court. (TO(RUM) Act s.131 A(6)
