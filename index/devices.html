Devices (7331)


Devices
What it isCompliance Officers use a range of devices to assist them in their official duties including iPad minis and ANPR Cameras.
Appropriate use of ICT servicesTMR mobile devices such as iPads minis, iPhones and laptops should not be taken home, they should be stored securely in the office unless the officer is not rostered to return to the office daily.

Relevant ICT policies include:

TMR use of ICT Services, Facilities and Devices Policy
TMR use of ICT Services, Facilities and Devices Standard
Authorised and unauthorised use of ICT Services, Facilities and Devices Guideline
iPad miniTMR Security procedures do not permit sharing iPads with other staff members.

Refer: TMR’s Information Policies and Standards: https://inside.tmr.qld.gov.au/sites/IPS/Pages/Home.aspx

Ensure the Find my iPad function is enabled when the iPad mini is set-up to assist locating the device if it is lost or stolen.

For information on how to set up an iPad mini, Refer: Systems > How To > iPad mini

Using your iPad mini
Using your iPad mini
    You will be supplied an iPad mini with rugged case and straps to use while on the road.

    You have the option to order a Stylus Pen through your local stationery ordering process to enable customers to sign for notices on the iPad mini.

    Systems and apps
    Systems and apps
      Officers may use the iPad mini to access the following applications:
      • QCIS
      • Axon View (Body Worn Camera)
      • Axon Capture (Evidence.com capturing)
      • Rego Checks App (NSW, VIC, SA and NHVR)
      • Logbook Me
      • Microsoft Authenticator App
      • Mail App (Work Emails)
      • Other Apple standard apps

      The iPad mini may also be used to access work related emails, and work related websites such as NPR, NHVR, CID and TMR intranet sites. The iPad mini functions such as calculator and camera can also be used.

      It is not recommended that TICA be used on the iPad mini, as the use of TICA on this device is not supported by IT.

      Internal TMR sites such as the intranet can be accessed via the iPad mini and you must have your mobile phone number recorded in the TMR business directory to allow multifactor authentication.

      Please note MyTime timesheets cannot be accessed on the iPad mini.
    Update device time format in system settings
    Update device time format in system settings
      The QCIS app allows for 24 hour time format.

      To update the device time on the iPad mini to 24 hour clock:
      Go to - Setting > General > Date & Time > 24-Hour Time
    iPad mini issues
    iPad mini issues
      If issues are experienced with the iPad mini, it should be reported to Mobile+ support, either by raising a SCO or by phoning the Service Centre on (07) 3066 2000 and selecting Mobile+ support from the phone menu.
      Forgotten Apple Passcode
      Forgotten Apple Passcode
        In the event a passcode cannot be recalled:
        • refrain from repeating incorrect attempts and call the Service Centre on (07) 3066 2000 and select Mobile+ support from the phone menu.

        After 10 attempts the device will revert back to factory settings. If this occurs enter your TMR email address (not username) and password to login to the iPad. The iPad will then self-configure, downloading applications, and be ready for use.
    What to do if the iPad mini is lost or stolen
    What to do if the iPad mini is lost or stolen
      If the iPad mini is lost or stolen, it must be reported immediately to an STI and the Service Centre.

      All employees are responsible for the security and safekeeping of mobile ICT devices both in and out of the controlled workplace environment. Please refer to TMR’s Mobile ICT Devices Standard, section 7.5 if further information is required.

      TMR's Mobile ICT Devices Standard can can be accessed via the following link:
      https://intranet.tmr.qld.gov.au/corp/ish/Shared%20Documents/Standard_
      Mobile_Device_Approved.pdf#search=mobile%20ict%20devices
    Changing the iTunes account
    Changing the iTunes account
      If you have set up iTunes with your personal account and need to change it to be your work account follow the steps below:
      1. Go to Settings
      2. Scroll down and tap on iTunes & App Store
      3. Your Apple ID should show in blue on the first line – tap on it
      4. A new menu should pop up – tap Sign Out
      5. You should then be able to enter details for a new account using your TMR email address
