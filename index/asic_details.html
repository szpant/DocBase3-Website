    ASIC Details
    An Australian Company Number (usually shortened to ACN) is a unique nine-digit number issued by the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) to every company registered under the Commonwealth Corporations Act 2001 as an identifier.

    You can establish the ACN for a company using the following steps.

        The search will generally return a list of results, rather than just one:

        Remember the register contains every business and company name ever registered – including those that have been cancelled, deregistered, struck off etc.

        There are some important things to note about these results:
        Type:
        • Australian Proprietary Company
          • Is a legal entity. A company. Can own assets and be responsible for its actions.
        • Business Name
          • Is just a name. It has no legal standing, no ability to own assets and no ability to be held legally accountable for something. Business names are usually registered to individuals, partnerships or companies.
        Status:
        • Registered
          • Is a current, registered, ongoing, active registration. Anything other than current should not be considered as it indicates the company is at the time is other than current. Much like auto cancelled, cancelled etc for a registration means it’s not current.
        • Deregistered, Cancelled etc
          • Indicates something has or is happening with the company registration. In the event a defendant company shows a status other than registered seek advice from your PA(I) in the first instance.
        • Status as applied to business names.
          • The same statuses apply to business names however as business names have no legal standing to begin with the status of a business name should be moot.
