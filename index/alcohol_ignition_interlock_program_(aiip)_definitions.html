Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program (AIIP) Definitions (3240)

AIIP TermsDefinition
Alcohol ignition interlock A device that, when fitted to a motor vehicle, prevents the vehicle from being started unless the device is provided with a specimen of a person’s breath containing either no alcohol or less than a particular concentration of alcohol.
Refer section 91I of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Approved interlock providerAn interlock provider that has a written agreement with TMR to provide interlocks for the AIIP, and that has been approved under TO(RUM-A&O) Reg 2005.
Refer section 100U of TO(RUM-AOP) Reg 2005
Approved interlockAn interlock approved under the TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010, for use in the AIIP.
Refer section 63B of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010
Interlock conditionA requirement that during the interlock period a person may only drive a nominated vehicle fitted with a prescribed interlock.
Refer section 91K of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Interlock driver A person whose Qld driver licence is subject to an interlock condition (including a person who has an interlock exemption).
Refer section 91I of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Interlock exemptionAn exemption from the requirement to only drive a vehicle fitted with a prescribed interlock and nominated to TMR, via an approved interlock provider.
Refer section 91I of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Interlock periodA maximum two year period during which the person is subject to the AIIP.
  • For persons subject to the AIIP as a result of conviction for a relevant Qld offence/s the interlock period will start on the day after the disqualification period for the latest offence ends.
  • For persons subject to the AIIP as a result of transferring a non-Qld licence with an interlock requirement the interlock period will start on the day they were first issued the non-Qld licence subject to the ‘I’ condition imposed in the other jurisdiction.
Refer section 91M of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Nominated vehicleA motor vehicle nominated to TMR as a vehicle that the person will drive during their interlock period that is fitted with a prescribed interlock.
Refer section 91I of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Prescribed interlockAn approved interlock that is supplied by an approved interlock provider and installed and maintained by a prescribed interlock installer.
Refer section 91I of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Prescribed interlock installerA business that has an arrangement with an approved interlock provider to install or maintain approved interlocks.
Refer section 91I of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Prescribed periodA minimum cumulative period of 365 days during which the person:
(a)holds a valid Qld licence with an ‘I’ condition; and
  • has a nominated vehicle fitted with a prescribed interlock; and/or
  • has a valid interlock exemption;
and/or
(b)holds a valid non-Qld driver licence with an ‘I’ condition; and
  • has a vehicle fitted with an interlock; and/or
  • has a valid non-Qld interlock exemption.

Only days where the requirements are met will count towards the prescribed period.
Any time the requirements are not met, such as if the person’s licence is not valid (not reissued after the disqualification or subsequently suspended or expired) or the interlock is not fitted to the vehicle, will not count as tenure for the prescribed period.
Refer section 91N of TO(RUM) Act 1995
Interlock driver recordA record, in the approved form, that identifies the driver of a nominated vehicle at a particular time during which the vehicle is nominated by more than one interlock driver.
Refer section 63A of TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010
