Electronic Storage of Documents (7554)


Electronic Storage of Documents
What it isWith the introduction of the Disposal of Digitised Records Procedure, a review of all Customer Service Centre Documents occurred.

This new process now allows Customer Service Centres & TMR-Led QGAP's to electronically store original documents into the TMR approved Recordkeeping System - Document Management System (DMS). Once these documents have been saved into DMS the original documents can be securely destroyed.

This new process is in line with the Paper-Lite 2020 Strategy, whilst also reducing storage costs and reduced administration at CSC's associated with manual archiving, reduced monitoring of time-frames for destruction of stored documents.

Note:The Electronic Storage of these documents MUST be completed by using the approved business system (DMS), they cannot be stored in a G Drive or any local Drives.
ContactAny enquiries regarding this procedure must be forwarded to either of the below contacts for response:

1. Local RBA's within each Customer Service Centre
2. Email: Risk and Assurance Team

Under no circumstances should staff be contacting Information Management with enquiries about this procedure.

Guide for Scanning Documents
Guide for Scanning Documents

    The guide is utilised by staff to ensure all documents are scanned in under the below outlined specifications, plus ensuring that the documents are captured and adhere to the early disposal policy prior to destruction.

    Refer: The How To: Filing and Business Exceptions Procedures > Filing Procedures > Electronic Document Storage - Scanning Procedures - 1. Guide to Scanning Documents

    Scanning Specifications
    Scanning Specifications
      These specifications are the minimum acceptable requirements for digitisation of original physical records. They take into consideration the currently available capabilities of scanning equipment across TMR. These specifications will be subject to ongoing review.
      • Format: PDF (PDF/A encouraged), JPEG, PNG or TIFF
      • Resolution: 300 dpi
      • Scanning Ratio: 100%
      • Colour Profile: greyscale by default, colour for photographs and where colour is essential for understanding the document
      • Bit-depth: 8 bits per channel RGB
      • Searchability: OCR encouraged (PDF or PDF/A complies)

    Quality Assurance of Scanned Documents

    Quality Assurance of Scanned Documents
      Digitised records must be reviewed to ensure the quality of its image is fit-for-purpose (is an accurate rendition of the original). This is to ensure that it can be accessed and produced when/if required.
      Quality assurance can be undertaken in a number of ways including, but not limited to:
      • reviewing each record that is scanned and saved into an authorised business or recordkeeping system
      • performing sample reviewing of large volume scanning, such as 10%, or
      • having a secondary officer perform a review of the digitised record to ensure it is fit-for-purpose.

Approved Lists
Approved Lists
    CSA Requirements - Electronic Storage of Documents
    Miscellaneous Documents - Electronic Storage of Documents


The 'CSA Requirements' List outlines all documents that MUST to be captured in DMS, as per the CSC Managers Assurance Checklist. These documents are captured in DMS once the task has been completed or at the end of every month. It is a mandatory requirement to capture these documents in DMS.

The 'Miscellaneous Documents' List outlines other documents that should be captured in DMS (in lieu of the hardcopy being retained at the location) but it isn't mandatory.

When creating a Container, the container title must match the wording in the first column of the lists - Item/Container Title column.

These lists also outline the Function Term and Activity Term that MUST be used to create a container for each of the documents listed.

The containers for these documents should be created for each respective financial year.
Example: Container Title: Document | 01 July 2020 - 30 June 2021 | Centre
Secure Disposal /Destruction of DocumentsOnce documents have been captured in DMS as per Refer: The How To: Filing and Business Exceptions Procedures > Filing Procedures > Electronic Document Storage > Scanning Procedures > 1.Guide to Scanning Documents, the physical documents should be securely destroyed.

Note:No further approval is required to be completed prior to the secure disposal/destruction of the DMS'd documents.

How to Securely Destroy Documents
How to Securely Destroy Documents
    Refer: https://intranet.tmr.qld.gov.au/sites/SRU/Pages/Po10-Secure-Destruction-Bins.aspx

    Notes to Consider:
    Before ordering and using a secure bin:
    • Only public records that have been authorised for destruction by the Information Management unit may be disposed of in a secure bin.
    • The shred material from information crosscut shredded does not need to be placed in a secure destruction bin.
    • Secure destruction bin services are corporately funded, although some regional areas have established their own local arrangements. Contact your local Recordkeeping Business Advisor to find out more.

    It is recommended that all documents are disposed of via a cross shredder and placed in general waste, in lieu of ordering any further destruction bins.

    Note:Smartcards are also able to be cross cut shredded and disposed of in general waste, therefore BOTH Smartcards and paper documents can be cross shredded into the same bag for disposal and placed into general waste.

    Refer: Smartcard > Smartcard > The Smartcard Guide > Transactions > Destroying returned cards/receipts/confirmation slips
