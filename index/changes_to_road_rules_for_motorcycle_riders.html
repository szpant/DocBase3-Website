Changes to Road Rules for Motorcycle Riders (5162)

Project NameChanges to Road Rules for Motorcycle Riders
When1 February 2015
BackgroundA Motorcycle Discussion Paper: Road Rules for Motorcycle Riders and an accompanying online survey were released for community feedback for a six week period in mid-2014.

The three topics considered in the discussion paper were:
  • introducing lane filtering
  • simplifying motorcycle control rules
  • broadening the approved motorcycle helmet standards.

Over 9000 responses were received with the majority of respondents indicating support for the proposed changes. Based on this response and road safety research, the Qld Government has supported their implementation.

The new initiatives are expected to benefit motorcycle riders and the broader community by improving transport outcomes for riders, and helping to relieve congestion and create a more efficient road network for everyone, while preserving road safety.
Who will be impacted and how?From 1 February 2015 the following road rule changes will commence:
  • Riders holding an open class RE or R class licence, or moped riders holding an open C class licence, will be able to:
    • Lane filter, which allows the rider to ride at speeds of 30km/h or less between stationary or slow moving vehicles travelling in the same direction as themselves, provided it is safe to do so. Lane filtering at speeds greater than 30km/h will be prohibited and will incur an infringement of $341 and three demerit points.
    • On major roads such as motorways and freeways where the speed limit is 90km/hr or more, ride at speeds of 30km/h or less past stationary or slow moving traffic on the road shoulder or in emergency stopping lanes.
  • Motorcycle riders will still be required to be astride their seat, but the strict rules about where they have their hands and feet are being relaxed allowing them to do every day riding tasks like stretching a leg to avoid fatigue or raising themselves from the seat on uneven road surfaces without unintentionally breaking the rules. Pillion passengers must be astride the seat and face forwards. If a motorcycle rider or pillion passenger breaks the new rules they may receive an infringement of $151.
  • Motorcycle helmets approved for use in Queensland are being expanded to include those that comply with the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (ECE) 22.05 standard. Breaking the motorcycle helmet rules will incur an infringement of $341 and three demerit points.

Motorcycle riders should be aware that the road rule changes only apply on Queensland roads.
