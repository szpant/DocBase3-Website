BoatSafe Training and Assessment (2616)


BoatSafe Training and Assessment
What it is?BoatSafe was introduced on 01 July 2004.

BoatSafe is a competency based training and assessment scheme to provide training and assessment for persons who wish to apply for a Recreational Marine Driver Licence (RMDL) and/or a Personal Watercraft Licence (PWCL).

The BoatSafe Scheme is delivered by TMR accredited providers who are either:

BoatSafe Training Organisations (BTO)
BoatSafe Training Organisations (BTO)
    External providers accredited by TMR to provide RMDL or PWCL training and assessment. Each BTO must nominate at least one BoatSafe Training Provider (BTP).
BoatSafe Training Provider (BTP)
BoatSafe Training Provider (BTP)
    Individuals nominated by a BoatSafe Training Organisation (BTO) who conduct the actual training and assessment.
Based on National Guidelines
Based on National Guidelines
    BoatSafe is modelled on nationally recognised guidelines for recreational ship operations ensuring that Queensland's RMDL assessment framework is in accord with national objectives.

For more information refer to website: www.msq.qld.gov.au
BoatSafe Training
Options
BoatSafe Training Options
BoatSafe Training Options
    All licence candidates for a RMDL or PWCL must satisfactorily complete:
    • An approved BoatSafe competency based training and assessment course with a BTP employed by a BTO; OR
    • Obtain recognition of prior learning assessment with a BTP employed by a BTO.

    For further information and course fees visit: www.tmr.qld.gov.au and Boatsafe Accreditation
Recognition of prior learning
Recognition of prior learning
    The recognition of prior learning process is an alternative to completing the full BoatSafe course and caters for those who can provide evidence of other relevant qualifications, training or experience in operation of a vessel.

    The BTP will determine if a licence candidates prior learning qualifies them for assessment either in part or full against the BoatSafe competency standards.

    Refer to the BoatSafe Manual for further details.
How to become a BoatSafe Training Organisation
How to Apply
How to Apply
    To apply to become a BTO, an applicant will need to:
    • Complete a BTO application (F4064)
    • Complete a BTP application (F4076) for each person being nominated as a BTP
    • Provide the proposed course lesson plan documentation
    • Provide details of the commercially certified vessel/s to be used for the training and assessment (for example; a copy of the current Certificate of Operation or the registration number).
    • Provide a current company extract showing all directors
    • Provide Evidence of Identity (EOI) for all director/s and BTP/s
    • Provide a copy of the public liability insurance
    • Pay the application fee
    • The proposed BTO is required to pay the relevant criminal history check for each person (director/s and BTP/s) listed on the application, at a Maritime Safety Queensland office and forward a copy of the receipt of the application.

    An authority is granted for a period of up to 3 years.

Training Facilities and Equipment
An applicant for a BTO Authority must:
An applicant for a BTO Authority must:
    • Have access to a commercially certified vessel, appropriate for training for a RMDL or PWCL. The vessel must be capable of performing all of the operational requirements specified in the BoatSafe RMDL Competency Standards and the BoatSafe PWCL Competency Standard with a minimum of two (2) adults on board.

    • Provide an area (internal or external) for the BTP to conduct the theory component of BoatSafe training.

    • Have resource requirements listed in the teaching and learning strategies contained in the BoatSafe RMDL Competency Standards and the BoatSafe PWCL Competency Standard.
How to become a BoatSafe Training Provider
Becoming a BoatSafe Training Provider
Becoming a BoatSafe Training Provider
    The BTO must nominate at least one (1) or more qualified BTP's for TMR to endorse and provide relevant information about the nominated BTP's shown in the BoatSafe Management Standard (PDF, 183 KB)

    BTPs who deliver personal watercraft training and assessment must have a personal watercraft licence in addition to their other qualifications and will need to be endorsed by TMR for the delivery of this training and assessment.

    Each nominated BTP must undergo a criminal history check to ensure they are a suitable person to deliver BoatSafe training and assessment.

How to Apply

Renewal of BoatSafe Training Organisation
Renewal
Renewal
    The BoatSafe Administrator will notify the BTO approximately two (2) months before their authority is due to expire. If a BTO does not renew their authority by the expiry date, they cannot provide BoatSafe training and assessment.
How to Renew
How to Renew
    To renew an authority a BTO must:
    • complete a BoatSafe training organisation application (F4064)
    • complete a BoatSafe training provider nomination form (F4076)
    • pay the application fee
    • pay a criminal history check fee for each person (Director/s and BTP/s) listed on the application. You can pay at a Maritime Safety Queensland office and forward a copy of the receipt with your application.

Cancellation of a BoatSafe Training Organisation Authority
Cancellation
Cancellation
    To cancel a BTO authority, the BTO must post the following information:
    • A signed letter of surrender of authority including:
      • Date of surrender; and
      • Address of the secure storage place for records of BoatSafe training and assessment (refer auditing requirements).
    • The BoatSafe Certificate of Authority
    • All unused BoatSafe Statement of Competency books and evidence of the request to remove all advertising

    Cancellation documents are to be mailed to:
      Department of Transport and Main Roads
      BoatSafe Administrator
      PO Box 673
      Fortitude Valley Qld 4006
AuditingA BTO must always comply with the conditions of their authority. Compliance is measured by audits conducted by TMR officers throughout the state. There are a number of audits that may be conducted throughout the currency of an authority.

BTO must keep learner's training records for at least three (3) years from the date the record was made. The training records must be kept in a safe and secure manner and must be accessible for inspection by TMR or be able to be retrieved and presented within five (5) working days if requested.

Refer: BoatSafe Audit Framework (PDF, 145 KB) for more information.
