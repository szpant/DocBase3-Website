05/11/2020 - Update - TICA issue impacting some users – Unable to open PDFs in TICA
05/11/2020 - Update - TICA issue impacting some users – Unable to open PDFs in TICA

    There are new instructions to resolve this issue. If you are still experiencing issues with TICA opening PDFs that appear blank, please follow the steps below to reinstall TICA.

    Before commencing, close TICA and Adobe Acrobat if open.

    1. Click the start button and open Software Centre


    2. Click on Installation Status status tab on the left hand side and then click on TICA – Production 1908.


    3. Click the Reinstall button. This will verify and update any missing TICA components and ensure the correct working version of Acrobat Reader is installed.


    4. Once this process is finished. Close the Software Centre window and launch TICA as normal.

    If you still experience issues after following these steps please contact the Service Centre.

    Lindsay Abdi
    Principal Advisor (Systems Support)
    Customer Services Branch

03/11/2020 - Blue Card and HRWL Applicants - Evidence of Identity
03/11/2020 - Blue Card and HRWL Applicants - Evidence of Identity

    Staff are reminded to please ensure that all customers attending a TMR CSC for the purpose of obtaining a Blue Card or HRWL, are presenting all EOI requirements to obtain a CRN.

    Operators must ensure that they capture:
    • an image set
    • proof of residential address
    • eContact details.

    As the customer does not need to be a Queensland resident to apply for a Blue Card or HRWL, an interstate proof of address document can be provided and recorded in TICA.

    DocBase has been updated to advise when this occurs, the following text is applied to the customer's record:

      'An interstate residency document has been recorded. If the customer presents to TMR to obtain a TMR product then they must provide a Queensland proof of residency document and this must be updated in TICA'

    If an address document is not recorded in TICA, the customer will not be able to validate successfully online and will be required to return to TMR. Please ensure these requirements are met.

    Please refer to DocBase updates:
    High Risk Work Licence
    Working with Children Card (Blue Card)

    Nicole Clues
    Project Manager, Identity and Card Management Services

29/10/2020 - TICA issue impacting some users – Unable to open PDFs in TICA
29/10/2020 - TICA issue impacting some users – Unable to open PDFs in TICA

    An update to Adobe Reader on Windows 10 computers has resulted in an issue opening PDFs in TICA.

    The update has been stopped but up to 300 computers are estimated to have already been updated.

    If you are experiencing this issue please follow the steps below to uninstall Adobe Acrobat Reader DC 20.012.20041 and reinstall the previous version 20.009.20063.
    Before commencing, close TICA and Adobe Acrobat if open.

    1. Click the start button and open Software Centre


    2. Click on Installation Status and then “Adobe Acrobat Reader DC 20.012.20041”


    3. Click Uninstall


    4. Initially, this may give an error when removing. If so, click Retry.


    5. Once completed, Click the Applications tab and then select the Acrobat Reader DC 20.009.20063 version


    6. Click Install


    7. Once this installation is complete, the process is finished. Close the Software Centre window and launch TICA as normal.

    If you still experience issues after following these steps please contact service centre.

    Lindsay Abdi
    Principal Advisor (Systems Support)
    Customer Services Branch

16/10/2020 - Learner Logbook lodgements in TICA
16/10/2020 - Learner Logbook lodgements in TICA

    Due to technical issues with the virtual machine, there are delays with electronic Learner Logbook App submissions being lodged in TICA.

    Whilst work is being undertaken to resolve this issue, please be assured that all (electronic & physical) logbooks are being assessed within the 12 business day Service Level Agreement.

    In conjunction with this, physical logbooks are also experiencing similar delays, as mail distribution has been interrupted during COVID-19.

    Please note, all learner logbook submissions are provided with correspondence after lodgement, advising to allow 12 business days for processing. This correspondence is as follows:

      • Physical Submissions: Published in learner logbook & provided with a tracking number from Australia Post

    If you have any urgent concerns relating to Logbook lodgement/approvals prior to Practical Driving Assessments, please do not hesitate to contact the Learner Logbook Unit.

01/10/2020 - Managing Blue Card Applicants – Evidence of Identity (including capturing an image set, residential address and eContact details)
01/10/2020 - Managing Blue Card Applicants – Evidence of Identity (including capturing an image set, residential address and eContact details)

    Please ensure that all customers attending a TMR CSC for the purpose of obtaining a Blue Card, are presenting all EOI requirements to obtain a CRN. Operators must ensure that they capture:
    • an image set
    • proof of residential address (for interstate customers, this can be an interstate address – the ability to provide an interstate address is for blue card customers only)
    • eContact details.

    Staff must be following this process and capture all of the above information on the customer’s TMR record. Without these details, the customer cannot proceed through the Blue Card application online and will be required to return to the CSC to complete their transaction with us.

    We are currently investigating alternate methods for capturing an interstate residential address for the purpose of obtaining a Blue Card.

    Nicole Clues
    Project Manager, Identity and Card Management Services
