General Adjustment Types
    Misc-Credit
    Misc-Credit
      This action creates a credit note for a miscellaneous non-customer product (MPRO), or credits a customer's account, which may be refunded if required.

      Miscellaneous Credit (TRAILS)
    Revenue Account Adjustments
    Revenue Account Adjustments
      Revenue Accounts are the department’s General Ledger accounts, where money stored is allocated to specific items or products. Revenue Account adjustments transfer monies either from a revenue account to a customer account or from a customer account to a revenue account.

      Any items that include Tax or GST are processed as a Liability Adjustment.

      Revenue Account to Customer Account (TICA)
      Customer Account to Revenue Account (TICA)
    Liabilities Account Adjustments
    Liabilities Account Adjustments
      Liability Adjustments are generally performed when a Tax / GST component needs to be adjusted to/from a customer account.

      Before performing the adjustment, operators are required to establish how much money should be adjusted to/from Liabilities, and perform a separate adjustment for any Revenue funds.

      Operators should contact Customer Account Management (CAM) if unsure of the correct account numbers before proceeding.

      Liabilities Account to Customer Account (TICA)
      Customer Account to Liabilities Account (TICA)

    Operator Losses Account
    Operator Losses Account
      This adjustment is used to transfer monies from the Receipting Office Operator Loss account to the Operator Loss General Ledger account to recognise the loss write-off as an expense to the department.

      All monies in the receipting office operator losses account should be transferred to the operator losses general ledger account. This will enable the write off to be recognised as a loss to the department.

      This adjustment can be performed when the money is originally transferred from the operator’s unders account into the receipting office operator losses account or may be regularly adjusted after several amounts have accumulated (for example weekly, monthly).

      To apply for relief from liability for Operator Losses Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Adjustments Guide > Rules > Operator Over/Under >$5 > Customer never identified > Relief from payment

      Receipting Office Operator Losses to Operator Losses Account (TRAILS)
    Amending Operator Over / Under Account
    Amending Operator Over / Under Account
      This adjustment is used when an under/over occurs for the same operator following the Amend action on the Maintain Payment Item screen.
      Example:
      Example:
        Customer presents a cheque for $100.00 and cash for $50.00 to purchase a product.

        The operator receipts the product as $50.00 cheque and $100.00 cash.

        The error is not identified until the end of day banking. The banking officer will need to amend the cheque on the Maintain Payment Item screen from $50.00 to $100.00.

        When the amend action is performed it will create an over of $50.00 into the Operator Over's account. (OPOVER).

        The operator will also have an over of $50.00 in their operator cashbook, as they only received $50.00 cash for the product but receipted the product as $100.00. The banking officer will need to create an Adjust_Under (OPUNDER) for the operator for the amount of $50.00.

        Therefore, the operator will have an Operator Over of $50.00 and an Operator Under of $50.00.

      Amend Operator Over/Under Account (TRAILS)
    Reversing an Account Adjustment
    Reversing an Account Adjustment
      An adjustment may be reversed if it was adjusted incorrectly.

      Reversing an adjustment may place a DR / CR in the account. Operators must check the payment screen and refund any credit if required.

      When performing several adjustments, operators should write down the adjustment numbers to assist in displaying adjustments.

      Reverse Adjustment (TICA)
    Offset Different Operators Under / Over
    Offset Different Operators Under / Over
      This adjustment is used when an operator over is offset against another operator's under

      Result of:
      • An operator has an Over or Under in their account and the funds should actually be in another operators account. eg. operators incorrectly exchanged change during the day or an operator forgot to log off after counter-hopping and money was taken.

      Offset Different Operators Under/Over (TRAILS)
