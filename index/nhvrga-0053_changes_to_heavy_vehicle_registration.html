NHVRGA-0053 Changes to Heavy Vehicle Registration (110)


    NHVRGA-0053 Changes to Heavy Vehicle Registration
    Subject Area: Heavy Vehicle Registration

    Issue Date: 18/06/2018

    Purpose:

    This NHVR General Advice is designed to provide information for Authorised officers and relevant staff about changes to heavy vehicle registration occurring from 1 July 2018.

    Background:

    Changes to heavy vehicle registration are to be progressively implemented to benefit industry by introducing common practices, processes and fees. These changes will provide for reduced fleet-management administrative costs.

    The changes will commence from 1 July 2018 and will include the following initiatives:
    • a national heavy vehicle plate
    • a nationally consistent plate fee in participating States and Territories (Jurisdictions)
    • a reduction in the number of jurisdictions requiring heavy vehicle registration labels
    • the option for fleet operators across all jurisdictions to set common registration expiry dates.

    The national heavy vehicle plate will not display a Jurisdiction identifier. Jurisdictions transport authorities, in conjunction with NEVDIS, are identifying a solution to ensure registration compliance checks can be completed by Authorised Officers without knowing the Jurisdiction of registration. NHVR will provide further information once a solution is identified.

    Jurisdictions transport authorities will continue to manage and administer heavy vehicle registration services, such as new registrations, renewals and changes to registration details. All enquiries regarding registration services should be directed to the relevant Jurisdictions in the first instance.

    From 1 July 2018 the Federal Interstate Registration Scheme (FIRS) will be closed to new entrants and renewals. Between 1 July 2018 and 30 June 2019 inclusive, once a heavy vehicles’ FIRS registration expires, the vehicle will be required to be registered in a Jurisdictions registration scheme.

    All enquiries regarding FIRS should be directed to the FIRS hotline number - 1800 026 170.
