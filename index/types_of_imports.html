Types of Imports - Non-RAV Entry (3106)


To determine what requirements apply to a vehicle, please refer to the import approval issued for the vehicle.

On an import approval, the top line should read:

"IMPORT APPROVAL ISSUED UNDER REGULATION ## - [Type of Import]"

The type of import is shown in the grey column below.

What is it?This page outlines all of the requirements for types of imported vehicles that are not on the Register of Approved Vehicles (RAV).

Imported vehicles not on the RAV are:
  • Imported prior to 1/7/2021
  • Imported on or after 1/7/2021 but is one of the following:
  • Race and Rally
  • Significantly modified
  • Exhibition
  • Film and TV
  • Temporary Import
  • Test or market evaluation
  • Re-importation

Refer Types of Imports - RAV (Concessional Pathway) for RAV imported vehicle requirements.
Vehicle manufactured before January 1989 and Interim measure for import approvals (vehicles > 25 years old) - Imported prior to 1/7/2021 or under Transitional Arrangement
Description
Description
    Any light vehicle manufactured before 1 January 1989 may be imported without restriction by the Commonwealth Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development.

    To determine whether a light vehicle was manufactured before 1 January 1989, the customer should contact the original manufacturer or provide evidence of when the vehicle was first registered.

    Replica/hotrod vehicles date of manufacture is taken from when the vehicle is complete, e.g. a replica 1964 Shelby Cobra completed in 2004 would be regarded as being a year of manufacture of 2004.

    For the requirements for heavy vehicle imported under this category, please refer to the 'Trucks and Buses' section below.

    A vehicle with an import approval under the interim measure for vehicles older than 25 years old can be registered using the same procedures as applies to vehicles manufactured before January 1989.

    Trucks and Buses
    Description
    Description
      A vehicle greater than 4.5t GVM, built on a conventional truck chassis, will be classed as a truck.

      If the vehicle has more than 9 seating positions, including the driver's seat, it is classed as a bus.

      The requirements for a bus or truck vary depending on the age and weight / seating capacity of the vehicle.

      Note:Trucks having a GVM less than 12t and buses with fewer than 12 seating positions need to meet the ADRs applicable at the date of original manufacture.

      A truck or bus can not have a GVM re-rating (S Code modification) performed prior to first registration.

      Standard vehicles plate fitted
      Description
      Description
        A vehicle that has been taken out of Australia (for a particular event, or while the owner lives overseas for a while or even to have work done on the vehicle), may be allowed to be imported back in to Australia.

        An Identification Plate (also known as a Compliance Plate) is fitted to a vehicle by the manufacturer to confirm that the vehicle complied with the applicable Australian Design Rules when it was first supplied to the market in Australia. Identification plates are normally fitted on the firewall on passenger cars and on the frame head on motorcycles, however, some vehicles may have identification plates fitted in the door frame or in the luggage compartment. Older identification plates were made from stamped aluminium, modern plates are adhesive labels.

        Note:Vehicles that have been extensively modified while overseas may not be eligible to be imported under this provision. Extensively modified vehicles may be considered new vehicles for the purpose of the Act because the original identification plate no longer describes the vehicle, for example in the case of a passenger car that has been stretched into a limousine with additional seats, the vehicle category may have changed and the number of seats listed on the plate may be incorrect. If a vehicle has been modified at all while overseas must provide details of the modifications.
      Vehicle Standards Requirements
      Vehicle Standards Requirements
        Vehicle must be returned to the same condition when the original identification plate was attached by the manufacturer. The identification (compliance) plate must still be attached to the vehicle.

        Any modifications made to the vehicle must be certified according to an approved Code of Practice and affixed with a modification plate or a letter of approval issued by TMR or the NHVR.

      Description

        Unrestricted Used Motorcycles
          The Registered Automotive Workshop Scheme allows the importation of used motorcycles with no restriction on the number of vehicles that may be imported and plated provided the vehicle make and model is listed on the Register of Unrestricted Motorcycle Rulings.

      Campervans - Imported prior to 1/7/2021 or under Transitional Arrangement
      First Registration
      First Registration
        This section provides specific information about the requirements for imported campervans and how they should be inspected when presented for first registration.

        NOTE:Vehicles imported as campervans must be presented and registered as a "campervan" at first registration.

        Inspecting Campervans at First Registration
        Inspecting Campervans at First Registration
          When presented for registration, the vehicle must be configured in the way outlined by the Consumer Information notice.

          When performing the normal pre-registration inspection for a campervan (using the Campervan/Motorhome Inspection Sheet (F4550)), please perform an additional check to ensure the seating requirements listed on consumer Information notice match the vehicle.

          Example:
          CONSUMER INFORMATION NOTICE

          ADR3 - 3rd row seatbelts removed and anchorages rendered inoperable. Centre seatbelt in 2nd row removed and anchorage rendered inoperable.
          INSPECTING VEHICLE
          • Make a visual check to ensure that the third row of seats has all seatbelts removed (inc. buckles) and that centre seating position in the 2nd row has the seatbelt (inc. buckle) removed. CSC Operators are not required to check anchorages.

          NOTE:If the vehicle does not match the details in the consumer information notice registration must be refused. The CSO must also send an email to 'Vehicle Standards' providing the VIN, Year, Make, Model and details of what did not match.

          A text restriction should also be placed on the vehicle similar to:

          *** Imported Campervan ***

          Vehicle inspected at X CSC on <date> and was refused.

          Seating in vehicle did not match import documents (change as necessary).

          Customer must re-present vehicle for inspection once corrected.

        Requirements to be classed as a campervan
        Requirements to be classed as a campervan
          Before a vehicle can be classed as a campervan, it must meet the following minimum requirements:
          • Table (may be easily removable)
          • Cooking facilities
          • Storage
          • Sleeping facilities
          • Fire extinguisher
          • Outward opening or sliding door opening to the left or rear of the vehicle that allows clear and unobstructed access to the living area.
          • The number of transport seats in the vehicle must be equal to or exceed the number of sleeping positions (eg sleeping positions for 4 people must have a minimum seating capacity of 4).

          Some vehicles may be fitted with additional seats that are used for fold down bedding but not for use as transport seating.

          In this situation a label must be clearly attached to the vehicle indicating which seats can be used as transport seats.

          Note:Not all seats in the vehicle may be able to be used as normal seats when the vehicle is in motion.

          See below for information that should be included on the label.

          "THIS MOTORHOME IS DESIGNED FOR THE CARRIAGE OF (x) PERSONS"
          {A seating plan showing all the provided seats, with an X shown on all seating positions that are NOT designated seating positions}
          WARNING: THE SEATING POSITION(S) SHOWN BY X ON THIS LABEL MUST NOT BE OCCUPIED WHILE THE VEHICLE IS IN MOTION

      Test or Evaluation Vehicle
      Test or Evaluation Vehicle
      Test or Evaluation Vehicle
        A test or evaluation vehicle is a vehicle imported by a commercial vehicle importer
        who wishes to import larger quantities of the vehicle.

        This evaluation vehicle or vehicles are used to ensure that the vehicles can be
        made compliant with the Australian Design Rules.

        A test or evaluation vehicle can only be driven on a road if a permit has been issued by the Vehicle Standards Unit (for light vehicles) or the NHVR (for heavy vehicles).

        If permission is granted for the vehicle to be used on roads by the Vehicle Standards Unit or the NHVR, the vehicle must be registered and affixed with Conditional Limited Use number plates only.

        Once a light vehicle is given a magenta compliance plate or a full manufactures compliance plate, it is no longer considered a test or evaluation vehicle and standard registration requirements apply.
