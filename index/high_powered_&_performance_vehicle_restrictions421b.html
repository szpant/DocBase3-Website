High Powered & Performance Vehicle Restrictions (3167)

Note: When communicating with customers regarding high powered vehicles it is recommended that you also refer to a performance vehicle (eg, high powered and performance vehicles).

Applies toRestriction
A person who holds a:
  • P1 Provisional licence
    or
  • P2 Provisional licence
    and
  • is under 25 years of age
or
P1 Probationary licence
or
P1 Restricted licence
or
P2 Probationary licence
or
P2 Restricted licence
and
The licence was issued because the person was disqualified for an offence committed when they were under 25
TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s70(1)		The person must not drive a high powered vehicle unless:
  • the person is carrying a current certificate of exemption issued under
TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s71
    and
  • Driving the vehicle in accordance with the certificate of exemption
    The certificate of exemption must be produced if requested by a police officer
TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s70(4)
    or
  • the vehicle is a Toyota Landcruiser
TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s70(2)(b)
Refer below: Certificate of Exemption - High Powered Vehicles

Definition of High Powered Vehicle
Check High Powered Vehicle Online
    Customers can check whether a vehicle they are planning to drive is considered a High Powered Vehicle by entering the plate number or vehicle identification number (VIN) using the TMR online service.

    Note:This service is limited to vehicles manufactured after January 2010. Vehicles manufactured prior to 2010 must be identified through the list of TMR Qld P1 and P2 approved cars. Refer below: HPV Status of Vehicles Manufactured prior to 2010

    The following results are returned to the customer:
    • Plate number
    • VIN
    • Description
    • High power status:
    • Not high powered - the vehicle can be driven without the need for a HPV exemption
    • High powered - a HPV exemption is required
    • Not assessed - the vehicle is pre-2010 (this result provides a link to the TMR Qld P1 and P2 Approved Cars
