Guide to Statement Taking (7506)



Guide to Statement Taking
What it isDuring the course of an investigation you will be required to talk to people or conduct interviews about alleged offences. A statement is a record of evidence given by a witness.

A written statement is prepared to provide a record of the evidence which a witness will give in court. When preparing a statement, it is important to remember that it will be used by the prosecutor to lead the witness when giving evidence.

Statements are also used in investigations as the basis for further enquiries.
Who can give a statement
Transport Inspector
Transport Inspector
    A Transport Inspector may provide a statement regarding an alleged offence they have investigated.
    For example
    • a statement that is required for a Full Brief of Evidence
    • to provide information about what they have done in an investigation when needs to go to another region.
Complainant
Complainant
    A complainant can provide a statement that provides initial information that triggers an investigation.
    For example a statement taken from a person who has a complaint against an AIS for a vehicle they purchased. This statement lays out the initial facts and circumstances that can lead to an investigation, facts and detail are very important.
Responsible Party
Responsible Party
    A responsible party is a person within the scope of the investigation who has not yet been cleared of an allegation. You may take a statement from this person only to find through other statements that this person is responsible. An interview would then follow.
Witness
Witness
    A witness may give a statement about direct or indirect evidence for a matter under investigation.

    For example a mechanic observes an Approved Examiner completing a Safety Certificate without carrying out an inspection of a vehicle
Principles
Principles of statement taking
Principles of statement taking
    1. Obtain accurate and reliable information
    2. Approach with an open mind
    3. Act fairly
    4. Always question what you are being told
    5. Establish the truth
    6. Be respectful.
    Effective communication skills will assist in statement taking
Required Information
Required information
Required information
Required information
    A statement should be in a narrative form and describe in detail the relevant events. The statement should focus on the experiences of the person giving the statement – what they did, saw, heard, felt and so forth.

    The statement should, to the extent appropriate be expressed in the language used by the witness. As the statement is a presentation of the witness' own evidence, it is inappropriate to prepare statements by copying and pasting from 'precedent statements'. Two witness statements should never be the same; this would cause doubt as to whether the statements accurately recorded the witness' evidence.

    Statements should avoid the use of overly-technical terms, jargon and acronyms. If the use of such language is unavoidable, a clear explanation as to what the terms mean should be provided the first time they are mentioned in the statement.

    The statement should record the events in chronological order so that what happened unfolds in a logical sequence
Elements of the offence
Elements of the offence
When preparing to take a statement, the interviewer should have regard to the elements of the offence that the prosecution is required to prove.

If there is more than one potential offence which could be charged, then the elements of all potential offences should be covered in interview and addressed in the statement if relevant.
Variation from NHVR work procedure
Variation from NHVR Work Procedure Affiidavit Preparation
Variation from NHVR Work Procedure Affiidavit Preparation
    TMR does not follow all elements of the NHVR work procedure for preparation of Affidavits. Notable variations are:
    • TMR only require the witness compile a witness statement and sign it.
    • A statement is not required to be witnessed by a JP.
    • Compliance does not use Affidavits for investigations.

    6.1.1 Prerequisites
    If a statement is required by Prosecutions, it must be checked by the STI and submitted by the date specified by Prosecution.

    6.1.2 Completing the affidavit
    The statement template is in DocBase Refer: Transport Compliance > 12.Investigations > Investigation Tools and Templates
    • Disregard the information table
    • Use the font in the statement template
    • The official caution does not need to be recorded in full but the fact it was given and the response from the defendant must be recorded.
    • All exhibits must be listed in a Full Brief of Evidence

    6.2 Affidavit review process
    The statement is not required to be signed in the presence of a JP.
