Infringement Notice Guide (2756)


What it isAn infringement notice is a notice issued to an alleged offender when a breach of legislation (which has associated penalty units) is detected.

    An Infringement Notice may be issued to the:
    • Registered operator of a vehicle
    • Person in charge of vehicle at time of offence
    • Person in charge of vehicle at time of offence as stated in a Statutory Declaration.
    • Person who acquired vehicle prior to offence as stated in a Statutory Declaration.


Note: If a vehicle with a stolen restriction is detected by an RSCO camera, the infringement will still be issued to the registered operator. The registered operator is required to contact the RSCO who will investigate to confirm the stolen restriction and withdraw the notice.

However, if an infringement notice is issued for speeds over 40km/h, QPS conduct further investigations; including a stolen vehicle check. If they confirm the vehicle is stolen, the infringement notice will not be issued.
Details Recorded
Details Recorded on Notice
Details Recorded on Notice
    An IN must include the following information:
    • A unique number
    • Issue Date
    • Place of offence
    • Police officer's number (for non-camera related offences)
    • Nature of the offence
    • Date of the alleged offence
    • Penalty amount

    Alleged Offender's details
    Alleged Offender's details
      • Private individual's full name and residential address
      or
      • Company/Organisation's full name and registered business address
      Note:Infringements for offences where there is no driver/responsible person present at the time the offence was detected (eg parking offences) are issued to the registered operator.
      TMR are to record the infringement against the registered operator of the vehicle.
      Note:Residential/Business address must be a Queensland address where possible
Service of NoticesThe infringement notice is deemed to be served if delivered by one of the following methods:
  • In person
  • By the post
  • By certified mail
  • If attached/placed on the vehicle
  • By MMS (QNotice only)
  • By email

Non camera infringement notices
Non camera infringement notices
    Non camera handwritten infringement notices
    1
    		An authorised officer completes IN
    2
    		The authorised officer forwards copy of IN to TMR within 7 days
    3
    		Offence Processing Operations enters IN into TICA
    4
    		Once finalised, a Driver Offence Notification will be issued if applicable (refer below)

    Non camera electronic issued infringement notices
    1
    		The officer identifies the alleged offender and checks their details:
    • The officer will create a new CRN if one does not exist
    • If any customer details have changed (e.g. address) the officer will not update the customer's existing CRN with their new details provided at the time of intercept
    2
    		The officer will complete the infringement details electronically (the address on the infringement will be the address provided by the alleged offender).
    3
    		The customer can choose to have the infringement mailed, emailed or sent by MMS. (MMS is QPS only.) If the customer chooses to have the infringement mailed it will be printed within 24 hours and posted to the alleged offender's postal or residential address, depending on where the customer wanted the notice sent at the time of intercept.

    Note:If the infringement is not received, the alleged offender can contact QPS or TMR for a replacement, obtain details from the "My account" portal on the TMR website or call 13 23 80. The time for payment will not be extended.
    4
    		The infringement details will be lodged with TMR electronically within 2 hours. The details can then be accessed and paid online from the "My account" portal.
    5
    		Payment can also be made with or without the physical infringement notice. Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Payment Methods
    6
    		If the offender wants to contest the fine in a Magistrates Court, they must complete a Court Election (F5111 - Individual or F5112 - Company) form. This form must be lodged at the nominated email or PO Box address within 21 days of the notice issue date.
    7
    		Once finalised, a Driver Offence Notification will be issued if applicable (refer below)

Options
Statutory Declaration
Statutory Declaration
    Road Safety Camera Office Detected Infringements
    Road Safety Camera Office Detected Infringements
      A registered operator can nominate the person responsible for the offence, or the responsible driver can nominate themselves:
      • Online Transport and Main Roads Online Services - Fines and Infringements
      • By completing and returning to the RSCO the statutory declaration on the back of the Infringement Notice to Queensland Police Service (Road Safety Camera Office).

      Further information about driver nominations via statutory declaration is available on the Queensland Police Service website.

      Once received and if accepted the issuing authority will waive the original notice and issue a new notice to the nominated offender.

      Payment should not be accepted when a statutory declaration is being completed. Any monies paid on the original infringement will be placed in the customers account and are to be refunded or, when customer nominates, the funds can be used to pay the notice issued to the nominated offender. Refer: Credit Balances in TICA customer Accounts


      Note:A Statutory Declaration can also be signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a conveyancer.

    Nomination a Responsible Operator
    Nomination a Responsible Operator
      The registered operator may nominate a responsible operator by written notification to the chief executive within 28 days of being requested to do so.

      The notification under TO(RUM) Act 1995 s170(1)must include written consent from the nominated responsible operator.
    ANPR Detected Unregistered or Uninsured Infringements
    ANPR Detected Unregistered or Uninsured Infringements
      If the vehicle was sold/disposed of or stolen/illegally taken a nomination can be completed:
      • Online Transport and Main Roads Online Services List
      • By completing and returning to the ANPR team, statutory declaration on the back of the Infringement Notice to Prosecutions Unit

      Payment should not be accepted when a statutory declaration is being completed. Any monies paid on the original infringement will be placed in the customer’s account and should be refunded.

      Note:A Statutory Declaration can also be signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a conveyancer.

    Parking Infringements
    Parking Infringements
      Infringements are usually left on an unattended vehicle and are recorded against the registered owner by the OPU. If the registered owner completes a statutory declaration and forwards it to TMR, the infringement is forwarded to the police station which issued the infringement. If the decision is made to re-issue the infringement the person will be sent a new infringement notice.

      Note:A Statutory Declaration can also be signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a conveyancer.

Finalising an Infringement Notice
Finalising an Infringement Notice

    The alleged offender must elect one of the following within 28 days of the issue of the notice:
      Pay in Full
      Pay in Full

        The notice amount may be paid in full prior to the notice being registered with SPER (TICA status NOTISS)

        Payments received by mail
        If a cheque for an infringement payment in full is:
        • Under the required amount by <$10 the customer should be allocated a credit limit of $10 and payment processed.
        • Over the required amount the full amount should be paid into the customers account and then paid from account and the remainder left in the customer account.
        Text must be placed against the payment to explain the action taken.
      Pay by Instalments (VIP)
      Pay by Instalments (VIP)

      Voluntary Instalment Plan (VIP)
      Contest Notice
      Contest Notice
        The alleged offender may elect to not pay the penalty amount and contest the notice on one of the following grounds:
      Contest - Court Election
      Contest - The Offence
      Contest - Person Named by Mistake
      Contest - Issued to Registered Operator


    Once an Infringement Notice is finalised, a Driver Offence Notification may be issued. (Refer below Driver Offence Notification)
    If no Action by Alleged Offender
    If no Action by Alleged Offender
      If the alleged offender does not complete one of the above options for the notice within 28 days, it will default. On default of an infringement notice, TICA sends a Default Certificate to have the notice registered with SPER.
      Exceptions to Defaulting to SPER
      Exceptions to Defaulting to SPER
        The following infringement notices do not go to SPER and are referred for alternate prosecution:
        • Juvenile (less than 18 years at time of offence) unless:
          • Offender enters into a VIP; or
          • Offender was aged 17 at time of offence and demerit points are attached to the offence.
        • Overseas offenders
          • Where the Offender identity is not clearly established (eg. date of birth 31/12/9999)
          • Offender is not a legal entity (e.g. deceased, closed organisation, business)

      Once registered with SPER:
      • The notice becomes final in TICA (FINSPER Status)
      • Demerit points are added to the offender's traffic history

When to Recall a TINThere may be instances where an infringement was not paid within the 28 day prescribed period and circumstances may warrant the recall of an infringement notice from SPER.

The Offence Processing Unit (OPU) will make the final determination but have provided the following scenario's to assist staff in assessing these requests.

Funds paid to customer account and not applied to infringement

    Funds paid to customer account and not applied to infringement

    		Applies to customers where:
    • infringement was not on the system/ not presented at time of payment and
    • made a payment to their customer account for the infringement amount or if eligible, VIP instalment and
    • payment was made within the prescribed period
    2
    		Confirm the above requirements prior to contacting OPU.
    3
    • OPU will arrange for the recall of the infringement back to NOTISS status
    • customer service staff are to apply funds in the customer account to the infringement
    • provide receipt to customer

Recalled/withdrawn infringements from SPER
Withdrawn
Withdrawn
    If an infringement is withdrawn from SPER (usually when it is waived, a court election processed or application to Cancel Enforcement Order is approved), the same infringement notice number cannot be re-lodged with SPER. If the matter goes through court, it can be re-lodged with SPER as a court order.
Recalled
Recalled
    If an infringement is recalled from SPER (usually for admin reasons - Refer: When to recall a TIN above), it can be re-lodged with SPER, including as a VIP providing it meets the VIP requirements.

    Reminder Notices for unpaid notices are no longer issued by post. It is the alleged offender's responsibility to act within 28 days of the date of issue of the notice.

Driver Offence Notification (DON)Once an Infringement Notice that is issued to a person roadside is finalised, a DON may be emailed. The email will go to the registered operator of the vehicle the offence was committed in if the offence was committed by anyone other than the registered operator. The DON will include the offender's name and details of the offence.

TMR is authorised to provide these details to the registered operator under Section 77AAA of the Transport Operations Road Use Management Act 1995.
