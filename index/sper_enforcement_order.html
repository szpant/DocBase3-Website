SPER Enforcement Order (2767)


SPER Enforcement order
What it isWhen an alleged offender takes no action on an Infringement Notice, the State Penalties Enforcement Registry (SPER) at the Justice Department issues an Enforcement Order to the alleged offender.
Process from TMR to SPERWhen an Infringement Notice is defaulted (no action taken):
Dept.Action
1
TMR
A Default Certificate is electronically lodged with SPER (automatically generated by TICA)
2
TMR
The IN become final in TICA (FINSPER Status)
3
TMR
Demerit points are added to the offender's driver licence
4
SPER
Registers the Infringement Notice
5
SPER
Adds an administration fee to the fine amount
6
SPER
Sends an Enforcement Order by ordinary mail to the alleged offender's last known address

Note:If the alleged offender doesn't receive the notice they are still liable for the debt (e.g. current address not provided).

Enforcement Options
Enforcement Order Options
Enforcement Order Options
    If alleged offender receives an Enforcement Order they can:
    Pay in Full
    Pay in Full
      Fine is paid in full (Offender admits guilt)

    Pay Instalments
    Pay Instalments
      • Minimum $60.00 per month
      • Can be weekly, fortnightly or monthly instalments

      Financial Assessment Instalment Plan
      Make application to SPER for reduced payments because alleged offender cannot afford $60.00 per month

    Court Election
    Court Election
      Alleged offender can apply to SPER to contest an Infringement Notice that has an Enforcement Order issued.

      Must apply within 28 days of issue of EO. SPER then notifies the administering authority to issue the complaint and summons.

    Fine Option Order
    Fine Option Order
      Contact SPER to apply for financial assessment to do unpaid community service

    Cancel
    Cancel
      Can be applied for at SPER when an alleged offender has one of the following:
      • Not received the infringement notice or enforcement order
      • Received notice after allowed time for taking action
      • Had an accident/illness preventing action
      • An enforcement order that is within 6 months of issue

    Extension
    Extension
      Apply to SPER within 28 days for an extension of time to pay (only one application allowed)

Consequences
Consequences of No Action
Consequences of No Action
    If alleged offender takes no action within 28 days of EO issue, the following may occur:
    Consequence Is..That May Result In..
    An Additional FeeEnforcement fee added to fine. Please refer to the SPER website for the current fee:
    http://www.sper.qld.gov.au/about-my-debt/fees-and-charges.php
    Issue of Enforcement Warrant
    • Seizure of real estate or personal property
    • Charges imposed on property (e.g. debentures & stocks)

    Note:If the Enforcement Warrant cannot be satisfied SPER may issue an Arrest & Imprisonment Warrant.
    Issue of Fine Collection Notice
    • Funds being taken directly from financial accounts (e.g. bank)
    • Funds being taken directly from earnings
    Sanction Applied to Driver LicenceSuspension of Driver Licence

    A SPER suspension is applied indefinitely until the person either pays the fine in full, pays by instalments or performs unpaid community service.

    If the alleged offender is found guilty of driving whilst under a SPER suspension penalties may include fines and/or imprisonment.


    If the customer has an eContact email address recorded with TMR they will be sent an email advising their licence has been SPER suspended, and for further information to contact SPER.
