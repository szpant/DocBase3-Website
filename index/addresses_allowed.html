Addresses Allowed (1004)

A current address must be supplied to the department in order for correspondence to reach the customer in a timely manner.

Each customer must supply to the department at least either a current Residential (individual) or Business (organisation) address. A postal address may also be added for correspondence purposes if different to the Residential or Business address.

Rules and Exceptions
Residential
Residential
    Driver Licence
    A person must have a Queensland residential address to maintain a Queensland driver licence.

    Under no circumstances should a non-Queensland address be recorded as a person's residential address if they hold a Queensland driver licence.

    If a person insists on notifying a non-Queensland residential address, then show cause action to cancel the person's Queensland licence should be considered, as the person is no longer a Queensland resident (refer section 124(g) of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management – Driver Licensing) Regulation 2010).

    Common sense should prevail where a person is temporarily out of Queensland, for example, on holiday.

    Please also remind people that they have three months in which to transfer their driver licence in the Australian jurisdiction where they are residing.

    Exceptions for currently serving Defence Force personnel
    Exceptions for currently serving Defence Force personnel
      The place of posting for members of the armed services is often temporary and it may be unreasonable that they transfer their licence with each posting.

      An interstate address may be endorsed on a current Queensland driver licence of a service person or immediate family members.

      If the person is still posted interstate when their licence expires, they must obtain a new licence in the State where they are posted.

      The Executive Director (Road Safety and System Management Division) may exercise discretion if the service person is shortly to be based again in Queensland.
    Vehicle Registration
    For the registration of a vehicle to an itinerant who does not hold a Qld driver licence, the Residential address may be one of the following:
    • The Australian address of a friend or relative
    Requirements to use friend/relative's address
    Requirements to use friend/relative's address
      The applicant must provide:
      • a letter from the friend/relative giving permission to use their address
      and
      • a document verifying the friend/relative's address. The same documents may be used as per the list for evidence of residency for driver licenses.

      Refer: Evidence of Identity, Residency, Garage Address > Evidence of Residency (Driver Licences only) > Evidence of Residency Documents

      Note: a photocopy of this document is acceptable

    • The address of an Australian Bank, Doctor or Organisation that provides mailing services to travellers
    Requirements to use Bank, Doctor or mailing service address
    Requirements to use Bank, Doctor or mailing service address
      The address must be verified (for example from a receipt for payment, invoice, business card etc)

    • An overseas residential address for itinerant overseas visitors only (if no Australian address available)
    Requirements to use overseas residential address
    Requirements to use overseas residential address
      Must be verified by sighting the address on one of the following:
      • an overseas driver licence
      • international driving permit
      • travel document such as a visa
Garage
Garage
    A Garage address must be a Queensland address.

    If not entered separately, the Garage address will default to the Qld Residential/Business address on TICA. A customer may have different garaging address for each vehicle if required.
