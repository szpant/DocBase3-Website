Operating a Driver Reviver Site (7404)


Operating a Driver Reviver Site
Operating a SiteThe aim of the program is to have all driver reviver sites to be open for the periods of the holiday campaigns which is outlined in the document titled campaign periods.
The campaign periods are displayed on the TMR website and hard copy is also mailed by SPT to the volunteer coordinators ahead of each holiday campaign period.

The Road Safety Product POW states that a key objective is to support driver reviver volunteers to open the site during holiday campaigns.
This support can be provided in various ways.

It is imperative that operational forms are prepared as advised by Department of Transport & Main Roads and forwarded to the Procedures & Training (P&T) at the times indicated on the forms.

The content of these forms can be used to prepare media information, forward stock requests to McCormac Cooper & Associates, to ensure the sites are in a serviceable condition meeting WHS standards, and to collect data on customer patronage and hours the site is open for.

For procedures and associated forms for Setting up a New Site Refer: Road Safety Policy > 2. Driver Reviver > TMR Driver Reviver Policy > Guide to Establishing a Driver Reviver Site > Setting up a New Site


Data Collection
Data Collection

    The Data Collection Form provides information on patronage by recording the number of cups used for each operational period and and the number of hours the site was operated by volunteers. It should be completed each day of operation. The form is to be forwarded to SP&T at the address provided on the form.

    SP&T also prepares the Annual Table of Statistics Notes that is used to monitor the patronage of Driver Reviver sites across the State. These statistics are available as the year progresses and can be forwarded to Manager (Road Safety) upon request.

    Supplies of Data Collection Forms can be obtained from SP&T
Stock Order
Stock Order

    The Stock Order Form should be completed prior to each campaign by the dates shown at the top of the form along with any media articles, photos or general comments about a campaign. This information is to be sent to P&T

    P&T will then collate the stock order for all the sites in Qld and forward the information to McCormac Cooper & Associates. Site Coordinators are advised to order excess stock as It is generally not possible for late orders to be filled.

    If a site runs out of stock the regional Department of Transport & Main Roads officer for that site must be contacted.

    The Department of Transport & Main Roads officer will then arrange for more stock to be purchased locally and delivered to the site or stock from another site to be used.

    Volunteers are not to purchase supplies and then submit a receipt to Department of Transport & Main Roads unless by prior arrangement with the Department of Transport & Main Roads office concerned.
Campaign Rosters
Campaign Rosters

    An agreed roster for each campaign allows for the organisation of staff and equipment needs well in advance.

    A roster containing names, shift times and contact phone numbers should be prepared and made available to all members of the group.

    Producing a roster will ensure maximum attendance of group members and enable shift swaps to be made easily if unforeseen circumstances arise. Group members will also be able to easily pass on messages and site requirements, particularly in relation to supplies.


    Note:For Workplace Health and Safety reasons and to ensure the security of volunteers, it is essential that a minimum of two people are rostered on and working at the site at any time. If this is not possible due to unforeseen circumstances (e.g. a person calls in sick) then the site should not open.

    Developing an annual roster for campaign periods is also encouraged.. It is not necessary for the site coordinator to provide TMR with a copy of the rosters.
Involving Local Sponsors
Involving Local Sponsors

    Volunteers and volunteer organisations may approach local sponsors to provide donations of additional supplies such as milk, ice, cordial and other consumables and equipment such as tarpaulins, generators and lighting. Donations must not conflict or compete with products supplied by national sponsors of the program.

    When approaching local businesses or individuals for donations it is important to offer something in return (e.g. opportunity to display logo at site). The logo must not be larger than that of the major sponsor.

    If sponsors approach Department of Transport & Main Roads regional offices to make a small donation, the enquiry should be directed to the appropriate volunteer or volunteer organisation.

    If potential sponsors approach Department of Transport & Main Roads regional offices to make a substantial donation (e.g. a product for many sites) then the regional office should direct the enquiry to RSPLT.
Fundraising
Fundraising by Selling Food
Fundraising by Selling Food
    Only volunteer organisations associated with the Driver Reviver program are permitted to sell food from a Driver Reviver site.

    Volunteer organisations wishing to provide food or other items for sale at Driver Reviver sites should satisfy all food hygiene regulations and obtain all relevant licences and approvals from the local authority.

    Also refer to the Main Roads Vending Policy for State-Controlled Roads in Queensland for your region which can be obtained by contacting the local PDO Corridor Management Team.

    The type of product offered for sale should not contradict the road safety message of the Driver Reviver program (e.g. alcohol is not permitted to be sold). Volunteer organisations should discuss the matter with local and surrounding retailers to seek their agreement, as it may impact on businesses.

    It is permissible for a volunteer group operating a site during a campaign to hold a sausage sizzle. The group must be working the site at the time satisfying being associated with the program at the site.

    Volunteers engaging in this type of fundraising should note, as the activity does not relate directly to the operation of a Driver Reviver site, volunteers are not covered by Department of Transport & Main Roads's indemnity against public liability or personal accident, bodily injury or death.

    Volunteer Indemnity


    Food and beverages sold should in no way conflict with any product provided by the national sponsors.
Fundraising by Donations
Fundraising by Donations
    TMR recognises that being able to source donations is a reason why some small groups volunteer at sites. Removing this opportunity may adversely impact upon the number of and types of organisations willing to volunteer at sites.

    Fundraising from donation is permitted but must be closely monitored by TMR to ensure it doesn’t impact upon the integrity of the program.

    A condition of the sponsorship the program receives is that products and services be provided free to the motoring public.

    However, many motorists are willing to make a donation to the registered volunteer organisation providing the service. It is a requirement that the donation containers not be presented in such a way that people feel obligated to make a donation.

    Donation containers should be clearly marked with the registered volunteer organisation's logo and identify the principal project for which the funds are sought.

    For sites that have multiple community groups volunteer, such as Lions, Rotary and others who seek donations, it may be necessary for each group to come to an agreement as to how donations are distributed. It may be more appropriate for the respective groups donation tin to be removed by that group at the conclusion of their shifts so as to not create any confusion.
Advertising and promotion at Driver Reviver SitesAdvertising at Driver Reviver sites is available to current sponsors, both national and local and current volunteer organisations only.

Road Safety staff should ensure any out-of-date advertising is removed.

Promoting Local Tourism
Promoting Local Tourism
    The Driver Reviver program provides an opportunity for locals to promote their communities in a unique way, relative to that community.

    It is permissible for volunteer organisations to provide information on local accommodation services, local history or surrounding tourist points of interest at the site.
Other Considerations
Volunteer requirements and recommendations
Volunteer requirements and recommendations
    Age of Volunteer Participants
    The site must at all times be under the management of an adult (18 years or older). Children are also encouraged to volunteer, however hot liquids are not to be served or received by persons under 15 years of age. If in doubt as to the child's age, volunteers should check with the parents/carers (see 3.1.11 Serving of Hot Beverages).Notes Link

    Recommended Hours of Work for Volunteer Participants
    It is recommended that volunteers do not work more than an eight-hour period in any given day. This is to ensure volunteers do not get fatigued, in particular if they have to drive home.
Serving of Hot Beverage
Serving of Hot Beverage
    Department of Transport & Main Roads has a responsibility to ensure that every volunteer is aware of the dangers of serving hot liquids while operating a Driver Reviver site and a warning notice such as the one below should be displayed in a prominent place.

    Hot Beverage Warning

    Road Safety staff should check this warning sign is displayed at signs when conducting safety audits.

    The use of plastic lids on hot beverages is not recommended due to the potential for burns to occur while the lid is being removed.

    When serving hot beverages it is advisable that volunteers do not hand the hot beverage to the visitor, but place the beverage on the counter for the visitor to pick up.

    Hot liquids must not be served to or received by persons under the age of 15 years. If in doubt as to the child's age, volunteers should check with the parents/carers.
No Smoking
No Smoking
    A total ban on smoking in Queensland Government buildings is in force. This policy applies to all employees of Queensland Government, as well as all other individuals visiting or conducting business. This includes clients, visitors and volunteers.

    The attached 'NO SMOKING' can be printed to be displayed in a prominent place at each Driver Reviver site.

    No Smoking Sign


    Road Safety staff should check this warning sign is displayed at sites when conducting safety audits.

    For current Queensland Health Tobacco Laws please visit the Queensland health web site:
    https://www.health.qld.gov.au/public-health/topics/atod/tobacco-laws
Emergency Contact List
Emergency Contact List
    In the event of an emergency the relevant services should be contacted. A current Emergency Contact List showing the phone numbers of the nearest fire station, ambulance station, hospital and police station must be displayed in a prominent position/s at the site. It is important that the information is kept up to date.

    Road Safety staff should check this warning sign is displayed at signs when conducting safety audits.
Security of Equipment
Security of Equipment
    It is the responsibility of the site co-ordinator and/or volunteers to ensure that all equipment provided for the site is secured to prevent theft.
Preparation and Sale of Food at Driver Reviver Sites
Preparation and Sale of Food at Driver Reviver Sites
    Department of Transport & Main Roads will indemnify anyone who is performing duties that fall within the guidelines of the Driver Reviver program. Any other functions (e.g. providing a sausage sizzle) not within the guidelines will receive no indemnification.

    Department of Transport & Main Roads stipulates that any food prepared at Driver Reviver sites should be in accordance with:

    The Food Act 2006 which relates to the preparation and sale of food, to make provisions for securing the wholesomeness and purity and fixing standards for food

    Food Regulations that relate to specifications for food stores with regard to the protection of food and appliances from contamination. This also includes personal hygiene, consignment and conveyance of food, hazardous food, packing materials, maintenance of food stores, food vehicles and appliances

    The regulations for the licensing and registration of food stores are the responsibility of the relevant local authority

    Any other relevant legislative requirements

    Volunteers should be made aware of their need to comply with the legislation and regulations that are pertinent to the preparation of food at Driver Reviver sites.
Product Freshness
Product Freshness
    The date on the biscuit packets is not a "use by" date, but a "best before" date. The only way of ensuring that a batch of biscuits has not gone stale is to try a sample from the batch. Site coordinators should use their own judgment about whether or not the biscuits taste stale.
