Alternate Compliance (ALTC)Alternative Compliance (ALTC) allows individuals or organisations accredited under TO(RUM) Act 1995 or the Heavy Vehicle National Law, with vehicles approved under the Maintenance Module (ATMT), to be exempt from the requirement of supplying a Certificate of Inspection (COI).


Note:The registered operator of a vehicle does not need to be the NHVAS scheme member. An NHVAS scheme member may accredit any heavy vehicle under their NHVAS accreditation. A heavy vehicle must only be accredited in one NHVAS scheme member's accreditation at one time.

A registered heavy vehicle that is accredited and operated under NHVAS maintenance accreditation is exempt an annual certificate of inspection (COI/Programmed Inspection (PRG)).

Check the vehicle is currently in the NHVAS maintenance module in TICA.

If the NHVAS maintenance module is not current in TICA, real time verification of heavy vehicles in NHVAS can be found at: NHVR Portal

If the NHVAS maintenance module is current in the NHVR portal AVM search, access the contact details for NHVAS to have it recorded on the vehicle.

Advise the customer that the vehicle will need a COI if it does not remain current in NHVAS maintenance module.

Transfer requirements
Transfer requirements
    If the registered operator of a vehicle accredited and operated under NHVAS is being transferred and the vehicle is NOT remaining under a NHVAS accredited operator, the disposer must do one of the following:
    • Provide the acquirer with a current COI/PRG for the vehicle before they sell the vehicle. Refer below for details on the type of COI required.
    • change the Purpose of Use to a type that does not need a COI/PRG and obtain a Safety Certificate (generally only light public passenger transport vehicles)
    • cancel the registration and dispose of the vehicle when it is unregistered.

    When the registered operator of a vehicle accredited and operated under NHVAS is being transferred and the vehicle is remaining under a NHVAS accredited operator, a COI is not required.
Type of COI Required
Type of COI Required
    If a current COI is required and the vehicle is a single axle truck with a single axle override the acquirer may obtain an AIS COI, in all other cases a TMR COI is required.

    The disposer of the vehicle must either:
    • obtain a COI/PRG for the vehicle before they sell the vehicle and must give a copy to the acquirer
    • change the Purpose of Use to a type that does not need a COI/PRG and obtain a Safety Certificate (generally only light public passenger transport vehicles)
    • cancel the registration and dispose of the vehicle when it is unregistered.

    For transfer requirements of vehicles on alternative compliance Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > transfer

The following are some of the examples of when the above process may be used:

1.New business transactions when vehicles have never been registered anywhere (Australia or a foreign country) and TICA is incorrectly asking for a COI.

This process to exempt the COI applies up to and including 12 months from the date of the new business transaction (does not apply when the vehicle has been disposed of and the registration is being transferred).

2.Updating the purpose of use for a vehicle from 'Commercial' or 'Private' to Limousine, Limousine Special Purpose or Taxi, provided the following criteria are met:
    a. The first registration was no more than 12 months ago; and
    b. The vehicle has not been / is not being transferred; and
    c. The registration has not been cancelled since it was first registered.
In this situation, the vehicle will require a COI 12 months after the date the vehicle was first registered.

3.Heavy vehicles registered with a Special Interest Vehicle concession are issued an inspectable vehicle registration notice but are exempt.

4.SES minibuses </=4.5t, 10 - 12 seats (i.e. troop carrier) with the purpose of use 'Emergency' are exempt a COI at new business, renewal and transfer.

New vehicles
The registered operator of a COI/PRG vehicle is exempt from having a COI/PRG for a new vehicle during the first 12 months (counting from date of first registration in Queensland).This includes vehicles that are supplying a Public Passenger Service and if a new vehicle’s POU is changed to a COI type POU within 12 months from the date of first registration.

This exemption only applies at first registration when the vehicle has not been previously registered anywhere in Australia or overseas. It does not apply if the vehicle:
  • registration is cancelled, or transferred to another person within this 12 month period, or
  • is registered in another state or territory then brought to Queensland inside the first 12 months.

The disposer of the vehicle must either:
  • obtain a COI/PRG for the vehicle before they sell the vehicle and must give a copy to the acquirer
  • change the Purpose of Use to a type that does not need a COI/PRG and obtain a Safety Certificate (generally only light public passenger transport vehicles)
  • cancel the registration and dispose of the vehicle when it is unregistered.

Some limited use is permitted prior to a new COI vehicle being registered, for example, travel to fit equipment/accessories, transportation to a dealer, or being test driven. However, if the vehicle does not appear to be new, based on factors such as age or odometer, it may not be eligible for a COI exemption. If it appears to be used, has excessive wear and tear, or is damaged, please contact Help Direct (who will refer to Vehicle Standards) for assistance in making a determination on whether the COI vehicle is eligible for a COI exemption as a new vehicle.

COI vehicles are exempt if they are a new vehicle during the first year of being registered. This includes vehicles that are supplying a Public Passenger Service.
Special Interest VehiclesA heavy vehicle that is registered under the Special Interest Vehicle Scheme (SIVS) is exempt from COI requirements.
Conditionally Registered VehiclesInspections are by self-assessment only.
Refer Vehicle Rego > 3 Types of Registration > Conditional registration
Tractor Based Special Purpose VehiclesVehicles registered as Tractor based mobile machinery and tractor based mobile cranes are exempt COI.
Disposal to a dealer The disposer of a COI vehicle does not need a COI to transfer the registration to a dealer.
After the vehicle is transferred to the POU of DEAL, the dealer must obtain a COI before:
  • offering the vehicle for sale
  • using the vehicle on the road (other than to get the vehicle repaired)
  • renewing the registration with a POU of DEAL.

If the dealer does not wish to obtain a COI, the dealer may de-register the vehicle.
