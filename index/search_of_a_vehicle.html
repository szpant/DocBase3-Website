Search of a Vehicle (5259)

Search of a Vehicle
What it isWhere a reasonable belief is formed that the vehicle is being, or has been used, to commit an offence under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act (TO(RUM) Act), Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act (TO(PT) Act) or Heavy Vehicle National (HVNL(Q)), or may contain evidence of an offence under TO(RUM) or HVNL Authorised Officers (AOs) may enter and search the vehicle or require reasonable assistance to enter the vehicle to conduct a search.
Powers to search a vehicle
Powers to search a vehicle
Powers to search a vehicle
    The TO(RUM) Act, TO(PT) Act and HVNL(Q), provides the power for authorised officers to enter vehicles. If the intention is to enter and search a vehicle the authorised officers must reasonably believe:
    • a vehicle is being, or has just been used, to commit an offence against a transport Act;
      or
    • a vehicle, or a thing in the vehicle, may provide evidence of an offence against a transport Act that is being, or has just been, committed;
      or
    • a vehicle in a place the officer has entered under s26 TO(RUM), or in a place the officer has, as an authorised officer under the HVNL(Q), entered under that Law, is used, or is being used, to transport dangerous goods;
      or
    • a heavy vehicle or a prescribed vehicle is being, or has just been, used to transport dangerous goods.
    Directions and Powers under HVNL
Power under TO(RUM) Act
Power under TO(RUM) Act
    Section 35(2) of the TO(RUM) Act permits an AO enforcing a transport Act (Note HVNL is not considered a transport Act), to:
    • enter the vehicle, using necessary and reasonable help and force;
      or
    • search any part of the vehicle;
      or
    • inspect, measure, test, photograph or film the vehicle or anything in the vehicle;
      or
    • take samples of the vehicle or anything in the vehicle;
      or
    • copy, or take an extract from, a document in the vehicle; e.g. download information contained on a disk, tape or other device in the vehicle;
      or
    • move the vehicle's load;
      or
    • take the persons, equipment and materials the officer reasonably requires into the vehicle.

    However the Authorised Officer (AO) may not exercise these powers in relation to the following things found in a vehicle:
    • a personal possession;
    • for a private vehicle - a document that is not issued, or required to be kept, under a transport Act or a corresponding law (refer section 35(3) of the TO(RUM) Act).

    Note:To enable the vehicle to be entered, the AO may stop the vehicle under section 31 (Power to stop private vehicles) or section 32 (Power to stop prescribed vehicles).

    An AO may seize anything in the vehicle if the officer reasonably believes the thing is evidence of an offence against a transport Act (refer section 40(4) of the TO(RUM) Act).

    An AO may not seize:
    • items that are a personal possession;
    • for a private vehicle - a document that is not issued, or required to be kept, under a transport Act or a corresponding law (refer section 35(3) of the TO(RUM) Act).
Powers under TO(PT) Act
Powers under TO(PT) Act
    Sections 123, 124, 125 and 126A-C of the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act provide for an Authorised officer to enter or board a vehicle and seize evidence.
Searching a vehicle
Searching a vehicle
Searching a vehicle
    Searching a vehicle can expose AOs to a variety of hazards including sharp objects such as hypodermic syringes and contaminated materials, protective gloves must be worn. Using Nitrile gloves is strongly recommended as they are resistant to many commonly used chemicals and are more resistant to puncturing.

    Searches must be conducted in the company of another AO to reduce the risk of allegations of misconduct and provide corroboration in subsequent proceedings, and in the presence of the vehicles driver.

    Before questioning the driver and entering the vehicle the AO must ensure their body worn camera is operating.

    Before entering the vehicle advise the driver that the AO has the power to enter, search and seize, as described above, and that the AO intends to exercise that power.

    The AO should then:
    1.Advise the driver the purpose of the search, that is what they are looking for (the item, for example a work diary);
    2.Ask the driver 'If I enter the vehicle, am I going to find the item'?
    3.If the driver answers 'no', warn the driver that it is an offence to provide a false or misleading statement to an AO, refer s52 TO(RUM) Act and s701 of HVNL). Give the driver another opportunity to produce the item.
    4.If the answer is still no, ask the driver 'Do you have any valuables such as money and wallet? If so please enter the vehicle and remove them before I enter the vehicle'.Ensure a response from the driver is captured on the BWC.
    5.The AO should position themselves so they see the activities of the driver when they enter the vehicle to remove their possessions, so they will see any attempt to destroy or otherwise interfere with evidence.
    6.After the driver leaves the vehicle ask 'Do you have all your valuables'?Ensure a response from the driver is captured on the BWC.
    7.The lead AO and partner AO should enter the vehicle at the same time, the lead AO from the driver's side, the partner AO from the passenger side.
    8.The role of the partner AO is to witness the search conducted by the lead AO.

    If an item is seized, the AO must give a receipt for it to the person from whom it was seized (refer to s42 of the TO(RUM) Act and s554 of the HVNL).

    The Seized Documents/Items Receipt must be recorded in QCIS. Refer: Queensland Compliance Information System (QCIS)

    Anything seized should be protected and not damaged.

    All details must be recorded in each officer's official note book, including:
    • the name of the driver;
    • the registration number or a description of the vehicle;
    • when and where the search took place;
    • the purpose of the search;
    • how long the vehicle was detained for the search;
    • a description of anything seized because of the search;
    • whether anything was damaged because of the search.

    If the AO observes any items considered unlawful such as a dangerous drug, a weapon the person may not lawfully possess, or stolen property, leave the item alone and do not mention them to the driver. Inform QPS as soon as practicable.
