    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records
    09/09/2020 - Inappropriate Access to Customer Records

      Please be aware that the confidentiality of customer records is paramount at all times. The TMR policy Access to Customer Records provides clear direction to all employees not to take action to access a customer record unless the access is authorised.
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
      Authorised access is accessing a record when:
        • There is a genuine “need to know” and the information is being used as part of the employee’s official duties and they would be hindered in their performance if they didn’t access the information;
        • The employee is entitled to access the information under law;
        • A customer has given permission for their login details to be used to access online services - this is for the customer contact centre/Help Direct/Online Support/Ebus/CAM only following completion of verbal EOI;
        • There is an official business transaction to be completed, including a transaction for a work colleague. The official business transaction must be done over the counter or via the call centre and the employee treated the same as any other customer (e.g. a ticket must be taken, full transaction requirements must be met). A SASM/Manager must also be informed to remove any perceived or potential conflict.

      Unauthorised access is:
      Unauthorised access is:
        • Accessing your own customer record;
        • Accessing customer records of friends or relatives (even if they have given permission) including online services if using a work computer;
        • Accessing a record where an actual, perceived or potential conflict of interest exists;
        • Accessing information because it would be convenient for a staff member to know;
        • Accessing a record on behalf of another TMR employee unless it is part of an official business transaction (e.g. contacting help direct or asking a colleague to access a record to assist with a transaction is acceptable);
        • Assisting a third party to access a customer’s record (e.g. mother calls and asks about an issue her family member is having with an online service and the employee assists the mother using the family member’s details).


      Please note: Customers contact the department in person, over the phone, via the internet or in writing. Transactions should be in line with your current role. If you are in doubt – ask your supervisor or manager.

      What can happen if I access a customer’s record inappropriately?

      Misuse of confidential information is a criminal offence under s408E Criminal Code. Unauthorised access and use of the information contained in this system may lead to criminal prosecution and/or disciplinary sanctions including:
      • Criminal prosecution;
      • Termination of employment;
      • Suspension from duties;
      • Reduction of classification level and a consequential change of duties;
      • Transfer or redeployment to other public service employment;
      • Forfeiture or deferment of a remuneration increment or increase;
      • Reduction of remuneration (increment) level;
      • A monetary penalty (which can be deducted from your pay);
      • A formal reprimand;
      • Record of disciplinary action - a permanent record of the incident on their file which they would need to disclose during pre-employment screening for all public sector roles.

      All staff are required to be familiar with the Access to Customer Records and Releasing Customer Information policies which are published in DocBase, available via the below links.

      Access to Customer Records policy summary v2
      Access to Customer Records Policy v5
      Releasing Customer Information

      Staff should also view the Access to Customer Records Policy page on SharePoint for more resources.

      For more information about access to customer records, please talk to your manager or supervisor.

    05/05/2021 - Stock Control for DAR books and National Driver Work Diaries - Updated

      DocBase has been updated to add Driver Assessment Record (DAR) books to the CSC Stock Register and included in the monthly stocktake. When a DAR book is taken out of stock and put into circulation, the book is to be actioned in the stock register as 'sold' for record keeping purposes. The information on who is responsible for the DAR book is to be recorded under customer as "Assigned to (Driving Examiner's name)". If a DAR book is to be assigned to multiple Driving Examiners, it is to be recorded as "Assigned to (CSC/QGAP)' with the Manager of the CSC/QGAP the responsible person.

      Additional information on damaged/lost National Driver Work Diaries has also been added. Staff are advised that although the cost to agencies is $0, they are included in CSB's yearly stocktake with a unit cost. Form M3819 must be completed when they are damaged or lost.

    02/06/2021 - National Class 3 Road Train Prime Mover Dimension and Mass Exemption Notice 2021
    02/06/2021 - National Class 3 Road Train Prime Mover Dimension and Mass Exemption Notice 2021

      The NHVR have released a new National Class 3 Road Train Prime Mover Dimension and Mass Exemption Notice 2021 which provides road train prime movers with mass and dimension exemptions when operating within eligible combinations in the Notice. The notice in the link has been provided in beta layout as a trial.

      The Notice provides length exemption to eligible combinations under the Notice and provides flexibility for operators of road train prime movers to continue their travel when decoupling from a road train combination and to access networks when not operating as a road train.

      The Notice provides 2 key access improvements for road train prime movers and eligible combinations:
      • Steer mass limits - Access for a road train prime mover to operate with a road train steer mass limit when operating in specific combinations
      • Longer combinations - Longer total combination lengths, including the 20m prime mover and semitrailer, 27m B-double, and 26m prime mover semitrailer and unladen converter dolly. The Notice's length exemption must be due to the road train prime mover length and is subject to conditions of operation and approved network conditions.
