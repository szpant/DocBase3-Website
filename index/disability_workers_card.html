Disability Workers Card (7572)

Project NameDisability Worker Screening
When01 February 2021
BackgroundThe Department of Seniors, Disability Services and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships (DSDSATSIP) is responsible for administering the disability worker screening system in Queensland which includes both NDIS worker screening checks and Queensland disability worker screening checks.

Worker screening checks are one of the strategies used to identify and minimise risk of harm to people with disability, receiving services and support.

DSDSATSIP has partnered with TMR to improve the disability worker screening system in Queensland, leveraging TMRs capabilities, specifically around streamlining the application process and strengthening the identity management system.
How will this process work?From 01 February 2021, the introduction of an online service on the DSDSATSIP website will allow customers to apply for and renew their Disability Worker Screening Card, using their TMR customer details.

The customers information will be validated against the information stored by TMR. The information that will be validated will include; Name, DOB, CRN, Image Set, Phone and Email address, and confirmation their record is not restricted (for example, NOAC).

Customers who successfully validate will progress through and lodge their application online with DSDSATSIP and will have no direct interaction with TMR. TMR will only be providing the identity, there is no requirement for TMR to accept applications and there is no process (other than image and signature capture) in TICA to undertake.

Like the current process for Blue Card, customers who are unable to complete validation of their identity will be able to attend a TMR CSC or third-party service delivery agent (QGAP/QPS) to have a CRN created and an image set captured. The ability to capture a stand-alone image set for the Disability Workers Card has been added to the capture image reason list in TICA.

Once an application is approved, DSDSATSIP will send a card production request to TICA and this request will be batched and sent with our standard card production requests. DSDSATSIP are responsible for assessing all applications and ordering cards for approved applicants.

Processes for the Disability Workers Card will mirror those for Blue Card, including the younger customer cohort from 10 years of age. Current policy and procedures will apply to how a record is created and managed.
Responsibilities of TMR staffDSDSATSIP are responsible for providing advice on the application for or issue, renewal, replacement or cancellation of a Disability Worker Screening Card. Customers may contact DSDSATSIP.

TMR operators are responsible for:
  • Answering enquiries related to a person's TMR record only
  • Capturing image and signature set, ensuring the 'Disability Workers Card' reason code is selected.
  • Ensuring customer eContact details (phone and email) are up to date and accurate.
    • This not only guarantees that the TMR record is correct but ensures that the customer's details will successfully validate through the DSDSATSIP online application service.
  • Providing new customers, the 'new TMR customer notification – Disability Worker Screening Card' by email or by printing. This notification will provide the customer:
    • DSDSATSIP Online Application Instructions;
    • Their TMR CRN, which they will require for the verification service;
    • Their contact details in the order in which they must enter them on the DSDSATSIP website; and
    • DCDSS contact information, for any enquiries.
  • Processing Remote Area Kits – Dalby CSC
Further informationTMR is responsible for providing advice and answering enquiries related to a person's TMR record only.

For enquiries relating to the Disability Worker Screening Card application process, please refer customers to the DSDSATSIP contact centre or website - https://workerscreening.communities.qld.gov.au/

As this initiative has not been announced publicly, details are not to be communicated externally to customers at this time.
