Types of Heavy Vehicles (2969)

ABB Quads
ABB Quads
    Registration Categories:
    • Prime Mover Road Train (PRRT)
    • Prime Mover B-triple Road Train (PRBT)

    An ABB-quad is a prime mover towing a single semitrailer and a set of B-triple trailers, connected by a converter dolly.

    A prime mover may be utilised in a ABB-quad if:
    Prime Mover Either:
    • S8 modification plate; or
    • Manufacturers Rating:
      • Road Train GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
    Semi-trailerTows a semitrailer, a dolly and then three B-double semitrailers
    Road Use/ LengthsTravels only type 2 road train routes
    Combination length >36.5m up to and including 53.5m
BAB Quads
BAB Quads
    Registration Categories:
    • Prime Mover Road Train (PRRT)
    • Prime Mover B-triple Road Train (PRBT)

    A BAB-quad is a prime mover towing two sets of B-double trailers, connected by a converter dolly.

    A prime mover may be utilised in a BAB-quad if:
    Prime Mover Either:
    • S8 modification plate; or
    • Manufacturers Rating:
      • Road Train GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
    Semi-trailerTows two B-double semitrailers, a dolly and then two B-double semitrailers
    Road Use/ LengthsTravels only type 2 road train routes
    Combination length >36.5m up to and including 53.5m
Prime Movers
Prime Movers
    Registration Categories:
    • Prime Mover (PMOV)

    A prime mover is a motor vehicle built to tow a semitrailer. A prime mover does not carry a load by itself.

    Note:When deciding the number of trailers a prime mover or truck may tow, s121(2) of the Vehicle Registration Regulation,provides that
    (a) a dolly and a semitrailer, when used together as in Figure 1, are taken to be one trailer; and
    (b) a low loader and low loader dolly, when used together as in Figure 2, are taken to be one trailer.

    As the Prime Mover is not carrying multiple trailers, it does not move into the multi-combination of prime mover, and its current registration category is sufficient.

Road Trains
Road Trains
    A road train is a combination (other than a B-double) consisting of a prime mover towing at least 2 trailers or a truck towing at least 1 trailer (counting as 1 trailer, a converter dolly supporting a semitrailer).

    All vehicles that make up the road train are registered individually. The road train registration category applies only to the towing unit.

    Types of Road Trains:
    Prime Mover Road Trains
    Prime Mover Road Trains
      Registration Categories:
      • Prime Mover Road Train (PRRT)
      • Prime Mover B-triple Road Train (PRBT)

      A prime mover may be utilised in a road train combination if:
      Prime Mover isEither:
      • S8 modification plate for a road train that is not a B-triple; or
      • S9 Modification plate for a B-triple; or
      • Manufacturers Rating:
        • GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
          • For a Road Train that is not a B-triple, the word ‘Road Train’
          • For a B-triple, the word ‘B-double’
      Road Train RoutesCombination uses either:
      Type 1
      • 2 or more trailers, with S11 rating (not compulsory for trailers used in road trains prior to 7/91)
      • Has a combination length >19m up to and including 36.5m

      Type 2
      • 3 or more trailers, with S11 rating (not compulsory for trailers used in road trains prior to 7/91)
      • Has a combination length >19m up to and including 53.5m
    Truck Road Train
    Truck Road Train
      Registration Categories:
      • Truck Road Train (TRRT)
      • Truck Tractor Medium (single trailer only) (TRTM)

      A truck (rigid body) may be utilised in a road train combination if:
      Truck isEither:
      • S8 modification plate; or
      • Manufacturers Rating:
        • Road Train GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
      Road Train RoutesCombination uses either:
      Type 1
      • 1 trailer with S11 rating (not compulsory for trailers used in road trains prior to 7/91).
      • Has a combination length >19m up to and including 31.5m

      Type 2
      • 2 trailers, with S11 rating (not compulsory for trailers used in road trains prior to 7/91).
      • Has a combination length >19m up to and including 47.5m
B-Double
B-Double
    Registration Categories:
    • Prime Mover B-double (PRBD)

    A B-double is a prime mover towing 2 semitrailers with the first semitrailer being attached directly to the prime mover, and the second semitrailer being mounted on the rear of the first semitrailer.

    All vehicles that make up the B-double are individually registered. The double registration category applies only to the prime mover.

    A prime mover may be registered as a B-double if:
    Prime MoverEither:
    • S9 modification plate; or
    • Manufacturers Rating:
      • B-double GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
    SemitrailersThe prime mover tows 2 semitrailers where:
    • 1st trailer is attached to a turntable (fifth wheel coupling) on the prime mover
    • 2nd trailer is attached to a turntable on the rear of the first trailer
    Road Use/ Lengths/
    GCM    		If access is to:
    • All roads (general access)
      • Combination length </= 19m
      • GCM </= 50t
    • Specified roads (restricted access)
      • Combination length </= 26m
      • GCM </=62.5t
    or
    Lesser of sum of the axles or Manufacturer's rating.
B-Triple
B-Triple
    Registration Categories:
    • Prime Mover B-triple (PRBT)

    A B-triple is a prime mover towing 3 semitrailers connected by "B" couplings (turntables), not dollies.

    A prime mover may be registered as a B-triple if:
    Prime MoverEither:
    • S9 modification plate; or
    • Manufacturers Rating:
      • B-double GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
    Semitrailers The prime mover tows 3 semitrailers:
    • 1st trailer is attached to a turntable on the prime mover
    • 2nd trailer is attached to a turntable on the rear of the 1st trailer
    • 3rd trailer is attached to a turntable on the rear of the 2nd trailer
    Road Use/ Lengths/ GCMTravel on specified roads only (under Multi-combination Guideline) with a combination length </= 36.5m
    GCM is determined by Manufacturer's plate where purpose built or S9 rating.
AB-Triple
AB-Triple
    Registration Category:
    • Prime Mover Road Train (PRRT)

    An AB-triple is a prime mover which tows a semitrailer, a dolly and two B-double semitrailers.

    A prime mover may be utilised in a road train combination if:
    Prime Mover Either:
    • S9 modification plate; or
    • Manufacturers Rating:
      • B-double GCM is stamped onto the compliance, manufacturers or vehicle plate
    SemitrailerTows a semitrailer, a dolly and then two B-double semitrailers
    Road Use/ LengthsTravels on either specified route:
    • Type 1 and Type 2
      • Combination length >19m up to and including 36.5m
    or
    • Type 2 only
      • Combination length >36.5m up to and including 44.0m

    Truck Tractor
    Truck Tractor
      Registration Categories:
      • Short Combination Truck Tractor (TRTT)
      • Medium Combination Truck Tractor (TRTM)

      A truck tractor is a body/rigid truck which may be used in combination with a pig, dog or tag trailer where the:
      • Combination length is </=19m (where registered as Medium Combination Truck Tractor (TRTM) for single trailer Road Train use, >19m but </= 31.5m)
      • Trailer has an ATM >4.5t

      A truck tractor is registered as:
      Short CombinationVehicle has both:
      • Less than 7 axles
      and
      • Operates with a combined total mass of </= 42.5t regardless of manufacturer's rating

      The GCM should be nominated by the registered operator where the manufacturers rating exceeds 42.5t.
      Medium CombinationVehicle has either:
      • 7 or more axles
      or
      • Operates with a combined total mass of >42.5t (Manufacturer's GCM must be >42.5t)

    Motorhome
    Motorhome
      Registration Category:
      • Special Vehicle Motorised Caravan (SVMC)

      A motorhome is a motor vehicle with a GVM > 4.5t and manufactured to include accommodation space which contains at least the following minimum requirements:
      • Table (may be easily removable)
      • Sleeping facilities
      • Cooking facilities
      • Storage
      • Fire extinguisher
      • Outward opening or sliding door opening to the left or rear of the vehicle that allows clear and unobstructed access to the living area.
      • The number of transport seats in the vehicle must be equal to or exceed the number of sleeping positions (eg sleeping positions for 4 people must have a minimum seating capacity of 4) Refer: Seating Requirements below, for more information.

      All of the above equipment must be fixed; however the table may be designed to be easily removable.

      Rules
      Rules
        • To be registered as a motorhome, the vehicle must be designed (as described above) for use as a motorhome. If the vehicle has a possible dual purpose, the vehicle must be registered as a truck with the higher class of insurance (eg class 7).
        • A vehicle cannot be registered as a motorhome if it has a designated load carrying area for vehicles/animals etc (not including a bike rack).
        • A motorhome/campervan with a GVM >4.5t requires a Motorhome/Caravan Inspection Sheet (F4550) or proof of previous registration from Queensland showing that the vehicle has been registered as a motorhome/campervan.

      CTP Rules
      CTP Rules
        MAIC describes class 2 insurance as being for a motorised home, but not if the part of the motor vehicle designed for residence is detachable from the part providing the motive power.

      Seating Requirments
      Seating Requirments
        The number of transport seats in the vehicle must be equal to or exceed the number of sleeping positions (eg sleeping positions for 4 people must have a minimum seating capacity of 4).

        Some vehicles may be fitted with additional seats that are used for fold down bedding but not for use as transport seating.

        In this situation a label must be clearly attached to the vehicle indicating which seats can be used as transport seats.

        Note:Not all seats in the vehicle may be able to be used as normal seats when the vehicle is in motion.

        See below for information that should be included on the label.

        "THIS MOTORHOME IS DESIGNED FOR THE CARRIAGE OF (x) PERSONS"
        {A seating plan showing all the provided seats, with an X shown on all seating positions that are NOT designated seating positions}

        WARNING: THE SEATING POSITION(S) SHOWN BY X ON THIS LABEL MUST NOT BE OCCUPIED WHILE THE VEHICLE IS IN MOTION

    Fire and Rescue
    Fire and Rescue
      Registration Category:
      • Fire and Rescue Vehicle (FRAV)

      Fire and Rescue Appliance Equipment applies to Qld Fire and Rescue Authority (QFRA) vehicles built or modified for the purpose of:
      • Fighting fires
      • Supporting or performing rescue operations

      Eligible vehicles include:
      • Mobile machinery or equipment (body shape MOBI)
      • Truck mounted mobile machinery (body shape TMMM)

      All other QFRA fire fighting equipment should be registered as mobile machinery.

      Non QFRA owned fire and rescue vehicles are registered according to the vehicle type eg. Fire Engines are truck mounted mobile machinery, except for special purpose built mobile machinery which is registered with a body shape: MOBI.
    Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)
    Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)
      A Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) is a vehicle with a GVM over 4.5t that has been built or permanently modified for a purpose other than for carrying goods or passengers.

      "Passengers" do not include the driver, a trainee driver or any person necessary for the normal operation of the vehicle.

      "Goods" include any item or substance such as any of the following:
      • Merchandise, wares, chattels (movable possession) and money;
      • Fluid, metal, stone, timber, and any other article (ie steel piping carried on the vehicle), substance, or material whatsoever;
      • Live or dead animals;
      • Containers, whether empty or not.

      "Goods" do not include any of the following:
      • Fuel, water, lubricants, tools and;
      • Any other equipment or accessories necessary for the normal operation of the vehicle (e.g. Water carried by emergency fire fighting vehicles).

      Any vehicle built for the primary purpose of carrying these items or substances is not a Special Purpose Vehicle.

      SPV Registration Classifications
      SPV Registration Classifications
        There are 3 classifications of SPVs for the purpose of registration:
        • Type O - is not a type P and has at least 1 axle or axle group loaded above the mass limits (examples could be a crane or a concrete pump truck). These would need to operate under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (NHVL) as an over size, over mass SPV. Refer: SPV Operating Requirements by classification
        • Type P - is really not used as we register vehicles that would normally fit under this classification under the conditional registration scheme. For example a grade.
        • Type T - is not a type P and that has no axle or axle group loaded above the mass limits. For example a truck mounted cherry picker.
      SPV Operating Requirements by Classification
      SPV Operating Requirements by Classification
        Type O SPV
        Type O SPV
          Must operate under a class 1 notice or permit issued under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (NHVL) and cannot tow a trailer unless specified in a permit or notice. Refer: SPV operating under NHVL'

        Type T SPV that does not exceed regulation dimensions
        Type T SPV that does not exceed regulation dimensions
          Can carry a load or tow a trailer with any goods provided the dimensions and mass limits comply with the Heavy Vehicle National Law. A “trailer” includes plant such as a generator.

        Type T SPV that exceeds regulation dimensions
        Type T SPV that exceeds regulation dimensions
          Must operate under a class 1 notice or permit issued under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (NHVL) and cannot tow a trailer unless specified in a permit or notice. Refer: SPV operating under the Heavy Vehicle National Law

        SPV operating under the NHVL
        SPV operating under the NHVL
          For the purpose of providing any NHVL exemptions, an SPV is a class 1 vehicle if it does not comply with a prescribed mass or dimension limit.

          The definition of an SPV under the the NHVL is a motor vehicle or trailer, other than an agriculture vehicle or a tow truck, built for a purpose other than carrying goods. Goods does not include fuel, water, lubricants and readily removable equipment required for the normal use of the vehicle or combination in which they are carried.

          For further information on the approvals available to access the road network, contact the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) call centre on 1300696487.
      Left Hand Drive Vehicles
      Left Hand Drive Vehicles
        If vehicle is a special purpose vehicle and is left hand drive, applicant will need to obtain a Left Hand Drive Vehicle Permit. Information about a Left Hand Drive Vehicle Permit may be obtained by writing to Vehicle Standards.
      Mandatory Intelligent Access Program (IAP) Monitoring for Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)
      Mandatory Intelligent Access Program (IAP) Monitoring for Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)
        From 1 December 2016, TMR will implement mandatory IAP enrolment for Class 1, 2 and 3 SPVs.

        These class categories may include vehicles such as heavy mobile cranes, concrete pumps and drilling rigs.

        A six month transition period for IAP enrolment will end on 1 June 2017.

        After this time, mandatory IAP monitoring will apply to the following SPVs operating within Queensland:
        Class 1 - SPVs which comply with TMR’s Vehicle Limits Manual (VLM) Single Trip permit requirements, that is, a vehicle with a total mass between 40t and 70t and complying with a Gross Mass Limit (GML) formula of 3L+15+/- G for all combinations of axle groups. (See over page for GML formula calculations).
        Class 2 - SPVs not complying with 3L+15+/- G that have been assessed and approved by TMR’s Bridge Construction, Maintenance and Asset Management (BCMAM) unit for operation under 48t SPV restrictions.
        These restrictions are based on the structural loading effects of a "standard" 4 axle all terrain crane operating at 12 tonnes per axle.
        Class 3 - SPVs not complying with 3L+15+/- G that have been assessed by TMR’s BCMAM unit and determined to not be suitable for operation under 48t SPV restrictions.
        Refer: Transport Compliance > 01. Procedures > On Road > Intelligence Access Program
    Trailers
    Trailers
      Registration Categories:
      • Trailers with ATM >4.5t (HTRL)
      • Mobile equipment trailers with ATM >4.5t (STRL)
      • Caravan trailer with ATM >4.5t (HCVN)

      A trailer is a vehicle that is built to be towed, or is towed by a motor vehicle, but does not include a motor vehicle being towed.

      This registration category includes equipment trailers towed by mobile machinery.

      A Gooseneck trailer is to be treated as a semi-trailer for the purpose of measuring and meeting vehicle standards.

      Caravan/Camper Trailer (including pop-top camper trailer)
      Caravan/Camper Trailer (including pop-top camper trailer)
        A caravan is an enclosed trailer designed for people to live in (includes camper trailers).

        A caravan or camper trailer must:
        • be equipped with cooking facilities; and
        • living or sleeping accommodation.
        • must have only outward-opening or sliding doors (with at least one such door located on the left hand side or at the rear). Exempt requirement - trailer based fold out camper trailers (refer diagram in attachment below).
        • must be fitted with a fire extinguisher as designed primarily for human occupation whilst stationary.

      Fire Extinguishers, Smoke Detectors and Fire Blankets
      Fire Extinguishers, Smoke Detectors and Fire Blankets
        It is not a requirement to check the safety or validity of the fire extinguisher, just confirm it is provided.

        Fire authorities strongly recommend that caravans or camper trailers equipped with cooking facilities, or living or sleeping accommodation be fitted with approved fire blankets and smoke detectors for the protection of occupants

      Lead/Middle Trailers
        Lead/Middle trailers attract different fees from Semi trailers. A lead/middle trailer is identified as a semi trailer with a turntable mounted over the axle group at the rear of the trailer.

        To ensure that all trailers are registered correctly and correct fees charged, refer to the Trailer Diagrams. Refer to: Heavy Vehicles > Sample Graphics > Lead/Middle Trailer Diagrams

        Note:From 1 September 2010, operators of lead and middle trailers used exclusively in road train combinations, except B-triples, can nominate to have lead/middle trailers charged at the semi-trailer rate.

        https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/business-industry/Heavy-vehicles/National-heavy-vehicle-charges

      Trailer Brakes
      Trailer Brakes
        Trailers with an ATM >4.5t must have:
        • Air, vacuum or electric brakes operated from driver's compartment, operating on all wheels
        and
        • A breakaway system which will automatically engage brakes in the event of the trailer becoming detached.

    Amphibious Vehicles/Vessels
    Amphibious Vehicles/Vessels
      There are some tour vehicles such as Aquaducks/Superducks that are ADR compliant.

      Where these amphibious vehicles are being operated in Queensland waters, they need to be registered both as a vehicle and as a vessel.

      Contact your Regional MSQ Office to obtain appropriate vessel registration advice (for example, either as a recreational or a commercial ship).
