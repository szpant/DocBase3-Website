Static Intercepts (7382)

Static Intercepts
Site RequirementsAny site selected for the interception of a vehicle or any other area designated as a ‘site’ by the Regional Manager (Compliance) or Senior Inspector is to meet the following requirements:
  • Large enough to accommodate the desired vehicle safely, which includes the safety of the Authorised Officers and other road users.
  • There should be clear visibility for at least 100 metres in both directions and inspectors should ensure that the intercepted vehicle is not likely to cause traffic congestion or hazards.
  • The area should be hard standing, reasonably level and of sufficient width to allow a vehicle to be clear of the carriageway.
  • The vehicle/s should not block access to driveways and/or business premises.
  • Weigh sites will be categorised as per the instructions in Interception Sites (7490)

Interception Sites
Signage
Signs
Signs
    If the interception site has speed signs installed to reduce the speed limit while the site is operational, the speed limit must be reduced, and the following details recorded:
    • Time
    • Date
    • Name and number of the officer changing the signs.

    Note:The details may be recorded on the Site Safety Plan.

    Powers of Authorised Officers to reduce speed limits at Intercept Sites

    If the interception site has electronic signs installed to direct vehicles into the interception site, the signs must be operated in accordance with the SOP for the site and any relevant register completed.
Traffic Management
Traffic Management
Traffic Management
    • Road traffic volumes will vary depending on location and time of day and must be taken into account when intercepting vehicles.
    • Vehicle traffic in the site must be managed to ensure the safety of all persons on site including Authorised Officers and drivers.
    • Do not allow traffic entering the interception site to bank up and cause a safety risk to other road users.
    • If an Intercepting Officer is positioned at the entry to the interception site they must maintain vision or communicate via radio with the site controller and only direct vehicles into the site when safe to do so.
    • If electronic signs are in use, close the site until traffic in the site has been processed and reopen the site when it is safe to do so.
    • If necessary vehicles may be directed to bypass the weighbridge to clear traffic more quickly.
Conducting a Static interceptA minimum of two (2) Authorised Officers are required to conduct static intercepts, unless the Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for the interception site state otherwise.

The SOP for the intercept site, if applicable, must be followed.

Conducting a Static Intercept
Conducting a Static Intercept
    Preparing an Intercept Site
    Preparing an Intercept Site
      • Conduct a site inspection to ensure the site is safe, check for damage to any signs or infrastructure and ensure equipment is operational. Report the details of any issues including photos if necessary to your STI.
      • Complete the Site Safety Plan captured in DMS.
      Site Safety Plans

      • Where applicable, all persons including visitors attending the site must complete a site safety induction and the site safety induction attendance form captured in DMS.
      • Park enforcement vehicles in the area designated in the Site Safety Plan.
      • 'Witches hats' should be used to delineate the edge of the carriageway from the interception site where applicable.
      • Reflective safety vests and relevant PPE must be worn.
      • a highprofile vehicle may be positioned at the entry to the interception site with the intercepting officer.
      • check any weighing equipment for calibration date, correct operation and display zeros.
      • Where applicable check operation of electronic equipment:
        • WIM
        • ANPR
        • Signs
        • Lights
        • CCTV
        • BWC
    Intercepting a Vehicle
    Intercepting a Vehicle
      Do not attempt to intercept a vehicle unless it is safe to do so. If it is not safe, allow the vehicle or vehicles to continue.

      Dangerous Good Vehicles
      Dangerous Good Vehicles
        Vehicles displaying ‘explosives’ signage are not to be intercepted in built up areas, under power lines or in heavy traffic areas.

        Radio transmitters and mobile phones should be immediately turned off when a Dangerous Goods vehicle is intercepted.
      Interception Sites on Both Sides of Road
      Interception Sites on Both Sides of Road
        If there is an interception on each side of the road, both sides can be used provided each site meets interception site requirements.

        Vehicles travelling in both directions can only be stopped on single lane two way roads (a lane consists of bitumen or a foundation between 3.5m and 3.7m wide)

        Authorised Officers must only cross the road when:
          a. the traffic conditions allow safe passage, and
          b. there is clear visibility for at least 300 metres.
    • The intercepting officer must be in a safe position on the edge of the carriageway. The intercepting officer should not attempt to physically direct a vehicle from the centre or median lane of a dual carriageway.
    • A single vehicle or a number of vehicles may be intercepted depending on the site and or number of inspectors on site. It may be safer to direct a number of vehicles into the interception site rather than a single vehicle travelling in a group.
    • When a vehicle or group of vehicles has been identified for interception, if applicable activate the flashing magenta lights to give the driver sufficient warning to slow down and enter the interception site safely. The flashing magenta lights should remain on until the intercepted vehicle has left the roadway and entered the site.
    • The official ‘STOP’ sign or signalling as prescribed under section 138 and schedule 4 of the Transport Operations (Road Use management – Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015 must be used when intercepting vehicles.
    • If the ‘STOP’ sign is used it should be raised in one hand and be clearly visible to oncoming traffic, the other hand should be used to direct the driver of the vehicle into the interception site.
    • Discontinue displaying the ‘STOP’ sign when the driver of the desired vehicle/s indicates acknowledgement of your direction so other drivers are not confused.
    • If the area where the vehicle stops is not considered safe, direct the driver to move the vehicle to a safe place. A safe place means a place where the vehicle is not likely to be a hazard to road users.
    • The intercepting officer may use signs to direct drivers to use the left lane, enter to exit the site.
    • The intercepting officer must record the start and finish time, for the period they are the intercepting officer, in their official note book, as they may be required to supply a statement for any enforcement action taken by Authorised Officers on site.

    Managing an Intercepted Vehicle
    Managing an Intercepted Vehicle
      • When there are only 2 TIs at a static site, once a vehicle has entered the site should accompany the lead inspector.
      • The lead inspector should verbally or with hand signals, direct the driver to stop.
      • The lead inspector should take up a safe position at the driver’s door ensuring they are outside of the opening arc of the door. Do not climb up onto the side of a heavy vehicle to talk to the driver.
      • The lead inspector should instruct the driver to stay in the vehicle while the relevant compliance checks are carried out.
      • The lead inspector should give the driver clear instructions on requirements while conducting compliance checks, for example:
      • Put the vehicle in Neutral/Park and apply the Park/Maxi brake.
      • Move the vehicle onto the scales
      • If the driver is required to exit the vehicle move the driver to a safe place. A safe place is a place where they are not likely to be injured by road users.
      • No vehicle should be allowed to move unless all staff are accounted for.

Roles and Responsibilities
Lead Inspector
Lead Inspector
    The lead Inspector should:
    • As the vehicle approaches activate their Body Worn Camera (BWC) and state:
    • the time,date and location
    • your partner's details
    • a description of the vehicle as you approach it.

    For example, "17:30 hrs Monday 6th February 2020 in accompany with Transport Inspector Peter Smith ID Q910, white bonneted Mack prime mover registration number ABC123, B Double combination fridge vans".
    • Watch the intercepted vehicles driver to observe driver behaviour.
    • Get to the intercepted vehicle promptly.
    • Take up a position at the driver’s door but outside of the opening arc of the door.
    • Provide the introduction:
    • Produce your ID Card and identify yourself as a Transport Inspector:
    • state for the record.why they have intercepted/stopped, and .
    • verbally give the privacy collection notice.

    For example, Good morning/evening/ afternoon. "My name is John Smith, I am a Transport Inspector with Transport and Main Roads, here is my ID (SHOW ID), you have been stopped today for a compliance check/safety check. This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes."

    • Instruct the driver to stay in the vehicle while you carry out the relevant compliance checks are being carried out.
    • Give the driver clear instructions on requirements while conducting compliance checks, for example:
    • Put the vehicle in Neutral/Park and apply the Park/Maxi brake.
    • Move the vehicle onto the scales
    • If the driver is required to exit the vehicle move the driver to a safe place. A safe place is one where they are not likely to be injured by other road users.
Accompanying Inspector
Accompanying Inspector
    The accompanying Inspector should:
    • Provide a running commentary on the BWC where necessary.
    • Provide assistance to the Lead Inspector where necessary.
    • Be alert and watch for any possible dangers
    • Stand single file at the driver’s door and observe driver and passenger behaviours
    • Take up a position to corroborate evidence.

Fail to Stop (Static Intercept)Do not under any circumstances step onto the roadway in front of the vehicle or put yourself or other road users at risk.

When a person fails to comply with a direction to stop during a static intercept:
  • Record all visible details of the vehicle and the driver
  • Submit a QCIS sighting report with the details and location, record "Fail To Stop" in the comments
  • Advise the site controller;
  • QPS may be contacted for any information relating to the vehicle and follow up action taken may be taken
Enforcement Action for Motorhomes
Enforcement Action for Motorhomes
    Fixed Signs
    Fixed Signs
      If a driver of a motor home does not obey a fixed sign to enter a weigh bridge or interception site, for example: “Trucks must use left lane next 5 klms” or “Trucks must enter if directed,” Transport Inspectors should not take enforcement action for failing to enter the site.
    Directed by a Transport Inspector
    Directed by a Transport Inspector
      If a Transport Inspector specifically directs a driver of a motor home to stop or enter an interception site using a hand signal, a hand held stop sign or the compliance vehicle flashing lights and or siren, and the driver fails to obey the direction, then a the Transport Inspector may take enforcement action regarding for not following an Authorised Officer’s direction.
