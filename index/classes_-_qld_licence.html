Foreign Licence without Qld Licence
Foreign Licence without Qld Licence
    If the holder of a foreign licence wishes to apply for a Qld licence of a higher class, without first obtaining a Qld licence for the equivalent class, they may only do so if:
    • they provide proof that they are authorised under their foreign licence to learn to drive the higher vehicle class.

    Do not assume that other countries have a graduated licensing system in place similar to Queensland. Some countries may require a learner permit or completion of extra training or testing before they allow the licence holder to learn to drive a higher class of vehicle.

    An applicant will be driving unlicensed if they are not authorised to drive a higher class of vehicle on their foreign licence. This makes them ineligible to undertake a PDT in a higher vehicle class in Qld using their foreign licence.

    If they cannot provide proof, the applicant must first obtain a class C licence in Qld if they wish to obtain a higher class. Refer Classes - Qld Licence > Learning to Drive a Higher Class or Same Class > Non Queensland Licence Holder

    and
    • They meet the experience requirement, refer Classes - Qld Licence > Driving Experience Required to Progress to Higher Class
Downgrading to Lower Class
Downgrading to a Lower Class
Downgrading to a Lower Class
    Voluntary (other than medical reasons)
    A Qld driver licence holder may voluntarily downgrade to a lower class than currently held. A new licence may be issued free of charge showing the lower class/es. A Driver Licence Application/Renewal form F3000 must be completed, if application is received at a non-formless centre, by the licence holder.

    Medical Reasons
    The Chief Executive may decide to amend a licence class on a Qld driver licence as a result of advice received that the holder has a medical condition that may affect their ability to drive safely.

    Refer: 4. Medical Condition Reporting > Show Cause - Medical > Immediate Amendment
