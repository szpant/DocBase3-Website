Driver Licence Definitions (2933)

Driver licence definitions
LicenceDefinition
Australian driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Australian driver licence means:
    a.A Queensland driver licence; or
    b.A corresponding document to a Queensland driver licence issued under a corresponding law to the provision of this Act under which a Queensland driver licence is issued.
    (e.g. A licence issued in another Australian State, Territory or External Territory (Inc. Cocos/Keeling, Norfolk & Christmas Islands)).
Damaged
in relation to a driver licence		Definition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    Damaged, in relation to a driver licence:
    a.means—
    i.The licence is damaged to an extent that—
    a.Any information on the licence is impossible or difficult to read without the use of technology; or
    b.A digital photo or digitised signature on the licence is impossible or difficult to recognise without the use of technology; or
    ii.Any information stored electronically on the licence is no longer accessible by using the holder's PIN; and
    b.Includes destroyed.
Defence force driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    Defence force licence means a defence driving licence granted by the Australian Defence Force.
Driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Driver licence means:
    a.An Australian driver licence; or
    b.A foreign driver licence.
Foreign driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Foreign driver licence means a licence to drive a motor vehicle issued under the law of another country. (Including New Zealand)
Interstate driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Interstate licence means:
    a.An Australian driver licence that is not a Queensland driver licence; or
    b.A driver licence granted in an external Territory that corresponds to a Queensland driver licence.
Learner licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Learner licence means a licence to drive a motor vehicle, while receiving driver training, issued under this Act.

For the purposes of sections 14, 15, 16, 37, 82 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    Learner licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a learner licence.
LicenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Licence means a licence, permit or certificate under a transport Act, and includes:
    a.A renewal of the licence, permit or certificate; and
    b.An endorsement on the licence, permit or certificate.

    For the purposes of section 17 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 licence means:
    a.A provisional, probationary or open licence; or
    b.A driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a licence mentioned in paragraph (a).
Licence holderFor the purposes of section 126 TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    Licence holder means a person who is the holder of a Queensland driver licence.
Non Qld driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Non-Queensland driver licence means:
    a.An interstate licence; or
    b.A foreign driver licence.
O Type/Open
O Type licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    O type licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to an open licence.
Open licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Open licence means a licence to drive a motor vehicle issued under this Act that is not:
    a.A learner, probationary, provisional or restricted licence; or
    b.A current driver licence receipt for a learner, probationary, provisional or restricted licence.
P Type
P Type licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A P provisional licence, P probationary licence or P restricted licence.

For the purposes of sections 11, 13, 14, 15, 16, 28, 82 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    P type licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a P type licence.
P1 Type licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A P1 provisional licence, P1 probationary licence or P1 restricted licence.

For the purposes of sections 11, 13, 14, 15, 16, 28, 82 of TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010:
    P1 type licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a P1 type licence.
P2 Type licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A P2 provisional licence, P2 probationary licence or P2 restricted licence.

For the purposes of sections 11, 13, 14, 15, 16, 28, 82 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    P2 type licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a P2 type licence.
Probationary
Probationary licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Probationary licence means a licence to drive a motor vehicle issued under this Act that is first issued after the person has, under an order made by an Australian court, served a period of disqualification from holding or obtaining a licence.

For the purposes of sections 13 & 14 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    Provisional or probationary licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a provisional or probationary licence.
P Probationary licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A probationary licence other than a P1 probationary licence or P2 probationary licence.
P1 Probationary licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A probationary licence that has the code P1 stated on the licence.
P2 Probationary licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A probationary licence that has the code P2 stated on the licence.
Provisional
Provisional licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Provisional licence means a licence to drive a motor vehicle issued under this Act that is subject to conditions, including restrictions, imposed because of the holder’s age or limited driving experience.

For the purposes of sections 13 & 14 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    Provisional or probationary licence includes a driver licence granted outside Queensland that corresponds to a provisional or probationary licence.
P Provisional licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A provisional licence other than a P1 provisional licence or P2 provisional licence.
P1 Provisional licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A provisional licence that has the code P1 stated on the licence.
P2 Provisional licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A provisional licence that has the code P2 stated on the licence.
Queensland driver licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Queensland driver licence means any of the following licences
    a.A learner, probationary, provisional, open or restricted licence issued under this Act;
    b.A current driver licence receipt issued under this Act for a learner, probationary, provisional, open or restricted licence.
Restricted
Restricted licenceDefinition from Schedule 4 of TO(RUM) Act 1995:
    Restricted licence means a licence to drive a motor vehicle, issued under this Act to give effect to a court order under section 87, that authorises the holder to drive only in stated circumstances directly connected with the person's means of earning a living.
P Restricted licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A restricted licence other than a P1 restricted licence or P2 restricted licence.
P1 Restricted licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A restricted licence that has the code P1 stated on the licence.
P2 Restricted licenceDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
    A restricted licence that has the code P2 stated on the licence.
Returning Driver
Returning DriverDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
A person is a returning driver if -
(a) the person held a valid provisional, probationary or open licence (the old licence) to drive a class of vehicle;
and
(b) the old licence stopped being valid more than 5 years before the person applies for a licence of the same class as the old licence.
Returning Driver CertificateDefinition from Schedule 9 of TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010:
A returning driver certificate means a certificate given to a returning driver, which states that they may learn to drive a class of vehicle stated in the certificate.
