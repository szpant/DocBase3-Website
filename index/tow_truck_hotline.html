Tow Truck Hotline (7336)


What it isIn response to rising public concern about the towing of private property parking areas, the Tow Truck Hotline (1800 681 636) was established in 2017.
Roles and ResponsibilitiesThe Call Centre within CSB Delivery is responsible for:
  • Managing telephone enquiries and complaints relating to the towing industry
  • Recording enquiries and complaints in the Tow Truck Hotline database held in Sharepoint

  • Complaints are system-referred by SharePoint, based on the complaint type, to the relevant area such as Compliance or the Tow Truck Unit. Transport Inspectors investigate complaints referred to them and take action where appropriate.

The Heavy Vehicle Analysis Unit is responsible for:
  • Uploading data generated from the Tow Truck Hotline into the Compliance Intelligence Database.

The Tow Truck Unit is responsible for:
  • Ascertaining what action needs to be taken for complaint type of 'Other'.
  • Maintaining a register of all complaints received and publishing a report on TMRs website every 6 months that details the types of complaints received and the outcome

    Note:This will be statistical information, no personal details will be published.

    Distance from where vehicle towed
    Distance from where vehicle towed
      The Independent investigation into the towing industry – Removal of vehicles from private property report found that the majority of vehicles were moved less than 10km. Regulating a maximum distance could inadvertently prevent a private property occupier with a genuine and substantial parking problem from removing offending vehicles, if there is no holding yard within the maximum towing distance.

      The investigation recommended that towing operators be required to take a vehicle removed from property by the most direct route to the nearest holding yard of the towing operator.

      The Independent investigation into the towing industry did not consider it appropriate to regulate signs on private property.

      However, it did recommend that signage guidelines be developed to promote visible, clear, comprehensive and consistent signs.

      The department has developed a signage guideline for private property owners and occupiers. The signage guideline includes sign size, positioning and illumination, content and design such as colours and text style to enhance readability.

      The signage guideline is available at Tow Truck Licensing Scheme

    Disagree with regulated fee
    Disagree with regulated fee
      Enter on Tow Truck Hotline New Call

      The regulated fee is for a standard tow from private property and includes up to one hour of working time at the private property, taking reasonable steps to locate the owner/driver and preparing the vehicle for towing. The fee also covers moving the vehicle to the holding yard, and 72 hours of vehicle storage at the holding yard.

      Fees for a non-standard tow from private property must be reasonable and if considered excessive may be investigated by the department.

      Given the nature of private property towing and understanding that a vehicle owner/driver may return before the vehicle has been removed from the property, an on-site release fee was set. The on-site release fee may be charged if the owner/driver returns before the tow truck leaves the property. The on-site release fee encourages towing operators to use discretion and reasonableness when charging the fee and to release a vehicle immediately when the fee is paid.

      If an owner/driver returns before the vehicle is secured on the tow truck, the vehicle must be released immediately without charge.

      Many fees must not be charged, such as, for responding to a request to tow a vehicle or to attend a property where a vehicle is located; travelling to a property where a vehicle is located; taking steps to find the vehicle owner; using a lifting or loading device; fuel; administrative work; taking and producing photographs; preparing and sending documents (for example, inventory of personal property); storing personal property; moving the vehicle within the holding yard and allowing the vehicle owner/agent to view or access the vehicle to take personal items from the vehicle during business hours.

      Felt unsafe, left stranded with no car
        There is no general right to park on private property. Motorists should not assume it’s ok to park somewhere just because the business premises are vacant, it’s outside business hours, they aren’t going to be very long, there are plenty of vacant spaces, they have parked there before, or other people are parked there.

        Motorists are responsible for determining if they are allowed to park somewhere and for complying with any terms and conditions of parking in that parking area.
        If motorists park somewhere where they are not allowed their car may be towed away. This can be stressful especially when it happens at night and even more so if they are alone.

    Complaints about being towed after a breakdown on a motorway, toll road or in a tunnel
    Complaints about being towed after a breakdown on a motorway, toll road or in a tunnel
      Enter on Tow Truck Hotline New Call

      This type of towing is not regulated. Breakdowns that occur on a motorway and toll road are usually removed rapidly to ensure they do not create a road safety or traffic management hazard.

      These tows are arranged by the department (for motorways) and the toll road operator (for toll roads). The department will contact the registered operator directly about collection of the vehicle.

      Vehicles towed by a toll road operator are towed far enough to ensure traffic flow is not inhibited. The owner/driver must then arrange a subsequent tow with a tow truck operator of their choice.

      The removal of these vehicles from the road network is authorised under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 section 51G.
