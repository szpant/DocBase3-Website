High Risk Work Licences (6059)

Project NameHigh Risk Work Licences
When16 May 2016
BackgroundThe Office of Industrial Relations Licensing and Advisory Services (LAS) team is responsible for the issue of High Risk Work Licences (HRWL) in Queensland. Currently only renewal transactions are able to be performed on-line, new applications are processed in person by Australia Post.

From the 16 May 2016, LAS will transition new applications to an on-line service and use the applicants TMR customer details, images and signatures and TMR’s card production facilities to produce a HRWL.

This is the first time TMR has allowed the use of image sets and customer data to produce an external product. It is extremely likely that the success of this project will create the opportunity for more interdepartmental partnerships of this nature. What this means is that the need to ensure that the data within TICA is complete and accurate is extremely important and that care must be taken when updating customer details.
How will the process work?Applicants will lodge their application through the LAS on-line Renewable and Photographic Licensing System (RAPS). To commence lodgement they will be required to enter in their TMR CRN, name and date of birth.

A TMR web service will validate the entered information against TICA and confirm that their customer details are correct, they have a valid image set (both image and signature) and their record is not restricted (for example, insufficient EOI or supressed).

Customers who successfully validate will progress through and lodge their application. The LAS team is responsible for assessing the HRWL application and ordering cards for approved applicants. A HRWL is issued for five years.

Once an application is approved the LAS team will send a card production request to TICA who will batch and send the request with our standard card production requests.

Customers who fail validation
There are several reasons why a HRWL applicant is not able to pass the validation which include:
  • They are not a TMR customer
  • Customer details are incorrect
  • Customer is supressed
  • Customer has a NOAC restriction
  • Customer has an image restriction
  • Customer has insufficient EOI
  • Customers TMR image expires within five years

Customers who fail validation will be referred to TMR to have their record corrected. Current policy and procedures apply to how we create, manage or fix their TMR record and every HRWL customer will have their image and signature captured.
Processing Impacts
Answering customer enquiries
Answering customer enquiries
    TMR operators are not responsible for providing advice on the application for or issue, renewal, replacement or cancellation of a HRWL. Customer’s must be advised to contact the LAS team for HRWL related information.

    The contact number for the LAS Team is 1300 631 948.

    We are responsible for answering enquiries related to a person’s TMR record only.
Maintaining TMR products
Maintaining TMR products
    When a customer fails validation LAS will contact the customer and advise them to attend a TMR location and to bring EOI. Privacy requirements limits the information we are able to provide the LAS team. While a customer is likely to identify as a HRWL customer if they have been referred to us there is an issue with their TMR record and you are responsible for ensuring that issues with the customer’s record and/or image set are resolved in line with current operational policy and procedures.

    Where the resolution of an issue will impact on a TMR product you must ensure all changes are appropriately actioned, for example if changing a customer name you must ensure that they meet EOI standards and all other licensing products are re-issued in the new name.
Remote Area Kits
Remote Area Kits
    The LAS team has modified our Remote Area Kits for their purposes and customers who are unable to attend a TMR location will be able to use this kit to have a CRN created, update customer details and have their image set imported.
Capturing an image set for a HRWL customer
Capturing an image set for a HRWL customer
    Currently, capturing an image is integrated into a transaction, for example during a licence renewal you are flowed to the image capture screen before the transaction is finalised.

    The ability to capture a standalone image set has been built into the Individual Customer Hub. It can be accessed by displaying the customer in the Individual hub then selecting ->Individual from the top navigation bar then Images -> Capture Image set.

    There is a reason code which must be selected, at this stage only HRWL is available. The actual image capture process and screens have not changed and images can be imported for remote and interstate customers via this new screen.

    A customer must have standard EOI recorded (excluding evidence of residency) and no image restrictions to be able to capture a HRWL image.

    An image set must be captured each time we interact with a HRWL applicant. Not only does this allow us to track how many HRWL customers we are dealing with, an image taken for the a HRWL product becomes a TMR image and can be used on our products. When a new CRN is issued you need to record the CRN on the HRWL Lodgement Advice for the customers future use.
Listing customer images
Listing customer images
    There is also the ability to list what images the customer has had taken in the past. This list provides the captured date and the image status. Images are valid for 10.5 years an image must be valid and not expire within 5 years to be able to be used on a HRWL. While signatures are not allocated a status they must also have at least 5 years remaining.
Lifting a suppression
Lifting a suppression
    The customer must appear in person to TMR and provide their consent to have the suppression temporarily lifted. The operator must contact the IMU to have this done. Once lifted the customer should be advised to contact LAS as soon as possible for advice on how to progress their application as the suppression will be reapplied after 48 hours.
HRWL Card Production
HRWL Card Production
    To request the production of a HRWL card, LAS will send to TICA the information to be printed on the card. TICA will then batch this information and send it to Placard for production. As such you will be able to see if the customer has had a HRWL issued via the Smartcard list on the Individual Customer Hub.
