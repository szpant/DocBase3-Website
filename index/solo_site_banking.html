Solo Site Banking (7410)

What it isThere are a number of sites across Queensland that deliver TMR services and are manned by one person (solo site).

Note: The cash limit to hold funds on premises overnight has been increased to $7000. Centres are expected to continue banking daily and funds are not to be held overnight unnecessarily.
Service Delivery Network SharePoint siteSolo sites are included on the Service Delivery Network SharePoint site that lists all Courts, QGAPs and QPS sites that deliver registration and licensing services.

http://corporate.qdot.qld.gov.au/sites/InCSB/Pages/TMR-Agencies.aspx
Compensating controlsSolo sites require special compensating controls to ensure segregation of duties, independence and sufficient management monitoring. Listed below are recommended compensating controls for solo sites to utilise to ensure an adequate level of quality assurance is maintained.
Banking ProceduresWhen a site commences or ceases operations outside of normal business hours, they are to advise Cash, Receipting, Banking & Reconciliation Unit at Cash and Banking

All sites that transport money via a security service must;
  • Secure funds in a CBA Quick Cash Envelope to deposit funds at a CBA; or where there is no CBA
  • Secure funds in a secure mechanism to deposit at an Australia Post agency

Solo sites and Remote sites that do not have a security service are to follow the guide for security in Solo Sites and Remote Areas.

Note: The operator EFTPOS total should not be counted or matched against the "List EFTPOS" TICA printout.

End of Day Banking Procedure
End of Day Banking Procedure
    At the end of business day the following procedure must be followed:
    • Site Officer follows end of day banking procedure
    • Any banking discrepancies must be finalised at this time
    • When banking is reconciled Close and Bank in TICA
    • Complete Bank Deposit Slip and seal in a CBA Quick Cash Envelope or other secure mechanism.
    • Send to bank or follow normal security practice for daily banking
    • This will set the Banking in TICA back to a NIL balance
    As end of day banking is prepared, banked and filed by the same person, a visiting manager/independent officer should complete the following compensating controls during regular (weekly/fortnightly/monthly) visits. In very remote areas, these checks should not exceed 3 months. The manager may also opt to have the solo site email the documents to them on a more frequent basis for checking.

    In each case the checking officer should:
    • Review the banking documentation (banking sheet and TICA banking summary) as well as bank/agent/Australia Post stamped deposit slip.
    • Ensure that the amount collected matches the amount deposited and make a notation “verified as deposited” on the banking sheet, together with their name, date and signature.

    Note:If the checking officer is checking multiple weeks of banking records at one time, they may elect to keep a register which summarises the date range checked. This register should also include the name and signature of the checking officer, as well as the date checked.

    Note:At this time all operators and the TICA collection screen should reflect a NIL balance.
Cash on premises
Cash on premises
    The amount of cash that can be kept on premises overnight is $7000, anything outside of this will need to be banked daily.

Skim
Skim
    The cash drawer total of any operator must never exceed $5000.

    By monitoring the operator's cash collections in TICA, the SASM/supervisor is able to identify when a skim is required.

    A skim may be conducted at any time to reduce the amount of cash held by an operator in their cash drawer.

    The skim should:
    • be counted and reconciled in a secure location by both the operator and an independent person; and
    • be recorded on the operator's daily banking sheet at the time the skim was conducted; and
    • secured immediately once money has been reconciled.

Problems
Problems with the Banking
Problems with the Banking
    Operator Overs/Unders
    Operator Overs/Unders
      On the rare occassion that a solo site receives cash and there is a discrepancy of more than $5, the following applies:
      • Bank the monies with the discrepancy in their banking
      • Send an email to Cash, Receipting, Banking & Reconciliation with comments e.g. "Banking deposit is $10 over compared to TICA. Unable to process adjusment due to solo site. Please process over against Ingelwood QGAP".
      • After cash and banking have completed this initial adjustment against the office, any further adjustments would be processed by another officer (e.g. rotating officer/visiting manager)
    Payment item entered with wrong amount
    Payment item entered with wrong amount
      When an incorrect amount is entered the banking officer will need to be told.

      To correct this an adjustment will need to be made to the receipt.

      TICA will automatically make an adjustment allocating the funds to the operators under or overs account.

      The banking officer will then need to adjust those amounts to the customers account.

    Payment Item with Incorrect Method of Payment
    Payment Item with Incorrect Method of Payment
      This might occur when:

      1.A physical payment item isn't listed on the list of payments in TRAILS or the Banking hub in TICA (ie item recorded as cash instead of cheque)
      2.The payment item has been listed incorrectly (eg as a cheque instead of money order)

      In these cases the operator will need to display the receipt and amend the payment method.
      3.A payment item is listed on the printout but they do not physically have the payment item.

      In this case the operator will need to physically locate the item. If not found the item will need to be put into suspense.
