Queensland Compliance Information System (QCIS) - Phase 2 (7454)

Project NameQueensland Compliance Information System (QCIS) - Enhancements
WhenMid March 2020
BackgroundThe Queensland Compliance Information System (QCIS) application was piloted in February 2018 and launched on Monday 4 June 2018. This app is used by Transport Inspectors in the field and helps to improve efficiency in many day to day tasks, including searching databases, creating customers and issuing infringement and warning notices.

During the development and pilot of the QCIS app, a number of possible enhancements were identified for future implementation.
Who will be impacted and how?Users of the QCIS app and portal may be impacted by the release of system enhancements, as it may change the labels or input options for some fields. Training material in DocBase will be updated to support the introduction of the enhancements.


Key enhancements to be released in March
Key enhancements to be released in March
    An intercept will be able to be edited or invalidated once the intercept has been submitted, this is only available in the portal.
    Any change (edit or invalidate) made to an intercept will be recorded in an audit history log. This will be accessible through the audit tab on the portal intercept record.
    • The following recorded information, will be able to be edited on a submitted intercept:
      • Change the Operation Name
      • Change the intercept's reported 'End Date' and 'End Time'
      • Add an additional person entity (one or more)
      • Add an additional vehicle entity (one or more)
      • Add additional photos (one or more)
      • Add additional summary notes
      • Add additional compliance action, with the exception of 'Seized Documents/Item Receipt' and 'Weighing Device notices'.
    • Invalidating an intercept
      • An intercept that has been invalidated will have a status of ‘invalid’ and can be searched via the app or portal using the 'Search for a Person or Vehicle' function. An invalidated intercept will not be able to be reversed.


Following the enhancement, a new version of the QCIS app and portal will be released. The app should update automatically, if not it can be downloaded from the TMR app catalogue.
Further informationInformation about the QCIS app and portal Refer: Transport Compliance > Systems > Queensland Compliance Information System (QCIS)

Further information about the enhancements will be provided as the development progresses.
