Class Conversions - Non Qld Licence (360)


Australian
national driver licence classification		Under the National Driver Licensing Scheme, licence classes and eligibility requirements are essentially uniform throughout Australian jurisdictions, with the exception of motorcycle licences. For information on licence classes in Australian jurisdictions and the Christmas/Cocos Keeling Islands prior to implementation of the NDLS, refer to Class Conversions prior to National Driver Licensing Scheme below.

Class Conversions prior to National Driver Licensing Scheme

Note:
NSW Q Condition
NSW Q Condition
    NSW puts a Condition Q on the licences of temporary overseas visitors who have not provided evidence of permanent resident status to Transport for NSW. Their licences are issued for 12 months only. A person with a Q condition licence (current or expired less than 5 years) is eligible to apply for a Qld licence of the same class and type.

ACT TN (Truncated Name) Condition
ACT TN (Truncated Name) Condition
    From 18 September 2013, ACT introduced the TN (Trucated Name) condition on a person’s driver licence to indicate their name has been shortened to fit on the licence.

    NEVDIS will record the full name of the customer, so other EOI documents that have the un-truncated name shown can be verified through NEVDIS.

    Advice from ACT outlines the application of the new TN condition as follows:
    • The TN condition is printed on the licence card if any part of the licensee’s name has been truncated in some way.
    • The TN condition is printed on the front of the licence alongside any other condition codes.
    • The TN condition is not recorded against the licence record within the ACT system (printed on licence card at the time of card production only).
    • Where there are more than 3 conditions on a licence the TN condition code may not be printed on the back of the card. Conditions on ACT licences are ordered sequentially by code and there is only room for 3 condition descriptions.
    • No record of the truncated name on the licence card is recorded on NEVDIS.
    • NEVDIS will record the full name of the client where NEVDIS data fields allow (there is a difference between the field sizes in the ACT system and NEVDIS).

Interim NT licence
Interim NT licence
    An interim NT Licence will display the class C(I) or R(I) and are issued when a:
    • person’s licence has lapsed more than 5 years and they require a licence to drive for training or re-testing purposes; OR
    • person is applying to transfer from a non-recognised country.
    An interim NT licence will also have an "X" condition displayed on the front of the licence. They must undertake a practical driving test before being issued with a Queensland licence.
    Note: NT licences may also have an I condition which means they require an Alcohol Interlock.
Australian motorcycle class conversions
Australian Capital Territory
Australian Capital Territory
    The ACT issue a class R motorcycle licence, they do not have the equivalent of a Qld RE licence. Class R licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles for the first year. The minimum age for an unrestricted class R in the ACT is 18 years.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence depends on the initial endorsement date for the class:
    > 1yr = Qld 'R' < 1yr = Qld 'RE'.
New South Wales
New South Wales
    NSW issue a class R P1 rider licence, which must be held for at least 12 months before being eligible to apply for a P2 class R licence. A P2 licence must be held for at least 2 years before applying for a full rider licence. Both NSW P1 and P2 class R licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles. The minimum age for an unrestricted class R in NSW is 20 years.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence:
    P1 R = Qld RE; P2 R licence = Qld RE; open R = Qld R.
South Australia
South Australia
    SA issue a R-DATE class licence which is equivalent to a Qld RE licence. An R-DATE licence must be held for at least one year before applying for a unrestricted R class licence. R-DATE licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles for the first year. The minimum age for an unrestricted class R in SA is 18 years.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence depends on the initial endorsement date for the R-DATE class:
    > 1yr = Qld 'R' < 1yr = Qld 'RE'.
Northern Territory
Northern Territory
    The NT issue a R(r) (restricted) class licence. Class R(r) licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles. The (r) restriction is automatically removed from the driver licence record after 12 months have elapsed. This may not be reflected on the driver licence. The minimum age for an unrestricted class R in the NT is 18 years and 6 months.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence depends on the initial endorsement date for the R(r) class:
    > 1yr = Qld 'R' < 1yr = Qld 'RE'.

    NT class R(m) should not be converted to a Queensland driver licence. This class allows the licence holder to ride a moped only. In Queensland, the licence holder would be required to hold a class C or class RE driver licence to ride a moped.
Tasmania
Tasmania
    TAS issue class R P1 and P2 licence. A class R P1 licence must be held for at least 12 months before being eligible to progress to a P2 licence. Only P1 licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles. A P2 licence holder can ride class R motorcycles but the minimum age for an open type unrestricted class R in Tasmania is 20 years.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence:
    P1 R = Qld 'RE'
    P2 R or open R = Qld 'R'
Victoria
Victoria
    VIC issue a class R-E licence which must be held for three years. Class R-E licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles. The (E) restriction is automatically removed after three years. This may not be reflected on the driver licence. The minimum age for an unrestricted class R in Victoria is 21 years.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence depends on initial endorsement date of the class R-E > 3 yrs = Qld 'R' < 3 yrs = Qld 'RE'

    Note:A Victoria issued Learner Permit (Permit Type - R) is equivalent to a Queensland class RE Learner licence.
Western Australia
Western Australia
    WA has an R-E class licence which is equivalent to a Qld RE licence. Class R-E licence holders are restricted to riding LAMS motorcycles. An R-E licence must be held for at least one year before applying for a WA unrestricted class R licence and further practical testing is required. The minimum age for an class R licence in WA is 18 years.

    Conversion to a Qld motorcycle licence:
    R = Qld R; R-E = Qld RE

    WA class R-N should not be converted to a Queensland driver licence as it allows the licence holder to ride a moped only. In Queensland, the licence holder would be required to hold a class C or class RE driver licence to ride a moped.

    Note:NEVDIS will show a WA class R/N as a Queensland class RE. This is an error and care must be taken that this class is not transferred to a Queensland class RE licence even though it appears on NEVDIS as this.
Christmas Island and Cocos Keeling Islands
Christmas Island and Cocos Keeling Islands
    These islands have the same licence classes as Western Australia - see above.
Norfolk Island
Norfolk Island
    Motorcycle licence holders are not trained or tested, therefore classes RE and R will not be recognised for conversion.

New ZealandNew Zealand introduced photo licences on 3 May 1999.

The colour coded licences record endorsements and classes on the front and conditions/restrictions on the back.

New Zealand licences show any conditions/restrictions in full text on the licence under the heading “Conditions”. The licence classes/endorsements that the conditions/restrictions apply to will appear on the licence under the heading “C/E”.

Where no licence classes/endorsements appear under the heading “C/E”, the conditions/restrictions will apply to all of the classes/endorsements shown on the licence.

Note:A person is not eligible for a P2 type licence unless they are at least 18 years and a person is not eligible for an open licence unless they are at least 20 years.

ForeignThe holder of a foreign Provisional or Open licence type may apply for the same class of Qld licence as held on the foreign licence.

However, do not assume that an equivalent class C foreign licence authorises the holder to learn to drive a higher class of vehicle in their country of origin. Some countries may require a learner permit or completion of extra training or testing before they allow the licence holder to learn to drive a higher class of vehicle. An applicant will be driving unlicensed if they are not authorised to drive a higher class of vehicle on their foreign licence. This makes them ineligible to undertake a PDT in a higher vehicle class in Qld using their foreign licence.

In these instances, the applicant must first obtain a class C licence in Qld if they wish to obtain a higher class. It is the applicant’s responsibility to prove that their licence authorises them to learn to drive a higher class of vehicle if they do not wish to apply for the car licence first


If the class of vehicle the person is authorised to drive or learn to drive under the foreign licence does not appear on the foreign licence, and a decision cannot be made based on the centre's standard resources/normal procedures, then it is the responsibility of the applicant to provide further details from the licence issuing authority. This also applies to determining whether foreign motorcycle licence holders should be issued with a class RE or class R licence.

Note:A person is not eligible for a P2 type licence unless they are at least 18 years and a person is not eligible for an open licence unless they are at least 20 years.

For more information, please Refer: Driver Licence > 1. Qld Driver Licence > When Required & Licence Type Eligibility > Qld Licence Type Eligibility by Licence Held

Class MC licence requirements
Class MC licence requirements
    A practical driving test is not required when applying for a Qld class MC licence. Requirements for overseas licence holders wishing to obtain a Qld MC licence differ according to their country of origin.

    Note:Very few countries have a licence class equivalent to an MC licence. To be equivalent to a Qld MC licence, an overseas licence needs to authorise the holder to drive a vehicle towing multiple trailers. If a country does not have an MC-equivalent licence then the customer must transfer to a Qld class equivalent to their overseas class before being eligible to undertake any training or driving experience towards obtaining an MC licence.

    Note:
    		The UK and India do not have an MC-equivalent licence class.
    For more information about class MC training requirements, please Refer: 5. Testing > Practical Driving Tests (inc Q-Ride, MC, UD) > Taking the Test > Test Fee > Forfeit of Fee
Indian Heavy Vehicle Classes
Indian Heavy Vehicle Classes
    India does not have an MC-equivalent licence class. Its heavy vehicle licences classes, HGV HPV and HTV, authorise a person to drive a motor vehicle with a GVM that exceeds 12t. The medium classes, MGV and MPV, authorise a GVM over 7.5t and up to 12t. The light class, LMV, authorises a GVM up to 7.5t.
