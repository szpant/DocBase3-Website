Driver Licence Release of Information (678)


Release of Licence/Traffic History/Personal Information
What it isInformation from the following records can be requested and released:
  • Traffic history
  • Driver licence history - includes address or full name history (including aliases)
  • Driver Licence details

Information on how a customer can apply for their Traffic History online is available at https://www.qld.gov.au/transport/licensing/history/traffic/index.html

Traffic History
Traffic History
    The printed traffic history provides the:
    • person's full name, gender, date of birth and residential address
    • Licence number (CRN)

    Where applicable it will also show:
    • offence details - including offence date, description, demerit points, result date, results and monetary penalty
    • information about licence sanction notices issued
    • periods of licence suspension, cancellation and/or disqualification
Driver Licence History
Driver Licence History
    A driver licence history will show the:
    • person's name, date of birth and residential address
    • licence number (CRN)
    • type of licence
    • class of licence
    • date licence was effective
    • date licence expires

    If a customer requires written confirmation that they have never held a Queensland driver licence, they will need to apply for a licence history.
Driver Licence Details
Driver Licence Details
    Driver licence details will show the:
    • person's name, date of birth and residential address
    • licence number (CRN)
    • type of licence
    • class of licence
    • date licence was effective
    • date licence expires
    • location where the current licence was issued
    • text restriction recorded against the licence (if applicable)

Note:A release of information does not include the driver licence holder's digital photo and digitised signature.

Request must be made by a personApplications for release of a person's licence or traffic history information can only be made by a person who:
  • is requesting their own information
  • has been authorised by the licence holder to obtain their information
  • has a proven legal authority to obtain the information
How to apply
Verbal request for information by licence holder
Verbal request for information by licence holder
    Staff may verbally advise a person of information about their licence or traffic history ONLY if satisfied that the person enquiring is the person to whom the information relates (ie their own information).

    The customer must either present standard EOI documents or answer a series of questions to satisfy staff of their identity. Refer: Customer > 4. Evidence of Identity > Standard Evidence of Identity > Step 2 - Check Standard EOI Requirements

    The following can be checked in TICA and provided verbally to customers:
    • total accumulated demerit points
    • details of any un-paid demerit point infringements

    Refer: Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > The How To: Enforcement > Enforcement Procedures (Licensing) > Check Demerit Points

    A licence card number* may be provided to a customer who verbally requests the information. Where a customer requests this information in person or over the phone, standard evidence of identity requirements must be met. The card number can be viewed on the List Smartcard screen in TICA.

    *The licence card number is not the customer's CRN. The card number is located at the bottom of the front of the card or the bottom right corner of the back of the card. Please refer to the images below.



      *Note: A person requesting complex information (ie extensive traffic history) should be encouraged to apply for a written/online release of information.

    Online Request for information
    Online Request for information
      Customers can access certain licensing information using the Demerit Points Online Service, including, the current status of their licence, the classes and types of licence held, basic details of infringements incurred (that contribute to the total number of demerit points accrued) and the total number of demerit points accumulated.

      They may also apply for their complete traffic history (a fee applies) using the Traffic History Online Service.
      Note:A person may only use the online services to access, or apply for their own licence or traffic history information. For more information about these services, please Refer: Online Services > 1. Online Services > TMR Online Services Guide > TMR Online Services List > Licensing.

      Check driver licence status
      Check driver licence status
        A person can check if a Queensland driver licence is current using the online service.

        This service is intended for use by the licence holder, or persons who have been provided the driver licence as evidence of the licence holder's authority to drive. The service only checks the status of a driver licence. It will not check the driver licence class or type, or any conditions imposed on the licence.

        The information provided does not provide legal proof of authority to drive as the status of the driver licence may change at any time.

        Note:Third parties may only check the status of another person's licence using the online service, or by lodging a written request for information (fees apply).

    Representing
    When representing a licence holder
    When representing a licence holder

      If a licence holder is unable to present the documents themselves they can have another person, legal entity or organisation conduct the business on their behalf.
      A representative of the entity or organisation is required to transact the business.

      When applying for information about another person's licence or traffic record, the licence holder's written authorisation is required. All other transaction requirements must be met.

      The Release of Information Application (F2121) provides an area for the licence holder’s written authorisation.
      • Persons with proven legal authority, or appointed as an executor of a Will for a deceased customer, do not require the licence holder's written authorisation.
      • In all other cases the licence holder must be contacted to verify the release of the information unless the applicant holds a power of attorney for the licence holder.
      Refer: Transaction Requirements Checklist below for full requirements.
    Representing deceased customer
    Representing deceased customer

      A deceased person's licence or traffic history information can only be released to a person appointed as executor of the Will of the deceased person. The licence holder’s written authorisation on the Release of Information Application F2121 is not required in this case.
      Administrators and other representatives must apply in writing to the Right to Information and Privacy Unit

      Note:A Grant of Probate is not mandatory. However, it must be identified if a Grant of Probate application is in progress as no business can be conducted until the Grant of Probate is finalised. If a Grant of Probate has been applied for and finalised, it must be sighted in addition to the Will to confirm the executors.

      Refer: Transaction Requirements Checklist below for full requirements.

      Will
      Will

        Executors must be confirmed by sighting the Will, and Grant of Probate if applicable. If TMR is advised that a Will is in dispute (e.g. due to the deceased marrying after the Will was made), business must not be conducted.

        Under no circumstances should a Will be tampered with (e.g. removing staples).
      Proof of Death
      Proof of Death

        To update a customer as deceased Refer: Customer > 3. Change of Customer Detail > Change Customer Details Guide > Updating a CRN as deceased
      Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or legal guardian
      Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or legal guardian

        A person who has a Power of Attorney, Enduring Power of Attorney or is the legal guardian for an executor or administrator of an estate cannot sign on behalf of the executor or administrator.

        For example:         Person A is named as the executor in a Will and Person B has a Power
        of Attorney / Enduring Power of Attorney for Person A.

        Person B cannot sign documents that Person A would have to sign in their capacity as an executor or administrator.

      Signature requirements
      Signature requirements

        When dealing with a deceased estate it is important that TMR ensures that the correct person/s that is representing the deceased signs the Release of Information Application (F2121).

        Where a Will only nominates one executor, this person must sign the Release of Information Application (F2121) (including the Executors Statutory Declaration) as a representative of the estate.

        Where a Will nominates more than one executor, the following applies:
        • Each executor must complete an Executor Statutory Declaration. The statutory Declaration may also be signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a Justice of the Peace. If the declarant is located interstate, the Declaration should be amended as appropriate.
        • Only one executor needs to sign the Release of Information Application (F2121)
        >Executor Statutory Declaration Traffic History
      Public Trustee
      Public Trustee

        When the Public Trustee is the executor and they require a deceased person's licence or traffic history, they must provide a Certificate of Authority. The Certificate of Authority must be on official letterhead and contain the name and address of the deceased.

        Generally, the Public Trustee will send a fax/email to Central Operations & Support - Customer Services Support (Paid Searches) who will record the customer as deceased and release the deceased person's licence or traffic history information. However, if a representative of the public trustee presents at the CSC, the transaction can be processed at the CSC.

        The public trustee does not need to complete an Executor Statutory Declaration.

        Where the Public Trustee is the executor, the deceased's next of kin or other representatives cannot apply for a deceased person's licence or traffic history.

    T.R.CTransaction Requirements Checklist
    Release Copy of Documents (eg. Driver Licence Application Forms)
    What it isUnder driver licensing legislation, individuals are entitled to copies of documents that were given at the time of an application for a Qld driver licence.

    They are entitled to copies of documents:
    • That they have provided to TMR and
    • Which contain their personal information.

    This applies to all application forms and accompanying documents (photocopied and scanned) eg. medical certificates or EOI documents.
    • Photocopied documents are requested from Iron Mountain.
    • Scanned documents are requested from IMU.

    Note:QPS officers may request copies of documents for enforcement purposes free of charge.
