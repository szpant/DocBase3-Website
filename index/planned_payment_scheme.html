Planned Payment Scheme (3002)

What is itThe PPS is a scheme which allows individuals to pay registration renewal fees by instalments at Australia Post (Queensland outlets only). The money is then electronically transferred to the customers Department of Transport and Main Roads account.

For further information regarding PPS payments Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Administration Money Guide > Australia Post Payments
Requirements
Joining the Scheme
Joining the Scheme
    Applicant must:
    • Complete a Vehicle/Queensland Regulated Ship Registration Planned Payment Scheme Application (F3885) form available on Corporate Forms or at Australia Post
    • Submit the form to Australia Post
    • Be the first registered operator of the vehicle
    • Provide information of a current Queensland Driver Licence or Adult Proof of Age/18+ card
    • Customers without a Customer Reference Number (CRN) must first obtain one from a TMR Customer Service Centre, then provide their CRN and appropriate evidence of identity to Australia Post
    • Accept the terms and conditions as presented


    A Queensland Vehicle/Queensland Regulated Ship Registration Payment Card (PPS card) is then issued by Australia post. Allow up to 4-6 weeks processing time to receive PPS card.

    Note:Organisations are not eligible to enter the Scheme
Making Payments by Instalments
Making Payments by Instalments
    • Payments can only be made at Australia Post
    • PPS card must be presented
    • Minimum payment of $40
    • Payments are paid into the card holders TMR customer account
Making Final Payment
Making Final Payment
    • Payment is made at either a TMR CSC's or Australia Post
    • Payment amount is the difference between the total of PPS payments made and the renewal amount
    • Renewal Notice must be presented when paying at Australia Post
    • Short payments of renewal will be rejected to a payments report and the payments will be re-processed
    • The fees for the registration period must be paid in full on or before the expiry date of the registration

    Note:Where the full amount for the registration period has not been paid by the expiry date of the registration, the customer may:
    • elect to vary the registration term, or
    • where the payments are being made for a 12 month registration term, elect to pay a lesser registration term (6 months), or
    • pay the outstanding amount (and any associated fees if required i.e. RRRF)
Withdrawing from the Scheme
Withdrawing from the Scheme
    • Application must be made is writing
    • A withdrawal fee will be charged

    Applications for the:
    • Withdrawal from the scheme
    • Exemption from withdrawal fee

    Can be mailed, emailed or faxed to the EBusiness Unit

    PPS cards are linked to the customer's CRN. PPS cards may become inactive if the customers account has a nil balance for 3 years or more, however the PPS card will still work when scanned. A PPS card is never cancelled.
Replacement Cards
Replacement Cards
    If a PPS card is lost, stolen or damaged, it can be replaced by completing a Vehicle/Queensland Regulated Ship Registration Planned Payment Scheme Application (F3885) form, available at Australia Post. Please allow up to 21 days processing time to receive a replacement card. There is no cost involved.
Rules
Refunds
Refunds
    Requests for refunds of planned payment scheme funds can be mailed, emailed or faxed to the EBusiness Unit

    A withdrawal fee will be charged if funds are not applied to a Department of Transport and Main Roads product.

    Only the person issued with the card may withdraw these funds.
Processing Final Payment
Processing Final Payment
    Instalments less than renewal amount
    Instalments less than renewal amount
      CSC
      • Payment amount is the difference between the PPS payments made and the renewal amount
      • Payment is part from the account and part other payment method
      Australia Post
      • Amount due is paid using the renewal notice for customer identification
      • Money is electronically transferred to the Department of Transport and Main Roads and allocated to the relevant account
      • Exception report is generated at TMR's Electronic Business Unit listing payments whose amount do not match the total payment request
      • Registration is paid by Electronic Business Unit

    Instalments = renewal amount
    Instalments = renewal amount
      CSC
      • Payment is processed from the account
      Australia Post
      • Renewal Notice is scanned and the amount paid is recorded as $0.00
      • Exception report is generated at TMR Electronic Business Unit
      • Payment is made from the customers account
Use of PPS monies for other than renewal payment
Use of PPS monies for other than renewal payment
    Customers can apply to use their PPS monies to pay for other TMR products in their own name.
    • The customer must be the PPS card holder (first registered operator)
    • The CSO can then apply the funds to other TMR products in the customer's name
    • If there are any surplus funds that the customer requests to be refunded, follow the Refunds section above.
Account Enquiries
Account Enquiries
    • Customers can be advised of the amount of payments received not the balance required
    • Payments can be viewed on the account statement screen and are shown as:
      • Planned Payment Scheme CR
    • Customers should be advised to retain Australia Post receipts as proof of payment
    • Account information is only available to the PPS cardholder
Customers with Australia Post Voucher Booklet
Customers with Australia Post Voucher Booklet
    Prior to PPS Australia Post offered a system where:
    • Customer were issued a voucher booklet
    • Part-payments of $30 to $50 could be made

    Australia post is advising voucher book holders they can either:
    • Redeem funds and withdraw from the scheme incurring no penalties
      or
    • Apply for PPS card

    If applying for a PPS card
    • Payment will be accepted using voucher
    • All monies previously paid using the voucher will be transferred to the PPS system
    • Voucher book will be destroyed
Contact information for enquiries
Contact information for enquiries
    The EBusiness Unit can assist with enquires about discrepancies.
