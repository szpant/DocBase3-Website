Production of Driver Licence (5255)

Production of Driver Licence
When a drivers licence can be required to be produced
Heavy Vehicle
Heavy Vehicle
    An Authorised Officer may require the driver of a heavy vehicle to produce their driver licence under HVNL (Q) Section 568(2).
Light Vehicle
Light Vehicle
    TO(RUM) Act section 49, If a driver has an open licence and is or has committed an offence against:
    • Transport Infrastructure Act 1994, section 46; or
    • Queensland Road Rules, section 100, 154(1) or 156(1) the driver must produce their licence within 2 business days at a place nominated by the Authorised Officer.

    An Authorised Officer may ask the driver of a light vehicle to produce their drivers licence however if they do not produce it no offence has been committed as there is no legal requirement. As an alternative the driver may be asked to provide some form of photographic identification.

    TO(RUM) Act Section 48 requires a person who is or has committed an offence to state their full name and address to an Authorised Officer. If you require a person to state their name and address you must warn them that it is an offence if they fail to do so. You may request they supply evidence of their name and address if you suspect the information is false.
Procedure
Procedure
Heavy Vehicle
Heavy Vehicle
    If the driver of a heavy vehicle fails to produce their drivers licence a PIN (offence code 3971) may be issued under HVNL(Q) 568(3)(a).
Light Vehicle
Light Vehicle
    Driver does not produce licence
    Driver does not produce licence
      If the driver of light vehicle fails to produce their drivers licence they have 2 business days to produce their drivers licence at a nominated Compliance office:
      • Caution the driver and record any conversation on DVR or body camera
      • Record all relevant information in your official note book
      • Question the driver to determine which Compliance Office the driver is able to attend within 2 business days to present their licence
      • Photograph the alleged offender
      • Send an email to the STI in charge of the nominated Compliance office advising that the alleged offender will be attending the office to present their drivers licence
      • If the driver fails to present their licence within 2 business days start investigation process.
    When drivers licence is presented at a Compliance office
    When drivers licence is presented at a Compliance office
      When a driver presents their drivers licence, staff at the nominated Compliance office are required to:
      • photocopy both side of the licence
      • request the driver certify the photocopy of the licence
      • scan and email the copies to the intercepting TI
Driver produces licence
Driver produces licence
    Incorrect class of licence
    Incorrect class of licence
    If the driver does not have a licence or does not have the correct class of licence:
    • the driver must be given a caution
    • any conversation must be recorded on DVR or body camera,
    • record all relevant information in the official note book
    • collect relevant evidence
    • consider contacting QPS for assistance depending on location,
    • inform the driver of the intention to provide a statement to QPS detailing the alleged offence/s for further investigation
    • a Prohibition Notice F3162 may be issued under TO(RUM) Act section 38 to prevent the driver from contravening the Act
    • inform the driver that if they continue to drive they are possibly committing further offences.
    Fraudulent licence
    Fraudulent licence
    If a Queensland drivers licence, which has been provided by the driver for identification purposes, appears to be obviously fraudulent, Transport Inspectors are to:
    • caution the driver
    • record any conversation on DVR or body camera
    • seize the licence and contact QPS immediately to take further action;
    • physically give the Queensland Driver's licence to QPS and record all details of this transaction in their notebook including the QPS officer's name, number, station and phone number
    • inform the driver of their intention to provide a statement to QPS detailing the alleged offence/s for further investigation
    • a Prohibition Notice F3162 may be issued under section 38 of TO(RUM) Act to prevent the driver from contravening the Act
    • inform the driver that if he/she continues to drive without a valid licence then they are possibly committing further offences against a transport act.
