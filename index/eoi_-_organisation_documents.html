Australian National Business Number - ANBN
Australian National Business Number - ANBN

    At least one registered operator must be a legal entity. For example, a Business Name is not a legal entity but can be recorded as the first registered operator, with the Proprietor/Holder being recorded as the second registered operator. The second customer is the legal entity, so responsible for the vehicle as the registered operator.

    Where stated, and rather than the customer having to provide documentation that is available online, the organisation details may be verified by an operator using the issuing authority’s website when the customer record is created.

    See Rules for a Legal Entity and Non-Legal Entities Registered Operators

    For more information on organisations Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > Customer Types > List of Organisation Types

    Evidence Required
    Evidence Required
      • Verify name and ANBN.
      • Verify status of the business is “Registered” on the ASIC website at www.asic.gov.au or "Active" on the ABR website at https://abr.business.gov.au, and
      • EOI for the legal entity (business holder/proprietor) behind the business name

      ANBN must have a status of “Registered” or "Active" for a vehicle to be registered or transferred into a business name.
      Interstate Business Name
      Interstate Business Name
        • Where the business name is registered by an Interstate Authority
        A business name registered by an Interstate Authority cannot purchase CPRO or MPRO products as a customer cannot be created.
        • Where the business name is registered by ASIC
        An interstate business registered with ASIC can be recorded as a TMR customer and can purchase CPRO and MPRO products (Refer: QT Shop). A vehicle can only be registered to an interstate business if it has a Queensland garage address. Proof must be provided. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 4. Registration > Garage Address > List of Garage Address Scenarios

        Note:Businesses are not a legal entity they still require the proprietor who is a legal entity to be recorded as the second registered operator.

        For further information Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > Customer Types > List of Organisation Types

    Evidence Required
    Evidence Required
      Verify name and that Partnership has a status of “Active” on the ABR website at https://abr.business.gov.au.

      If the partnership cannot be located on ABN lookup, an original/certified copy of the formal Deed of Partnership listing all involved parties and proves the validity of the Partnership is required.

      For further information on partnerships Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > Customer Types > List of Organisation Types
Trust
Trust
    Evidence Required
    Evidence Required

      To prove that an entity is a Trust:
      • Verify name and that the status of the trust is "Active" on the ABR website at https://abr.business.gov.au and provide one of the following;
        • Original or certified copy of the Deed of Trust plus EOI for the legal entity (trustee) behind the trust name, or
        • An original official document (such as a letter from a solicitor confirming the existence of the trust) plus EOI for the legal entity (trustee).
        Note:The legal entity must be the trustee of the trust and could be either an individual or any other legal entity such as a company.

Body Corporate

    Evidence Required
      To prove the entity is a Body Corporate one of the following must be provided:
      • Original Registration Confirmation Statement issued by the Department of Natural Resources and Mines (DNRM), or
      • Original Document from DNRM with Community Management Scheme (CMS) number, for example, a current title search, or;

Associations
Associations
    Incorporated Under Qld or Federal Legislation
    Incorporated Under Qld or Federal Legislation
      To prove the entity is an Incorporated Association, one of the following must be established:
      • Verify association is an Incorporated Association issued by the Office of Fair Trading (OFT) under the Associations Incorporations Act 1981by visiting the Queensland Government website at Check a Charity or Association

      Note: Incorporated Association must have a:
      • IIA number
      • ZZ number (ZZ numbers are no longer issued but organisations with Certificates of Incorporation displaying a ZZ number are still a legal entity)

      • Original Registration Certificate issued by OFT for a co-operative registered under the Cooperatives Act 1997
      • Verify an Indigenous Corporation number and name by visiting the Office of the Registrar of Indigenous Corporations website at http://www.oric.gov.au
      • Below is a historical list of Aboriginal Corporations that can also be used:
          List of Aboriginal Corporations
            This list is no longer updated.
            Contact the OFT if you require further information about historical Aboriginal Corporations.

      • Copy of Letters Patent, issued by the Department of Premier and Cabinet, as proof of registration under the Religious Educational and Charitable Institutions Act 1861 to 1967 (repealed) - Letters Patent are no longer issued but an organisation that holds a Letters Patent is still a legal entity.
        If a Non Profit Organisation cannot supply the required documentation, it is sufficient for them to be shown on the following historical list of Non Profit Organisations:
          List of ZZ Non-Profit Organisations
            This list is no longer updated.
            Contact the OFT if you require further information about ZZ Non-Profit Organisations.
    Incorporated Under Interstate Legislation
    Incorporated Under Interstate Legislation
      To prove the entity is an interstate Incorporated Association one of the following is required:
      • Verify the ACN, name of company and status of the Incorporated Association is "Registered" on the ASIC website at http://asic.gov.au
      • Original or certified copy of the 'Certificate of Incorporation' issued by an interstate Office of Fair Trading (or their equivalent)
Company (ACN)
Company (ACN)
    Australian Company
    Australian Company
      To prove the entity is a Company:
      • Verify the company ACN, name and has a status of "registered" on the Australian Securities and Investment Commission (ASIC) website at http://asic.gov.au
    Overseas Company
    Overseas Company
      The Australian Registered Body Number (ARBN) has a status of active on the ASIC website http://asic.gov.au, or original or certified copy of the Certificate of Registration of a Registerable Australian Body displaying the ARBN

    Note:A check must be conducted on ASIC to confirm the status/exact name and abbreviation (for example, Pty Ltd or limited). If the abbreviation/name is incorrect, correct the record in TICA using the exact name and abbreviation from the ASIC website.
Licensed Motor Dealer
Licensed Motor Dealer
    Queensland Licensed Motor Dealer - Used Motor Vehicles and Caravans ONLY
    Queensland Licensed Motor Dealer - Used Motor Vehicles and Caravans ONLY
      Identification required to prove that the entity is a Licensed Motor Dealer, is one of:
      • Current Queensland Motor Dealers Licence issued under the Motor Dealers and Chattel Auctioneers Act 2014
      • Current Corporation Licence issued by OFT under the Motor Dealers and Chattel Auctioneers Act 2014
      • Existing TMR customers may provide a Receipt of payment for the Dealers Licence issued by the OFT
    Note:Motor Dealer Managers licence is not acceptable

    Queensland Motor Dealer - Used Trailer, tractor,ship and farm machinery ONLY
    Queensland Motor Dealer - Used Trailer, tractor,ship and farm machinery ONLY
      Identification required to prove that the entity is a Licensed Motor Dealer, is one of:
      • Current Second hand dealer licence issued by OFT under the Second-hand Dealers and Pawnbrokers Act 2003.
      • Existing TMR customers may provide a Receipt of payment for the Dealers Licence issued by the OFT
    Interstate Motor Dealers
    Interstate Motor Dealers
      Identification required to prove that the entity is a Licensed Motor Dealer, is one of:
      • A current interstate Motor Dealers Licence issued by that states Regulating Body
      • Existing TMR customers may provide a Receipt of payment for the Dealers Licence issued by the OFT

Legal entities created by legislation (i.e. church)
    Documents required to prove an entity has been established as a LEGO:
    • Copy of the relevant section in the legislation establishing the entity; or
    • For a church organisation registered under legislation such as the Religious Education Charitable Institutions Act 1861 to 1967, a copy of their Letters Patent, issued by the Department of Premier and Cabinet, as proof of registration under that legislation.

    If a Non Profit Organisation cannot supply the required documentation, it is sufficient for them to be shown on the following historical list of Non Profit Organisations:List of ZZ Non-Profit Organisations

    This list is no longer updated

    Contact the OFT if you require further information about ZZ Non-Profit Organisations.
