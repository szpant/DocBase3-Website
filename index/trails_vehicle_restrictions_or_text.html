Vehicle Restrictions or Text (3120)


What Are They?Vehicle Restrictions or text are additional elements of a record created to alert other operators to special circumstances relating to a vehicle, customer or registration

For customer related restrictions Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System Information > Qualifications, Restrictions & Text > Customer Restrictions
Vehicle Restrictions
Vehicle Restrictions
    Types of restrictions
    Types of restrictions
      CDCH
      CDCH
        Dishonoured Cheque at Conversion

        This restriction:
        • results from a dishonoured cheque that has come across from ROLS on conversion
        • is used by Customer Account Management Unit (CAM) for disputed transactions
        • Is manually applied to the vehicleiIs removed by CAM when either payment made or vehicle cancelled.

      COBI
      COBI
        Missing Data for Registration

        This restriction is:
        • created when vehicle details such as a vehicle identifier or descriptor is missing
        • system generated
        • automatically removed by the system when missing details are supplied.

          Note: This restriction is referred to as COBI because each instance of a vehicle entered into the register is stored as a Controlled Object, with a code of COB.

      CONF
      CONF
        Number Plate Confiscation QPS
          This restriction:
          • is created when a vehicle has been issued with a Number Plate Confiscation Notice by the Queensland Police Service (QPS). The number plates are held by the QPS whilst the restriction is in place.
          • is applied against a vehicle (not customer - they can still drive other vehicles provided they have a valid driver licence)
          • QPS will be responsible for adding and removing this restriction
          • a vehicle cannot be transferred, cancelled or re-registered until the confiscation period is over.


            Note:If the CONF period has ended/been removed by QPS and the vehicle status is cancelled, Help Direct must remove the restriction.
            Only Help Direct can remove/invalidate the restriction.

          • Means that the vehicle cannot be moved for any reason (even if it has been sold). The owner/operator cannot get an unregistered vehicle permit to move it.
          • Prevents the following transactions being processed during the confiscation period:
            • New business
            • Replace standard plates
            • Attach/remove personalised/customised plates
            • Cancel
            • Transfer

          Customers can be referred to the QPS if they have any queries about a CONF restriction including:
          • Penalties
          • Applying for early release
          • Retrieval of plates
          • Removal of the sticker
          • Complaints about condition of plates following them being held in custody by QPS
          • Reporting a person driving a vehicle with a CONF

        CREG
        CREG
          Registration Cancelled Today

          This restriction:
          • is created when a registration is manually cancelled
          • prevents transactions affecting the registration eg update of pay term from being performed against a registration once cancelled
          • is removed if registration is reinstated or invalidated.

            Note: Vehicle details can be updated when restriction exists.

        DEFT
        DEFT
          Defect

          This restriction:
          • is created when a defect notice is added to a vehicle by a TMR Inspector or QPS Officer
          • prevents transactions being processed (excluding dealer to dealer transfers)
          • is manually removed when defect notice requirements are met.

        DCHQ
        DCHQ
          Payment not Honoured

          Restriction is:
          • created when a cheque is dishonoured and the receipt is voided by finance
          • manually removed after payment is honoured or vehicle cancelled

        NONC
        NONC
          Non Compliant

          This restriction:
          • is created when vehicles do not comply with the registration requirement eg weights and measurements
          • is removed when non-compliant data is corrected.

            Note: Some vehicles can exceed regulated dimensions and be registered

        NORA
        NORA
          No Renewal Allowed (NORA) restriction

          This restriction:
          • prevents the renewal of a registration online, in-person (excluding Australia Post payments), by BPAY or Direct Debit.
          • prevents the generation of a registration renewal notice/

          Customers may apply for a NORA to prevent the renewal of registration for a vehicle that has been sold.

          The NORA will be automatically removed when the registration is transferred or cancelled.

          To apply for a NORA restriction to be added to their vehicle registration the customer must complete a No Renewal Allowed (NORA) Restriction Request that details the circumstances and reason for their request. Standard evidence of identity requirements must be met.

          Note: Call centre operators can email the application to the customer. The customer must complete and sign the application and send it back by email for approval/processing.

          NORA/NOAC Application Form


          When a completed request for the addition or a NORA is received:
          • the CSC Manager, SA(SM), SCCC or SPECPRO Operator must assess the application and, if approved, sign the completed request, and then;
          • a SPECPRO Operator or SCCC will add/remove the restriction against the customer's account. The operator must record their user id, location and the date against the restriction so that the filing can be recalled.

          Note: For a vehicle registered in joint names and has been sold to a third party where the transfer has not occurred, only one registered operator is required to sign the NORA application.

          Once applied, the operator should confirm the application to the customer before they leave the CSC or end the phone call.

          Where insufficient information is provided or if there are doubts about the authenticity of the application, the CSC Manager/SA(SM)/SCCC/SPECPRO Operator may verbally refuse the application made by the customer.

          Note: Offline agencies and QPS licence issuing sites must contact their supporting CSC to commence this process.

          Refer:Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > The How To: Registration > Registration Procedures >Add NORA Restriction

          Scenarios where a NORA cannot be applied
          Scenarios where a NORA cannot be applied
            A NORA restriction cannot be applied to a vehicle if:
            • it is registered in joint names and one registered operator submits the application to stop the other registered operator from renewing the registration, regardless of the circumstances.

        RCAL
        RCAL
          Recall (RCAL) Vehicle Restriction

          This restriction:
          • prevents the vehicle from being transferred or registered (new business)
          • prevents a registration renewal being raised
          • prevents adding the vehicle to Direct Debit
          • removes the vehicle from Direct Debit, if already added
          • will show text Recall Vehicle Restriction
          • is removed once customer provides evidence from the vehicle manufacturer that defect has been rectified.

            Note: The above rules are only used for vehicles that have had the RCAL restriction applied. This restriction is currently only being applied to vehicles with a cancelled or auto cancelled status.

            Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Critical Compulsory Recalls > Current Critical Compulsory Recalls > Takata Airbag Recall > Advice for Customers

            Note: Unregistered vehicle with a RCAL cannot have an Unregistered Vehicle Permit issued.

          When TMR is advised of a group of vehicles that are subject to a compulsory recall because of a critical defect, a batch process will be run to apply the new RCAL restriction to each of these vehicles.

          Not all recalls are compulsory, with some vehicle suppliers issuing voluntary recalls (for example; ABS brake recall). Critical compulsory recalls are nationwide and issued following an investigation by the Australian Competition and Customer Commission (ACCC). The RCAL restriction will only be applied to vehicles listed as a part of a critical compulsory recall. Staff can view these current recalls, and further information about each, via the below link.

          Critical Compulsory Recalls

          The RCAL restriction will be added by batch process but can only be applied within certain timeframes; prior to the registration renewal notice being raised or after the registration has auto-cancelled.

          A courtesy notice will be issued to affected customers by email or post advising they are to provide evidence that the fault has been rectified. Once the customer provides this evidence, the restriction will need to be manually expired.

          Customers will have 14 days from the date of the courtesy notice to provide evidence the fault has been rectified. If more than 14 days has passed, TMR will issue a defect notice giving 28 days to rectify and then commence show cause action on the vehicle.

          If 28 days have passed after the issue of the show cause notice, a notice of intention to cancel will be sent. If evidence still has not been provided by the date on the decision notice, the registration will be cancelled.

          If a customer with an unregistered vehicle enquires on how to get their vehicle to a vehicle manufacturer to fix the defect due to the RCAL restriction, they are to be advised that the vehicle must be towed and cannot be driven.

          Note: If a customer requires a replacement of their courtesy notice, staff are to send an email request to: Policy Procedures Support team


          Manually Applying/Removing the RCAL Restriction
          Manually Applying/Removing the RCAL Restriction
            Only staff with a SPECPRO, CUSTPRO, IA CSC MANAGER (DAMANGR), SENIOR TRANSPORT INSPECTOR (STRPTINS), or DATAMAN profile will be able to manually apply/edit/remove the RCAL restriction.

            Applying the restriction
            Applying the restriction
              Prior to applying the RCAL restriction to a vehicle, staff are required to email PolicyProceduresSupport to confirm on the defect is on the critical compulsory recall list.

              The below text must be pasted into TICA when applying the RCAL restriction manually:

              "Vehicle Rego/VIN <NUMBER/VIN> cannot be renewed, transferred, new business conducted or have an Unregistered Vehicle Permit issued due to an unresolved serious manufacturer defect recall. Customer must contact manufacturer and have the defect rectified before registration business can be conducted".

              Instances where staff will be required to add the RCAL restriction manually may include:
              • if the registration is cancelled due to the show cause as a result of the customer failing to provide evidence the recall had been resolved and the restriction had not applied to the vehicle by batch process
              • once the registration has been renewed if the restriction was unable to be applied due to the renewal notice already being generating
              • once the registration is auto-cancelled and the restriction had not been applied.

            Removing the restriction
            Removing the restriction
              The RCAL restriction may only be expired once the customer provides evidence from the manufacturer, or approved repairer, that the fault has been rectified.

        REGI
        REGI
          Registration Inconsistent

          This restriction is:
          • Created when all fields don’t match the requirements of that particular registration (eg Primary producer concession must have FARM purpose of use and CTP Class 17 - registration can be processed with CTP Class 7)
          • Removed when registration is corrected

            Note: For clarification of action please check with Systems (Business Systems)

        STOL
        STOL
          Stolen Vehicle Restriction

          This restriction is:
          • Recorded against a vehicle/trailer/vessel that is reported stolen to the QPS
          • Is added by QPS
          • Removed by QPS and/or Help Direct when vehicle recovered

          For restriction removal process Refer: Vehicle Rego > 06. New Business > NEVDIS > NEVDIS Restrictions > Stolen

          Note: If a vehicle with a stolen restriction is detected by an RSCO camera, the infringement will still be issued to the registered operator. The registered operator is required to contact the RSCO who will investigate to confirm the stolen restriction and withdraw the notice.

          However, if an infringement notice is issued for speeds over 40km/h, QPS conduct further investigations; including a stolen vehicle check. If they confirm the vehicle is stolen, the infringement notice will not be issued.

        TEMP
        TEMP
          Temporarily Compliant

          This restriction is:
          • Used when a vehicle is being tested (eg a b-triple prior to being accepted as a registration category)
          • Removed when compliance type is altered

        TEXT
        TEXT
          Text

          In general customers cannot request a text restriction be inserted on their vehicle record. Text restrictions are for the information of operators only and customers should not be made aware of this function. A current text restriction will display each time the associated vehicle record is accessed.

          In approved circumstances, such as those outlined below, a text restriction may be placed against a vehicle record to provide information to other operators.

          Operators must ensure that they include their UserId, location and contact phone number when adding a text restriction.

          Note:A text restriction will automatically apply a default expiry date 12 months from the
          effective date. The expiry date should be manually changed to a nominated date (if applicable). Alternatively, it may be changed to 31/12/9999 until the matter can be resolved.

          SPER Text Restriction
          SPER Text Restriction
            The State Penalties Enforcement Registry (SPER) requires TMR's assistance to restrict enforcement debtors who have current enforcement action from dealing with a vehicle's registration as any action on a vehicle's registration may imped SPER's enforcement process. Requests from SPER to restrict vehicles will be managed by Help Direct within a service level agreement of 2 business days. The expiry date of the restriction will align with the Enforcement action expiry (12 months) unless SPER requests it be manually removed earlier (e.g. when the customers pays).

            SPER can only request a restriction if:
            • Vehicle is solely registered to the enforcement debtor; and
            • An Enforcement Warrant Imposition of a Charge over Property; Notice of Intention to Issue Immobilisation Warrant; or Vehicle Immobilisation Warrant or Enforcement Warrant Seizure and Sale of Property has been issued (SPER will advise if any of these actions are accompanied by a court order); and
            • Interest has been registered over the vehicle with the Personal Property Security Register (PPSR)


            When a SPER Text Restriction is in place, the following transactions are not allowed:
            • Transfer
            • Voluntary cancellation
            • New business

            SPER will be monitoring vehicles with these text restrictions applied. They will contact Help Direct if it is discovered that a transaction has been processed despite the restriction. Help Direct will investigate the transaction and determine if corrective action is required. If the transaction occurred:
            • In a CSC, Help Direct will refer the request for corrective action to the CSC where the error occurred and provide advice on what action/letter to use per the table below.
            • Online (transfer only) or was due to a delay in applying the text restriction, corrective action will be completed by Help Direct.

            Enquiries/disputes
            Enquiries/disputes
              • Customers can be informed that all vehicles subject to SPER text restrictions have had an interest registered with the Personal Property Security Register (PPSR). It is an acquirer's responsibility to ensure that the vehicle does not have any encumbrances.
              • All disputes/enquiries about the Enforcement Action/Restriction by SPER must be directed to SPER Intelligence
            Corrective Action
            Corrective Action
              To be completed when:
              • an operator error is discovered (text restriction has been overlooked)
              • a transfer has been completed online (text restriction does not prevent transfer online)
              • transaction occurred after the SPER/TMR Service Level Agreement, but before restriction was applied
              • Immediate reversals on new business can only occur if SPER has possession/clamped the vehicle, or the customer has been informed by phone.
              • TMR's reversal of a transaction does not have any impact on legal ownership of the vehicle because TMR's authority is limited to registering a vehicle to allow it to operator on the road and, recording the registered operator of the vehicle.

            Review of Decision
            Review of Decision
              If a customer does not agree with TMR's reversal of a transaction:
              • Following a Supreme Court decision, they should seek legal advice;
              • And no court order exists, they can apply for a review of decision to the Manager of the unit issuing the letter.

            Jointly Registered Vehicles - Dispute of Ownership
            Jointly Registered Vehicles - Dispute of Ownership
              A text restriction must be added to the vehicle in the situation where a jointly registered vehicle has been transferred from two registered operator names to one operator name using one signature and there is now a dispute of ownership. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Rules > Jointly Registered Vehicles - Dispute of Ownership
            State or Federal agencies
            State or Federal agencies
              State or Federal agencies may require a text restriction be inserted on a vehicle for the purpose of carrying out their business. Agencies must present court documents which adequately identifies the vehicle and provide a contact name and number. Staff are to direct any future enquiries to this contact.

              Sample Text
              Important message: This vehicle cannot be transferred to another party without written approval from insert title named in court documents from the insert agency name.The contact from this agency is insert contact officer's name on insert contact number.
              Operator ID and Centre

            Transfer documents not lodged
            Transfer documents not lodged
              If a customer has sold their vehicle and
              • the acquirer has failed to lodge the transfer form
              • the disposer does not have the proper documents to complete a Notice of Disposal Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Transferring the Vehicle > Disposers/Acquirers Responsibilities

              A text restriction can be placed against the vehicle advising operators not to renew the registration until a transfer application is lodged. The customer should also be advised of the process of obtaining a NOAC restriction so that the registration is unable to be renewed online. Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System Information > Qualifications, Restrictions & Text > Customer Restrictions > NOAC

          TIST
          TIST
            Transfer Interstate Restriction

            This restriction is:
            • Recorded against a vehicle/trailer that has been transferred interstate
            • Added by NEVDIS
            • Removed by the Vehicle Identification Unit
            For restriction removal process Refer: Vehicle Rego > 06. New Business > NEVDIS > NEVDIS Restrictions > Interstate Transfer > TIST (Transfer Interstate Restriction)

          TRFR
          TRFR
            Transfer Not Lodged

            This restriction is:

            Created when:
            • Transfer processed using the different seller action is used
            • Disposer lodges a Notice of Disposal

            Removed when:
            • Missing transfer is lodged
            • Acquirer lodges transfer and Notice of Disposal is invalidated

          WOVR
          WOVR
            Written of Vehicle Restriction

            This restriction:
            • Results from a vehicle being added to the written-off vehicle register (WOVR)
            • will show text Repairable Write Off or Statutory Write Off
            • Repairable write-offs will also have a PRI restriction added that when WOVI is added. PRI is not required, and the restriction is to be expired.

        Text Messaging
        Text Messaging
          A text message is created for a specific transaction or a general explanation.

          Required for the following transactions:
          • Invalidation of transaction
          • Cancellation of receipts
          • Account Adjustment

        Note: This list is not inclusive of all transactions requiring a text message.

          For information about vehicle text restrictions, Refer above to: Vehicle Restrictions > TEXT
