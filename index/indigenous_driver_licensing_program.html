Indigenous Driver Licensing Program (4611)

About the Indigenous Driver Licence Program (IDLP) and the Indigenous Driver Licensing Unit (IDLU)
What is itThe IDLP was established in conjunction with the Queensland Police Service (QPS) and TMR in 1998 as a means of delivering an alternative driver licence testing regime for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islanders (ATSI) through a 'hands-on' instructional process.

The IDLU was established to deliver learner licence testing, offline hazard perception testing, practical driving tests, licence renewals and upgrades, Photo ID cards and all other TMR driving licensing matters.

The IDLU travels to remote indigenous communities to deliver these services with IDLU staff members who are approved Driving Examiners and Principal Customer Service Officers.
Why has the IDLP & IDLU been introduced
Why was IDLP and IDLU developed
Why was IDLP and IDLU developed
    The program was developed as an effective means of increasing Indigenous participation in the driver licensing process. It operates successfully in remote indigenous communities.

    It is a pro-active method for reducing the number of ATSI people entering the prison system for licensing related matters and other offences.

    The program also operates in support of the Department of Corrective Services, Transitions Program and Youth Detention Centres. The program enables ATSI people to exit the correctional system with improved employment prospects by obtaining a learner licence prior to their release.
What does the IDLU program involve
The IDLU program includes:
The IDLU program includes:
    • The delivery of the road rules in a basic, logical, visual format designed specifically for people with literacy, numeracy and cultural challenges.
    • Conducting offline hazard perception tests and practical driving tests in remote communities.
    • Issuing learner licences to applicants on the day if they pass the required test.

Who is eligible to participate in the IDLU program
Who is eligible, who may be considered
Who is eligible, who may be considered
    The program was originally developed for people of ATSI descent. However, other community groups may be considered eligible for this program.

    Eligibility for other community groups or customers is determined by the IDLU Manager.

    Further enquiries may be directed internally to the Indigenous Driver Licensing Unit

IDLU testing officers
Who is approved to administer the test
Who is approved to administer the test
    Written road rules tests, offline hazard perception tests and practical driving tests are delivered by the IDLU team.

    Practical driving tests are conducted using community or applicant supplied vehicles that meet standard pre-drive inspections as per TMR Q-Safe requirements.

    Practical driving tests are conducted as per section 2.6 of Q-SAFE manual (Rural/remote area testing).

    The IDLU also deliver written road rules tests in Correctional Services facilities.

    For remote communities or indigenous groups not serviced by the IDLU, the QPS administer the written test using standard TMR procedures.
Waiving the learner logbook exemption application and associated feeDelegated officers within the IDLU may waive the requirements for a learner licence holder who resides within a remote indigenous community to apply for, and pay the fee associated with lodging an exemption from the learner logbook requirements.
Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility Criteria
    A person residing within a remote indigenous community who is not able to meet the logbook requirements due to either of the following:
    • A car or a supervisor is not reasonably available to them;
      or
    • The person lives in an area with a limited road network;
      and
    • There is no significant benefit for the person to satisfy the logbook requirements by driving a car on the road network;
      and
    • There is no reasonable likelihood of the person moving from the area or having an opportunity to drive a car in an area with a diverse road network.

TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s161B


If the requirement to apply for a learner logbook exemption is waived, the approval is valid indefinitely. The decision is to be recorded on TICA by lodging and approving a logbook exemption. It will be necessary to process an adjustment from the 440202000 account to the customer account to waive the application fee. Refer: DocBase Adjustments Book

The decision should also be recorded in the 'Comments by authorising person' section of the Driver Licence Application/Renewal (F3000) form. The applicant is to be verbally advised of the decision to exempt the logbook requirements by the IDLU.
Tenure Requirements if waiving Exemption granted
Tenure Requirements if waiving Exemption granted
    The person must hold their learner licence for a minimum cumulative period of 2 years within the 3 years prior to apply for a provisional driver licence.

    The 2 year period is overridden by presenting a completed learner logbook with 100 hours of driving experience including 10 hours of night driving to TMR. In this instance the original 1 year learner licence tenure period would still apply.
Non-IDLU application process
Non-IDLU application process
Approved testing officer
Approved testing officer
    TMR has one approved testing officer, Norman Clarke (Uncle Norm), who acts independently of the IDLU to deliver written tests to Aboriginal and Torres Strait peoples (ATSI) applicants.

    The testing officer:
    • Checks the applicant’s EOI
    • Delivers the training package
    • Issues the written test
    • Marks the written test
    • Completes and has applicant sign Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples (ATSI) Licensing Program (Form 4)Driver Licence Application/Renewal (F3000)
    • Gives the applicant an Information Sheet (Form 5)
    • Forwards the completed forms and passed written test to the nominated CSC or licence-issuing police station, together with a copy of the current letter of authority issued to him by TMR.
