Bus Frame Inspections (5356)



Bus Frame Inspections
What it isA structural inspection of a heavy bus is required under Schedule 1 of the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Standard 2010 to check the structural integrity of the bus to ensure passenger safety.

A ‘Bus Frame Inspection Certificate’ is to be completed and issued to the owner after a frame inspection, if the frame is considered to be in a satisfactory condition.
When required
When required
When required
  • When a heavy bus is 20 years old, but before it is 21 years old,
  • If an operator of a relevant service wants to introduce a heavy bus that is at least 21 years old into service in Queensland,
  • If a heavy bus has substantial rust that may affect the structural integrity of the bus.
  • Any bus where it is necessary to carry out structural repairs due to corrosion or accident damage, operators must have the repairs inspected by a prescribed inspection entity before painting the frame and refitting the body panels.
  • If an operator of a heavy bus is applying for a 5 year life extension and the bus has not passed a passed fame inspection in the previous 5 years or the body work shows signs of structural damage or corrosion.
Requesting a Bus Frame Inspection
Requesting a Bus Frame Inspection
    The operator or a repairer can make a request to the Senior Transport Inspector for a bus to be inspected.

    The operator must contact the local passenger transport office if they are unable to have the vehicle inspected before the vehicle is 21 years old.

    The ‘Structural Inspection of Buses’ Form F2450 is to be completed for the following categories of vehicles:
    Category 1: Before a bus is 21 years old in good condition.
    Category 2: At any age if there is evidence major structural damage.
Category 1
Category 1
    Category 1 applies to:
    • buses over 20 years old, in good condition
    • with no evidence of corrosion damage,
    • no evidence of possible structural damage
    OR
    Buses of any age with evidence of possible structural damage; e.g. buses with loose panel rivets and rust stains on panels.

    Transport Inspectors are to use their professional discretion to decide the number of external panels and or windows and internal trim to be removed in order to issue a certificate.

    A ‘Structural Inspection of Buses’ form F2450 is to be issued directing the owner to remove nominated panels or windows and trim by a particular date. A Defect Notice is not to be issued in conjunction with this form.
    Policy for vehicles with stretch panels
    Policy for vehicles with stretch panels
      With the implementation of ADR59 Omnibus Roll Over Strength in 2007, modern busses and coaches have been built to a high safety standard and use galvanised or stainless-steel material in the frame.
      Modern busses and coaches also use stretch panels on the outside to improve the cosmetic appearance of the vehicle.

      If a vehicle fitted with stretch panels requires a “Bus Frame Inspection” the inspector may allow the owner to remove half the windows and the interior trim on both sides of the vehicle to inspect the frame for signs of corrosion or cracked welds.

      This process is more cost effective for bus operators without reducing the standard of inspection.

      The inspector must complete the Structural Inspection of Buses’ form F2450 directing the owner to remove the nominated windows and interior trim by a particular date.

      If during the inspection the inspector discovers rust or cracks the owner may be required to remove the outer stretch panels to carry out repairs.


    Buses in this category may be given up to six (6) months to have the vehicle inspected, provided they are in a safe condition.
Category 2
Category 2
    Category 2 applies to:
    Buses of any age with obvious major structural defects; e.g. significant rust damage evidence in panels or frame.

    In this instance, a Defect Notice is to be issued directing the owner to cease using the bus after midnight on the date of issue of the Defect Notice, and to remove panels and or windows indicated on the ‘Structural Inspection of Buses’ form F2450. This form must be completed and attached to the Defect Notice.

    Enter the Defect Notice in TICA as a failed Road Patrol (RP), batch file copies of Defect Notice and "Structural Inspection of Buses" Form F2450.
When not Required
When not required
When not required
    A structural inspection may not be required:
    • If the frame and body of the bus was inspected:
      • when the bus was 19 years old, but before it was 20 years old; and
      • within the last 50000km travelled by the bus; and
      • the inspection showed that the body framework was in a satisfactory structural condition, a further inspection when the bus is 20 years old is not required.
    • If a prescribed inspection entity has inspected the body framework of the bus:
      • within 1 year immediately before the operator introduces the bus into service in Queensland; and
      • within the last 50000km travelled by the bus; and
      • the inspection shows that the body framework is in a satisfactory structural condition, a further inspection prior to the bus entering service in Queensland is not required.
    • A bus may be exempted from a structural inspection if documentary evidence is provided to show that it has undergone an age zero refurbishment service life extension and the extension is in force.
