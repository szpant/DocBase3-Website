Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Guide (1879)


About Dangerous Goods Licences
Definitions
Definitions
Definitions
    Dangerous goods driver licence
    Means a dangerous goods driver licence under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management- Dangerous Goods) Regulation 2018

    Industry Authority Definitions
What it is
What it is
What it is
    A Dangerous Goods Driver Licence is an approval that allows a driver to operate a vehicle that is carrying dangerous goods.
When required
When Required
When Required
    A dangerous goods driver licence is required if the applicant wishes to drive a vehicle that is transporting dangerous goods in a receptacle with a capacity of more than 500kg or 500L.

    Note:If any further information is required, the customer must be referred to the website - http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/licensing/driver-licensing/applying/dangerous/index.html
When not required
When Not Required
When Not Required
    It is not required when the;
    • Load only contains dangerous goods in a receptacle with a capacity of less than or equal to 500kg or 500L.
    • Goods are transported in an Intermediate Bulk Container (IBC) and the:
      • IBCs are not packed or unpacked on the vehicle
      • Total capacity of the IBCs on the vehicle is not more than 3000L

    Any person carrying dangerous goods is required to obtain adequate instruction and training when transporting any amount of dangerous goods, whether or not a dangerous goods driver licence is required.
    • This is to ensure the task is performed carefully and safely.

    Note:If any further information is required, the customer must be referred to the website - http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/licensing/driver-licensing/applying/dangerous/index.html

    Course Certificate
      The applicant must provide a valid course certificate. The course certificate must indicate that the applicant ‘has fulfilled the requirements for TLILIC0001'.

      Scanned or photocopied documents are acceptable. If an original certificate is received, the CSC operator must photocopy and return the original to the customer.

      Course Requirements
      Course Requirements
        An approved training course must be successfully completed each time a new/renewal application for a dangerous goods driver licence is made.
        • A training certificate is valid if issued within 6 months prior to the date of application.

        Note:The training certificate must be lodged in TICA before the 6 months expiry, to be valid.

        • If an application is approved in TICA after the 6 months period, (because of CIB requirements), TICA will request another course certificate:
          • The CSC operator must not request another certificate;
          • They must 'exempt' the course requirements in TICA,
          • Record the exemption as 'valid course at time of lodgement';
          • This exemption must be checked by the supervisor.

        Note:
        • Under no circumstances must a CSC operator manipulate the date on the course certificate to enable an approval to occur.
        • Under no circumstances must a course certificate be accepted if lodgement is to occur after the 6 months validity of the course certificate.

        Validity of Training Certificates
        Validity of Training Certificates
          A training certificate issued in QLD is valid if it:
          • is issued by one of the registered training organisations (RTO) on the above list
          • has the course number of TLILIC0001
          • the QLD Registered Training Organisations (RTO) list indicates the RTO is approved to deliver the course for which the training certificate has been issued
          • has the relevant RTO reference code
          • was issued within 6 months prior to the date of application.

          Note:The training certificate must be lodged in TICA within 6 months of the issue date, to be valid.

          If an application is approved in TICA after the 6 months period, (because of CIB requirements) TICA will request another course certificate.

          The CSC operator must not request another certificate but 'exempt' the course requirements in TICA and record the exemption as 'valid course at time of lodgement'. This exemption must be checked by the supervisor.

          Under no circumstances must a CSC operator manipulate the date on the course certificate to enable an approval to occur.

          Under no circumstances must a course certificate be accepted if lodgement is to occur after the 6 months validity of the course certificate.

          Note:If a training certificate is received for an RTO not on the list of approved RTOs, or does not have the correct course number or RTO reference code, please contact both the Dangerous Goods Unit and Industry Licensing Unit

    Issue period
    Issue Period
    Issue Period
      A dangerous goods driver licence is issued for a three year period.

      Note:Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Dangerous Goods) Regulation 2018 allows for the issue of a dangerous goods driver licence for a period not longer than 5 years. It is a policy decision that a dangerous goods driver licence is issued for only 3 years at this time.
    Using a Dangerous Goods Licence
    Conditions of Use
    Carry a Current Dangerous Goods Driver Licence while performing the role
    Carry a Current Dangerous Goods Driver Licence while performing the role
      A document evidencing a Queensland Dangerous Goods Driver Licence must be carried by an authorised Dangerous Goods Driver at all times when performing their duties. This includes an Industry Authority Smartcard, an interim industry authority or another non-smartcard document.
    Hold a current Queensland Driver Licence
    Hold a current Queensland Driver Licence
      If the Queensland driver licence is suspended, cancelled or void, the applicant is not authorised to drive any vehicle on the road including Dangerous Goods Vehicles.
      Refer: Transaction details > Cancel/Suspend/Void/Expired
    Conditions imposed by TMR - Policy Team
    Conditions imposed by TMR - Policy Team
      If a condition is imposed on a Dangerous Goods driver by the policy unit, the Dangerous Goods driver must, while performing the role:
      • Carry the original conditional letter (or other required supporting documentation) and
      • Produce the letter to an authorised officer if required

      If a condition is imposed on a Dangerous Goods Driver by the policy unit will:
      • Advise the customer of the conditions by letter, which will advise the customer to attend a CSC or QGAP office to replace the Smartcard.
        Note:A copy of this letter will be kept at the Industry Licensing Policy Unit.
      • The policy unit will monitor and if the Smartcard has not been surrendered, returned and reissued within the specified period, the unit will forward to prosecutions for possible action.
      Note:To indicate that the original conditional letter (or other required supporting documentation) is required to be carried, a 'Y' condition code will be on the face of the Smartcard and the word 'TEXT' will be recorded on the chip. The policy team will advise of the text to be recorded on the restriction screen in TICA.

      Refer: The Smartcard Guide > Replacement
    Conditions imposed by TMR - IL Hub Manager
    Conditions imposed by TMR - IL Hub Manager
      If either the traffic/criminal history is of concern but does not warrant a refusal of the application, a conditional approval may be issued. The Dangerous Goods Driver licence is issued for the full term and the applicant should be provided with a 'conditional approval' letter.

      Note:The conditional approval letter does not need to be carried while operating and to indicate this a 'N' condition will be on the face of the Smartcard. If the licence is not subject to conditions, it will also show 'N'.

    Grant
    Grant
      The following requirements must be met before an application may be approved in TICA:
      • The applicant must supply the completed Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Application (F4486). This form must be signed by the applicant in the presence of a TMR Officer or QGAP Officer.
      • Evidence of Identity documents.
      • A valid course certificate that has been completed with an approved training provider within 6 months prior to application.
      • A medical certificate or Truck Safe Medical that has been completed within 6 months prior to application.

        Refer: Industry Authority > 1. Industry Authority > Criminal history request
        Refer:         Eligibility Criteria > Assessing Traffic & Criminal Histories > Assessing applicant's suitability for issue/renewal
      • Consent to taking and storing of digital facial image and digitised signature.
      • Payment of the prescribed fee.

      If any of these prerequisites are not met, the application is not to be accepted.

      Refer: Refuse to consider below.

    Renew
    Renew
      All TMR Customer Service Centres and on-line QGAP agencies may accept applications and payment for renewing Dangerous Goods driver licences.

      The following requirements must be met before an application may be approved in TICA:
      • The applicant must supply the completed Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Application (F4486). This form must be signed by the applicant in the presence of a TMR Officer or QGAP officer.
      • Evidence of Identity Documents.
      • A valid course certificate that has been completed with an approved training provider within 6 months prior to application.
      • A medical certificate or Truck Safe Medical that has been completed within 6 months prior to application.
      • Consent to taking and storing of digital facial image and digitised signature.
      • Payment of the prescribed fee.

      Note:Where an applicant declares criminal history charges/offences since last successful application, the customer’s declaration must to be recorded in TICA. This will prompt the system to generate the criminal history request (fee exempt) automatically. The criminal history will require to be assessed and recorded as finalised prior to the finalisation of the renewal application.
      Note:Customers are mailed a Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Renewal Notice (F4527) and Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Application (F4486) approximately 7 weeks prior to the expiry date. Notification that the Dangerous Goods Driver Licence is due for renewal is shown in the My account portal. If the customer is signed up for e-reminders they will be emailed a reminder 3 days before their Dangerous Goods Driver Licence expiry date.

      Refer: Eligibility Requirements above.

      If any of these prerequisites are not met, the application is not to be accepted.

      Cancel, Suspend and Amend
      Cancel, Suspend and Amend
        Grounds for amending, suspending or cancelling a Dangerous Goods Driver Licence (DGDL) include:
        • The DGDL was issued because of a document or representation was false or misleading or obtained or made in another improper way
        • The applicant has been convicted of:
          • An offence against the TORUM Act or a corresponding law, or
          • An offence against the Criminal Code or a law (within Australia)
        • Public safety has been endangered or is likely to be
        • The applicant has contravened TORUM Act or a corresponding law
        • The applicant is suffering from a medical condition or has a physical or mental incapacity and the holder is unsuitable to hold the licence
        Driver Licence Suspended or Cancelled
        Driver Licence Suspended or Cancelled
          If a driver licence is suspended or cancelled the Dangerous Goods Driver Licence is:
          • Automatically suspended (applicant is unable to perform the duties of a Dangerous Goods Driver during suspension or cancellation period);
          • Automatically reinstated on the completion of the driver licence suspension period or where driver licence has been reissued after cancellation period;
          • Not to be granted or renewed during the suspension or cancellation period;
          • Not required to be surrendered.

          Note:As the actions above are automatic, no action is required on TICA.


        The Policy unit receives a report (IA55) of any driver licence that has been cancelled or suspended while holding a current Industry Authority. If information contained in an IA55 report indicates that action may need to be taken against an Industry Authority Holder, an email will be forwarded to the Manager of the Customer Service Centre closest to the applicant for appropriate action.

        Refer: IA55 report

      Void
      Void
        If a payment for a Dangerous Goods Driver licence has been dishonoured, the Policy unit will receive advice from finance. The Policy unit will advise the applicant that they must pay the relevant fees immediately. Any follow up action required due to non payment of fees will be handled by the Policy unit.

      Expired
      Expired
        If the customer submits an application for renewal and the Dangerous Goods Driver licence has expired for more than 3 months, the application is to be a new issue application with full requirements including a new CrimTrac search and fee.
    Surrender
    Surrender
      The dangerous goods licence must be surrendered for the following reasons:
      • Refused on renewal of application;
      • Merged;
      • Multiple Industry Authority holder (if removing one or more of the current authority types);
      • If the licence has been suspended due to an action by an authorised enforcement officer.

      The dangerous goods driver licence can be surrendered for the following reasons:
      • No longer required (voluntary);
      • On behalf of the deceased.

      Note:Multiple card holders wanting to voluntarily surrender one authority but retain other current authorities are to complete a Replacement Industry Authority (F7429)
    Reinstate
    Reinstate
      A dangerous goods may be reinstated due to:

      A review of a decision made by Regional Director or delegate.
      The applicant needs to provide:
      • The letter issued by the Regional Director or delegate stating the licence is to be reinstated; and
      • Standard EOI.

      A stay or review of a decision made by QCAT.
      • The applicant needs to provide:
        • The letter issued by QCAT stating the licence is to be reinstated; and
        • Standard EOI.

      Note:The applicant does not have to complete another application form. A copy of the letter by the authorised agent is used for filing.
    Refuse to consider
    Refuse to grant application
    Refuse to grant application
      The application may be refused if any of the following are not met:
      • Traffic History
      • Criminal History
      • Failed 1:N (if application is lodged and not approved on same day)
      • For a new licence, when the applicant already holds a dangerous goods driver licence under the same name or another name

      The decision to refuse an application for a Dangerous Goods Driver should be made by the Manager (Customer Service Centre).

      A written refusal notice must be sent to the applicant within 14 days of the decision advising:
      • The decision
      • The reason for the decision
      • Information regarding reconsideration or appeal of the decision
      • That they can apply for a stay of the decision through QCAT

      Note:If an applicant is refused their accreditation, a copy of the refusal letter and the file should be forwarded to Iron Mountain (RECALL) the next business day and not retained in the office in case of appeal:
      • Filing must be in a filing envelope indicating Industry Authority filing only (tick the 'Industry Authority' box on the filing envelope)
      • Name of Office sending work
      • Refusal dates for the documents e.g. 03/08/2009 (there must only be one envelope per refusal date)

    Lapsed applications
    Lapsed applications
      Lapsed Status on IL applications in TICA occur when:
      • Lodged applications are not finalised (outstanding requirements) within 3 months of the application date; or
      • Renewals are not finalised within 3 months of the expiry date

      In these instances the following will apply:
      • The application is to be refused and the authority term fee and the smartcard production fee are to be refunded to the customer.
        Refer: Refunds

      A lapsed application that has been refused is sent to Iron Mountain (Recall) for filing on the day the application is refused.

      If the customer requests to proceed with an application after a lapsed status, the applicants will be required to re-apply and meet full requirements including the payment of all prescribed fees (including CrimTrac fees).

      Note:If the application has not been finalised due to a possible TMR error please consult with your SASM/Manager for advice on how to proceed.

    Interstate Operators
    Interstate Operators
    Interstate Operators
      If a holder of an interstate Dangerous Goods Driver Licence becomes a resident of Queensland, they may use their current interstate Dangerous Goods Driver Licence until it expires.

      Six weeks before expiry of the interstate Dangerous Goods Driver licence, the Licence holder, is required to apply for a Queensland Dangerous Goods Driver Licence and meet all Queensland's requirements.
    Mutual recognition
    Mutual Recognition
    Mutual Recognition
      Under the Mutual Recognition Act 1992,a current Dangerous Goods Drive Licence accreditation can be recognised and transferred to the equivalent Queensland accreditation for the same classes held interstate or New Zealand. However applicants must make application for recognition of their existing interstate or New Zealand accreditation and pay the applicable fee.

      Applicants eligible for mutual recognition are therefore exempt from the qualification requirement (e.g. course certificate/statement of attainment) for the same class/es but must meet all other application requirements (such as driver licence tenure, medical certificates and traffic history checks).

      Interstate
