Special Circumstances Permit (SCP) (7586)

Department of Transport and Main Roads Special Circumstances Permit
What it isThe Department of Transport and Main Roads has been issued a Special Circumstances Permit (SCP) under section 128 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Accreditation and Other provisions) Regulation 2015.

The SCP exempts authorised officers from specific provisions of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Road Rules) Regulation 2009 (Road Rules) while undertaking official enforcement activities.

The SCP is in force from the issue date to the expiry date unless it is suspended, surrendered or cancelled.
Exempt Road Rules
Exempt Road Rules
Exempt Road Rules
    Authorised TMR employees undertaking official compliance and enforcement activities are exempt from the following:
    • Rules regarding no stopping, parking signs and road markings, sections: 167, 168 and 169.
    • Rules regarding stopping, sections: 170,172, 174, 176, 177, 178, 179, 180, 181, 182, 183, 185, 186, 187, 188, 190, 191, 193, 194, 195, 196, 197, 199, 200, 201, 202 and 203A.
    • Rules regarding parking, sections: 205, 205A, 208, 208A, 209, 210, 211 and 212.
    • Rules regarding pedestrians, sections: 228, 229, 230, 231, 231, 233, 234, 236, 238 and 239.
    • Rules regarding traffic signs and road markings, sections: 39 and 104.

    The SCP is not to be used for parking.
Conditions
Conditions
Conditions
    The Special Circumstances Permit only applies under the following conditions:
    • The permit must only be used by an authorised person employed by TMR in the role of Manager (Compliance), Principal Advisor Investigations, Senior Investigator, Senior Transport Inspector or Transport Inspector.
    • While the authorised person is driving a vehicle to undertake compliance and enforcement activities in the course of their employment with TMR.
    • The authorised person must take reasonable care when contravening a Road Rule and contravention of the rule must be safe in the circumstances.
    • It must be reasonable that the authorised person not comply with the rule in the circumstances.
    • The authorised person must perform their duties in compliance with the relevant TMR policies and guidelines.
    • A copy of this permit must be carried in the vehicle being driven by the exempted driver. The permit must be produced, with appropriate identification as an authorised driver of a Traffic Incident Management vehicle, at the request of an enforcement officer.
Additional conditions apply to section 39
Additional conditions apply to section 39
    • Magenta flashing and or alternating flashing headlights to be activated while making a U-turn, then de-activated on completion of U-turn.
    • Only to be used when adequate visibility of oncoming traffic is available.
    • Not to be used in heavy rain or fog conditions.
    • Not to be used by vehicles towing trailers.
    • Any vehicle using this permit must give way to any Emergency Vehicle in or entering the median turning bay.
