Register of Approved Vehicles (RAV) (7647)

Project NameRegister of Approved Vehicles (RAV)
When1 July 2021
BackgroundThe Federal Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development and Communications (DITRDC) is implementing the Register of Approved Vehicles (RAV) across Australia from 1 July 2021.

The RAV is an electronic register (online database) containing details for most vehicles approved for road use in Australia under the Road Vehicle Standards Act 2018 (RVSA). Once implemented, the RAV will replace the need to fit most road vehicles with compliance plates. The information will be stored in the RAV instead of on a compliance plate. Trailers will still be required to fit a trailer plate in addition to the RAV entry. For more information, Refer: https://www.infrastructure.gov.au/vehicles/rav/index.aspx

Manufacturers/importers will be responsible for ensuring vehicles are recorded on the RAV before providing them to the Australian market.

There will be a 1 year transition period whereby compliance plates may still be used. From 1 July 2022, RAV entry will be mandatory and compliance plates will no longer be used for most vehicle categories.

Some vehicles will not be required to be on the RAV. Further information about these vehicles will be provided closer to go live.
Who will be impacted and how?
Manufacturers/importers:
Manufacturers/importers:
    • Will be responsible for ensuring applicable vehicles are recorded on the RAV prior to sale.
CSC Operators/DAIS dealers:
CSC Operators/DAIS dealers:
    • Will be required to look up the RAV database to determine if a RAV entry exists when completing new business transactions.
    • Will be required to select a new Compliance type Z - RAV Entry Exists when entering new business information for vehicles with a manufacturer date on or after 1 July 2021, if a RAV entry exists.
    • Will continue to be able to select an existing Compliance Type for vehicles not on the RAV.
Further informationMore information will be provided closer to go live.
