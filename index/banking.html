Banking (1977)

What it isBanking is an end of day procedure which:
  • Reconciles operators actual takings with recorded takings
  • Closes operators TICA dealings for that day/period
  • Concludes CSC trading for that day
  • Removes cash from the premises (unless approval to store funds overnight or out of hours business)

Revenue collected is categorised as controlled or administered, and must be promptly receipted, recorded, banked and reconciled to ensure it is correctly recorded in the departmental General Ledger, so that TMR can determine its financial position on a daily basis.
Cash Collection LimitsThe cash limit to hold funds on premises overnight has been increased to $7000.

Where the total of cash collected exceeds $7000 in any one day, and a Night Safe is NOT available at the bank site, a skim and bank procedure must be completed prior to close of business. Refer below: Skim & Bank Procedure

Where the total value of cash collected is $7000 or less, collections must be banked weekly or when the value of the cash exceeds $7000, whichever occurs first.

Cash and cheques held on a premise overnight should be kept in a lockable secure safe. Refer: Admin > Administration > Security > Security Of Information > Safe Combination

Note: >$7000 Cash collected means cash only. This excludes cheques, eftpos, and office floats (operator and change floats).
Banking ProceduresBanking must be actioned daily.

All sites that transport money via a security service must;
  • Secure funds in a CBA Quick Cash Envelope to deposit funds at a CBA; or where there is no CBA
  • Secure funds in a secure mechanism to deposit at an Australia Post agency

Departmental collections may be held on behalf of TMR by an approved security company who hold sufficient insurances to cover any potential loss. A record of all instances where monies have been held overnight by a security service must be maintained by the relevant business area.

Note: The operator EFTPOS total should not be counted or matched against the "List EFTPOS" TICA printout.

Skim and Bank Procedure
Skim and Bank Procedure
    For centres who are not using an approved security company and take more than $7000 in cash per day the following procedure must be used:
    • At 2pm, banking officer is to skim all operators with Cash (as per skim procedure)
    • Operator to record the amount on their banking sheet (in the banked skim section) and both parties are to initial
    • Record operators skimmed amount on excel operators summary sheet
    • Complete CBA deposit book/slip with cash amount only and place in Quick Cash Envelope
    • Complete Quick Cash Envelope
    • DO NOT close and bank in TICA until end of day banking procedure is completed
    • Monies are to be collected by a security provider or deposited at an Australia Post Agency

    Note:If a centre takes over $7000 in cash between their mid-afternoon skim and bank and the end of the day, an email must be sent by the SASM/Banking Officer before close of business to cashandbanking@tmr.qld.gov.au and CC the Manager and PCM, to seek endorsement of the approval to hold more than $7000 in cash on premise overnight.

    Approval requests must be supported with details explaining why the approval is required including:
  • the timing of the skim and bank
  • extraordinary cash transactions
  • remedial actions taken where available.
Banking Procedure for the Day
Banking Procedure for the Day
    At the end of business day the following procedure must be followed:
    • Operator follows end of day banking procedure
    • Banking Officer follows end of day banking procedure
    • Any banking discrepancies must be finalised at this time
    • When banking is reconciled Close and Bank in TICA
    • Complete Bank Deposit Slip and seal in a CBA Quick Cash Envelope or other secure mechanism.
    • Monies are collected via a security provider, held in office in secure location (if under $7000) or deposited at an Australia Post Agency
    • The TICA Collection screen should reflect a NIL Balance

    Note:If you forget to Close & Bank in TICA you will be required to perform a Close and Bank prior to commencing business the next day.

Skim
Skim
    The cash drawer total of any operator must never exceed $5000.

    By monitoring the operator's cash collections in TICA, the SASM/supervisor is able to identify when a skim is required.

    A skim may be conducted at any time to reduce the amount of cash held by an operator in their cash drawer.

    The skim should:
    • be counted and reconciled in a secure location by both the operator and an independent person; and
    • be recorded on the operator's daily banking sheet at the time the skim was conducted; and
    • secured immediately once money has been reconciled.

    Operator Overs/Unders
    Operator Overs/Unders
      For information and instruction regarding operator overs and unders, please refer to Admin > 3. Money > Adjustments Guide

    Payment item entered with wrong amount
    Payment item entered with wrong amount
      When an incorrect amount is entered the banking officer will need to be told.

      To correct this an adjustment will need to be made to the receipt.

      TICA will automatically make an adjustment allocating the funds to the operators under or overs account.

      The banking officer will then need to adjust those amounts to the customers account.

    Payment Item with Incorrect Method of Payment
    Payment Item with Incorrect Method of Payment
      This might occur when:
      1.A physical payment item isn't listed on the Lpay (ie item recorded as cash instead of cheque)
      2.The payment item as been listed incorrectly (eg as a cheque instead of money order)

      In these cases the operator will need to display the receipt and alter the payment method.

      3.A payment item listed on the printout but they do not physically have the payment item.

      In this case the operator will need to physically locate the item. If not found the item will need to be put into suspense.
