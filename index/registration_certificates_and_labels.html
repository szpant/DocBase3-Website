Registration Certificates (3033)


What is a registration certificate?
A vehicle registration certificate provides the vehicle and registration details of a registered vehicle.
Generation of certificates
Organisations without an ACN recorded in TICA
Organisations without an ACN recorded in TICA
    Registration certificates are automatically generated at:
    • New Business
    • Transfer
    • Renewal payment when:
      • vehicle/registration details have been changed
      • a registration period is varied
      • the vehicle is a common due date vehicle
    • When the following details are changed:
      • Plate number
      • Insurance Class
      • Registration Category
      • Number of trailers to be towed

    Registration certificates should be manually generated (emailed or printed) by a CSO when personalised or customised plates are attached to a vehicle.

    Organisations without an ACN recorded in TICA can't use the "Get Registration Certificate" online service as they are unable to verify their details.
Individuals or organisations with an ACN recorded in TICA
Individuals or organisations with an ACN recorded in TICA
    If an individual customer is signed up for e-notices a registration certificate will be generated and emailed overnight after completing the following transactions:
    • New Business
    • Transfer
    • Renewal payment when:
    • vehicle/registration details have been changed
    • a registration period is varied
    • When the following details are changed:
    • Plate number
    • Insurance Class
    • Registration Category
    • Number of trailers to be towed

    If an individual customer who is signed up for e-notices requests a registration certificate after completing one of the above transactions they should be advised that the certificate will be emailed to them within the next 24 hours.

    Individuals or organisations with an ACN in TICA will not be posted a registration certificate. Organisations without an ACN recorded in TICA or individuals not enrolled in e-notices can access their registration details by:
    • accessing the Get Registration Certificate online service to print, save or email themselves a PDF copy of the certificate,
    • using the Rego Check App or online service to confirm that a registration is current,
    • requesting that a copy of their registration certificate be emailed to them or printed out by a customer service centre or call centre operator.

    The certificates that the customer are able to access will be listed as a "Self-Serve" option in the correspondence hub in TICA. "Self-Serve" indicates that the correspondence was not generated through the mail house but can be accessed by the customer via other methods.

    Operators must manually generate (email or print) a registration certificate when personalised or customised plates are attached to a vehicle.
Rules
Certificate information
Certificate information
    A standard registration certificate displays:
    • Vehicle details
    • Registered operator name
    • Expiry date of registration
    • Statement that 'A registration certificate does not prove ownership'
    • Learner approved motorbike LAM indicator Yes or No

    Conditional registration certificates display:
    • Standard registration certificate information
    • Conditions of Use Codes
    • Full written explanation of all conditions

Removal of registration labels
Removal of registration labels
    Vehicles do not require, and will not be issued, registration labels.

    If a customer is concerned that they will not remember when their registration is due, advise them they can use the QLD Rego Check app or Check Rego Status Online service to verify their registration expiry date at any time.

    Customers can also sign up for e-reminders which will send an email reminder 3 days prior to, on the day of and 7 days after, the registration expiry date if registration remians unpaid.

    Refer: Services > Online Services > TMR Online Services Guide
Carriage of registration certificate
Carriage of registration certificate
    There is no requirement for registration certificates to be carried in the vehicle.
Removing interim label
Removing interim label
    Interim registration labels are issued to customers by dealerships who participate in the Rego Easy Plates/Business Scheme.

    The interim label should be removed as soon as the vehicle has been registered. Customers that are signed up for eNotices will receive a registration certificate by email to advise them of this. Alternatively, customers who are not signed up for eNotices will need to use the Rego Check App or Online service to confirm that their registration is current.

    If 28 days has passed since the date of purchase, customers will need to directly contact the dealership they purchased the vehicle from if:
    they have not received a registration certificate; or
    cannot confirm that their vehicle registration is current on the Rego Check App or Online service.
Interstate Travel
Interstate Travel
    If a Queensland registered vehicle is travelling to a jurisdiction that still issues registration labels, the Queensland registered vehicle will be recognised by the interstate authority without a registration label.
