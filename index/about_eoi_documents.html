Details Required to be Shown on Documents
Details Required to be Shown on Documents
    • Issuing Organisation's name
    Customers Name in Full
    • Customer's signature
      Signature on Document
      Signature on Document
        • The customer's signature should appear on at least 1 document.
        • The signature must form part of the document, e.g. bank card, student ID.
        • The signature must appear in the section/strip provided for the signature.
          • Signatures added to another part of the document are not acceptable:
            e.g. signatures added to the back of student ID cards after laminating.

      Unable to Provide Document with Signature
      Unable to Provide Document with Signature
        If a customer produces:
        • The required 3 EOI documents and cannot obtain an EOI document with a signature, the Customer Identity Declaration may be completed.
    • Customer's date of birth
      Date of birth
      Date of birth
        • The customer's date of birth must be shown on at least one Category A document.
        • If the customer's date of birth on a Dept. of Immigration and Border Protection - DIBP (formerly known as Dept. of Immigration & Citizenship - DIAC) document is 00/00/year of birth, the DOB is taken to be 01/01/year of birth.
        • If the customer's date of birth on the Northern Territory Aboriginal Population Records certificate is recorded as a year only, eg 1953, then the DOB is taken to be 01/07/year of birth.

Originals/ Copies /Laminated/Digital/PDF documents
Originals/ Copies /Laminated/Digital/PDF documents
    Original documents must be sighted unless one of the following exemptions applies:
    Exemptions
    Exemptions
      Certified Copies Certified copies may only be accepted when received:
      • From an organisation if it is specified in the EOI Organisation Documents that a certified copy may be accepted.
      • With a driver licence renewal kit, replacement driver licence kit or release of information kit.
      • With a release of information request lodged by mail from outside Qld or from remote Qld.
      • With an Industry Authority Remote Area Kit.
      CopiesCopies may be accepted when received:
      • From legal entities created by legislation (i.e church). Refer: EOI Organisation Documents
      • From a Dealer transacting business on behalf of a customer
      • For a Transfer Lodged by email/fax/mail
      Digital DocumentsThe Driver Licence, Marine Licence and Photo Identification card displayed within the Queensland Digital Licence Application are acceptable standard EOI documents. 
       
      Document can be visually verified by looking for the unique security features- see verifying documents and visually comparing the image on the DLA to the person presenting. 
       
      Important:  The digital document is not acceptable if the mobile device screen is cracked or damaged to such an extent that the data is unreadable or the security features cannot be viewed. 
       
      Digital Licences issued by other jurisdictions (NSW and South Australia) are not acceptable evidence of identity documents. They can be used as evidence of holding an interstate driver licence if the details are confirmed via NEVDIS.  

      Original EOI documents that have been laminated are acceptable e.g. laminated birth certificate.

Manually Verifying Authenticity of Documents
Manually Verifying Authenticity of Documents
    CSO Responsibilities
    CSO Responsibilities
      Officers should verify authenticity of documents by:
      • Thoroughly checking identification documents (see below for guides)
      • Ensuring the applicant signs the relevant application form in your presence and then comparing that signature to the signature present on Category A or Category B EOI supplied.
      • Gaining approval from a Senior Advisor/Manager before processing the application on TICA if the authenticity of the documents are in question

      Digital Documents
      A digital document within a Qld Digital Licence Application can be: 
      • Visually verified by asking the customer to display the digital document and looking for the pulsing Queensland Government crest in the background of the document (the crest will be in black and will have a have a deer to the left and a brolga to the right facing the shield) 
      • Digitally verified by requesting the customer to share their digital document and using a Verifier App to scan the QR code presented on the customers screen 



      At no stage should the Operator take the phone from the customer, if having difficulty seeing the document ask the customer to place the phone on the counter. 

      Important:  The digital document is not acceptable EOI if the mobile device screen is cracked or damaged to such an extent that the data is unreadable.
