Rego Easy Performance Evaluation (6027)

What is a Performance EvaluationPerformance evaluations are mandatory under the Deed of Agreement and allow TMR to ensure that Rego Easy Clients (REC) are meeting their legislative and agreement obligations and can continue to do so in a way that enhances TMR’s reputation and the integrity of the scheme.

It is important to remember that performance evaluation is an on-going process and while these evaluations are available to address poor performance at a point in time, it is important that any non-compliant behaviour is addressed as close as possible to the event and that compliance is closely monitored on an on-going basis.
Types of Performance Evaluations
Entry Evaluation
Entry Evaluation
    An entry evaluation is conducted for Easy Plates and Easy Business clients in person at the dealership within one month of enrolment in a Rego Easy Scheme.

    The purpose of this is to:
    • Commence the business relations and advise of their obligations, roles and responsibilities under the agreement.
    • Provide training to Easy Plates customers.
    • Confirm Easy Plates and Easy Business customers have a secure storage location for number plates and interim labels if applicable.

    Easy Pay customers are provided scheme information when enrolling at the CSC.
Conducting an Entry Evaluation
Conducting an Entry Evaluation
    Entry Evaluations must be conducted for Easy Plates and Easy Business customers:
    • within one month of signing the Deed of Agreement
    • at the principal place of business (where the plates are to be stored)
    • by a minimum of two TMR representatives.

    The dealership must be advised by email or in writing when the evaluation will occur and who will be attending. Confirmation should be sought by phone at least two working days prior to the nominated date.

    During the evaluation the TMR representatives will provide training in:
    • Conducting pre-registration vehicle inspections (PRI)
      Pre Registration Inspection Training Material.
    • TMR customer and registration documentation requirements.
    • TMR EOI requirements, including Category A and B documents and Letter of Authorities.
    • Number plate management.
    • Ensure submission time frames are understood.
    • Ensure that a secure plate storage location exists.
    • Advice of their legislative and agreement obligations such as privacy requirements, conflicts of interest and the use of the TMR logo.
    • Confirm that no personal data of TMR customers is stored off-shore.
    • Provide advice on ongoing audit requirements.
Desktop Performance Evaluation
Desktop Performance Evaluation
    A desktop evaluation may be conducted every six months instead of an on-site audit and its purpose is to ensure that the dealership is complying with the terms of the Agreement to remain eligible to participate in the scheme. If appropriate an on-site evaluation can be conducted in lieu of a desktop evaluation.

      Bulk Iron Mountain Process
      Bulk Iron Mountain Process
        At least one month prior to your planned evaluation date, a bulk request for transaction documents must be made based on how many transactions (including acquisitions) on average the dealership completes per month:
        • Less than 20 - review not less than 5 transactions
        • Between 20-50 - review not less than 10 transactions
        • More than 50 - review not less than 20 transactions

        Transactions are identified by reviewing the account statement for the dealership and transactions must be spread out across the previous 5 months. The sample selected should be a mixture of New Business, Registration Transfer and Cancellation applications.


        Note: If evaluating transactional paperwork during an on-site evaluation and there is an insufficient amount of documentation present at the dealership you must obtain the remainder via the bulk Iron Mountain process.

        To send the request:
        • Complete the Iron Mountain template
        • Prepare email to Iron Mountain
        • Include in the subject line "Document Service Request Iron Mountain"
        • In the body of the email include the requesting officers details:
          • Requesting Centre
          • Name
          • Phone number

      Successful Evaluation
      Successful Evaluation
        A successful audit is one where:
        • No corrective action requests (CAR) were required
        • Where a CAR has been satisfactorily dealt with

        When a successful evaluation is conducted, the following steps are taken:
        • Advise in writing of the successful evaluation accompanied by any suggestions for improvement (SFI), within one week of determining that the evaluation was successful.
        • A copy of the completed Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation Reportand supporting documentation/correspondence must be scanned and emailed to Rego Easy Unit.
        • A copy of the completed Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation Reportand copies of any correspondence/supporting documentation can be retained locally. Once scanned, the Rego Easy Unit will archive the documents electronically as per TMR Recordkeeping guidelines.

      Corrective Action Request required
      Corrective Action Request required
        Where a Corrective Action Request (CAR) has been sent to the dealership a copy of the CAR and the Vehicle Registration Scheme performance Evaluation Report must be recorded in the Follow-up Register as soon as possible. This will provide visibility as to whether any evaluations still in progress.

        A successful audit letter should not be sent if a CAR has been issued. Issues raised in a CAR must be resolved and a further evaluation conducted before the evaluation result can be finalised.

      Failed Evaluation
      Failed Evaluation
        An audit is considered failed when the dealership has not satisfactorily addressed issues raised in a CAR.

        Appropriate steps listed below in Corrective Action Request (CAR) must be followed before the results of the failed evaluation can be determined.

        If the REC does not respond to the CAR notice(s) or there is evidence during the follow-up evaluation that the issues have not been rectified, a failed evaluation result can be recorded and the following steps taken:
        • Prepare written advice to the dealership advising that the audit has been failed;
        • Scan and email a copy of the failed evaluation letter, supporting documents from the follow-up evaluation and the completed Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation Reportforms to the Rego Easy Unit;
        • Finalise the relevant item in the Follow-up Register;
        • A copy of the completed Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation Reportand copies of any correspondence/supporting documentation can be retained locally. Once scanned, the Rego Easy Unit will archive the documents electronically as per TMR Recordkeeping guidelines.

On-site Performance Evaluation
On-site Performance Evaluation
    An on-site evaluation is an on-site visit to the dealership by TMR representatives who complete the evaluation process on behalf of the dealership, or is an adhoc evaluation conducted when the dealership has been exhibiting poor performance and/or has been issued a Corrective Action Request (CAR).

    Preparation for a standard 6-monthly on-site evaluation, and the finalisation of the results, are the same procedures as listed for desktop evaluation. However please note that the Bulk Iron Mountain process only applies to Easy Business desktop audits. All evaluation criteria and document requirements are necessary for both on-site and desktop evaluations.

    If the on-site evaluation has been triggered by poor performance or a previously issued CAR, evidence of the non-compliance issues and appropriate actions to correct the issues, should be presented to the dealership at the time of the evaluation.
Exit Evaluation
Exit Evaluation
    Exit Evaluation
    Exit Evaluation
      A dealership may leave the Rego Easy Scheme either voluntarily or at the request of TMR through a Show Cause process. Once the decision has been made to leave the scheme an exit evaluation must be conducted.

      This evaluation is conducted on-site when a dealer is exiting the scheme. The purpose is to ensure all TMR customer related documentation and number plates (Easy Plates and Business only) are removed from the site.

      If a client wishes to leave the scheme they must notify TMR in writing.

      All notices provided to Rego Easy Scheme clients to terminate an agreement as a result of non-compliance must be issued by the Rego Easy Unit.
    Conducting an exit evaluation
    Conducting an exit evaluation
      During the exit the CLO must:
      • Conduct a number plate stocktake
      • Remove stock from premises including number plates and interim labels (if applicable).
    Finalising the exit evaluation
    Finalising the exit evaluation
      Once on-site component has been completed the CLO must:
      • Have the plate re-order level set to zero by emailing Plate Management.
      • Once reset transfer plate stock from dealership to CSC location.
      • Finalise any outstanding plate issues.
      • Complete a Vehicle Registration Scheme Final Performance Evaluation Report form.
      • Send the Dealer a Final Audit confirmation letter.
      • Scan and email a copy of the completed Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Final Evaluation Report, final audit letter and, if applicable, the Dealerships request to leave the scheme notice to the Rego Easy Unit. Once scanned, the Rego Easy Unit will archive the documents electronically as per TMR Recordkeeping guidelines.

Suggestion for Improvement (SFI)
Suggestion for Improvement (SFI)
    SFI's are:
    • Issued where the non-compliant behaviour identified during the evaluation is not considered to be of high risk.
    • Are issued to address deficiencies and improve on the dealers performance.
    • Must be addressed with the dealer and confirmed in writing by completing a SFI and forwarded to the REC.
    • An evaluation is considered to be passed even if a SFI is issued. However, monitoring of the SFI should be undertaken at all times and special attention to the issue should be targeted on the next evaluation. Failure to implement the agreed action by the REC will result in a CAR and a failed performance evaluation. Copies of the SFI(s) must be sent to the Rego Easy Unit with the completed Vehicle Registration Scheme Performance Evaluation Report.

Corrective Action Request (CAR)
Corrective Action Request (CAR)
    A CAR is a request for the client to take immediate action to remedy any high risk non-compliant behaviour.

    The CAR must include:
    • reference to the relevant section of the Agreement;
    • details of the non-compliant activity (that is, findings of the evaluation or examples);
    • the date for completion of action;
    • the corrective action to be taken;
    • the time frame for action.

    An evaluation cannot be passed until all CAR’s have been resolved and an additional follow-up evaluation has been conducted. The REC has seven days from the date of receiving the CAR to provide evidence that the CAR has been rectified. The REC must provide evidence to satisfy the performance reviewer of the action to correct the cause of the non-compliance.

    A follow-up on-site evaluation must be conducted 14 days after the CAR is issued to confirm that the issues have been rectified.

    If the REC does not respond to the CAR or corrective action is not taken the performance evaluation is considered failed and Show Cause action must be taken by the Rego Easy Unit to terminate the Rego Easy Agreement.

    Suitable evidence of the non-compliance issues found and all CAR correspondence must be must be recorded in the Follow-up Register.
