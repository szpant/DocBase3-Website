Stopping automatic postage of Compulsory Third Party (CTP) tax invoices (7366)


Project NameStopping automatic postage of Compulsory Third Party (CTP) tax invoices
When1 November 2018
BackgroundTo reduce the amount of correspondence that is generated from the mail house, CTP tax invoices will no longer be automatically posted to individuals or Organisations with an ACN recorded in TICA. Organisations that don't have an ACN recorded in TICA will continue to receive their invoices by post.

Affected customers will be able to access the Get CTP tax invoice online service to print, save or email themselves a PDF copy of the Invoice.

Invoices are sent by TMR on behalf of insurers and the Motor Accident Insurance Commission (MAIC) to operators that are recorded as claiming the Input Tax Credit Entitlement (ITCE). CTP tax invoices are only generated when payment for CTP has been receipted in TICA. If a registered operator has purchased a CTP policy from an insurer directly, they will be required to contact the insurer for their invoice.
Who will be impacted and how?
Customers
Customers
    Customers who no longer receive their Tax Invoice by post will be able to access the Get CTP tax invoice online service to print, save or email themselves a PDF copy of the Invoice.

    The online service is capable of generating statements of up to 50 invoices at one time. If a search returns more than 50 invoices customers can either:
    • Update their search parameters (eg. minimise the date range) to display fewer invoices
    • Opt to have their invoices sent by post.
Operators
Operators
    Communications regarding the change will be sent to stakeholders but operators may notice an increase in enquiries regarding CTP tax invoices.

    Customers who do not wish to use the online service may also request a copy of the tax invoice through the call centre or a CSC. Operators should reprint the tax invoice as per the procedure in TICA. Future TICA system changes will allow operators to email a copy of the invoice to customers.
Further informationThe format of the CTP Tax Invoice has been updated. The new format will collate details of multiple vehicles by insurer rather than sending an invoice for each vehicle.
