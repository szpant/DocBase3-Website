Chain of Responsibility Audit (7654)


What it isThe Chain of Responsibility (CoR) Audits aim to enhance the safety of the personalised transport industry by ensuring all persons in the CoR have systems, processes and actions in place to operate their services safely and comply with all legislative requirements.

The CoR framework sets out the obligations of each participant in the CoR, this includes :
  • Authorised booking entities (and any local nominee),
  • other persons who provide booking services,
  • operators of taxi services or booked hire services (booked hire services includes limousines),
  • holders of taxi, limousines or booked hire service licences,
  • drivers of vehicles used to provide the services,
  • registered operators of vehicles being used to provide the services.

Under TOPTA s91ZN a CoR audit includes the 'service', the 'vehicles used to provide the service' and the 'person business activities' related to providing the service.
Audit Types
Scheduled Audits
Scheduled Audits
    All BEAs will be audited within 3 years. There will a rolling schedule to plan and manage the programme. While all BEAs will be audited, large BEAs (>50 affiliated vehicles) will be scheduled for a CoR audit within the first year.
Triggered Audits
Triggered Audits
    A BEA can have a Compliance Check triggered by a complaint/investigation or they may be randomly selected.

Issue Audit Notice
Issue Audit Notice
    Contact the person to be audited
    Contact the person to be audited
      Once the pre-audit checks have been complete the Lead Transport Inspector will make contact with the person to be audited to:
      • confirm the following details for the Audit Notice, ensuring it matches with the information in TICA, CID Intell or ASIC search:
        • Full company Name (Pty Ltd)
        • Full Name of Director of Company
        • Business address – where the audit will be conducted
        • Confirm an audit date not less than 4 weeks in advance. (There is no upper limit for the advance notice, work with your STI on the audit time frame).
      • Advise they must provide TMR with the following:
        • Driver licence and driver authorisation details for all drivers currently affiliated.
        • Registration details of vehicles used in conjunction with the operation of the personalised transport service.
        • Determine if any records that the person is required to keep and/or will provide access to during the audit are in a language other than English, if so advise the person the make their own arrangements for the records to be translated to English.

          Preparing the Audit Notice
          Complete the Audit Notice template and attach the Facts Sheet for chain of responsibility audits.

    Serving the Audit Notice
    Serving the Audit Notice
      The Audit Notice must be served on the person personally, preferably on the Director of the Company. If this is not possible then proof of service by other means is necessary.

      A written confirmation of service is required and must retained with a copy of the Audit Notice.
Conducting the Audit
Failure to comply with Audit Notice
Failure to comply with Audit Notice
    Failure to comply with the Audit Notice is an offence and may be prosecuted by PIN or BTA, Refer: s91ZO if TOPTA.
Entry to Premises
Entry to Premises
    The power for an Authorised Officer to enter to conduct an audit, following issue of the Audit Notice is provided in s120(d) TOPTA.

    Section 91ZN(1)(b) of the Act also states that the person is '… to cooperate with every reasonable requirement of the chief executive or authorised person'.
    Commencing Onsite Audit
    Commencing Onsite Audit
      Both Authorised Officers must:
      • activate their BWC prior to entering the premises
      • identify themselves as Transport Inspectors
      • produce their ID cards
      Record in the official notebook:
      • the name of the company
      • name of the person
      • the address of the location where the audit is taking place
      • the name of the corroborating Transport Inspector

      The primary auditor will explain the reason for the PT CoR Audit and advise the person the interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes, and verbally provide the privacy collection notice.

      For example:

      "Good evening/morning/afternoon. My name is John Smith, I am a Transport Inspector with Transport and Main Roads, here is my ID (SHOW ID), we are conducting an Audit to assess your compliance with relevant transport legislation in relation to -
      • providing the service, or
      • a motor vehicle used to provide the service
      to verify information given to the chief executive about the business activities of [insert name of business] relating to providing the booking service. This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes.
PT COR Audit Checklist
PT COR Audit Checklist
    The PT COR Audit Checklist, for the relevant category of audit, must be used when conducting a PT COR audit.

    The checklist must be fully completed. Be aware the audit checklist is provided to the customer with the Audit Report and is a discoverable document for any hearings.

    BEA CoR Audit Checklist Final 300121 (2)

    The front page of the Audit Checklist is completed first, except for the 3rd last row Audit Result, this is completed when the audit has been assessed.

    If non-compliance is detected, record the evidence in the Audit Checklist in the comments section.
Exit Interview
Exit Interview
    Advise the authority holder that they will be given an Audit Report, detailing the outcome of the audit, as soon as practicable. The report will provide the findings and the reasons for the findings.

    If there are any instances of non-compliance, a Direction to Comply notice/s will also be issued.
Audit Report
Assessment
Assessment
    Assess the Audit Checklist to determine if the person was compliant or non-compliant with the audit.

    If there is any area of the business that was non-compliant in the Audit Checklist then that section of the audit is officially recorded as non-complaint.

    Update CID when the assessment has been completed.

Audit Report

    Non-Compliant
    Non-Compliant
      The Audit Report non-compliant report is to be used for audits where there is non-compliance.
      Risk Assessment of non-compliance
      Risk Assessment of non-compliance
        Any non-compliance must be risk assessed. The following guidelines should be used for a risk assessment
        Red (Cat 1)
        Red (Cat 1)
          Where an individual or corporation engages in conduct related to a duty which exposes an individual or class of individuals to a risk of death, serious injury to illness, without a reasonable excuse and they are reckless to the risk, a Direction to Comply notice must be issued for immediate correction.

          Examples:
          • Failure of a service provider (BEA) to have specific risk control strategies/systems to discharge duties relating to driver fatigue.
          • Failure for a service provider to have a system which ensures all affiliated vehicles are appropriately licenced.
          • Failure of a service provider to have a safety management system in place to identify and mitigate risk in their business.

          A Category 1 non-compliance must be referred to the TRO for possible Show Cause action. Any Show Cause will be managed by the TRO, Compliance will continue with the Direction to Comply notice.
        Amber (Cat 2)
        Amber (Cat 2)
          A risk which exposes an individual or class of individuals to a risk of death or serious injury or illness.

          Examples:
          • Failure to provide periodic data/service reporting (quarterly/annual).
          • Failure of the service provider in establishing and implementing a training regime that addresses TMR’s training requirements, (anti-discrimination, disability awareness and providing wheelchair services).
          • Failure of a Taxi BEA to use a system which ensures bookings for person using a wheelchair or TSS member can be received at any time.
        Green (Cat 3)
        Green (Cat 3)
          If a person or corporation has a duty under the framework and contravenes a duty.

          Examples:
          • Failure to have a system to ensure every affiliated driver displays the taxi driver display card (Taxi BEA’s only).
          • A service provider having no specific training around the minimisation of driver distraction and correct use of technology.

        Audit Report Non Compliant v1.1

    Direction to ComplyIf an audit is formally assessed in the Audit Report as being non-compliance then a Direction to Comply Notice must be created and served on the person.

    Direction to Comply Notice
    Direction to Comply Notice
      The following information must be included in the Direction to Comply notice:
      • The specific area of non-compliance, as listed in the Audit Checklist
      • The specified action required to correct the non-compliance
      • The time period for which the correction is to be undertaken

      Other legislative requirements for s91ZQ are contained within the Direction to Comply notice.

      There are only two time-frame options available for a person to undertake the corrections:
      1.Immediate – where it was assessed that there is a risk of death or serious injury or illness to an individual
      2.Minimum 5 business days – for all other non-compliant matters

      Direction to Comply Notice v1.7 300421
      Serve Direction to Comply Notice
      Serve Direction to Comply Notice
        Contact the person to arrange the service or giving of the Direction to Comply notice.

        If you have determined that a minimum five days is required to undertake the corrections, consult with the person to determine the timeframe so that it is both practical and reasonable.

        Two copies of the Direction to Comply notice are required (one for the person and one for TMR).

        Once completed serve the notice, using BWC, following DocBase procedures.

        Update CID with actions taken.

        Advise your TransLink regional office of action taken.
      Follow-up on a Direction to Comply Notice
      Follow-up on a Direction to Comply Notice
        Contact the person and arrange for a follow-up of the audit. You can use a new Audit Notice and include the date and time. The date is dependent upon the time schedule set in the Direction to Comply Notice, 4 weeks notice is not required.
        Full Compliance
        Full Compliance
          If after the assessment of the corrections listed in the Direction to Comply notice you decide that all the corrections have been made then you endorse the last column in the notice with:
          • Date
          • Time
          • Your name and Q Number
          • Your signature
        Partial Compliance
        Partial Compliance
          If the outcome is partial compliance, that is, not all the areas specified for correction are compliant, you are to:
          1.endorse the fully compliant items on the Direction to Comply notice with the:
          • Date
          • Time
          • You name and Q Number
          • Your signature
          2.leave the areas that are non-compliant blank.

        Non-compliant
        Non-compliant
          If, after an extension of time the persons business activity is assessed as still being non-compliant further action is required.

          The options are:
          1.Approve a further extension of time granted under section 91ZQ(4) – limited to Direction to Comply notice issued under section 91ZQ(2)(b)
          Commence enforcement action for Contravention of a Direction to Comply Notice– s.91ZQ(5). This may be a:
          • BTA only;
            or
          • PIN and BTA.
          The TRO must be informed so they can consider show cause action.

        Extension of Time
        Extension of Time
          An extension of time cannot be granted for a Direction to Comply Notice where the timeframe for correction was determined to be 'immediate' under section 91ZQ(5)2(a).

          If a person seeks an extension of the timeframe to correct the non-compliance, you can give that extension in accordance with section 91ZQ(5)2(b) provided the timeframe for correction was not 'immediate'.

          If an extension of time is granted advise your TransLink regional office of the extension details and update CID.
      Requests for Review
      Request for review
      Request for review
        Internal CSB Compliance
        Internal CSB Compliance
          Under s102 of the TOPT Act a person may apply to the department for an internal review of the decision to give this direction. Part 5, division 2 of the Transport Planning and Coordination Act 1994 applies to the review.

          The application for the internal review must be made within 28 days of being given Direction to Comply notice. The application must be written and state, in detail, the grounds on which the person wants the decision reviewed.

          These requests are to be directed to the Manager, Compliance.
        Translink - Regulation Notice
        Translink - Regulation Notice
          TransLink Regional Operation (TRO) will manage requests related to Regulation Notices.

          If both a Direction to Comply and Regulation Notice were issued as a result of a serious non-compliance, TRO and Compliance will jointly manage the review process.
        External Review - QCAT
        External Review - QCAT
          If an internal review decision is adverse to the applicant, the applicant may apply to QCAT for an external review of the decision.

          An application to QCAT for external review of a reviewed decision must be made within 28 days of the person being notified of the decision and of the reasons for the decision. QCAT may extend the period for making an application for external review.

          A fact sheet providing further information in relation to the review process can be found in the PT Document Database, Refer: Passenger Transport database
