Unregistered Vehicle Permit (3098)

What is it?An unregistered vehicle permit allows for an unregistered vehicle to drive on the roads to and from specified destinations for specific purposes, provided the vehicle meets the requirements of:
  • the Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010; and
  • the Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Mass Dimension and Loading) Regulation 2005.
RequirementsDocuments required:
  • Completed Unregistered Vehicle Permit Application (F3523)
  • Where a CTP insurer has not been nominated on (F3523), the customer must present a class 22 CTP insurance certificate for the required time.

    Note:While an unregistered vehicle permit is required for a caravan/trailer, CTP is not required for a caravan/trailer towed by:
    • Queensland registered vehicle
    • Unregistered vehicle with a Qld Unregistered Vehicle Permit

    Note:The Class 22 CTP insurance certificate does not have to be in the same name as the customer applying for an unregistered vehicle permit.
  • Any permit issued under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 (if applicable) for example:-
    • Police Permit
    • Excess Mass Permit
    • A Permit issued for an exemption from a Vehicle Standard

    Note:Unregistered Vehicle Permit Application (F3523) indicates when the above permits may be required.

Rules
  • If a vehicle, which includes a trailer, does not have an identifier (VIN/Chassis number), an unregistered vehicle permit cannot be issued. The vehicle must be towed or transported. However, if the vehicle/trailer was manufactured prior to 1930, an engine number may be used.

  • Return trips can be made as long as they are:
      • Completed within the allowed time
      • Within Queensland

When Permit is not required
When Permit is not required
    An unregistered vehicle can be moved without a permit for the purpose of registration, to obtain a:
    • Gas certificate
    • Weighbridge Certificate
    • Safety Certificate / COI Certificate (including road testing as part of the inspection) Written-off Vehicle Inspection (Repairable write-offs only)
    • Queensland Police Service Inspection for surrogates
    • Surrogate identification number/s stamped on the vehicle

    Customers must carry a CTP Certificate issued by a CTP insurer in the vehicle (this excludes trailers.)

    Customers who are not travelling to a formless centre or who are registering a vehicle/trailer with a GVM/ATM > 4.5 t GVM/ATM will still need to complete a Vehicle Registration Application (F3518) to register the vehicle. In these instances it is recommended that the customer complete the application form prior to attending the centre

    The vehicle can be driven back to its garaging address if it is not possible to register the vehicle on that day due to insufficient time or other registration requirements that were not able to be obtained.

    If the vehicle does not pass the:
    • Gas Certificate, Safety Certificate/COI Certificate or WOVI inspection, it must be transported.
    • Any of the other inspections, the customer can purchase a UVP (Refer below When Permit Cannot be Issued for further details)
Section 15 of the Transport Operations (road Use Management - Vehicle Registration) Regulation 2010
When Permit is required
When Permit is required
    A permit is required:
    • To effect repairs
    • Get a modification plate
    • Tow an unregistered vehicle, trailer or caravan

    Note:A vehicle being towed by a Tow Truck does not require a permit

    Refer: Registerable Vehicles > Exempt Vehicles

    • To use an unregistered vehicle in a procession e.g. a parade/festival
    • To use a Special Interest Vehicle (vehicle >30 years old, historic ambulance, historic firefighting equipment or hot rod) in a rally provided all other permit requirements are met
    • To allow charitable organisation to move an unregistered vehicle for display purposes, in conjunction with raffle ticket sales

    To move an unregistered vehicle:
    • For registration in another state
    • Prior to/after sale
    • To move a vehicle used exclusively on private property to another location
    • When not practicable or reasonable to have registered (eg. using the vehicle carry personal possessions when moving house)
    • To move a caravan/motorised van used as permanent living accommodation to a new site
    • To move an expired COI vehicle (number plates must be detached and can be reattached once the vehicle has passed inspection and/or the registration renewed)
When Permit Cannot be Issued
When Permit Cannot be Issued
    Permit Cannot be used for:
    • Taking rubbish to the dump in an unregistered box trailer
    • Vehicles not complying with:
      • TO(RUM - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010 unless relevant permit is issued
      • TO(RUM - Mass Dimensions and Loading) Regulation 2005 unless relevant permit is issued or vehicle operating under a performance guideline (other than the Form 17 - Guideline for conditionally registering a vehicle in Queensland)
    • Registered vehicles
    • Vehicles with plates attached
    • Vehicles currently under the following restrictions:
      • CONF (a Number Plate Confiscation Notice issued by the Queensland Police Service)
      • RCAL (Recall Vehicle Restriction due to critical manufacturer defect)
    • Vehicles carrying a load for hire or reward
    • Vehicle operating for hire, reward, wedding or formal
    • Moving vehicles:
      • to Queensland from interstate
      • that have been modified (for example, stock cars)
      • that have been written-off (for any purpose)
      • that have been imported (inc trailer) prior to being certified as a compliant vehicle
      • that are left-hand drive and under 30 years of age
      • that have failed a:
        • Gas certificate
        • Safety certificate / COI certificate
        • Written-off vehicle inspection
Nomination of CTP Insurer
Nomination of CTP Insurer
    The customer may nominate a CTP insurer at the time they purchase the UVP by nominating their preferred insurer in question 10 on the Unregistered Vehicle Permit Application (F3523). The cost of the CTP class 22 nomination is then calculated and included in the same transaction as the UVP.

    Alternatively, customers may pre-purchase CTP insurance, include the CTP insurance certificate number in question 11 on the application form and present the certificate at the time of purchase of the UVP.

    Note:Class 22 CTP insurance can not be nominated and purchased at off-line government agency offices and off-line police stations. Customers purchasing UVPs at these offices must pre-purchase Class 22 CTP insurance directly from the insurer prior to purchasing the UVP.
Purchase of Permit and CTP online
Purchase of Permit and CTP online
    An online service for the purchase of a UVP (including nomination and purchase of Class 22 CTP insurance) is available to eligible customers.

    The UVP cannot be purchased online where:
    • An excess mass permit is required
    • A police permit is required
    • Class 22 CTP insurance has already been purchased (CTP must be nominated as part of the online transaction)

    For more information regarding customer eligibility for online services, please refer to the TMR website: http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Online-services.aspx
Travelling Interstate
Travelling Interstate
    • If bringing a vehicle from interstate to Queensland, an unregistered vehicle permit must be issued by the State or territory where the journey starts.
    • A new unregistered vehicle permit must be obtained from the interstate authority if a vehicle that was moved to another state is returned to Queensland.
    • All states and territories (excluding WA in some circumstances, see below) have reciprocal arrangements so that a vehicle may travel through other states and territories with a Queensland issued unregistered vehicle permit.

    Note:If a person resides in Western Australia and wants to move an unregistered vehicle from Queensland to Western Australia, they require a Queensland issued unregistered vehicle permit to move the vehicle to the Western Australian border, then they require a Western Australian issued permit for the remainder of their journey.

    If they do not reside in Western Australia, a Queensland issued unregistered vehicle permit can be used throughout Western Australia.

    Customers should be advised to contact the Department of Transport in Western Australia for more information prior to commencing their journey.

    • If a trailer or caravan is being towed from Qld by a vehicle registered interstate applicant must ensure that the CTP insurance of the towing vehicle covers the unregistered unit.
Period of Issue
Period of Issue
    • Can be issued for 1 to 7 consecutive days
    • For the shortest possible route in the shortest possible time

      Note: If a permit is required for non-consecutive days, a permit and CTP certificate for each period of consecutive days must be purchased.

      For example, if a permit is required for two days that are not consecutive, two permits and two CTP certificates must be purchased for the specific dates.

    Effective either:
      • At the time of issue;
        or
      • The permit is valid from 12:01am of the first day of the journey

    Expires:
      • At midnight on the last day of the journey.

    Note: If the permit is to be used immediately it is valid until midnight the day of issue. It is not issued for 24 hours from the time of purchase, (e.g. if purchased at 4:00pm it will still expire at 12:00 midnight).
Display of Permit
Display of Permit
    • The driver of the unregistered vehicle must carry the permit in the vehicle;
    • If the vehicle is a trailer, the permit must be carried in the vehicle towing the trailer;
    • A UVP must be produced (including via electronic copy) on demand to an authorised person.
Reprint of Permit
Reprint of Permit
    An Unregistered Vehicle Permit can be reprinted at any time.

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 10. Permits > The How To: Permits > Permits Procedures > Reprint UVP
UVP refunds
UVP refunds
    • Requests for UVP refunds must be investigated and assessed by a local Manager (or equivalent e.g. Principal Advisor) according to the below scenarios.
    • Once a decision is made, a request for refund can be emailed to Customer Account Management (CAM) and must include details of the UVP/s, customer and payment.
    • CAM will complete the refund adjustment in TICA and arrange for the funds to be refunded electronically by submitting a request via the approved template to Cash and Banking.
    • The customer must destroy any UVP that they receive a refund for.
      Refund scenarios:
      Refund scenarios:
        Unexpired portion
        Unexpired portion
          If a customer either purchases additional days in error or doesn't use as many days as they thought they needed, they can request a refund only if the request is received prior to the UVP expiry date. The first day is un-refundable.
        Duplicate UVP purchased in error
        Duplicate UVP purchased in error
          If a customer purchased duplicate UVP's in error they can request a refund if they are for the same vehicle, date, time and route (includes if purchased by different family members).
        System issues
        System issues
          If a customer was charged more than once for a UVP, or another issue occurred during the UVP purchase process, refer to CSB systems for investigation. CSB Systems will advise if a system issue occurred.
        Other
        Other
          Any other requests (e.g. purchased two permits, one with wrong details) can be assessed using Manager's discretion.
