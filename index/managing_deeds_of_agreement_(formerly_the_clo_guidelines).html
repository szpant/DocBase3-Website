Managing Deeds of Agreement (formerly the CLO Guidelines) (6026)

Role of the CLOThe role of the TMR Client Liaison Officer is to be the TMR representative with Rego Easy Clients (REC).
This involves:
  • Determining client eligibility to enter a Rego Easy Scheme using the applicable criteria listed below.
  • Preparation of documentation for new, amendments, renewals and closure applications.
  • Provide training to REC's in their responsibilities under the Deed of Agreement.
  • Monitor the performance of REC.
Eligibility
Requirements		The decision to allow entry onto a Rego Easy Scheme is the primary responsibility of the CLO. The CLO must consider the following:
  • The length of time that the applicant has been a TMR customer.
  • Volume of transactions processed.
  • Customers financial history.
  • Current quality of dealers paperwork.
  • Compliance history.
  • Business requirements of the applicant.
  • Location of dealership must be in Qld or in close proximity of the Qld border.

Note:A dealership located where a Qld registered operator may reasonably travel to purchase a vehicle is considered to be within close proximity.

Rego Easy Pay
Rego Easy Pay
    To be eligible to enter into any of the Rego Easy Pay Scheme the applicant must meet the following requirements:
    • Has been a TMR customer, and transacting as an Offline Dealer, for at least three months.
    • Be a legal entity.
    • Transact business in the sale of motor vehicles (new or used vehicles). Please note that the Rego Easy Business scheme does not support heavy motor vehicles with a GVM >4.5t.
    • Licensed under the Property Agents and Motor Dealers Act 2000 (Qld) or equivalent Interstate Motor Dealer Licensing Act (used car dealership only).
    • Have a good financial history with TMR, including no history of dishonoured payments or failed direct debits.
    • Be located in Qld or within close proximity of the Qld border.

Rego Easy Business
Rego Easy Business
    Clients participating in the Rego Easy Business Scheme are provided authorisation to conduct registration transactions on behalf of TMR using approved DAIS software. All Rego Easy Business clients must use supported software provided by a TMR approved software provider (ASP).

    To be eligible to enter the Rego Easy Business scheme, the client must have a computer terminal with approved DAIS software located and used at the nominated DX location.

    If transactions are to be completed at an alternate site (for example, central location processing work for numerous Dealerships) then additional information and approval for Centralised Processing must be given at the time of application.

    Agreement RequirementsThe Deed of Agreement must be executed (signed) by a customer that is a legal entity.
    Refer: Customer > Customer Types to identify relevant legal entities.

    Where the application is an individual the execution (signing) must be witnessed. This witness may be the CLO.

    Where the legal entity is a partnership, all partners are required to execute the Deed of Agreement. To accommodate this, additional copies of the signatory page can be printed as required.

    The following sections of the Agreement must be completed:
    • Cover page by inserting the name of the legal entity.
    • Execution (signatory) page at the end of the Agreement (before schedule 1) where:
      • The first section must be left blank for the Director of Customer Service Operations to sign. The following sections on the page are completed depending on the legal entity type (Company or Individual).

    Note:Generally the Registration Clerk's details are provided in Schedule 3 Item 2

    Deed of Guarantee
    Personal/Bank    		A Deed of Guarantee must be completed by the legal entity unless a bank guarantee of at least $30,000 is provided or an exemption is granted by the Rego Easy Team.

    The customer is responsible for organising a bank guarantee with their financial institution. The CLO is to ensure that the dealer data base and the file has it clearly noted that a Bank Guarantee is held. The amount of the guarantee and expiry date is to be recorded at each renewal of the Deed of Agreement.

    Public listed companies are eligible for a Cross Deed of Guarantee with the department, therefore do not require the Deed of Guarantee to be prepared or signed as part of the Agreement documents. The Cross Deed of Guarantee is renewable every five years with the agreement and a credit check is completed at this time to determine credit rating risk. Rego Easy team will complete the credit check upon receipt of the signed agreement. The CLO is to ensure that the dealer data base and the file has it clearly noted that Cross Deed of Guarantee is held by the public listed company.

    The Deed of Guarantee must be signed by a signatory to the Deed of Agreement and all parties to the Deed of Guarantee must be recorded in Schedule to Deed of Guarantee. Signatures must be witnessed.

    The original Deed of Guarantee/Bank Guarantee must be forwarded to the Rego Easy Unit

    Please note that the:
    • Bank Guarantee term is for the currency of the Deed of Agreement.
    • original Bank Guarantee will be returned to the client should they exit the Vehicle Registration Scheme or when the Bank Guarantee expires.
    • A Bank Guarantee will be accepted with or without an expiry date.

    Renewals

    The Rego Easy Team will assess application and provide pre-approval notification by email to the CSC/CLO. The CSC/CLO must then forward all original documents by mail to the Rego Easy Team, along with a copy of the pre-approval email. Do not send original documentation until pre-approval has been provided.

    The Rego Easy Team will finalise the application once the original documents are received and then take the following action:
    • Email copy of agreement to dealership and CSC/CLO with letter of introduction or renewal.
    • Organise Standard number plates (New Applications - Easy Plates and Business only).
    • Order TICA/TRAILS filing envelopes and organise StarTrack courier stationery and service for weekly filing pick-up (New Applications - Easy Business only).
    • Liaise with DAIS Approved Software Provider to ensure correct set up of software and operator accreditation (New Applications – Easy Business only).

    The set up process undertaken by the Rego Easy Team can take approximately 21 days, from receipt of original documents depending on set up requirements.

    On completion of this set-up the CSC/CLO is then required to conduct an entry evaluation (refer to Performance Evaluation Guidelines).

    Renewal of AgreementsA Deed of Agreement is valid for five years from the date of the signed welcome/renewal letter.

    A new deed of agreement must be provided to Rego Easy at least six weeks prior to the expiry of the current agreement.

    Legal Entity with multiple DX sites
    Legal Entity with multiple DX sites
      Legal entities with more than one DX site only require one Deed of Agreement and one Deed of Guarantee, but a Rego Easy Application must be completed for each site.

      The CSC with the lowest DX number is responsible for the preparation of the Deed of Agreement. It is the responsibility of the CSC/CLO to liaise with other CSC’s to attain all supporting documents for all other DX numbers and return them to Rego Easy Unit as one application.
    Exiting the SchemeA dealership can exit the scheme at any time by providing written notice of their intent.

    TMR may also terminate a dealerships enrolment in the scheme due to poor performance.

    An exit evaluation Rego Easy Performance Evaluation must be conducted when a dealership leaves the scheme.

    A dealership may apply for re-entry three months after they have exited the scheme. It is considered a new application and full requirements must be met.
