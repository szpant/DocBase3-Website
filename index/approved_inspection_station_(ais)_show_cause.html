Approved Inspection Station (AIS) Show Cause (3453)


What it isIf the delegate of the Chief Executive considers a ground exists to amend, suspend or cancel an approval, then the delegate must give the holder a written notice (Show Cause Notice) – stating the proposed action, grounds, facts and circumstances to support the proposed action.

Delegation to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation approval under s19 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995

Please see the following webpage link: Delegations Database

Refer: Inside TMR home page > Policies and Procedures > Delegations > Legislation Services > Delegations > Delegations Database

Grounds to amend, suspend or cancel
    Grounds to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation approval for an AIS/AE are listed under s18 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995.

    A show cause action to amend, suspend or cancel an approval may be triggered by such things as:
    • a complaint raised
    • criminal history check
    • desk top reviews
    • compliance history

    Refer to the Compliance Management Policy and Procedures for accreditation schemes and industry authorities for determining the appropriate action to take when contraventions by an accreditation holder have been identified.

    Compliance Management Policy and Procedure

    Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an accreditation holder when grounds exist to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation.

    Show Cause Policy and Procedure

    In conjunction with the Show Cause Policy and Procedures, refer to the
    Show Cause Process Guideline for Accreditation Holders (Matters of Public Interest and Serious Non-compliance) for further assistance when dealing with Accreditation Holders when grounds to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation exist and there is a Public Interest or serious non-compliance factor.

    Show Cause Process Guideline for Accreditation Holders (Matters of Public Interest and Serious Non-Compliance)
Immediate suspensionThe delegate of the Chief Executive may immediately suspend an approval if necessary to do so in the public interest.

Refer to the Compliance Management Policy and Procedures for accreditation schemes and industry authorities and the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an accreditation holder and determining if grounds exist to immediately suspend an accreditation.

Compliance Management Policy and Procedure
Show Cause Policy and Procedure
NoticesA written notice must be given (served in person or by mail to the postal address) to the accreditation holder when there are grounds as stated under TO(RUM) Act 1995, s18(1) to amend, suspend or cancel a approval.

Refer to the Show Cause Template Letter Guide for template letters to be used when the delegate of the chief executive is proposing amending, suspending or cancelling an accreditation approval.

The decisionIf no representations are received from the accreditation holder, then the decision is made based on the information currently before the delegate of the Chief Executive.

Any submissions made by the accreditation holder must be taken into account when the decision is made by the delegate of the chief executive.

Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an AP when making a decision to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation, take no further action or issue a warning.

Show Cause Policy and Procedure

The accreditation holder must be informed by written notice of the decision.

Decision on reviewOnce the decision has been made by the delegate of the chief executive, an accreditation holder can:

1.Apply to TMR for an internal review of the decision.
2.Apply for a stay of the decision with the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT).
3.Once the original decision has been reviewed by TMR and the reviewed decision is not the decision sought by the accreditation holder, the accreditation holder may seek an external review of TMR’s reviewed decision at QCAT.

Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an accreditation holder who requests a review or stay of the decision to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation.

Show Cause Policy and Procedure

Refer to the Policy and Procedures for matters before the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal on how to manage the QCAT process when an accreditation holder has applied to QCAT for a stay or external review of a decision.
