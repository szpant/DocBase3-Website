    Queensland court
    Queensland court
      Effect of Disqualification
      Any person disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence by a Queensland Court cannot apply for a licence anywhere in Australia during the disqualification period.
      (TO(RUM) Act s.127(6))

      The licence record shows a 'disqualified' sanction and a 'cancelled' status. If unlicensed, a skeleton licence is created to record the sanction.

      Absolute Disqualification
      If a person is convicted of disqualified driving, an absolute disqualification may be imposed by a Magistrate.
    Interstate Jurisdiction
    Interstate Jurisdiction
      Effect of Interstate Disqualification
      A Qld licence holder may be disqualified from driving by an interstate jurisdiction. The person cannot drive in Qld while disqualified by the interstate court.

      A Qld licence will be cancelled as a result of the interstate/overseas disqualification.

      When a person is disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence from another Australian State or Territory, they are also immediately disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence in Queensland.

      They are not afforded a show cause process. The person's licence may be cancelled on notification from the other jurisdiction. The person should be issued with a Cancellation notice. The person should surrender their driver licence to TMR.
      Cancellation notice - QLD licence holder disqualified in another Australian State or Territory

      Court Consent to re-apply for Licence
      On completion of the disqualification period, some interstate disqualifications require the person to apply to the court for consent before the issue of another licence. This only applies if requiring a licence in that state or territory.
      Overseas Disqualification
      Overseas Disqualification
        Effect of Overseas Disqualification
        A Qld licence holder may be disqualified from driving by an overseas jurisdiction. The person cannot drive in Qld while disqualified.

        If the chief executive becomes aware of an overseas disqualification, action should be taken to cancel the customer's licence on this ground through a show cause procedure.

        Refer: Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > Show Cause Licensing and Young Driver Exemption > Reapply after Cancellation> Reapply after Cancellation > Show cause cancellation (overseas disqualification)

      During the disqualification period the person cannot apply for a licence in Qld (e.g. additional classes, replacement).

      If the Qld licence has not been cancelled, the person may resume driving when the disqualification period is over, provided there are no other suspensions/ cancellations are in force.
    Return
    of Licence    		A cancelled licence must be returned to either:
    • A TMR licence issuing office
    • The court where the conviction was recorded

    Refer: Smartcard Guide > Return of Smartcard > Cancelled Licence

    Licence smartcards returned at the court house are forwarded to Garbutt CSC for processing.

    Once the licence is cancelled the customer is automatically sent:
    • Driver Licence Return Notice (F4804) which requests the return of a specific smartcard within 28 days
    • Return/Surrender Declaration (4778) which should be completed by a customer:
      • when surrendering their smartcard in person
      • who has already surrendered the smartcard and either lodged in person or by mail.

    Failure to meet either of the above requirements will result in the matter being referred to TMR Prosecutions.

    Note:A person with a marine licence indicated on their cancelled driver licence may be eligible to apply for a Marine Licence Indicator Card. They will need to visit a licence issuing centre to apply.

Cumulative Disqualifications
Cumulative Disqualifications
    PeriodsCumulative disqualification periods will apply when a person has been disqualified by a court for:
    • more than one drink or drug driving related offences (committed at different times);
      or
    • a drink or drug driving related offence and at the same time they commit a relevant unlicensed driving offence.

    Example:
    A person is serving a 3 month disqualification for drink driving imposed by the court on 25 November 2008 (the 1st offence). On the 26 November 2008 the person is charged with drink driving and disqualified driving. These charges are heard on the 24 December 2008 and the court disqualifies the person for 6 months for the drink driving offence (the 2nd offence) and 2 years for the disqualified driving offence (the third offence. The period of disqualification (6 months) for the 2nd offence will not start until 25 February 2009. The period of disqualification (2 years) for the 3rd offence will not start until 25 August 2009.

    A cumulative disqualifications fact sheet is available on the TMR website and can be accessed from the following link:
    http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Licensing/Licence-suspensions-and-disqualifications.aspx

    Notices
    Written notices
    Written notices
      The Chief Executive is NOT required to give a written notice.

      However, a Cumulative Disqualification Courtesy Notice will be sent by ordinary mail to the customer's postal address (if provided).

      The notice will be sent when a person is disqualified for:
      • 1 drink or drug driving related offence
      • A subsequent drink or drug driving related offence
      • 2 or more drink or drug driving related offences
      • 1 or more drink or drug driving related offence and an unlicensed driving offence that was committed at the same time

      When a subsequent drink or drug driving related offence is recorded, a new notice is sent showing the updated end date of the total disqualification period.

      For a Description of Notices Refer: Fees and Forms > 2. Forms > Forms > Driver Licensing > Cumulative Disqualification Notice

    Re-issue of returned notices

    If a Notice is returned not delivered, the Notice may be re-issued within 28 days of the original issue.

    The re-issued Notice may be served:
    In PersonThe Notice may be printed and handed to the customer.

    Note:This is the preferred option as mail generated Notices are subject to further return.
    By MailThe Notice is generated by the next TICA batch run and sent by mail contractor.

    Note:If a customer's address is updated, TICA will NOT automatically re-issue a returned Notice by mail.
