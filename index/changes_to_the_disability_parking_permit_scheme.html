Changes to the Disability Parking Permit Scheme (7503)

Project NameChanges to the Disability Parking Permit Scheme
WhenMonday 31 August 2020
BackgroundOn 14 July 2020, the Queensland Parliament passed legislation to expand the eligibility criteria for the scheme in Queensland to include people with a vision-impairment who are diagnosed as ‘legally blind’, as defined in the Australian Government’s Social Security Guide under the Social Security Act 1991.

Permits under the new eligibility criteria are provided for people who meet the definition on a temporary and permanent basis. Further, to deter people illegally parking in a disability parking bay, the legislation also increases the current police fine amount to $533, a doubling of the existing fine.

Changes to the eligibility criteria and fine will come into effect on 31 August 2020. People who have been assessed as legally blind should not apply for the scheme until this date.
However, if an application for a legally-blind person is received at a CSC from Monday, 24 August it can be accepted and forwarded to the Disability Parking Unit. The customer should be advised that the application will be held and not processed until 31 August 2020 or after.

Additionally, a new application form Australia Disability Parking Permit - Legally Blind Application F5280 will be made available from Friday, 28 August 2020.
Who will be impacted and how
Members of the Public
Members of the Public
    Members of the public, particularly those diagnosed as legally blind. A new application form and process has been developed for people to who are legally blind to apply to TMR. As with existing permits, applications will be able to be submitted via TMR’s online service on the website, in person at a CSC or QGAP or by mail.
Disability Parking Unit
Disability Parking Unit
    Disability Parking Unit staff will be provided with updated procedures in order to review new applications to ensure all requirements have been met. System changes are being made to facilitate the changes to the scheme.
