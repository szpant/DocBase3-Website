Background
BDM Deceased Data Matching Pilot
BDM Deceased Data Matching Pilot
    Currently, TMR relies on the next of kin or an executor of a deceased estate to notify the department when a customer passes away. Not only is the notification process difficult for the customer but if TMR is not notified of their passing, any camera detected fines are unenforceable and registration concessions granted to the deceased may continue to be claimed.

    To ensure that TMR is notified when a customer has passed away and to help identify deceased customers, a data matching process has been piloted using Australia-wide Fact of Death data from Births Deaths and Marriages (BDM).

    Fact of Death data for the 2017 and 2018 calendar years was used for the purpose of the pilot which matched FOD records against TICA and reported on full, partial and duplicate matches.

    As the pilot was deemed successful, TMR is now investigating phase 2 which will include processing and updating FOD files more regularly (possibly quarterly) and updating fully matched records as Deceased in TICA.
Deceased Estates Policy Changes - EFFECTIVE 01/08/2020
Deceased Estates Policy Changes - EFFECTIVE 01/08/2020
    In addition to the data matching pilot, the deceased estates policy is being streamlined. Insights and recommendations from TMR's Human-Centred Design – Deceased Estates Report were used to develop the following changes, which have been approved by Legal Services:
    • One form for all customers regardless of whether they are an executor, administrator or next of kin:
      • for dealing with multiple vehicle registration/plate products
      • which includes relevant fields to remove the requirement to complete additional forms in certain circumstances
      • which includes a statutory declaration if required to be completed
    • Amendments to the policy so that:
      • customers who have a grant of probate or letters of administration do not need to sign the statutory declaration part of the new form or present the Will
      • only one executor or administrator has to sign (where multiple executors or administrators exist)
      • where the vehicle is being transferred immediately prior to cancellation (so that the cheque can be issued to another person), standard transfer requirements and forms are not required.

    To assist customers in identifying what they need to provide based on their scenario, a new Help Tool has been developed and will be available online from 01/08/2020. The Help Tool will also be linked from DocBase and can be used by staff when assisting customers.
How will the data matching process work?Robust data matching rules have been developed that compare the Fact of Death data against TICA records to confirm full, partial and duplicate matches.

Once the matching process is complete, a report is generated containing the full, partial and duplicate matches. The report will also indicate if the customer is already deceased on TICA and whether they have current/expired registrations and/or personalised/customised plates.

An operational area of CSB will be tasked with manually confirming/denying the partial and duplicate matches and updating as deceased/completing relevant merging. For the purposes of completing the pilot, CSB will also update full matches, however Phase 2 will look at automating this process when the data matching process is completed.

Once records are updated as deceased (either through the automated or manual process) the existing NORG process will be activated - NORG restriction applied and deceased letter issued if applicable.
Who will be impacted and how?
Operators
Operators
    • May receive calls from next of kin when the deceased letters are generated.
    • Will see that the system ID updated the deceased information rather than an individual user ID where an automatic update occurred (Phase 2).
    • Will no longer need to request or update proof of death as the system has already recorded the customer as deceased.
Customers
Customers
    • May receive a deceased letter where they haven't informed TMR of the customer's death
    • Will not have to:
      • provide proof of death (where a match has been confirmed and updated to the deceased's record)
      • complete a statutory declaration if they have a grant of probate or letters of administration
      • get multiple executors or administators to sign
      • complete a cancellation form if the vehicle is being cancelled
      • meet transfer requirements when the vehicle is transferred immediately prior to cancellation (to enable the check to be issued to another person)
      • complete a transfer form if:
        • transferring to an executor, administrator, beneficiary or someone else entitled under the Succession Act 1981
        • transferring immediately prior to cancelling (to enable the cheque to be issued to another person)
