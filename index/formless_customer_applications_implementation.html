Formless Customer Applications Project (7281)

Project NameFormless Customer Applications Project
When26 March 2018
WhereStatewide
BackgroundThe Formless pilot held at Toowoomba and Harristown was successful in confirming that application forms can be removed with no loss of transaction integrity, a significant increase in customer satisfaction and decreases in the financial cost of creating, maintaining and storing application forms.

CSB with TRB has committed to implement formless customer applications in all TMR led-CSC's and QGAP's. This means non-TMR led QGAP's and QPS offices are excluded from the rollout. RSO’s are also excluded at this stage and advice will be provided if and when these will be rolled out.

To enable the removal of the application form, legislation that prescribed a customer must apply on the approved form, was amended to allow the Director General to specify the method in which the customer may make application.

For the pilot this notification was part of the contact details listed on the TMR website for the pilot locations.

Not all legislation has been amended and TRB is progressively amending as opportunities become available. What this means is that products such as APA cards and Plates still require an application form.

The transactions included are:
  • Driver licence:
    • Issue/re-issue
    • Transfer
    • Renewals
    • Upgrades
    • Replacements
    • Practical driving tests
  • Change Customer Details
    • Where a customer holds a driver licence or vehicle registration
  • Vehicle registration:
    • New business
    • Vehicles < =4.5t
    • Trailers < = 4.5 t
    • All conditional Registration
Implementation PlanThe implementation will be staged as planned to roll out as follows:
1.Applicable Driver Licensing transactions for existing customers (26 March)
2Driver Licensing transactions for all customers (23 April)
3.New business transactions for individuals and organisations (3 September)
How does a formless application work?For eligible transactions in place of the application form, you will ask the customer a series of mandatory questions. These questions are designed to:
  • Obtain the customers consent to verify documents
  • Identify the customer
  • Confirm eligibility for the product
  • Satisfy the requirements of the product.

The mandatory questions will be published in DocBase closer to implementation.

You will still process the transaction the same way you do now. The questions are structured to flow with the transaction and the screens you use.

The customer must also provide all necessary supporting documents. For example to renew a driver licence, while the customer does not need to complete the F3000, they will need to provide a valid medical certificate if they are over 75 or have a relevant medical condition.

At the end of the transaction you will print either the driver licence receipt or TICA registration history report and then you will carefully confirm with the customer the details of the transaction. The customer signs the receipt/report which contains a declaration that the information they provided is true and correct and a copy of the receipt/original report and supporting documentation is filed with your daily filing.

Back office processes such as filing and business exceptions remain primarily unchanged. Variations to existing processes will be communicated and published in DocBase.
TrainingEach region will have a Regional Representative and each location will have a Site Representative to provide support throughout the implementation.

Training for Phase Three will be completed via a short online course in Accelerate. All staff at locations that are accepting formless applications must complete the training in Accelerate in the LED Session on 29 August. The course will include:
  • New policy and procedure information related to Formless Phase 3
  • Information on the new Operator Evaluation Log
  • Activities to consolidate staff’s understanding of formless processes
  • Links to DocBase changes and mandatory questions for registration transactions

Training is mandatory and will be recorded in Accelerate
