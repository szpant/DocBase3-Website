Note:		A Northern Territory (NT) open licence with a XI, XJ or XH condition is issued to customers who:
  • transferred to the NT licence from a non-recognised country, or
  • have not held a licence in the last 5 years.

If the customer transferred to the NT licence from a non-recognised country, they are eligible for an open Queensland licence once they pass a practical driving test.

If they have not held another licence in the last 5 years they are eligible for a Queensland learner licence.

Customers are required to meet the minimum age requirements
Refer: Learner Licence Guide or Provisional/Probationary Licence Guide
New Zealand
Note: Paper Licence Validity
Note: Paper Licence Validity
    From 3 May 1999 NZ paper driver licence remains valid and may be used to obtain a QLD driver licence up to 5 years & 3 months from return date to NZ.
Note: 'Reinstate' status on Certificate of Particulars
Note: 'Reinstate' status on Certificate of Particulars
    If a NZ licence holder has the licence status 'Reinstate' on their
    Certificate of Particulars it means their period of suspension or disqualification has ended but they have not applied to the NZ Transport Agency to get their licence reinstated. They should not be directed to a NZ consulate or embassy, because the only way they can get their NZ licence reinstated is to return to NZ and visit the NZTA.

    The NZTA has now agreed to indicate in the additional comments on a relevant Certificate of Particulars if the person had been disqualified and is therefore eligible for a Qld probationary licence, or suspended and is therefore eligible for a Qld learner/provisional/open licence.
