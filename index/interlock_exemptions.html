Interlock Exemptions (3241)

What it isAn interlock exemption allows a person who holds a current Qld driver licence with an ’I’ condition to drive a vehicle that is not fitted with a prescribed interlock and nominated to TMR. A copy of the fact sheet can be obtained from:
https://www.qld.gov.au/transport/safety/road-safety/drink-driving/penalties/interlocks/index.html
Who may ApplyA person who is subject to the AIIP.
When can application be made A person subject to the AIIP as a result of a Queensland drink driving offence can submit an application for an interlock exemption up to six weeks before the end of their relevant disqualification period or at any time during their interlock period.

The holder of a non-Qld licence with an ‘I’ condition who will be subject to the AIIP on licence transfer can submit an application for an interlock exemption at any time.
EligibilityA person may be eligible for an exemption from the interlock condition if one of the following circumstances applies.

Interlock Exemption (Medical Condition)
Interlock Exemption (Medical Condition)
    TMR will approve the grant of an exemption if satisfied that the applicant has a medical condition that prevents them from providing a specimen of breath for analysis by an approved interlock.

    Note: A treating doctor must advise of the applicant's specific rate of breath (over a continuous period of 5 seconds) on the application form e.g. "1.2 litres over a continuous period of 5 seconds".

    Please ensure that this assessment is clearly indicated as advice such as "less than 1.2 litres..." will not be acceptable.
TO(RUM) Act 1995 s91Q(3)
Interlock Exemption (Geographical)
Interlock Exemption (Geographical)
    Remote Area
    TMR will approve the grant of an exemption if satisfied that the applicant’s principal place of residence is outside a radius of 150km from the place of business of the nearest prescribed interlock installer.
TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s63J
    Island Living
    TMR will approve the grant of an exemption if satisfied that the applicant’s principal place of residence is on a Queensland island (excluding those listed below) and
    • a prescribed interlock installer does not have a place of business on the island; and
    • the island is not connected by a bridge to the mainland or to another island that is connected by a bridge to the mainland

    The following islands are not eligible islands for an interlock exemption: Coochiemudlo Island; Fraser Island; Karragarra Island; Lamb Island; Macleay Island; Magnetic Island; North Stradbroke Island; Orpheus Island or Russell Island.
TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s63I
Interlock Exemption (Severe hardship)
Interlock Exemption (Severe hardship)
    TMR will only approve the grant of an exemption on severe hardship grounds if satisfied that one of the following circumstances applies:
    (a)It is not physically possible to fit an approved interlock to the only motor vehicle reasonably available for the applicant to drive; AND
    • refusal to grant the exemption would cause severe hardship to the applicant or a member of their family; AND
    • no other transport is reasonably available to the applicant or the family member.
    (b)A family member of the applicant has a medical condition (as evidenced by a medical certificate) that prevents the family member from providing a sufficient breath sample to operate an approved interlock; AND
    • there is only 1 motor vehicle reasonably available to be driven by the applicant or family member; AND
    • refusal to grant the exemption would cause severe hardship to the applicant; AND
    • no other transport is reasonably available to the applicant or the family member.
    (c)Refusal to grant the exemption would cause severe hardship to the applicant in a way other than by preventing them from driving:
    • in the course of their employment, to or from their place of employment or to or from an educational institution that they attend; AND
    • no other transport is reasonably available to the applicant.
    (d)Refusal to grant the exemption would cause severe hardship to a family member of the applicant in a way other than by preventing the applicant from driving the family member:
    • to or from the family member’s place of employment or to or from an educational institutions the family member attends; AND
    • no other transport is reasonably available to the family member
TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s63K

    Important Note:
    An interlock exemption cannot be granted on the grounds that the person cannot afford the cost of the interlock or they are unable to fit an interlock because they need to drive for employment or educational reasons.


For more information about interlock exemptions and requirements, please refer to the TMR website:
https://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Licensing/Licence-suspensions-and-disqualifications/Alcohol-igni
tion-interlocks.aspx#exemptions
Application RequirementsThe applicant must:
  • Complete and submit an Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program – Interlock Exemption Application (F4862) (available from DLIC’s or the TMR website)
  • Provide supporting documentation where necessary (refer Application (Document) Requirements Checklist below)

For more information about the supporting documentation required, operators may contact the Interlock Processing Unit
Lodgement ChecklistWhen an application is lodged in a CSC, the operator must complete the Checklist Template and email the application and supporting documents to the AIIP.IPU

Any applications forwarded to the IPU hub incorrectly (including form not completed/signed, email template not used etc) will be reported to an operators SASM as feedback via the Feedback Register. If the customer does not provide all supporting documents, inform them that the IPU will be in contact about the missing documents.
Application ProcessThe person may submit the application:
  • In person at a TMR Driver Licence Issuing Centre
  • By mail to Interlock Processing Unit
Further InformationThe chief executive may request further information that it reasonably believes is required to make a decision about the application. The request will be made in writing and will allow the applicant at least 28 days to provide the information.

If the applicant fails to provide the requested information within the stated timeframe the chief executive may declare that the application is withdrawn. If the application is withdrawn the person will receive a written notice stating:
  • The reasons for the application being withdrawn
  • That the person apply for a reconsideration of the decision
TO(RUM) Act 1995 s91Q
Assessment of ApplicationIn deciding whether to issue an interlock exemption, the chief executive must ensure the eligibility criteria are met. TMR has 28 days in which to decide whether to grant an interlock exemption, following which the applicant must be notified in writing of the outcome.

Application GrantedIf the application is granted the person will receive a written notice and an Exemption Certificate.

Change of CircumstancesInterlock exemptions are granted based on specific circumstances; if any of these circumstances change the person must notify TMR in writing within 14 days of the change occurring. The circumstances that the person must notify TMR about are outlined in their approval letter.

The interlock exemption will stop having effect 14 days after TMR is notified of the change.
If the person’s new circumstances will meet the criteria for an interlock exemption the person must submit a new application.

For example, if a person was granted an interlock exemption based on their residential address being outside a 150km radius from the nearest prescribed interlock installer and they move house, they must notify TMR in writing of the move and the exemption will cease to have effect 14 days after the notification is received.

If the person’s circumstances change in a way that is not relevant to the criteria on which the exemption was granted and they require the change to be reflected on their Exemption Certificate they may apply for an amendment. These changes are limited to those relating to the restrictions such as if they were only permitted to drive on Mondays and due to appointments changing they now need to drive on Thursdays.
Requests for amended or replacement exemption certificatesIf a person requires an amended or replacement certificate they may request one in writing.

These requests do not require:
  • an application form
  • a fee
  • TICA processing by the DLIC

The person may submit the request:
  • In person at a TMR Driver Licence Issuing Centre, or
  • By mail to:

    Interlock Processing Unit
    Department of Transport and Main Roads
    GPO Box 1412
    Brisbane Qld 4000

Interlock Exemption – Cease to have effectAn interlock exemption will cease to have effect:
  • the day after the certificate expiry date; or
  • 14 days from the date the holder advises TMR their circumstances have changed (refer Change of Circumstances); or
  • the date advised by TMR if it is cancelled as a result of show cause action; or
  • the day after a person completes their prescribed period; or
  • the day after the person’s interlock period ends; or
  • if the holder’s driver licence is cancelled because of a disqualification for a relevant drink driving offence, the day the disqualification is imposed. Refer: Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > Alcohol Ignition Interlock Program > Convicted and Disqualified for Queensland offence/s
