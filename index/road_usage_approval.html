Alternative Compliance
Alternative Compliance
    What is it?Alternative Compliance (ALTC) allows individuals or organisations accredited under TO(RUM) Act 1995 or the Heavy Vehicle National Law (Qld)to be:
    • exempt from the requirement of supplying a Certificate of Inspection (COI) if approved under the Maintenance Module (ATMT)
    • to carry loads above general mass limits if approved under the Mass Module (ATMS)

    Customer Qualification - ALTC
    Req'ments
    • Operators must be participating in the Mass Module (ATMS) and/or Maintenance Module (ATMT) of the National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS).

      Note:Contact details for NHVAS issues is nhvas@tmr.qld.gov.au
    ApplicationApplying for ALTC

    Applicant's Role
    Applicant's Role
      Applicants seeking ALTC are required to apply to the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR) for accreditation under the Mass Module and/or Maintenance Module. Details on how to apply are available on the NHVR website or directly at the URL:

      https://www.nhvr.gov.au/safety-accreditation-compliance/national-heavy-vehicle-accreditation-scheme/applications-and-forms

      Applications and fees are not to be accepted at TMR CSCs or agencies.
Remote Area
Remote Area
    What is it?The remote area road usage exempts vehicles within designated areas from providing a COI or Safety Certificate at new business, transfer and renewal.

    Road Usage Code - RMTE

    If inspection is requested at renewal:

    Add a Remote Area 'RMTE' qualification to both:
    • The Customer qualification screen
    • The Registration Road Usage screen
    • Once the renewal is paid, expire the RMTE qual on both screens
    Req'mentsVehicle must be garaged in a remote area
    Rules
    Safety Certificate Vehicle Exemptions
    COI Vehicle Exemptions

    Note: Partially Exempt areas and Islands are not exempt COI. This exemption applies to safety certificate vehicles only

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Inspections > Cert of Insp > Exemptions > Exempt Areas
Route Service Approved
Route Service Approved
    What is it?This road usage approval allows individuals/organisations who operate a commercially scheduled bus service on approved routes to carry standing passengers.

    Road Usage Code: RSER
    Req'ments
    • Statement from the registered operator that they hold or intend to apply for authority from the Passenger Transport Division
    • Approved areas of operation
