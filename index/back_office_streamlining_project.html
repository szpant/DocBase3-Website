Back Office Streamlining Project (5359)


Project NameBack Office Streamlining (BOS)
WhenEstimated project finalisation early 2016
BackgroundIn 2014, Astute Management Consulting completed a resource analysis of TMR’s Customer Service Centres (CSC) to identify the proportion of back office work completed across the service delivery network versus core customer service activities.

The report produced as a result of this analysis included several recommendations to streamline and reduce back office work in CSCs, in an effort to release capacity in the network so more customer service activities could be delivered.

Since the completion of the report, changes have been made to a number of processes, policies and systems which have addressed some of the recommendations outlined in the report. A summary of these changes is outlined below.

Summary of implemented recommendations
Summary of implemented recommendations
    Identified task (Astute Report)Work completed to date
    Industry Authority assessment and follow upAs a result of the Industry Licensing and Driver Authorisation Lean initiatives, a trial of centralised application assessment and processing hubs began in February 2015.

    Refer: IA Hub Pilot project update for more information
    Court documentationCourt certificates were reviewed and updated templates included in DocBase in early 2014.
    CSC Equipment (printers)As a result of the OKI printer replacement project, Kyocera laser printers are now in use across the state. These printers are multi-function and reduce the time it takes to print, and the noise level of printing in CSCs.
    Internet access for CSC staffAs a result of the implementation of the Microsoft Upgrade Project, all staff now have internet access.
    Pre-Registration Inspections (PRI)As a result of the New Registration Lean initiative, a three phase review of the PRI process has been initiated. The first phase of this review (implemented on 1 September 2014) saw the requirement to conduct PRI on light vehicles presenting for registration removed (where there is no issue identified with the vehicle identifiers).
    Online servicesSeveral new online services have been implemented since this report was generated, and at the end of June 2014, 57.6% of all transactions were completed through self-service channels (online services, IVR etc).
    Forms reviewA full review of all TMR forms is underway, with a view to minimise the number of forms required to complete transactions with TMR, and improve and simplify the content and layout of forms for customers and staff.
    NQDLThe Smartcard Rightsizing project in September 2013 saw the removal of the requirement to capture a PIN and Shared Secret.
    The Customer Interaction Device (CID) replacement project, which began in November 2014 saw the roll out of new signature capture technology, resulting in a decrease in customer complaints about the signature capture devices in CSCs.
    CSC ManagementSeveral CSCs across the state have implemented a concierge function, to provide an initial point of contact and advice for customers entering the Centre.

The BOS project addresses the key remaining activities identified in the report, in an effort to reduce the back office/administrative burden on CSCs state-wide.
