Registration Transfer Online (7036)

What is it?Registration Transfer Online (RTO) was introduced on 1 August 2016 and is an online service that allows the disposer or acquirer of a vehicle or ship to transfer a registration online, without either party needing to present an application at a CSC or agency.

The RTO service enables the disposer to complete a Notice Of Disposal (NOD) and provide their consent to transfer the vehicle.

There is no need to complete an application form with signatures if customers utilise this service. It is recommended that the Registration Transfer Form F3520 or F3521) be completed as a backup.
RulesAn acquirer is able to lodge their part of the transfer online if the disposer has lodged a NOD either online or in person.

If the disposer completed the NOD using the RTO, the acquirer will receive an email notification advising they can complete the transfer. If the acquirer does not receive an email, they may still complete the transfer online by logging in through the RTO service.

Refer: Services > 1.Online Services > TMR Online Services Guide > TMR Online Services List

When the service can be used
    The service can be used if:
    • The disposer has:
      • an active valid email address and can login to online services using extra details
      • the acquirer's first and last name, Driver Licence number/QLD CRN, email address
      • date of sale
      • odometer reading (if applicable)
      • dutiable value (if applicable)
      • acquirers purpose of use (if applicable)
      • storage method, place and postcode (if applicable)
    • The acquirer has:
      • an active valid email address and can login to online services using extra details
      • sellers first and last name
      • an Australian American Express, Visa or MasterCard credit card/debit card
      • odometer reading (if applicable)
      • dutiable value (if applicable)
      • acquirers purpose of use (if applicable)
      • storage method, place and postcode (if applicable)
    • The registration is being transferred into one acquirer only
    • The acquirer is an individual
    • One of the disposers is an individual
    • The vehicle’s purpose of use is private or commercial
    • There is no exemption from providing a safety certificate (excluding vehicles/ships which are automatically exempt)
    • The buyer is at least 18 years old (heavy vehicle transfers only)
    • A current safety certificate or COI is provided for vehicles and trailers (with an aggregate trailer mass over 750kg), this can be in the form of an:
      • assigned electronic safety certificate
        or
      • certificate of inspection, or an unassigned or hand written certificate that has been recorded in TICA

    For information referring to assigned and unassigned certificates Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Inspections
When the service cannot be used
Acquirer Email Notification
Incorrect Acquirer email notification
Acquirer email notification not received
