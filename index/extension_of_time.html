Extension of Time (7156)


Extension of Time (EOT)
What it isAn Extension of Time (EOT) allows:
  • a vehicle to legally operate on a road for a specified period of time:
    • after the expiry date of the last Certificate Of Inspection (COI)
    • time to clear a defect notice after the time allowed has elapsed
    • the registration to be paid if the COI has expired
Legislations29 Transport Operations Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010
When required
When Required
When Required
    The Registered operator of a COI vehicle may apply for an EOT:
    • when the operator is unable to book an inspection with SBS to obtain a COI within two months of the expiry date of the last COI.
    • To allow the renewal of a registration where the:
    • Customer has been issued a defect notice and/or
    • Registration renewal is due before the booking date.
When not requiredTMR Inspectable Vehicles that have a booking in SBS for an inspection within 2 months of their Certificate of Inspection (COI) expiry date, do not need to have an EOT recorded in TRAILS/TICA. They are automatically deemed as having an approved Extension of Time, and therefore compliant with legislation.
Eligibility for EOTA registered operator is eligible to apply for an EOT if the vehicle registration is expired or current.
Customers are not eligible for an EOT:
  • if the registration has been cancelled (either voluntarily or automatically)
  • an SBS inspection booking is reasonably available
  • special circumstances do not exist.
SBS booking more then 2 months
SBS booking more then 2 months
    • If the next available SBS booking is more than two months from the expiry date of the last COI, one of the following special circumstances must apply:
    • Due to unforeseen circumstances by the customer. For example:
    • When travelling to an inspection there was a traffic incident or mechanical breakdown and the customer is unable to rebook in SBS within the time frame
    • A death in the immediate family has occurred
    • Hospitalisation or illness suffered by the registered operator
    • There are no bookings in SBS available within a reasonable distance. As a guide it is not reasonable to expect the customer to travel more than 50km or 40 minutes to obtain a COI.
    • The vehicles garage address is an area where a Natural Disaster has been declared or the vehicle has been impacted by the natural disaster eg road closures means the vehicle cannot travel.
    • Closure of inspection locations due to natural disaster or technical issues with the inspection equipment.
    • Each case must be judged on its merits.
Time to comply with a defect notice
Time to comply with a defect notice
    If the time required to comply with a defect notice has expired:
    • and the repairs have not been completed
    • and the authorised officer is satisfied that issuing the EOT:
      • would not create an unacceptable safety risk; and
      • the operator can demonstrate that they have taken necessary steps to have the vehicle repaired within the period stated in the defect notice.
      For further information refer: Extension of Time - Defect Notice Issued
Compliance Discretion
Compliance Discretion
    Authorised Officers in Compliance have the discretion to issue an EOT. When making this decision they should consider the current operators inspection history. For example, a customer who has a history of obtaining an EOT to extend the COI period or who has had multiple EOT's for a single inspection period should not be allowed to continue this practice without a reasonable excuse.

    Authorised Officers in Compliance may impose reasonable and relevant conditions on the EOT, for example. The operator may be required to supply a completed Motor Vehicle Mechanical Inspection form F2542, to ensure the vehicle is in a safe and serviceable condition to be used on a road, or the vehicle may not be used on a road until the operator obtains a current COI.

    Compliance Authorised Officers may issue an EOT for more than two months when special circumstance apply.
Issue periodAn EOT can only be issued for a period up to 2 months unless special circumstances exist.
Applying for an EOT and who issues an EOT
Applying for an EOT
Applying for an EOT
    Applications can be made by the registered operator directly to SBS or Compliance, based on the applicant's circumstances outlined in the table below under Who can issue an EOT.

    If a customer requires an EOT to pay a registration renewal, the customer should be referred to SBS.

    Note:The registration cannot be paid until the EOT application has been approved.

    An Extension of Time Application form F2483 may be required.

Issuing an EOT
Issuing an EOT
    An EOT can only be issued from TRAILS.
    Refer: HOW TO Issue an EOT procedure.
    Unless the customer specifically requests a copy, printing the EOT is not required.
    To stop TRAILS print the EOT:
    • type SPAR in the command line at the bottom of the screen
    • remove the front office printer details and update

Refer: Vehicle Rego > 0.5 Vehicle Requirements > The How To: Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Requirements Procedures > Inspections > Assess EOT Application
Rules
Effective date of EOT
Effective date of EOT
    An EOT is effective from the date of application. If the previous COI has expired the vehicle cannot be used on the road until either an EOT has been approved or a COI issued.
Effective date of COI where EOT was granted
Effective date of COI where EOT was granted
    The COI expiry date is not affected if an EOT is issued.
EOT for Defective Vehicles
EOT for Defective Vehicles
    Refer: Extension of Time - Defect Notice Issued
EOT for expired vehicles
EOT for expired vehicles
    An EOT can be issued if the registration has expired or it can be voluntarily cancelled and a new business transaction performed.

    TMR does not encourage or support a currently registered vehicle being voluntarily cancelled to avoid a TMR inspection.

    Standard rules for moving an unregistered vehicle apply.
