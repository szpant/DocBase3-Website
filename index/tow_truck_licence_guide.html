Tow Truck Licence Guide (1889)


What it isA tow truck licence authorises the licence holder to operate tow trucks in defined circumstances within regulated areas.
When required A tow truck licence is required to operate a tow truck in a regulated area to:
  • Tow a damaged vehicle from the scene of a crash
  • Tow a vehicle seized by Police under the Police Powers and Responsibility Act 2000
  • Tow a vehicle from private property where a written contract and Towing Consent is in place between the licence holder and the property owner/occupier authorising the removal of vehicles from the property

A licensed tow truck may be used for non-regulated towing services for example vehicle break downs and trade towing.
When not requiredA tow truck licence is not required for areas outside the regulated areas and for breakdown and trade towing work.

    Proof of Business/ CompanyIf the application is in a business or company name, proof of the business/company must be provided, along with the name/s of the individual/s who own/operate the business. This can be done by submitting either of the following with the application:
    • a business certificate of registration or business name summary issued by ASIC, displaying the proprietor's name/s
    • a certificate of registration of a company with all company directors listed.

    The names of the individuals behind the business/company name must be recorded in SILAS with their CRN from TICA.
    EOIStandard EOI is required for the company/business and for each operator/director.
    Assessing Applicant's SuitabilityAn applicant's suitability to hold a tow truck authority must be assesed using the following criteria.

    Criminal History
    Criminal History

      A criminal history check must be conducted for all persons applying for the grant or renewal of a tow truck licence or certificate, including:
      • all operators listed on new applications
      • all operators at renewal
      • all new operators/directors when added to an existing licence

      A criminal history report obtained for another purpose, such as a different transport authority cannot be used for a tow truck licence or certificate.

      Only criminal histories that are questionable or deemed unsuitable are printed and retained for filing.

      Refer:
      Assessing Suitability for Tow Truck Licence or Certificate
    Traffic History
    Traffic History
      Traffic history checks must be completed for all operators/directors for:
      • New applications
      • Renewal applications

      The check must be undertaken in all Australian states and New Zealand where the applicant has indicated on the application that they have:
      • lived in the last 10 years
      • traffic history in the jurisdiction.

      Only traffic histories that are questionable or deemed unsuitable are printed and retained for filing

      Refer:
      Assessing Suitability for Tow Truck Licence or Certificate
    Previous Conduct
    Previous Conduct
      Any history of non-compliant behaviour while the holder of a tow truck licence must be considered when assessing the applications. This may include period of suspension of a previously held tow truck licence.

      Refer:
      Assessing Suitability for Tow Truck Licence or Certificate
    Approved Premises, Holding Yards and Tow TrucksThe business premises, holding yards and tow trucks must be inspected by a Transport Inspector before an application can be processed.

    The purpose of this inspection is to ensure:
    • The business has the required processes in place to operate a compliant regulated tow truck business
    • The premises and holding yard/s meet the requirements of the legislation
    • The vehicle/s meet the requirements of the legislation.
    Exemption from the requirement to have holding yards
    Exemption from the requirement to have holding yards
      The holder of Class 4 tow truck licence may apply for an exemption from having a holding yard in recognition that the heavy vehicles being towed are most likely being towed to the registered operators nominated location and do not require storage.

      The licence holder must only have a licence for Class 4 tow trucks, if they add any other class of tow truck the exemption is void.

      The application for an exemption must be made in writing to the Manager (Customer Service Centre) or Regional Director.

      A new exemption request must be made with renewal of the licence or when a tow truck is added to the licence.

    Towing Consent - Private Vehicle Towing
      Licence holders must enter into a written contract with the property owner/occupier before allowing drivers to tow vehicles from the property. Once a written contract is in place a Towing Consent must also be signed. The Towing Consent is evidence that the contract exists.
    Towing Authority Books - Incident/seizure towing
    Towing Authority Books - Incident/seizure towing
      A towing authority book is required for each tow truck listed on the licence. These forms authorise the driver of the tow truck to tow a vehicle that has been damaged as a result of a collision or impact, or is a seized by QPS.

      Once the entire book is completed it should be kept in a safe place at the licence holder's registered place of business for a minimum of 12 months.

        Towing authority books must only be issued to tow truck licence holders or an authorisedrepresentative therefore, prior to issuing a towing authority book, the CSC operator must check SILAS to confirm the book is being issued to a tow truck licence holder or their representative. The licence holder representative must provide written authority from the licence holder authorising them to purchase Towing Authority Book on their behalf. The representative’s driver licence should be sighted before selling the Towing Authority Book.

        Tow Truck Numbers
        Tow Truck Numbers
          A four digit tow truck number is required to be displayed on both sides of each tow truck on the licence. The four digit tow truck number is non-transferable and when a tow truck is modified, transferred, sold or otherwise removed from the licence for any reason, the number must be removed from the vehicle and destroyed. The number must not to be used on the same or any other tow truck once removed.

          Issue Tow Truck Number
          Each CSC is required to have a register that records the:
          • tow truck numbers available for allocation
          • tow truck numbers allocated with their relevant:
          • registration number of the vehicle
          • name of the business/company
          • tow truck licence number
          • date of issue

          The tow truck number is also required to be recorded in SILAS against the vehicle details and on the application form.

          Once the application is approved:
          • Allocate a tow truck number from the CSC register

        Transactions
        New application
        New application
          Before an application may be finalised in SILAS:
          • All eligibility requirements must have been met
          • Evidence of identity documents must be provided for all business owners/proprietors or company director/s
          • Criminal history requests finalised
          • Traffic history assessed
          • The customer must pay all prescribed fees
          • A completed and signed holding yard*, business premises (F5213) and tow truck vehicle inspection (F5212) (for each vehicle listed on the licence) must have been received from the Transport Inspector who conducted the inspections. Both forms must be approved by the Transport Inspector.

          Refer to 'Eligibility' section above for a checklist which can be given to the applicant to prepare for the site visit from the Transport Inspectors.

          *Unless exempt (Refer above: Approved Premises, Holding Yards and Tow Trucks
          If any of the prerequisites are not met, the application is not to be approved.
          Term of licence
          Term of licence
            New licenses are issued for 12 months only. The CSC operator should check that the expiry date printed on the licence and recorded in SILAS is for a 12 month term.
        Transfer
        Transfer
          Tow truck licences are not transferable therefore, where all the operators (ie legal entities) are changing or the business is sold a new application must be lodged. All requirements outlined in the New Applications section must be completed by the new applicant.

          Where it is a change of name or they are adding removing operators and at least one legal entity is remaining the same refer to Amending a Tow truck licence for the requirements.

          Please refer to Cancel/Suspend a tow truck licencefor information regarding the voluntary surrender/cancellation of tow truck licences which have been sold or 'transferred'.
        Renewal
        Renewal
          Before a licence can be renewed in SILAS:
          • All eligibility requirements above must have been met
          • Evidence of Identity documents must be provided for any new business owner/s operator/s or company director/s
          • Proof of business/company must be provided
          • The customer must pay all prescribed fees
          • A criminal history search must be conducted for all business owner/s operator/s or company director/s on the licence. Fee exempted with reason Investigation.
          • Traffic history assessed

          If any of the prerequisites are not met, the licence must not be renewed.

          Note:A decision must be made to grant, grant with conditions or refuse the application within 3 months from when the application was lodged.

          Term of licence
          Term of licence
            At renewal licence holders can choose from terms of 1, 2 ,3 4 or 5 years. The CSC operator should check that the expiry date printed on the licence and recorded in SILAS is for the correct term and matches with the fee paid.
        Amending a Tow Truck Licence
        Amending a Tow Truck Licence
          Change of details
          Change of details
            The holder of a Tow Truck Licence is required to notify TMR of any of the following change of details within 14 days:
            • a change of the holder’s name
            • a change of the holder’s address (including postal address, if one is recorded)
            • the damage, loss or theft of the licence document
            • if the holder or a business owner/s operator/s or company director/s is:
              • convicted of a disqualifying offence; or
              • charged with a disqualifying offence and the charge has not been finally disposed of; or
              • convicted of an offence against the Act.
            • if the holder is a business/company, the appointment of new business owner/s operator/s or company director/s OR the removal of existing business owner/s operator/s or company director/s

            Where there is a change to the licence holder's name, or a new business owner/s operator/s or company director/s is added to the licence, a criminal history check is required on the new name/new owner/s operator/s/director/s, and a CrimTrac fee must be charged. Refer: Fees above.

          Adding/removing vehicles
          Adding/removing vehicles
            Adding Vehicles
            Adding Vehicles
              Adding vehicles
              Where a tow truck licence holder wishes to add a vehicle to the licence, they are required to:
              • attend a CSC to have the licence amended, with a completed Tow Truck Licence Application Form (F4644)
              • provide a certificate of inspection for each new tow truck, issued by a TMR Transport Inspector
              • have had a Tow Truck Licence Vehicle Inspection(F5212) for each new tow truck approved by a TMR Transport Inspector
              • pay the prescribed fee*

              Once the Tow Truck Licence Application application is lodged the application must be scanned and emailed to your Compliance Office who will schedule the vehicle inspections.

              The inspection must be completed within 10 business days.

              Once completed the Transport Inspector will sign the inspection forms (F5212) indicating the vehicle/s are approved then scan and forward them by email to the lodging office.

              *Where a customer is adding and removing a tow truck on the same application, there is no fee payable.

              Each new tow truck must be registered in Queensland with a purpose of use LTOW, and meet the vehicle requirements outlined in the Tow Truck Act 1973 and Tow Truck Regulation 2009 before it can be added to the licence.

              The tow truck licence holder must pay the prescribed fee to add each new tow truck.

              Refer: Fees and Forms>1.Fees>Fees (2018-2019)>Industry Authority>Prorata Fees

              Refer: Eligibility Requirements > Approved Premises, Holding Yards and Tow Trucks > Tow Trucks above for tow truck vehicle classes

              Refer: Transport Compliance > 11. Tow Trucks for more information

            Removing Vehicles
            Removing Vehicles
              Removing vehicles
              Where the holder of a tow truck licence intends to remove a vehicle from the licence, they must:
              • attend a CSC to have the licence amended, with a completed Tow Truck Licence Application Form (F4644)
              • return any towing authority books issued for the tow truck to TMR
              • remove any markings identifying the tow truck as belonging to that licence before disposing of the vehicle
              • within 7 days of disposing of a tow truck, notify TMR in writing of the name and address of the person acquiring the tow truck

              Note:If a tow truck licence holder removes all vehicles from their licence they have 3 months to attach another tow truck to the licence before they must surrender the licence.


          Change/addition of holding yard
          Change/addition of holding yard
            A TMR holding yard inspection must be approved on form F5213 before the holding yard can be added to SILAS.

          Refunds
          Refunds
            Where an application has been refused (not approved) or withdrawn by the applicant, the applicant is entitled to a refund of the fees paid (provided the fees were not exempted).

            These fees should be credited to the customers account to be refunded if required. The refund should be authorised the same day as the refusal letter is sent.

            Where an application has been lodged and the criminal history search has NOT been requested by the CSC operator, the criminal history fee may be refunded.
        Cancel/Suspend/Surrender a Tow Truck Licence
        Cancel/Suspend/Surrender a Tow Truck Licence
          A cancellation/suspension/surrender is recorded in SILAS on advice from enforcement officers, TMR, Court or Police.

          A tow truck licence can also be voluntarily cancelled/surrended if a tow truck operator has sold their business (including the tow trucks attached to the licence), or if the business is no longer in operation.

          This can be done by providing TMR with written notification of the intent to cancel, with a date specified and surrender their tow truck licence and towing authority books.
        Refusals
        Refusals
          Grounds for refusal include (but are not limited to) the applicant/s:
          • Having an unsatisfactory criminal history
          • Having an unsatisfactory traffic history
          • Having a previous tow truck licence that was suspended or cancelled

          The decision to refuse an application for a tow truck licence           can be made by:
          • Manager (Customer Service Centre)
          • Senior Advisor (Service Management)

            A written refusal notice must be sent to the applicant within 14 days of the decision advising:
          • The decision
          • The reason for the decision
          • Information regarding reconsideration or appeal of the decision
          • That they can apply for a stay of the decision through QCAT (Qld Civil and Administrative Tribunal)(www.qcat.qld.gov.au)

          Note:Application documentation, including refusals, is to be manually added to the Industry Authority TRAILS Report and sent to Recall with the operator's daily filing.

          A copy of the refuse letter, application and supporting documents should be emailed to tow.trucks@tmr.qld.gov.au

        Review of Decision
        Review of Decision
          Refusal
          Refusal
            A Review of a Decision is a customer's request to review a decision made on behalf of the Chief Executive. The review of the decision is made by the:
            • Manager (CSC)
            • Senior Advisor (Service Management)
            • Product Centre Manger (PCM); or
            • Regional Director

            If the customer wishes to apply for the review of a decision they must:
            • Apply in writing to the Manager (CSC)
            • Apply within 28 days of the notification of refusal
            • Include the reasons why the decision needs to be reviewed

            The customer may also apply for a stay of the decision to refuse a renewal through QCAT.

            Once a review of original decision is received, the original documents should be requested from Recall.

            Following a review of a decision, an information notice (sample below) must be given to the applicant to advise them of the decision to:
            • reaffirm the original decision to refuse and advise that they can have the decision reviewed again by QCAT
            or
