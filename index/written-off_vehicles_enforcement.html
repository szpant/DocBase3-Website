Written-Off Vehicles Enforcement (5281)

Written-off Vehicle Enforcement
What it isA written-off vehicle is a vehicle that has been damaged to the point that repairing it would cost more than the market value of the vehicle.

To maintain the integrity of information recorded in TICA/TRAILS. TMR must be notified if the written-off vehicle is up to 16 years old and one of the following applies:
  • a vehicle with a GVM not over 4.5 tonne or
  • a motorcycle or
  • a caravan or
  • a trailer with an ATM over 4.5
Refer to: Written-off Vehicles
EnforcementTransport Inspectors (TIs) may come across written-off vehicles when inspecting vehicles on the road, in use car yards, roadside sales and through programmed inspections or during complaint inspections where evidence could be obtained of a breach.

They may also receive information or allegations that would indicate a breach of the legislation. While the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU) is responsible for following-up most alleged breaches, TI's are required to take certain actions:
  • If TI's receive information or a complaint, they should refer the information to the VIU for investigation.
  • If TI's intercept a written-off vehicle without a written-off vehicle label attached they should refer the matter to VIU for investigation.
  • The VIU may request assistance from TI's for specific investigations.

For legislation on Written-off vehicle please refer to - TO(RUM-Vehicle Registration) Regulation 2010.

Vehicle Identification Unit
Role of Vehicle Identification Unit
Role of Vehicle Identification Unit

    The VIU are responsible for managing the:
    • vehicle cancellation process for written-off vehicles;
    • Relationship with external notifiers;
    • Updating and correcting written-off vehicle details;
    • Management of written-off vehicle restrictions;
    • Issuing of replacement identification numbers to vehicles that have missing or suspect identification numbers;
    • Granting and refusing approvals;
    • conducting desktop audits of Written-Off Vehicle Register (WOVR) notifiers and Written-Off Vehicle Inspection (WOVI) inspection sites;
    • Investigating complaints;
    • Investigation of possible criminal fraud matters.
