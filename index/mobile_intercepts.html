Mobile Intercepts (7380)

Mobile Intercepts
Conducting a Mobile Intercept
Intercepting a Vehicle
Intercepting a Vehicle
    A minimum of two (2) Transport Inspectors are required to conduct mobile intercept.
    A mobile intercept can be carried out from the front or rear of the target vehicle depending on the circumstances based on the safety risk.

    Note: If it is not safe, do not attempt to carry out a mobile intercept.

    Once the target vehicle and method of intercept has been identified:
    • Be aware of other road users and obey the road rules at all times.
    • Travel in front, or rearward, of the target vehicle at a safe and appropriate distance.
    • Ensure the roadway is suitable and safe to intercept the vehicle.
    • Ensure the driver has visibility of the Compliance vehicle.
    Mobile intercept from behind
    Mobile intercept from behind
      • The assisting inspector must activate their camera as soon as the decision to intercept a vehicle has been made. The driver must activate their camera when they deem it safe to do so, but it must be activated prior to exiting the Compliance vehicle.
      • The driver or the assisting inspector must give a running commentary of the intercept until the target vehicle stops.
      • The commentary must include the following information:
        • the time, day and date
        • your partner's details
        • direction of travel, name of road a location
        • a description of the vehicle attempting to intercept.
        • For example, 10:00 hrs Monday 6th February 2020 in accompany with Transport Inspector Peter Smith ID Q910, travelling north Leichhardt Hwy, north of Wowan. Intercepting a type 1 road train loaded with containers. Registration number of last trailer ABC123.
      • Maintain a safe following distance that allows for extended forward vision and selection of a suitable interception location.
      • Attempt to isolate the vehicle from other traffic before preparing to intercept.
      • Once you have identified a safe place to stop activate magenta flashing lights and alternating headlights.
      • For a heavy vehicle attempt to contact the driver via UHF radio and give a direction to stop the vehicle or at a predetermined safe location.
      • If the driver fails to respond, operate the horn/siren for one to two seconds.
      • When the driver acknowledges the direction to stop, follow until the vehicle has stopped. Do not drive parallel to or overtake the vehicle.
      • Once the vehicle has stopped, position the compliance vehicle approximately three (3) metres behind the intercepted vehicle and approximately one and a half (1.5) metres proud on the roadway to provide a safety corridor.
      • Switch on the compliance vehicle hazard lights and ensure the alternating headlights are off.
      • For your safety, leave the magenta flashing lights on until the conclusion of the intercept.
      • While the vehicle is un-attended the vehicle must be locked.
    Mobile intercept from in front
    Mobile intercept from in front
      This method is normally used to get the target vehicle to follow the compliance vehicle to a static interception site. Caution must be exercised as the Compliance vehicle must be parked behind the target vehicle when it stops to provide a corridor of safety.
      • The assisting inspector must activate their camera as soon as the decision to intercept a vehicle has been made. The driver must activate their camera when they deem it safe to do so, but it must be activated prior to exiting the Compliance vehicle.
      • The driver or the assisting inspector must give a running commentary of the intercept until the target vehicle stops.
      • The commentary must include the following information:
        • the time, day and date
        • your partner's details
        • direction of travel, name of road a location
        • a description of the vehicle attempting to intercept.
        • For example, 10:00 hrs Monday 6th February 2020 in accompany with Transport Inspector Peter Smith ID Q910, travelling north Leichhardt Hwy, north of Wowan. Intercepting a type 1 road train loaded with containers. Prime mover registration number ABC123.
      • Activate magenta flashing lights.
      • If applicable activate the rear facing electronic message board to display ‘Follow me’.
      • For a heavy vehicle attempt to contact the driver via UHF radio and give a direction to stop the vehicle or at a predetermined safe location.
      • Pull off the road at a safe location with enough room to enable both the compliance and target vehicle off the road or position the compliance vehicle approximately three (3) metres behind the intercepted vehicle and approximately one and a half (1.5) metres proud on the roadway to provide a safety corridor.
      • Leave the magenta flashing lights on until the conclusion of the interception.
      • If the driver fails to respond, operate the horn/siren for one to two seconds.
      • If the driver fails to stop do not continuously use the horn/siren.
        • Stay a safe distance in front and continue until you can find a safe site to perform a static intercept.
        • If the driver fails to stop, do not chase the vehicle or break any road rules.
        • Record as must detail as you can in your notebook and report it to police
Roles and Responsibilities
Lead Inspector
Lead Inspector
    Lead Inspector should:
    • Watch the intercepted vehicles driver side mirror to observe driver behaviour.
    • Do not stand between the compliance vehicle and the back of the intercepted vehicle
    • Get to the intercepted vehicle promptly using the safety corridor.
    • Take up a position at the driver’s door but outside of the opening arc of the door. Face the oncoming traffic.
    • Provide introduction:
      • Produce your ID Card and identify yourself as a Transport Inspector:
        • state for the record why they have intercepted/stopped, and
        • verbally give the privacy collection notice.

      For example, Good morning/evening/ afternoon. My name is John Smith, I am a Transport Inspector with Transport and Main Roads, here is my ID (SHOW ID), you have been stopped today for a compliance check/safety check. This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes."

    • Instruct the driver to stay in the vehicle while you carry out the relevant compliance checks.
    • Give the driver clear instructions on requirements while conducting compliance checks, for example:
      • Put the vehicle in Neutral/Park and apply the Park/Maxi brake.
      • Move the vehicle onto the scales
    • If the driver is required to exit the vehicle move the driver to a safe place.
    • If the area where the vehicle stopped is not considered safe, direct the driver to another location which is safe.
    • Do not leave the safety corridor when standing on the driver’s side of the intercepted vehicle unless it is necessary for your safety.
Assisting Inspector
Assisting Inspector
    Assisting Inspector should:
    • Take charge of the compliance vehicle mobile phone and carry throughout the intercept.
    • Provide a running commentary on the BWC where necessary.
    • Provide assistance to the Lead Inspector where necessary.
    • Be alert and watch for any possible dangers Proceed down the safety corridor behind the Lead Inspector to the driver’s door.
    • Stand single file at the driver’s door and observe driver and passenger behaviours facing oncoming traffic.
    • Take up a position to corroborate evidence and to observe approaching traffic.
    • Do not leave the safety corridor when standing on the driver’s side of the intercepted vehicle unless it is necessary for your safety.
Fail to Stop
Mobile Intercept
Mobile Intercept
    If a person fails to stop during the first attempt of a mobile intercept, make a second attempt to carry out a mobile intercept if safe to do so.
    If the second attempt fails:
    • Leave the alternating headlights and roof or vehicle mounted magenta flashing lights on.
    • If the driver fails to stop do not continuously use the horn/siren
    • If the driver speeds off or drives in a dangerous manner, do not chase the vehicle or break any road rules.
    • Do not pull alongside or attempt to perform an intercept by overtaking the vehicle that is failing to stop.
    • Contact QPS for assistance, if available and any information relating to the target vehicle.

    Record the full vehicle identification to relay to police. QPS Contact Numbers
    • Record all relevant details in your notebook.
    • Abandon the intercept and make further inquiries relating to the vehicle and registered owner.

    CAUTION: The driver may not stop because of any number of reasons. DO NOT put yourself, your partner or other road users at risk.

    Note:The road rules must be obeyed at all times.

    If Vehicle Stops
    If Vehicle Stops
      If the vehicle stops, perform the compliance checks and any necessary enforcement actions. Question the driver regarding why they failed to stop and record details in your official notebook. As a minimum ask the following questions:
      • Did you see the operating magenta lights on my vehicle?
      • Did you hear the siren/horn?
      • Did you see my flashing headlights?
      • What is your reason for not stopping?

      Drivers can not be breached for failure to stop for disobeying the lights and siren. The lights and siren are only used to attract the driver's attention.

      The additional information may support other offence questioning in demonstrating the offender's intent for committing other offences. These questions can then be included in any BoE to prosecutions if the matter proceeds to Court due to other offences being contested. FOR EXAMPLE, Mass, registration or fatigue offences where it can be implied that the driver tried to evade being stopped and subsequently detected committing these types of offences
Entering Private Property
Entering Private Property
    In cases where vehicles are driven onto private property after having disobeyed a direction to stop, or to evade directions to stop, or which are suspected of having committed an offence, the following procedure is to be adopted:
    • Ensure the BWC is still recording.
    • Provide running commentary.
    • Identify yourself as a Transport Inspector and produce your ID card.
    • Advise the owner or occupier of the property:
      • "This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes."

    Note:A privacy collection notice must be provided to an individual when the BWC has been activated. The privacy collection notice can be provided verbally.

    • Interview the owner or occupier of the property and explain the circumstances of your entry. Seek permission to interview the driver of the suspect vehicle.

    Permission Denied
    Permission Denied
      If denied permission by the owner of the property, you must depart forthwith. No discussion or argument is to be entered into.
    Permission Granted
    Permission Granted
      If permission is granted:
      Enquire of the owner or occupier of the property if the vehicle load (if any) was scheduled for that property. If not, and permission to interview the driver is granted:
      • Identify yourself as a Transport Inspector:
        • produce your ID card, and verbally state for the record
        • why they have been intercepted/stopped.

        For example,         My name is John Smith, I am a Transport Inspector with Transport and Main Roads, here is my ID (SHOW ID), I am conducting a compliance check/safety check. This interaction is being recorded for transparency and accountability purposes."

      Note:A privacy collection notice must be provided to an individual when the BWC has been activated. The privacy collection notice can be provided verbally.

      • Advise the driver of the suspect vehicle that the owner of the property has granted permission to interview;
      • Interview the driver of the suspect vehicle;
      • If it is necessary to weigh the vehicle, direct the driver to an area suitable for that purpose and clear of the property;
      • Submit a full report of the incident and action taken to your respective Senior Inspector. Attach breaches if any. Refer: Advice of Problems Encountered When Conducting Interceptions

    Note:No entry to property may be made unless the vehicle was sighted travelling on public roads prior to entry. No Inspector may give directions to the driver of any vehicle on private property without first being given permission by the owner or occupier to be on that property.
