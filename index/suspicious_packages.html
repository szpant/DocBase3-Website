Lost Property
Lost Property
    Assessment must be conducted by a staff member/manager of the area where the lost property is located. Lost property that is designated non-suspect must be added to the lost property register in SharePoint. Staff are to ensure that the register is updated as soon as the item is found.

Assessment
Using the HOT UP principle
Using the HOT UP principle
    Is the item:
    • Hidden
    • Obviously Suspicious
    • Typical of its Environment

    Has there been:
    • Unauthorised Access
    • Perimeter Breach

    Note:If the item fails a HOT UP examination, it does not mean the item is an explosive device it simply warrants further examination. This may include the decision to call QPS or evacuate the area around the item.

    If you deem an item suspect, do not touch it or pick it up. Assess it only by looking at it.

Other Aids for assessment
Other Aids for assessment
    Has there been:
    • a recent threat against the facility
    • a recent threat against a member of staff, either personally or professionally
    • a history or security threats against the facility

    Does the package have:
    • protruding wires
    • oily stains or discolouration
    • a stiff or rigid appearance
    • an unusual smell
    • a distinctive sound

    If the answer is yes to one or more of these indicators the package may be deemed as suspect and require examination by an expert

    Other options are:
    • question staff and members of the public to see if anyone has knowledge of the package
    • review any closed circuit television (CCTV) tapes to determine how the package came to be placed there
    Note:Queries relating to CCTV, including QPS requests for footage, must be made to Facilities and Accommodation Solutions on 3066 8729.
Packages received in the mailIf you have received a package that may contain hazardous material:
  • Do not disturb the item any further. Do not pass it around. If any material has spilt from the item, do not try to clean it up or brush it from your clothing.
  • If possible place an object over the package without disturbing it.
  • Stay in your immediate work area.
  • Prevent others from entering the area
  • Remember you are not in immediate danger.
  • Call for help.
  • Keep your hands away from your face.
  • If possible wash your hands.
  • If possible, have the building ventilation system shut down.
  • Wait for help to arrive.

Actions
ResponsibilitiesManagement Responsibilities
  • The below responsibilities reside with the senior person present:
    • devise and maintain a safe evacuation plan
    • assess the package and the long and short term threat
    • contact QPS and the Safety and Wellbeing hotline to seek advice
    • consider the decision to evacuate
    • make the decision to evacuate
    • make the decision to reoccupy the business unit
    • conduct staff training and security drills to rehearse response
Evacuation
Evacuation
    Evacuation planning should be in consultation with your local Workplace Health and Safety Officer.

    If an evacuation occurs:
    • staff and members of the public should take their personal property with them (including mediation)
    • keep your senior management informed of the situation as soon as possible by phone
    • account for all evacuees and check the premises to ensure nobody is left behind
    • advice adjacent business units of what is happening
    • consider the need to shut down machinery or data links if necessary
Reoccupation
Reoccupation
    Reoccupation can not occur until the QPS commander on site declares the scene safe.
Reporting requirements
Reporting requirements
    Each incident (real, hoax or accidental) must be reported. As soon as possible telephone advice is to be provided to the Safety and Wellbeing hotline on 1800 867 947.
