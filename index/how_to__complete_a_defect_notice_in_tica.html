Completing a Defect Notice in TICA (7582)

Applies to
Applies to
    This procedure will outline how to issue a defect notice from TICA as a result of a failed Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI).
1
Log In
Log In
    • Log into TICA
    • Select location if applicable
    • Enter front office printer details in Session if applicable
    • Select Inspection HUB



2
Enter Registration Number
Enter Registration Number
    • Enter Registration Number
    • Select Display


3
Check Vehicle Details and History
Check Vehicle Details and History
    • The vehicle details and inspection history will be displayed
    • Check the VIN and vehicle details before proceeding
    • Ensure there are no outstanding defect notices
    • Ensure there is a booking for the Programmed Vehicle Inspection (PVI)

4
Select Inspection Type
Select Inspection Type
    • Select Programmed - Fail from Common Inspections

5
Inspection Details
Inspection Details
    • Date will be pre-populated
    • Enter the time of inspection 24 hour
    • Enter your ID including Q prefix
    • District will be pre-populated from the location you select when you log in
    • Enter post code
    • Enter odometer reading from vehicle if applicable
    • Select Fail

6
Record Defect Notice
Record Defect Notice
    Severity
    Severity
      • Select "Severity" from the drop down menu
      • Self-Clearing should not be used as a severity as there is no requirement under HVNL(Q) for the owner to return the defect notice for clearance so the defect notice can not be entered in TICA
      • Select the severity based on the safety risk posed by the continued use of a defective vehicle on the road
      • If you select Major as a category, a Defective Vehicle Label must be attached to the vehicle

      Categorising Defects

    Cease Use
    Cease Use
      • All defect notices must stipulate a cease use time (24 hour format) and date.
      • The cease use time and date for a PVI should be the end of business day or next business day.
    Re-inspection Due
    Re-inspection Due
      • The location will pre-populate from the TICA log in location
      • Date may be 7 or 14 but must not be more than 28 days
      • If a TMR re-inspection is required select "Appointment required"
      • Enter the date the appointment must be made by
    Clearance
    Clearance
      • Select the clearance method from the drop down menu
      • When selecting the clearance method the following factors should be considered:
        • Severity and nature of defects
        • Defect history, are the current defects the same as the last inspection
        • Re-inspection resources
        • Self Clearing must be selected if clearance by a Qualified Person is allowed.
      • Rectify by Date
        • May be 7 or 14 but must not be more than 28 day

7
Defects
Defects
    • Select the relevant components
    • Enter brake test results
    • Select Record Additional Details to enter a description of the defects

8
Additional Details
Additional Details
    • Number each defect separately.
    • When describing the defect, use the following format ‘Component’, ‘Fault’, ‘Additional’, to ensure an accurate and informative description of the fault is provided to the person who will be carrying out the adjustments or repairs.
    • Do not use "ensure complies with". You must prove the vehicle is defective.
    • If the severity is classified as major you must stipulate where the vehicle may be driven to.
    • Conditions may be added, for example:
      • The vehicle may be allowed to be driven during the day only if a head light is not operational.
    • When all defects listed select OK.


9
Individual Served
Individual Served
    • QLD driver licence:
      • Enter the CRN of the person the defect notice is being issued to
      • Select display
      • Confirm the details
    • Non-QLD driver licence:
      • Enter the licence number of the person the defect notice is being issued to
      • Enter the state the licence is issued by
    • Select OK to print the defect notice.

10
Front of the Defect Notice
Front of the Defect Notice
    • Sign the front of the defect notice
    • Provide postal and or email address to return the notice if applicable.
    • Provide SBS phone number if required 132380/132390

11
Complete the Reverse Side of the Notice
Complete the Reverse Side of the Notice
    • Highlight the applicable clearance method and strike out non-relevant sections.


12
Issuing a Defect Notice to a Person
Issuing a Defect Notice to a Person
    • Explain the notice, clearance method and requirement to give the notice to the owner if the notice is issued to a driver.
    • Ask the person to sign the notice.
    • If they refuse to sign the notice, make a note on the inspection check sheet (NO Offence).
