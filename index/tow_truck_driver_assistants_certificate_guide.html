Tow Truck Driver/Assistants Certificate Guide (1886)


About a Tow Truck Driver/Assistant Certificate
Definitions
Industry Authority Definitions
What it is
Tow Truck Driver
Tow Truck Driver
    A Tow Truck Drivers Certificate authorises a person to operate a tow truck as a driver in circumstances where a certificate is required.
Tow Truck Assistant
Tow Truck Assistant
    A Tow Truck Assistant certificate authorises a person to travel in a tow truck and assist a tow truck driver in circumstances where a certificate is required.
When required
Tow Truck Driver Certificate
Tow Truck Driver Certificate
    A Tow Truck Driver certificate is required when:
    • Attending the scene of an accident/incident; or
    • Seizure of a motor vehicle under Police authority
    • Towing a vehicle from a private property, where the Tow Truck Licence holder has entered into a contract with the property owner or occupier
Tow Truck Assistant Certificate
Tow Truck Assistant Certificate
    A Tow Truck Assistant certificate is required when:
    • Going to the scene of an incident or seizure of a motor vehicle
    • At the scene of an incident or seizure of a motor vehicle
    • Assisting to tow a vehicle from a private property, where the Tow Truck Licence holder has entered into a contract with the property owner or occupier
    • Travelling in a tow truck that is towing a vehicle that has been damaged as a result of a collision or impact
    • Travelling in a tow truck that is towing a seized vehicle

    Tow Truck Regulated Areas

Note: Towing in regulated areas of Queensland must be performed by accredited drivers and assistants using licensed Tow Trucks.
When not required
When not Required
When not Required
    A Tow Truck Driver / Assistant Certificate is not required for areas outside the regulated areas and for general breakdown and trade work.

Eligibility requirements
Eligibility requirements
Eligibility requirements
    All requirements listed in this section (excluding traffic and criminal history checks) must be met at time of lodging the application. If the customer has not met all requirements:
    • The application must not be accepted (Refer: Refuse to consider below);
    • No details are to be recorded on TICA;
    • No fees should be applied
    Note:Traffic and criminal checks are conducted after the lodgement of the application.
    Eligibility Criteria
    A person
    Must be an individual
    Must be an individual
      The applicant must be an individual (i.e. be a natural person not a business or company)
    Age
    Minimum age - Tow Truck Driver
    Minimum age - Tow Truck Driver
      A Tow Truck Driver must be at least 18 years of age.
    Minimum age - Tow Truck Assistant
    Minimum age - Tow Truck Assistant
      A Tow Truck Assistant must be at least 16 years of age.
    Driver Licence
    Licence requirements - Tow Truck Driver
    Licence requirements - Tow Truck Driver
      • Hold a current Australian driver licence for the class relevant to the class of tow truck being applied for.

      Note:Refer to the section below - "Tow Truck Driver training requirements" as the length of time the applicant has held the relevant driver licence class, determines if the applicant needs to undertake the Tow Truck operational test.
    Licence requirements - Tow Truck Assistant
    Licence requirements - Tow Truck Assistant
      • There is no drivers licence requirement for a Tow Truck Assistant certificate as they do not drive the vehicle.
    Traffic & Criminal
    Suitable Traffic and Criminal History - TTA & TTD
    Suitable Traffic and Criminal History - TTA & TTD
      An applicant for a TTD must have a suitable criminal and traffic history to be granted a TTD certificate. Refer: Industry Authority > 1. Industry Authority > Criminal History Request

      An applicant for a TTA must have a suitable criminal history to be granted a TTA certificate. A traffic history check is not required as TTAs are not required to hold a driver licence.

    Medical Requirements - Tow Truck Driver and Tow Truck Assistant
    Medical Requirements - Tow Truck Driver and Tow Truck Assistant
      If an applicant has a medical condition or physical or mental incapacity that will prevent them from satisfactorily performing the activities authorised under a licence or certificate;
      • the applicant must have their treating doctor complete the Medical Practitioner Certification section on the application form to verify whether or not they are mentally and physically an appropriate person to hold an approval.
    Training
    Tow Truck Driver training requirements
    Tow Truck Driver training requirements
      To become a tow truck driver the applicant must demonstrate their ability to operate a tow truck.

      This is demonstrated by one of the following:
      • the applicant has held the class of Australian driver licence that is required for the type of tow truck vehicle they want to drive, for 3 continuous years immediately prior to making the application

        For example: If the tow truck is a vehicle that would require a HR driver licence, the applicant must have held a HR driver licence for 3 continuous years prior to making the application.
      or
      • if the applicant has not held the class of licence for 3 continuous years immediately before the application they must undergo a QSafe tow truck operational test. The test must be conducted in a vehicle of the class they are applying for. The driving component of the test is conducted whilst towing a vehicle.

      Note:The Q-Safe tow truck operational test assesses the applicants ability and knowledge:
      • to drive and properly control the tow truck whilst towing a vehicle
      • to position the tow truck to prepare a vehicle for towing
      • their understanding of the proper procedures for towing various classes of vehicle.
      The test is arranged by phoning the SBS call centre on 13 23 90. There is no fee for the test and the result can be viewed on SBS.
    Tow Truck Assistant training requirements
    Tow Truck Assistant training requirements
      There are no training requirements for a Tow Truck Assistant Certificate.

Tow Truck Driver/Assistant Certificate conditions/transactions
Conditions of Use
Carry a current certificate while performing the role
Carry a current certificate while performing the role
    A document evidencing a current Queensland Tow Truck Driver Certificate or Tow Truck Assistant Certificate must be carried at all times when performing their duties. This includes an Industry Authority smartcard or an interim industry authority.

    The following conditions apply:
    • Drivers and assistants must produce their certificates at the request of an authorised officer (including police officers)
    • The certificate must be produced before, or while making, an offer to tow
    • Drivers/Assistants must notify TMR within 14 days of a change of name or address

    The owner/agent/authorised person of the vehicle being towed decides:
    • Who is to tow the vehicle
    • Where the vehicle is to be towed

    Agent/Authorised Person Defined
    Agent/Authorised Person Defined
      An agent/authorised person is the person in control of the vehicle who is not the owner of the motor vehicle.

    Crash/Seizure Towing
    Private Vehicle Towing

Hold a current Driver Licence
    If the driver licence is expired, suspended, cancelled or void, the certificate holder is not authorised to drive any vehicle on the road including Tow Truck Vehicles.

Conditions imposed by TMR - Policy Team
Conditions imposed by TMR - Policy Team
    If a condition is imposed on a Tow Truck Driver or Assistant by the policy team, the driver or assistant must carry the original conditional letter (or other required supporting documentation) while performing the role.

    If a condition is imposed on a Tow Truck Driver or Tow Truck Assistant by the policy team, the policy team will:
    • Advise the customer of the conditions by letter, which will advise the customer to attend a CSC or QGAP office to replace the Smartcard

      Note:A copy of this letter will be kept at the Industry Licensing Policy Unit.

    • The policy unit will monitor and if the Smartcard has not been surrendered, returned and reissued within the specified period, the unit will forward to prosecutions for possible action.

      Note:To indicate that the original conditional letter (or other required supporting documentation) is required to be carried, a 'Y' condition code will be on the face of the Smartcard and the word 'TEXT' will be recorded on the chip. The policy team will advise of the text to be recorded on the restriction screen in TICA.

    Refer to: The Smartcard Guide > Replacement

Transaction details
New Application
New Application
    All TMR Customer Service Centres and on-line QGAP agencies may accept applications and payment for Tow Truck Driver and Tow Truck Assistant Certificates.

    Before an application may be finalised in TICA:
    • All eligibility requirements above must have been met
    • Evidence of Identity documents confirmed.
    • A traffic history and criminal history searches must be conducted
      Refer to: Industry Authority > 1. Industry Authority > Criminal history request
    • Traffic history and criminal history requests finalised
    • Consent to taking and storing of digital facial image and digitised signature.
    • Payment of all prescribed fees.

    If any of the prerequisites are not met, the application is not to be approved. If a criminal history has not been returned or there are outstanding charges, a decision must be made to either grant, grant with conditions or refuse the application within 3 months to ensure an application does not lapse.

    Note:For new applications only - If the applicant has signed up to receive e-reminders, upon approval of their application they will receive their interim industry authority by email.

    Refer: Interim Industry Authority below.

    Cancelled/Suspend and Amend
    Cancelled/Suspend and Amend
      Grounds for amending, suspending or cancelling of the Tow Truck Driver or Assistant Certificate are:
      • The certificate was granted or renewed because of a false or fraudulent document, statement or representation;
      • The authority holder:
      • Is convicted of an offence against the Act; or
      • Has, since the issue of the certificate, been charged with or convicted of a disqualifying offence; or
      • Contravenes a condition of the certificate
      • The holder cannot properly drive the class of vehicle stated in the certificate because of a medical condition, or physical or mental incapacity;
      • The holder has given false or misleading information to an authorised officer

      The applicant will need to re-apply for a Tow Truck Driver Certificate, meeting full eligibility requirements once the cancellation period has expired.

      Further grounds
      Driver Licence Cancelled
      Driver Licence Cancelled
        If a driver licence is cancelled, the Tow Truck Driver Certificate is:
        • Automatically cancelled;
        • A new certificate is not to be granted
        • A cancelled certificate cannot be renewed
        • To be surrendered and returned if requested by the Chief Executive.

      Driver Licence Suspended
      Driver Licence Suspended
        • If a driver licence is suspended the driver or assistant certificate is:
        • Suspended (applicant is unable to perform the duties of a Tow Truck Driver during suspension period);
        • Automatically reinstated on the completion of the driver licence's suspension period;
        • Not surrendered and no action is required on TICA.

      Note:Applicants with a Multiple Industry Authority smartcard which has another authority that is not affected by the cancellation will require a replacement Industry Authority smartcard which is to be issued for the non affected industry authority/s. (e.g. Tow Truck Assistant or Traffic Controller)

      Refer to: Smartcard > Replacement

      The Policy unit receives a report (IA55) detailing any driver licence that has been cancelled or suspended while holding a current Tow Truck Driver certificate. If information contained in an IA55 report indicates that action may need to be taken against an Industry Authority Holder, an email will be forwarded to the Manager of the Customer Service Centre closest to the applicant for appropriate action.

      Refer to: IA55 report

    Void
    Void
      If a payment for a Tow Truck Driver/Assistant certificate has been dishonoured, the Policy unit will receive advice from finance. The Policy unit will advise the applicant that they must pay the relevant fees immediately.

      Any follow up action required due to non payment of fees will be handled by the Policy unit.

    Expired
    Expired
      If the customer submits an application for renewal and the Tow Truck Driver/Assistant certificate has expired more than 3 months, the application is to be treated as a new issue application with full requirements including a new crimtrac search and fee.
Surrender
Surrender
    A document evidencing a Tow Truck Driver/Assistant certificate must be surrendered and returned or the following reasons:
    • The certificate was refused on renewal of application;
    • Merged;
    • Multiple Industry Authority holder (if removing one or more of the current authority types);
    • If the licence has been suspended due to an action by an authorised enforcement officer
    • The certificate is cancelled

    The Tow Truck Driver/Assistant certificate can be surrendered for the following reasons:
    • No longer required (voluntary);
    • On behalf of the deceased

    Note:Multiple card holders wanting to voluntarily surrender one authority but retain others are to complete a Replacement Industry Authority (Form F7429)
Reinstate
Reinstate
    A Tow Truck Driver/Assistant certificate may be reinstated due to:
    • A review of a decision made by Regional Director or delegate
      The applicant needs to provide:
      • The letter issued by the Regional Director or delegate stating the certificate is to be reinstated; and
      • Standard EOI.

    • A stay or review of a decision made by QCAT
      The applicant needs to provide:
      • The letter issued by QCAT stating the certificate is to be reinstated; and
      • Standard EOI.

    Note:The applicant does not have to complete another application form. A copy of the letter by the authorised agent is used for filing.
Refuse to consider
Refuse to Grant application
Refuse to Grant application
    Grounds for refusal include (but are not limited to) the applicant/s:
    • Having an unsatisfactory criminal history
    • Having an unsatisfactory traffic history
    • Failed 1:N (if application is lodged and not approved on same day)
    • For a new licence, already holds a driver's or assistant's certificate under the name in the application or another name

    The decision to refuse an application for a Tow Truck Driver or Tow Truck Assistant Certificate can be made by:
    • Manager (Customer Service Centre)
    • Senior Advisor (Service Management)
    • Principal Customer Service Officer
    • Customer Service Officer
    A written refusal notice must be sent to the applicant within 14 days of the decision advising:
    • The decision
    • The reason for the decision
    • Information regarding reconsideration or appeal of the decision
    • That they can apply for a stay of the decision through QCAT
    Note:If an applicant is refused their accreditation, a copy of the refusal letter and the file should be forwarded to Recall the next business day and not retained in the office in case of appeal:
    • Filing must be in a filing envelope indicating Industry Authority filing only (tick the 'Industry Authority' box on the filing envelope)
    • Name of Office sending work
    • Refusal dates for the documents e.g. 03/08/2009 (there must only be one envelope per refusal date)
    • before sending to Recall - send copy of refuse letter, application and attachments to tow.trucks@tmr.qld.gov.au

    Lapsed applications
    Lapsed applications
      Lapsed Status on IL applications in TICA occur when:
      • Lodged applications are not finalised (outstanding requirements) within 3 months of the application date; or
      • Renewals are not finalised within 3 months of the expiry date

      A decision must be made to either grant, grant with conditions or refuse the application within 3 months to ensure an application does not lapse.

      In these instances the following will apply:
      • If the application is to be refused the authority term fee and the smartcard production fee are to be refunded to the customer.

        Refer to: Refunds

      A lapsed application that has been refused is sent to Recall for filing on the day the application is refused.

      If the customer requests to proceed with an application after a lapsed status, the applicants will be required to re-apply and meet full requirements including the payment of all prescribed fees (including Crim Trac fees).

      Note:If the application has not been finalised due to a possible TMR error please consult with your SASM/Manager for advice on how to proceed.

    Refunds
    Refunds
      Where an application has been refused (not approved) or withdrawn by the applicant, the applicant is entitled to a refund of the authority term fee and smartcard production fee components only (provided the fees were not exempted). These fees will automatically credit to the customers account to be refunded if required. The refund should be authorised the same day as the refusal letter is sent.

      Process the adjustment from the CrimTrac Search Account to the customer account.
    Mutual recognition
    Interstate Mutual Recognition
    Interstate Mutual Recognition
      There is no mutual recognition for an interstate Tow Truck Driver Certificate or Tow Truck Assistant Certificate. The applicant must apply to be a Tow Truck Driver or Tow Truck Assistant in Queensland.
    New Zealand Mutual Recognition of Tow Truck Driver's Certificate
    New Zealand Mutual Recognition of Tow Truck Driver's Certificate
      Under the Mutual Recognition Act 1992, a New Zealand Tow Truck Driver endorsement may be recognised and transferred to the equivalent Queensland Tow Truck Driver Certificate. However applicants must make application for recognition of their existing New Zealand accreditation and pay the applicable fee.(Please note that New Zealand does not have an equivalent to Queensland’s Tow Truck Assistant).

      Applicants eligible for mutual recognition as a Tow Truck Driver are therefore exempt from the requirement to either hold the driver licence continuously for 3 years or complete the QSafe practical test but must meet all other application requirements (such as minimum age, driver licence, medical certificate, criminal and traffic history checks).

      Applicants applying under mutual recognition must complete:
      • Application for Tow Truck Driver/Assistant's Certificate F4481 and
      • Equivalent Occupation Registration Notice (F2762)
      • Provide a NZ licence check from Driver.Check@nztz.govt.nz that is no more than 6 weeks old showing the endorsement "V" for vehicle recovery

      Note:Recognised equivalent Tow Truck Endorsement's from New Zealand.
