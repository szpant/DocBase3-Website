Sanction Types
    Good Driving Behaviour (GDB) period of 1 year
    Good Driving Behaviour (GDB) period of 1 year
      If a GDB is chosen, the licence holder can continue driving.

      Although a GDB can be chosen if the points were accumulated for a High Speed infringement a High Speed Suspension (HSS) will also be issued to the customer resulting in a 6 month suspension. The GDB that has been chosen will still need to be served for the 12 months, as the choice date for the GDB is prior to the HSS commencement date. The customer has the option to choose a Suspension or a GDB when the notice is received. When the Demerit point suspension is selected it can be served concurrently with the HSS. If a customer choses a GDB the GDB period will continue after the end date for the HSS.

      If 2 or more demerit points are accumulated during this one year period, the licence will be suspended for double the original suspension period that would have applied had the suspension option been taken.

      Customer cannot choose a GDB if any of the following apply:
      • the customer's nominated choice date is prior to the current date
      • the customer's choice date has passed
      • they are disqualified by order of an Australian court from holding or obtaining a driver licence
      • their licence is currently cancelled or they are serving a suspension period
      • their licence is expired
        Note:Their licence may be renewed if eligible and the GDB applied if on or before the choice date.
      • they have accumulated 4 or more points in a continuous 12 month period when they held a learner licence
      • the customer holds an interstate or overseas licence.
        Note:If the customer has accumulated more points than their demerit point limit they have the option to transfer to a Queensland licence before the date listed in their Suspension of Authority to Drive Notice and nominate a GDB (if eligible.)

    Suspension for the requisite period
    Suspension for the requisite period
      The requisite suspension period will depend on the type of licence held and the number of points accumulated during the specified period.

      A Demerit point suspension can be served concurrently with the HSS.

    Ineligible to hold a Qld licence
    Ineligible to hold a Qld licence
      The person may not obtain a Qld licence while their licence is suspended, cancelled or if they have been disqualified by order of an Australian court from holding or obtaining a driver licence.

Notices
Written Notices
Written Notices
    The Chief Executive must give written notice when a person has accumulated more demerit points than they are allowed.

    Sanction Notices are sent by ordinary mail to the customer's postal or residential address.

    If a legislated notice is to be sent and the customer has a:
    • Postal address - an original notice will be sent to the postal address. No ‘duplicate’ notice will be sent to the residential address.
    • Residential address - the original notice will be sent to the residential address.

    All courtesy notices are sent to a customer’s postal address or residential address where no postal address has been provided.

    No further demerit point notices are sent if:
    • A demerit point notice already exists for which no sanction action has been taken
    • If a Special Hardship Order(SHO) is lodged for a Notice of Accumulation of Demerit Points during a Good Driving Behaviour Period (F4008) (i.e. no result recorded)
    • If a speed suspension notice exists with a status of RTND (Returned).

    If the customer has been sent a Notice to Choose and has signed up to receive e-reminders an email will be sent to them three days prior to the choice date if they haven’t yet notified the department of their choice.

    The email reminder notice will include:
    • Issue date of notice to choose
    • Final date for choice to be made
    • Link to Choose your demerit point online service

    For a Description of Notices Refer: Fees and Forms > 2. Forms > Forms > Driver Licensing > Demerit Points Sanction Notices

    For address rules Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer > Standards > Customer Name & Address Standards > Address rules for correspondence
Returned Notices
Returned Notices
    If not claimed, notices are returned to TMR and the notice is marked as unclaimed on TICA.

    If an original legislated notice is marked unclaimed for any of these reasons on TICA a mail sanction is applied, no sanction action is taken until the notice is re-issued.

    ReasonDescription
    DeceasedAddressee has passed away
    IncompleteAddress incomplete
    Left Addr Addressee no longer at address
    Not ExistAddress does not exist
    Refused Addressee refused mail
    UnclaimedMail not claimed by addressee
    Unknown AdAddress not know by postal system

    The mail sanction will prevent:
    • the issue of any further demerit point notices should the person continue to accrue demerit points
    • licensing transactions from being conducted

    To remove the sanction the address must be updated, the restriction expired and the notice reissued.
Re-Issue of Returned Notices
Re-Issue of Returned Notices
    If a notice is returned not delivered, the notice may be re-issued. A new notice will be generated and the process will re-commence.

    The re-issued notice may be served:
    In PersonThe notice may be printed and handed to the customer.

    Note:This is the preferred option as mail generated notices are subject to further return.
    By MailThe notice is generated by the next batch run and sent by mail contractor.

    Note:If a customer's address is updated, TICA automatically re-issues a returned notice by mail. However, only the oldest notice will be re-issued automatically. The first re-issued notice must be actioned before any additional notices can be issued. The notice may be printed & handed to the customer.

    Withdrawing an Unlicensed Person (DEFA) Sanction
    Withdrawing an Unlicensed Person (DEFA) Sanction

      A DEFA sanction can be withdrawn if the person holds a valid non-Queensland licence.

      The person can apply for a Queensland licence before the DEFA sanction commences. Operators are to update the NQPR screen and the transfer of licence will clear the DEFA sanction.

      If the DEFA sanction is already applied, operators are to update the NQPR screen and must ask the person if they have been charged with unlicensed driving since the commencement date of the sanction.

      If the person has not been charged with unlicensed driving, the DEFA sanction can be withdrawn.

      If the person has been charged with unlicensed driving, the DEFA should not be withdrawn as TMR cannot intervene in police/court matters.

      Note:If operators are required to request licence details from an interstate licensing authority, the person should be advised that they will be considered unlicensed in TMR's database until the non-Queensland licence details have been recorded. If the person continues to drive prior to the non-Queensland licence details being recorded, they could be charged with unlicensed driving.


    Note:When adding Foreign Licence details to the NQPR screen a copy of the Licence and translation (if required) is to be retained in daily filing.
Accumulation of Demerit Points - Notice to Choose (F4011)
Accumulation of Demerit Points - Notice to Choose (F4011)
    Notification
    Notification
      A Notification of Driver Licence Enforcement Option - In Person Nomination (F4012) is sent with the Accumulation of Demerit Points Notice to Choose (F4011).

      To make a sanction choice, the licence holder must notify TMR of their choice on or before the choice date specified on the notice by:
      • Completing the Online Licence Enforcement Option Nomination Service (OLEONS)
      • Presenting the form in person to a CSC or a driver licence issuing agent
      • Have an authorised representative present their notification to a CSC or a driver licence issuing agent

      If the licence holder is genuinely unable to notify TMR of their choice by one of the above methods, they may be provided the option to mail, fax or email their nomination to Customer Service Support If they are mailing, faxing or emailing their notification the licence holder should be advised to contact TMR to confirm receipt of their nomination.

      If the licence holder has signed up to receive e-reminders an email will be sent to them three days prior to the choice date if they haven’t yet notified TMR of their choice.

    Failure to Notify
    Failure to Notify
      If the sanction notice is not returned unclaimed, and the person fails to notify TMR of their sanction choice on or before the choice date specified on the notice, the licence will be automatically suspended and the Good Driving Behaviour option cannot be taken.
      A Courtesy Notice - Your Licence Option Choice (F4010) to advise the licence holder that they have yet to notify a sanction option is also sent 14 days before the choice date.

      A Courtesy Notice - Failure to Notify Driver Licence Option Choice (F4926) is sent to advise the licence holder that their driver licence has been suspended because they failed to notify TMR of their sanction choice by their choice date.

      These notices are sent to the customer's postal address or to their residential address if they do not have a postal address recorded.

Notice of Acc. of Demerit Points during a GDB Period (F4008)
    The Notice of Accumulation of Demerit Points while on a Good Driving Behaviour Period (F4008) will advise the licence holder of the date their licence will be suspended.

    No notification is required. The suspension period will commence on the date specified on the notice.

    They have the option to attend TMR or a licence issuing agent to begin the suspension period earlier than this date.

    The notice also advises the licence holder that they may be eligible to apply for a     Special Hardship Order.

    Note:The person must notify by written request at a driver licence issuing centre, if they wish to start their driver licence suspension period earlier. There is no nomination form for this purpose.

    A Notice of Confirmation of Driver Licence Suspension (F4009) will be sent to confirm the suspension period.

Recognising a Prior Suspension Period A prior suspension period may be recognised if:

    a) Multiple sanction notices have been incorrectly issued as a result of departmental or other agency error, or

    b) The customer received a sanction notice prior to a mail sanction being recorded.

And the eligibility assessment criteria has been met.

Eligibility Assessment Criteria
Eligibility Assessment Criteria
    CSOs and CSD Consultants must use the following criteria to determine if a prior suspension period can be recognised.

    To be eligible:
    • the customer must be able to produce a copy of a sanction notice they received to show that they were aware of the suspension period, this includes copies of the:
      • Accumulation of Demerit Points Notice to Choose (F4011)
      • Courtesy Notice – Your licence option choice (F4010
      • Courtesy Notice - Failure to Notify Driver Licence Option Choice(F4926) can be the electronic version emailed to customer
      • Accumulation of Demerit Points - Notice to Choose eReminder (aka Demerit points choice reminder)
      OR
    • The TICA correspondence history must show that the sanction notice was reprinted at a CSC and handed to the customer before the mail sanction was applied
      OR
    • The address history in TICA shows that the mail sanction was incorrectly applied. For example:
      • Accumulation of Demerit Points Notice to Choose Sanction notice mailed 1st July
      • Address updated 3rd July
      • Courtesy Notice - Your licence option choice (F4010) mailed to updated address 15th July
      • Accumulation of Demerit Points Notice to Choose recorded as returned in TICA on 27th July and a mail restriction applied.
      The customer would have received F4010 and been aware of their upcoming suspension.

    If eligible, customers may have their suspension period recognised provided:
    • Their traffic history does not show any traffic infringements during the original suspension period.
    • They verbally confirm they have not been involved in a motor vehicle crash as the driver during the original sanction period.
    • They verbally confirm they have not been issued a notice to appear for unlicensed driving during the original sanction period.

    If the customer meets the requirements to have their prior suspension period recognised, complete the below checklist and email to Help Direct.

    Licence Suspension Checklist


    Help Direct will correct the customer record to show that the previous suspension period has been recognised and will withdraw the re-issued notice/s.

    Note:Should a customer have evidence they were aware of the suspension please refer to Help Direct for assessment.
