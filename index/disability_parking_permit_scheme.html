Disability Parking Permit Scheme (220)


About Disability Parking Permits
What it isA Disability Parking Permit provides parking concessions in designated areas to eligible persons who have a severe restriction to their functional ability to walk or persons who have been assessed as legally blind.
Types and Period of Issue
Permit Types and Period of Issue
Permit Types and Period of Issue
    TypePeriod of Issue
    Australian Disability Parking Permit (ADPP) - Individual
    • Five Year Permit (A5)
    		Can be issued for a 5-year period where the applicant's medical condition or disability is permanent, or where they have been assessed as legally blind.
    • Temporary 6-12 month permit (A1)
    		Can be issued for the duration of a temporary medical condition/disability/legal blindness or more than 6 months, or for a period of one year.
    Australian Disability Parking Permit (ADPP) - Organisation
    • Five Year Permit (AC)
    		5 years
    Queensland Disability Parking Permit - Red
    • Five Year Permit (R5)
    • These permits are only granted to existing R5 permit holders, as long as they continue to meet the eligibility criteria, and apply within 3 months of the permit expiry date. If any application is made after the 3 month period, the permit holder must apply for an ADPP, obtain medical certification and pay the non-refundable fee.
    • Not issued to new applicants.
    • Red permits can only be reissued to existing Red permit holders for a period of 5 years where they continue to meet the eligibility criteria. If a red permit expires for more than 3 months, the permit holder will need to apply for an ADPP, including medical certification and payment of the application fee.
    Queensland Disability Parking Permit - Blue
    • Blue permits are no longer valid and cannot be used.
    • Displaying a blue permit may incur a fine.

    Where one of these cards is not currently held by the applicant, the Medical Certification on the application form must be completed by a doctor, optometrist or ophthalmologist.

    A temporary medical condition or disability must be of at least six (6) months’ duration and certified as above.

Organisations
Organisations
    To be eligible for an ADPP an organisation must:
    • Meet Standard EOI requirements
    • Transport individuals who are eligible for the grant of a disability parking permit

    Organisation permits cannot be granted for vehicles:
    • Used as a taxi or limousine
    • With a Gross Vehicles Mass of more than 4.5 tonne
    • registered as a booked hire service (Uber, Ola etc)

    The number of permits to be granted is equal to the lesser number of either the:
    • Number of nominated vehicles or
    • Total number of staff (including volunteers) who provide the service

    Example:
    • If an organisation has 5 vehicles and 15 employees, only 5 permits can be granted.
    • If an organisation has 10 vehicles and 8 employees, only 8 permits can be granted

Forwarding an application to the Disability Parking Permit Unit

To avoid any unnecessary delays with the application process, the How to: Process Application for a Disability Parking Permit must be followed.

When a customer presents an applicable concession/benefit card, staff must copy both front and back of the concession card when accepting a Disability Parking Permit application for a person who is legally blind. The copy of the card as well as the application form must then be sent to the Disability Parking Unit.

Using a Disability Parking Permit
Conditions of Use
The Permit
The Permit
    • Must be displayed so that the permit number and expiry date are clearly visible from the outside of the vehicle
    • Must be displayed when the permit holder is accessing a disability parking bay and is entering or exiting the vehicle
    • Must only be used on a vehicle that is 4.5t GVM or less
    • Must not be damaged, altered or copied.
TransactionsIf a permit is granted, the department will post the permit to the applicant.

Unsuccessful applicants will be notified by mail.

Notification of approval or refusal of a permit will be sent by mail within approximately 10 business days of the application being received for processing. Postage time can take a further 5-10 days.

Note:The permit cannot be collected personally.

Duplicate Permits for Island Residents
Duplicate Permits for Island Residents
    Permit holders living on a Queensland island may be issued a second permit upon request. The permit holder must reside on an island not accessible to the mainland by bridge.

Invitation to Apply (formerly known as renewal)
Invitation to Apply (formerly known as renewal)
    An 'Invitation to Apply' notice is sent to A5 and AC permit holders 6 weeks prior to the permit expiry date. The term Renewal is no longer used. Each application is assessed independently of previous applications and therefore permits are not automatically granted.

    A copy of the front page of the application should be date stamped and given back to the customer for their record keeping.
    Note:If the customer is unable to present the Invitation to Apply notice because it has been lost, stolen or destroyed, they may complete an Australian Disability Parking Permit Application (Individual)(F4814) and write on the top of the form that their application is an invitation to apply.
    6-12 Month Permit (A1)
    6-12 Month Permit (A1)
      6-12 month permit holders are not invited to apply for a further permit.

      As a courtesy, an expiry notice is sent to the address recorded on the Disabled Parking (Dispark) database approximately 6 weeks before the expiry date.

      An expiry notice advises the applicant that if they continue to experience severe functional mobility impairment or an ongoing diagnosis of legal blindness, they may submit a new application for a permit.

      See Transaction Requirements Checklist for application requirements.

    Five Year Permit Holder (A5 and AC)
    Five Year Permit Holder (A5 and AC)
      6 weeks prior to the expiry date of an A5, AC permit, an Invitation to Apply notice (F4823 for ADPP) will automatically be sent to the address recorded on the Dispark database.

      If the permit holder requires another permit, the Invitation to Apply notice must be completed and returned to the department within 3 months of the permit expiry date. Applications are independently assessed irrespective of previous successful applications.

      Note:Expired permits are not valid and cannot be used. Using an expired permit may incur a fine.

      See Transaction Requirements Checklist for application requirements.
Changing from R5 to ADPP
Changing from R5 to ADPP
    An R5 permit holder may apply for an ADPP at any time.

    See Transaction Requirements Checklist for application requirements.

    If the permit holder is unsuccessful in their application for an ADPP they are able to continue to use their valid red permit.

Review of a decision
Review of a decision
    If a permit is refused or the applicant is granted a permit other than that originally applied for, the applicant may request a review of that decision.

    This review of decision application request must be made within 28 days from the department giving the applicant notification of the original decision.

    The applicant may apply for a review by either:
    Applying to TMR
    • Submit a completed Application for a Review of a Decision(F3859)
      or
    • Submit a written application detailing any further information that may support their application

    TMR review requests (including any supporting documentation) can be mailed or emailed to Disability Parking Scheme

    Following a review, the applicant will be given written notification of the decision. If the applicant is still not satisfied with the decision from the Principal Advisor (Central Operations and Support) they may request this decision be reviewed by QCAT.
    Applying to Queensland Civil Administrative Tribunal (QCAT)
    Alternatively, the applicant may apply to QCAT. Applications to QCAT must be made in the approved form and submitted to QCAT. This application may incur a fee.
Show Cause
Show Cause
    Note: CSC staff are not required to conduct show cause proceedings. The following is for information purposes only.

    Show Cause proceedings may be initiated against the permit holder for either:
    • no longer being eligible for a permit, but retaining it;
      or
    • misuse of a permit.

    The permit holder must provide written representation to TMR by the date on the Show Cause notice. If no response is supplied by the date given, TMR will initiate cancellation proceedings.

    Once written representation is received from the permit holder and TMR has contacted and received MP/OT additional information (if required), TMR will review the information.

    The permit holder will receive written notification regarding the outcome of the decision. If the permit holder is not satisfied with the decision the permit holder may ask for a review of that decision by following the Review of decision above.

    TMR review requests, (including any supporting documentation) can be mailed or emailed to Disability Parking Scheme.
Cancellation
Cancellation
    A cancellation process may occur for any of the following reasons:
    • A permit is no longer required
    • Show Cause process recommends the cancellation
    • Applicant has been confirmed to be deceased by the Birth Deaths and Marriages cross match process
    • TMR has been informed that a permit holder is deceased and sufficient evidence is provided
Return of Permit
Return of Permit
    A permit must be returned if:
    • customer is deceased;
      or
    • the permit has expired

    Expired permits must be returned within 14 days of the expiry date.

    If a permit is returned for any other reason, it must be returned to the Department with a note briefly explaining the reasons for its return.

    Customer is to be given document receipt for the returned permit
    ADPP Return Receipt

    CSC staff should destroy the permit, place in a secure destruction bin and email notification of permit details and reason for customer return to the Disability Parking Permit Unit (applications only)
Permit Validity
A valid permit is one that:
A valid permit is one that:
    • Is being used by the applicant it is granted to
    • Is not expired, suspended or cancelled
    • Is not a copy
    • Has not been altered
    • Has not been damaged

    Use of a permit that is not valid is an offence and the permit holder may incur a fine.
Parking Concessions in Qld
Permit holders are entitled to park in:
Permit holders are entitled to park in:
    Designated disability parking spaces:
    ADPPAny designated disability parking space, identified by the international symbol of access (regardless of the colour of the signage), in on-street locations or off-street locations such as shopping centres, entertainment areas, hospitals and the like.
    RedAny designated disability parking space, identified by the international symbol of access, (regardless of the colour of the signage) in off-street locations, such as shopping centres, entertainment areas hospitals and the like.

    Note:Red permit holders are not entitled to on-street parking unless the relevant local government authority extends the ADPP permit concession to red permit holders (refer customer to the local government authority).
    Metered and regulated areas:
    ADPPAny parking space in metered and regulated areas, free of charge, if the time limit on the sign is:
    • less than 30 minutes (parking is allowed for up to 30 minutes); or
    • 30 minutes or more (parking is allowed for an unlimited period)
    RedRed permit holders are not entitled to on-street regulated parking unless the local government authority for the regulated parking extends the ADPP permit concession to red permit holders (refer customer to the local government authority).
Use of permits while travellingValid ADPPs are accepted for use across Australian states and territories. Parking concessions may differ between states so the permit holder is advised to contact the jurisdiction of travel for further details.

Red permit holders may use their permit when travelling interstate.
Non Qld PermitsAll overseas and interstate permits are valid for use in Queensland providing they are not expired, suspended or cancelled. Parking concessions are the same as ADPP parking concessions (Refer: Parking Concession in Qld above).

If a holder of an interstate permit resides permanently in Queensland, the permit holder must apply for a Queensland granted ADPP within 14 days of becoming a permanent Queensland resident.
