TMR Online Services Guide (5101)


What it isThe Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR) offers customers a wide range of online services that enable them to complete transactions and obtain specific information regarding their licence/s, industry authorities and vehicle/vessel registration without attending a Customer Service Centre (CSC).
Terms and conditions of useBefore a customer can use any TMR online services, they must read and agree to the general terms and conditions of use. Some services may have further specific conditions which may also need to be agreed.
Online Services Screen Shots/ e-correspondence examplesSupporting screen shots of TMR's Online Services can be found on the Business Systems Support SharePoint Page. This page also contains examples of emails, eNotices and eReminders that are sent to customers.

Information specific to Direct Debit can be found on the Direct Debit Support Materials page.
Accessing TMR Online ServicesTo access online services, certain information must be entered by the customer. The amount of information required depends on the online service being accessed and the associated risk to the customer's online information.

The levels of access and requirements to use online services are outlined below.

Create Customer Login
Create Customer Login

    To access PrepL the customer must provide an email address and password. Access to the service is available once the customer has confirmed their account using an emailed confirmation code or link.
Open Access Services
Open Access Services
    These online services are open to all without the customer logging in or verifying details.

    Information can be queried (such as a licence or registration status) however no personal customer information is displayed.
Verifying Details Services
Verifying Details Services
    These online services require the customer to enter some identifying details relevant to the transaction, but do not require customer login.

    The service being accessed determines what information is required as it relates to the service itself. (e.g. the unique payment reference number (located only on the infringement notice sent to the customer) is required to pay an infringement online.
Customer login services
Customer login services
    These are online services which require the customer to identify themselves before they can conduct business online, similar to providing Evidence of Identity in person or over the phone.

    For all customer login services, the following information is required to be entered:

    Individual:Organisation:
    • CRN
    • CRN
    • First Name
    • Company Name
    • Last Name
    • ACN
    • Date of Birth
    Note:Customers with only one name must leave the first name field blank and only enter their name in the last name field.
    Note:Only organisations that have an ACN can login to the TMR online services.
Extra Details
Extra Details
    Some online services, which are only available to individuals, require the following extra details:
    • Online access code
    OR
    • Answer two questions about items such as a customer's licence, renewal notice or traffic fine. Questions will only be displayed if the customer has been issued the relevant documents previously.

    Online Question:Customer will need to provide:
    What is the number on the back of your licence, authority or adult proof of age card?Smartcard number with a status of Active or Pending Returned (cannot be expired)
    What are the details on your registration certificate or Vehicle Registration Notice (VRN)?The receipt number and receipt date from the Registration Certificate
    or
    The document number from the Vehicle Registration Notice (VRN) issued within the last 15 months.
    Trouble Shooting
    Trouble Shooting
      If the customer cannot find the above details, use the below guide to assist.

    VRN
    VRN
      The document number is only printed on the VRN:
      • if the customer is eligible to renew using the online services; and
      • the document was posted to them.

      Note:This means a document number will not be displayed on an emailed VRN.

      If the VRN was issued to two registered operators and the second registered operator is an individual, the second registered operator can use the document number to authenticate when accessing the online services.

    Registration Certificate and Receipt Number
    Registration Certificate and Receipt Number
      If a registration certificate is issued following payment, it will contain the receipt date and number. The receipt number must meet the following criteria to be used:
      • Must be one of the three most recent receipt numbers for the relevant registration.
      • The customer must have been the R/O (including being the second R/O) at the time the receipt number was issued

    What are the details on your fine (infringement or camera detected offence notice)?The customer will need to provide:
    • Infringement number and issuing officer number from infringement notice (Electronic or handwritten) issued by Queensland Police, Maritime Safety Queensland or Translink.
    • Notice number and offence date (from Camera Detected Offence Notice and the Online Enforcement Option Nomination Service (OLEONS))

    The infringement must be:
    • One of the 3 most recent manually issued infringements or
    • One of the 3 most recent camera detected offence dates; or
    • Issued within 30 days of the third oldest infringement.
    What is the document number on your driver licence renewal notice?The document number is valid for 3 months from the date of issue. It is only printed on the driver licence renewal notice if the customer is eligible to renew online and the document was posted to them.

    Note:This means a document number will not be displayed on an emailed VRN.

Additional Level of Security
Additional Level of Security
    Individuals can choose to apply an additional level of security to make it more difficult for someone else to access online services without their authorisation to view, change or request changes to their information held by the department.

    If an individual applies an additional level of security:
    • extra details are always required to use any customer login services or to cancel the additional level of security.
    • they can change their email address but they cannot remove it without first cancelling the additional security.
QGov ID
QGov ID
    An individual customer can register for a QGov ID on the TMR or Queensland Government websites to gain access to multiple Queensland Government services securely using the same login details (email address and password).

    When a customer logs into TMR for the first time using their QGov ID, they will be required to identify themselves by providing certain details Refer: Customer login services. Once this has occurred, any future logins using the QGov ID will only require the customer to enter their QGov login (email address and password).

NOAC RestrictionsA No Online Access Customer (NOAC) restriction is available to customer who wish to prevent access to TMR on-line services. The NOAC must be requested by the customer and will remain in place until the customer requests its removal.

For the application an removal process refer to:

Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System Information > Qualifications, Restrictions & Text > Customer Restrictions > NOAC
