Refuse to grant (1040)


What it isThe Chief Executive may refuse to grant or renew a licence if the person does not meet the required eligibility or requirements
Notification to ApplicantUnless otherwise stated below, a written notice must be given to the applicant advising:
  • The decision
  • The reason for the decision
  • Information regarding reconsideration or appeal of the decision (where applicable)
Grounds for Refusal & NoticesThe written notice must be manually typed. The written notices are included below within the relevant refusal reason sections.
Applicant Disqualified
Applicant Disqualified
    The applicant is disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence.
    TO(RUM) Act s.127(6)

    If the disqualification is recorded, a refusal letter may be generated from TICA if available. Otherwise the following letter should be completed and issued to the applicant:
    Refusal to Grant a Licence - Disqualified from Holding a Licence
Non-Qld Resident
Non-Qld Resident
    The applicant is not a resident of Queensland.
    TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s19

    The following letter should be completed and issued to the applicant:
    Refusal to Grant a Licence - Not a Queensland Resident
Mental or Physical Incapacity
Mental or Physical Incapacity
    Information has been given by the licence holder or health professional with or without a Medical Certificate for Motor Vehicle Driver (F3712), indicating that they have a medical condition that affects their ability to drive safely.

    The following letter should be completed and issued to the applicant:
    Refusal to Grant a Licence - Medical Grounds
Authority to Drive in Qld Suspended
Authority to Drive in Qld Suspended
    The applicant has had their authority to drive in Qld under their non-Qld licence suspended.
    TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s19

    The following letter should be completed and issued to the applicant:
    Refusal to Grant a Licence - Authority to Drive in Queensland Suspended
Failed Prescribed Tests
Failed Prescribed Tests
    If the applicant failed a written or practical driving test, oral notice of refusal may be given, unless otherwise requested by the applicant.
    TO(RUM-DL) Reg 2010 s19

    A refusal letter can be generated from TICA if available, or the following letter may be completed and issued to the customer:
    Refusal to Grant Licence - Failed Test

    Foreign Licence Holder - Authority to Drive Withdrawn due to Failed Practical Driving Test

    Where a foreign licence holder fails a practical driving test, their authority to drive will be withdrawn. The customer must be issued with a Notice of Withdrawal of Authority to Drive (F5044).

    Note: The customer must be advised that they may use their foreign licence to undertake a subsequent practical driving test; however, if they wish to gain further practical driving experience, they will be required to obtain a Queensland Learner Licence.
Learner Tenure Period not Met
Learner Tenure Period not Met
    Special Need - Tenure or Age Exemption
Non Surrender of Non-Qld Licence
Non Surrender of Non-Qld Licence
    The applicant holds a non-Qld licence and will not surrender the licence when making the application for a Qld driver licence.

    A refusal letter can be generated from TICA for authorities linked to NEVDIS. For other authorities the following letter should be completed and issued to the applicant:
    Refusal to Grant a Licence - Holds a Valid Non-Queensland Licence
