Rest Areas and Camping Compliance (7612)

Rest Areas and Camping Compliance
What it isDriver fatigue has been established as a leading source of road trauma, and has been classed as one of the 'fatal five'. For this reason the Department of Transport and Main Roads provides rest areas and permits in limited circumstances a person to camp for fatigue management on a state-controlled road where no other options are available.

All road users are vulnerable to fatigue. However, as commercial trucks and fatigue-regulated heavy vehicles do not have access to the same types of rest opportunities as the general road user, heavy vehicle rest areas and general vehicle rest areas are provided to assist with fatigue management.

Part 3 of the Transport Infrastructure (State-controlled Roads) Regulation 2017 (Regulation) sets out the rules for designated rest area use (both heavy vehicle and general vehicle) and for camping on a state-controlled road outside a designated rest area.
Rest Area Use
Heavy Vehicle Rest Area
What is a Heavy Vehicle Rest Area
What is a Heavy Vehicle Rest Area
    A heavy vehicle rest area is for the sole use of heavy vehicles.

    A heavy vehicle for the purpose of heavy vehicle rest areas is:
    • a commercial truck, which is a vehicle over 4.5t designed to carry goods, and can also be towing a trailer or a combination of trailers;
      or
    • a fatigue-regulated heavy vehicle under the Heavy Vehicle National Law Queensland, section 7;
      or
    • an escort vehicle or pilot vehicle accompanying a commercial truck.

    The use of a heavy vehicle rest area:
    • is solely for drivers and passengers of heavy vehicles;
      and
    • is for heavy vehicle drivers to manage their fatigue;
      and
    • must be in compliance with the sign or marking in the heavy vehicle rest area;
      and
    • may be used by a road user other than a heavy vehicle if they have a reasonable excuse.

    The maximum penalty for non-compliance is 20 penalty units.

    Reasonable excuse
    Reasonable excuse
      A reasonable excuse may include:
      • a breakdown;
        or
      • a flat tyre;
        or
      • a medical emergency.
Who can use a Heavy Vehicle Rest Area
Who can use a Heavy Vehicle Rest Area
    The use of a heavy vehicle rest area:
    • is solely for drivers and passengers of heavy vehicles;
      and
    • is for heavy vehicle drivers to manage their fatigue;
      and
    • must be in compliance with the sign or marking in the heavy vehicle rest area;
      and
    • may be used by a road user other than a heavy vehicle if they have a reasonable excuse.

    The maximum penalty for non-compliance is 20 penalty units.

    Reasonable excuse
    Reasonable excuse
      A reasonable excuse may include:
      • a breakdown;
        or
      • a flat tyre;
        or
      • a medical emergency.
General Vehicle Rest Area
General Vehicle Rest Areas
General Vehicle Rest Areas
    A general vehicle rest area is for the use of general vehicles, which includes:
    • a bicycle;
      or
    • a motorbike;
      or
    • a vehicle other than a heavy vehicle, escort vehicle or pilot vehicle;
      or
    • a recreational vehicle.
Heavy Vehicles using General Vehicle Rest Areas
Heavy Vehicles using General Vehicle Rest Areas
    Heavy vehicles can use general vehicle rest areas if permitted by the signage or marking.

    The use of a general vehicle rest area:
    • must be in compliance with the sign or marking in the general vehicle rest area;
      and
    • must not exceed 20 hours in a 24-hour period from when the person first entered the rest area, unless otherwise signed.

    The maximum penalty for non-compliance is 20 penalty units.
Prohibited Conduct in a Rest Area
Prohibited Conduct
Prohibited Conduct
    The Regulation lists conduct which is prohibited in a rest area (heavy vehicle and general vehicle).

    A road user must not:
    • deposit:
      • litter at the rest area unless a container is provided for depositing litter;
        or
      • commercial or residential waste at the rest area;
      or
    • damage any wall, fence, building, barrier, sign, receptacle or other structure on, or attached to, the rest area;
      or
    • light a fire or use a fire pit at, or in, the rest area;
      or
    • dispose of any human waste at the rest area, unless a facility is provided for disposing of human waste.

    For a general vehicle rest area, a road user must not:
    • operate a generator or engine-driven equipment between 7pm and 7am;
      or
    • do any other activity or use any other machinery or appliance that unreasonably interferes with another person's use or enjoyment of the rest area.

    The maximum penalty for non-compliance is 20 penalty units.
Camping
General Requirements
General Requirements
General Requirements
    Camping is generally prohibited on a state-controlled road. However, a person may camp if necessary for fatigue management purposes, if:
    • in accordance with the permitted camping conditions;
      or
    • permitted under another Act;
      or
    • the person has a reasonable excuse.

    Reasonable Excuse
    Reasonable Excuse
      A reasonable excuse may include:
      • a breakdown;
        or
      • a flat tyre;
        or
      • a medical emergency.
    Permitted camping conditions
    Permitted camping conditions
      A person may only camp when all the following conditions have been met:
      • the road is not a motorway or toll road;
        and
      • there is no sign prohibiting camping at the area;
        and
      • the person camps between the hours of 4pm and 8am;
        and
      • the camp site is located:
        • outside a built-up area (as per Queensland Road Rules);
          and
        • at least 200m from any kerb, channel or footpath;
          and
        • at least 100m from entries and exits of rest areas;
          and
        • at least 100m from a construction site on a state-controlled road, gravel stockpile, pit or quarry;
          and
        • at least 100m from a residence;
          and
        • at least 100m from the high-water mark of any Queensland waters;
          and
        • at least 9m from the nearest traffic lane.

      The maximum penalty for non-compliance is 20 penalty units.
Prohibited Conduct at a Camp site
Prohibited Conduct
Prohibited Conduct
    Where camping is permitted, a person must not:
    • deposit:
      • litter unless in a container provided for depositing litter;
        or
      • commercial or residential waste at the rest area;
      or
    • damage any wall, fence, building, barrier, sign, receptacle or other structure;
      or
    • operate a generator or engine-driven equipment;
      or
    • light a fire or use a fire pit;
      or
    • dispose of any human waste at the rest area, unless a facility is provided for disposing of human waste;
      or
    • detach any trailer that is attached to the person's vehicle;
      or
    • interfere with the state-controlled road or the operation or use of the road;
      or
    • do any activity or use any machinery or appliance that unreasonably interferes with another person's use or enjoyment of the area.

    Interfere
    Interfere
      Interfere means to affect the safe operation, or use, of a state-controlled road.

      Examples of interfering
      Examples of interfering
        • attaching a thing to a tree or thing established or used by the chief executive for the operation or use of the road;
        • erect any thing or structure that overhangs any portion of a traffic lane on the road;
        • do any activity, or erect a thing or structure that is reasonably likely to:
          • create a hazard on the road;
            or
          • restrict or obstruct the movement, or field of vision, of a road user.

        The maximum penalty for non-compliance is 20 penalty units.
    Sanctions
    Available Sanctions
    Available Sanctions
      When a contravention of the Regulation is detected, sanctions available to authorised officers include:
        • Direction Notices;
        • Formal Cautions (minor breaches);
        • Penalty Infringement Notices for minor or substantial breaches;
        • Prosecution for severe breaches or breaches for which infringement notices are not suitable.
      Direction Notice
      Direction Notice
        An authorised officer should only issue a direction relating to rest area use or camping if the authorised officer believes on reasonable grounds that a person has committed a rest area or camping offence or an emergency situation exists.

        A direction notice requires a person to leave the area and take their property with them.

        When issuing a Direction Notice, the authorised officer must warn the person that non-compliance is an offence.

        A person may only refuse if they have a reasonable excuse.
        Reasonable excuse
        Reasonable excuse
          • a breakdown;
            or
          • a flat tyre;
            or
          • a medical emergency.
      Formal Caution
      Formal Caution
        A Formal Caution is a penalty infringement notice of $0 value.

        An authorised officer may issue a caution if the authorised officer reasonably believes:
        • a person has contravened the Regulation;
          and
        • the contravention is not substantial, severe or critical;
          and
        • the person is unaware of the contravention;
          and
        • the contravention may appropriately be dealt with by way of a Formal Caution.
        Penalty Infringement Notice
        Penalty Infringement Notice
          A Penalty Infringement Notice may be issued as an alternative to court proceedings. An authorised officer who reasonably believes that a person has contravened the Regulation provisions may issue the person with a Penalty Infringement Notice.

          Each Penalty Infringement Notice is 2 penalty units.

          Refer: Penalty Infringement Notice Guide

          Prosecution
          Prosecution
            An authorised officer who reasonably believes a person has contravened the Regulation may charge a person for an offence which cannot be dealt with by way of a penalty infringement notice.

            A person may be charged for occasions where there is a serious or major breach of legislation, serious risk of harm or danger or when multiple offences have been detected
