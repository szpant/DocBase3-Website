About Restricted Licences
Definitions
Driver Licence Definitions
What it is
Licence to Drive in stated Circumstances
Licence to Drive in stated Circumstances
    A Restricted licence is issued:
    • After a person's previous Queensland open licence was cancelled because of a disqualification period imposed by a Queensland court as a result of a drink or drug driving conviction
    and
    • Authorises the holder to drive only in stated circumstances directly connected with the person's means of earning a living.
TO(RUM) Act 1995 s87
Getting a Restricted Licence
EligibleA person is only eligible to apply for a Restricted licence if, when they committed the drink or drug driving offence they were the holder of -
(a)a Qld class C open driver licence and were driving a class C motor vehicle at the time of the drink driving offence; or
(b)a Qld class RE open licence and held that class of licence for at least 1 year in the last 5 years and were riding a class RE motorcycle at the time of the drink driving offence; or
(c)a Qld class R open licence and were riding a class R motorcycle at the time of the drink driving offence.

Note: The highest class held in each hierarchy is shown on a person's licence. For example, a person holding a class HC licence is also authorised to drive a class HR, MR, LR and C vehicle.

The person must also:
  • Be a resident of Queensland.
  • Meet the evidence of identity requirements. Refer: Evidence of Identity
  • Provide any documents required for the application. Refer: Transaction Requirements Checklist
Not Eligible
Not Eligible
Not Eligible
    1. A person is NOT eligible to apply if, when they committed the drink or drug driving offence —
    (a)they were the holder of a non-Qld driver licence;
    (b)they were the holder of a Qld learner, provisional or probationary licence;
    (c)they were not the holder of a valid driver licence;
    (d)they were learning to drive the next higher class of licence under the authority of their Qld open licence;
    (e)they were the holder of a Qld restricted licence issued to them under a court order;
    (f)they were not authorised under their Qld open licence to drive the motor vehicle (for example, they were riding a motorcycle but they only held a class C licence);
    (g)they were using the motor vehicle in an activity directly connected with their means of earning a living;
    (h)they were driving a truck, tractor, specially constructed vehicle, bus (a motor vehicle built and fitted to carry more than 12 adults, including the driver), articulated motor vehicle, B-double, road train, taxi, limousine, tow truck, pilot or escort vehicle escorting an oversize vehicle, a vehicle carrying a placard load of dangerous goods or a vehicle used by a driver trainer to give driver training;
    (i)the concentration of alcohol in their blood or breath was equal to or more than 0.15%.
    2. A person is also NOT eligible to apply for a restricted licence if, within the last 5 years before making the application the person -
    (a)has been previously convicted in Qld of an offence under the Act, s79 (drink or drug driving offence) or s80(5A) (fail to supply specimen of breath for a breath test or a specimen of saliva for a saliva test);
    (b)has been previously convicted outside Qld of a drink or drug driving offence that would have been an offence under the Act, s79 or s80(5A), if it had been committed in Qld;
    (c)has been previously convicted in Qld of an offence under s328A of the Criminal Code (dangerous driving of a vehicle);
    (d)has been disqualified from holding or obtaining a Qld licence, or had a provisional or open licence suspended or cancelled, other than a suspension or cancellation for any of the following -
      • An immediate suspension as a result of a middle alcohol range offence.
      • A suspension, cancellation or disqualification that was set aside on appeal.
      • A suspension, cancellation or disqualification because of a mental or physical disability.
      • A suspension because the person did not pay a fine.
      • A suspension because the person failed to answer a charge of drink or drug driving.
      • A 24 hour suspension because of a drink or drug driving related matter.
      • A suspension where a court has granted a special hardship order.
    (TO(RUM) Act s.87(5B))
    3. A person is also NOT eligible to apply if, immediately prior to being convicted of the drink or drug driving offence, they no longer held a Qld open licence.
Application to Court
If Summonsed to Appear in Court (Notice to Appear)
If Summonsed to Appear in Court (Notice to Appear)
    The applicant must complete the Application for an Order directing the issue of a Restricted Licence (s87) (F3181) before the court hearing and give the application to the Magistrate immediately after they have been found guilty but before the Magistrate disqualifies them from holding or obtaining a driver licence.

    Once the Magistrate disqualifies the applicant (under Section 81(7)), they may not apply for a restricted driver licence.
If Infringement Notice Issued
If Infringement Notice Issued
    Note:At present QPS have withdrawn from using infringement notice PT56C and a Notice to Appear (similar to a summons) is being issued instead.

    Pay the Fine, Apply to the Court
    Pay the Fine, Apply to the Court
      To be eligible for a Restricted Driver Licence the applicant must do all of the following within 28 days from the issue of the ticket:
      • Pay the fine
      • Lodge an application for the issue of a Restricted Licence (F3181) with the Court in the district where they reside
      • Return the copy of the lodged application to TMR
    Don't Pay the Fine, Apply to the Court
    Don't Pay the Fine, Apply to the Court
      If the customer elects to not pay the fine and have the ticket heard in court, application for a Restricted Licence may be made at the court hearing.

      No advice is required to be given to TMR until the matter is determined.

Licence Period
Licence Period determined by Court
Licence Period determined by Court
    The period of a Restricted licence is determined by the Court. The period specified may contain full and partial years.

    Recording Partial Years
    Licences can only be issued on TICA as multiples of 1 year. If a restricted period contains a partial year the remainder of that year will be issued as the next eligible licence type, to a maximum of 5 years.

    Example

Using a Restricted Licence
Conditions
of Use
Conditions of Use
Conditions of Use
    A court that grants an application must make an order directing that a restricted licence be issued to the applicant during the period of the applicant's disqualification subject to restrictions specified in the order.

    The order will restrict the applicant to specified circumstances directly connected with the applicant's means of earning the applicant's livelihood and may include the class of vehicle, the purpose and the times at which or period of time during which a vehicle may be driven.

    Note:No further licence classes or learner licences may be issued to the person during the restricted period

    Carrying Licence while Driving
    Carrying Licence while Driving
      Restricted licence holders must:
      • Carry their driver licence with them at all times when driving
      • Carry and drive in accordance with, an order under s87 of the TO(RUM) Act 1995.
      • Produce the licence when requested by a Police Officer Police Powers & Responsibilities Act 2000 s.58
    Only Drive in Queensland
    Only Drive in Queensland
      Only drive in Queensland, unless specifically granted otherwise on the Court order
    Observe Classes & Conditions
    Observe Classes & Conditions
      • Only drive the vehicles of the classes specified on the licence TO(RUM) Act s 78
      • Comply with any licence conditions imposed (Eg: B = Synchromesh; M = Carry medical)
      • Only drive in accordance with the conditions (including class of vehicle) as specified on the Court order (indicated by an X1 condition on the licence)
    Observe the relevant Drink Driving Limit
    Observe the no Drug Driving Requirements
Disobey Court Order
Driving outside of Court Order Restrictions
Driving outside of Court Order Restrictions
    Where the holder of a restricted licence is convicted for driving contrary to the restrictions on their court order, the person is disqualified from holding or obtaining a driver licence for one of the following:
(TO(RUM) Act s87(10), (10A)(a) & (b))
    If the original restricted period has not yet expired:
    • For the remainder of the original disqualification period plus an additional 3 months
      Example
      Example
        • Date of offence 17/2/01
        • Convicted on 18/3/01
        • Original disqualification period will expire
        • on 5/6/01
        The person is now disqualified for the original period (5/6/01) plus an additional 3 months = 4/9/01

    Where the conviction date is later than the expiry date of the original disqualification period:
    • The person will be disqualified for 3 months from the date of conviction
      Example
      Example
        • Date of offence 30/5/01
        • Convicted on 7/7/01
        Original disqualification period expired on 5/6/01. The person is disqualified for 3 mths from 7/7/01 = 6/10/01
Changing the Court Ordered Restrictions
Change to Court Ordered Restrictions
Change to Court Ordered Restrictions
    A restricted licence holder may apply to the Court (in the district where they live) for a change to the court ordered restrictions due to their means of earning their livelihood changing. TO(RUMS) Act s.88

    The Application for an Order Varying Restrictions on a Restricted Licence (F3182) may be supplied by TMR, but TMR personnel do not lodge this application or the outcome of the application on TICA in any way.

    The customer is responsible for carrying the court order, which states any conditions the customer must comply with, whilst driving.

    Note:The holder of a Restricted licence may not obtain;
    • Learner licenses
    • Additional classes
    unless the class was held on their Qld Open licence immediately prior to the disqualification

    Tenure & Licence Progression
    Time may be accumulated over a 5 yr period
    Time may be accumulated over a 5 yr period
      The required period:
      • May be accumulated over the preceding 5 years
      • May be broken by expiry, suspension or cancellation
      • Can include time spent serving a period of good driving behaviour (GDB)
      • May include time spent on other P Type licences including Interstate / Overseas / Defence Force / Restricted

      Only those periods that the licence is actually valid will be considered toward the required period.
    When application for progression can be made
    When application for progression can be made
      Progressing from P Type to Open
      An application to progress from a P Type to an Open licence can be made once the person has held the P Type licence for the required period.

      The application can be made:
      • 6 weeks before the expiry of the P Type licence
      or
      • Up to 5 years after completion of the P Type required period
