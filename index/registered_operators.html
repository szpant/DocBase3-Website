Registered Operators (3022)


What is it?A registered operator is the entity that is responsible for:
  • The operation of the vehicle
  • The vehicle being in a safe condition for use on the roads

All costs including:
  • Registration renewals
  • Infringements

Being the registered operator does not infer legal ownership of the vehicle. The legal owner is the entity that purchased the vehicle. Department of Transport and Main Roads records up to two registered operators, one of which must be a legal entity.
Requirements
  • At least one registered operator must be a legal entity
  • Must be over 18 years old to be the registered operator of a heavy vehicle
  • Must pay the appropriate costs

There are no age restrictions on the registered operators of a light vehicle.
Rules
Legal Entity
Legal Entity
    A legal entity is defined as one who can:
    • Sue and be sued
    • Acquire, hold and dispose of property
    • Enter into contracts

    For further details on Organisations which are legal entities Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > Customer Types > Organisations > List of Organisation Types

    Valid legal entities include:
    • Living people (individuals)
    • Government departments, bodies or agencies
    • Companies registered with the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC)
    • Overseas corporation registered with ASIC
    • Incorporated organisations
    • Organisation incorporated interstate and registered with ASIC
    • Queensland licensed motor dealers
    • Interstate licensed motor dealers
    • Body corporate registered with the Department of Natural Resources
Non-Legal Entities
Non-Legal Entities
    TMR may record a non-legal entity as a registered operator provided that the associated legal entity is recorded as the second registered operator (for example, a Business name as first registered operator with the individual or company who owns the business as the second registered operator).

    Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > Customer Types > Organisations > List of Organisation Types

    Non-legal Entities Include:
    • Business name registered with ASIC
    • A trust not registered with ASIC
    • Partnerships operating as a business (Partnerships of two persons are treated as individuals)

    Note: An interstate registered business number cannot be used to register vehicles in Queensland even if they have a legal entity behind it.

    Correspondence
    Correspondence
      If the non-legal entity is recorded as the first registered operator they will receive all correspondence except infringements, registration certificates (if applicable) and VRRNs/direct debit notifications if the vehicle is enrolled in Direct Debit.

      Infringements will be sent to the associated legal entity.

      If the associated legal entity is an individual they will be emailed a registration certificate if they are signed up for eNotices.

      Note: A vehicle with an organisation as the first registered operator can be enrolled in direct debit if an individual is recorded as the second registered operator is the direct debit authority holder. The individual will receive all VRRNs and direct debit notifications.

    Closed organisations
    Closed organisations
      An organisation must be updated as ‘closed’ in TICA when:
      • It is no longer a legal entity (for example, a company that has a status of de-registered with ASIC)
      • It is no longer operating (for example, a business or trust has a status of cancelled on the Australian Business Register (ABR), or a child record that has closed their branch).
      • A request is received from the organisation to close their CRN

      No supporting documentation is required to close an organisation.

      An organisation can be closed by a CSO or Data Integrity. Requests to close CRN’s from other areas or agencies (for example, TMR prosecutions or SPER) should be emailed to CSB.Data Integrity Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > The How to: Customer > Customer Procedures > Close organisation customer

      Once a CRN is closed, NORG and NOAC restrictions are automatically applied to the CRN to restrict registration and plate transactions. Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > System information > Customer Restrictions.

      If a closed CRN is reinstated (that is, the “closed” details are removed) the NORG and NOAC will automatically be removed and a medium level business exception will be created. Supporting documentation (E.g. ASIC print out for re-registered company, evidence supporting the removal for correction or to allow a transaction) is required to reinstate a closed CRN and is to be retained in the operator’s daily filing.

    Deceased Individuals
    Deceased Individuals
      An individual is to be updated as deceased when evidence of their death is received. That is, an original or certified copy of an acceptable Proof of Death document.

      An individual’s record in TICA can be updated as deceased by a CSO, certain COS operators and Business Systems. Requests to update a CRN as deceased from other areas or agencies (for example, TMR prosecutions or SPER) should be emailed to Business Systems Proof of death must be included with their email.
      Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > The How To: Customer Procedures > Individual Customers > Record customer as deceased

      Once a customer is updated as deceased, a NORG and NOAC restriction will be automatically applied to their record to restrict registration and plate transactions. Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > System information > Customer Restrictions.

      If a deceased CRN is reinstated (that is, the deceased details are removed) the NORG and NOAC restrictions will automatically be removed and a medium level business exception will be created. If a manually added NOAC existed prior to the record being deceased, it will remain in place when the CRN is reinstated. Supporting documentation is required to reinstate a deceased CRN and must be retained in the operator’s daily filing. Examples of supporting documents are:
      • Standard EOI (photocopy retained) or 1:1 image match for smartcard transaction if customer is alive
      • Note on application if reinstated to allow transaction
      • Report for file if correcting an error.

Responsible Operators Nomination
Responsible Operators Nomination
    If a person has previously claimed that as a registered operator they were not the driver of a vehicle and have failed to notify the name and address of the driver of a vehicle that was involved in a camera detected offence, written notice requesting the registered operator to nominate a responsible operator may be issued under the TO(RUM) Act 1995 s170(1).

    Responsible operator in these circumstances must be either an:
    • Individual who is both:
      • 17 years old or more
        and
      • The holder of a driver licence issued in Qld or interstate
    • Organisation that is a legal entity

    Note:A 'Responsible Operator' cannot be nominated for Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) detected unregistered or uninsured infringement notices.
    Refer: Offence Management > 2. Infringement Notices > Infringement Notice Guide
Paying Agents
Paying Agents
    A paying agent is:
    • A legal entity that is not a private individual
    • Responsible for the payment of renewal fees
    • Recorded as the second registered operator

    Note: A vehicle registered with a non-legal entity as the first registered operator can't have a paying agent.


    The leasing company/paying agent will receive the vehicle registration notice only, all other documents including certificates and infringements will be sent to the first registered operator.

    Add a Paying Agent
    Add a Paying Agent
      Before a leasing company/paying agent can be attached, a written request from both registered operator (individual or organisation) and the leasing company/paying agent must be obtained.

      Requirements
      Requirements
        Both leasing company/paying agent and registered operator requests must:
        • Be in writing (letter or email to the paying agent's responsible CSC)
        • Be on official letterhead from the organisation's email address and include an appropriate signature block
        • Include the registration number, make, model and VIN or engine number

        Leasing company/paying agent must also provide:
        • Their CRN and postal address
        • Confirmation that they manage the vehicle and wish to be applied as the paying agent

        The registered operator (individual or organisation) must also provide:
        • CRN and confirmation that they wish to add the leasing company/paying agent as the paying agent (this can be on application at time of New Business)


    Note:If a leasing company/paying agent is being attached when an application for registration or transfer is lodged in the paying agent/leasing company name only, the paying agent/ leasing company must sign departmental forms.
    Refer: Required Signatories on Letters of Authority- Paying Agent/Leasing Company below

    Required Signatories on Letters of Authority- Paying Agent/Leasing Company
    Required Signatories on Letters of Authority- Paying Agent/Leasing Company
      Paying Agents and Leasing companies are to be advised of the following requirements when completing departmental forms, where a vehicle is registered to:
      • First Registered Operator Only
        The First Registered Operator must sign departmental forms unless a Letter Of Authority has been completed by First Registered Operator. The Letter Of Authority must state that the person acting on behalf of the first registered operator can sign the documents on their behalf.
      • First and Second Registered Operator
        The First and Second Registered Operator must both sign departmental forms unless a Letter Of Authority has been completed by both operators. The Letter of Authority must state that the person acting on behalf of the first and second registered operator can sign documents on their behalf.
      • First Registered Operator and Paying Agent (or Leasing Company)
        First Registered Operator must sign departmental forms unless a Letter of Authority has been completed by First Registered Operator. The Letter of Authority must state that the person acting on behalf of the first registered operator can sign the documents on their behalf.

    Remove a Paying Agent
    Remove a Paying Agent
      The paying agent must advise in writing:
      • they no longer manage the vehicle and to remove them as leasing/paying agent
        or
      • the organisation/private person is no longer their client and to remove them as the Leasing/Paying agent.
    QFLEET
    QFLEET
      TMR has an individual administrative arrangement with QFLEET (who administer vehicles on behalf of the Queensland Government).

      To cancel a government registered vehicle:
      • QFLEET can sign and lodge a cancellation application on behalf of the registered operator
        or
      • Manheim may sign and lodge the cancellation application on behalf of the registered operator (QFLEET have provided a letter of authority for Manheim Pty Ltd to sign and transact cancellations of government registered vehicles on their behalf. A copy of the letter of authority should be filed with each cancellation application. (For a copy of the letter of authority - contact the Carseldine CSC).

      Note:Refer to above Required Signatories on Letters of Authority for all other QFLEET transactions.
