Background
Disability Parking Permits
Disability Parking Permits
    The Disability Parking Permit (DPP) Unit receives approximately 180-250 applications per day through a number of different channels, including:
    • Online - 15%
    • Post - 20%
    • In person (CSC) - 65%

    The opportunity exists to partially automate how Disability Parking Permit (DPP) applications are received through the CSC network and Online. Assessing an application is a highly repetitive process which has limited outcomes (e.g. Approve, Seek further information and Reject).
Robotics Process Automation (RPA)
Robotics Process Automation (RPA)
    As part of Customer Services Branch (CSB) Mobility Strategy, CSB approached the TMR Digital Capability Unit to partner on an opportunity to explore the application of Robotics Process Automation (RPA) in its business.

    RPA is the application of technology that allows a business to configure computer software or a “robot” to capture and interpret existing applications for processing a transaction, manipulating data, triggering responses and communicating with other digital systems.

    While the concept of automation has been around for a while, it now finds itself surrounded by an evolution of RPA which is attracting interest from more companies at the beginning of their digital transformation journey due to its ability to rapidly automate repeatable, mundane daily processes.

    CSB has identified RPA as a potential key tool and capability as part of the CSB Mobility Strategy, to not only generate efficiencies for the branch, but enable the business to focus on more customer value adding activities.

    There are several applications for RPA. These range from customer facing tasks (such as application processing for different registration and licensing products), to internal business tasks in CSB (for example, processes in finance, data analysis, data integrity and human resources).
How it worksThe solution for in-person and online Disability Parking Permit (DPP) applications involves partially automating and improving the quality of the DPP procedure to process Individual DPP applications.

Operators will submit DPP applications to a newly created SharePoint list that will have logic to identify customers and confirm other details on the form. The application will still be emailed as a PDF attachment to the DPP team so that it can be uploaded to the DPP system. The email template will no longer need to be completed in addition to emailing the application. The SharePoint list will aid the DPP unit in processing applications quicker resulting in increased customer satisfaction and decreased complaints and queries on permit statuses.

Organisation applications are out of scope of this release due to the complexity around verifying whether they are a legitimate business transporting people that meets the legislative criteria. These applications can still be lodged through the SharePoint list with some validation but will require a more detailed assessment by DPP staff.
Who will be impacted and how?
DPP Unit
DPP Unit
    DPP staff will continue to process applications that are received via post, online, or are identified by the robot as either rejected or requiring further information.
CSC Staff
CSC Staff
    CSC staff will be given access to a newly developed SharePoint site which will allow Operators to access DPP details which previously have been unavailable to CSC staff.
    The additional visibility means that Operators will no longer need to call the DPP team to confirm permit requirements, such as whether the permit is a 5 year or 1 year permit, or whether payment is or isn't needed.

    Staff will enter the required details into the SharePoint list which replaces the manual checklists (i.e. email template). This process is beneficial as it guides Operators through a workflow in lodging DPP applications that is clear and easy to follow. The application form will still need to be emailed to the Disability Parking Applications email address as a PDF attachment. The email template will no longer need to be completed in addition to emailing the application.
Contact Centre Staff
Contact Centre Staff
    During the staged release the CCC won’t have access to the portal as the load needs to be tested to ensure stability while being introduced to the CSC network.

    Once the portal is stabilised it's proposed the CCC could use the look-up functionality of the form in SharePoint to see the current status of an application and eligibility for renewal/new applications/expiry dates etc.
Customers
Customers
    It's anticipated that customers will benefit from the new process by experiencing:
    • an increase in quality customer service
    • a reduction in application errors
    • an improvement in data accuracy
    • timely processing of applications.
Further informationThere will be dedicated resources in the DPP Unit to support Operators when rolling out the new process.

After the roll out to all regions is completed, DocBase will be updated with the new process and the escalation path for any ongoing issues using the new process.

More detail around the dedicated resources will be advised at a later date after the implementation in the DPP Unit.
