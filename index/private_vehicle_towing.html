Private Vehicle Towing (7305)


Private Vehicle Towing
What it isThe Tow Truck Act and Regulations are designed to govern the conduct of tow trucks and/or the operators of tow trucks within regulated areas at:
  • the scene of road incidents,
  • police authorised seizures and
  • From the 16 April 2018, when towing a vehicle from private property.

It creates the requirement for tow truck operations performing the above functions within regulated areas to:
  • Have a valid Tow Truck licence
  • Use appropriately licensed drivers and assistants
  • Have compliant business address
  • Have suitable and compliant holding yards
  • Use compliant vehicles as tow trucks.

Conditions of UseA tow truck licence holder must (unless an exemption is granted) from the 16th April:
  • Enter into a written contract with a property owner authorising the licence holder to remove vehicles from the private property before allowing their drivers to tow a vehicle parked on private property
  • Once a written contract is in place a Private Property Towing Consent Form (F5209) must also be completed as proof that the contract exists
  • Ensure that their tow truck drivers carry a copy of the Towing Consent when removing vehicles from the property; and
  • Ensure their drivers take reasonable steps to locate the vehicle owner or driver before loading a vehicle onto the tow truck

Note:Tow truck licence holders/drivers have been advised that reasonable steps includes asking other people (at the carpark of nearby shops) if they know where the driver went, looking around the immediate area to see if the vehicle owner is nearby or approaching the carpark, looking for contact or business name details on the vehicle.

  • Notify Queensland Police Service as soon as possible but no later than 1 hour after a private property motor vehicle arrives in the holding yard; and
  • Show the Towing Consent to a vehicle owner or authorised officer when requested; and
  • Ensure an inventory of found property is made for each vehicle towed; and
  • Make an entry in the inventory as soon as possible after the vehicle is towed; and
  • Not allow a vehicle to be towed from private property unless the licence holder and the property owner/occupier have entered into a written contract and Towing Consent authorising the removal of vehicles from the property
  • Release a vehicle to the owner immediately after they pay any fees required under the Tow Truck Regulation 2009
  • Take all reasonable precautions to prevent loss or damage to a motor vehicle while it is being towed and while it is in the holding yard

From the 16 April 2018 a licence holder must not:
  • Allow a vehicle to be towed from private property for which they do not have a written contract and Towing Consent in force
  • Allow a vehicle to be towed from private property, without taking reasonable steps to locate the vehicle’s owner/driver
  • Ensure that if the vehicle owner returns before the vehicle is loaded and secured, the vehicle must be released to them immediately with no charge.
  • Ensure that if the owner returns after the vehicle has been loaded and secured but before the driver has left the property, they must release the vehicle immediately if the owner pays the on-site release fee.
  • Move a vehicle from a holding yard without written authority from the owner or their agent
  • Unlock a private property motor vehicle without the consent of the vehicle’s owner or agent
  • Charge more than the regulated fee
Requirements
Towing Consent - Private Vehicle Towing
Towing Consent - Private Vehicle Towing
    Licence holders must enter into a written contract with the property owner/occupies before allowing drivers to tow vehicles from the property. Once a written contract is in place a Towing Consent (F5209)must also be signed. The Towing Consent is evidence that the contract exists.
Regulated Towing Fees
Regulated Towing Fees
    Private parking towing maximum regulated fees:
    • $255.65 - for a standard tow (including 72 hours storage)
    • $153.40 - for the onsite release of the vehicle after it is secured ready for towing
    • $25.55 - per day for storing a private property towed vehicle in the holding yard.

    Tow truck licence holders/drivers have been advised that it is recommended to have appropriate on-the-spot payment mechanisms available for motorists to pay the on-site release fee (EFTPOS or credit card facilities)
    A ‘standard tow' includes:
    • 60 minutes working time at a crash scene or private parking area (e.g. loading vehicle on tow truck, cleaning up broken glass and other debris)
    • transportation of the crashed vehicle from the accident to the agreed destination
    • transportation of the private property towed vehicle to the licence holders nearest holding yard
    • 72 hours storage in holding yard (crash and private property towed vehicles).

    A customer cannot be charged call-out fees and other fees such as for taking steps to locate the owner, travelling to the place where the vehicle is located, allowing the vehicle owner access to the vehicle at the holding yard (within business hours) and other incidental fees cannot be charged. A property owner and motorist cannot both be charged for towing the vehicle.
