Traffic History Guidelines (4757)

Traffic History Guidelines
A traffic history check must be undertaken for all industry licensing applicants (i.e. with the exception of Dangerous Goods Vehicle Licences).

Note:New Zealand traffic and criminal history checks should be assessed using the relevant guidelines that are considered for Australian traffic and criminal history checks for industry authority applications.

Perform a Traffic History Check
Perform a Traffic History Check
    • A traffic history check must be completed for all states where the applicant has lived including QLD, and
    • Any states where the applicant has had traffic offences (indicated in question 6 on the application form).

    Note:Check interstate/NZ only if applicant has declared they have lived interstate or in NZ in the past 5 years.

    Northern Territory Applicants
    Northern Territory Applicants
      Where an applicant has declared that they have resided in the Northern Territory (NT) in the last five years and the licence history returned from the NT indicates periods of cancellation or suspension that do not correspond to offences listed on their criminal history report (eg DUI offences in the NT are shown on the applicants criminal history) the applicant is required to obtain a traffic history from the NT Police, Fire and Emergency Services. Applications can be made in person or via mail, please refer the applicant to the following website for further information, there is a fee for this service.
      http://www.pfes.nt.gov.au/Access-to-information/Complete-Traffic-History-Check.aspx

      An application form can be printed from the following location:
      http://www.pfes.nt.gov.au/Access-to-information/Complete-Traffic-History-Check.aspx

      Once received by the customer it is important to ensure that the issue date of the document is no more than one month prior to the lodgement date of the application.

      Printing of Traffic Histories
      Printing of Traffic Histories
      An on screen assessment of the traffic history should be performed before it is printed. It is only necessary to print and retain questionable and unsuitable traffic histories.

New Application
New Application
    Consider offences committed within the past 5 years, with the more recent offences (within 2-3 years) being more relevant.
Renewal Application
Renewal Application
    Consider any new traffic offences that have been committed since the last application / renewal was approved.



    Note: If a repetitive offence behaviour pattern is observed the application may be refused.
Table of guidelines
Table of guidelines
    The table below is not intended to include an exhaustive list of relevant offences, but rather provide some guidance for the decision maker to assess an Industry Authority.


    When assessing a traffic history some useful questions to consider are:
    Is this person road safety conscious? Do they obey the road rules? Would a person with this traffic history be considered an unacceptable safety risk to the public and to property?

    Someone who is not appropriate based on traffic history
    • Someone with a record of poor driving behaviour may not be considered appropriate to hold an accreditation or licence. Traffic history offences for speeding, dangerous driving, not obeying road rules, driving while under the influence, driving while unlicensed or suspended may not be considered appropriate.
    • Granting an accreditation or licence to someone with poor driving behaviour could undermine the government’s administration of the tow truck scheme and negatively impact the reputation of the towing industry. The community have a high expectation that the government only issues licenses and accreditations to those who are appropriate.
    Type of AccreditationRelevance to role Decision Guidelines:
    Tow Truck Driver Tow truck drivers must be road safety conscious. They are expected to be responsible road users. It must be remembered that they can operate in an environment that is sometimes dangerous with people who are vulnerable.Applicants for the grant or renewal should be refused if they have:
    • been convicted of dangerous driving or driving while under the influence in the past 5 years
    • had their driver licence cancelled for a driving related offence in the last 1 year; or
    • had their driver licence suspended due to a high speed offence or the accumulation of demerit points in the last 3 years for one offence, 5 years for multiple offences.
    • been convicted of using hand held mobile phone, cross continuous line/s, make u-turn at intersection controlled by traffic lights and other road rules offences in the last 3 years for one offence, 5 years for multiple offences.
    Tow Truck AssistantTow Truck Assistants must be road safety conscious. They are expected to be responsible road users. It must be remembered that they can operate in an environment that is sometimes dangerous with people who are vulnerable.Applicants for grant or renewal should be refused if:
    • Exceeding speed limit, dangerous driving and not obeying road rules, driving while under the influence, driving while unlicensed or suspended in the last 1 year.
    Tow Truck Licence holderTow truck licence holders must be road safety conscious. They are expected to be responsible road users.Applicants for grant or renewal should be refused if they have:
    • been convicted of an offences for exceeding the speed limit, dangerous driving, not obeying road rules, driving while under the influence
    • had their driver licence cancelled for an unlicensed driving offence in the last 1 year.
    Driver Trainer Driver trainers are expected to be responsible road users and to model the highest standards of driving behaviour.Applications for grant or renewal should be refused if the person has offences that suggest they are not road safety conscious or they do not model a high standard of driving/riding behaviour, such as:
    • been convicted of dangerous driving in the past 3 years
    • had their driver licence cancelled for a driving related offence in the last 1 year
    • had their driver licence suspended due to a high speed offence, drink or drug driving offences or accumulation of demerit points in the last 1 year.
    Rider TrainerRider trainers are expected to model the highest standards of motorcycle riding behaviour. Rider trainers are in a position of trust in that they deliver Q-Ride motorcycle training and assessments.Applications for grant or renewal should be refused if the person has offences that suggest they are not road safety conscious or they do not model a high standard of driving/riding behaviour, such as:
    • been convicted of dangerous driving in the past 3 years
    • had their driver licence cancelled for a driving related offence in the last 1 year
    • had their driver licence suspended due to a high speed offence, drink or drug driving offences or accumulation of demerit points in the last 1 year.
    Pilot Vehicle Driver and Escort Vehicle DriverPilot and Escort Vehicle Drivers have a specialised role. They should be safety conscious and responsible road users. Applications for grant or renewal should be refused if the person has offences that suggest they are not road safety conscious or have no regard for the road rules, such as:
    • been convicted of dangerous driving in the past 3 years
    • had their driver licence cancelled for a driving related offence in the last 1 year
    • had their driver licence suspended due to a high speed offence, drink or drug driving offences or accumulation of demerit points in the last 1 year.
    Traffic ControllerTraffic controllers must be safety conscious and demonstrate an ability to control traffic around work sites.Applicants for grants or renewal should only be considered for refusal if:
    • serious driving offences or licence suspensions have occurred. Consideration should be given to whether the offences are relevant to the function of and ability to perform the role.
    Dangerous Goods Driver LicenceDangerous Goods Drivers must be safety conscious and responsible road users.Applicants for grant or renewal should be refused if the person has offences that suggest they are not road safety conscious or have no regard for the road signs and road rules, such as:
    • been convicted of dangerous driving in the past 3 years
    • had their driver licence cancelled for a driving related offence in the last 1 year
    • had their driver licence suspended due to a high speed offence, drink or drug driving offences or accumulation of demerit points in the last 1 year
    • been convicted of an offence for transporting dangerous goods in a tunnel or other prohibited area in the last 1 year.
