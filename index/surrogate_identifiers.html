Surrogate Identifiers (Motor Vehicles) (3257)

What it isA surrogate identifier is a replacement VIN, chassis or engine number for a vehicle. All vehicle surrogate identifiers are authorised by Queensland Police Service (QPS) and are issued by the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU).

The VIU will place a text against the vehicle record advising that a surrogate identifier has been issued to the vehicle.
Reasons for issuing
Reasons for Issuing
Reasons for Issuing
    Stolen and Recovered
    • Stolen vehicle that has been recovered and the VIN, chassis or engine numbers have been tampered with
    Seized and Cleared
    • Vehicle has been seized by Police, due to original identifiers having been tampered with, and has been cleared after investigation
    Duplicate Identifiers
    • A vehicle that has a duplicate identifier recorded on NEVDIS or TICA
    • Duplicate VIN/chassis is recorded as stolen but the vehicle presented for registration has been cleared/proven by QPS not to be stolen or a vehicle of interest
    Damaged, Erased, Altered or Missing Identifiers
    • Vehicle presented for registration has damaged, erased altered or missing identifiers
    • Details stamped on vehicle do not match identification plate details (excludes previously issued surrogates)
      • Vehicle to be referred to QPS
    • Engines with no identifiers
    Individually Constructed Vehicle (ICV)
    • Kit vehicles (no VIN number is allocated when purchased)
    • Vehicles assembled from parts from various sources that do not result in a vehicle identical to that produced by the original manufacturer

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Modifications > Specific Modifications > Individually Constructed Vehicle (ICV)
    Pre 1939 Vehicles (excluding Street Rods)Some early model vehicles and motorcycles manufactured before 1939 may not have had a chassis number stamped.

    Note:It is a condition of registration that the vehicle be allocated an identifier, but is not mandatory as the stamping of numbers onto the vehicle may affect the authenticity and value of the vehicle.

Location of original identifiers
Location of original identifiers after surrogate issued
Location of original identifiers after surrogate issued
    Where the vehicle's original identifier is located after the surrogate has been issued the customer must return to the QPS issuing office, who will investigate and advise the VIU if the surrogate identifier is no longer required.

    If QPS request cancellation of an issued surrogate, application for vehicle registration can only be processed once the VIU have:
    • cancelled the issue of the surrogate
    • updated the text restriction with this advice
    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > The How To: Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Requirements Procedures > Flowchart - Location of Original Identifiers after surrogate issued
