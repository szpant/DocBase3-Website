Providing Assistance to Complete Forms (4633)


What it isTMR has an obligation to assist customers who have:
  • A learning incapacity
  • Difficulty speaking/reading English
  • Difficulty writing
  • Hearing impairment
  • Difficulty completing paperwork or written tests

Assistance should not be provided for Industry Licensing applicants. Minimum literacy standards are part of the training for a Dangerous Goods Driver and all other industry authority schemes require the accredited person to complete/maintain documentation as part of their duties.
What assistance can be providedA CSO may assist a customer to complete a form by:
  • Clarifying what the question means and providing examples
  • Reading the questions to the applicant who records the response on the form themselves
  • Completing the form on behalf of the applicant

An Operator cannot sign on behalf of the customer, a customer may “make their mark” on the application form. EOI requirements must be met.

A CSO may refuse a request for operator assistance and offer the customer an alternative service provided on behalf of TMR such as Interpreter Services
How an Operator can provide assistance
    When reading an application form or completing the form on behalf of the customer:
    • All questions must be read in full
    • All statements, declarations, warning and disclaimers must be read
    • The customer must be advised:
    • the form must be signed by you in your own handwriting, and
    • any suggestions or advice given by the CSO must not be taken to be the correct answer to any question, but is simply being given as a guide to assist in identifying the correct answer for their circumstances
    • there is a penalty for providing false or misleading information
Providing a record of the assistanceWhere an operator has completed or read the form on behalf of/to the applicant the following information must be noted in the Office Use Only section prior to the customer signing the applications form:
  • Type of assistance provided
  • Why assistance was required
