Acceptance of Contactless Payments and update to surcharge requirements (7476)

Project NameAcceptance of Contactless Payments and update to surcharge requirements
WhenGradual roll out from 29 August 2019
BackgroundFrom Thursday, 29 August 2019 the eftpos devices at Brisbane City CSC were updated to accept contactless payments (Visa PayWave, MasterCard PayPass and eftpos Tap & Pay.) This pilot has now been extended to Caboolture CSC, Logan City CSC and six centres in Northern region (including Cairns Kenny Street CSC and Bentley Park CSC, Innisfail CSC, Richmond QGAP, Ingham QGAP and Ayr QGAP).

A state-wide roll out will commence in November 2019 which will be scheduled per Region. This is subject to change.

It has also been identified that TMR hasn't been charging the correct surcharge for transactions paid using Visa and MasterCard branded cards, to rectify this the procedure for taking payments in TICA will be updated.

Note: You will not need to enforce the new surcharge structure until contactless payments has been rolled out in your centre.

Who will be impacted and how?
Operators
Operators
    The implementation of contactless payments will be gradually rolled out through CSCs. SA(SM)s/Managers will be contacted prior to the roll out in their centre.

    Operators will have to communicate to customers that contactless payments are now accepted, they may also have to field questions from customers regarding the updated surcharge structure. Suggested wording for customer enquiries regarding the surcharge has been included below under "Supporting documents."
Customers
Customers
    Customers can make contactless payments using a bank card that displays the contactless icon or by other methods such as an App on their mobile phone or smart watch.

    There will also be a significant increase to the amount of customers that will have to pay a surcharge for their transactions as ALL Visa/MasterCard branded cards will incur a surcharge regardless of what account type is selected on the eftpos device or whether the customer chooses to tap, insert or swipe their card.
Further information
Contactless Payments
Contactless Payments
    Once updated, the eftpos devices will display the contactless icon, this will help customers identify that they can tap to make payment. All contactless transactions >$100 will require the customer to enter their PIN.

    Refunds can also be issued using the contactless function. A PIN will be required for all contactless refunds, regardless of the amount.
Surcharge
Surcharge
    When a customer presents a Visa/MasterCard branded card they must be advised that their payment will incur a surcharge, regardless of the type of account their card is linked to (savings, cheque or credit.) The only acceptable card types that won't attract a surcharge are BasicsCards or eftpos cards with no Mastercard or Visa logo.

    Operators should select the following card types from the payment screen in TICA to ensure the correct surcharge is charged:
    • DRCARD - Used for payments made by BasicsCard or eftpos debit card. This includes contactless payments made using an eftpos debit card.
    • VISA - Used for any card displaying a Visa logo, including Visa debit cards
    • MCAR - Used for any card displaying a MasterCard logo, including a MasterCard debit card.

Trouble shooting information
USB adaptors
USB adaptors
    Terminals running off a USB adaptor are not compatible with the contactless setting. Ensure terminals are plugged directly into the computer via a PC USB port.
Contactless drop-outs
Contactless drop-outs
    If contactless temporarily disables or a drop-out is experienced, complete the following:
    • Restart computer
    • Complete manual TMS download and check for ‘successful’ status
    Refer: Admin > 1. Administration > Security > Security of Information > EFTPOS > EFTPOS Firmware Updates
    • Attempt contactless payment
Other error messages
Other error messages
    Terminal error messages such as Decline – Modem Error, No connection to cash register or PIN Pad Offline should be directed to the CBA Help Desk (ph 1800 230 177) if a PC restart and TMS download has not resolved the issue.
