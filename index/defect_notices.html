Defect Notices (5180)


Defect Notices
What it isA Defect Notice is an instruction for the registered operator to repair non-compliant aspects of a vehicle.

A Defect Notice may be issued under the following legislation:
  • Transport Operations (Road Use Management—Vehicle Standards and Safety) Regulation 2010 section 8 , if an authorised officer reasonably believes:
    • that a light vehicle is defective; or
    • that a light vehicle does not comply with vehicle standards.
  • Heavy Vehicle National Law (Queensland) section 256, if an authorised officer who has inspected a heavy vehicle under this Law reasonably believes:
    • the vehicle is a defective heavy vehicle; or
    • a number plate of the vehicle is wholly or partly obscured, defaced or otherwise not legible.
When to issue
When to issue a defect notice
When to issue a defect notice
    A defect notice must be issued if a vehicle fails any of the following inspections:
    • programmed
    • compliant
    • police road patrol
    • PVO
    • accident
    • IMA

    A Defect Notice should be issued if a vehicle fails Road Patrol inspection. If a decision is made not to issue a Defect Notice sufficient details must be recorded in the official notebook to support the decision.
Categorising Defects and Clearance Method
'Self-Clearing' is not to be used as a category for a Defect Notice. Defect Notice categories to be used are Minor, Major or Major (Grounded). Self-Clearing category is not to be used as the HVNL(Q) does not require an operator to return the Defect Notice categorised as 'Self-Clearing'.

'Self-Clearance' however can still be used as a clearance method were appropriate. For example: for a defect categorised in TRAILS/TICA as minor and the owner is only required to sign the self-clearance declaration and return the notice.

Categorising Defects (Heavy and Light Vehicles)
Issuing a Defect Notice
Issuing a defect notice to a person
Issuing a defect notice to a person
    When issuing the defect notice to a person the Authorised Officer must:
    • Inform the person of their obligations and responsibilities to comply with all conditions of the Defect Notice
    • Inform the person of the clearance requirements
    • Request the person sign the Defect Notice

      Note:If the person refuses to sign the Defect Notice: (NO OFFENCE)
      • For a PVI make a note on the inspection check sheet
      • For on hand written Defect Notices make a note on the Defect Notice and in your official note book

    • If given to a person other than the registered operator advise the person to provide the Defect Notice as soon as practicable to the registered operator:
      • Heavy vehicle within 14 days
      • Light vehicle within 48 hours
Issuing a defect notice to an unattended vehicle
Issuing a defect notice to an unattended vehicle
    A Defect Notice may be issued to a unattended vehicle that is standing or being used on a road related area. If issuing a Defect Notice to an unattended vehicle all reasonable steps must be taken to contact the registered owner.
Defective Vehicle Label
Defective Vehicle Label
    A defective vehicle label:
    • Must be attached to a vehicle issued a major or major grounded Defect Notice
    • May be attached to a vehicle issued with a minor Defect Notice.
    • Must state the day and time the notice was issued and the Defect Notice number

    Attaching a defective vehicle label to a:
    • Car/truck, the label should be attached to the inside of the windscreen, bottom, passenger side, so not to obstruct the drivers view.
    • Trailer, can be attached to the rear of the trailer on the red section of a rear marker plate or near the number plate.

    A defective vehicle label can be removed by an Authorised Officer or Approved Examiner once the vehicle is compliant with the relevant vehicle standards.

Mandatory Directions (Conditions)
Mandatory Directions (Conditions)
    • All Defect Notices must stipulate a cease use time and date. This will be determined by the nature of the defects and the category of the Defect Notice. Refer: Categorising Defects.
    • 'Minor Defect Notice' - "vehicle must not be used on a road after a stated time unless stated action required to stop the vehicle from being a defective vehicle is taken"
    • 'Major Defect Notice' - "vehicle must not be used on a road after the notice is issued other than to move it to one or more stated locations in one or more stated ways.
    • A Defect Notice classified as major must stipulate the location the vehicle may be drive to. For example: The vehicle may be driven back to the Cluden depot. The vehicle may be driven Charters Towers for repair.
Discretional Directions (Conditions)
Discretional Directions (Conditions)
    Conditions are in addition to the mandatory directions and are only used for minor or major Defect Notices.

    Conditions are in addition to the mandatory directions and may be imposed by an Authorised Officer on the vehicle's usage during the stated time to limit its exposure to external factors that may impact on the safety risk.
    For example:
    • The head lights on a car may not be operational, you may allow the car to be driven in day light only.
    • A bus may have one defective seat belt, you may allow the bus to be used with the exception of the seat with the defective seat belt.
Clearance Method
Clearance Method
    Self-Clearance
    Self-Clearance
      Self-clearing is cleared, without an inspection.
      The registered operator is only required to complete the certification on the back of the Defect Notice and return it to TMR.

      An Authorised Officer may request the production of documentation evidence to prove the rectification of defects.
    AIS
    AIS
      A Defect Notice can be cleared by an AIS by either:
      • Completion of the certification on the back of the Defect Notice by an Approved Examiner (AE). The Defect Notice must be returned to TMR; or
      • Recording the results in the ICO system

      If a defective vehicle label was attached to the vehicle it may be removed by the AE and securely destroyed.
      An Authorised Officer may request the production of documentation evidence to prove the rectification of defects.
    Qualified Person
    Qualified Person
      If there isn't an AIS within 50km of the business address of the vehicle or TMR will not be returning to the inspection location before the defect period has ended the registered operator may be referred to:
      • Queensland Police Service
      • Qualified mechanic
      • Boiler Maker for cracks and welds
      • Panel Beater for rust
      • Council Workshop (who has agreed to provide the service)
      to have the vehicle inspected.

      If the Qualified person considers the vehicle compliant they must complete the rear of the Defect Notice including:
      • name
      • signature
      • business name
      • business address.

      The Qualified person can remove the defective vehicle label.

      When entering the Defect Notice clearance in TICA, as there is no "Qualified Person" clearance method use Self-Clearance as the clearance method.

      The vehicle owner must sign the owner declaration.

      An Authorised Officer may request the production of documentation evidence to prove the rectification of defects.
    TMR
    TMR
      TMR clearance via an inspection may be required for a Defect Notice categorised as Major.
      A vehicle categorised as Major Grounded should be reinspected by TMR.
Returning Defect Notices to TMR
Returning Defect Notices to TMR
    Defect Notices can be returned to TMR:
    • By mail to the address specified on the front of the notice
    • In person to the issuing location
    • Electronically to the email address of the issuing location.
Notify TransLink
Notify TransLink
    Where a defect notice is issued to an operator who is contracted to TMR to provide public passenger services, a copy of the notice must be sent to the local TransLink Regional Operations unit. The notice should be sent to TransLink on the day of issue or as soon as practicable so Translink can take prompt action against operators contracted to provide public passenger vehicles on behalf of TransLink. This applies to all inspection types.

    Your Senior Transport Inspector will liaise with TransLink Regional Operations to maintain a current list of operators for your region.

    Authorised Officers should follow the local process for sending these Defect Notices to TransLink.
For information on how to complete a handwritten Defect Notice or issue a Defect Notice from TICA Refer: How To: Inspections - Inspection Procedures
Cancelling/Amending a Defect Notice
Amending a Defect Notice
Amending a Defect Notice
    An Authorised Officer may amend a Defect Notice if:
    • It contains incorrect details (eg incorrect make or model of vehicle)
    • A permission is granted or varied under s529A HVNL

    If a Defect Notice for a heavy vehicle is amended the Authorised Officer must give a copy of the amended Defect Notice to the person to whom the original Defect Notice was issued.
Invalidating a Defect Notice
Invalidating a Defect Notice
    A Defect Notice must be invalidated if:
    • it has been issued to the incorrect vehicle or,
    • has been issued to a vehicle and the alleged defects have been proven not to be defective by the operator producing documentation from the manufacturer that shows the vehicle within the manufacturer's specifications.

    Contact Help Direct (3097 3160) to request the invalidation of a Defect Notice.

    If the defect relates to a TransLink contracted operator, TransLink Regional Operations must be advised of the invalidation.

    If the vehicle has a defective vehicle label attached, the Authorised Officer must remove the label.

    If the Defect Notice contained other non-tolerance related items (eg bald tyres) a new Defect Notice must be issued for these items.
EOT for Defect Notices
Extension of Time - Defect Notice Issued
Use of a vehicle subject to a Defect Notice
Use of a vehicle subject to a Defect Notice
Use of a vehicle subject to a Defect Notice
    The time permitted to comply with a Defect Notice forms part of the mandatory directions/condition. It is an offence to use, or permit to be used, a vehicle in contravention of the conditions of the Defect Notice.

    A vehicle is not in contravention of a Defect Notice and may be used on the road during the Defect Notice:
    • prior to the cease use time and date
    • for a Minor/Major Defect Notice and the defects have been rectified or the vehicle is being taken to a place of inspection and the vehicle is:
      • not carrying goods and/or passengers
      • not a safety risk

    An Authorised Officers may require the driver of the heavy vehicle to produce evidence of the relevant repairs within a reasonable time and way.

    The rectification of defects does not remove the need for the Defect Notice to be cleared in TICA.

    If the Defect Notice has not been cleared by the stated time on the Defect Notice the vehicles use on the road is prohibited without an approved Extension of Time
Additional Enforcement Action
Additional Enforcement Action at time of issuing Defect Notice
Additional Enforcement Action at time of issuing Defect Notice
    It is an offence to use or permit to be used a vehicle that does not comply with the relevant vehicle standards. As such an Authorised Officer may in addition to issuing a minor, major or major grounded defect notice issue an infringement notice or initiate court proceedings/BTA.

    A Penalty Infringement Notice (PIN) should not be issued when a minor or self-clearing defect notice is issued.
    When considering issuing a PIN, the Authorised Officer should take into consideration:
    • Whether the offence is a driver or owner offence
    • If the defect is the result of a catastrophic failure or systematic neglect
    • Would the impact of the defect on the vehicles performance be such that the driver would have been alerted
    • Would it be reasonable to assume the driver could have been aware of the defect prior to commencing the journey
    • What steps (if any) has the driver taken to rectify the defect
    • Who is responsible the driver or the owner
      Vehicle Intercepeted subject to a Defect Notice
      Vehicle Intercepeted subject to a Defect Notice
      If a vehicle is intercepted with defects listed on a Defect Notice that has passed the cease use date, issue a new Defect Notice with reference to the previous Defect Notice. Enforcement action should be taken against the registered operator. Ensure sufficient evidence is collected at the time of intercept.
Clearing Defect Notices in TICA
Clearing Defect Notices in TICA
Clearing Defect Notices in TICA
    • When a Defect Notice has been returned and has been completed correctly, it must be cleared in TICA.
    • If the clearance is completed correctly an Authorised Officer may sign the Defect Notice Clearance section to allow Compliance Administration staff to clear the notice in TICA and issue a COI if applicable.
    • If the Defect Notice clearance is not completed correctly, attempt to contact the responsible person for signing the notice or the owner, if not successful return the defect notice to the owner with an explanation of the issue.
    • If the Defect Notice has an AIS clearance:
      • check RUMiiS to ensure the AIS/AE was qualified to inspect the vehicle and current at the time of signing the Defect Notice clearance
      • clearance by an interstate AIS is acceptable, in line with reciprocal COI policy. If in doubt about the validity of the AIS, check with the interstate authority.
    A Defect Notice can only be cleared in ICO within the Defect Notice time frame. If the clearance date is outside the time frame, the AIS must sign the back of the Defect Notice and return the Defect Notice to TMR for clearance.

    Safety Certificate or COI issued instead of a clerance report
    Safety Certificate or COI issued instead of a clerance report
      Where an AIS has issued a Safety Certificate or a COI to a TMR COI vehicle instead of a defect clearance inspection and the Safety Certificate or COI has been recorded in ICO the Safety Certificate or COI must be removed (invalidated) from the system to allow the Defect Notice to be cleared by TMR.

      Contact: HelpDirect or phone 3097 3160.

Failing to comply with a Defect Notice
Failing to Comply with a Defect Notice
Failing to Comply with a Defect Notice
    Failure to comply with a Defect Notice may result in further enforcement action (e.g. Penalty Infringement Notice and or registration cancellation).

    An Infringement Notice can be issued via the Enforcement Screen in TRAILS to the registered owner of a light vehicle. Refer: How To: Inspections - Inspection Procedures

    An Infringement Notice cannot be issued via the Enforcement Screen in TRAILS to the registered owner of a heavy vehicle. Refer: How To: Inspections - Inspection Procedures
Cancelling Registration
Cancelling Registration
    Queensland legislation has grounds for Show Cause which is under Section 19 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 states:
    If the chief executive considers a ground exists to amend, suspend or cancel an approval, (the proposed action), the chief executive must give the holder written notice—
    (a) stating the proposed action; and
    (b) stating the ground for the proposed action; and
    (c) outlining the facts and circumstances forming the basis for the ground; and
    (d) if the proposed action is to amend the approval (including a condition of the approval)—stating the proposed amendment; and
    (e) if the proposed action is to suspend the approval—stating the proposed suspension period; and
    (f) inviting the holder to show (within a stated time of at least 28 days) why the proposed action should not be taken.

    If an operator fails to comply with a Defect Notice, Show Cause action may be taken. The Warning and Show Cause Notice must be signed by a Senior Transport Inspector.

    A Warning Notice must be sent to the vehicle operator at least 14 days before the Show Cause Notice is sent. A Warning Notice can be automatically generated by TICA/TRAILS, however this version has some minor inaccuracies caused primarily by changes to the NHVL. The Warning Notice template is provided in "Grounds for Show Cause."

    When completing the Show Cause Decision Notice replace "General Manager (Customer Services)" with Manager (Compliance) and supply the postal address.

    Refer: Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Registration Show Cause
Interstate Registered Vehicles and Defect Notices Issued by Interstate Authorities.
Defect Notices Issued by Interstate Authorities
Defect Notices Issued by Interstate Authorities
    Interstate and Territory authorities forward Defect Notices they issue to QLD registered vehicles to: DarraMVIC which are entered into TICA.

    An interstate issued Defect Notice may also be received from the owner of a QLD registered vehicle which must be entered in TICA if not already entered.

    AIS Clearance or Self Clearing
    AIS Clearance or Self Clearing
      Defect Notices issued by interstate authorities to QLD registered vehicles should be entered in TICA with a clearance method of AIS or Self-Clearing as specified on the Defect Notice.
    TMR Clearance
    TMR Clearance
      If the Defect Notice specifies inspection by TMR to clear the Defect Notice, a QLD Road Patrol Defect Notice must be entered in TICA using the information from the interstate Defect Notice.

      Add the following text in the Additional Details section of Record Defect Notice Defect Notice issued from <State> <Defect Notice number> to allow vehicle to be inspected by TMR and Defect Notice cleared if applicable.

      The customer is required to book and pay for an inspection so the vehicle can be inspected and the Defect Notice cleared if applicable.

    These Defect Notices assist investigations when a profile is created on operators or vehicles.

    Refer: How To: Inspections - Inspection Procedures
