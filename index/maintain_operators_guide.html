TICA/TRAILS Access
Eligibility for Access
Eligibility for Access
    Applicants seeking access to TRAILS/TICA must complete the TRAILS/TICA Access Application(F3277) form and obtain approval from their Manager.

    Note:Managers at non TMR led QGAPS are responsible for access and profile approvals at their location.

    Only staff who have completed the TICA Basics course can be given access to TRAILS/TICA.

    The approving Manager or Senior Transport Inspector must sight evidence that the applicant has completed the TICA Basics course. Evidence can be obtained by:
    • Accessing Accelerate and viewing the applicants completion records
    • Sighting a printed course completion certificate

    For access to Accelerate Reporting lodge a Service Centre Online request:

    Assigning profile in TRAILS
    Assigning profile in TRAILS
      Once approved, for CSC, TMR led QGAP and Customer Service Direct staff, it is the role of the applicant's manager to update TRAILS to enable access.

      For Compliance staff the Senior Transport Inspector may update TRAILS, alternatively they may forward the completed TRAILS/TICA Access application to SysSec for processing.

      For all other applicants, (including staff from non TMR led QGAPs) the completed TRAILS/TICA Access application should be forwarded to Systems Security

      The original TRAILS/TICA Access application must be retained on the applicant's employee file. There is no requirement to file evidence of the TICA Basics course completion.
QDOT Screen
QDOT Screen
    When TRAILS is newly installed on a PC, or the IP Address of the terminal has changed, TRAILS displays a security screen. This screen shows QDOT in large letters. This screen can only be removed by CITEC.

    Requests for removal of QDOT screens must be made by e-mail to Systems Security and include:
    • IP Address of the terminal
    • Business Unit required
    • Printer allocation requirements
Movement/Change of TRAILS Terminal
Movement/Change of TRAILS Terminal
    All TRAILS terminals are attached to a business unit in TRAILS (eg. Chermside CSC, Customer Service Direct).

    If a terminal is moved to a different business unit, the change is required to be made within TRAILS.

    Requests to add or amend terminal locations must be made by e-mail to Systems Security and include:
    • IP Address of the terminal
    • Business Unit required
    • Former Business Unit (must be suppled if moving terminals between locations)
    • Printer allocation details (if applicable)
Op Accounts
Operator Accounts
Operator Accounts
    When an operator who is required to receipt money is created they are allocated the following accounts:
    OPCASHBK - Operator Cash book
    This account is used to record all receipts for payment received by operators at receipting offices. Each operator is allocated an individual account.
    OPFLOAT - Operator Float
    This is used to record the issue of floats to each operator from the receipting office float account.
    OPOVERS - Operator Overs
    Used to record any surpluses resulting from the operator balance at the end of banking.
    OPUNDERS - Operator Unders
    Used to record and deficiencies resulting from the operator balance at the end of banking.

    Terminating Operator Accounts
    Terminating Operator Accounts
      Operators that cease to have an ongoing need to access TICA/TRAILS, for example cessation of employment or permanent assignment to a role that does not require TICA/TRAILS access, are to have their operator accounts terminated within 5 business days of the need to access ceasing.

      For users that work at multiple locations (eg casual Staff), while profiles assigned to them are to be expired as per the requirements in the below section 'User Profiles', their operator accounts do not need to be terminated if the user is required to return to those locations to perform a role that requires operator accounts.
User Profile
What is it?
What is it?
    A profile defines what actions a user can take in the system.

    All users are:
    • Assigned a profile
    • Can only hold one profile at a business unit at a time unless they consistently work from multiple business units/locations
Assigning/Changing Profiles
Assigning/Changing Profiles
    User Profiles are assigned by a SASM/Manager/Senior Transport Inspector at the business units where the staff member works.

    A profile should only be allocated for the time the staff member is either working consecutive periods at a location/business unit or undertaking a specific role.

    Staff members (including casual staff and driving examiners) who move around to various business units will need to have a user profile created at each business unit where they work and the process above must be followed for each site. The ability to have multiple locations is removed if the staff member is assigned to a single location for a period exceeding one month or is no longer performing the role.

    If a staff member's assigned profile is changed the Manager/SASM/Senior Transport Inspector is required to:
    • Email the staff member advising them of the change to their user profile, the email must:
      • include start and end dates
      • be printed and signed by both parties or an acknowledgement email provided by the staff member
      • be retained for audit purposes.

    For CSC Managers, SASMs, Senior Transport Inspectors, Principal Advisors - Investigations their profile must be approved by a higher level manager typically the Manager (Client Service Delivery) or Manager (Compliance).

    Staff who perform Driver Examination duties on Saturdays at various customer service centres will not be required to update their existing Driver Examiner profiles for each site visited. The Data Analysis and Reporting Centre’s “After Hours Report”, distributed to Product Centre Managers (CSD), will be used to provide an audit trail of these accesses.

    SPECPRO
    SPECPRO
      Only a limited number of operators in each centre can be granted SPECPRO access.

      Approval must be granted by Business Systems to ensure that the amount of SPECPRO profiles per centre does not exceed business requirements.

      Refer: Transport Services Training SharePoint site for training material and application form

      Refer: Admin > 2. Maintain Operators > The How To: Maintain Operator > Maintain Operator Procedures > Grant SPECPRO access
    CSDirect
    CSDirect
      Management requires staff member to undertake additional activities and therefore require a change in TRAILS profile:
      • The Team Leader processes change and sends e-mail (using a standard template) to the Operational Support Officer's (OSO) inbox and to the consultant involved,
      • Email advises of the change and expiry date.
      • Email DMS by Team Leader
        Note: Start date is date of email.


      Consultant requests the change:
      • Consultant e-mail Team Leader requesting change
      • Team Leader processes change
      • Email to the consultant ('cc'ing OSO) with the requested change and expiry date.
      • Email DMS by Team Leader
        Note: Start date is date of email.

Expiring User Profiles
Expiring User Profiles
    A profile must be expired when the staff member leaves the location/business unit or ceases to perform the specific role permanently or for a period that exceeds ten consecutive business days unless the user's role routinely has them returning to that location (for example regional Driving Examiners that rotate between delivery locations).

    The ability to have profiles at multiple locations is removed if the staff member is assigned to a single location for a period exceeding one month or is no longer performing the role.
Kyocera Printer M2040dn Multi Functional Device (MFD)The Kyocera Multi-Functional Device (MFD) is a printer used in Customer Service Centres and other sites to print from TICA. The MFD is provided under a managed print service agreement. Toner, maintenance and repairs are provided by Kyocera. TMR pays a fee for each page printed.

There is currently one model in use within TMR, M2040dn (with duplex lid).
WH&S Guidelines
WH&S Guidelines
    Using the MFD
    Using the MFD
      Staff should:
      • use the swivel on their chair when accessing a printer located to the side of the operator instead of twisting the body while seated.
      • stand to access the scanner function instead of stretching to reach.
    Moving or relocating an MFD
    Moving or relocating an MFD
      When moving the MFD, staff need to be mindful of the weight of these units (16.3kg). In the event the MFD needs to be moved or manoeuvred, staff should follow the guidance in the Toolbox Talk for Manual Handling in an Indoor Environment (located on the Zero Harm website) - Toolbox Talk: Safe Manual Handling
      Mobile units
      Mobile units
        In the mobile units, similar steps should be taken, bearing in mind the more restricted conditions in the mobile CSCs. Staff should be aware of ‘pinch points’ when manoeuvring the MFD.
      Remote sites
      Remote sites
        In remote sites, where access to a Kyocera technician is limited, there will be a Cold Spare Hotswap Process in place in the event of hardware failure. At these sites, an additional spare MFD (not plugged in) will be provided, until alternate arrangements can be made.

        In this situation, staff need to ensure that correct manual handling processes are utilised when moving these MFD's. This may include:
        • utilising two staff to remove the faulty MFD and lift the spare MFD into position;
        • using a trolley (preferably one with an adjustable height mechanism) to lift and manoeuvre the MFD;
        • storing the MFD at a similar height to where the existing MFD is (to eliminate the need to lift the MFD);
        • ensuring that the path is clear when taking the MFD from one place to the next.

Relocating MFD's
Relocating MFD's
    Within a site
    Within a site
      MFD's can be relocated within a site as long as the person relocating the MFD does so in accordance with TMR’s WH&S guidelines.
      Refer:       Toolbox Talk: Safe Manual Handling
    Between sites
    Between sites
      To relocate MFD's between sites:
      • The Site Representative must raise a SCO request to arrange for the MFD to be relocated.
Toner Cartridges
Toner Cartridges
    Supplying toner
    Supplying toner
      Kyocera routinely poll the MFD's using NET Admin agent software to gather information on the MFD including toner levels and number of pages printed.

      Kyocera will dispatch replacement toner cartridges to sites when a MFD's toner level reaches 10%.

      Toner will be deliver to:
      • Metropolitan and Regional Sites within two (2) business days.
      • Remote Sites within three (3) business days.
      • Store toner in a cool, dry place, preferably a stationery cupboard or equivalent.
      • Spare toner cartridges should be rotated with delivered cartridges to ensure they are used before expiry.

      Note:To minimise business impact, sites with 10 or more counters will have 3 spare toner cartridges on site, all other locations will have 2 spare toner cartridges on site.

      If the toner supplies at your sites falls below these minimums, please email: Capability Engagement and Learning

    Disposing of toner cartridges
    Disposing of toner cartridges
      There is no TMR policy regarding the disposal of used toner cartridges. The following options may assist some sites:
      Planet Ark collection
      Planet Ark collection
        Kyocera is an active partner in the ‘Cartridges 4 Planet Ark’ recycling program which provides a free, easy and environmentally-accredited way to recycle used printer cartridges.

        TMR sites using more than three toner cartridges (all manufacturers accepted) a month can register online at http://www.closetheloop.com.au/register/ to receive a collection box.

        Once the box is full, sites contact ‘Close the Loop’ for the box to be collected at no cost.

        Note:Collection is only available for Metropolitan/CBD locations. You can contact Close the Loop on 1800 24 24 73 to find out if you are eligible for a collection before registering with their program.

      Drop off
      Drop off
        All Office Works, Harvey Norman, Australia Post stores and Kyocera Branches have a ‘Cartridges 4 Planet Ark’ collection boxes.

        The website http://recyclingnearyou.com.au/cartridges may assist in you in finding your nearest drop off point.

Kyocera model M2040dn
Replacing toner
Replacing toner
    The MFD will report 'low toner' once it reaches 5%. The remaining print capacity is approx. 360 pages. Continue using the MFD until the machine reads 'Add toner'.

    To replace a toner, Refer: Replace Toner on Kyocera M2040dn

    Each time a toner cartridge is replaced, the inside of the machine should be cleaned to maintain optimum print quality, Refer: M2040 Main Charge Clean Instructions
Faults
Faults
    All faults other than paper jams should be reported to the ITB Service Centre on 3066 2000.
    Clearing paper jams
    Clearing paper jams
      If a paper jam occurs, a message will be displayed about the jam and copying and printing will stop.

      Leave the power switch on and to remove the jammed paper. To locate and remove the paper jam refer to: Clear a paper jam on Kyocera M2040dn

      Note:
      • do no reuse jammed papers;
      • if the paper tears during removal, be sure to remove any loose paper from inside the machine. Scraps of paper left in the machine could cause subsequent jamming.

Hints/Tips

Terminate OperatorWhen an operator ceases to have an ongoing need to access TICA/TRAILS, for example cessation of employment or permanent assignment to a role that does not require TICA/TRAILS access, the operator's access to TICA/TRAILS is to be terminated within 5 business days of the need to access ceasing. Terminating access requires the expiration of any profiles assigned to the user, termination of all operator accounts and deactivation of the operator's involved party record in TRAILS.
Feedback Register

The Feedback Register is an electronic register where the TransLink BHSL/BEA hub, Interlock Processing Unit (IPU), Disability Parking Unit, Capability, Engagement and Learning and the Industry Hub can record and automatically provide feedback to the #SASM group for the CSC (or the supporting CSC where the operator was located in a QGAP/QPS).

When a feedback item is received by a SASM, they must:
  • Provide feedback/training to the operator within 5 business days (where possible).
  • Update the Feedback Register to reflect that feedback has been provided.

If a customer presents to lodge:
  • An Interlock Exemption or Financial Assistance Scheme application without supporting documents, the application can be accepted and the customer advised that the IPU will be in contact about the missing documents.
  • A BHSL/BEA application without supporting documents, the application must not be accepted. If the customer insists on lodging it, the operator must record this information on the email to the BHSL/BEA hub.
  • A Driver/Rider Trainer or Traffic Controller accreditation application without supporting training/course requirements, the application can be accepted to allow the criminal and traffic history checks to commence. The operator must record this on the lodgement checklist.
