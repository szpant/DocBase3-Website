Drug Driving (4565)

A person must not drive or be in charge of a vehicle
  • with the presence of a relevant drug in their body
  • while under the influence of any drug.

Road-side drug testsRoad-side drug testing is for the detection of the presence of a relevant drug.
A relevant drug is:
  • THC (the active ingredient in cannabis)
  • Methylamphetamine (speed)
  • MDMA (ecstasy)
Other testsPolice conduct other tests (eg observing behaviours) to detect the offence of driving under the influence of any drug.
