Standard Plates (3081)


What are theyStandard plates are issued to registered vehicles to display a registration number. They are issued to all registered vehicles unless otherwise specified.
Transactions
Replacing Plates
Replacing Plates
    The registered operator of the vehicle must apply to the chief executive in writing for a replacement plate within 14 days after discovering the plate has been:
    • Damaged
    • Destroyed
    • Lost
    • Stolen
    • Cloned

    A new number combination is issued when replacing the plate. To retain the same number combination standard plates must be customised and at least one plate surrendered. The remaining plate must be attached to the rear of the vehicle.

    Refer: Customised Plates

    Notes:If a vehicle has a current Number Plate Confiscation Restriction, replacement standard plate/s cannot be issued.

    If the vehicle has a BHSL the customer must also complete Taxi/Limousine/Special Purpose Limousine/Booked Hire Licence Amendment Application F2976 and the plate details must be updated in the Managed booked hire staff UI.

    Replacing National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate (NHVNP)
    Replacing National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate (NHVNP)
      NHVNP are issued to all heavy vehicles, except:
      • Special Interest Vehicles (SIVE)
      • Farm concession registered vehicles (FARM)
      • Conditionally registered vehicles
      • Fire Rescue and Ambulance Vehicles (FRAV)

      Customers applying to replace currently issued heavy vehicle/trailer standard plates for their heavy vehicles should be issued an NHVNP.
    Replacing standard with special edition plates
    Replacing standard with special edition plates
      When replacing the current standard plates "Smart or Sunshine State" plates with the special edition plates "8 in a row" the surrender reason of "Other" is selected and "Special Edition" recorded as the reason for replacement.

      The currently attached set of standard number plates must be surrendered before issue of the special edition set.

      If replacing with customised plates. Display the remaining plate on the rear of the vehicle and when the customised plates are received, return the remaining standard plate to TMR.
    Replacement timeframes
    Replacement timeframes
      The registered operator must attach the number plate to the vehicle within 1 day after receiving the number plate.
    Upgrade of Standard Plates to Customised/Personalised Plates
    Upgrade of Standard Plates to Customised/Personalised Plates
      Standard plates (excluding National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates) can be re-issued as Customised / Personalised Plates if application is made:
      • 3 mths from the date of surrender if all plates have been surrendered and cancelled
      • 14 mths from date of cancellation of registration if all plates have not been surrendered and cancelled

      As National Heavy Vehicle Number Plates can be reused interstate their plate status will never be updated to cancelled or lapsed.



      Note:The plate history must show an action of LAPS

      • Qld Police Service vehicles

      Approval for Standard Plates for TMR Employees
      Approval for Standard Plates for TMR Employees
        TMR has been given approval to replace Government plates with Standard issued number plates for some of their employees.

        This transaction will not require payment up-front as the TMR employee will present with a letter of authority from TMR containing the account numbers/details required for the TMR CSC to adjust the debt raised in the paying agents account (in most instances - QFLEET) for the replacement plate to a CRN created solely for this purpose in the name of TMR.
    Ambulances using Normal Plates
    Ambulances using Normal Plates

    National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate (NHVNP)
    National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate (NHVNP)
      The National Heavy Vehicle Number Plate is issued by the following jurisdictions:
      • Queensland
      • New South Wales
      • Australian Capital Territory
      • Tasmania
      • South Australia
      • Victoria

      They were introduced in Queensland on 1 July 2018.

      The plates should displayed on all heavy vehicles and trailers (over 4.5t GVM or ATM) included in the NHVRS, excluding:
      • Special Interest Vehicles (SIVE)
      • Farm Plate concession registered vehicles (FARM)
      • Conditionally registered vehicles
      • Fire Rescue and Ambulance Vehicles (FRAV)


      A NHVNP must be issued to all eligible heavy vehicles at new business and replacement plate transactions, unless a personalised plate or customised plate is attached.


      Reusing the NHVNP
      • A plate issued in participating jurisdictions can be reused in another participating jurisdiction at new business only and the plate fee is exempt.
      • If the registration is cancelled the plates must be surrendered and a new NHVNP issued if the vehicle is re-registered unless the plate has been reused interstate and is returning to Queensland.
      • If the registration is transferred within the jurisdiction the plate is also transferred to the new registered operator.
      • If the NHVNP has been customised and the current registered operator intends to retain the NHVNP it must be replaced with another NHVNP prior to transfer.
      • If the vehicle is changing registration between participating jurisdictions and the registration:
        • Is current/expired and the registered operator is the same the NHVNP can be re-used and the plate fee will be automatically exempted
        • Has been cancelled in the previous jurisdiction a new NHVNP must be issued and a plate fee charged


      A report to identify heavy vehicles that have incorrectly been issued standard plates instead of NHVNPs is sent to CSC Managers. Refer:Admin >TRAILS Reports Guide > Types of Reports >Rego > Pending Plate Numbers Report (PL55)> Incorrectly Issued Heavy Vehicle Number Plates (W363)

    Plate Fee Exemptions
    Plate Fee Exemptions
      A standard plate fee is not payable if:
      • A wheelchair is being registered.
      • A customer is registering a heavy vehicle that was previously registered in the same name in another jurisdiction and they are retaining their NHVNP
      • Warranty claim successful.
      • Replacing a wheelchair plate.
      • The vehicle is stolen and recovered without plates and a reference number from the police is provided. For example, if reported to the Qld Police, a QP number.
      • Replacing plates that have been copied/cloned and a reference number from the police is provided. For example, if reported to the Qld Police, a QP number.

        Note:If plates attached to stolen vehicle were customised or personalised a set of standard plates will be issued for free. If customer chooses not to accept the standard plates, the personalised/customised replacement fee must be paid.
