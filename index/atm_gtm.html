ATM/GTM (4556)

Determining ATMManufacture date prior to July 1988 an assessment must be made by the registered operator. (First registration only)

Criteria that may assist the registered operator in making an ATM assessment are the:
  • braking system
  • tyre rating
  • coupling capacity as stamped on the coupling of the trailer
  • for trailers up to 3.5 tonne ATM, Vehicle Standards Bulletin 1 titled 'Building Small Trailer' includes a guide to assessing the ATM
  • Manufacture date from July 1988 onward must display a manufacturers' plate detailing the ATM.
    Note:Staff are not to make this determination

Manufacturer date July 1988 and onward must have a manufacturers plate fitted which displays the ATM.
Upgrade/ Downgrade ATM/GTMWhen a trailers ATM and/or GTM is modified under the LS 12 modification code and a modification certificate is presented there is no requirement to inspect the trailer.

When the ATM and GTM is modified by the original manufacture and an additional trailer plate and letter authorising the increased/decreased (on the manufacturer’s letterhead) is supplied the trailer needs to be sighted by TMR (not HVRAS authorised officer) to ensure a new trailer plate (in addition to the original) has been fitted or a photo of the new affixed plate, next to, the old affixed vin plate is acceptable.

If the change to the ATM:
  • Increases the ATM from <=4.5 tonnes to over 4.5 tonnes;
    or
  • Increase in ATM and changes the type of trailer (e.g. from a pig trailer to a dog trailer),

The trailer is considered to be a new trailer; and
  • A new identification (compliance) plate for the vehicle must be obtained from a Federal Certification (RVCS) Agent
  • The trailer can not be modified (by affixing a modification plate)

Trailers with ATM/GTM above 4.5 tonne
Trailers with ATM/GTM above 4.5 tonne
    Requirements
    • S7/S12 rating through the Approved Person Scheme indicating new ATM/GTM
    • Modification Certificate
    • Modification Plate attached to vehicle
      Information on plate to include:
      • Carrying Capacity
      • Load & Loaded Mass
      • GTMR (Gross Trailer Mass Rating)

    Note: Should a light trailer's ATM/GTM be increased above 4.5 tonne, the trailer must be fitted with a new manufacturers plate detailing its compliance with ADR's under federal certification (DoI). A TMR VIN plate is not sufficient in this instance.
Trailers less than 4.5 tonne ATM/GTM
Trailers less than 4.5 tonne ATM/GTM
    If ATM is being changed to less than/not exceeding 4.5t, the following is required:

    Previous ATM <4.5t
    If the re-rating has been conducted by:
    • the original trailer manufacturer (excluding homemade trailers), the original plate must remain attached and a new Manufacturer's Plate attached to the trailer containing:
      • Same info as original plate
      • Amended ATM weight
        or
      • An approved person:
        • Prior to the 01/03/2018 a S7 or S12 modification plate
        • After the 01/01/2018 a LS12 or LS 14 modification plate
      • If the modification was certified before the 1 March 2018 the S7 and S12 can be accepted.

    If the trailer is homemade it can only be re-rated by an approved person

    Note: Should a light trailer's ATM/GTM be increased above 4.5 tonne, the trailer must be fitted with a new manufacturers plate detailing its compliance with ADR's under federal certification (DoI). A TMR VIN plate is not sufficient in this instance.
