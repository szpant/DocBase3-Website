Certificate of Inspection (COI) (7136)


What are they?The Department of Transport and Main Roads requires regular inspections on specific types of vehicles. These inspections ensure that these vehicles comply with set safety standards.

A Certificate of Inspection (COI) is issued as proof that the vehicle has passed the inspection.
Requirements
  • A COI is issued by either TMR or an AIS, depending on the type of vehicle and it’s purpose of use. Only vehicles registered in Qld can be inspected by TMR. All unregistered or interstate registered vehicles are inspected by an AIS.
  • COI Requirements
    COI Requirements - TMR or AIS
  • Inspection must be booked
    Booking an Inspection

Who Inspects the Vehicle
Who Inspects the Vehicle
    A COI can be issued by either:
    • TMR Transport Inspector
    • Approved Inspection Station

    COI's issued by TMR Transport Inspectors will be automatically entered on TICA.

    TMR Inspection Stations

    An online service that allows customers and staff to search for an AIS is located on the TMR website at: Find an AIS

    An AIS can issue a COI for an unregistered vehicle.
    However, it is an offence for an AIS to issue a COI for a vehicle that is:
    • registered in Queensland, and has a GVM over 16t. or
    • registered in Queensland and has an ATM over 10t.
When is an Inspection Required
When is an Inspection Required
    A registered COI vehicle must have a current COI or an Extension of Time unless eligible for a COI Exemption

    A COI must be produced (unless exempt) when:
    • New business is being transacted for a COI vehicle, unless the new owner is exempt from providing a COI.
    • COI Vehicle is being transferred (unless acquirer is exempt from providing a COI)
    • Change to a Purpose of Use that now requires a COI.

    A paper COI must be produced using the original or a copy emailed or faxed from the AIS. Refer to 'When a copy can be accepted' below.
    An electronic COI can be produced by providing the COI number only. A copy of the COI is not required. Refer: Access ICO as CSO


    After transfer from an exempt individual/organisation, the vehicle needs a current COI, unless the acquirer is exempt or changes to a Purpose of Use that does not need a COI.

    Note:
    • A new vehicle exemption does not apply after the transfer
    • The disposer of a COI vehicle with an alternative compliance (ALTC) exemption is responsible for supplying a COI to the acquirer, unless the acquirer intends to continue in the alternative compliance scheme. Refer: COI Exemption

Validity of COI
Validity of COI
    A COI is valid if it:
    • Is for the correct vehicle
    • Has no alterations
    • Is signed (if manually issued)
    • Is fully complete
    • Is issued by correct issuing authority
    • Is current
    • Is an electronic certificate

    Note: COI's endorsed with any unapproved stamp are not to be accepted by CSC's and Compliance Units. These stamps are not in line with the intent of the AIS scheme and implies to the general public that the inspection process may be of little value.


    Cancelled COI's
    Cancelled COI's
      A COI is cancelled if:
      • the vehicle registration is voluntarily/automatically cancelled
      • the vehicle is a private vehicle that has been disposed of (other than to a dealer)
      • the vehicle is transferred and the purpose use changed to a non-COI type use
      • the vehicle is modified and the modification is not approved by TMR type approval or under the Approved Persons Scheme
      • there has been a significant change to the condition or construction of the vehicle since the COI was issued

    Exceptions
    Exceptions
      For any of the below scenarios, telephoneHelp Direct for advice on whether the original COI expiry date can be reinstated:
      • COI was cancelled due to POU change where no transfer occurred (e.g. change to and from personalised/passenger service POU to private and back cancelled COI).
      • COI was cancelled due to change in POU during transfer (customer/dealer wasn't aware to change POU as part of transfer transaction).
      • Vehicle sold as unregistered with expired registration. When expired registration was cancelled for new business transaction the COI was cancelled.
    Registration Transfer - Future COI Exists
    Registration Transfer - Future COI Exists

      A vehicle registration is unable to be transferred if the message displays “future COI exists”.

      To rectify this issue please follow the below process:

      1. Contact SBS to have future booking removed from TRAILS* (email or phone HELPSBS).
      2. Transfer the registration
      3. Adjust the inspection booking fees from the disposer's account to the acquirer's account.
      4. SBS will re-establish the booking in the system using the funds from the acquirer's account.

      Note:*If additional reprocessing is required (removal of a pass or defect) this may take longer to process as Compliance need to be contacted prior to removal of history.

    Use of COI
    Use of COI
      A current COI is not restricted to one transaction it may be used as many times as required while the vehicle remains a COI vehicle and the COI remains current.

      If the vehicle is transferred using a COI, and the new registered operator changes the POU to private, the COI is then cancelled. A new COI is needed to change the POU back to a COI vehicle category.

    COI and Dormant Registration
    COI and Dormant Registration
      The COI is valid up to the COI's expiry date if the registration has been made dormant due to Seasonal Registration.

    Expired COI's
    Expired COI's
      TICA will display a message if the COI recorded has expired indicating that the transaction cannot continue without a new COI. Expiry dates do not need to be manually calculated.
    Primary Producer qualification and COI validity
    Primary Producer qualification and COI validity
      The COI validity/currency (24 months) is applicable once a Primary Producer concession has been applied to the registration and the COI renewed.
Inspection Certificates Online (ICO)
Inspection Certificates Online (ICO)
    ICO is an online portal that allows Approved Examiners at an Approved Inspection Station (AIS) to create electronic safety certificates and certificates of inspection which are stored and emailed to the customer.

    ICO issues both assigned and unassigned inspection certificates, both of which can be viewed in full by CSO's on the ICO portal

    All safety certificates issued to motorcycles must be checked to ensure seating capacity matches TICA.
    Assigned certificates
    Assigned certificates
      Assigned certificates:
      • Begin with the number 1 and are automatically applied to vehicles that currently exist in TICA
      • Have had the vehicle details (VIN or plate and chassis number) verified against TICA and the approved examiner has confirmed the basic vehicle details (make, model, body shape, compliance date) returned from TICA match the vehicle they are inspecting
      • Have the ICO inspection certificate details automatically lodged in TICA, including the issue date, inspection certificate number, AIS number and odometer reading. (This information is located in the Inspection Hub).

      The customer only needs to provide the complete certificate number (eg. LV-10876608-6) to TMR for a registration transaction.

      As the details of the certificate are recorded in TICA, operators are not required to access the ICO system or print the certificate. However, operators may access ICO and view the certificate to check those vehicle details that are not validated such as colour and seating.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > The How To: Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Requirements Procedures > Access ICO as CSO

    Unassigned Certificates
    Unassigned Certificates
      Unassigned certificates:
      • Begin with the number 3 and are issued to vehicles that:
      • Do not have a VIN or chassis number recorded in TICA, or
      • The vehicle details recorded in TICA are different to the details on the vehicle (therefore not verified through ICO);
      • Details are not automatically lodged in TICA.
      • Must be manually entered into TICA - Operators are required to access ICO to look up/print the certificate and enter the details into TICA (in the same way a hand-written certificate is entered).
      • CSC staff can view the full inspection certificate in ICO.

      The customer only needs to provide the complete certificate number (eg. LV-30876608-6) to TMR for a registration transaction.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > The How To: Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Requirements Procedures > Access ICO as CSO

When a copy can be accepted
When a copy can be accepted
    An AIS can forward a copy of a COI by fax or email Customer Services Support.

    Note:Each work unit may provide to an AIS a generic email address and/or fax number for their unit. Personal email addresses are not to be provided.
Effective and Expiry Dates
Effective and Expiry Dates
    On 19 April, a new system was implemented for the calculation of COI currency by use of a common anniversary date (known in TICA as the effective date).

    This new system allows an operator to obtain a COI up to 2 months before the expiry of the previous certificate, without losing any time on the previous certificate. Alternately, if an operator is late obtaining a new COI (regardless of whether an EOT is obtained) the length of the new COI will be reduced to reflect the expiry of the previous certificate

    Clarification of COI Effective and Expiry Dates
    Clarification of COI Effective and Expiry Dates
      Items to note:
      • Inspection expiry date cannot be updated past the system generated date, it can be only updated to bring the expiry date forward
      • An EOT will not extend the period of time the inspection is valid (excluding the granting of an EOT to an unregistered Taxi prior to a new business registration)

      Situation 1 - Inspection occurs within 2 months of the current COI expiry date

      Rule
      When a new COI inspection date is up to two months prior to the expiry date of the current COI:
      • The COI effective date of the new COI must be equal to the current COI expiry date plus one day;
        and
      • The COI expiry date of the new COI must be equal to the new COI effective date plus the regulatory period minus one day.



      Situation 2 - Inspection occurs more than 2 months prior to the current COI expiry date

      Rule
      When a new COI inspection date is more than two months prior to the expiry date of the current COI:
      • The COI effective date of the new COI must be equal to the inspection date of the new COI;
        and
      • The COI expiry date of the new COI must be equal to the new COI effective date plus the regulatory period minus one day.



      Situation 3 - Inspection occurs after the current COI expiry date

      Rule
      When a new COI inspection date is after the previous COI was expired:
      • The COI effective date of the new COI must be equal to the previous COI expiry date;
        and
      • The COI expiry date of the new COI must be equal to the new COI effective date plus the regulatory period.

      Where the shortened inspection period is:
      • greater than 10 weeks the customer will be sent a reminder notice 10 weeks prior to the expiry of the current COI
      • less than 10 weeks no reminder notice will be sent (pending system enhancement) and the customer will need to be advised to book for another inspection to ensure they are always operating with a current COI



      Situation 4 - When a new COI is issued after the Registration has been cancelled

      Rule
      • The COI effective date of the new COI must be equal to the inspection date of the new COI;
        and
      • The COI expiry date of the new COI must be equal to the new COI effective date plus the regulatory period minus one day.


Exemptions
Interstate at Time Inspection Required
Interstate at Time Inspection Required
    If the vehicle is interstate when the inspection is due they must:
    • Contact the relevant Compliance Office to gain approval to obtain an Interstate COI.
    • Obtain an Interstate COI equivalent to the inspection they were required to have in Qld. For example, if an AIS inspection is required in Qld, the COI must come from an equivalent inspection station in the state they are in.
    • Forward the Interstate COI to their relevant Compliance Office The Inspector will issue a reciprocal COI and update TICA allowing the renewal payment to be made.
Extension of Time
Duplicate COIs
Duplicate COIs
    If a replacement COI is required advise the customer to:
    • Complete and Replacement Application - Inspectable Vehicle (F2299)
    • Contact the TMR Inspection Station/AIS that issued the original

    The customers account will be debited with the required fee and the duplicate COI posted in the mail to the customer.
Discrepancies and Illegible Identifiers
Data Entry Errors
Data Entry Errors
    Manual (Paper) AIS COI
    SPECPRO operators can only invalidate manually issued AIS COI for TMR operator related data entry errors only. All other errors such as duplicate certificates must be referred to HelpDirect for investigation.

    Manual (Paper) TMR Certificates of Inspection
    All other errors on a TMR issued COI must be referred to the issuing Officer for investigation and correction.

    If a COI is being incorrectly requested you must contact HelpDirect for investigation.

    Electronic (ICO) COI
    All errors on an electronically issued safety certificates must be referred back to the issuer.
Inspections and GST
Inspections and GST
    As the service of inspecting a vehicle is deemed to be a 'fee for service' GST is payable. GST is payable when the inspection occurs. If the inspection payment is invalidated both the fee and the GST is refundable.

    GST Exempt
    GST Exempt
      GST is exempt for:
      • Inspections performed prior to 01 July 2000
      • Duplicate COI
      • Certificate of Extension
      • Any fees or fines from an enforcement action because the vehicle:
      • Failed the inspection
      • Failed to comply with a defect notice
Document Retention
Document Retention

Payment of TMR COI Booking fees
Payment of TMR COI Booking fees
    Booking fees can be paid either:
    • At the time of booking via credit card using theTMR Online Service
    • Via the My Account Portal at https://www.service.transport.qld.gov.au/tmrportal/ (requires level 2 authentication)
    • By phoning the SBS Call Centre on 132390 to make the booking, then paying:
      • online (credit card only)
      • in person at a CSC
      Once an inspection is booked by SBS, a payment request is raised in TICA, and monies paid in person must be paid to account. Once paid the CSO is to immediately email HelpSBS (email template) with details of the customer and payment to allow booking finalisation.
    • Pre-paying the booking fee in person (all acceptable payment methods) where the money is paid to the customer account, then phoning SBS Call Centre to book, or booking through the on-line service
Exemption from Payment of TMR COI booking fee
Exemption from Payment of TMR COI booking fee
    A customer is exempt from payment of the TMR booking fee if they:
    • have presented a valid interstate COI
    • have a charitable concession on the registration
    • are a primary producer in a drought declared shire, and eligible for the Primary Producer concession
    • are unable to make it to an inspection site due to a natural disaster
    • TMR is unable to perform the original inspection (eg brake rollers not working or there is a loss of power)
    • exceptional circumstances approved by a Senior Transport Inspector
    • Are required to have a TMR inspection as a result of a Compliance audit or complaint investigation.

      Note:COI booking fees are not exempt when the booking is to clear a defect notice.
