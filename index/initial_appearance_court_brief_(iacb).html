Brief to Adjudicator (5227)


Brief to Adjudicator
When requiredA Brief to Adjudicator (BTA) is required for all proceedings progressing to court:
  • to record details of offences which are not eligible for an infringement notice to be issued.
  • when multiple offences have been detected.
  • when the offending poses a serious safety risk and warrants the reporting of the offence via an BTA.

A BTA may also be requested by Prosecution Services for a contested PIN.
Completing a BTA
Completing a BTA
Procedure
Procedure
    A Brief to Adjudicator (BTA) for a simple offence should be completed within 28 days of the offence being detected unless extenuating circumstances apply.
    If Prosecutions Services request a BTA for a contested matter the BTA must be submitted to Prosecutions by the date nominated.

    Proceedings for alleged offences must start within 12 months from the date of the alleged offence.

    When completed, submit the BTA to the Senior Transport Inspector for review.
BTA requirements
BTA requirements
    The BTA must include:
    • summary of the facts with sufficient evidence to prove the offence(s)
    • description of the evidence being relied upon to support the charge(s)
    • witness statements
    • scanned copy of all original note book pages
    • copy of any audio, video recording, link to BWC recording
    • copy of Record of Interview (RoI) if applicable
    • relevant photos
    • copy of relevant documents such as work diary pages, transport documentation, etc,
    • any additional evidence such as extracts of registration checks, use of mass calculators, weigh documents
Responsibilities
Responsibilities
Transport Inspector
Transport Inspector
    • Create a case in CID. The case number will be required for the BTA
      Compliance Intelligence Database (CID) Procedures
    • Complete the BTA using the template in the required time frame.
    • Elementise the offence and ensure all elements of the alleged offence are proven and any defence negated.
    • Using the checklist review the BTA ensuring the report and all supporting evidence is correct and proves every element of the offence before submitting to the STI.

      Note:Spell check the BTA prior to submitting to the STI for review.

    • When completed submit the BTA to the STI for review.
    • When approved:
      • sign the BTA and save as PDF file
      • save all evidence to a secure folder and zip the file
    • Send an email to your STI with a link to the location of the zip file
Senior Transport Inspector
Senior Transport Inspector
    • Using the checklist review the BTA ensuring the report and all supporting evidence is correct and proves every element of the offence
    • Sign the BTA if approved and return it to the TI
    • Submit the BTA with all the evidence to Prosecutions Services within five (5) working days of receipt or within the required legislative time frames (whichever is the sooner)
    • The BTA is to be submitted to Prosecution Services via DMS
    • Send an email using the Brief To Adjudicator email template to Prosecution Unit with DMS details (cc TI)
    • Update CID
