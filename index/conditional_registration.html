Conditional Registration (3016)


What is it?Conditional registration allows for the registration of special vehicles (light and heavy) that:
  • Do not comply with the standard regulations for registration
  • Have a genuine need to access the road network.
Requirements
  • Vehicles must be an approved non-complying vehicle (refer Vehicle Codes below)
  • Customer must specify a registration category
Registration Categories
Registration Categories
    CRLA
    		Limited Access
    • Restricted to operating in a defined worksite or designated area (eg, resort or car park)
    • All conditionally registered vehicle can have this option
    CRZA
    		Zone Access
    • Various levels of access to the road depending on area of operation
    • Distance restrictions of 20, 40 and 80kms apply depending on zone of operation
    • Utility Off-Road vehicles operated by Queensland State Emergency Services (SES) may be conditionally registered as utility off-road (agricultural primary producer category) with zone access.
    CRUA
    		Unrestricted Access
    • Unlimited use of road network
    • Restriction concerning hours of operations, mass or dimensions

    There are specific 'conditions of use' for each vehicle under each of the above registration categories.

    Note: A picture or brochure may be supplied by the customer to assist with the allocation of the correct condition codes. Vehicles eligible for conditional registration are allocated these codes to allow the vehicle to be moved safely when used on the road.


Conditionally registered vehicles must comply with the conditions for use outlined in the Guideline for Conditional Registration. A copy of the Guideline is available at: http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Registration/Conditional-registration/Conditional-registration-explained.aspx

Quad Bikes and Utility Off-Road Vehicles
Quad Bikes and Utility Off-Road Vehicles
    It is mandatory under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management-Road Rules) Regulation 2009 that:
    • Quad bike riders, the drivers of utility off-road vehicles and any passengers carried, wear approved motorcycle helmets when the vehicle is operating on a road or road related area. Complying with this condition is the responsibility of the registered operator and the rider/driver.
    • Children under the age of 8 are prohibited from being carried as passengers on quad bikes and utility off-road vehicles. This also applies to any child of any age if they are unable to sit with their feet flat on the floor and hands on handholds.

    Failing to comply with the mandatory helmet requirements will result in a fine and the incursion of 3 demerit points. Helmet offences will be subject to existing motorcycle helmet double demerit point penalties. If two or more motorcycle helmet offences are committed within a 12 month period, the second and subsequent offences will incur double demerit points.

    Information about approved motorcycle helmets is available on the Queensland Government website at:
    http://www.qld.gov.au/transport/safety/road-safety/motorcycle-safety/safety-gear/index.html.

    Please refer to the Q&A's for more information:
    Q&A Quad Bikes and Utility Off-Road Vehicles February 2017

    Exemptions
    Exemptions
      Quad Bikes
      Quad Bikes
        There are no exemptions from wearing an approved motorcycle helmet for quad bike operations.
      Utility Off-Road Vehicle
      Utility Off-Road Vehicle
        If the customer’s utility off-road vehicle is factory fitted with seat belts and a roll over protection system, and is solely used in a designated area under code LO3 of the guideline, then there is no requirement for the driver or passengers to wear an approved motorcycle helmet. The department still recommends helmets be used in these areas.

        Further, if wearing an approved motorcycle helmet will compromise a person's safety, a written application may be made for a 'Special Circumstances Permit' to consider an exemption from this requirement, or to wear a substituted helmet.

        Written requests must be made to:
        Executive Director (Transport Access & Use)
        PO Box 673
        Fortitude Valley Qld 4006

        or by email to - Driver_Licensing_and_Road_Rules@tmr.qld.gov.au

        The request must clearly detail how wearing a motorcycle helmet will compromise an individuals safety.

Conversion to Conditional Registration
    Tractors
    Tractors
      • On 30 April 2003 all registered tractors were converted to conditional registration with unrestricted access (CRUA)

      Registered operators were notified in writing that:
      • Tractor registration was converted
      • They have the option to convert to Zone (CRZA) or limited (CRLA) access

    Conditional Registration Arrangement
    Rules
    Cannot be conditionally registered
    Cannot be conditionally registered
      Trailers and Cranes
      Trailers and cranes cannot be conditionally registered.

      Complying motorcycles
      Complying motorcycles that are currently registered under the Conditional Registration Scheme will remain in scheme until the registration is cancelled.

      Previous policy allowed for a primary producer to register a complying motorcycle-off-road agricultural conditionally. However, upon review of conditional registration requirements, complying motorcycle should not be included in this scheme.

      Future applications to register a complying motorcycle-off-road agricultural conditionally, can not be accepted. Complying motorcycle must be registered under standard registration.
    Plates (incl. dealer/trade plates)
    Plates (incl. dealer/trade plates)
      • Conditionally registered vehicles can have personalised plates except if it is a Rally vehicle with Conditional Plates attached
      • Conditionally registered vehicles cannot have customised plate
      • Conditionally registered vehicle plates cannot be customised
      • A Dealer/Trade Plate cannot be attached to a vehicle eligible for conditional registration with limited access registration or zone access registration.

      Note:A Dealer Plate can be attached to a vehicle eligible for conditional registration with unrestricted access.

      However, the vehicle must still abide by the conditions that would apply if the vehicle was to be registered, e.g. permit for overmass and/or dimensional.

    Use on road
      A person must not permit a Conditionally Registered vehicle to be used on the road unless the conditions for use in the Guideline for Conditional Registration allow.
    Non-compliant off-road trail and quad bikes
    Non-compliant off-road trail and quad bikes
      Non-compliant off-road trail and quad bike operators can apply for conditional registration for recreational purposes, without first having to provide TMR with authorisations and permits when the vehicle is being registered or transferred. TMR does however recommend that the required permits are obtained prior to registration.

      Registered Operators must still obtain authorisation from the land/and or road owner before operating the vehicle in designated areas.

    Authorisations/Permits
    Authorisations/Permits
      Under certain condition of use codes, the operator of a vehicle must comply with authorisations and/or permits issued by the authority responsible for the management of the road or other agencies such as the NHVR or the QPS. This is to ensure that the local authorities most familiar with the road or area where the operator wants to operate can consider the request to decide if it is safe.

      Authorities responsible for the management of roads may include Transport and Main Roads, Local Government or the Department of Environment and Science.

      For vehicles operating under LO6 (A & B) condition of use codes, it is recommended that the relevant authorisations and/or permits are obtained prior to registration.
      Light vehicles
      Light vehicles
        Operators must obtain authorisation from the authority responsible for the management of the road as well as a permit from the QPS. For state controlled roads, this is in the form of a letter of authorisation issued by the TMR region in control of the road where the operation is going to take place.
      Heavy vehicles
      Heavy vehicles
        Operators must obtain a route specific permit from the NHVR. The NHVR will seek consent from the QPS and the relevant authorities responsible for the management of the road.

      Although having the relevant authorities and/or permits prior to registration is not compulsory, the vehicle will not be able to be used on a road unless the route-specific authorisations and/or permits are held.

      Permits must be carried by the operator in the vehicle when used on a road or made available to an Authorised Officer on request.
    Operating a Conditionally Registered Vehicle Interstate
    Operating a Conditionally Registered Vehicle Interstate
      Before operating a vehicle that requires an authorisation/permit interstate, the registered operator should contact the relevant interstate police and transport authority to confirm conditions of use.
    Conditionally Registered Amphibious Vehicles
    Conditionally Registered Amphibious Vehicles
      Where a conditionally registered amphibious vehicle is operated in Queensland waters, it ALSO needs to be registered as a ship. Contact Regional Office MSQ, to obtain advice about appropriate vessel registration requirements (for example, either as a recreational or a commercial ship).
