Electronic Communication (2975)


Electronic Communication
What it isElectronic communication is communication of information in the form of:
  • Data, text or images by guided or unguided electromagnetic energy (eg. e-mail, fax, internet) or sound processed at its destination by an automated voice recognition system. Electronic Transactions (Queensland) Act 2001 (ET Act)
When Can it Be UsedA customer may communicate electronically with TMR when:
  • policy allows a transaction or notification to be done electronically
  • Requested to do so by TMR (eg following up BE errors, providing missing documents eg training certifications for Industry Authority applications)

TMR may send automatic electronic communications or SMSs to customers when a customer is signed up to:
  • eNotices/eReminders Refer below: eNotices/eReminders
  • direct debit

TMR may use a customer's eContact to send:
  • communications about transactions/proceedings where further action/information is required
  • advice on the outcome of applications (e.g. exemptions/RRRF/learner log book approvals)
  • Driver Licence receipts to customers who have applied using an interstate or overseas renewal kit or remote area kit.
Rules
  • All the transaction requirements must be satisfied and must not require TMR to:
    • Inspect a vehicle
    • Sight original documentation as part of the transaction (eg safety certificate, EOI)
  • All additional requirements must have been met (e.g. plates surrendered, plate declaration, image and signature requirements, payment is in customer's account).
  • The sender must be able to be identified from the transmission and must be the product holder (licence holder/registered operator) or their authorised representative.
  • The time and place the information is sent by the sender and received by TMR must be able to be identified and included in retained documents
eNotices
/eReminders		Individual customers can sign up to, or opt out of, eNotices or eReminders:
  • online through the My account portal
  • in person at a CSC
  • over the phone with the Call Centre
  • by completing the eContact details on a transaction form and providing EOI

Notes:If a customer is enrolled in direct debit they will be unable to opt out of eNotices or eReminders or remove their mobile number as they are mandatory requirements.
If the customer has a NOAC restriction they will not be able to access their My account portal. However, if they had previously signed up to receive eNotices or eReminders they will continue to receive email notifications.
