Copies
Copies
    What is it?A request for the copies of documents involved in a registration transaction by an authorised person.
    Who Can Apply/ Requirements
    Registration Transfer On-Line
    Registration Transfer On-Line
      When a disposer finalises a transfer online, they are automatically emailed a ‘Buyer confirmation for transfer of registration’ document. This document is also stored in the Customer History portal and can be reprinted by the disposer after they have logged in to their My Account portal.

      When an acquirer finalises a NOD they have the option of sending themselves a ‘Seller confirmation for transfer of registration’ document. If they opt to do this, this document is also stored in the Customer History portal and can be reprinted by the acquirer after they have logged in their My Account portal.

      Operators can view and print these documents through the Customer Online Account Management screen. Standard rules for release of information apply.

    Requests from the registered operator (individuals and organisations)
    Requests from the registered operator (individuals and organisations)
      Requirements
      • Completed application for copy of Documents Release Request F4013
      • Standard EOI (for representatives, refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity > Step 2 - Check Standard EOI Requirements > Representing > What it is > Documents Required)
      Rules
      Requested documents must:
      • Contain the applicant's personal information*
      • Be sent by mail to the applicant or customer collects the documents

      *Historical documents relating to registration transactions can be released to the customer if they were the registered operator of the vehicle in question at the time the transaction was conducted.

      For example
      • The customer is requesting a copy of a registration application form from February 2008
      • They are not the current registered operator of the vehicle
      • They were the registered operator of the vehicle at the time the transaction was conducted (February 2008)

      A copy of the form can be released to them.

      Where another individual's details are on the requested form, all identifying information for the other individual must be blacked out, excluding the signature.

      For example
      • The customer is requesting a copy of a transfer form for a vehicle they disposed of
      • The transfer form contains information about the acquirer

      Any identifying information (including name, CRN and address) about the acquirer must be blacked out on the copy of the document before it is issued to the customer. The signature of the other individual may remain visible.
      For example
      • The customer is requesting a copy of a transfer from for a vehicle that was jointly registered to them and another individual and the vehicle has been transferred out of their name
      • The transfer form contains information about the other individual disposing of the vehicle and the acquirer

      Any identifying information (including name, CRN and address) about the other individual disposing of the vehicle and the acquirer must be blacked out on the copy of the document before it is issued to the customer. The signature of the other individuals may remain visible.

      Note 1: The scenarios in which this process can be followed are not limited to those outlined above. If you require assistance in determining whether a copy of a document can be released, please contact Help Direct.
      Note 2:When blacking out individuals details on copies of documents, you must ensure that the information cannot still be seen beneath the black.

      It is recommended that the information is first blacked out on the copy with a black marker pen. That document should then be photocopied, and the photocopy provided to the customer. The copy that has the black pen on it should then be securely destroyed.
      All other requests must be directed to RTI. Please refer All other requests below for RTI contact details.
