ROI Written Requests (3071)

Accidents
Accidents
    What is it?Request for an ROI statement showing current or historical information, for a vehicle involved in an accident or incident. It includes hit and run accidents.

    Under no circumstances should a search be conducted to trace witnesses or possible witnesses to an accident/incident.
    Who Can ApplyIndividuals/Organisations who are:
    • Involved in the accident
    or
    • Representing those involved in accidents/incident
    Requirements
    Requirements
    Requirements
      • Search of Vehicle/Recreational Ship Registration Records Request (F3522)
      and either:
      SituationDocuments
      Accident
      • Request on letterhead detailing the accident and personal injury from the insurance company or Solicitor/Lawyer
      or
      • Statutory declaration witnessed by a JP or Commissioner of Declarations describing the accident. The statutory declaration must include the vehicle details (Registration number and Make/description of vehicle).

      Note:A Statutory Declaration can be also signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a conveyancer.

      and either a
        • Vehicle repair Quote; or
        • a copy of any available witness statements, which must include the full name and address of the witness; or
        • Police Motor Vehicle Incident Report available from CITEC
      Incident
      • Request on letterhead detailing the incident from a Solicitor/Lawyer
      or
      • Statutory declaration witnessed by a JP describing the incident plus property damage. The statutory declaration must include the vehicle details (Registration number and Make/description of vehicle).

      Note:A Statutory Declaration can be also signed and witnessed by a lawyer and/or a conveyancer.

      and either a:
        • Repair quote for property damage
        • Copy of any available witness statements. The witness statement must include the full name and address of the witness.
    TICA ReasonAccident
    CostFee is Charged

Registered Operator
    What is it?Request for an ROI statement showing current or historical information of a registered operators registration or for the transfer date to/from their name.
    Who Can Apply
    • Registered Operator
    • Representative of the Registered Operator
    Requirements
    Requirements
    Requirements
      • Search of Vehicle/Recreational Ship Registration Records Request (F3522)
      and:
      If Request made by:Documents Required
      Deceased Registered Operators Representative
      Solicitor/public trust
      Solicitor/public trust
        Letter stating they are either:
        • The administrator/executor of a deceased estate; or
        • Acting for the administrator/executor of a deceased estate
      Other
      Other
        If not a solicitor/public trustee, evidence that the person is the executor or administrator of the estate:
        • a will naming them as the executor; or
        • Letters of Administration (issued by the supreme court) naming them as administrator.
        If there is no will or letters of administration, the representative can provide a letter declaring that they are entitled to the information as the deceased's next of kin under the Succession Act 1981
        and

    Note: The amount of vehicle registration duty paid may be released to the registered operator or representative of the registered operator (free of charge when a breakdown of the fees paid on transfer is the only information being requested).
Local Authorities
Local Authorities
    What is it?Request for an ROI statement showing current or historical information from a Local Authority.

    Requests involving:
    • A dangerous animal in a car (other than a serious dog attack as outlined below)
    • An animal locked in a car
    cannot be processed and the customer should be referred to either the TMR Right to Information and Privacy Unit or QPS.
    Who Can ApplyLocal Governments - Queensland and interstate (ie City/Shire Councils)
    Requirements
    Requirements
    Requirements
      • Search of Vehicle/Recreational ship Registration Records Request (F3522) and one of the following:
      • Request on letterhead stating information is required for matters of:
      • Regulated parking
      or
      • Enforcing law about vehicles in an area under its control
      or
      • Enforcing vehicle littering or illegal dumping offences under s117 of the Waste Reduction and Recycling Act 2001
      or
      • a dog attack that has resulted in either bodily harm, grievous bodily harm or death to a person or animal. The letter must state that the request is made under s207A of the Animal Management (Cats and Dogs) Act 2008, detail the injury that occurred as a result of the attack and confirm that all other reasonable methods of identifying the person responsible for the dog have been exhausted.

      and a
      • Statement that they are the person delegated to request the information
      or
      • Copy of formal approval from the TMR's Chief Executive allowing access to the information

    CostFee is Charged

Queensland Police Service (QPS)
    What is it?Request for an ROI statement showing current or historical information from QPS.
    Who Can ApplyA QPS representative.

    Note:Requests for information from police outside the state of Queensland are not to be treated with equal authority as requests made by the QPS. Refer: “Interstate police agencies and Australian Federal Police” section below.
    Requirements
    • Request on QPS letter head
    Request from QPS email
    Request from QPS email
      • Each centre/central processing hub may provide to QPS a generic email address for their centre. Personal email addresses are not to be provided

Interstate Police Agencies and Australian Federal Police
Interstate Police Agencies and Australian Federal Police
    Requests from an interstate police agency or the Australian Federal Police are not to be processed unless the request clearly demonstrates that the information is to be used for a court proceeding (see below).

    If the information is to be used for a pending court proceeding, the interstate police agency or the Australian Federal Police must provide a written request for the information on letterhead which includes a statement advising that the information is required for court purposes including the name of the defendant, the time and place of the trial.

    Special rules apply to information only TMR can provide (see below).

    Where a general request is made that is not related to court proceedings (i.e. it relates to an investigation only) the person requesting the information is to be advised to use their agency's central information area which will request the information from QPS if appropriate. The interstate police agency or the AFP must not direct their request directly to TMR.

    Information only TMR can provide
    Information only TMR can provide
      In some instances, QPS will not be able to provide the specific information that has been requested. For example:
      • If originals or copies of forms lodged with TMR are requested (such as Registration or Registration Transfer Applications);
        or
      • If an extract from the Vehicle Register is to be certified by TMR as correct (i.e. a certified registration history)
      In these instances, the police agency should not be referred to QPS. Instead, they should be advised to issue TMR with a subpoena requesting the information. The subpoena is to be sent by post to:
      Legal Services Unit

    Information for court proceedings
    Information for court proceedings
      The chief executive may be required, by court subpoena, to release licence/authority/card holder's information to a court.

      The information is to be produced to a court by the issue of a court certificate by a delegated officer.

      A delegated officer may be required to physically attend court and produce and explain the information or documents held by TMR.

      Refer: Offence Management > 1. Court Cases > The How To: Court Cases > Court Cases Procedures > Issue Court Certificate

      All internal enquiries about subpoenas can be directed to Legal Services Unit

      All subpoenas are to be processed by Legal Services Unit. The subpoena must be sent by POST to
      Legal Services Unit

      Once it has been processed, the Legal Services Unit will then send the subpoena to the relevant area of TMR for them to provide the information to the court.

Family Court
Family Court
    What is it?Request for an ROI statement showing current or historical information relevant to family court matters
    Who Can ApplySolicitors/Lawyers

    Note:Search requests for Family court matters from individuals must be referred to the Department of Transport and Main Roads' Right To Information Unit for processing
    Requirements
    Requirements
    Requirements
      • Search of Vehicle/Recreational Ship Registration Records Request (F3522)
      and:
      SituationDocuments
      If Starting Court Proceedings
      • Solicitors/Lawyers letter stating litigation is about to be commenced in the Family Court and that the person's address is needed from the register as the last known address is incorrect and the address is needed to serve/file court documents
      or
      • Copy of proposed claim to be lodged to the Family Court fully completed except for the defendant's address
      or
      • Family Court order made against the person whose information is being requested
      If Finalising Court Proceeding
      • Request on letterhead detailing the Family Court matter
      or
      • Court/legal document detailing the Family Court matter
    TICA ReasonLegal (Starting Court Proceedings)
    Court (Finalising Court Proceedings - certified details)
    CostFee is Charged
