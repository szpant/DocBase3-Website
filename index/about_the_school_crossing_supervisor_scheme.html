About the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme
Eligibility
School Crossing Warrant
School Crossing Warrant
    A school crossing warrant is based on a set of objective criteria linked to the road safety risks of an individual school's situation. Emphasis is placed on the most common problems in school zones, which are excessive vehicle speed, traffic volume and visibility (road design and fixed hazards). The risk analysis process outlined below provides all schools within Queensland with a fair and equitable eligibility criteria.

    A risk analysis will be carried out as per the instructions detailed on the Risk Assessment Report (F1840) form.

    CSB Road Safety staff submits a completed Risk Assessment Report (F184) with a memo to the Manager (Road Safety) recommending:
    • Supervision to be established
    • Continuation of current level of supervision
    • A reduction or increase in time of supervision
    • A change in operating times
    • Withdrawal of supervision

    The information contained on this form is to be entered onto the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme database with endorsed by Manager – Road Safety. Crossings deemed to meet warrants for funding of a supervised crossing are recorded by Manager-Road Safety on a state-wide list for prioritisation

    Under the General Employees (Queensland Government Departments) and other employees Award – State 2015, a school crossing supervisor must be employed for a minimum of 30 minutes and then in additional 15 minute blocks.

    Following the result of the Risk Assessment Report, the Manager (Road Safety) will confirm the outcome of the risk analysis by letter to the principal that funding is/is not available for the crossing/s at the school.
School Crossing Warrant Criteria
School Crossing Warrant Criteria
    • The school must be a primary school or a special school. A secondary school may participate in the scheme but will not be funded.
    • Before an approved school crossing can commence operation the local management authority (local council or Main Roads) must be prepared to provide the necessary crossing infrastructure in accordance with the Queensland Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices. This places responsibility for the commencement of operations with the local authority or Main Roads.
    • The outcome of the Risk Assessment Report (F1840) form recommendation will be taken into account to determine the requirement for a school crossing supervisor. The risk assessment threshold factor shall be calculated using the following formula: RAR = pc x v x (as x c x i x g) divided by 100.
    Thresholds
    Thresholds
      All crossings are banded into three groups depending on the outcome of the risk assessment factor formula. The three groups are:
      • High Risk: over 399.91
      • Medium Risk: 24.91 to 399.9
      • Low Risk: 0 to 24.9

      It will be deemed essential for schools that are determined to have a high risk assessment to be given precedence. Schools falling into the medium risk threshold will be placed on a priority list and eligibility will be determined by both priority and available regional funds.

      Schools assessed as having a low risk threshold generally do not meet the current warrant criteria. Schools with a low risk threshold will be approached by their local CSB road safety staff to discuss alternatives to a supervised crossing at the discretion of the Manager-Road Safety in consultation with local government authority (local Council /Main Roads)

      Refer: DocBase > Road Safety Policy > 1. School Crossing Supervisor Scheme > School Crossings that do not meet current warrant
    Risk Factor Components
    Risk Factor Components
      PCPrimary & infant children who cross the road within 50m of formal crossing or identified crossing point in the case of a new crossing application for 1 hour in the morning and 30 minutes in the afternoon - for definition of a pedestrian refer to Note below.
      VVehicles traversing crossing per hour in the morning and 30 minutes in the afternoon. Where a crossing is operated for 45 minutes the number of children and vehicles counted during that period are used in the calculation) N.B. Heavy vehicles will have the value of two light vehicles.

    Hazard Index Components
    Hazard Index Components
      as
      		Signed approach speed
      c
      		Obstruction to visibility (crest, dip, curve etc)
      i
      		Proximity of crossing to intersection
      g
      		Gradient of road
      iv
      		The nominated crossing should be less than 200 metres from the school or within sight of the school boundary.
      v
      		Where workplace health and safety conditions dictate that a school crossing supervisor cannot operate safely due to unfavourable speed limits, environmental or road conditions, the local management authority (Main Roads/council) will be required to implement appropriate traffic calming/remedial measures before the approved school crossing supervisor can commence duty.
      vi
      		A single school crossing supervisor shall not normally be appointed to control more than two lanes of traffic.
      vii
      		A single crossing supervisor shall not normally be appointed to stand on a traffic island to control traffic on both sides of the island at the same time. See Section 80(5) of Transport Operations Road Use Management - Road Rules .
      viii
      		Following the installation of traffic signals or a crosswalk, and where there is a perceived degree of road safety risk due to changed traffic conditions. In this instance consideration will be given to the ongoing employment of a school crossing supervisor or supervisors for a specified period, usually two weeks, following the installation of electronic signals or a crosswalk. School crossing supervisors are to educate students in the use of pedestrian activated traffic lights and to ensure safety outcomes are achieved. Each case will be determined on its merits. Refer below withdrawal of upgraded supervised crossing > Upgrade of a Children's Crossing and Supervision at Crosswalks with Traffic Signals Exception).



    Note:Under TRUM Pedestrian Crossing Facility Guidelines and Prioritisation System User Guide technical note 3:13 a Pedestrian is defined as:

    Any person walking, running, standing or sitting on a road or persons in a toy vehicle, a pram or in a mobility device not capable of exceeding 10km/h. Dismounted cyclists are also included.
    For further guidance on definitions and the selection of traffic control devices see the Traffic & Road Use Management Manual - Pedestrian Crossing Facility Guidelines and Prioritisation System User Guide 3:13

Establishing a New Crossing
Establishing a New Crossing
    When road safety concerns are identified around a school, CSB Road Safety staff will assess the situation to determine the best solution.

    If the need for a supervised crossing is identified the School Principal must complete and forward School Crossing Supervisor Scheme Application form (F1845) for assessment by Transport and Main Roads (TMR). CSB Road Safety staff will apply the criteria set out in the Policy.

    The following process is carried out to establish a new supervised crossing.

    CSB Road Safety staff send a request to the local government authority or TMR for the installation of a crossing and appropriate signage.

    CSB Road Safety staff meets with the principal to discuss:
    • Anticipated date of commencement
    • Responsibilities of school principals
    • Recruitment of school crossing supervisor/s
    • Administration of the scheme
    • Hours of work to be funded
    • Times of operation
    • Notification to the school community through the school newsletter of location, times of operation and names of supervisors
    • On going review process
    • Letter of Understanding.
School Community Consultation
School Community Consultation
    Department of Transport and Main Roads School Crossing Supervisor Scheme is a major component of the Safe School Travel Program (SafeST) which is a collection of programs, schemes and initiatives that are designed to improve road safety for Queensland school students travelling to and from school.

    As a prerequisite when making a decision whether a school crossing supervisor is the most suitable option for a school, specific road safety concerns should first be identified by CSB Road Safety staff and SafeST Committee. In areas where there is no SafeST Committee the P&C, school principal or interested group may identify road safety concerns.

    An application for a supervised school crossing may result from the SafeST Committee’s review of the traffic situation at a school. However, approval of the application by the department is subject to an investigation of the school and its environment and whether the school meets the established criteria. Further to this, all applications are subject to the availability of adequate funds.
Resourcing and Prioritising Decisions
Resourcing and Prioritising Decisions
    Prioritising the expansion of the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme (SCSS) is a state-wide decision-making process facilitated through the Road Safety Product Leadership Team (RSPLT).

    In response to a request for a supervised school crossing, regional CSB road safety staff conduct a site assessment to determine a Risk Assessment Factor (RAF). The RAF calculates potential risk based on a number of variables including pedestrian and vehicle movements, speed, visibility, length of the school zone, gradient and proximity to an intersection.
    Eligibility Criteria for Potential School Crossing Sites
    Eligibility Criteria for Potential School Crossing Sites
      The RSPLT propose, to be considered in deliberations about expansion of the SCSS a site must score a high or medium RAF and meet a series of eligibility criteria.
      • A Safe School Travel (SafeST) investigation has been conducted and a supervised school crossing is deemed an appropriate treatment type (fit-for-purpose);
        and
      • The school community supports the installation of a supervised school crossing and undertakes to promote safe use of the facility;
        and
      • There is a commitment from the road authority (state or local government) to deliver the required infrastructure;
        and
      • There is knowledge of future development or growth in the region requiring connectivity, whereby vehicle and pedestrian movements are not likely to decrease.
    Prioritisation of Eligible School Crossing Sites
    Prioritisation of Eligible School Crossing Sites
      • Once a site is deemed eligible, locations are tabled on the master list of potential crossings for expansion in risk rank order (highest to descending RAF). This master list informs the deliberations of the RSPLT which is maintained on SharePoint and revisited regularly.
      • The RSPLT will endeavour to provide a level of equity across metropolitan, regional and rural areas based on local knowledge, however the RAF will be the primary tool used for prioritising expansion of the SCSS.
        Based on the current number of eligible sites on the master list (n = XX) and forward projections on schools currently in the development assessment process, the RSPLT recommends, an expansion of:
        • 25 crossings in the first year
        • 25 crossings in the second year
        • 20 crossings in the third year

      Note:The demand for crossings in the third year should be revisited in the second year to reflect the landscape at that time

      A CBRC submission has been prepared seeking funding for 25, 25, 20 new school crossing supervisors for the next three years, commencing financial year 2018/19. In addition to new school crossing supervisors, funding is also sought for an additional fulltime road safety officer and capital infrastructure to manage expansion of the scheme.

      The Eligibility Criteria for prioritising the expansion of the SCSS will be adopted as School Crossing Database Policy for the prioritisation of new supervised school crossings across the State. This meets the recommendations set out in 3.2.2A and 3.2.2B Internal Audit Report: School Crossing Supervisor Scheme – Resourcing and prioritisation decisions 21 May 2018.

      The management function associated with the additional crossings will require resourcing and will be apportioned in accordance with CSB Road Safety staff FTE calculation ratios previously adopted by the RSPLT.
    Recommendation
    Recommendation
      • CSB Road Safety proposed expansion to the SCSS to meet future demand. The demand is based on a current number of identified sites eligible for inclusion as part of the expansion across the State.
      • Prioritising the expansion of new supervised school crossings is a state-wide decision-making process facilitated through the RSPLT for endorsement by the General Manager, CSB, TMR.

When to Conduct a Risk Assessment
When to Conduct a Risk Assessment
    A Risk Assessment Report will be completed in the following circumstances:
    • A school may apply to a Transport and Main Roads (TMR) road safety office to have a supervised crossing.
    • Where a school's SafeST committee identifies road safety concerns. Note a SafeST committee must be convened to formalise the process as identified in the School Environment Safety Guidelines.
    • Where a crossing has been upgraded from a single to a dual crossing.
    • Where there are dual crossings and only one supervisor currently operating them. Note under the School Environment Safety guidelines there is a requirement for two supervisors.
    • The local road authority or Main Roads are responsible for all crossing infrastructure. Should an established or proposed crossing have high risk factor of 1500 and above, additional consultation is suggested with the road authority for them to consider pedestrian activated traffic lights.
    • Where a workplace health and safety issue is raised by the school or school crossing supervisor.

    For information on business rules around performing a Risk Assessment.

    Refer: School Crossing Warrant Criteria above or Performing a Risk Assessment below.

    CSB Road Safety staff and the school principal agree on times of operation. These times are based on pedestrian and traffic counts carried out as per school crossing warrant. Refer: Eligibility > School Crossing Warrant above

School Crossing Supervisor Complaints
School Crossing Supervisor Complaints
    All complaints from the public or members of the school community in relation to a school crossing supervisor’s operational duties will be followed up.

    CSB Road Safety staff will determine further action. If no further action is identified, the complaint notes are to be filed on the personnel file.

    If further action is required, CSB Road Safety staff will:
    • Investigate the complaint;
    • Note details of investigation and school crossing supervisors response;
      and
    • Determine any further action required e.g. retraining, diminished performance.
    When the investigation is completed, complaint details are filed on the school crossing supervisor’s personnel file or confidential complaint file. If complaints continue, diminished performance procedure is to be implemented. Complainant advised their complaint has been followed up.

    Refer: Performance Management (Inside CSB)

Alteration of Hours
    Prior to any change in hours of supervision at a school, the safety needs at that site must be fully considered.

Staffing levels at Non-Standard School Crossing Infrastructure
Staffing levels at Non-Standard School Crossing Infrastructure
    1.0 Purpose/Rationale
    • Clarify the number of School Crossing Supervisors required to staff unusual types of school crossing infrastructure.

    2.0 Relates To Transport Policy
    • School Crossing Supervisor Scheme policy.

    3.0 Scope
    • This policy applies to Transport and Main Roads controlled school crossings only.

    4.0 Current Policy
    • Staffing levels at unusual types of school crossing infrastructure.

    5.0 Background
    Under transport legislation, most car parks would be considered a road with schools becoming larger in size, some schools are requesting supervision in car parks.
    • Regional staff have identified that there are current crossings with an 'L shape' infrastructure that are staffed by one crossing supervisor rather than two supervisors. Policy provides that two supervisors would normally staff what would be considered two separate crossings points. However, these 'L shape' crossings generally have low traffic and pedestrian volumes as well as road infrastructure that provides good observation of traffic and pedestrian movements. For these reasons, regional road safety staff believe these crossings do not warrant the staffing level of two or more crossing supervisors.
    • The proposed policy aims to clarify the number of School Crossing Supervisors required to staff unusual types of school crossing infrastructure.

    6.0 Definitions
    For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply.
    • SCSS – School Crossing Supervisor Scheme
    • SCS – School Crossing Supervisor
    • TMR – Transport and Main Roads
    • TORUM – Transport Operation (Road Use Management)
    • RAR – Risk Assessment Report
    • Unusual types of school crossing infrastructure - Includes but is not restricted to:
    • 'L shape' crossings
    • A road functioning as a car park
    Excludes:
    • A single crossing - traffic flowing in one or both directions on one lane requiring one supervisor
    • Dual crossing infrastructure - traffic flowing in one or both directions on two lanes requiring two supervisors
    • A children’s crossing that extends across a road with a dividing strip, the part of the children’s crossing on each side of the dividing strip is taken to be a separate children’s crossing under the Transport Operational Road Use Management 2009 – Road Rules Regulation section 80 (5).

    7.0 Policy
    Road Safety Managers may approve the number of School Crossing Supervisors required to staff unusual types of school crossing infrastructure provided:
    • The crossing is located on a road as defined under TORUM Act 1995 under schedule 4. When considering what is a road ask two questions:
      • Does the public have access?
      • Are motor vehicles allowed there?
    • If the answer is 'yes' to both then it is likely to be a road. If any doubt exist over whether or not an area is a road, legal advice should be sought that relates to the specific circumstances of the area.
    • The local road owner or/and authority (Main Roads/council or school) has implemented appropriate traffic calming/remedial measures to address workplace health and safety conditions that dictate a school crossing supervisor cannot operate safely due to unfavourable speed limits, environmental or road conditions.
    • The crossing risk assessment score for existing crossings is within the prescribed low (0 to 24.9) to low- medium (24.9 – 200) threshold range therefore reducing the level of risk for staff and pedestrians.
    • The regional WPH&S officer supports the staffing level at unusual school crossing infrastructure.
    • The most effective and safe crossing drill has been adopted.
    • If considering supervision of a road which functions as a car park, think about the impact of the following issues, note these are not mandatory criteria:
      • Does the car park road have a name (for example Smith Street or Jones Road);
      • Does the car park provide access to areas other than the school (for example a business, a sports club, or residential housing);
      • Is the area an off-street car-park;
        and
      • Is the Risk Assessment Report of the crossing in the medium to high risk threshold range therefore presenting a higher risk to staff and pedestrians.
      • Any variation to the crossing drill must meet legislative and the School Environmental Guidelines standards.

    8.0 Procedures
    • Managers or their delegated officers must document the decision making process used and include this as an attachment to the RAR.
    • Crossings that are staffed by one SCS are to be recorded on the SCSS database as one SCS position regardless of the infrastructure type.
    • Crossings that are staffed by two SCS's are to be recorded on the SCSS database as two SCS positions regardless of the infrastructure type.

School Crossing Site Inspections
School Crossing Site Inspections
    1.0 Background
    • Before an approved school crossing can commence operation the local road authority (local council or Main Roads) must be prepared to provide the necessary crossing infrastructure in accordance with the Queensland Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices.
    • CSB Road safety staff currently check the maintenance of existing crossings every 2 years to ensure crossings are maintained and to identify any issues which require attention. The Corporate Form School Crossing Site Inspection Report (F1830) is used to document the process.
    • Under the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme policy 4.1.04 Management and Delegations road safety staff are required to conduct site inspections and liaise with local government and the Department of Main Roads regarding the maintenance of supervised school crossings.
    • Under the Workplace Health and Safety Act 2011 section 22(2) employers are required to conduct every 2 years work place assessments to ensure a safe work environment.

    2.0 Definitions
    For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply:
    • SCSS – School Crossing Supervisor Scheme
    • SCS – School Crossing Supervisor
    • TMR – Transport and Main Roads
    • SCSI – School Crossing Site Inspection
    • RSPLT – Road Safety Product Leadership Team
    • MUTCD – Manual Uniform of Traffic Control Devices
    • RD (Regional Director), Lead RD (Lead Regional Director), ED SD (Executive Director Services Division)

    3.0 Policy
    Site Inspections
    • Site inspections at supervised crossings must be undertaken every 2 years unless there is an identified need to conduct the inspection earlier.
    • Site inspections of supervised school crossings can only be conducted by appropriately trained staff.
    • The inspection and any further action necessary will be documented using corporate form SC Site Inspection (F1830) form.

    4.0 Procedures
    Regional Road Safety Managers are responsible for ensuring:
    • Staff undertaking site inspections are appropriately trained.
    • Assessments at supervised school crossings are conducted every 2 years or earlier if necessary.
    • SC Site Inspection (F1830) form and the Users Guide for School Crossing Site Inspections are used by CSB Road Safety staff.
    • Results of site inspections are used when liaising with local government and the Department of Main Roads regarding the maintenance of supervised school crossings.
    • The RD, lead RD and ED (SD) are informed of crossings that have the potential to cause adverse media or have political implications.
    • The following process applies to CSB Road Safety staff undertaking the inspection of a supervised school crossing:
      • Conduct inspection at supervised school crossings.
      • Document the inspection using SC Site Inspection (F1830) form – SCSI in accordance with the Users Guide for SCSI.
      • Follow up on actions resulting from the inspection including file notes if required.
      • Escalate actions that are deemed a safety risk and have not been completed as soon as practical. In particular, Road Safety Managers and Senior Advisors should be aware of crossings that have the potential to have a continued elevated safety risk and/or the potential to cause adverse media or have political implications.
      • File the completed form and any associated documented information on the school file.
      • Record each completed inspection on the SCSS database.

    5.0 Systems
    • SCSS Database – Maintenance will continue to be used to document the inspection details and track any issues that need to be actioned.
    • SCSS Database will include reminder bring up dates and drop down boxes to record the condition of crossing infrastructure. These enhancements have been listed as a recommended change to the database when funding becomes available.

    6.0 Products
    • Not Applicable.

    7.0 Forms
    • SC Site Inspection (F1830) form updated and name changed from School Crossing Maintenance Report to School Crossing Site Inspection. The purpose of the SCSI form is to capture all elements of infrastructure to be inspected, corrective action taken, by who and when at existing supervised crossing.
    • The new SCSI form includes a field for the date of the last Risk Assessment Report and Performance Assessment but excludes any detailed information contained within these reports which are currently dealt with in separate corporate forms.

    8.0 Legislation
    • Work Health and Safety Act 2011
    • Section 27a - requirement for a risk assessment
    • Section 28 - obligations of the relevant employer
    • Section 96a - requirement for an annual assessment.

    9.0 Review
    • Three years from date of approval.
Withdrawal of upgraded Supervised School Crossing
Withdrawal of upgraded Supervised School Crossing
    1.0 Purpose/Rationale
    • To review Transport and Main Road’s policy on managing the withdrawal of a supervised children’s crossing due to an upgrade to signalised lights, a crosswalk, underpass or overpass.

    2.0 Relates To Transport Policy
    • School Crossing Supervisor Scheme - Refer below: Supervision at Crosswalks with Traffic Signals Exception policy.

    3.0 Scope
    • This policy applies to Transport and Main Roads supervised children’s crossings being upgraded to signalised lights, a crosswalk, underpass or overpass.

    4.0 Current Policy
    This policy replaces the following sections of the SCSS Policy database:
    • 4.1.15 Upgrade of Children's Crossing Policy
    • 4.1.16 Withdrawal of a Supervised Crossing Policy
    • 3.1.08 Upgrade of a Children's Crossing Procedure
    • 3.1.09 Withdrawal of a Supervised Crossing Procedure

    5.0 Definitions
    For the purpose of this policy, the following definitions apply:
    • SCSS – School Crossing Supervisor Scheme
    • SCS – School Crossing Supervisor
    • CSB – Customer Services Branch
    • TMR – Transport and Main Roads
    • MP – Member of Parliament

    6.0 Policy
    Pre-consultation
    • Where a supervised crossing is to be upgraded to signalised lights, a crosswalk, underpass or overpass the Regional Director is to develop a brief to the General Manager (CSB).
    • The brief must explain the reasons for the upgrade, who will be consulted, details of current supervision, where the crossing funding will be redirected and the expected date of withdrawal. The brief is to be copied to the Director, Service Delivery & Policy Branch.
    • Crossing funding is to be redirected to suitable crossing(s) dependant on the state-wide priority list and considering the electorate, then the region, at the discretion of the Manager (Road Safety).

Unstaffed school crossings
Unstaffed school crossings
    What it is
    What it is
      Unstaffed school crossing hours is when a funded supervised school crossing is not manned for the normal hours of operation due to the unavailability of an authorised school crossing supervisor or other extenuating circumstance i.e. natural disaster, road construction.

      School Crossing Supervisor’s are required to report any unstaffed hours under their employment obligations to both CSB Road Safety staff and the school as soon as immediately possible refer School Crossing Supervisor Scheme Guidelines – 2.3 Relief Supervision.

      School administration staff are required to report any unstaffed school crossing hours to the road safety office as soon as immediately possible under the agreed responsibilities, refer Memorandum of Understanding – School Crossing Supervisor Scheme 2016 Section - Relief Supervision.

      Road Safety staff are required to enter unstaffed school crossing hours into the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme Database to meet both financial and performance management accountabilities related to the management of the Supervised School Crossing Scheme.

      Unstaffed school crossing hours are collated and reported one month in arrears.

      To ensure consistent state-wide processing of unstaffed school crossing hours and ensure data integrity for high level reporting requirements, road safety staff are required to have all unstaffed school crossing hours entered into the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme Database by the final day of the following month.

      i.e. January unstaffed school crossing hours must be entered by the final working day in February.

Supervision at Crosswalks with Traffic Signals Exception
Supervision at Crosswalks with Traffic Signals Exception
    What is is
    What is is
      The upgrade of crossing to traffic lights represents a change to a higher order road safety device and represents a safety improvement for all pedestrians.

      Transport and Main Road's policy Upgrade of Children's Crossing provides for retention of a crossing supervisor for two weeks after the installation of traffic lights.

      Crossing Supervisors are only retained beyond this period if the exceptional circumstances process outlined in this policy has been undertaken, analysed and approved by the Manager Road Safety, Regional Director and/or General Manager.
    Supervision at Traffic Lights
    Supervision at Traffic Lights
      Before a school crossing supervisor can perform at a set of lights, a workplace health and safety assessment must be carried out. Appropriate training must be provided to the crossing supervisor by the CSB Road Safety staff.

      The school crossing supervisor drill functions at a set of lights are to:
      • marshal students on the footpath;
      • instruct students to look and listen when crossing the road;
      • instruct students to activate the control button;
      • advise students to look for the green 'Walk' signal and watch for traffic;
        and
      • school crossing supervisor can only monitor one crosswalk at the signals.

      Supervision at a crosswalk can only be provided by voice instruction. The STOP sign and hand signals must not be used.
Signal Failure
Signal Failure
    In the event of signal failure, the road reverts to a thoroughfare only, depending on the road marking's, and the crosswalk no longer exists - the give way rules apply.

    The crossing supervisor should oversee the safety of the students and upon finishing their rostered shift, notify the school of the signal failure.

    Refer above: Supervision at Crosswalks with Traffic Signals Exception for additional information.
Dependent Children/Family and Friends
Dependent Children/Family and Friends
    Dependent children/family and friends are not to accompany a school crossing supervisor while on duty. Children in the care of the school crossing supervisor may be permitted to stay in the grounds of the school subject to prior approval by the school principal.
Media
Media
    In the event of an injury on the crossing, school crossing supervisors are to be instructed not to discuss the incident with people other than a member of the road safety team or the school principal. Importantly, the School Crossing Supervisor shall not communicate with the media (Refer: SCSS Guidelines Booklet Section 4.5) about such an incident.
Use of recording devices
Use of recording devices
    Capturing images or videos around schools is contentious and may result in complaints from parents and schools due to the possibility of either intentional or accidental recording of children/students.

    As a result the use of recording devices by SCSS whilst performing the role of a SCSS is strictly prohibited.

    Devices include but are not limited to:
    • Cameras (including phone camera’s)
    • Video recorders
    • Body worn Cameras (eg Go Pro’s)

    Policy Rationale:
    • The need to protect the privacy of those within the school environment
    • The administrative burden of managing the information (eg downloading, identifying and storing)
    • The need to monitor the use of recording devices to ensure that the information is captured in accordance with TMR established guidelines.
Crossing Drill
Standard Crossing Drill
Standard Crossing Drill
    What it is
    What it is
      The standard drill will be used at all supervised school crossings, except those crossings requiring dual supervision, on one way streets, signalised crossings or where a special variation to the drill has been authorised by (CSB Road Safety staff).

      School crossing supervisors must allow emergency vehicles approaching their crossing to have unhindered progress through the crossing. Heavy vehicles and buses should also be allowed unhindered progress whenever possible.

      School crossing supervisors should allow a greater stopping distance for motorists to respond on wet roads.

      The Traffic Operations (Road Use Management - Road Rules) Regulations 2009 section 15 defines bicycles as vehicles and as of 1 January 2015, a cyclist is able to ride across a zebra or children's crossing providing they give way to pedestrians. If their action is impacting on pedestrian safety in the crossing environment, just like scooters, roller blades and skateboards, a school crossing supervisor can ask the person to dismount/remove/wheel/carry. However, they can't be forced to do so, however the safety aspect of doing this should be promoted.

      On arrival at the crossing, and just before the approved operating time, a school crossing supervisor will display the Children Crossing flags.
    Procedures
    Procedures
      • Check the vicinity of the crossing for illegally parked vehicles on both approaches.
      • Drivers of illegally parked vehicles that may obstruct a driver’s view of the crossing supervisor and children, or the crossing supervisor’s view of approaching traffic, are to be courteously asked to move the offending vehicle.
      • If an offending motorist does not, or will not comply with this request, the registration number should be recorded on an Incident Report form and reported to CSB Road Safety staff.
      • School Crossing Supervisors have no authority to move or use traffic cones.
      • When on duty it is essential to be alert at all times. School Crossing Supervisors must not be distracted from their duties in any way. For example, reading, minding children, bringing pets, smoking or listening to headsets on duty is not permitted.
      • Mobile phones are not to be used for the duration of the shift except for emergency calls.
      • The use of two-way radios or walkie talkies are not to be used for the duration of the shift except for emergency calls to a base station located in the school premises. These devices should be approved by the Manager Road Safety.
      • The STOP sign must be held in the hand at all times, therefore, umbrellas cannot be used whilst on duty.
      • School crossing supervisors are only to touch children in an emergency situation to remove them from the path of danger.
      • School crossing supervisors normally stand near the kerb on the right hand side of the crossing.
      • School Crossing Supervisors stand on the school side in the afternoon and the opposite side to the school in the morning. CSB Road Safety staff may direct variations to this part of the drill for safety reasons.
      • Pedestrians waiting to cross are asked to stand behind the pedestrian halt line, if one exists, or to the rear but not directly behind the supervisor.
      • When pedestrians are ready to cross and there is a suitable gap in the traffic, school crossing supervisors step forward to the kerb and raise the STOP sign to a 45 degree angle.
      • Provided it is safe to enter the carriageway, school crossing supervisors walk to the centre of the carriageway and place the STOP sign vertically with the word STOP facing approaching traffic.
      • School crossing supervisors stand on the right hand side of the crossing to allow clear passage for pedestrians.
      • School crossing supervisors ensure the STOP sign remains facing oncoming traffic, turn and face the direction they came from. They must not stand with their back to approaching traffic.
      • Before blowing the whistle, school crossing supervisors check both directions to ensure that approaching traffic has stopped and it is safe for pedestrians to cross.
      • When school crossing supervisors consider it safe for pedestrians to enter the crossing, two distinct whistle blasts are blown.
      • One blast with one hand raised towards pedestrians means STOP immediately and should only be used to halt a continuous stream of pedestrians or in an emergency.
      • While pedestrians are crossing, school crossing supervisors maintain a watch in both directions to ensure that any approaching traffic is observing the STOP sign.
      • School crossing supervisors are not authorised to direct traffic with hand signals or give hand signals to motorists.
      • Check both sides of the crossing to ensure that all pedestrians have cleared the crossing. School crossing supervisors should be particularly alert for stragglers, children who double back and pedestrians who approach from the opposite side of the carriageway.
      • Return the STOP sign to the 45 degree angle, move to the kerb.
      • School crossing supervisors adopt the rest position approximately half a metre behind the kerb whilst holding the STOP sign upside down, and not facing oncoming traffic.
      • If a large number of pedestrians are waiting to use the crossing, allow as many pedestrians as possible to cross, halt the remainder and allow traffic to clear. This procedure is then repeated as often as necessary.
Variations to the Standard Crossing Drill
Variations to the Standard Crossing Drill
    CSB Road Safety staff may vary the standard crossing drill where:
    • a crossing supervisor is to work on a one way street or where there are two lanes of traffic moving in one direction;
    • there is a divided road with a median strip, a pedestrian shelter or a runway provided by the local government;
    • two crossing supervisors operate on the same crossing;
      and
    • experience has shown that a variation of the standard drill is necessary for safety reasons.
Drill for Crossing requiring dual supervision
Drill for Crossing requiring dual supervision
    • Both school crossing supervisors take up their appointed positions on either side of the crossing.
    • One crossing supervisor assumes the role of team leader. The team leader will observe traffic in both directions, seeking a suitable gap to allow pedestrians to cross safely.
    • When a suitable gap appears, the team leader indicates to the other crossing supervisor, who has checked for themselves that their lane is clear of traffic to begin.
    • Both school crossing supervisors take up a position in the centre of their respective side of the road.
    • The team leader checks that traffic in both directions is stopped and that the other school crossing supervisor is in position before signalling pedestrians to cross.
    • Once all pedestrians have cleared the crossing, the team leader indicates to the other school crossing supervisor to return to the kerb.

    Refer: Standard Crossing Drill above
Legislation
Legislation
Legislation
    All relevant legislation relating to the School Crossing Supervisor Scheme is contained with the Transport Operations (Road Use Management Act) 1995.

    The Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 was amended in 2005 by the Transport Legislation Amendment Bill to enhance and strengthen the regulation of school crossing supervisors by way of:
    • providing clear guidance on when the department should refuse an application – for example, if the applicant has been convicted of a 'disqualifying offence';
    • ensuring 'disqualifying offences' include all serious criminal offences committed in or outside Queensland (for example, possession of child pornography);
    • introducing clear grounds and procedures for taking disciplinary action against a crossing supervisor including an express power to immediately suspend on the ground of public safety or public interest;
      and
    • ensuring crossing supervisors have full review and appeal rights of any decision adversely affecting them (eg to suspend or cancel their authority).

    A copy of the Act is available at the following website:
    Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995

    The relevant sections that refer to the scheme in the Act are:
    • Division 1 Definitions 122 Definitions for pt 7A;
    • Division 2 Scheme and authorisation of persons under scheme - 122A Chief executive may authorise scheme;
    • Division 3 Criminal history - 122F Criminal history to be disclosed by applicants and crossing supervisors;
    • Division 4 Amendment, suspension and cancellation of authorities.

    The department has a duty of care to its employees and members of the Queensland community accessing the services provided by employees of the department. For this reason, the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 and the PSC Directive on Employment Screening (07/11) both impose conditions on the employment of School Crossing Supervisors to create a safe workplace for employees and members of the school communities (For further information see inside HR - Employment Screening).

Business Rules
Business Rules
    Purpose
    Business rules for reporting have been created to clearly define reporting criteria and to ensure the validity and consistency of statistical information.

    The business rules are as follows:

    Business rule
    The number of crossings incorporates all supervised crossings; including those funded by third parties such as a school, property developer or council; and crossings where school paid/volunteer school crossing supervisors are engaged.

    Report format:
    Crossings funded by TMR: XX
    Crossings funded externally: XX
    Volunteer crossings: XX
    Total number of supervised crossings: XX

    Business Rule
    The number of school crossing supervisors includes all authorised supervisors, including TMR employees, school paid/volunteer supervisors, and those engaged by a third party.

    Report format:
    Supervisors employed by TMR: XX
    Supervisors employed by other: XX
    Volunteer supervisors: XX
    Relief supervisors: XX
    Total number of authorised school crossing supervisors: XX

    Business Rule
    The number of schools includes all schools with supervised school crossings, regardless of whether the crossing is externally funded or school funded/volunteer based.

    Total number of schools with supervised crossings: XX

    When required to provide SCSS statistics, where possible use the full report format. If restricted to reporting on totals, please ensure definitions are included in the document.
