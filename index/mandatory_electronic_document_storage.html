Mandatory Electronic Document Storage (7605)

Project NameMandatory Electronic Storage of Documents
WhenJuly 2020
BackgroundIn January 2020 Northern Region successfully trialled electronic storage of the CSC daily TRAILS reports in the DMS system. This removed the requirement to keep the physical documents locally or in secondary storage, meaning the hard-copies were securely destroyed and in turn reducing archiving costs, and creating efficient document retrieval for auditing purposes.

The trial was replicated in Southern and Central regions, where all other operational documentation, in addition to the CSC daily TRAILS reports, e.g. end of day banking, stocktake, were uploaded and stored in DMS.

The successful implementation is in line with the Paper-Lite 2020 Strategy, Information Management Strategy 2020-2022, Digitisation and Disposal Policy and the CSB Controlled Self-Assessment Framework.

The procedure was endorsed by CSB PLT, and since 1 July 2020 has been made mandatory for all CSC's and TMR-Led QGAP's to follow the Electronic Storage of Documents.
Who will be impacted and how?Customer Service Centre Staff and SASM/Managers must commence electronic storage of all their operational documentation (excluding daily filing) from the 1 July 2020. Staff no longer have the option to manually archive or hold these documents locally.

The Electronic Storage of Document List outlines the Product and Documents that can be stored in DMS. Once these documents have been saved into DMS the originals can be securely destroyed.
