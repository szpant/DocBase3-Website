COVID 19 - Medical Certificates for drivers aged 75+ (7547)


What is the rule?Any person 75 years or older who holds a licence, must carry a current medical certificate at all times when driving and comply with any stated conditions.
Changes during the COVID-19 emergency
Licence holders who turned 75 since 29 January 2020
Licence holders who turned 75 since 29 January 2020
    If a licence holder turned 75 during the COVID-19 emergency period and cannot obtain a medical certificate due to COVID-19 restrictions, they do not have to obtain a medical certificate to drive on a road.

    This will apply even if the licence holder has received recent correspondence from TMR advising that they are required to obtain a valid medical certificate.

    If they already had a medical certificate issued prior to turning 75 they must follow the below requirements.

    If a licence holder develops a permanent or long-term condition that is likely to affect their ability to drive safely during the emergency period, they must notify TMR and must not drive until they are assessed by a doctor and have a medical certificate issued.
75 years or older with no 'M' condition
75 years or older with no 'M' condition
    If a licence holder aged 75 or over has a medical certificate which expired after the COVID-19 emergency was declared on 29 January 2020 or will expire during the COVID-19 emergency period, they will not be required to obtain a new medical certificate.

    The "Renew Driver Licence" Online Service will allow these customers to renew their licence during the COVID period.

    Licence holders must still carry their expired certificate with them while driving and continue to follow any conditions listed on the certificate.

    If a licence holder develops a permanent or long-term condition that is likely to affect their ability to drive safely during the emergency period, they must notify TMR and must not drive until they are assessed by a doctor and have a medical certificate issued.
75 years or older with an 'M' condition
75 years or older with an 'M' condition
    TMR can extend the current medical certificate for a period of 6 months from the expiry date listed on the certificate, if it hasn’t expired yet. The conditions or restrictions that are listed on the current medical certificate will continue to apply and the licence holder must drive in accordance with these conditions and/or restrictions.

    Note: ONLY ONE EXTENSION IS ALLOWED
ActionsAll affected customers will be sent an informational email (if email address is recorded) or letter advising them of the changes.
Processing the extensions for customers with 'M' conditions
CSC Staff
CSC Staff
    CSC staff will be required to record the medical certificate extension in TICA and issue the relevant letter to the customer.
    Operators will have to:
    • Record a new Medical Certificate in the customer's prerequisite screen with the correct expiry date (6 months). The Medical Condition indicator will be the same as what is currently recorded against the customer's licence
    • Add a new report to the Medical Condition Reporting Hub with the following information recorded:
      • Method – In person
      • Received from – customer
      • Additional Notes – Automatic extension granted as customer is unable to provide medical certificate due to COVID-19. Extension granted until <Expiry Date.>
      • Finalise the report in the MCR Hub
    • Issue the extension letter. Refer Below: Extension Letters
    • Add a text restriction advising that a medical extension has been granted. The text restriction expiry date should be the same as the date the medical was extended to.
    • Complete a "Report for File," including the customer's CRN and name. The report should detail the reason for the extension and be filed with the daily filing.
Customer Contact Centre Operators
Customer Contact Centre Operators

    A new task force has been created to issue the extensions of the medical certificates, their email address is medcert@tmr.qld.gov.au.

    If the customer is eligible for an extension the CCC operator should send an email to the medcert@tmr.qld.gov.au with the Subject line of "Medical Certificate Extension – CRN." Please include the customer's name, CRN, contact phone number and email address and advise that an extension is required.

    The task force will grant the extension (if eligible) and email the extension letter to the customer.

    Operators should advise the customer that they have submitted a request to have the medical certificate extension granted, and if eligible, they should receive an email within 5 business days.

Updates to the Online ServiceThe "Renew Driver Licence" Online Service has been updated to allow customers who are over 75 and have no M condition to renew if they:
  • have no medical certificate previously recorded
  • have a medical certificate recorded that expired after 29 January 2020.

The customer must confirm that they have no medical conditions. If they answer 'Yes' to any of the medical related questions on the Online Service they won't be able to renew their licence.

The functionality will be removed when the COVID-19 period ends.
