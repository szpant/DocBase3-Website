Marine licensing General Information (92)


Marine Licence Indicator
What it isAs recreational marine licences are virtual licences, the Marine Licence Indicator (MLI) provides the licence holders with evidence that they have been granted a marine licence.
MLI FeaturesThe MLI will show:
  • RMDL for a Recreational Marine Drivers Licence, and
  • PWCL for a Personal Watercraft Licence.
Types of MLI
Types of Marine Licence Indicators
Types of Marine Licence Indicators
    An MLI may take the form of a:
    Marine licence Indicator Receipt (MLIR)
    Marine licence Indicator Receipt (MLIR)
      A MLIR is issued to the customer once the Marine Licence is granted. The MLIR is effective until the driver licence (Smartcard) with MLI or Smartcard MLI is received.

    Smartcard marine licence indicator
    Smartcard marine licence indicator
      A Smartcard MLI will be issued to a RMDL or PWCL holder who does not hold a valid Qld driver licence.

    Driver licence smartcard with MLI or its receipt
    Driver licence smartcard with MLI or its receipt
      RMDL holders who hold a valid Qld driver licence smartcard will be given a new driver licence smartcard with an MLI code displayed on the reverse side.

    Confirmation Report
    Confirmation Report
      A confirmation report issued pre-NQDL or during a NQDL rollout is valid until superseded by an:
      • MLIR, or
      • driver licence smartcard with MLI or its receipt, or
      • a Smartcard MLI.

Confirmation of marine licence
Confirmation of marine licence
    When confirmation of a Recreational Marine Driver Licence or Personal Watercraft Licence is required by an authorised person (e.g. the police), the customer will need to provide one of the following:
    • A Qld Driver Licence with MLI or its receipt;
    • A Smartcard MLI;
    • A MLIR (Marine Licence Indicator Receipt);
    • Their customer reference number (CRN);
    • Their name and address (or some form of identification).

    Confirmation is made by accessing the customer's record through TICA.

    Note:If the Marine licence holder is unable to immediately comply with the requirements, the shipping inspector may allow the marine licence holder 48 hours to comply. The information is to be provided to the officer in charge of an office stated by the inspector.
MLI Expiry
Expiry of a MLI
Expiry of a MLI
    Marine licence Indicator Receipt (MLIR)
    Marine licence Indicator Receipt (MLIR)
      The MLIR does not expire, however, it will be superseded when the customer receives their smartcard.

    Smartcard Marine Licence Indicator (MLI)
    Smartcard Marine Licence Indicator (MLI)
      A Smartcard MLI expires 10 years from the date the application is approved.
      • A Courtesy reminder notice will be sent to the customer 42 days (6 weeks) prior to the smartcard expiry date
      • The digital certificate will require updating after 5 years to have continued access to on-line services. A notification will be sent 6 weeks prior to digital certificate expiry.
        Refer: Smartcard > Replacement Smartcard Reasons

    Driver licence smartcard with MLI or its receipt
    Driver licence smartcard with MLI or its receipt
      The expiry date for the driver licence smartcard with an MLI is the same as the expiry date for the driver licence.
Keeping the MLIThe holder of a marine licence is required to keep the marine licence indicator unless TMR directs the person to destroy the MLI because it has been superseded.

It is an offence not to comply with a notice to destroy a superseded MLI.
When required to be returned
Return of a Marine Licence Indicator
Return of a Marine Licence Indicator
    A confirmation slip is to be issued to the customer when a smartcard is returned.

    When MLI is not required to be returned
    When MLI is not required to be returned
      A marine licence indicator is not required to be returned if the recreational marine licence is suspended or an appeal has been lodged against a disqualification.

    Return
    Return
      A driver licence with MLI or Smartcard MLI must be returned when:
      • A Recreational Marine Licence is Cancelled;
      • A Recreational Marine Licence is Surrendered;
      • A condition is added or removed that changes the face of the smartcard;
      • Details on the face of the card change.

      Unless the person has a reasonable excuse.

      Reasonable excuse for not returning the physical card
      Reasonable excuse for not returning the physical card
        If the customer does not have the physical card to return they must complete a declaration stating the card was:
        • Destroyed;
        • Lost;
        • Stolen; or
        • Expired (for surrender of Marine licence only).

        Situations where a declaration is not required
        Situations where a declaration is not required
          The department does not require a declaration for the following situations:
          • The Manager's approval (for reissue of a Smartcard after an invalidation where a digital certificate has been revoked or a smartcard is physically destroyed)

    Cancelled Marine Licence - DL smartcard with MLI
    Cancelled Marine Licence - DL smartcard with MLI
      If the Marine licence is cancelled the MLI code must be removed from the driver licence. The driver licence must be returned within 7 days of the cancellation. The customer must apply for a replacement driver licence using form F3006.
      • No fee applies in this situation.

    Cancelled Marine Licence - Smartcard MLI
    Cancelled Marine Licence - Smartcard MLI
      If a Marine licence is cancelled the Smartcard MLI is to be returned within 7 days of the cancellation.

      If the Marine licence holder held both an RMDL and a PWCL and continues hold one of them after the cancellation, the customer may apply for a replacement Smartcard MLI using the Replacement Marine Licence Indicator (F4817) form.
      • No fee applies in this situation.

    Surrendered a Recreational Marine Licence
    Surrendered a Recreational Marine Licence
      A Smartcard MLI or driver licence Smartcard with MLI must be returned when a customer surrenders their Recreational Marine Licence, even if the smartcard has expired.

      A Marine Licence can be surrendered at any time for any of the following reasons:
      • Voluntary surrender by the customer;
      • On behalf of the deceased;
      • Merged;
      • Multi-licence holder.

    MLI to be returned for amendment or correction
    MLI to be returned for amendment or correction
      Customer request
      Customer request
        The holder of a marine licence may apply to correct or amend their MLI. They must return their MLI, unless it has been destroyed, lost or stolen.

      TMR request
      TMR request
        If TMR requires a correction or an amendment of a MLI, the holder will be asked, by written notice, to return the MLI. The holder will be given at least 30 days to return the MLI.

        Notice:
        Incorrect info on ML - TMR error
        Note:If a customer surrenders their marine licence due to Cancellation or the result of a Show Cause notice an email should be sent to Maritime Safety Queensland (Maritime Compliance) advising of the surrender.

    Proof of MLI while Driver Licence is Suspended
    Proof of MLI while Driver Licence is Suspended
      A driver licence with an MLI can be used as proof of holding an RMDL/PWCL even when the driver licence is suspended.

    Learning, Skiing, Travelling, Conditions of use
    Learning to operate
    Learning to Operate a Ship
    Learning to Operate a Ship
      A person (no age limit applies) may learn to operate a Queensland regulated ship or PWC provided;
      • For a Queensland regulated ship, they are supervised by the holder of a RMDL
      • For a PWC, they are supervised by the holder of a PWCL
      • The holder of the RMDL or PWCL is able to take immediate control of the ship.

      Note:A person who does not hold a RMDL or PWCL cannot operate a ship while towing. For example: a skier.
    Water Skiing
    Rules for Water Skiing and Observers
    Rules for Water Skiing and Observers
      A licence holder must not operate a ship while towing, for example a skier unless:
      • They are the holder of a current RMDL or PWCL depending on ship being used to tow the person;
      • The skier is wearing a life jacket;
      • There is another person (an observer) on the vessel, who is 13 years or older watching the skier at all times.

      The observer must immediately tell the operator if:
      • There is a danger or potential danger to the skier;
      • The skier signals the observer;
      • The skier has a mishap.
    Travelling interstate or overseas
    Qld Licence Holders Travelling Interstate or Overseas
    Qld Licence Holders Travelling Interstate or Overseas
      Queensland residents travelling interstate or overseas where Recreational Marine Driver Licences or Personal Watercraft licences are required, will need to carry one of the below:
      • Marine Licence Indicator Receipt (MLIR);
      • Smartcard MLI;
      • Driver licence smartcard with MLI or its receipt;
      • Confirmation report (pre NQDL).

    Recognition of Interstate Marine Licences
    Recognition of Interstate Marine Licences
      The holder of an equivalent interstate Recreational Marine Licence may lawfully operate a Queensland regulated ship in Queensland provided;
      • Their interstate licence is current
      • They drive in accordance with any conditions on the licence
      • They are at least sixteen (16) years of age

      The holder of an equivalent interstate Personal Watercraft Licence may lawfully operate a personal watercraft in Queensland provided;
      • Their interstate licence is current
      • They drive in accordance with any conditions on the licence
      • They are at least sixteen (16) years of age

      For conversions and requirements Refer: Marine Licence > Commercial and Non-Qld Marine Licence

      It is not mandatory to transfer an interstate marine licence to a Queensland marine licence.

      If the customer's marine licence is on the same card as their driver licence, Refer: Driver Licence > 8. Open Licences > Open Licence Guide > Transfer > Driver Licence Transfers

      Interstate visitors who hold a Commercial Marine Masters Licence issued under the National Law may operate a Queensland regulated ship in Queensland waters.

    Recognition of Foreign Licences
    Recognition of Foreign Licences
      International visitors
      International visitors
        International visitors who hold a recognised current equivalent foreign licence may operate a Queensland regulated ship in Qld waters.

      Non-Australian citizens with a resident visa
      Non-Australian citizens with a resident visa
        Non-Australian citizens with a resident visa and a recognised current foreign licence may operate a Queensland regulated ship in Qld water for 3 months after either:
        • entering Australia, if they were issued a resident visa prior to entering Australia, or
        • from the date their resident visa was granted, if the visa was granted after entering Australia.

      Australian citizens
      Australian citizens
        Australian citizens who hold a recognised current equivalent foreign licence may operate a Queensland regulated ship in Qld waters for 3 months after entering Australia.

      Note:To operate a personal watercraft in Qld the foreign licence must be recognised as equivalent to a QLD PWCL.
