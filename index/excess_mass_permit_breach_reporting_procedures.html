Excess Mass Permit Breach Reporting Procedures (5216)

Procedure
Some operators do not comply with all the requirements of a permit. Inspectors checking vehicles that are travelling under permit are to adopt the following procedures:

Note:There are also Guidelines that allow certain vehicles to exceed regulation mass.

From 10 February 2014 under the National Heavy Vehicle Law, electronic copies of permits and notices will be allowed, e.g. iPad, tablet etc. must be available at the time of intercept - not downloaded after the intercept and must be readable.

A copy on an iPhone would not be acceptable.

Note: Inspectors should either photograph the device clearly showing the image or take details of the permit/notice number or both for later clarification or use as evidence.
1. Load Carrying Vehicles
1. Load Carrying Vehicles
    Inspectors intercepting a vehicle that appears to be travelling under permit, should request the driver to produce an Excess Mass Permit.
    1.1 Permit not produced
    1.1 Permit not produced
      If a permit cannot be produced, the vehicle should be weighed and a WDN submitted in the usual manner if regulation weight/s are exceeded.

      1.1.1If regulation weights are exceeded by 20% or less, the vehicle should be allowed to proceed after breach particulars have been recorded.
      i.The driver should be advised that a permit is required and should the vehicle be intercepted at a later time, they may be further BREACHED if a permit has not been obtained.
      1.1.2If regulation weights are exceeded by more than 20%, the vehicle is to be prohibited from moving until a permit is obtained. An inspector shall ensure that assistance as described in Instruction ‘Assistance to Drivers and Passengers’ is provided when a prohibition notice has been issued.

    1.2 Permit produced
    1.2 Permit produced
      If a permit is produced check the vehicle and load in relation to the details shown on the permit, in particular the maximum axle and/or axle group loads allowed, axle spacing’s and track widths.

      1.2.1If possible, weigh the vehicle and compare each axle load and/or axle group load with the maximum loads allowed on the permit. If an axle, or group of axles, is found to be overloaded then the permit is:
      i.Suspended in the case of single trip permits. Inspectors are to stamp (using log book stamp) the permit listing details, date, time, place and ID number. If permit is already stamped, the permit is "VOID" and the inspector is to take possession of the permit. You should then advise the operator to obtain a new permit. Submit original permit to your senior inspector.
      ii.Suspended in the case of period permits until such time as the load is reduced or adjusted to comply with permit maximum limits.

      Note:A "Suspended" permit is by law "NULL AND VOID" as per condition 2.3 on the back of the permit form.
      1.2.1.1Regulation weights are now applicable (in both cases), therefore breach accordingly. A special report detailing all aspects of non-compliance with permit conditions should be forwarded to your senior inspector for distribution to:
      i.
      		The Prosecutions Unit together with WDN's and PIN's;
      and
      ii.
      		Senior Engineer (Permit System Management).

2. Non Load Carrying Vehicles
2. Non Load Carrying Vehicles
    A non-load carrying vehicle is defined as a vehicle which carries no load and travels on the road at its determined GVM. Such vehicles include:
    • Mobile cranes (including rough terrain and off highway cranes);
    • Earthmoving Equipment (e.g. loaders and scrapers);
    • Drilling Rigs;
      and
    • Other specially constructed plant items (e.g. mobile crushers).

    The following procedure is to be adopted in relation to all non-load carrying vehicles:
    • Request the driver to produce an Excess Mass Permit for inspection.
    2.1 Permit not produced
    2.1 Permit not produced
      The vehicle should be weighed and breached if regulation weights are exceeded.

      2.1.1If regulation weights are exceeded by 20% or less, the vehicle should be allowed to proceed.
      Note:The driver should be advised as per 1.1.1.(i)
      2.1.2If regulation weights are exceeded by more than 20%, the vehicle is to be prohibited from moving until a permit is obtained. An inspector shall ensure that assistance as described in Instruction ‘Assistance to Drivers and Passengers’is provided when a prohibition notice has been issued.
    2.2 Permit produced
    2.2 Permit produced
      If a permit is produced, check that all conditions have been complied with. The vehicle is to be weighed, if possible, and the actual weights compared with the maximum axle and/or axle group weights as shown on the permit.

      Note:If an axle or group of axles is found to be overloaded, then, as with a load carrying vehicle, the permit is either void or suspended and regulation limits apply and the vehicle breached accordingly.

Excess Mass Permits - An Interpretation
Many items of machinery and plant cannot be moved on the State's road system within legal loads or under performance guidelines. A concession is extended to Industry, through the excess mass permit system, to enable the transport of these loads and vehicles.

Briefly, the principles of the system are:
  • Maximum legal loads (excluding concession vehicles, Road Trains and B Doubles) are 6 tonnes for a certified steer axle, 16.5 tonnes for a tandem drive with dual tyres and 20 tonnes for a tri-axle trailer - a Gross Combination Mass of 42.5 tonnes.
  • The items which cannot be moved within these load limitations require a permit, provided the load or machinery is INDIVISIBLE.
  • Indivisibility means a load which cannot be taken apart without considerable expense.
  • Dozer blades, rippers and tynes are often removed to reduce the load and restrict the effects of road damage.
  • A permit is required for movements - single trip or a period permit (usually 3 months and for a radius of operation).
  • The fee charged for the permit is to cover the administrative costs of the system - not a cost recovery for road damage.
  • Divisible items (gravel, bulk commodities, containers) are not covered. In special circumstances approval may be granted. E.g. a quarrying contractor carrying material across a road under MRD control.
  • For a load carrying vehicle, the permit is issued for the prime mover, and in the case of a period permit, includes any nominated trailers and dolly\s on attached lists.
  • The permit must be carried in the vehicle.
Vehicle Safety
Vehicle Safety
    With increased axle group loads allowed since May 89, many general transport vehicles may not be able to take advantage of the increase, due to strength limitations, particularly suspensions. To upgrade a vehicle from 41 tonnes GCM to 42.5 tonnes:
    • a manufacturer's letter is required stating the capacity of steer axle, drive axle group, GVM and GCM;
    • the vehicle must be registered to the maximum GCM, 42.5 tonnes with a certified 6 tonne steer axle, 41.9 tonnes for a 5.4 tonnes steer axle;
    • the operator must be paying the appropriate maximum registration fee;
      and
    • permits for load carrying vehicles will not be issued in excess of the manufacturer's rating of axle group or GCM - the aim is operational safety.

    Implied in these statements is the fact that single drive prime movers are excluded and a number of vehicles previously used for overload work do not have the necessary capacity - particularly in the house moving industry. Unless these vehicles can be upgraded, they will be excluded from overload use. For houses:
    • many houses are heavier than generally stated,
    • the number of axles used is insufficient,
    • the axles are positioned incorrectly,
    • overload permits are often now required (with the associated fee) whereas the same amount of clerical processing and District inspection was being done for a free letter of no objection.

    The permit system covers load carrying and non-load carrying vehicles (cranes, mobile machinery), and may be for a single trip or for a period and area of operation. In many cases the permit documents consist of a large number of pages. The purpose of these notes is to explain the requirements of the permits and to identify important areas to be checked when vehicles are intercepted, so that:
    • correct interpretation of the permit is possible,
    • delays during the check and vehicle inspection are kept to a minimum,
    • our personnel will be knowledgeable and efficient,
      and
    • perhaps the attempts by some of the transport industry to frustrate the aim of road protection are minimised.
Single Trip Permit - Load Carrying Vehicles
Single Trip Permit - Load Carrying Vehicles
    The permit consists of:
    • the permit itself - be aware there are conditions on the back of the permit,
      and
    • the attached special conditions for the approved route to be taken.

    The rear of the permit contains a list of general conditions for travel. Of particular interest is condition 2.5, concerning suspension of the permit during periods of prolonged wet weather.

    All pages attached to a permit should be marked with the permit number. It is not uncommon that attachments for the wrong permit are being offered to our Inspectors. The practice of sending single trip permits by fax means that the front and the rear of the permit are now separate sheets, and the rear sheet should have the permit number displayed.

    Special Conditions have the date of the permit, the permit number and the issue number shown. The permit conditions will be revised approximately every 4 months, and the issue number (001 in this example) will be important for prosecutions for checking the conditions in force at the time of an offence.

Permits for Cranes and Machinery
Permits for Cranes and Machinery
The single trip or period permits for cranes, concrete pumping units and drilling rigs are similar to those for load carrying vehicles with the following additions:
  • The radius of operation is chosen to be an operating area from which the crane operator can make an economic living, or it may be very limited for large cranes. The radius is marked on a map and may vary for various areas of the State.
  • The crane is initially tare weighed, and an extra tonne is added to this mass for a small amount of equipment, provided the approved permit axle limits and gross mass limits are not exceeded.
  • The registration certificate should show the original tare mass at the time of weighing as the registered tare mass.
  • The approved permit masses for travel is the registered GVM shown, and should be no more than approximately 1 tonne above the tare mass.

Cranes under permit are not allowed to tow a trailer except, where the original axle loads were too high, the crane is required to face the boom to the rear and support it on a dolly.
Mandatory Intelligent Access Program (IAP) Monitoring for Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)
Mandatory Intelligent Access Program (IAP) Monitoring for Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs)
    From 1 December 2016, TMR will implement mandatory IAP enrolment for Class 1, 2 and 3 SPVs.
    These class categories may include vehicles such as heavy mobile cranes, concrete pumps and drilling rigs*.
    A six month transition period for IAP enrolment will end on 1 June 2017.
    After this time, mandatory IAP monitoring will apply to the following SPVs operating within Queensland:
    Class 1 - SPVs which comply with TMR’s Vehicle Limits Manual (VLM) Single Trip permit requirements, that is, a vehicle with a total mass between 40t and 70t and complying with a Gross Mass Limit (GML) formula of 3L+15+/- G for all combinations of axle groups. (See over page for GML formula calculations).
    Class 2 - SPVs not complying with 3L+15+/- G that have been assessed and approved by TMR’s Bridge Construction, Maintenance and Asset Management (BCMAM) unit for operation under 48t SPV restrictions.
    These restrictions are based on the structural loading effects of a "standard" 4 axle all terrain crane operating at 12 tonnes per axle.
    Class 3 - SPVs not complying with 3L+15+/- G that have been assessed by TMR’s BCMAM unit and determined to not be suitable for operation under 48t SPV restrictions.
