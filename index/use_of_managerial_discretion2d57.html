Use of Managerial Discretion (6036)


About Managerial Discretion
What it isManagerial discretion is the ability for a manager to make a choice about whether to act or not act, to approve or not approve or approve with conditions taking into account all relevant information.
When can it be usedDiscretion can be applied only where it is lawful (ie does not contravene legislation) and as part of:
  • Fulfilling responsibilities to ensure the efficient and effective management of the Queensland Government
  • Taking action or making decisions under TMR specific Legislation
Rules for Applying Discretion
Must be LawfulThe decision made must not contravene legislation, discretion can only be applied to policy, if it is a clear legislative requirement no discretion can be applied.
Who can exercise discretionFor the purposes of this document discretion is primarily exercised by the manager. Where the manager is not on-site or available and the customer is present, discretion can be applied by the next senior officer at the location.

Evidencing DecisionsWhere the Manager has exercised their discretion, evidence of that decision should be available should the decision be revisited. When recording discretionary decisions the following information should be recorded:
  • Date of decision
  • Customer details
  • Brief outline of situation
  • Reasons for decision
  • Name of any other parties to the decision

This does not have to be lengthy documentation, but must be sufficient to make the decision defensible.

This information can be recorded in a variety of tools such as a Work Diary, electronic document (eg spreadsheet, one note notebook).

Where a transaction is to be performed on the system by a person other than the manager a notation must be made in the Office Use Only authorising the transaction and identifying the decision maker.

Note:A discretionary decision does not need to be "right" as such but it must be defensible and as a result of a reasoned process and made on the information available.
