Accessing Q-Flow and using the Service Console (7356)

When to log inOperators must log into Q-Flow before they have successfully logged into TICA. This provides SA(SM)s/supervisor's with a visual display of how many counters are staffed and the 'Functions' assigned to the counter.

Operators can check with their SASM if they have any questions about the ‘Functions’ that have been assigned.
Getting StartedBelow is a step by step guide for accessing and using the Q-Flow Web Terminal system from a workstation.

Signing into Q-Flow
Signing into Q-Flow
    Sign into Q-Flow by selecting the Q-Flow Icon on the desktop.

    On the Q-Flow Web page select 'Sign in Automatically', as shown below, this will automatically sign the operator into Q-Flow using the TMR Network connection.




    Once signed into Q-Flow, the operator will be directed to the 'Home Screen' as shown below:

Q-Flow Profile/Physical Location
Q-Flow Profile/Physical Location
    Access the assigned Q-Flow profile via the 'User' button on the left hand menu as shown below:



    A 'Current Unit' will be automatically assigned, for example: Carseldine CSC, if this is incorrect advise a SA(SM)/Manager.
    It is the SA(SM)/Manager responsibility to update the assigned ‘Current Unit’. CSO/PCSO are not able to update this themselves.

    If required, update the 'Location' and 'Function' in Q-Flow:
    • 'Location' is the physical location for example: Counter 1, Counter 2, etc.
    • 'Function' is the group of Services that you will be called to your counter for example: Fast licensing only, etc. Operators should only update the 'Function' if advised by the SA(SM/Supervisor.




    Once the 'Location' and 'Function' have been selected, click the 'Update' button
Service Console Overview
Service Console Overview
    The 'Service Console' is a software component of the Q-Flow System and is where operators will spend the majority of time. The 'Service Console' is made up of a number of panels, each of which has its own particular area of function. Operators will access the service console via the 'Tools' button on the left hand menu as shown below:

    Specifics

      'Function' will be allocated by the SA(SM)/Manager at the start of the operator’s shift. Operators will are not to change their 'Function' unless advised by a SA(SM)/Manager.

Calling Tickets
Calling Tickets
    The typical way to call a customer to the counter for service will be by clicking on the 'Next' button as shown below:



    Clicking the 'Next' button will call the next queued customer and ensures that the 'First in First Out' (FIFO) ticket is called.

    Operators have the option to select the 'Call Again' button if the customer has not arrived at your counter for service, as shown below:



    Auto Next
    Auto Next
      Q-Flow has the ability to have an operator’s tickets called to the counter automatically using 'Auto Next'. 'Auto Next' will turn on if the operator has selected the ‘Auto-Next on’ button on the top right of the Service Console. If 'Auto Next' is turned on, a customer will be called to the counter as soon as they have arrived and taken a ticket from the kiosk.

      When a case is automatically called, the operator can Classify and close the case by selecting either the ‘Close’ button or the ‘Complete’ button. This will return the page to the Service Console and when the page refreshes (approximately 15 seconds) the next case will be automatically called to the counter.
      Alternatively, a case can be Classified and the ‘Next’ button can be used to close the current case and immediately call the next available ticket.

      Once Auto-Next has been turned on, an operator must log out to turn it off. An operator must Classify the current case and select either the ‘Close’ or ‘Complete’ button, then log oout immediately when the page returns to the Service Console. The Service Console has a 15 second page refresh, therefore no new cases should be called before an operator logs out.

      Operators should check with their SASM/Manager to see if their centre will be using the ‘Auto Next’ option.

      If a centre is not using the ‘Auto Next’ functionality, the operator will need to click ‘Close’ at the end of each transaction, then ‘Next’ when ready to serve the next customer.



    Other ways of Calling tickets are:
    Other ways of Calling tickets are:
      Silent Call
      Silent Call
        A 'Silent call' means that there will be no audio or visual notification on the TV display in the CSC. This function should be used when the customer is already at a counter and the case needs to display in order to serve the customer.

      Direct Admit
      Direct Admit
        A 'Direct Admit' will create a case in Q-Flow but will not produce a physical ticket. This function should be used if a customer attends a counter and has not taken a ticket from the Q-Flow kiosk.
      Cancelling case/marking a ticket absent
      Cancelling case/marking a ticket absent
        Cancel Case
        Cancel Case
          In circumstances where tickets have been taken in error from the kiosk for example: a child has been playing with the kiosk and has taken multiple tickets, the operator can use the 'Cancel Case' button to cancel those tickets.

        Mark Absent
        Mark Absent
          In circumstances where a ticket has been called multiple times and no customer has arrived at the counter, a case can be marked as absent using the 'Absent' button.



          If a case is marked as 'Absent', it will remain in the queue for 1 hour, as shown below, before being classed as 'Abandoned' and automatically removed from the queue. If a customer arrives at the counter after their ticket has been marked as:
          • 'Absent', their case can be called and the customer served.
          • 'Abandoned', the original ticket is no longer accessible and the customer must obtain a new ticket or be served via Direct Admit.

          An ‘Absent’ ticket will be displayed in the queue as shown below.
      Serving a Customer (Software only sites)
      Serving a Customer (Software only sites)
        When using Q-Flow at a CSC that does not issue tickets to customers (software only site), the process to start a case is different.

        After operators have logged in and opened the 'User' option on the left hand menu, a 'Location' (counter number) and Function must be selected. For software only sites, the Function selected must be ‘Direct Walk’.

        When a customer approaches the counter to be served, a case must be opened. To do this, the operator must click on ‘Direct Admit’ on the right hand side of the 'Direct Walk' function listed on the Service Console, as shown below.

        The case must then be classified and closed as per the standard processes.

      Documenting a Case
      Documenting a Case
        After calling a customer to the counter the below screen will appear to document the case:


        Summary
        Summary
          The summary section will display the following details:
          • Customer Arrival Time
          • Wait Time
          • Case Status
          • Service Time


        Document and Classify
        Document and Classify
          In the 'Document and Classify' section the following will display:
          Case Subject
          Case Subject
            The case subject will be blank.
          Interaction Notes
          Interaction Notes
            Specific information about the case can be added into the 'Interaction Notes' section. Do not include any information that would identify the customer. For example, if a customer has borrowed a screwdriver, this could be added into the interaction notes prior to clicking Freeze. The person picking the case up will then have that information.

          Classifications
          Classifications
            It is mandatory to classify a case in Q-Flow before marking it as complete. The Q-Flow system will not allow the case to be closed/completed or for the operator to select next unless they have selected a 'Classification'. To serve the next customer and close the case select the 'Next' button.



            Shown below, is the Q-Flow error message when a classification has not been selected:



            For a list of Classifications Refer: Admin > 1. Administration > Q-Flow Customer Flow Management > Using Q-Flow on Counter > Q-Flow Classification Codes

        Q-Flow has the functionality to allow an operator to 'Route', 'Freeze' and 'Unfreeze' a case for certain circumstances.
        Routing a Case
        Routing a Case
          A case can be transferred to any other service queue in the same CSC (unit) by clicking on the 'Route' button. This is to be used when operators are unable to assist the customer that has presented to their counter, as they have selected the incorrect ticket. The case will be placed in the first position in the queue it has been transferred to and the status will be 'Waiting'.





        Freezing a Case
        Freezing a Case
          A case can be frozen when the customer needs to leave the counter and return at a later time that day. The customer will be placed in the queue as frozen and will not be called. To mark a customer as frozen the operator will need to click on the 'Freeze' button.




          When a case has been frozen it will display as shown below:


        Unfreezing a Case
        Unfreezing a Case
          When the customer returns to the counter the operator will need to 'Unfreeze Silent Call' the case so that they can complete their case. Any operator can 'Unfreeze' a 'Frozen' case providing it is a part of their function. To find a frozen case, change the filter to ‘Frozen’. If it is not showing, change the function to 'LWT' to see all services. The function must be changed back once the operator has finished serving the customer.



          Note: Do not select 'Unfreeze' and then 'Silent Call' separately as the customer will be returned to the queue and may be called by another staff member.

          Selecting 'Unfreeze Call' will call the customer's ticket number over the audio system.

        Dealer processingUsed to sign out and action dealer work

        TICA filing and reportsUsed to sign out and action TICA filing and reports


        To sign out of Q-Flow either click on the 'Sign Out' button at the top right hand corner of the screen or select 'User' from the left hand side menu and then click on 'Sign Out' as shown below:




        Q-Flow will prompt the operator to select a reason that describes why they are signing out of the system. Select the reason and then click the 'Sign Out' button.
        Back office work
        Back office work

          If the operator is signing out to complete back office work, for example ‘Dealer processing’, and will not be going back on counter, they will need to sign back in prior to the end of the day, and sign out again using the ‘End of day’ reason. This will ensure the time they have spent doing back of office work is captured.
