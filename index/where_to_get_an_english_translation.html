Recognised Translations of non-English Documents (2634)


What it isA recognised translation of a document that is not in English is one that has been translated by:
  • A NAATI certified/recognised translator
  • Consular officers
  • Multicultural NSW - Language Services translations
  • Department of Home Affairs free translating services, using a NAATI or professional translator.
NAATI Translator Qualification LevelsIn January 2018, NAATI transitioned to a new certification system. Translators with an existing NAATI accreditation were not required to transition to this new system and those who choose not to retained their existing qualification level. Translations from all of the below qualification levels may be accepted, provided the translator's credentials can be verified with NAATI.

Verifying NAATI certification/recognitionA translator's certification/recognition must be verified via the NAATI website (www.naati.com.au). To verify the translator, click on the Find a Translator or Interpreter link, complete all relevant fields then submit. A list of certified/recognised translators will display on the screen for you to check against.

In the instance a translator's credentials are unable to be verified using the online NAATI verification tool, or a translation is presented with a qualification level issued prior to January 2018, staff are to contact NAATI directly by telephone or email to confirm the accreditation is still valid.
Requirements for NAATI Translated DocumentsThe customer must provide the original of the translation, which should* contain a NAATI Official Stamp. Once the original has been sighted and copied, it can be returned to the customer and "original sighted" noted on the copy.

*Official NAATI translation stamps should be on the translation documents, however NAATI translated documents without a stamp may still be accepted.

Consular TranslationsTranslations provided by the applicants consulate must be:
  • On official letterhead
  • Signed by the Consul-General (if Consul-General is not available, an authorised officer may sign on behalf of the Consulate) or an Honorary Consul
  • Stamped with official consulate stamp
Multicultural NSW – Language Services TranslationsMulticultural NSW Language service translations can only be accepted if it is a translation of a foreign driver licence.

The Multicultural NSW document will bear individual Multicultural NSW stamps on translations.

To verify a translation contact Multicultural NSW
Department of Home Affairs (DHA)The Australian Government’s Department of Home Affairs (DHA) provides the Free Translating Service (FTS) through the Migration Translators. Through the FTS, eligible customers are able to get a foreign driver licence translated into English, free of charge.

Eligible customers can apply online for the FTS.

For further information, customers may contact the DHA directly via the website, using the ‘Contact us’ form or the FTS helpdesk on 1800 962 100.

DHA use NAATI accredited translators wherever possible. If a NAATI translator is not available, the documents will be translated by a professional translator from the Migration Translators’ panel and these translations can be accepted.
