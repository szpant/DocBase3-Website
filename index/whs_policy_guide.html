Camera Issues
Camera Issues
    A small number of incidents were reported recently by staff interacting with Facial Image Capture (FIC) cameras in Customer Service Centres (CSCs).

    These issues occurred when staff have reset the cameras after they have frozen during operational use.

    Investigation has deemed that the FIC devices are electrically safe and noted that the crackling/arcing noises heard when switching the FIC on and off do not represent a safety issue. The most probable cause of incidents is static electricity build up discharge through the earthed camera.

    A likely explanation for increased reports of static electricity is due to the atmospheric conditions of cool dry air that is prevalent during that time of year.

    Action to be taken
    Action to be taken
      Please report all camera freezes to the ITB Service Centre immediately after the customer has been processed.

      Recommendations:
      • Do not turn cameras off at the black box on the counter, cameras should be turned off at the power point.
      • In some CSCs, the power point may be labelled 'camera' and be found under the counter.
      • You may wish to consider evaluating the use of anti-static mats as a way of reducing the incidence of static electricity shocks when CSC operators turn the FICs on or off.
