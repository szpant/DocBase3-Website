Deceased Data Cleanse (7438)

Project NameDeceased Auto Transfer and Data Cleanse
WhenAuto Transfer (18 March 2019)
Data cleanse (15 April 2019)
BackgroundOn 25 April 2015, the Not Eligible to Hold Registration (NORG) Project was implemented. As this project was not implemented retrospectively, a cleanse of customers who were recorded as deceased prior to 25 April 2015 is required. The data cleanse will be carried out at a rate of 50 records per day until completed.

To compliment the cleanse, a new enhancement is being introduced whereby if an individual's record is updated as deceased and they have jointly registered vehicle/s and or jointly owned personalised plate/s, the products will be automatically transferred to the surviving operator/owner providing certain rules are met. A new letter (Notice of Transfer of Vehicle Registration/Personalised Plates) has been developed to notify the operator/owner of the auto transfer.

The auto transfer will apply to both records cleansed and those records updated as deceased from 18 March 2019. If a customer doesn't meet the auto transfer rules, a Registration Notice Individual Restriction (NORG) letter will be issued to the estate instead.

In addition to the above, 11 customers who have personalised plates (only) which have been surrendered but the combination has not been cancelled, will be sent personalised letters (see below Supporting Information). This is because they don't have any other current/expired products and it was not appropriate to send the NORG letter when they may have thought that surrendering the product also cancelled it.
Who will be impacted and how?
Auto transfer
Auto transfer
    Customers who are the joint registered operators/owners of products that meet the auto transfer rules will no longer have to attend a CSC to have their products transferred.
Data cleanse
Data cleanse
    A NORG letter will be issued to the Estate of Deceased at their last known address advising that current/expired products need to be transferred or cancelled. Motorised scooters will no longer be included on the NORG letters.

    As these customers were updated as deceased 4+ years ago, their estate may have already been finalised. To assist with this scenario, the executor can use a new Statutory Declaration to declare that the appropriate documentation (e.g. will) is no longer available. In addition, FAQ's have been developed to assist with customer enquiries.
