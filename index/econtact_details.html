eContact Details (7033)

About eContact Details
What are they?eContact details are a customer's email address and mobile/SMS number.
What are they used for?eContact details are used to:
  • Issue an on-line access code
  • Send email reminders and notices
  • Send e-correspondence
  • Manually communicate with customers about:
    • Transactions/proceedings where further action/information is required
    • The outcome of applications or requests/enquiries (e.g. exemptions/RRRF)
How are they collected?A customers eContact details can be collected:
  • Verbally over the phone (by the call centre only)
  • In-person using details provided on a form or provided verbally by the customer
  • By the customer via an online service

Note:If a customer updates their email address details in-person, an email will be sent advising of the change to the new email address recorded. If a customer updates their email address details online, an email will be sent advising of the change to both the new and previous email addresses.
Where are they recorded?eContact details are recorded in the Customer Online Account Management user interface which is accessed via the following TICA screens:
  • Individual Customer
  • Update customer address

Organisations:
  • Standard EOI for the organisation; and
  • The person must provide their name and confirm if they are an authorised representative. These details do not need to be recorded in TICA.
When can they be updatedeContact details can be updated:
  • by the customer online (through the My Account Portal)
  • In person at a CSC
  • Over the phone
  • From a form when it is presented:
    • By the customer
    • By an authorised representative. For example where a transfer application containing eContact details is presented by a dealer authorised to conduct business on behalf of TMR.

    Acquirer email address on NOD

    Other forms
      If the customer or their authorised representative is not present, their eContact details can only be updated if they have provided EOI with the form (by post/email). If EOI has not been provided, the customer can be contacted by phone (EOI verbally confirmed) and then their eContact details updated.
Mandatory RequirementsIt is mandatory that all customers who sign up for direct debit have:
  • Their email address recorded
  • Their mobile phone number recorded
  • Signed up for eNotices and eReminders

If the customer tries to opt out of eNotices or eReminders or remove their email address or mobile number they will receive an error message. Operators will receive a similar message if they try to remove these details from the customers Customer Online Account Management (COAM) screen.

Customers must un-enrol from direct debit in order to opt out of eCorrespondence or remove their email address/mobile phone number.
Mobile phone numbersOnly mobile phone numbers can be recorded as eContact details in the Customer Online Account management user interface.

The mobile number must be recorded without any spaces, for example 0408097123.

The mobile number recorded for their eContact may differ to the number recorded as their contact details in the Individual Customer Hub. (There is no automatic update between the Individual customer hub or the Customer Online Account Management user interface.)

If the customer has two mobile phone numbers, confirm which one they would like recorded for eContact purposes in the Online Customer Account Management user interface and record the other one in the individual customer hub.

For information on what to record in the Individual Customer Hub please Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > Standards > Phone numbers

Direct debit customers

Updating eContact DetailsIn the rare circumstance that a customer's eContact details are being updated after the date the customer requested the change, care should be taken to ensure that the details have not been updated in the meantime, either by the customer or another operator.
Advising the customerRecording eContact details does not automatically enrol customers for e-Notices/e-Reminders. Operators should actively promote e-Notices/e-Reminders and sign customers up either over the phone or in person after they have completed EOI.

For further information about accessing eContact details, Refer: Admin > 1. Administration > Security > Access to Records > Customer Records > Use of eContact details
