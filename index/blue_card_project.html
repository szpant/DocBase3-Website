Blue Card Project (7501)

Project NameBlue Card
WhenMonday 31 August 2020
BackgroundThe Department of Justice and Attorney General (DJAG) Blue Card Services (BCS) team is responsible for the management of the Queensland Blue Card System and the issue of the Working with Children Card (Blue Card).

The Blue Card System forms part of a wider child protection system that aims to manage the risk of harm to children from individuals working with them in either a volunteer or paid capacity.

DJAG has partnered with TMR to improve the Queensland Blue Card system, leveraging TMRs capabilities, specifically around streamlining the application process and strengthening the identity management system.
How will this process work?From Monday, 31 August 2020, the introduction of an online service on the BCS website will allow customers to apply for and renew their Working with Children Card (Blue Card), using their TMR customer details.

The customer's information will be validated against the information stored by TMR. The information that will be validated will include; name, DOB, CRN, image set (must have three years life left on the image), phone and email address, and confirmation their record is not restricted (for example, NOAC).

Customers who successfully validate will progress through and lodge their application online with BCS and will have no direct interaction with TMR. TMR will only be providing the identity, there is no requirement for TMR to accept applications and there is no process (other than image and signature capture) in TICA to undertake.

Like the current process for High Risk Work Licence (HRWL), customers who are unable to complete validation of their identity will be able to attend a TMR CSC or third party service delivery agent (QGAP/QPS) to have a CRN created and an image set captured.

TMR will only be providing the identity, there is no requirement for TMR to accept applications and there is no process (other than image and signature capture) in TICA to undertake. The ability to capture a stand-alone image set for Blue Card has been built in TICA.

Once an application is approved, BCS will send a card production request to TICA and this request will be batched and sent with our standard card production requests. BCS are responsible for assessing all applications and ordering cards for approved applicants.

Processes for Blue Card will largely follow those for HRWL, with the exception that individuals from 10 years of age can apply for a Blue Card. Creating records for individuals under the age of 15 is a new transaction for TMR, and our current policies and procedures will apply to how we create and manage a TMR record. This include procedures for managing Evidence of Identity (EOI)
Responsibilities of TMR staffBlue Card Services are responsible for providing advice on the application for or issue, renewal, replacement or cancellation of a Blue Card.

TMR operators are responsible for:
  • Answering enquiries related to a person's TMR record only
  • Capturing image and signature set, ensuring the Blue Card Applicant reason code is selected.
  • Ensuring customer EContact details (phone and email) are up to date and accurate. This not only guarantees that the TMR record is correct but ensures that the customer's details will successfully validate through the BCS online application service.
  • Processing Remote Area Kits – Dalby CSC
  • Providing new customers, the 'new TMR customer notification – Blue Card' by email. This notification will provide the customer:
    • BCS Online Application Instructions,
    • Their TMR CRN, which they will require for the verification service
    • Their customer details in the order in which they must enter them on the BCS website and
    • BCS contact information, for any enquiries.
Templates have been established for ease of use. (emails will be sent from a no reply inbox, accessible via DocBase templates – no responses will be sent to individual offices)
Further informationTMR is responsible for providing advice and answering enquiries related to a person's TMR record only.

For enquiries relating to the Blue Card application process, please refer customers to the Blue Card Services contact centre or website. www.qld.gov.au/bluecard
