Suspected counterfeit money (notes)
Suspected counterfeit money (notes)
    CSC identifies counterfeit notes
    CSC identifies counterfeit notes
      Suspected counterfeit money (notes) must be checked against the Reserve Bank of Australia Counterfeit Detection Guide

      When performing the recommended checks, the notes should be handled as little as possible.

      If the notes meet any of the counterfeit criteria, a colour photocopy must be made for the office records and QPS immediately contacted to arrange for collection of the notes. QPS will lodge the notes with the Australian Federal Police (AFP).

      Note:The notes should be placed in a clear zip lock bag before being scanned/photocopied. They should then be securely stored until QPS arrive.

      Any relevant information about the suspect notes, such as how they came into the CSCs possession, must be documented and passed on to QPS.

      The QPS officer/s will issue a field property receipt for the notes. This must be kept for the banking and office records.

      Every effort should be made to identify the operator/s and transaction/s that resulted in the suspect notes to allow the closing of that day's banking. No action should be taken against the actual transaction until the police investigation has been completed. Where an operator / transaction (and customer) cannot be identified, any adjustment action must be taken against the receipting office.

      Explanatory text must be added to the system against any adjustments performed. Examples of the required text are below.
      Example Text
      Example Text
        Operator under:
        Suspected fraudulent notes were identified in Operators Cashbook (for example: DAHAWTH). Amount of (for example, $10.50) adjusted to operator under, pending advice from QPS. Refer to adjustment number (for example, 15200000). Include your name, CSC, phone number and date.

        Receipting office under:
        Suspected fraudulent notes were identified during banking and the the transaction and operator could not be identified. Amount of (for example, $10.50) adjusted to receipting office under, pending advice from QPS. Refer to adjustment number (for example, 15200000). Include your name, CSC, phone number and date.

    CBA identifies counterfeit notes
    CBA identifies counterfeit notes
      If the local CBA branch suspects counterfeit notes in the CSC end of day bank deposit, they will send the notes directly to the AFP. CBA will then adjust the CSC end of day banking by the full amount and send an adjustment letter to Cash and Banking confirming the amount for TMR records.

      Cash and Banking will perform a Receipting Office Under (ROOUNDERS) adjustment and advise the CSC of the suspected counterfeit notes. The CSC will be unable to take any action until the AFP conclude their investigations and advise of the outcome accordingly.

    QPS determines notes are not counterfeit
    QPS determines notes are not counterfeit
      In the event the notes are found to be genuine, they will be returned to the CSC.

      If the original under adjustment was processed against an operator, process an operator over to amend the operator cash book and the totals on the TICA collections screen.

      Explanatory text must be added in the system regarding the suspect notes and action taken, for example:
      Suspected fraudulent notes were identified in Operators Cashbook (for example: DAHAWTH) and have now been returned by QPS/AFP. Amount of (for example, $10.50) adjusted to operator over. Refer to adjustment number (for example, 15200000). Include your name, CSC, phone number and date.

      The operator under must then be offset against the operator over.

      If the original under adjustment was processed against the receipting office, the receipting office bank deposit must be adjusted to the receipting office bank deposit over account (TRBNKROO).

      Explanatory text must be added in the system regarding the suspect notes and action taken, for example:
      Suspected fraudulent notes were identified during banking and the the transaction and operator could not be identified. The notes have now been returned by QPS/AFP. Amount of (for example, $10.50) adjusted to receipting office under. Refer to adjustment number (for example, 15200000). Include your name, CSC, phone number and date.

      Cash and Banking must then be emailed with a request to offset the receipting office under against the receipting office over.

    QPS determines notes are counterfeit
    QPS determines notes are counterfeit
      In the event the notes are found to be counterfeit, they will not be returned.

      Where a customer is identified, at QPS discretion, the process for dishonoured payments must be followed.

      Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Account Management Guide > Dishonoured Payments

      Where a customer is not identified, a Loss of Property Monies (M3819) form must be completed, with copies of:
      • the scanned/photocopied suspected counterfeit notes,
      • if the operator was identified, the operator receipts report for the days takings (identifying the transactions that generated the notes if possible), and
      • the field property receipt/s from QPS

      Loss of Property Monies Form (M3819)

      The form and all supporting documentation must be lodged with the Office Manager for endorsement, then passed to the Regional Director for approval.

      Once the completed Loss of Property Monies form has been approved, the receipting office operator loss must be transferred to the operator losses general ledger (TRFROOL) account. This will enable the write off to be recognised as a loss to the department.

      Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Adjustments Guide > Types of General Account Adjustments > Operator Losses Account Adjustments for more information.

      Once the losses adjustment has been processed, forward the completed Loss of Property Monies form and all supporting documentation to TMRGeneralLedger@ssa.qld.gov.au. The money will then be written off by SSA.

      Note:For assistance with this process or for more information, please contact Manager (Finance), Financial Management.


    For the procedure for the action required when it is suspected that counterfeit notes have been received, Refer: Admin > 3. Money > The How To: Money > Money and Banking Procedures > Suspected Counterfeit Notes

When to perform Unmatch Payment
When to perform Unmatch Payment
    The Unmatch Payment action may be used to separate a payment from a particular product where an invalidation or cancellation of the receipt is not possible.

    The outcome is a credit in the customer account without the need to manually adjust any other accounts affected by the original payment.
    When to perform Unmatch Payment
    When to perform Unmatch Payment
      The Unmatch Payment may be performed:
      • Where a payment has been applied to the incorrect customer product via the online service to enable the payment to be matched to the correct product so the application can be processed (eg payment applied to incorrect Driver licence exemption), or
      • where a transaction has been incorrectly processed on a previous day (not the same day), or
      • where a payment is required to be separated from a transaction and invalidation or cancellation of the receipt is not possible (eg to credit customers account to allow reprocessing of licence due to operator/system error while customer is present)

      Certain payment types cannot be unmatched, error message will display in TICA and advise if this option is not available and an account adjustment or invalidation will need to be performed.
Cancelling a Receipt
Cancelling a Receipt
    Cancellation of a receipt will result in the payment being re-listed as a payment due.

    Unlike an invalidation no money will be returned to the customers account, it is as if the payment was never made.

    For this reason a receipt can only be cancelled the same day as the transaction was processed.

    All cancelled receipts MUST be pre-approved before the operator cancels the receipt. The approval is sought from a SASM, SPECPRO or Manager. Refer: Admin > Money > The How To: Money > Money and Banking Procedures > Cancel a Receipt

    If the customer requested a physical copy of the receipt at time of payment and it is subsequently cancelled it must be physically marked as cancelled and new receipt number to be clearly noted on receipt. When new receipt is not issued reason for not issuing is to be clearly noted. Text entered will be printed once cancellation is successful and must be included with the operators banking for that day. Once the transaction has been reprocessed the new receipt number must be written on the cancelled receipt.

    There is no requirement to print a cancelled receipt if the original receipt was not physically generated. TICA will still automatically print the cancelled receipt text but this can be printed on scrap paper and destroyed.

    Text must be recorded when cancelling the receipt and include as a minimum:
    • CRN/Registration Number
    • Product/transaction the receipt relates to eg Renew DL, purchase of modification book
    • Reason for cancellation, Operator error is not sufficient on its own, eg Operator Error - incorrect CRN **** selected correct CRN is 1234567890 transaction will be reprocessed.
    • Userid of the operator performing the transaction
    • Userid and position of approving officer.
Floats
Floats
    Receipting Office Float Control
    Receipting Office Float Control
      The Receipting Office Float Control is used to record the total of all floats (including change floats) held in a receipting office.

      There are no adjustments in TRAILS required, and floats can be allocated either:
      • To a specific counter; or
      • Allocated as a numbered float (for example float 1, float 2)
      When a monthly reconciliation is performed, the total of all office floats (including change floats) must match the Receipting Office Float total on the List of Operational Account Balances report. Refer below: Reconciling Floats

      Where possible, all floats should be issued to a counter as office floats. A float may only be issued to an individual operator where there is a genuine business need.
      In locations where a float must be issued to an individual, these floats are
      are allocated to the operator USERID from the receipting office float account

      When an individual operator returns a float, an adjustment must be performed to return the operator float to the receipting office float account crediting the receipting office and debiting the operator’s account.

      A location should not maintain more floats than 1.5 times the number of counters at which cash can be receipted.
    Reconciling Floats
    Reconciling Floats
      The management of cash floats is governed by s8.3 of the Financial Management Practice Manual. This document requires the monthly reconciliation to be conducted by the custodians manager which for the purposes of Customer Service Centres is the custodians direct line manager and includes SASM's.

      All used floats must be reconciled daily by either:
      • The user of the float on that day; or
      • The person to who the float is allocated on a continual basis.

      Evidence of the reconciliation must be recorded on the Record of Float Control
      (F1383). All fully completed sheets should be retained with the End of Day Banking Documents or collated at the end of each month.

      Retention of Completed Sheets:
      • Electronically stored in DMS. Refer: Admin > Filing and Business Exceptions > Electronic Storage of Documents

      All used and unused floats must be reconciled monthly by:
      • The custodians manager - must be independent, that is not a person who last reconciled any float; and
      • Reconciled against the Operational Account Balance - Receipting Office Float Control.

      Evidence of the reconciliation can be recorded on the Monthly Banking Reconciliation spreadsheet or noted on the Record of Float control.

      Retention of Completed Sheets:
      • Electronically stored in DMS. Refer: Admin > Filing and Business Exceptions > Electronic Storage of Documents

    Eftpos Only (Cashless) Counters
    Eftpos Only (Cashless) Counters
      In 2014, TMR trialled the implementation of EFTPOS only (cashless) counters in a number of CSCs across the state. The aim of the trial was to reduce the time and effort expended in the end of day banking process.

      An analysis of the trial revealed benefits in the implementation of cashless counters, seen in a reduction in banking time in the majority of the CSCs that participated. As a result, CSCs are encouraged to consider implementing cashless counters, where viable to do so.

      In considering the implementation of this process, the following should be taken into account:
      • the demographic of the area (if the majority of customers pay with cash, cashless counters may increase wait times and have a negative impact on the customer experience, therefore it would not be recommended. Data on payment methods in CSCs can be obtained from the Intelligence and Data Analysis Team)
      • the number of operational counters in the CSC (cashless counters are recommended for CSCs that operate with 4 or more counters)
      • the number of counters that should be made cashless (it is recommended that at least 60% of the counters in the CSC should be made cashless to achieve a reduction in banking time, for example, where a CSC has 8 counters, at least 4 or 5 counters should be made cashless to see a benefit)

      Approval must be sought from the Product Centre Manager of the region to implement this process.

    Write-Offs
    Who can write-off public money/property
    Who can write-off public money/property
      The authority to write off losses (including writing off bad debts) is as follows:
      • Write-off loss of public property - less than $5000:
        • Director (or above)
        • Operations Manager (specific to RoadTek)
      • Write-off loss of public property - $5000 or more:
        • General Manager (or above)
      • Write-off loss of public moneys - less than $500:
        • Director (or above)
        • Operations Manager (specific to RoadTek)
      • Write-off loss of public moneys - $500 or more:
        • General Manager (or above)

      For more information regarding write-off of money Refer: Admin > 3. Money > Adjustment Guide > Banking Adjustments > Rules

      Motor Vehicle Plate Inventory Losses
      Motor Vehicle Plate Inventory Losses
        All losses relating to Motor Vehicle plate inventory need to be journaled as outlined below.

        To write plates off the following journal is to be prepared and posted to SAP by the business unit.

        DR57420Loss of Property and Monies
        CR53026Rego Plates Issued

        The above accounting entry reallocates the expense from a rego plate issued to a loss.

        For more information regarding write-off of number plates Refer:Admin>4. Stock Control > The How To: Stock Control > Stock Control Procedures > Write off Plate Inventory

        To distinguish between a loss reportable in SAP and a loss reportable to Financial Accounting, please refer to the Losses Fact Sheet
