Refund of Licence Fee (357)

Refund of Licence Fee
EligibilityA refund of the unexpired portion of paid licence term fee may be given for:
  • Surrender under TORUM-DL Reg, s31 due to medical reasons
    or
  • Cancelled under TORUM-DL Reg, s33(6) due to medical reasons
    or
  • Deceased licence holders (proof that the customer is deceased must be presented)

Note:The deceased customer’s CRN should be checked for registered vehicles or TMR products that have been issued, e.g. industry authority or APA card. If necessary, advise the representative of the deceased that these products should be surrendered to TMR.

Note:Customers who have transferred an interstate licence to Queensland are eligible for a refund of the unexpired portion of their Queensland licence (if they meet another eligibility requirement for a refund).

Note:If a customer is unable to surrender the smartcard (lost, stolen or destroyed) they are still eligible for a refund if all other requirements are met. The smartcard is still required to be surrendered in TICA using the appropriate reason.

Applying for Refund
On behalf of a deceased person
On behalf of a deceased person
    To obtain a refund the application must be made in writing and provide:
    • Licensee's details
    • Name and address of person requesting refund
    • Copy of Licence Surrender Receipt
    • Original or certified copy of an acceptable Proof of Death document.

    Representatives of the deceased can use the below template to surrender the licence.

    Licence Refund Request - March 2021

    Once the surrender is processed, a pro-rata refund of unexpired portion of licence fee will be credited to the customer account. The amount will be refunded by cheque to the "Estate of <deceased>" as they are not able to be refunded by EFTPOS.

    If issuing the cheque to the "Estate Of" causes difficulties for the customer (e.g. unable to be cashed), an SCCC/SASM can approve for the executor/administrator/beneficiary/next of kin to submit a written request stating what the difficulty is and include (if applicable) that they are entitled to the refund under the will (if one exists) or the Succession Act 1981. If the refund has:
    • not yet been issued, the funds can be adjusted to the executor/administrator/beneficiary/next of kin's account and the refund issued (by EFTPOS if applicable).
    • already been issued to the 'estate of' and the executor/administrator/beneficiary/next of kin:
      • requests the cheque be reissued without the prefix 'Estate of', the operator can send a request to Trails Unclaimed Cheques (Cash and Banking) to have a cheque in lieu produced without the prefix (a written request from the beneficiary/executor/next of kin is not required).
      • has a CRN, the operator can send a request to Trails Unclaimed Cheques (Cash and Banking) to have a stop put on the cheque and the funds banked into the deceased customer's TICA account. The funds can then be adjusted to the executor/administrator/beneficiary/next of kin's account and the refund issued (by EFTPOS if applicable)
      • does not have a CRN, the operator can send a request to Trails Unclaimed Cheques (Cash and Banking) to have a cheque in lieu issued to the executor/administrator/beneficiary/next of kin.
Medical Reasons
Medical Reasons
    When requirements for the refund of a driver licence fee are met, TICA will automatically credit the customer’s account with the pro-rata refund amount.
    In person
    In person
      A customer who attends a CSC must receive a refund by EFTPOS (if they meet EFTPOS refund criteria - see below) or Cheque.

      To be eligible they must return their driver licence and provide either of the following:
      • written notice that they are surrendering their licence on medical grounds,
      • show as medically cancelled in TICA.

      Written notice can include:
      • a medical certificate (F3712);
        or
      • a completed Medical Condition Notification (F4355);
        or
      • any other form of written notice from the customer or a health professional (at the Manager's discretion).

      EFTPOS refunds
      EFTPOS refunds
        To receive a refund for a licence surrendered or cancelled on medical grounds by EFTPOS/Credit card the following requirements must be met:
        • The transaction must have been processed at a centre that is approved to issue registration cancellation or concession pro-rata refunds by EFTPOS/Credit Card*
        • An electronic refund can only be granted to an individual
        • The licence holder must be present
        • The EFTPOS/Credit Card must have the customer signature, name and financial institution displayed. If these details are not displayed the customer must supply a confirmation letter from the bank.
        • Refunds cannot be split over more than one card**

        *Note:If you are unsure whether your centre is approved to process these refunds please consult with your SA(SM)

        **Note:The CBA enforces that EFTPOS/Credit card refunds will only be issued to the registered operator. The value of the refund may only be returned to one card and not be split over multiple cards

      In writing
      In writing
        Customers eligible for a refund may apply in writing and will receive their refund by cheque.

        To be eligible for a refund they must provide a copy of the Licence Surrender Receipt and either of the following:
        • written notice that they are surrendering their licence on medical grounds,
        • show as medically cancelled in TICA.

        Written notice can include:
        • a medical certificate (F3712);
          or
        • a completed Medical Condition Notification (F4355);
          or
        • any other form of written notice from the customer or a health professional (at the Manager's discretion).

        Note:It is an offence to provide false or misleading information to TMR

        Licence Refund Request - March 2021

    Refunds by Cheque
    Refunds by Cheque
      The refund may be made by cheque. The cheque will take at least 15 working days to reach the recipient.
