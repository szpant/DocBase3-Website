Evidence of Residency (5018)


About Evidence of Residency
What it isEvidence of Residency (EOR) provides proof to TMR that the person is a resident of Qld.
Why requiredA person is not eligible for the issue of a Queensland driver licence unless the person is a resident of Queensland.
T.R.C.Transaction Requirements Checklists
When RequiredOnce a customer has EOR recorded in TICA they will not need to supply further EOR documentation unless it is determined that they have moved away from Queensland and returned (eg. Interstate or Overseas licence transfer.)

To determine if EOR is required, check the EOR column in the Transaction Requirements Checklist.
Address on Document differs to TICA - Replacement Lic.
Address on Document differs to TICA - Replacement Lic.
    Evidence of residency is required when applying for a replacement licence.

    If the supplied residency address differs to the address on TICA the customer must be able to tell the CSC operator the address recorded on TICA.

    If they can confirm the address on TICA then the new address should be updated on TICA and the replacement licence issued.
Evidence of Residency Documents
Features Documents must containDocuments must:
  • Be originals only*^
  • Identify the issuing organisation
  • Clearly display customer's name
  • Contain a current Qld residential address which forms part of the document eg. pension card

Note:Address added to another part of the document are not acceptable eg. address on back of birth certificate

*Printed internet/electronic bank statements, bills and BPay View documents (for electricity, gas, telephone and local government rates) are considered originals for this purpose.
^Digital Concession Cards presented on a smart device using the Australian Government's Centrelink Express mobile app are considered to be originals for this purpose.

            Australian Defence Force
              The statement or confirmation (electronic copy is acceptable) must:
              • Be on the Defence Housing Australia's letterhead
              • Provide the following information about the defence force member, or an eligible family member of a defence force member:
              • Full name
              • Residential address
              • Entitlement to accommodation, which is paid out of the defence force member's salary.

              Note:The defence force member, or eligible family member of a defence force member, must show a current Australian Defence Force photo identification card.

              A defence force member is a person on full-time permanent service with the Australian Defence Force; or a person on full-time duty with the Australian Defence Force Reserve.
              An eligible family member, of a defence force member, means a person who, resides with the defence force member and is entitled to be transferred with the defence force member at the expense of the Australian Defence Force.
