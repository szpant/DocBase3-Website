Dealer Registration Schemes/Arrangements (3001)


Off-Line
Off-Line
    What is it?This is an arrangement where a dealer (not participating in a Rego Easy Scheme or business client direct debit arrangements) lodges documentation to TMR on behalf of the customer. An off-line dealer:
    • Pays Monies and lodges documentation on behalf of the customer
    • Pays TMR using a cheque/cash/Eftpos
    Definitions
    Motor Vehicle
    Motor Vehicle
      The Motor Dealers and Chattel Auctioneers Act 2014 defines a motor vehicle as:
      (1)a vehicle that moves on wheels and is propelled by a motor that forms part of the vehicle, whether or not the vehicle is capable of being operated or used in a normal way, and includes a caravan.
      (2)Motor vehicle does not include:
      (a)a hovercraft; or
      (b)a motorised golf buggy or;
      (c) a motorised scooter or;
      (d)a motorised wheelchair or;
      (e)a trailer, other than a caravan; or
      (f)a tractor or farm machinery; or
      (g)a vehicle designed for use exclusively in the mining industry; or
      (h)a vehicle designed for use exclusively on a railway or tramway.
    New Motor Vehicle
    New Motor Vehicle
      A motor vehicle that has never, at any time, registered in Australia.
    Used Motor Vehicle
    Used Motor Vehicle
      The Motor Dealers and Chattel Auctioneers Act 2014 defines a used motor vehicle as:
      (i) a motor vehicle that has, at any time, been licensed or registered, whether under a law of this State or of any other State or Territory; or
      (ii)a motor vehicle that, had it not been registered as mentioned in subparagraph (i) for use for demonstration or sales promotion, would have been a new motor vehicle; or
      (iii) a used imported vehicle
    Motor Dealer Licence
    Motor Dealer Licence
      A licence issued under the Motor Dealers and Chattel Auctioneers Act 2014 that allows the holder to:
      • obtain used motor vehicles for resale
      • sell used motor vehicles
      • sell used motor vehicle on consignment (on behalf of others for profit)
      • sell a leased motor vehicle to the lessee under the terms of the lease
      • obtain used motor vehicles, in parts or whole, to sell as complete units or parts
      • sell used motor vehicles as parts
      • negotiate on a persons behalf for the sale or purchase of a used motor vehicle

      Processing dealer work
      Processing dealer work
        All dealer work must be processed under the dealer's agent reference (dealer qual). Most dealer work is processed offline at a later date, however, the following transactions must be processed immediately:
        • those requested as urgent by the dealer,
        • new business transactions, and
        • renewals for vehicles about to expire.

      Documents required
      Documents required
        Dealers must complete and provide the applicable form/s for the transactions they are lodging for processing (e.g. transfer form).

        Where the dealer is signing on behalf of the customer, they must provide a letter of authority from the customer.

        All offline dealers (not on direct debit or a rego easy scheme) must also ensure payment for the transactions is included with their paperwork and placed on a Schedule of Applications for Transfers, Renewals and Pro-Rata Payments (F1309). The dealer is required to calculate the stamp duty and fees for each vehicle on the schedule.

        Note:If there is more than one vehicle on the schedule, the dealer must calculate the fees for each vehicle, then total these amounts. The total amount is then paid into their account.

        When paying monies into a dealer account, text listing all registrations on the schedule must be placed against the account.

      Office administrative requirements
      Office administrative requirements
        Administrative arrangements for the processing of dealer work vary between CSCs.

        Many offices have a dealer drop off point at the front of their dealer counter/s. This point generally contains a tray for paperwork to be placed into, and both a date stamp and cheque stamp for dealers to use when they drop off paperwork.

        All dealer work must be date stamped and any cheques stamped “Not Negotiable” by the dealer dropping off paperwork for processing within 2-3 business days.

        Dealers who require work to be processed immediately must queue in the centre.

        Faxed or emailed paperwork must be date stamped and placed at the dealer counter for processing with other dealer work.

        Where faxed or emailed paperwork is urgent, it must be date stamped and provided to the relevant operator for immediate processing.

        For transactions where original supporting documents are required (e.g. safety certificates, gas certificates etc), the dealer must then provide the original paperwork (in person or by post) to the CSC, so it can be sent as additional filing to Iron Mountain.
    Processing transactions in sequence
    Processing transactions in sequence
      All transactions (including acquisitions) must be processed in sequence. This has major implications in respect to transfer of registration and may require the invalidation and reprocessing of all related transactions.
    Dealer acquisitions
    Dealer acquisitions
      An acquisition is when a dealer acquires a vehicle from another dealership, an auction or as a trade in vehicle.

      Dealers can only acquire a vehicle through TMR if it is currently registered in Queensland.

      A cancelled vehicle or interstate registered vehicle cannot be acquired by a dealer through TMR; a new business application is required.

      Dealers must lodge their dealer acquisition form within 14 days of acquiring the vehicle and before disposal of the vehicle to another dealer/auction house or private sale.

      For dealer to dealer acquisitions, if both dealers agree, in writing by completing F5204, the disposing dealer can lodge the transfer Form F5204 (immediately after the sale, there is no need to wait 14 days). No action is required from the acquiring dealer in this circumstance.

      If the seller is a disposing dealer who has not yet acquired the vehicle, the different seller functionality can be used, however operators should ensure that:
      • The CRN of the seller/disposer of the vehicle listed on the Transfer of Registration - Vehicle/Vessel Acquisition by a Licensed Dealer F1310 form can be identified in TICA. If the seller/disposer exists as a legal entity, the transfer of registration can be processed.
      • The VIN number listed on the application form matches the information in TICA
      • The registration history is reviewed to ascertain whether the acquisition has already been processed or if a Dealer Transfer Note is required.
      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transferring the Vehicle > Different Seller Transfers
      Dealer Transfer Note
      Dealer Transfer Note
        A Dealer Transfer Note (DTN) is required when the acquisition of the vehicle has to be processed out of order. This occurs when the vehicle has been sold to another dealership and they have already processed their acquisition before TMR received the first acquisition.

        To process a DTN the vehicle's history must be checked to ensure the acquisition will be placed in the correct date order and from the correct person. The history must show the vendor as being current at the date nominated by the acquirer. The nominated date must be prior to the last acquisition date.

        If a transaction is processed out of sequence without a DTN, all related transactions must be invalidated and reprocessed in the correct order.

        Note:If a concession is valid at the time of transfer on a DTN, all transactions must be invalidated and reprocessed with the concession.

    Registration cancellation
    Registration cancellation
      When a dealer applies to cancel a registration, the plates must also be provided for the vehicle. Operators should check to ensure that the plate number matches the registration number written on the cancellation form.

      Where a dealer has purchased a vehicle without plates attached (e.g purchased from auction), the declaration on the cancellation form must be completed and signed by the dealer before the cancellation can proceed.

      When processing the cancellation, operators must ensure that the vehicle is registered to the correct dealer. If not, check to see if an acquisition has been processed/processed out of sequence. Some transactions may require previous transactions to be invalidated to allow the cancellation to be processed on the correct date.

      Once the cancellation has been processed refund any monies to the dealer.

      For more information regarding the requirements to cancel a registration, Refer: Vehicle Rego > 09. Cancellation > Cancellation > Voluntary Cancellation
    New business
    New business
      If a vehicle is being registered to a dealer, a Safety Certificate is not required and stamp duty is not payable.

      A completed Vehicle Details Inspection Sheet (F3529) is required unless a Safety Certificate is provided. If a Safety Certificate has been provided, all vehicle details can be taken from the certificate. A copy of the certificate must be included in the operator's daily filing.

      All other standard requirements must be met.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Inspections > Safety Cert > Exemptions > New Business and Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Duty > Duty Exemptions > Dealer

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 06. New Business > New Business > Transaction Requirement Checklist

      New business transactions, where the vehicle is being registered to a customer who has purchased the vehicle must meet all standard requirements.

      Note: If the customer does not have a CRN, the dealer must supply a New Customer Application (Individual/Organisation) (F3503) form, along with a copy of the customer’s EOI.

      Where the new customer is an organisation, a new Customer Application (Individual/Organisation) (F3503) form and a copy of the ASIC Certificate of Registration or Business Name Summary displaying the legal entity as the holder must be presented with the new registration application.

      If the customer does not have a Queensland Address, a Queensland Garage Address Statement (F4408) form must also be supplied.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 06. New Business > New Business > Transaction Requirements Checklist

      If the dealer is signing on behalf of the customer, they must provide a letter of authority from the customer.

      Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity > Representing (Individuals/Organisations) > What it is > Document Requirements - Representing Individual/Organisation > Registration > Dealer
    Transfer
    Transfer
      All dealer transfers (from the dealer to an individual/organisation) are processed the same way they are for standard registration transfers. Dealer transfers, however, are processed under the agent reference (dealer qual) on the date you are processing the transfer (do not change the date to the transfer date).

      All standard transfer requirements must be met.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Transaction Requirements Checklist

      If the dealer is signing on behalf of the customer, they must provide a letter of authority from the customer.

      Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Standard Evidence of Identity > Representing (Individuals/Organisations) > What it is > Document Requirements - Representing Individual/Organisation > Registration > Dealer

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer > Transfer > Failure to lodge transfer
    Renew
    Renew
      All renewals for dealers are processed the same way they are for standard registration renewals.

      The vehicle should be registered in the dealers name, unless it has been sold and renewal of the registration was agreed as part of the sale.

      Where a dealer does not have a renewal notice for the vehicle and it is not in their name (i.e. they are renewing for a customer as an agreed part of the sale), they must follow the same process as Renew Registration - In Person, not registered operator and without renewal notice

      If the request is made via fax or email, the written request must include that they have permission from the registered operator to renew the registration.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 07. Renewal > Registration Renewal

    Underpayment of Schedule
      Transactions should not be processed if the Dealer has not paid sufficient funds to meet all application costs. The dealer should be contacted for an additional payment to be made.

      If no resolution reached refer the matter to the Customer Account Management (CAM) Unit

    Dealer Ceases Trading
    Dealer Ceases Trading
      When a customer queries why a vehicle purchased from a dealership is not registered into their name and it is determined that the dealership has ceased trading. The CSC should contact the liquidators to retrieve:
      • All original documentation
      and
      • Fees paid for the transaction.

      Where this is unsuccessful:
      • Accept the customers copy of the Safety Certificate (must be valid at the disposal date)
      • Process a transfer application charging full fees
      • Have the customer complete a declaration stating:
        • Why the dealer's signature is missing
        • Any fees paid to the dealership
      • Advise the customer that they may apply for a duty refund from the Office of State Revenue for the original duty paid to the dealer.
    Dealers Selling Unregistered Vehicles
    Dealers Selling Unregistered Vehicles
      A dealer may sell an unregistered vehicle to a non dealer provided:
      • They obtain a statement from the purchaser that:
        • The vehicle is not to be used on the road whilst unregistered
        or
        • The vehicles garage address will not be in Queensland
      • The dealer keeps the statement for at least 1yr after the delivery date

    Notice of Disposal
    Notice of Disposal
      A dealer who is not part of a rego easy scheme may lodge a Notice of Disposal if they sell a vehicle to an individual/organisation and do not receive any monies for transfer fees or duty payment and advise the customer that they are responsible for the lodgement of the transfer.

      However, where the dealer has received monies from the customer, they must lodge the transfer paperwork on behalf of their customer and CANNOT lodge a Notice of Disposal.

    Direct Debit Rejections (Business and Non-Rego Easy Scheme Clients)
    Direct Debit Rejections (Business and Non-Rego Easy Scheme Clients)
      When a customer using Direct Debit has a failure that is, TMR was unable to electronically obtain the required amount from the customers bank account, Customer Account Management (CAM) Unit will receive a report or notification.

      Advice of the direct payment failure will not display in the customer TRAILS/TICA account. TRAILS/TICA will show as paid. Outstanding amounts from Direct Debit rejections sit in the customers SAP account waiting for reconciliation.

      CAM will contact either the customer directly or the relevant CSC to request for a further payment.

      Action PartyAction Required
      CSC
      • If necessary, contact customer and arrange for payment to be made (cash or Eftpos is preferable)
      • Pay money into customers account with text explaining what the payment relates to. Text must include the invoice number, which has been provided by CAM as part of the rejected payment notification and/or registration details relating to payment.
      • Contact Customer Account Management (CAM) Unit (must notify the day the money is paid)
      CAM
      • CAM monitors payment failures to ensure debts are cleared.
      TMR Accounts Receivable
      • Will reconcile credit with the customers SAP account.

Rego Easy
Rego Easy
    What is it?The Rego Easy Schemes allows approval for Licensed Motor Dealers to transact registration on behalf of TMR. A Rego Easy Scheme Agreement is a legally binding contract between a legal entity and TMR. The level of interaction is dependant on the type of scheme entered into. There is currently three schemes being Easy Pay, Plates or Business.

    Eligibility requirements for being considered for the Rego Easy Scheme include:
    • Have been a TMR customer (in business/organisation name) for at least three months.
    • Have been an Off-Line Dealer (Non-Rego Easy Scheme) for at least three months, without issues.
    • Meet all other requirements as listed in the application process.
    Benefits of Entering a Scheme
    Benefits of Entering a Scheme
      • Allows dealership to provide a faster and more complete registration service
      • Reduces or eliminates the need to do business in person with TMR
      • Ability to pay via Direct Debit

    Roles and Responsibilities
    Roles and Responsibilities
      Commercial clients wishing to participate in one of the Rego Easy service options are required to meet certain entry criteria and enter into a Deed of Agreement with the State. Company Directors are also required to execute a Deed of Guarantee.

      The Rego Easy Pay and Plates schemes are managed by the Central Operations and Support (COS), Rego Easy Team (RET) and Client Liaison Officers (CLO) from Customer Service Centres.

      The Rego Easy Business scheme involves Accredited Software Providers (ASP's) who provide system, policy and technical support to Easy Business clients.

      RET responsibilities include:
      • Manage Deed of Agreements and operationally “set up” new scheme clients
      • Engagement with scheme clients, ASP's and TMR regional staff
      • Provide first level support to ASP's
      • Provide second level support to those participating in the Easy Business dealerships

      Accredited Software Providers (ASP's) responsibilities include:
      • Providing the system interface between the Easy Business clients and TMR (Dealers do not have direct access to TRAILS/TICA)
      • First level support to the Easy Business clients
      • Creation and maintenance of customer reference numbers
      • Provide training to new scheme clients in registration requirements
      • Perform registration invalidation's within TMR guidelines

      CLO’s are responsible for:
      • Determining client eligibility to enter a Rego Easy scheme
      • Preparation of documentation for new, amendments, renewal and closure applications
      • Provide training to Rego Easy Clients (REC) in their responsibilities under the Deed of Agreement
      • Monitor the performance of REC

      Collecting Scheme Clients Performance Information
      Collecting Scheme Clients Performance Information
        CLO's review REC performance every 6 months. To inform TMR of this review, staff are required to record performance information to assist the CLO's decision about the dealer’s continued enrolment.

        Performance information about a dealer should be recorded progressively, that is, at the time of processing the dealers work (refer below for a recommended collection tool).

        For Easy Pay and Plates clients, it is essential that transaction information is collected progressively to obtain sufficient transactions on which to assess the dealers performance. While information is to be used during the performance evaluation all issues should be addressed once identified.

        Information includes but is not limited to:
        • Direct debit rejections
        • Information from CSC staff including Compliance Officers on issues they have experienced (these should be substantiated)
        • Identified gaps in the currency of their Motor Dealers Licence or ASIC’s registration
        • Complaints from customers (both written and verbal)
        • Instances of incorrect or incomplete documentation
        • Notifications by the dealer of any breaches of privacy
        • Notification by the dealer of any actual or potential conflicts of interests
        • Any infringements for the following offences:

          • Inaccessible to the public
          • Protected from:
            • Unauthorised access and removal
            • Accidental loss
            • Physical deterioration

      Direct Debit Rejections
      Direct Debit Rejections
        If an attempt to direct debit payments from the customers bank account fails, the Rego Easy Unit will receive notification from Queensland State Services (QSS) of the direct debit rejection.

        No further transactions should be processed under Direct Debit until the outstanding payment is reconciled. All transactions should be paid for by Cash, Eftpos or Cheque. Rego Easy (ESP Support) will issue the customer with a Breach Notice via email to advise a resweep of their account will automatically take place. This resweep will occur in two business days from the issue date of the Breach Notice.

        Outstanding Direct Debit rejections will not appear as a debit in the customer's account in TICA. The customer account will show as being paid in TICA.

        Transactions are processed in TICA on the date specified on the application form.

        • when registering a vehicle as a New Business transaction in the name of an individual using a Safety Certificate that was issued prior to registration cancellation Rego Easy clients must present paperwork to their managing CSC. As this transaction requires a TICA inspection compliance exemption rule it is unable to be processed in DAIS.
        • obtain all required documentation and ensure requirements have been met. For further information in regard to Rego Easy Scheme Dealers conducting Pre-Registrations Inspections refer to Pre-Registration Inspection (PRI).

      Dealer Ceases Trading
      Dealer Ceases Trading
        When a customer queries why a vehicle purchased from a dealership is not registered into their name and it is determined that the dealership has ceased trading. The CSC should contact the liquidators to retrieve:
        • All original documentation
          and
        • Fees paid for the transaction.

        Where this is unsuccessful:
        • Accept the customers copy of the Safety Certificate (must be valid at the disposal date)
        • Process a transfer application charging full fees
        • Have the customer completed a declaration stating:
          • Why the dealer's signature is missing
          • Any fees paid to the dealership
        • Advise the customer that they may apply for a duty refund from the Office of State Revenue for the original duty paid to the dealer.
