Nominee (4654)


What is a nominee of an Approved Inspection StationA nominee is an individual nominated by an Approved Inspection Station (AIS) approval holder and approved by TMR. A nominee is responsible for:-
  • the conduct of the AIS approval holder's business and operation of the AIS

Refer customers to the TMR website for further information about nominees.
http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/ais
Transaction Requirements
New Application
New Application
    Individuals applying to become a Nominee must:
    • Be 18 years of age of more.
    • Lodge a completed Nominee Application (F2203)
    • Pay the criminal history check fee

    Criminal History Checks
    The criminal history check must be conducted by TMR to ensure the integrity of the results.

    A criminal history check provided by the individual, (for example, a Queensland Police Service (QPS) police certificate or a third party vendor criminal history check) are not to be accepted.

    A criminal history check is valid for a period of three months.

    If the individual also submits an approved examiner and/or an AIS application, the individual only pays for one criminal history check fee and request only one criminal history check in TICA.

    Refer: Accreditation > Approved Inspection Station > The How To: Approved Inspection Station (AIS) > Approved Inspection Station (AIS) Procedures > Process New Application for a Nominee

Period of Issue
Period of Issue
    A nominee's approval ends when the individual is no longer employed by the AIS.
Exiting the AIS Scheme
Exiting the AIS Scheme
    The AIS approval holder must complete the relevant sections of AIS Scheme Change/Replacement Application (F4638)
