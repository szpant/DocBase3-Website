29/03/2021 - Customers unable to upgrade their RE (L) licence over the weekend

    Please be advised that there was an issue with the “Upgrade my motorcycle licence” online service over the weekend where the service was incorrectly requesting that a motorcycle hazard perception test be passed prior to upgrade.

    If a motorcycle learner licence holder passed their Q-Ride course and attempted to use the online service to upgrade their licence on Saturday the 27th or Sunday the 28th of March they are not required to pass the motorcycle HPT.

    If an affected customer presents at a CSC and advises that they were unable to upgrade their licence over the weekend the HPT prerequisite can be exempted by a SPECPRO operator or Help Direct and the licence can be upgraded. To exempt the prerequisite SPECPRO/Help Desk officers must lodge and approve a hazard perception test rider exemption in the “Driver Licence Applications” page in TICA.

    Any customers who have not attempted to upgrade their licence prior to today must pass the motorcycle HPT before their licence can be upgraded.

26/03/2021 - Procedure to Follow Before Signing into TICA on Monday 29 March 2021
26/03/2021 - Procedure to Follow Before Signing into TICA on Monday 29 March 2021

    Registration and Licensing Systems are conducting an application release this evening 26/03/2021. From time to time, changes impacting TICA have triggered intermittent issues with TICA post deployment.

    To avoid these issues, we advise you carry out the following procedure before logging into TICA on 29/03/2021.

    It is recommended to clear your cache (in Internet Explorer).

    To clear your cache please follow the below process:

    1. Open Internet Explorer (If you are using Windows 10, please note that it must be Internet Explorer and not Edge. To find Internet Explorer, click on the windows button in the start bar and type in Internet Explorer)

    2. Select 'Tools' from the menu line (or alt X on keyboard when you are in IE) and then 'Internet options'



    3. Choose to 'Delete' Browsing History



    4. Please uncheck the ‘Preserve Favourites website data’ box and then check the boxes as per below in the example. Once completed click ‘delete’ and this will return you to the Internet Option page and click ‘OK’.



    5. Repeat step 2 and select 'Tools' from the menu line (or alt X on keyboard when you are in IE) and then 'Internet options'



    6. Click 'Settings' button



    7. Click 'View files' button as below



    8. Upon clicking View files the windows explorer will launch with directory location on your local machine containing the temporarily cached files. Please verify the directory location looks like the below screenshot " <<logged in username>>\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows\INetCache"



    9. Select All temporary files within the above directory location. You can use your mouse to select all files or you can select one file on the right and click "Ctrl +A" combination to select all files. Once all files within the directory are selected, please click 'Delete' key on your keyboard to delete the files. You will be prompted with a Yes/No dialogue box to confirm, please verify the message looks similar to below screenshot and click 'Yes' to delete the temporary files.



    10. Close ALL IE windows, then only launch TICA

    If, after following these instructions, you are still unable to access TICA, please contact the Service Centre on 3066 2000.

22/03/2021 - Process to follow for MERGES associated to PrepL duplicate CRN’s
22/03/2021 - Process to follow for MERGES associated to PrepL duplicate CRN’s

    If a customer has created a duplicate CRN throughout the PrepL enrolment process and has since had the duplicate CRN merged, the following details are required to be forwarded through to CSO_onlinesupport@tmr.qld.gov.au to enable a Datafix to correct the record in Auth0:
    • Customers name
    • Customer’s email address used for PrepL
    • Primary CRN
    • Secondary CRN

    Future National Hazard Perception Test (NHPT) issues may arise for customers where this process has not been followed.

    Staff are reminded that if a customer has created a second CRN due to PrepL enrolment NEVER MERGE records prior to customer completing PrepL, as per DocBase instruction, Refer: Driver Licence > Testing > PrepL > Common PrepL scenarios that require operator action and Merging CRN’s > PrepL Merge Scenarios

31/03/2021 - Accepting licences shown in the Digital Licence Application
31/03/2021 - Accepting licences shown in the Digital Licence Application

    Legislation amendments supporting the introduction of the digital licence have now been approved and the following authorities, when displayed and in the digital licence application, can be accepted evidence of holding the authority:
    (a) a photo identification card under the Photo Identification Card Act 2008;
    (b) a personal watercraft licence granted under the Transport Operations (Marine Safety) Regulation 2016, section 61;
    (c) a recreational marine driver licence granted under the Transport Operations (Marine Safety) Regulation 2016, section 61;
    (d) a Queensland driver licence under the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995.

    A customer who has the Digital Licence Application can be asked to display their digital licence.

    A digital licence displayed and visually verified in the Qld Digital Licence App is evidence of holding a Qld licence. To verify the licence, look for the pulsing Queensland Government crest in the background of the document.

    At no stage should you take the phone from the customer. If you have difficulty seeing the document, ask the customer to place the phone in a position where you can see it.

    The digital document is not acceptable if the mobile device screen is cracked or damaged to the extent that the data is unreadable.

    Refer: Customer > 4. Evidence of Identity > About EOI Documents > Recording & Verifying Documents > Manually Verifying Authenticity of Documents > Document Checking Guides > Digital Documents
