Goods and Services Tax (GST) (2986)


About GST
What it isGST is a broad based tax on the supply value of most goods and services. GST is charged on each individual product/service.
Rate
Rate of GST
Rate of GST
    The current rate of GST is:
    • 10% if it is not included in the price (exclusive price)
    • 1/11th of the price if GST if already included (inclusive price)

    TMR will display inclusive prices. This rate is subject to change.
Calculating
Calculating GST
Calculating GST
    Fee Rounding
    Fee Rounding
      Rounding is calculated on the total amount of each product. The rule is to round to the nearest five (5) cents. .11c and .12c will round to .10c and .13c and .14c will round to .15c.
    Calculating of GST
    Calculating of GST
      The rounding and calculating is as follows:
      Determining GST on a Saleable product
      GST exclusive price $ 4.20
      apply 10% GST$ 0.42
      Total inclusive price $ 4.62
      Round to nearest .05c$ 4.60
      (TICA amount)

      Determining GST on a Saleable product
      GST inclusive price$ 4.60
      Divide by 11 to determine GST$ 0.41
      Subtract GST for exclusive price$ 4.19
      Round to nearest .05c$ 4.20

Claiming & Refunding
Claiming GST
Claiming GST
    Entities who:
    • Have an ABN
    • Are GST registered can claim input tax credits for GST paid.

    The refund will occur through their Business Activity Statement (BAS).
Refunding GST
Refunding GST
    When refunding, any GST paid will also be refunded.

    A tax adjustment may include the return to a supplier or a change in the agreed price.

    Products delivered by TMR cannot generally be returned for a refund or discount.

    This action will result in tax adjustment which is reflected on the BAS.

    Adjustment notes record the adjustments made to the GST attracting product. An adjustment note will be issued for all adjustments, both increasing and decreasing.

Tax Invoice
What it isA tax invoice shows the GST charged on TMR’s product and services. For CTP Insurance it is the CTP insurers product.

Tax legislation states than any taxable supply less than $1000.00 does not need to have a customers name and address.

A tax invoice is issued for each product. A Tax invoice for less than $1000.00 will need to include:
  • The words Tax Invoice
  • Date of Issue
  • ABN
  • Description of items supplies
  • Price of the taxable item
When is it Needed
When is an Invoice Needed
When is an Invoice Needed

    Tax invoices should only be requested by those customers who are entitled to claim input tax credits from the Australian Tax Office (ATO). Individuals and Organisations with an Australian Business Number (ABN) can register to claim input tax credits through the ATO.

    Tax invoices must be provided:
    • For products/services valued over $82.50 (GST inclusive)
    • Within twenty-eight days if requested by customer
    • Overnight when GST applies to a fee category (excluding CTP Tax Invoices)

    For customer products a tax invoice is automatically issued by mail if the customer has an ABN recorded.

    It will be incorporated on receipts issued over the counter for non-customer products (eg toll cards, Your Keys)

    Note:Input tax credits can still be claimed without a tax invoice for amounts less than $82.50. Tax invoices are only required as evidence for purchases above $82.50 for customers to claim input tax credits.

    CTP tax invoices
    CTP tax invoices

      CTP tax invoices are not mailed to individuals or organisations with an ACN recorded in TICA, these customers can use the "Get CTP tax invoice" online service or request a certificate be issued by a TMR operator.

      CTP tax invoices will be posted to any other organisations (such as a registered business, incorporated association etc) that do not have an ACN recorded in TICA but who have ITCE recorded against a vehicle registration.
Requesting an Invoice
Duplicates
Duplicates
    Only one (1) original tax invoice can be issued but many duplicated or reprints can be produced by TMR. Duplicates may only be obtained from the TMR Call Centre.

    A copy of the GST Tax Invoice will be stored in a customer's Customer Online Account Management (COAM) screen if the customer purchases a non-customer product (such as Your Keys) and the payment is recorded against their CRN.

    Note:Credit card surcharge tax invoices must be requested by Finance (refer to Requesting a Tax Invoice below for process).
Requesting a Tax Invoice
Requesting a Tax Invoice
    Tax invoices should only be requested by those consumers who are entitled to claim input tax credits from the Australian Tax Office (ATO).

    Note:However, if any person requests a Tax Invoice, TMR must issue one within 28 days

    Only those entities who are in possession of an ABN and are registered for GST can claim input tax credits.

    Card Surcharge Tax Invoices
    Card Surcharge Tax Invoices
      This process only applies to customers requesting a tax invoice for the GST component of a card surcharge they have paid. All other tax invoice request follow the current procedure.

      Requests for tax invoices are to be forward to trails_debit@tmr.qld.gov.au
      The following information must be provided:

      Subject line: Request for Card Surcharge Tax Invoice

      Content:
      • Customer name
      • Address
      • Contact Number
      • Receipt number
      • Transaction details

    Australian Business Number (ABN)
    Australian Business Number (ABN)
      An ABN is a single business identifier.

      It is available to:
      • All companies registered under the Corporations Law
      • Government
      • Other business Entities
      • Charitable, community service organisations
      • Institutions
      • Individuals
