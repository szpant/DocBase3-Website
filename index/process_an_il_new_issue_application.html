    • Driver/Rider Trainer
    • Pilot/Escort Vehicle Driver
    • Tow Truck Driver/Assistant

    • Traffic Controller

Conduct 1:1 Image Match (if required)
Conduct 1:1 Image Match (if required)
    Conduct 1:1 Image Match
    Related TICA Steps
    Capturing facial image
    Capturing facial image
      If TICA prompts to capture the customer's facial image, direct the customer to
      the seat for the default camera and request them to:
      • Sit on the seat provided and to stay within the markings
        Note: If customer uses a mobility aid, retract or remove the chair and have the customer position themselves within the designated markings of the photo area
      • Look straight ahead at the camera (a frontal image of their face is required)
      • Remove any head attire or sunglasses (unless there are valid religious, cultural or medical reasons for not doing so, then refer to Manager)
        .
        The customer must remain in your line of vision during the entire transaction.
        .
      • Once the facial image is successfully captured, request the customer to return to your counter.
    1:1 image match fails
    1:1 image match fails
      If the 1:1 image match fails contact the Identity Management Unit for an assessment of the image

      If IMU advises that the image match is unacceptable:
      • IMU will advise what action is to be taken
        Refer: Smartcard facial image comparison

        End of procedure
      .
      If IMU advises that the image match is acceptable:
        Continue with procedure

Applying under Mutual Recognition
Applying under Mutual Recognition

    Relevant valid interstate Dangerous Goods Driver Licence, Pilot/Escort Vehicle Driver and Driver/Rider Trainer can be recognised and transferred to the equivalent Queensland accreditation.

    Applicants applying under mutual recognition must complete a Equivalent Occupation Registration Notice (F2762).

    Refer: Industry Authority > 2. Dangerous Goods > Dangerous Goods Driver Licence Guide
    Refer: Industry Authority > 4. Driver and Rider Trainers > Driver/Rider Trainer Guide
    Refer: Industry Authority > 5. Pilots and Escorts > Pilot / Escort Licence Guide

Conduct written Pilot Driver Acceditation test (if required)
Conduct written Pilot Driver Acceditation test (if required)
    Give customer
    • Give the applicant a copy of the knowledge test and an answer sheet
    • Explain test requirements
    Mark test
    • Compare the answers with the Examination Answer Sheet
    • Circle any incorrect answers and write the correct answer beside it (use a different colour pen)
    • Calculate the result (Pass = 23/25)
    • Explain any incorrect answers
10
Give/Advise customer
Give/Advise customer
    Give customer
    • Their original EOI/competency documents
    • Financial Receipt
    • Confirmation Slip
    • Customer portion of Medical Certificate (if applicable)
    Advise customer
    • Searches are required and it may take up to 6 weeks for the customer to be advised of the outcome of the application
    • Customer may be contacted to come back into the CSC,
    • If the application is approved and they reside in a:
      • Regional or remote area - an Interim Industry Authority (IIA) will be sent via their preferred contact method (fax, email or mail)
      • City area - an IIA will only be sent if requested
    • They can log in and check the progress of their application via their TMR Online Account Portal. Upon approval, if they would like their IIA emailed to them and do not reside in a regional or remote area, they can contact the TMR Call Centre.
    • All applicants will receive their smartcard which will be mailed to their postal address, or if none recorded, their residential address.
11
Forward to Industry Licensing Hub
Forward to Industry Licensing Hub
    Scan all sides of the completed Lodgement Checklist, Application form and all supporting documentation to your CSC mailbox or your CSC Industry Mailbox, and email to the Industry Licensing Hub

    The subject line of the email must contain the following information:
    • Customer CRN
    • Customer Name
    • Type of licence/authority being applied for
    • Lodging CSC

    For example: “123456789, John Smith – Dangerous Goods Driver Licence application, Macgregor CSC”.

    Note:The receipt number must be written on the application form.
