Heavy Vehicle Registration Assessment Scheme (HVRAS) Guide (7280)


Who is a HVRAS Accredited PersonA HVRAS accredited person is an individual who is accredited to inspect and measure vehicles of more than 4.5 tonnes gross vehicle mass (GVM) and trailers of more than 750kg aggregate trailer mass (ATM) prior to vehicle registration.
The objective of the scheme is to ensure unregistered heavy vehicles and trailers comply with:
  • Dimensional limitations; and
  • Approved load sharing axle systems guidelines; and
  • Safe tyre limits; and
  • Vehicle identifiers (for example: engine number, vehicle identification number (VIN) and chassis number).

A new HVRAS Accredited Person applicant is defined as an individual who is not recorded in the HVRAS spreadsheet.

HVRAS accreditation may be approved for up to 3 years.

There is no regulated fee for the inspection of vehicles under the HVRAS scheme.

Refer customers to the TMR website for further information regarding the scheme:
www.tmr.qld.gov.au/hvras
New Application
Application for approval as a HVRAS Accredited Person
Application for approval as a HVRAS Accredited Person
    Applicants who wish to become a HVRAS Accredited Person must:
    • Lodge the completed HVRAS Application (F4331) with the regional HVRAS Hub
    • Pay the training course fee
    • Provide standard EOI to the Training Officer on the first day of their training course
    • Successfully complete the TMR HVRAS training course.

    The regional HVRAS hub will post the completed applications after each training course to the HVRAS Administrator.

    The HVRAS Administrator will email/post the approved accreditation document to the customer.
Renewal
Renewing HVRAS Accreditation
Renewing HVRAS Accreditation
    HVRAS Accredited Persons can email their completed HVRAS Application (F4331) to the HVRAS Administrator. No renewal fees apply.

    The HVRAS Administrator will email/post the approved accreditation document to the customer.

    The HVRAS Administrator will send a HVRAS renewal reminder notice to the customer 30 days prior to their accreditation expiry date.

    Early renewal
    Early renewal
      Where there are exceptional circumstances (i.e. the person will not be able to action their renewal notice as they are travelling interstate or overseas) the HVRAS accredited person may request to renew their accreditation up to three months prior to their expiry date and without having received their renewal notice.

      The applicant must email their request for an early renewal to the HVRAS Administrator and provide details of their exceptional circumstances for consideration.
Expired/Lapsed HVRAS Accredited Person
Expired/Lapsed HVRAS Accredited Person Policy
Expired/Lapsed HVRAS Accredited Person Policy
    From 1 December 2020, if a HVRAS Accreditation has expired for a period of 90 days or more, the Accreditation will lapse.

    If a HVRAS Accreditation lapses, a new application must be made. Lapsed HVRAS Accredited Persons can email or post their completed HVRAS Application (F4331) to the HVRAS Administrator.

    No application fees apply and lapsed HVRAS Accredited Persons do not need to complete the HVRAS training course again.

    The HVRAS Administrator will email/post the approved accreditation document to the customer.

Conditions of HVRAS AccreditationHVRAS accredited persons must follow the administrative requirements contained in the “HVRAS Business Rules”.

HVRAS accredited persons are expected to conduct inspections in accordance with “The Guide to Completing Inspection Documents”.

Further information can be found on the TMR website at www.tmr.qld.gov.au/hvras

Imported Vehicles
Imported Vehicles
    Imported vehicles that have not be previously registered in Australia cannot be inspected and measured by HVRAS Accredited Persons.
