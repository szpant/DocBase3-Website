Vehicles Registered Overseas (temporarily in Queensland) (7239)

RequirementsA vehicle which is registered overseas can be temporarily used in Queensland by anyone with a current driver licence without an overseas vehicle permit, provided certain requirements are met.

The overseas visitor needs to ensure the correct approvals are obtained prior to bringing the vehicle to Queensland and abide by the rules and requirements outlined below:
Overseas vehicle being used for holiday
Overseas vehicle being used for holiday
    For a vehicle that is registered overseas and is brought to Australia to use on a holiday, the customer must obtain Carnet de Passages en Douane issued by the motoring association in the country of origin. Or a document of the same nature as a Carnet de Passages en Douane relating to the presence in Australia of a vehicle registered in another country.
Foreign defence personnel on exchange
Foreign defence personnel on exchange
    Foreign defence personal who are in Australia on exchange with Australian Forces are allowed to bring their overseas vehicle with them for personal use under the Status of Forces Agreement (SOFA).

    To bring a vehicle to Australia under this agreement, the defence member must obtain a vehicle import approval issued under SOFA, issued by the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development.
Documentation to be purchased/carried
Documentation to be purchased/carried
    CTP insurance must be purchased prior to the vehicle being driven.

    The vehicle operator is required to carry the following documentation:
    • Proof of current overseas registration
    • Carnet de Passage, or Import Approval issued under the Status of Forces Agreement
    • Current driver licence
    • QLD CTP insurance certificate
Left hand drive vehicles over 4.5t GVM
Left hand drive vehicles over 4.5t GVM
    If the vehicle is over 4.5t GVM and has its steering controls on the left side of the vehicle, a left hand drive permit must be carried. Left hand drive permits for heavy vehicles are issued by the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR).
Vehicle requirements
Vehicle requirements
    The vehicle must have:
    • Working headlights
    • Working tail lights, including stop and turn lights
    • Working windscreen wipers
    • Working seat belts (if fitted).
Driving an overseas registered vehicle in Queensland
Driving an overseas registered vehicle in Queensland
    In addition to the above rules, a vehicle registered overseas can only be operated on Queensland roads if:
    • The vehicle’s overseas registration plates and registration labels are attached to the vehicle if they are required by law in the country of origin.
    • The overseas registration is current.
    • The vehicle’s operator has a current driver licence.
Registering the vehicle in Queensland
Registering the vehicle in Queensland
    If the visitor decides to register the vehicle in Queensland, they need to apply to the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development (DIRD) for a personal import approval. TMR cannot register the vehicle until an import approval has been issued by DIRD.

    Refer: Imported Vehicles

    All import and registration requirements must be met.
Disposing of the vehicle
Disposing of the vehicle
    A temporary overseas vehicle cannot be sold in Australia and must be exported. The vehicle may be dismantled for parts only if the relevant Authority to Import/Carnet de Passage allows for the vehicle to be dismantled. The customer will need to contact DIRD for approval to dismantle, otherwise the vehicle must be exported.
