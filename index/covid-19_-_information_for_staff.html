Customer Contact Centre – Replace Driver Licence with a Condition Change
Customer Contact Centre – Replace Driver Licence with a Condition Change
    The Customer Contact Centre (CCC) are now delegated to process a replacement driver licence where a condition change has occurred. This includes adding and removing an S condition, and replacing a licence when an X1, X3 or Interlock condition has elapsed. Prior to ordering a replacement licence where the X1, X3 or interlock period has elapsed, staff must ensure the condition is no longer showing on the customer’s record in TICA. A new driver licence receipt must be printed and sent to the customer (mail or email). At this stage, processing is limited to SCCC's and any enquiry received by a consultant will be escalated via the standard process to an SCCC.

    From Saturday 18 April 2020 the online driver licence renewal service will be amended to allow P, P1, P2 or Open licence holders which are current (within 6 weeks prior to expiry) or expired by up to 5 years to be able to renew online.

Shelf Life of Digital Photos - Extended
Shelf Life of Digital Photos - Extended
    From Wednesday 8 April 2020, the shelf life of a digital photo will be extended from 10 years to 11 years.

    The 11 year shelf life may be extended by a maximum of 6 months, making the total maximum shelf life of an image 11 years and 6 months.

    This change will allow customers who have an expiring image set to renew their licence online for another 12 month period if they can’t attend a centre to have their image captured.

Extension of Overseas Driver Licences
    As a result of COVID-19, some international licensing authorities are offering their customers an extension of their driver licence.

    Current countries known to be offering an extension include:
    • Italy

Temporary Vehicle/Trailers Inspection Procedure
Temporary Vehicle/Trailers Inspection Procedure

    In response to the current health emergency, TMR has reviewed legislation, policies, procedures and systems to manage the risk to staff.
    This procedure is to be followed by Customer Service Staff conducting Pre-registration Inspections (PRI) for Vehicles and Trailers and will remain valid until otherwise advised.

    Additional PPE/Actions
    • Customer to be asked screening questions on entry to CSC.
    • Practice social distancing.

    • CSC staff are not to enter any vehicle during the pre-registration inspection.
    • To check the compliance plate/build plate information the CSC staff to ask the customer to open doors/bonnet to check details. If access to the vehicle is necessary to check details on the plates ask the customer to read out the plate details from the vehicle.

    • All equipment should still be sanitised before and after use.

Restricted LGAs Essential Services
    To align with the Queensland Health Directive, from Monday 9 August 2021, TMR CSC and QGAP team members in a lockdown area (Refer: Restrictions for Locked Down Areas – South East Queensland | Health and wellbeing | Queensland Government (www.qld.gov.au), will have the authority to refuse service to a customer for a transaction that is not essential.

Authority to Ask Customer Screening Questions
Authority to Ask Customer Screening Questions
    The establishment of the COVID-19 customer screening process is just one of the measures put in place to contribute to a significant reduction in risk to our staff. Since the screening questions were introduced, some customers have enquired about the authority of TMR staff in asking these screening questions. If a customer enquires about the authority of a TMR staff member to ask screening questions upon entry to a centre, they can be advised these questions are required as part of TMR's workplace health and safety plan to manage and minimise the risk of transmission of COVID-19 in its customer service centres. Customers can also be advised under the Workplace Health and Safety Act and Regulations, TMR has an obligation to provide a safe working environment for all staff of, and visitors/customers to its workplaces. Failure to answer the questions may cause employees to feel the health or safety of themselves, or others may be at risk and as such, they have the right to refuse service to any visitors/customers for this reason. Should visitors/customers refuse a request to leave a customer service centre after failing to answer the questions, staff can contact the police to assist.

    TMR Legal Advice
    TMR Legal Advice
      Like all workplaces in Queensland, under the Workplace Health and Safety Act and Regulations, the Department of Transport and Main Roads has an obligation to provide a safe working environment for all staff of, and visitors/customers to, its workplaces. As part of TMR’s workplace health and safety plan to manage and minimise the risk of transmission of coronavirus (which leads to the development of the infectious disease COVID-19) in its customer service centres, TMR asks all visitors/customers a set of questions about their possible exposure to the coronavirus. Failure to answer the questions may cause TMR employees to feel that the health or safety of themselves, or others, may be at risk. TMR employees have the right to refuse service to any visitors/customers where they feel that the health or safety of themselves, or others, may be at risk. Should visitors/customers refuse a request to leave a customer service centre, after failing to answers the questions, staff can contact the police to assist.

Remote TICA Access
Remote TICA Access
    • Accessing sensitive information, from a non-TMR environment heightens the departments information security risk. As such, all TMR staff accessing TICA remotely (for example personal residences) must comply with the Access Control Policy - Remote Access to TICA. For CSB, this includes:
      • Vulnerable CSC operators
      • All other CSB employees working remotely, who have previously been approved to use TICA from a TMR location.

      Note:At this time Transport Inspectors (TIs) are exempt from the Remote Access to TICA policy and work is in progress to provide provisions relevant to their roles. However, TIs must ensure that all equipment is secure, locked when not in use and customer records are not visible to non-authorised individuals.

    • Staff are only allowed to access TICA remotely if all of the requirements detailed in the policy have been complied with.

    • Before accessing TICA remotely, staff must:
      • read the Access Control Policy - Remote Access to TICA
      • login to their laptop at their usual TMR location and ensure the TICA application is downloaded

    • Filing and Business Exceptions will be sent to the user's usual location and will need to be actioned by the supervisor in that location.

    • All staff working remotely are reminded of their obligations to comply with the Access Control Policy - Remote Access to TICA.

    If an exemption is required for any reasons outlined in section 6 of the policy, staff must speak with their Manager before contacting Manager (Systems Security) Land Transport Safety and Regulation.

NSW Driver Knowledge Test
NSW Driver Knowledge Test
    There are no provisions in Queensland legislation that allow TMR to provide a written road rules test to a person whose licence is suspended in another jurisdiction.
    Customers who are unable to return to NSW must be advised to contact Transport for NSW to discuss the matter. TMR cannot intervene in another jurisdictions' policy and processes.
    Therefore, the Qld written road rules test cannot be issued for the purpose of removing a licence suspension applied in NSW. Likewise, an interstate written test cannot be sent to TMR for the customer to take in Qld.
