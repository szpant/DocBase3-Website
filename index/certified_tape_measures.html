Certified Tape Measures (7434)

Certified Tape Measures
What it isTransport Inspectors are required to measure length, width and height using a tape measure that meets or betters the accuracy levels of EU Class III. To ensure the tape measure meets the minimum requirement it must be certified.
Deviation Specifications
Specifications for maximum deviation
Specifications for maximum deviation

    The specifications for maximum deviation is provided from the European Committee standards. The three classes according to their level of accuracy is below.

    EC Class I: they are the most accurate tape measures on the market. They tend to be slightly more expensive than the average DIY tool but are perfect for professional work where precision is not something to compromise on. The maximum error expected in class I tapes is of 1.1 mm in 10 meters – 0,001 % of margin.

    EC Class II: slightly less accurate than class one, but still significantly more precise than the average commercial tape and suitable for all uses and pockets. The maximum error expected over 10 meters total length is 2.30 millimetres – slightly more than the 0.002%.

    EC Class III: the least accurate on the scale, therefore they might be preferred where a difference of a few millimetres does not make a difference on the overall result and does not impact the stability or the safety of the project. The maximum margin of error on a 10 metres long measure is 4.60 millimetres – 0.004 % difference.

Managing Tapes
Certified tape
Certified tape
    Each compliance office is provided with a certified JIS class 1 tape, these tapes are individually certified and are equivalent to the EU Class I measurement standard. Each certified Tape has a Measuring Tape Test Certificate.
    The Cert No. from the Measuring Tape Test certificate must be engraved onto the JIS certified tape.
Assessing tapes used operationally
Assessing tapes used operationally
    All tapes used operationally must be assessed against the certified tape annually. The assessment should test at intervals 2.5mtrs/ 12.5mtrs /19mtrs/ 22mtrs/ 26mtrs. Variations should be reviewed against the Deviation of tape under test information on the JIS tape certificate. Any concerns about the accuracy of the tape should be escalated to the regional compliance manager.
    Calibration area
    Calibration area
    • The STI responsible for each office must set up safe work area suitable for calibrating tape measures.
    • The area must be flat and free of debris.
    • Using a post or fixed object as the anchor point, mark the area at each end using the certified tape.
    • If a post is not available drill a hole in the concrete and use steel pin or bolt as the anchor point.
    • Ensure the anchor point does not introduce a trip hazard to the work area.
    Calibration procedure
    Calibration procedure
    • Inspect the area to ensure it is free of loose debris.
    • Attach the operational and certified tape to the anchor point.
    • Unroll both tapes to the maximum required measurement of 26 metres and ensure they are both flat on the surface.
    • Ensure both tapes are taught during the assessment.
    • Note any variation between the certified and the operational tape on the Tape Register.
    • Complete all relevant fields on the Tape Measure Register.
    • If the operational tape is outside the maximum variation specification the tape measure is not to be used for compliance activities.

Managing tapes
Managing tapes
    All operational tapes used by the office must be engraved with a unique identifier using the following format:
    • Region (SEQS, SEQN, CENT, NTH, STH)
    • First 3 letters of office name
    • Number - must be unique for each tape in the office

    For example a tape at Toowoomba would be STHTOO(number), at Darra it would be SEQSDAR(number)

    Each office must maintain the attached Tape Measure Register template.


    Tape Measure Calibration Register template - May 2019

    The certificate for the certified tape and the Tape Measure Register must be DMSd so it can be retrieved if required for court proceedings.
Recording tape identifier for dimension offences
Unique Tape Identifier
Unique Tape Identifier
    The unique tape identifier must be recorded on Briefs to Adjudicator and PINs related to dimension offences.
    Brief to Adjudicator
    Brief to Adjudicator
    Record unique tape identifier on the Brief to Adjudicator and include the statement 'The measure tape used by the Transport Inspector has been inspected and is identified as accurate'.
    PINs
    PINs
    Record the unique tape identifier in the QCIS notes/conversations section or on the back of the pink copy of manually issued PINs.
