Imported Vehicles (3043)


What it isAn imported vehicle is any vehicle that has been brought into Australia from another country.
Requirements
Authority to Import
Authority to Import
    Prior to importing a vehicle, a customer must apply to the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development and Communications (DITRDC) for approval.

    All applications and approvals are managed via the Commonwealth's ROVER system.
Obtaining and Import Approval
Obtaining and Import Approval
    Prior to importing a vehicle, the customer should contact the Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Regional Development and Communications for requirements.
Customer does not have original or copy of Import Approval
Customer does not have original or copy of Import Approval
    If the vehicle was imported:
    • after 1/7/2021, the import details can be verified on the RAV
    • before 1/7/2021, and they cannot provide the original or copy of the Import Approval (for example: the person was not the original importer), the person can contact DITRDC and may request a letter to confirm details of the import approval issued.

    The letter from DITRDC will confirm details of the vehicle’s import approval such as import type but will be limited due to privacy reasons.

    To confirm the authenticity you must contact DITRDC Import Team for verification.

    Sample Letter:
    Vehicle Import Approval - Sample Letter

    Note:If DITRDC has issued a letter to the customer advising that there is no record of Import Approval for the vehicle, the customer may still be able to register the vehicle.
    Refer: Types of Imports > Vehicles Manufactured prior to 1969 > Registration Requirements or Vehicles Manufactured from 1969 > Registration Requirements.
Imported Vehicles that cannot be registered in Queensland
Imported Vehicles that cannot be registered in Queensland
    A vehicle presented for registration with any of the following conditions recorded on the Import papers CANNOT be registered:
    • Imported for Exhibition purposes
    • Import for Non-transport purposes
    • Imported as Racing Vehicle
    • Incomplete Vehicle Imported
    • Conditional Dismantle
    • Import Refused
    • Not for use on Public roads
    • Vehicle in Transit
    • Conditional Test Vehicle

    The Customer should be advised that TMR must comply with any restrictions contained on the Import Approval.
Imports Previously Registered In Australia
Imports Previously Registered In Australia
    Documents Required
    • Proof of previous Australian registration
    • Safety Certificate
    • LS2 Certificate of Modification (if steering changed)
      Refer to: Specific Modifications
No Proof of Previous Registration in Australia
No Proof of Previous Registration in Australia
    If no proof of previous registration in Australia available, vehicle is to be treated as first registration after import.

Vehicles Seeking Conditional Registration
Vehicles Seeking Conditional Registration
    Vehicles that are to be registered in Queensland under the Conditional Registration Scheme do NOT require import approval as they do not meet ADR's.
Additional Requirements
Left Hand Drive Vehicles
Left Hand Drive Vehicles
    Light Motor Vehicles
    Light Motor Vehicles
      Before a vehicle with a left hand drive and is less than 25 years old can be registered in Queensland, the vehicle must be converted to right hand drive and issued an LS 1 modification code through the Approved Persons Scheme according to the Code of Practice - Light Motor Vehicles. The Find an Approved Person online service provides the contact information for current Approved Persons who have consented for TMR to release their information to the public for the purpose of vehicle modification certifications.

      Left hand drive vehicles 25 years or older with a GVM </= 4.5t can have standard registration.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 02. Registerable vehicles > Light vehicles > Left hand drive vehicles

    Heavy Motor Vehicles
    Heavy Motor Vehicles
      According to Schedule 2 Section 4(1) of the Heavy Vehicle (Vehicle Standards) National Regulation, any heavy vehicle (GVM >4.5t GVM) must be right hand drive to be eligible for full registration.

      Should a customer have:
      • a heavy vehicle; and
      • the vehicle is at least 30 years old; and
      • the operator only wishes to register the vehicle with a SIVs concession;

      the registered operator may apply for an exemption permit to remain LHD.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > Permits > 10. Permits > Permits > Left Hand Drive Vehicle Permit

      Note:If a currently registered left hand drive heavy vehicle is presented with a SIV concession, the vehicle must be converted to right hand drive prior to updating to registration.

Use of Vehicles on roads prior to first registration
Use of Vehicles on roads prior to first registration
    An imported vehicle (motor vehicle or trailer) cannot be used on a road or issued an Unregistered Vehicle Permit (UVP) prior to the vehicle demonstrating that it complies with vehicle law (mass, dimensions and the vehicle standards).

    To move an imported vehicle before it is complied, it must be transported (ie cannot be driven or towed).

    A vehicle is considered to be compliant when:
    • It has a RAV entry of Vehicle Type Approval, Concessional - RAWS, Concessional - Trailers
      or
    • the vehicle standards have been checked and a plate or certificate has been issued.

    To determine when a vehicle is considered to be compliant, to Types of Imports
