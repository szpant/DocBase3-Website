Immediate - Drink/Drug Driving
Immediate - Drink/Drug Driving
    What it isA person may have their driver licence suspended, or be disqualified from obtaining a driver licence, if the person is charged with one of the following:
    • Driving a motor vehicle when the person is over the middle or high alcohol limit(equal to or over 0.10% )
    • Failure to provide a specimen of breath, saliva or blood
    • Charged with a 2nd drink or drug driving offence while an earlier drink or drug driving offence is still to be heard
    • Operating a motor vehicle dangerously (under s328A Criminal Code) when the person was adversely affected by an intoxicating substance.
       Prescribed Alcohol Limits
    An Immediate Licence Suspension fact sheet is available on the TMR website and can be accessed from the following link:
    http://www.tmr.qld.gov.au/Licensing/Licence-suspensions-and-disqualifications.
    aspx
    Notices
    Notice of Suspension of Licence (F4394)
    Notice of Suspension of Licence (F4394)
      Queensland Police will give a Notice of Suspension or Disqualification (F4394) when the person is charged advising the person that their Qld licence is suspended or they are disqualified from obtaining a licence
    Immediate Suspension of Driver Licence Courtesy Notice
    Immediate Suspension of Driver Licence Courtesy Notice
      The Department of Transport and Main Roads will also send an Immediate Suspension of Driver Licence Courtesy Notice (F4393) to provide the licensee with information about the immediate suspension/ disqualification process.

      This notice is for the person's information only and the suspension/disqualification remains in force even if the notice is not received.
    Sanction PeriodThe immediate suspension or disqualification begins immediately when the person is charged and remains in force until the charge is dealt with in court.

    While the suspension/disqualification remains in force until the court hearing, the person may apply for and be granted permission to continue to drive until the court hearing. Refer: Application to Continue to Drive below
    Application to Continue to Drive
    What is it
    What is it
      A person immediately suspended for drink or drug driving may apply for a section 79E order to continue to drive until the matter is heard in court. If granted, the person is required to be issued with a replacement licence with an X4 condition code. The licence authorises the holder to drive only in the circumstances stated on the court order.
    Eligibility Criteria
    Eligibility Criteria
      The person must:
      • Hold a Qld Open Licence that was current at the time when charged
      • Apply only for the same class as on their suspended open licence
    Ineligibility to Apply
    Ineligibility to Apply
      A person is not eligible to apply for a s79E court order if any of the following apply:
      1.They have a previous drink or drug driving offence that has not been dealt with by a court, withdrawn or otherwise discontinued
      2.Within the previous five years:
      • Their Qld driver licence has been suspended or cancelled or they have been disqualified (Does not include the immediate licence suspension for the relevant drink or drug driving related offence)
      • They have been convicted of a drink or drug driving offence anywhere in Australia or Overseas
      • They have been convicted of a dangerous driving offence in Qld
      • Their authority to drive in Qld under a non-Qld licence had been suspended
      • They were ineligible to apply for a Qld licence because they had exceeded their demerit points threshold or been convicted of a high speed offence
      3.The suspension charge is for an offence committed when any of the following applied to the person:
      • They were engaged in an activity directly connected with their earning their livelihood
      • Drove a motor vehicle they were not authorised to drive
      • Were the holder of a Restricted licence
      4.The offence was committed when the licensee was required to observe a no alcohol limit
      5.The licensee did not hold an open licence at the time of the offence
      6.The licensee was driving under an agreement to be of good driving behaviour (GDB) for 1 year
      7.The licensee had previously been given a notice to choose between a licence suspension for a stated period or a GDB but they had not made their choice.

      Note: Eligibility not affected
      A person's eligibility is not affected by:
      • An immediate suspension as the result of a middle range alcohol offence
      • A suspension because they failed to appear in court to answer a charge of drink or drug driving
      • The original drink or drug driving suspension under s79B(2)
      • A 24 hour suspension for a drink or drug driving related offence
      • A suspension or cancellation that was set aside after a reconsideration of the Decision of the Chief Executive
      • A suspension, cancellation or disqualification that was set aside on appeal
      • A suspension or cancellation because of a medical condition
      • A suspension for unpaid fines
    Grounds for Application
    Grounds for Application
      The person needs to satisfy the court of all of the following:
      • They are a fit and proper person to continue to drive, having regard to the safety of other road users and the public generally
      • If the application is granted, there would not be an unacceptable risk of the person committing a further drink or drug driving offence
      • A refusal to grant the application would:
        • Cause extreme hardship to the person, or the person's family by depriving them of their means of earning a living and/or
        • Otherwise cause severe and unusual hardship for the person or their family other than depriving the person of their means of earning a living eg. they are the only one who can drive an invalid relative to receive regular and necessary medical treatment
    Time Limits
    Time Limits
      The licensee must apply within 21 clear days from the start of the Suspension to lodge the application.
    How to Apply
    How to Apply
      FormPerson completes Section 79E Order Application (F4395) obtained from the Magistrates Court or TMR
      Lodge (Court)The form must be lodged at the Magistrates Court either in the district where the person resides or where the charge was laid:
      • With a copy of their Notice of Suspension or Disqualification (F4394)
      • Together with any supporting documents (e.g. affidavit)
      • Within 21 clear days after the suspension starts
        Note:The 21 Clear days excludes the day the person is charged
      Lodge (QPS)A copy of the stamped application together with the supporting documents must be served to the nearest QPS office
      SuspensionLodgement does not stay the suspension. The suspension remains in force until the court hearing.
      DecisionIf the court grants the application a s79E order is issued stating the conditions in which the person can drive. The person must return to TMR to get a replacement licence.
      Licence IssueAn application to replace the licence is made and a fee is charged.

      The replacement licence will be issued with an X4 condition code.
    Recording the Application
    Recording the Application
      Lodgement
      The Magistrates Court will forward a copy of the lodged application to the Prosecution Unit within TMR for recording on TICA.

      Address for Prosecution Unit
      Address for Prosecution Unit
        Prosecutions Unit (Asst Prosecution Officer-Licence Appeal Section)


      Applications may also be lodged or updated on TICA by TMR/licence issuing agency operators if required (e.g. not recorded)

      Result
      The result of the court hearing will be recorded by QPS operators. If the s79E order is granted TMR may also record the result and issue the replacement licence.
High Speed
High Speed
    What it isA person found driving more than 40km/h over the speed limit may be issued an infringement notice of which one of the penalties is:
    Qld Licence Holder (not on s79E order)A 6 month suspension
    Non-Qld Licence Holder (not on s79E order)Authority to drive in Queensland suspended for 6 months
    Unlicensed (not on s79E order)6 month period of ineligibility to apply for a Qld driver licence
    On s79E orderA minimum 6 month suspension or disqualification
    or
    If the original drink or drug driving charge has not been decided by a court during this 6 mth period, the suspension or disqualification will continue until the charge has been dealt with by a court
    Notices
    Written Notices
    Written Notices
      A person who has a finalised offence of driving more than 40km/h over the speed limit must be given written notice stating:
      For an unlicensed person
      • That they are not eligible to hold a Qld licence for a period of 6 months from the date of the Notice of Ineligibility for Driver Licence for Speeding Offence - Not the Holder of a Driver Licence (F4107)TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s88

      For a Qld licence holder
      • That the holder is suspended from driving for a period of 6 months from a date stated in the Notice of Driver Licence Suspension for Speeding Offence (F4105) TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s86
      • Accumulation of Demerit Points Notice to Choose (F4011) may also be sent if the 6 points allocated for the HSS exceed customer's demerit point limit. Refer: Driver Licence > 3. Enforcement > Demerit Point Sanctions

      For a s79E licence holder
      • That the holder is suspended from driving for a minimum period of least 6 months from a date stated in the Driver Licence Suspension for Speeding Offence while driving under a Section 79E Order (F4410)TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s102
    Notice sent by Ordinary Mail
    Notice sent by Ordinary Mail
      The legislated notice is to be sent by ordinary mail, if the customer has a:
      • Postal address - the original notice will be sent to the postal address. No ‘duplicate’ notice will be sent to the residential address.
      • Residential address - the original notice will be sent to the residential address
    Returned Notices/Re-issue of Notice

    Note: Unlike demerit points notices, further speed suspension notices will be sent regardless of existing outstanding notices (status of PEND (Pending), RTND (Returned)), or any outstanding appeals.
    Sanction Start DateFor Qld licence holders the notify action can be taken immediately after the finalisation of the infringement.
    A HSS can be served concurrently with a Demerit point suspension that can be chosen if option to choose has been received.

    For unlicensed persons, the sanction starts the day the Notice is issued. TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s88(1)(a)

    For Non-Qld licence holders (including s79E orders) the sanction starts on the date stated in the Notice. The suspension cannot be started before this date. TO(RUM-DL)Reg 2010 s87(1)(a) and s102(4)

SPER
SPER
    What it isThe State Penalties Enforcement Act 1999, allows State Penalties Enforcement Registry (SPER) to suspend driver licences for unpaid fines resulting from all types of offences, not just motor vehicle related offences.

    The licence is suspended by electronic advice to TMR and the suspension lifted by SPER upon payment of the fines.

    Customers who have an eContact recorded will be sent an email advising them when their licence has been suspended and for further information to contact SPER.
    EnquiriesAny enquiries received regarding a SPER suspended licence should be directed to:

    State Penalties Enforcement Registry (SPER)

    SPER Call Centre Business Hours
    • Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm
    • Not open Saturdays
Passenger Transport D/Licence Sanctions
Passenger Transport D/Licence Sanctions
    What it isAny person who commits three or more relevant driver offences within three years may be issued with a driving sanction under S91ZJ of the Transport Operations (Passenger Transport) Act 1994 (TOPTA).
    The sanction term is one month for a first sanction and three months for a second or further sanction.

    Qld Licence Holder 1 or 3 months driver licence suspension.
    Driver licence from another jurisdiction1 or 3 months removal of the authority to drive on QLD roads.
    Non licence holdersNot eligible to apply for a QLD driver licence for 1 or 3 months.

    Notices
    Written Notices
    Written Notices
      Courtesy Notice for a Qld licence holder (F5215- Courtesy Notice – Driving Sanction for Passenger Transport Offences)
      After a person commits two relevant driver offences within three years, they will receive a courtesy notice advising that their driver licence may be suspended if they commit further relevant offences.

      Sanction notice for a Qld licence holder (F5216 - Notice of Driver Licence Suspension for Passenger Transport Offences
      This notice will advise the person that their driver licence will be suspended for a future dated period. One month for a first sanction and three months for a second or further sanction.

      Courtesy notice for a non-licence holder or non-Queensland driver licence (Form - TBC)
      After a person commits two relevant driver offences within three years, they will receive a courtesy notice advising that their authority to drive on QLD roads or apply for a Qld driver licence will be removed if they commit further relevant offences.

      Sanction notice for a non-licence holder or non-Queensland licence holder (Form – TBC)
      The person is advised that they are not able to apply for a QLD driver licence or drive on QLD roads for a period of 1 or 3 months.

    Notices sent by ordinary mail
    Notices sent by ordinary mail
      Notices are sent by ordinary mail if the customer has a:
      • Postal address - the original notice will be sent to the postal address. No ‘duplicate’ notice will be sent to the residential address.
      • Residential address – If no postal address, the original notice will be sent to the residential address.
    TICA/TRAILS codes and informationThe Qld driver licence suspension is actioned in TICA via a show cause process using the reason of “SUSP PT GROUNDS”. Following the suspension period, the person’s driver licence is reinstated without any further action required.

    If a person's QLD driver licence is suspended on PT grounds, the printed Traffic History and Licence History TICA screen will show "Suspended PT Grounds" for the defined period.

    For unlicensed persons and non-Qld licence holders, a text restriction must be added to their customer record stating that the person is ineligible to apply for a QLD driver licence, or to drive on QLD roads.

    As there is no system automation for the PT sanction, all notices and TICA suspensions are manually actioned by TransLink staff and nominated CSB staff.
    No Review of decision availableA customer cannot apply to the chief executive for reconsideration or apply to the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal for a review of a driver licence sanction under TOPTA.
