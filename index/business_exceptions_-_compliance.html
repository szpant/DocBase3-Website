Business Exceptions - Compliance (7251)



Business Exceptions - Compliance
What it isTICA records a business exception when an operator processes a transaction that has been assigned a risk rating or the transaction overrides a pre-set business rule.

Business exceptions are assigned a high, medium or low risk rating. Business exceptions with a high or medium risk rating are reported in TICA and must be authorised by an independent officer. Low risk business exceptions are logged by the system and automatically authorised.



List of Business Exceptions
List of Business Exceptions
    Business Exceptions June 2019
Who can Authorise
Who can Authorise
    TICA does not permit an officer to authorise their own business exceptions.

    The authorising officer may be a Senior Transport Inspector or a Transport Inspector. An STI may allow a Compliance Administration Officer to authorise BEs if they have sufficient experience.
Authorising Business Exceptions
Authorising Business Exceptions
    Business exceptions must be reviewed and cleared daily. Any follow-up action must be initiated within 7 days of the exemption being created.

    If an issue is identified, the Business Exception must be left unauthorised until the issue is fully resolved.

    A text must be added to the BE to explain why it has not been authorised.

Roles and responsibilitiesProcessing Officer
To ensure supporting documentation is collated and available to the checking officer.

Authorising Officer
  • Check TICA Business Exceptions according to the Checking Business Exceptions Guidelines below
  • Authorise approved BEs
  • Add text to the BE if follow-up action is required before authorisation
  • Authorise BEs when the follow-up actions are completed.
Checking Business ExceptionsWhen reviewing BEs the following must be checked before a BE can be authorised.
Does the transaction comply with policy
Does the transaction comply with policy

    The purpose of authorising a business exception is to ensure that the transaction has been processed in line with legislation and relevant policy.

    Confirm the action is in accordance with TMR policy. IF the BE relates to exempting a requirement, confirm the exemption reason is valid.
Has the transaction been processed correctly in TICA
Has the transaction been processed correctly in TICA

    Check TICA to ensure the action has been correctly processed
Documentation
Documentation

    Check the supplied documentation to ensure it satisfies requirements including:
    Correct documents have been provided, includes forms and supporting documentation:
    1. Documents provide the required information
    2. Documents have been correctly completed
    3. Documents, including supporting documents, are current, according to TMR policy

Checking Compliance Business Exceptions Guidelines
Follow Up ActionsErrors that are identified when reviewing business exceptions should be followed up and a corrective action should be taken. If the error is due to the incorrect exemption of a fee, the correct fee must be recovered from the customer.

Refer: Transport Compliance > Administration > The How To: Compliance Administration > Administration Procedures - Compliance > Follow up incorrect exemption of booking fee

Where follow up action is required, text must be added to the business exception to record details of the issue and the follow up action to be taken.

How To add text to a BE
How To add text to a BE
    • Highlight the required Business exception from the list.
    • Click the Edit menu item.
    • Type details into the Edit Business Exception dialog box.
    • Click OK to save the details

The customer can be contacted by phone to advise of any correction actions that are required of them. If the customer does not follow the requirements or is not contactable by phone a letter should be sent.
