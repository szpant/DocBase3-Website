Late Night Driving Restriction (4885)


Night Driving Restriction
What it isA Late Night Driving Restriction (LNDR) prohibits a person from driving between the hours of 11pm and 5am.
Applies toA LNDR will be imposed if a person commits an offence/s that results in:
  • a licence suspension for the accumulation of excessive demerit points
  • a period of good driving behaviour being imposed
  • a high speed suspension; and
  • at the time of the triggering offence the person was under 25; and:
    • held a P, P1 or P2 type licence; or
    • did not hold a valid driver licence and was not eligible for an open licence

A LNDR will also be imposed if a person is disqualified from holding or obtaining a Qld driver licence and at the time of committing the offence they were under 25; and:
  • held a P, P1, P2 type or open licence; or
  • did not hold a valid driver licence.

Note:A late night driving restriction does not apply to a person whose only valid driver licence is a learner licence.
RestrictionThe person must not drive between 11pm and 5am for 1 year from the relevant date unless the person is carrying and driving in accordance with a:
  • current Certificate of Exemption; or
  • valid Restricted licence order;
  • valid Special Hardship Order

The exemption certificate must be produced if requested by a police officer.
TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s72(6)

NoticesA notice will be sent to the licence holder to advise:
  • that they are subject to a LNDR
  • the period of the restriction
  • when the restriction starts

Notices are sent:
  • via mail contractor only
  • as ordinary mail
  • to the customer's postal address (if provided)

Notices
Notices
    F4451 - Late Night Driving Restriction after Suspension - Confirmation Notice
    • If the licence will be current at the expiration of the suspension the notice is sent 14 days prior to expiry of the suspension period
    • If the licence expired during the suspension period the notice is sent within 48 hours of the licence being renewed

    F4452 - Good Driving Behaviour Period and Late Night Driving Restriction - Confirmation Notice
    • Notice is sent within 48 hours of the good driving behaviour period being applied

    F4453 - Late Night Driving Restriction after Cancellation - Confirmation Notice
    (If the offence that caused the cancellation was committed before 1 January 2014)
    • Disqualification - no restricted licence issued
      • Notice sent within 48 hrs of a probationary licence being issued
    • Restricted licence issued
      • If the licence expires at the same time as the restriction the notice is sent within 48 hours of the licence being renewed
      • If the licence will be current at the expiration of the restricted period the notice is sent 14 days prior to expiry of the restricted period

    F5029 - Late Night Driving Restriction after Cancellation Notice
    (If the offence that caused the cancellation was committed on or after 1 January 2014)
    • Disqualification - no restricted licence order - Notice sent 3 weeks prior to the end of the disqualification period
    • Restricted licence order - Notice sent the day the court result is recorded
Multiple LNDRsA person can have more than one LNDR at the same time. The restrictions will run concurrently for the period they overlap.

Certificate of Exemption - Late Night Driving
    What it isA Certificate of Exemption allows a person ordinarily restricted from late night driving, to drive between 11pm and 5am during the restriction period.
    Who may ApplyA person restricted from late night driving under TO(RUM-DL)Reg. 2010 s72(1)
    Eligibility
    Eligibility Criteria
    Eligibility Criteria
      In order to grant a Certificate of Exemption, the Chief Executive must be satisfied that:
      • The person needs to drive between 11pm and 5am:
        • To, or from, the person’s place of employment
          or
        • In the course of the person’s employment
        and
      • A refusal to issue the certificate of exemption would cause severe hardship - TO(RUM-DL) Reg. 2010 s73(4)
    Information Sheet
    Information Sheet
      An Information Sheet - Application for Certificate of Exemption to drive a motor vehicle late at night (S4479) - is available on the TMR website and in the Corporate Forms database.
    Application Req'mentsThe applicant must:
    • Complete an online exemption application or a Certificate of Exemption for late night driving Application (F4468)*.
    • Pay the non-refundable application fee.
    • Provide a signed statement on letterhead from their employer supporting the application.

    * Forms are available at CSCs or on the TMR website

    Note:QPS and some agents will not accept applications or provide information on this exemption.
