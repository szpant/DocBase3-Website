Open Licence Guide (1085)


About Open Licences
Definitions
Driver Licence Definitions
What it isAn open licence is issued to a person who has completed the required period of holding a Provisional, Probationary or Restricted driver licence.
When Issued
When an Open Licence is Issued
When an Open Licence is Issued
    An Open licence is to be issued to a customers who:
    • Is at least 20
    • Has held a P, P1 or P2 licence current or expired less than 5 years
      and
    • Has held the licence for the required total period (including an equivalent Interstate, NZ or Foreign licence).
    • Transfers a current or expired less than 5 years Interstate licence equivalent to a Qld Open licence.
    • Transfers a current or expired less than 5 years Foreign licence equivalent to a Qld Open licence.
    • Holds a current 'unrestricted' Defence Force driver licence and who is currently serving in the ADF.
    • Has had a cheque payment dishonoured on an existing open licence and has a current P, P1, P2 or Open licence in the last 5 years.
    • An eligible Returning Driver or Currently holds a C(L) with an RD condition
    Note:Customers issued a Queensland licence prior to 1 January 2014 are not required to meet the minimum age requirements to progress through the GLS.

Produce Licence when Requested
Produce Licence when Requested
    Produce the driver licence (includes driver licence receipt) when requested by a Police Officer
    Police Powers & Responsibilities Act 2000 s.58
    Failing to comply with the requirement of a police officer may attract a maximum penalty of more than $4800.
    Police Powers & Responsibilities Act 2000 s.791

    The holder of a learner, provisional or probationary licence must be able to produce their licence (this includes a valid Driver Licence Receipt) upon request.

    The driver of a heavy/prescribed vehicle(refer below) must be able to produce their licence (this includes a valid Driver Licence Receipt) upon request.
    Open Licence Holders of Private Vehicle
    Open Licence Holders of Private Vehicle
      If the open licence holder of a private vehicle is unable to comply immediately with the requirement, the holder may be taken to have complied with this requirement if they produce their licence (includes driver licence receipt) within 2 business days after the requirement is made, at a place nominated by the police officer.

    Drivers of Prescribed Vehicles
    Drivers of Prescribed Vehicles
      A driver of one of the following vehicles must be able to produce their licence (this includes a valid Driver Licence Receipt) upon request:
      • a vehicle with a GVM of more than 4.5t
      • a public passenger vehicle
      • another vehicle providing services on a road for which a licence is required under a transport Act, for example, taxi, limousine, bus, tow truck, pilot vehicle
      • a vehicle transporting dangerous goods
      • for reward
      • if the amount of dangerous goods is greater than the amount prescribed under a regulation
      • a vehicle used for driver training for reward

Motorcycle/Moped Passenger
Motorcycle/Moped Passenger
    A person can only carry a passenger when riding a motorcycle on a road if they:
    • Hold a class RE open/provisional or probationary licence, which they have held for at least one year and are riding a class RE motorcycle(does not include any period where the licence was not valid); or
    • Hold a class R open licence.

    Note:A passenger is someone that sits on the pillion (behind the rider) or in a sidecar attached to the motorcycle.

    All pillion passengers must be at least eight years old and must be able to place their feet on the footrests designed for use by a pillion passenger.

    A person learning to ride a class RE or R motorcycle is NOT permitted to carry a pillion passenger. Instruction by a suitably licensed person may be given from either a sidecar attached to the motorcycle, another motorcycle, or a car following the learner rider.

    Refer: 6. Learner Licence > Learner Licence Guide > Conditions of Use > Drive Accompanied/Under Direction > Motorcycle

    Note:A moped is defined as a motorcycle. A person riding a moped under their class C P type, P1 type, P2 type or Open licence must not carry a passenger while riding the motorcycle on a road.
Learning to drive the next eligible class or another class
Learning to drive the next eligible class or another class
    An Open licence holder must comply with the same conditions of use as a learner licence holder if learning to drive a vehicle:
    • Using the authority of their Open licence to learn to drive the next eligible class (eg, learning to drive a class LR vehicle under the authority of a class C licence), or
    • Using a Learner licence (eg class RE Learner licence endorsed on a class C Open licence), or
    • Using the authority of a returning driver certificate, if they are a returning driver (Refer: Returning Drivers).
      Refer:       Learner Licence Guide

    Note:The holder of an Open licence to which s79E (X4) applies is not authorised to learn to drive the next eligible class.
