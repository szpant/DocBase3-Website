NEVDIS (3061)


What is it?The National Exchange of Vehicle and Driver Information System (NEVDIS) is a database that contains current driver licence and vehicle registration information. All states are currently on NEVDIS.
Objectives
NEVDIS Objectives
NEVDIS Objectives
    To improve road safety, vehicle theft reduction and customer service across all jurisdictions by:
    • Detecting non-compliance with vehicle standards and driver behaviour
    • Identifying stolen and written-off vehicles
    • Information exchange between jurisdictions
    • Quicker, more convenient conversion/renewal of driver licences and vehicle registrations
Information Available on NEVDIS
Registration Information Available
Registration Information Available
    Currently NEVDIS registration allows users to view the following details:
    • Registered Operator
    • Vehicle Identifiers
    • Interstate/Territory registration details (ie registration status)
    • Stolen vehicle restrictions
    • Written off vehicle restrictions
    • Interstate/Territory vehicle registration restrictions

NEVDIS Restrictions
NEVDIS Not Responding
NEVDIS Not Responding
    Applications cannot be accepted when NEVDIS is not responding.
NEVDIS Errors
Duplicate VINS
Duplicate VINS
    When processing New Business transactions, it is essential that the vehicle presented for registration can be matched against the vehicle record listed by NEVDIS. Failure to select a matching record results in a warning message being displayed in TICA:

    If OK is pressed to continue, a Duplicate VIN will be created and a Business Exception will be created that will require action.

    Before proceeding with the creation of a duplicate VIN, the operator must be satisfied that the vehicle presented for registration is definitely not any of the vehicles listed by NEVDIS.

    Selecting a Record from NEVDIS
    Selecting a Record from NEVDIS
      To assist staff in determining whether or not a record listed by NEVDIS should be selected the following clarification is provided:
      • Where a record is displayed on the Search NEVDIS Vehicle page and the VIN, engine, colour, Mth/Yr etc. match the details of the vehicle that has been presented, this record must be selected. 
      •  In situations where the latest plate number displayed on the Search NEVDIS Vehicle page does not match the plate number that the customer has advised, the operator should still be looking at the vehicle details (i.e. VIN Make/Model etc. and make the selection based upon these details). 
      • Where more than one record is displayed on the Search NEVDIS Vehicle page, the operator, before making a selection from this list, must first   determine which is the most recent registration record and base their selection on this.

    Vehicles (Stamped with a VIN)
    Vehicles (Stamped with a VIN)
      If the vehicle is not a new vehicle and the VIN does not decode 
      • Contact Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU) for advice

        Note:VIU will attempt to decode the VIN

      If VIU cannot decode the VIN, they will advise the CSO to either: 
      • Email import papers, supporting documents and VIN problem sheet (below) to Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU) (imports only);
      OR 
      • Check the VIN on the vehicle 
      • Record the details on the VIN Problem Sheet (below) and the VIU operator's name for authorisation
      • Email a VIN problem sheet and supporting documents to Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU).

    If the vehicle is stamped with a chassis number (vehicles manufactured pre 1989)
    If the vehicle is stamped with a chassis number (vehicles manufactured pre 1989)
      If Vehicle is stamped with a CHASSIS NUMBER and the chassis number is not on the vehicle register: 
      • Search all registers - including ROLS by both chassis number and engine number
      • If found, proceed with registration.
      • If a record is found on the system but is recorded differently to the paperwork/receipt, or the Safety Certificate, update the record with the details of the Safety Certificate and proceed with registration.
      • If vehicle is exempt from a safety certificate the registered operator must conduct a PRI and complete a Vehicle Details Inspection Sheet (F3529).

Note:Ensure that the vehicle being registering is not an IMPORT.
