Written-off Vehicles (3053)


What is a Written-off vehicle?A written-of vehicle is a vehicle that has been damaged to the point that repairing it would cost more than the market value of the vehicle.
Why was the written-off vehicle scheme introduced?The written-off vehicle scheme was a national project to help reduce professional vehicle theft.

The scheme was first introduced in Queensland on 30 September 2002, and was updated on 14 December 2012.
Checking if a vehicle is written-off
Internal
Internal
    When displaying a written-off vehicle a TICA dialogue box will display advising that the vehicle has been recorded as either a repairable or statutory write-off.
External
External
    The Personal Property Security Register (PPSR) allows consumers to obtain information about a vehicles:
    • Registration details
    • Collateral details (who hold a financial interest and how to contact this person)
    • History Summary outlining details of any amendments to the registration, this includes whether a vehicle has been recorded on the Written-off Vehicle Register and information about the incident and vehicle damage.

    There is a fee payable for this service. Customer should be referred to the PPSR Website for more information.

    Note:The PPSR replaced the previous VCheck and Written-off Vehicle Certificate systems that were offered by TMR.

Road use requirements for a Written-off VehicleA vehicle that is recorded in the Written-Off vehicle Register (WOVR) must not be driven on the road without approval from the chief executive, unless being driven to obtain a:
  • Safety Certificate/COI Certificate (including road testing that is part of the inspection)
  • Written-off Vehicle Inspection
  • Gas certificate
  • Weighbridge certificate
  • QPS inspection for a surrogate VIN
  • Surrogate identification number stamped on the vehicle
