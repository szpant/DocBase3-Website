19/02/2021 - Agency Cards: HRWL, Blue Card & Disability Worker Screening Card
19/02/2021 - Agency Cards: HRWL, Blue Card & Disability Worker Screening Card

    Staff are reminded to follow TMR's standard processes and procedures when creating customer records for agency cards (HRWL, Blue Card & Disability Worker Screening Card)

    New customers need to complete a New Customer Application (F3503) if it is the first time the customer is conducting business with TMR and there is no other product/transaction required.

    All customers must have standard EOI and evidence of their residential address recorded in TICA before progressing with the image set. Please also ensure eContact details (both phone and email) are being recorded for every customer. Without eContacts, sufficient EOI including address and a valid image, customers will not be able to verify successfully online via the agency website.

    Please note, it is common for customers to require both a Blue Card and a Disability Worker Screening Card. Only one image reason is to be selected when capturing their image. Any time a customer image is captured by TMR (TMR product or agency card) their image is allocated the same image shelf life. This image can then be used for any product with TMR, including an agency card.

    For more information, please refer to the relevant DocBase pages located under Services > 2. Qld Government Partnerships.


    Raechelle Persich
    Senior Project Advisor
    (Identity and Card Management Services)



17/02/2021 - Possible Rebirthing of Toyota Landcruiser’s - Updated
17/02/2021 - Possible Rebirthing of Toyota Landcruiser’s - Updated

    QPS have advised that they have begun experiencing a noted trend in the theft of (or attempted theft of) Toyota Landcruiser’s from holding yards/businesses in North Brisbane.

    At this stage the activity has only been identified in North Brisbane, however it is unknown where vehicles may be taken for rebirthing.

    It is advised that any Landcruiser’s presented for registration for the first time in Qld (other than new vehicles) be inspected to ensure the stamped VIN number matches the compliance plate.

    If the stamped VIN number does not match the compliance plate, please ensure you follow the current process in DocBase for surrogate VINs. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Identifiers and Descriptors > Surrogates

    For all enquires please email policyproceduressupport@tmr.qld.gov.au

05/02/2021 - National Hazard Perception Test
05/02/2021 - National Hazard Perception Test

    From Monday 29 March 2021, all learner licence and existing P1 licence holders will be required to complete the new National Hazard Perception Test.

    The new test will require all:
    • Learner licence holders to pass the hazard perception test before progressing to the next licence phase, regardless of whether this is a provisional or open licence
    • Existing P1 licence holders (who have not completed the hazard perception test at the learner phase) to complete the test before they progress to their P2 or open licence.
    • Motorcycle learner licence holders to complete the test before they progress to the next licence phase.

    Further information can be found in the Project Update.

04/02/2021 - Defective Work Diaries
04/02/2021 - Defective Work Diaries

    It appears work diaries in the range QBI8540 – QBI9999 and QBJ0000 - QBJ3539 have an issue with writing on the top page not duplicating on subsequent pages.

    If you have stocks of these diaries please put them aside in secure storage and order new stock. Do not destroy the diaries as we may need to return them to the printer.

    If these are the only diaries you have in stock you may consider getting some diaries from other centres until your new stock arrives.

    Other batches may be affected so please check diaries when issuing. If you identify faulty books outside this range please email the book number to Compliance_Support@tmr.qld.gov.au .

    If a customer requests a new diary and you don’t have any stock available please assist the customer to locate a centre that does have diaries available. Customers also have the option to use one of the available electronic work diaries refer Electronic Work Diary | NHVR

    Any defective diaries returned by customers should be replaced free of charge. On the application page in the diary mark it as “Cancelled”, date and stamp with CSC stamp if available, return the diary to the customer.

01/02/2021 - Disability Workers Screening Card
01/02/2021 - Disability Workers Screening Card

    What you need to know: We’ve teamed up with the Department of Seniors, Disability Services and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Partnerships (DSDSATSIP) to improve their disability worker screening card application process and strengthen their identity management system. From today, Monday 1 February 2021 customers can use their CRN to log into a worker portal on the DSDSATSIP website and apply for a Disability Worker Screening Card. Similar to the Blue Card process, customers who don’t have a CRN will be able to attend a TMR CSC or QGAP/QPS to create a CRN and have an image set captured.

    What you need to do: Customer service staff should refer to the Disability Workers Card DocBase webpages and become familiar with new instructions about creating a CRN and capturing an image set for Disability Worker Screening customers. During this process it is also important to confirm the customer’s image is in a valid state and the customer’s email address and phone number are captured, as this is an important part of the online validation service. All other aspects of the application and approval process is managed by DSDSATSIP.

    Contact: Please follow standard escalation procedures for transactional advice or direct project enquiries to Program Manager (External Service Delivery) Nicole Clues at Nicole.p.clues@tmr.qld.gov.au or Senior Project Advisor (Strategy and Engagement) Raechelle Persich at Raechelle.l.persich@tmr.qld.gov.au.

    Raechelle Persich
    Senior Project Advisor

    It appears work diaries in the range QBI8540 – QBI9999 and QBJ0000 - QBJ3539 have an issue with writing on the top page not duplicating on subsequent pages.

    If you encounter customers using these books please advise them to attend a CSC where the diary will be replaced free of charge.

    Where a customer is using one of these diaries no enforcement action should be taken for failing to write with enough pressure (s301 NVNL, Pin Code N912)

    Where the customer attends a CSC to replace a faulty diary, the CSC will mark the application page as cancelled and write the date cancelled, the diary will be returned to the customer so they can comply with the requirement to carry it for 28 days.
