TICA/TRAILS Access
Eligibility for Access
    Applicants seeking access to TRAILS/TICA must complete the TRAILS/TICA Access Application(F3277) form and obtain approval from their Manager.

    Note:Managers at non TMR led QGAPS are responsible for access and profile approvals at their location.

    Only staff who have completed the TICA Basics course can be given access to TRAILS/TICA.

    The approving Manager or Senior Transport Inspector must sight evidence that the applicant has completed the TICA Basics course. Evidence can be obtained by:
    • Accessing Accelerate and viewing the applicants completion records
    • Sighting a printed course completion certificate

Hints/Tips
Terminate OperatorWhen an operator ceases to have an ongoing need to access TICA/TRAILS, for example cessation of employment or permanent assignment to a role that does not require TICA/TRAILS access, the operator's access to TICA/TRAILS is to be terminated within 5 business days of the need to access ceasing. Terminating access requires the expiration of any profiles assigned to the user, termination of all operator accounts and deactivation of the operator's involved party record in TRAILS.
Feedback Register

The Feedback Register is an electronic register where the TransLink BHSL/BEA hub, Interlock Processing Unit (IPU), Disability Parking Unit, Capability, Engagement and Learning and the Industry Hub can record and automatically provide feedback to the #SASM group for the CSC (or the supporting CSC where the operator was located in a QGAP/QPS).

When a feedback item is received by a SASM, they must:
  • Provide feedback/training to the operator within 5 business days (where possible).
  • Update the Feedback Register to reflect that feedback has been provided.

If a customer presents to lodge:
  • An Interlock Exemption or Financial Assistance Scheme application without supporting documents, the application can be accepted and the customer advised that the IPU will be in contact about the missing documents.
  • A BHSL/BEA application without supporting documents, the application must not be accepted. If the customer insists on lodging it, the operator must record this information on the email to the BHSL/BEA hub.
  • A Driver/Rider Trainer or Traffic Controller accreditation application without supporting training/course requirements, the application can be accepted to allow the criminal and traffic history checks to commence. The operator must record this on the lodgement checklist.
