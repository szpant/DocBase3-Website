Demerit Point System (1238)

What it isCertain offences carry a predetermined number of demerit points.

Points are accumulated against a customer's record for traffic offences committed anywhere in Australia. Some offences attract additional demerit points.
Accumulation of Points
When points are allocated
When points are allocated
    A Customer starts with zero points and points are allocated when the person:
    • Pays an Infringement Notice fine
    • Is convicted by court for a demerit point offence
      Note:Demerit points are still allocated for results of "no conviction recorded"
    • Is issued an Enforcement order for non payment of an Infringement Notice fine
    • Is issued an order for an interstate offence

    Points are taken to be allocated on the day the offence was committed.
When Sanction action is taken
When Sanction action is taken
    Sanction action is taken when the demerit points accumulated reach the point limit determined by:
    • The type of licence held (including suspended licences)
    • Whether the person is serving a period of Good Driving Behaviour (GDB)

    Note:A High Speed Suspension (HSS) will also allocate 6 demerit points to customer that can result in customer also receiving Notice to Choose (F4011). The HSS and Demerit point suspension are able to be served concurrently.

When Points are considered dealt with
When Points are considered dealt with
    Demerit points are only dealt with once and cannot be included in any future demerit points sanction action once they are:
    • Listed on a sanction notice
    • Dealt with by serving the associated sanction

    Example
    Example
      First sanction notice
      • Provisional licence holder accumulates 4 points
      • Licensee sent Accumulation of Demerit Points - Notice to Choose
      • Licensee is required to make a choice by the choice date stated in the notice

      Before the Choice Date the person accumulates 4 more points.

      These points are not included in the sanction served for the Notice already issued.

      Second sanction notice
      Once the choice is made from the first notice, the person is sent a further Accumulation of Demerit Points - Notice to Choose for a further 4 points accumulated prior to the commencement of the first sanction.

      Licensee is again required to make a choice by the choice date stated in the second notice.

      The points on the second notice are not included in the sanction to be served from the first notice.

      If the licensee fails to make a choice, the licence will be suspended.

      Each sanction will be served concurrently (at the same time).


    Note:Only those points listed on the Notice are included in the sanction served for that notice

Speeding more than 20 km/h
    Double demerit points apply to a second or subsequent speeding offence that is more than 20 km/h over the speed limit committed within one year of an earlier offence.

    Example 1
    Example 1
      If the first speeding offence committed is 31 km/h over the speed limit, 6 demerit points will be recorded.

      The next speeding offence within 1 year is 41 km/h over the speed limit (8 demerit points), a total of 16 demerit points (2 x 8 demerit points) will be recorded.

Eligibility Criteria
Eligibility Criteria
    Customers will be eligible to use the free service if they:
    • Hold a current Qld driver licence or have held a Qld driver licence in the last 5 years
    • Do not have a good driving behaviour (GDB) sanction or special hardship order (SHO/X3) condition applied on their licence.
What will display online?
What will display online?
    The online service will only display the number of demerit points the customer has accumulated during the period appropriate to their licence type and based on our records.

    (The customers drivers licence type is the time frame used to calculate the total number of demerit points).

    A customer’s demerit point total may not reflect:
    • Enforcement actions for going over their demerit point limit (suspensions and good driving behaviour sanctions) yet to be applied to their driver licence.
    • Infringement notices that have not been finalised (for example, the customer has not paid the fine or they are disputing the infringement).
    • Any offences that are being dealt with in court or have recently been heard by a court.
    • Unprocessed fine payments (for example, payments made at a courthouse, using credit cards or BPay, or sent by mail to TMR) which may take several days to process.
