Written Off Vehicle Register (4961)

What is the Written-off vehicle registerThe written-off vehicle register (WOVR) is a National list of notifiable vehicles that have been deemed written-off vehicles, and is kept in NEVDIS.
Notifiable vehiclesTo be eligible to be written-off , a vehicle must be:
  • A compliant vehicle (meet the Australian Design Rules or ADRs); and
  • Be not more than 16 years old from its date of manufacture; and
  • Be a:
    • Motor vehicle with a GVM </= 4.5t; or
    • Trailer with an ATM > 4.5t; or
    • Motorbike; or
    • Caravan
Classifying a Written off vehicle
1. Determine if vehicle is a total loss
1. Determine if vehicle is a total loss
    A vehicle is a total loss if:

    (Cost of repairs) + (Expected price for the damaged vehicle if sold)
    is more than
    (Market value of the vehicle before the accident that caused the damage.)

    If a vehicle is a total loss, it must then be classed as either a Statutory or Repairable Write-off
2. Determine type of Write-off
2. Determine type of Write-off
    Statutory Write-Off
    Statutory Write-Off
      A statutory write-off is a vehicle that has been damaged to the extent that it can not be repaired back to a standard that would be safe for use on roads. The vehicle can only be used for scrap metal or parts.

      Any vehicle that is recorded as a statutory write-off is to be refused registration.

      Note:Special rules may apply to a vehicle recorded as a Statutory Write off before 30 September 2002. Please contact the Vehicle Identification Unit for instructions when dealing with this type of vehicle.

      To be deemed a statutory write-off a vehicle must:

      1.Be damaged in a way that meets the criteria listed in either the:
      a.Damage Assessment Criteria for the Classification of Statutory Write-offs (the National Written-off Vehicle Criteria); or
      b.Damage Assessment Criteria for the Classification of Statutory Write-offs—Motorbikes, Caravans, Heavy Trailers and Other vehicles.
      2.Be damaged by corrosion.

      Note:The two damage assessment criteria mentioned above are available online at www.tmr.qld.gov.au.

    Repairable Write-Off
    Repairable Write-Off
      A repairable write-off is a vehicle that is a total loss, but does not meet the criteria to be classed a statutory write-off.

      A repairable write-off can only be re-registered after it has passed a written-off vehicle inspection (WOVI) or in special circumstances has been given an exemption from a WOVI.
Recording a WOV when cancelling a registration
TMR records Written-off vehicle
TMR records Written-off vehicle
    When a customer cancels the registration of a notifiable vehicle for the reason of “WREK” they must supply a completed and signed:
    • Written-off vehicle Notification (Caravan/Trailer – Individual)
    • Vehicle/Recreational Ship Cancellation of Registration Application F3517

    All other voluntary cancellation requirements must be met.

    Operator's must:
    • Cancel the vehicle as a repairable write-off in TICA recording all relevant damage points as detailed on F4069
    • Scan and email all forms and supporting documentation to the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU)
    • Give the customer a Blue and White Repairable Write-off Sticker
      Repairable write off Stickers
      Repairable write off Stickers
        It is a legal requirement that when recording a vehicle on the register, TMR must give the customer a blue and white 'Repairable Write-off Sticker'.

        Note:Legislation requires that this sticker must be attached to a vehicle in a conspicuous place. A customer must be advised that the sticker must be attached to the vehicle in a conspicuous place within 24 hours.

        The Vehicle Identification Unit has stocks of Repairable Write-off Stickers for vehicles. If a CSC requires more stock of these stickers, please email the VIU
External Notifier records written-off vehicle
External Notifier records written-off vehicle
    External organisations involved in the motor industry (eg Insurance companies, wreckers) are required by law to notify the department if a notifiable vehicle is written off.

    Notification is either:
    • Electronically
    • Manually by supplying relevant paperwork to the VIU

    External notifiers are responsible for manufacturing (to TMR specifications) and distributing their own written-off vehicle labels.
Role of Vehicle Identification Unit
Role of Vehicle Identification Unit
    It is essential that when an Operator records a vehicle on the WOVR, the Registration Cancellation Application and Written-off Vehicle Notification forms are emailed to the Vehicle Identification Unit (VIU)

    The VIU are responsible for managing the:
    • vehicle cancellation process for written-off vehicles and require all documentation to perform this role
    • relationship with external notifiers
    • updating and correcting written-off vehicle details
    • management of written-off vehicle restrictions

Vehicle Retained by Registered Operator
Vehicle Retained by Registered Operator
    A Written-off Vehicle Notice (F4056) is automatically generated and sent to the registered operator. Notice advises that the registration will be auto-cancelled after 28 days unless the registered operator can either:
    • Provide a reason why it should not be cancelled to the VIU for decision
    • Pass a WOVI (unless exempt - refer to Exemptions from WOVI)
    or
    • Voluntarily cancel the registration (Full requirements)

    Registered Operator Unable to meet auto-cancellation timeframes
    Registered Operator Unable to meet auto-cancellation timeframes
      A registered operator of a repairable write off may apply for an extension of the 28 day period specified on the Written Off Vehicle Notice in which they are required to obtain a WOVI before the vehicle is auto cancelled.

      If approved the extension will expire 28 days from the end of the original period as specified on the Written-Off Vehicle Notice. If the applicant requires a further extension a new extension of time request must be made. There are no limits on the number of extensions that may be granted with each application being assessed on its merits.

      Reasons why an extension may be required include but are not limited to:
      • Vehicle parts in transit and not expected to be received in time
      • Unable to obtain inspection slot within the 28 day timeframes
      • Vehicle repairs will not be completed within the 28 day timeframes

      To apply for an extension the customer must provide in writing the following information:
      • Registered operators contact details
      • Vehicle registration number
      • Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)
      • Why the extension is required (applicant should be encouraged to provide supporting evidence, such as evidence of WOVI inspection booking, mechanics contact details to confirm vehicle part availability)

      Applicant is required to forward their request to the       Vehicle Identification Unit.

      Operator is to advise the customer that their application will be assessed and they will be contacted by a member of the VIU. All enquiries should be directed to the VIU by email unless urgent (eg original period expires within 5 working days)
