Compulsory Third Party (CTP) (3026)


What it isCompulsory third party (CTP) is indemnity for the registered vehicles owners/drivers who are legally liable for personal injury to any other party in the event of a motor vehicle accident.

From 1 July 2016, CTP insurance also includes a National Injury Insurance Scheme Queensland (NIISQ) levy. Under this scheme, eligible people seriously injured in motor vehicle accidents will receive essential care and support benefits, regardless of fault.

Requirements
Vehicles Requiring CTP
Vehicles Requiring CTP
    • Motor Vehicles/Motorbike
    • Vehicles (other than trailers) operating under an unregistered vehicle permit

    Dealer/Trade plates also require CTP prior to issue of plate.

    Trailers are covered by the CTP of the Queensland registered towing vehicle.

    If towed by an interstate registered vehicle, the registered operator should check with the CTP insurer of the towing vehicle to see if cover is provided to the trailer being towed.

    Registered operators of heavy trailers may however choose Class 24 insurance.

Changes
Changes
    Buses with class 9 have an option for a temporary change to their purpose of use relative to class 10A or 11 for not more than 14 nominated days during a 12 month term of CTP insurance. This is handled by the CTP insurer - there are no changes to be made to the record in TICA. TMR does not issue any letters of authorisation for these changes. Customers requesting these changes are to be referred to the CTP Insurer of the vehicle involved.

    For primary producer's trucks with class 17 there is an option for a temporary change to class 7 for commercial use up to 3 months. A pro-rata of one quarter of the registration fee for the difference between the standard and concessional fees will be charged. The concession is not to be removed.

    Proof of payment to the CTP insurer for the temporary CTP class change should be carried in the vehicle. For class 17 vehicles, the TMR letter authorising the 3 months commercial use should be carried in the vehicle. The CTP class on TICA must NOT be changed even when pro rata fees are charged for the temporary class 17 change. A copy of the authorisation letter together with a copy of the proof of the temporary CTP change should be kept in the CSC that issues the letter. Further information on this can be found in Vehicle Rego > Concessional Registration > Primary Producers > Rules & Rates.

    The procedure for receipting the pro rata registration fee is detailed in DocBase under: Concession - Primary Producers > Quarterly commercial rates and Rules and Rates > Rules.

National Injury Insurance Scheme Queensland (NIISQ)
National Injury Insurance Scheme Queensland (NIISQ)
    The National Injury Insurance Scheme Queensland (NIISQ) covers people who have sustained an eligible serious personal injury in a motor vehicle accident to which the National Injury Insurance Scheme (Queensland) Act 2016 applies. This scheme applies regardless of who was at fault and whether the vehicle was covered by a CTP insurer or the Nominal Defendant levy.

    Customers can still make a claim with their CTP insurer or the Nominal Defendant for relevant compensation such as general damages or economic loss. However, they can also receive the necessary treatment and care through the NIISQ.

    While the NIISQ levy is collected with CTP insurance, the National Injury Insurance Agency Queensland administers the NIISQ separately.

    Customers may obtain further information on the NIISQ by:
    • contacting the enquiry line on 1300 302 568
    • visiting the website at www.niis.qld.gov.au
Nominal Defendant
Nominal Defendant
    The Nominal Defendant is a body corporate that can sue and be sued.

    It covers claims for personal injury if someone is injured by:
    • An unidentified vehicle
      or
    • Unregistered vehicle at fault
    • Qld registered trailers towed by some interstate registered vehicles except if the interstate registered vehicle's CTP insurance policy also covers the trailer. The Nominal Defendant is not the insurer of a trailer with an ATM greater than 4.5t if the motor vehicle accident involving the trailer happens outside Queensland.
    • Qld registered wheelchairs
    • Registered vehicles where the Insurer has become insolvent
    • Unregistered vehicles past the end of the 30 day grace period
TMR's CTP Role
TMR's CTP Role
    It is not permissible for any TMR office to undertake an insurance agent's role for any form of insurance including the:
    • Writing of new insurance policies
    • Renewal of existing insurance policies

    TMR only collects money on behalf of the Insurance Company

    Operators should never issue a refund of CTP fees, except those that are automatically calculated in a registration cancellation transaction.

    If a TMR processing error results in the customer paying extra CTP fees for a period the customer should be provided with a letter detailing the error, the customer should then present the letter to their CTP insurer to apply for a refund.

Who is covered
Who is covered
    CTP Insurance
    CTP Insurance
      CTP insurance covers personal injury claims by other road users including:
      • Drivers
      • Passengers
      • Pedestrians
      • Cyclists
      • Motorcyclists
      • Pillion passengers

    NIISQ
    NIISQ
      The NIISQ covers people who have sustained an eligible serious personal injury in a motor vehicle accident regardless of who was at fault.
      A detailed list of eligible injuries can be found at www.niis.qld.gov.au
Who is Not Covered
Who is Not Covered
    CTP Insurance
    CTP Insurance
      The CTP scheme does not cover:
      • Injury to the owner/driver of the vehicle at fault. (Some insurers do offer options for owners/drivers of vehicles at fault)
      • Property damage
    NIISQ
    NIISQ
      The NIISQ does not cover customers who do not meet the criteria for an eligible serious personal injury. Refer above: Who is covered.
Currency of CTP
Currency of CTP
    Period of Grace
    Period of Grace
      Ends on the first of the following to happen:
      • On the renewal (payment) of the registration or the granting of a permit allowing the vehicle to be driven on roads while unregistered
      • 30 days after the registration expiry date

      However:
      • If the registration is cancelled before the due date there is no period of grace as the CTP insurance policy ceases to be in force from the date the cancellation of the registration takes effect.
      • If a cancellation of registration takes effect during the period of grace, the period of grace ends when the cancellation takes effect.
      • The period of grace does not include a period for which the vehicle has plates attached to it that allows it to be driven while unregistered. (e.g. driven with dealer plates attached)
      • If the registered operator has nominated a new insurer before the expiry date to become the insurer for the next registration period, the new nominated insurer is the insurer for the grace period.
      • If the vehicle remains unregistered the nominal defendant covers the remaining period until auto cancellation (3 months from expiry date).

    Adult Seating Capacity >8 (incl driver)
    Adult Seating Capacity >8 (incl driver)
      The CTP fee tables for class 10A, 10B and 11 include costs per seat for each adult passenger seat over 7 seats as well as the base premium.

      As the seating capacity in TICA is based on the actual adult seating capacity which includes the driver, 8 seats are deducted from the recorded seating capacity when calculating the CTP insurance premium for the seating component e.g. recorded seating capacity in TICA is 48 seats - therefore the premium for the seating cost will be calculated on 40 seats.

    Vehicle Falls into Two Classes
    Vehicle Falls into Two Classes
      If a motor vehicle falls into two or more classes the vehicle's class is taken to be the class that attracts the highest insurance premium
