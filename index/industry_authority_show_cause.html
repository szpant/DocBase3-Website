Industry Authority Show Cause (3451)



What it isIf the delegate of the Chief Executive considers a ground exists to amend, suspend, cancel or immediately suspend an authority, then the delegate must give the holder a written notice – stating the proposed action, grounds, facts and circumstances to support the proposed action.

Delegation to amend, suspend or cancel an Industry Authority is under s19 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995.
Refer: Delegations Database
GroundsGrounds may exist to amend, suspend, cancel or immediately suspend an accreditation for a;
  • Dangerous Goods driver or vehicle licence
  • Driver Trainer or Driver Rider Trainer
  • Pilot vehicle or Escort vehicle
  • Traffic Controller
  • Tow Truck Show Cause Refer:Industry Authority > 6. Tow Trucks > Tow Truck Show Cause

Grounds to amend, suspend or cancel
Grounds to amend, suspend or cancel
    Grounds to amend, suspend or cancel are listed in the TO(RUM) Act 1995, s18(1)
    Refer: Delegations Database to determine who may issue a show cause.
    Immediate Suspension
    Immediate Suspension
      The delegate of the Chief Executive may immediately suspend an authority if necessary to do so in the public interest.
      “Public Interest” is not formally defined in legislation, Refer to: Show Cause Policy and Procedures document below for guidance.

Refer to the Compliance Management Policy and Procedures for accreditation schemes and industry authorities and the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an authority holder and determining if grounds exist to immediately suspend an authority.

The DecisionAny submissions made by the authority holder must be taken into account when the decision is made by the delegate of the Chief Executive.

If no representations are received from the authority holder, then the decision is made based on the information currently before the delegate of the Chief Executive.

Show Cause Policy and Procedure

The authority holder must be informed by written notice of the decision.
Decisions on ReviewOnce the decision has been made by the delegate of the Chief Executive, an accreditation holder can:

1.Apply to TMR for an internal review of the decision.
2.Apply for a stay of the decision with the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT).
3.Once the original decision has been reviewed by TMR and the reviewed decision is not the decision sought by the accreditation holder, the accreditation holder may seek an external review of TMR’s reviewed decision at QCAT.

Refer to the Show Cause Policy and Procedures when dealing with an accreditation holder who requests a review or stay of the decision to amend, suspend or cancel an accreditation.

Giving of NoticesThe Show Cause Notice may be served to the authority holder by mail, email or in person.

Returned Notices
Returned Notices
    Sent by Email
    Sent by Email
      If a notice sent by email returns a non-delivery error it must be mailed through Australia Post (if it wasn’t already mailed)
    Sent by Mail
    Sent by Mail
      If a notice sent via Australia Post is returned unclaimed, this does not affect the process. The matter must proceed and a decision made based on the evidence mentioned in the notice.

      A text message should be placed on TICA to alert any CSCs transacting business with the licence or certificate holder to obtain a new address then contact the relevant office progressing the show cause or immediate suspend action.
Return of
Industry Authority licence/card		When an Industry authority is cancelled the authority holder must return their authority to a CSC in person or by mail to the address listed on the show cause notice. The authority must be returned within 14 days of receiving their notice.
