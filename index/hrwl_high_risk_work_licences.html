High Risk Work Licences (7058)


What are theyThe Office of Industrial Relations Licensing and Advisory Services (LAS) team is responsible for the issue of High Risk Work Licences (HRWL) in Queensland.

A HRWL is a nationally recognised photographic card that is issued for a period of five years and covers cranes, hoists, fork-lifts, dogging and scaffolding.
How are they issued
On-Line Application Service
On-Line Application Service
    From the 16 May 2016, LAS will transition to an on-line service and use the applicants TMR customer details, images and signatures and TMR’s card production facilities to produce a HRWL.

    Applicants will lodge their application through the LAS on-line Renewable and Photographic Licensing System (RAPS). To commence lodgement they will be required to enter in their TMR CRN, name and date of birth.

    A TMR web service will validate the entered information against TICA and confirm that their customer details are correct, they have a valid image set (both image and signature) and their record is not restricted (for example, insufficient EOI or suppressed).

    Customers who successfully validate will progress through and lodge their application. The LAS team is responsible for assessing the HRWL application and ordering cards for approved applicants. A HRWL is issued for five years.
    Customers who fail validation will be referred to TMR to have their record corrected.

    Refer Below: What is TMR's role

    This is the first time TMR has allowed the use of image sets and customer data to produce an external product. It is extremely likely that the success of this project will create the opportunity for more interdepartmental partnerships of this nature. What this means is that the need to ensure that the data within TICA is complete and accurate is extremely important and that care must be taken when updating customer details.
Remote Area Kits
Remote Area Kits
    The LAS team has modified our Remote Area Kits for their purposes and customers who are unable to attend a TMR location will be able to use this kit to have a CRN created, update customer details and have their image set imported.

    Remote Area Kits are sent to the Atherton CSC for processing.

    Customers are encouraged to contact LAS for any enquiries.
What is TMR's role
Customer has failed validation
Customer has failed validation
    Customers who fail validation will be referred to TMR to have their record corrected.
    There are several reasons why a HRWL applicant is not able to pass the validation which include:
    • They are not a TMR customer
    • Customer details are incorrect
    • Customer has a NOAC restriction
    • Customer has an image restriction
    • Customer has insufficient EOI
    • Customers TMR image expires within five years

    LAS will contact the customer and advise them to attend a TMR location and to bring EOI. Privacy requirements limits the information we are able to provide the LAS team. While a customer is likely to identify as a HRWL customer if they have been referred to us, there is an issue with their TMR record and you are responsible for ensuring that issues with the customer’s record and/or image set are resolved in line with current operational policy and procedures.

    Where the resolution of an issue will impact on a TMR product you must ensure all changes are appropriately actioned, for example if changing a customer name you must ensure that they meet EOI standards and all other licensing products are re-issued in the new name.

    Current policy and procedures apply to how we create, manage or fix their TMR record and every HRWL customer must have their image and signature captured.
Capturing an image set for a HRWL customer
Capturing an image set for a HRWL customer
    An image and signature must be captured for each customer applying for a HRWL. You must:
    • Select the 'HRWL' option when capturing the image set
    • Provide the customer with their CRN card (if they’re a new customer).
    • Record the customer's eContact details (phone AND email)

    Refer: Services > 2. Qld Government Partnerships > The How To: Qld Government Partnerships > Qld Government Partnerships Procedures for how to capture an Image Set in TICA.

    Evidence of Identity
    A customer must have standard EOI recorded, AND evidence of their residential address.

    Note:An address document must be recorded in TICA. If it’s not recorded, the customer won’t be able to validate online to complete their application

    Image Requirements
    The customer's image must be in a 'VALID' state. If you receive an image failure, contact IMU to have this resolved.


    Note:Customer's with a 'verified' image will be restricted from using the HRWL online service and won’t be able to complete their application.

    The image will expire after 6 months if not attached to a product.

    Customer Requirements
    New customers need to complete a New Customer Application (F3503) if it is the first time the customer is conducting business with TMR and there is no other product/transaction required.

    All customers must have standard EOI and evidence of their residential address recorded in TICA before progressing with the image set.

    Note:An address document must be recorded in TICA. If it’s not recorded, the customer won’t be able to validate online to complete their application.

    Interstate residents/customers may apply for a HRWL. If required, the New Customer Application (F3503) should be completed with the interstate proof of address document written in Question 2 on the form. In the ‘Comments by authorising person’ make a note that an interstate residency document was sighted. When an interstate address document is presented for a HRWL, the following text must be applied to the customer's record:
    'An interstate residency document has been recorded for the purpose of a HRWL. Customer must provide a Queensland proof of residency document prior to obtaining any TMR product. Please expire this text once a Qld residency document has been presented. USERID, CSC name, CSC phone'



    Listing customer images
    Listing customer images
      There is also the ability to list what images the customer has had taken in the past. This list provides the captured date and the image status. Images are valid for 10.5 years an image must be valid and not expire within 5 years to be able to be used on a HRWL. While signatures are not allocated a status they must also have at least 5 years remaining.
Customers with a NOAC Restriction
Customers with a NOAC Restriction
    There are two types of customers who may have a NOAC restriction against their TMR records:
    • Suppressed customers with a NOAC restriction applied by IMU; and
    • Customers who have voluntarily requested TMR apply the NOAC restriction.

    Customers who have a NOAC, applied for any reason, will be unable to use the HRWL online verification service. These customers will be required to attend a TMR location in person and provide consent to have the NOAC lifted for a period, to allow them to apply for a HRWL online.

    Customers are required to complete the No Online Access Permitted (NOAC) Customer Restriction Request, Refer: Customer > 1. Customer > Customer Details > System information > Qualifications, Restrictions and Text > Customer Restrictions > NOAC > Manually added NOAC > Requested by customer > No Online Access Permitted (NOAC) Customer Restriction Request

    Customers with a NOAC restriction applied by the Identity Management Unit (IMU) due to having a suppressed record are not to be altered without consent from IMU. Contact IMU in the first instance.

    In this scenario, the suppression will remain in place and only the NOAC restriction will be lifted for a 24-hour period. Customers should be advised to access the LAS website and submit their application as soon as possible within this timeframe.

    Customers who do not apply via the LAS website before the NOAC is reapplied will need to return to TMR and repeat the process. Only in extenuating circumstances, can this timeframe be extended to a maximum of 72 hours. This can be considered at the request of the customer only and will require approval from the manager of the IMU.

    For customers with a voluntary NOAC, it will be their responsibility to reapply for the NOAC once they have submitted their HRWL application, if they still wish to have this restriction.

    Before these customers leave the office, ensure that you have checked that their details are accurate, including up to date eContact details, and ensure they have a valid image set.

    Customers are to contact LAS if they have concerns with their application. If customers are not willing to give consent to the lifting of the NOAC, please refer them to the LAS team to discuss their options.

Refer: The How To: Qld Government Partnerships > Manage a High Risk Work Licence Applicant
HRWL SmartcardTo request the production of a HRWL card, LAS will send to TICA the information to be printed on the card. TICA will then batch this information and send it to Placard for production. As such you will be able to see if the customer has had a HRWL issued via the Smartcard list on the Individual Customer Hub.

Refer: Smartcard > Smartcard Guide > Non-TMR Smartcards for Card Specifics
