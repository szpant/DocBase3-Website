About "Customer"
What it isA customer is an entity that does business with the Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR). TMR customers are issued with a Customer Reference Number (CRN).

Note:This does not include persons who purchase non-customer products (eg. Marine maps, Your Keys to Driving in Qld)
Customer TypesThere are 2 types of TMR customers:
Individuals
Individuals
    • An individual is simply a person.
    • An individual is a legal entity.
Organisations
Organisations
    • A body of persons organised for some end or work.
    • The administrative personnel or apparatus of a business.

    An organisation may or may not be a legal entity. Some TMR products may require an organisation to be a legal entity in order to conduct a particular transaction. Refer to the relevant business area for legal entity requirements.

Customer Signatures
    On Written Applications/Advices
    On Written Applications/Advices
      All required written applications (forms or advices) for TMR products must have the original signature of the applicant/s or product holder/s or authorised company representative. An original signature is a wet ink signature or an original form printed with an electronic signature.

      The only exceptions are:
      • The applicant/product authorises another person
      • Representing an Organisation/Individual using an original Authority to represent registered operator vehicle registration form (F5225)
      • An Adult Proof of Age applicant is unable to sign due to a permanent intellectual or physical disability
      • Registration of a motorised wheelchair or light vehicle (registered for private use) for a person <18 years of age who is unable to sign due to a permanent intellectual or physical disability. Refer: Document Requirements > Registration
      • If the application meets the criteria outlined in the Electronic Communications policy

      e-signatures
      e-signatures

        • TMR e-signature enabled forms allows 'draw, type or image' signature types.
        • Some examples of when this capability may be useful are:
          • A vehicle is sold privately online and transported to the aquirer on a trailer. The disposer could electronically sign the transfer form and email it to the acquirer. The acquirer could then sign (either electronically or wet ink), and present the printed (original) form when completing the transfer over the counter.
          • An organisation is based interstate and wants to complete a registration transaction for a Queensland garaged vehicle. The director/proprietor can complete/e-sign the authority to represent form electronically and email it to their Queensland office. The Queensland representative can then present the printed original (not scanned copy) form over the counter.
          Applications signed using a digital ID will contain a digital stamp. Examples of digital signatures with and without a digital stamp are below:



          For a list of e-signature capable forms Refer: Fees and Forms > 2. Forms > Forms > e-signature capable.

Address Standards
Address Standards
    The department's address standards are based on Australian Standards. When an address is entered in TICA it is compared with the Australian Standard held in Australia Post's Postal Address File (PAF). If the address entered is different to the address on PAF, TICA corrects the address so that it is compliant with the PAF. This ensures that TMR has accurate address information recorded.

    Having correct address information recorded ensures that mail sent to a customer by TMR, is delivered to the correct address and received in a timely manner. The residential, business, registered, postal and garage address will be compared with the PAF.

    Override Function
    Override Function
      What is it?
      What is it?
        The Override Function allows TICA to accept an address that is showing as non-compliant.

        Note:A Suburb/State/Postcode cannot be overridden.

      When is it used?
      When is it used?
        The override function is used if an address is not found by TICA. This might arise for a number of reasons including (but not limited to):
        • The customer lives outside of an Australia Post delivery area and therefore their address details might not appear on the PAF.
        • The customer lives in a new street which has yet to appear on the current PAF.
        • An error exists in either the customer's address or in the PAF.
        • A street name is found, but, a street or unit number is not.

        In these circumstances, it will be necessary to enter a non-compliant address into TICA using the override function.

      Who can use the Override Function?
      Who can use the Override Function?
        The following TICA profiles can use the Override function.
        SSUPVRSUPVR
        DAMANGRDASUPVR
        QPSADJQPSINFO
        QPSSUPQPSSNADJ
        SPECPROVIU
        CSO & DACSO do not have the Override Function.

      When the System will NOT allow an Override Action
      When the System will NOT allow an Override Action
        When an address is unable to be overridden staff must contact CSB.Data Integrity by email or phone and send a copy of the customer's rate notice.

        It will then be investigated and if suitable, the address will be updated as a special case. Once updated staff should notify the customer. If the PAF is updated in the future, they will then remove the special reason code from the customer address record.
        Examples where this problem can occur:
        Examples where this problem can occur:
          • subdivision of a block of land with an existing address consisting of a range of street numbers (for example, 2-14). Individual addresses are introduced (2, 4, 6, etc). These will only be added to the PAF on advice from the local council.
          • a block with one number is divided into 2 parcels of land (for example 1 becomes 1A and 1B).
          • a gated community was number 150 Smith street now divided into several house numbers (1/150 Smith St, 2/150 Smith St etc).
          • a new number has been added to a street where the numbers are not continuous. This number already exists in a street with a similar name in an adjoining suburb or town (for example, existing Smith street numbers in Cloncurry may be 2, 4, and 8 and now 6 has been added. The numbers 2,4,6,8 may exist in Smith Street in Mt Isa. The system will correct the Cloncurry address to Mt Isa).

      When it is used?
      When it is used?

        • Data Integrity will investigate and add the suburb to TICA if required.

Updating phone numbers
Updating phone numbers
    If the customer's current transaction information provides a mobile phone number that is different to the number in the Individual Customer Hub it must be updated in eContact details.

    It is only updated in the Individual Customer Hub if the customer confirms it is the number they want TMR to use if contact is required.

    If the customer provides a landline and a mobile phone number, the landline must be updated in the Individual Customer Hub Contact details field and the mobile number in the eContact details.

    If the customer has two mobile phone numbers, confirm which one they would like recorded for eContact purposes and record the other one in the Individual Customer Hub.


    Note: A customer who is enrolled in direct debit is unable to remove their mobile number as it is a mandatory requirement to be enrolled in direct debit.

    Expiring a Customer Qualification
    Expiring a Customer Qualification
      A qualification should only remain on a customer record while the customer is eligible. If evidence is provided that a customer is not entitled to a qualification, the qualification should be expired.

      TMR can either:
      • Be notified in writing by the customer that they are no longer entitled to a concession
      • Perform a search on a State or Federal government register (e.g. ASICS or Motor Dealer Register) to confirm the currency of a customer's registration or licence

      Where TMR identifies that a person is no longer eligible for a qualification, the customer should be contacted and given the opportunity to provide current evidence of entitlement.

      The customer should also be informed of the consequences to their TMR products if they do not provide this evidence; for example, the expiry of a Dealer Qualification may result in changes to the vehicles registered under that CRN.

      A qualification should be expired:
      • Upon customer confirmation that they are no longer eligible
      • If a customer has not responded to a request to prove ongoing entitlement within 14 days of the initial contact.

      When a Dealer Qualification is expired, it is important to check if there are any vehicles registered under that CRN, as each vehicle will need to be updated to change the purpose of use from DEAL to COML.

      The dealer will be required to provide a copy of the change (i.e. registration certificate) to the Office of State Revenue so duty can be paid.

      Refer: 04. Registration > Purpose of Use > Rules > Changing POU

    NOAC
    NOAC
      The No Online Access (NOAC) restriction prevents a customer from accessing and performing any updates via the TMR online services.

      The NOAC restriction will prevent the customer from:
      • being issued with an Online Access Code,
      • updating their customer details online,
      • accessing their account details in the "My account" home page,
      • applying for or renewing any TMR products online,
      • paying for products recorded against their CRN, including registration renewal payments or using the online services, and
      • applying for a high risk work licence online.

      Registration Transfer Online:
      • A seller is prevented from using the "Transfer registration as a seller" online service when the buyer has a NOAC restriction. Refer: Registration Transfer Online > Rules > When the service cannot be used

      The NOAC does not prevent payments made by Australia Post, BPay or over the counter at TMR customer service centres. If a customer wants to prevent ALL renewal methods for a vehicle registered in their name they can apply for a No Renewal Allowed (NORA) restriction. Refer:Vehicle Rego > 04. Registration > Vehicle Restrictions or Text > Vehicle Restrictions

      Operators should remind customers that a NOAC restriction does not prevent the payment via BPay if their static PRN is known by another person. Where customers have a concern, they can apply for a new static PRN. Refer: Vehicle Rego > 07. Renewal > Registration Renewal > Rules > Fixed payment Reference Number (PRN) > Additional Rules

      A NOAC is applied:
      • Automatically when a CRN is closed or updated as deceased
      • Manually if requested by the customer or applied by the MCR unit (individual CRN only)

      NODD
      NODD
        The No Direct Debit (NODD) restriction prevents a customer from enrolling in the Direct Debit service.

        A NODD restriction may be placed on a customer’s account if they have had three consecutive unsuccessful registration renewal payments. This may occur as a result of:
        • Not supplying a current COI for an inspectable vehicle;
        • Payment being made by an alternative payment channel;
        • Insufficient funds in the customer’s account;
        • Incorrect bank details captured in the customers Direct Debit Authority; or
        • The financial institution dishonouring the customer’s payment.

        If this restriction is applied it will prevent the customer from enrolling in the service for a period of nine months.

        Once the nine month time frame has passed the restriction will be automatically removed.

        Central Operations and Support (COS) manage this restriction and can also apply a NODD at their discretion.

        Enquiries relating to this restriction on a customer account should be forwarded by email to: Direct Debit
      NOLR
      NOLR

The restriction can be applied to both current and expired driver licences however is predominately used to prevent customers with expired driver licences from renewing when TMR has received advice that would normally result in a show cause process. TMR cannot show cause an expired driver licence so a NOLR is applied instead until the customer presents a medical certificate.

        Medical certificates received where a NOLR exists must be referred to the MCR unit for clearance and removal of the NOLR before the customer can renew.

    Seatbelt Exemption Certificates
    Seatbelt Exemption Certificates

    		Customers who have been issued with a valid seatbelt exemption certificate can register their certificate with TMR, to avoid being issued with a camera-detected Warning Letter or Infringement Notice for failing to wear a seatbelt while driving a motor vehicle.

    If a certificate is not recorded before a customer is issued a Warning Letter or Infringement Notice, they can lodge it after the fact. If it is lodged:

      • During the Warning Letter Phase, any further Warning Letters or Infringement Notices for driver seatbelt offences won't be issued for the remaining period the recorded certificate is valid for
      • After an Infringement Notice has been issued, their fine can be waived if the certificate is lodged within 28 days of the notice and it was valid on the date of the offence.

    Important: Although the exemption is recorded on TICA, the customer must still carry it with them at all times when driving (as a passenger, the driver must be carrying your certificate). This is because the driver must show the certificate to the Police if requested.
    Rules
    Rules
      • A customer may have multiple seatbelt exemption certificates recorded (e.g. their issue/expiry dates overlap).
      • A certificate can be entered in TICA up to 1 month in advance. If a customer has extenuating circumstances and wishes to provide it more than 1 month in advance of the effective date the CSC may hold the certificate until it able to be entered.
      • A certificate holder does not need to hold a driver licence for their certificate to be recorded (a SKEL record can be created).
      • The doctor on the certificate/letter must be registered on the ahpra.gov.au website for the certificate to be accepted.
    Acceptable seatbelt exemption certificate
    Acceptable seatbelt exemption certificate
      A seatbelt exemption certificate can be any of the following:
        • Exemption from wearing a seatbelt certificate (F2690)
        • Medical certificate for motor vehicle driver (F3712)
        Another form/letter that includes all of the following:
          • Doctor's signature/stamp
          • Statement that, in the opinion of the doctor, the person should not wear a seatbelt due to a medical condition or disability
          • Date of issue
          • Date of expiry (of not more than 12 months after the date of issue)

      Note:Customers requesting a seatbelt exemption certificate should be advised by staff to complete the F2960.
    Passenger seatbelt offences
    Passenger seatbelt offences
      If a seatbelt exemption certificate is recorded, it will not stop a customer from being issued with a camera detected Warning Letter or Infringement Notice for failing to ensure a passenger is restrained while driving a motor vehicle. Although the passenger may also have a seatbelt exemption recorded, there is no way of knowing who the passenger is at the time the camera detects the offence.

      If an unrestrained passenger has a seatbelt exemption certificate and the registered operator is issued:
      • A Warning Letter, the certificate holder should lodge it with TMR so that it's recorded for the future (for example, if they are the driver for a future driver seatbelt offence, no notice will issue). No other action is required.
      • An Infringement Notice, they must lodge it within 28 days so that the fine can be waived.
    Lodging/processing a seatbelt exemption certificate
    Lodging/processing a seatbelt exemption certificate
      • No EOI is required to lodge the certificate
      • A third party can lodge on someone's behalf, for example a health professional or relative.
      • The certificate can be a copy (if an original is lodged it must be returned to the customer as they are required to carry it when driving/as a passenger, the driver is required to carry it)
      • No fee applies to lodge the certificate with TMR
      • The Service Level Agreement (SLA) for processing certificates lodged online, by email or post is 3 business days. Exemptions lodged at a CSC/QGAP must be processed on the spot.
      • Once processed, a confirmation email in Email Templates can be sent advising that the seatbelt exemption certificate has been processed. If no email exists, copy and paste content onto letterhead.

      Note:If the application is lodged by a third party, the letter/email must be sent to the customer (not third party).

      Seatbelt exemptions can be lodged via the following methods:
        1. Online Transport and Main Roads Online Services List
        2. Email to seatbeltexemptions@tmr.qld.gov.au
        3. Post to PO Box 645 Toowoomba Qld 4350
        4. CSC/QGAP (in person)

      Refusing a seatbelt exemption certificate
      Refusing a seatbelt exemption certificate
        A certificate must be refused if:
        • The form has not been completed/signed
        • The doctor does not exist on the ahpra.gov.au website
        • The expiry date is more than 12 months from the issue date
        • The expiry date has passed
        • The form does not state that the customer should not wear a seatbelt due to a medical condition or disability
        • An invalid attachment was provided (e.g. blank piece of paper).
    Editing an existing seatbelt exemption certificate
    Editing an existing seatbelt exemption certificate
      • The issue or expiry date on a seatbelt exemption certificate may be edited if a mistake has been made.
      • If a new/different certificate is received, a new certificate must be processed (the original one must not be edited)
