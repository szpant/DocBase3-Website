Formless Customer Applications (7296)


What it isTMR is committed to improving customer experience and providing more sustainable services. In-line with this commitment TMR has introduced a different way of processing individual customer applications for specific products without the need for a paper application form. This change will make application for these service simpler for our customers and eliminate the need for CSCs to print, distribute and store many forms.

CSB with LTSR has committed to implement formless customer applications in all TMR led-CSC's and QGAP's. This means non-TMR led QGAP's and QPS offices are excluded from the rollout. RSO’s are also excluded at this stage and advice will be provided if and when these will be rolled out.

For further information Refer below: Eligible locations
What transactions are affectedTo enable the removal of the application form, legislation that prescribed a customer must apply on the approved form, was amended to allow the Director General to specify the method in which the customer may make application.

Not all legislation has been amended and LTSR is progressively amending as opportunities become available. What this means is that products such as APA and Photo ID cards and Plates still require an application form.

The transactions included are:
Driver Licence
Driver Licence
    • Issue/re-issue
    • Transfer
    • Renewals
    • Upgrades
    • Replacements
    • Practical driving tests

    Formless transactions do not apply to other Driver Licence related transactions such as, Driver Licence Enforcement Nominations and Driver Licence Traffic Histories.

    All processes for Suppressed Customers remain unchanged.

    Formless Transactions are not applicable to Marine Licence applications, however, a Driver Licence which has an existing Marine Licence Indicator displayed can be replaced/reissued/renewed/upgraded as a formless transaction.

    S Conditions
    S Conditions
      A customer may add or remove an S condition as part of formless transaction without completing a Driver Licence Amendment (F4358) form.

      If the customer's advice differs from the medical certificate, the S condition is added or removed as per the customer's advice. The CSO must note on the Driver Licence Transaction History that the customer has advised they do not require spectacles to drive. A Driver Licence Amendment (F4358) form is not required.

      The medical certificate must be recorded in the MCR Hub.

    The exception is an existing MLI attached to a driver licence.

How does a formless application workFor eligible transactions in place of the application form, you will ask the customer a series of mandatory questions. These questions are designed to:
    • Obtain the customers consent to verify documents
    • Identify the customer
    • Confirm eligibility for the product
    • Satisfy the requirements of the product.

You will still process the transaction the same way you do now. The questions are structured to flow with the transaction and the screens you use.

Mandatory questions
Mandatory questions
    Mandatory Questions Licensing - February 2021

    Mandatory Questions - Registration 2 June 2020

The customer must also provide all necessary supporting documents. For example to renew a driver licence, while the customer does not need to complete the F3000, they will need to provide a valid medical certificate if they are over 75 or have a relevant medical condition.
Filing RequirementsAt the end of the transaction you will print either the TICA driver licence history detail or TICA registration history report and then you will carefully confirm with the customer the details of the transaction. The customer signs the receipt/report which contains a declaration that the information they provided is true and correct and a copy of the receipt/original report and supporting documentation is filed with your daily filing.

If multiple licensing transactions are completed at the same time, only the driver licence history detail report showing all transactions needs to be signed by the customer and put in daily filing. For example:
    • Customer renews their licence and adds a class,
    • Customer renews their licence and a medical condition is added,
    • Customer renews and upgrades their licence and an email address is added.

In the first example, the operator would:
    • Keep the signed licence history detail report showing the renewed effective dates and both classes, and
    • Securely dispose of the unsigned licence history detail report (which only shows the renewed effective dates for one class).

For replacement driver licence smartcard transactions, photocopy the signed driver licence receipt and include this with the filing.

Back office processes such as filing and business exceptions remain primarily unchanged.
Quality ControlAs part of the quality control processes for formless applications SA(SM)s/Managers or a suitable subject matter expert (must be approved by the Manager or SA/SM) will be required to monitor operators to ensure that they are adhering to all of the applicable mandatory questions. Observations are to occur regularly for all available staff (minimum 2 transactions per operator per month) and are to be recorded on the Observation Check Register. Operators are to be made aware of any errors and actions put in place to rectify. Available means that a staff member has been at work, in a front counter role for more working days in the month than they have been absent.

Solo Sites evaluation sheets are to be completed by visiting manager from supporting CSC at time of visit, no minimum requirement.

The observation check details can either be entered directly into the Observation Check Register on SharePoint or the completed evaluation sheets can be saved electronically and uploaded to the register. Entry into this register and acknowledgement by the processing operator satisfies the CSA requirements for a signature and the performance discussion component of the EDP.

Processing Operator Acknowledgement
Processing Operator Acknowledgement
    When an observation check entry has been made by the SA(SM)/Supervisor the processing operator that has been observed will receive an email notification that requires their action.

    The operator must take the following steps:
    1. Read the instructions in the email
    2. click on the link and complete the bottom section either acknowledging that the SA(SM)/Supervisor has or has not had a conversation with them about the observation (a simple 'that was good' comment or a lengthy detailed feedback session both mean you had a conversation in relation to the observation)
    3. Once completed press the purple icon in the top left corner. Please Note: If you click on the ‘Save and Submit to Processing Operator’ button you will receive another email notification.

The register provides a central repository for all observations and reduces the requirement for CSCs to store paper copies of the observation sheets. QAOs will be verifying the completion of Operator Evaluation Logs as part of the regular audit for each centre.

Note:The Operator Evaluation Log Sheets are not required to be completed and uploaded into the Observation Check Register, if the Observation Check Details in the register have been fully completed.
