Restricted Access Vehicles (5384)

Restricted Access Vehicles (RAV)
Restricted Access Vehicle means a heavy vehicle that (together with its load) is:
  • higher than 4.3m; or
  • wider than 2.5m; or
  • longer than:
    • if a single vehicle other than an articulated bus - 12.5m; or
    • if an articulated bus - 18m; or
    • if a combination - 19m.
  • This does not apply to a class 2 heavy vehicle.

A person must not use a Restricted Access Vehicle, or permit a Restricted Access Vehicle to be used, on a road unless the road is one on which the vehicle is allowed to be used under a mass or dimension authority applying to the vehicle.
Procedure - Inspections
To carry out a Restricted Access Vehicle inspection, Authorised officers should:
  • determine the class of vehicle
  • determine whether that class of vehicle is operating in a manner consistent with a Notice or Permit at the time of intercept.
Carriage of Notices and Permits
Notices
Drivers are not required to carry a copy of a Class 1, 2 or 3 heavy vehicle notice, or an information sheet about the Notice unless the Notice includes a specific condition stating that it must be carried by the driver while operating under the Notice.

A list of Notices that must be carried can be located on the NHVR website: www.nhvr.gov.au > Law & Policies > Notices and permit based schemes.

Permits
Drivers of Class 1, 2 or 3 heavy vehicles operating under a permit must keep a copy in their possession while operating under the permit.
Production of a Notice or Permit
Notices or Permits can be present to Authorised Officer in either hard or electronic copy.

Note:An electronic Notice or Permit must be produced upon request, it is not acceptable that the Notice or Permit has to be downloaded from another location at the time of intercept.

If the required Notice or Permit cannot be produced upon request, Authorised Officers should either:
  • Check if a Gazette Notice exits for the vehicle
  • Confirm if a Permit exists for the vehicle by contacting the NHVR

Where it is confirmed that a Notice or Permit exists for the vehicle, Authorised Officers should assess the vehicle against the conditions of the relevant Notice or Permit.

Where it is confirmed that a Notice or Permit does notexist for the vehicle, Authorised Officers should assess the vehicle against the relevant prescribed limits.

Contravening a requirement to keep relevant documents
If a driver operating under a Class 1, 2 or 3 Notice or Permit cannot produce the Notice or Permit he/she is required to keep in his/her possession, Authorised Officers should:
  • Note that this is not a condition offence; and
  • consider issuing the relevant sanction related to the requirement to keep documents.

Note:For PBS vehicles, a driver operating under a Class 2 authorisation must keep a copy of the PBS vehicle approval in their possession.
Operating Conditions
To determine whether a vehicle is complying with operating conditions, Authorised Officers should compare the vehicle to the conditions specified in the Notice or Permit, which may include (but not be limited to): Mass, Dimensions, Specified load, Vehicle type, Travel restrictions, Warning devices/signage, Pilot/Escort vehicle.

Permits that have been issued to Queensland registered vehicles can also be reviewed at:
https://www.service.qdot.qld.gov.au/ExcessMass/MainMenu.jsp

Note:Specific user access is required for this database. Access can be requested by lodging a Service Centre Online request.

If non-compliance with the Notice or Permit requirements/conditions is established, Authorised Officers may consider issuing the relevant sanctions.
Class 1 and Class 3 Vehicles
Non Mass or Dimension Condition Offences
Where a Class 1 or Class 3 Notice or Permit condition offence is detected that is not a mass or dimension offence, Authorised officers may consider issuing a Notice or Permit condition sanction.

Mass or Dimension Condition Offences
Where a Class 1 or Class 3 Notice or Permit Mass or Dimension condition offence is detected, Authorised Officers should:
  • disregard the exemption; and
  • categorise the offence/s and consider issuing a sanction with reference to the relevant prescribed mass and dimension limits applying to the non-compliance. (Only the offences of non-compliance with the Notice or Permit are action from prescribed limits).

Class 1 or Class 3 Vehicles - Operating without a Notice or Permit
For Restricted Access Vehicles detected operating on a road without a Notice or Permit, Authorised Officers may:
  • consider issuing the relevant sanction under HVNL s153A; or
  • consider issuing the relevant mass and/or dimension sanction under HVNL s96 and s102 respectively
Class 2 Vehicles (Non-Mass Exception Vehicles)
Condition Offences
Where a contravention of a Class 2 Notice or Permit (Heavy Vehicle Authorisation) is detected, Authorised Officers may consider issuing the relevant condition sanction.

Mass or Dimension Offences
The mass or dimension requirements for Class 2 vehicles are prescribed in the National Regulation and any offences detected will be categorised with reference to those prescribed limits.


Note:A Class 2 PBS vehicle that contravenes a mass or dimension requirement will be assessed against the mass and dimension limits prescribed in the PBS vehicle approval.

Note:If a Class 2 Notice or Permit specifies a limit lower than the prescribed limit apply to the vehicle, the limits in the Notice or Permit apply for enforcement action.

Class 2 vehicles - Operating without a Notice or Permit
Where a mass and dimension compliance Class 2 heavy vehicle detected operating without a Notice or Permit the Authorised Officer may consider issuing a sanction under HVNL s137.
Mass Exception Vehicles
Mass Exception vehicles are:
  • Concessional Mass Limits (CML)
  • Quad Axle Group Mass Exception Limits
  • Higher Mass Limits (HML)
  • 1-Tonne Mass Transfer Allowance (1TMTA).
Mass Exception Vehicles - Offences
Condition Offences
Where a vehicle operating under a mass exception is detected in contravention of a condition of the mass exception:
  • the mass exception does not apply; and
  • Authorised Officers should categorise the offence and consider issuing a sanction with reference to the relevant prescribed mass limits applying to the vehicle.

Note:A HML vehicle that has contravened a condition applying to the HML mass exception may be eligible for CML providing all CML requirements are met.
Note:The HML vehicle would not automatically be assessed against General Mass Limits (GML) because of the HML condition offence.

Mass Offences
Where a vehicle operating under a mass exception (other than the 1TMTA) is detected in contravention of a mass requirement applying to the vehicle, Authorised Officers should:
  • note that this is not a mass exception condition offence; and
  • categorise the offence/s and consider issuing a sanction with reference to the relevant mass exception limits apply to the vehicle as prescribed in Schedules 2 to 5A of the National Regulation.
Where a combination operating under the 1TMTA contavenes a mass requirement of the 1TMTA, the mass exception no longer applies and the combination will be assessed against GML.
Warning Signs
A person must not use, or permit to be used on a road a heavy vehicle that has the warning sign displayed on it unless the vehicle is of the particular type, size or configuration. E.g. 'LONG VEHICLE' or 'ROAD TRAIN'.

There is no specific HVNL requirement to remove warning signs when a vehicle is no longer in a configuration that requires them (e.g. B-Double or Road Train split up to form compliant 19m combination).

Where a vehicle is detected operating with warning signs fitted that are not required, providing at least half of the warning sign is removed or covered, not sanction should be issued. E.g. 'Long' or 'Vehicle' removed or covered - 'Road' or 'Train' removed or covered.
