Jointly Registered Vehicles - Dispute of Ownership (3105)

What is it?An ownership dispute where a jointly registered vehicle has been transferred from two registered operator names to one operator name using one signature.
Notification to TMR of DisputeCustomer provides written declaration, by completing F623, stating that the ownership of the vehicle is in dispute and one of the following documents:
  • Purchase receipt in the name of the disputing party
  • Court documents notifying that there are legal proceedings for property settlement either in the Family Court, Federal Magistrate’s Court or a property dispute in a State Court (must contain official court seal)
Recording Dispute and Advice to Parties
Customer Service Officer
Customer Service Officer
    Document dispute evidence
    Document dispute evidence
      Complete report for file (F1288) detailing supporting documents provided.
      • If a court document is provided, record the reasons for the court document, type of court document, date of court document and a contact person.
      • Attach copies of supporting documents.
    Add Text restriction
    Add Text restriction
      Attach a Text restriction to the registration record of the vehicle in dispute.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 08. Transfer >The How To: Transfer > Registration Transfer Procedures > Update Records > Update a TRAILS/TICA Record > Process change in TRAILS/TICA > Maintain Vehicle, Trailer and Recreational Ship Restrictions

      The Text restriction must advise the dispute details and CSC contact person of the issue. The TEXT must also advise that all transfers and cancellations are to be refused until resolved by either a court order or a document signed by both parties in the dispute is supplied.

      Example Text Message:
      Dispute of ownership recorded between Jennifer Ryan and Charlie Brown. Customer has provided court notification of a property settlement to be held at the Brisbane Family Court 01/06/2016 Reference D12456413.

      All transfers and cancellation applications are to be refused until either a court order or a document signed by both involved parties is provided.

      USERID, Carseldine CSC, x65555
    Send “Notice to Restrict Transactions” to parties
    Send “Notice to Restrict Transactions” to parties
      The notice must be sent to both parties involved in the dispute. A copy of the letter must be included with the report for file.

      Notice to Restrict Registration Transactions - July 2016

    File documents

Customer Service Officer
    • Refuse transfer application under section 51 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Registration) Regulation 2010 which allows the chief executive the right to refuse to register a transfer of a vehicle's registration if there is uncertainty about the ownership, possession or control of the vehicle.

Customer provides required documentation to resolve dispute
Customer
Customer
    Either of the disputing parties provides:
    • a court order; or
    • a consent agreement signed by both parties.
Customer Service Officer
Customer Service Officer
    Update Text restriction:
    • Update Text restriction attached to the registration record of the vehicle in dispute.
      Refer: Customer > 3. Change Customer Detail > The How To: Change Customer Detail > Change Customer Details Procedures > Customer Restrictions > Update Customer Restriction > Update Restriction (TICA)
    • The Text restriction must include how the matter was resolved and the resolution date.
    • Expire the Text restriction on the registration record of the vehicle in dispute.
      Refer: Customer > 3. Change Customer Detail > The How To: Change Customer Detail > Change Customer Details Procedures > Expire Restriction > Expire Restriction (TICA)
    Adjust registration records if required:
    Note:As a result of either a court order or consent agreement, there may be a requirement to process further registration transactions.
    • If the current registered operator needs to be amended the CSO must transfer the vehicle back into the joint names (the transfer must not be invalidated). This transfer is to be processed waiving all fees (Use the "Swap Names" exemption code for transfer and stamp duty fees).
    • A transfer application should then be completed to record the new registered operator as indicated on the court order or consent agreement (all applicable fees and requirements apply).

    File documents:
    • Include report for file and copies of supporting documentation with the daily registration filing.
