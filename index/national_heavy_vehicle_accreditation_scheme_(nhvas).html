National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS) (5244)

National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS)
What it isThe National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS) is an alternative compliance scheme that recognises operators who have robust safety and other management systems in place.

Heavy vehicle operators can apply for accreditation under the following NHVAS modules:
  • Mass Management
  • Maintenance Management
  • Fatigue Management:
    • Basic Fatigue Management (BFM)
    • Advanced Fatigue Management (AFM)

It is increasingly being used to show compliance with general duty requirements under road transport law.
Accreditation RequirementsA NHVAS accredited operator must comply with the relevant standards and business rules.

Refer to NHVAS Business Rules.
https://www.nhvr.gov.au/safety-accreditation-compliance/national-heavy-vehicle-accreditation-scheme/business-rules-and-standards.

Mass and Maintenance
Mass and Maintenance
    Documentation
    Drivers of heavy vehicles operating under the NHVAS Mass or Maintenance modules are not required to carry the operator's accreditation certificate or induction letter. How ever each nominated vehicle must carry a hard copy NHVAS Interception Report Book.

    There is no offence if a driver is not carrying a hard copy of the NHVAS interception book, the operator has failed to comply with the business rules.

    Forward all relevant details via email to accreditation@nhvr.com.au within 14 days

    Identification
    Vehicles operating under Mass and Maintenance modules must display labels. (Refer NHVAS work procedure 6.1.1)
    Accreditation labels, nominated vehicles and interception report books fact sheet (PDF, 326KB) (nhvr.gov.au)

    The NHVR portal contains an Active Vehicle Module (AVM) search tool that provides information about the accreditation status of heavy vehicles nominated in the Mass and/or Maintenance Management module(s) of the NHVAS.

    Information obtained from the AVM can assist Transport Inspectors when making decisions regarding possible enforcement action.

    https://www.service.nhvr.gov.au/#page=informationHub/nHVASAVMSearch

Buying and selling vehicles
Buying and selling vehicles
    Between scheme members
    • A vehicle may be transferred between accredited operators without the requirement for inspection documentation.
    • The disposer must submit an application the NHVR Accreditation team to delete vehicles to ensure the vehicles are deleted from their fleet listing.
    • The acquirer then must submit a separate application the NHVR Accreditation team to add the vehicles to their fleet listing.

    Dispose to non-scheme member
    • A QLD registered vehicle that is currently nominated in the maintenance management module that is disposed of to a non-member of the maintenance management module will require the disposer to obtain a Certificate of Inspection.
    • The acquirer must ensure they meet the state road transport authority’s requirements with regard to vehicle inspection or Certificate of Inspection requirement before they can use any vehicles on a road.
    • The disposer must submit an application to delete vehicles to ensure the vehicle is deleted from their fleet listing.
    Note: It is an offence to falsely claim accreditation through use of a label or any other means. Anyone who does so may be subject to a financial penalty in addition to other sanctions.
Fatigue Management (AFM & BFM)
Fatigue Management (AFM & BFM)
    Documentation
    Under section 468 of the HVNL – A driver operating under BFM or AFM accreditation must carry accreditation details. (Refer 6.2.1 NHVAS Work Procedure)

    Identification
    Drivers operating under BFM or AFM are not required to display labels on the vehicle as the driver and the operator are accredited not the vehicle.
    A driver who is not accredited under BFM or AFM may drive for an operator who is accredited under BFM or AFM.
Mass Management
Mass Management Accreditation
Mass Management Accreditation
    Heavy Vehicle Operators in the Mass Management module can access additional mass concessions. These concessions allow vehicles to operate at Concessional Mass Limits (CML) for general access to the road network.
    Participation in the NHVAS Mass Management Module is a pre-requisite for access to Higher Mass Limits (HML).
    NHVAS Mass Management Accreditation Guide (nhvr.gov.au)
Concessional Mass Limits (CML)
Concessional Mass Limits (CML)
    Concessional Mass Limits (CML) allows an operator to operate at mass limits above the national general limits provided the operator is accredited under the National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS).

    Companies wishing to benefit from the productivity gains of CML, are required to meet the eight standards of compliance in the Mass
    Management Accreditation Guide of the National Heavy Vehicle Regulator (NHVR).

    Refer NHVR Mass assessment work procedure 6.2.1, Appendix A and CML fact sheet for mass limits.

    https://www.nhvr.gov.au/files/fscp0001-nhvr-info-sheet-concessional-mass-limits-2019.pdf

    Conditions:
    • Manufacturers rating limits must not be exceeded.
    • Signed load limits on particular roads and bridges must be adhered to.

    One Tonne Mass Transfer Allowance (1TMTA) does not apply to vehicles operating on CML.
Higher Mass Limits (HML)
Higher Mass Limits (HML)
    Higher Mass Limits (HML) allows particular heavy vehicles to access additional mass entitlements providing:
    • operators of vehicles or combinations running HML on tri-axle groups are accredited under the Mass Management Module of the National Heavy Vehicle Accreditation Scheme (NHVAS), with an accreditation
      label fitted to the hauling unit
    • vehicles are fitted with certified road friendly suspension
    • vehicles are on an authorised HML route.

    HML provides a significant increase in the productivity of road freight transport vehicles.

    Refer to:
    NHVR Mass assessment work procedure 6.2.1, appendix A
    NHVR HML
    https://www.nhvr.gov.au/road-access/mass-dimension-and-loading/higher-mass-limits
    Conditions
    Conditions
      • In Queensland and NSW, Intelligent Access Program (IAP) participation is a requirement to operate under HML.
      • Manufacturers rating limits must not be exceeded.
      • Signed load limits on particular roads and bridges must be adhered to.
      • One Tonne Mass Transfer Allowance (1TMTA) does not apply to vehicles operating on HML.

Maintenance Management Accreditation
Maintenance Management Accreditation
    Maintenance Management Accreditation encourages heavy vehicle operators to take more responsibility for maintaining their vehicles correctly and ensuring they are always in good mechanical condition

    There are eight maintenance Management Standards and one optional standard with which operators need to comply.
    Refer: Maintenance Management Accreditation Guide
    Maintenance Management Accreditation Guide (PDF, 2.7MB) (nhvr.gov.au)
    Accredited Operators are exempt from complying with the requirement for a current Certificate of Inspection.
Fatigue Management Accreditation
Basic Fatigue Management (BFM)
Basic Fatigue Management (BFM)
    Operators with BFM accreditation can operate under more flexible work and rest hours, allowing for (among other things) work of up to 14 hours in a 24 hour period. BFM gives operators some flexibility in when drivers can work and rest, as long as the risks of driver fatigue are properly managed.
    Fatigue Management Accreditation Guide (2.5MB) (nhvr.gov.au)
Advanced Fatigue Management (AFM)
Advanced Fatigue Management (AFM)
    AFM is a risk management approach to managing heavy vehicle driver fatigue. Rather than prescribing work and rest hours, AFM offers more flexibility than Standard Hours or BFM in return for the operator demonstrating greater accountability for managing their drivers’ fatigue risks.
    https://www.nhvr.gov.au/safety-accreditation-compliance/fatigue-management/work-and-rest-requirements/advanced-fatigue-management-afm
