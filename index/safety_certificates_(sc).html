Safety Certificates (SC) (7135)

What are theySafety Certificates (SC) are issued by Approved Inspection Stations (AIS) confirming that the vehicle has passed the prescribed inspection. This inspection ensures that the vehicle complies with set safety requirements. SC can be either manually issued or electronically issued via the Inspection Certificates Online (ICO) portal.
Requirements
When Required
When Required
    A valid SC is required (unless exempt)
    • When registering a used vehicle at new business
    • When offering a registered vehicle for sale (including interstate vehicles)
      • Manually issued SC must be attached/displayed on a conspicuous part of the vehicle
      • Electronic SC are issued via Inspection Certificate Online (ICO). Both assigned and unassigned electronic SC, don't need to be displayed, but must be provided if requested by a buyer or buyers agent
    • When transferring registration of a vehicle
    • To be produced upon request by an authorised officer, for booked hire vehicles (excluding taxis and limousines). Refer below: Currency of Safety Certificate for more information.
    An online service that allows customers and staff to search for an AIS is located on the TMR website at: Find an AIS

Rules
Conditions of Use
Conditions of Use
    A SC:
    • Can be used for one New Business then one transfer while current (determined from Date of Issue)
      Note:Where the SC was issued electronically, the customer acquiring the vehicle is required to present the SC number only at time of lodging the transfer.
    • Cannot be used for two transfers
    • Cannot contain alterations
Exemptions
Validity of Safety Certificates
Validity of Safety Certificates
    A SC can be accepted if it:
    • Is issued by a Queensland AIS
    • Is the correct type
    • Is current
    • Is fully completed
    • Is the original (if manually issued)
    • Contains no alterations
    • Is signed (if manually issued)
    • Is an electronic certificate

    Electronic SC are to be treated the same as handwritten SC. This means if the SC does not meet all of the above requirements it is to be refused. Advise the customer to return to the issuing AIS or get a new SC.

    If the AIS number consists of 2 or 3 digits, you must add zero’s (0) before the AIS number (to make the AIS a 4 digit number) when entering into TICA.
    • For example, if the AIS number recorded on the inspection certificate is 12, you need to enter 0012 in to the AIS number field in TICA.
    • Similarly, if the AIS number recorded on the inspection certificate is 345, you need to enter 0345 in to the AIS number field in TICA.
    Alternatively, you can select the AIS name from the drop-down button.
    Cancellation of a safety certificate due to registration cancellation
    Cancellation of a safety certificate due to registration cancellation

      A SC is void if issued prior to the registration being cancelled except where:
      • the registration is cancelled by a Customer Service Officer* for the purpose of processing a new business (Cancelled for reason of Re-registration [RERG]); or
      • the registration is auto-cancelled after the date the customer purchased the vehicle and the safety certificate has been issued after the date the vehicle was sold.
      *this option is not available for dealers.
      Example when certificate is still valid: Sold on 1 May 2010, Safety Certificate issued on 10 May 2010 but registration has been auto-cancelled after 1 May 2010.
      Example when certificate is not valid: Sold on 1 May 2010, Safety Certificate issued on 29 April 2010 (prior to sale), registration has been auto-cancelled on 3 May 2010.

      If the registration has been cancelled for any other reason including stolen, wrecked or destroyed the Safety Certificate should not be accepted. (Refer: Quick Reference Table below)


      Certificate issued prior to cancellation date
      Certificate issued prior to cancellation date

        Inspection Certificate Online (ICO)
        If the vehicle has an electronic certificate issued from ICO prior to the cancellation date, it will automatically cancel when the registration is cancelled. If the certificate should still be valid (per above rules), contact HelpDirect by phone.

        Manually issued certificates
        TICA will not automatically identify if a manually issued Safety Certificate has been dated prior to the cancellation of the vehicle's registration, it is the responsibility of the processing officer to check the cancellation date.

      Note:In the instance where the SC issue date and the cancellation date are the same, check the time on the Safety Certificate and the time in TICA history for the cancellation action. This will confirm if the SC was issued before or after the cancellation.

Currency of Safety Certificate
Currency of Safety Certificate
    Currency is calculated from the SC issue date to either the:
    • New business date
    or
    • Disposal date for a transfer

    SC can be used for one New Business and one Transfer provided they are within the 2 mths/2000 km limit. It cannot be used for two transfers.

    Where the SC details are already recorded on TICA, the customer acquiring the vehicle is not required to present a copy of the SC at time of lodging transfer. If the SC is current, TICA will not require SC details to be entered. However, If the SC is no longer valid or is expired, TICA will prompt for a new SC.

Inspection Certificates Online (ICO)
Inspection Certificates Online (ICO)
    ICO is an online portal that allows Approved Examiners at an Approved Inspection Station (AIS) to create electronic safety certificates and certificates of inspection which are stored and emailed to the customer.

    ICO issues both assigned and unassigned inspection certificates, both of which can be viewed in full by CSO's on the ICO portal

    All safety certificates issued to motorcycles must be checked to ensure seating capacity matches TICA.

    Assigned certificates
    Assigned certificates
      Assigned certificates:
      • Begin with the number 1 and are automatically applied to vehicles that currently exist in TICA
      • Have had the vehicle details (VIN or plate and chassis number) verified against TICA and the approved examiner has confirmed the basic vehicle details (make, model, body shape, compliance date) returned from TICA match the vehicle they are inspecting
      • Have the ICO inspection certificate details automatically lodged in TICA, including the issue date, inspection certificate number, AIS number and odometer reading. (This information is located in the Inspection Hub).

      The customer only needs to provide the complete certificate number (eg. LV-10876608-6) to TMR for a registration transaction.

      As the details of the certificate are recorded in TICA, operators are not required to access the ICO system or print the certificate. However, operators may access ICO and view the certificate to check those vehicle details that are not validated such as colour and seating.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > The How To: Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Requirements Procedures > Access ICO as CSO

    Unassigned Certificates
    Unassigned Certificates
      Unassigned certificates:
      • Begin with the number 3 and are issued to vehicles that:
      • Do not have a VIN or chassis number recorded in TICA, or
      • The vehicle details recorded in TICA are different to the details on the vehicle (therefore not verified through ICO);
      • Details are not automatically lodged in TICA.
      • Must be manually entered into TICA - Operators are required to access ICO to look up/print the certificate and enter the details into TICA (in the same way a hand-written certificate is entered).
      • CSC staff can view the full inspection certificate in ICO.

      The customer only needs to provide the complete certificate number (eg. LV-30876608-6) to TMR for a registration transaction.

      Refer: Vehicle Rego > 05. Vehicle Requirements > The How To: Vehicle Requirements > Vehicle Requirements Procedures > Access ICO as CSO

      • There is one ICO account and password for each CSC. Your ICO password must be reset every 30 days.

When Can a Copy be Accepted
When Can a Copy be Accepted
    A copy of a SC is only acceptable when a:
    • Dealer has ceased trading but not transferred the vehicle to the new registered operator
    • A notice of disposal has been lodged by the disposer
Discrepancies and Illegible Identifiers
Data Entry Errors
Data Entry Errors
    Manual (Paper) Safety Certificates
    SPECPRO operators can only invalidate manually issued AIS safety certificates and certificates of inspection for TMR operator data entry errors only. All other errors such as duplicate certificates must be referred to HelpDirect for investigation.

    Electronic (ICO) Safety Certificates
    All errors on an electronically issued safety certificates must be referred back to the issuer.

Display of SC on Registered Vehicle for Sale
Display of SC on Registered Vehicle for Sale
    From the moment that a registered vehicle is offered for sale a SC or the blue cover label must be displayed on a conspicuous part of the vehicle and all information stated on the front of the SC or blue cover label must be clearly legible from outside the vehicle. This is a requirement even if the disposer of the vehicle is going to cancel the vehicle registration when it is sold.

    If the disposer has an electronic SC it does not need to be displayed, however, they must produce for inspection a copy of the certificate at the request of a prospective buyer or buyer's agent.

    Note:If the disposer fails to display a manually issued SC on the vehicle they may receive an on-the-spot fine under Transport Operations (Road Use Management - Vehicle Standards and Safety Regulations s23
Complaints about Non-display of SC
Complaints about Non-display of SC
    If any complaints from the public are received in relation to the non-display of a SC, the operator is to contact their region's Senior Transport Inspector for possible investigation.
